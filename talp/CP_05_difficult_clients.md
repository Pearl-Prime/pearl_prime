# Red Flag Clients: A Field Guide

## For: Anyone who's ever said "this project turned into a nightmare"

Not all clients are good clients. Some will waste your time. Some will drain your energy. Some will make you question your career choices.

**Learn to spot them early. Walk away often.**

### The Red Flags (Before You Start)

**🚩 "This should be easy/quick"**  
Translation: They don't value your work and will fight every invoice

**🚩 "I have a small budget but huge exposure"**  
Translation: They're broke and exposure doesn't pay rent

**🚩 "I'll know it when I see it"**  
Translation: Infinite revisions incoming

**🚩 "Can you just mock up a few ideas first?"**  
Translation: Free work disguised as "audition"

**🚩 They want to meet multiple times before hiring**  
Translation: They're collecting free consulting

**🚩 "We'll start small and scale up"**  
Translation: They'll milk the small project forever

**🚩 They bad-mouth their previous creatives**  
Translation: They'll bad-mouth you next

**🚩 They won't sign a contract**  
Translation: They'll screw you over

**🚩 They want work before discussing payment**  
Translation: They don't plan to pay fairly

**🚩 They ghost between messages**  
Translation: Unreliable, will disappear mid-project

### The Framework: VETTING

**V - Verify they can pay**  
Ask budget upfront. If they dodge, walk away.

**E - Expectations in writing**  
Scope, deliverables, timeline, revisions. Everything.

**T - Trust your gut**  
If something feels off, it is.

**T - Test their communication**  
Slow to respond now = nightmare later

**I - Insist on contracts**  
No contract = no work. Period.

**N - No free work**  
Spec work, test projects, "auditions" = unpaid labor

**G - Get deposit upfront**  
50% before you start. Protect yourself.

### Client Types to Avoid

**The Micromanager**
- Needs approval on every tiny decision
- Questions your expertise constantly
- Drains time with endless check-ins
- **How to spot:** Wants daily updates, CCs everyone, sends messages at midnight

**The Scope Creeper**
- "While you're at it, can you also..."
- Keeps adding "small" requests
- Treats you like their employee
- **How to spot:** "Just one more thing" in every email

**The Ghost**
- Urgent project! Then disappears
- Takes weeks to give feedback
- Blames delays on you
- **How to spot:** Inconsistent communication from the start

**The Negotiator**
- Always trying to lower your rate
- Questions every line item
- Wants more for less
- **How to spot:** Asks for "friendly discount" before you've even started

**The "Creative"**
- Has tons of ideas (all bad)
- Wants to art-direct you
- Fights every professional suggestion
- **How to spot:** "I'm pretty creative myself"

**The Exploiter**
- "This will be great for your portfolio!"
- "Think of the exposure!"
- "We're like a family here"
- **How to spot:** Selling you on the opportunity instead of offering fair pay

### What Good Clients Look Like

**They:**
- Respond promptly and professionally
- Have a clear brief and budget
- Respect your process
- Pay on time
- Give useful feedback
- Trust your expertise
- Refer other good clients

**If a client doesn't match this, think twice.**

### How to Fire a Bad Client

**Do this:**
1. Deliver what's been paid for
2. Send professional exit email
3. Return any unearned deposit
4. Document everything
5. Move on

**Email template:**

"After further consideration, I don't think I'm the right fit for this project. I'm returning your deposit and wish you the best in finding someone who better matches your needs."

**That's it. No explanation needed.**

### Setting Boundaries Mid-Project

**When scope creeps:**  
"That's outside the original scope. I can do it for $[X] additional. Would you like a quote?"

**When they micromanage:**  
"I need space to work. Let's schedule weekly check-ins instead of daily messages."

**When they want free revisions:**  
"The contract includes [X] rounds. Additional revisions are $[Y] per hour."

**When they push deadlines:**  
"I can rush this for a 50% rush fee, or we can stick to the original timeline."

**When they don't pay:**  
"Work stops until payment is received. No exceptions."

### Permission Slips

You're allowed to:
- Turn down work
- Fire clients
- Charge more for difficult clients
- Require deposits
- Enforce contracts
- Walk away mid-project (with refund)
- Say no to rush jobs
- Block them after the project
- Not work with referrals from them
- Protect your energy

### The Cost of Bad Clients

**They cost you:**
- Mental health
- Time you could spend on good clients
- Creative energy
- Money (when you undercharge to get it over with)
- Reputation (if they bad-mouth you)
- Confidence

**Not worth it. Ever.**

### Bottom Line

**Good clients exist. Work with them.**  
**Bad clients exist. Avoid them.**

Your business, your rules.

You're not desperate. You're selective.

**Raise your standards. Watch your business improve.**
