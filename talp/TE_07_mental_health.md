# Your Mental Health Matters (Even Though the System Acts Like It Doesn't)

## For: Everyone crying in their car before going into the building

You're:
- Anxious every Sunday night
- Crying regularly
- Not sleeping
- Dreading work
- Feeling hopeless
- Questioning everything
- Wondering if you can make it

**This isn't weakness. This is what happens when you do emotional labor in a broken system.**

### The Teacher Mental Health Crisis

**The stats:**
- 61% of teachers report work as "always" or "often" stressful
- Teachers have higher rates of depression and anxiety than general population
- 44% of teachers report high daily stress (highest of all professions)
- Burnout rates keep climbing
- Mental health is why teachers leave

**This is a systemic problem, not a personal failure.**

### The Framework: WELLNESS

**W - Watch for warning signs**  
Depression/anxiety don't announce themselves

**E - Establish boundaries**  
Protect your mental space

**L - Let go of savior complex**  
You can't fix everything

**L - Lean on support**  
Therapy, friends, colleagues

**N - No martyrdom**  
Suffering doesn't make you a better teacher

**E - Exercise and basics**  
Sleep, movement, food

**S - Separate work from self**  
You are not your job

**S - Seek professional help**  
Therapy isn't optional for many of us

### Compassion Fatigue

**What it is:**
- Secondary trauma from hearing student stories
- Absorbing their pain
- Emotional exhaustion from caring
- Numbness or over-sensitivity
- Unable to separate their problems from yours

**How it shows up:**
- Dreading certain students' stories
- Avoiding emotional conversations
- Feeling nothing when you used to feel everything
- Taking on their trauma as your own
- Can't stop thinking about students at home

**This is different from burnout. This is trauma exposure.**

### Specific Teacher Mental Health Issues

**Anxiety:**
- Sunday dread
- Constant worry about lessons/students
- Fear of parent complaints
- Observation anxiety
- Performance pressure
- Feeling like you're never doing enough

**Depression:**
- Can't find joy in teaching anymore
- Hopelessness about making a difference
- Exhaustion that sleep doesn't fix
- Isolating from colleagues
- Loss of purpose

**Burnout:**
- Emotional exhaustion
- Depersonalization (not caring)
- Reduced sense of accomplishment
- Cynicism
- Just going through motions

**PTSD (yes, really):**
- From school shootings/lockdowns
- Student threats
- Violent situations
- Trauma exposure

### Warning Signs You Need Help

**Immediate help needed:**
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Suicidal ideation
- Can't function
- Panic attacks preventing work
- Complete breakdown

**Professional help recommended:**
- Crying multiple times per week
- Can't sleep for weeks
- Anxiety interfering with teaching
- Depression lasting months
- Drinking to cope
- Avoiding work regularly
- Physical symptoms (headaches, stomach issues, etc.)
- Can't enjoy anything
- Rage/irritability constant

**Self-care might help:**
- Occasional bad days
- Stress about specific situation
- Temporary overwhelm
- Normal work stress

**Be honest about which category you're in.**

### What Actually Helps

**Therapy:**
- Find therapist who understands teaching
- CBT for anxiety
- EMDR for trauma
- Regular sessions, not just crisis
- Not weakness, necessary

**Medication:**
- Sometimes needed
- Work with psychiatrist
- Can help you function enough to do therapy
- Not a character flaw

**Boundaries:**
- Work stays at work
- Time off is OFF
- Email limits
- Saying no
- Protecting weekends

**Community:**
- Teacher support groups
- Trusted colleagues
- Non-teacher friends (perspective)
- Online communities
- People who get it

**Basics:**
- Sleep (non-negotiable)
- Exercise (walk counts)
- Eat real food
- Limit alcohol
- Sunlight/outside

### The Sunday Scaries

**Every. Single. Sunday.**

Afternoon hits and the dread begins.  
Anxiety rises.  
Weekend is over.  
Here we go again.

**Managing it:**

**Saturday boundary:**
- No school thoughts Saturday
- Full day off
- Do fun things
- Recharge completely

**Sunday prep:**
- Morning: No school talk
- Afternoon: Max 2-3 hours work
- Evening: Relax/prep for Monday
- Early bedtime
- Positive Sunday tradition

**Acceptance:**
- Some dread is normal
- Not going away completely
- Manage it, don't eliminate it

**If Sunday dread is debilitating, that's a sign something needs to change.**

**Teaching is lonely:**
- In classroom all day alone
- Everyone's too busy
- Can't talk about struggles (judgment)
- Competitive environment
- Hard to ask for help

**Breaking isolation:**

**Find your people:**
- One trusted colleague
- Teacher friend at different school
- Online teacher groups
- Former colleagues
- Your person who gets it

**Regular connection:**
- Lunch together
- After-school decompression
- Text check-ins
- Vent sessions (scheduled)

**Professional community:**
- Subject-specific groups
- Grade-level teams
- Online forums
- Conferences

**Non-teacher friends:**
- Perspective from outside
- Remind you there's life beyond school
- Don't talk about school

**You need both.**

### Dealing With Trauma Exposure

**What you hear from students:**
- Abuse
- Neglect
- Violence
- Poverty
- Loss
- Trauma

**This affects you. Acknowledge it.**

**Processing:**
- Talk to someone (therapist)
- Don't carry it alone
- Journal
- Creative outlets
- EMDR if needed

**Boundaries:**
- Listen, but don't take it home
- Report what you must
- Connect to resources
- But you're not their therapist

**Self-care:**
- Extra support when heavy stuff happens
- Don't minimize impact on you
- Allow yourself to feel
- Then let it go

### When Medication Helps

**Signs you might need medication:**
- Can't function despite therapy
- Anxiety prevents teaching
- Depression won't lift
- Sleep severely disrupted
- Physical symptoms
- Nothing else helping

**Common medications:**
- SSRIs for anxiety/depression
- Sleep aids if needed
- Anti-anxiety as needed
- Work with psychiatrist

**Myths to ignore:**
- "You're weak if you need meds"
- "Just try harder"
- "Teachers shouldn't need medication"
- "It'll change who you are"

**Reality:**
- Brain chemistry is real
- Medication enables functioning
- You can't therapy your way out of biology
- Teachers are human

**If you need it, take it.**

### The Perfectionism Trap

**Teacher perfectionism:**
- Every lesson should be amazing
- Every student should succeed
- You should never have bad days
- You should do it all yourself
- Asking for help is failing

**This will destroy you.**

**Reality check:**
- Some lessons suck. That's okay.
- Some students fail despite your best efforts
- Bad days are normal
- You need help
- Asking for help is smart

**Good enough is actually good enough.**

### Taking Mental Health Days

**You should take them when:**
- Panic attack incoming
- Can't stop crying
- Didn't sleep
- Dread is overwhelming
- Need to avoid breakdown
- Mental health urgent

**Don't feel guilty:**
- These are YOUR sick days
- Mental health = physical health
- Taking one day prevents longer absence
- You're useless to students if falling apart

**Use your sick days. That's what they're for.**

### When To Consider Leaving

**Red flags:**
- Dreading work every single day
- Physical health suffering
- Relationships destroyed
- Medication + therapy not enough
- No joy, only survival
- Counting days to retirement (and you're 28)
- Fantasizing about any other job
- Your mental health getting worse, not better

**Leaving teaching doesn't make you a failure.**

**Options:**
- Different school
- Different district
- Different role in education
- Different career entirely
- Take a break/sabbatical

**Your mental health > staying in a job that's killing you**

### Managing During School Day

**Quick resets:**
- Bathroom breathing break
- Close door between classes
- Eat lunch away from classroom
- Walk outside during planning
- Call trusted colleague

**Longer-term:**
- Planning period for planning (not other stuff)
- Leave building for lunch sometimes
- Don't work through prep
- Take your contracted breaks
- Ask for coverage if needed

**Your well-being during the day matters.**

### Talking to Admin About Mental Health

**If you need accommodations:**
- Be specific about needs
- Frame as temporary support
- Suggest solutions
- Get it in writing
- Know your rights (ADA if applicable)

**What you might ask for:**
- Reduced load temporarily
- Schedule adjustments
- Support coverage
- Mental health days without questions
- Resources

**Some admin will support. Some won't. Try anyway.**
