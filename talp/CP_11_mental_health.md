# Your Mental Health Matters More Than Your Portfolio

## For: Every creative struggling with anxiety, depression, or burnout

You can make incredible work while being deeply unhappy.  
You can build a successful career while falling apart inside.  
You can have thousands of followers while feeling completely alone.

**Success means nothing if you're miserable.**

### The Creative Mental Health Crisis

**What's normalized in creative industries:**
- Working through depression
- Anxiety as "part of the process"
- Burnout as a badge of honor
- Sacrificing mental health for deadlines
- "Tortured artist" as an identity

**What should be normalized:**
- Taking mental health days
- Therapy as standard practice
- Boundaries around work
- Rest without guilt
- Asking for help

### The Specific Struggles

**Anxiety:**
- Imposter syndrome
- Fear of rejection
- Comparison to others
- Financial instability
- Performance pressure
- Client demands

**Depression:**
- Creative blocks feel like failure
- Isolation in solo work
- Boom-bust income cycles
- Lack of structure
- Purpose questioning
- Comparison despair

**Burnout:**
- Creating becomes obligation
- Everything feels like work
- Joy is gone
- Exhaustion is constant
- Can't rest effectively
- Resentment builds

### The Framework: WELLBEING

**W - Watch for signs**  
Depression, anxiety, burnout don't announce themselves

**E - Establish routines**  
Structure helps even when you resist it

**L - Limit triggers**  
Unfollow accounts that make you feel inadequate

**L - Lean on support**  
Therapy, friends, peers, community

**B - Boundaries are essential**  
Work stops. Life exists.

**E - Exercise and sleep**  
Basic but critical

**I - Invest in treatment**  
Therapy isn't optional for many of us

**N - Normalize struggle**  
You're not weak for struggling

**G - Give yourself grace**  
Bad days happen. They pass.

### Warning Signs You Need Help

**Immediate help needed:**
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Can't get out of bed for days
- Substance use to cope
- Complete loss of function
- Suicidal thoughts

**Professional help recommended:**
- Constant anxiety
- Persistent depression
- Panic attacks
- Sleep disruption for weeks
- Can't enjoy anything
- Isolation increasing
- Work quality declining

**Self-care might help:**
- Occasional bad days
- Stress about specific project
- Temporary creative block
- Comparison spirals
- Mild overwhelm

**Be honest about which category you're in.**

### What Actually Helps

**Therapy:**
- Find someone who gets creative work
- CBT for anxiety
- DBT for emotional regulation
- EMDR for trauma
- Regular sessions, not just crisis management

**Medication:**
- Not weakness
- Sometimes necessary
- Can enable you to do therapy work
- Work with a psychiatrist
- Give it time to work

**Structure:**
- Regular sleep schedule
- Designated work hours
- Meal routines
- Exercise habits
- Social time

**Community:**
- Other creatives who understand
- Non-work friends
- Support groups
- Online communities
- Regular connection

**Boundaries:**
- When work stops
- When you check email
- How many revisions
- What projects you take
- Who gets your time

### The Creativity-Mental Health Loop

**Mental health affects creativity:**  
Depression kills motivation. Anxiety paralyzes. Burnout destroys joy.

**Creativity affects mental health:**  
Comparison breeds anxiety. Rejection triggers depression. Hustle culture causes burnout.

**They're connected. Treat them together.**

### Specific Strategies

**For Anxiety:**
- Name what you're anxious about specifically
- Reality-test worst case scenarios
- Box breathing: 4-4-4-4
- Limit caffeine
- Move your body
- Talk to someone
- Use deadlines strategically

**For Depression:**
- Small wins count
- Move even when you don't want to
- Connect even when you want to isolate
- Create schedule even when motivation is gone
- Don't believe the negative self-talk
- Get outside
- Track mood patterns

**For Burnout:**
- Stop. Actually stop working.
- Take a real break
- Evaluate workload honestly
- Drop commitments
- Protect rest time
- Reassess priorities
- Consider therapy

### The Medication Question

**If medication helps you function, take it.**

You wouldn't shame a diabetic for insulin.  
Don't shame yourself for brain chemistry help.

**Common myths:**
- "It'll kill my creativity" (usually doesn't)
- "I should tough it out" (that's not how brain chemistry works)
- "It's a crutch" (it's treatment)
- "Natural solutions only" (try them, but don't suffer unnecessarily)

**Work with a psychiatrist. Give it time. Adjust as needed.**

### When Work Makes It Worse

**If your creative work consistently:**
- Triggers anxiety attacks
- Deepens depression
- Causes insomnia
- Ruins relationships
- Makes you hate yourself

**Something needs to change. Options:**
- Different type of work
- Different clients
- Part-time creative, stable day job
- Career pivot
- Extended break

**Your mental health > your career. Always.**

### The Comparison Trap (Again)

**Social media is designed to make you feel inadequate.**

Everyone's showing highlights.  
No one posts their breakdowns.  
Success looks effortless from outside.

**What you don't see:**
- Their therapy sessions
- Their medication
- Their crying at 3am
- Their imposter syndrome
- Their rejections
- Their financial stress

**Stop comparing your full reality to their curated facade.**

### Building Support Systems

**You need:**
- Professional help (therapist, psychiatrist)
- Creative community (people who get it)
- Non-creative friends (perspective)
- Emergency contact (who to call in crisis)
- Accountability (someone who checks in)

**Don't try to do this alone. Seriously.**

### Permission Slips

You're allowed to:
- Take mental health days
- Step back from projects
- Say no to opportunities
- Take medication
- See a therapist
- Not be productive
- Rest without guilt
- Choose mental health over deadlines
- Leave toxic situations
- Change careers if needed
- Ask for help
- Not be "on" all the time

### The Bottom Line Truth

**Your worth isn't your productivity.**  
**Your value isn't your portfolio.**  
**Your success isn't your follower count.**

You are a person who creates things.  
Not a creating machine.

**Take care of the person. The work will follow.**

If it doesn't? The person still matters more.

### What to Do Today

**Pick one:**
- Schedule a therapy appointment
- Text someone you trust
- Take a full day off
- Delete social media for 24 hours
- Write down what you need help with
- Research therapists
- Tell someone you're struggling
- Take a walk
- Sleep

**Then tomorrow, pick another.**

**Small steps. Consistent care. That's how you survive a creative career.**
