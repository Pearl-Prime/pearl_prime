# Stop Undercharging - A Survival Guide

## For: Every creative who's terrified to name their price

Let's get one thing straight: Undercharging isn't humility. It's self-sabotage.

You're not doing anyone a favor by working for pennies. You're just teaching the market that creative work is worthless.

**Stop it.**

### The Lies You Tell Yourself

**"I'm not experienced enough to charge that much"**  
False. You have skills they don't. That's worth money.

**"They can't afford it"**  
Not your problem. If they can't afford you, they can't afford the project.

**"I just need the exposure"**  
Exposure doesn't pay rent. Next.

**"What if they say no?"**  
Then they say no. Someone else will say yes.

**"I don't want to seem greedy"**  
Corporations charge what they're worth. You should too.

### What You're Actually Worth

**Base calculation:**
- What do you need to live? (rent, food, insurance, student loans)
- Add 30% for taxes and business expenses
- Divide by billable hours (NOT total hours - account for admin, sick days, slow months)
- That's your MINIMUM hourly rate

**Then add:**
- Your skill level
- Your experience
- The client's budget
- The project's value to THEM
- Rush fees if they're in a hurry
- Difficulty/complexity multiplier

### The Real Talk Pricing Framework

**Starter rate:** Covers survival + some savings  
**Professional rate:** Covers life + retirement + growth  
**Premium rate:** What you charge when you're in demand

**You should be moving up this ladder, not staying stuck at starter rates because you're "being nice."**

### What to Say When They Balk

**Client:** "That seems high"  
**You:** "That's my rate for this scope of work. Would you like to adjust the scope?"

**Client:** "My budget is lower"  
**You:** "I can work within that budget if we reduce deliverables. What's most important?"

**Client:** "Can you do it for exposure/experience/portfolio?"  
**You:** "I appreciate the offer, but I'm not taking unpaid work right now. Let me know if budget becomes available."

**Client:** "Other designers charge less"  
**You:** "That's great! You should hire them. My rates reflect my experience and approach."

### The Hard Truth

**Cheap clients:**
- Demand the most revisions
- Respect you the least
- Pay the slowest
- Cause the most stress

**Clients who pay well:**
- Value your expertise
- Trust your process
- Pay on time
- Refer other good clients

**You're not pricing people out. You're pricing the wrong people out.**

### Permission Slip

You're allowed to:
- Say no to low-paying work
- Raise your rates
- Turn down dream projects that underpay
- Charge more than your peers if you're better
- Walk away from negotiations that don't value you

### Do This Today

1. Calculate your actual minimum rate
2. Add 20% to account for your skills
3. That's your new baseline
4. Don't quote below it
5. Ever

### Bottom Line

**Your work has value.**  
**Your time has value.**  
**Your expertise has value.**

Stop giving it away.

**Charge what you're worth. Then charge a little more.**
