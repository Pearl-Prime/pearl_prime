# WRITER ONBOARDING — SHAME ENGINE

One Page. Read Before Writing.

---

## WHAT SHAME IS

**Core Question:** Self collapsing after being seen?

Shame is **exposure + contraction**. Someone saw something. Now the self feels smaller, wrong, wanting to disappear.

Not fear. Not thoughts. Not distance. Not volume. Not loss.
**Exposure.**

---

## THE SHAME PATTERN

1. A moment of being seen (real or perceived)
2. Internal collapse ("something is wrong with *me*")
3. Urge to hide, shrink, disappear, undo

The danger isn't external. The danger is **being known**.

---

## SHAME TRIGGERS (ROTATE THESE)

| Trigger | Example |
|---------|---------|
| Saying wrong thing | Comment lands weird at lunch |
| Corrected publicly | Teacher fixes answer in front of class |
| Laughed at | Someone snickers in hallway |
| Singled out | Called on when unprepared |
| Others noticed | Saw you fail, struggle, slip |
| Body exposed | Tripped, spilled, visible sweat |

Every atom needs a **specific exposure moment**. Not vague. Not abstract.

---

## SHAME BODY SIGNALS (USE THESE)

- Face heat / flushing
- Chest drop / sinking
- Shoulders curling inward
- Stomach sinking
- Throat tightening
- Urge to look away / down
- Legs wanting to move / exit

**NOT:** Racing heart, sweating palms, checking behavior (that's FALSE_ALARM)

---

## SOCIAL CONTEXT (REQUIRED)

Shame requires a witness (real or implied).

Every shame atom must include:
- Named person who saw, OR
- Group who was present, OR
- Implied audience (group chat, post, etc.)

If no one could have seen it, it's not shame.

---

## ROLE CONSTRAINTS

| Role | What Happens | Ending |
|------|--------------|--------|
| Recognition | Inside the collapse, wanting to disappear | No exit, still shrinking |
| Mechanism Proof | Pattern repeats—same collapse, different moments | Collapse is reliable |
| Turning Point | Something cracks the collapse (time passed, someone else too, named it) | Smaller than expected, not fixed |
| Embodiment | Small protective action taken | Action only, no confidence |

---

## TURNING POINT MECHANISMS (PICK ONE)

- **Time passed** — The moment faded naturally
- **Someone else too** — They shared their own mistake
- **Named it** — "That's shame" (internal recognition)
- **Reality didn't match** — They forgot / didn't notice
- **Smaller than felt** — Context revealed scale

Turning point is **crack**, not cure. No self-esteem boost.

---

## EMBODIMENT ACTIONS (PICK ONE)

- Leaving early (physical exit)
- Looking at phone (visual escape)
- Changing seats (spatial move)
- Grounding through touch (object contact)
- Staying quiet on purpose (chosen silence)

**NOT:** Standing tall, speaking up, owning it, laughing it off.
Embodiment is **protective**, not triumphant.

---

## SHAME vs. OTHER ENGINES

| If You See This... | It's NOT Shame |
|--------------------|----------------|
| Thought chains (A→B→C) | That's SPIRAL |
| Checking behavior | That's FALSE_ALARM |
| Watching self from outside | That's WATCHER |
| Too many inputs | That's OVERWHELM |
| Relative position / "they have" | That's COMPARISON |
| Absence / loss | That's GRIEF |

Shame is collapse from exposure. Nothing else.

---

## LANGUAGE BANS (SHAME-SPECIFIC)

Auto-reject if you write:

- "embarrassed" (too soft)
- "humiliated" (too dramatic)
- "ashamed" (naming the engine)
- "self-esteem"
- "confidence"
- "owned it" / "laughed it off"
- "no one cares" (insight)
- "everyone makes mistakes" (comfort)

Show the collapse. Don't name it or fix it.

---

## COMMON FAILURE MODES

| Failure | Why It's Wrong |
|---------|----------------|
| Moral growth ending | Shame atoms don't teach resilience |
| "I realized no one noticed" | That's insight, not embodiment |
| Character becomes confident | That's a different story |
| Shame about systemic issue | Too abstract—needs specific moment |
| No witness present | Shame requires exposure |

---

## SELF-CHECK (SHAME-SPECIFIC)

Before submitting, verify:

- [ ] Specific exposure moment (not vague)
- [ ] Someone saw or could have seen
- [ ] Body signal present (face heat, chest drop, etc.)
- [ ] No thought chains (not spiral)
- [ ] No empowerment or growth
- [ ] Ending matches role exactly

---

## ONE-LINE TEST

> **If the character could feel this alone in an empty room, it's not shame.**

Shame needs eyes. Real or imagined.

## QUESTIONS?

- Shame vs. Comparison confusion → COMPARISON is about *relative value*, SHAME is about *being seen*
- Shame vs. Spiral confusion → SPIRAL builds thoughts, SHAME collapses self
