Section 12: TTS Prose Requirements
If books are delivered via robot TTS (Google Play auto-narrate, standard TTS engines), the following prose constraints apply universally. Human narration may relax some constraints, but TTS is the production baseline.

12.1 The TTS Reality
Robot narration cannot perform:

Subtle emotional coloring
Rhetorical question emphasis
Tentative hedging as authentic uncertainty
Long embedded clauses with breath control
Abstract language without sensory anchors
The prose must carry 100% of the emotional weight in the text itself.

12.2 Universal TTS Prose Law
Applies to all 10 section types unless otherwise specified.

Sentence Length Caps
By function:

Section Type	Max Words	Why
Teaching (Doctrine, Key Concepts)	12	Mechanism explanation needs compression
Sensory Scene (Opening Scene, Grounding)	10	One sensory detail per sentence
Emotional Moment (Story Injection)	15	TTS can't hold tension longer
Exercise Instructions	8	One step per sentence
Integration/Closing	12	Carry lines must land cleanly
Exception: Opening hooks may use one 18-20 word sentence IF followed immediately by 3-5 word sentence for rhythm.

No Rhetorical Questions
Forbidden:

"Have you ever felt your body shut down when someone asks too much?"
"What would it mean to trust your nervous system again?"
"Why do we carry shame in our hips?"
Correct (stated questions):

"You want to know why your body freezes when someone asks too much. Here is why."
"You have wondered what it would mean to trust your nervous system. Let me show you."
"The question is not why. The question is where. Shame lives in the hips because..."
Why: TTS reads questions as statements. The "?" mark adds nothing. Stated questions keep the cognitive structure without the punctuation ambiguity.

No Tentative Language
Forbidden in all sections:

"Perhaps you might consider..."
"It's possible that..."
"You may want to try..."
"Sometimes it can feel like..."
Correct:

"Consider this..."
"Here is what happens..."
"Try this..."
"It feels like..."
Why: Tentative language sounds uncertain in TTS. The teacher's voice must be calm authority, not hedging.

Active Verbs Only
Forbidden:

"She was feeling overwhelmed by the conversation."
"He had been carrying that weight for years."
"The body is trying to protect you."
Correct:

"The conversation crushed her."
"He carried that weight for years."
"Your body protects you."
Why: Passive construction flattens in TTS. Active verbs create momentum.

Concrete Sensory Anchors Required
Every abstract statement must be followed by a body anchor within 2 sentences.

Forbidden:

"You are held by something larger than yourself." [stops there]
"Presence is the foundation of healing." [no body reference]
Correct:

"You are held by something larger than yourself. Feel your seat on the chair. The ground beneath your feet. That is being held."
"Presence is the foundation of healing. Presence is your breath. Your skin. The weight of your body. Start there."
Why: TTS cannot add warmth to abstractions. The body anchor makes it real.

One Instruction Per Sentence (Exercises)
Forbidden:

"Breathe in for four counts, hold for seven, and exhale for eight while noticing the sensation of release."
Correct:

"Breathe in. Count to four.
Hold. Count to seven.
Exhale. Count to eight.
Notice the release."
Why: TTS cannot chunk complex instructions. Break them apart.

Strategic Repetition Allowed
TTS loses emphasis, so repetition must be intentional.

Allowed:

"Not because you are broken. Not because you failed. Not because you are too much. Because your nervous system is doing exactly what it was built to do."
"Still here. Still breathing. Still held."
Rule: Repetition must be structural (building a list, landing a point), not accidental (saying the same thing twice because you forgot).

Paragraph Breaks = Pause Markers
TTS does not pause for commas or breath. Use paragraph breaks.

Forbidden (one paragraph):

"Your body remembers everything. It holds the joy and the grief and the terror and the longing, and it does not sort them into neat categories, it just holds them, all of it, and sometimes that feels like too much, but it is not too much, it is the truth of being alive."
Correct (broken into pauses):

"Your body remembers everything. It holds the joy and the grief and the terror and the longing.
It does not sort them into neat categories. It just holds them. All of it.
Sometimes that feels like too much.
It is not too much. It is the truth of being alive."
Why: Each paragraph break gives the listener's nervous system a micro-pause to integrate.

12.3 Reflection Ceiling by Tier
Even within the 12-chapter structure, reflections vary by intensity.

Chapter Type	Max Words	Chunk Size
HARDSHIP (intense)	180	60-80 word bursts with breaks
HELP (teaching)	220	80-100 word bursts
HEALING (practice)	200	70-90 word bursts
HOPE (integration)	140	50-70 word bursts
Rule: After any high-emotion story injection, cut the following reflection by 20%. The story did the work. The reflection just names it.

12.4 Dialogue in Stories
Dialogue is one of the few things that differentiates well in TTS.

Use it strategically in story injections:

Weak (no dialogue):

"The teacher asked her to share what was present. She froze. She couldn't find words. The silence stretched."
Strong (dialogue breaks the monotone):

"The teacher asked, 'What's present for you right now?'
She froze.
No words came.
The teacher waited.
'I don't know,' she finally said.
'That's a good place to start,' the teacher replied."
Why: The shift to quoted speech changes TTS cadence just enough to hold attention.

12.5 Testing for TTS Readability
Before approval, run the Monotone Test:

Read the section aloud in a flat, robotic voice with zero emotional coloring
No pauses except paragraph breaks
No emphasis except periods
If it still moves you, it passes. If it sounds like a Wikipedia article, rewrite.

Common failures:

Too many abstract nouns (consciousness, awareness, presence, alignment)
No sensory anchors
Sentences too long (listener loses the thread)
Rhetorical questions that sound like statements
Passive voice that flattens action
12.6 CI Gates for TTS Compliance
Gate T01 (Sentence Length): No sentence in teaching/reflection sections >12 words. Hard fail.

Gate T02 (Rhetorical Questions): Scan for "?" marks in all sections except exercises. Flag for review. Require rewrite as stated questions.

Gate T03 (Tentative Language): Scan for "perhaps," "might," "maybe," "possibly," "you may want to," "consider trying." Hard fail.

Gate T04 (Passive Voice): Scan for "was feeling," "had been," "is trying to," "are being." Warn if >3 instances per section.

Gate T05 (Abstract Without Anchor): If abstract noun detected (consciousness, presence, energy, alignment), verify body anchor within 2 sentences. Warn if missing.

Gate T06 (Exercise Chunking): Exercise instructions must be ≤8 words per sentence. Hard fail if exceeded.

Gate T07 (Paragraph Density): Warn if any paragraph >100 words without break. TTS needs pause points.

Section 14: Emotional Force Thresholds (Somatic)

Passive voice (being held, being seen)
Gentle teaching with no volatility
Reassurance without cost-naming
This produces books that are:

Structurally sound
Therapeutically coherent
Emotionally inert
The listener recognizes the pattern but doesn't feel it in their body.

HARDSHIP	Hot	4-5	Grounding verbs, body states, visible consequence
HELP	Warm	2-3	Teaching clarity, mechanism naming
HEALING	Warm-Hot	3-4	Emergence verbs, sensory present, no resolution
HOPE	Cool	1-2	Integration, quiet, synthesis
Plan compiler assigns temperature during chapter planning.

14.3 HARDSHIP Chapter Requirements (Hot)
HARDSHIP chapters confront difficulty. They must feel difficult.

Required Elements
1. Grounding Verbs (≥2 per chapter):

collapse
shatter
fragment
dissolve
abandon
crumble
unravel
break
fall
lose
Example:

"The structure she built for twenty years collapsed in one conversation."
"His sense of self shattered when the diagnosis came."
2. Body State Words (≥2 per chapter):

tight
numb
hollow
frozen
raw
clenched
heavy
empty
small
trapped
Example:

"Her chest tight. Her throat closed. No words came."
"He sat frozen. The room continued around him. He could not move."
3. Visible Consequence Required:

HARDSHIP chapters cannot end with "and everything was okay."

Something must change, break, or stop:

A relationship shifts
A belief dissolves
A practice ends
A door closes
Forbidden:

"She felt the grief and then felt peace."
"He cried and released the pain."
Correct:

"She felt the grief. It did not leave. She learned to carry it differently."
"He cried. The pain remained. He stopped pretending it was gone."
Enforcement
Gate E01 (Grounding Verb Count): Scan HARDSHIP chapters for grounding verbs. Hard fail if <2 found.

Gate E02 (Body State Presence): Scan for body state words. Warn if <2 found.

14.4 HEALING Chapter Requirements (Warm-Hot)

Required Elements
1. Emergence Verbs (≥2 per chapter):

soften
open
return
notice
allow
settle
land
release
breathe
feel
Example:

"Her jaw softened. Not because the fear left. Because she noticed it and stopped fighting."

breath
skin
ground
warmth
weight
texture
contact
sound
pulse
surface
Example:

"Feel your breath. The ground beneath you. The weight of your body on the chair."
"Notice the texture of the fabric against your skin. The warmth of your hands."
3. No Resolution Allowed:

HEALING chapters cannot end with transformation complete.

Forbidden:

"She practiced the breath and felt whole again."
"He returned to his body and the anxiety was gone."
Correct:

"She practiced the breath. The anxiety remained. She stayed anyway."
"He returned to his body. It was still afraid. He did not leave."
Enforcement
Gate E04 (Emergence Verb Count): Scan HEALING chapters. Warn if <2 emergence verbs.

Gate E05 (Sensory Present): Scan for sensory words. Hard fail if <3.

Gate E06 (No Resolution): Human review. If chapter ending implies completion/transformation/cure, flag for rewrite.

14.5 Cognitive/Body Ratio Ceiling
Spiritual content defaults to cognitive language. This enforces groundedness.

Definition
Cognitive verbs:

understand
realize
believe
think
recognize
learn
know
see (metaphorical)
Body/sensory verbs:

feel
sense
notice
breathe
soften
tighten

Count cognitive verbs in chapter
Count body/sensory verbs in chapter
Calculate ratio

Ratio >5:1 → hard fail (too abstract)
Ratio >3:1 → warn (review recommended)
Ratio <2:1 → ideal (embodied teaching)

Example pass:

"She sent the text. She told the truth. She said what she needed to say. Three days passed. No reply. She learned something about silence."
Correct: Action → reaction → meaning.

# Section 16: Emotional QA & Drift Governance

**Writers must understand these rules exist. QA enforces them. CI blocks violations.**

This section prevents:
- Emotional flatness (books that teach mechanisms but never move the listener)
- TTS rhythm collapse (robot narration that sounds robotic)
- Book-level sameness (every chapter feels identical)
- Catalog-level drift (1,200 books blur into one voice)

---

## 16.1 Phase 3 Emotional Force Validation

**Not all chapters need the same intensity. But intensity must be explicit and enforced.**

### Hot Chapter Requirements

**Every book must contain ≥1 hot chapter** (tagged `emotional_temperature: hot` in compiled plan).

Hot chapters must contain:

**1. Escalation Verbs (≥3 per chapter)**
- snap, slam, cry, send, delete, confront, lock, leave, say, hit, break, shatter

**Example:**
- "She sent the text. She told the truth. The door closed."
- "His chest broke open. The words came anyway."

**2. Sensory Stress Words (≥2 per chapter)**
- jaw, breath, throat, chest, heat, silence, stare, shaking, tight, frozen, numb

**Example:**
- "Throat tight. Jaw clenched. Silence stretched."
- "Her hands shook. She could not make them stop."

**3. Reaction Markers (when action present)**

If chapter contains action verbs (send, say, leave, lock, post, confront, ask, decide), it must also contain reaction markers within same section:
- looked (away/down/confused)
- paused
- didn't reply
- walked away
- went quiet
- door closed
- silence
- seen (was seen / wasn't seen)

**Example of violation:**
- "She sent the text. She told the truth." [STOPS HERE - no reaction]

**Example of compliance:**
- "She sent the text. She told the truth. Three days passed. No reply. She learned something about silence."

**Why:** Action without visible reaction feels incomplete. The listener needs to see the consequence.

---

### Cognitive/Body Ratio Ceiling

**All chapters (not just hot) must maintain cognitive/body balance.**

**Cognitive verbs:**
- understand, realize, believe, think, recognize, learn, know, see (metaphorical)

**Body/sensory verbs:**
- feel, sense, notice, breathe, soften, tighten, move, hold, pulse, contract

**Thresholds:**
- Ratio >5:1 → Hard fail (too abstract)
- Ratio >3:1 → Warning (review recommended)
- Ratio <2:1 → Ideal (embodied teaching)

**Example failure (10 cognitive, 1 body = 10:1):**
- "You realize your patterns. You understand the mechanism. You recognize the cycle. You learn to see the triggers. You believe you can change. You know you are held. You think about your body. You see the truth. You understand why. You realize what matters. Feel your breath."

**Example pass (6 cognitive, 12 body = 0.5:1):**
- "You notice your breath. You feel your chest tighten. You sense the fear rising. You realize the pattern. You soften your jaw. You allow the sensation. You recognize the cycle. You breathe anyway. You feel the ground. You stay. You notice it shifting. You understand: the body leads, the mind follows."

---

### CI Gates

**Gate E01:** Scan hot chapters for escalation verbs. Hard fail if <3.

**Gate E02:** Scan hot chapters for sensory stress words. Warn if <2.

**Gate E03:** If action verbs present, verify reaction markers exist. Hard fail if action without reaction.

**Gate E04:** Calculate cognitive/body ratio per chapter. Hard fail if >5:1, warn if >3:1.

---

## 16.2 TTS Rhythm Governance

**Robot narration cannot add emphasis. The text must carry 100% of emotional weight through rhythm variation.**

### Rules

**1. Question Mark Ceiling**
- Max 2 question marks per chapter
- TTS reads "?" as flat statement
- Use stated questions instead: "You want to know why. Here is why."

**Gate T01:** Scan for "?" marks. Warn if >2 per chapter.

---

**2. Long Sentence Ceiling**
- Max 30% of sentences can exceed 18 words
- TTS loses listener attention on long clauses

**Gate T02:** Count sentences >18 words. Hard fail if >30% of chapter total.

---

**3. Sentence Distribution Balance**

**Every chapter must contain:**
- ≥1 sentence of 3 words or fewer (percussion hit)
- ≥1 sentence of 15-20 words (complexity)
- Range between shortest and longest sentence ≥12 words

**Example compliance:**
- Shortest: "Gone." (1 word)
- Longest: "You wave at someone across the hallway and they look away and you will never know if they saw you or if they're pretending they didn't." (26 words)
- Range: 25 words ✓

**Gate T03:** Verify sentence distribution. Warn if no 3-word sentence exists. Warn if range <12 words.

---

**4. No Monotone Paragraph Runs**

**Forbidden:**
- 4+ consecutive paragraphs where every sentence is 10-15 words
- TTS flattens into white noise

**Required:**
- Rhythm variation within every 4-paragraph window

**Gate T04:** Flag monotone runs (4+ paragraphs, all sentences within 5-word range). Warn for review.

---

## 16.3 Book-Level Drift Detection

**These rules prevent a book from feeling samey across its own chapters.**

### Temperature Progression Rule

**No 3 consecutive chapters with same temperature.**

**Allowed:**
- cool → warm → hot → warm (varies)
- warm → hot → warm → cool (varies)

**Forbidden:**
- warm → warm → warm → warm (flatlines)
- hot → hot → hot (exhausting)

**Why:** Emotional range requires contrast. Monotemperature = monotone.

**Gate D01:** Scan chapter temperature sequence. Hard fail if 3 consecutive same.

---

### Reassurance Phrase Frequency Cap

**No single reassurance phrase may appear in >60% of chapters within one book.**

**Monitored phrases:**
- "You are not broken"
- "You are enough"
- "Still here"
- "The alarm is real"
- "Your nervous system"
- "Choose yourself"
- "You've got this"

**Example violation (8-chapter book):**
- "Still here" appears in chapters 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8 = 6 of 8 = 75% → FAIL

**Allowed:**
- "Still here" in chapters 1, 8 only = 2 of 8 = 25% → PASS

**Gate D02:** Count reassurance phrase occurrences per book. Hard fail if any phrase >60%.

---

### Carry Line Uniqueness

**The final sentence of each chapter must be unique within the book.**

**No two chapters may end with:**
- Exact same sentence
- 6-gram overlap >50%

**Example violation:**
- Ch3: "Still here. Still breathing. Still going."
- Ch7: "Still here. Still breathing. Still moving."
- 6-grams shared: "Still here. Still breathing. Still" → 75% overlap → FAIL

**Gate D03:** Extract final sentence from each chapter. Check for exact duplicates or high n-gram similarity. Hard fail if found.

---

### Metaphor Family Compliance

**Each book owns 3-5 core metaphor terms. These terms should appear in ≥50% of chapters but ≤40% of any single chapter.**

**Example (social anxiety book):**
- Owned terms: alarm, smoke detector, false signal, radar, watcher
- Should appear across 5+ of 8 chapters
- No single chapter should use all 5 (becomes formulaic)

**Gate D04:** Verify owned metaphors appear across ≥50% of chapters. Warn if concentrated in <3 chapters (underused) or if any chapter uses >40% of the family (overused).

---

## 16.4 Catalog-Level Drift Detection

**These rules prevent 1,200 books from blurring into sameness.**

### Structural Similarity Ceiling

**Across any release batch (typically 10-20 books), structural overlap must be ≤20%.**

**Structural fingerprint includes:**
- Beat map sequence
- Section type distribution
- Chapter count and word count distribution
- Temperature curve shape

**Gate C01:** Generate structural fingerprint for each book. Compare within batch. Hard fail if any two books >20% similar.

---

### Story-Body Overlap Ceiling

**Across batch, story atom text bodies must not overlap >15%.**

**Why:** If 3 books share 8 of the same story atoms, listeners notice the repetition.

**Gate C02:** Compare story atom IDs used across books in batch. Hard fail if overlap >15%.

---

### Emotional Waveform Diversity

**Emotional waveform = sequence of chapter temperatures (cool/warm/hot) across book.**

**Example waveforms:**
- Book A: [cool, warm, hot, warm, cool, warm, hot, warm]
- Book B: [warm, hot, warm, cool, warm, hot, warm, cool]

**Requirement:** Within any 10-book window, ≥3 distinct waveform shapes must exist.

**Gate C03:** Compare waveforms within rolling 10-book windows. Warn if <3 distinct shapes (lack of diversity).

---

## 16.5 Authenticity Scoring (Self-Help Cliché Detection)

**Self-help content has high cliché risk. Certain phrases signal "safety" to writers but produce generic, unmemorable books.**

### Monitored Phrases

**Category: Generic Self-Help (High Risk)**
- "You've got this"
- "You are enough"
- "Choose yourself"
- "Trust the process"
- "Everything happens for a reason"
- "You deserve" (without specificity)

**Category: Therapy-Speak Without Grounding**
- "Hold space" (overused)
- "Sit with discomfort" (overused without guidance)
- "Lean into" (overused)
- "Do the work" (vague)
- "Set boundaries" (generic without how-to)
- "Unpack" (overused without follow-through)

**Category: Neuroscience Buzzwords Without Explanation**
- "Nervous system" (valid but overused — max 4× per chapter)
- "Dysregulation" (valid but often used vaguely)
- "Trauma" (valid but must specify what kind)

---

### Cliché Density Threshold

**No chapter may contain >2 monitored phrases.**

**Allowed exceptions:**
1. **Phrase is immediately grounded:**
   - "Set boundaries. Not the Instagram version. The version where you say 'I cannot do that' and your body stops apologizing."
   - "You are enough. Not in the meme sense. In the sense that your nervous system does not require external validation to regulate."

2. **Phrase is part of persona's established vocabulary** (persona-locked):
   - If healthcare_rn persona uses "hold space" as signature language, allowed in their books but flagged if it appears in gen_alpha books.

---

### Enforcement

**Gate SA01 (Cliché Density):**
- Scan each chapter for monitored phrases
- Count instances
- Hard fail if >2 found in single chapter

**Gate SA02 (Cliché Catalog Frequency):**
- At catalog level, count how many books contain each phrase
- Warn if any phrase appears in >30% of books in stack

**Gate SA03 (Grounding Check - Human Review):**
- If monitored phrase is present, QA reader verifies it's followed by grounding/specificity within 2 sentences
- Flag for rewrite if phrase stands alone

---

## 16.6 Voice Bleed Prevention

**Each persona must sound distinct. Generic language creates voice bleed.**

### Persona Voice Markers

**Each persona owns signature vocabulary:**

| Persona | Owned Terms | Forbidden in Other Personas |
|---------|-------------|------------------------------|
| gen_alpha_students | group chat, DM, story, post, screenshot, meme | Corporate speak, medical terminology |
| healthcare_rns | shift, chart, code, attending, patient load, moral injury | Teen slang, academic theory |
| gen_z_professionals | Slack, stand-up, sprint, burnout, scope creep | Medical procedures, classroom references |

**Gate VB01:** Scan for persona cross-contamination. Hard fail if gen_alpha book uses "shift handoff" or healthcare_rn book uses "group chat drama."

---

### Generic Voice Detection (Human Review)

**QA reader flags chapters where vocabulary could belong to any persona.**

**Example of generic (fails):**
- "You feel overwhelmed. Your body tenses. You want to escape."
- **Problem:** Could be anyone. No persona markers.

**Example of persona-specific (passes):**
- "The group chat is blowing up. Forty-three messages. You open it. Your chest locks. You close it. Thirty seconds later you open it again."
- **Correct:** Clearly gen_alpha_students.

**Gate VB02 (Human Review):** QA reader scans 2 chapters per book. Flags if voice feels generic/interchangeable. Requires persona-specific detail injection.

**When emotional QA gates fail, the system must block progression.**

- Cannot be selected by plan compiler until fixed

## 6.4 Hook Diversity (Opening Collision Prevention)

### Pattern Detection

- Opening word (You / Your / The / Here / This)
- Second sentence rhythm

**Example collision:**

Book A: "You are sitting somewhere ordinary. Nothing threatening is happening."  
Book B: "You are standing somewhere familiar. Nothing dangerous is present."

**Structure match:** [You are] + [location] + [period]. [Nothing] + [state] + [period].

This is a collision. Flag for rewrite.

### Enforcement

**Gate H01 (Hook Structural Fingerprint):**

- Cross-check against catalog

### Enforcement

- Hot temperature: 4-5

**Generate waveform:** [2, 3, 4, 3, 2, 3, 4, 2]

### Enforcement

## 6.7 Structural Similarity Limits

**At catalog scale, books must feel distinct even if using same beat maps.**

### Structural Fingerprint Components
- Beat map sequence (which maps used in which order)
- Section type distribution (how many HOOK vs STORY vs REFLECTION)
- Chapter count and average word count
- Temperature curve shape

### Enforcement

**Gate S01 (Structural Overlap):**
- Generate structural fingerprint for each book
- Compare within release batch (typically 10-20 books)
- Hard fail if any two books >20% similar

**Gate S02 (Story-Body Overlap):**
- Compare which story atom IDs are used across books in batch
- Hard fail if >15% overlap (e.g., 3 books share 8 of same story atoms)

**Why:** If multiple books share too many atoms, listeners notice repetition.
