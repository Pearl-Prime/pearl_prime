# You're Broke Because Teachers Are Underpaid (Not Because You're Bad With Money)

## For: Everyone with a master's degree who can't afford basic expenses

You have:
- Bachelor's degree (minimum)
- Master's degree (probably)
- Continuing education credits
- Professional development
- Years of experience

You make:
- Less than starting salary in tech/finance/healthcare
- Sometimes less than some hourly jobs
- Barely above poverty line in expensive cities
- Not enough to buy a house where you teach

**This isn't a you problem. This is a system problem.**

### The Pay Reality

**National average teacher salary:** ~$65,000

**But that includes:**
- Teachers with 30 years experience
- Districts in high cost-of-living areas
- Administrative stipends
- Everything averaged together

**New teacher reality:**
- Starting salary: $35,000-$50,000 depending on location
- Before taxes
- Before insurance
- Before retirement contributions
- Before union dues
- Before classroom supplies you buy

**Take-home might be $2,000-$3,000/month.**

**Now try to live on that.**

### The Math That Doesn't Math

**Monthly costs (conservative):**
- Rent: $1,200-$2,500 (depending on city)
- Car payment: $300-$500
- Insurance (car/health): $300-$500
- Student loans: $200-$500
- Utilities: $150-$200
- Food: $300-$400
- Gas: $100-$200
- Phone: $50-$100

**Minimum: $2,600-$5,400/month**

**Teacher take-home: $2,000-$3,000/month**

**You're negative before fun, savings, or emergencies.**

### The Student Loan Trap

**The problem:**
- Need degree to teach
- Often need master's
- Loans to pay for degrees
- Teaching salary doesn't cover loans
- Trapped in career you can't afford

**Options:**

**Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF):**
- 10 years of qualifying payments
- Must be at public school/nonprofit
- Must have qualifying loans
- Make sure you're enrolled correctly
- Not automatic

**Income-Driven Repayment:**
- Payment based on income
- Extends loan term
- More interest long-term
- But manageable monthly payment

**Teacher loan forgiveness:**
- $5,000-$17,500 forgiven
- After 5 years teaching
- Low-income schools
- Specific subject areas
- Different from PSLF

**Look into all options. Might save you.**

### The Benefits (Sometimes Worth It)

**What teaching might offer:**

**Health insurance:**
- Usually available
- Quality varies wildly
- Costs vary
- Sometimes only thing making it worth it

**Pension/retirement:**
- If you stay long enough (usually 5-10 years to vest)
- Better than 401k if you stick it out
- Worthless if you leave early

**Job security:**
- After tenure (if your state has it)
- Harder to fire
- Worth something

**Summers:**
- Not paid but time off
- Valuable for family
- Mental health reset

**Schedule:**
- Same as your kids' schedule
- Holidays off
- Predictable

**For some people, these make up for low pay. For others, they don't.**
