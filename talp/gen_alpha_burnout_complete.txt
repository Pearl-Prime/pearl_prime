# GEN ALPHA STUDENTS - BURNOUT SCENES
## Ages 12-16 | Middle School Context | 2026

---

## SCENE B-01 | The After-School Calendar
**Setting:** Car ride home, 3:45 PM, Monday
**Engine:** Overwhelm

Tyler's mom picks him up. "How was school?"

"Fine."

"Ready for piano?"

Piano's at 4:30. Then homework. Then dinner. Then youth group at 7. Then more homework.

Tomorrow: school, soccer practice, tutoring. Wednesday: school, debate club, piano again. Thursday: school, soccer, youth group. Friday: school, game, homework he didn't finish during the week.

His mom's talking about weekend plans. Something about his cousin's birthday party.

Tyler stares out the window. He can't remember the last time he just… sat. Did nothing. Had a whole afternoon with no place to be.

He's twelve. He's tired in a way that sleep doesn't fix.

## SCENE B-02 | The Grade Check Ritual
**Setting:** Kitchen table, 6:47 PM, Tuesday
**Engine:** Spiral

Anika's mom opens her laptop. "Let's check grades."

They do this every Tuesday. Her mom clicks through each class. Math: A. Science: A-. English: B+.

"B+? What happened in English?"

"I got an 88 on the essay."

"Did you revise it?"

"Yeah."

"Did you use the feedback from the outline?"

"Yeah."

Her mom's face does that thing. Not mad. Disappointed. Concerned. "You're capable of A's, honey. You know that."

Anika nods. She does know. She also knows she rewrote that essay three times and her brain stopped having thoughts halfway through the third draft.

"Let's talk about time management."

They talk about time management. Again. Anika nods. Takes notes. Promises to do better.

She goes to her room. Opens her planner. It's already full. Every hour blocked. Every day planned.

She's doing fine. Better than fine. But fine isn't enough.

## SCENE B-03 | The Mandatory Fun
**Setting:** School cafeteria, 12:17 PM, club fair day
**Engine:** Grief

The cafeteria's full of tables. Every club recruiting. Science Olympiad. Model UN. Yearbook. Robotics. Student Government.

Riley walks past them. Every table has someone calling out: "You should join!" "We meet Tuesdays!" "Great for college apps!"

Her guidance counselor said she needs extracurriculars. Three, minimum. "Well-rounded," she said.

Riley stops at the art club table. Draws sometimes. Could be fun.

"We meet Monday and Thursday, 3 to 5. Plus weekends before competitions."

Riley nods. Keeps walking. Stops at environmental club.

"Tuesdays and Fridays. Beach cleanups most Saturdays."

She takes a flyer. Doesn't sign up.

Every club wants ten hours a week. She's already taking honors classes. Already behind on sleep.

She leaves the cafeteria without joining anything. Knows her counselor will ask. Knows "I'm tired" isn't an answer that counts.

## SCENE B-04 | The Sunday Night Dread
**Setting:** Bedroom, 8:36 PM, Sunday
**Engine:** Overwhelm

Kai's homework is spread across his bed. Pre-algebra worksheet. Science project proposal. English reading. Spanish vocab.

He finished two things. Still has five more.

His phone buzzes. Group chat making plans for next weekend. He doesn't respond. Doesn't know if he'll have time.

His mom knocks. "Bedtime in thirty."

Thirty minutes. He has three hours of work left.

He picks the easiest thing—vocab. Copies definitions from Google Translate. His teacher says not to do that, but it's 8:37 and he has a math test tomorrow he didn't study for.

He's not behind. He's not failing. He's just… always underwater. Always one assignment behind where he should be.

The weekend's over and he didn't rest. He did homework. Like he does every weekend.

Monday's six hours away. He's already exhausted.

## SCENE B-05 | The College Talk (Age 13)
**Setting:** Dinner table, 6:52 PM, Wednesday
**Engine:** False Alarm

Zara's in eighth grade. Thirteen years old. Her dad starts talking about college.

"Have you thought about what you want to study?"

"Not really."

"You should start thinking. High school goes fast."

Her mom nods. "And colleges look at everything. Grades, activities, leadership."

Zara cuts her chicken. She's in middle school. College is five years away.

"Your cousin Maya got into Berkeley. Started prepping in ninth grade."

Zara nods. Feels something tighten in her chest.

She's thirteen. She doesn't know what she wants to study. Doesn't know where she wants to go. Doesn't know if she's doing enough to get there.

Her parents mean well. She knows that. But every conversation about the future makes her feel behind on a race that apparently started years ago.

## SCENE B-06 | The Practice That Never Ends
**Setting:** Soccer field, 5:47 PM, Thursday
**Engine:** Grief

Jordan's legs hurt. Practice was supposed to end at 5:30. Coach is making them run drills.

"One more!" Coach yells.

They've done six "one mores."

Jordan's phone is in his bag. Homework waiting. Test tomorrow. His mom's probably outside wondering why he's late.

He runs the drill. Misses the goal. Coach sighs.

"Again."

Jordan used to like soccer. Wanted to play club. Made the team. Thought it would be fun.

Now it's two practices a week, games on weekends, a group chat that never stops, and a coach who texts his mom when he misses practice for a dentist appointment.

He runs again. Makes the goal this time.

"Good. See you Saturday."

Saturday. He forgot about Saturday. He has a birthday party. Had a birthday party.

He'll tell his friend he can't make it. Soccer's important. Soccer looks good.

He gets in the car. His mom asks how practice was.

"Fine."

It wasn't.

## SCENE B-07 | The Sleep Deficit
**Setting:** Classroom, 10:24 AM, Friday
**Engine:** Overwhelm

Riley's head dips. She catches herself. Blinks hard.

Her teacher's talking about photosynthesis. Writing on the board. Riley copies the words without reading them.

She went to bed at 11. Woke up at 6. Five hours. Not enough.

This is the third day this week she's done this. Phone confiscated at 9 PM. Still awake at midnight, brain spinning through everything she didn't finish.

Her eyes close again. Just for a second.

Someone laughs. She jerks awake. Her teacher's looking at her.

"Riley? You with us?"

"Yeah. Sorry."

Her face burns. She sits up straighter. Tries to focus.

The words on the board blur together. Her body's so tired it hurts.

She makes it to lunch. Sits in the cafeteria. Puts her head down.

Just five minutes. Just until the bell.

Someone shakes her shoulder. "Riley. Class."

She fell asleep. Again.

She gathers her stuff. Walks to next period. Wonders when she'll get to sleep for real.

Wonders if weekends even count when they're full of homework and practice and family obligations.

Wonders if this is just what life is now.
