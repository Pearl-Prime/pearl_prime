# "Do What You Love" Is a Scam

## For: Anyone who thinks loving your work means never wanting money

"Follow your passion!"  
"Do what you love and you'll never work a day in your life!"  
"If you're good enough, the money will follow!"

**These are lies sold by people who had financial safety nets.**

### The Passion Economy Lie

**What they sell you:**  
Turn your passion into profit! Build a business around what you love! Monetize your creativity!

**What they don't tell you:**  
Monetizing your passion can kill the thing you loved about it.

### The Reality Check

**Doing what you love for money means:**
- Creating on deadlines you didn't choose
- Making work for clients who may not appreciate it
- Compromising your vision for budgets
- Turning joy into obligation
- Dealing with business admin that has nothing to do with the creative part
- Working on projects that pay but don't excite you

**Sometimes that's fine. Sometimes it ruins the thing you loved.**

### Types of Creative Work

**1. Passion Work**
- What you'd do for free
- What feeds your soul
- What you make for yourself
- May or may not make money

**2. Profit Work**
- Pays the bills
- May be creative, may not
- Client-driven
- Market-driven

**3. Prestige Work**
- Builds your reputation
- Portfolio pieces
- May not pay well or at all
- Opens doors

**Successful creatives usually balance all three. Not just passion.**

### The Framework: BALANCE

**B - Bills first**  
Secure your survival before optimizing for passion

**A - Assess energy**  
What gives you energy? What drains it?

**L - Limit passion exploitation**  
Don't let "but you love it!" justify underpayment

**A - Allocate deliberately**  
Schedule time for passion work AND profit work

**N - No shame in profit work**  
Commercial work isn't "selling out"

**C - Create boundaries**  
When does work stop being work?

**E - Evaluate regularly**  
Is this balance sustainable?

### The Questions to Ask

**Before monetizing your passion:**

1. Will doing this for money change how I feel about it?
2. Am I okay with client demands on my creative vision?
3. Can I handle the business side (taxes, marketing, contracts)?
4. Do I have financial cushion for slow periods?
5. Is there market demand for what I love making?
6. Am I prepared for rejection of my passion work?
7. Will I resent this if it doesn't make enough money?

**Be honest. These answers matter.**

### Models That Work

**1. Day Job + Passion Projects**
- Stable income funds creative freedom
- Create what you want, when you want
- No pressure to monetize
- **Works for:** People who value creative freedom over creative income

**2. Creative Day Job + Side Passion**
- Use creative skills to make money
- Different creative outlet for self
- Security + creativity
- **Works for:** People who can separate professional and personal creative work

**3. Profit Work Funds Passion Work**
- Take commercial clients
- Use income for passion projects
- Commercial work pays, passion work satisfies
- **Works for:** People who can emotionally separate the two

**4. Hybrid Projects**
- Find clients whose work aligns with your passion
- Not always possible but ideal when it happens
- **Works for:** Established creatives with leverage to choose

**5. Full Passion Economy**
- Make only what you love
- Charge premium prices
- Accept smaller client pool
- **Works for:** Established creatives with strong market position

### The "Selling Out" Question

**Here's the truth:**  
Paying your rent with your skills isn't selling out. It's being a professional.

**You're not selling out when you:**
- Take work that pays well but isn't your dream project
- Adjust your vision to client needs
- Make commercial work alongside personal work
- Choose stability over pure passion
- Work with commercial clients
- Make art you wouldn't make for free

**You ARE selling out when you:**
- Violate your core values for money
- Promote things you know are harmful
- Compromise your ethics, not just your aesthetic
- Do work that makes you fundamentally unhappy

**There's a difference between commercial work and soul-crushing work.**

### Permission Slips

You're allowed to:
- Make commercial work that pays
- Make passion work that doesn't
- Keep your passion as a hobby
- Not monetize every skill
- Love your work without making it your job
- Make money from creative skills without it being your "passion"
- Change what you're passionate about
- Work to live, not live to work
- Set boundaries on passion work

### The Burnout Warning Signs

**When "doing what you love" becomes a problem:**

- You resent creating
- Everything feels like an obligation
- You can't make anything just for fun
- Client work killed your personal work
- You're always "on"
- Joy became dread
- You're chasing money instead of craft
- You hate the thing you used to love

**If this is you, something needs to change.**

### What "Success" Actually Looks Like

**Not this:**  
Working 80 hours a week on your passion, burning out, barely surviving

**This:**  
Sustainable balance between passion, profit, and rest

**Success isn't:**  
Monetizing every creative impulse

**Success is:**  
Making enough money to live + time/energy for creative work you care about

### The Reality of Creative Careers

**Very few people:**  
Make their living purely from their passion

**Most successful creatives:**  
- Balance commercial and personal work
- Have multiple income streams
- Protect time for passion projects
- Accept that not all work is inspiring
- Find meaning in craft even when it's not passion

**This isn't failure. This is sustainable creativity.**

### Bottom Line

**Do what you love? Maybe.**  
**Do what pays? Definitely.**  
**Do what's sustainable? Always.**

You don't owe capitalism your passion.

**Make money. Make art. Know the difference.**

---

**Now honestly assess: What's passion? What's profit? What's sustainable? Then build your life accordingly.**
