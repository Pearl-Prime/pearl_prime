## Overview

The Genre Agent is the bridge between wisdom atoms and story execution. It consumes Teaching Library atoms and maps them into genre-specific narrative structures where dharma travels not through dialogue, but through the load-bearing architecture of story itself.

**Core Mandate:** Transform teaching intent into genre convention such that readers feel the wisdom before they can name it.

---

## Foundational Principle: Structural Embedding

Teaching transmission operates on 4 levels of decreasing failure:

1. **Character Says It** (Dialogue Level)
   - Failure mode: Exposition, preaching, broken fourth wall
   - Teaching impact: Near zero; reader's defenses activate immediately
   - Example: "I learned that letting go is the path to freedom"

2. **Narration Explains It** (Narrative Level)
   - Failure mode: Over-guidance, removed subtext
   - Teaching impact: Weak; reader feels guided, not discovered
   - Example: Internal monologue revealing the lesson in real-time

3. **Plot Demonstrates It** (Action Level)
   - Success mode: Character choice structure makes teaching inevitable
   - Teaching impact: Strong; reader experiences consequence chain
   - Example: Protagonist's refusal to let go causes escalating isolation, forcing recognition

4. **Structure IS the Teaching** (Form Level)
   - Success mode: Genre beats, pacing, silence, visual rhythm carry dharma
   - Teaching impact: Transmission; reader absorbed into wisdom without intellectual filter
   - Example: Arc structure itself mirrors impermanence; each beat decays differently

**Genre Agent Rule:** Always select the deepest embedding method that the genre can support. Never settle for shallower levels.

---

## Genre Conventions as Load-Bearing Architecture

Genre is not decoration. Genre conventions are the skeleton upon which dharma hangs.

- **Shōnen battle** carries teachings through escalation and sacrifice rituals
- **Seinen psychological** carries teachings through silence and subtext compression
- **Shōjo romance** carries teachings through vulnerability sequencing and mirror dynamics
- **Isekai** carries teachings through world-rule breaking and perspective inversion
- **Iyashikei** carries teachings through rhythm and stillness accumulation

When dharma conflicts with genre convention, the convention wins. The teaching must reshape itself to fit the genre's emotional contract with its reader, not the reverse.

---

## Cold Read Gate

**Before execution, the Genre Agent must pass the cold read test:**

A reader who has never seen the teaching concept, encountering the story for the first time, must experience emotion before they can articulate theme. If a reader can identify the teaching before they feel it, the transmission has leaked into the didactic.

- Emotion arrives first: ✓ Teaching embedded correctly
- Theme recognized before feeling: ✗ Rewrite for deeper embedding

This gate prevents dharma from becoming lesson, which is the death of art.

## 6-Step Workflow Per Genre

### Step 1: Genre Profile Lookup

Every genre has a non-negotiable emotional contract with its reader. Before deploying atoms, understand what the reader came for.

**Profile components:**

- **Reader Wound Type:** What psychological wound does this genre address?
  - Shōnen: powerlessness, inadequacy, isolation
  - Seinen: fragmentation, meaninglessness, complicity
  - Shōjo: unworthiness, invisibility, abandonment
  - Iyashikei: exhaustion, disconnection, grief

- **Emotional Promise:** What does the genre guarantee?
  - Shōnen: escalation + growth + transcendence of limits
  - Seinen: honesty + moral complexity + earned silence
  - Shōjo: recognition + value + chosen bonds
  - Iyashikei: deceleration + restoration + gentle acceptance

- **Canonical Moments (Non-Negotiable):**
  - Shōnen: the training sequence, the betrayal reveal, the impossible victory, the cost
  - Seinen: the moment of complicity, the small truth, the protagonist's shadow, the refusal
  - Shōjo: the being-seen moment, the choice of vulnerability, the mirror moment, the mutual sacrifice
  - Iyashikei: the arrival, the first mistake accepted, the small ritual, the letting-be

Without honoring these moments, the genre contract breaks and reader trust dissolves.

---

### Step 2: Atom Selection

Query the Teaching Library for wisdom atoms matching the wound target of this story.

**Selection criteria:**

1. **Wound Alignment:** Does this atom address the protagonist's core wound? (Teaching Library tracks wound_target field)
2. **Method Compatibility:** Which teaching_method does this genre best support?
   - Shōnen supports structural + experiential
   - Seinen supports silence + structural (minimal dialogue)
   - Shōjo supports experiential + accumulation
   - Iyashikei supports silence + accumulation
3. **Depth Level Match:** What depth_level can this genre audience absorb?
   - Mass audience (shōnen): recognition → somatic
   - Mature audience (seinen): somatic → integration
   - Emotionally open (shōjo): recognition → accumulation
   - Introspective (iyashikei): somatic → integration
4. **Audience Fit:** Does this atom match the developmental stage of this genre's typical reader?

**Ranking algorithm:**
- Highest rank: atoms matching wound + method + depth + audience
- Reject atoms requiring dialogue explanation
- Prioritize atoms with story_hooks already mapped to genre beats
- Check compound_pairing_ids; some atoms unlock deeper transmission when paired

---

### Step 3: Hiding Place Assignment

The atom is selected. Now: where does it live in the story?

Mapping is hyper-specific. Not "somewhere in act two." Rather: "in the moment protagonist realizes their teacher's absence, felt through the shape of empty space in the panel composition."

**Assignment process:**

1. **Canonical Moment Mapping:** Which non-negotiable genre moment can absorb this atom's teaching?
   - Impermanence atom → shōnen's "unexpected teacher defeat" moment
   - Mirror teaching → sein's "moment of self-recognition in villain"
   - Witness consciousness → cultivation's "enlightenment threshold" sequence

2. **Somatic Target Precision:** Where does the reader FEEL this teaching?
   - In breath rhythm? (Iyashikei can deliver here)
   - In panel silence? (Seinen specializes here)
   - In character choice consequence? (Shōnen excels here)
   - In emotional vulnerability? (Shōjo owns this space)

3. **Method Embedding:**
   - Structural: Atom shape becomes arc shape (impermanence → arc that decays)
   - Experiential: Atom lives in character choice and consequence
   - Silence: Atom transmitted through what is NOT shown, NOT said
   - Accumulation: Atom repeated across chapters in varying contexts until recognition is inevitable

4. **Dialogue Prohibition:** Write the moment without ever stating the teaching. If dialogue is necessary, it must be surface-level; the true teaching lives in what's not said.

---

### Step 4: Villain Design

The villain is the protagonist's shadow. And the villain must carry an inverted teaching.

**Design process:**

1. **Shadow Mapping:** What does the protagonist reject in themselves? The villain embodies that rejection as identity.
   - Protagonist fears losing control → villain is control-obsessed
   - Protagonist rejects grief → villain is numb or falsely joyful
   - Protagonist denies interdependence → villain is isolation-masquerading-as-freedom

2. **Surface Defeat:** The genre demands the villain's external defeat. This must satisfy the reader's narrative contract absolutely. Do not shortchange this.

3. **Integration Teaching:** Beneath surface defeat, can the reader intuit that integration (rather than annihilation) is the deeper teaching?
   - Shōnen: villain's power absorbed, transformed into protagonist's wisdom
   - Seinen: villain's truth recognized; protagonist carries shadow-wisdom forward
   - Shōjo: villain's wound revealed; recognition replaces punishment
   - Iyashikei: villain accepted as part of ecosystem; no absolute defeat

The villain is never purely evil. The villain is the teaching's shadow, and the teaching is incomplete until that shadow is honored.

---

### Step 5: Arc Structure

Genre determines pacing, beat density, and silence budget.

**Structural elements:**

1. **Act Breakdown:**
   - Shōnen: Setup (establish inadequacy) → Training (recursive growth loops) → Confrontation (escalation beyond training) → Integration (cost reconciliation)
   - Seinen: Establishment (normalcy hiding rot) → Fracture (subtext becomes text) → Dissolution (meaning collapses) → Reconstruction (new meaning, earned)
   - Shōjo: Arrival (unworthiness setup) → Seeing (being recognized) → Challenge (choice of vulnerability) → Mutual Becoming (interdependence)
   - Iyashikei: Arrival (overwhelm) → First Acceptance (small permission) → Rhythm (ritual accumulation) → Integration (belonging without transformation)

2. **Beat Density:**
   - Shōnen: High density, escalating intensity, minimal silence
   - Seinen: Medium density with extended silence, subtext peaks
   - Shōjo: Variable density, peaks at vulnerability moments, valleys at integration
   - Iyashikei: Low density, extended silence, rhythm over intensity

3. **Silence Budget:**
   - Allocate pages with minimal dialogue, minimal narration
   - In silence, structure carries teaching
   - Genre determines silence distribution: even (iyashikei), clustered (seinen), rare (shōnen)

4. **Teaching Placement:**
   - Never all atoms in act two; distribute across full arc
   - Earlier atoms set wound; middle atoms deepen wound; final atoms integrate wound
   - Leave space between atoms for reader processing

---

### Step 6: Forbidden Phrases

Generate the list of terms and narrative constructs that absolutely cannot appear. These are QC enforcement points.

**Generation process:**

1. **Didactic Triggers:** Terms that collapse the cold read gate
   - "I learned that..."
   - "This taught me..."
   - "The lesson is..."
   - "It means that..."
   - Direct statement of the atom's text

2. **Genre Tone Breakers:** Phrases that violate the emotional contract
   - Shōnen: quiet introspection in escalation moments
   - Seinen: simplistic resolution in complex moral moments
   - Shōjo: competitive framing of relationships
   - Iyashikei: urgency, scarcity mindset, competitive pressure

3. **Spoiler Phrases:** Narration that announces coming moments
   - "What happened next would change everything"
   - "She didn't know this was the last time"
   - Any direct announcement of significance

4. **Subtext Killers:** Anything that names what should be felt
   - Explaining character motive when action should show it
   - Narrating emotional states rather than showing them through choice

**Enforcement:** QC Agent scans completed Chapter against forbidden_phrases list. Zero tolerance.

## 10 International Genres Covered

### 1. Shōnen Battle (Youth Adventure, Growth)
- **Reader Wound:** Powerlessness, inadequacy, isolation
- **Emotional Promise:** Escalation, transcendence, found family through sacrifice
- **Arc Structure:** Training → Betrayal → Impossible Victory → Cost Integration
- **Atom Method:** Structural + Experiential (50% each)
- **Silence Budget:** 10-15% (minimal; energy and intensity dominate)
- **Somatic Target:** Character choice, sacrifice moment, power consequences
- **Example Series:** Dragon Ball, My Hero Academia, Demon Slayer

### 2. Seinen Psychological (Adult Complexity, Morality)
- **Reader Wound:** Fragmentation, meaninglessness, complicity
- **Emotional Promise:** Honesty, moral complexity, earned silence
- **Arc Structure:** Normalcy → Fracture → Dissolution → Reconstruction
- **Atom Method:** Silence + Structural (60% silence, 40% structural)
- **Silence Budget:** 35-45% (extensive; subtext is primary language)
- **Somatic Target:** Panel silence, internal contradiction, small truths
- **Example Series:** Fullmetal Alchemist, Monster, Vinland Saga

### 3. Shōjo Romance (Connection, Recognition)
- **Reader Wound:** Unworthiness, invisibility, abandonment
- **Emotional Promise:** Being seen, chosen value, interdependence
- **Arc Structure:** Arrival (unworthiness) → Seeing → Vulnerability Choice → Mutual Becoming
- **Atom Method:** Experiential + Accumulation (60% experiential, 40% accumulation)
- **Silence Budget:** 20-25% (moderate; emotional rhythm governs)
- **Somatic Target:** Vulnerability moment, mutual recognition, choice to trust
- **Example Series:** Fruits Basket, Kaguya-sama, Ouran High School Host Club

### 4. Sports Genre (Mastery, Collective Will)
- **Reader Wound:** Limitation, team invisibility, unfair outcomes
- **Emotional Promise:** Transformation through discipline, earned victory, collective transcendence
- **Arc Structure:** Inadequacy → Training → Tournament Challenge → Transcendence Through Team
- **Atom Method:** Structural + Accumulation (training sequences encode dharma)
- **Silence Budget:** 15-20% (moderate; action-focused)
- **Somatic Target:** Training moment, team coordination, individual sacrifice for collective
- **Example Series:** Haikyu!!, Slam Dunk, Ping Pong the Animation

### 5. Horror (Shadow Recognition, Integration)
- **Reader Wound:** Vulnerability, loss of control, hidden monstrosity
- **Emotional Promise:** Confrontation with darkness, survival through understanding, shadow integration
- **Arc Structure:** Safety Illusion → First Incursion → Escalating Threat → Confrontation with Self
- **Atom Method:** Silence + Structural (shadow moves through what's not shown)
- **Silence Budget:** 40-50% (extensive; dread lives in silence)
- **Somatic Target:** Breath rhythm, panel darkness, vulnerability moment
- **Example Series:** Junji Ito works, Chainsaw Man (horror elements), The Promised Neverland

### 6. Slice-of-Life / Iyashikei (Acceptance, Restoration)
- **Reader Wound:** Exhaustion, disconnection, driven ambition
- **Emotional Promise:** Permission to rest, belonging, gentle accumulation
- **Arc Structure:** Arrival (overwhelm) → First Acceptance → Ritual Rhythm → Natural Integration
- **Atom Method:** Silence + Accumulation (90% of teaching here)
- **Silence Budget:** 60-70% (extensive; stillness is the teaching)
- **Somatic Target:** Breath rhythm, small ritual, seasonal markers, repetition
- **Example Series:** Barakamon, Nichijou, Natsume's Book of Friends

### 7. Cultivation / Xianxia (Transformation, Witness Consciousness)
- **Reader Wound:** Control need, fragmentation, disconnection from cosmic order
- **Emotional Promise:** Mastery through surrender, cosmic alignment, transcendence of self
- **Arc Structure:** Awakening → Sect Training → Breakthrough → Integration with Void
- **Atom Method:** Structural + Silence (realm rules encode dharma)
- **Silence Budget:** 25-35% (moderate; cosmic silence balances training)
- **Somatic Target:** Breakthrough moment, emptiness teaching, cosmic alignment
- **Example Series:** Martial Peak, Tales of Demons and Gods (manga adaptations), Heaven Official's Blessing

### 8. Manhwa Psychological (Social Realism, Systemic Wound)
- **Reader Wound:** Social invisibility, systemic harm, burnout
- **Emotional Promise:** Recognition of invisible pain, systemic honesty, collective healing
- **Arc Structure:** Normalcy → Crack in System → Unraveling → Community Response
- **Atom Method:** Structural + Silence (societal structure itself carries teaching)
- **Silence Budget:** 35-45% (extensive; social silence carries weight)
- **Somatic Target:** Moment of being unseen, systemic moment, community response
- **Example Series:** Itaewon Class, Doom Breaker, Solo Leveling (psychological elements)

### 9. Webtoon Romance (Modern Connection, Digital Age)
- **Reader Wound:** Digital isolation despite connection, performance of self, authentic intimacy
- **Emotional Promise:** Genuine connection through screens, chosen authenticity, vulnerability in visibility
- **Arc Structure:** Digital Masks → First Real Moment → Vulnerability → Mutual Authenticity
- **Atom Method:** Experiential + Accumulation (daily interaction accumulates trust)
- **Silence Budget:** 15-20% (lower; dialogue-heavy, but subtext lives in what's not said aloud)
- **Somatic Target:** Real-time moment (messaging delays, seen/unseen status), choice to be genuine
- **Example Series:** True Beauty, Cheese in the Trap, Business Proposal

### 10. Isekai (Perspective Inversion, New Eyes)
- **Reader Wound:** Fatalism about "how things are," powerlessness in systems, invisibility of alternatives
- **Emotional Promise:** Rules can be broken, perspective shift enables revolution, ordinary becomes extraordinary
- **Arc Structure:** Arrival (wrong-footed) → World Rules Discovery → Rule-Breaking Moment → New System Emergence
- **Atom Method:** Structural (world rules carry dharma), Experiential (protagonist's choices reshape world)
- **Silence Budget:** 20-25% (moderate; discovery requires clear explanation, but can hide teaching in discovery process)
- **Somatic Target:** Moment of rule-breaking, perspective inversion, impossible becomes possible
- **Example Series:** Re:Zero, That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime, Sword Art Online (isekai elements)

## JAPAN-ALL: Genre Workflow Tuned for Japanese Manga Reader Contracts

The same 6-step workflow applies to all genres, but Japanese manga reader expectations add specific constraints:

### Japanese Manga Reader Contracts (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Effort-Reward Symmetry:** Reader invests 200+ pages; teaching and catharsis must justify that investment absolutely.
2. **Serialization Patience:** Japanese readers accept slow burn (1-3 year serialization); early atoms seed deep teaching for later payoff.
3. **Respect for Effort:** Main character's effort must be respected; shortcutting via talent alone violates contract.
4. **Collective Resonance:** Teaching must resonate with implicit Japanese cultural values (impermanence, duty, sacrifice, acceptance).
5. **Panel Precision:** Japanese manga's visual discipline creates trust; teaching lives in precise panel composition and negative space.
6. **Silence as Cultural Language:** Silence in manga is not emptiness; it's the space where reader completes meaning. Japanese readers are trained to read silence.

### Japan-Specific Atom Selection Adjustments

- **Prioritize Impermanence Over Permanence:** Buddhist impermanence resonates deeply; Vedic eternal consciousness requires careful framing
- **Honor Duty and Sacrifice:** Atoms teaching autonomous choice over duty may conflict; frame as "duty honored through wisdom, not blind obedience"
- **Leverage Seasonal Markers:** Japanese readers respond to seasonal metaphor; atoms about cycles/renewal fit perfectly
- **Group vs. Individual Balance:** Atoms about individual transcendence must balance with collective responsibility; pure individualism breaks contract
- **Acceptance Over Resistance:** Teaching must show acceptance before transcendence; Western "overcome and win" narratives require adjustment

### Japan-Specific Arc Structure Adjustments

For shōnen (most popular):
- Escalation must feel earned, not gifted
- Training sequences are sacred; cannot be shortcut (unlike some Western action narrative)
- Sacrifice moments carry enormous weight; cost must be genuinely felt
- Victory is hollow if effort and duty are not honored
- Integration of cost is the actual ending (not the victory moment)

For seinen (sophisticated audience):
- Subtext is expected; Japanese readers will hunt for hidden meanings
- Silence can extend further than Western seinen tolerance
- Moral ambiguity is not weakness; it's honesty
- Integration may be "acceptance of irresolution," not triumphant resolution
- Shadow-work is expected in villain design

For shōjo (emotionally literate audience):
- Recognition must be mutual; protagonist and love interest grow together (not one transforms the other)
- Vulnerability is strength, not weakness; no rescue fantasies
- Small gestures carry enormous weight; Japanese shōjo readers decode micro-expressions
- Chosen family equals romantic family in emotional weight
- Independence within connection is the teaching

For iyashikei (introspective, burned-out audience):
- Permission to exist without transformation is itself the teaching
- Small moments accumulate into belonging; no grand revelation needed
- Seasonal rhythm is teaching rhythm; flow with seasons, not against them
- Solitude and company are equally valuable; neither is superior
- Integration means "fitting naturally into ecosystem," not "standing out"

### Japan-Specific Somatic Targets

Japanese manga readers embody dharma through:
- **Breath rhythm:** Pacing of panels, silence duration, beat spacing (readers unconsciously match their breath to page rhythm)
- **Seasonal embodiment:** Recognition of seasonal metaphor in body/emotional state
- **Micro-expression reading:** Trained by years of manga to read feelings in tiny facial changes; teaching can be entirely non-verbal
- **Panel flow:** Reading right-to-left creates specific cognitive flow; dharma moves with that flow
- **Negative space:** Japanese aesthetic values empty space; dharma can live purely in what's absent
- **Ritual repetition:** Small repeated moments (character's morning tea, seasonal greeting) accumulate into belonging teaching

### Japan-Specific Silence Budget

Japanese manga culture tolerates (even prefers) extensive silence:
- Seinen: 40-50% silence is acceptable and expected
- Iyashikei: 60-70% silence is preferred; any more dialogue feels intrusive
- Shōnen: Even 15% silence carries weight; used strategically at arc turning points
- Shōjo: 20-25% silence; often at vulnerability/recognition moments

Japanese readers are trained to fill silence with meaning; you can trust the reader to complete the teaching.

### Forbidden Phrases (Japan-Specific Additions)

- Anything that explains emotion rather than showing it (readers find this insulting)
- Dialogue that names obvious things ("This hurts" when already shown through art)
- Narration that announces what's about to happen (undermines seasonal patience)
- Western-style affirmation ("You can do it!" feels shallow to mature Japanese readers)
- Simplistic resolution after complex setup (betrays reader trust in serialization patience)

## Implementation Workflow

**For Each Story in Pipeline:**

1. **Receive atom_ids from Teaching Library**
2. **Look up target genre; load genre_blueprint.json**
3. **Execute 6-step workflow:** Profile → Selection → Assignment → Villain → Structure → Phrases
4. **Generate all 7 outputs:** arc_structure, villain_spec, transmission_map, forbidden_phrases, somatic_targets, series_constraints, silence_budget
5. **Pass arc_structure + villain_spec to Story Architect**
6. **Pass transmission_map to Chapter Writer (with atom text withheld; only hiding places given)**
7. **Pass forbidden_phrases to QC Agent**
8. **Flag any atoms requiring Japan-ALL adjustments**

- Arc structure honors all non-negotiable canonical moments for genre
- All selected atoms have assigned hiding places (no orphaned atoms)

- Villain design includes both surface defeat + integration teaching
- Silence budget aligns with genre norms (±5%)
- Forbidden phrases list catches all didactic triggers
- Cold read gate would pass: reader feels before analyzing
- Japan-ALL adjustments applied if targeting Japanese manga format
