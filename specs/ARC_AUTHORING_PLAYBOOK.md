# ARC AUTHORING PLAYBOOK

# (How To Design Arcs That Don't Break the System)

---

## 0. WHY THIS EXISTS

Arc is now the single emotional authority.

If arcs are weak:

* Books feel flat.
* Engines drift.
* Writers compensate with melodrama.
* Validators creep back in.

So arcs must be designed intentionally.

This playbook prevents arcs from becoming the new failure point.

---

# 1. WHAT AN ARC IS (NOT POETRY)

An Arc Blueprint is:

> An emotional pacing map for a specific persona processing a specific engine pattern.

It is not:

* A plot outline
* A transformation promise
* A motivational journey
* A dramatic screenplay

It is emotional calibration.

---

# 2. ARC DESIGN SEQUENCE (STEP-BY-STEP)

Design arcs in this order:

### Step 1 — Define Engine Reality

Before drawing any curve, answer:

What does this engine actually feel like over time?

Examples:

* Shame → Exposure → Freeze → Social narrowing
* Anxiety → Scan → Spike → Control attempt → Exhaustion
* Burnout → Overextension → Flatness → Cynicism → Withdrawal

If you cannot describe the pattern in 4 lines, do not design the arc yet.

---

### Step 2 — Choose Resolution Type

From engine-allowed types:

* open_loop
* internal_shift_only
* grounded_reframe
* identity_shift

Never choose identity_shift unless engine explicitly allows.

This single field prevents 80% of past drift.

---

### Step 3 — Draw Emotional Band Curve

Rules:

* Bands change by max 2 between chapters
* Peak occurs before final chapter
* Final chapter cannot be peak
* One hot zone only

For 8 chapters, default healthy curve:

1–2–3–4–5–4–3–2

Do not overdesign.

If curve looks dramatic, it's probably wrong.

---

### Step 4 — Assign Emotional Temperature

Temperature is tonal intensity.

* cool → calm, observational
* warm → emotionally engaged
* hot → confrontation / cost

Temperature and band are related but not identical.

Hot cannot occur more than twice in 8 chapters.

---

### Step 5 — Place Cost Chapter

Exactly one.

Must:

* Sit in hot zone
* Follow at least one escalation chapter
* Precede descent

Cost is visibility, not catastrophe.

---

### Step 6 — Assign Reflection Strategy

Rotation should:

* Avoid monotony
* Fit emotional intent

Example healthy sequence:

didactic, socratic, narrative_embedded, didactic, socratic, narrative_embedded, didactic, socratic

Arc declares this.
Compiler enforces match only.

---

### Step 7 — Define Motif

Motif anchors emotional coherence.

Must include:

* Primary symbol
* Recurring question
* Tonal signature

Motif is glue, not decoration.

Bad motif: "Phoenix rising"

Good motif: "Glass walls" (for shame in corporate setting)

---

# 3. ARC FAILURE MODES

Watch for:

* Over-escalation (too many hot chapters)
* False transformation ending
* Emotional whiplash
* Identity rewrite in non-identity engine
* No descent after cost

If arc reads like a movie trailer, it's wrong.

---

# 4. ARC QUALITY TEST

Ask:

If prose were perfect, would this curve feel psychologically believable?

If answer is no: Redesign.

---

# 5. ARC SCALE STRATEGY (FOR 10K BOOKS)

You do not need 10K arcs.

You need:

~300–500 well-designed arcs covering: persona × topic × engine × format

Arcs are reusable scaffolds.
Atoms create variation.

Invest in arc quality.

---

# 6. ARC AUTHORING DISCIPLINE

Arc authors must:

* Understand engine deeply
* Resist dramatic temptation
* Prioritize emotional realism over intensity
* Accept smaller arcs over cinematic arcs

This is therapeutic pacing, not drama writing.
