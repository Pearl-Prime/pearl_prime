## Chapter logline

Mei signs up for a body-awareness class to "manage stress efficiently," then nearly walks out when asked to do nothing but feel her own hands. In the moment before she leaves, her body says something her words have been unable to reach for years.

## Panel script (render-ready, ~35 panels)

### Page 1 — The woman who forgot she has a body

**P1** — *SILENT.* Wide: a quiet community-center room, late afternoon light, a few mats on a wood floor. Soft and a little plain.
**P2** — Mei in the doorway, coat still on, phone in hand, glasses catching the light. Slightly out of place, scanning the room like a problem to solve.
> **CAP:** She came to manage her stress. Efficiently. The way she manages everything.
**P3** — Close: her shoulders, up near her ears, where they have apparently lived for years without her noticing.

### Page 2 — Sitting down, still working

**P4** — Mei sits on a mat, posture stiff, still mentally somewhere else. A half-formed work thought.
> **THT:** *if I leave by six I can still finish the report—*
**P5** — *SILENT.* The instructor — older, unhurried, no clipboard — sits at the front. Says nothing for a moment.
**P6** — Close on Mei's hand, sneaking the phone face-up in her lap. Old reflex.

### Page 3 — The instruction

**P7** — The instructor speaks. Warm, plain.
> **DLG (instructor):** "For the next few minutes, we're not going to do anything. Just feel your hands."
**P8** — Mei's face: a flicker of *that's it?* Faintly impatient. Glasses pushed up.
> **THT:** *...feel my hands. Okay.*
**P9** — *SILENT.* Around her, others settle, eyes closing. Mei is the only one still half-braced.

### Page 4 — The resistance

**P10** — Close on Mei's hands resting on her knees. She looks at them like data that won't load.
> **THT:** *I don't — feel anything. This is a waste of—*
**P11** — Her eyes flick to the door. The exit. Her weight shifts to leave.
> **CAP:** She has spent so long living from the neck up that below the shoulders is a country she stopped visiting.
**P12** — *SILENT.* The instructor, eyes closed, unbothered. The room breathing without her.

### Page 5 — The pause before leaving

**P13** — Mei half-rises — then stops. One more second. She lets her hands rest, just to say she tried.
> *(no text)*
**P14** — *SILENT.* Close on her hands. Really close now. The lines of the palm, the slight warmth.
**P15** — *SILENT.* Closer still — a faint pulse visible at the wrist. The smallest thing. Alive.

### Page 6 — The body speaks first

**P16** — Mei's face shifts — surprise she didn't plan. She felt something. A warmth. A weight.
> **THT:** *...oh.*
**P17** — Close on her hands again — and now, drawn faintly, the sensation she's noticing: warmth pooling in the palms, a tingle in the fingertips.
> **CAP:** The body had been talking the whole time. She just never stopped long enough to listen.
**P18** — *SILENT.* Her shoulders — the ones up near her ears since page 1 — drop. A visible centimeter. Unbidden.

### Page 7 — Below the shoulders

**P19** — The instructor, gently:
> **DLG (instructor):** "Now your shoulders. Don't fix them. Just notice where they are."
**P20** — *SILENT.* Mei notices. Her own shoulders, for what might be the first time in years. The held-ness of them.
**P21** — Close: the shoulders soften further, and her breath — caught high and shallow since she walked in — drops lower into the belly.
> **CAP:** Nobody told the shoulders to relax. They softened because, finally, someone was home.

### Page 8 — The stomach that always knew

**P22** — Mei's hand drifts to her stomach — the one that tightens before every meeting, every hard conversation.
> **THT:** *It's tight. It's always tight. I never... noticed it was a feeling.*
**P23** — *SILENT.* Close on the belly under her hand, rising now with a slower breath.
**P24** — Mei's face — something between recognition and grief. The body has been carrying this alone for a long time.
> **CAP:** Not a problem to fix. A message she'd been too far up to receive.

### Page 9 — The breath

**P25** — *SILENT.* Mei's eyes close — not because she was told to, but because she wants to stay with this.
**P26** — Close: a full breath in, the whole torso moving for once, not just the upper chest.
> **CAP:** In through the nose. All the way down to the hands she'd forgotten.
**P27** — *SILENT.* The long exhale. Shoulders, stomach, jaw — all of it letting go a fraction at once.

### Page 10 — Decompression

**P28** — *SILENT, LONG DROP.* Wide: the quiet room, the mats, the late light, Mei among the others now — no longer the stiff outsider. Just a body in a room, resting.
**P29** — Close: her phone, still face-up in her lap, screen dark. Forgotten. For once.
**P30** — *SILENT.* Mei's face, soft, eyes still closed. The work thought from page 4 simply... not there anymore.

### Page 11 — Closing returns

**P31** — The class ends. Mei opens her eyes slowly, blinking back into the room, reluctant.
> **THT:** *I almost left.*
**P32** — Close on her hands, resting open on her knees — the same hands from page 3, but the fists are gone.
> **CAP:** Healing that starts below the shoulders. She found the doorway in her own palms.
**P33** — *SILENT.* Mei stands, rolls her shoulders once — a small, new habit being born — and picks up her coat.
**P34** — *SILENT.* Wide pull: Mei in the doorway again (echo of P2), coat in hand, but standing differently now. Lower. More here.
**P35** — *TYPOGRAPHIC ONLY* on soft cream. Episode card.
> **Hands, Shoulders, Breath** — Episode 1
> *"Your body has been trying to tell you something."*
