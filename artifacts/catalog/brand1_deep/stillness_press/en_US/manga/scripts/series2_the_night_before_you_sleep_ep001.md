## Chapter logline

It is 11:40 p.m. and Yuki cannot make herself go to bed, because going to bed means lying in the dark with her mind. Over one slow hour by a single lamp, she stops trying to fall asleep — and discovers that resting was the thing she'd been refusing all along.

## Series-memory notes (for episode 2 continuity)

- **Visual through-lines established:** the single warm lamp (the series' anchor image — light that stays on so the dark isn't faced alone); the oversized cardigan; the hand-on-chest gesture (shared motif with series 1, intentional brand cohesion); the clock (mark time's passage to show the hour is survivable, not eternal).
- **Character state at chapter end:** Yuki has learned *one* move — dropping the second arrow, permission to be awake. Ep 2 should test it on the harder case: actually waking at 3 a.m. (this chapter was the *going-to-bed* problem; ep 2 is the *waking* problem).
- **Silence discipline:** this chapter ran ~20 silent panels of 35 (~57%) — on target for the 52% series ratio. The *slow_descent* engine means each page should feel quieter than the last; resist the urge to add caption text in the back half.
- **Palette:** warm cream / dawn gold / amber lamplight by night; keep contrast even lower than series 1 (4% black fill). No flashback palette this chapter — series 2 stays in the present, in the room, by design.
