## [PHASE 1 — OPENING · ~10% · voice moderate, pitch normal · pause ~800ms]

It's late. The bed has been ready for an hour, and you haven't. [PAUSE]

This is The Stillness Check-In, from Stillness Press. I'm Ahjan. This is a different kind of episode — one you're not really meant to finish. You're meant to fall asleep somewhere in the middle of it, and that's not a failure. That's the whole design. So get comfortable. Turn the lights down. Put the phone face-down, somewhere you can't see it. There's nothing left to do tonight but rest. [PAUSE]

Let's begin where you are. Lying down. The weight of your body held by the mattress. You don't have to hold yourself up anymore — the bed is doing that now. Notice the places where you're supported: the back of your head, your shoulders, the backs of your legs. [PAUSE] All of it, held.

## [PHASE 2 — NARRATIVE · 10-50% · voice gentle, pitch slightly lower · pause ~1200ms]

There's an old teaching about two arrows. [PAUSE]

The first arrow is the thing that happens. Tonight, maybe, the first arrow is simply this: you're awake when you wanted to be asleep. That's it. That's the first arrow. It's just a fact, and facts don't have to hurt. [LONGER PAUSE]

The second arrow is the one you fire into yourself. It's the panic about being awake. The arithmetic — *only five hours left, then four, and tomorrow will be ruined.* The story that something is wrong with you. [PAUSE] The first arrow is unavoidable. The second arrow is optional. And the second arrow is the one that keeps you awake.

So tonight, we're going to set the second arrow down. [PAUSE] Not the first one — you can't always control whether you're awake. But the second one, the panic, the arithmetic, the story — that one, you can lower. Just lower it. Let it rest on the ground beside you. [LONGER PAUSE]

Because here is the truth your tired mind has trouble holding: the list it wants to make right now is not a nighttime list. Whatever it is — the conversation, the email, the thing you're behind on — none of it can be done at this hour. This is the least urgent hour of the entire day. Nothing has to be solved right now. Nothing. [LONGER PAUSE]

The mind generates the worry because, in the dark, with nothing to distract it, it finally hears what's been humming under the whole day. Nothing new arrived tonight. The volume just came up, because the day got quiet. [PAUSE] And what's humming is not danger. It's an old habit of keeping watch — a watch you can let go of now. [LONGER PAUSE]

## [PHASE 3 — DEEPENING · 50-75% · voice low, pitch lower · pause ~1800ms · ambient bed slightly up]

Let your breath get a little longer on the way out. [LONGER PAUSE] In through the nose… [PAUSE] and out, slow, longer than the in. [LONGER PAUSE] You don't have to count. You don't have to do it right. Just let the breath out be a little longer than the breath in. [LONGER PAUSE]

On the inhale, the heart speeds up, just slightly. On the exhale, it slows. So a longer exhale tips everything toward slowing. Heart rate, down. The body, softening. [LONGER PAUSE] The long breath out is the signal the body has been waiting all day to hear. The signal that says: there is nowhere to run. You can rest. [LONGER PAUSE — 5s]

Let's move attention slowly down through the body. Not to fix anything. Just to notice, and let go. [LONGER PAUSE] The forehead. Let it be smooth. [LONGER PAUSE] The jaw. Let the teeth part, just slightly. [LONGER PAUSE] The shoulders. Let them drop, away from the ears, into the bed. [LONGER PAUSE — 5s] The hands. Let them be heavy. [LONGER PAUSE] The belly, rising and falling on its own. [LONGER PAUSE — 5s] The legs, heavy, held. [LONGER PAUSE]
