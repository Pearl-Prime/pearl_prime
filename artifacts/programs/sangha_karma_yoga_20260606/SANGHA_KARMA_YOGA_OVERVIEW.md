The hands that move it are the sangha. The arrangement of those hands is what this program is.

## What sangha karma yoga is

Karma yoga is service as practice. Work done in dedication, with attention to the inner state of the doer. The sangha already knows the teaching. The operator already gave it. This program is the field where the teaching becomes load-bearing.

Seven work pillars. One weekly Sunday Zoom for coordination. Four quarterly gatherings — one teaching from a master, one Pearl empowerment from Ahjan. One annual in-person USLF retreat. That is the entire rhythm. It is built deliberately to be **extremely powerful, not extremely frequent**, because the sangha already carries its own weekly and monthly classes; this program adds one weekly touch and four quarterly gifts and one annual gathering, and nothing more.

## The seven pillars

The pillars are the work. Each pillar shadows specific Pearl_* agents in the technical system, but the pillar itself is human — a group of sangha members serving together under a Dharma Steward (the pillar's lead).

**P1 — Pearl Prime contributor pool.** Authors atoms, story-cells, gratitude practices. Cleans teacher doctrine. Backfills persona × topic atoms. This pillar is where the books get better. Active Karma Yogis here are part of the Tier I profit-share pool.

**P2 — 48 Social contributor pool.** Authors short-form social distribution — clips, hooks, platform-specific copy, the daily and weekly flow of social posts that brings new students into contact with the teachings. Active Karma Yogis here are part of the Tier I profit-share pool.

**P3 — Pearl News contributor pool.** Authors and fact-checks Pearl News articles, runs locale review, validates teacher attribution. The work here is shaped by `docs/PEARL_NEWS_WRITER_SPEC.md`. Tier II spiritual access for V1; Tier I considered in a follow-up cap once Pearl News revenue matures.

**P4 — United Spiritual Leaders Forum administration.** Coordinates the forum cadence — master scheduling, panelist coordination, recording, translation, downstream distribution to Pearl News and Pearl Prime and 48 Social. The first three USLF events (Koyasan, Tokyo, Los Angeles) already happened; this pillar makes the fourth and fifth and sixth possible. Tier II spiritual access + Tier III recognition for sustained service.

**P5 — United Nations Spiritual Alliance for Youth (UNSAY) administration.** Youth-outreach extension of USLF — taking the alliance teachings to the next generation through whatever forms the youth respond to. Scope and operating cadence still need operator clarification (a question card item in §13 of the spec). Tier II + Tier III.

**P6 — Sangha brand administrators.** This is the largest pillar by potential headcount. Each Sangha brand administrator runs a Sangha-themed book lane — composite teachings from the alliance of masters, CTAs to Sangha materials and free meditations, onboarding funnels for new students. One Sangha karma yogi runs each lane. The work is shaped by `BRAND_ADMIN_CANONICAL_PACKAGE.md` and the existing brand-admin tooling. Tier II spiritual access plus **Sangha Author credit** on every book published in the lane.

**P7 — Sangha new-student onboarding ops.** Funnel mechanics from discovery (someone hears about the sangha) through to intake (they show up at a sangha event with consent recorded). Lives downstream of P2 and P6. Tier II + Tier III.

## The role ladder

Roles are dharma-shaped, not corporate-shaped. Movement up happens by sustained service, not application.

A **Karma Yogi** is any active sangha volunteer in any pillar — the foundation. A **Dharma Steward** is a pillar lead, coordinating one pillar's weekly heartbeat. The initial seven Dharma Stewards are nominated by the operator post-spec-merge. A **Sangha Author** runs a P6 brand lane and gets the Sangha Author credit on every book published in that lane. A **Pearl Empowered** is a Karma Yogi who has received their first Pearl empowerment from Ahjan — recognition of a lineage milestone, not a promotion in rank. A **Cross-pillar Coordinator** is an optional rotating role bridging two or three pillars, pulling Sunday agenda items together; one quarter on, the next quarter off.

The Pearl Empowered designation deserves a careful note. It is the operator's transmission — the work he describes in the LA transcript when Pamela introduces him doing transmission earlier that day: *"Ajahn did his transmission work, uh, energy. It was beautiful. It was so strong. It was very, very strong."* It is a unique offering only Ahjan can give. The brochure deck frames it with appropriate gravity but without overusing the word "exclusive." The gravity speaks for itself.

## The Sunday Zoom

Sundays, sixty minutes (the spec proposes; operator can hold at sixty or revise). Nine to ten in the morning Pacific Time, five in the afternoon UTC. This timeslot is recoverable for nearly every region; for the regions where it isn't, the recording goes up the same day and reading the Sunday-notes doc plus acknowledging commitments asynchronously counts as attendance.

The agenda is structured but light. Five minutes of opening dharma reflection — a Dharma Steward, rotating, offers a brief anchoring from the operator's teachings or the master alliance. Not a class. Not a sermon. An anchoring. Fifteen to twenty minutes of pillar heartbeat reports — each Dharma Steward reports what their pillar shipped this week, two minutes each, and if a pillar has nothing to report it says so. Ten minutes of cross-pillar issues — anything where one pillar is waiting on another, or where two pillars are stepping on each other, surfaced and resolved or escalated. Ten to fifteen minutes of next-week commitments — each pillar names what it intends to ship, publicly, so it can be tracked. Five minutes of closing dedication — a different Dharma Steward than the opener, reverent.

That is the meeting. Coordination, not teaching. The teaching cadence lives elsewhere in the sangha.

## The quarterly gifts

Four quarters in the year. Each quarter contains two gifts that come back to those who serve.

A **master teaching** — one of the masters in the alliance offers a private teaching to the Karma Yogi cohort. Joshin Sensei or Master Feung or Master Wu or Master Sha or Sai Maa via Dayananda Das or another lineage holder in the forum. The operator picks the quarter's master based on availability. The teaching is recorded for the volunteer-only archive (with the teacher's consent), so Karma Yogis who couldn't attend live can receive it later.

A **Pearl empowerment** from Ahjan — group transmission, quarterly, for all active Karma Yogis. There is also a 90-day milestone individual empowerment for those completing their first quarter active. The exact distinction between Pearl empowerments and Pearl transmissions — whether they are the same thing, or whether transmissions are something more — is a question card item the operator can answer in his own voice when he is ready. The spec records the operator's own framing as authority and otherwise defers to the operator's teaching at delivery.

Then once a year, the **in-person USLF retreat**. The operator and the masters gather in person; active Karma Yogis are invited. "Active" defaults to three of four quarters active in the prior year. Travel stipend is operator-discretion, case by case.

These three gifts — the master teaching, the Pearl empowerment, the annual retreat — are not contractual entitlements. They are dharma support flowing back to those who serve. The brochure language stays humble about this. The gravity speaks for itself.

## The profit-share

For Karma Yogis active in Pillar P1 (Pearl Prime) and Pillar P2 (48 Social), there is also a structured profit-share — Tier I in the spec's tier vocabulary.

The mechanism, as proposed: a distributable surplus pool is established quarterly from Pearl Prime and 48 Social net surplus. Each active contributor receives a one-percent allocation per quarter active. "Active" defaults to four-plus Sundays attended in the quarter plus at least one shipped deliverable in their pillar. A total per-project cap defaults to twenty percent — meaning if there were more than twenty active contributors in a quarter, the percentages would be distributed by contribution-weight rather than expanded past twenty percent total.

This Tier I one-percent flow is layered on top of, not in place of, the operator's pre-existing ten-percent-to-teachers flow that he describes in the LA transcript when explaining how brand directors share revenue with the masters. Both flows operate; they apply to different parties.

The exact legal structure of the profit-share pool — whether it is distributable surplus from a for-profit, member-allocation from a member association, nonprofit grant from a 501(c)(3), or some hybrid — is the single most important open question in the spec, and the operator's legal counsel must confirm the structure before any actual allocation is paid. The spec uses placeholder mechanism language until then.

Pearl News (P3) is in Tier II only for V1. It receives all the spiritual-access gifts but is not in the profit-share pool until a follow-up cap addresses the Pearl News revenue model.

## How a sangha member joins

Five stages. The first three are warm; the next two are commitment.

**Discovery** — someone in the sangha hears about it. A current Karma Yogi mentions it in conversation. A Dharma Steward names it at a sangha event. The brochure deck circulates at quarterly satsang. The operator names it directly at a teaching. Word of mouth, in the sangha's own slow way.

**Discernment** — the sangha member sits with which pillar fits. The seven-pillar overview slide from the deck helps; one-to-one conversations with current Karma Yogis or Dharma Stewards help; an optional one-page skill / interest matching form helps if more structure is wanted.

**First Sunday — warm-welcome** — the matched pillar's Dharma Steward greets the new Karma Yogi by name in the opening dharma reflection. Introduces them to one current Karma Yogi in the pillar — the "first friend." Walks them through one shipped deliverable so they see what shipped looks like.

**30-day check-in** — Dharma Steward 1:1. What is your felt experience so far? Is this pillar a fit, or would another be better? What support do you need? The conversation is warm and frank.

**90-day milestone** — sustained service makes the Karma Yogi eligible for their first Pearl empowerment. The default is invitation to the next quarterly group ceremony from Ahjan. For Karma Yogis completing their first quarter active, a 90-day individual milestone empowerment can also be offered. This is also when the Karma Yogi becomes eligible for Sangha Author consideration (P6 only) and for full Tier II access.

The 90-day milestone is also the moment the program lets someone pause or step out without stigma. The next section covers that.

## Pausing and stepping out

Sustained service over years is a high asking. The program holds space for life seasons.

A Karma Yogi who needs to **pause** notifies their Dharma Steward thirty days ahead when possible. They are welcomed to return at any time without re-onboarding for up to twelve months; longer absence means standard onboarding flow on return. Tier II spiritual access continues for the remainder of the current quarter. Tier I profit-share allocations are prorated to active quarters only.

A Karma Yogi who **steps out permanently** has a conversation with their Dharma Steward and, if desired, with the operator. A dignified send-off in the next Sunday closing dedication. Sustained-service titles are retained on the lineage record — once a Sangha Author, always a Sangha Author, on the books that were published in their lane. Tier II spiritual access continues for the remainder of the current quarter and is not extended.

No public stigma. No "leaving the sangha" framing. Karma Yoga participation is one expression of sangha membership, not a precondition.

## Governance

Pillar leads — the initial seven Dharma Stewards — are nominated by the operator post-spec-merge. Subsequent Dharma Stewards are nominated by current Dharma Stewards by consensus and confirmed by the operator. Annual rotation review happens at the USLF retreat; rotation is encouraged after two years of service but not mandatory.

Disputes resolve through proximity. Pillar-internal disputes go to the Dharma Steward and then to the operator. Cross-pillar disputes go to the Cross-pillar Coordinator and then to the operator. Profit-share allocation disputes go through a three-Dharma-Steward review panel with operator confirmation, and legal counsel is consulted if structural questions surface. A Karma Yogi who feels the program is misframing them can always request a one-to-one with the operator.

Operator decisions categorize by envelope. Decisions that fit within already-given operator-policy can be routed through Pearl_Operator_Proxy and logged to the operator decisions log. Decisions that don't fit get escalated to the operator directly. This program's launch and first-year operation is out-of-envelope by default — operator-attended throughout. Specific in-envelope routings can be added by the operator as the program matures.

## Phasing

**Phase A** is the pilot. Twelve to twenty sangha members for one quarter. Initial seven Dharma Stewards in place. Twelve Sundays, one master teaching, one Pearl empowerment, three monthly check-ins per Karma Yogi. Tier I profit-share is placeholder during Phase A — the legal counsel review concludes before any actual allocation. Operator-attended throughout.

**Phase B** expands to the full sangha. A second Sunday cohort opens if demand justifies. Tier I profit-share activates once legal-counsel review concludes. Cross-pillar Coordinator rotation begins.

**Phase C** formalizes. The legal profit-share structure becomes binding. The USLF authority doc is authored as a follow-up cap. V1.1 follow-up caps address Sangha brand wizard mode, composite voice rules, Sangha freebie deltas, Sangha cover treatment. The annual USLF retreat establishes a recurring rhythm.

**Phase D** is steady state. The quarterly cadence and annual retreat operate without further structural change. Sangha Author lineage records grow. The Pearl Empowered population grows organically through 90-day milestones and quarterly group ceremonies.

## What this program asks of the sangha

It asks for sixty minutes on Sundays and a willingness to ship something in your pillar each week. It asks for honesty in the cross-pillar discussion and reverence in the opening and closing. It asks for sustained presence over months, which becomes years.

It asks for the sangha to recognize that the operator's mission has a body now — Pearl News, Pearl Prime, 48 Social, the alliance, the books and the manga and the audio — and the body has a use for hands. Many hands. Each hand placed deliberately.

What it gives in return is not contractual. The master teachings, the Pearl empowerments, the in-person retreat — those are dharma support, flowing back to those who serve. The profit-share, for those in P1 and P2, is structured mechanism, pending legal counsel. The Sangha Author credit, the lineage record of sustained service, the recognition — that is the sangha holding its own.

And the program holds gracefully for the seasons when a Karma Yogi needs to pause or step out. No stigma. The sangha continues. The work continues. The operator continues.
