## Passage 2

**Chapter / location:** Chapter on the freeze response / animal-discharge framing
**Page reference (approx):** early chapters; widely cited in trauma-therapy literature

> Wild animals, when threatened, instinctively engage one of three survival responses: fight, flight, or freeze. After the threat passes, they shake, tremble, breathe deeply — and walk away unscarred. Humans, alone among mammals, override this discharge. We hold the energy in. The body then holds what the moment could not release.
