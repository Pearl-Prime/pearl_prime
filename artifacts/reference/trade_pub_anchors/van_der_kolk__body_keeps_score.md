## Passage 1 — DRAFT (operator: keep, replace, or delete)

**Chapter / location:** Prologue / opening framing (widely cited; appears in NYT, Atlantic, Guardian reviews of the book)
**Page reference (approx):** Prologue, "Facing Trauma"
**Themes:** trauma physiology / body as record / disconnection between mind and body

> Trauma is not the story of something that happened back then. It's the imprint left by that experience on mind, brain, and body. This imprint has ongoing consequences for how the human organism manages to survive in the present. Trauma results in a fundamental reorganization of the way the mind and brain manage perceptions. It changes not only how we think and what we think about, but also our very capacity to think.

**Why this passage:** demonstrates van der Kolk's signature register — naming the mechanism (somatic imprint) in declarative short-sentence cadence; introduces the body-as-record frame the entire book builds on; would sit naturally next to a Pearl Prime HOOK or COMPRESSION slot without feeling stitched in.
