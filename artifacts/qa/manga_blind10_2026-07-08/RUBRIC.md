# Manga Blind-10 — 8-Axis Rubric (human judges)

**Scale:** 1–5 per axis. Score **the candidate episode only**; use comparators to calibrate what 4 and 5 mean.

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Broken / amateur — would not publish |
| 2 | Below serial minimum — obvious craft failures |
| 3 | Competent web serial — publishable with revision |
| 4 | Professional — matches licensed serial quality |
| 5 | Top reference tier — would sit beside landmark works in genre |

---

## A1 — Panel-flow readability

Can a reader follow action, eyeline, and time across panels without confusion?

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Yotsuba&!-grade clarity; eyelines and panel order invisible |
| 4 | Professional webtoon; one re-read at most per page |
| 3 | Followable but occasional jump cuts or crowded gutters |
| 2 | Frequent confusion; wrong read order |
| 1 | Illegible flow |

---

## A2 — Page-turn / tobira placement

Do beat drops, reveals, and decompression land at correct rhythm for vertical scroll or page turn?

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | March Comes in Like a Lion–grade emotional pacing |
| 4 | Strong hook cadence; long-drop used sparingly and correctly |
| 3 | Adequate rhythm; some beats early/late |
| 2 | Monotonous panel sizing; no emphasis hierarchy |
| 1 | No discernible pacing craft |

---

## A3 — Character consistency

Does each named character read as the same person across panels?

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Print-native consistency (face, hair, costume, proportions) |
| 4 | Webtoon pro bar; 1–2 minor drift panels |
| 3 | Recognizable but hair/face shifts noticeable |
| 2 | Frequent identity breaks |
| 1 | Different character per panel |

---

## A4 — Lettering craft

Bubble placement, font/size hierarchy, integration with art (not pasted-on).

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Hand-lettered integration; bubbles compose with negative space |
| 4 | Professional digital lettering; no occlusion failures |
| 3 | Readable but generic fonts or overlap issues |
| 2 | Obstructs art; inconsistent hierarchy |
| 1 | Missing, wrong, or illegible lettering |

---

## A5 — Genre-trope fluency

Does the episode speak the genre's visual and narrative language (not generic AI mush)?

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Genre-native tropes deployed with intent (iyashikei silence, battle impact frames, etc.) |
| 4 | Clear genre read; tropes present |
| 3 | Generic illustration; genre readable but thin |
| 2 | Wrong genre signals |
| 1 | No genre identity |

---

## A6 — Anatomy / hands

Bodies, faces, hands, props — no gross defects.

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Studio-clean; hands and joints correct |
| 4 | No gross errors; minor stylization OK |
| 3 | Occasional hand/limb issues |
| 2 | Repeated anatomy failures |
| 1 | Unpublishable defects |

---

## A7 — Tone / screentone quality

Value range, atmosphere, texture — matches genre register (watercolor iyashikei vs high-contrast battle).

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Mushishi / Mori-tier atmospheric control |
| 4 | Coherent tone throughout episode |
| 3 | Uneven tone; some panels off-register |
| 2 | Flat or clashing palettes |
| 1 | No tonal craft |

---

## A8 — Dialogue register

Do captions and dialogue sound like the genre + demographic (not exposition dump, not off-voice)?

| Anchor | Example signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Barakamon-grade natural speech; subtext present |
| 4 | Professional; on-genre |
| 3 | Functional but expository |
| 2 | Stilted or wrong demographic voice |
| 1 | Broken / placeholder dialogue |

---

## Aggregation (recorder computes — judges do not)

Per episode, per axis: **median** across judges.
**Episode PASS:** all axis medians ≥ 4 AND no axis median < 3.
**Run PASS:** ≥ 8 / 10 episodes PASS.
