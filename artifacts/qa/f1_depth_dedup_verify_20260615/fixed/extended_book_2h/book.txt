Where Ahjan departs from the usual framing is here... Ahjan teaches that one moment of noticing changes the situation. Not the
facts of your life. Not the size of your problem. The situation. The
internal coordinates. The relationship between you and what is happening.

This is the most ordinary teaching he gives, and also the one most people
underestimate. They think they need a thousand moments of noticing,
arranged in a daily practice, sustained for years, before anything will
shift. Ahjan looks at this and says, "No. One. You only need one. And
then you need to be willing to let one mean something."

Here is how he describes it. The body is tight. The mind is racing. The
pattern is running its familiar program. You are about to react the way
you always react. And then, somewhere in the middle of it, you notice.
You see the tightness in the chest. You see the racing thought. You see
the pattern getting ready to fire. The noticing takes about half a second.

Most people dismiss this half-second. They say, "But I still reacted. I
still got swept up. The noticing did not stop anything." Ahjan smiles. He
says, "You are evaluating the half-second by the wrong standard. The
half-second was not supposed to stop anything. It was supposed to be
there. And it was."

Because here is the thing about that half-second of noticing. It is not
the same thing as being entirely inside the pattern. There was a witness.
There was, however briefly, a part of you that was not the reaction. That
part is the practice. That part is what Ahjan calls awareness. And
awareness, even for half a second, is the seed of every freedom that
comes later.

Ahjan says: do not require the noticing to be long. Do not require it to
fix anything. Do not require it to feel good. Just let it count.

The permission he gives, over and over, is the permission to let small
moments of awareness be the practice. Not the perfect twenty-minute sit.
Not the year-long retreat. The half-second. The one breath. The single
glance at the chest that says, oh. There is the tightness. Hello.

That is the path. Not all at once. One half-second at a time. And every
one of them counts.
