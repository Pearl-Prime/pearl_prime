Empirical diagnosis of `phoenix_v4.planning.registry_resolver._parse_canonical_txt`
block-shape sensitivity (2026-07-11, Pearl_Writer)

Synthetic fixture A (single-delimiter shape, matches existing
atoms/educators/imposter_syndrome/REFLECTION/CANONICAL.txt v01-v05):

  ## REFLECTION v01
  ---
  family: F1
  voice_mode: teacher

  Body paragraph one here. Body paragraph two here.
  ---

Result: _parse_canonical_txt(..., slot_type='REFLECTION') -> 0 atoms parsed.

Synthetic fixture B (two-delimiter shape):

  ## REFLECTION v01
  ---
  family: F1
  voice_mode: teacher
  ---

  Body paragraph one here. Body paragraph two here.
  ---

Result: -> 1 atom parsed, metadata populated, full body content preserved.

Real-file cross-check:
- atoms/educators/imposter_syndrome/REFLECTION/CANONICAL.txt (pre-repair, 5 headers,
  fixture-A shape): _parse_canonical_txt(...) -> 0 atoms.
- atoms/educators/anxiety/REFLECTION/CANONICAL.txt (40 headers, mixed shapes):
  _parse_canonical_txt(...) -> 10 atoms (only the fixture-B-shaped blocks, v16-v25
  range; the fixture-A-shaped blocks, the majority, parse to 0 silently).
- atoms/nyc_executives/anxiety/REFLECTION/CANONICAL.txt (proven-working per July 10
  reconcile doc's render proof): _parse_canonical_txt(...) -> 10 atoms (same pattern).

Conclusion: the "thin pool" framing (5 vs 20-40 header count) undercounts the real
problem. Header count is not a reliable proxy for usable-atom count in this file
family. The correct diagnostic is a direct call to the real production parser, not a
grep for "## REFLECTION v" headers. This lane's 15 new atoms were authored in the
verified two-delimiter shape and confirmed to parse 15/15 via the same direct call
before the full spine-render proof was run.

Root cause is a pre-existing quirk in _parse_canonical_txt's delimiter-count state
machine, not something introduced by this lane. Not fixed here (out of WRITE_SCOPE —
code changes are excluded; this lane authors canonical atom content only). Flagged
as a residual, out-of-scope finding worth a dedicated lane: potentially many
"authored" REFLECTION atoms across other personas/topics may be silently inert at
the real parser despite passing a header-count-based audit.
