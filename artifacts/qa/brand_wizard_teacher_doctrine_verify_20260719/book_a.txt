=== TEACHER_WRAPPER_INTRO ===
For Master Feung, the work begins in the body, not the idea.

When I teach calligraphy, I ask my students to look at the full scroll before they
look at any single character. The eye wants to go immediately to the detail — to judge
this stroke, to correct that proportion. But the scroll speaks first as a whole.
You must learn to receive it that way.

Humanity is a Grand Painting. I do not mean this as a beautiful phrase. I mean it as
a way of seeing that changes what you do next. In a painting, every mark belongs. The
dark areas are not mistakes waiting to be lightened. The empty spaces are not
absences waiting to be filled. The character placed at the edge of the composition is
not less important than the one at the center — it is exactly where it needs to be to
make the center possible.

You are one mark in this painting. Your life, your particular shape, the specific weight
and direction of your stroke — these are not accidental. You did not land in the wrong
part of the composition. The painter placed you here, in this moment, in this
relationship to everything around you.

When people come to me carrying the feeling that they have failed to become what they
were supposed to be, I show them the scroll. Not to comfort them — to reorient them.
The question is not whether you match an ideal. The question is whether you are fully
yourself. That is the only contribution the painting needs from you.

=== TEACHER_WRAPPER_EXERCISE ===
Use this practice to make the idea physical:

Stand. Feel the feet. Let the breath drop into the lower belly for three cycles.

=== TEACHER_WRAPPER_CONCLUSION ===
Return to the scroll. One mark is enough for today.

As Master Feung reminds us, Taoist is not a destination but a way of returning.
