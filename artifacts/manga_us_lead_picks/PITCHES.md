# US Manga — Lead-Series Picks (1 per Brand)
**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Scope:** 13 US English-market brands, 1 lead manga series each
**Source profiles:** `config/source_of_truth/manga_profiles/brands/*.yaml`
**Status:** Pitches ready — pending Pearl Star (Ollama) + image bank gates before script generation

---

## Reading the deck

Each pitch has the same structure so a manga editor can scan a portfolio without flipping back:

| Field | What it answers |
|---|---|
| **Logline** | Single-sentence pitch (≤25 words). The hook. |
| **Reader Promise** | The core felt-shift the brand profile already locked. Source-of-truth. |
| **Premise** | Setup, situation, status quo before the inciting interruption. |
| **Main Character** | Who the reader rides shotgun with — name, age, occupation, the wound. |
| **5-Arc Spine** | One-line beat per arc — the emotional + plot turn. Roughly 6–8 chapters per arc. |
| **Teacher Anchor** | The named teacher whose tradition supplies the inner-life vocabulary. |
| **Visual Grammar** | The art-direction handle from the brand profile. |
| **Comp Titles** | What it sits next to in a Webtoon/Crunchyroll grid. Tells acquisition where it lives. |
| **Target Reader** | Demo + emotional state at moment-of-pickup. |

## 1. Body Memory · *Body Memory* (josei · grief)

- **Logline:** A trauma-informed bodyworker discovers that the chronic pain she's been treating in her clients lives, untreated, in her own back — and the body is the only one who knows the timeline.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will find that the body has been keeping the grief safe until you were ready.*
- **Premise:** Yui (32) runs a small somatic therapy practice in Kyoto. She's brilliant with other people's bodies and a stranger to her own. A new client — a teenager who looks exactly like her dead younger sister — triggers the pain to start speaking.
- **Main Character:** Yui Tanaka. Thirty-two. Senior somatic therapist, single, lives alone with one extremely judgmental cat. Wound: the eight-year-old version of her decided "the body" was something to manage so the family could keep moving after her sister's accident. Twenty years of perfect management, until now.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Body Speaks.** A chronic pain Yui has explained away for years suddenly has a memory attached.
  2. **The Sister.** Yui realizes the new client is reactivating an unmourned grief — and avoiding therapy will not work this time.
  3. **The Mother.** Yui visits home. The pain has a sound now. The mother has been carrying her own version of it.
  4. **The Practice.** Yui learns from her own teacher (Omote Sensei) that purification is not erasing the past — it is letting it complete.
  5. **The Return.** Yui treats her client differently — and herself differently. The body relaxes a kind of vigilance it has held for decades.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Omote Sensei** (Japanese sacred way-of-life and purification teaching). Vocabulary: *misogi*, *harai*, the body as a vessel that holds and releases.
- **Visual Grammar:** Iyashikei minimalism. Light line weight, 50% silent panels, 8% black fill. The body shown as architecture — joints as rooms, breath as weather.
- **Comp Titles:** *A Silent Voice* (grief mechanics), *Komi Can't Communicate* (somatic isolation), *March Comes In Like A Lion* (slow healing arc).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 27–38 sitting with unprocessed grief whose body has started showing up. The cohort that has done two rounds of therapy and still wakes at 3 AM.

## 2. Bright Presence (TW) · *Bright Presence* (seinen · nondual contemplative)

- **Logline:** A burnt-out Taipei investigative journalist starts seeing — literally seeing — the gap between events and the stories he writes about them, and the gap is growing.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will see what was always already here, before the seeking began.*
- **Premise:** Wei (38) is the city's most respected long-form reporter. After a decade of exposing political corruption he has stopped feeling anything when his exposés land. Then, at a press conference, he watches a politician lie and sees — clearly, in waking life — the lie itself as a small grey shape leaving the politician's mouth.
- **Main Character:** Wei Lin. Thirty-eight. Senior investigative reporter, divorced, one daughter (16) who barely talks to him. Wound: he became a journalist to make truth matter. Truth started mattering less every year. Now something else is asking him to look.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Gap.** Wei starts seeing a gap between speech and speaker that no one else sees. He thinks he's having a breakdown.
  2. **The Old Man.** A street vendor in Wanhua tells him the gap has a name and offers him tea. The tea is dangerous in ways Wei doesn't understand.
  3. **The Story That Won't Land.** Wei tries to write the most important story of his life and discovers his words are now slightly transparent.
  4. **The Daughter.** His daughter asks him a single question that breaks the seeing open.
  5. **The Return to Print.** Wei files the story. It changes nothing in the polls. It changes everything in him.
- **Teacher Anchor:** A composite contemplative voice in the brand profile (the original `adi_da` tag predates the current Pearl Prime roster — production should re-anchor to **Ra** (Advaita Vedanta) for the same nondual register before the script step). Vocabulary: the witness, the gap, presence as the ground that does not move.
- **Visual Grammar:** Symbolic reflection — long architectural panels, water and glass, the lie made visible only in the protagonist's POV.
- **Comp Titles:** *Mushishi* (contemplative seinen with seeing), *Vagabond* (interior weather), *The Way of the Househusband* (Taipei-coded ordinary world).
- **Target Reader:** Seinen readers 28–45 who have stopped believing their own work matters and are quietly wondering what does.

## 3. Cognitive Clarity · *Cognitive Clarity* (shonen · zen action)

- **Logline:** A high-school esports prodigy whose racing-mind made him a champion is forced to learn the one technique that loses every game he plays — silence — when his opponent is now a silence-trained Shingon monk his own age.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will stop explaining yourself and discover what remains when the thought stops.*
- **Premise:** Kenji (16) is the youngest pro fighting-game player in Japan. His mind moves at 280 inputs per minute. His mother is a Shingon nun who watches his streams with the sound off and shakes her head. A new tournament rule — five-second mandatory stillness pauses — collapses his entire technique. The kid who beats him in the qualifiers turns out to be a temple-raised teenager named Sora.
- **Main Character:** Kenji Mori. Sixteen. Competitive gamer, sponsored, has never lost a five-game series. Wound: he became fast because slowness, in his house, meant being the kid the family was worried about. Speed kept the worry off him.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Pause.** New tournament rule. Kenji loses for the first time in two years.
  2. **The Temple.** Sora invites him to train at the temple. Kenji agrees only because losing is unacceptable.
  3. **The Mantra.** Kenji discovers his mother's tradition is a competitive technology — single-pointed attention — that makes his 280 APM look frantic.
  4. **The Final.** Kenji and Sora meet again in the tournament final. The match is decided in the silence between rounds.
  5. **The Quiet Rivalry.** They keep competing. The competition is now in service of something neither would have named at the start.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Joshin** (Shingon Mikkyo). Vocabulary: *sanmitsu* (body-speech-mind alignment), *mantra* as a technology for attention, the trained pause.
- **Visual Grammar:** Stark zen-ink illustration. High contrast black-and-white, *enso* brush marks framing key beats, minimal screen-tone. Each silence rendered as a full visual event, not absence.
- **Comp Titles:** *Hikaru no Go* (shonen built around contemplative skill), *Shogi 81 Diver*, *Yawara!* (training arc with quiet inner core).
- **Target Reader:** Shonen readers 14–22 who feel addicted to their own speed and suspect — without quite saying it — that something is being missed.

## 4. Devotion Path · *Devotion Path* (shonen · sacred love)

- **Logline:** A teenage boy in a near-future Mumbai signs up for a service-as-spiritual-practice gap year — and discovers that devotion, real devotion, is the most dangerous emotional skill he has ever tried to learn.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will feel what devotion actually is — not performance, but opening.*
- **Premise:** Arjun (17) defers his engineering admission to do a year at a small ashram-aligned community kitchen serving displaced families. He's there because his late grandmother asked him to be. He thinks devotion is a series of correct gestures. Within a month, the work has cracked something open and he doesn't know where to put what is now coming through.
- **Main Character:** Arjun Iyer. Seventeen. Top of his class, accepted to IIT, deferred. Wound: he was raised to be useful and never to be touched. Service was the family's love language. He learned to give and to never receive, and the tradition his grandmother actually carried — opening — was something he watched her do without knowing what it was.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Kitchen.** Arjun arrives. He thinks devotion is competence. The kitchen is unimpressed.
  2. **The Family.** A displaced mother and her two sons take to Arjun. The youngest reaches up. Arjun does not know how to be reached for.
  3. **The Teacher.** Sai Maa visits the ashram. She does not teach Arjun anything. He cannot stop crying after she leaves.
  4. **The Crack.** Arjun's parents come to take him home for a family wedding. He cannot pretend to be the person he was when he left.
  5. **The Return.** Arjun goes back to the kitchen. He finally reaches back. Engineering is on the table again — but on a different table.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Sai Maa** (Vedic teacher of consciousness and grace). Vocabulary: *shakti* as creative force, *grace and mastery* as twin movements, devotion as opening rather than performance.
- **Visual Grammar:** Flowing devotion illustration. Purple and blush palette, ornate panel borders that hold the emotional weight, warm soft light.
- **Comp Titles:** *Frieren* (slow opening to feeling), *Barakamon* (rural service arc), *March Comes In Like A Lion* (boy's interior weather).
- **Target Reader:** Shonen readers 15–22 who were raised to perform competence and are quietly aching to be seen as someone who can be opened to.

## 5. Digital Ground · *Digital Ground* (shojo · light-language manhwa)

- **Logline:** A 19-year-old K-pop idol trainee discovers that the "voice" she has been told she lacks is not a vocal weakness — it is a frequency she has been trained, since age twelve, to suppress.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will find yourself underneath all the screens and notifications.*
- **Premise:** Ji-eun (19) has been at the same Seoul agency for seven years. She is technically excellent, visually correct, and the producers cannot say what is missing from her. She begins working with an unusual vocal coach — a Tokyo-based light-language teacher named Miki — who tells her, in the first session, that the frequency she has been suppressing is the only reason any of this matters.
- **Main Character:** Ji-eun Park. Nineteen. Trainee at a top-tier idol agency. Wound: at twelve she was told her natural voice "wouldn't sell" and was trained out of it. She kept the original voice in a small inner room. The room has been getting louder.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Frequency.** Ji-eun's vocal evaluation flags her as "technically perfect, emotionally absent." She is given Miki as a last resort.
  2. **The First Session.** Miki does not have her sing. She has her listen to a single tone for an hour. Ji-eun cries afterward and does not understand why.
  3. **The Debut Window.** The agency moves her debut up. Miki tells her: do not perform what they trained out of you. Sing the thing you kept.
  4. **The Stage.** Ji-eun debuts. The performance breaks the algorithm in a way no one expected.
  5. **The Aftermath.** Ji-eun keeps training with Miki. The voice is the through-line; the industry is now a side problem.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Miki** (light language, ascended-master transmission, crystal-bowl healing). Vocabulary: frequency, transmission, the suppressed natural voice as a sacred channel.
- **Visual Grammar:** Digital minimalism. Midnight blue and cyan palette, flat clean aesthetic, vertical webtoon-native pacing, panels that feel like phone screens until they don't.
- **Comp Titles:** *Lookism* (idol industry mechanics), *Bug Players* (audio-as-craft webtoon), *True Beauty* (visual industry interior).
- **Target Reader:** Shojo / manhwa readers 15–24 who have been trained to perform a version of themselves they no longer recognize and are looking for permission to stop.

## 6. Heart Balance · *Heart Balance* (josei · catharsis)

- **Logline:** A Cairo-trained therapist who specializes in emotional regulation discovers that her hardest client — the one she cannot crack — is showing her the exact patterns she has been refusing to see in her own marriage.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will feel the weight leave the heart that has been judging itself for too long.*
- **Premise:** Layla (35) runs a successful private practice in Dubai. She has a husband who is "fine," two kids, a clean apartment, and a chronic chest tightness she has explained to herself as caffeine. A new client — a poet — refuses every regulation tool Layla offers. The poet keeps asking her one question: *what would you say if no one was watching?*
- **Main Character:** Layla Saad. Thirty-five. Therapist, mother of two, quietly furious. Wound: she was raised in a family where peace was achieved by no one ever saying the true thing. She made a career out of the technology of containment. The container has started leaking.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Client.** The poet arrives. Layla's standard toolkit fails for the first time in eight years of practice.
  2. **The Question.** Layla starts hearing the poet's question in her own voice. She does not say anything to her husband.
  3. **The Music.** A friend takes her to a *qawwali* concert. Something dissolves. She does not know what to do with the dissolving.
  4. **The Sentence.** Layla writes one true sentence to her husband. The marriage does not end. It changes shape.
  5. **The Practice.** Layla returns to her client list. She is now, for the first time, doing the work she had been describing for years.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Ma'at** (South Indian Sufi). Vocabulary: *sama* (devotional listening), *dargah* (the threshold space), *haal* (the inner state that arrives, not the one you produce).
- **Visual Grammar:** Geometric mysticism with indigo and gold, Arabic-calligraphy-inflected borders on chapter openings, warm interior lighting.
- **Comp Titles:** *Nana* (josei interior life with music), *A Bride's Story* (cross-cultural domestic), *Princess Jellyfish* (woman finding her actual voice).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 28–40 in functional marriages, careers, families — and a body that knows something has not been said.

## 7. Qi Foundation · *Qi Foundation* (seinen · cultivation)

- **Logline:** A junior Hong Kong architect tasked with designing an "energy-flow optimized" tech campus for a billionaire client realizes the building is being haunted not by a ghost but by the unprocessed grief of the land itself — and the only person who can read the land is the eighty-year-old master his client wants him to fire.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will feel what it is to be rooted. The ground does not shake if you are the ground.*
- **Premise:** Daniel (29) is a sharp, bilingual architect who scored the dream commission. The client is a Silicon Valley billionaire who wants a "feng-shui aligned" tech campus on a piece of land in the New Territories. The client has a consultant — Master Feung, eighty, a Taoist cultivation teacher — that he wants Daniel to override politely. Daniel agrees, because the contract is huge. The land starts refusing the design.
- **Main Character:** Daniel Cheung. Twenty-nine. Junior architect, Cambridge-educated, fluent in three languages and one tradition's worth of self-suppression. Wound: he learned in school that the practical answer was always the right answer. The land is now insisting on a different definition of practical.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Commission.** Daniel wins the project. Master Feung is introduced as a client courtesy. Daniel plans to manage him.
  2. **The Refusal.** The first foundation pour fails in ways the engineers cannot explain. Master Feung does not say *I told you so*.
  3. **The Walk.** Daniel walks the land with the master. He begins to feel the *dragon vein* — the continuous energetic flow he has been taught to ignore.
  4. **The Redesign.** Daniel proposes a redesign that respects what the land is asking. The client threatens to fire him.
  5. **The Building.** A different version of the campus is built. It is the first project of Daniel's actual career.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Master Feung** (Taoist cultivation through art and embodied practice). Vocabulary: *dragon vein*, *qi*, the land as a teacher with its own memory and refusal.
- **Visual Grammar:** Traditional Chinese brushwork meeting modern architectural rendering. Earth and gold tones, seal-script accents, generous use of negative space as a structural element.
- **Comp Titles:** *Vagabond* (seinen with master/student), *Spirit Circle* (place memory), *Drops of God* (cultivation expertise narrative).
- **Target Reader:** Seinen readers 26–42 in technical professions who suspect the discipline they trained in is actively missing the most important variable.

## 8. Relational Calm · *Relational Calm* (josei · belonging)

- **Logline:** A 31-year-old Tokyo "professional friend" — paid by lonely clients to share meals — meets a new client whose request, "teach me how to be alone," forces her to confront that she became a professional friend so she would never have to learn the same lesson.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will remember what it feels like to belong to yourself first.*
- **Premise:** Mio (31) runs a small rental-companion business — meals, wedding plus-ones, conversation. She is excellent. She has not been on a real date in four years. A new client, a woman her age, hires her not for company but to be "witnessed while learning to sit alone." The contract is for ten weeks. By week three, Mio is the one being changed.
- **Main Character:** Mio Sato. Thirty-one. Rental-companion business owner, single, lives in a one-room apartment in Shimokitazawa with two plants and an extremely well-organized planner. Wound: at twenty-three she lost the friend group she thought she would have for life. She rebuilt belonging as a service business. It worked. It is also a wall.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Strange Contract.** A client hires Mio to witness her, not perform friendship. Mio does not know what to do with the request.
  2. **The Witnessing.** Week three. Mio realizes she has not sat with herself, in her own life, in years.
  3. **The Channeled Hour.** A friend drags her to a session with Junko, a channeler. The transmission is a single line: *you are allowed to belong to yourself first.*
  4. **The End of the Contract.** Week ten. The client is changed. Mio is more changed.
  5. **The New Practice.** Mio keeps the business but adds a category — *witnessed solitude* — that becomes the most-requested service.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Junko** (contemporary Japanese channeling and light-language spirituality). Vocabulary: *transmission*, *soul remembrance*, *alignment* as the inner posture that lets requests actually land.
- **Visual Grammar:** Wabi-sabi minimal illustration. Warm white and stone tones, asymmetric compositions, maximum negative space — every panel slightly under-filled the way Mio's life is.
- **Comp Titles:** *Solanin* (josei interior solitude), *Skip and Loafer* (warm relational arc), *Witch Hat Atelier* (apprenticeship-into-self).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 27–38 who have built effective social armor and are starting to feel the cost.

## 9. Sleep Restoration · *Sleep Restoration* (josei · night healing)

- **Logline:** A São Paulo ER nurse who hasn't slept more than four hours a night in two years discovers that her insomnia is not a medical problem — it is a refusal to enter a state her own body associates with the night her brother died.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will stop dreading the night and remember that sleep is not something you earn.*
- **Premise:** Marina (34) is a senior ER nurse on the night shift at the largest public hospital in São Paulo. She has not slept properly since the night her younger brother — who she was supposed to pick up — was killed. She manages the city's worst medical nights with chemical precision. Sleep is a war she has been losing. A patient — an elderly Chinese man named Master Sha — refuses sedation and asks her, in halting Portuguese, *who decided you must stay awake?*
- **Main Character:** Marina Costa. Thirty-four. ER nurse, single, lives with her mother. Wound: at twenty-six she was driving to pick up her brother. He died waiting. The night has been the place where the responsibility lives. Sleep felt like absence. Absence felt like the night again.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Patient.** Master Sha refuses sedation. Marina asks him why. He asks her why.
  2. **The Recognition.** Marina realizes her insomnia has a date attached. She has never said the date out loud.
  3. **The Sacred Song.** Master Sha gives her one phrase to chant before sleep. She refuses for two weeks. Then she tries it.
  4. **The Mother.** Marina returns home to the city she grew up in. Her mother has been waiting for this conversation for eight years.
  5. **The Sleep.** Marina sleeps for nine hours. She wakes and cries. She returns to the ER changed — not softer, more accurate.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Master Sha** (Tao Source, Soulfulness, sacred song, love-forgiveness-compassion). Vocabulary: sacred song as healing technology, the night as the place forgiveness becomes possible.
- **Visual Grammar:** Luminous night illustration. Deep violet and soft glow, starfield backgrounds, healing energy rendered as visible warmth in dark spaces.
- **Comp Titles:** *Insomniacs After School* (sleep as story engine), *Mushishi* (night-coded healing), *Goodnight Punpun* (interior weather with grief).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 30–42 with chronic insomnia they have stopped trying to explain medically.

## 10. Solar Return · *Solar Return* (shonen · isekai with purpose)

- **Logline:** A 17-year-old Lagos university student who burned out before he started his first semester is pulled into a parallel world where the sun is dying — and the only person who can wake it back up is someone who has remembered why he was made for fire.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will remember that you were made for fire, not for ash.*
- **Premise:** Tunde (17) was the smartest kid in his secondary school. By the time he reached university, he was exhausted, performance-tracked, and quietly numb. He falls asleep in the library reading about a 14th-century Sufi cosmology and wakes inside it. The world he wakes into is going dark. A teacher who calls himself only Ra finds him and tells him the work is not to save the world. The work is to remember what he was made for.
- **Main Character:** Tunde Adeyemi. Seventeen. First-year medical student, eldest son of a doctor and a teacher. Wound: he was the family's promise made flesh. The promise consumed the person. He arrived at university already burnt out and assumed it was his fault.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Fall.** Tunde falls asleep in the library and wakes in a darkening world. The locals tell him only the sun-rememberer can return.
  2. **The Teacher.** Ra appears. He does not give Tunde a quest. He gives him a mirror.
  3. **The First Burn.** Tunde tries to perform purpose, the way he always has. The world reads it as ash.
  4. **The Real Fire.** Tunde finally drops the performance. The fire that comes through is not what he expected. It is older.
  5. **The Return.** Tunde wakes back in the library. The book is a different book. So is he.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Ra** (Advaita Vedanta — using the sun-as-witness register from the brand profile's `solar` motif). Vocabulary: the witness, *I AM* consciousness, fire as recognition rather than effort.
- **Visual Grammar:** Ember abstraction. Soot and orange palette, cosmic geometry overlays, hard-edged contrast — the dying world as a beautiful charcoal sketch waiting to be re-lit.
- **Comp Titles:** *Frieren* (purpose-rediscovery isekai), *Fire Punch* (fire as ontology), *Made in Abyss* (descent that becomes return).
- **Target Reader:** Shonen readers 15–22 in their first burnout, who have not yet been told the burnout is information rather than failure.

## 11. Somatic Wisdom · *Somatic Wisdom* (josei · body grief)

- **Logline:** A 36-year-old Boston physical therapist who built her practice on "mind over matter" is forced to revisit every certainty when her own body — with no warning, no diagnosis, and no remediation — starts grieving on its own schedule.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will finally stop fighting the body that has been carrying everything.*
- **Premise:** Hannah (36) is a senior PT who specializes in athletes recovering from injury. Her motto, the one printed on her clinic wall, is *the body is a tool*. After her partner's miscarriage — which Hannah handled "well" — her body begins to do things her training has no name for. A friend recommends a Vajrayana-trained somatic coach named Pamela. Hannah books one session out of skepticism. She does not stop going.
- **Main Character:** Hannah Klein. Thirty-six. Physical therapist, partnered, no children — yet. Wound: she was raised in a family where competence was the love. Bodies were assets to manage. Her training taught her to weaponize that. Her body has now opted out.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Symptom.** Hannah's body starts grieving on a schedule she did not choose. Her clinical mind cannot model it.
  2. **The Coach.** Pamela does not give Hannah a protocol. She gives her permission to *not* manage the next wave.
  3. **The Partner.** Hannah finally lets her partner see what she has been hiding. The relationship deepens in a way it could not before.
  4. **The Practice.** Hannah brings what she has learned back into her PT practice — but now her clients are using their bodies, not having them be used.
  5. **The Body.** Hannah is changed in a way that has no diagnosis code. Her motto comes off the wall.
- **Teacher Anchor:** **Pamela Fellows** (Vajrayana-trained, somatic, life coaching). Vocabulary: *boundary as care*, the body as a site of intelligence rather than a tool.
- **Visual Grammar:** Soft clinical illustration. Cream and rose palette, clean modern lines, warm light — every clinical scene drawn with the kind of care that disarms its own coldness.
- **Comp Titles:** *Bunny Drop* (caregiver becoming care-receiver), *Kids on the Slope* (interior shift inside a competent profession), *I May Be a Guild Receptionist* (work-life boundary inversion).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 30–42 who built a life on competence and are quietly being asked, by their own body, to become receivable.

## 12. Stillness Press · *Stillness Press: Anxiety & Overwhelm Vol. 1* (josei · cozy restoration)

- **Logline:** A 28-year-old NYC consultant who has been "managing" her anxiety with productivity hacks for a decade is sent to a corporate "wellness retreat" run by an unlikely Theravada Buddhist teacher — and discovers that the alarm has been ringing for a reason her resume could not hear.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will find your way back to stillness even when the alarm is still ringing.*
- **Premise:** Priya (28) is a senior management consultant at a top-tier firm. She is exceptional at what she does and exhausted in a way she will not yet name as anxiety. Her firm sends her to a mandatory wellness retreat staffed by an actual Theravada Buddhist nun — Ahjan — who has been teaching for twenty-five years and who is unimpressed by Priya's calendar.
- **Main Character:** Priya Mehta. Twenty-eight. Management consultant, single, lives in a sublet in Brooklyn. Wound: anxiety is the cost of admission to the life her parents immigrated for. She metabolized that as duty. Duty became identity. The body is now sending the bill.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Retreat.** Priya arrives expecting a spa. Ahjan asks her, in the first session, *where do you live in your body?* Priya cannot answer.
  2. **The Saṃvega.** Priya learns that the alarm has been ringing because something is real. She had been treating the alarm as the problem.
  3. **The Client.** Mid-retreat, Priya gets a crisis call from her biggest client. The old self answers. Ahjan watches.

- **Teacher Anchor:** **Ahjan** (Theravada Buddhism, 25 years). Vocabulary: *saṃvega* (the urgency of clear seeing), *vicikicchā* (paralyzing doubt), the trained pause.
- **Visual Grammar:** Iyashikei minimalism. Slate and gold palette, watercolor textures, generous negative space — the retreat drawn as a place where the visual noise finally drops.
- **Comp Titles:** *Sweat & Soap* (workplace josei with body), *Wotakoi* (urban professional interior), *A Man and His Cat* (the cozy-restoration register).
- **Target Reader:** Josei readers 25–35 in high-performing roles who have been functionally anxious for so long they no longer notice it.

## 13. Warrior Calm · *Warrior Calm* (shonen · cultivation rivalry)

- **Logline:** A 16-year-old kendo prodigy in Kyoto whose anger has made him a national champion meets, for the first time, an opponent who refuses to be angered — and the rivalry that forms is the most useful relationship of his life.
- **Reader Promise:** *You will discover that calm is not weakness — it is the sharpest weapon.*
- **Premise:** Hayato (16) is the country's top-ranked junior kendo competitor. His secret: a controlled, weaponized rage. His opponents read it as ferocity. His coach reads it as a ceiling. At the regional invitational, he draws a new opponent — a quiet kid from a rural cultivation lineage named Ryo, taught by a land-cultivation master called Master Wu. Ryo is smiling. Hayato cannot get him to flinch.
- **Main Character:** Hayato Yamamoto. Sixteen. Kendo prodigy, only child, father is a salaryman who has not been to a single match. Wound: he learned to use anger as a substitute for being seen. It worked at the cost of every interior thing.
- **5-Arc Spine:**
  1. **The Match.** Hayato meets Ryo. He wins on points and loses on something he cannot name.
  2. **The Visit.** Ryo invites him to the rural training ground. Hayato goes because he needs to know how he was beaten without being beaten.
