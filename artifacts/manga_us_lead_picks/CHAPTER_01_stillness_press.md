# Stillness Press: Anxiety & Overwhelm Vol. 1
## Chapter 1 — *The Calendar That Stopped Working*

**Format:** Text-only chapter script (no panel art). 18 pages target. Iyashikei minimalism — light line weight, ~50% silent panels, ~40 words/page average.
**Author note:** Written by Claude (Tier 1, operator-present) as a format reference for what Pearl Star will produce at scale once online. Each page below is delivered as: **page-level shot list + dialogue/narration**. Panel-level breakdown comes from the next pipeline step (`flux_txt2img_manga.json` ComfyUI workflow on the image-bank PNGs).
**Word count:** ~720 (intentionally under target — iyashikei honors silence).

---

### Page 1 — Cold Open · Establishing

**SHOTS:** (1) Wide aerial of midtown Manhattan at 6:14 AM — slate sky, no people. (2) Close on a single lit window forty floors up. (3) Inside the apartment: a phone face-down on a nightstand, vibrating. (4) The phone, picked up, scrolling — calendar app filling the screen with twenty-three blocks of color.

**NARRATION (top of page):**
> Priya Mehta has not been late to a meeting in four years.

**(no dialogue, three silent panels under the narration)**

---

### Page 2 — Routine · Pre-Dawn

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya at the bathroom mirror — twenty-eight, sharp, tired in a way only the mirror can see. (2) Coffee dripping into a glass carafe. (3) A laptop opening to forty-seven unread emails. (4) Priya's hand pausing over the keyboard — a single second of held breath.

**INTERNAL (Priya, narration):**
> The trick is to never stop moving. If you stop, the noise catches up. So you don't stop.

---

### Page 3 — The Office · Morning

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya entering the consulting firm's lobby — glass, brass, hush. (2) A senior partner, MARK, intercepting her at the elevator. (3) Mark holding a printed form. (4) Priya's face — measured, unreadable.

**MARK:** Priya. Walk with me.

**MARK:** HR is sending you to the corporate retreat. Two weeks. Starting Monday.

**PRIYA:** I have the Goldstein deliverable on—

**MARK:** I know what you have. I'm telling you to go.

---

### Page 4 — The Argument She Doesn't Make

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya in her glass-walled office, door closed. (2) The retreat brochure on her desk — photos of a low wooden building, a stand of pines. (3) Her hand — steady. (4) Her jaw — not steady.

**INTERNAL:**
> The brochure says *wellness*. I know what wellness is. Wellness is what people who can afford to slow down call slowing down. I cannot afford to slow down.

**(beat — silent panel)**

**INTERNAL:**
> But Mark is not asking.

---

### Page 5 — The Drive Up · Saturday

**SHOTS:** (1) A rental car on a two-lane road, leaves turning. (2) Priya's hands on the steering wheel — knuckles relaxing for the first time in the chapter. (3) A wooden sign: *Pine Hollow Retreat Center · est. 2003*. (4) Her phone, on the dashboard, losing signal.

**(no dialogue — full page silent except for the sign)**

---

### Page 6 — Arrival

**SHOTS:** (1) The retreat building — modest, low-slung, cedar, no logo. (2) An older woman in plain robes raking gravel by the entrance. The woman is **AHJAN** — sixty-something, weathered, calm in a way that does not announce itself. (3) Priya getting out of the car, pulling a hard-shell suitcase. (4) Ahjan looks up. Smiles slightly. Returns to raking.

**PRIYA:** Excuse me — is the front desk inside?

**AHJAN:** *(without stopping)* There's no front desk. The lockers are by the door. Your name is on one.

**PRIYA:** I'm sorry — are you on staff here?

**AHJAN:** *(small smile)* Something like that.

---

### Page 7 — The Locker

**SHOTS:** (1) A cedar wall of small lockers. (2) PRIYA M. on a hand-lettered card. (3) Inside the locker: a folded set of plain clothes, a notebook, a pencil, and a single index card. (4) Priya holding the index card. The card reads, in handwriting: *No phones. No watches. No news. Welcome.*

**INTERNAL:**
> Two weeks.

**INTERNAL:**
> I am going to lose my mind.

---

### Page 8 — The First Session · Late Afternoon

**SHOTS:** (1) A simple meditation hall — eight cushions, eight people. Priya is one of them. (2) Ahjan, now formally seated at the front. (3) The other seven retreatants — varied ages, all corporate-coded, all visibly uncomfortable. (4) Ahjan's eyes — open, soft, not theatrical.

**AHJAN:** I will not give you a teaching today.

**AHJAN:** I will ask you one question.

**AHJAN:** Where do you live in your body?

---

### Page 9 — The Question Lands

**SHOTS:** (1) The room — silent. (2) A man across from Priya — visibly searching. (3) Priya — looking down at her hands, then her chest, then nowhere. (4) Ahjan — waiting. Not impatient. Not warm. Present.

**INTERNAL (Priya):**
> I don't.

**INTERNAL:**
> I don't live anywhere in my body. The body is the thing the calendar moves around in. I have not been inside it in—

**(beat)**

**INTERNAL:**
> I cannot remember.

---

### Page 10 — The Unsayable

**SHOTS:** (1) A wide reverse — the room from Ahjan's POV. Eight people, none of them looking up. (2) Priya's hands — folded too tight. (3) Tight on Priya's eyes — wet without permission. (4) Ahjan — registering, not responding.

**AHJAN:** *(softly, to the room)* Good. That is the right answer for this moment.

**AHJAN:** Tomorrow we will start.

---

### Page 11 — Night · The Cabin

**SHOTS:** (1) A simple cabin — single bed, a desk, a window. (2) The window — pines, dark. (3) Priya sitting on the edge of the bed. (4) The notebook open on her knees. The page is blank.

**(silent page — full)**

---

### Page 12 — The Phone She Doesn't Have

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya's hand reaching, automatically, for a phone that is locked in a cedar box six buildings away. (2) The hand stopping mid-air. (3) A wide on Priya — alone, awake, no exit. (4) Outside the window — a single owl call.

**INTERNAL:**
> The alarm is still ringing.

**INTERNAL:**
> But there is no phone to silence it on.

---

### Page 13 — Morning Two · The Garden

**SHOTS:** (1) Pre-dawn light. The same gravel path. (2) Ahjan, raking again. (3) Priya, in the plain retreat clothes, watching from a distance. (4) Ahjan — without turning — extends a second rake toward her.

**AHJAN:** Come.

---

### Page 14 — The Rake

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya, raking — uncertain. (2) Ahjan, raking — even, steady. (3) A wider shot — both of them, side by side, raking gravel into long parallel lines. (4) Priya's face — confused that anything has loosened.

**PRIYA:** What is this for?

**AHJAN:** It is not *for* anything.

**AHJAN:** That is the point.

---

### Page 15 — The Honest Sentence

**SHOTS:** (1) The two of them on a low bench at the edge of the garden — break time. (2) Two cups of green tea. (3) Priya, holding her cup with both hands. (4) Steam — the only motion in the panel.

**PRIYA:** *(quiet)* I have been functionally anxious for so long I do not know what the body without it would be like.

**AHJAN:** *(also quiet)* Yes.

**AHJAN:** That is what we will start with.

---

### Page 16 — The Teaching · Brief

**SHOTS:** (1) Tight on Ahjan — speaking slowly, without performance. (2) Priya — listening with her whole face. (3) The tea, cooling. (4) A wider shot — the garden, the morning, the two of them.

**AHJAN:** In the tradition I carry, we have two words.

**AHJAN:** *Saṃvega* — the urgency of seeing clearly how serious things are.

**AHJAN:** *Vicikicchā* — the paralysing doubt that follows. The shutdown. The scrolling past.

**AHJAN:** You have been treating both as the same thing.

**AHJAN:** They are not.

---

### Page 17 — The First Locator

**SHOTS:** (1) Priya — eyes closed. Hand on her own chest. (2) Tight on the hand — steady. (3) Tight on her face — something easing she did not know was held. (4) Ahjan — eyes also closed. Smiling slightly. Not at Priya. At the morning.

**INTERNAL (Priya):**
> Chest.

**INTERNAL:**
> I live in my chest. It has been waiting.

---

### Page 18 — End of Chapter

**SHOTS:** (1) A wide on the meditation hall, late afternoon — eight cushions, eight people, all closer to themselves than they were yesterday. (2) Ahjan at the front — eyes open. (3) The window — long light. (4) A close on Priya — not transformed. Located.

**NARRATION (bottom of page, single line):**
> The alarm was still ringing.
>
> But Priya had stopped treating the alarm as the problem.

**END CHAPTER 1.**
