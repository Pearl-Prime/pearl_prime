## Japan (`translate-ja`)

- **Accurate directionally, numbers need caveats**: X (ж—§Twitter) usage skews older/heavier-text-culture in Japan vs. US, so "primary news/discussion platform" is right вҖ” Japan is the one major market where X still dominates real-time discourse. - **Anonymous/semi-private trend: real but overstated as "Gen Z mental-health specific."** Anonymity culture in Japan is structural (real-name social pressure, е»әеүҚ/жң¬йҹі divide), not a new Gen Z wellness behavior вҖ” it's decades-old (2channel lineage вҶ’ 5ch, alt accounts "иЈҸеһў"). - **"Detailed/thorough over brevity" вҖ” mixed, platform-dependent.** True for note, blog-style long-form, and YouTube; less true for Instagram/X captions, which still favor short, scannable text with line breaks and emoji/symbols. - **Human-localization premium claim: plausible, unverifiable, low risk to state softly.**
- **"Modesty" register: still correct but needs precision, not drift.** Wellness/self-help content in Japan avoids hard-sell CTAs, superlatives ("жңҖй«ҳгҒ®дәәз”ҹгӮ’жүӢгҒ«е…ҘгӮҢгӮӢ"), and command-form imperatives вҖ” these read as pushy/foreign. - **Common Western-draft pitfalls**: (1) direct imperative verbs feel bossy; (2) first-person confident claims ("I healed my anxiety by...") read as boastful вҖ” Japanese wellness voice favors distanced/observational framing; (3) over-literal translation of encouragement phrases ("You've got this!") has no natural JP equivalent and sounds childish or thin вҖ” needs full re-localization, not translation.

## Hong Kong (`translate-zh-hk`)

**(1) Accurate**
- Multi-platform bilingual reality (FB/IG/WhatsApp core, Xiaohongshu rising fast among Gen Z) matches lived experience; treat the exact 58%вҶ’62% figure as directional, not verified.
- Cantonese-for-casual / English-for-premium is a real directional tendency, not a myth.

**(2) Stale / wrong / overgeneralized**
- "Traditional Chinese outperforms English on Xiaohongshu" is the weakest claim. XHS's dominant register in HK is often *mainland-inflected* written Chinese (because the algorithm surfaces mainland creators heavily), not HK-flavored Cantonese-Chinese. Brands using XHS need to actively guard against sounding mainland there вҖ” it's a bigger risk than the spec implies, not a safe win for "local Chinese" content.
- The alternating-full-sentence model of code-switching is wrong. - The Instagram vs Threads distinction is missing: Threads skews more English-forward/media-savvy in HK; IG Stories still lead for informal wellness confessional content. XHS suits lifestyle/beauty better than mental-health confession tone.

**(3) Missing, important for credibility**
- Cantonese sentence-final particles (г—Һ/е–Һ/еӣү/е‘Җ/еҳӣ) are what makes text read as genuinely HK vs generic written Chinese вҖ” their absence is often *the* mainland/Taiwan tell, more than vocabulary choice.
- Mainland-vs-HK term traps for wellness copy specifically: жІ»ж„Ҳ / жӯЈиғҪйҮҸ ("positive energy") read earnest-to-preachy and mainland-flavored in HK; HK wellness voice is more self-aware/ironic (e.g. self-deprecating "еәҹйқ’"/"emo" used as adjective: "жҲ‘иҖҢе®¶еҘҪemo") rather than uplifting-slogan register.

## Singapore (`translate-zh-sg`)

**Accurate**
- PDPA opt-in consent and the pre-ticked-box invalidity are correct and current.
- ASAS/CASE disclosure enforcement (#ad, #sponsored, #spon) on undisclosed paid partnerships is real вҖ” but this has been active since ~2022вҖ“2023, not a "2026 requirement." Framing it as newly arriving in 2026 is stale/wrong; it should read as already-established norm.

**Stale / overgeneralized**
- Xiaohongshu (Rednote) in SG is real but niche, not broad-market. It skews toward Chinese-Singaporean women, PRC expat/student residents, and beauty/luxury micro-influencers вҖ” it is closer to a diaspora/China-adjacent channel than a mainstream SG wellness platform. Framing it as generally valuable for wellness/mental-health across the SG market overstates reach; Instagram, TikTok, and especially WhatsApp/Telegram community broadcast channels are the actual dominant wellness-engagement surfaces here вҖ” that's a real gap in the draft.
- "Dialect diversity matters for resonance" is backwards for anyone under ~50. What actually resonates is colloquial "Singdarin" (Mandarin-English-Malay code-switch), not heritage dialect.

**Register question вҖ” the core issue**
"Concise/pragmatic/data-driven" alone reads as generic corporate copy, which is exactly wrong for mental-health content. Singapore wellness/mental-health voice (see IMH, SOS destigmatization campaigns) has moved warmer and more relatable, not clinical вҖ” light Singlish flavor markers (lah, can, steady, shiok, sian) signal authenticity and community warmth even in otherwise practical copy. **PDPA and caption voice**
PDPA consent language belongs in lead-gen forms/backend, not captions вҖ” don't let it leak into voice. Sponsorship disclosure is the exception: that DOES need to sit in the caption itself, by law.

**Missing**
No mention of Malay/Tamil/Eurasian audiences вҖ” wellness copy shouldn't default Chinese-only register.
