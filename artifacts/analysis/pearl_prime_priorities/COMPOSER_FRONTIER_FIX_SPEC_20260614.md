# Pearl Prime — Composer Frontier Fix Spec (verified 2026-06-14)

_Source: `composer-frontier-diagnosis` workflow (9 agents, ~912k tokens), verified on disk against the 9 pilot books in PR #1585. Read-only diagnosis; no code changed._

## Thesis verdict

MIXED, leaning CONFIRMED for the render-quality frontier. The thesis "composer/renderer scaffolding is the frontier, not content/atom scarcity" is CONFIRMED for everything that makes the prose read as machine-assembled, and REFUTED for the two hard build-blockers, which are genuine content gaps. The register-gate HARD_FAIL (the most universal blocker, 9/9 books) is itself MIXED — composer-enabled but content-rooted.

DECISIVE EVIDENCE FOR COMPOSER-AS-FRONTIER (cross-book byte-identical = scaffolding, not scarce atoms):
- The defining signature of a composer bug is a SINGLE string emitted byte-identically across books with different persona+topic. Verified: "What remains is the moment after the alarm fires..." = exactly 48 occurrences across 6 books (10x each in 01/06/08/10); "What remains is the next ordinary moment..." = 15x across ALL 9 books. These are hardcoded literals at chapter_composer.py:1899/1904-1910, NOT atoms. The data-driven bank that SHOULD supply varied per-book bridges (`Ahead of you:` in config/rendering/bridge_transition_families.yaml) returns 0 occurrences across all 9 books — the bank is fully authored and fully bypassed. This is the cleanest possible proof: healthy data exists, the composer never reaches it.
- The truncation orphan "Start with the pressure under the sternum. still bracing." = exactly 153 occurrences, collapsing to exactly 1 distinct form, while the CORRECT full sentence survives exactly 2x per book (== the cap's max_each=2). A scarce-atom problem cannot manufacture 153 identical broken copies of one sentence; only an occurrence-cap that empties a substring (golden_chapter_synthesis.py:1372 alt="") can.
- §10 review package's own bottom line (line 64): "The strongest passages (named-character vignettes, sustained doctrine prose) are genuinely good; the connective scaffolding between them is the visible defect." The atoms are healthy ~13-30 variants where authored; the seams are the defect.

EVIDENCE THAT REFUTES "composer-only" (genuine content scarcity exists):
- Book #3 fatally aborts pre-render on a TRUE content shortage: atoms/gen_z_professionals/financial_anxiety/EXERCISE/CANONICAL.txt has only 3 variants, 0 practice-shaped survivors. Its sibling book #5 (same topic, corporate_managers, 13 variants / 10 survivors) built cleanly. Low atom variants = content, exactly as predicted. NOT a composer bug.
- The register F2 HARD_FAIL (9/9 books — note book 10 also HARD_FAILs with 26 F2, which the per-defect booksAffected list omits) is driven by atom-bank DATA CORRUPTION: 546 of 3,521 primary CANONICAL.txt banks have malformed (no-"##") block headers (full-tree count 1,619/16,292), causing raw atom-id labels ("INTEGRATION v06", "RECOGNITION v04") to leak verbatim into reader prose. This cannot be fixed in the composer because the source files are wrong. A missing renderer GUARD lets it through, but the root is content. So the register blocker is composer-ENABLED, content-ROOTED.

NET: The render-QUALITY frontier (verbatim transitions, truncation orphans, placeholder/cross-persona leaks, register seams) is overwhelmingly composer/renderer scaffolding — CONFIRMED. But two of the three hard BLOCKERS (book #3 EXERCISE abort; the data half of the register failure) are real content/data gaps. The original glue-bank-repair assumption ("atoms are healthy") is correct for the GLUE/bridge banks but FALSE for the corrupted CANONICAL.txt headers and the under-authored EXERCISE/HOOK/registry banks.

### [1] DEFECT 7 — register-gate F2 HARD_FAIL via leaked atom-id labels (raw 'INTEGRATION v06' / 'RECOGNITION v04' lines reach reader prose). CONFIG (secondary): config/catalog_planning/teacher_wrapper_templates.yaml ellipsis-lead/dangling-prep templates (L15,27,28,62) drive the wrapper F2 hits; register_gate.py F2.C lowercase-noun-start heuristic also throws false positives on grammatical mid-atom sentences.
- **Fix:** THREE LANES. (1) CONTENT (the unblocker, no code): repair the 546 corrupted CANONICAL.txt by prepending '## ' to every line matching ^(HOOK|SCENE|STORY|REFLECTION|PIVOT|EXERCISE|INTEGRATION|THREAD|TAKEAWAY|PERMISSION|COMPRESSION|RECOGNITION|MECHANISM_PROOF|TURNING_POINT|EMBODIMENT|COST_REVEAL|RECKONING)\s+v\d+\s*$ whose next non-empty line is '---'. (2) COMPOSER GUARD (fail-closed backstop): extend registry_resolver._parse_canonical_txt to recognize bare block headers, stop _scrub_inline_leaked_slot_markers early-returning when '##' absent, and promote a delivery_contract_gate assertion that any standalone ^<UPPER_TOKEN> v\d+$ line raises DeliveryContractError. (3) CONFIG: fix teacher_wrapper_templates.yaml ellipsis/dangling-prep tails + the register_gate F2.C false-positive split.
- **Effort:** M for content repair (scriptable header-fix across 546 files + spot-author the few '[... content for ... × ...]' stub bodies, Tier-1) + S for the composer guard + S for config. ~1-1.5 days total. Lane (1)+(2) clear the HARD_FAIL.  |  **Books affected:** 9

### [2] DEFECT 2 — template-tail truncation orphans. ### [3] DEFECT 1 — verbatim cross-book chapter bridges. Inter-chapter thread-forward sentences are byte-identical across books and repeat up to 10x WITHIN a single 12-chapter book, with zero persona/topic variation. The authored data-driven bridge bank is never reached.

(C) Wire _fallback_thread to bridge_memory.phrase_used_book/chapter (as _pick_legacy_bridge_with_memory already does) so no bridge repeats >1x within a book. Regression: no chapter-bridge repeats >1x per book; two books with different persona/topic + same thesis produce different bridges.
