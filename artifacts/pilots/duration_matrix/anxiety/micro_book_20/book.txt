Before you keep reading, try breathing through just your left nostril. It sounds simple but it sends a specific calming signal to your nervous system.

This is moon breath or Chandra Bhedana. Breathing exclusively through the left nostril activates the right hemisphere and the parasympathetic branch of your nervous system. In yogic and Ayurvedic traditions this is the cooling, calming channel. Modern research confirms the lateralized effect on autonomic balance.

Close your right nostril with your thumb. Breathe in through your left nostril for four counts. Breathe out through your left nostril for six counts. Keep the right side closed the entire time. Repeat for eight cycles. The left nostril is associated with the parasympathetic branch. Breathing exclusively through it shifts your system toward calm.

Now, I want you to notice a calming that settled in from a direction different than usual relaxation techniques. Left nostril breathing shifts the autonomic balance laterally. The effect often feels more whole-body than breathing techniques that work through rhythm alone.

Now, before you move on, moon breath is the practice to use before sleep. Five cycles of left-nostril breathing in bed with lights off is more effective than most sleep supplements for people whose primary barrier is an overactive mind.
