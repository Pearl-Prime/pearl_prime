This corrects a cruelty you may have been inflicting on yourself for years — the
belief that you must have everything in order before you are allowed to turn toward
the light. Heal first, then come. Stop struggling first, then pray. Become the
disciplined, finished person you imagine is required, and only then approach. Under
this belief, you never quite qualify, and the door stays shut, and the not-yet
stretches across a whole life. Sai Maa says the belief is backward. You do not clean
yourself up to receive grace. Grace is what does the cleaning, and it begins the
instant you turn.

Notice how small a turning she asks for. Not a transformation. Not a guarantee. A
single sincere movement toward the light — one honest moment of willingness, one
breath taken with the door cracked open instead of barred. The lineage she carries
holds grace to be infinite and already pouring; what has been missing was never the
grace but the turn. And the turn can be as quiet as the decision, right now, to stop
holding the whole weight alone.

This is why she will not let you postpone. The mind says begin when you are ready, and
ready never comes, because readiness was never the requirement. The fisherman does not
wait for calm seas that may never arrive; he puts the boat in the water he has. Your
seas are these — the exhaustion, the fear, the unfinished self. Put the boat in
anyway. The current Sai Maa points to is already moving, and it carries vessels that
are far from seaworthy, because seaworthiness was never the price.

So take the step you have been deferring until you were better. Take it as you are,
tired and unsure and only half-believing. Sai Maa teaches that the smallest genuine
turn toward light is met before it is completed — that grace runs to meet the one who
has barely begun to move, and has never once arrived too late.
