Chapter 1

The person you've been supporting is now taking credit for your ideas. Your chest tightens with the recognition that you gave and gave and they received and received. The asymmetry becomes visible and it stings.

The Slack channel for team chat is where people vent now. You've read every single message even the ones not tagged to you. Your WFH bed has become your office again. You close your laptop and lie back.

After the third crisis call of the day, the WFH desk feels wrong. Your workspace is mixed with other people's suffering now. morning sun cutting through the blinds settles over the city outside your window. You close everything and step outside.

Your coworker is sitting across the café table from you. Their sandwich is untouched while they talk about their divorce. the avenue outside outside is blurred by morning sun cutting through the blinds rain streaks. You nod and push your own food around your plate.

this was true? How long have you carried it? If you set it down right now, what
would be different? Not forever. Just for today. What changes when the story stops?

The wound is not your enemy. It is the doorway you keep walking past. Turn toward it once and the whole hallway disappears.
