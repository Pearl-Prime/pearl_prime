## Executive Summary

- Global manga market: **USD 14.84–15.4B in 2024** (range reflects differing methodologies across research firms); projected to reach USD 22–54B by 2032–2033 depending on scope and CAGR assumptions.
- Japan dominates with ~85% of global market (¥704.3B / ~USD 4.65B domestic); digital now **73% of Japan's total manga revenue**, the first year it exceeded print by such a margin.
- The US is the largest non-Japan market; manga in the US **book channel declined 13.2% in dollars and 17.1% in units in 2024**, normalizing from pandemic highs — but comic shop channel grew 12.2%, offsetting declines.
- Webtoon market (separate segment): **USD 7–9.17B globally in 2024**, growing at 28–34% CAGR — significantly faster than traditional manga.
- Korea's webtoon industry reached **2.2856 trillion KRW (~USD 1.7B) in 2024**, up 4.4% YoY; Japan, North America, and China are the top export destinations.
- France is the world's #2 manga consumer market outside Japan; Germany is Europe's largest audiobook market and #2 manga market. Both represent first-mover opportunities for therapeutic content.

## 1. Japan — Domestic Market

### 1.1 Market Size 2024

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Total manga market (Japan) | ¥704.3B (~USD 4.65B) | ICv2 / Statista, 2025 |
| Digital segment | ¥512.2B (~73% of total) | ICv2 "Japan Manga Market Slows as Digital Captures 73% Share" |

| Print volume decline | -8.6% YoY (2024 vs 2023) | ICv2 |

Japan's manga market hit a record high in 2024 in yen terms, driven by digital. However, digital growth is slowing — the single-digit pace for three straight years signals the post-pandemic digital boom has normalized.

### 1.2 Platform Dominance (Japan)

| Platform | Revenue / Notes | Source |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| Piccoma (Kakao) | ¥60B+ (~USD 400M) annual revenue; Japan's #1 consumer-spending app for 2024 (all categories, excl. games) | Anime News Network, Jan 2025 |
| LINE Manga | USD 418M; #3 top-grossing app Japan 2024 | Statista |
| BookWalker | ~¥13B (~USD 91M) annual revenue 2024 | Statista |

### 1.3 Top Sellers 2024 (Oricon)

| Title | Copies (2024 ranking year) | Notes |
|-------|--------------------------|-------|
| ONE PIECE vol. 108 | 1,552,215 | #1 individual volume |
| Jujutsu Kaisen 25 | 1,542,345 | Series total: 7,610,995 copies |
| Frieren: Beyond Journey's End | 4,988,170 (series, full year) | +99% YoY; 9 volumes in top 50 |
| Delicious in Dungeon | 14M total copies by March 2024 | Anime-driven surge |

**Genre dominance:** Shonen (action, adventure) controls the top-sellers chart. Frieren's significant showing is notable — it is a slow-paced, philosophical, contemplative fantasy with iyashikei elements, demonstrating that gentler emotional registers can achieve massive commercial scale.

### 1.4 Digital vs Print Trend (Japan)

- 2019: Digital ~50% / Print ~50%
- 2024: Digital **73%** / Print **27%**
- Direction: digital-only growth; print declining ~8-9% per year
- Key platforms: Piccoma, LINE Manga, BookWalker, Comic C'moA

**AI-generated manga milestone:** In January 2026, a fully AI-generated manga (*My Dear Wife, Will You Be My Lover?*) ranked #1 on Comic C'moA, Japan's largest digital bookstore, outperforming established titles including *Kingdom*. This is the first documented AI-manga #1 in Japan's digital market.

### 2.1 Market Size 2024

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Total comics & GN market (US) | USD 1.9B (+4% YoY) | ICv2 "Comics and Graphic Novel Sales up in 2024" |

| Total global manga market (includes US) | USD 14.84–15.4B | Grand View Research, IMARC Group, various |

The manga book channel contraction is a normalization event, not structural collapse. Covid-era demand created multi-year stockpile effects; normalization was expected and flagged in 2023 reports.

### 2.2 Platform Dominance (US)

| Platform | Notes | Source |

| ComiXology (Amazon) | Integrated into Kindle ecosystem | Phoenix research |

### 2.3 Bestseller Pattern (US, Full Year 2024 Circana BookScan)

Top 20 Manga consisted almost entirely of:
- Shonen Jump titles (Jujutsu Kaisen dominated with 7 of 20 slots)
- Premium collections (Berserk Deluxe, Uzumaki 3-in-1)

No wellness, self-help, or iyashikei title appeared in the US Top 20 in 2024. This is both a structural fact (genre does not chart there) and an opportunity signal (the category is not being served at volume).

### 2.4 Market Trajectory

- 2024: Declining (normalization from pandemic highs)
- 2025+: Projected recovery; comic shop channel growing; digital subscription expanding
- Key growth driver: anime adaptation correlation (Jujutsu Kaisen, Frieren, Dungeon Meshi all surged on anime release)

### 2.5 Entry Barriers (US)

| Barrier | Level | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Amazon KDP self-publishing | Low | Open to all; AI disclosure required; 35-70% royalty |
| WEBTOON Canvas | Low | Free to publish; monetization via views and tips |
| Traditional publisher (Viz, Yen, Kodansha) | High | Closed to AI content; portfolio/contest-based submissions |
| Bookstore distribution (print) | Medium | Requires distributor (Ingram Spark, etc.) |
| AI content disclosure | Required | KDP, FTC rules for health claims |

## 11. Entry Barriers for Self-Published Content

### Language/Localization
- Japanese: Nearly impossible without native-quality localization; traditional publishers control shelf space; manga magazines gatekeep
- Korean (webtoon): More open via Canvas; Korean-language quality required for Naver/Kakao organic discovery
- French: Open platforms (Izneo, KDP.fr); French translation quality critical; Loi Lang affects print pricing
- German: KDP.de open; evidence-based framing required; German translation quality essential
- Spanish: KDP.es and Audible.es both open; Castilian vs. Latin American distinction critical

### Distribution
- Self-publishing platforms (WEBTOON Canvas, Amazon KDP): **Low barrier** — open access, global reach
- Traditional publisher distribution: **High barrier** — closed/contest-based; explicit AI content bans at major houses (Shueisha, Viz)
- Aggregators (PublishDrive, StreetLib): **Medium barrier** — enable Google Play Books, Kobo, Apple Books multi-channel distribution
- Print distribution (Ingram Spark): **Low-medium** — POD available; retail placement harder

### Regulatory
- EU: GDPR applies to all; reduced VAT on ebooks/audiobooks (4–7% depending on country)
- US: FTC truth-in-advertising; Amazon KDP AI content disclosure required
- Korea: Korean publisher/distributor partnership recommended for Naver/Kakao platform access
- Japan: Traditional publisher relationships near-required; open to digital-only self-pub via platforms

### AI Content Policy
- Amazon KDP: **Permitted** with disclosure
- WEBTOON Canvas: **No explicit ban** but AI policy evolving; editorial monitoring
- Traditional publishers (Viz, Yen Press, Kodansha, Shueisha): **Explicit bans** on AI-generated content
- Piccoma / Kakao: No explicit AI ban but AI assistance tools (Helix Shorts) internally used
- Comic C'moA (Japan): AI-generated manga has already ranked #1 (January 2026 documented case)

- The USD 15.4B global manga market figure (IMARC Group) vs. USD 14.84B (Grand View Research) vs. USD 12.6B (Straits Research) — significant variance in methodology; treat all global figures as directional estimates only.
