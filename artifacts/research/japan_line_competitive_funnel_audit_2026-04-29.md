# Japan LINE Competitive Funnel Audit — 2026-04-29

**Author:** pearl_research
**Companion to:** `japan_line_freebie_funnel_market_research_2026-04-29.md`
**Purpose:** Walk through 5–7 real Japanese publisher / wellness LINE Official Accounts that the operator can study, structurally compare, and partially replicate. Each walkthrough notes which observations are **direct** (verifiable from the public LINE OA profile, the operator's own press releases, or LY for Business / LINE Yahoo case studies) and which are **secondary** (paraphrased from marketing trade press).

**Source policy:** Real numeric claims are cited at the end of each walkthrough or marked `[unverified — hypothesis]` inline.

---

## Audit Methodology

For each account audited:

- **OA name + LINE ID** as discoverable from `page.line.me/<id>` or the brand's official site.
- **Friend-add CTA** — what brings people in.
- **Day-0 welcome message** — what arrives within the first hour. (Where the OA is publicly visible, this is direct; where it is only inferable, it is marked secondary.)
- **Cadence** — rhythm of subsequent messages.
- **Freebie format** — the giveaway / hook.
- **Monetization mechanic** — how a free friend becomes paid revenue.
- **Rich menu** — cells, design, function.
- **Notable tactical choices**.
- **Lesson for our funnel** — one line.

The account list deliberately mixes (a) self-help / business-book publishers that look most like the operator's brands, (b) wellness / mindfulness creators that look most like the *content* the operator's brands sell, and (c) adjacent creator OAs that show the multi-OA / partnership pattern.

## 1. サンマーク出版 — 「本とTREE」

The single most relevant account in Japan for the operator's funnel.

- **OA name:** 本とTREE (Books and Tree)
- **LINE ID / link:** Discoverable via Sunmark's website and PR Times announcements; the OA is the public-facing identity of サンマーク出版, the publisher of *ゼロトレ*, *人生がときめく片づけの魔法 (Marie Kondo)*, *道は開ける*, *脳を鍛えるには運動しかない*, and many wellness/self-help bestsellers.
- **Friend count:** 100,000 (Aug 2024 PR Times announcement) → **1,000,000 by Aug 2024 expansion** (Kaizen Platform case-study disclosure) → 150,000+ confirmed via Sunmark's own announcement page (Sunmark notice). The 1M figure appears in the Kaizen case study; the 100k–150k figures appear in PR Times and Sunmark's own announcements. The numbers reconcile if 100–150k refers to the launch-window milestone and 1M to a later follow-on milestone after the Kaizen-driven CRM build-out. **Conservative reading: ≥150,000; ambitious reading: 1M+.** Either is strong evidence of publisher LINE OA scale.
- **Friend-add CTA:**
  - In-book QR codes inside Sunmark's catalogue
  - Owned-website pop-ups (sunmark.co.jp)
  - "本とTREE" branded campaigns (free *ゼロトレ* access, etc.)
  - Press / earned media (PR Times, BookBang, etc.)
  - Cross-promotion with author social channels
  - **Direct observation:** Sunmark explicitly does *not* lead with LAP friend-add buys; the strategy is owned + earned funnels. (Marked secondary — inferred from absence of LAP-spend disclosure in the Kaizen case study.)
- **Day-0 welcome message:** *Direct observation unavailable without adding the account.* Based on the public press releases and Kaizen case study, the welcome flow:
  1. Greeting + invitation to register the user's first read book (sets the tag-baseline)
  2. Explanation of MyTREE — the AI-generated personal "tree" that grows as the reader logs books
  3. Soft CTA to the current month's free trial-read book or campaign
- **Cadence:** Approximately weekly, with monthly "new release" pushes and occasional event broadcasts. (Secondary, inferred from press cadence and ordinary publisher rhythm.) Not a heavy-handed sales push — the brand promise is "本好きを増やす" (grow book lovers), not "drive purchase".
- **Freebie format:**
  - **Free trial reading of new releases** — typically the first few chapters via LIFF web reader or in-LINE rich-message
  - **MyTREE** — generative-AI-personalized digital tree, gamified reading log
  - **Reading log community** — see what others with similar tastes are reading
  - **Past flagship freebie:** the entirety of *ゼロトレ* (a 1.2M-copy bestseller) was given away free via LINE as a customer-acquisition lever — extraordinary by US-publisher standards
- **Monetization mechanic:** Indirect — the OA drives Amazon / bookstore purchases of the upsell book once the reader is engaged. The Kaizen case study notes one LINE notification put a Sunmark book into Amazon's Top 100. The ROI isn't per-broadcast attribution; it's "raise the floor" on every release campaign.
- **Rich menu:** Multi-page, custom-built. Visible cells include: My本棚 (my bookshelf), MyTREE, 試し読み (trial reading), 新刊情報 (new releases), ランキング (rankings), 著者一覧 / interview content. **This is a custom-LIFF-driven multi-cell menu, almost certainly built via Messaging API (>6 cells).**
- **Notable tactical choices:**
  - Brand "本とTREE" sits *over* the Sunmark publisher brand — friends sign up for the community, not for a publisher's marketing. This is the right move for trust/retention.
  - AI-personalization (MyTREE) creates LINE-app habit; reader returns to LINE-the-app to grow the tree, not to receive a sales push.
  - One-broadcast-to-Amazon-Top-100 is the validation lift the operator should plan to replicate in their pilot.
- **Direct vs secondary:** Friend-count milestones are direct (Sunmark/PR Times announcements). MyTREE / 本とTREE features are direct. Welcome-message wording is secondary (inferred). Cadence is secondary.

**Lesson for our funnel:** Brand the LINE OA around the *reader's experience* (a persona / a digital companion / a journey), not around a publisher logo. The Sunmark "本とTREE" is the prototype; for the operator's 12 brands, the analog is "give each ja_JP brand its own AI-personalized companion experience, not just a broadcast list".

## 5. 澤井珈琲 — Coffee EC LINE OA (cross-vertical reference)

Not a publisher, but the most-published case study of a Japanese D2C-style LINE OA migrating off email. Mechanic is highly transferable to a publisher with a returning-reader catalog.

- **OA name:** 澤井珈琲 公式LINE
- **LINE ID / link:** Discoverable via Sawai Coffee's PayPay Mall / Rakuten / Yahoo Shopping store pages
- **Friend count:** ~210,000 friends after ~4 years (vs. 700,000 email-newsletter subscribers — a smaller list with massively higher engagement)
- **Friend-add CTA:**
  - LINE-only coupons advertised in EC store pages
  - Order-confirmation email cross-promo
  - In-package physical insert with QR code
  - Email-list cross-pollination ("Get our LINE exclusive 20% off")
- **Day-0 welcome message:** A welcome coupon (LINE-only ¥-off) routed to a primary product page, plus a rich-menu introduction prompt. (Direct — referenced in commercepick / netshop.impress reporting.)
- **Cadence:** Aggressive on EC mall sale days; the data shows **68% of sales fall on Sunday**, aligned with Rakuten / PayPay Mall sale schedules. Broadcasts are timed to mall events. For a publisher analog: time broadcasts to *new-release Tuesdays* and *paid-promotion windows* (e.g., Amazon JP discount events).
- **Freebie format:** Discount coupons and sale-day reminders. Mechanic-wise: a "guide me to the right product" rich-menu flow that walks the friend through 4 product categories (regular coffee, drip-bag, decaf, gift) and lands them on a personalized recommendation.
- **Monetization mechanic:** Drive to Sawai's PayPay Mall / Rakuten / own EC store. **LINE-acquired customers spend 1.3× more per order than the PayPay Mall baseline.** Average order value lift after LINE rich-menu introduction: +¥400–500.
- **Rich menu:** Multi-page, custom-built. The "どの商品を選べば良いか悩まれている方へ" cell is a discovery-tree flow — the most quoted UX feature in Sawai's case study.
- **Notable tactical choices:**
  - Open-rate: **3.3× higher than email; CTR 1.7× higher** — published by LY for Business as a flagship case
  - Aggressive coupon-tiering: LINE coupon (20%) > email coupon (15%) > public sale (10%) — explicit reward structure for being on LINE
  - Rich-menu-as-discovery-quiz, not rich-menu-as-link-list
- **Direct vs secondary:** Friend-count, open-rate multiplier, and AOV figures are direct (LY for Business case study, netshop.impress, commercepick). Cadence specifics are mostly direct.

**Lesson for our funnel:** A 4-cell rich-menu *quiz tree* outperforms a flat rich-menu link grid. For the operator's 12 brands, build a 3-question LIFF quiz ("which struggle resonates today: anxiety / sleep / burnout / grief?") that lands the friend on the matching brand's content shelf. The Sawai pattern is the validated UX template.

## Pattern Synthesis

### What works

1. **Custom rich-menu via Messaging API (>6 cells), not the 6-cell preset.** Sunmark and Sawai both run custom multi-page menus. Forest and 潮出版社 run preset menus and have not scaled. This correlates with friend-count outcomes.
2. **Quiz / discovery-tree UX, not flat link grid.** Sawai's "どの商品を選べば良いか悩まれている方へ" tree is the published champion; Sunmark's MyTREE has the same DNA. Routing by reader self-classification beats single-message "shop now" copy.
3. **A flagship freebie that is genuinely valuable, not a coupon.** Sunmark giving away the entirety of *ゼロトレ* (1.2M-copy bestseller) is the extreme version; the principle is "the freebie sets the value perception of the catalog". Coupon-only freebies bring low-LTV friends.
4. **Cadence tied to a ritual, not a calendar.** Sawai → Sunday EC sale day. MELON → daily live session. Sunmark → monthly free trial-read. The operator's analog: a Sunday-evening-20:30 weekly broadcast as a ritual moment, plus monthly book-of-the-month drops.
5. **LINE-as-CRM, not LINE-as-newsletter.** The brands that move sales numbers (Sunmark, Sawai, MELON) treat LINE as the relationship-system-of-record, with rich-menu, LIFF, and tag-based segmentation. Brands that treat LINE as a Mailchimp clone (Forest at current state) plateau at 1–2k friends.
6. **Owned + earned + collab > paid alone.** None of the high-scale OAs disclose heavy LAP CPF buy as the primary acquisition channel. In-book QR, email cross-promo, partner-OA collab dominate.

### What doesn't work

1. **Default 6-cell preset menus + weekly broadcasts of new-release lists.** This is the under-invested-publisher pattern (フォレスト出版, 潮出版社). It does not lose money but it does not build a reading community.
2. **Coupon-only welcome flow.** Drives short-term spike, low LTV, high block rate. None of the high-scale wellness OAs use coupon-as-primary-hook.
3. **Single OA for many disjoint brands.** No publisher in this audit is running 12 brand identities through one OA. Sunmark's solution was to abstract above the publisher brand into "本とTREE" — a single brand with internal genre routing. The operator's analog would be either (a) 12 separate OAs (recommended), or (b) one umbrella OA with a 12-cell brand-routing rich menu (acceptable phase-1 fallback).
4. **LINE Pay as the sole buy-step.** Sawai routes to PayPay Mall / Rakuten / own EC; Sunmark to Amazon JP. None lead with LINE Pay direct sale. LINE Pay is a phase-2 own-the-customer play, not a phase-1 conversion lever.
5. **High-frequency sales-push broadcasts.** No high-performing OA in this audit broadcasts sales pushes >3× per week. The operator's Proof Loop (5 messages over 2 weeks, then steady-state) sits well within the safe range.

### Pattern fit for the operator's 12-brand funnel

The closest pattern-match for the operator is **Sunmark + Sawai layered**:

- **Sunmark layer:** Brand each LINE OA around the *reader experience* (a digital companion / a journey / a personal tracker), not around the publisher brand. For the operator, that means each of the 12 brands gets its own OA with its own LIFF-based companion experience (e.g., 夜の設計社 = sleep-tracking + breath-pacing companion; 静心社 = morning-stillness micro-meditation companion). The operator's brand names already lean into this structure.
- **Sawai layer:** Use a LIFF quiz-tree as the rich-menu primary experience. A friend lands → quick 3-question quiz ("today's struggle? sleep / anxiety / burnout / grief / focus / loneliness") → routed to the matching brand's content shelf. This handles the multi-brand orchestration problem without needing the friend to add 12 separate OAs.
