# Manga Reader Promises — Craving-to-Genre Map
## Phoenix Omega Research Artifact
**Date:** 2026-04-20

---

## Purpose

Reader promise = the emotional product a title sells. Every `manga_profile.reader_promise` should anchor to one of the canonical cravings below. Cravings map to genre families and emotional engines, giving the writing system a concrete target for what the reader is chasing chapter to chapter.

---

## The 10 Canonical Cravings

### 1. Aspiration
> "I will feel capable of becoming more than I am."

**Emotional engine:** aspiration
**Primary genres:** battle, sports
**Chapter hook affinity:** vow, new_rival, impossible_task
**Market demo affinity:** shonen
**Reference titles:** My Hero Academia, Haikyuu!!, Blue Lock
**What fails this craving:** protagonist who wins too easily; stakes that don't escalate; no external measure of growth

---

### 2. Catharsis
> "I will feel my suppressed feelings validated and released."

**Emotional engine:** catharsis
**Primary genres:** workplace, social_issue, essay
**Chapter hook affinity:** revelation, emotional_rupture, almost_confession
**Market demo affinity:** seinen, josei
**Reference titles:** Monster, March Comes in Like a Lion, Blue Period
**What fails this craving:** emotional moments that are told rather than built to; resolution that comes too easily; protagonist who doesn't break

---

### 3. Tenderness
> "I will feel small moments of care and connection."

**Emotional engine:** tenderness
**Primary genres:** healing, school, supernatural_everyday
**Chapter hook affinity:** ambiguous_line, almost_confession, arrival
**Market demo affinity:** josei, shojo
**Reference titles:** Yotsuba!, Natsume's Book of Friends, Flying Witch
**What fails this craving:** conflict that overwhelms the quiet; moments that are rushed; characters who don't notice each other

---

### 4. Dread
> "I will feel exquisite unease I can close the book on."

**Emotional engine:** dread
**Primary genres:** horror, mystery, thriller
**Chapter hook affinity:** ominous_image, hidden_truth_glimpse, betrayal
**Market demo affinity:** seinen
**Reference titles:** Uzumaki, Tomie, Monster
**What fails this craving:** dread that resolves cleanly; over-explanation of the threat; no visual language for wrongness

---

### 5. Obsession
> "I will feel pulled forward, unable to stop reading."

**Emotional engine:** obsession
**Primary genres:** mystery, survival, revenge, psychological
**Chapter hook affinity:** revelation, interruption, hidden_truth_glimpse
**Market demo affinity:** seinen, shonen
**Reference titles:** Death Note, 20th Century Boys, The Promised Neverland
**What fails this craving:** chapters that answer their own questions; reveals that are satisfying rather than addictive; protagonist who knows too much

---

### 6. Wonder
> "I will feel the world is larger and stranger than I knew."

**Emotional engine:** wonder
**Primary genres:** fantasy, supernatural_everyday, healing (somatic wonder), sci_fi
**Chapter hook affinity:** arrival, vow, hidden_truth_glimpse
**Market demo affinity:** shonen, seinen, josei
**Reference titles:** Mushishi, Witch Hat Atelier, Made in Abyss
**What fails this craving:** world-building explained rather than revealed; no sense of edge (the world's limit never glimpsed); protagonist who takes wonders for granted

---

### 7. Grief (Processed)
> "I will feel loss held, not ignored."

**Emotional engine:** grief
**Primary genres:** memoir, essay, healing, social_issue
**Chapter hook affinity:** almost_confession, revelation, emotional_rupture
**Market demo affinity:** josei, seinen
**Reference titles:** A Silent Voice, March Comes in Like a Lion, My Lesbian Experience with Loneliness
**What fails this craving:** loss resolved too quickly; grief that is clean rather than physical; protagonist who moves on without a scar

---

### 8. Cozy Restoration
> "I will feel my nervous system down-regulate while reading."

**Emotional engine:** cozy_restoration
**Primary genres:** healing, food, club_hobby, supernatural_everyday
**Chapter hook affinity:** almost_confession, ambiguous_line, small_recognition
**Market demo affinity:** josei, shojo
**Reference titles:** Sweetness and Lightning, Laid-Back Camp, Non Non Biyori
**What fails this craving:** conflict that doesn't resolve; pacing that escalates; unresolved tension that carries into the next chapter

---

### 9. Rivalry
> "I will feel the heat of being opposed by a worthy other."

**Emotional engine:** rivalry
**Primary genres:** battle, sports, workplace (ambition), psychological
**Chapter hook affinity:** new_rival, betrayal, revelation, vow
**Market demo affinity:** shonen, seinen
**Reference titles:** Slam Dunk, Hikaru no Go, Ping Pong the Animation
**What fails this craving:** opponent who isn't worthy; rivalry that becomes friendship too fast; no scoreboard for the reader to track

---

### 10. Longing
> "I will feel the ache of wanting something just out of reach."

**Emotional engine:** longing
**Primary genres:** romance, school, josei workplace (unrequited love)
**Chapter hook affinity:** almost_confession, ambiguous_line, arrival
**Market demo affinity:** shojo, josei
**Reference titles:** Honey and Clover, Nana, Fruits Basket
**What fails this craving:** confession that happens; resolution that satisfies; anything that extinguishes the ache

## Phoenix Therapeutic Extensions

Three cravings specific to the therapeutic lane, activated in the Phase 7 healing-lane expansion:

### 11. Named-ness
> "I will feel my private struggle named in specific language."

**Emotional engine:** cozy_restoration (primary) or catharsis
**Primary genres:** graphic_medicine, essay, healing
**Chapter hook affinity:** revelation (the name arrives), reader_mirror, hidden_truth_glimpse
**Market demo affinity:** josei, seinen
**Commercial rationale:** Bestselling therapeutic content (The Body Keeps the Score, Adult Children of Emotionally Immature Parents) succeeds because it names the un-nameable. Manga can do this better than prose because image carries the specificity — the panel that shows exactly what 3am anxiety looks like is more visceral than the sentence that describes it.
**Reference titles:** My Lesbian Experience with Loneliness (naming depression + self-harm), Inside Mari (naming dissociation)
**Phoenix profiles activated:** burnout_corporate_en, burnout_healthcare_en, burnout_parent_en, overthinking_3am_en, graphic_medicine_anxiety_en, graphic_medicine_chronic_illness_en

---

### 12. Permission
> "I will feel that it's okay to be the way I am."

**Emotional engine:** tenderness or cozy_restoration
**Primary genres:** healing, essay, spiritual_essay
**Chapter hook affinity:** ambiguous_line, small_recognition, almost_confession
**Market demo affinity:** josei, shojo
**Commercial rationale:** Identity-level payoff rather than moment-level payoff. Different from Tenderness (#3) because Permission operates at "I am allowed to be this person" rather than "I was seen in this moment." Highest therapeutic impact.
**Reference titles:** My Lesbian Experience with Loneliness (ch. 1 permission beat), Flying Witch (quiet permission to exist slowly)
**Phoenix profiles activated:** overthinking_rumination_en, spiritual_essay_meditation_en, spiritual_essay_grief_path_en, spiritual_essay_everyday_sacred_en

---

### 13. Reframe
> "I will feel the same thing from a new angle that releases it."

**Emotional engine:** wonder or catharsis
**Primary genres:** graphic_medicine, essay, psychological
**Chapter hook affinity:** revelation, mechanism_reveal, reader_mirror
**Market demo affinity:** seinen, josei
**Commercial rationale:** Core move of every therapeutic bestseller — Atomic Habits, Set Boundaries Find Peace, The Body Keeps the Score all work via reframe. Manga form uniquely delivers reframe via visual metaphor (the diagram-panel that suddenly makes sense, the image that defamiliarizes the familiar feeling). Underused in manga outside graphic medicine.
**Reference titles:** Stitches (medical reframe of childhood), El Deafo (disability reframe)
**Phoenix profiles activated:** overthinking_decision_paralysis_en, graphic_medicine_anxiety_en, graphic_medicine_grief_en

## How reader_promise Plugs Into manga_profile

```yaml
# In any manga_profile YAML:
reader_promise: "You will feel your body's cycle become something you live with rather than fight."
# ^ This anchors to craving #8 (Cozy Restoration) + therapeutic extension #11 (Named-ness)
```

The `MDLG-10 Reader Promise Fulfillment` gate (currently heuristic) validates whether a chapter delivers on the declared craving. Chapters that resolve too fast fail Longing; chapters that explain too much fail Dread; chapters that rush past the naming moment fail Named-ness.
