## 1. Physical Location Requirements per Platform

### Classification Key

| Level | What It Means | Example |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **A -- Language Only** | Anyone worldwide can upload content in the target language. No local presence needed. | Google Play Books, Amazon KDP |
| **B -- Local Bank / Entity** | You need a local business entity or bank account, but it can be foreign-owned (e.g., WFOE in China). | Ximalaya (via WFOE + domestic publisher), LINE Official verified |
| **C -- Local Business Registration** | Need a locally registered entity with local directors or partners. | China ISBN (CSBN requires state-owned partner) |
| **D -- Local National ID** | Only individuals with local citizenship/residency ID can register. | Douyin (Chinese ID required for verification) |

## 2. Teacher Brand Exclusivity + Cross-Border Reach

### The Rule: 1 Teacher = 1 Brand per Lane

Each teacher brand (e.g., inner_light_press) has localized display names in all 13 locales. The question: can one operator in the US run multiple language lanes?

### Answer: YES for most platforms, NO for China and partially for Japan/Korea

**Person A in USA can directly operate:**

| Lane | Platforms Reachable | Notes |
|------|-------------------|-------|
| inner_light_press **en-US** | Amazon KDP, Google Play, Apple Books, Kobo, Spotify, Audible, YouTube, TikTok, Instagram | Full access, no restrictions |
| inner_light_press **es-US** | Same as en-US (same storefronts, different locale) | US Spanish market, same territory |
| inner_light_press **es-ES** | Amazon KDP Spain, Google Play ES, Apple Books ES, Kobo ES, Spotify ES | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **fr-FR** | Amazon KDP France, Google Play FR, Apple Books FR, Kobo FR, Spotify FR | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **de-DE** | Amazon KDP Germany, Google Play DE, Apple Books DE, Kobo DE, Spotify DE, Audible DE | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **it-IT** | Same pattern as above | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **hu-HU** | Google Play HU, Apple Books HU, Kobo HU, Spotify HU | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **pt-BR** | Amazon KDP Brazil, Google Play BR, Apple Books BR | Cross-border publishing fully supported |
| inner_light_press **zh-TW** | Google Play TW, Apple Books TW, Kobo TW, Spotify TW, Readmoo (likely) | Readmoo may need publisher agreement but no residency |
| inner_light_press **zh-HK** | Google Play HK, Apple Books HK, Kobo HK, Spotify HK | Cross-border publishing supported |
| inner_light_press **zh-SG** | Google Play SG, Apple Books SG, Kobo SG, Spotify SG | Cross-border publishing supported |
| inner_light_press **ja-JP** | Amazon KDP Japan, Audible JP, Google Play JP, Apple Books JP, Kobo JP, Spotify JP | All major platforms accessible. audiobook.jp needs local partner. YouTube JP no restrictions. |
| inner_light_press **ko-KR** | Google Play KR, Apple Books KR, Kobo KR, Spotify KR | All major global platforms. Naver/Kakao need local partner. |
| inner_light_press **zh-CN** | Apple Books CN only | **CANNOT reach** Ximalaya, WeChat Read, Dedao, Douyin, Bilibili (verified) without Chinese partner |

### Summary: A US-based operator can reach 12 of 13 lanes on global platforms

The only lane with significant restrictions is **zh-CN**, where all major domestic platforms (Ximalaya, WeChat Read, Dedao, Douyin) require Chinese business entity + domestic publisher partnership + CSBN.

For **ja-JP** and **ko-KR**, the global platforms (KDP, Google Play, Apple Books, Kobo, Spotify) are fully accessible from the US. The local-only platforms (audiobook.jp, Naver Audiobook, Kakao Page) need local partners but represent incremental reach, not the primary channels.

## 3. Cross-Border Reach Matrix

### "If I'm physically in Country X, which lanes can I operate?"

| Operator Location | Can Directly Operate (Level A platforms) | Needs Local Partner (Level B-C) | Cannot Reach Without Partner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **USA** | en-US, es-US, es-ES, fr-FR, de-DE, it-IT, hu-HU, pt-BR, zh-TW, zh-HK, zh-SG, ja-JP, ko-KR (all via global platforms: KDP, Google Play, Apple, Kobo, Spotify, YouTube) | audiobook.jp (ja-JP), Naver/Kakao (ko-KR), Readmoo (zh-TW -- likely accessible but may need agreement) | zh-CN domestic platforms (Ximalaya, Douyin, WeChat Read, Dedao) |
| **Japan** | ja-JP (all platforms including audiobook.jp, LINE Official), plus all global platforms for other lanes same as USA | Naver/Kakao (ko-KR), zh-CN domestic | zh-CN domestic platforms |
| **China (mainland)** | zh-CN (all domestic platforms: Ximalaya, Douyin, Bilibili, WeChat Read, Dedao) | ja-JP local platforms, ko-KR local platforms | Some Western platforms may have friction. Google Play, YouTube blocked in mainland China without VPN |
| **Taiwan** | zh-TW (all platforms including Readmoo, LINE Official TW), plus all global platforms for other lanes | zh-CN domestic (still needs mainland partner/CSBN), ko-KR local | zh-CN domestic platforms (Taiwan entity alone insufficient) |
| **South Korea** | ko-KR (all platforms including Naver, Kakao, Yes24, Kyobo), plus all global platforms | zh-CN domestic, ja-JP local | zh-CN domestic platforms |
| **Hong Kong** | zh-HK, plus global platforms for all lanes. Bilibili accepts HK travel permit. | zh-CN domestic (complex), ja-JP local, ko-KR local | zh-CN full domestic access is post-2020 more restricted |
| **Singapore** | zh-SG, plus global platforms for all lanes | zh-CN domestic, ja-JP local, ko-KR local | zh-CN domestic platforms |
| **EU (France, Germany, Italy, Spain, Hungary)** | Their respective locale + all global platforms for all lanes (same as USA) | Same restrictions as USA for East Asian local platforms | zh-CN domestic platforms |

### Key Insight

**Global platforms (KDP, Google Play, Apple Books, Kobo, Spotify, YouTube, Instagram) have NO location restrictions for publishers.** Any operator anywhere can reach all 13 locales through these platforms.

**The location-restricted platforms are:**
1. **zh-CN domestic** (Ximalaya, Douyin, WeChat Read, Dedao) -- requires Chinese entity + CSBN
2. **ja-JP local** (audiobook.jp, LINE Official, Piccoma) -- requires Japanese entity or partner
3. **ko-KR local** (Naver Audiobook, Kakao Page, Yes24/Kyobo) -- requires Korean entity or partner
4. **Douyin specifically** -- requires Chinese national ID (Level D)

## 4. Anti-Spam Rules for Teacher Brands

### Scenario: 2 operators, same teacher (Ahjan), different lanes

- Operator A (US): inner_light_press_en_us
- Operator B (Japan): inner_light_press_ja_jp

### Will platforms flag this as duplicate/spam?

| Platform | Risk Level | Analysis |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **Amazon KDP** | **LOW** | Different-language editions are explicitly treated as separate products. Each gets its own ASIN. Translator must be credited. KDP Select exclusivity applies per-language. Having "Inner Light Press" publish in English AND Japanese is normal publisher behavior. |
| **Google Play Books** | **MODERATE** | Strictest anti-duplicate among Western platforms. Different-language editions with unique ISBNs and different metadata are treated as separate works. However, if covers/descriptions look too similar across languages, Google may flag. **Recommendation: ensure cover designs have locale-appropriate variations, not just translated text on identical artwork.** |
| **Kobo** | **LOW** | No known issues with same publisher in multiple languages. |
| **Apple Books** | **LOW** | Standard practice for publishers to have multi-language catalogs. |
| **Spotify** | **LOW-MODERATE** | Tightening spam filters in 2025. Different-language content from same publisher should be fine as long as audio is genuinely different (not just metadata changes). |
| **YouTube** | **LOW** | Same channel can have content in multiple languages. Separate channels per language is also fine. YouTube explicitly supports multi-language content. |
| **TikTok** | **LOW** | Separate accounts per language/market is standard practice for brands. |
| **Ximalaya** | **N/A** | Would need separate Chinese partner arrangement regardless. |

### Same teacher, same platform, 2 languages

Having both "Inner Light Press" (English) and (Japanese) on Google Play is **standard publisher behavior** and will NOT be flagged as spam, provided:

1. Each edition has a **unique ISBN**
2. **Metadata is in the target language** (not just translated titles on English metadata)
3. **Cover designs are locale-appropriate** (not identical)
4. **Audio content is genuinely in the target language** (not a metadata-only change)
5. **Descriptions and keywords are native-quality** (not machine-translated boilerplate)

### Same teacher, 2 operators, same platform

This is transparent to the platform. Both operators publish under the same brand name ("Inner Light Press"). To the platform, it looks like one publisher with a multi-language catalog. **No anti-spam risk** as long as the content is genuinely different (different language = different content).

## 5. Practical Recommendations for Brand Wizard

### What to show operators by location

#### Tier 1: "You can reach these lanes right now" (Level A -- no partner needed)

For **any operator anywhere** (US, EU, Japan, Korea, Taiwan, etc.):

```
DIRECTLY REACHABLE (via global platforms):
  - en-US: KDP, Google Play, Apple Books, Kobo, Spotify, Audible, YouTube, TikTok, Instagram
  - es-US: Same US storefronts, Spanish locale
  - es-ES: KDP Spain, Google Play ES, Apple Books ES, Kobo ES, Spotify ES
  - fr-FR: KDP France, Google Play FR, Apple Books FR, Kobo FR, Spotify FR, Audible FR
  - de-DE: KDP Germany, Google Play DE, Apple Books DE, Kobo DE, Spotify DE, Audible DE
  - it-IT: KDP Italy, Google Play IT, Apple Books IT, Kobo IT, Spotify IT
  - hu-HU: Google Play HU, Apple Books HU, Kobo HU, Spotify HU
  - ja-JP: KDP Japan, Audible JP, Google Play JP, Apple Books JP, Kobo JP, Spotify JP
  - ko-KR: Google Play KR, Apple Books KR, Kobo KR, Spotify KR
  - zh-TW: Google Play TW, Apple Books TW, Kobo TW, Spotify TW
  - zh-HK: Google Play HK, Apple Books HK, Kobo HK, Spotify HK
  - zh-SG: Google Play SG, Apple Books SG, Kobo SG, Spotify SG
  - zh-CN: Apple Books CN ONLY
```

#### Tier 2: "These lanes have bonus platforms if you have a local partner"

```
BONUS PLATFORMS (need local partner/entity):
  - ja-JP: + audiobook.jp, LINE Official (verified)
  - ko-KR: + Naver Audiobook, Kakao Page, Yes24, Kyobo
  - zh-TW: + Readmoo (publisher agreement, likely accessible without residency)
  - zh-CN: + Ximalaya, WeChat Read, Dedao, Bilibili (verified), Douyin
```

#### Tier 3: "These platforms are effectively blocked without the right ID"

```
REQUIRES LOCAL NATIONAL ID:
  - Douyin (zh-CN): Chinese ID card required. No foreign passport accepted.
  - Ximalaya/WeChat Read/Dedao: Chinese CSBN required (needs state-owned publisher partner).
```

### Teacher Lane Availability Display

When an operator selects a teacher (e.g., Ahjan / Inner Light Press):

```
TEACHER: Ahjan (Inner Light Press)
CURRENT OPERATORS:
  - Operator A (US): en-US, es-US, es-ES, fr-FR, de-DE, it-IT, hu-HU
  - [OPEN]: ja-JP, ko-KR, zh-TW, zh-HK, zh-SG, zh-CN, pt-BR

YOU ARE IN: [user location]
LANES YOU CAN CLAIM:
  [Based on location, show available open lanes]
  [Flag zh-CN as "requires Chinese partner" regardless of location]
```

### Recommended Operator Coverage Model

For maximum reach with minimum operators:

| Operator Location | Lanes Covered | Platform Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **1 operator in USA** | en-US, es-US + all European + zh-TW, zh-HK, zh-SG, ja-JP, ko-KR (global platforms only) | 90%+ of addressable market via global platforms |
| **1 partner in China** | zh-CN | Ximalaya, Douyin, Bilibili, WeChat Read, Dedao |
| **(Optional) 1 partner in Japan** | ja-JP bonus | audiobook.jp, LINE Official (verified) |
| **(Optional) 1 partner in Korea** | ko-KR bonus | Naver Audiobook, Kakao Page |

**Bottom line: A single US-based operator can reach 12 of 13 lanes on all major global platforms. The only hard blocker is zh-CN domestic platforms, which require a Chinese entity.**

## 6. Config Implications

### locale_registry.yaml -- No changes needed
The 13 locales are correctly defined. The storefront_ids already reflect the platform reality (zh-CN has `google_play: null`, `findaway: null`).

### platform_knob_tuning.yaml -- No changes needed
The `locale_platform_defaults` mapping is accurate and matches the research findings.

### teacher_brand_map.yaml -- Potential addition
Consider adding a field per teacher brand indicating current operator assignments and open lanes:

```yaml
inner_light_press:
  operator_assignments:
    en_US: operator_a_us
    ja_JP: open
    zh_CN: open  # requires_chinese_partner: true
```

### Brand wizard should enforce
1. **1 teacher = 1 brand per lane** (existing rule)
2. **1 operator can claim multiple lanes** for the same teacher (new finding: this is fully supported on global platforms)
3. **zh-CN lane always shows "requires Chinese partner" warning**
4. **ja-JP and ko-KR lanes show "bonus local platforms available with local partner" note**
5. **Same teacher content in multiple languages on same platform is NOT spam** (confirmed across KDP, Google Play, Kobo, Apple Books)
