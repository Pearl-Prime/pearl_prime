### 1. Ahjan (Buddhism contemporary / Theravada / inner light)

**Tradition:** Contemplative inner light, karma yoga, self-effort, Dharmakaya. Trauma-sensitive meditation and mindfulness.

**Market resonance:**
- **en-US (STRONG):** "Mindfulness," "meditation," "grounding," and "self-compassion" are top wellness search terms. The US mindfulness market is mature and growing. Ahjan's trauma-sensitive framing aligns with the somatic/nervous-system trend dominating US wellness.
- **ja-JP (STRONG):** Japan has deep Buddhist cultural familiarity. "Mindfulness" (マインドフルネス) has entered mainstream Japanese wellness vocabulary. Zen and contemplative traditions are native. Non-fiction wellness is the fastest-growing audiobook segment.
- **ko-KR (MODERATE):** South Korea's "healing" (힐링) culture is growing. Buddhist meditation retreats are popular. However, secular framing preferred over explicitly Buddhist branding.
- **zh-TW (MODERATE):** Taiwan has Buddhist cultural affinity (Fo Guang Shan, Tzu Chi). Meditation apps growing. But "nervous system" framing outperforms "meditation" framing in titles.
- **de-DE (MODERATE):** Mindfulness (Achtsamkeit) is established in DACH market. Requires science-grounded framing per locale notes.
- **fr-FR (MODERATE):** French market accepts philosophical contemplative framing.

**Consumer language mapping:**
- en-US: "guided meditation," "mindfulness for anxiety," "grounding practice," "inner calm," "self-compassion"
- ja-JP: "マインドフルネス" (mindfulness), "瞑想" (meditation), "心の静けさ" (inner calm)
- ko-KR: "명상" (meditation), "힐링" (healing), "마음 챙김" (mindfulness)

**Competitive landscape:**
- Osamu Tezuka's "Buddha" manga is a canonical classic but is narrative/historical, not wellness-instructional. Multiple Buddhist meditation manga exist in Japan (monk-authored business manga, cafe-meditation manga) but NONE in wellness-manga-as-self-help format.
- US market saturated with mindfulness books but manga/graphic-novel format is a whitespace.
- No localized Asian-language polyvagal/nervous-system audiobooks exist (per Rakuten research) — Ahjan's nervous-system-compatible contemplative framing fills this gap.

**Risk level:** LOW. Buddhism is well-understood globally. Trauma-sensitive framing reduces cultural misunderstanding. No medical claims in doctrine.

**Recommended launch locales (priority order):**
1. en-US (baseline, highest existing config scores: anxiety 0.9, self_worth 0.9)
2. ja-JP (Phase 2, cultural alignment)
3. ko-KR (Phase 3, healing culture)
4. zh-TW (Phase 1, Buddhist familiarity)
5. de-DE, fr-FR (Phase 4, secular mindfulness angle)

- **zh-TW (MODERATE):** Theravada has presence in Taiwan. Forest monastery retreats known.
