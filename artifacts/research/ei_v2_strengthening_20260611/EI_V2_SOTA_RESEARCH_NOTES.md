## Topic 1 — Cross-Document Knowledge Synthesis / Concept Clustering

Extracting a "shared root" across many doctrine corpora is fundamentally a cross-document concept-alignment + clustering + structured-representation problem. The defensible pipeline is: contrastive sentence embeddings → density clustering / hierarchical topic structure → cross-document coreference to align the *same* concept expressed differently across teachers → LLM-assisted thematic naming, gated by quantitative validity checks.

- **SimCSE: Simple Contrastive Learning of Sentence Embeddings — Gao, Yao & Chen (EMNLP) — 2021 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08821** — The standard recipe for high-quality sentence embeddings (supervised NLI variant ≈81.6 Spearman on STS). These are the representation substrate for clustering doctrine sentences; the paper's anisotropy/uniformity analysis matters because doctrinal corpora are stylistically narrow and prone to embedding collapse.
- **BERTopic: Neural topic modeling with a class-based TF-IDF procedure — Grootendorst (arXiv) — 2022 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.05794** — The reference modular pipeline (transformer embeddings → UMAP → HDBSCAN → c-TF-IDF) that beats LDA/NMF on coherence and works in low-resource/short-text settings. Directly maps to extracting interpretable "themes" from teacher corpora; its modularity lets you swap in SimCSE embeddings.
- **A Survey on Neural Topic Models: Methods, Applications, and Challenges — Wu et al. (arXiv) — 2024 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.15351** — Situates BERTopic among neural topic models and catalogues evaluation pitfalls (coherence ≠ human judgment, topic-count sensitivity). Use it to justify *which* topic model and *how* to evaluate, rather than treating BERTopic as a default.
- **Contrastive Representation Learning for Cross-Document Coreference Resolution of Events and Entities — Cattan et al. (arXiv) — 2022 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.11438** — Cross-document coreference is the mechanism for recognizing that two teachers describe the *same* underlying concept in different words — exactly the "shared root" alignment step that naive clustering misses. Contrastive mention representations are the bridge from Topic-1 embeddings to concept identity.
- **Cross-document Event Coreference Search: Task, Dataset and Modeling — Eirew et al. (arXiv) — 2022 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.12654** — Reframes coreference as *search*: "find all mentions of this concept across the corpus," which is the operational query EI v2 needs when assembling a composite-essence node from scattered doctrinal passages. `[verify]`
- **Performing an Inductive Thematic Analysis of Semi-Structured Interviews With a Large Language Model — De Paoli (Social Science Computer Review) — 2024 — https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/08944393231220483** — Evidence that LLMs can recover most main themes from text inductively, *with caveats* on interpretive depth. This is the validity warrant (and the warning) for using a Tier-1 Claude pass to name/synthesize the composite essence rather than trusting clustering labels alone.

**Design takeaway.** Build the composite essence as a three-stage structured-knowledge pipeline, not a single LLM summarization call. Stage 1: embed doctrine at sentence/claim granularity with a SimCSE-style contrastive encoder and cluster with BERTopic (HDBSCAN + c-TF-IDF) to get candidate themes. Stage 2: run cross-document coreference/concept-search to *merge* clusters that are the same root concept worn in different vocabulary across teachers — this is what turns 15 parallel taxonomies into one shared-root graph. Stage 3: use a Tier-1 Claude pass only to *name and articulate* each merged node and write its provenance back to source passages, and treat every LLM-asserted theme as a hypothesis validated by cluster coherence + human spot-check (per the thematic-analysis literature), never as ground truth. Represent the output as a typed concept graph (node = root concept, edges = teacher-specific instantiations + somatic-exercise affordances) so "spiritual-root-fidelity" can be *measured* against named nodes rather than vibes.

## Topic 2 — Multi-Objective Fitness / Optimization for Content

Today's weighted-sum scorer is the single most fragile design choice. The literature is decisive that a fixed linear scalarization (a) cannot reach non-convex regions of the trade-off surface and (b) invites objective collapse / reward hacking as you optimize harder. The fix is Pareto-aware objective handling plus collapse safeguards.

- **A fast and elitist multi-objective genetic algorithm: NSGA-II — Deb, Pratap, Agarwal & Meyarivan (IEEE Trans. Evolutionary Computation, 6(2):182–197) — 2002 — https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/996017** — The canonical Pareto-front algorithm; non-dominated sorting + crowding distance give a *set* of trade-off solutions instead of one weighted-sum point. The reference for producing a menu of catalog/book plans spanning the therapeutic↔engagement↔fidelity trade-off for an operator to choose from.
- **Revisiting Scalarization in Multi-Task Learning: A Theoretical Perspective — Hu et al. (NeurIPS) — 2023 — https://neurips.cc/virtual/2023/poster/72766** — Proves linear scalarization *cannot* trace non-convex Pareto fronts — the formal indictment of EI's current weighted-sum. Justifies either Pareto methods or non-linear (Chebyshev) scalarization. `[verify]`
- **Scaling Laws for Reward Model Overoptimization — Gao, Schulman & Hilton (ICML) — 2023 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.10760** — Shows true reward predictably *degrades* as you optimize a proxy harder (Goodhart made quantitative). The central caution for EI: optimizing a learned therapeutic/engagement proxy too aggressively will produce books that score high and help less. Motivates KL/divergence penalties and proxy-vs-true gap monitoring.
- **MORLAIF: Multi-objective Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback — Williams et al. (arXiv) — 2024 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.07295** — Decomposes a global preference into *separate* principle-level reward models instead of one scalar — directly analogous to EI's three named objectives. Argues per-objective modeling is both more learnable and more auditable than a collapsed score.
- **Rewarded Soups: towards Pareto-optimal alignment by interpolating weights fine-tuned on diverse rewards — Ramé et al. (NeurIPS) — 2023 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.04488** — Train one specialist per objective, then *interpolate weights* to get a continuous, near-Pareto-optimal family at inference — no retraining per weight setting. A cheap way for EI to expose a tunable fidelity-vs-engagement dial. (Verified: authors/venue/year confirmed via fetch.)
- **Projection Optimization: A General Framework for Multi-Objective and Multi-Group RLHF — Liu et al. (arXiv) — 2025 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15145** — Generalizes beyond linear aggregation so policies can favor a *specific* objective (e.g., protect root-fidelity as a floor) rather than averaging it away. `[verify]`

**Design takeaway.** Replace the weighted-sum scorer with explicit Pareto handling: keep the three objectives as separate scored channels (per MORLAIF) and never collapse them with fixed weights before optimization. For plan selection, surface a non-dominated *set* of candidate catalog/book plans (NSGA-II–style) and let the operator pick the trade-off, or expose a continuous dial via weight-interpolation (Rewarded Soups). Treat spiritual-root-fidelity as a *constraint/floor*, not a summand — projection-style or Chebyshev scalarization can hit non-convex regions and protect the worst objective, which a weighted sum provably cannot. Critically, instrument the Gao overoptimization gap: hold out a "gold" therapeutic-quality signal, watch proxy-vs-gold divergence as you optimize, and cap optimization pressure before the proxy detaches from real reader benefit.

## Topic 3 — Genetic / Evolutionary Algorithms vs Bayesian Optimization

EI has two distinct optimization problems that call for *different* optimizers: (A) selecting and *sequencing* discrete content atoms — a large combinatorial/permutation space — and (B) tuning a handful of continuous scorer weights with expensive (human-feedback-limited) evaluations. The literature cleanly separates these regimes.

- **A fast and elitist multi-objective genetic algorithm: NSGA-II — Deb et al. (IEEE TEVC) — 2002 — https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/996017** — (Also cited in Topic 2.) Evolutionary operators (crossover/mutation on permutations and subsets) are the natural fit for combinatorial selection+ordering of atoms, and NSGA-II gives it a multi-objective form. The optimizer for problem (A).
- **Differentiable Expected Hypervolume Improvement for Parallel Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization (qEHVI) — Daulton, Balandat & Bakshy (NeurIPS 33) — 2020 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.05078** — Sample-efficient multi-objective BO for *expensive* evaluations, with exact gradients and parallel (batch) candidate selection — but noted to become computationally prohibitive past ~5 objectives. The optimizer for problem (B): tuning a few continuous weights when each evaluation costs scarce human feedback. (Verified: authors/venue/year confirmed via fetch.)
- **Parallel Bayesian Optimization of Multiple Noisy Objectives with Expected Hypervolume Improvement (qNEHVI) — Daulton, Balandat & Bakshy (NeurIPS) — 2021 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2105.08195** — Extends qEHVI to *noisy* objectives — essential because EI's reader-engagement and efficacy signals are noisy human feedback, not clean function evaluations. The noise-aware acquisition is what makes BO trustworthy on sparse review/sales data.
- **A Statistical Comparison Study between Genetic Algorithms and Bayesian Optimization Algorithms — (comparison literature) — https://www.researchgate.net/publication/243569804** — Empirical grounding that BO wins on smooth, low-to-moderate-dimensional, expensive problems (near-optimal in tens of evals) while GA scales better to high-dimensional/cheaper-evaluation spaces. The explicit "which regime → which optimizer" evidence. `[verify]` (older journal source; treat specific numbers as indicative).
- **Hybridization of Evolutionary Algorithms — survey (arXiv) — 2013 — https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.0929** — Survey of EA+local-search hybridization, the conceptual basis for memetic methods. Supports a hybrid EI design where global evolutionary search over plans is refined by exploitation steps. `[verify]`
- **A Memetic Algorithm for the Linear Ordering Problem with Cumulative Costs — Schiavinotto & Stützle (arXiv) — 2014 — https://arxiv.org/abs/1405.4510** — A memetic (GA + local search) approach to an *ordering* problem with sequence-dependent costs — a close structural analogue to sequencing somatic exercises where order and accumulated "dwell"/integration cost matter. `[verify]`

**Design takeaway.** Use the right tool per sub-problem and do not conflate them. For atom *selection + sequencing* (large discrete/permutation space, cheap to evaluate against a learned scorer), use an evolutionary algorithm — NSGA-II for the multi-objective set, ideally *memetic* (evolutionary search + local-swap/insertion refinement) since the ordering literature shows local search materially improves permutation problems with sequence-dependent costs (relevant to integration/dwell pacing). For *scorer-weight tuning* (a handful of continuous knobs, each evaluation gated by scarce, noisy human feedback), use multi-objective Bayesian optimization with a noise-aware acquisition (qNEHVI), which reaches good solutions in tens of evaluations. Do not use GA to tune 3 weights (wastes feedback budget) and do not use GP-BO to search the combinatorial atom space (GP surrogates degrade in high-dimensional discrete spaces).

## Topic 4 — Closed-Loop Learning from Real-World Content Signal

EI is currently open-loop (EMA on sparse feedback). Closing the loop safely means: (a) an exploration-aware selection policy (bandits/Thompson sampling) rather than greedy exploitation, (b) off-policy/counterfactual evaluation so you can learn from logged engagement without naive bias, (c) online experiment designs (interleaving/A-B), and (d) explicit guards against feedback-loop degeneration and reward hacking.

- **Constitutional AI: Harmlessness from AI Feedback (RLAIF) — Bai et al. (Anthropic, arXiv) — 2022 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.08073** — Establishes RLAIF: replace scarce human labels with model-generated, principle-guided preference signals. For EI's *unattended Tier-2* pipelines, the pattern for generating cheap fidelity/efficacy preference data from a written rubric ("constitution" of doctrinal-fidelity principles) instead of waiting on sparse human review.
- **Estimation Considerations in Contextual Bandits — Dimakopoulou et al. (arXiv) — 2017 — https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.07077** — Contextual-bandit formulation (persona = context, atom/plan = action, engagement = reward) with the estimation/bias caveats that matter when the *same* policy both selects and is evaluated. `[verify]`
- **Learning Neural Ranking Models Online from Implicit User Feedback — Jia & Wang (WWW) — 2022 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.06658** — Learning-to-rank directly from on-the-fly implicit feedback while explicitly managing position/selection bias. Applicable to ranking candidate atoms/books from real engagement signal rather than offline scores. `[verify]`
- **Counterfactual Online Learning to Rank — Zhuang & Zuccon (ECIR) — 2020 — https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7148247/** — Combines counterfactual (off-policy) estimation with online ranking, so logged engagement can be reused to evaluate new rankers without re-deploying. The bias-correction layer EI needs before trusting sales/review logs as a reward. `[verify]`
- **Counterfactual Inference under Thompson Sampling — (RecSys) — 2025 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.08773** — Derives exact action propensities under Thompson-sampling policies, enabling unbiased importance-sampling off-policy evaluation when your *own* selection policy is randomized. `[verify]`
- **Degenerate Feedback Loops in Recommender Systems — Jiang, Chiappa, Lattimore, György & Kohli (AIES) — 2019 — https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10730** — Formalizes how a system that learns from feedback it caused drives interests to extremes. The safety warning for EI: a closed loop optimizing engagement will narrow the catalog and amplify whatever it already pushes unless explicitly counteracted.
- **How Algorithmic Confounding in Recommendation Systems Increases Homogeneity and Decreases Utility — Chaney, Stewart & Engelhardt (RecSys) — 2018 — https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.11214** — Shows training on self-influenced logs homogenizes recommendations and *reduces long-run utility*. Argues for keeping an exploration/randomization budget and exposure-aware modeling in EI's loop. `[verify]` (confirm authors/venue).

**Design takeaway.** Close the loop as a *contextual-bandit / off-policy-learning* system, not a greedy re-scorer. Make the selection policy explicitly exploratory (Thompson sampling over plans, with persona as context) so that (i) you balance exploiting known-good content against discovering new combinations, and (ii) the randomization gives you logged propensities for *unbiased* counterfactual evaluation of candidate scorers before deployment. Use interleaving or A-B for the high-stakes "ship this plan vs that plan" decisions. Hard-guard the two failure modes the literature flags: reward hacking (cap optimization pressure, monitor proxy-vs-gold gap per Gao) and degenerate feedback loops (reserve an exploration budget, add exposure-aware/diversity terms — Jiang and Chaney show an engagement-only loop predictably collapses catalog diversity and long-run utility). For unattended Tier-2 refresh cycles, generate fidelity-preference data via an RLAIF-style doctrinal rubric so the loop is not starved while waiting on human review.

## Topic 5 — Validating Persona / Affective Models Against Real Outcomes

EI's personas and somatic "felt-experience" heuristics are *latent constructs*. Before any optimizer is allowed to trust them as targets, they must clear construct-validity and predictive-validity bars — otherwise the optimizer faithfully optimizes a fiction.

- **Creating and validating predictive personas for target marketing — Salminen et al. (Int. J. Human-Computer Studies) — 2023 — https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1071581923001568** — Moves personas from descriptive artifacts to *predictive* models validated against real behavioral outcomes — the bar EI personas must meet before driving content selection. `[verify]`
- **On the Evaluation / Validation of Quantitative Personas — (persona-validation literature) — https://www.researchgate.net/publication/221054744** — Documents that quantitative personas often go *unvalidated*, with no shared credibility metric — the explicit danger of unvalidated segmentation that EI must avoid. `[verify]`
- **Knowing Your Annotator: Rapidly Testing the Reliability of Affect Annotation — Melhart et al. (arXiv / ACII) — 2023 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.16029** — Practical methods to test inter-annotator reliability for affect labels before trusting them — the affective-computing analogue of checking whether your "felt-experience" labels are even consistent. `[verify]`
- **Affective Computing and Emotional Data: Challenges, Implications, and Ethics in LLMs — (arXiv) — 2025 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.20153** — Surveys the validity, privacy, and over-claiming risks of emotion models — including that general-purpose emotion recognition lacks a good accuracy guarantee because emotion constructs are contested. `[verify]`
- **A dataset of continuous affect annotations and physiological signals for emotion analysis (CASE) — Sharma et al. (Scientific Data) — 2019 — https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-019-0209-0** — Exemplifies the rigor needed to ground affect labels in measured signals + continuous annotation. The methodological standard if EI ever validates "felt-experience" claims against an observable.
- **Reflections on Inductive Thematic Saturation as a metric for the validity of an inductive Thematic Analysis with LLMs — De Paoli (arXiv) — 2024 — https://arxiv.org/abs/2401.03239** — Proposes a *quantitative validity metric* (saturation) for LLM-derived themes — applicable to checking whether persona/segment definitions are saturated and stable rather than artifacts of a single LLM run.

**Design takeaway.** Gate the optimizer on validity: a persona or somatic heuristic earns the right to be an optimization target only after it (a) clears *construct validity* — it measures the intended latent thing, with reliable underlying annotations (test inter-annotator agreement on any felt-experience labels before modeling them), and (b) clears *predictive validity* — it demonstrably predicts a held-out real outcome (completion, re-engagement, self-reported benefit) on data the persona was not fit to. Adopt predictive personas (Salminen) over descriptive ones, and a stability/saturation metric (De Paoli) so segments aren't single-run LLM artifacts. Heed the affective-computing caution: emotion/somatic constructs are contested and have weak accuracy guarantees — so encode felt-experience effects as *low-confidence priors with explicit uncertainty*, not precise efficacy multipliers, and let the closed loop update them against real outcomes rather than freezing them as authored truth.

## Cross-Cutting Recommendations for EI v2

- **Separate the objectives; never pre-collapse them.** Keep therapeutic-efficacy, reader-engagement, and spiritual-root-fidelity as three independently scored channels (MORLAIF-style) and make fidelity a *floor/constraint*, not a summand. Linear weighted-sum is provably unable to reach non-convex trade-offs and invites objective collapse — replace it with Pareto selection (NSGA-II set) plus an optional continuous dial via weight interpolation (Rewarded Soups).
- **Match optimizer to sub-problem.** Evolutionary/memetic search for the *combinatorial* atom selection+sequencing; noise-aware multi-objective Bayesian optimization (qNEHVI) for the *handful of continuous scorer weights*. Do not swap these — it is the single highest-leverage architecture decision after de-scalarizing.
- **Close the loop as a contextual bandit with off-policy evaluation, not a greedy re-scorer.** Thompson-sample over plans (persona = context), log propensities, and use counterfactual estimators to validate new scorers on logged engagement *before* shipping. Use interleaving for high-stakes plan-vs-plan calls.
- **Instrument two failure modes from day one.** (1) *Reward overoptimization* (Gao): hold out a gold therapeutic-quality signal, monitor proxy-vs-gold divergence, cap optimization pressure. (2) *Degenerate feedback loops* (Jiang, Chaney): reserve an exploration budget and add exposure/diversity-aware terms.
- **Make the composite essence a validated structured artifact, and gate persona/somatic targets on validity.** Build the shared root via contrastive embeddings → BERTopic clustering → cross-document coreference merging → LLM naming with provenance and a saturation/stability check — so "fidelity" is measured against named nodes. A persona or felt-experience heuristic may drive the optimizer only after passing construct + predictive validity; encode affective effects as uncertainty-bearing priors the loop refines.
