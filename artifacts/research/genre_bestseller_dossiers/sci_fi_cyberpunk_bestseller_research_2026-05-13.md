## 3. Common arc structure

**Mystery Box + Power Escalation hybrid.** The protagonist is usually a low-status investigator (cop, mercenary, courier) who pulls at one thread of a conspiracy that reveals the actual nature of their world.

The signature pacing move is the **dump-of-lore-as-set-piece**: a 6-page sequence where the protagonist learns the system's architecture, often via a guide character (Pluto's robot scientists, GitS Section 9 briefings).

## 4. Visual register

- **Architectural splash panels**: the city / megastructure IS a character. Akira's Neo-Tokyo, Blame's city, GitS Newport.
- **Dense paneling**: 9–12 panels per page during interrogation/dialogue scenes; full-bleed splash for revelation moments.
- **Linework**: heavy, ink-saturated; tech detail is hand-drawn (not CG), unlike mecha which tilts CG-friendly.
- **Color tilt** (cover): neon (electric pink/cyan/yellow) over deep blacks; or monochrome (Blame's grayscale).

## 7. Therapeutic-embed natural landing moments

- **The chrome-arm fitting scene** — protagonist gets a new prosthetic; body-modification body-image processing.
- **The hacker's apartment / their server cluster** — solitude, hyper-focus as autistic-coded sanctuary.
- **The "I logged off for a week" plotbeat** — digital-detox, attention-restoration.
- **The android-as-mirror scene** — the AI says the protagonist's emotion back to them; reflective listening.
- **The corporate-burnout flashback** — the protagonist was a salaryman before; the cyberpunk world is the consequence of unprocessed late-capitalist exhaustion.

## 8. Operator-example: the sci-fi-cyberpunk version of the elf-grief pattern

> "A courier in a megacity, Kei, lost their childhood friend Tama three years ago to a corporate experimental implant. Kei has had panic attacks ever since. Kei buys an aftermarket emotion-dampener implant on the black market. It works — Kei stops panicking. Kei also stops sleeping. Kei stops eating. A street-doc named Yuda notices Kei thinning out, calls them in. Yuda says: 'Your dampener is suppressing the signal that you're alive. Pain is information. Take it out. I'll give you something else.' Yuda doesn't prescribe. Yuda teaches Kei breath-counting in the back of the clinic — 4 in, 7 hold, 8 out — while the city sirens scream outside. 'When the panic comes, count. The count is the only implant you need.' Kei takes the dampener out. The grief returns. Kei counts. The reader gets: medication-vs-coping skill, interoceptive recovery, somatic alternative to chemical suppression. No therapy word."

## 9. Phoenix series concepts

1. **The Aftermarket Implant** — exactly the above; cyberpunk + somatic-recovery.
2. **Logged Off** — corporate-burnout protagonist takes a digital-sabbatical; recovers attention.
