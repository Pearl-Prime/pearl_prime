## 3. Common arc structure

**Case-of-the-week (CoTW) is dominant** (`manga_bestseller_magical_serial_framework_2026-05-12.md` §2.5). Each chapter = one dish, one diner, one emotional arc. The long arc is either:
- a competition ladder (Food Wars), or
- a quest map (Toriko's "Gourmet World"), or
- a relationship spine (Sweetness & Lightning — father raising daughter).

The dish is always the resolution device. The character names a feeling; the dish answers it.

## 4. Visual register

- **Food panels are SACRED**: a 1-page or 2-page food reveal panel, hyper-detailed (steam lines, sheen, texture).
- **Ingredient cross-section diagrams**: educational panels showing what's inside.
- **Reaction shots**: the "first bite" reaction is genre-signature (Food Wars made it absurd, Oishinbo kept it solemn).
- **Color tilt** (cover): warm gold + brown + cream + one accent (red for chili, green for herb).

## 5. Reader demographic

- **Age:** 16–55 (one of the widest age bands)

- **Cultural anchor:** care, hospitality, knowledge-as-pleasure

## 7. Therapeutic-embed natural landing moments

- **The shopping at the market scene** — mindfulness of seasonal produce; somatic grounding via smell and color.
- **Prepping mise-en-place** — repetition as anxiety regulation; one task at a time.
- **The shared meal scene** — co-regulation, relational repair (the dish is the apology).
- **Eating alone at the counter** — solitude as nourishment, not isolation. (Wakakozake's whole register.)
- **The unmet expectation** (a dish doesn't go right) — perfectionism, letting-go, "the next service".

## 8. Operator-example: the food version of the elf-grief pattern

> "An old ramen master loses his apprentice (the apprentice moves to Tokyo to chase fame). The master keeps making the broth alone, exactly as before. Six months in, a regular customer notices the broth has changed — it's deeper, sadder. The master refuses to admit it. A traveler stops by, eats the bowl, and says quietly: 'this is grief broth.' The master cries into his apron. Next service, he calls the apprentice. They video-call while both prepping mise-en-place in their separate kitchens. No therapy word. The reader gets: complicated grief, the way taste carries emotion, parasocial repair through ritual."
