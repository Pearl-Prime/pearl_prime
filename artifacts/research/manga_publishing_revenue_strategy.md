therapeutic and spiritual content. The global manga market reached USD 15.4 billion

## 1. Publisher Submission Analysis

### 1.1 Viz Media

**Submission status:** Limited, portfolio-based
**AI policy:** Not accepting AI-generated manga (Shueisha, Viz's parent, explicitly
disqualified AI manga from Jump+ contests in February 2026)

Viz operates on a portfolio review basis and occasionally opens specific submission
windows. The company primarily licenses and distributes Japanese products rather
than publishing original works, though "VIZ Originals" is a dedicated line for
original graphic novels from manga-inspired creators.

Key facts:
- Writers without artists face significant barriers; Viz rarely pairs writers with
  artists
- Original content submissions are accepted through viz.com/submissions
- Shonen Jump app content comes directly from Japanese Weekly Shonen Jump; it does
  not accept external original submissions
- The new VIZ Manga app (launched May 2023) features Shogakukan and Shueisha
  licenses, not original content

**Phoenix Omega fit:** LOW. Viz's focus is licensing Japanese IP. Their Originals
line is small and highly selective. AI-generated content is a non-starter given
Shueisha's explicit disqualification of AI manga.

## 2. Self-Publishing Platforms

### 2.1 Amazon KDP (Kindle Direct Publishing)

**Status:** OPEN -- explicitly permits AI-generated content with disclosure
**Revenue potential:** HIGH

Amazon KDP is the most AI-friendly major platform for manga/graphic novel publishing.

**Requirements:**
- File formats: DOCX, EPUB, or PDF (Kindle Comic Creator tool available for
  formatting manga)
- Cover image required
- Book details: title, description, keywords, categories
- AI disclosure MANDATORY: must indicate AI-generated text, images, or translations

**Royalty Structure (updated June 2025):**
- eBooks: 35% or 70% royalty (70% requires price between $2.99-$9.99)
- Print books at $9.99+: 60% royalty
- Print books under $9.99: 50% royalty (reduced from 60% in June 2025)
- KDP Select: higher royalties plus access to Kindle Unlimited, Kindle Countdown
  Deals, and Free Book Promotions

**AI Policy (critical for Phoenix Omega):**
- AI-generated content (text, images, translations) is permitted
- MUST disclose during publishing process
- "AI-generated" = created by AI tool, even with substantial human edits afterward
- "AI-assisted" (e.g., grammar checking, brainstorming) does NOT require disclosure
- Failure to disclose risks account suspension or book takedowns

**Phoenix Omega fit:** HIGH. This is the primary channel for collected volumes.
The AI disclosure requirement is manageable. The 70% eBook royalty at $2.99-$9.99
pricing is competitive.

Sources: [KDP Comics Publishing](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/how-to-publish-comics-graphic-novels),

## 3. Therapeutic and Self-Help Manga Market

### 3.1 Market Context

The global manga market reached USD 15.4 billion in 2024 and is projected to
reach USD 54.3 billion by 2033 (CAGR 15%). Within this, therapeutic and mental
health manga represents a growing but under-served niche.

Key market indicators:
- Manga consumption is increasingly notable in educational and therapeutic
  settings
- Psychologists and therapists are leveraging manga's visual storytelling
  for mental health and emotional well-being
- The integration of manga into therapeutic and workplace settings is opening
  new growth avenues
- Academic research supports cognitive and emotional benefits of visual
  storytelling

Sources: [IMARC Manga Market Report](https://www.imarcgroup.com/manga-market),
[ScienceDirect Case Study on Manga as Self-Regulation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S3050713825000282)

### 3.2 The Iyashikei (Healing) Genre

Iyashikei -- from "iyashi" (healing/soothing/therapy) and "kei" (type) -- is
a sub-genre of slice-of-life manga portraying characters living peaceful lives
in calming environments. It is intended to have a therapeutic effect on the
audience.

**Origin:** Emerged as a distinct subgenre in 1995 in the wake of the Great
Hanshin earthquake and Tokyo subway sarin attack. The combination of traumatic
events and economic recession led to what scholar Paul Roquet calls the "iyashi
trend" or "healing boom."

**Current popularity:** The genre experienced a resurgence during the COVID-19
pandemic when audiences sought calming content. It continues to grow in both
Japanese and Western markets.

**Notable successful titles:**
- **Fruits Basket** (Natsuki Takaya): 30M+ copies sold globally, multiple
  anime adaptations
- **Yuru Camp / Laid-Back Camp**: Major anime franchise, tourism tie-ins
- **A Man and His Cat**: Bestselling manga, anime adaptation
- **Flying Witch**: Ongoing series, anime adaptation
- **Aria**: Cult classic iyashikei manga

**Phoenix Omega alignment:** The iyashikei genre is a strong precedent for
Phoenix Omega's therapeutic content. However, traditional iyashikei focuses on
ambient healing through atmosphere rather than explicit therapeutic techniques.
Phoenix Omega's content is more directly therapeutic (anxiety tools, grief
processing, etc.), which positions it as a **new sub-category** -- therapeutic
manga that teaches coping techniques through narrative.

Sources: [The Conversation on Iyashikei](https://theconversation.com/iyashikei-healing-manga-comforts-readers-with-attention-to-small-joys-237117),
[CBR on Iyashikei Popularity](https://www.cbr.com/iyashikei-cozy-fantasy-popularity/)

### 3.3 Mental Health Manga -- Existing Market

There is a growing body of manga and graphic novels addressing mental health:

**Autobiographical/memoir manga:**
- "My Lesbian Experience with Loneliness" (Nagata Kabi) -- depression, anxiety,
  eating disorders, ADHD; critically acclaimed, multiple sequels
- "Rock Steady: Brilliant Advice from My Bipolar Life" (Ellen Forney) --
  self-help format for bipolar disorder management

**Therapeutic manga/manhwa:**
- Multiple manhwa addressing PTSD, anxiety, and eating disorders
- "Drop-In to Manga" -- community initiative combining manga, mental health,
  and community engagement

**Market gap Phoenix Omega can fill:**
Most mental health manga are autobiographical memoirs or fiction that depicts
mental illness. Very few are **prescriptive therapeutic tools** that actively
teach coping techniques through visual storytelling. This is Phoenix Omega's
unique positioning: manga as a delivery vehicle for therapeutic interventions,
guided by character-authors who are spiritual/therapeutic teachers.

Sources: [Goodreads Mental Health Graphic Novels](https://www.goodreads.com/list/show/82993.Graphic_Novels_about_Mental_Illness),
[Bookstr Mental Health Manga](https://bookstr.com/article/terrific-manga-stories-about-mental-health-you-need-to-read/),
[Drop-In to Manga](https://www.dropintomanga.com/)

## 4. AI-Generated Manga: Legal and Market Status

### 4.1 United States Copyright Law

**Current status (as of March 2026):**

- **Purely AI-generated works cannot be copyrighted.** The U.S. Supreme Court
  declined (March 2, 2026) to hear Stephen Thaler's appeal, leaving intact
  lower court rulings that works without a human creator are ineligible for
  copyright protection.

- **Human-assisted AI works MAY qualify for copyright** depending on the
  nature and substantiality of human involvement. Creative human involvement
  must be "substantial, demonstrable, and independently copyrightable."

- **Copyright Office guidance (January 2025):** Published Part 2 of its
  Copyright and AI report, addressing copyrightability of AI-generated outputs.
  Part 3 released May 9, 2025.

**Implications for Phoenix Omega:**
The key is ensuring "substantial human creative input" in each work. This means:
- Human selection and arrangement of AI-generated panels
- Human-written dialogue and narrative
- Human creative direction for character design and scene composition
- Human editorial decisions about pacing, layout, and storytelling

If Phoenix Omega can demonstrate substantial human creative control over the
AI-generated visual elements, copyright protection is achievable for the
composite work.

Sources: [Congress.gov AI Copyright](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB10922),
[Copyright Office AI Page](https://www.copyright.gov/ai/),
[NeoLemon AI Copyright Characters](https://www.neolemon.com/blog/can-you-copyright-ai-generated-characters/)

### 4.2 Japan Copyright and AI Law

**Current status:**

Japan has one of the world's most liberal AI copyright regimes:
- Copyright Act Article 30-4 permits use of copyrighted works for AI training
  as long as it is not for the "purpose of enjoying the work"
- The AI Promotion Act (passed May 28, 2025; effective September 1, 2025) aims
  to make Japan the most "AI-friendly country"
- Infringement only occurs if AI output has "similarity" AND "dependence" on
  an existing copyrighted work

**However:**
- Japan's government has warned OpenAI about violating anime and manga
  copyrights (October 2025), specifically regarding Sora 2 video generation
  of anime-style content featuring existing characters
- The protection is for training data usage, not for copying specific
  characters or styles

**Implications for Phoenix Omega:**
Japan's legal environment is favorable for AI-generated original content
(not copying existing characters). Phoenix Omega's original character-authors
and original therapeutic content would likely be protected under Japanese law.

Sources: [Japan AI Copyright Overview](https://www.bunka.go.jp/english/policy/copyright/pdf/94055801_01.pdf),
[Japan AI Promotion Act](https://www.ibanet.org/japan-emerging-framework-ai-legislation-guidelines),
[Japan Warns OpenAI](https://www.medianama.com/2025/10/223-japan-openai-copyright-anime-manga/)

### 4.3 South Korea AI Regulation

**Current status:**

- **AI Basic Act** effective January 22, 2026 -- first comprehensive AI
  regulation in Asia
- Mandatory disclosure of AI-generated or AI-assisted content
- Applies to all platforms serving Korean market (WEBTOON, Tapas, Tappytoon)
- One-year grace period focused on guidance rather than punishment
- Requires visible logo, watermark, or metadata disclosure before content
  reaches readers

**Implications for Phoenix Omega:**
All content published on platforms with Korean presence must include AI
disclosure. This is manageable but affects Webtoon Canvas distribution.

Sources: [Korea AI Basic Act](https://thenewpublishingstandard.com/2026/01/28/korea-ai-act-webtoon-creators/),
[Korea Times on AI Webtoons](https://www.koreatimes.co.kr/lifestyle/trends/20251106/webtoon-industry-seeks-ai-edge-amid-legal-ethical-challenges)

### 4.5 Industry AI Tools (Not Content)

Notably, major publishers are developing AI **tools** for creators, not AI
**content**:
- Shonen Jump+'s "Comic-Copilot" (Comicopa): AI tool for refining dialogue
  and creating character names -- for human creators to use
- WEBTOON's AI-powered translation program (Spring 2026): translates creator
  content into 7 languages
- Authors Guild "Human Authored Certification" program (expanded 2026): allows
  authors to certify content as human-written

**Key insight:** The industry is embracing AI as a tool for human creators
while rejecting AI as a replacement for human creators. Phoenix Omega should
position its pipeline as "AI-assisted human creativity" rather than
"AI-generated content."

Sources: [Shonen Jump AI Tool](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/interest/2023-05-17/shonen-jump-editors-co-develop-ai-tool-for-manga-writing/.198079),

### 4.6 Successful AI Manga Publications

As of April 2026, there are **no known successful AI-generated manga
publications** through traditional publishers or major manga platforms.

The closest examples:
- AI-generated manga "NTR Kaeshi" ranked #1 in Shueisha's Jump Rookie contest
  (February 2026) before being disqualified
- Amazon KDP has numerous AI-assisted manga/comic publications, though
  sales data is not publicly reported
- Several AI webtoon generators exist (Jenova.ai, etc.) but successful
  commercial publications from these tools are not well-documented

**This represents both a risk and an opportunity for Phoenix Omega.** Being
among the first to establish a successful AI-assisted manga brand could
create first-mover advantage. However, the lack of precedent means there is
no proven playbook.

## 7. Risk Assessment

### 7.1 Critical Risks

| Risk                                | Severity | Probability | Mitigation                          |
|------------------------------------|----------|-------------|-------------------------------------|
| Platform bans AI content            | HIGH     | MEDIUM      | Diversify across platforms; lead with KDP |
| Copyright challenges to AI works    | HIGH     | LOW-MEDIUM  | Ensure substantial human creative input |
| Audience rejection of AI art        | MEDIUM   | MEDIUM      | Transparent disclosure; emphasize human direction |
| Platform policy changes             | MEDIUM   | HIGH        | Never rely on single platform       |
| Low initial readership              | MEDIUM   | HIGH        | Long-term content strategy; cross-promotion |
| Competitor enters therapeutic manga | LOW      | LOW         | First-mover advantage; deep content library |

### 7.2 AI-Specific Risks

1. **Webtoon Canvas could ban AI content at any time.** Tapas did so in
   January 2023. WEBTOON has not, but community pressure is strong. Mitigation:
   always maintain KDP as primary revenue channel.

2. **US Copyright Office could issue stricter guidance.** Part 3 of their AI
   report was released May 2025 and Congress continues to examine the issue.
   Mitigation: document all human creative decisions thoroughly.

3. **RunComfy or image generation quality could degrade.** AI art tools are
   dependent on third-party services. Mitigation: maintain relationships with
   multiple AI art providers; consider fine-tuning own models.

4. **Hachette precedent:** In early 2026, Hachette Book Group canceled a novel
   over suspected AI use. Traditional publishers are actively hostile. Mitigation:
   avoid traditional publisher submissions until market sentiment shifts.

### 7.3 Market Risks

1. **Therapeutic content may not "feel like manga" to manga readers.** The
   audience expects entertainment, not therapy. Mitigation: lead with engaging
   stories that incorporate therapeutic elements organically (like iyashikei),
   not overtly clinical content.

2. **Manga purists may reject non-Japanese-origin manga.** "Global manga" or
   "OEL manga" (Original English Language) has faced this stigma historically.
   Mitigation: position as "webtoon" rather than "manga" on Western platforms;
   use "manga" branding for Japanese-market releases.

3. **Mental health content requires sensitivity.** Irresponsible depiction
   of mental health conditions can cause harm. Mitigation: content review by
   licensed mental health professionals before publication.
