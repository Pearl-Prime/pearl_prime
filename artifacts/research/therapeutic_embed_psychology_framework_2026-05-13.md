## Author's principle (operator's example, verbatim)

> "the little elves are fighting against the trolls, but then one of the elves gets killed, and then the other elf is dealing with grief. And then there's the teachings from the teachers that help the elf. But it still has to be an engaging story, so it has to be bestseller."

That's the model: **genre engine drives the story → death/loss/event creates emotional ground → mentor or co-regulator delivers teaching at the right moment → genre engine resumes**. The teaching is *diegetic* (inside the world, inside the character's mouth, inside the somatic body) — never authorial-voice instruction.

## The five universal pitfalls (avoid in every genre)

1. **Preachy monologue.** Character stops the plot to deliver a TED talk. NEVER do this.
2. **Therapy-jargon.** Using "boundaries", "self-care", "trauma response" inside the dialogue. The reader will close the book.
3. **Therapist-mouthpiece.** The mentor becomes the author's surrogate; the character loses interiority.
4. **Fast-recovery arc.** The protagonist learns the lesson once and is healed. Real recovery is recursive, non-linear, partial.
5. **Self-help horror mislabel.** Marketing the book as horror/fantasy when it's actually a therapy manual. The genre tags must match the story, not the teaching.

## The seven natural landing moments (universal vocabulary)

These are the diegetic positions where teaching lands without breaking story. Every genre uses some subset.

1. **Post-defeat / aftermath scene** — protagonist just lost / failed; their guard is down.
2. **3am alone scene** — somatic body is restless; truth surfaces in solitude.
3. **Mentor-doing-task-while-talking scene** — the teaching arrives while a hand is occupied (sweeping, polishing, cooking, prepping). Indirection lowers defense.
4. **Co-regulator-in-silence scene** — two characters sit together without speaking; the teaching is the *not-speaking*.
5. **Witness-not-fixer scene** — another character sees the protagonist's pain but does not try to fix it. The witnessing IS the teaching.
6. **Body-first scene** — a somatic intervention (breathing, walking, eating, touch) precedes any cognitive teaching.
7. **Refusal-to-resolve scene** — the arc closes without the teaching landing perfectly; the protagonist is mid-recovery. Realism IS the teaching.

These are the seven moves that future Pearl_Writer subagents should rotate through when authoring V2 themes.

## Per-genre therapeutic-embed table

For each of the 21 manga-applicable genres, this table gives: (a) 3–5 natural landing moments, (b) 3 pitfalls particularly easy to fall into here, (c) 1–3 self-help topics that map naturally, (d) one bestseller-pattern reference.

### 1. supernatural_everyday (Mushishi, Natsume, Flying Witch)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the yokai-return-naming scene; (2) the encounter-with-the-mushi scene at dusk; (3) the village-elder-doing-task-while-talking scene; (4) the tea-on-the-veranda witness scene.
- **Pitfalls:** treating yokai as literal metaphor labels ("this is the depression-yokai"); over-narrating the lesson; resolving the mushi too cleanly.
- **Self-help topics:** named emotions (yokai = externalized emotion), loneliness, found-family, contemplative awareness.
- **Reference:** Mushishi's case-of-the-week loop, where Ginko encounters one mushi per chapter and names its nature without judgment.

### 2. romance (Kimi ni Todoke, Horimiya, My Happy Marriage)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the misread-text-message anxiety spiral scene; (2) the co-regulator-friend-eats-with-them scene; (3) the "I noticed you've been avoiding me" witness scene; (4) the body-first scene (holding hands, the first hug, the panic that precedes them).
- **Pitfalls:** the love interest as therapist (anti-pattern — love is not therapy); jealousy resolved by reassurance-only; consent treated as plot obstacle rather than ongoing practice.
- **Self-help topics:** attachment styles (avoidant, anxious, secure), shame-around-needs, communication scripts, post-rejection self-soothing.
- **Reference:** My Happy Marriage — Miyo's gift surfaces ONLY when she's safe; magic = "I feel safe enough to be visible". This is somatic-safety theory rendered as fantasy mechanic.

### 3. mystery_cozy (Apothecary Diaries, Detective Conan, Death Note)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the case-debrief over tea scene; (2) the "I missed this clue because I assumed" reflection scene; (3) the witness-interview where the protagonist *doesn't* push the witness; (4) the case-solved-but-grief-remains scene.
- **Pitfalls:** the detective as omniscient (anti-pattern — let them be wrong); resolution-via-confession-monologue; trauma-as-clue without aftercare for the witness.
- **Self-help topics:** cognitive-distortions (jumping to conclusions, mind-reading), curiosity-over-judgment, the limits of analysis (some grief is not a puzzle).
- **Reference:** Apothecary Diaries — Maomao solves the poisoning, but the *woman who was poisoned* is what stays in the chapter's last panel.

### 4. slice_of_life (Yotsuba!, Aria, Yokohama Kaidashi)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the small-moment-noticing scene (a leaf falls, the protagonist sees it); (2) the routine scene (tea, walk, market); (3) the unchanging-friend scene (the neighbor who's always there); (4) the season-passes scene.
- **Pitfalls:** treating slowness as boredom (anti-pattern); over-explaining why the small moment matters; nostalgia-as-substitute-for-presence.
- **Self-help topics:** mindfulness, depression-recovery via behavioral activation, the "just one small thing" technique, savoring.
- **Reference:** Aria — Akari's "miracles" are the smallest moments named as miracles. Permission to notice = the entire therapeutic content.

### 5. healing (iyashikei) (Frieren, Apothecary, A Man and His Cat)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the long-walk scene; (2) the meal-cooked-for-someone scene; (3) the silence-between-companions scene; (4) the visited-the-grave scene.
- **Pitfalls:** "healing" used as marketing tag for what's actually just slow pacing; the protagonist as fully-recovered mentor (anti-pattern — they're still recovering); explicit grief-stages enumeration.
- **Self-help topics:** complicated grief, late-life-meaning, found-family, end-of-life dignity.
- **Reference:** Frieren — Himmel's flashbacks aren't "grief processing", they're Himmel being alive in Frieren's memory. The grief is the structure, not a topic.

### 6. comedy (Spy×Family, Saiki K, Gintama, Dandadan)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the joke-that-lands-then-the-silent-beat-after scene; (2) the absurd-situation-genuine-feeling scene; (3) the running-gag-that-deepens scene (the gag becomes affection); (4) the laugh-cry transition scene.
- **Pitfalls:** therapy as the butt of the joke (anti-pattern — once is okay, twice ruins it); a "serious episode" disconnected from the comedy register; sentimentality.
- **Self-help topics:** masking (especially neurodivergent/queer), humor as defense + as connection, social anxiety, the relief of being known.
- **Reference:** Spy×Family — Yor's "I'm a normal office worker" mask IS the comedy AND the trauma; the gag and the grief are the same beat.

### 7. family (Yotsuba!, Spy×Family, Sweetness & Lightning)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the meal-at-the-table scene; (2) the bedtime-routine scene; (3) the "I overheard my parent worry about me" scene; (4) the multi-generational-photo scene.
- **Pitfalls:** the perfect-parent fantasy (anti-pattern); resolving family-of-origin trauma in one volume; over-romanticizing "found family" without naming the bio-family pain.
- **Self-help topics:** parenting under stress, intergenerational trauma, attachment-repair, the "good-enough parent" frame.
- **Reference:** Sweetness & Lightning — single dad Inuzuka cooks with daughter Tsumugi; competence-as-care, the lunchbox as love-language.

### 8. school (My Hero Academia, Kimi ni Todoke, Honey Lemon Soda)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the after-school walking-home scene; (2) the empty-classroom solo scene; (3) the teacher-pulls-them-aside scene; (4) the cafeteria-eating-alone scene.
- **Pitfalls:** the inspirational teacher as savior (anti-pattern — they can be present without being the answer); bullying resolved in one arc; academic pressure as backdrop without weight.
- **Self-help topics:** social anxiety, performance perfectionism, identity-formation, peer-loneliness, queer-adolescence.
- **Reference:** Honey Lemon Soda — Uka changes her hair, NOT her personality; identity work as small visible moves, not transformations.

### 9. battle_internal (Vagabond, Berserk, Aku no Hana)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the post-fight injury-tending scene; (2) the "what was I really fighting" reflection scene; (3) the silence after the kill scene; (4) the body-pain-as-emotion-pain scene.
- **Pitfalls:** romanticizing rage; the "I had to do it" justification monologue; recovery via revenge.
- **Self-help topics:** moral injury, rage as grief-displacement, masculinity-without-violence, somatic discharge of anger.
- **Reference:** Vagabond — Musashi's deepening understanding that the strongest is not the most-killing.

### 10. historical (Vinland Saga, Kingdom, Cesare)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the campfire-mentor scene; (2) the field-labor scene (Vinland Saga gold standard); (3) the funeral-and-the-river scene; (4) the letter-home scene.
- **Pitfalls:** anachronistic-therapy-language (a Viking does not say "I have trauma"); over-mythologizing the historical figure; forgetting that historical war was real war.
- **Self-help topics:** moral injury, complicated grief, the recovery-of-capacity-to-feel-after-dissociation, generational succession.
- **Reference:** Vinland Saga farm arc — Thorfinn recovers via labor; behavioral activation rendered as sagas-of-the-fields. (Detailed in `historical_bestseller_research_2026-05-13.md`.)

### 11. cultivation (Solo Leveling, Tales of Demons and Gods)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the post-breakthrough meditation scene; (2) the qi-deviation crisis (burnout) scene; (3) the master-sweeps-courtyard scene; (4) the lower-cultivator-encounter scene.
- **Pitfalls:** treating the level-up as the goal (anti-pattern — the level is the symptom); revenge-cultivation; the mentor's wisdom as fortune-cookie.
- **Self-help topics:** achievement-anxiety, burnout, comparison-trap, beginner's-mind, equanimity.
- **Reference:** Detailed in `cultivation_bestseller_research_2026-05-13.md` (Yan's courtyard-sweeping example).

### 12. social_issue (A Silent Voice, Goodnight Punpun, Welcome to the NHK)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the "you don't have to be okay" scene; (2) the "I noticed" small-specific scene; (3) the failed-therapy scene (validates real readers); (4) the unchanged-world re-entry scene.
- **Pitfalls:** the fast-recovery arc (genre-killing); the therapist-saves-the-day plot; resolving the social issue rather than the *character's* relationship to it.
- **Self-help topics:** depression, hikikomori, suicidality, abuse-survival, the legitimacy of slow recovery, "anti-self-help self-help".
- **Reference:** Detailed in `social_issue_bestseller_research_2026-05-13.md` (Forty-Three Nights example).

### 13. fantasy_adventure (One Piece, Frieren, Witch Hat Atelier, Solo Leveling)
- **Landing moments:** (1) the camp-after-the-quest-leg scene; (2) the companion-roster-rotation scene; (3) the village-the-party-saves scene where one villager is *not* okay; (4) the magic-system-as-emotion scene.
- **Pitfalls:** the magic system as power-fantasy without cost; the quest as escape-from-life metaphor without resolution; party-as-family-substitute that displaces real attachment work.

### 14. dark_fantasy (Berserk, Chainsaw Man, Attack on Titan)

## Cross-genre patterns: the four most-leverageable embedding moves

After auditing all 21 genres, four patterns recur across the most successful embeddings:

### Move 1: The mentor-doing-task-while-talking move
The teaching arrives while the mentor is occupied with hands. Examples:
- Vinland Saga: Sverkel hoeing alongside Thorfinn
- Mecha: Hina polishing the mecha gauntlet
- Cultivation: Bai Yi sweeping the courtyard
- Workplace: Takeda fixing his Excel macros
- Food: ramen master prepping broth
- Horror: Baba polishing tea cups

**Why it works:** indirection lowers the listener's defense. The hands-occupied mentor doesn't pin the listener with eye contact. The teaching arrives sideways.

### Move 2: The "I noticed" small-specific witness move
The witness names something *specific*: "you stopped going to the convenience store"; "you held your breath when you came in"; "the broth is sadder this week".

**Why it works:** specificity = the witness is actually paying attention. Generic care reads as performance.

### Move 3: The body-first move
A somatic intervention (breathing, walking, eating, touch, repetitive task) precedes any cognitive teaching.

**Why it works:** matches actual recovery neuroscience. The body must regulate before the mind can integrate. (Polyvagal Theory, Levine's Somatic Experiencing.)

### Move 4: The refusal-to-resolve move
The arc closes without the teaching landing perfectly. The protagonist is mid-recovery when the volume ends.

**Why it works:** real recovery is partial. Resolving it cleanly is the genre's most-common cause of being read as preachy.

## How Pearl_Writer subagents should use this framework

When authoring a V2 theme for any of the 21 genres:

1. **Pick the genre dossier** in `artifacts/research/genre_bestseller_dossiers/` (or the magical_serial_framework for COVERED genres).
2. **Choose 2 of the 4 universal embedding moves** for this series.
3. **Place at least 1 of the genre's 3–5 natural landing moments per volume.**
4. **Confirm none of the 5 universal pitfalls + 3 genre-specific pitfalls are violated.**
5. **Map the series's self-help anchor to the 1–3 topics that map naturally to the genre.** Do NOT force a topic that doesn't map (e.g. don't put eating-disorder-recovery in a sports series unless the sport is one where the body is the topic — gymnastics, dance, wrestling).
6. **Write the operator-style example paragraph** for the series, BEFORE writing the synopsis. If the example doesn't read as compelling story, the synopsis won't either.

## Closing principle

Story is the vehicle. Teaching is a passenger. If the passenger drives, the bus crashes. If the passenger is silent, the trip is meaningless. The passenger speaks **sometimes**, in **specific moments**, **in their own voice** — not the author's.

That is the entire framework.
