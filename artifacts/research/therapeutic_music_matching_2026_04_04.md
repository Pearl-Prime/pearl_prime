## Part 1: Research Foundation

### 1.1 Music Therapy Frequency Ranges (Evidence Summary)

| Frequency Band | Hz Range | Therapeutic Association | Evidence Level |
|---------------|----------|----------------------|----------------|
| Delta | 0.5-4 Hz | Deep sleep, unconscious healing | Moderate (EEG studies) |
| Theta | 4-8 Hz | Meditation, emotional processing, grief work | Strong (multiple RCTs) |
| Alpha | 8-13 Hz | Relaxation, stress reduction, calm focus | Strong (HRV + EEG) |
| Beta | 13-30 Hz | Active focus, alertness, concentration | Moderate |
| Gamma | 30-100 Hz | Cognitive enhancement, working memory | Emerging |

**Key Finding:** 528 Hz music reduced cortisol and Chromogranin A while increasing oxytocin in a 2018 Japanese study. Alpha-range (10 Hz) acoustic neurostimulation decreased symptoms of stress, depression, anxiety, and improved sleep quality.

**Evidence caveat:** Solfeggio frequencies (174, 285, 396, 417, 432, 528, 639, 741, 852, 963 Hz) have plausible physiological mechanisms but lack conclusive RCT evidence. We use them as texture anchors, not clinical claims.

### 1.2 The ISO Principle (Mood-Matching-Then-Shifting)

The ISO principle is the core therapeutic music mechanism:
1. **Match** the listener's current emotional state with congruent music parameters
2. **Hold** that match long enough for the listener to feel understood (solace)
3. **Gradually shift** parameters toward a desired state

For our 30-second loops, we apply ISO at the **temperature level**: cold/crisis music matches the listener's distress, while warm/settling music represents the target state. The listener's journey across multiple loops implements the shift.

### 1.3 Autonomic Nervous System Response

Music affects the ANS through:
- **Tempo entrainment:** Heart rate tends to synchronize with musical pulse (60 BPM -> resting HR)
- **Vagal tone modulation:** Slow, predictable music increases parasympathetic (vagal) activity and HRV
- **Cortisol suppression:** Ambient textures below 70 BPM reduce cortisol after ~5 minutes
- **Sympathetic withdrawal:** Excitatory music with negative valence withdraws vagal activity

### 1.4 BPM Ranges for Therapeutic States

| Therapeutic Goal | BPM Range | Rationale |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| Deep relaxation / sleep | 40-55 BPM | Below resting HR; induces delta/theta |
| Calm processing | 55-65 BPM | Matches resting HR; alpha entrainment |
| Neutral grounding | 60-72 BPM | Slightly above resting; steady presence |
| Warm settling | 65-80 BPM | Gentle forward motion; resolution feeling |
| Activated engagement | 75-95 BPM | Above resting HR; beta-range alertness |
| Pure ambient (no pulse) | N/A | Drones and textures; no rhythmic entrainment |

## Part 4: MusicGen Prompt Engineering Guidelines

### Prompt Template Structure

All prompts follow this pattern:
```
[texture type], [instrument list], [tempo/rhythm], [key/harmonic], [emotional quality], [constraints]
```

### Mandatory Constraints for All 75 Prompts

Every prompt must include:
- "continuous ambient texture" or "seamless loop drone"
- "no drums" (unless pulse is specifically needed, then "very subtle kick at [BPM]")
- "no vocals"
- "no melody resolution" (prevents verse-chorus structure)
- "continuous" (prevents start/end framing)
- "30 seconds" (generation length)

### Temperature-Specific Prompt Patterns

**Cold (crisis):** "sparse", "fragmented", "breathing", "dissonant undertone", "no rhythm"
**Cool (processing):** "slow undulating", "gentle movement", "spacious reverb", "minor key"
**Neutral (grounding):** "steady", "grounded", "earth tone", "modal", "predictable"
**Warm (settling):** "warm", "glowing", "gentle pulse", "major inflections", "soft shimmer"
**Hot (activated):** "bright", "confident", "forward motion", "clear harmonics", "subtle rhythm"
