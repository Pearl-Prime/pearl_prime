## 3. Platform Revenue Share Details (All Markets)

### Audible / ACX (Global, including JP/KR)
- **New 2025 model (rolling out):**
  - Exclusive distribution: **50% royalty** (up from 40%)
  - Non-exclusive distribution: **30% royalty** (up from 25%)
- Early adopters seeing average **45% increase in earnings** vs previous model
- New model: member's plan value divided among titles listened to, multiplied by royalty rate
- **Note:** New model currently by invitation only; standard ACX rates remain for most creators
- **Traditional publishing:** Publishers typically pay authors 25% of what they receive from Audible

### Kobo (including Taiwan)
- **Audiobook royalty:** 55–68% of list price (varies by pricing and promotional participation)
- **Kobo Plus (subscription):** 60% royalty based on listening time share
  - Formula: 60% × (Monthly Revenue / Minutes Read) × Minutes of Your Book
- **Ebook comparison:** 70% for books above price threshold, 45% below

### Spotify (Global)
- **A la carte sales:** 50% of list price to rights holder
  - Indie authors net ~45–50% after distribution fees
- **Spotify Premium (subscription pool):** Pro-rata engagement model
  - Authors paid based on listening time relative to all content consumed
  - 15 audiobook hours included with Premium subscription
- **Traditional publishers:** Negotiate per-book deals (prices and triggers vary)
  - Authors receive ~25% of publisher's earnings (standard audiobook licensing share)
- **NOT a fixed per-stream rate** — different from music model

### Ximalaya (China)
- **Specific revenue share percentages not publicly disclosed**
- Partnership model: cooperates with 220+ leading publishers (CITIC Press, etc.)
- 150+ online literature platform partnerships (Yuewen Group, etc.)
- 5.2 million audiobooks in catalog (2023)
- Revenue model: subscription + pay-per-chapter + ad-supported freemium
- **Subscription revenue share:** 45.1% of total revenue (decreasing)
- **Ad revenue share:** 43.4% of total revenue (growing)
- Creators likely receive negotiated deals based on content type and exclusivity

### Naver Audiobooks (Korea)
- **Specific revenue share not publicly available**
- Operates subscription model
- Integration with Naver ecosystem provides discovery benefits

### Kakao Page (Korea)
- **Specific revenue share not publicly available**
- Wait-and-pay model for serialized content
- Acquired Welaaa for audio content capabilities

### Readmoo (Taiwan)
- **Specific revenue share not publicly available**
- Individual purchase model (no subscription)
- Largest Traditional Chinese ebook platform (130,000+ volumes)

## 5. Platform Upload Requirements

### Audible / ACX (Global Standard — applies to JP/KR)
- **Audio format:** MP3
- **Bitrate:** 192 kbps or higher, constant bit rate (CBR)
- **Sample rate:** 44.1 kHz
- **RMS level:** between -23 dB and -18 dB
- **Peak amplitude:** under -3 dB
- **Noise floor:** no higher than -60 dB RMS
- **Channels:** Mono required (mixing mono and stereo = rejection)
- **Chapter structure:** One file per chapter/section
- **Max file length:** 120 minutes (split if longer)
- **Chapter markers:** Each chapter begins with title announcement + 1 second silence, ends with 1-2 seconds silence
- **Credits required:**
  - Opening: audiobook name, author name, narrator name
  - Closing: must state "the end"
- **Retail sample:** 1–5 minutes, starts with narration (not credits/music)
- **ISBN:** Required for distribution
- **Content review:** ACX reviews all submissions; timeline typically 1-4 weeks

### Kobo Writing Life (including Taiwan)
- Accepts audiobook uploads via Kobo Writing Life platform
- Audio format and quality requirements similar to ACX standards
- ISBN recommended for wider distribution
- Metadata: title, author, narrator, description, genre categories, language
- Cover art required (specific dimensions)

### Spotify
- Distribution through aggregators (FindawayVoices, Author's Republic, PublishDrive, etc.)
- Audio quality requirements set by aggregator
- ISBN required
- Metadata through aggregator platform
- Content available in markets where Spotify operates

### Ximalaya (China)
- **Domestic publishing partner required** for foreign content
- **CSBN (China Standard Book Number)** required — international ISBN not valid
- Content review mandatory (government compliance)
- Simplified Chinese audio only
- Audio format: platform-specific requirements (contact directly)
- Partnership agreements typically handled through Chinese publisher intermediary

### Naver / Kakao (Korea)
- Platform-specific requirements not publicly documented in English
- Typically require Korean publisher or distributor partnership
- Korean-language content
- ISBN (Korean system) required

### Readmoo (Taiwan)
- Traditional Chinese content
- Contact directly for upload specifications
- ISBN (Taiwan system) accepted
