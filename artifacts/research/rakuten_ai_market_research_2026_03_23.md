## Risks and Challenges

### Cultural and Localization Risks
- Mental health stigma still exists in all three markets — content must be positioned sensitively
- "Polyvagal" as a term may not attract laypeople in Korea/Taiwan if not explained simply
- Consider user-friendly subtitles/renaming (e.g. "Nervous System Reset" rather than "Polyvagal Theory")
- Must balance theory and practice — too academic early content risks dropout
- Respectful integration with local wellness traditions (TCM, Zen, 한의학) essential

### Market Education Challenge
- Audiobooks themselves are still relatively new format in Taiwan especially
- Must sell format AND content simultaneously
- Risk: consumers may think "I can just read a book faster than listening"
- Mitigation: emphasize unique audio value (guided exercises, eyes-closed practice)

### Competition and Discovery
- Platform algorithms: if initial uptake is slow, titles may not be surfaced to new users
- External marketing and early reviews critical to break through
- Free podcast/YouTube content competing for same wellness audience
- Must differentiate as premium, structured, comprehensive program vs. entertainment

### Pricing and Piracy
- Piracy is significant restraint across region
- Include value-adds (PDF supplement, Q&A access) that pirates wouldn't get
- Price sensitivity varies: Taiwan most price-sensitive, Japan willing to pay premium
- Consider regional pricing strategy

### Attention Span
- Audiobooks require longer attention than articles/videos
- Risk of users starting but not finishing
- Mitigation: modular design (15-minute sessions), storytelling, real-life examples
- Clear session delineation so listeners feel accomplishment

### Long-Term Trajectory
- Must be prepared for slower build — may not take off immediately
- Early listeners dictate long-term trajectory through reviews and word-of-mouth
- Remain culturally empathetic and responsive to feedback
- Adaptive pricing and promotion strategies based on initial response
