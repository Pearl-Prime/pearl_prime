# RCG-008 — Average debut self-pub earns < $500 lifetime

**Source claim (Batch 2 prompt):** “Average debut self-pub earns < $500 lifetime” (MEDIUM).

## Claim

Typical first-time self-published titles generate very low lifetime author earnings (order of hundreds of dollars).

## Sources

1. **Industry blog synthesis (SudoWrite, 2025 data article):** states many books sell **<500 copies lifetime** (proxy for revenue, not identical to “<$500”). https://sudowrite.com/blog/how-much-do-authors-really-make-2025-data-on-royalties-advances-and-income/  
2. **Zenodo — “Indie Authors' Earnings 2023” (Alli-related survey materials):** reports **typical indie author income ~$12,755 in 2022** for respondents (selection bias: active authors), which **contradicts** a universal “<$500 lifetime for debut” generalization. https://zenodo.org/records/8043463  
3. **Practitioner summaries (e.g. Unanswered / Neucite):** often claim **majority earn under $1,000/year** — directional support for **long tail** but not a rigorous “debut lifetime” median. https://unanswered.io/guide/self-published-authors-earnings-amazon  

## Verdict

**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED** — **Long-tail low earnings** are well supported directionally [1][3], but **“average debut < $500 lifetime”** is **not** pinned to a single peer-reviewed median in this pass; survey-based “typical indie income” can be **higher** for engaged cohorts [2]. Label as **heuristic / long-tail warning**, not a hard statistic.
