# RCG-008 — Healthcare RN workforce size (MEDIUM)

**Audit ref:** `RESEARCH_AUDIT_2026_03_30.md` section A item 8
**Source file:** `unified_personas.md` (L66)
**Claim:** "Healthcare RNs — Niche ($3.4M workforce)"
**Research query:** U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics registered nurses employment 2024 2025

---

## Claim analysis

The persona table labels "Healthcare RNs" as a niche segment with a $3.4M (3.4 million) workforce figure. This presumably refers to the total number of employed registered nurses in the United States.

## Research findings

1. **U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS), Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics (OEWS), May 2023.** SOC code 29-1141 (Registered Nurses): **3,175,390** employed nationally. This is the most recent complete annual OEWS release as of early 2026.
   - URL: https://www.bls.gov/oes/current/oes291141.htm

2. **BLS Occupational Outlook Handbook (2024-2025 edition).** Reports approximately **3.1 million** registered nurses employed in 2022, with projected growth of 6% from 2022 to 2032 (about 177,400 new jobs).
   - URL: https://www.bls.gov/ooh/healthcare/registered-nurses.htm

3. **National Council of State Boards of Nursing (NCSBN) & National Forum of State Nursing Workforce Centers (2023).** "2022 National Nursing Workforce Survey." Reports approximately **4.7 million** RNs with active licenses in the U.S., though not all are employed in nursing roles. The "employed in nursing" figure from this survey is approximately **3.4 million**.

## Verdict

**CONFIRMED** — The 3.4M figure aligns closely with available workforce data. BLS OEWS reports ~3.18M employed RNs (May 2023), and the NCSBN workforce survey reports ~3.4M RNs employed in nursing roles. The figure is in the correct range.

## Recommendation

Add an inline citation: "3.4M workforce per BLS OEWS May 2023 (~3.18M) and NCSBN 2022 National Nursing Workforce Survey (~3.4M employed in nursing)." The figure should specify the source year.
