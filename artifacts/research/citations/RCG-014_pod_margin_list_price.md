# RCG-014 — Print-on-demand margin 40–60% of list price

**Source claim (Batch 2 prompt):** “Print-on-demand margin is 40–60% of list price” (MEDIUM).

## Claim

Author/publisher **net** on KDP print (POD) is **40–60%** of customer list price.

## Sources

1. **Vappingo — KDP print royalty explainer (2025–2026):** **royalty = list × rate − printing cost**; rates **50–60%** depending on price thresholds; **net share of list** varies strongly with **page count, trim, color**. https://www.vappingo.com/word-blog/kdp-print-royalties/  
2. **HMD Publishing — royalty calculator guide (2026):** worked examples showing **author take** often **~30–45% of list** for typical paperbacks after print cost — **can overlap** the band but **not guaranteed**. https://hmdpublishing.com/blog/amazon-kdp-royalty-calculator-2026-guide  
3. **Book Upload Pro — margin explainer:** emphasizes **printing cost dominance**; warns **thin margins** on low list prices. https://blog.bookuploadpro.com/amazon-kdp-profit-margins/  

## Verdict

**PARTIALLY CONFIRMED** — **Gross royalty rates** of **50–60%** exist in Amazon rules [1], but **net % of list** after print is often **lower** and **highly parameter-sensitive** [2][3]. Avoid fixed **40–60%** in specs; use **formula + example SKUs**.
