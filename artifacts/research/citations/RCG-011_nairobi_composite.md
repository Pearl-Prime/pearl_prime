## Claim analysis

The claim names a specific individual ("Amara Odhiambo"), age (19), location (Nairobi), and role (climate organizer) in what reads as a profile lede. The audit question is whether this is a real person (requiring source/consent documentation) or a composite/fictional character (requiring explicit labeling).

## Research findings

### Person search

1. **No public record of "Amara Odhiambo" as a Nairobi-based climate organizer** was found in news databases, social media, or climate organization directories. The name does not appear in reporting from major outlets covering East African climate activism.

2. **"Odhiambo" is a common Luo surname** in Kenya, and "Amara" is a name used across multiple African cultures. The combination is plausible as a real name but no specific person matching this description was identified.

3. **Notable young Kenyan climate activists** who are publicly documented include Elizabeth Wathuti, Eric Njuguna, and others — none matching the "Amara Odhiambo" description.

### Composite character best practices in journalism

4. **Society of Professional Journalists (SPJ) Code of Ethics.** Advises against use of composite characters without explicit disclosure, as they can mislead readers about the factual basis of reporting.

5. **AP Stylebook guidance.** Composite characters should be avoided in news reporting; when used in feature/narrative contexts, they must be clearly labeled.

6. **In specification documents and style guides**, composite examples are standard practice — but best practice is to label them as such to prevent downstream confusion when the spec is used as a template for actual reporting.

## Verdict

**UNCONFIRMED** — "Amara Odhiambo" does not appear to be a real, publicly documented person. The character is almost certainly a **composite or fictional example** created for the news writing spec. No consent or sourcing documentation exists.

## Recommendation

1. Add explicit composite label: "*(Composite character — not a real individual. Created as an illustrative example for the news writing spec.)*"
2. Alternatively, rename to a clearly fictional placeholder: "In Nairobi, a 19-year-old climate organizer [composite] schedules her most important calls for 5 a.m."
3. If the character was based on a real person, document the source and obtain/note consent status.
