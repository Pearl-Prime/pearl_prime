# RCG-010 — Dhaka floods / 40,000 students lede (MEDIUM)

**Audit ref:** `RESEARCH_AUDIT_2026_03_30.md` section A item 10
**Source file:** `docs/PEARL_NEWS_WRITER_SPEC.md` (L117)
**Claim:** "Three weeks after the floods receded from Dhaka's outer districts, 40,000 students had not returned to school."
**Research query:** Bangladesh Dhaka floods school attendance year news source OR confirm composite

---

## Claim analysis

The claim appears in a lede example within the Pearl News Writer Spec. It reads as factual journalism — a specific city (Dhaka), specific number (40,000 students), specific timeframe (three weeks after floods receded). The audit question is whether this describes a real event or is a hypothetical/composite example.

## Research findings

### Bangladesh flooding — general context

1. **Bangladesh experiences severe annual flooding.** Major flood events in recent years include 2017, 2019, 2020, 2022, and 2024. Dhaka and its outer districts are frequently affected.

2. **UNICEF Bangladesh (2022).** Reported that the August 2022 floods in Bangladesh affected approximately 7.2 million people, with significant impact on schools — over 4,500 schools damaged and hundreds of thousands of children's education disrupted. However, the specific figure of "40,000 students not returned to school three weeks after floods receded from Dhaka's outer districts" was not found in any UNICEF report.

3. **UNICEF Bangladesh (2024).** The August 2024 monsoon floods displaced millions. Reports referenced school closures and education disruption but the specific 40,000 figure for Dhaka's outer districts was not located in public reporting.

4. **No news article or official report matching this exact claim** was found — i.e., "40,000 students not returned to school three weeks after floods receded from Dhaka's outer districts" does not appear to be a direct quote from any identifiable news source or government report.

### Assessment

The lede appears to be a **composite example** — it uses real-world context (Dhaka flooding is real and recurring, school disruption is a documented consequence) but the specific figure and timeframe do not trace to a named source. This is consistent with it being a **constructed illustrative example** for the news writing spec.

## Verdict

**UNCONFIRMED** — The specific claim (40,000 students, Dhaka outer districts, three weeks after floods) cannot be traced to any identified news report or official source. Bangladesh floods and resulting school disruption are well-documented realities, but this particular formulation appears to be a composite/illustrative example rather than a reported fact.

## Recommendation

Label explicitly as a composite example: "*(Composite example — not sourced from a specific news report. Dhaka flooding and school disruption are well-documented realities; see UNICEF Bangladesh flood reports.)*" This follows journalistic best practice for spec documents that use illustrative ledes.
