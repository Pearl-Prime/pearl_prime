## Part 1: Manga on Every Platform (Per Market)

### 1.1 United States (en_US)

#### Amazon KDP (Kindle Direct Publishing)

**Can you sell manga on Amazon KDP?** Yes. KDP is fully open to manga, comics, and graphic novels through the Comics & Graphic Novels category system.

**Format Requirements:**
- Standard page dimensions: 1600x2560 pixels (portrait, 5:8 aspect ratio)
- Alternative accepted: 1536x2048 (3:4) or 1200x1920 (5:8)
- Minimum resolution: 300 DPI at final page dimensions
- Black-and-white manga: 300-400 DPI recommended
- Cover image: minimum 2560px on longest side, ideal 2560x4096, RGB, JPEG at 90-95% quality
- File upload: Use Kindle Comic Creator tool, supports PDF/JPG/TIFF/PNG import
- Output: KPF (Kindle Package Format) with Guided View data
- Fixed-layout EPUB supported with image pop-ups and virtual panels
- Reading direction: supports both LTR and RTL (for traditional manga)
- Facing pages enabled for landscape viewing on tablets

**Categories:**
- Amazon limits each book to 3 categories per format (ebook, paperback, hardcover)
- Key categories: Comics & Graphic Novels > Manga (various sub-categories)
- WARNING: ~27% of KDP category options are "ghost categories" with no real browsing page
- WARNING: ~54% of KDP category paths are duplicates leading to same page
- Each format has independent category slots (ebook + paperback = 6 total placements)

**Revenue:** 70% royalty option available (requires KDP Select enrollment for some markets). 35% royalty option available without restrictions.

**Source:** [Anifusion KDP Manga Formatting Guide](https://anifusion.ai/articles/amazon-kdp-manga-formatting/), [KDP Categories](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G200652170), [KDP Comics Publishing](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/how-to-publish-comics-graphic-novels)

#### Google Play Books

**Can you sell manga?** Yes. Google Play Books has a dedicated manga section under Comics & Graphic Novels.

**Requirements:**
- File format: PDF or EPUB (fixed-layout for manga)
- Cover: high-quality JPEG or PNG
- No cost to sell books on Google Play
- Sign up via Google Play Books Partner Center
- BISAC category: Comics & Graphic Novels > General (or specific manga subcategories)
- Manga genre categories available for Japan and South Korea specifically
- Content policy: no nudity without educational/artistic value

**Revenue:** Publisher sets the price; Google takes approximately 48% commission (publisher keeps ~52%).

**Source:** [Google Play Books Partner Center](https://play.google.com/books/publish/), [Manga Genres](https://support.google.com/books/partner/answer/6358351)

#### WEBTOON Canvas (US)

**How it works:** Free-to-publish platform for any creator. Part of WEBTOON Entertainment's ecosystem with ~170M monthly active users globally.

**2026 Major Overhaul:**
- Unified global CANVAS platform launching spring 2026 across 7 languages (English, Spanish, French, Indonesian, Thai, Traditional Chinese, German)
- AI-powered Translation Program for cross-language distribution
- Lowered minimum payout threshold from $100 to $25
- Expanded Reward Ads access for CANVAS creators
- New Creator Residency Program (twice annually, 2-week immersive at LA HQ, up to 9 creators)
- VIP Recognition Program with trips to Korea
- Digital Open Houses for new creators

**Revenue Model:**
- Super Like Program (reader tips)
- Ad Revenue Sharing Program
- Patreon integration
- Reward Ads (expanded access in 2026)
- Minimum payout: $25 (reduced from $100)

**Upload specs:** 800px wide, 1280px per strip segment, JPG or PNG, under 2MB per image, 20MB per episode

**Creator payout track record:** Naver WEBTOON reported US$2.7 billion in creator payouts over 5 years (as of March 2026).

**Source:** [WEBTOON 2026 Creator Programs](https://www.webtoons.com/en/notice/detail?noticeNo=3553), [AIPT Coverage](https://aiptcomics.com/2025/12/11/webtoon-creators-2026/), [Morningstar Unified CANVAS](https://www.morningstar.com/news/business-wire/20260326392552/webtoon-entertainment-announces-unified-international-canvas-platform-helping-creators-grow-their-audience-and-earnings)

#### Tapas

**Still viable?** Yes. Tapas remains an active platform with strong indie creator community, owned by Kakao Entertainment.

**Revenue Model:**
- Ad Revenue: ~70% goes to creators
- Tipping: readers contribute directly
- Premium Program: paid episodes unlocked with coins
- Minimum payout: $25 via PayPal

**AI Policy:** As of January 2023, AI-generated content is NOT allowed on Tapas. This includes comic episodes, novel episodes, covers, and banners. Tapas follows copyright law interpretation requiring human-created artwork.

**CRITICAL FOR PHOENIX OMEGA:** If our manga is AI-generated or AI-assisted, Tapas is likely OFF-LIMITS unless the policy changes. Need to monitor their stance on AI-assisted (vs. fully AI-generated) art.

**Upload specs:** 940px wide, no height limit (1000px max for GIFs), PNG/JPG/GIF, under 20MB per episode, under 2MB per individual file. Episode thumbnail: 300x300px.

**Source:** [Tapas Creator Portal](https://www.creators.tapas.io/), [Tapas AI Policy](https://tapas.io/newsfeed/224), [Tapas File Size Guide](https://www.creators.tapas.io/file-size-guide)

**Key facts:**

**Source:** [GlobalComix](https://mwm.ai/apps/globalcomix-the-comics-app/6444957733), [Seven Seas Partnership](https://kcomicsbeat.com/2026/02/18/globalcomix-partners-with-seven-seas-to-offer-manga-manhwa-manhua/)

### 1.2 Japan (ja_JP)

#### LINE Manga

**Market position:** #1 manga app in Japan by revenue, topping app revenue rankings in 1H 2025, overtaking Piccoma.

**Submission process:** LINE Manga operates primarily through publisher partnerships, not open self-publishing. Foreign publishers typically need:
- Agent-mediated submissions
- Localization to Japanese
- Editorial relationship with LINE Digital Frontier (Naver WEBTOON affiliate)

**Revenue model:** Revenue sharing with publishers; specific splits not publicly disclosed but industry standard is 50-60% to publisher.

**Format preferences:** Both vertical scroll (webtoon style) and traditional page manga are supported. Vertical scroll growing rapidly.

**Verdict:** HIGH-VALUE but HIGH-BARRIER. Requires Japanese localization and likely a distribution partner like MediaDo.

**Source:** [ANN: LINE Manga Revenue](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2025-07-11/line-manga-tops-japan-app-revenue-rankings-in-1st-half-of-2025/.226535)

#### Piccoma

**Market position:** #2 in Japan (was #1 until 2025), top consumer-spending app in Japan for 2024 (US$418M+ revenue). Operated by Kakao's Japanese subsidiary.

**Submission process:** Requires localization and editorial relationships. Not fully open for self-publishing. Foreign publishers need to work through established channels.

**Business model:** "Wait or pay" freemium model -- readers wait for free episodes or pay coins to read immediately.

**Format:** Supports both vertical scroll webtoon and traditional manga pages. Vertical scroll dominates new content.

**Verdict:** Similar to LINE Manga -- requires distribution partner. Very high revenue potential.

**Source:** [ANN: Piccoma Top App](https://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2025-01-17/webtoon-platform-piccoma-named-japan-top-consumer-spending-app-for-2024/.220135)

#### Amazon KDP Japan (kdp.amazon.co.jp)

**Can manga sell on Kindle JP?** Yes. Amazon.co.jp has robust manga categories.

**Key details:**
- 70% royalty available (requires KDP Select enrollment for Japan)
- Series Collections supported on co.jp
- Same format specs as US KDP (1600x2560, 300 DPI, Kindle Comic Creator)
- Contact Amazon to create series in Japanese marketplace
- Pricing set in JPY

**Verdict:** ACCESSIBLE and does not require a distribution partner. Brand admins can self-publish directly. Good supplementary channel.

**Source:** [KDP Japan Pricing](https://kdp.amazon.com/en_US/help/topic/G201849770), [KDP Japan](https://kdp.amazon.co.jp/ja_JP/)

#### Rakuten Kobo Japan

**Manga section:** Yes. Kobo has extensive manga from major publishers (Kadokawa, Kodansha, Shogakukan, etc.). Kobo Plus subscription offers 2M+ ebooks and 300K audiobooks.

**Foreign publisher access:** Kobo Writing Life is the self-publishing portal. Manga can be uploaded as fixed-layout EPUB. Not specifically optimized for manga self-publishing but technically possible.

**Verdict:** Secondary channel. Major publishers dominate. Self-published manga possible but not a primary target.

**Source:** [Kobo Manga](https://www.kobo.com/us/en/ebooks/manga)

#### BookLive, Renta!, Comic Cmoa

**BookLive:** Accessible to international users (no Japanese VPN or address required). Affiliated with Tsutaya chain. Large catalog.

**Renta!:** Accepts PayPal payment internationally. Known for exclusive content and early releases. Rental-based model.

**Comic Cmoa (NTT Solmare):** 40M+ monthly users, 1.43M+ titles, 812 billion yen revenue (FY2024). International purchases possible via credit card/PayPal. Their overseas platform **MangaPlaza** grew 300%+ YoY.

**Foreign publisher access:** These platforms primarily work through established Japanese publishing channels. Direct self-publishing is not standard.

**Verdict:** NOT directly accessible for brand admin uploads. Would require MediaDo or similar distributor.

**Source:** [Blackbox JP: Digital Shift](https://www.blackboxjp.com/stories/japans-manga-industry-the-digital-shift-and-rise-of-ai-powered-localization)

#### Google Play Books Japan

**Manga section:** Yes. Active with dedicated manga genre categories for Japan.

**Access:** Same as global Google Play Books Partner Center. Self-publishing possible.

**Verdict:** ACCESSIBLE. Same process as US. Good supplementary channel for JP market.

#### Japan Format Summary

| Format | Trend | Platform Support |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Vertical scroll webtoon | Growing rapidly, dominant for new content | LINE Manga, Piccoma, WEBTOON JP |
| Traditional page manga | Still strong for established IP | All platforms |
| Both supported | Yes | Most major platforms |

**Key insight:** Japan is shifting toward vertical scroll for new series, but page-format manga remains viable. Dual-format production is ideal.

## Part 2: What Do Brand Admins Actually Upload?

### 2.1 Platform-by-Platform Upload Specifications

#### Amazon KDP (All Markets)

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **File format** | KPF (via Kindle Comic Creator) or fixed-layout EPUB |
| **Source images** | JPG, PNG, TIFF, PDF accepted by Kindle Comic Creator |
| **Page dimensions** | 1600x2560 px (5:8 portrait) recommended |
| **Resolution** | 300 DPI minimum |
| **Cover** | 2560x4096 px ideal, JPEG, RGB, 90-95% quality, 2-5MB |
| **Reading direction** | LTR or RTL configurable |
| **Guided View** | Auto-generated virtual panels or creator-defined |
| **Metadata** | Title, subtitle, author, description, 3 categories, 7 keywords, series info, age rating |
| **Max file size** | 650MB for ebooks |
| **Scheduling** | Set release date up to 90 days in advance |
| **Bulk upload** | No native bulk upload. One book at a time through dashboard. API exists for large publishers. |
| **Revenue** | 35% or 70% royalty (70% requires KDP Select for some markets) |
| **Payout** | Monthly, 60-day delay, minimum varies by method ($10 direct deposit) |

#### WEBTOON Canvas

| Spec | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| **File format** | JPG or PNG images |
| **Episode dimensions** | 800px wide x 1280px per segment (auto-sliced if larger) |
| **Working resolution** | Create at higher res, export at 800px wide |

### 5.3 Simultaneous vs. Staggered Release

**Recommendation: STAGGERED by platform type, SIMULTANEOUS within type.**

**Rationale:** This maximizes cross-platform discovery while allowing different consumption patterns. Weekly readers get episodes; volume buyers get complete collections.

### 6.2 Should Manga Titles Appear on Amazon KDP Too?

4. **Volume compilation model:** Release weekly on WEBTOON, then compile episodes into volumes for KDP every 4-8 weeks.

**Recommended approach:**
```
Week 1-4: Release episodes 1-4 on WEBTOON/Tapas (free, ad-supported)
Week 4: Compile episodes 1-4 into Volume 1 EPUB, publish on KDP/Google Play/Apple Books
Week 5-8: Release episodes 5-8 on WEBTOON/Tapas
Week 8: Compile into Volume 2
...repeat...
```

## Appendix D: Format Production Pipeline

To serve all platforms from a single production pipeline:

```
MASTER ASSET: High-resolution panels (2400px wide minimum, 300+ DPI)
  |
  |-- Export: WEBTOON format (resize to 800px wide, slice to 1280px segments)
  |-- Export: Tapas format (resize to 940px wide)
  |-- Export: KDP format (resize to 1600x2560 per page)
  |-- Export: EPUB fixed-layout (for Google Play, Apple Books, Kobo)
  |-- Export: Marketing crops (1080x1350 Instagram, 1200x675 Twitter)
  |-- Export: Video assets (9:16 panel animations for TikTok/Shorts)
```

**Tool chain recommendation:**
1. **Creation:** Clip Studio Paint (manga creation with webtoon export)
2. **Batch processing:** ImageMagick or Photoshop actions for multi-format export
3. **EPUB creation:** Sigil or Calibre for fixed-layout EPUB
4. **KPF creation:** Kindle Comic Creator (Amazon's free tool)
5. **Video:** After Effects, CapCut, or LlamaGen.ai for panel-to-video
6. **Distribution:** PublishDrive for ebook store aggregation
