## Overview

Unlike the US or UK, there is no single, unified, public-facing bestseller list that spans all Asian markets. Each region has distinct platforms, language nuances, and consumer behavior. This plan is structured in three phases: Preparation, Data Collection (Market-by-Market), and Synthesis.

## Phase 2: Market-by-Market Data Collection

### 1. Japan (ja_JP)
**Market structure:** Dominated by domestic conglomerates. Audible Japan has distinct catalog and rankings from US.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Audible Japan (amazon.co.jp/audible)**
  - Navigate to "Audibleオリジナル" (Originals) and "ベストセラー" (Bestsellers)
  - Filter by: "自己啓発 / 生き方" (Self-development) and "健康 / 美容" (Health/Beauty)
  - Rankings split between "総合" (Overall) and "会員限定" (Members Only) — capture both
  - Note if title is "Audible限定" (Audible Exclusive) — these dominate top spots
- **Rakuten Kobo Japan**
  - Search for オーディオブック, filter by ビジネス・自己啓発 (Business/Self-help)
- **audiobook.jp** — check for rankings

### 2. South Korea (ko_KR)
**Market structure:** Highest audiobook penetration in Asia. Split between three major domestic players.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Naver Series (series.naver.com)**
  - Select "오디오북" → Click "인기순" (Popularity) or "베스트" (Best)
  - Filter: "자기계발" (Self-development) and "건강" (Health)
- **Millie's Library (밀리의 서재)**
  - Subscription service — may need free trial
  - Search "웰니스" or browse "에세이" (Essays)
  - Look for "많이 듣는 오디오북" (Most Listened Audiobooks)
- **Ridi (리디)**
  - Sort by "인기순" (Popularity)
  - Filter by "자기계발" and "인문" (Humanities) — philosophical wellness falls under Humanities in Korea

**Key insight:** Korean charts are usually real-time popularity (based on consumption), not just sales — better indicator of engagement.

### 3. Taiwan (zh_TW)
**Market structure:** Traditional Chinese. Mix of local players and Kobo.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Kobo Taiwan (kobo.com/tw/zh/audiobooks)**
  - Filter by "暢銷排行榜" (Bestseller List)
  - Drill down: "非文學" (Non-fiction) > "自我成長" (Self-growth) or "健康生活" (Healthy Living)
- **Readmoo (readmoo.com)**
  - Search "有聲書" (Audiobook), sort by "暢銷度" (Bestseller)
- **PressPlay Academy** — curated audio content

**Critical keyword:** "身心靈" (Body, Mind, Spirit) — massive genre keyword in Taiwan that reveals bestsellers not categorized under standard "Wellness"

**YouTube:** Many Taiwanese wellness authors release audio chapters on YouTube. Check trending under Education.

### 4. China (zh_CN)
**Market structure:** Most complex. Standard Audible does not operate here. Dominated by super-apps.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Ximalaya FM (喜马拉雅)**
  - ESSENTIAL platform. Download app or use website
  - Navigate to "排行榜" (Ranking) → "畅销榜" (Bestseller Chart)
  - Filter: "个人成长" (Personal Growth), "健康养生" (Health & Wellness), "心理" (Psychology)
  - Data presented as "播放量" (Plays) — 10M+ plays = functionally a bestseller
- **Dedao (得到)**
  - Unique "knowledge payment" app
  - Search for "健康" and "自我管理"
  - Bestseller lists highly indicative of affluent urban consumer preferences
- **Lizhi FM (荔枝)** and **Qingting FM (蜻蜓FM)** — secondary platforms

### 5. Hong Kong (zh_HK)
**Market structure:** Bilingual (Cantonese/Mandarin). Often grouped with Taiwan by distributors but consumer preferences differ.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Google Play Books**
  - Set region to Hong Kong, filter by "有聲書" (Audiobook)
  - Sort by "暢銷商品" (Top Selling)
- **Kobo Hong Kong**
  - Switch region to HK, filter by 中文有聲書
  - Look for "非文學" and "自我成長"
- **HyRead** — used by public libraries

**Critical:** Run TWO searches — one Traditional Chinese, one English. Cross-reference lists.

### 6. Singapore (zh_SG)
**Market structure:** Trilingual (English, Mandarin, Malay). Smaller Chinese market but strategic as regional hub.

**Primary Platforms:**
- **Audible Singapore (audible.com)**
  - Set location to Singapore, filter by "Chinese" language
  - Sort by "Best Sellers"
- **Kobo Singapore (kobo.com/sg)**
  - Navigate to Audiobooks, filter by "Chinese" language, check "Bestsellers"
- **National Library Board (NLB) via Libby**
  - Check "Popular" section — longest waitlists = highest demand indicator

**Key insight:** Singapore's Chinese market often imports Taiwanese (zh_TW) editions. Note publishing origin — affects distribution rights.

## Phase 3: Synthesis & Verification

### Cross-Reference Titles
1. **Local Originals vs. Translated:** Separate local authors from translated Western titles (e.g., Atomic Habits). Local authors often dominate top spots, but Western self-help staples usually appear in top 5 consistently.
2. **Universal Hits:** Titles appearing across multiple platforms within the same market = true bestsellers.

### Analyze Metadata
For each title:
- **Narrator:** Celebrity narrators or author-narrated = massive sales driver in Asia
- **Format:** Full audiobook vs. "summary" vs. "lecture series" — in China and Korea, short-form knowledge content often outsells full-length audiobooks in wellness
- **Price vs. Subscription:** In subscription-dominant markets (Korea, China), "most listened" charts more valuable than "top sold" charts

### Limitations & Workarounds
- **App-Only Data:** Ximalaya (China) and Millie's (Korea) hide analytics behind mobile apps
- **Dynamic Charts:** Rankings change daily. Scrape at same time of day over 3 consecutive weeks for stable bestseller identification
- **Monday 10:00 AM local time** recommended for consistent data capture
