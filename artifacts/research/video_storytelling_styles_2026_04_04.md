## 1. Therapeutic Short-Form (15-60s)

**Platforms:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 15-60 seconds |
| Word count | 40-160 words |
| WPM | ~160 WPM (fast, punchy delivery) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 70% visual / 30% narration (silent-first design) |

### Hook Structure (First 3 Seconds)

The first 3 seconds determine whether viewers stay or scroll. Over 70% of viral videos include a strong hook in this window. Target: 65%+ retention at the 3-second mark.

**Three-layer hook model (verbal + visual + text simultaneously):**

1. **Pattern interrupt** — A visual or statement that breaks the scroll rhythm.
   - Example: Dark screen snap-cut to bold text "YOUR THERAPIST WON'T TELL YOU THIS"
   - Example: Close-up of hands doing a grounding exercise, no face
2. **Curiosity gap** — Open a loop the viewer needs closed.
   - "The one thing anxious people do every morning that makes it worse..."
   - "If you were raised by a narcissist, you probably do this..."
3. **Social proof / credential flash** — Establish authority in under 2 seconds.
   - Text overlay: "Licensed therapist | 12 years"
   - Statistic that signals expertise: "I've worked with 3,000+ clients..."

### Key Writing Patterns

**The @theholisticpsychologist model (1.5M TikTok, 46.8M likes):**
- Skit/role-play format to dramatize behaviors
- Attachment theory, narcissism, generational trauma as recurring pillars
- Direct-to-camera "If you were raised by..." hooks

**The @lisaoliveratherapy model:**
- Green-screen over relatable scenarios
- "Let me translate what your body is telling you" framing

**The @mindbodygreen model:**
- Polished visual branding, text-heavy overlays
- Trend-jacking wellness vocabulary (cozymaxxing, friction-maxxing, 75 Smart)

### Caption Writing for Silent Autoplay

85% of short-form social videos are watched muted. Design for silence first.

- **Text overlays:** 3-8 words per frame, high contrast, within safe zones
- **Performance impact:** Text overlays boost engagement 25%, retention 28%, saves 40%, shares 2x
- Build the story visually first, then layer audio as enhancement
- Key stat or CTA in the first 3 seconds as text
- Use kinetic typography (animated text reveals) for emphasis beats

### Platform-Specific Rules

**TikTok:**
- Vertical 9:16 only. 1080x1920 minimum.
- Trending audio boosts discovery but wellness content often performs better with original audio + text
- Hashtag strategy: mix broad (#mentalhealth) with niche (#generationaltrauma #nervoussystem)
- Post 1-3x/day during growth phase

**Instagram Reels:**
- Reels with captions perform significantly better in the algorithm (higher watch time signal)
- Cross-post from TikTok but remove TikTok watermark (penalized)
- Carousel + Reel combo strategy: educational carousel → emotional Reel

**YouTube Shorts:**
- 60-second maximum. Vertical 9:16.
- Shorts can drive subscribers to long-form (funnel strategy)
- Less trend-dependent than TikTok; evergreen wellness content works

### Music / Audio Approach

- Ambient pads or soft piano under narration (royalty-free)
- Sound effects for emphasis (whoosh on text reveal, soft chime on key point)
- Voice: warm, steady, confident — not clinical. Conversational authority.
- Trending sounds can help discoverability but should not clash with therapeutic tone

### Pipeline Requirements

- Text overlay renderer with safe-zone awareness (9:16 format)
- Hook-first shot planner (maps to existing `arc_section_pacing.HOOK` config)
- Silent-mode preview pass (verify content works without audio)
- Caption burn-in system (not relying on platform auto-captions)
- Aspect ratio: 1080x1920 (9:16)

## 2. Therapeutic Mid-Form (2-8 min)

**Platforms:** YouTube (primary), Facebook, website embeds

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 2-8 minutes (sweet spot: 5 min) |
| Word count | 300-1,200 words (750 for 5 min) |
| WPM | 130-150 WPM (measured, warm delivery) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 50% illustrated overlay / 30% B-roll / 20% talking head equivalent |

### Episode Structure (5-Minute Model)

Channels like Therapy in a Nutshell (Emma McAdam, LMFT) and Kati Morton (1.4M subs, 130M views) follow a consistent pattern:

**0:00-0:30 — Hook + Promise**
- State the problem the viewer is feeling
- Preview what they will learn (creates commitment)
- "If you struggle with [X], this video will give you [Y]"

**0:30-1:30 — Context / Why This Matters**
- Normalize the experience ("You're not alone in feeling...")
- Brief psychoeducation (name the mechanism)
- One relatable example or scenario

**1:30-3:30 — Core Teaching (2-3 concrete tools)**
- Numbered list or framework (viewers love structure)
- Each tool: name it, explain it, give a micro-example
- Visual metaphors for abstract concepts (illustrated overlays)

**3:30-4:30 — Application / "Try This"**
- Guided micro-exercise or journaling prompt
- Specific scenario: "Next time you notice [trigger], try [technique]"

**4:30-5:00 — Close + Community Hook**
- Recap the 2-3 takeaways
- CTA: "Comment which one you'll try" or "Share with someone who needs this"
- End screen / subscribe prompt

### Key Writing Patterns

**Two-column script format:**
Left column = spoken narration. Right column = visual notes (B-roll cues, overlay text, animation triggers).

**"Write for the edit" principle:**
Script visual pattern interrupts every 15-20 seconds to prevent visual monotony:
- Cut to illustrated concept diagram
- Text overlay with key phrase
- B-roll of nature/hands/journaling
- Animated metaphor (e.g., "nervous system as thermostat")

**HealthyGamerGG (Dr. K) variation:**
- Longer episodes (10-30 min) but uses conversational, unscripted style
- Heavy use of whiteboard/drawing while explaining
- Combines gaming culture references with clinical concepts
- Audience Q&A format drives engagement and watch time

### Platform-Specific Rules (YouTube)

- 2025-2026 algorithm shift: satisfaction-weighted discovery (not just watch time)
- YouTube now surveys viewers: "Was this video worth your time?"
- A 5-min video watched to 100% with a "like" outperforms a 20-min video at 40% retention
- Thumbnails: face + emotion + 3-5 word text. High contrast.
- Chapters in description improve retention and SEO
- 3 uploads/week = 8x faster view growth vs <1/month

### Music / Audio Approach

- Soft ambient bed throughout (volume at ~15% of voice)
- Music swell during emotional peaks, pull back during teaching sections
- Sound design: gentle transitions between sections (soft whoosh or chime)
- Voice: professional but warm. Not podcast-casual, not lecture-stiff.

### Pipeline Requirements

- Two-column script template (narration + visual cues)
- Shot planner mapping to HOOK/BUILD/PEAK/RELEASE/RESOLVE arcs
- B-roll library integration (nature, hands, journaling, abstract textures)
- Illustrated overlay renderer (concept diagrams, metaphor animations)
- Chapter marker generator for YouTube descriptions
- Thumbnail generator (face + emotion + text overlay)
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 (16:9)

## 3. Cinematic Long-Form (15-30 min)

**Platforms:** YouTube (primary)

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 15-30 minutes |
| Word count | 2,000-4,500 words |
| WPM | 130-140 WPM (deliberate, contemplative pacing) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 80% illustrated/cinematic visuals / 20% silence or ambient |

### Reference Channels and Their Approaches

**School of Life (Alain de Botton):**
- Animated essay format with custom illustrations
- Combines philosophical concepts with everyday emotional experiences
- Warm, didactic narration over playful-yet-elegant animation
- Topics: relationships, work, self-knowledge, culture

**Einzelganger (~300K subs, 20M+ views):**
- Anonymous creator (no face shown, values privacy)
- Applies Stoicism, existentialism, Buddhism, Taoism to personal growth
- Essay-style narration over curated stock footage and art
- Contemplative tone — less about debate, more about introspection
- Videos feel like reading a philosophical essay aloud

**Pursuit of Wonder:**
- Philosophy, science, literature, emotional well-being
- Mix of video essays, short stories, and guided experiences
- Mission: foster curiosity, reflection, and appreciation

### Script Structure (20-Minute Model)

**0:00-2:00 — Philosophical Hook**
- Open with a universal human experience or paradox
- "We all know the feeling of [X], but rarely do we ask why..."
- Often starts with a story, quote, or historical anecdote

**2:00-6:00 — The Problem Defined**
- Name the psychological or existential tension
- Draw from philosophy, psychology, or cultural observation
- Multiple examples that the viewer recognizes in their own life

**6:00-14:00 — Exploration / The Journey**
- This is the documentary heart — sustained intellectual exploration
- Weave between philosophical traditions, research, and narrative
- Use visual metaphors extensively (the animation carries the abstraction)
- Pace: longer shots (8-15 seconds), slower cuts, room to breathe

**14:00-18:00 — Synthesis / The Turn**
- Bring threads together into a coherent insight
- The "aha moment" — reframe the original problem
- Often delivered as a quiet, powerful statement

**18:00-20:00 — Contemplative Close**
- Return to the opening image or metaphor (bookend structure)
- Leave the viewer with a question, not an answer
- No aggressive CTA — the tone itself is the invitation to subscribe

### Key Writing Patterns

**Philosophical narration technique:**
- First person plural ("We tend to...") creates shared experience
- Avoid jargon — translate concepts into felt experience
- Use concrete sensory language for abstract ideas
- Sentence rhythm: alternate between long flowing thoughts and short punchy lines

**Pacing for illustrated content:**
- Cut interval: 8-15 seconds per shot (vs. 2-4s for short-form)
- Allow visual breathing room — not every second needs new information
- Music drives emotional arc more than narration density
- Silence is a tool: 2-3 second pauses after key statements

### Platform-Specific Rules (YouTube)

- 30+ minute content sees 35-45% better algorithmic promotion (if retention holds)
- But: quality > length. A well-paced 18-min video beats a padded 30-min one.
- Satisfaction signals matter more than raw watch time in 2026
- Session continuation (viewer watches another video after yours) is a strong ranking signal
- Premiere feature works well for this format (builds anticipation)

### Music / Audio Approach

- Orchestral or ambient soundtrack that follows the emotional arc
- Music is a co-narrator in this format (not background)
- Swells at emotional peaks, minimal during contemplative passages
- Consider original score or licensed cinematic music
- Voice: measured, thoughtful, slightly intimate — like a wise friend, not a professor

### Pipeline Requirements

- Long-form script template with act structure (5-act or 4-part)
- Illustration pipeline for sustained visual metaphors (not just stock footage)
- Music arc planner (maps emotional trajectory to soundtrack)
- Longer render pipeline (15-30 min output)
- Quality-over-speed rendering settings
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 or 2560x1440 (16:9)

## 4. Motion Comic (3-10 min)

**Platforms:** YouTube, Webtoon (vertical), social media clips

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 3-10 minutes |
| Word count | 300-1,000 words (dialogue-driven, sparser than essay) |
| WPM | 100-130 WPM (paced for dramatic effect with pauses) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 90% panel art / 10% narration gaps |

### What Motion Comics Are

A motion comic sits between static manga/comics and full animation. Static panels are enhanced with:
- Camera movements (pan, zoom, parallax)
- Minimal character animation (lip sync, eye blinks, hair movement)
- Sound effects and ambient soundscape
- Voice acting replacing speech bubbles
- Background music synchronized to emotional beats

Text boxes, speech bubbles, and onomatopoeia are typically removed to feature more of the original artwork.

### Panel Transition Techniques

**Camera-based transitions:**
- **Pan:** Slow horizontal/vertical movement across a wide panel
- **Zoom:** Push into a detail (eyes, hands, key object) for emphasis
- **Ken Burns effect:** Slow zoom + slight pan on a static panel (creates life)
- **Parallax:** Multi-layer depth — foreground moves faster than background

**Cut-based transitions:**
- **Hard cut:** Direct jump between panels (action beats)
- **Dissolve/crossfade:** Emotional transitions, time passage
- **Wipe:** Panel edge slides to reveal next (preserves comic feel)
- **Iris/spotlight:** Focus narrows to a detail, then opens on new scene

**Webtoon-specific (vertical scroll to video):**
- The vertical scroll format means panels are designed to be viewed sequentially
- Transition timing must account for the original reading rhythm
- Long vertical panels convert to slow vertical pans in video

### Voice Acting vs. Text-on-Screen

| Approach | When to Use | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full voice acting | Character-driven drama, action | Immersive, emotional range | Expensive, casting complexity |
| Narrator + text | Philosophical/therapeutic | Consistent tone, easier production | Less character differentiation |
| Text-on-screen only | Budget, accessibility, silent platforms | Works muted, lowest cost | Less cinematic feel |
| Hybrid | Mix of drama + reflection | Best of both worlds | Requires careful balance |

### Reference Examples

**Solo Leveling (webtoon to anime):**
- Art emphasizes dynamic action with extreme poses, speed lines, motion blur
- Chromatic aberration as a digital effects technique
- Debris and smoke shapes for kinetic energy

**Tower of God:**
- Pioneered webtoon-to-anime pipeline
- Demonstrated that vertical scroll storytelling translates to horizontal screen
- Two seasons of anime adaptation validated the format commercially

### Sound Design

- Sound effects layered to match visual transitions (impacts, whooshes, ambient)
- Background music synchronized to emotional beats
- Environmental ambiance (rain, wind, city sounds) creates depth
- Silence used strategically before dramatic reveals

### Platform-Specific Rules

- YouTube: 16:9 horizontal format. Chapters for scene breaks.
- Vertical (Webtoon/TikTok): 9:16 with panel-by-panel reveal pacing
- Social clips: Extract 30-60s high-action sequences as teasers

### Music / Audio Approach

- Full cinematic sound design (this is the most audio-intensive format)
- Character-specific musical themes if recurring characters
- Action: percussion + tension strings. Reflection: piano + ambient pads.
- Sound effects budget: every transition, every impact, every environment

### Pipeline Requirements

- Panel segmentation engine (extract individual panels from manga pages)
- Camera movement controller (pan, zoom, parallax timing)
- Character animation layer (lip sync, eye blinks, minimal body movement)
- Sound effect library mapped to action types
- Voice synthesis or voice acting integration (ElevenLabs for AI voices)
- Multi-track audio mixer (voice + SFX + music + ambient)
- Panel manifest schema (existing: `panel_images_manifest.schema.json`)
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 (YouTube) or 1080x1920 (vertical platforms)

## 5. Lofi / Ambient (1-4 hours)

**Platforms:** YouTube (primary, including livestream)

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 1-4 hours (or infinite livestream) |
| Word count | 0 words (no narration) |
| WPM | N/A |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 100% visual + music |

### Why the Lofi Girl Format Works

The Lofi Girl channel (est. 2017) runs 24/7 livestreams of lo-fi hip hop with anime-style animation. The format works because of several psychological mechanisms:

**Parasocial companionship:**
The animated character studying creates a one-sided sense of companionship. For isolated students or remote workers, "someone else studying alongside them" reduces loneliness.

**Psychological anchoring:**
Predictability reduces cognitive load. When the environment feels safe and familiar, the brain allocates more resources to thinking. The visual anchor (girl studying through day/night, seasons shifting) creates a narrative of persistence and routine.

**Prospect-refuge theory:**
The scene balances openness (city view through window) with shelter (cozy room interior), creating a feeling of safety with alertness — ideal for focused work.

**Minimal cognitive demand:**
The animation loops are designed to be visually interesting without being distracting. Subtle effects — light shifts, stars glimmering, slight character movements — maintain engagement without pulling attention from work.

### Visual Production Approach

**2.5D animated loops:**
- Multi-plane technique creates depth from 2D art
- Foreground, midground, background layers move at different speeds
- Subtle parallax on scroll or gentle camera drift

**Animation elements (all seamless loops):**
- Hair movement / page turning (character)
- Steam from cup / pen movement (props)
- Light shifts / cloud movement / rain (environment)
- Day/night cycle variations (time of day)
- Seasonal variations (spring blossoms, autumn leaves, snow)

**Technical loop requirements:**
- 10-30 second base loop, seamlessly repeating
- Multiple loop variations to prevent detection of repetition
- Resolution: 1920x1080 minimum, 4K preferred for quality perception

### Music Selection and Mood Maintenance

- Lo-fi hip hop beats at 70-90 BPM
- Consistent volume and energy level (no spikes)
- Jazzy chords, vinyl crackle, mellow piano samples
- Tracks blend seamlessly — no jarring transitions
- For livestreams: algorithmic playlist that maintains mood without repetition

### Platform-Specific Rules (YouTube)

- Livestream format gets algorithmic boost for consistent viewership
- Upload format: 1-4 hour videos also perform well (background play)
- Community engagement through live chat (moderators keep it positive)
- Seasonal visual updates drive social media sharing moments
- Revenue model: ads + super chats + merchandise

### Pipeline Requirements

- Seamless loop animation renderer (10-30s loops)
- Multi-layer parallax compositor
- Day/night and seasonal variant generator
- Music playlist engine with seamless crossfading
- Livestream packaging (RTMP output capability)
- Long-form video renderer (1-4 hour output files)
- Thumbnail: single frame that captures the cozy aesthetic
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 or 3840x2160 (16:9)

## 6. Narrated Audiobook Visualization

**Platforms:** YouTube, podcast apps with video, meditation apps

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 5-60 minutes (chapter-based) |
| Word count | 750-9,000 words (matches full chapter/section) |
| WPM | 130-150 WPM (audiobook standard) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 100% narration / visuals serve the audio |

### How Audiobook Content Becomes Video

**Tier 1 — Static image + audio (minimal effort):**
- Book cover art displayed for the entire duration
- Waveform animation synced to audio levels
- Chapter titles appear at transitions
- Lowest production cost, still monetizable on YouTube

**Tier 2 — Animated text + ambient visuals:**
- Highlighted text scrolling in sync with narration (karaoke-style)
- Soft background imagery (nature, abstract textures)
- Key phrases emphasized with size/color changes
- Chapter markers in YouTube description for navigation

**Tier 3 — Illustrated visualization (Headspace/Calm model):**
- Custom animation that visualizes the narrative content
- Rounded, soft character designs (no angular geometry)
- Color palette mapped to emotional states (calm = cool blues, focus = warm amber)
- Abstract visuals during meditation/reflection sections
- Multiple animation studios can contribute while maintaining consistent "vibe"

### The Headspace Visual Language

Headspace's Netflix series demonstrates the gold standard for therapeutic audiobook visualization:
- Narration by a former Buddhist monk with unassuming delivery
- Every illustration mapped to an emotional state
- Palette avoids pure black and white (warm neutrals instead)
- Characters are intentionally simple: dots for eyes, curved line for mouth
- Stripped-back, abstract visuals during actual meditation exercises
- 20-minute episodes balancing education + guided practice

### Key Writing Patterns

**Chapter structure for video:**
- Clear chapter breaks with visual title cards
- Each chapter: opening hook, teaching, guided practice, transition
- Progress indicators (chapter 3 of 7, time remaining)
- Bookend technique: return to opening image/metaphor at close

**Text animation techniques:**
- Word-by-word highlight synced to audio timing
- Key phrases grow/glow on emphasis
- Paragraph-level scroll for longer passages
- Font: high readability, not decorative. Sans-serif for screen.

### Platform-Specific Rules

- YouTube: Chapter markers in description massively improve retention
- Videos with chapter navigation get more clicks from search results
- Background-play audience (audio-only) is significant — audio quality is paramount
- Monetization: longer content = more ad breaks (but don't over-monetize therapeutic content)

### Music / Audio Approach

- Narration is the primary audio — music is purely ambient support
- Soft pads underneath narration (volume at ~10% of voice)
- Music only rises during transitions between chapters
- For meditation sections: binaural tones or singing bowls (optional)
- Voice quality: warm, steady, intimate — recording studio quality, not podcast quality

### Pipeline Requirements

- Text-to-speech engine with natural prosody (ElevenLabs)
- Text highlight/scroll synchronization engine
- Chapter marker generator (timestamps for YouTube descriptions)
- Ambient visual library (nature loops, abstract textures, soft gradients)
- Illustrated character/scene renderer (for Tier 3 production)
- Audio mastering pipeline (voice + music + ambient mixing)
- Progress bar / chapter indicator overlay
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 (16:9) or 1080x1080 (square for social clips)

## 7. Educational / Explainer

**Platforms:** YouTube (primary), course platforms, social clips

### Script Specs

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 5-15 minutes (sweet spot: 8-10 min) |
| Word count | 750-2,250 words |
| WPM | 140-150 WPM (clear, energetic, never rushed) |
| Visual-to-narration ratio | 85% illustrated animation / 15% text overlays |

### The Kurzgesagt Script Structure

Kurzgesagt is the reference standard for animated explainer content. Their process:

1. **Script is the bottleneck** — Writing and rewriting a logical, easy-to-understand yet comprehensive script is described as extremely time-consuming. Scripts go through many revision rounds, balancing simplicity and accuracy. Expert review for factual correctness.

2. **Storyboarding before animation** — The entire video is storyboarded scene by scene, with visual metaphors and transitions planned alongside the script.

3. **Script structure (Problem - Mechanism - Hope):**

**0:00-1:00 — The Hook (Problem Statement)**
- Present a surprising fact, counterintuitive question, or scale comparison
- "What if [seemingly impossible thing] were actually [surprising truth]?"
- Immediately establish stakes: why should the viewer care?

**1:00-3:00 — Context / Scale Setting**
- Ground the viewer in the relevant science/history
- Use visual metaphors to make abstract concepts tangible
- "Imagine [complex concept] as [familiar analogy]..."

**3:00-7:00 — The Mechanism (Core Explanation)**
- Step-by-step walkthrough of how the thing works
- Each concept introduced with its own visual metaphor
- Layer complexity gradually — never dump all information at once
- Transitions between concepts are animated, not just narrated

**7:00-9:00 — Implications / So What?**
- Connect the mechanism to real-world consequences
- Show scale: "This means that by 2050..."
- Create emotional investment in the outcome

**9:00-10:00 — Hope / Agency**
- End on a note of possibility, not despair
- What can be done? What is already being done?
- Invite curiosity rather than anxiety

### TED-Ed Writing Patterns

- Every concept gets a visual metaphor (never just illustrate the literal words)
- Script is written for animation, not adapted to it
- Questions > statements (keep the viewer's mind active)
- Sentences are short and direct during complex explanations
- Humor is used strategically to release tension during dense sections

### Visual Metaphor Scripting

The key to great explainer content is that the visuals do NOT just illustrate the words. Instead:

- **Abstract concepts become physical objects** (anxiety = a growing shadow, neural pathways = a river carving through rock)
- **Scale comparisons use familiar references** (this many bacteria = the weight of 3 elephants)
- **Processes become journeys** (a molecule's path through the body = a character walking through a city)
- **Data becomes landscape** (statistics = mountains, valleys, weather patterns)

### Platform-Specific Rules (YouTube)

- Educational content benefits from YouTube's "satisfaction" signal — viewers feel their time was well spent
- Longer sessions (viewer watches multiple videos) boost channel ranking
- Evergreen educational content gets sustained search traffic for years
- Titles: balance keyword-rich (SEO) with curiosity-gap (CTR)
- Thumbnails: bold colors, simple composition, one visual concept + 2-3 words

### Music / Audio Approach

- Upbeat, curious tone — music that says "learning is exciting"
- Tempo increases during build-up, resolves during conclusions
- Sound effects for visual transitions (whoosh, pop, chime)
- Voice: energetic, clear, slightly fast but never incomprehensible
- Consistent narrator voice builds channel identity

### Pipeline Requirements

- Visual metaphor planning tool (maps concepts to animated metaphors)
- Storyboard generator (scene-by-scene visual planning from script)
- Animated illustration renderer (Kurzgesagt-style flat design)
- Fact-check annotation layer (tracks claims to sources)
- Script-to-animation timing engine (sync narration to visual beats)
- Color palette system (consistent brand colors across episodes)
- Aspect ratio: 1920x1080 (16:9)
