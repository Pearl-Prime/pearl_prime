## ⚡ Executive Summary (read this first)

### The headline

**Color vertical webtoon is the dominant growth lane globally, but B&W page manga remains the dominant revenue-per-title lane in Japan + the backlist-economics lane in en_US.** Phoenix Omega should weight catalog production **55–65% color vertical webtoon, 25–30% B&W / color page manga (for KDP collected volumes + Japan), and 5–10% direct illustrated self-help** — with market-by-market variation.

### The strongest external signal (added post-web-research)

**Every major Japanese and Korean incumbent is investing INTO color vertical-scroll, not B&W page manga.** Shueisha — the B&W flagship publisher of *ONE PIECE* — launched **JUMP TOON on 29 May 2024** as a dedicated **full-color vertical-scroll platform** with *ONE PIECE* editor Takanori Asada leading it, explicitly framed as "full color + vertical scroll, a different reading experience from the original right-to-left, black & white."¹³ Separately, **WEBTOON + Warner Bros. Animation** announced 10 color-vertical adaptations in Nov 2025¹⁴; **Disney took a 2% WEBTOON stake in Jan 2026** ($32.77M) plus a joint 35,000-title global platform¹⁵; **LINE Digital Frontier** took a stake in Tokyo studio No. 9 in Jan 2025 and committed **20 webtoon-based anime adaptations for 2025**¹⁶; and **KADOKAWA × Kakao Piccoma** announced a Dec 2024 alliance that funnels traditional Japanese page-manga IP *into* Piccoma's vertical format.¹⁷ Hollywood, Disney, and the Japanese page-manga flagship are all betting adaptation dollars on color vertical. This is the clearest forward-looking signal in the entire research pass.

### 4.2 Revenue per artist-hour (the decision-relevant metric)

Production-hour assumptions:
- **1 color vertical webtoon episode** = 30–50 panels = ~80–120 hours traditional; **~8–20 hours with AI-assisted pipeline** (Phoenix's 289-portrait + FLUX reuse reduces this dramatically)
- **1 B&W page chapter** = 18–22 pages = ~60–80 hours traditional; **~20–40 hours with AI** (B&W rendering adds conversion step + line-art cleanup)

| Lane | Revenue/hour (hit) | Revenue/hour (mid) | Revenue/hour (long-tail) |
|------|-------------------:|-------------------:|-------------------------:|
| Color webtoon × Phoenix AI pipeline | $50–200/hr | $10–40/hr | $0.50–5/hr |
| B&W page × Phoenix AI pipeline | $100–500/hr (hit) | $12–50/hr (mid) | $1–10/hr |
| Color page × Phoenix AI pipeline | $30–150/hr | $6–20/hr | $0.30–3/hr |

**Decision signal:** Color webtoon wins on volume and growth; B&W page wins on per-hit economics in established channels (KDP + Japan). Phoenix should pick **both, with different weights per market** — not one or the other.
