Chapter 2

Your belly clenches when love demands you risk your constructed identity. The Bright does not protect the person you think you are.

The team standup and your technical insight was correct but overshadowed. Your manager built on it and took it further. Your WFH desk credit has become a footnote. rain streaking the glass light shows invisible work being done. You don't speak next time.

What are you attached to that you pretend you are not? Look at it without judgment.
Why do you grip it? Is the thing itself the problem, or the story you built around
having it? Can you hold it lightly and still have it? What happens then?

Where in your life do you run from what is difficult? Can you name one place where
you use busy-ness, distraction, or sleep to not feel something? Not to change it,
but to see it. What would happen if you stopped running for just five breaths?

You can see now where you turn away. The place in your chest where you say no. The Bright doesn't judge this movement. It simply illuminates it. Your nervous system is recording this moment. What you avoided is now available to conscious relationship. Feel this in your belly. The separate self's gesture becomes transparent when you stop defending it. Your hands know what your mind is still protecting. This seeing is the completion. Just be here with it. The contraction has shown you its face. Your breath participates in this revelation. The body speaks what the mind still refuses to acknowledge.
