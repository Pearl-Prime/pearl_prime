Chapter 4

Your neck muscles tighten as you meet genuine love. This is the precise edge where the false self begins to yield.

LinkedIn shows people with your same start date running departments. Your WFH desk is quiet and you're very still. late afternoon light slanting across the desk evening brings the comparison home. the metro below brings the world you're not part of. Your fingers hover over the update button and don't press it.

What are you attached to that you pretend you are not? Look at it without judgment.
Why do you grip it? Is the thing itself the problem, or the story you built around
having it? Can you hold it lightly and still have it? What happens then?

Notice a thought that repeats itself in your mind. Follow it back. Who told you
this was true? How long have you carried it? If you set it down right now, what
would be different? Not forever. Just for today. What changes when the story stops?

Where in your life do you run from what is difficult? Can you name one place where
you use busy-ness, distraction, or sleep to not feel something? Not to change it,
but to see it. What would happen if you stopped running for just five breaths?

Recall a recent moment you defended an image of yourself. Write one sentence describing the fear beneath the defense. Then sit three minutes without rewriting your story—only feel the vulnerability. Recognition begins where the performance ends.

Return your attention to where your body touches the ground. The Bright remains. The contraction exists. So does the solid earth beneath you. Feel your sitting bones. Notice the place where your back meets the chair. Feel this support. The gesture of avoidance is real and your body has a ground. Both are true. Return here. The ground doesn't require anything from you. Your feet can rest. Your weight is held by something more reliable than your own effort. The earth receives you exactly as you are with your contraction intact. This support is always here.
