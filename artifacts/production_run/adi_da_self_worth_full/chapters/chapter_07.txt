Chapter 7

Someone you love is pulling away. Or you are pulling away from them. Either way, the space between you is filling with something heavy. Not anger exactly. Something older than anger. A holding that neither of you can name but both of you can feel.

LinkedIn shows your former peer is now your manager's manager. Your coffee shop stalking of their career is pathetic but you continue. the avenue outside success is visible in their profile picture. Your profile says you're still doing the same job. You close the app.

Place attention in the chest or belly. Without naming it, feel contraction—tightening, bracing, withdrawal. Do not fix it. For three to five minutes, let contraction be fully felt without story. The Bright is not elsewhere; it is the openness that can notice contraction without war.

Contraction mapping. Sit quietly and bring to mind a recent moment of difficulty—an argument, a worry, a disappointment. Do not analyze the content. Instead, notice where the body tightens in response. Jaw, shoulders, belly, chest, throat. Place your hand on the area of greatest tension. Breathe into it without trying to change it. Simply notice: this is the contraction. This is the gesture of the separate self. Stay with the noticing for five minutes. The goal is not release. The goal is visibility.

Health as conductivity. Choose one physical practice today—a walk, stretching, swimming, any movement the body enjoys. As you move, feel the energy moving through the body. Do not exercise to achieve a goal. Move as an expression of the body's natural vitality. When the mind turns the movement into a project—counting reps, measuring progress, comparing to yesterday—notice that as contraction. Return to the simple feeling of the body in motion. Conductivity is life-energy flowing without the self managing it.

What are you attached to that you pretend you are not? Look at it without judgment.
Why do you grip it? Is the thing itself the problem, or the story you built around
having it? Can you hold it lightly and still have it? What happens then?

Avoidance carries a cost you feel in your spine right now. The separation you maintained required energy. That tightening in your neck, your lower back, this is the price of the contraction. The Bright illuminates what the gesture of self-protection actually costs. Your shoulders have held this tension. The price was paid in your cells, your breath. Now you see it. Your jaw clenches with the effort. Feel this directly in your body. The clarity itself is costly. You must feel what you were avoiding. The muscles in your neck are exhausted from holding the defended posture. Your lower back aches with the weight of the separation you've maintained. The body never forgets what it costs to refuse connection.
