Chapter 3

Your wrists tense as you reach toward another and feel the habitual pull back. This reaching is everything.

LinkedIn shows your former peer is now your manager's manager. Your coffee shop stalking of their career is pathetic but you continue. the road past the parking lot success is visible in their profile picture. Your profile says you're still doing the same job. You close the app.

Notice a thought that repeats itself in your mind. Follow it back. Who told you
this was true? How long have you carried it? If you set it down right now, what
would be different? Not forever. Just for today. What changes when the story stops?

What are you attached to that you pretend you are not? Look at it without judgment.
Why do you grip it? Is the thing itself the problem, or the story you built around
having it? Can you hold it lightly and still have it? What happens then?

The turned-away gesture has consequences in your body. Your breath knows it. The way you hold your ribs, the tension behind your eyes, this is what resistance costs. The Bright shows the price without softness. You cannot unsee it now. The contraction has marked you visibly. The separation created real friction in your nervous system. Feel where this friction lives. Your chest is compressed. Your throat is tight. This is not punishment. This is truth becoming apparent in your tissue. Your eyes are tired from the vigilance required to maintain the gesture. Your whole being is marked by what the contraction demanded.
