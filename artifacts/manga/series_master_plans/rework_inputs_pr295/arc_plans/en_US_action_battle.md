## morning_momentum_workplace

Demographic: shonen | Primary topic: burnout | Genre: action_battle

**Arc 1 — Inciting Fight (ep_001-012):** Taro Beck runs the same client-delivery route every night to protect a promotion he's chasing, his chat status glowing green long after his body has quit. A rival crew jumps his route and drags him into "the Circuit" — an underground, timed relay-duel league fought lap by lap through the loading docks and parking garages beneath the corporate towers, half footrace and half brawl. Taro gets torn apart on his first lap and meets Reiko Vance, a burned-out third-year rider who clocks the hollow, adrenaline-only competence under his win streak before anyone in the office does.
**Arc 2 — Training/Rival (ep_013-024):** Vance drills Taro on "the second lap" — the honest pace a body reveals only after the first-lap adrenaline burns off and faking it stops being possible. A same-tier coworker, Marcus Oyelaran, becomes his rival for the same promotion and the same league seed, pushing insult-clash-respect through dock-yard sparring. The dispatch-floor ensemble — each carrying a different overwork wound — gets pulled into late-shift Circuit runs, and the arc closes on Taro and Marcus fighting to an exhausted draw that neither can call a win.
**Arc 3 — Tournament/Escalation (ep_025-036):** The Circuit turns out to be a company-sanctioned "efficiency trial" run by management to rank couriers for cuts — a citywide bracket tournament between crews, refereed by the same dashboard that used to just track his status light. Stakes personalize hard when a dispatch teammate collapses mid-lap from the exact grind Taro's been chasing, and the "green light" antagonist gets a face: a proctor who scores exhaustion as commitment.
**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** The tournament final puts Taro against the company's champion enforcer — a rider who fully surrendered to the grind and burned into someone who no longer feels the cost he's inflicting on new hires. Vance, Marcus, and the dispatch-floor ensemble converge on the last lap. Taro wins not by outrunning burnout but by running the route clean and honest at the pace his body can actually hold — the scars from thirty-six episodes of overwork stay visible, reconciled rather than erased, and the second lap finally tells the truth.

## solar_return_isekai

Demographic: shonen | Primary topic: self_worth | Genre: action_battle

**Arc 1 — Inciting Fight (ep_001-012):** Pax Orion, rejected from his eleventh interview in a row, gets a dead video-call link reopen itself at 3 a.m. and is pulled through into a portal world with no résumé system at all — worth here is proven only by demonstrated action. A guardian at the threshold immediately tests him in forced combat ("who are you without the paper?"), and he barely survives on raw improvisation. The cost lands the instant he's dragged back to Earth at dawn: his verified college degree has silently vanished from every record he can check.
**Arc 2 — Training/Rival (ep_013-024):** A grounded companion — a sky-world armorer who reads a fighter's worth by how they hold their weapon, not their credentials — trains Pax in a power system that scales with earned belief, not granted talent. A native-born rival, contemptuous of "earthborn résumé-thinking," pushes him through insult-clash-respect duels. Pax meets other portal-summoned "resume refugees," each missing a different piece of their old identity, and the arc closes on a draw with the rival that costs Pax another credential — his last job reference — gone from Earth's records overnight.
**Arc 3 — Tournament/Escalation (ep_025-036):** A sky-world tournament to earn a permanent "Sworn Name" — an identity anchor that would stop the erasure — forces Pax to risk the last thing he has left on Earth: his own name in the system. Mid-tournament, Pax and his companion uncover that the Gate is quietly tied to the same Earth-side hiring algorithm that rejected him eleven times, profiting off exactly the kind of erasure it's doing to him.
**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** The final confrontation is against a former Gate-crosser who let the portal erase him completely — no name, no history, immense power, nothing left to lose or protect. Every ensemble refugee's stake converges on whether Pax spends his last fragment of Earth identity to win. He does — and wins not by getting his résumé back but by proving, in front of both worlds, that what he built with nothing left to prove it with was real; he returns to Earth with an unrecognizable transcript but an unmistakable sense of what he's worth.

## stoic_edge_battle
Existing series: `stoic_edge_battle__dane_holt__en_US__courage__the_guard_who_lowers_the_shield` (REUSE_EXISTING — arc plan feeds authoring into this existing series identity, do not propose a new one)
Demographic: seinen | Primary topic: courage | Genre: action_battle

**Arc 1 — Inciting Fight (ep_001-012):** Dane Holt, a junior close-protection guard, fights in pure defense — riot shield up, absorb everything, never initiate — because the one time he broke formation to counterattack, his mentor died covering the gap he left open. On his first solo detail, an ambush on his principal forces the same choice again, and his refusal to ever lower the shield nearly gets the client killed anyway, because pure defense buys time but never ends a fight.
**Arc 2 — Training/Rival (ep_013-024):** A veteran instructor drills Dane on the difference between discipline and cowardice wearing the same posture. An aggressive-first rival guard, contemptuous of Dane's passivity, needles him through sparring bouts that end in grudging respect. Dane starts learning to read the half-second window where dropping the shield is the only way to actually protect someone, and the arc closes with him taking a real hit for the first time by choice, not accident.
**Arc 3 — Tournament/Escalation (ep_025-036):** The agency runs inter-firm "readiness trials" — competing security details bidding for a high-value protection contract in escalating live-threat scenarios. Dane's team advances, but the trials expose that agency leadership has been quietly manufacturing some of the threats to justify bigger contracts, and the moral ground under his discipline gets genuinely uncertain — the seinen register holds the ambiguity rather than resolving it.
**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** The real threat behind the manufactured ones turns out to be a former guard who lowered his shield too late once and became exactly the kind of attacker Dane has spent his career fearing becoming. In the final defense, Dane has to drop the shield at precisely the right instant — not abandon the discipline, integrate it — to save the people in his formation. The win costs him the agency job that defined him; the courage lands anyway, carried by the body that finally moved, not by a speech.

## warrior_calm_cultivation
Existing series: `warrior_calm_cultivation__master_wu__en_US__burnout__the_chassis_is_listening` (REUSE_EXISTING — arc plan feeds authoring into this existing series identity, do not propose a new one)
Demographic: shonen | Primary topic: burnout | Genre: action_battle

**Arc 1 — Inciting Fight (ep_001-012):** Wen Kaida awakens a dormant ancestral war-chassis in a mountain forge-shrine — a bio-mechanical armor that channels a cultivator's qi and reports the wearer's true condition through its own diagnostics, unable to be lied to. A rival sect's raiders attack before he's ready; Kaida oversells his effort to win fast, and the chassis locks up mid-fight — a hard stall, not a power-down — nearly costing him everything until the shrine's armorer reads the redline in its plating and hauls him out.
**Arc 2 — Training/Rival (ep_013-024):** The armorer trains Kaida to listen to the chassis's breath-cycle warnings instead of overriding them, treating rest as a piloting skill, not a weakness. A fellow disciple, Ren Suo, pushes his own chassis past every redline and wins bout after bout in the short term — insult-clash-respect between them sharpens both — while the arc quietly shows Ren's plating scarring from damage that never gets to heal. It closes on Kaida landing his first clean win by disengaging one exchange early, chassis calm, and Ren refusing to believe restraint beat him.
**Arc 3 — Tournament/Escalation (ep_025-036):** A ranked sect tournament pits piloted chassis against each other in public duels, and Kaida discovers the sect elders have been quietly overclocking promising young disciples' chassis before matches to win prestige for the mountain — Ren among them, his wins never fully his own. The revelation doesn't break the sect's fundamental promise, but it does personalize the stakes: Kaida has to fight for Ren's chassis to be powered down before it burns him out for good.
**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** The sect's champion — a disciple who redlined so long his chassis fused past safe release and can no longer power down — is the final wall. Every scar the chassis has logged since episode one shows in its plating going into this fight. Kaida wins not with a bigger surge but by reaching true stillness at full output — proving, chassis diagnostics and all, that power which can rest is stronger than power that can't — and the reconciliation is visible: a battle-worn armor finally allowed to power down clean.
