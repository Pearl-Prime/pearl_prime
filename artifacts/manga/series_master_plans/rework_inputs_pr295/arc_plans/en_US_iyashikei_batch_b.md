The everyday ritual is the boiler dial, the row of wooden bath tokens, the fold of towels still warm from the dryer, and the small window where steam meets night air. Regulars drift in at odd hours — a night-shift nurse, an insomniac baker proofing dough three doors down, a teenager who can't face an empty house yet. Tone: warm, low-lit, unhurried; the keeper listens more than she talks.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Register shift into the town's rainy season: the bathhouse roof develops a slow, familiar leak the keeper catches in a tin bucket, and its rhythm becomes its own kind of lullaby for regulars. Still atmospheric — no crisis, just the sound of rain replacing the sound of the boiler as the night's metronome.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn cools the changing room; the keeper starts leaving out a single extra towel, folded, for whoever needs it most that night — never announced, just there. A cycle follows the return of a regular who'd stopped coming, now visibly tireder, and the quiet accommodation the keeper makes without asking why. The seasonal thread: chrysanthemum-scented bath salts arrive for the autumn stock, and their smell becomes a recurring sensory anchor.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter, and the bathhouse's steam against cold glass becomes the visual signature of the whole book. The keeper's gentle mastery has deepened into something almost wordless — she now reads a regular's tiredness by the way they set down their sandals, not by asking. No cure, no dramatic breakthrough: just a longer stretch of nights where the tin bucket stays dry, the tokens get returned on time, and the sleepless find their hour arriving a little sooner than before.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer brings the stall's open garage door and street noise mixing with the whetstone's rasp; the keeper starts leaving a blade unfinished overnight more than once, an old habit he's noticing rather than hiding. **Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn brings the harvest-season rush of kitchen knives from the town's canning season, and the keeper begins teaching the apprentice the angle of the blade against stone, a wordless, patient handoff of the trade. **Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter quiet in the garage, breath visible in the cold air over the stone. The keeper's gentle mastery: he finally sharpens the one knife he'd been avoiding, and it sings again, faint but true — not a cure for the loss, just proof the ear for it was never really gone.
