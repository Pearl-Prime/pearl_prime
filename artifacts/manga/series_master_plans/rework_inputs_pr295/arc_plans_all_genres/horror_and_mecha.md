# Full-genre-coverage exercise: horror + mecha (bright_presence_tw_seinen, bio_flow_healing, longevity_lab_healing, hormone_reset_healing)

Planning-only structural outlines (4 arcs x 12 episodes = 48 episodes per brand).
Genre sources: `docs/research/manga_craft/psychological_horror.md` (horror, via
`psychological_horror: horror` alias) and `docs/research/manga_craft/mecha.md`.
Assignment source: `genre_assignment_37brands.md`. All four brands are
reassigned from their established genre (psychological_thriller) for this
full-taxonomy coverage exercise per operator instruction — not a correction.
Doctrine followed throughout: arcs escalate materially per act/arc shape in
the respective craft bible; the self-help topic stays subtext, carried only
through genre mechanics (the uncanny, the machine); no brand teacher is named
in-panel — each genre's teacher-mode vessel appears only in survivor/mentor
function; no claimed cure — every arc ends on a "grip loosens one notch" /
"fractional integration" turn, never resolution. `acceptance_layer` for all
four outlines stays `authored_candidate`.

## bright_presence_tw_seinen
Genre (this exercise): horror | Primary topic: social_anxiety
Note: reassigned from established genre (psychological_thriller) for full-taxonomy coverage exercise (see genre_assignment_37brands.md)

Register: **social_anxiety as uncanny presence** — the horror-object is a
figure that is always almost-seen at the edge of group scenes: a presence
that only manifests when the protagonist is watched, judged, or about to
speak. Vessel (never named): a survivor-neighbor who has learned to turn
toward the presence instead of fleeing rooms. Recurring visual through-line:
a doubled shadow that lags a half-beat behind the protagonist's own
movements in any panel with three or more onlookers.

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012):** Wound/refusal. Protagonist (a Taipei office temp,
new to a floor of established coworkers) starts noticing the lagging-shadow
figure only in rooms where she is being evaluated — a meeting, a welcome
lunch, a group photo. She reads it as stress and avoids gatherings, which
visibly strengthens the figure (it gets a half-step closer, gains a second
lagging shadow of its own) each time she cancels or hides. Arc ends with the
figure fully visible for the first time, standing where she would have stood
at a work dinner she skipped.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024):** The figure begins appearing even in rooms where no
one is watching her — it has learned to enter alone-time, which is worse.
She meets the survivor-neighbor (an older tenant in her building who never
looks directly at group photos on the stairwell wall) who models the "one
breath toward it" turn: staying in the room one beat longer than instinct
demands. First small experiment — she stays seated through an entire team
review instead of excusing herself — and the figure doesn't vanish, but it
stops copying her exact posture. Arc ends on a company all-hands announced
for arc 3, the largest audience yet.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036):** Escalation to institutional scale — a company-wide
presentation she is assigned to give. The figure now has a voice-adjacent
sound (not words, a register under the room's ambient noise) that only
crescendos when she rehearses alone. A rival coworker's public humiliation
in a meeting (not the protagonist's) shows her the cost of the old
flee-pattern taken to its extreme — that colleague's shadow-figure is now a
crowd. The presentation forces a full meeting-toward: she gives it with the
figure standing in the front row, unresolved but not fled from. Grip loosens
one notch; the figure's copied posture finally breaks from hers mid-scene.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048):** The figure recurs at a personal milestone (a wedding
or family gathering) where the stakes are relational rather than
professional, testing whether the arc-3 turn generalizes. A final wrong-quiet
sequence — the ordinary room (her own apartment) is revisited, slightly
changed: the doubled shadow is still there, still lags a half-beat, but no
longer requires her to leave the room to survive it. No defeat, no cure —
she ends able to sit in a full room while it stands in the doorway.

## bio_flow_healing
Genre (this exercise): horror | Primary topic: somatic_healing (body-horror register)
Note: reassigned from established genre (psychological_thriller) for full-taxonomy coverage exercise (see genre_assignment_37brands.md)

Register: **the body itself as the uncanny thing** — classic body-horror
device (per operator note: a legitimate manga subgenre, not a topic
mismatch). The horror-object is not external; it is the protagonist's own
somatic symptoms — tension, numbness, a chronic ache — rendered as something
with its own will, growing and relocating in the body across the arcs.
Recurring visual through-line: a stain/bruise-like discoloration that
migrates panel to panel and is never in the same place twice.

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012):** Wound/refusal. Protagonist (a massage-therapy
apprentice, ironically numb to her own body) starts feeling a foreign
tightness under her sternum that she diagnoses away as stress, caffeine,
posture — anything but the body's own signal. Fleeing (icing it, ignoring
it, working through it) causes the tightness to migrate — it surfaces next
in her hands, then her jaw. Arc ends with the migrating stain visibly
breaching skin for the first time, in a mirror panel she can't unsee.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024):** The survivor-vessel (a retired client with a decades-
old chronic-pain history who describes her body "like a room with a tenant
you have to negotiate with") models the turn: one breath directed at the
sensation instead of away from it. First small experiment — she places a
hand on the migrating stain instead of icing it — and it doesn't disappear,
but it stops relocating for the length of one held breath. Arc ends with a
second, independent stain appearing elsewhere on a coworker who has been
copying the protagonist's old flee-habits.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036):** Escalation — the stain begins to have texture and
faint warmth, unmistakably alive-adjacent, and starts appearing in dreams as
a shape with weight. A high-stress professional exam (her therapist
certification) forces a full-body flare under observation — the stain
surfaces on her forearm mid-practical in front of an examiner. She must meet
it in real time rather than retreat to a bathroom to hide it, the first
full public "meeting" of the arc. Grip loosens one notch: the stain
recedes to a faint shadow, still present, no longer spreading.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048):** The stain recurs under a new stressor (caring for a
family member, mirroring her own old avoidance) — testing generalization.
Final wrong-quiet sequence: the ordinary room (her treatment room, now
professionally hers) is revisited, slightly changed — the stain is a faint,
permanent shading on her sternum, visible only in certain light, no longer
migrating, no longer feared. No cure claimed: it is met, not gone.

## longevity_lab_healing
Genre (this exercise): mecha | Primary topic: somatic_healing
Note: reassigned from established genre (psychological_thriller) for full-taxonomy coverage exercise (see genre_assignment_37brands.md)

Chassis mechanism: **the body-as-instrument exosuit** — a diagnostic/
rehabilitation exo-frame ("the Chassis") built by a longevity research lab,
worn by the pilot to correct degenerative somatic damage. It reads and
externalizes the wearer's interoceptive signals as HUD telemetry (heart
rate, cortisol proxy, muscular guarding) — the cockpit is literally her own
nervous system rendered as instrument panel. Cast: reluctant pilot (a former
elite dancer, injured, recruited into a longevity trial she distrusts),
chief engineer (built the Chassis after losing her own mobility, primary
attachment figure to the machine), lab director (authority figure,
compromised — pushing the trial faster than the body can integrate), rival
pilot (an older trial subject whose Chassis-integration went too fast and
too far, cautionary parallel).

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012) — Origin, baseline machine:** The dancer is forced into
the longevity trial after an injury ends her career; she resists wearing the
Chassis, whose telemetry exposes every guarded, numbed sensation she'd
rather not know about. First tentative pilot-machine sync in a hangar test
sequence — pre-launch stillness, cockpit-crisis of information overload, then
a small supervised "battle" (a rehab stress-test) that ends with the
Chassis's HUD flatlining a signal she'd been suppressing for years. Theme:
what is being asked of this person. Ends on a debrief where the cost (what
integration will require of her) is named for the first time.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024) — Trial, scarred machine:** The Chassis begins
surfacing the full scope of old injuries the dancer had never processed —
its telemetry doesn't just measure, it forces confrontation with buried
somatic memory. First major damage: a rehab session pushed too hard by lab
pressure causes a Chassis malfunction that leaves her temporarily unable to
feel her own legs inside the frame — a mechanical failure standing in for a
somatic collapse. The rival pilot's over-integration (skipping the "meet it
slowly" doctrine) is shown as a warning: her rival's Chassis now overrides
her natural signals entirely. Theme: what does the machine cost. Ends on a
scarred, repaired Chassis and a director's ultimatum to accelerate the
program.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036) — Ascendance, transformed machine:** Pilot and Chassis
reach peak integration — she can finally read her own body's true signals
through the frame without dissociating — just as the lab's real agenda
surfaces: the "longevity trial" was never about her recovery, it was data
harvesting to accelerate a commercial rollout that would push other pilots
the way the rival was pushed. A redesigned Chassis (new, more transparent
interface) marks the arc turn. Confrontation with the lab director in a
command-room sequence, and a decision to keep the machine but reject the
program's terms. Theme: what are they fighting for.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048) — Meaning-making, machine altered:** The Chassis is
fundamentally altered — stripped of its lab-controlled overrides, rebuilt
by the chief engineer as a machine that serves the pilot's own signals only.
Final battle-adjacent sequence is a public demonstration where the dancer
must integrate everything without lab support, in front of the rival pilot
she may still be able to help. Debrief-register ending, not a cure: she
answers "who am I without the cockpit" by choosing to keep piloting on her
own terms, integration fractional and ongoing, not complete.

## hormone_reset_healing
Genre (this exercise): mecha | Primary topic: somatic_healing
Note: reassigned from established genre (psychological_thriller) for full-taxonomy coverage exercise (see genre_assignment_37brands.md)

Chassis mechanism: **the cyclical bio-rhythm mecha** — distinct from
longevity_lab_healing's diagnostic exosuit: this is a full-scale piloted
mecha ("Tidal-unit") whose reactor output and combat capability fluctuate on
a rhythmic cycle tied to the pilot's own endocrine rhythm, rendered visually
as a pulsing core-light that waxes and wanes chapter to chapter. Where the
Chassis is corrective instrumentation, the Tidal-unit is a full mecha whose
power is inherently cyclical and cannot be forced to a constant output — the
genre mechanism for a body that runs on rhythms, not willpower. Cast:
reluctant pilot (a shift-worker recruited mid-cycle-crash, distrustful of a
machine that seems to fail her at the worst moments), mech engineer (built
the Tidal-unit's rhythm-sync system, treats its fluctuation as a design
feature, not a flaw, and has to keep convincing command of that), commander
(demands constant-output performance, antagonist-adjacent), rival pilot (a
veteran who burned out forcing her own unit to ignore its cycle, now
grounded — the cautionary parallel).

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012) — Origin, baseline machine:** Pilot is drafted after her
predecessor collapses; she resents the Tidal-unit's unpredictable power
troughs, reading them as the machine (and her own body) failing her at the
worst moments in early sorties. Hangar-briefing-launch-battle-debrief
rhythm established; first battle stalls mid-fight when the unit's core dims
during a trough, nearly costing the squad the engagement. Theme: what is
being asked of this person. Ends on a debrief where the engineer names the
trough as structural, not a defect — first hint the fight isn't to eliminate
the fluctuation.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024) — Trial, scarred machine:** Command pressures the
engineer to install an override that suppresses the trough artificially;
pilot initially wants this too. First major damage arrives when the
override is used in a real battle — the suppressed trough rebounds mid-
combat as a violent overcorrection spike, badly damaging the unit and
injuring the pilot. The grounded rival's backstory is revealed here: she is
grounded precisely because her old unit's override burned out its rhythm-
sync permanently. Theme: what does the machine cost. Ends on the damaged,
scarred Tidal-unit and the override disabled — a turn, not a triumph.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036) — Ascendance, transformed machine:** Pilot learns to
read her own trough-windows and plan engagements around them rather than
against them — a redesigned core-housing (visibly changed, softer pulsing
light instead of a suppressed flat one) marks the shift. A larger campaign
forces the squad to depend on her honestly-scheduled peak windows rather
than a falsely constant output, which the commander resists until a mission
only succeeds because they worked with the rhythm. Theme: what are they
fighting for. Ends on the commander's grudging concession and a harder
enemy escalation revealed for arc 4.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048) — Meaning-making, machine altered:** Final campaign
demands the pilot face a battle that lands squarely in a trough window with
no way to reschedule it — the arc's real test of whether "working with the
rhythm" holds under maximum pressure rather than favorable conditions. She
fights the trough battle at reduced output, deliberately, rather than
forcing an override, and the squad adapts around her instead of her
adapting to false constancy. No cure, no permanently "fixed" reactor —
debrief-register ending: she answers "who am I without the cockpit" by
carrying the same rhythm-honoring practice into ordinary life, fractional
integration, cycle ongoing.
