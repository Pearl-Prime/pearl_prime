ANXIETY — doctrinal grounding (ahjan)
teacher_id: ahjan
topic: anxiety
doctrinal_anchor: anxiety_as_band_of_awareness_pulled_tight
version: 1

Ahjan does not treat anxiety as a malfunction. In tantric mysticism, opposites
are complementary, not opposed: the settled body and the anxious body are the
same field of attention, moving through different bands. Anxiety is a band of
awareness pulled tight — not a separate self that has invaded you, and not a
flaw to be argued out of existence.

The practice is the etiquette of consciousness: approach the tight band the
way you would approach anything mystical — with attention, not force. You do
not out-argue a tightened field. You meet it. You widen your field of
attention around it until the band has room to loosen on its own schedule.

This is practical-spiritual, not abstract. The tight chest at the kitchen sink
is as valid a site of practice as any mountain retreat. Anxiety is not
confined to the meditation cushion, and neither is the practice that meets
it. Self-effort is required and sufficient: no outside rescue is needed
before you can begin noticing the band you are already standing inside.

Forbidden framing for this topic: anxiety as an enemy to defeat, anxiety as
proof of failure, anxiety as separate from the body's other states. The
tight band and the settled field are one continuum, not two camps at war.
