## REFLECTION v01
---
I have noticed that depression in students often does not look like sadness. It looks like nothing. It looks like going through the day without feeling much of anything. It looks like getting good grades and being fine and not really being present in any of it.

There is a mechanism called anhedonia, and what it does is flat. The mechanism turns off the sense of interest. The mechanism turns off the sense of pleasure. The mechanism turns off the capacity to feel moved by things. And the result is a kind of walking through the world that looks like functioning but is actually a kind of absence.

What I have come to understand is that depression is often invisible because it looks like okay. You are doing the things. You are going to school. You are getting decent grades. You are showing up. No one is concerned because you are not falling apart. You are just not really there.

The mechanism that is creating this flatness is not the same as sadness. Sadness is a response to loss. Sadness is a feeling. The flatness is the absence of feeling. The mechanism is not making you feel bad. The mechanism is making you feel nothing.

What I have come to think about is the way that the nothing is often harder to name than sadness. The nothing does not announce itself. The nothing is quiet. The nothing looks like being fine. But underneath the fine, there is an absence. There is a kind of disconnection from everything that is supposed to matter.

The mechanism is protecting you by turning everything off. But the protection comes at a very high cost. The cost is that you are not really alive.
