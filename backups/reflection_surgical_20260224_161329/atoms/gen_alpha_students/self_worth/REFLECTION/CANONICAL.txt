## REFLECTION v01
---
I have noticed that self-worth in students is often directly tied to academic performance. A good grade means you are good. A bad grade means you are bad. The grade is not feedback about one moment of performance. The grade is feedback about who you are as a person.

There is a mechanism called identity fusion, and what it does is fuse the grade with the self. The mechanism is not separating the performance from the person. The mechanism is treating the performance as if it is the person.

What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is learning this fusion from the environment. Parents ask about grades before asking about the day. Schools rank students and communicate rankings constantly. College applications reduce you to numbers. The culture is teaching you that grades are what matters. Grades are what measure your value.

What I have come to think about is the way that the mechanism is preventing you from having a sense of self that is separate from your performance. The mechanism is making your entire worth dependent on one thing: your ability to perform well academically.

But the performance is not the whole of you. The performance is one small part of who you are. The mechanism is taking one small part and making it the entire person.

And the fusion is creating a situation where the stakes are always impossibly high. Every grade is not just a grade. Every grade is a judgment about your fundamental worth.

There is a mechanism that has extended the achievement-based worth into the physical realm. The mechanism is treating the body as if it is something to be evaluated. The mechanism is treating the body as if it has to meet a particular standard in order to be acceptable.

What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is creating a kind of alienation from the body. The mechanism is not inhabiting the body. The mechanism is judging the body. The mechanism is treating the body as if it is an object to be improved rather than a home to be lived in.

What I have come to think about is the way that the mechanism is preventing the simple experience of having a body. The mechanism is preventing the presence in the body. The mechanism is preventing the acceptance of the body.

And the judgment is creating a kind of suffering that is lived in the body but is not about the body.

The mechanism is teaching you that the body has to meet external standards. And the teaching is preventing you from being at home in the body.

## REFLECTION v30
---
I have noticed that self-worth in students is often built on something very unstable. The grades can change. The accomplishments can be displaced. The future can be different from the plan. The entire structure of worth is very fragile.

There is a mechanism that has built the entire sense of self on something that cannot be depended on. The mechanism is building on sand. The mechanism is creating a structure that will collapse when the foundation shifts.

What I have come to understand is that the mechanism is never actually secure. The mechanism is always vulnerable to the change. The mechanism is always at risk of the entire structure collapsing.

What I have come to think about is the way that the mechanism is preventing the development of a sense of worth that is actually stable. The mechanism is preventing the understanding that worth is inherent. The mechanism is preventing the sense that you are worth something simply because you exist.

The mechanism is building on something external. The mechanism is building on something that can be taken away. The mechanism is building on something that can change.

And the building is creating a sense of self that is very fragile.

The mechanism is teaching you that your worth comes from what you accomplish. And the teaching is preventing you from ever feeling secure in who you are.
