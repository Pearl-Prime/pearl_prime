## REFLECTION v01
---
What I notice is a particular quality of absence in the presence. I am teaching the lesson. I am managing the classroom. I am responding to students. But something fundamental is not there. The enthusiasm that used to animate the work has become absent. The sense of purpose that used to drive the work has become distant. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as presence-without-aliveness.

The mechanism is the capacity to show up and do the work while experiencing a kind of internal deadness. The work happens. The output is there. But the inner experience is flat. The work is happening but I am not particularly there for it.

What I have come to understand is that this mechanism can persist for a long time without being recognized. From the outside, everything appears normal. The work is being done. The students are being taught. But internally, the sense of being alive is absent.

I have noticed that the mechanism often makes the work feel like going through motions. I know what comes next. I execute it. But the execution is automatic, not engaged. The mechanism has converted teaching from an act of presence into a series of behaviors.

What the mechanism does is create a kind of invisibility about the absence. The work is visible. The absence is invisible. So for a long time, I might not recognize that something is fundamentally wrong.

The mechanism is the way that depression removes the sense of possibility from the future. The future is just more of the same. The future is just more of this flatness. The mechanism cannot access the sense that things could be different or better.

What I have come to understand is that this mechanism is particularly painful in a profession that is supposed to be about the future. Teaching is oriented toward growth and possibility. When depression removes the sense of possibility, the profession becomes fundamentally misaligned with how I am experiencing reality.

I have noticed that the mechanism often affects planning and hope. I do not make long-term plans because the future does not feel real. I do not invest in possibilities because the possibilities feel unlikely or unappealing.

What the mechanism does is collapse the timeline. I can barely imagine next week. I cannot imagine next year. I cannot imagine the trajectory of my career.
