## 0. What This Document Is

This guide governs the upgrade of STORY and SCENE atoms from structurally valid to psychologically specific. It does not change how atoms are assembled, what their role is, or how arcs are designed. It governs one thing: **how to make an atom feel like it was written by someone who has been in the room with this persona.**

A structurally valid atom passes lint. A Golden Phoenix atom creates recognition.

This document does NOT:
- Override PHOENIX_V4_5_WRITER_SPEC.md on any point
- Change STORY tense (third-person present remains canonical)
- Modify emotional band definitions
- Modify arc design rules
- Apply to Teacher Mode atoms (governed separately)

---

## 1. The Upgrade Problem

Template atoms typically pass structural checks and fail at experience.

**What passes lint but fails the reader:**

```
Maya sits at her desk. Her chest feels tight. She knows something is wrong
but she cannot name it. The weight of the day presses down. She breathes.
```

This has: emotional content, present tense, no resolution. It passes.

It fails because: no one who has been in this persona's situation recognizes themselves in it. "Chest feels tight" and "weight of the day" are placeholders for lived experience, not lived experience itself.

**Golden Phoenix equivalent:**

```
Maya's Slack inbox shows 47 unread messages. She stopped opening them
around noon. Her manager walked past her desk twice without making eye
contact. The laptop screen is too bright. She knows she should respond
to the thread about Thursday's review, but her hands aren't moving
toward the keyboard.
```

Same emotional band. Same structure. Completely different recognition coefficient.

## 2. Micro-Stakes — Definition and Application

A micro-stake is a small, socially meaningful signal that something is at risk. It is not a catastrophe. It is not dramatic. It is the specific kind of small thing that someone in this persona's life would feel in their body before their mind has processed it.

**Properties of a valid micro-stake:**
- Specific to the persona's environment (not universal)
- Recognizable to someone inside that world (not explained to someone outside it)
- A consequence or signal, not a feeling (the feeling follows from it)
- Present in the physical or social environment of the scene

**Not a micro-stake:** "She felt nervous." "He worried." "She sensed something was wrong."

**Valid micro-stakes by persona:**

### gen_z_professional
```
- Manager stops making eye contact during standup
- Slack message left on read by someone who usually responds in minutes
- Group chat quiet after a post that was supposed to land
- Calendar invite declined without a note
- LinkedIn notification that a peer got promoted
- The "seen" receipt on a message to a friend who hasn't replied in two days
- Performance review language changes from "strong" to "meeting expectations"
- Standing desk neighbors start lowering their desks around her without saying anything
```

### nyc_executive
```
- Investor pauses mid-sentence and checks their phone
- Bonus cycle memo arrives two weeks later than last year
- Board chair uses "I want to make sure we're aligned" for the third time in one meeting
- CFO declines a lunch invitation by email (previously accepted by text)
- Quarterly summary uses "cautious optimism" instead of "strong momentum"
- An introduction that was promised two weeks ago still hasn't come
- The deal room goes quiet when he enters
- Her name is not on the email thread it should be on
```

### healthcare_rn
```
- Charge nurse does not make eye contact at shift change
- Patient-to-nurse ratio goes from 4:1 to 6:1 without announcement
- Chart backlog at 11pm that was supposed to be cleared by 9
- A family member asks a question she has answered three times this shift
- Her badge scanner beeps wrong twice in a row
- The supply cart is missing what she needs for a procedure she's done a hundred times
- A colleague she trusts asks to switch a shift without explaining why
- Moral injury moment: she documents what happened, not what she saw
```

### gen_z_la (creative/cultural)
```
- A project she pitched gets greenlit for someone else without being told
- Her collaborator posts something without tagging her
- A party she expected to be invited to shows up in her feed
- The DM she sent three days ago is still unread
- A creator she admires doesn't follow back after she followed
- Her streak breaks on the app she's been using to track her habits
- The comment she hoped would get traction gets three likes
- The neighborhood she loves is getting a second juice bar and she doesn't know how to feel about that
```

## 3. Environmental Cues — What They Are and How to Use Them

An environmental cue grounds the atom in a physical or social location. It tells the reader where the character is without describing the setting.

**Properties:**
- Specific (not "office" but "open office, no dividers between her and the team")
- Sensory (not described analytically but present as sound, light, weight, temperature, object)
- Socially coded (the cue implies something about the character's status or position)

**Weak environmental cue:**
```
She is at work. The office is busy.
```

**Strong environmental cue:**
```
The printer is going. The person who sits closest to her is on a call with their camera on.
```

**Environmental cue inventory by persona:**

### gen_z_professional
```
Open-plan office, no assigned seating | Laptop on a kitchen table with roommate visible in background |
AirPods in as a social barrier | Slack on phone during commute | Team Zoom with two people on camera |
Standing desk in a space-sharing office | Co-working space with a hot-desking login that keeps expiring
```

### nyc_executive
```
Glass-walled conference room visible from the open floor | Car service with cell signal dropping in the tunnel |
Hotel lobby adjacent to a dinner that starts in 20 minutes | Board room table with water glasses and no food |
Flight delayed on a Wednesday | Building lobby at 6:45am before the elevators fill
```

### healthcare_rn
```
Nurses' station with one chair and four people | Supply closet with the light that flickers |
Break room with the microwave that runs two minutes slow | Patient hallway at 2am |
Locker room at end of a 12-hour shift | Parking garage alone after overnight
```

## 4. Social Presence — Peer and Hierarchy Signals

Most high-stakes emotional moments for these personas happen in social context. Isolation is context, not the default.

Every STORY atom should contain one of the following (when socially appropriate for the scene's emotional position):

- A peer who is present but not helping
- A superior who does not acknowledge something
- An absence that signals something
- A comparison loop (seeing someone else who is managing)
- A social norm being broken or enforced without words

These do not need to be named characters. They can be unnamed presences.

**Examples:**
```
The two people she came in with are now laughing at something she doesn't know.

His assistant scheduled the call without asking him first.

Everyone on the floor seems to know what's happening except her.

The other nurses finished charting. She hasn't started.
```

## 5. Language Texture — Matching the Persona's Register

Word choice must feel native to the persona's world, not imported from outside it.

### gen_z_professional
```
Use: thread, standup, sync, deck, capacity, bandwidth, ping, sidebar
Avoid: synergy, leverage (verb), circle back (ironic use only), "going forward"
Rhythm: Short sentences. Direct. Slight understatement before emotional peak.
```

### nyc_executive
```
Use: alignment, runway, optics, exposure, trajectory, board, fiduciary
Avoid: hustle culture language, vulnerability-as-performance, anything that sounds like it comes from a podcast
Rhythm: Measured. Longer clauses that compress into short conclusions.
```

### healthcare_rn
```
Use: charting, handoff, patient load, acuity, floor, shift, attending, rapid
Avoid: clinical jargon that reads as performative, overly corporate wellness language
Rhythm: Declarative. Practical. Fatigue in the phrasing, not named.
```

### gen_z_la
```
Use: specific platform references where appropriate, neighborhood references, cultural signal objects
Avoid: irony that ages (pop culture references with a short shelf life)
Rhythm: Observation before feeling. Trust the image.
```

## 6. Persona Specificity Score — Internal QA Standard

Each STORY and SCENE atom receives an internal persona specificity score from 0 to 1.0 at QA review. This is not automated at time of writing; it is a human reviewer judgment.

**Scoring criteria:**

| Criterion                        | Weight | Notes                                              |
|----------------------------------|--------|----------------------------------------------------|
| Micro-stake present and specific | 0.30   | Generic stakes score 0 on this criterion           |
| Environmental cue present        | 0.20   | Must be sensory or socially coded                  |
| Persona-native language          | 0.20   | Would this word choice pass a native speaker check |
| Social context present           | 0.15   | Peer, hierarchy, absence, or comparison signal     |
| No generic emotional placeholders| 0.15   | "felt overwhelmed" with no specific signal scores 0|

**Threshold for promotion:** 0.70 or above

**Atoms scoring below 0.70 remain `status: provisional_template`.**

Reviewer documents the score in atom metadata:

```yaml
persona_specificity_score: 0.72
score_notes: "Strong micro-stake (calendar invite declined). Environmental cue present. Slightly generic on social context."
```

## 7. Repetition Entropy — Scale Control

When a book contains 40–80+ atoms, the same micro-stake appearing repeatedly collapses the emotional range. Repetition entropy is the measure of micro-stake diversity across an atom pool.

**Rules across a full atom pool (per persona × topic):**

| Repetition type                        | Maximum allowed  |
|----------------------------------------|------------------|
| Same micro-stake (verbatim)            | 1 occurrence     |
| Same micro-stake category (e.g. "Slack message signal") | 2 occurrences |
| Same environmental cue                 | 2 occurrences    |
| Same emotional image or metaphor       | 1 occurrence     |
| Same social hierarchy signal           | 2 occurrences    |

**QA method:** Reviewer lists all micro-stakes from the atom pool, groups them by category, and flags duplicates for replacement before promotion.

## 8. Promotion Workflow

```
Atom written (may use Claude Prompt Spec as draft support)
        ↓
status: provisional_template (default for all new atoms)
        ↓
Reviewer evaluates:
  - Persona specificity score ≥ 0.70?
  - Micro-stake present and specific?
  - Environmental cue present?
  - Repetition entropy acceptable across pool?
  - Language texture matches persona?
        ↓
If any check fails → return to writer with specific note
        ↓
If all checks pass → reviewer sets:
  status: confirmed
  persona_specificity_score: [score]
        ↓
Atom eligible for production wave
```

No automated promotion. Human sign-off required at final step.

## 9. If Claude Assists in Draft Generation

Claude may be used to generate draft atoms under the Claude Prompt Spec. All Claude-generated atoms:

- Are automatically `status: provisional_template`
- Must pass the full promotion workflow before use in production
- Must be reviewed for persona specificity score by a human
- Must be checked for repetition entropy against the existing pool

Claude is a drafting tool, not a promotion authority.

## 10. Micro-Stake Research Process — Content Team Workflow

For each new persona × topic combination, the content team must produce a **micro-stakes research note** before atom writing begins.

**Micro-Stakes Research Note (template):**

```yaml
persona_id: {persona_id}
topic_id: {topic_id}
research_note_by: {content_team_member}

primary_environment:
  # Where does this persona encounter this topic? Be specific.
  # e.g. "Open-plan tech office, startup scale, no private offices"

daily_ritual_sites:
  # Where do they spend time? (commute, home, work, third places)

social_hierarchy_signals:
  # Who has power over them in this environment? How is that power signaled?

peer_comparison_loops:
  # Where do they compare themselves to others? What triggers it?

micro_stake_list:
  # Minimum 10 specific micro-stakes
  # These become the atom writer's source material
  - 
  - 
  - 

language_notes:
  # What words are native to this persona's world?
  # What words would immediately read as imported/external?
  native: []
  avoid: []

emotional_cost_note: >
  # 2–4 sentences on what this topic costs this persona specifically.
  # Not a clinical description. The felt experience.

approved_by:
status: draft | approved
```

**Content lead approves this note before atom writing begins.**
Atom writers use this note as their primary source material.

## 11. Atom Pool Targets by Duration Tier

These are the minimum confirmed (promoted) atom counts required before a production wave can run:

| Duration tier         | STORY atoms (confirmed) | SCENE atoms (confirmed) |
|-----------------------|-------------------------|-------------------------|
| Micro (9–11k)         | 20–30                   | 5–10                    |
| Standard (20–30k)     | 40–60                   | 10–20                   |
| Full-length (45–60k)  | 60–80                   | 20–40                   |
| Masterclass (70k+)    | 80–120                  | 40–60                   |

Provisional template atoms may supplement in development runs only.
