## 1. YouTube (Long-form + Shorts)

### 1.1 Algorithm & Discovery (2025-2026)

The YouTube algorithm has undergone significant shifts in 2025-2026 [1][2][3]:

| Signal | Weight (2025-2026) | Notes |
|--------|-------------------|-------|
| **Viewer satisfaction** | Primary | Replaced raw watch time as top signal. Surveys, sentiment, repeat views matter more [1] |
| **Channel-level evaluation** | High | Algorithm judges channel patterns, not individual video spikes [2] |
| **Session depth** | High | Whether viewers watch multiple videos in a session [1] |
| **Interaction quality** | Growing | Comments, replies, creator engagement valued over passive views [2] |
| **Long-term personalization** | Growing | Recommendations shaped by watch history, session habits over time [3] |
| **Completion rate** | High | 100% of an 8-min video > 40% of a 25-min video [1] |

**Key 2025 change:** YouTube fully decoupled the Shorts recommendation engine from long-form. Poor Shorts performance no longer drags down long-form recommendations [2].

**Inauthentic content policy (July 2025):** Mass-produced, template-like videos (slide shows with no commentary, verbatim readings) are ineligible for ads. Unique insights, personality, meaningful commentary remain monetizable [2].

### 1.3 YouTube Shorts vs Long-Form Strategy

The consensus is clear: use both in a strategic funnel [6][7]:

| Metric | Shorts | Long-form |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Daily views** | 200B+ (up from 70B in Mar 2024) | N/A |

| **Revenue per view** | ~3x lower than long-form | 3x higher than Shorts |

1. Shorts (3-5/week): Test hooks, quick tips, emotional hooks, myth-busting
2. Long-form (1-2/week): Deep dives on topics validated by Shorts performance
3. Track "hybrid health" — viewers who land on long video after watching a Short [7]

### 1.5 Animated/Illustrated vs Talking-Head Performance

A 2025 study in the *British Journal of Health Psychology* (n=358) directly compared formats [11]:

- **Animated vs talking-head received similar average ratings** for knowledge transfer
- **Almost half of participants didn't finish watching** either format — attention capture is the real challenge [11]
- **Whiteboard animation** beats talking head for attention, retention, and engagement according to neuroscience research [11]
- **Best practice 2025-2026:** Blend both — animated illustrations reinforce key concepts while narration/on-camera maintains human connection [11]
- **VTubers and AI-driven avatars** becoming mainstream in 2025; motion capture tools increasingly accessible [11]

## 2. TikTok

### 2.1 Algorithm (2025-2026)

TikTok's algorithm in 2025-2026 prioritizes watch time over views, rewards niche-specific content, and favors authentic human creators over AI-generated videos [13][14]:

| Signal | Weight | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Completion rate** | Strongest | >50% completion gets significantly more distribution [13] |
| **Watch time** | Primary | Evaluated across interest groups, not just individual [14] |
| **Rewatch ratio** | Growing | Signals deep engagement |
| **Content relevance** | Core | Prioritized over creator popularity (unlike IG/FB) [13] |
| **Metadata fit** | Important | Captions, hashtags, sounds, on-screen text must match content [13] |

**Distribution process:** Video shown to 200-500 non-followers matching topic area; performance determines next boost [14].

**FYP vs Search divergence:**
- FYP: Completion rate in first 24-48 hours; short videos generate signals faster [13]
- Search: Longer videos win — critical for wellness content where users search for specific advice [13]

### 2.2 Optimal Video Length for Wellness Content

| Content Type | Optimal Length | Notes |
|--------------|---------------|-------|
| Quick tips / motivational clips | 15-30 seconds | Entertainment-first |
| **Educational / wellness** | **30-60 seconds (up to 90s)** | **Core sweet spot for self-help** [13] |
| Storytelling / personal journeys | 60-90 seconds | Narrative depth |
| Deep dives / tutorials | Up to 3 minutes | Value-driven search content |
| Viral/entertainment | 11-18 seconds | Maximum completion rate |

Buffer analysis of 1.1M TikTok videos: Videos >60 seconds achieve 43% more reach than shorter videos [13].

**Key insight:** TikTok extended to 10-minute in-app recording and 60-minute uploads [15]. Long-form on TikTok is growing but completion rate remains king — a 20-second video with 80% completion beats a 2-minute video with 20% completion [13].

## 6. Therapeutic Video Research

### 6.1 Embedding Therapeutic Value Without Labeling It Therapy

Research on cinematherapy and therapeutic filmmaking reveals key principles [38]:

| Principle | Mechanism | Evidence |
|-----------|-----------|----------|
| **Third-person perspective** | Characters on screen face same problems as viewers; offers new coping approaches without direct confrontation [38] | Cinematherapy/video therapy research (PMC 2022) |
| **Intentional ambiguity** | "The work is intentionally ambiguous, so the population may not be intimidated by the stigma of a diagnosis" [38] | Veterans therapeutic filmmaking research |
| **Not labeling = engagement** | "The lack of pressure to call it 'therapy' opens up a space to allow for new and old healthy memories to form" [38] | Same source |
| **Common vocabulary** | Shared media creates shared reference points between viewer and their emotional experience [38] | Therapeutic alliance research |
| **Self-help via media** | "Just as books can help people learn and integrate therapeutic tools, so can films" [38] | Movie therapy literature |
| **Emotional distancing** | Digital animation allows emotional distancing through media while building rapport [38] | Youth digital art therapy research |

### 6.2 Breathing Entrainment Through Visual Pacing

Slow-paced breathing (SPB) research shows significant therapeutic benefits [39]:

| Finding | Data | Source |
|---------|------|--------|
| HRV improvement | 23 studies noted significant improvements [39] | Narrative review of breathwork (PMC 2025) |
| Heart rate decrease | Commonly observed across studies [39] | Same |
| Anxiety reduction | Reported in 10 studies [39] | Same |
| Mechanism | SPB increases vagus nerve activation (parasympathetic nervous system) [39] | Psychophysiology (Wiley 2022) |
| Brain effects | SPB induces oscillatory activity enhancing functional connectivity in emotion regulation networks [39] | Same |
| Visual biofeedback | Visualizing heart rate/HRV/respiratory signal during SPB enhances outcomes [39] | HRV biofeedback research |

**Application to video:** Mapping cuts per minute to breath rate (e.g., 6 cycles/minute = one cut every 10 seconds for visual breathing cue) is supported by the biofeedback literature showing that visual pacing of breathing enhances parasympathetic activation [39].

### 6.3 Color Grading for Emotional Regulation

Direct evidence from ASMR + color research (Applied Sciences, 2024) [40]:

| Visual Feature | Physiological Effect | Brain Activity |
|----------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **Darker, muted colors** + low-frequency sounds | Increased delta wave activity (frontal lobe); enhanced parasympathetic activity (higher HRV) [40] | Greater relaxation and emotional calm |
| **Brighter elements** + mid/high-frequency sounds | Increased beta wave activity (frontal lobe); modest HRV improvement [40] | Heightened cognitive engagement and positive arousal |

**Practical implication:** LUT-based warm-to-cool color arcs in video can be designed to guide emotional regulation — starting with warmer/brighter tones (engagement) and transitioning to cooler/muted tones (relaxation) [40].

### 6.4 ASMR-Adjacent Techniques & Parasympathetic Response

| Technique | Effect | Data |
|-----------|--------|------|
| **ASMR video viewing** | Heart rate reduction of -3.41 bpm [41] | Comparable to music-based stress reduction in cardiovascular disease trials |
| **ASMR vs nature videos** | ASMR caused greater pulse rate reduction than nature videos [40] | Neuroscience of Consciousness (2025) |
| **ASMR + binaural beats** | Binaural beat (6 Hz theta) combined with ASMR most effective for inducing sleep brain signals [40] | Lee et al. (2019) |
| **Skin conductance** | Increased during ASMR (indicates emotional engagement alongside relaxation) [41] | PLOS ONE (Poerio et al., 2018) |

### 6.6 Lofi Girl / ChilledCow Calming Effects

The Lofi Girl phenomenon demonstrates how animation achieves therapeutic calm [43]:

| Technique | Implementation | Psychological Effect |
|-----------|---------------|---------------------|
| **Muted color palette** | Analogous cool colors (blue, pink, purple) for nighttime/rain | Reduces visual stimulation [43] |
| **Warm lighting contrast** | Desk lamps, glowing screens against cool backgrounds | Draws focus, enhances coziness [43] |
| **Comforting domestic details** | Sleeping pets, houseplants, solitary activities (reading, studying) | Creates sense of safety [43] |
| **Nostalgic analog filters** | Film grain, VHS static, soft focus | Evokes pre-digital nostalgia [43] |
| **Anime slice-of-life tone** | 1990s-2000s Japanese animation background art style | Quiet moments, detailed environments [43] |
| **80-second looping animation** | Continuous 24/7 repetitive loop | Meditative quality; predictability mirrors looping beats [43] |
| **"Body doubling" effect** | Non-intrusive companion studying alongside viewer | Reduces isolation; improves focus [43] |

**Key insight:** Lo-fi art is designed to complement calming music by creating visual atmosphere — the primary goal is emotional, not informational [43].

### 6.7 Nature Footage Integration

| Finding | Effect Size | Source |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Nature videos increase RSA (respiratory sinus arrhythmia) and decrease heart rate [44] | Robust relaxation response | Frontiers in Psychology (2022) |
| Nature videos increase alpha and theta brain waves [44] | Calm + focused states (similar to meditation) | Frontiers in Psychology (2022) |
| Nature exposure reduces anxious arousal (panic) and anxious apprehension (worry) [44] | Significant over several weeks | Scientific Reports (2023) |
