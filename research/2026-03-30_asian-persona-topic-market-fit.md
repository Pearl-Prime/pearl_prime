# Asian Persona-Topic Market Fit Research

**Date:** 2026-03-30
**Researcher:** pearl_research
**Scope:** Market data, demand evidence, platform intelligence, and cultural framing for 6 locales (zh-TW, zh-HK, zh-CN, zh-SG, ja-JP, ko-KR)

---

## Executive Summary

The Asian self-help/wellness audiobook market represents a convergence of acute mental health crises and rapidly growing digital audio consumption. Key findings:

- **China's sleep economy** alone is projected to exceed RMB 1 trillion (~$140B) by 2030 [1], with 500M+ people experiencing sleep disorders [2]
- **Japan** sleeps the least among all OECD nations (7h42m avg) [3] and recorded 1,057 work-related mental health disorder cases in FY2024 -- the first time exceeding 1,000 [4]
- **Korea's** digital mental health market is projected to reach $3.6B by 2035 (18.55% CAGR) [5], with suicide remaining the #1 cause of death for ages 10-39 [6]
- **Hong Kong** saw burnout nearly double YoY to 49% of the workforce [7], with 46% of adults showing depression/anxiety symptoms [8]
- **Singapore** Gen Z reports 68% burnout rate -- highest of any generation [9]
- **Taiwan's** insomnia prevalence has risen to ~40% of adults [10], with mental health disorder prevalence doubling over 20 years [11]

### 1.2 Top 3 Topics with Local Demand Evidence

#### 1. Sleep Anxiety (失眠/睡眠焦慮) -- `sleep_repair_tw`

- **~40% of Taiwanese adults experience poor sleep**, up from 25% in earlier surveys [10]
- Insomniacs have 8.83x higher risk of anxiety and 8.48x higher risk of depression [16]

- **Cultural angle:** Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) sleep remedies are gaining traction alongside modern approaches [13]

#### 2. Burnout Recovery (職業倦怠) -- `stabilizer_tw`

- Taiwan's high-pressure work and education culture drives persistent burnout
- CMD prevalence doubled from 11.5% (1990) to 23.8% (2010), paralleling rises in unemployment and divorce [11]
- Middle-aged adults showed worst mental health outcomes linked to work stress [18]
- **Cultural angle:** "Work-life balance" (工作與生活平衡) is the preferred framing over direct burnout terminology

#### 3. Gen Z Mental Health (青年心理健康) -- `gen_z_grounding_tw`

- COVID-19 induced stress affected 45.4% of surveyed Taiwanese [19]

- High-pressure education system identified as key driver [12]
- Digital mental health tools and Mandarin-language wellness chatbots gaining traction [13]

- Insurance rarely covers psychotherapy for children/adolescents [44]

### 5.2 Research Priority: Karoshi & Burnout (過労死/燃え尽き症候群)

- **1,057 work-related mental health disorder cases** recognized in FY2024 -- first time exceeding 1,000 [4]
- **89 involved suicide or attempted suicide** [4]
- Record **3,780 employees filed mental health compensation claims** -- nearly 3x the 308 cases from 15 years ago [58]
- **>60% cite "relationships with people"** (especially supervisor conflicts) as leading cause [58]
- Work Style Reform Act (2018) capped overtime at 45hrs/month, but **>16% of businesses exceed this** [4]
- Japan has the **lowest productivity among G7 nations** despite long hours -- the "overwork paradox" [60]

### 5.3 Research Priority: Sleep Deprivation

- **7h42m average sleep** -- shortest among 33 OECD countries (vs 8h22m OECD average) [3]
- **38.5% of men and 43.6% of women** sleep less than 6 hours nightly [61]
- Estimated **¥18 trillion economic impact** from lost productivity due to sleep deprivation (2025) [60]
- **"Social jet lag"** study found ¥1 trillion annual economic loss [62]
- Women bear **4.76 hours more housework/child-rearing** -- one of the largest gender gaps in developed nations [63]
- University students average only **6 hours of sleep** (vs 7+ hours in other countries) [64]

### 5.4 Research Priority: Hikikomori & Social Withdrawal

- **1.46 million people** living as hikikomori (2023 Cabinet Office survey) [59]
- Expert Saito Tamaki suggests true figure may reach **10 million** [65]
- **600,000+ aged 40-64** -- the "80-50 problem" (elderly parents caring for middle-aged recluses) [59]
- **52.3% of hikikomori aged 40-64 are women** -- challenging the male-dominated stereotype [59]
- COVID-19 significantly increased numbers through normalized isolation [59]
- Hikikomori is **not a diagnosis but a condition** -- definition challenges affect policy [66]

**Key insight:** Stigmatizing attitudes toward mental health in Japan are **stronger than in Taiwan or Australia** [68]. The culturally preferred approach is to frame mental wellness through physical health, lifestyle management, and self-development rather than psychological/psychiatric terminology. Successful reframing precedent: schizophrenia was renamed from 精神分裂症 to 統合失調症 (integration disorder) in 2002, significantly improving public perception [68].
