## 6. Therapeutic Duration Science

### 6.1 Breathing Exercises — Minimum Effective Duration

| Duration | Effect | Evidence Level | Source |
|----------|--------|---------------|--------|
| **5 minutes** | Significant HRV increase (acute effect) | RCT; Frontiers in Psychology | [16] |
| 5–10 minutes | Daily practice recommendation | Multiple studies | [16] |
| 10 minutes | Commonly used research protocol | Multiple RCTs | [16] |
| **20 minutes daily x 4 weeks** | Sustained parasympathetic increase + reduced sympathetic activity | RCT, N=randomized | [16] |

**Optimal breathing rate:** 5.5–6 breaths per minute (resonance frequency ~0.1 Hz) [16]

**Key finding:** A single 5-minute slow-paced breathing session produces **mostly significant effects on HRV** — no biofeedback device required. [16]

### 6.2 Guided Visualization — Minimum for Parasympathetic Activation

| Duration | Effect | Source |
|----------|--------|--------|
| **10–20 minutes** (Benson's Relaxation Response) | Full parasympathetic relaxation | [17] |
| **15 minutes** | Powerful effect on parasympathetic reactivation | [17] |
| **20 minutes** | Significant relaxation via PMR, deep breathing, or guided imagery | [17] |
| Single guided imagery session | Facilitates self-forgiveness, reduced physiological stress | [17] |

**Benson's recommendation:** Practice twice daily, 10–20 minutes each time. [17]

### 6.3 Bibliotherapy — Minimum Reading Time for Emotional Processing

No specific minimum reading time threshold has been established in the literature. [30]

| Finding | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Effective format | Weekly reading + 5-min phone call can replace standard therapy | [30] |
| Mechanisms | Identification, catharsis, insight, universalization | [30] |
| Effect size (depression) | d = 0.77 (comparable to psychotherapy d = 0.75) | [30] |
| Long-term effects | Sustained reduction in depressive symptoms over 3 months–3 years (adults) | [30] |
| Key limitation | "Difficult to accurately assess therapeutic outcomes within short timeframes" | [30] |

**Implication:** Reading even a short self-help chapter (5–10 pages) may trigger recognition and reframing processes, but robust emotional processing likely requires sustained engagement across multiple sessions.

|---------|----------|--------|

| Attention residue after task-switching | **15–23 minutes** | APA research [43] |
| Time to return to original task after distraction | **23 min 15 sec** | Gloria Mark research [43] |
| Stronger residue with | Emotionally charged or unfinished tasks | Leroy 2009 [43] |

**Therapeutic implication:** After engaging with therapeutic content, the emotional/cognitive effects persist for **15–23 minutes**. This means a 5-minute breathing exercise continues to affect the user for 20+ minutes total. Content designers should leverage this "afterglow" window. [43]
