## 1. Manga de Wakaru (漫画でわかる) Self-Help Manga Series

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total volumes (Japan) | 50+ |
| English translations | 14 |
| Chinese translations | 49 |
| Pages per volume | ~236 pages (confirmed for Vol. 1: Statistics) |
| Format | Tankoubon, ~B6 size |
| Structure | Manga narrative chapters alternating with text explanation sections |

**Subjects include:** Statistics, Calculus, Biochemistry, Databases, Cryptography, Physics, Electricity, Linear Algebra, Relativity, Regression Analysis, and more.

**Key insight for production:** Each volume is self-contained at ~236 pages, combining manga story with educational breakdowns. The manga portions are estimated at ~60% of page count (~140 pages manga, ~96 pages text/diagrams).

The most directly relevant "manga de wakaru" series for self-help/mental health.

| Author | Yu Yuuki (psychiatrist) / Illustrator: Sou |
| Volumes | 32 tankoubon (as of August 2025) |
| Serialization | Young King Ours (Shonen Gahosha), from May 2010; also online at Yu Mental Clinic website since 2008 |
| Format | Short episodic chapters, each covering one psychosomatic/mental health topic |

| Anime adaptation | 20 episodes x 5 minutes each (ONA, 2015) |

**Key insight:** This is the gold standard for "self-help manga" -- a psychiatrist writing comedic short-form manga about specific mental health conditions. 32 volumes proves massive commercial viability. Short chapter format (8-16 pages) makes each topic digestible.

### 2.2 Detailed Chapter/Volume Breakdowns

**Yotsuba&!**
- 7 chapters per volume (standard); each chapter = one day in Yotsuba's life
- Chapter length: 20-30 pages
- Volume length: 200-250 pages
- Pacing: Each chapter covers a single day, nearly sequential. This creates a gentle, episodic rhythm
- Therapeutic mechanism: The "endless summer day" feeling; wonder at ordinary life through a child's eyes
- Sales: One of the best-selling manga in Japan; multiple Eisner nominations
- English publisher: Yen Press

**Laid-Back Camp (Yuru Camp)**
- 6 chapters per volume (consistent across all volumes)
- Chapter length: ~26 pages (confirmed from volume page breakdowns: chapters start at pages 003, 029, 055, 081, 107, 133)
- Volume length: ~160 pages
- 100th chapter milestone celebrated with campaign
- Therapeutic mechanism: Detailed camping scenes with real locations; food preparation sequences; wide landscape panels
- Bonus Volume 13.5 given to film theatergoers
- 10th anniversary in 2025

**March Comes in Like a Lion (3-gatsu no Lion)**
- 10-15 chapters per volume (short chapters)
- Recent chapters: 10-12 pages each (getting shorter over time)
- Volume length: ~170 pages
- Magazine chapters sometimes combined in volume releases
- **Explicitly deals with depression**: protagonist Rei Kiriyama's isolation, grief, and gradual healing
- Awards: Manga Taisho (2011), Kodansha Award (2011), Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize Grand Prize (2014)
- 3 million+ copies in circulation
- Author plans volume 19 as final volume
- Therapeutic mechanism: Slow pacing mirrors depression recovery; shogi matches as metaphor for internal struggle; the Kawamoto sisters' warmth as chosen family

**Flying Witch**
- Monthly serialization = longer chapters (~20-40 pages)
- ~5-6 chapters per volume
- Therapeutic mechanism: Rural Aomori setting; quiet magic integrated into daily life; seasonal rhythms

**Non Non Biyori**
- 7-8 chapters per volume
- ~15-20 pages per chapter
- 180 pages per volume (confirmed from English editions)
- Therapeutic mechanism: Rural countryside (inaka) setting; childhood nostalgia; nature observation

**Sweetness and Lightning**
- ~5 chapters per volume (61 chapters / 12 volumes)
- ~30-36 pages per chapter (longer chapters)
- Each chapter includes a real recipe readers can cook
- Therapeutic mechanism: Cooking as healing from grief (widowed father raising daughter); food as connection

### 2.3 Iyashikei Pacing Principles (Production-Relevant)

1. **Decompressed time**: Wide panels, landscape shots, moments of silence (1-3 panels with no dialogue)
2. **Episodic structure**: Each chapter is self-contained; no cliffhangers
3. **Borderless "bleed" panels**: Used for nature/landscape moments to create breathing space
4. **Kishotenketsu rhythm**: Introduction > Development > Twist > Conclusion per chapter, but the "twist" is gentle (a funny moment, a small discovery)
5. **Real-world anchoring**: Real locations (Yuru Camp), real recipes (Sweetness & Lightning), real seasonal markers
6. **No antagonist**: Conflict comes from daily life, not villains

## 8. Production Planning: Minimum Viable Content Units

### 8.1 By Platform and Genre

| Content Type | Platform | Minimum Viable Episode | Minimum Viable Series |
|-------------|----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Wellness gag strip | Webtoon Canvas | **5 panels** (Sarah's Scribbles model) | 8+ episodes |
| Self-help short-form | Webtoon Canvas | **12 panels** | 8+ episodes |
| Therapy education (Comical Psychosomatic Medicine model) | Print manga / Web | **8-16 pages** per chapter | 1 volume (8-10 chapters) |
| Iyashikei chapter manga | Print / Web | **20-30 pages** per chapter | 1 volume (6-7 chapters, ~160-200 pages) |
| Self-help book adaptation | Print manga | **187 pages** single volume (KonMari model) | 1 volume minimum |
| Graphic memoir (mental health) | Print / Web | **142 pages** (Nagata Kabi model) | 1 volume minimum |
| Psychology manhwa (Dr. Frost model) | Webtoon | **40-60 panels** per episode | 25+ episodes (Kakao contract minimum) |
| Webtoon Originals wellness | Webtoon Originals | **12-40 panels** per episode | Season of 20-25 episodes |

### 8.2 Proven Commercial Models

| Model | Example | Unit Size | Success Metric |
|-------|---------|----------|---------------|
| Ultra-short wellness strip | Sarah's Scribbles | 5 panels, 2x/week | 64M views, 4 Goodreads Choice Awards |
| Short gag + education | Comical Psychosomatic Medicine | 8-16 pages/chapter | 32 volumes, anime adaptation |
| Self-help book adaptation | KonMari Manga | 187 pages, 1 volume | #1 NYT Bestseller |
| Iyashikei weekly | Yotsuba&! | 20-30 pages/chapter, monthly | 16 volumes, 3,200+ total pages, perennial bestseller |
| Psychology case-study | Dr. Frost | ~40-60 panels/episode, weekly | 251 episodes over 10 years, TV drama adaptation |
| Autobio mental health | Nagata Kabi | 142 pages, 1 volume | Harvey Award, spawned 5-volume series |
| Long-form healing | March Comes in Like a Lion | 10-20 pages/chapter | Manga Taisho + Tezuka Prize, 3M+ copies |

### 8.3 Optimal Production Targets (Recommended)

Based on the data above, the recommended content units for a wellness/self-help visual media production system:

**Tier 1: Micro-content (Social/Discovery)**
- 3-5 panels per episode
- Vertical scroll format (800px wide)
- 2-3x per week posting cadence
- Model: Sarah's Scribbles / Bluechair
- Total season: 50-100 episodes = 150-500 panels

**Tier 2: Short-form educational (Platform Series)**
- 12-20 panels per episode
- Vertical scroll format
- Weekly posting cadence
- Model: Comical Psychosomatic Medicine adapted to webtoon
- Total season: 25 episodes = 300-500 panels

**Tier 3: Chapter manga (Print + Digital)**
- 20-30 pages per chapter
- Traditional manga layout OR vertical scroll
- Monthly or biweekly cadence
- Model: Yotsuba&! / Laid-Back Camp pacing
- Total volume: 6-7 chapters = 140-200 pages

**Tier 4: Complete work (Book/Graphic Novel)**
- 142-256 pages single volume
- Traditional page layout
- One-shot or limited series
- Model: KonMari Manga / Nagata Kabi
- Total: 1 volume at 142-256 pages
