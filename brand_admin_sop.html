Brand Admin SOP

What you do each week. The system gives you the files — you upload them.

How This Works

Each week, Pearl Prime generates your upload batch. You receive platform-specific CSV files — one for Google Play, one for Amazon KDP, one for Findaway. Each CSV has the titles, descriptions, metadata, keywords, categories, and pricing already set. Your job is to upload them to each platform, confirm they go live, and report back.

Receive Your Weekly Batch
Weekly ▶

What You Get From Pearl Prime

The system generates everything. You receive:

  • Google Play CSV — audiobook titles, descriptions, metadata, keywords, categories, pricing (USD). Plus the MP3 audio files.
  • Amazon KDP CSV — ebook titles (DIFFERENT titles from audiobooks). These are workbooks, guides, journals — not audio transcripts. Plus the EPUB files and cover images.
  • Findaway CSV — same audiobook titles as Google Play, formatted for Findaway Voices / INaudio. Uses the MP3s you download from Google Play after approval.
The system decides what ships each week based on the release wave schedule and platform caps. You don't pick which books — you upload what the system gives you.

Wait for Google Play → Download MP3s
Day 2–3 ▶

What To Do

  • Google Play processes uploads in 24–48 hours
  • Check back next day — look for Approved or Rejected status
  • Download the approved MP3 files — you need these for Findaway
  • If any titles rejected: note the reason and report to Nihala immediately
Google's content moderation is automated. If a title gets rejected, it's usually a metadata keyword trigger — words like "therapy," "treatment," or "diagnosis" in the description. The system avoids these, but flag anything that slips through.

What To Do

  • Enter metadata from the KDP CSV (title, description, keywords, categories, pricing)

KDP Ebooks Are DIFFERENT Products From Audiobooks Amazon gets exclusive ebook workbooks, journals, and guides — NOT audio transcripts. The KDP CSV has completely different titles from the Google Play CSV. This is by design.

Why KDP Select

  • Kindle Unlimited access (4.8M subscribers — KU borrows often 3× sales in non-fiction)
  • 70% royalty in ALL 13 Kindle stores including Japan, India, Brazil
  • Kindle Countdown Deals + 5 free promo days per 90-day cycle
  • Amazon Ads perform 40% better inside KDP Select
  • 90-day exclusivity costs us nothing — ebooks are Amazon-only by design

Upload Cap

1–2 ebook titles per week. Stagger releases — no bulk dumps.

Cultural Calibration Check
Ongoing ▶

You Are the Cultural Gate

The system generates content, but you verify it lands correctly in Japanese culture. Check every batch for:

  • Seasonal resonance — cherry blossom (spring renewal), summer festivals (community), autumn reflection (mono no aware 物の哀れ), winter solitude (introspection). Content should feel seasonally appropriate.
  • Politeness register — 敬語 (keigo) in all copy. Formal, respectful tone.
  • Banned framing — no "mental health" (精神的健康), no "therapy" (セラピー), no "diagnosis" (診断). The nervous system / somatic framing IS the positioning.
  • Karoshi positioning — 過労死 (death by overwork) resonates deeply. Use it as a framing device, not a scare tactic.
  • Nature imagery — Mount Kurama, Kyoto temples, seasonal references work well in titles and descriptions.
  • Dialogue format — for audiobooks, dialogue-style narration performs exceptionally well in Japan (ref: 嫌われる勇気 "The Courage to Be Disliked")

Checklist

Seasonal alignment verified for this week's batch
Keigo register confirmed in all metadata
No banned clinical terms found
Manga reading direction correct (right-to-left)
