# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 7: Digital Wellness - Unf*ck Your Relationship with Technology

### You're Not Addicted, You're Engineered
Your phone isn't a tool anymore. It's a slot machine designed by people with PhDs in addiction.

Every app is optimized to hijack your dopamine system.
You're not weak. You're up against billions in behavioral psychology research.

### The Reality Check
Track your screen time right now. Look at it.

Ask yourself:
- If I spent this time on literally anything else, where would I be?
- Am I using my phone, or is it using me?
- What am I actually avoiding when I pick it up?

### The Harm Reduction Approach
(Because "just delete social media" is unrealistic for most)

**Level 1: Damage Control**
- Grayscale mode (Settings → Accessibility → Display)
- Delete apps, keep browser versions (extra friction)
- Turn off ALL notifications except calls/texts
- Screen time limits (but actually respect them)

**Level 2: Intentional Use**
- Morning rule: Don't touch phone for first 30 minutes awake
- Evening rule: No screens 1 hour before bed
- Bathroom rule: No phone in bathroom (seriously, try it)

**Level 3: Digital Sabbath**
- One full day per week: no social media
- Start with 12 hours if a full day feels impossible
- Plan what you'll do instead BEFORE starting

### The Unfollow/Mute/Block Protocol

**Unfollow if:**
- They make you feel worse about yourself
- You compare yourself to them constantly
- They're selling you something (lifestyle, body, success)
- You don't actually know them or care about their life

**Mute if:**
- You need to keep them for social reasons but don't want the content
- They're in your life IRL but exhausting online

**Block if:**
- They're harassing you
- Your mental health requires it
- You don't owe anyone access to you

### Scripts for Digital Boundaries

**When someone asks why you're not on [platform]:**
"I'm taking a break for my mental health."
(You don't owe more explanation than that)

**When you miss a message:**
"Sorry, I don't check messages constantly anymore. Here for emergencies."

**When FOMO hits:**
"I'm not missing out. I'm opting out. There's a difference."

### The Comparison Trap
You're comparing your:
- Behind-the-scenes to their highlight reel
- Worst day to their best moment  
- First draft to their final edit
- Reality to their performance

**The Reminder:**
Everyone you're comparing yourself to is also comparing themselves to someone else. It's comparison turtles all the way down.

### Creating Instead of Consuming

**The 1:3 Rule:**
For every 1 hour of consuming content, spend at least 20 minutes creating something:
- Write
- Draw
- Make music
- Build something
- Cook
- Move your body

Your brain needs output, not just input.

### The Doom Scroll Detox

**When you catch yourself scrolling:**
1. Stop mid-scroll
2. Ask: "What am I actually looking for right now?"
3. Close the app
4. Do literally anything else for 5 minutes
5. Notice if you still want to scroll

Usually, you don't.

### Making Tech Work FOR You

**Curate Ruthlessly:**
- Follow accounts that teach you skills
- Join communities solving problems you care about
- Subscribe to creators making the world better
- Block/mute everything else

**Use Tools:**
- Freedom app (blocks apps/sites)
- Forest app (gamifies not using phone)
- Opal (screen time tracker that actually helps)
- One Sec (adds friction before opening apps)

### The Weekly Digital Audit
Every Sunday:
1. Check screen time report
2. Identify top 3 time-suck apps
3. Ask: "Did this serve me or was I serving it?"
4. Adjust for next week

### When Tech Use Becomes a Real Problem
Signs you need professional help:
- Can't sleep without scrolling for hours
- Panic when phone isn't nearby
- Missing IRL obligations for online activities
- Using tech to escape reality completely
- Screen time over 8 hours/day consistently

**Resources:**
- Center for Humane Technology
- Digital Detox resources
- Therapy specializing in tech addiction

### The Uncomfortable Truth
Every minute you spend scrolling is a minute you're not:
- Building the life you actually want
- Connecting with people IRL
- Developing skills that matter to you
- Being present in your own life

You don't get those minutes back.

Choose accordingly.
