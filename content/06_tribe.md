# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 6: Tribe - Build Your People Intentionally

### The Loneliness Epidemic Is Real
You're the most "connected" generation ever.
You're also the loneliest.

400 Instagram followers ≠ community
Being in 12 group chats ≠ intimacy
Having a Discord server ≠ support system

### What Actually Constitutes Your Tribe

**Level 1: Safe People**
They don't drain you. You can be yourself. No performance required.

**Level 2: Growth People**  
They challenge you, support you, call you on your BS lovingly.

**Level 3: Ride-or-Die**
3am emergency? They show up. No explanation needed. Rare AF.

You need all three levels. You don't need 100 of them.

### The Audit
Look at your last 10 text conversations.

Ask yourself:
- Do I feel better or worse after talking to this person?
- Is this relationship reciprocal or one-sided?
- Am I being authentic or performing?
- Would I want to know them in 5 years?

If the answer is consistently negative, you have permission to adjust.

### Boundary Scripts for Energy Vampires

**The Complainer (never takes advice):**
"I hear you're struggling. I'm not equipped to help with this repeatedly. Have you considered talking to a therapist?"

**The Taker (only reaches out when they need something):**
"I'm tapped out right now. I can't take this on."

**The Critic (disguised as 'just being honest'):**
"I didn't ask for feedback. If I need your opinion, I'll ask."

**The Ghost (inconsistent, unreliable):**
"I need people who show up consistently. I'm investing my energy elsewhere."

### How to Find Your Actual People

**IRL Spaces:**
- Classes/workshops in something you actually like
- Volunteer for causes you care about
- Local meetups (yes, they still exist)
- Gym/climbing gym/martial arts (shared suffering bonds)
- Bookstores, coffee shops (be a regular)

**Online → Offline:**
- Discord communities with IRL meetups
- Reddit local subreddits
- Bumble BFF (yes, really)
- Eventbrite for niche interests

**The Approach:**
"Hey, I'm [name]. I'm trying to make friends IRL like a person from 2003. Want to [specific activity]?"

### Friendship Maintenance (Because Nobody Taught Us)

**Weekly:**
- Check in with at least 2 people (not transactional, just "thinking of you")

**Monthly:**  
- Plan something IRL with at least 1 person
- Even if it's just coffee. Especially if it's just coffee.

**When They Share Something Hard:**
Don't say: "At least..." or "Everything happens for a reason"
Do say: "That sucks. I'm here." (Then be quiet and listen)

### The Vulnerability Equation
Intimacy = vulnerability × time × consistency

You can't speedrun deep friendships.
But you can:
- Share something real
- Show up consistently  
- Give it time

### Scripts for Making the First Move

**Following up after meeting someone:**
"Hey! I really enjoyed talking about [specific thing]. Would you want to grab coffee/walk/[activity] sometime?"

**Deepening an existing friendship:**
"I've been thinking about how we mostly just text. Want to actually hang out?"

**Expressing appreciation:**
"I just wanted to say I really value our friendship. You've been there for me and it matters."

### When Your Tribe Shrinks (And That's Okay)
Not everyone is meant to stay in your life forever.

Some people were for a season.
Some outgrow you.  
You outgrow some.

This is normal. This is healthy.

**The Script:**
"I'm grateful for what this friendship was. I'm releasing what it no longer is."

### Building Chosen Family
If your biological family is toxic/absent/unsafe:
- You get to build a chosen family
- Blood doesn't obligate you to endure harm
- Family is about who shows up, not DNA

**Resources:**
- r/raisedbynarcissists
- Adult Children of Alcoholics (ACA)
- LGBTQ+ centers (even if you're not LGBTQ+, they get chosen family)

### The Final Word on Tribe
Quality >> Quantity
Reciprocity >> Performance  
Authenticity >> Approval

Three real friends > 300 followers

Build accordingly.
