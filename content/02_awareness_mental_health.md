# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 2: Awareness - Mental Health Reality Check

### The Truth About Your Mental Health
You're not broken. The world is legitimately overwhelming right now. Climate anxiety isn't irrational—the planet is literally on fire. Economic anxiety isn't weakness—you're watching housing become unaffordable in real time. Social anxiety makes sense when algorithms are literally designed to make you feel inadequate.

### What Actually Helps: Naming It
**Script for Anxiety:**
"My nervous system is responding to real threats. I'm not overreacting. AND I can learn to regulate even when external circumstances don't change."

**Script for Depression:**
"My brain chemistry is affected by systemic burnout, chronic stress, and legitimate grief about the state of the world. I deserve support, not shame."

**Script for Overwhelm:**
"I'm being asked to process more information in a day than humans used to encounter in a lifetime. My brain is doing its best."

### The Self-Compassion Check
Before you try to "fix" yourself, ask:
- Am I actually struggling, or am I just human?
- Is this a me-problem or a society-problem?
- What would I say to my best friend right now?

### Red Flags - When to Get Professional Help
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide (988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
- Can't get out of bed for days
- Substance use to cope is escalating
- Dissociating/losing time regularly
- Panic attacks interfering with daily life

There's no shame in therapy. Your brain is an organ. Sometimes organs need medical help.

### Daily Practice: Reality Check-In
Set a phone reminder for 2pm every day:
1. How am I actually feeling? (Name it)
2. Is this mine to carry? (What's external vs internal)
3. What's one small thing I can control right now?

Takes 60 seconds. Changes everything.
