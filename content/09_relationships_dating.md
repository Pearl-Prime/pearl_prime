# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 9: Relationships & Dating - Navigate Connection in the Digital Age

### The Dating Apocalypse Is Real
Dating apps turned people into products.
Everyone's shopping for someone better.
Intimacy is scary when you've been trained to swipe.

This isn't just you. The system is broken here too.

### What You're Actually Looking For

Not "The One" (that's Disney nonsense).
You're looking for: Someone who's emotionally available, shares your values, you're actually compatible with, and willing to do the work.

That's it. The rest is chemistry + choice.

### The Self-Work Before Dating

**Red Flags in YOU to Address:**
- Anxious attachment → Work with therapist before serious relationships
- Unhealed trauma → Will repeat patterns until you address it
- Poor boundaries → Learn to say no before learning to say yes
- Codependency → Learn to be whole alone first

You don't need to be perfect to date.
But you do need to be actively working on your shit.

### The Dating App Reality

**What Works:**
- Good photos (clear face, doing activities, with friends)
- Bio that's specific (not "I like travel and tacos")
- Message with something from their profile
- Ask to meet within 5-7 messages

**What Doesn't:**
- "Hey" (they get 50 of these)
- Complaining in bio
- Only group photos
- Texting for weeks before meeting

**The Truth:**
- It's a numbers game  
- Most people are window shopping
- Ghosting is normal (shitty, but normal)
- You need thick skin

**Better Approach:**
Meet people IRL doing things you actually like. Dating apps as supplement, not main strategy.

### First Date Scripts

**Setting up:**
"I've enjoyed talking. Want to grab coffee this week? I'm free [day] or [day]."
(Specific > "sometime")

**During the date:**
Ask: "What are you passionate about right now?"
Not: "What do you do?" (boring, capitalist)

**Ending if you're interested:**
"I had a really good time. I'd like to see you again."
(Direct > games)

**Ending if you're not:**
"I appreciate you taking the time. I don't think we're a match, but I wish you well."
(Honest > ghosting)

### Reading the Red Flags

**Run if they:**
- Love-bomb early (intense, too fast, overwhelming)
- Disrespect boundaries when you set them
- Trash all their exes (you'll be next)
- Can't take accountability (everything is someone else's fault)
- Check your phone, isolate you from friends, jealous early
- Push physical stuff before you're ready

**Yellow flags (investigate further):**
- Bad relationship with family (might be their family's fault)
- Never been in long relationship (might just be young)
- Different political values (depends on which ones)

### Green Flags (The Good Stuff)

- Communicates clearly
- Takes accountability when wrong
- Respects your no
- Has their own life/interests
- Introduces you to friends/family
- Shows up consistently
- Makes plans, keeps them
- Can handle conflict without yelling/stonewalling

### The Situationship Trap

You're "hanging out" for months but not defining it.
You're basically dating but they won't call it that.
You're available for them but they keep options open.

**The Script:**
"I like you. I want to date exclusively. If that's not what you want, I need to know so I can move on."

If they waffle, that's your answer. Leave.

### Fighting Fair in Relationships

**Don't:**
- Bring up past resolved issues
- Call names
- Threaten breakup unless you mean it
- Stonewall (shut down completely)
- Make it about who's right

**Do:**
- Use "I feel X when Y" (not "You always Z")
- Take breaks if escalating (20 min, come back)
- Focus on the current issue
- Apologize when wrong (really apologize, not "I'm sorry you feel that way")
- Seek to understand, not to win

### The Compatibility Questions

Before getting serious, discuss:
- Kids (want them? how many? timeline?)
- Money (how do you handle it? who pays for what?)
- Location (where do you want to live?)
- Religion/values (compatible worldviews?)
- Conflict style (how do you fight?)
- Life goals (where do you see yourself in 5 years?)

These seem heavy. They'll save you heartbreak.

### When to Break Up

**End it if:**
- You're staying because it's comfortable, not because it's good
- You're hoping they'll change
- You can't be yourself
- There's abuse (emotional or physical)
- You've grown in different directions
- You want fundamentally different things

**The Breakup Script:**
"I care about you, but this relationship isn't working for me anymore. I need to end it."

You don't owe them a dissertation. Be kind, be clear, be done.

### Dealing with Heartbreak

**Weeks 1-2:**
- Feel everything (cry, rage, grieve)
- Lean on friends
- Block/unfollow them (seriously)
- Don't drunk text

**Weeks 3-4:**
- Start moving again (literal exercise helps)
- Pick up a hobby
- Journal about what you learned
- No contact (stay strong)

**Month 2+:**
- Notice you're thinking about them less
- Start feeling like yourself
- Maybe ready to date again (or not, that's fine too)

**The Truth:**
It gets better. Not in some inspirational Instagram way. In a real "one day you'll realize it doesn't hurt anymore" way.

### Modern Relationship Challenges

**Porn:**
Ethical consumption conversations are important.
Addiction is real. Communication is key.

**Social Media:**
Don't post every fight.
Your relationship isn't for external validation.
Some things stay private.

**Different Politics:**
Depends on the issue. "Different tax policy" is one thing. "Different views on my rights as a human" is another.

### The Relationship Audit

Every 6 months, ask yourself:
- Am I happy more often than not?
- Do I feel safe (emotionally and physically)?
- Are we growing together or apart?
- Would I choose this person again today?

If the answers are mostly no, you have decisions to make.

### Being Single Is Not Failure

You don't need a partner to be complete.
Being single > being in a bad relationship
It's better to be alone than lonely with someone
Take time between relationships to know yourself

### The Final Word

Good relationships:
- Feel easy more often than hard
- Make you more yourself, not less
- Challenge you to grow (not change who you are)
- Feel like partnership, not performance

You deserve that.
Don't settle for less because you think you should be paired up.
