# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 8: Work & Money - Navigate the Broken System

### The Truth About "Following Your Passion"
"Do what you love and you'll never work a day in your life" is a lie sold by people who already have money.

The real question: How do I survive financially while protecting my mental health?

### The New Career Reality
- Job loyalty is dead (companies proved that)
- Pensions don't exist  
- "Entry-level" requires 5 years experience
- Unpaid internships are classist gatekeeping
- The degree you were told to get might not pay the loans back

This isn't your failure. The system is rigged.

### The Survival Framework

**Tier 1: Basic Survival**
Get money flowing. Period.
- Gig work, service industry, temp agencies
- No shame in this. Bezos isn't better than you because he's rich.
- This funds tier 2 and 3

**Tier 2: Skill Building**  
While surviving, develop valuable skills:
- Free resources: YouTube, Khan Academy, freeCodeCamp
- Library cards = free Coursera/LinkedIn Learning
- Build portfolio doing free work initially (time-boxed)

**Tier 3: Strategic Positioning**
Once you have skills + runway:
- Apply to 50+ jobs (numbers game)
- Network on LinkedIn (I know, but it works)
- Reach out to people doing what you want (informational interviews)

### The Side Hustle Reality Check

**Don't Start a Side Hustle If:**
- You're burnt out from your main job
- You have no emergency fund
- It's just another way to avoid rest

**Do Consider It If:**
- You have 5-10 hours/week actually available
- You've validated people will pay for it
- It aligns with skills you want to build

Popular but saturated: Dropshipping, Amazon FBA, "passive income" courses
Actually viable: Freelancing skills you already have, teaching things you know, building specific solutions to specific problems

### Money Scripts That Don't Suck

**"I'm bad with money"**
→ "I wasn't taught financial literacy. I'm learning now."

**"I'll never be able to afford [house/retirement/freedom]"**
→ "The system makes this hard. I'm building skills and knowledge to navigate it anyway."

**"I should be further along by now"**
→ "I'm dealing with economic conditions my parents didn't face. I'm doing my best."

### Actual Financial Advice

**The Foundation:**
1. Emergency fund: $1000, then 3 months expenses (build slowly)
2. Pay minimums on everything
3. Extra money → highest interest debt first
4. Once debt manageable → invest 10-15% in index funds

**Apps That Help:**
- YNAB (You Need A Budget) - actually good
- Mint - free tracking
- Acorns - micro-investing
- Digit - automatic saving

**Free Resources:**
- r/personalfinance wiki
- Khan Academy: Finance
- Your Money or Your Life (book, library)

### The Job Hunt That Doesn't Destroy Your Soul

**Applications:**
- Customize resume for each role (yes, really)
- Cover letter = 3 paragraphs max, show you researched them
- Apply to 50-100 before you panic

**Networking (Ugh):**
- "Hi [Name], I'm interested in [field]. Could I buy you coffee and ask about your experience?"
- LinkedIn: Post about what you're learning, engage with others
- Alumni networks, professional associations (often have student rates)

**Interviews:**
- Research company (reviews, news, competitors)
- Prepare stories using STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- Ask them questions (culture, growth, why they like working there)
- Send thank you email within 24 hours

### Dealing with Rejection

**After 10 rejections:**
"This is normal. I'm building resilience and refining my approach."

**After 50 rejections:**
"I might need to adjust my strategy. Let me get feedback on my resume/approach."

**After 100 rejections:**
"The market might genuinely be brutal right now, or I'm targeting wrong roles. Let me pivot."

### Negotiation Scripts (Yes, You Can Negotiate)

**When they make an offer:**
"Thank you! I'm excited about this opportunity. Based on my research and experience, I was hoping for [10-20% higher]. Is there flexibility there?"

**If they say no:**
"I understand. Can we discuss [signing bonus/extra vacation/remote days/professional development budget]?"

**If you have another offer:**
"I have another offer for [amount]. I prefer your company. Can you match?"

### The Anti-Grind Reminder
- You are not your job
- Your worth ≠ your productivity
- Rest is not laziness
- Burnout is not a badge of honor
- "Rise and grind" culture is designed to exploit you

### Work-Life Balance in Late-Stage Capitalism

Set boundaries:
- Email off after 6pm
- No slack on weekends  
- Use your PTO (seriously, use it)
- Sick days are for mental health too

If your job punishes boundaries, document it and start looking. That's a toxic workplace, not a you problem.

### When to Quit

**Quit when:**
- Your mental health is genuinely suffering
- You have 3-6 months runway
- You have a plan (even rough)

**Don't quit when:**
- You're just having a bad week
- You have no savings
- You're running from problems that'll follow you

**The Exception:**
If you're being abused, harassed, or discriminated against: Document everything, report to HR, contact employment lawyer, leave ASAP. Your safety matters more than money.

### Building Wealth While the World Burns

You can care about climate change AND invest in your future.
You can critique capitalism AND participate in the stock market.
You can want systemic change AND secure your own survival.

These aren't contradictions. They're survival in a broken system while working toward a better one.

**The Long Game:**
- Max out employer 401k match (free money)
- Roth IRA if you qualify ($6500/year, grows tax-free)
- Index funds > individual stocks
- Time in market > timing the market
- Start small, compound over decades

### The Final Reality
The American Dream as sold is dead.
But you can still build:
- Financial stability
- Career satisfaction
- Work that doesn't destroy you
- Enough money to live and help others

It's just going to look different than what they promised.

And that's okay.
