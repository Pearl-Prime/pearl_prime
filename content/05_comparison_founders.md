# They Raised $10M and You're Still Pre-Seed (So What?)

## For: Everyone scrolling TechCrunch feeling like they're failing

Another day, another announcement:
- "X raises $15M Series A"
- "Y acquired for $200M"
- "Z hits $10M ARR in 6 months"

And you're sitting there with:
- 200 users
- $5K MRR
- No funding
- Wondering what you're doing wrong

**You're not doing anything wrong. You're just seeing everyone else's highlight reel.**

### The TechCrunch Effect

**What you see:**
- Company raises huge round
- Massive user numbers
- Hockey stick growth
- Founder interviews

**What you don't see:**
- Their 3 failed companies before this
- The rich friend/family who seeded them
- The pivot they just did before traction
- The unsustainable burn rate
- The team that's about to quit
- The business model that doesn't work yet

**Press releases are marketing. Not reality.**

### The Comparison Trap

**You think:**
- "They're 6 months in and already raised a Series A"
- "They have 10K users and we have 200"
- "They're growing 50% month-over-month"
- "We must be doing something wrong"

**Reality:**
- They might be 6 months post-pivot but 3 years total
- They might have bought those users
- That growth might not be sustainable
- Different markets, different trajectories

**You're comparing your day 1 to their day 1,000.**

### The Raise Announcement Lies

**The announcement:** "Startup X raises $5M seed"

**What it doesn't say:**
- Raised at a $15M valuation (they gave up 33%)
- Took 8 months to close
- Got rejected by 50 other investors
- Had to take worse terms than they wanted
- Their previous startup failed

**Every raise sounds easy in the press. None of them were.**

### The Different Starting Lines

**Some founders:**
- Stanford/Harvard MBA
- Parents who are VCs
- Sold previous company
- Already have investor network
- Can afford to bootstrap for years

**Other founders:**
- No degree or state school
- No connections
- First company
- Need salary to survive
- Bootstrapping from savings

**These are not the same race.**

### The Privilege You Don't See

**That founder who raised $2M in 2 weeks?**

Probably had:
- Investor relationships from previous job
- Ivy League network
- Credibility from name-brand company
- Enough savings to build for a year first
- Family safety net

**They didn't just "have a great idea and hustle."**

### The Metrics That Don't Matter

**Vanity metrics:**
- Total users (if they don't come back)
- Amount raised (if you're burning it stupidly)
- Press mentions (if users don't care)
- Social media followers (if they're not customers)
- Office space (if it's empty)

**Actual metrics:**
- Revenue
- Retention
- Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value
- Path to profitability

**You can have millions in funding and still fail.**  
**You can have 100 users and build a real business.**

### The Growth Rate Lie

**Announcement:** "Growing 100% month-over-month!"

**Reality check:**
- Month 1: 10 users
- Month 2: 20 users (100% growth!)
- Month 3: 40 users (100% growth!)

**Percentages lie when the base is small.**

Also:
- That growth might not be sustainable
- They might be paying for it
- Churn might be 80%

**Don't compare your sustainable growth to their unsustainable spike.**

### The Different Paths to Success

**Path A: Raise big, scale fast**
- High risk, high reward
- Need investor appetite
- Winner-take-all markets
- Burn cash for growth

**Path B: Bootstrap, grow steady**
- Lower risk, slower growth
- No investor dependency
- Can be profitable early
- Sustainable from day 1

**Path C: Hybrid**
- Raise some, keep control
- Sustainable growth
- Balance risk and speed

**None of these is "better." They're just different.**

### The Success Timeline Myths

**The myth:** 
- Year 1: Build MVP
- Year 2: Get traction
- Year 3: Raise Series A
- Year 4: Scale
- Year 5: Exit

**The reality:**
- Year 1-2: Flailing around
- Year 3: Found something that might work
- Year 4-5: Actually getting traction
- Year 6-8: Scaling (maybe)
- Year 10+: Exit (if you're lucky)

**Or:** Never exit, build profitable business, run it forever.

### Your Only Real Competition

**Not:**
- The startup that just raised $10M
- The company with 10,000 users
- The founder who's all over Twitter

**But:**
- Yourself last month
- Your own metrics trending
- Your own customers' problems

**Are you better than you were 30 days ago? That's the only question.**

### The Social Media Trap

**What founders post:**
- Funding announcements
- Growth milestones
- Awards and recognition
- Team celebrations

**What founders don't post:**
- The 6 months before funding of rejections
- The growth plateau they're hiding
- The team member who just quit
- The existential crisis at 3am

**You're seeing a curated version of reality.**

### The Ecosystem You're In Matters

**In SF/Silicon Valley:**
- Everyone's raising millions
- Everyone's scaling fast
- Everyone's going for unicorn
- Bootstrapping seems like failure

**Outside SF:**
- Building sustainable businesses
- Growing profitably
- Solving real problems
- Success looks different

**Your ecosystem shapes your definition of success. Choose wisely.**

### When Comparison is Actually Useful

**Good comparison:**
- "How are they solving [specific problem]?"
- "What can I learn from their approach?"
- "What mistakes did they make that I can avoid?"

**Bad comparison:**
- "Why don't we have their numbers?"
- "They must be better than us"
- "We're failing because we're not them"

**Learn from others. Don't measure yourself against them.**

### The Survivorship Bias

**You see:**
- All the companies that succeeded
- All the founders who raised big rounds
- All the exits and unicorns

**You don't see:**
- The 90% that failed quietly
- The raised rounds that led to nowhere
- The companies that shut down

**For every success story, there are 10 failures you never heard about.**

### The Different Risk Tolerances

**Some founders can:**
- Go without salary for 2 years
- Risk everything on one shot
- Raise at any valuation
- Burn through cash quickly

**Some founders need to:**
- Pay themselves ASAP
- Build sustainably
- Keep control
- Grow profitably

**Neither is wrong. Just different circumstances.**

### What Actually Predicts Success

**Not:**
- How much you raised
- How fast you're growing
- How many users you have
- How much press you got

**But:**
- Are you solving a real problem?
- Are customers willing to pay?
- Are they coming back?
- Are you learning and adapting?
- Can you sustain this?

**The fundamentals haven't changed.**

### The Mindset Shift

**From:** "They raised $10M, we must be failing"  
**To:** "They raised $10M, now they have different pressures"

**From:** "They have 10K users, we only have 200"  
**To:** "We have 200 users who love us, that's enough to learn"

**From:** "Everyone else is ahead"  
**To:** "We're on our own timeline"

### The Celebration Practice

**Every week, celebrate:**
- One thing you shipped
- One customer you helped
- One thing you learned
- One small win

**Stop waiting for the big milestone to feel good about your progress.**

### The Reality Check Questions

**When you see a competitor's success, ask:**
1. Do I actually know their real numbers?
2. Do I know what they gave up to get there?
3. Is their path even relevant to mine?
4. What can I learn from this?
5. Does this change what I should do tomorrow?

**If the answer to #5 is "no," stop thinking about it.**

### The Unfair Advantages

**Stop comparing if you don't have the same:**
- Network
- Capital
- Credentials
- Experience
- Market timing
- Team
- Distribution

**Play the hand you're dealt, not the hand you wish you had.**

### Bottom Line

Someone will always have:
- More users
- More funding
- More growth
- More press
- More everything

**That doesn't make your progress less real.**

Every successful company started with:
- Few users
- No funding
- Slow growth
- No press

**The difference is they kept going.**

---

**Now close TechCrunch, close Twitter, and go talk to one actual user. That's where your success lives.**
