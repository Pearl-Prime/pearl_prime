# Burnout Isn't a Badge of Honor

## For: Everyone grinding themselves into dust for a company that'll replace you in a week

Real talk: Hustle culture lied to you.

Working yourself to exhaustion doesn't make you dedicated. It makes you unavailable for the life you're supposedly working towards.

### The Trap

Tech glorifies burnout. They dress it up as "passion" and "commitment." They give you free snacks and ping pong tables and call it a culture.

Then they expect you to:
- Answer Slack at 11 PM
- Ship features on weekends
- Skip lunch to make deadlines
- Sacrifice your health for "the mission"

Here's what they don't tell you: **The mission will be fine without your burnout.**

### What Burnout Actually Looks Like

Not the dramatic breakdown. The quiet erosion:

- You can't focus anymore
- Simple tasks feel impossible
- You're tired but can't sleep
- You don't care about things you used to love
- You're constantly irritable
- Your body hurts for no reason
- You can't remember the last time you felt okay

Sound familiar? That's not weakness. That's your body screaming that something's wrong.

### The Math That Doesn't Math

You think: "If I work 80 hours, I'll get twice as much done"

Reality: You'll get maybe 1.2x done, and half of it will need fixing later because you were too tired to think straight.

You're not productive. You're just present.

### What Actually Matters

**Not this:**
- Hours logged
- Weekends sacrificed
- Sleep skipped
- Meals missed

**This:**
- Problems solved
- Code that works
- Impact created
- Sustainable pace

### The Permission You Don't Need (But Here It Is Anyway)

You're allowed to:
- Have hobbies that aren't coding
- Take your lunch break
- Use your PTO
- Not check Slack after hours
- Say "I'm at capacity"
- Leave at 5
- Not be passionate about every ticket

Your worth isn't measured in overtime.

### What To Do Instead

**Set boundaries:**  
"I don't work weekends" isn't negotiable. It's information.

**Protect your time:**  
Calendar blocks for actual breaks. Enforce them.

**Remember the ratio:**  
For every year you work here, you'll live 50+ years after. Plan accordingly.

**Measure different:**  
Success isn't hours worked. It's impact per sustainable hour.

### The Real Flex

You know what's actually impressive? Still being in tech in 10 years because you didn't burn yourself out in year 2.

Longevity beats intensity. Every single time.

### When They Push Back

"We need you to..."  
"I'm at capacity. What should I deprioritize?"

"Everyone else is working late..."  
"I'm optimizing for sustainable productivity, not performative hours."

"This is how we do things here..."  
"Then we should change how we do things."

### Bottom Line

Your company will survive if you take a break.  
You might not survive if you don't.

**Choose you.** Every time.

The code will still be broken tomorrow. Your health won't wait.
