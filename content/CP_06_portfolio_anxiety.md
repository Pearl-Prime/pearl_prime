# Your Portfolio Is Scaring Away Clients

## For: Everyone overthinking their portfolio

Your portfolio has one job: Show you can do the work.

That's it. Not to be perfect. Not to showcase every skill. Not to win awards.

**Just to prove you can deliver.**

### The Portfolio Myths

**Myth:** "I need 20+ perfect pieces"  
**Reality:** 5-8 strong pieces beats 20 mediocre ones

**Myth:** "Everything must be client work"  
**Reality:** Personal projects show initiative and creativity

**Myth:** "I can't show work until it's award-worthy"  
**Reality:** Good enough work gets you hired

**Myth:** "My portfolio needs to show everything I can do"  
**Reality:** Show what you WANT to do more of

**Myth:** "I need a perfect website before I can get clients"  
**Reality:** A PDF portfolio gets you started

### What Actually Matters

**1. Relevant work**  
Show the type of work you want to get hired for

**2. Quality over quantity**  
5 great pieces > 50 okay ones

**3. Process (sometimes)**  
For some clients, seeing your thinking matters

**4. Results (when possible)**  
"This increased sales by X%" > "I made this pretty thing"

**5. Professionalism**  
Clean presentation, no typos, works on mobile

**6. Easy to contact you**  
Email/phone visible on every page

### The Framework: SHOWCASE

**S - Select strategically**  
Show what you want more of, not everything you've ever done

**H - Highlight results**  
Client outcomes > pretty pictures

**O - Order matters**  
Best work first. They might not scroll.

**W - Write case studies**  
Problem → Your solution → Result

**C - Clean presentation**  
Simple, fast-loading, mobile-friendly

**A - Authenticity wins**  
Your voice > generic corporate speak

**S - Scrap the fluff**  
No long artist statements. No pretentious descriptions.

**E - Easy navigation**  
3 clicks max to see everything

### Portfolio Types That Work

**1. Simple PDF**
- 5-10 pages
- One project per page or spread
- Brief description + visuals
- Contact info on every page
- **Good for:** Starting out, applying to jobs

**2. Basic Website**
- Homepage
- Portfolio grid
- About page
- Contact
- **Good for:** Established freelancers

**3. Case Study Site**
- Detailed project breakdowns
- Problem/solution/results
- Process documentation
- **Good for:** Strategic work, high-end clients

**4. Instagram/Behance**
- Curated feed
- Consistent style
- Link in bio to full portfolio
- **Good for:** Visual work, younger audience

**5. Hybrid**
- Simple site for credibility
- PDF for applications
- Social for discovery
- **Good for:** Most people

### What to Include for Each Piece

**Minimum:**
- Project title
- What it is (logo, website, illustration, etc.)
- Visual(s)

**Better:**
- Client/context
- Your role
- The challenge
- Your solution
- 1-3 visuals

**Best:**
- All of the above
- Measurable results
- Process/iterations
- Skills used

### Building Your First Portfolio (When You Have Nothing)

**Option 1: Personal projects**
- Redesign a local business
- Create a poster series
- Design a concept brand
- Write spec work

**Option 2: Volunteer work**
- Help a nonprofit
- Design for friends' businesses
- Community projects
- Student organizations

**Option 3: Challenges**
- Daily creative prompts
- 30-day challenges
- Design briefs online
- Hackathons

**Option 4: Case studies of learning**
- Document your process learning something
- Before/after of skill development
- Show problem-solving ability

**The key:** Make the work you want to get hired for

### What NOT to Do

**Don't:**
- Include student work if you have professional work
- Show 10 variations of the same project
- Use lorem ipsum text
- Display work you're not proud of
- Make people hunt for contact info
- Auto-play music/video
- Use jargon-filled descriptions
- Leave broken links
- Forget to update it

**These kill credibility. Fix them.**

### The "Good Enough" Portfolio

**Here's the truth:** Your portfolio will never feel perfect.

There will always be something to improve. A better project. A cleaner layout. A smarter case study.

**Ship it anyway.**

A good enough portfolio that exists > a perfect portfolio that's always "almost ready"

### Update Strategy

**When to update:**
- Got better work? Add it.
- Project doesn't represent you anymore? Remove it.
- Every 6 months minimum

**What to remove:**
- Anything you wouldn't want to do again
- Work that no longer represents your skills
- Projects you're not proud of
- Anything that makes you cringe

**Your portfolio should show where you're going, not where you've been.**

### Permission Slips

You're allowed to:
- Show unfinished work (if you explain it)
- Use personal projects
- Have a simple portfolio
- Update constantly
- Remove old work
- Focus on one type of work
- Say "I specialize in X" even if you can do Y and Z
- Not have a fancy website
- Use a template
- Start with what you have

### The Real Goal

Your portfolio isn't a shrine to your ego.

**It's a sales tool.**

It needs to convince someone you can solve their problem.

That's it.

**Stop perfecting. Start shipping.**

---

**Now take 30 minutes. Pick your 5 best pieces. Put them in a PDF. Share it with one person.**

**That's your portfolio. Done.**
