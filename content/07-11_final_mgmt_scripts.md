# FILE 7: The Layoff List Might Have Your Name On It

## For: Everyone refreshing LinkedIn during the quarterly earnings call

**The signs:**
- Sudden "reorganization"
- Consultants in conference rooms
- Leadership acting weird
- Budget freezes
- Hiring freeze
- "Town hall" scheduled

**You know what's coming.**

### The Waiting Game

**The rumor phase:**
- Whispers in Slack DMs
- Closed-door meetings
- People suddenly working from home
- Your manager seems stressed

**The dread phase:**
- Checking email compulsively
- Calendar invites from HR
- Can't focus on work
- Updating resume just in case

**The announcement:**
- Company-wide email
- "Difficult decision"
- "Restructuring for growth"
- X% of workforce

**Then you wait to see if you're X%.**

### The Meeting Invite

**From:** HR  
**Subject:** Quick Check-in  
**Time:** 15 minutes, tomorrow 9am

**Your brain:** This is it. I'm being laid off.

**Two outcomes:**
1. You're right (you're laid off)
2. You're safe (but traumatized and guilty)

### The Layoff Conversation

**15 minutes:**
- Your position is being eliminated
- Last day is Friday (or today)
- Here's your severance package
- Questions?
- IT is disabling your access
- Please return your laptop

**Everything you built:** Gone  
**Your projects:** Someone else's now  
**Your team:** Moving on without you

### The Survivor's Guilt

**If you survived:**
- Relief (you're employed)
- Guilt (others aren't)
- Fear (am I next?)
- Anger (at leadership)
- Overwork (to prove value)

**Your workload:**
- Now doing 2-3 people's jobs
- No additional pay
- "We all need to step up"
- Burnout incoming

### The Economic Reality

**Companies do layoffs for:**
- Stock price boost (immediate)
- Investor appeasement
- Cost cutting
- "Right-sizing"
- Preparing for sale

**NOT because:**
- You weren't good enough
- You didn't work hard enough
- You failed

**You're a line item on a spreadsheet.**

### The Resume Update

**If you think layoffs are coming:**

**Do now:**
- Update LinkedIn (complete profile)
- Refresh resume (quantify everything)
- Document your wins
- Export work samples (while you can)
- Connect with colleagues
- Download contacts
- Back up important files

**Don't wait until you're laid off.**

### The Severance Negotiation

**They offer:** X weeks  
**You can ask for:** X+4 weeks

**Negotiate:**
- Extended health benefits
- Positive reference letter
- Career transition support
- Unused PTO payout
- Earlier vesting of stock

**Everything is negotiable. They expect you to negotiate.**

### The Job Search Reality

**Timeline:**
- Month 1: Applications, networking
- Month 2-3: Interviews starting
- Month 4-6: Offers (maybe)

**During layoffs:**
- Market floods with talent
- Companies slow hiring
- Takes longer than normal
- Competition is fierce

**Have 6+ months expenses saved if possible.**

### What To Do After

**Week 1:** Feel feelings  
**Week 2:** File unemployment  
**Week 3:** Assess finances  
**Week 4:** Job search begins

**Don't rush into wrong job out of panic.**

# FILE 8: You're Stuck and You Know It

## For: Everyone who's been in the same role for 4 years

**You've been a middle manager for:**
- 4 years? 5? 6?
- Time blurs together
- Same level, same title
- No promotion in sight

**You're stuck. And everyone knows it.**

### The Plateau

**Your trajectory:**
- Year 1-2: Learning, growing
- Year 3-4: Mastery, comfortable
- Year 5+: Stagnant, invisible

**The truth:**
- You've learned everything there is to learn
- You're bored
- You're not challenged
- You're coasting

### The Reasons You're Stuck

**Organizational:**
- No senior roles opening up
- Company isn't growing
- Your department is shrinking
- Political landscape frozen

**Personal:**
- You're in the "too good to move" trap
- You don't play politics well
- You're not visible enough
- You stopped trying

**Both:** Probably both.

### The Golden Handcuffs

**Why you can't leave:**
- Salary is good (ish)
- Benefits are solid
- You know the system
- 401k is vested
- Comfortable

**But:**
- Your skills are atrophying
- Your market value is declining
- You're becoming unmarketable
- Every year makes it harder to leave

### The Two Paths

**Path 1: Stay**
- Accept the plateau
- Optimize for lifestyle
- Coast intentionally
- Find meaning elsewhere

**Path 2: Leave**
- Find growth elsewhere
- Take the risk
- Start over
- Potential upside

**The middle path (hope it changes) doesn't work.**

### What To Do

**If staying:**
- Negotiate for what you can get
- Max out development budget
- Find projects you care about
- Build skills for eventual exit

**If leaving:**
- Update skills
- Network externally
- Apply strategically
- Have financial cushion

# FILE 9: Quiet Quitting vs. Actually Quitting

## For: Everyone doing the bare minimum

**You're:**
- Doing exactly what's in your job description
- No extra projects
- No overtime
- No above-and-beyond

**They call it "quiet quitting."**

**You call it "having boundaries."**

### The Quiet Quitting Reality

**What it is:**
- Working your contracted hours
- Not volunteering for extra work
- Focusing on work-life balance
- Saying no to non-essentials

**What it's not:**
- Actually quitting
- Sabotage
- Laziness
- Doing bad work

**It's doing your job. Just your job.**

### Why You're Quiet Quitting

**Burnout:**
- Years of going above-and-beyond
- No recognition
- No promotion
- No raise that matches effort

**Resentment:**
- Watched others get promoted over you
- Seen loyalty not rewarded
- Lost faith in the system
- Checked out emotionally

**Pragmatism:**
- Realized extra effort doesn't matter
- Optimizing for personal life
- Protecting your energy
- Working to live, not living to work

### The Employer Reaction

**They:**
- Call it a crisis
- Blame Gen Z/millennials
- Write think pieces
- Don't raise wages or improve conditions

**They want:**
- Your loyalty
- Your extra hours
- Your discretionary effort
- For free

### The Career Implications

**If you quiet quit:**
- Won't get promoted (probably)
- Won't get exciting projects
- Won't get visibility
- Might get managed out

**But:**
- You'll be healthier
- You'll have work-life balance
- You'll preserve your sanity
- You might find happiness

**Choose your trade-off.**

### The Alternative

**Actually quit.**

**If:**
- You've given up on the job
- You're just collecting a paycheck
- You have better options
- You're miserable

**Then:**
- Leave
- Find somewhere that values you
- Don't half-ass it here
- Full-ass it somewhere else

# FILE 10: You Feel Like a Fraud in Every Leadership Meeting

## For: Every middle manager with imposter syndrome

**In leadership meetings:**
- Everyone seems confident
- They use jargon you don't fully get
- They reference frameworks you've never heard of
- You nod along pretending to understand

**You think:** "How did I get here? When will they figure out I don't belong?"

### The Imposter Syndrome Spiral

**You:**
- Overprepare for everything
- Second-guess every decision
- Attribute success to luck
- Attribute failure to incompetence
- Constantly feel like you're faking it

**Meanwhile:**
- Others feel the same way
- They're also pretending
- Confidence is often performance
- Everyone's making it up

### The Truth

**Being in leadership doesn't mean:**
- You know everything
- You're the smartest
- You have all the answers
- You never doubt yourself

**It means:**
- You make decisions with incomplete info
- You learn as you go
- You pretend to be confident
- You fake it till you make it

**Everyone is winging it.**

### What Helps

**Remember:**
- You were hired for a reason
- Your skills got you here
- Doubt is normal
- Asking questions is strength
- You belong here

# FILE 11: The Corporate Long Game is 20+ Years

## For: Everyone wondering if this is it

**You thought:**
- Work hard for 10 years
- Climb to senior leadership
- Make bank
- Retire early

**Reality:**
- 20+ years to VP/SVP
- Politics matter more than merit
- The climb is slow
- Retirement at 65 (if lucky)

### The Timeline

**Years 1-5:** Entry to mid-level  
**Years 6-10:** Mid to senior  
**Years 11-15:** Senior to director  
**Years 16-20:** Director to VP  
**Years 20+:** VP to C-suite (maybe)

**Or:** You plateau at year 8 and stay there forever.

### The Long Game Strategy

**If playing for 20 years:**
- Pace yourself (don't burn out)
- Build relationships (they compound)
- Learn constantly (stay relevant)
- Play politics (unfortunately necessary)
- Have life outside work (you need it)

**If not playing for 20 years:**
- Exit strategy
- Skills that transfer
- Financial independence
- Alternative paths

### The Question

**Every few years ask:**
- Is this worth another 5 years?
- Am I growing?
- Am I happy (enough)?
- Is there a better path?

**Be honest. Adjust accordingly.**

### Bottom Line

**Corporate life is long.**

Longer than you think.  
Slower than you want.  
More political than it should be.

**Decide:**
- Am I in this for the long haul?
- Or am I building an exit?

**Both are valid. Just be intentional.**
