# GEN ALPHA STUDENTS - IDENTITY CRISIS SCENES
## Ages 12-16 | Middle School Context | 2026

## SCENE I-01 | The Pronoun Question
**Setting:** School counselor's office, 2:14 PM, Monday
**Engine:** Watcher

The form asks for pronouns. Riley stares at the dropdown.

She/her. He/him. They/them. Other (please specify).

Riley's finger hovers. She's always been "she." That's what her parents use. What teachers use. What everyone's always used.

But lately… it doesn't feel right. Doesn't feel wrong either. Just feels like clothes that don't quite fit.

She clicks "she/her" because it's easier than explaining.

The counselor smiles. "Great, all set."

Riley nods. Leaves.

Walks to her locker. Sees her reflection in the bathroom mirror. Doesn't recognize the person looking back.

Or maybe she does and that's the problem.

She texts her friend: "do you ever feel like ur pretending to be yourself"

The dots appear. Disappear. Appear again.

"Sometimes yeah. why"

Riley doesn't know how to answer. Types "nvm" instead.

She's thirteen. Still figuring out who she is. But everyone wants an answer now.

## SCENE I-02 | The Culture Day Assignment
**Setting:** Classroom, 10:37 AM, Wednesday
**Engine:** Shame

Mr. Patterson assigns Culture Day presentations. "Share something about your heritage."

Kai's Korean-American. Fourth generation. Doesn't speak Korean. Doesn't know traditional dances or recipes.

His grandma makes kimchi. That's about it.

Everyone else seems excited. Maya's doing a Bollywood dance. Jordan's bringing his abuela's tamales.

Kai doesn't know what to bring. He's Korean on paper. American in practice. Both. Neither. In-between.

He googles "Korean culture for kids." Gets a bunch of stereotypes. K-pop. Taekwondo. Hanbok.

None of it feels like his.

He asks his mom. She shrugs. "We're American, Kai. Just be yourself."

But himself isn't what the assignment is asking for.

He brings bulgogi his mom ordered from a restaurant. Wears a shirt his grandma bought him from Korea twenty years ago.

Presents. Everyone claps. Someone asks if he speaks Korean.

"No."

"Oh."

Kai sits down. Feels like he failed at being something he's supposed to be but never learned how to be.

## SCENE I-03 | The Friend Group Shapeshifter
**Setting:** School hallway, 11:52 AM, Friday
**Engine:** Comparison

Maya has different versions of herself.

With Priya and the girls: she's bubbly, talks about boys, laughs loud, cares about makeup.

With the art kids at lunch: she's quiet, thoughtful, wears black, talks about deep stuff.

With her family: she's responsible, respectful, helps with her little brother.

Online: she's whoever gets likes.

She walks between them all day. Switching. Adjusting. Performing.

Sometimes she forgets which one is real.

Or maybe they're all real. Or none of them are.

She's in the bathroom between classes. Looks in the mirror. Doesn't know who's looking back.

The bell rings. Time to be the next version.

She walks out. Smiles at Priya. Becomes whoever Priya needs her to be.

## SCENE I-04 | The "What Are You Good At" Question
**Setting:** Guidance counselor meeting, 1:18 PM, Tuesday
**Engine:** Overwhelm

"So, Zara, what are your strengths?"

The counselor's trying to help. Talking about high school planning. Extracurriculars. Future.

Zara stares. Doesn't know how to answer.

She's not bad at things. Not failing. Just… not exceptional at anything.

Not artistic like Maya. Not athletic like Jordan. Not smart like Dev.

Average at math. Okay at English. Fine at everything. Great at nothing.

"I'm not really sure."

The counselor nods. "That's okay. You're young. You'll figure it out."

But everyone else seems to already know. Everyone has a thing.

Zara has… nothing. No identity. No direction. No obvious talent.

She's fourteen and completely unremarkable.

The counselor gives her a list of clubs. "Try some things. See what sticks."

Zara takes the list. Knows she won't join any of them.

Because what if she tries and finds out she's not good at those either?

Better to be undefined than defined as mediocre.

## SCENE I-05 | The Sexuality Spiral
**Setting:** Bedroom, 10:47 PM, Saturday
**Engine:** Spiral

Tyler's scrolling TikTok. Stops on a video. Guy with messy hair doing a lip sync.

Tyler watches it three times. Saves it.

His friend Dev texted earlier about a girl in their math class. "She's so hot, right?"

Tyler said yeah. Because that's what you say.

But he's not sure he meant it.

He searches "am I gay quiz" on Google. Takes four different ones. Gets different answers.

His chest feels tight. He doesn't know what he is.

Straight? Gay? Bi? Something else?

Maybe he's too young to know. Maybe he should know already.

His friend came out last year. Everyone was supportive. It was fine.

But Tyler's not sure. And "not sure" feels worse than knowing.

Because everyone wants a label. Everyone wants an answer.

And Tyler just… doesn't have one yet.

He closes his phone. Stares at the ceiling.

Thirteen years old and drowning in questions nobody asked him to answer but everyone expects him to know.

## SCENE I-06 | The Hobby Crisis
**Setting:** Bedroom, 4:35 PM, Sunday
**Engine:** Comparison

Mira tries hobbies like other people try clothes.

Piano: three months. Quit. Too hard.

Drawing: six months. Got bored.

Volleyball: one season. Wasn't good enough.

Photography: two weeks. Everyone else's photos were better.

Coding: one YouTube tutorial. Too confusing.

Now she's scrolling Instagram. Everyone has a thing. Someone's posting art. Someone's posting gymnastics videos. Someone's singing.

Everyone has an identity. A passion. Something they're known for.

Mira has a graveyard of abandoned interests.

Her mom says she's "still exploring." Her dad says she'll "find her thing eventually."

But Mira's fifteen. When does "eventually" arrive?

She watches a video of someone her age playing guitar. Perfectly.

She used to play guitar. Quit after four months.

Closes Instagram. Opens TikTok. Same thing. Everyone's exceptional at something.

She's exceptional at starting and stopping.

That's her identity: the girl who tries everything and commits to nothing.

## SCENE I-07 | The Online Persona Problem
**Setting:** Bedroom, 8:52 PM, Thursday
**Engine:** Shame

Jordan has three Instagram accounts.

Main account: 847 followers. Curated. Perfect photos. Funny captions. Version of himself that gets likes.

Finsta (fake insta): 23 followers. Close friends. Real thoughts. Messy photos. Actually him.

Private account: 0 followers. Just him. The stuff he can't even show close friends.

He's editing a photo for main. Adjusting filter. Fixing lighting. Writing caption. Deleting it. Rewriting.

Takes twenty minutes to post one photo.

His friend comments: "bro you're so funny"

Jordan's not funny. He spent an hour writing that caption. Tested it on three people first.

He's performing. Constantly. Even for people he calls friends.

His finsta is closer to real. But even that's curated. Even that's a version.

The private account is the only place he exists unedited.

And it's the loneliest one.

He posts to main. Gets 64 likes in ten minutes.

Feels nothing.

Because those likes are for someone who doesn't exist.

Jordan—actual Jordan—is sitting alone in his room, wondering if anyone would like him without the filter.

Wondering if he even knows who "actual Jordan" is anymore.

Wondering when he started performing his own life instead of living it.
