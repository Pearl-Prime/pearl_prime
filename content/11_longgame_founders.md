# Building a Company Takes 10 Years (Plan Accordingly)

## For: Everyone who thought they'd exit in 3 years

**You've been building for 18 months.**

You thought you'd:
- Hit product-market fit in 6 months
- Raise a Series A by month 12
- Scale quickly
- Exit or be profitable by year 3

**Reality:**
- Still figuring out product-market fit
- Raised a small seed
- Growing but slowly
- Exit timeline: ???

**Welcome to the actual startup timeline. It's measured in decades, not quarters.**

### The Lies You Were Told

**The narrative:**
- Year 1: MVP and first users
- Year 2: Product-market fit and Series A
- Year 3: Scale
- Year 4-5: Exit or IPO

**The reality:**
- Years 1-3: Flailing, pivoting, learning
- Years 4-6: Found something that might work
- Years 7-9: Actual scaling
- Years 10+: Exit or build forever

**Instagram: 2 years to exit** ← This is the extreme outlier, not the norm  
**Airbnb: 14 years and counting**  
**Stripe: 13 years and counting**  
**Notion: 8 years to real traction**

**You're not slow. You're normal.**

### The Marathon Mindset

**Sprint mentality:**
- All-in 100% all the time
- Sacrifice everything
- Burn out in 2 years
- Give up

**Marathon mentality:**
- Sustainable pace
- Life exists outside work
- Consistent effort over years
- Still building at year 10

**Choose the pace you can maintain for a decade.**

### The Decade Breakdown

**Years 0-2: Survival**
- Figure out if anyone wants this
- Learn to build and sell
- Don't die

**Years 3-5: Validation**
- Find repeatable sales motion
- Build actual product
- Get to $1M+ ARR

**Years 6-8: Scaling**
- Hire team
- Build systems
- Get to $10M+ ARR

**Years 9-10+: Outcomes**
- Exit options appear
- Or build profitably forever
- Or keep scaling

**Where you are in this timeline matters.**

### The Comparison Trap (Again)

**That company that "raised $50M in year 2":**
- Probably had connections
- Maybe amazing timing
- Possibly unsustainable
- Definitely not comparable to you

**Your job:** Run your own race at your own pace.

**Not:** Try to match someone else's manufactured timeline.

### The Financial Reality

**If you're bootstrapping:**
- Years 1-2: No salary or minimal
- Years 3-4: Survival salary
- Years 5+: Market rate maybe
- Build wealth slowly through ownership

**If you raised money:**
- Years 1-3: Below market salary
- Years 4-6: Market rate maybe
- Years 7+: Make up for lost years if you exit
- Or not if you don't

**You're trading salary now for potential outcome later.**

**Much later.**

### The Relationship Test

**Your startup will test every relationship:**

**Romantic partner:**
- Can they handle 5+ years of this?
- Will they resent the sacrifice?
- Are you present enough?

**Family:**
- Do they understand the timeline?
- Can you still show up for important stuff?
- Are you neglecting them?

**Friends:**
- Will they still be there in 5 years?
- Are you maintaining these relationships?
- Or are you just talking about your startup?

**The startup might succeed and the relationships fail. Plan for both.**

### The Health Debt

**What you're doing now:**
- Skipping sleep
- Bad diet
- No exercise
- Constant stress
- Ignoring symptoms

**What's coming in 5 years:**
- Burnout
- Health issues
- Mental health crisis
- Physical breakdown

**You can't build for 10 years if your body gives out at year 3.**

**Invest in health now. It compounds.**

### The Skill Development Arc

**Years 1-2:** Generalist, doing everything  
**Years 3-4:** Specialist in a few areas  
**Years 5-6:** Expert in your domain  
**Years 7-8:** Teaching others  
**Years 9-10:** Strategic thinking

**You're building skills that will serve you for life.**

**Whether this company succeeds or not.**

### The Multiple Outcome Paths

**Path 1: Big Exit**
- Acquired for $100M+
- You make life-changing money
- 5% chance

**Path 2: Small Exit**
- Acquired for $10-50M
- You make good money
- 15% chance

**Path 3: Profitable Business**
- Build to $5-20M ARR
- Take distributions
- Run it forever
- 20% chance

**Path 4: Shut Down**
- Doesn't work out
- Learn and try again
- 60% chance

**Play for Path 1. Prepare for Path 4. Be happy with Path 2 or 3.**

### The Opportunity Cost

**Every year you spend on this startup:**
- You could be making $150-300K at a big tech company
- Building your savings
- Having work-life balance
- Climbing a corporate ladder

**Over 10 years that's $1.5-3M in salary + equity + 401k**

**Your startup needs to return more than that to be worth it financially.**

**Or: You value the learning/experience/ownership enough to trade the money.**

### The Pivots and Iterations

**Most successful companies pivoted:**
- YouTube: Video dating → Video platform
- Slack: Gaming → Communication
- Instagram: Check-in → Photos
- Twitter: Podcasting → Microblogging

**Pivoting in Year 2 isn't failure. It's normal.**

**You might pivot 3-5 times before finding what works.**

**Build that into your timeline.**

### The Funding Rounds Over Time

**Realistic timeline:**
- Pre-seed: Month 0-12 ($250K-500K)
- Seed: Month 12-24 ($1-3M)
- Series A: Month 36-48 ($8-15M)
- Series B: Month 60-72 ($25-40M)

**Each round takes 3-6 months to close.**

**Add 6-12 months between rounds for buffer.**

**That's 6+ years just for Series B.**

### The Team Evolution

**Year 1:** You and co-founder  
**Year 2:** 3-5 people  
**Year 3:** 10-15 people  
**Year 5:** 30-50 people  
**Year 7:** 100+ people  
**Year 10:** 300-500 people

**Managing 3 people ≠ managing 50 people ≠ managing 300 people.**

**You'll need to learn new skills every 2 years.**

### The Burnout Cycles

**You will burn out.**

**Not if. When.**

**Plan for it:**
- Take real vacations
- Have recovery periods
- Don't sprint constantly
- Build support systems
- Get help when needed

**Burning out in Year 2 means you can't build for Year 10.**

### The Market Timing Luck

**Sometimes:**
- The market isn't ready yet
- You're 3 years too early
- You need to wait

**Example:**
- Video conferencing existed before COVID
- COVID made Zoom
- Same product, different timing

**You can't control market timing. You can control persistence.**

### The Metrics That Matter at Each Stage

**Years 1-2:**
- Are users coming back?
- Will anyone pay?
- Is growth happening at all?

**Years 3-5:**
- Month-over-month growth rate
- Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value
- Net revenue retention

**Years 6+:**
- Revenue growth
- Profitability or path to it
- Market share

**Don't measure Year 1 with Year 6 metrics.**

### The Mental Game

**You need:**
- Patience (years of grinding)
- Resilience (dozens of setbacks)
- Adaptability (constant change)
- Optimism (despite evidence)
- Realism (about timeline)

**This is why most people don't do this.**

**Not because they can't. Because they won't stay in it long enough.**

### The Success Redefinition

**Year 1:** Success = Anyone uses it  
**Year 3:** Success = People pay for it  
**Year 5:** Success = $1M+ ARR  
**Year 7:** Success = Sustainable business  
**Year 10:** Success = Whatever you defined it as

**Your definition will change. That's okay.**

### The Exit Timeline Reality

**When exits actually happen:**
- Median time to exit: 8 years
- Top quartile: 6 years
- Bottom quartile: 10+ years
- Many never exit

**If you can't stomach 10 years, don't start.**

### The Alternative: Build Forever

**You don't have to exit:**
- Build profitable business
- Take distributions
- Control your destiny
- Never deal with VCs
- Run it for 20 years

**This is valid. Maybe better.**

**Basecamp: 20+ years, never raised VC, still printing money.**

### The Question to Ask Now

**Where will you be in 10 years if:**

**A) This startup succeeds**
- What does success look like?
- What did you give up to get there?
- Was it worth it?

**B) This startup fails**
- What did you learn?
- What's your next move?
- How do you feel about the time spent?

**If both answers are acceptable, keep going.**

**If neither is acceptable, reconsider.**

### The Sustainable Pace

**What you can maintain for 10 years:**
- 45-55 hour weeks (with sprints)
- Regular exercise
- Maintaining key relationships
- Taking vacations
- Having hobbies
- Protecting sleep

**What you can't maintain:**
- 80 hour weeks
- No breaks ever
- Sacrificing health
- Destroying relationships
- Living for work alone

**Choose sustainability over intensity.**

### The Learning Compounds

**Year 1:** Learning everything from scratch  
**Year 5:** Expert in your space  
**Year 10:** Could start next company way faster

**The knowledge compounds.**

**Whether this company succeeds or fails:**
- You know how to build products
- You know how to sell
- You know how to fundraise
- You know how to hire
- You know what works

**That's worth something.**

### Bottom Line

Building a real company takes 7-10 years minimum.

**If you thought you'd exit in 3:**
- Adjust expectations
- Recalibrate timeline
- Build for the long haul

**If you can't commit to 10 years:**
- Maybe get a job instead
- Maybe build a side project
- Maybe do something else

**But if you can:**
- Pace yourself
- Play the long game
- Enjoy the journey
- Build something that lasts

**The companies that win aren't the ones that sprint fastest.**  
**They're the ones still standing after everyone else quit.**

---

**Now ask yourself: Can I do this for 10 more years? If yes, great. If no, what needs to change?**
