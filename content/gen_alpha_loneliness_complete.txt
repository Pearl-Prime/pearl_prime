# GEN ALPHA STUDENTS - LONELINESS SCENES
## Ages 12-16 | Middle School Context | 2026

---

## SCENE L-01 | The Birthday Party That Wasn't
**Setting:** Bedroom, 3:47 PM, Saturday (their birthday)
**Engine:** Grief

It's Maya's birthday. She's fourteen.

Her mom made cupcakes. Sang happy birthday at breakfast. Posted a photo on Facebook.

Maya checks her phone. Sixteen happy birthday messages. Half from people she doesn't really talk to. Three from relatives. One from her best friend from elementary school who moved away.

No one asked to hang out. No one suggested plans.

Last year she had a party. Invited twelve people. Seven came. This year she didn't plan anything. Told herself she didn't want a party anyway.

She scrolls Instagram. Someone from her grade posted a pool party. Wasn't invited. Didn't know it was happening.

Her mom knocks. "Want to go out for dinner? Your choice."

"Sure."

They go to her favorite restaurant. Just the two of them. The waiter brings a free dessert, sings off-key.

It's nice. Her mom's trying.

But Maya sits there, watching other tables—groups of friends, people her age laughing—and feels the loneliness like a weight in her chest.

She's fourteen. She has friends. Technically.

She just doesn't have people who remembered to make plans.

## SCENE L-02 | The Group Chat Geography
**Setting:** Living room couch, 7:33 PM, Tuesday
**Engine:** Watcher

Tyler's in three group chats. The main one with his whole friend group. The soccer team one. And one with just Dev and Aiden.

The main chat is blowing up. 47 unread messages. He scrolls through.

They're making plans for Saturday. Movies. Someone's basement. Probably staying over.

He reads through. No one tagged him. No one asked if he could come.

He starts typing: "yo can i come"

Deletes it. That sounds desperate.

Types: "what movie"

Sends it. Waits.

Three people typing. None of them respond to him. They're responding to each other.

He locks his phone. Tells himself it's fine. They probably just assumed he's busy. Or forgot. People forget.

He checks again five minutes later. They've moved on to talking about something else.

He's in the chat. Technically included. But he might as well be watching through glass.

## SCENE L-03 | The Lunch Table Overflow
**Setting:** School cafeteria, 12:08 PM, Monday
**Engine:** Shame

Kai gets his lunch. Looks for his table.

They're all there. His friend group. Eight people. Every seat taken.

He stands there, tray in hand. Waiting for someone to notice. Scoot over. Make room.

No one looks up.

He walks to the table. "Hey."

"Oh hey Kai." Someone glances up. Goes back to their conversation.

There's no chair. No space. He could pull one from another table but then it's weird. Then everyone has to rearrange.

"I'll uh… I'll sit over there." He points vaguely.

No one protests.

He sits at the next table. By himself. Still technically with them—close enough to hear their conversation—but not in it.

He eats his sandwich. Scrolls his phone. Pretends this is a choice.

Someone from his table laughs. Inside joke. He doesn't know the context.

He finishes eating early. Throws away his trash. Goes to the library.

Doesn't text the group chat to explain. They probably didn't notice he left.

## SCENE L-04 | The Friday Night Scroll
**Setting:** Bedroom, 9:52 PM, Friday
**Engine:** Comparison

Mira's phone lights up. Instagram story. Then another. Then another.

Everyone's out. Pool party at Emma's. Late night Denny's run. Someone's basement with blanket forts.

Mira's in bed. In pajamas. Scrolling.

She wasn't invited. Didn't even know these things were happening.

Her friend Zoe posted a video. Dancing in someone's kitchen. Six other girls in frame. Mira recognizes three of them from school.

She keeps watching. Story after story. Evidence of a Friday night she's not part of.

Her mom knocks. "Why are you still up?"

"Just on my phone."

"Well, time for bed."

Mira locks her phone. Lies there. Stares at the ceiling.

She has friends. They text her sometimes. Sit with her at lunch.

But Friday nights? Apparently those are for other people.

She tells herself it doesn't matter. That she didn't want to go anyway.

Her chest feels hollow. The kind of empty that doesn't go away when she closes her eyes.

## SCENE L-05 | The Project Partner Problem
**Setting:** Science class, 1:43 PM, Wednesday
**Engine:** Shame

"Pick your partners for the project."

Everyone moves at once. Chairs scraping. People crossing the room.

Zara looks around. Her usual partner—Jenna—already paired with someone else. Didn't even glance her way.

She makes eye contact with Riley. Riley nods at someone else.

Everyone's pairing up. Zara's still sitting.

"Need a partner, Zara?" Her teacher's voice. Kind, but loud enough that everyone hears.

"I'm good."

She's not good. She's the one left over.

The teacher pairs her with Marcus. Marcus who sits in the back and never talks.

They make awkward eye contact. Move their desks together. Don't speak.

Zara pulls out her notebook. Starts writing. Pretends this is fine.

But she knows what just happened. Everyone else had someone. She was the remainder.

## SCENE L-06 | The Discord Server Lurk
**Setting:** Bedroom, 5:34 PM, after school
**Engine:** Watcher

Jordan's in a Discord server. Gaming community. People from school and some older kids.

He's been a member for two months. Never talked in voice chat.

He scrolls through messages. Inside jokes. References to games he doesn't play. Conversations that happened while he was offline.

Someone posts a meme. Three people reply immediately.

Jordan types a response. Deletes it. It's not funny enough. Or maybe it is and he's overthinking.

He hovers over the voice channel. Six people talking. Laughing. Mid-game.

He could join. Just click. Say hi.

His hand doesn't move.

What if no one responds? What if they go quiet when he joins? What if he doesn't know what to say?

He closes Discord. Opens YouTube. Watches someone else play the game.

It's easier this way. Watching. Not participating.

No risk of saying the wrong thing. No risk of being ignored.

No risk of finding out he doesn't actually belong.

## SCENE L-07 | The Moved-Away Friend
**Setting:** Bedroom, 8:16 PM, Sunday
**Engine:** Grief

Dev's best friend moved three months ago. Across the country. Different time zone.

They FaceTime sometimes. Text. But it's not the same.

Tonight Dev texts: "wanna call?"

Three hours pass. No response.

Dev checks Instagram. His friend posted a story. New friends. New school. Skateboarding at some park.

He looks happy. Looks like he's doing fine without Dev.

Dev locks his phone. Tells himself it's good. That his friend made new friends. That he's not supposed to need him anymore.

But Dev's still here. In the same town. Same school. Same lunch table.

Without the one person who actually knew him.

He makes new friends. Tries to. But it's not the same.

No one here knows about the time they snuck out at 2 AM. No one knows why he hates mushrooms or what his middle name actually means.

He's building friendships from scratch while everyone else already has years of history.

His phone buzzes. Finally.

"sorry was at dinner. Tomorrow?"

Dev types back: "yeah sounds good"

Tomorrow in his friend's timezone is late afternoon. Dev will be in class.

They'll figure it out. They always do.

But the gap gets a little wider every week.

And Dev's starting to wonder if long-distance friendship is just slow-motion grief.
