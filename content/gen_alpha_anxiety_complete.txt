# GEN ALPHA STUDENTS - ANXIETY SCENES
## Ages 12-16 | Middle School Context | 2026

---

## SCENE A-01 | The Group Chat Pivot
**Setting:** Bedroom, 8:47 PM, homework half-done
**Engine:** False Alarm

Zara's phone lights up. The group chat she's not in. She can see it in the notification preview—Mia posted a photo. Three laughing emojis already.

She opens Instagram instead. Scrolls. Locks her phone. Unlocks it. The preview is still there.

Her chest feels tight. They're making plans. They made a separate chat without her. Or maybe they didn't—maybe this is the random one from two months ago and she's overthinking.

She opens Spotify so something else will cover the screen. Her homework sits there. Pre-algebra. She's been on the same problem for eleven minutes.

Her mom knocks. "Phone away, honey."

"I'm using it for homework."

She's not. She's watching the typing indicator appear and disappear in a chat she's not part of.

## SCENE A-02 | The Presentation Morning  
**Setting:** Kitchen, 7:12 AM, before school
**Engine:** Spiral

Kai's stomach hurts. The kind that started last night and lived through sleep and woke up with him.

He has to present third period. English. Five minutes on a book he actually read. He practiced in the mirror. Twice. But his brain keeps jumping to the moment where he forgets what comes next and everyone just… stares.

His dad slides a waffle across the counter. "Big day?"

"Just a presentation."

"You'll crush it."

Kai nods. He won't crush it. He'll probably say "um" seventeen times and talk too fast and someone will definitely whisper something.

He checks his phone. His friend Dev hasn't texted. Dev said he'd text before school. Dev probably forgot. Or maybe Dev thinks he's annoying now.

The waffle sits there. His stomach's too tight to eat.

## SCENE A-03 | The Lock Combination  
**Setting:** School hallway, 11:43 AM, between classes
**Engine:** Overwhelm

Riley stands at her locker. She turns the dial. 14… 32… she forgets the third number.

Starts again. 14… 32… 8? No. 6.

Someone bumps her shoulder. Mutters "sorry." Doesn't slow down.

She tries again. The bell's going to ring. She needs her science binder. She has the combination in her phone but her phone's in her backpack and she can't open her locker.

Her hands are shaking a little. Which is stupid. It's just a lock.

She closes her eyes. Breathes. Tries to remember the number she's entered five hundred times.

The hallway's getting louder. Everyone has somewhere to be. She's just standing here, failing at a combination lock like it's fifth grade.

## SCENE A-04 | The Test Hand-Back
**Setting:** Math class, 2:18 PM, Friday afternoon  
**Engine:** Shame

Mr. Patterson walks down the rows, dropping tests face-down on desks. Jordan watches his hand. Tries to read whether the way he puts the paper down means good or bad.

He doesn't look at Jordan when he drops it.

Jordan flips the corner. Red 68. Circle around it. Not just wrong—dramatically wrong.

His face gets hot. He slides the test into his folder before anyone can see. Sophia next to him got a 91. He saw the corner when she picked it up.

He studied. Not a lot, but some. Apparently not enough. Apparently some isn't the same as understanding.

The bell rings. Everyone stands. Jordan stays seated an extra three seconds, pretending to pack his bag, waiting for the heat in his face to go away.

It doesn't.

## SCENE A-05 | The Lunch Table Geography
**Setting:** Cafeteria, 12:04 PM, Monday
**Engine:** Watcher

Maya gets her tray. Chicken tenders. Fries. Chocolate milk. Same as always.

She scans the cafeteria. Her usual table—Priya and Jenna are already there. But there's someone new. A girl from homeroom. Sitting in Maya's spot.

Not on purpose. Probably. But it's where Maya always sits.

She walks over. Stands at the end of the table with her tray. Priya looks up, smiles. "Hey! You know Aisha, right?"

Maya nods. Doesn't know where to sit. There's technically space, but it's weird space. The geometry's wrong.

She sits at the far end. Not in her spot. Just… near her friends. Close enough to hear them but not quite in the conversation.

She opens her milk. Takes a bite of chicken. Chews longer than she needs to.

## SCENE A-06 | The Discord Server Silence
**Setting:** Bedroom, 4:47 PM, after school
**Engine:** Comparison

Tyler opens Discord. The server he joined three weeks ago. Gaming server. Kids from school and some older people.

Last message: two hours ago. Everyone else has moved to voice chat. Seven people in the channel. He can see their icons—talking, laughing, probably mid-game.

He hovers over "Join Voice." His hand doesn't click.

If he joins, he has to say something. If he says something, it better be funny. Or useful. Or at least not awkward.

His cursor hovers. Moves away. Hovers again.

He types in text chat instead: "what are yall playing"

No one responds. They're in voice. They can't see text. Or they can and they're ignoring it.

He closes Discord. Opens TikTok. Scrolls. His brain's still half in that voice channel he didn't join.

## SCENE A-07 | The Group Project Exile
**Setting:** Library, 3:22 PM, Thursday after school
**Engine:** Grief

Anika sits at the library table. Her group project partners—Emma and Chloe—texted the chat: "meeting at public library 3:30."

Anika's at the school library. She didn't know there was a difference. Or maybe they meant this one and they're late.

She checks her phone. 3:37. No new messages.

She opens the group chat. Scrolls up. "Public library." She misread it. Or didn't pay attention. They're at a completely different building.

She could text. Say she messed up. Ask if she should come. But they're probably already working. Showing up late makes it worse.

She stays. Opens the Google Doc they're supposed to be editing. Sees their cursors moving. Adding sentences. Working without her.

She types a sentence. Deletes it. Doesn't know what to add that won't mess up what they're already building.

Closes her laptop. Tells herself it's fine. She'll do her part tonight.

Her chest feels tight anyway.
