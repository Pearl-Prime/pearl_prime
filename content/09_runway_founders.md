# You Have 3 Months of Runway Left (Now What?)

## For: Everyone watching their bank account tick down to zero

**Current bank balance:** $47,000  
**Monthly burn:** $15,000  
**Math:** 3 months left

**Panic level:** Maximum

You've been here before in your mind. Late at night, doing the runway calculations. But now it's real. The countdown clock is ticking.

**Welcome to the most stressful part of building a startup.**

### The Runway Reality

**Every startup faces this moment:**
- Your current funding is running out
- The next round isn't closed yet
- Revenue isn't where it needs to be
- You're about to run out of money

**This either kills the company or forces you to level up.**

### The Stages of Runway Panic

**12 months left:** "We're fine, plenty of time"

**6 months left:** "We should probably start fundraising soon"

**4 months left:** "Okay, now we're actually fundraising"

**3 months left:** *this is where you are* "Oh shit"

**2 months left:** "EVERYTHING IS ON FIRE"

**1 month left:** "Burn it all down or miracle"

**You waited too long. Don't beat yourself up. Fix it now.**

### The Options (None Are Fun)

**Option 1: Raise money**
- Start pitching aggressively
- Close in 3 months (unlikely but possible)
- Dilute equity
- Buy 12-18 more months

**Option 2: Get profitable**
- Cut burn to zero
- Hit break-even
- Grow slower
- Keep control

**Option 3: Bridge round**
- Small round from existing investors
- Buy 6 months
- Worse terms
- Kick the can

**Option 4: Shut down**
- Return money to investors if any
- Pay final salaries
- Move on
- Live to fight another day

**Option 5: Hybrid**
- Cut burn drastically
- Raise smaller amount
- Get to profitability
- Then scale

### The Math You Need to Do Today

**Current situation:**
- Monthly burn: $
- Current runway: X months
- Revenue: $Y
- Growth rate: Z%

**Scenario 1: Cut burn by 50%**
- New runway: 2X months
- What do you cut?
- Can you still operate?

**Scenario 2: Hit default alive**
- What revenue do you need?
- How long to get there?
- Can you reach it in time?

**Do this math. Actually do it. Right now.**

### The Brutal Cuts

**If you need to extend runway, cut:**

**First:**
- All nice-to-haves (office, tools you barely use, subscriptions)
- Travel and conferences
- Contractors and freelancers
- Marketing spend that isn't working

**Second:**
- Reduce founder salaries (to minimum survival level)
- Pause hiring
- Non-essential tools
- Office space → remote

**Third (if desperate):**
- Layoffs
- Freeze all salaries
- Founders go to $0 salary

**Every $1,000/month you cut = 2 weeks more runway**

### The Team Conversation

**When to tell them:**
- Now
- Not when you have 1 month left
- Not when you're letting them go
- Now

**What to say:**
"We have X months of runway. Here's the plan: [specific plan]. Here's what I need from you: [specific asks]. Here's the timeline: [dates]. Questions?"

**Be honest. Be direct. Be clear.**

### The Investor Conversation

**If you're trying to raise:**

**Don't say:** "We're running out of money, please help"  
**Say:** "We've hit [milestone] and are raising to accelerate [growth]. We have runway until [date]."

**Desperation kills deals. Urgency with traction creates them.**

### The "Default Alive" Calculation

**Default alive =** At current growth, you'll reach profitability before running out of money

**Default dead =** At current growth, you'll run out of money before profitability

**Formula:**
- Months to profitability at current growth rate
- Months of runway remaining
- If runway > months to profitability → default alive
- If not → default dead

**If you're default dead, you NEED to raise or cut burn. Pick one.**

### The Revenue Acceleration Plan

**If you're trying to get profitable:**

**What you need:**
- $X in additional monthly revenue
- In Y months
- To reach break-even

**How:**
- Can you increase prices? (fastest)
- Can you upsell existing customers? (fast)
- Can you accelerate sales cycles? (medium)
- Can you launch paid tier? (medium)
- Can you cut costs and lower break-even? (fast)

**Do all of these simultaneously.**

### The Bridge Round Reality

**When to bridge:**
- You're close to next milestone
- Existing investors believe
- You need 3-6 months more
- You have a clear plan

**Terms:**
- Usually worse than last round
- Convertible note or SAFE
- Higher interest or discount
- Sometimes with extra warrants

**A bridge on bad terms > shutting down if you're close.**

### The Layoff Decision

**Signs you might need to layoff:**
- Burn is too high
- Can't raise
- Can't hit revenue targets
- Have redundant roles

**How to do it:**
- Be quick and direct
- Severance if you can afford it (1-2 months)
- Help them find new jobs
- Don't drag it out

**It will feel terrible. Do it anyway if you need to.**

### The Founder Salary Sacrifice

**Your options:**
1. Cut to minimum survival level
2. Cut to zero, live on savings
3. Take on contract work to pay bills
4. Get your partner to support you

**Reality:** You might need to go to $0 for a few months.

**This is why you need a financial cushion before starting.**

### The Creative Options

**Get money fast:**
- Annual prepay deals (12 months up front, discount)
- Sponsored features (customer pays to build what they need)
- Consulting services using your expertise
- Sell non-core assets
- Partner deals with revenue share
- Find strategic angels

**Think sideways. Everything is negotiable.**

### The Metric to Watch

**The one number that matters:**
- Months of runway remaining

**Track weekly:**
- Cash in bank
- Weekly burn
- Revenue growth
- Runway calculation

**This is your countdown clock. Respect it.**

### The Psychological Toll

**You will:**
- Wake up at 3am calculating runway
- Feel like you're failing everyone
- Want to quit
- Stress eat or not eat at all
- Snap at people you love
- Feel hopeless

**This is normal. It's also temporary.**

**Get support:**
- Talk to other founders who've been here
- Therapist if you can afford it
- Your co-founder
- Don't suffer alone

### The Fundraising Sprint

**If you're raising with 3 months left:**

**Week 1-2:**
- Update deck and metrics
- List 50+ potential investors
- Get warm intros
- Schedule 20 meetings

**Week 3-6:**
- Pitch, pitch, pitch
- Follow up aggressively
- Run a tight process
- Create FOMO if possible

**Week 7-12:**
- Close whoever's interested
- Don't be picky (you can't afford to be)
- Get to yes
- Wire the money

**You need to close in 2.5 months. It's possible. It's just really hard.**

### The Profitability Sprint

**If you're going for profitability:**

**Week 1:**
- Cut burn by 30-50%
- Identify all revenue opportunities
- Make the hard calls

**Week 2-4:**
- Launch paid features
- Upsell existing customers
- Increase prices
- Close deals faster

**Month 2-3:**
- Hit break-even
- Then push for profit
- Then breathe

**It's a sprint. Treat it like one.**

### When to Actually Shut Down

**Shut down if:**
- Can't raise after exhausting all options
- Can't get to profitability in time
- The team is burned out beyond recovery
- You don't believe anymore
- The market told you no

**Don't:**
- Keep going on pure denial
- Damage your reputation trying to survive
- Go into personal debt for the company
- Sacrifice your health completely

**Sometimes the right answer is to shut down gracefully.**

### The "One More Month" Trap

**"If we just have one more month..."**

**Reality:**
- One month becomes two
- Two becomes three
- You end up owing people money
- Burning personal relationships
- Damaging your reputation

**If you're going to shut down, do it clean.**

### The Comeback Path

**If you shut down:**
- You can start another company
- Investors invest in repeat founders
- Failure is a credential in Silicon Valley
- You learned more than you would have otherwise

**This isn't the end. It's just the end of this company.**

### What Actually Works

**Companies that survived this:**
- Cut burn aggressively and early
- Got creative with revenue
- Founders took no salary
- Raised bridge rounds on tough terms
- Went remote and lean
- Said no to everything non-essential

**It's brutal. It's possible.**

### Bottom Line

You have 3 months.  
Maybe less if you're unlucky.  
Maybe more if you act now.

**Your options:**
1. Raise money (hard)
2. Get profitable (hard)
3. Bridge (medium)
4. Shut down (hardest emotionally, easiest practically)

**Pick one. Execute. Don't freeze.**

**The only way to fail is to do nothing.**
