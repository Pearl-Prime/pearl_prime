# GEN ALPHA STUDENTS - SHAME SCENES
## Ages 12-16 | Middle School Context | 2026

## SCENE S-01 | The Gym Class Picking
**Setting:** Gymnasium, 10:14 AM, basketball unit
**Engine:** Shame

They're picking teams. Captains standing in the middle. Everyone else lined up against the wall.

Dani's fifth to last picked. Not last—that's something. But close enough that everyone knows she's not athletic. Close enough that when Tyler finally points at her, it's not because he wants her. It's because there's only six people left.

She jogs to his team. Doesn't make eye contact. Someone mutters "we're cooked" under their breath. Could be about her. Could be about someone else.

Probably her.

The game starts. She stays near the back. Doesn't call for the ball. When it comes to her anyway, she passes immediately. Safer that way.

Her face is hot the whole game. Not from running. From being visible in a body that doesn't do what other bodies do.

## SCENE S-02 | The TikTok That Flopped  
**Setting:** Bedroom, 9:33 PM, Saturday night
**Engine:** Comparison

Mira posted the video six hours ago. She planned it all week—outfit, audio, transitions. Practiced the dance fifteen times until her knees hurt.

Forty-three views. Eight likes. Three from her cousins.

She scrolls her For You Page. Everyone else's videos: 2.4K likes, 847 likes, 12.3K likes. Girls her age who move better, look better, have better rooms.

She deletes the video. Reuploads. Changes the caption. Adds hashtags.

Still nothing.

Her phone buzzes. Her friend Zoe: "loved your vid!!"

One comment. From someone obligated to be nice.

Mira locks her phone. Stares at the ceiling. Wonders what she's doing wrong that everyone else is doing right.

## SCENE S-03 | The Wrong Answer Out Loud
**Setting:** History class, 1:47 PM, Wednesday
**Engine:** Shame

Mr. Kim asks the question. Jordan's hand goes up before he's thought it through. Old habit. Participation points.

"Jordan?"

"Uh… the Treaty of Versailles?"

Silence. The bad kind.

"Anyone else?"

Three hands shoot up. Someone says the actual answer. Something about the Marshall Plan. Not even the same war.

Jordan's ears burn. He stares at his desk. His brain knew this. He studied last night. But the question came too fast and he grabbed the first treaty-sounding thing in his head.

Sophia behind him whispers something to her friend. They laugh. Not loud. Just enough.

He doesn't raise his hand again for the rest of class.

## SCENE S-04 | The School Photo Preview
**Setting:** Computer lab, 11:52 AM, photo ordering day
**Engine:** Watcher

They're supposed to pick which school photo they want. The photographer took three. The school uploaded them to the portal.

Kai clicks through. First one: his eyes are half-closed. Second: weird smile, too much teeth. Third: he looks scared.

Everyone else around him is laughing at their photos. Making jokes. Picking the least bad one.

Kai just… stares. He looks wrong in all of them. His face doesn't look like his face. Or maybe it does and that's the problem.

He picks the third one. Closes the tab. Doesn't order any prints.

His mom will ask why. He'll say he forgot.

## SCENE S-05 | The Presentation Grade
**Setting:** Bedroom, 7:18 PM, checking grades online
**Engine:** Spiral

Riley refreshes the grade portal. The presentation grade is finally posted.

C+.

Not failing. But not good. The comment says: "Solid research, but delivery lacked confidence. Work on eye contact and pacing."

Riley read off the slides. She knows she did. She practiced. But her voice shook and she talked too fast and now everyone who was there knows she got a C+.

She closes her laptop. Opens it. Reads the comment again.

Her chest tightens. It's just one grade. Doesn't matter. Except it does because she tried and trying got her a C+.

She texts her friend: "what did u get on the presentation"

"B+ wbu"

"Same"

She lies. Puts the phone face down. Stares at the wall.

## SCENE S-06 | The Instagram Post Comparison
**Setting:** Couch, 5:44 PM, scrolling after school
**Engine:** Comparison

Dev scrolls Instagram. Stops on Aiden's post. Beach photo. California trip. Six-pack abs. 340 likes.

Dev looks down at his own stomach. Soft. Not abs. Not even close.

He keeps scrolling. Soccer team photo. Everyone flexing. Everyone grinning. He's in the back row, half-hidden, arms crossed.

He closes Instagram. Opens it again thirty seconds later. Same photos. Same faces that look better than his.

His mom walks by. "Dinner in ten."

He nods. Doesn't look up. Just keeps scrolling through evidence that other people's bodies work better than his.

## SCENE S-07 | The Borrowing Clothes Moment
**Setting:** Friend's house, 4:12 PM, Saturday hangout
**Engine:** Shame

Maya spills soda on her shirt. Huge stain, right across the front.

Her friend Priya laughs. "Here, borrow one of mine."

Maya follows her to her room. Priya pulls out a crop top. "This one's cute."

Maya takes it. Holds it up. It's… small. Priya's smaller than her. Everything in Priya's closet is smaller.

"I can find something else—"

"No, this is fine."

Maya pulls it on in the bathroom. It fits. Technically. But it's tight across her stomach. The kind of tight that makes her hyper-aware of every breath.

She comes out. Priya smiles. "Looks good!"

Maya nods. Spends the rest of the afternoon holding her arms across her stomach, wishing she'd just lived with the stain.
