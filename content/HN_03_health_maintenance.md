# Healthcare Nurse Self-Help Master Script
## File 3: Health Maintenance - Your Body Is Taking a Beating

### The Physical Toll of Nursing

**You tell patients to take care of their health.**
**You neglect your own.**

12-hour shifts on your feet. Lifting patients. Skipping meals. Holding your bladder. No time to drink water. Interrupted sleep. Physical assault. Exposure to infectious diseases.

Your body is paying the price.

### The Common Nursing Injuries

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Lower back pain (chronic)
- Knee problems
- Shoulder injuries
- Carpal tunnel
- Plantar fasciitis
- Hip pain
- Neck strain

**Vascular:**
- Varicose veins
- Deep vein thrombosis risk
- Circulation problems
- Leg swelling/pain

**Other physical:**
- Chronic bladder infections (holding pee)
- Dehydration (no time to drink)
- Digestive issues (irregular eating)
- Sleep disorders (shift work)
- Weakened immune system (exposure + stress)
- Weight gain or loss
- Chronic pain

**You know all this. You're still doing it.**

### The Immediate Physical Survival

**On shift basics (non-negotiable):**

**Hydration:**
- Water bottle at your station
- Drink every hour (set phone reminder)
- Minimum 8oz per 4 hours
- Not just coffee

**Nutrition:**
- Pack food from home (can't rely on cafeteria timing)
- Protein + complex carbs
- Snacks you can eat in 2 minutes
- Emergency protein bars in locker
- Eat SOMETHING even if rushed

**Bathroom breaks:**
- Go when you first feel the urge
- Don't hold it for hours
- 2-minute bathroom breaks won't destroy patient care
- Chronic UTIs will destroy your health

**Foot care:**
- Compression socks (yes, really)
- Good shoes (invest in them)
- Replace every 6 months
- Stretch calves/feet mid-shift

### Body Mechanics (Save Your Back)

**You know proper lifting technique. Do you use it?**

**Reality check:**
- Always get help for patients >25lbs transfer weight
- Use lift equipment (mechanical lifts, slide sheets)
- Never twist while lifting
- Keep loads close to body
- Squat, don't bend
- If it feels wrong, it is wrong

**When you're short-staffed:**
Better to take an extra minute than be out 6 weeks with injury.

**The cost of "just this once":**
One improper lift = herniated disc = surgery = disability

**Protect your body. No one else will.**

### Sleep (The Impossible Task)

**Shift work destroys circadian rhythms.**

**The reality:**
- Night shift: You'll never fully adjust
- Rotating shifts: Even worse for your body
- 12-hour shifts: Recovery takes 2-3 days

**Survival strategies:**

**Night shift:**
- Blackout curtains (total darkness)
- White noise machine
- Sleep mask, earplugs
- Melatonin (talk to doc first)
- Keep same sleep schedule on days off if possible
- Warn family: You're sleeping, don't disturb

**After night shifts:**
- Don't drive if severely fatigued
- Nap 20-30 min before driving if needed
- Transition slowly back to day schedule

**12-hour shifts:**
- Sleep debt is real
- First day off = recovery day
- 7-9 hours sleep minimum
- Naps are legitimate recovery tools

**Can't sleep:**
- No caffeine after noon
- Screen-free 1 hour before bed
- Cool, dark room
- Consistent bedtime routine
- See doctor if chronic insomnia

### Nutrition (When You Have No Time)

**The problem:**
- 12+ hours without proper meal
- Vending machines and cafeteria food
- Eating in 5 minutes standing up
- Too tired to cook at home
- Stress eating

**Realistic solutions:**

**Meal prep:**
- 1-2 hours on day off
- Make 3-4 meals
- Portable, can eat cold if needed
- High protein, vegetables, complex carbs

**On-shift food:**
- Lunch box with ice pack
- Protein-rich (keeps you full longer)
- Easy to eat quickly
- Doesn't need refrigeration backup option

**Quick options:**
- Protein shakes (30 seconds)
- Nuts, cheese, hard-boiled eggs
- Pre-cut vegetables, hummus
- Protein bars (quality ones)

**After shift:**
- Meal delivery services (if budget allows)
- Crockpot meals (prep in AM, ready when home)
- Pre-made healthy frozen meals
- Don't skip dinner because you're exhausted

### Physical Exercise (Yes, Even Though You're On Your Feet All Day)

**Walking ≠ exercise.**

**You need:**
- Strength training (prevent injuries)
- Cardiovascular (actual heart health)
- Flexibility (prevent tightness/pain)
- Core strength (protect back)

**Minimum effective dose:**
- 2x week strength (20-30 min)
- 2x week cardio (20-30 min)
- Daily stretching (10 min)

**For injury prevention:**
- Deadlifts, squats (lifting patients safely)
- Core work (planks, dead bugs)
- Shoulder strengthening
- Hip mobility
- Glute activation

**Reality check:**
"I'm too tired" is valid.
But exercise actually increases energy long-term.

**Start small:**
- 10-minute walk on days off
- 5-minute stretch routine
- One strength session per week
- Build from there

### Chronic Pain Management

**If you're already hurting:**

**See a professional:**
- Physical therapist
- Chiropractor
- Sports medicine doctor
- Orthopedist if serious

**Don't:**
- Just take ibuprofen forever
- Ignore it until it's severe
- Think "it's just part of nursing"
- Wait until you can't work

**Self-care:**
- Ice/heat as appropriate
- Foam rolling
- Epsom salt baths
- Compression garments
- Proper posture awareness

**Work accommodations:**
- Request ergonomic assessment
- Modified duty if injured
- Equipment that helps (lift assist, etc.)

### Immune System Support

**You're exposed to everything:**

**Protect yourself:**
- Hand hygiene (but you know this)
- Flu shot, COVID vaccine, etc.
- Don't work sick (hard, but necessary)
- Vitamin D (most people deficient)
- Adequate sleep (immune function)
- Stress management (stress kills immunity)

**When you get sick:**
- Actually call in sick
- Rest (actually rest)
- Don't work through it
- You're not helping patients by spreading illness

### Occupational Hazards

**Needle sticks:**
- Follow protocol immediately
- Post-exposure prophylaxis if indicated
- Report it (even if minor)
- Track for pattern

**Chemical exposure:**
- Proper PPE always
- Ventilation
- Report spills
- Document exposure

**Infectious disease:**
- PPE non-negotiable
- If hospital won't provide adequate PPE, that's reportable
- Your health > their budget

**Violence:**
- Report every incident
- Document injuries
- Press charges if assault
- Request safe patient assignment

### The Annual Physical (That You Skip)

**Get your own healthcare:**

**Annual:**
- Full physical
- Blood work
- Age-appropriate screenings
- Mental health screening
- Back/joint assessment

**Don't:**
- Skip because you're "too busy"
- Minimize symptoms
- Self-diagnose and ignore
- Wait until it's an emergency

**Find a provider:**
- Not where you work
- Who takes you seriously
- Who understands nursing demands

### Substance Use Red Flags

**Nurses have high rates of:**
- Alcohol use
- Prescription medication misuse
- Stimulants (to stay awake)
- Sedatives (to sleep)

**Warning signs:**
- Using to cope with shifts
- Using to fall asleep after every shift
- Increasing amounts needed
- Hiding use
- Thinking about it at work
- Using at work

**If this is you:**
- State nursing board peer assistance programs
- Addiction treatment
- Leave of absence if needed
- This is treatable

**Don't lose your license or your life.**

### Burnout Physical Symptoms

**When burnout manifests physically:**

**Common symptoms:**
- Chronic fatigue (not just post-shift tired)
- Frequent illness
- Headaches/migraines
- GI issues
- Chest pain/palpitations
- Muscle tension/pain
- Insomnia
- Changes in appetite

**These are your body screaming that something needs to change.**

### Work-Related Injury Protocol

**If you get hurt at work:**

**Immediately:**
- Report to supervisor
- Fill out incident report
- Seek medical attention (ED or occupational health)
- Document everything

**Follow-up:**
- Workers' comp claim
- Follow all medical advice
- Keep documentation
- Don't let them pressure you back before ready

**Long-term:**
- Physical therapy as recommended
- Modified duty if needed
- Fight for appropriate accommodation
- Consider if this injury changes career trajectory

### The Body Maintenance Schedule

**Daily:**
- Hydrate (8+ glasses)
- Eat meals (3 minimum)
- Bathroom breaks
- Stretch (10 min)
- Sleep (7-9 hours)

**Weekly:**
- Exercise 3-4x
- One full rest day
- Meal prep
- Check in: Any new pain?

**Monthly:**
- Self-massage or professional
- Replace worn shoes
- Assess injury risk factors
- Mental health check

**Yearly:**
- Full physical
- Vision/dental
- Vaccinations
- Career sustainability check

### The Hard Truth About Nursing and Your Body

**Nursing is physically destroying you.**

The research is clear:
- Higher injury rates
- More chronic pain
- More musculoskeletal disorders
- Cardiovascular strain
- Premature physical aging

**This isn't about being tough enough.**
**This is about whether you can physically do this for 20-30 years.**

**Options:**
- Protect your body now (prevention)
- Move to less physical nursing roles
- Leave bedside nursing
- Leave nursing entirely

**All valid choices.**

**The invalid choice:**
Ignoring your body until it forces you to stop.

Take care of the body that takes care of patients.

You only get one.
