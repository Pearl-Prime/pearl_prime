# Creative Block Isn't What You Think It Is

## For: Everyone staring at a blank canvas/screen/page

You're not blocked because you're not talented enough.  
You're not blocked because you've "lost it."  
You're not blocked because the universe is punishing you.

**You're blocked because you're either exhausted, scared, or both.**

### The Real Reasons You Can't Create

**1. You're Burnt Out**
- Creating for money killed creating for joy
- You've been pushing too hard for too long
- Your creative well is empty

**2. You're Terrified**
- Of failing
- Of succeeding
- Of being seen
- Of being judged
- Of not being good enough

**3. You're Over-Stimulated**
- Too much input, not enough processing
- Scrolling instead of making
- Consuming everyone else's work instead of making your own

**4. You're Under-Resourced**
- No time
- No energy
- No space
- No support

**5. You're Perfectionist-Paralyzed**
- It has to be perfect
- It has to be original
- It has to matter
- So you don't start

### What "Block" Actually Means

**Creative block = Your nervous system saying "I need a break"**

It's not laziness. It's not failure. It's your body protecting you from pushing past your limits.

**Listen to it.**

### The Framework: REST

**R - Recognize the real issue**  
Burnout? Fear? Depletion? Name it.

**E - Eliminate pressure**  
Stop trying to make something good. Make something. Period.

**S - Simplify the task**  
Don't write the novel. Write one sentence.  
Don't design the brand. Doodle for 5 minutes.  
Don't compose the symphony. Hum a melody.

**T - Trust the process**  
Creativity is a cycle. Fallow periods are part of it.  
The block will lift. It always does.

### What to Do Right Now

**If you're burnt out:**
- Stop creating for clients/audience for a week
- Make something with zero stakes
- Do a different creative thing (draw if you write, write if you design)
- Take an actual break (yes, guilt-free)

**If you're scared:**
- Make something deliberately bad
- Share something unfinished
- Lower the stakes: "This is just practice"
- Remember: Done is better than perfect

**If you're over-stimulated:**
- Go offline for 24 hours
- Stop consuming other people's work
- Get bored on purpose
- Let your brain wander

**If you're under-resourced:**
- Protect your creative time like it's a meeting
- Ask for help
- Lower your standards temporarily
- Work with what you have

### Permission Slips

You're allowed to:
- Create garbage
- Take breaks
- Make for yourself, not an audience
- Copy/reference/be inspired (that's not stealing)
- Restart abandoned projects
- Abandon projects that aren't working
- Make the same thing over and over until you get it right
- Not be constantly creating

### The Comparison Trap

**What you see:** Their polished final work  
**What you don't see:** Their 47 failed drafts, creative blocks, breakdowns, and impostor syndrome

**Stop comparing your behind-the-scenes to their highlight reel.**

### Bottom Line

Creative block isn't a character flaw. It's a sign you need to adjust something.

**Less pressure.  
More play.  
Lower stakes.  
Actual rest.**

**The work will come. It always does.**

**Just make something.**
