# The Job Search Will Break You (Unless You Treat It Like a Job)

## For: Everyone sending 100 applications into the void

You've sent out 50 applications.  
You've gotten 2 responses.  
Both were rejections.

And you're starting to think: "Maybe I'm just not good enough."

Stop. The job search isn't a meritocracy. It's a numbers game wrapped in chaos.

### The Reality Nobody Tells You

**The stats:**
- 250 applications per job posting
- 2% of applicants get interviews
- 75% of applications are filtered by ATS before a human sees them
- Most jobs are filled through referrals, not applications

**Translation:**  
You can be qualified and still get rejected 98 times.

It's not you. It's the system.

### Why Your Applications Disappear

**You're being filtered out by:**
- Robots scanning for keywords
- Recruiters spending 6 seconds per resume
- "Must haves" that aren't actually must haves
- Someone's nephew getting priority
- Budget freezes
- Re-orgs
- Timing

**None of which have anything to do with your abilities.**

### The Mental Game

The job search will mess with your head:
- Every rejection feels personal
- Every silence feels like failure
- Every "not selected" chips away at confidence

**Remember:**  
You're not getting rejected as a person. A robot is rejecting keywords on a PDF.

### How to Actually Get Hired

**The strategy that doesn't work:**
1. See job posting
2. Send generic application
3. Wait
4. Repeat 100 times
5. Wonder why nothing happens

**The strategy that works:**
1. Find companies you actually want to work for
2. Find people who work there
3. Talk to them like a human
4. Ask about their experience
5. Mention you're interested in opportunities
6. Apply with their referral
7. Get actual human eyes on your application

### The Application Formula

**Resume:**
- Tailored to the job (yes, every time)
- Numbers and metrics, not vague descriptions
- Keywords from the job description
- GitHub link with actual projects
- Keep it to 1 page

**Cover letter:**
- Why this company specifically
- What you'll contribute (not what you'll learn)
- Proof you've done your research
- Keep it to 3 paragraphs max

**Portfolio:**
- 3-5 projects that actually work
- Clean code (not perfect, just clean)
- README that explains what it does
- Deployed and live

### The Networking Reality

"Networking" sounds gross. Do this instead:

**Don't:**
- DM strangers asking for jobs
- Send copy-paste LinkedIn messages
- Fake enthusiasm for companies you hate

**Do:**
- Engage genuinely with people's content
- Ask questions that show you care
- Share what you're learning
- Be helpful when you can
- Build relationships before you need them

People help people they like. Be likeable.

### When to Follow Up

**Email applications:** Never (they won't read it)  
**Referral applications:** After 1 week  
**After interview:** Next day with thank you  
**After final round:** After timeline they gave you

Don't be annoying. Be appropriately persistent.

### The Interview Prep

**Before:**
- Research the company for real
- Prepare stories using STAR method
- Practice talking about your projects
- Prepare questions that show you care
- Test your tech setup

**During:**
- They're evaluating cultural fit more than skills
- Ask questions that matter to you
- Be honest about what you don't know
- Show you can learn and adapt
- Be yourself (they're hiring you, not a character)

**After:**
- Send thank you email
- Mention specific things you discussed
- Wait for timeline they gave
- Keep applying elsewhere

### The Rejection Handling

**Every rejection, ask yourself:**
1. Was I actually qualified? (Be honest)
2. Did I tailor my application?
3. Did I follow up appropriately?
4. What can I improve?

**Then:**  
Improve that thing. Apply to the next one. Move on.

### The Numbers You Need

**For juniors/career changers:**
- 100-200 applications
- 10-20 interviews
- 1-2 offers

**For experienced devs:**
- 30-50 applications
- 5-10 interviews
- 2-3 offers

If you're below these numbers, you haven't applied enough yet.

### What Success Actually Looks Like

**Not this:**
- "I applied to 5 perfect jobs and got 3 offers"
- "I networked for a week and landed my dream job"
- "Companies are fighting over me"

**This:**
- Applied to 150 jobs
- Got 10 phone screens
- 3 technical interviews
- 1 offer
- Negotiated up 15%
- Took it

### When to Adjust

**If you're getting no responses:**  
Your resume/portfolio is the problem. Fix them.

**If you're getting interviews but no offers:**  
Your interview skills are the problem. Practice more.

**If you're getting to final rounds but no offers:**  
You're close. Keep going. The next one might be it.

### The Things You Can Control

**Can't control:**
- How many people apply
- Internal hiring decisions
- Economic conditions
- Whether they like you

**Can control:**
- Quality of your applications
- Number of applications
- Your portfolio projects
- Your interview preparation
- Your follow-up
- Your persistence

Focus on what you control. Accept what you can't.

### The Timeline Reality

**Optimistic:** 1-2 months  
**Realistic:** 3-6 months  
**Totally normal:** 6-12 months

This isn't a sprint. It's a marathon where the finish line keeps moving.

### Permission to Struggle

This is hard.  
It's supposed to be hard.  
Everyone finds it hard.

**You're not:**
- Doing it wrong
- Too slow
- Not good enough
- The only one struggling

**You're:**  
In the middle of a broken process that's hard for everyone.

### Bottom Line

The job search sucks.  
It's not fair.  
It's not fun.  
It's not about merit.

**But it ends when you don't give up.**

Every no gets you closer to a yes.
