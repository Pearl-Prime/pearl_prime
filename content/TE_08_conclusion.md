# You're Going to Make It (Or You're Going to Make a Different Choice - Both Are Okay)

## For: Everyone who's made it this far

You've read about:
- Emotional labor that isn't sustainable
- Classroom management that's impossible
- Workload that doesn't fit work hours
- Admin that doesn't have your back
- Pay that doesn't cover expenses
- Parents who treat you like the enemy
- Mental health that's hanging by a thread

**And you're still here.**

### The Hard Truth

**Teaching is:**
- One of the most important jobs
- Incredibly difficult
- Structurally unsustainable
- Underpaid and undervalued
- Often thankless
- Hazardous to your health

**And also:**
- Sometimes deeply rewarding
- Full of moments that matter
- Capable of real impact
- A genuine calling for some
- Worth it for the right people at the right time

**Both things are true.**

### The Decision Only You Can Make

**Stay if:**
- You can set sustainable boundaries
- Your mental health can handle it
- You find meaning in the work
- The pension/benefits matter to you
- You have good admin/colleagues
- You can make peace with limitations
- You're building toward something

**Leave if:**
- It's destroying your health
- You dread every single day
- The pay makes life impossible
- You've tried everything
- No joy remains
- Better opportunities exist
- You need to survive

**There's no wrong choice. Only your choice.**

### If You Stay: The Survival Plan

**Immediate (this week):**
- Pick one boundary to enforce
- Document one thing you've been avoiding documenting
- Say no to one extra thing
- Leave at contract time one day
- Take one genuine break

**Short-term (this month):**
- Set email boundaries
- Create grading system that's sustainable
- Build relationship with one colleague
- Start therapy if needed
- Review your finances

**Medium-term (this year):**
- Establish non-negotiables
- Build sustainable routines
- Develop support network
- Take all your sick days when needed
- Reevaluate regularly

**Long-term (career):**
- Plan for pension if staying
- Keep exit options open
- Grow professionally only if it serves you
- Check in yearly: still worth it?
- Adjust as needed

### If You Leave: The Exit Plan

**Your skills are transferable:**
- Instructional design ($60,000-$100,000)
- Corporate training ($55,000-$85,000)
- EdTech ($65,000-$120,000)
- Curriculum development ($50,000-$80,000)
- Project management ($65,000-$95,000)
- Learning and development ($60,000-$90,000)

**Steps to leave:**

**1. Explore options**
- What interests you?
- What pays better?
- What uses your skills?
- What protects your mental health?

**2. Update materials**
- Resume (translate teaching experience)
- LinkedIn
- Portfolio if relevant
- References

**3. Apply strategically**
- Target companies/roles
- Network (most jobs aren't posted)
- Use teacher skills in interviews
- Don't undersell yourself

**4. Time it right**
- Finish school year if possible
- Or take semester break
- Financial cushion if you can
- Line up next thing

**5. Actually leave**
- Give notice
- Don't burn bridges
- Process the grief (it's real)
- Move forward

**You're not betraying education by needing to survive.**

### Scripts for Common Situations

**When colleague asks you to do extra:**  
"My plate is full. I can't take that on."

**When admin pressures you:**  
"I'm following the policy. Can you clarify if that's changed?"

**When parent is unreasonable:**  
"Let's schedule a time with admin present to discuss this."

**When you need a mental health day:**  
"I'm taking a sick day today." (No explanation needed)

**When someone says "those who can't do, teach":**  
"Teaching is incredibly difficult work. You should try it." (Or just walk away)

**When you're considering quitting:**  
"I'm evaluating what's sustainable for me long-term."

### What Success Actually Looks Like

**Not this:**
- Perfect teacher with perfect classroom
- Working 80 hours/week
- Saving every student
- Never having problems
- Loving it every day

**This:**
- Surviving sustainable
- Teaching effectively enough
- Helping some students
- Solving most problems
- Having more good days than bad

**Lower the bar. Survive. That's success.**

### For New Teachers (Year 1-3)

**It gets easier.**

Year 1 is survival.  
Year 2 is still hard but you know more.  
Year 3 you start to feel competent.

**But also:**
- Some people never love it
- That's okay
- Three years is enough to know
- Don't martyr yourself

**Give it a fair shot. Then reassess honestly.**

### For Veteran Teachers

**You're allowed to:**
- Be tired
- Not love it anymore
- Consider retirement early
- Change careers even after 15 years
- Rest without guilt
- Not be the teacher you were at 25

**Years of service don't obligate you to stay if it's destroying you.**

### For Teachers Considering Leaving

**The grief is real:**
- You're losing an identity
- Community
- Purpose
- Investment

**Feel it. Process it. Then decide.**

**And know:**
- Leaving doesn't mean you failed
- Your years mattered
- You can still make impact elsewhere
- Your health matters more

### The Questions to Ask Yourself

**Monthly check-in:**
- Can I sustain this?
- Is my health okay?
- Are my boundaries working?
- Am I still growing?
- What needs to change?

**Yearly check-in:**
- Do I still want to be here?
- What would make next year better?
- What's my exit plan if I need it?
- Am I staying for right reasons?

**Be honest. The answers matter.**

### What to Tell Yourself

**When it's hard:**  
"I'm doing my best in a broken system. That's enough."

**When you're burning out:**  
"Taking care of myself helps more students than destroying myself."

**When you feel guilty:**  
"I don't owe anyone my mental health."

**When considering leaving:**  
"My worth isn't determined by staying in a job that's killing me."

**When parents are awful:**  
"Their anger isn't really about me."

**When admin doesn't support you:**  
"I can only control what I can control."

**When you're exhausted:**  
"Rest is not weakness. It's survival."
