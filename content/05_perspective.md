# Gen Z Self-Help Master Script
## File 5: Perspective - Reframe Without Denying Reality

### The Problem with "Just Be Positive"
"Everything happens for a reason" ← Gaslighting
"Good vibes only" ← Toxic positivity  
"Just be grateful" ← Dismissive BS

Real perspective shift acknowledges pain AND finds agency.

### The Dual Reality Framework
Two things can be true at once:
- The system is broken AND I can build skills to navigate it
- I'm struggling AND I'm doing my best
- Life is unfair AND there's still meaning I can create
- I'm anxious about the future AND I can be present now

**The Script:**
"This is hard. I'm not pretending it isn't. AND I get to choose how I respond."

### Perspective Shifts That Actually Help

**VICTIM → AGENT**
❌ "This always happens to me"
✅ "This happened. What's my next move?"

**CATASTROPHE → CONTEXT**  
❌ "I failed, I'm a failure"
✅ "I got one outcome. What did I learn?"

**FIXED → GROWTH**
❌ "I'm just not good at this"
✅ "I'm not good at this YET. What's the next rep?"

**COMPARISON → JOURNEY**
❌ "Everyone else has it figured out"
✅ "I'm seeing their highlight reel, not their struggle. I'm on my own timeline."

### The 10-10-10 Rule
When spiraling, ask:
- Will this matter in 10 minutes? (Usually yes)
- Will this matter in 10 months? (Sometimes)
- Will this matter in 10 years? (Rarely)

Not to minimize your feelings. To right-size the crisis.

### Reframing Common Gen Z Struggles

**"I'm behind in life"**
Behind compared to what? The artificial timeline capitalism sold you? You're exactly where you are. Start from here.

**"I wasted my 20s"**
You survived a pandemic, economic collapse, and social upheaval. You didn't waste anything. You endured.

**"Everyone's more successful"**
They're showing you what they want you to see. You're comparing your behind-the-scenes to their curated performance.

**"The world is ending"**
The world is changing. Some things are ending. That's terrifying. AND humans have survived every prior "end of the world." Your anxiety is valid. Your agency still matters.

### The Gratitude Practice That Doesn't Suck
Instead of "I'm grateful for..." (which can feel forced):

Try: "One small good thing today was..."
- The sun on my face for 30 seconds
- My friend's random meme
- My coffee was actually good
- I didn't doom scroll for an hour

Not to bypass pain. To train your brain to notice good exists alongside bad.

### Perspective Check-In Questions
When you're stuck in a thought loop:
1. Is this thought 100% true? (Usually no)
2. Is this thought helping me? (If no, what would?)
3. What would I tell my younger self about this?
4. What would future me (1 year from now) say?

### The Serenity Prayer, Gen Z Version
"Grant me the audacity to change what I can, the resilience to accept what I can't, and the discernment to know the difference."

Also: therapy. Therapy is good.
