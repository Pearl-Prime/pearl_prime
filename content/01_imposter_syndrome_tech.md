# You're Not Faking It - You're Learning It

## For: Every Gen Z dev who thinks they don't belong

Listen up. That voice in your head saying you're not a "real" developer? It's lying.

You know what makes someone a real developer? They write code. That's it. That's the whole list.

**You don't need to:**
- Know every language
- Have started coding at 12
- Understand legacy systems from the 90s
- Solve LeetCode hards in your sleep
- Build the next Facebook

**You just need to:**
- Show up
- Try
- Google things (yes, senior devs do this too)
- Ask questions
- Learn from mistakes

### The Truth About "Real Developers"

That senior dev who seems to know everything? They're Googling "how to center a div" just like you. They've just been doing it longer.

The tech lead who confidently makes decisions? They're guessing too. Educated guessing, but still guessing.

The difference isn't talent. It's just time.

### What Imposter Syndrome Actually Means

If you feel like an imposter, it means you care about being good. People who genuinely don't belong? They don't worry about it.

Your anxiety is proof you give a damn. That's actually the qualification.

### Do This Instead

**When the voice says:** "Everyone here is smarter than me"  
**You say:** "Everyone here knows different things than me"

**When the voice says:** "I should quit before they find out"  
**You say:** "They hired me knowing I'd learn on the job"

**When the voice says:** "I don't deserve this opportunity"  
**You say:** "I'm making the most of this opportunity"

### The Real Flex

The real flex isn't knowing everything. It's admitting when you don't know something and figuring it out anyway.

Being vulnerable enough to ask "dumb" questions? That's courage.  
Saying "I don't understand, can you explain it differently?" That's strength.  
Googling basic syntax you forgot? That's being human.

### Bottom Line

You're not an imposter. You're a beginner. Or intermediate. Or even advanced in some things and beginner in others.

That's not a flaw. That's how learning works.

The only way to fail at tech is to stop trying.

Everything else? That's just Tuesday.
