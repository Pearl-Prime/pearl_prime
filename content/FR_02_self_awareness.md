# First Responder Self-Help Master Script
## File 2: Self-Awareness - Know What You're Carrying

### The Invisible Load
Every call adds weight. Most you shake off. Some stick.

After years on the job:
- The pediatric codes
- The DOAs who remind you of someone
- The ones you couldn't save
- The senseless violence
- The preventable deaths
- The smell, the sounds, the faces

You don't forget. You just learn to function anyway.

### What Cumulative Trauma Actually Is
It's not one big incident. It's death by a thousand cuts.

Your nervous system is designed to handle acute stress (the call) and then reset. But when you're running multiple calls per shift, year after year, your body never fully resets.

**The result:**
- Baseline stress level keeps climbing
- Smaller things trigger bigger reactions
- You're always at 60% activation, never truly at rest
- Emotional numbness or sudden outbursts
- Physical symptoms with no medical cause

### The Self-Check
Answer honestly (nobody's grading you):

**Sleep:**
- Trouble falling asleep? 
- Nightmares/replaying calls?
- Waking up exhausted?

**Mood:**
- Irritable over small things?
- Feeling nothing (emotional flatness)?
- Sudden rage or sadness out of nowhere?

**Physical:**
- Tension headaches, jaw clenching?
- Stomach issues, chest tightness?
- Always on alert, can't relax?

**Behavior:**
- Drinking more than you used to?
- Avoiding people, places, things?
- Reckless behavior (driving, risk-taking)?

**Relationships:**
- Withdrawing from family/friends?
- Snapping at people you care about?
- Feeling like nobody gets it?

**3+ yes answers:** Your load is heavy. Time to actively address it.
**5+ yes answers:** Your system is overwhelmed. Professional help recommended.

### The Honest Conversation With Yourself

**"I'm fine" Translation Guide:**
- "I'm fine" = I'm not fine but I can't talk about it
- "It's part of the job" = I'm normalizing something harmful
- "I've seen worse" = I'm comparing instead of processing
- "Everybody deals with this" = I'm suffering in silence with everyone else

**Better Scripts:**
"I'm carrying more than I realized."
"This job is affecting me and that's not weakness."
"I need to deal with this before it deals with me."

### What Normal Looks Like vs. What's Not

**NORMAL (Adaptive):**
- Brief stress response during/after critical incidents
- Able to decompress and reset within 24-48 hours
- Can separate work from home
- Still feel joy, connection, humor
- Alcohol/substances in moderation
- Sleep mostly works

**NOT NORMAL (Maladaptive):**
- Constantly on edge, waiting for the next call
- Can't turn it off at home
- Emotional numbness or extreme reactions
- Using substances to cope regularly
- Sleep is broken (chronic insomnia, nightmares)
- Intrusive thoughts about calls days/weeks later
- Avoiding things that used to bring joy
- Suicidal thoughts (even fleeting)

### The Types of Trauma You Carry

**Direct Trauma:**
What you witnessed personally. The calls that stick.

**Vicarious Trauma:**
Absorbing others' pain repeatedly. Changes how you see the world.

**Moral Injury:**
When you had to do/not do something that violated your values:
- Couldn't save someone due to protocols/resources
- Saw injustice and couldn't fix it
- Were forced to choose who gets help first
- Witnessed preventable suffering

**Betrayal Trauma:**
When the department/system/leadership fails you:
- Admin throws you under the bus
- Politics over people
- No support after critical incidents
- Retaliation for speaking up

### The Body Keeps Score
Trauma isn't just mental. It lives in your body:
- Chronic muscle tension (shoulders, jaw, back)
- GI issues (IBS, acid reflux)
- Heart racing at rest
- Panic attacks
- Autoimmune issues
- Chronic pain

Your body is trying to tell you something. Listen.

### Daily Self-Awareness Practice

**After Shift Check-In (2 minutes):**
1. What's my activation level? (1-10)
2. What call is still with me?
3. Do I need to process this or can I let it go?
4. What does my body need right now?

**Weekly Pattern Check (5 minutes):**
1. How's my sleep been this week?
2. Mood trends - flat? Irritable? Normal range?
3. Drinking/substance use - same, more, less?
4. Relationships - connecting or withdrawing?
5. Physical - pain, tension, GI issues?

### When to Escalate
**Get help NOW if:**
- Suicidal thoughts (even passive "wouldn't care if I didn't wake up")
- Self-harm urges
- Substance use spiraling
- Can't function at work
- Violent urges toward others
- Complete emotional shutdown

**Resources:**
- 988 then press 1 (Veterans Crisis Line - they get first responders too)
- Fire/EMS Helpline: 1-888-731-3473
- Copline: 1-800-267-5463 (for police)
- First Responder Support Network

**Get help SOON if:**
- Check-in shows consistent problems for 2+ weeks
- Using alcohol daily to sleep
- Avoiding things you used to enjoy
- Family/friends expressing concern
- Physical symptoms without medical explanation

### The Cultural Barrier
"Real firefighters/cops/medics don't need help."

**The truth:**
Real first responders recognize when they're compromised and get their equipment serviced. Your brain is equipment. Maintain it.

Asking for help isn't weakness. It's tactical intelligence.

You wouldn't go into a fire with broken gear. Don't go through life with a broken nervous system.
