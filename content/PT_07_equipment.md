# Parents of Teens Self-Help Master Script
## File 7: Equipment - Give Yourself What You Need to Survive This

### You're Running on Empty
You're managing their emotions while suppressing yours.
You're staying calm while you're terrified.
You're being patient when you want to scream.
You're holding boundaries while grieving the child they used to be.

Who's taking care of you?

### The Oxygen Mask Principle

**You can't parent well from depletion.**

This isn't selfish. This is survival.

If you burn out, everyone suffers.
Taking care of yourself IS taking care of your teen.

### The Emotional Toll Inventory

**What parenting teens does to you:**

**Grief:**
- The child who loved you unconditionally
- Being their whole world
- The closeness you used to have
- Their innocence
- Your role as protector

**Fear:**
- What if they get hurt?
- What if they make a terrible choice?
- What if I'm failing them?
- What if something happens to them?
- What if our relationship never recovers?

**Rejection:**
- Their casual cruelty
- Choosing friends over you
- Eye rolls and door slams
- The way they used to light up when you came home
- Feeling unwanted in their life

**Inadequacy:**
- Am I doing this right?
- Should I be stricter/looser?
- Why can't I reach them?
- Other parents seem to have it together
- I have no idea what I'm doing

**All of this is normal.**
**All of this needs processing.**

### Self-Care (That Actually Works)

**Not the Instagram version:**
Bubble baths and wine won't solve this.

**What actually helps:**

**Physical:**
- Sleep (prioritize it)
- Exercise (burns stress hormones)
- Nutrition (stress eating is real, but your body needs fuel)
- Medical care (don't neglect your health)

**Emotional:**
- Therapy (you need support too)
- Journaling (process the feelings)
- Crying when needed (release valve)
- Talking to friends who get it

**Mental:**
- Breaks from parenting (yes, you're allowed)
- Hobbies unrelated to kids
- Learning something new
- Time to think your own thoughts

**Spiritual (however you define it):**
- Meaning-making
- Connection to something larger
- Practices that ground you
- Community

**The minimum:**
- 7+ hours sleep
- 20 minutes of movement daily
- One thing each week just for you
- Support system you actually use

### The Support System You Need

**You need different types of support:**

**Peers (other parents of teens):**
- They get it
- No judgment
- Share strategies
- Commiserate

**Friends (without teens):**
- Remind you of who you are beyond parent
- Different conversations
- Perspective

**Partner (if you have one):**
- Co-regulation
- Shared burden
- Someone who loves this kid too
- Connection

**Professional:**
- Therapist (for processing)
- Parenting coach (for strategies)
- Support groups

**Family:**
- Your parents (if supportive)
- Siblings
- Extended family

**Build this before you're in crisis.**

### The Marriage/Partnership Piece

**If you're parenting with a partner:**

**Parenting teens stresses relationships:**
- Disagreements about parenting
- Less time together
- Different stress responses
- Resentment building
- Intimacy decreasing
- Only talking about the kids

**Protecting your relationship:**

**Weekly:**
- One conversation not about the kids
- Physical connection (even just hand-holding)
- Appreciation expressed

**Monthly:**
- Date night (yes, really)
- Check-in: How are WE doing?
- Reconnect

**The co-parenting alignment:**
- Present united front (even if disagree privately)
- Don't undermine each other
- Support each other's decisions
- Debrief privately
- Regular check-ins on approach

**If you're struggling:**
Couples therapy. Now. Not when it's destroyed.

### The Single Parent Reality

**If you're doing this alone:**

**It's harder:**
- No one to tag out with
- All decisions yours
- No one to debrief with
- Financial stress
- Exhaustion

**What helps:**

**Build your team:**
- Other family members
- Friends who'll help
- Other single parents
- Hired help if possible
- School counselors/support

**Be realistic:**
- You can't do everything
- Some things will be "good enough"
- It's okay to ask for help
- Your teen sees your effort

**Self-care is non-negotiable:**
You have no backup. You must maintain yourself.

**Give yourself credit:**
You're doing an incredibly hard thing.

### The Guilt Management

**You will feel guilty about:**
- Everything you're doing wrong
- Not being patient enough
- Losing your temper
- Not being the parent you planned to be
- Needing breaks
- Not enjoying every moment
- Feeling relieved when they're not home

**The truth:**
- Perfect parents don't exist
- "Good enough" is actually good enough
- You're human
- They'll be fine despite your imperfections
- Your mistakes are teaching moments

**The mantra:**
"I'm doing my best with what I have right now."

### The Comparison Trap

**Stop comparing:**
- Your family to other families
- Your teen to other teens
- Your parenting to social media parents
- This stage to when they were little
- Yourself to impossible standards

**The reality:**
- Every family struggles
- Social media is highlights only
- Other parents are also confused
- Comparison kills joy

**Focus on:**
Your family. Your values. Your teen. Your relationship.

That's all that matters.

### The Triggering Effect

**If your teen triggers your own trauma:**

**Common triggers:**
- If you had difficult teen years
- If your parents were harsh/absent
- If you have unresolved issues
- If their behavior reminds you of something painful

**What happens:**
Your reaction is disproportionate because it's not just about now.

**What to do:**
- Recognize when you're triggered
- Take a break before responding
- Process your own trauma in therapy
- Don't make your teen responsible for your healing

**The script (to yourself):**
"I'm reacting to my past, not just this moment. Let me separate those."

### The Anger Management

**You will get angry:**
- At their choices
- At their attitude
- At the situation
- At how hard this is
- At them (temporarily)

**Anger is normal. Acting on it destructively isn't.**

**When angry:**

**In the moment:**
- Walk away
- Deep breaths
- Don't say what you can't take back
- "I need a minute" (then take it)

**Later:**
- Process why you were so angry
- Repair if you said something hurtful
- Address the issue when calm
- Figure out if anger is about them or you

**If anger is constant:**
Therapy. Your teen shouldn't bear the brunt of your unprocessed emotions.

### The Loneliness of It

**Parenting teens is lonely:**
- They don't need you the same way
- Your role is diminishing
- Friends without teens don't get it
- Your life revolves around someone who doesn't want you around

**This is a genuine loss.**

**What helps:**
- Name it (you're grieving)
- Connect with other parents in this stage
- Rebuild your own identity
- Invest in your own relationships
- Remember it's a transition, not permanent

### The Identity Recovery

**You're not just "[Teen's name]'s parent":**

**Who are you?**
- What do you enjoy?
- What are you good at?
- What matters to you?
- Who were you before kids?
- Who are you becoming?

**Rebuilding:**
- One hobby
- One interest
- One connection
- One thing that's just yours

**Start small. Start now.**

In a few years, they'll launch. Who will you be then?

### The Permission Slips

**You need permission to:**

✓ Not enjoy every moment of parenting teens
✓ Feel relief when they're at a friend's house
✓ Miss who they used to be
✓ Be sad about the relationship changing
✓ Need a break
✓ Make mistakes
✓ Not always like them (while still loving them)
✓ Prioritize your marriage/partnership
✓ Have your own life
✓ Ask for help
✓ Say "I don't know"
✓ Go to therapy
✓ Not be perfect
✓ Be tired of the drama
✓ Want your house to yourself sometimes

**Consider this permission granted.**

### The Crisis Plan

**For when you're overwhelmed:**

**Immediate (today):**
- 10-minute break minimum
- Deep breathing
- Call a friend
- Don't make big decisions

**This week:**
- Extra sleep
- One thing just for you
- Therapy appointment
- Support group

**Ongoing:**
- Regular self-care
- Support system activated
- Boundaries enforced
- Help requested

**Emergency:**
If you're having thoughts of harming yourself or your teen:
- National Crisis Hotline: 988
- Leave the situation
- Get immediate help
- You need support now

### The Survival Mode Management

**Sometimes you're just in survival mode:**
- Getting through the day
- Can't think long-term
- Just trying not to lose it
- Barely functioning

**This is temporary. This is okay.**

**Survival mode basics:**
- Sleep
- Eat
- Minimal self-care
- Ask for help
- Lower expectations
- Get through today

**When you're through crisis:**
Rebuild. Don't live in survival mode permanently.

### The Friend/Supporter Role

**For your friends/family supporting you:**

**What helps:**
- Listening without judgment
- Normalizing the struggle
- Offering specific help
- Not giving unsolicited advice
- Being available
- Remembering to ask how you are

**What doesn't help:**
- "It goes so fast, enjoy it"
- "At least they're not..."
- "Have you tried..."
- "My kid never..."
- Disappearing when it's hard

**Tell people what you need.**
Not everyone knows how to support a parent of teens.

### The Long Game Self-Care

**This phase will end:**

**In the meantime:**
- Maintain yourself
- Build support
- Process emotions
- Keep perspective
- Take care of your body
- Nurture your relationships
- Develop your own interests

**After they launch:**
You want to still be standing.
You want a life to return to.
You want a relationship with yourself.

**Invest in that now.**

### The Maintenance Plan

**Daily:**
- 7+ hours sleep
- 10 minutes to yourself
- One deep breath when triggered
- Gratitude for one thing (even small)

**Weekly:**
- Exercise 3x
- Connect with support person
- One thing just for you
- Check in with partner

**Monthly:**
- Self-assessment: How am I doing?
- Therapy/support group
- Date night or friend time
- Something that brings joy

**Quarterly:**
- Bigger break (weekend away, day off, etc.)
- Life audit: What's working, what's not?
- Adjust approach as needed

### The Final Truth

**You can't do this well while running on empty.**

The better you take care of yourself:
- The more patient you can be
- The clearer your thinking
- The healthier your relationship
- The better you model self-care
- The more you have to give

**This isn't selfish.**
**This is essential.**

Put on your own oxygen mask.

Then help them with theirs.

You both need you to be okay.

Make that a priority.
