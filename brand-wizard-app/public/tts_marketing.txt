[EN | landing]
Email and freebie strategy. The proof loop sequence E1 through E5: welcome email with free chapter, value content demonstrating the book's method, soft sell with social proof, testimonials and reviews, then the offer. Eighty percent value content, twenty percent promotion — wellness audiences have the highest unsubscribe rates of any vertical.

[EN | landing]
Freebie performance: workbook companions increase completion rates by forty-nine percent. Ebooks are the most popular lead magnet — twenty-seven-point-seven percent of marketers use them. But checklists and guides convert at sixty-seven-point-two percent — higher than full ebook previews. Keep free content under thirty pages. Bundle free chapter with a companion worksheet for higher perceived value.

[EN | landing]
The freebie CTA funnel: freebie download captures email, proof loop E1 through E5 nurtures trust, ninety-nine cent challenge book makes first sale, standard ebook upsell follows, then premium deep book or series. Every one thousand email subscribers equals fifty to one hundred fifty sales per launch. The email list is the single most valuable marketing asset.

[EN | landing]
ARC strategy: twenty to fifty advance review copies per debut title. Expect twenty to forty percent to post reviews. Books with fifty-plus Amazon reviews at launch sell roughly two times more copies. Start ARC distribution four to six weeks before publication. Use BookFunnel, StoryOrigin, or NetGalley.

Fleet rollout plan. Do not run ads on all three hundred twelve brands at once. The phased approach:

[EN | landing]
Phase 1: ten brands at five hundred dollars each. Total monthly budget: five thousand dollars. Duration: three months. Goal: identify the top three to five performing brands and channels. Test across at least three platforms per brand. Kill underperformers fast.

[EN | landing]
Phase 2: twenty brands. Budget per brand: five hundred to one thousand dollars — winners get more. Total monthly: ten thousand dollars. Duration: three months. Goal: scale winners, kill non-performers. Double down on the platforms and creative that delivered the best ROAS in Phase 1.

[EN | landing]
Phase 3: fifty brands. Budget per brand: one thousand to five thousand dollars, tiered by performance. Total monthly: fifty thousand dollars. Goal: full scale with optimized allocation. By this phase, you know which brands, which platforms, which creative formats, and which audiences convert. The fleet runs on data, not guesses.
