## RECOGNITION v01
---

BAND: 4
---

Priya posts in #general at 10:47am. Three sentences about the Q2 rollout timeline. She has been at the company four months. She has been waiting to say something smart in #general.

Daniel replies forty seconds later. In the thread, public, for everyone to see: "Actually the deadline moved to the 14th last Tuesday. Priya—check the Notion page."

The period after her name lands somewhere in her chest.

She sees it on her laptop. Then on her phone. Same words. Period after her name. The channel has seen it. Whoever opens #general today will see it. The thread is right there. Her three sentences and then Daniel's correction and then the period after her name like a closed door.

She doesn't reply. Her face gets hot. She opens a new tab. Closes it. Opens Notion. Finds the updated deadline. Stares at the 14th. She was in that meeting. She typed the wrong date anyway.

Her shoulders pull in. Not a lot. Just enough to make herself smaller at her desk.

She will not reply in the thread. She does not know how to reply in a way that doesn't make it worse. So the thread will sit there. Daniel's correction will sit there. The period after her name will sit there. And whoever scrolls #general this afternoon will read it and know.

She opens a Google Doc. Types nothing.

## RECOGNITION v02
---
path: atoms/gen_z_professional/self_worth/shame/CANONICAL.txt
BAND: 3
---

The all-hands is forty people on Zoom. Marcus has his camera on. He has been at the company six weeks. His manager told him last week that leadership wants to hear more from new hires.

Nadia, the CEO, says: "Marcus, you were in the client call yesterday. What was their main concern?"

He knows this. He was there. He took notes. The notes are in a tab somewhere behind the three other tabs he has open right now. His mind goes flat. Not blank exactly. More like a surface with no grip. His mouth opens.

"I think—the timeline was part of it."

Nadia nods. "Sure, but what was the core concern?"

Twelve seconds of silence. Not twelve. Maybe four. But inside those four seconds, forty cameras. Forty people watching the little rectangle with his name and his face and the expression that is trying very hard to look like he is thinking and not like he has forgotten everything he heard in a fifty-minute call less than twenty-four hours ago.

"Implementation costs," says Dev, who was also on the call. "They kept coming back to the integration fees."

Nadia says, "Right, yes." Moves on.

Marcus's camera is still on. His face is hot. He does not reach up to touch it. He does not look away from the screen. He holds very still, the way you hold still when you think stillness might make you invisible, even though forty cameras are still on and his name is still at the bottom of his little box and Dev just said the thing Marcus could not remember and everyone heard it.

The call continues for twenty-two more minutes. Marcus does not say another word.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: atoms/gen_z_professional/self_worth/shame/CANONICAL.txt
BAND: 3
---

The first time, it was the sprint retro. Jonah said something about the backend delay and looked at Zoe and Zoe's throat went tight and her face got warm and she looked at her laptop and said "yeah, we're looking into it."

The second time, it was the client update call. She mispronounced the client's company name. Once. Out loud. In front of three people who definitely knew the right pronunciation. Her chest dropped. She kept talking. Her voice stayed even. Nobody said anything.

The third time, it was Slack. She sent a message to the wrong channel. Not a bad message. Not embarrassing content. Just visibly the wrong channel. A message that was meant for her team's private channel landing in #product-all at 6:43pm on a Thursday. She deleted it in eleven seconds. Enough people were online to see the notification. The message was gone. The notification was not.

Three times. Different rooms. Different people. Different kinds of wrong.

The same collapse every time.

Throat first. Then chest. Then the specific way her shoulders move inward without her telling them to. Then the heat in her face. Then the calculation: who saw, how many, what do they think now.

The collapse does not check first to see if the moment is big enough. It fires for mispronunciations. It fires for wrong channels. It fires for the look someone gives when your answer is incomplete. It fires for everything. Reliable. Precise. Indifferent to scale.

By the third time, Zoe recognizes it the way you recognize a smell. Before she can name it. Before she decides anything. It is already there.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: atoms/gen_z_professional/self_worth/shame/CANONICAL.txt
BAND: 2
---

After the presentation, Devin goes to the bathroom on the 12th floor. The one nobody uses. Stands at the sink. Cold water on his wrists. The presentation was not a disaster. It was not good. His voice got thin in the middle. He skipped a slide. Lauren from product asked a question he did not fully answer and he saw her write something down after he finished and he does not know what she wrote.

He is at the sink when Cam from his cohort comes in.

Cam washes his hands. Looks at Devin in the mirror. "You okay?"

Devin says: "Yeah."

Cam says: "I threw up before my first product review."

The sentence just sits there between them.

Cam dries his hands. "Not before. During a break. In this bathroom actually." He gestures at the stall. "Lauren asked me something and I completely blanked. Like, for a real amount of time. She started to rephrase it and I still had nothing."

Devin looks at his own face in the mirror. The heat in his face has not gone away but something has shifted slightly underneath it. Not gone. Shifted.

"What happened?" he says.

"Nothing happened. She moved on. Nobody cared as much as I did." Cam shrugs. "Still think about it though."

He leaves. Devin stands at the sink another minute. The heat is still in his face. His chest is still low. The not-good presentation still exists. Lauren still wrote something down.

But Cam threw up in this bathroom. And Lauren moved on. And Cam still thinks about it.

Something is slightly smaller than it was. Not fixed. Smaller.

## EMBODIMENT v01

The presentation ends at 4:17pm. The room is the glass-walled one on the 9th floor and everyone can see in from the open floor and that was true during the presentation too, which Simone did not think about when she agreed to this room.

The Q&A goes eleven minutes. She answers most of it. On one question, about the data source, she says "I'll follow up on that" and the person who asked writes something on their notepad and Simone watches them write it and does not know what they are writing.

At 4:28pm it is over. People stand up. Some of them say good job. They say it the way people say good job. Simone says thank you the way people say thank you.

At 4:31pm she picks up her laptop and her notebook and her water bottle and she takes the stairwell instead of the elevator. Down two flights to 7, where her team sits, where she does not have to walk back through the main floor past the people who were in the glass room.

She sits at her desk. Opens her laptop. The presentation is still in her recent files. She does not open it. She opens her email instead. Reads three emails she already read this morning. Refreshes her inbox. Reads them again.

Her shoulders are still pulled in. She does not try to straighten them. She just stays here. Small at her desk. Laptop open. Not working. Waiting for the heat in her face to go away on its own.

At 5:03pm she puts her headphones on. She does not play anything. Just the headphones. Just the signal: not available. Not here. Leave me alone.

## EMBODIMENT v02
---
path: atoms/gen_z_professional/self_worth/shame/CANONICAL.txt
BAND: 4
---

After the all-hands, the team goes to the rooftop bar on 27th. It was planned. Nadia said everyone should come. It is the kind of thing you are supposed to go to.

Ryan goes.

He gets a drink. Stands near the railing. The city is doing what the city does at 6pm on a Thursday. The Hudson is visible if you look left. Ryan looks left. His face is still warm from the call. From the thing he said in the breakout room that landed wrong. Not devastatingly wrong. Just wrong enough that two people exchanged a look and he saw the look and they did not know he saw the look.

Someone from finance whose name Ryan doesn't know starts talking to him about the Knicks. Ryan talks about the Knicks. He is talking about the Knicks and watching the door to the stairwell over this person's shoulder.

At 6:22pm his phone buzzes. It is nothing. A newsletter. He looks at it for longer than he needs to. Long enough that the finance person's attention drifts. Long enough for the conversation to close without Ryan having to close it.

He keeps his phone out after that. Thumb moving. Not reading anything. Just the phone in his hand as evidence of somewhere else to be. Of something that requires his attention. Of not standing here with nothing to do but be in this body at this party where two people exchanged a look.

At 6:41pm he says he has an early morning. He says it to the person closest to him who is not in his immediate team. He leaves through the stairwell. The door closes behind him.

The look is still in his chest. He takes the stairs all the way down. All twenty-seven flights. His legs ache by the 10th floor. He keeps going.
