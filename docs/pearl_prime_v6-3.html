Market entry · How to operate in China

China requires
a Chinese company.
Here's how it works.

Western platforms like Google Play and Findaway cannot distribute inside mainland China at all — even through a Chinese company. Distribution in China means using China's own platforms: Ximalaya (China's Audible — 345M users), Dedao (China's MasterClass — 50M paying customers), WeChat Read (ebook reader inside WeChat, 1.3B users), and NetEase Cloud Music (expanding into audiobooks).

To upload to these platforms, you need a Chinese-registered company. You have three paths:

This is not a barrier — it is the entry point to 1.4 billion people and the world's fastest-growing audio content market.
Option A — Use a company you already have
If you or a partner already owns a Chinese company
Register it as a content creator on Ximalaya, Dedao, WeChat Read, and NetEase. Pearl Prime provides the content. You upload it through your existing entity. Fastest path — can start immediately.
Option B — Create your own Chinese company
A WFOE — a company you fully own, registered in China
A Wholly Foreign-Owned Enterprise (WFOE) is a Chinese company that you — as a foreigner — own 100%. You set it up through a Chinese business attorney. Takes 3–6 months. Pearl Prime connects you with trusted legal partners who handle the process. Best for long-term control and revenue ownership.
Option C — Work through a licensed Chinese distributor
A distribution partner holds platform accounts and uploads on your behalf
This partner — called a Multi-Channel Network (MCN) agent — already has verified accounts on Ximalaya, Dedao, and WeChat Read. You provide the content. They handle uploading, platform compliance, and account management. Your revenue flows from the platforms through them to you. Fastest way to start — no company setup required.
