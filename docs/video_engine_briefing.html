5-Layer Image Building

Every frame is built from 5 separate layers, stacked to create depth and cinematic quality. Each layer can move independently to create a parallax 3D effect.

5

Color grading, vignette, cinematic grain. Sets the overall mood.

4

Particles, bokeh, atmospheric effects. Moves at 2x speed for depth. Fireflies, rain, dust motes.

3

Main character or subject. Ken Burns zoom at 2%/sec. The focal point of every scene.

2

Six Hidden Mechanisms

💬

Breathing Entrainment

Visual elements pulse at 6 breaths per minute — the rate that maximizes heart rate variability and vagal tone. Viewers' breathing unconsciously synchronizes with the subtle scale changes on screen. They never notice it happening.

23 studies show HRV improvement from slow-paced breathing
PMC 2025 Narrative Review of Breathwork
🌈

Color Temperature Arcs

Every video follows a 5-phase color journey: warm engagement tones at the start, gradually shifting to cool blues and greens during the calming resolution. Your autonomic nervous system responds to these palette shifts without conscious processing.

Darker/muted colors increase parasympathetic activity (higher HRV)
Applied Sciences, 2024 — ASMR + Color Research
🌳

Fractal Nature Backgrounds

Backgrounds use natural fractal patterns (trees, clouds, water) at a specific complexity level (FD 1.3-1.5) that research shows triggers maximum stress reduction. Every calming section is backed by these patterns.

Up to 60% stress reduction from mid-complexity fractals
Taylor, University of Oregon — Fractal Fluency Research
🔇

Strategic Silence

Deliberate silence periods are placed throughout longer videos. Research shows silence between music sections drops heart rate and blood pressure below baseline — silence is more regulatory than the music itself.

Heart rate drops below baseline during 2-min silence
Bernardi et al. — Cardiovascular Effects of Music
🎵

Pentatonic Therapeutic Music

All calming music uses the pentatonic scale — a universal musical structure found across every world culture. Research shows pentatonic melodies reduce sympathetic nervous system activity. Tempo follows the video arc: faster during hooks, slowing to 60-66 BPM in resolution.

Pentatonic scale reduces sympathetic activity
Frontiers in Psychology, 2024
🎥

Edit Rhythm Regulation

The pace of cuts in each video follows a therapeutic arc. Fast cuts in the hook to grab attention, gradually slowing to long, unbroken shots in the resolution. Viewers' respiration entrains to the cut rhythm — one cut every 10 seconds matches 6 breaths per minute.

Scene cuts at specific rhythms entrain viewer respiration
Frontiers in Neuroscience, 2023

The 5-Section Therapeutic Arc

Every video 60 seconds or longer follows this structure. The viewer experiences an engaging story. Their body experiences a complete regulation cycle.

0-15%

Hook — Grab Attention

Fast/medium cuts (2-4 sec). Warm colors (3200-4000K). 80-100 BPM music. Mild activation to draw the viewer in.

15-55%

Build — Tell the Story

Medium cuts (4-6 sec). Neutral colors. 72-90 BPM music gradually slowing. Narrative progression.

55-70%

Peak — Emotional Weight

Slowest cuts (8-12 sec). Cool colors begin. 60-72 BPM. Maximum emotional weight. Nature fractals appear.

70-85%

Release — Calm the Body

Slow pans, nature footage, wide shots (8-15 sec). Cool palette. 60-66 BPM pentatonic. Strategic silence. Parasympathetic activation.

85-100%

Resolve — Complete Regulation

Static or slow dolly-out. Unbroken shots 15+ sec. Warm glow. 54-66 BPM fading to silence. Longest held note. Heart rate below baseline.
