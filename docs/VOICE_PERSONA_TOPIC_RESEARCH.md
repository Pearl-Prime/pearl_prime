## 1. Voice Psychology Foundations

Voice parameters directly affect listener trust and engagement in therapeutic audio:

- **Pitch:** Lower pitch correlates with perceived authority and calm. Higher pitch signals approachability and energy. Therapeutic content benefits from slightly below-average pitch.
- **Pace (BPM):** 120-140 BPM reading pace is optimal for contemplative content. Below 120 risks losing attention. Above 155 creates urgency inappropriate for most wellness topics.
- **Warmth:** Measured by tonal richness and slight breathiness. High warmth (0.7-1.0) for grief/trauma. Moderate warmth (0.4-0.6) for cognitive topics. Lower warmth (0.2-0.4) for action-oriented content.
- **Pause duration:** 300-500ms inter-sentence pauses. Grief/meditation content benefits from 400-500ms. Anxiety content needs 300-380ms (too long triggers impatience in anxious listeners).
- **Stability:** Higher stability (0.70+) for authority. Lower stability (0.45-0.65) for emotional resonance. Very low (<0.40) risks inconsistency.

## 2. Positioning Profile Voice Design

### somatic_companion (38% of authors)
- **Stability:** 0.60-0.75 — warm, present, not clinical
- **Pace:** 125-140 BPM — unhurried but not slow
- **Pause:** 380-450ms — space for felt sense
- **Pitch:** slightly below average — grounding
- **Warmth:** 0.7-0.9 — embodied, compassionate
- **Rationale:** Somatic work requires the listener to feel safe enough to notice body sensations. A warm, steady voice creates a "co-regulating" effect where the listener's nervous system entrains to the narrator's calm.

### research_guide (42% of authors)
- **Stability:** 0.75-0.90 — clear, authoritative
- **Pace:** 140-155 BPM — efficient, confident
- **Pause:** 300-360ms — purposeful, not lingering
- **Pitch:** average — neutral authority
- **Warmth:** 0.4-0.6 — credible, not cold
- **Rationale:** Evidence-based content needs vocal authority. Too warm reads as unscientific. Too cold reads as dismissive. The "professor who cares" register.

### elder_stabilizer (20% of authors)
- **Stability:** 0.70-0.75 — steady, unhurried
- **Pace:** 120-135 BPM — deliberate, wise
- **Pause:** 400-500ms — weight, patience
- **Pitch:** below average — gravitas
- **Warmth:** 0.6-0.8 — warm but not soft
- **Rationale:** Wisdom content benefits from vocal weight. The pace communicates "I have time for this." Longer pauses signal that silence is safe.

## 4. CJK Cultural Voice Preferences

### Japan (ja-JP)
- **Register:** Keigo (formal polite) for authority content, casual for Gen Z. Iyashikei (healing) genre uses measured, gentle female voices.
- **Pace:** 140-150 BPM (Japanese speech is faster than English for same content). Meditation content slows to 125-135.
- **Cultural context:** Mental health stigma remains strong. Frame as "wellbeing" (ウェルビーイング) not "mental health." Silence in audio is valued, not awkward.
- **Voice preference:** Female voices preferred in wellness audiobooks (70%+ of top-selling narrators on Audible JP are female for non-fiction wellness).

### Korea (ko-KR)
- **Register:** 힐링 (healing) content is a massive category. K-wellness is aspirational, not clinical.
- **Pace:** 145-155 BPM (Korean speech trends faster). Ppalli-ppalli culture = listeners expect efficient delivery.
- **Cultural context:** Webtoon-native audience. Short-form preferred. Micro-content (30-60s clips) is king on Naver, Kakao.
- **Voice preference:** Clear, slightly younger voices. "Unni/oppa" (older sibling) energy resonates for 20-30 audience.

### Taiwan (zh-TW)
- **Register:** Warm but direct. 竹科 (Hsinchu Science Park) tech burnout is a cultural touchpoint.
- **Pace:** 135-145 BPM (Traditional Chinese speech is measured). Academic pressure context.
- **Cultural context:** Mandarin with Taiwanese accent nuances. Avoid mainland Chinese vocal mannerisms.
- **Voice preference:** Warm female for somatic, authoritative male for research.

### Hong Kong (zh-HK)
- **CRITICAL:** Must use Cantonese (yue-HK), NEVER Mandarin. Cantonese cadence patterns differ significantly.
- **Pace:** 140-150 BPM. Finance sector stress register — direct, efficient.
- **Cultural context:** IFC/Central burnout is the emotional hook. English-Cantonese code-switching common.
- **Voice preference:** Professional register. Not overly warm (reads as insincere in HK business culture).

### China Mainland (zh-CN)
- **Register:** 996 work culture (9am-9pm, 6 days). Platform expectations vary by distribution: Ximalaya (intimate), Dedao (authoritative), WeChat Read (book-adjacent).
- **Pace:** 140-150 BPM. Mainland Mandarin is slightly faster than Taiwanese Mandarin.
- **Cultural context:** Wellness framed as 自我提升 (self-improvement) not therapy. Government sensitivity around "mental health" framing.
- **Voice preference:** Varies by platform. Ximalaya: warm female. Dedao: authoritative male.

### Singapore (zh-SG)
- **Register:** Singlish-influenced Mandarin context. Multicultural, pragmatic.
- **Pace:** 140-150 BPM. Similar to mainland but with local accent tolerance.
- **Voice preference:** Neutral, professional. Not overly mainland or Taiwanese in accent.

## 6. Voice Uniqueness Strategy

**Problem:** 74 brands need distinct voice identities across 13 locales. Within any locale, no two brands should sound the same.

**Solution:** Voice archetype clustering + parameter modulation.

- **6 voice registers:** intimate, warm, neutral, authoritative, direct, elder
- **2 genders** per register = 12 base archetypes per locale
- **Parameter modulation** (stability, pace, pitch, pause) creates sub-variants
- **CJK locales:** 5 locales × ~5 unique archetypes = ~25 CosyVoice2 voices
- **EN/EU locales:** 8 locales × ~5 unique archetypes = ~40 ElevenLabs voices
- **Built-in voices cover 7** → need ~18 CosyVoice2 cloned archetypes
- **Cross-locale sharing OK** (same voice in different language sounds different)
- **Within-locale uniqueness enforced** by brand_narrator_voice_map.yaml
