The Core Insight

Why Duration Matters

Most content creators pick a length based on gut feel. They wonder why completion rates are low, why the algorithm buries them, and why readers leave after 3 minutes. The answer is almost always duration mismatch.

Duration determines three things simultaneously.

Whether the content actually works therapeutically. Whether the platform algorithm promotes it. And whether the audience finishes it. Miss any one, and the content underperforms.

Therapeutic Dose Science

Clinical research establishes minimum exposure times for measurable physiological or psychological effect. A breathing exercise below 5 minutes produces no significant HRV change. Content shorter than the therapeutic minimum is a wasted asset.

⚙

Platform Algorithm Fit

Every platform has a duration sweet spot where the algorithm gives you free reach. YouTube rewards completion rate over raw watch time. TikTok videos under 30s drive 280% higher completion. Miss the sweet spot and quality becomes irrelevant.

⏲

Persona Attention Windows

How long each audience actually consumes content in a single session. Gen Z reads 9-19 minutes per day. Gen X reads 32-46 minutes. A 7-hour audiobook takes Gen Z three weeks to finish. A 3-hour audiobook takes one week.

Three phases from audit to full automation. Each phase builds on the last. By Day 90, every piece of content in the pipeline is duration-optimized by default.

Phase 1: Audit + Implement (Days 1-30)

Week 1

Catalog Duration Audit

Map every existing title's duration against the persona optimal range matrix. Identify gap analysis: which titles are too long, too short, or mismatched to their target persona.

Week 1-2

Duration Template System

Establish duration templates for each format: audiobook chapter lengths, ebook chapter page counts, manga panel targets, video duration brackets. Codify as config files in the pipeline.

Week 2-3

Apply to Production Pipeline

All new titles in production receive duration targets from the template system. Every brief includes recommended duration, page count, and panel count.

Week 3-4

First Optimized Content Live

Produce first batch: 3 short audiobooks (2-3hr), 10 micro-manga episodes (5 panels), 50 duration-targeted video shorts. Split zh-CN audiobooks into 15-30 min Ximalaya episodes.

Phase 2: A/B Test + Measure (Days 31-60)

Week 5-6

Duration A/B Tests

Same content at two durations: 5-min vs 10-min YouTube video. 3hr vs 5hr audiobook. Measure completion rates, algorithm placement, and review velocity for each variant.

Week 6-7

Manga Panel Count Tests

Test 12 vs 20 vs 40 panels per episode on WEBTOON Canvas. Measure episode completion rate, return visit rate, and subscriber growth per variant.

Week 7-8

TikTok Duration Brackets

Test 15s vs 30s vs 60s vs 90s for same therapeutic content. Confirm optimal duration per persona. Measure completion rate, Creator Rewards earnings, and follower conversion.

Week 8

Correlation Report

Analyze completion rates by duration bracket across all platforms. Produce the duration-completion correlation report that validates or adjusts the matrix.

Phase 3: Full System (Days 61-90)

Week 9-10

Duration Recommendation Engine

Build intelligence into the production pipeline: auto-suggest durations based on persona + platform + format + therapeutic intent. Input: (brand, platform, locale, persona, format, intent). Output: recommended duration + fit score.

Week 10-11

Locale-Specific Duration Rules

Implement Korea 30-min summary audiobooks on Millie, China episodic format on Ximalaya, Japan manga chapter lengths for LINE Manga. Each locale gets its own duration config.

Week 11-12

Full Rollout + Dashboard

All new content produced with duration-optimized specs. Weekly duration performance dashboard tracks completion rates, algorithm placement, and revenue per minute produced.

Day 90

Retrospective

Compare duration-optimized content performance vs pre-optimization baseline. Produce ROI report with revenue impact data.
