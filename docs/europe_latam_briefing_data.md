### Cultural Framing Do/Don't
- **DO:** Use *sanacion* (healing), *bienestar emocional*, *salud mental*. Honor *familismo* while empowering boundary-setting. Use Latin American accents (preferred over Castilian). Warm, relational, family-centered tone.
- **DON'T:** Use clinical jargon, English-first approaches, or "therapy-speak."

### Top 3 Topics with Demand Data

| Rank | Topic | Key Evidence |

| 2 | Onismeret (Self-knowledge) & Onelfogadas (Self-acceptance) | Jaffa Publishing has published psychology/self-development for 20+ years. Experiential avoidance is the strongest predictor of public stigma and depression. |

- **Google Play Books:** Secondary; mobile-first access for younger demographics
- **Bookline Reader app:** Growing subscription model for e-audiobooks
- **Amazon Kindle:** Available but not localized for Hungarian market

### Cultural Framing Do/Don't
- **DO:** Use *onismeret* (self-knowledge), *lelki egeszseg* (soul health), *szemelyes fejlodes* (personal development). Use gentle, non-judgmental, normalizing tone. Frame as self-knowledge, not "fixing" mental illness. Leverage digital platforms to reach underserved rural areas.
- **DON'T:** Use clinical or stigmatizing language. Do not assume Western European levels of mental health acceptance. No national anti-stigma program exists yet.
