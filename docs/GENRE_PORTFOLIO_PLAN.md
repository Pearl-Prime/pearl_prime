## Strategic Premise

Explicit self-help manga revenue ceiling: **~3M copies** (Oyasumi Punpun, hard ceiling without genre shell).
Genre manga with embedded wellness: **7M–70M copies** (Vinland Saga → Berserk).
Evangelion (depression as mecha): **$16B franchise**.

**Rule:** Every brand publishes across multiple genres. The wellness content is the interior architecture — never the facade. The reader comes for the genre. They stay because the psychological content named something real.

## Genre Taxonomy

12 genre shells, each with natural wellness content capacity:

| # | Genre Shell | Revenue Tier | Wellness Content It Carries | Best Persona Fit |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Iyashikei / Slice-of-Life** | Anchor (loyalty-builder) | Somatic healing, sleep, rest permission, daily joy, subtle boundaries | All josei/healing |
| 2 | **Dark Fantasy** | Mega | Grief, trauma recovery, perseverance, identity under extreme pressure, meaning-making | Adult men + women, 25–45 |
| 3 | **Psychological Horror** | Large-growing | Anxiety (externalized as entity), intrusive thoughts, shame (creature only you see), imposter (the doppelgänger) | Adult women; horror CAGR 18.74% |
| 4 | **Supernatural Mystery** | Large | Grief (unfinished business), boundaries (spirit/human world), identity, generational trauma | Josei + seinen women |
| 5 | **Isekai** | Mega (digital) | Second chances, purpose-finding, imposter syndrome ("am I meant to be here?"), self-worth | Digital-native; Gen Z + millennial |
| 6 | **Sci-Fi / Cyberpunk** | Mid | Burnout (machine-body metaphors), digital anxiety, identity (human vs. system), cognitive clarity | Tech workers; digital_ground persona |
| 7 | **Psychological Thriller** | Mid | Overthinking (paranoia as anxiety), unreliable narrator = imposter syndrome, obsession loops | Seinen + josei adults |
| 8 | **Romance / Josei Drama** | Large | Social anxiety, attachment patterns, boundaries in intimacy, self-worth through relationship | Josei women primarily |
| 9 | **Workplace Drama / Comedy** | Mid | Burnout, imposter syndrome, social anxiety, financial anxiety — Aggretsuko register | Millennial professionals |
| 10 | **Action / Battle** | Large | Courage, resilience, somatic (body as tool not enemy), perseverance, finding strength | Shonen + young seinen |
| 11 | **Sports / Competition** | Large | ADHD focus, performance anxiety, imposter syndrome at peak moments, financial anxiety (career pressure) | Gen Z + young adults |
| 12 | **Historical / Period Drama** | Mid | Grief across time, legacy and self-worth, courage in context, honor vs. authenticity | Seinen + older josei |

**Bonus genres used by specific brands:**
- **School / Coming-of-Age** → study anxiety, identity formation, social anxiety in formative contexts
- **Cultivation / Martial Arts** → Eastern somatic (qi, body awareness), inner peace through discipline, spiritual grounding

## The Golden Principle

**The reader does not know they are reading a self-help book. They know they are reading the best dark fantasy / horror / isekai they have ever encountered. The psychological content is the reason it is the best — but it is delivered as story, not as prescription.**

The wellness teaching baked into a Berserk-register dark fantasy series about grief:
> The reader does not read: *"Grief is processed in stages."*
> The reader reads: *A warrior stands over a grave in a ruined world and realizes she has been fighting to get back to a person who is already gone — and something in her shoulders finally drops.*

Same content. One sells 3 million copies as a self-help manga. One sells 30 million copies as a dark fantasy.
