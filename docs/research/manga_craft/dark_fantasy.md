# Dark Fantasy Manga — Craft Bible

> Genre Shell #2 (Mega tier). Revenue exemplars: Berserk 70M, Frieren ~31M.
> Carries: grief, trauma recovery, perseverance, identity under pressure, meaning-making.
> Style archetype: Dark Psychological (MANGA_MODE_STRATEGY.md table §4.2 — Ahjan: shame, spiral engines).

---

## 1. Market contract

**Target demographic:** Seinen core — men 18–35; secondary pull from women 20–35 (Frieren expands the tent). French collector market (prestige print) and older Gen-Z EU/US digital readers are priority locales.

**Emotional promise:** The reader arrives carrying real-world weight — grief, shame, failed identity. The story does not relieve that weight immediately. It *validates* it by placing a protagonist inside it, then earns a hard-won increment of meaning. The contract is: *I will not look away from what hurt you, and by the end of the volume you will feel less alone in it.* Catharsis is permitted but must be earned through cost, not rescued by plot convenience. Comfort is not guaranteed — only witness.

**Character design notes:**
- Protagonist: realistic proportions, visible damage (scars, exhaustion). Not idealized. Berserk's Guts — armor is earned, never decorative.
- Eyes rendered with internal detail but NOT shojo-large; expression carries in jaw and brow set.
- Antagonists: stillness over exaggeration. Monster's Johan as template.
- Cover palette: Ink-and-Rust or Arctic-Saga registers. Never warm-orange shonen palette.

## 3. Pacing

**Chapter hook archetype:** *Cost-first.* Open on consequence, not setup. The reader enters a scene already mid-grief or mid-wound. Environmental shot first (2–3 silent panels), then locate the character within it. Mirrors Vagabond's landscape-before-character approach.

**Chapter ending archetype:** *Ambiguous forward.* Not a cliffhanger (that is shonen grammar). An unresolved image — a figure walking into dark, a question answered with silence, a small act of tenderness that does not resolve the surrounding violence. The last panel is what the reader carries.

**Transition style:** Environmental bridge (rain, ash, falling leaves carry eye between scenes). Hard cuts reserved for trauma flashbacks. Time-skip via seasonal marker or architectural decay.

**Per-volume emotional shape:** Opens at cost baseline → compresses (things get worse, identity cracks) → a moment of unexpected human warmth (Berserk's campfire scenes) → cost deepens → volume closes slightly below where it opened but with one thing newly named. No volume should close at resolution — only at *increment*. The reader must want the next volume not from curiosity but from concern.

## 4. Dialogue

**Register:** Full adult range — crude soldier speech to formal philosophical. Berserk's soldiers vs. its apostles. Specific character voice matters more than genre defaults. *Ore* for rough protagonists; *watashi* for formal antagonists with pretension.

**Density:** Conversations circle. Characters approach topics obliquely. Subtext over text — the gap between spoken words and interior monologue IS the story.

**Narration:dialogue ratio:** ~60:40 narration-heavy. Interior thought boxes run 10–40 words per bubble.

**Interior monologue conventions:**
- Inserted between dialogue lines to reveal self-deception or suppressed grief.
- The unreliable self-narration: character's interior contradicts what the reader sees happening.
- Never repeated — state it once, trust the reader to carry it.

**Common patterns:**
1. The Uncomfortable Silence — 3–4 panels, neither character speaks.
2. The Philosophical Provocation — "Do you believe people can truly change?" asked sincerely.
3. The Quiet Confession — delivered mid-action, not as a set piece.
4. The Environmental Transition — 2–3 silent landscape panels open the chapter.
5. Exclamations drawn from seinen register: ふん (dismissive), なるほど (thoughtful), 面白い (villain evaluation).

## 5. Character

**Protagonist archetype:** The Damaged Carrier — someone whose past has broken them in a specific, nameable way (grief, shame, betrayal of values). They continue anyway. Not because they have healed, but because stopping is not available. Guts persists through loss of everything. Frieren carries centuries of grief without resolution. The protagonist's wound is the story engine, not the backstory.

**Supporting cast size:** Small and meaningful. 3–6 recurring. Each has a distinct wound that rhymes with or counterpoints the protagonist's. No throwaway allies.

**Antagonist conventions:** Moral ambiguity is the default — not clear villain but a person with coherent reasons who has made different choices. Monster's Johan as the outer limit: antagonist who reveals the horror already latent in the world. When a pure antagonist is needed, use stillness and reasonable speech — no theatrical monologue.

## 6. Failure modes

1. **Grimdark for shock.** Violence or misery without thematic purpose signals immaturity, not depth.
2. **Philosophical monologue as essay.** Characters lecture instead of wrestle. Kills immediacy.
3. **Pacing so slow it stagnates.** Contemplative is not the same as nothing happening.
4. **Nihilism without warmth.** Even Berserk has campfire scenes. Without something to lose, darkness has no weight.
5. **Complexity without connection.** Multiple threads that don't converge or resonate.
6. **Trauma as spectacle.** Showing suffering in extended detail for reader reaction rather than character consequence. Show aftermath, not action.
7. **Protagonist immunity.** A protagonist who cannot be genuinely diminished removes all stakes.
8. **Resolution before cost is paid.** Hard-won meaning cheapened by premature rescue arcs.

---

## 7. 48-volume shape

Dark fantasy requires a long-form arc with genuine irreversibility at each stage.

- **Vols 1–6 — Wound Introduction:** Establish the protagonist's specific damage and the world's cruelty as structural, not incidental. Each volume costs the protagonist something that cannot be returned. Reader learns: this story does not protect its characters.
- **Vols 7–18 — The Long Compression:** The protagonist's identity is systematically dismantled. Supporting cast introduced and placed at risk. Antagonist's coherent worldview revealed. One major irreversible loss per 4-volume block.
- **Vols 19–30 — The Naming:** The protagonist cannot restore what was lost but begins to name what the losses meant. Frieren model — grief acknowledged, not resolved. Small acts of meaning-making appear. The antagonist's perspective earns genuine reader consideration.
- **Vols 31–42 — The Carried Weight:** Protagonist acts from named values rather than wound-reaction. Identity rebuilt, not restored — different shape. Secondary characters reach their own hard-won increments. The world does not improve; the protagonist's relationship to it does.
- **Vols 43–48 — The Long Aftermath:** No triumphant resolution. The final arc is about what it means to continue. Closes on an image that rhymes with the opening wound but at a deeper resonance — the reader understands something they did not have language for in volume 1. The protagonist is still carrying weight; they are simply no longer alone in how they carry it.

## 8. Panel scaffolding

Per-panel fields (9):

1. `framing` — ELS / LS / MS / MCU / CU / insert / env-insert / two-shot
2. `beat_role` — one of {cost_reveal, wound_echo, philosophical_provocation, env_transition, warmth_moment, silence_hold, action_consequence, interior_contradiction, threshold}
3. `dialogue` — ≤20 words; fragments permitted; null for silence panels
4. `caption` — interior monologue or narrator; ≤30 words; unreliable register permitted
5. `mood_tone` — {deep_shadow, ash_grey, fog_wash, blood_accent, ember_warmth, stark_white, none}
6. `character_pose` — still_observer / walking_away / weapon_rest / battle_scarred_stand / small_in_landscape
7. `atmosphere` — environmental detail driving mood (rain / ash fall / wind through ruin / firelight / pre-dawn dark)
8. `silence_flag` — boolean; true forbids dialogue AND caption (env sfx permitted)
9. `cost_weight` — integer 0–3 (0=neutral, 1=acknowledged loss, 2=named grief, 3=identity fracture); must not exceed 3 in consecutive panels without a warmth_moment beat

**Generator constraint:** `silence_flag=true` on ≥28% of panels; max 5 consecutive non-silent panels; `cost_weight=3` never appears in first or last panel of a chapter.
