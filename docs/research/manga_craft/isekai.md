# Craft Bible — Isekai

**Phase 2X.3 backfill (M3 Wave 1).** Pattern follows existing bibles in this directory.
**Genre slug:** `isekai` · **Demos:** shonen, seinen · **en_US product shape:** illustrated self-help picture-book lane may use isekai as a *shell* (second-chance world), not pure power-fantasy.

## Reader promise

Wake in a world with different rules. The fantasy is not omnipotence — it is *permission to begin again* without the old body's habits. Best isekai carries the wound from the old world into the new one; the new world does not erase it, it gives it room.

## Proven comps (study, don't copy)

- *Re:Zero* — death-loop as trauma; growth is costly, not free
- *The Rising of the Shield Hero* (early) — betrayal wound, trust rebuilt slowly
- *Frieren* (adjacent) — time and grief inside a genre shell
- Wellness embed: burnout → world without the old job's rules; self-worth → no resume follows you

## Three-act skeleton (ep_001)

1. **Wound / arrival** — Protagonist arrives (or wakes) carrying an old-world cost (exhaustion, shame, a name they no longer want). New world is sensory and specific — not a tutorial dump.
2. **Turn** — A genre-native vessel (a guide who never lectures; a rule of the land; a small ritual) shows that the body knew the cost before the mind admitted it. Doctrine is *earned by doing*, not explained.
3. **Renewal** — One small act kept on purpose. No mastery. The old wound is still there; the protagonist has a first tool.

## Panel craft

- Open on the *body in the new world* (hands, breath, wrong gravity) before exposition.
- Limit dialogue; isekai exposition kills pace — show rules through failed attempts.
- One long-drop or wide panel at the turn (the land answers).
- Close on a kept ritual, not a power-up.

## Teacher-mode vessel (from `manga_mode_vessels.yaml`)

If teacher-mode: doctrine enters through a **genre-native guide** (cartographer, innkeeper, land-spirit) — **never named as the brand's teacher**. Wound → turn → renewal.

## Anti-patterns

- LitRPG stat screens as the emotional climax
- Instant overpowered protagonist with no cost
- Topic preached ("you must rest") instead of enacted
- Teacher named in-story
