# Craft Bible — School / Coming-of-Age

**Phase 2X.3 backfill (M3 Wave 1).** Pattern follows existing bibles in this directory.
**Genre slug:** `school_coming_of_age` · **Demos:** shojo, shonen · **en_US product shape:** YA/MG picture-book and webtoon-adjacent; anxiety and self-worth as *inner* arcs inside school genre.

## Reader promise

A specific school day (or week) where the social weather is the plot. The reader remembers being watched, graded, and sorted — and wants one honest moment where the protagonist chooses a smaller, truer move.

## Proven comps (study, don't copy)

- *March Comes in Like a Lion* — depression and belonging without lecture
- *Keep Your Hands Off Eizouken!* — creative obsession as coming-of-age
- *Heartstopper* (adjacent romance) — social fear as texture, not thesis
- Wellness embed: anxiety → the test/performance moment; social anxiety → the lunch table / club door

## Three-act skeleton (ep_001)

1. **Wound** — A public or semi-public pressure (exam board, club tryout, group project) activates the body's alarm. Show the body first (hands, throat, posture).
2. **Turn** — A genre-native vessel (a quiet upperclassman, a club room ritual, a teacher who *models* ease without naming the doctrine) lets the guard slip for one beat.
3. **Renewal** — One kept act (stay one minute longer; ask one real question; leave the performance smile off for a corridor length).

## Panel craft

- Hallways, lockers, fluorescent light as emotional weather.
- Silence panels between dialogue — school stories breathe in the gaps.
- Avoid adult-voice captions that explain the lesson.
- Close on motion (walking on) rather than a moral.

## Teacher-mode vessel

Doctrine through a **peer or club ritual**, never a named brand teacher. Wound → turn → renewal.

## Anti-patterns

- Adult therapist monologue in a school setting
- Topic title-dropped ("my anxiety")
- Instant friend-group salvation
- Teacher named in-story
