# Cultivation / Progression Fantasy Manga — Style Bible

Touchstones: *Battle Through the Heavens* (Tian Can Tu Dou / Youka Animation), *Tales of Demons and Gods* (Mad Snail), *Soul Land* (Tang Jia San Shao), *Martial Peak* (Momo), *Solo Leveling* (Chugong — Korean progression-fantasy crossover, manhwa register), *Reincarnation of the Strongest Sword God*.

## 1. Market Contract

Readers are teen-to-young-adult men (core 15–30), heavily overlapping with the light-novel/web-novel-to-manga adaptation audience, who arrive for the feeling of *disciplined, legible ascent* — a protagonist who starts weak, is underestimated, and climbs a named, countable ladder of power through effort, insight, and (ideally) real cost. The genre's unspoken promise: rank is earned, not given; the system is fair even when people inside it are not; humiliation is temporary and reversible through demonstrated strength. Readers carry real-world status anxiety, comparison fatigue, and a hunger for a world where effort visibly and proportionally converts to standing — the opposite of how status often feels to work in real life. They will accept: heavy exposition of power taxonomy, formal/hierarchical speech, arrogant-young-master antagonists, auction/treasure-appraisal set pieces. They will reject: power gains with no visible training or cost, a "system" that substitutes for personality, and a protagonist who wins purely by luck rather than by insight earned through failure. The genre's contract is: *the ladder is real, and you will watch me climb it honestly.*

## 2. Visual Rules

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Panels per page | 4–7 (manhua-influenced; panels run larger than shonen-battle grids to carry text overlays and status/technique callouts) |
| Words per page | 60–110 (canonical pacing floor/ceiling; exposition-dense breakthrough and sect-lecture chapters spike toward 150) |
| Words per balloon (max) | 18 |
| Words per volume/season | ~25,000–40,000 |
| Dialogue:narration ratio | 40:60 (narration-heavy — this genre is always explaining a tier, a technique, or a lineage) |
| Silent-panel ratio | 15–25% (canonical range; the lowest-silence end of the catalog's genre spread — cultivation rarely lets a panel pass without narrating something) |
| Reaction-shot frequency | High (crowd/observer gasp panels are load-bearing after every face-slap or breakthrough) |
| Spread frequency | Periodic (reserved for breakthroughs, tier reveals, and realm-ascension moments — never spent on ordinary combat) |
| Narration tolerance | Medium |
| SFX usage | Heavy kinetic |
| Background density | Medium |
| Screentone | Radiating qi-aura tone (concentric gradient burst) on breakthrough panels; cracked-glass or fracturing-line overlay for qi deviation; dense hatch cross-shading for meridian/energy-channel diagrams; auction/treasure panels use a formal gold-foil-style border tone distinct from combat panels |
| Line weight | Heavy contour on musculature and robes in motion; fine, almost technical linework for cultivation-tier diagrams, talismans, and pill/elixir illustrations — the diagram register must read as distinct from the character-action register |

Cover palette references: Ascension Gold (`#D4A017` aura burst on `#1A0F2E` deep violet-black) for sect/immortal-realm register; Tempered Jade (`#2E7D5B` on `#0D0D0D`) for body-cultivation/martial register (*Battle Through the Heavens*-adjacent); Void Blue (`#0A1A3A` deep navy with `#4FC3F7` qi-glow accent) for system/dungeon-adjacent progression fantasy (*Solo Leveling*-register).

SFX vocabulary: ゴゴゴ (rising qi pressure), ドクン (pulse of breakthrough), ビキビキ (cracking qi deviation / meridian strain), ズオォ (qi surge released), パリン (barrier/restriction shattering), シュン (technique materializing), ドサッ (defeated opponent collapsing), シーン (stunned-crowd silence after a face-slap reveal). Status/technique-name callouts render as a distinct vertical or boxed typographic insert, separate from dialogue balloons — this is the genre's equivalent of the sports bible's technique title-card convention, but triggered by tier/technique reveal rather than in-competition execution.

## 3. Pacing

**Breakthrough-arc structure (chapter level):**
1. Plateau — protagonist stalled at current tier, peers or rivals visibly advancing faster; the stall is named specifically (a blocked meridian, a technique that won't stabilize, a resource the protagonist lacks)
2. Provocation — a rival or hierarchy figure asserts superiority explicitly (the Tier Assertion pattern, see §4); status stakes are made public, not private
3. Insight — the protagonist locates the actual obstacle through failure, observation, or a cryptic master's non-answer, not through a convenient revelation
4. Breakthrough — the tier advance lands on-panel as a spread or near-spread; qi/aura visualization is the genre's spectacle beat, earned by the plateau that preceded it
5. Recalibration — new tier tested immediately against a real stake (the same rival, a fresh threat); the volume/arc does not let a breakthrough go unverified

**Per-volume arc shape:** Each volume tracks one full tier-transition (e.g., Qi Gathering → Foundation Establishment) nested inside one relationship arc (rival, master, or sect-political thread). The tier transition provides the measurable spine; the relationship arc provides the reason the reader cares which body the power lives in.

Chapter hook family: *status-check-first* — open on a ranking board, a sect roster, an auction opening bid, or a rival's public boast before the scene locates the protagonist's response. The hook is the hierarchy before the person.

Chapter ending convention (page-turn triggers, canonical): `cultivation_breakthrough` (tier advance lands mid-chapter, propelling the reader into the next), `rival_challenge` (a named rival issues a public challenge or ultimatum), `sect_revelation` (a hierarchy secret — true lineage, hidden technique, sect politics — surfaces), `inner_realm_descent` (protagonist enters a spirit-realm, secret ruin, or trial ground that resets the power baseline). A chapter should end on exactly one of these, never stacked.

## 4. Dialogue

**Register:** Two registers coexist and must not be flattened into each other.

- *Formal/hierarchical speech* — sect elders, masters, and status-conscious rivals speak in proverbs, four-character idioms, and explicit tier-and-title address ("Junior," "Elder," "this one"). Chinese web-novel-derived register: verbose, philosophically inflected, "face culture" governs almost every exchange — an insult to standing is never just words, it is a plot trigger.
- *Interior breakthrough monologue* — during cultivation/technique attempts, prose shifts to sensory, present-tense interior narration (qi flowing, a barrier cracking, consciousness expanding) distinct from the formal exterior speech surrounding it. This is where the genre's japanese-manga-adjacent compression shows up: shorter, more emotionally immediate than the surrounding dialogue register.

**Key dialogue patterns:**
1. **The Tier Assertion** — a status challenge stated as a taunt or a warning: "A mere Foundation Establishment cultivator dares challenge me?" Always public, always overheard.
2. **The Cryptic Master** — a question or aphorism that resolves nothing on first read: "When the mountain stream meets the sea, does it lose itself or find its true nature?" Understanding must come from the protagonist's later experience, never from the master explaining the line.
3. **The Breakthrough Narration** — interior monologue during the advance itself: qi flow, barrier resistance, the specific physical sensation of a tier giving way. Never generic ("I feel stronger") — always located in a specific channel, organ, or sensation.
4. **The Face-Slap Exchange** — arrogant challenger insults the protagonist's apparent rank → protagonist reveals true strength or hidden cultivation → onlookers gasp. Genre-essential; must be varied in staging (venue, stakes, audience) so it does not read as a template repeating verbatim.
5. **The Auction/Treasure Dialogue** — characters bidding, appraising, or identifying rare materials/artifacts; functions as world-building through commerce and as a venue for face-slap and rival-reveal beats without combat.

**Chinese web-novel vs. Japanese-manga register note:** when adapting for a manga-native reading experience, compress the formal register's verbosity — keep the idiom and the hierarchy-consciousness, but cut the paragraph-length internal justification typical of the source web-novel form down to panel-appropriate bursts (≤18 words/balloon per §2).

## 5. Character

**Protagonist:** Underestimated by design — low apparent rank, a hidden lineage/technique/vessel, or a body others dismiss as a poor cultivation vessel. Progress must be shown as specific and costly: failed breakthroughs, qi deviations, resource scarcity, not montage. The genre's version of the Sakuragi pattern: a plateau that is boring and repeatable before the breakthrough is allowed to land.

**Supporting cast:**
- Master/mentor — often reluctant to teach; earning instruction is itself an arc beat, not a given. Speaks in cryptic tests and withheld answers more than direct lessons (see §4 Cryptic Master pattern).
- Rival — the "arrogant young master" archetype at introduction, but must be revealed as a mirror over the arc: someone who solved a technical problem the protagonist hasn't, or someone whose own forcing/cost pattern eventually backfires. A rival who stays purely an obstacle for 48 volumes reads as flat.
- Sect/circuit hierarchy — elders, disciples, and rank-holders whose treatment of the protagonist shifts visibly and immediately as apparent rank changes; this is how the genre dramatizes status without narration.

**Antagonist:** Either a rival cultivator (see above) or a hierarchy figure whose power is real and whose reasons are coherent — sect politics, resource competition, a prior grudge with the protagonist's lineage. Pure "cosmic evil" antagonists should be reserved for the highest-tier, late-series threats; most conflict should be sourced in status, resources, and lineage rather than abstract villainy.

## 6. Failure Modes

1. **Exposition avalanche.** Three pages of tier taxonomy before anything happens. Weave power-system information into a character's struggle or failure, not a sect elder's lecture.
2. **"Courting death" cliché fatigue.** The face-slap encounter is genre-essential but becomes formulaic fast. Vary the venue, the audience, and what's actually at stake in each instance.
3. **Power fantasy without cost.** If a breakthrough has no price — health, relationships, sanity, resources — progress reads as weightless and the ladder stops mattering.
4. **Generic JRPG-fantasy setting.** Cultivation fiction has rich, specific mythology (Daoist cosmology, sect structures, elemental/spiritual taxonomies). A generic fantasy backdrop wastes the genre's actual strength.
5. **Status-screen-as-crutch.** Delivering all power information through cutaway UI/stat-block slides (the *Solo Leveling* criticism) substitutes for character; mix narrative exposition with any system-screen device, never rely on the screen alone.
6. **System over character.** Stat blocks and tier names are not a substitute for a protagonist with a personality, fear, and specific relationships.
7. **Rival who never becomes a mirror.** If the humiliated-then-defeated rival never gets an interior beat or a moment of being right about something, the genre's face-slap engine curdles into cruelty rather than earned status reversal.
8. **Breakthroughs that arrive without a plateau.** A tier advance with no preceding stall or failure reads as unearned regardless of how well the breakthrough panel itself is drawn.

## 7. 48-Volume Shape

Cultivation's macro-structure maps directly onto its own tier taxonomy — each stage is both a power-level and a volume band, so the ladder and the book count are the same shape.

**Volumes 1–10 — Qi Gathering (Foundation).** Protagonist enters the hierarchy at the bottom, apparent weakness established by chapter 2, first plateau and first face-slap humiliation by volume 2. A reluctant master or mentor found by volume 4. First real breakthrough — small, specific, costly — by volume 6. Volume 10 closes on the protagonist's first public demonstration that they are not who the hierarchy assumed.

**Volumes 11–22 — Foundation Establishment / Core Formation (Rising).** Rival from the opening arc reappears, now genuinely dangerous. Sect-political stakes widen beyond personal rivalry — resource competition, succession politics, a hierarchy figure whose interests conflict with the protagonist's. At least one qi deviation or forced-progress crisis that benches the protagonist and forces a genuine reckoning with the "cost" failure mode. A second technique or cultivation path mastered.

**Volumes 23–36 — Nascent Soul (Threshold).** Stakes escalate from circuit/sect scale to regional/realm scale. The protagonist confronts a psychological threshold, not just a physical one — the fear that their advantage is borrowed, stolen, or undeserved. The master's own backstory and cost are revealed. The rival's forceful/shortcut technique begins visibly backfiring, exposing the hidden price of their apparent head start.

**Volumes 37–48 — Immortal Ascension (Mastery).** The protagonist's cultivation base now dominates the visible hierarchy outright — and mastery is deliberately redefined here as restraint and discernment rather than raw output. The former rival, now unlearning their own forcing habit, becomes an ally. A realm-level threat is resolved through insight rather than an escalating power contest. Final volume closes on a technique introduced in volume 1 as "weak" or "beneath consideration," now revealed as the actual mastery — a closing beat that rhymes with and inverts the opening humiliation.

## 8. Panel Scaffolding

Per-panel fields (9):

1. `framing` — ELS / LS / MS / MCU / CU / insert / status-callout / diagram-insert
2. `beat_role` — one of {plateau_stall, tier_assertion, cryptic_lesson, breakthrough_interior, breakthrough_reveal, face_slap_setup, face_slap_payoff, auction_appraisal, aftermath_recalibration}
3. `cultivation_tier` — string; current named tier/rank of the focal character, drives aura-tone density and crowd-reaction scale
4. `technique_name` — string or null; if set, triggers boxed/vertical status-callout typographic treatment on this panel
5. `qi_visualization` — one of {none, faint_glow, radiating_aura, full_burst_spread, fracturing_deviation}; governs screentone gradient density and whether the panel qualifies for spread treatment (§2)
6. `hierarchy_stakes` — one of {private, witnessed_by_peers, witnessed_by_elders, public_ranking_event}; governs reaction-shot frequency requirement (§2 — `reaction_shot_frequency: high`)
7. `sfx` — from cultivation vocabulary (ゴゴゴ, ドクン, ビキビキ, etc.) or null; never generic combat sfx during breakthrough-interior panels
8. `dialogue` — ≤18 words per balloon (canonical `words_per_balloon_max`); formal/hierarchical register ≤18 words preferred, interior breakthrough monologue may run as caption instead of balloon
9. `silence_flag` — boolean; true on face-slap-payoff panels and the single beat immediately following a breakthrough reveal; consecutive non-silent panels should stay low given the canonical 15–25% silent-panel range — this genre spends silence sparingly and only at its highest-stakes beats

Constraint hint: `technique_name` must appear in at least one training/plateau panel with `qi_visualization=faint_glow` or `none` before it appears at `qi_visualization=full_burst_spread`. `hierarchy_stakes=public_ranking_event` panels must be followed within 1–2 panels by a `reaction_shot` per the high reaction-shot-frequency default.
