## S1 Executive Summary

Phoenix Omega targets 10 personas across 12 locales with therapeutic audiobooks, ebooks, and manga. The combined addressable market exceeds $53B globally (self-improvement) [1] plus $19.35B (manga) [4], with audiobooks growing at 10.58% CAGR [1] and webtoons at 29.7% CAGR [4].

**Key findings across 12 locales, 10 personas, 12 topics:**

- Sleep and burnout are universal crises: 500M+ sleep-disordered in China [2], 49% burnout in HK [2], 80% compassion fatigue in US nurses [1]
- Gen Z mental health is CRITICAL in 7 of 12 locales -- 54.7% at risk in China [2], 68% burnout in Singapore [2], 43% suicidal ideation in Korea [2]
- Asian markets require radically different framing: "body-mind health" (zh-TW), "condition management" (ja-JP), "self-development" (ko-KR) -- never "mental illness" [2]
- First responders and sandwich generation are the two most underserved US personas with near-zero dedicated supply [1]
- Platform fragmentation demands locale-specific distribution: Ximalaya (345M MAU, China), Millie (Korea), Readmoo (Taiwan), Audible (US/Japan) [1][2]

## S3 Per-Persona Playbooks

### 3.1 Millennial Women Professionals (28-45)

**Global demand:** Largest self-help buyer demographic (avg buyer age 38); women 2x more likely to experience anxiety; 25-44 year-olds are 68% of self-help ebook/audiobook buyers [1].

**Top 3 topics by locale:**

| Locale | Topic 1 | Topic 2 | Topic 3 |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| en-US | Overthinking (12,626 reviews) | Burnout (8,582 reviews) | Boundaries (NYT bestseller) [1] |
| zh-TW/HK | Burnout (CMD 23.8%) | Sleep (40% insomnia) | Self-care [2] |
| zh-CN | 996 burnout (>75% fatigued) | Sleep (500M+) | Emotion management [2] |
| ja-JP | Karoshi (1,057 cases) | Sleep (OECD worst) | Fatigue recovery [2] |
| ko-KR | Burnout | Self-development | Habit-building [2] |

**Platform strategy:**

| Locale | Primary | Secondary |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| en-US | Audible | Libby |
| zh-TW | Readmoo | Kobo |
| zh-HK | Readmoo | Storytel |
| zh-CN | Ximalaya | WeChat Read |
| ja-JP | Audible.jp | audiobook.jp |
| ko-KR | Millie | YES24 |

**Competitive landscape:** "Stop Overthinking" (Trenton, 12,626 reviews), "Burnout" (Nagoski, 8,582), "Set Boundaries, Find Peace" (Tawwab, 5,000+) [1]. In Asia: "The Courage to Be Disliked" dominates across ja-JP, ko-KR, zh-CN [2].

**Market gap:** No title integrates overthinking + burnout + boundaries for professional women. In Asia, no locale-specific burnout title exists for women -- all bestsellers are translated Western titles [1][2].

**EI v2 priorities:** therapeutic_value (burnout recovery efficacy), engagement (commute-length format), listen_experience (TTS optimized for 2.5-3hr sessions) [3].

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

1. "The Overthinking Cure for Professional Women" -- integrated framework, audiobook-first, en-US then adapt to 6 Asian locales
2. "996 Recovery: From Burnout to Balance" -- zh-CN native, Ximalaya-first, adapt to zh-TW/HK/SG
3. "Boundaries at Work and Home" -- en-US, Audible, short-form (2.5hrs) for working mothers

### 3.4 Working Parents (25-50)

**Global demand:** 48% of US parents report overwhelming stress (APA) [1]; parental burnout only formally studied since 2019 [1]; education anxiety in zh-CN (54.7% students at risk) and ko-KR (43% suicidal ideation from academic stress) [2].

**Market gap:** No bestseller combines parental burnout + boundary-setting + co-parenting. Father/co-parent burnout is 95%+ invisible [1].

**EI v2 priorities:** safety_compliance (parenting content requires extra care), emotional_coherence (guilt-to-relief arc), listen_experience (hands-free for parents) [3].

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

1. "Parental Burnout Recovery" -- first dedicated parental burnout audiobook, en-US
2. "The Co-Parent's Guide to Boundaries" -- father-inclusive, en-US, adapt to Asian locales
3. "Education Anxiety: A Parent's Companion" -- zh-CN/ko-KR native, addresses academic pressure culture

### 3.5 Gen X Sandwich Generation (35-65)

**Global demand:** 25% of US adults are sandwich caregivers; 54% of those in their 40s; ~$10K/year caregiving costs [1]. Japan's 80-50 problem: 600K+ hikikomori aged 40-64 with elderly parents [2].

**Market gap:** Most underserved US persona. No bestseller addresses triple burden of caregiver burnout + financial anxiety + anticipatory grief [1]. Zero-free-hands constraint makes audiobook essential [1].

**EI v2 priorities:** therapeutic_value (grief + burnout), somatic_precision (caregiver physical toll), listen_experience (must work during caregiving tasks) [3].

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

1. "The Sandwich Generation Survival Guide" -- audiobook-first, en-US, Audible + Libby
2. "Caregiver Burnout: When Love Isn't Enough" -- grief + boundaries, en-US
3. "80-50: Supporting Adult Children and Aging Parents" -- ja-JP native, Audible.jp

### 3.6 Corporate Managers (25-55)

**Market gap:** Male manager imposter syndrome invisible; no title addresses manager-specific combo of imposter syndrome + team burnout responsibility [1].

**EI v2 priorities:** engagement (leadership framing), safety_compliance (workplace-appropriate language) [3].

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

1. "The Imposter Manager" -- gender-inclusive imposter syndrome for managers, en-US
2. "Leading Without Burning Out" -- manager burnout + team wellness, en-US, adapt to ja-JP/ko-KR
3. "Workplace Boundaries for Leaders" -- en-US, Audible, adapt to zh-HK/SG (finance-sector angle)

### 3.9 Gen Alpha Students (13-22)

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

**Recommended first 3 titles:**

2. "Sleep After the Shift" -- first responder sleep recovery, en-US, Audible

## S5 EI v2 Marketing Integration

EI v2's six dimensions (config: `ei_v2_config.yaml` [3]) map directly to marketing decisions:

### Dimension-to-Decision Mapping

| EI v2 Dimension | Config Gate | Marketing Application |
|-----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **therapeutic_value** | pass >= 0.60 [4] | **Topic selection**: Only produce titles scoring above therapeutic threshold. Prioritize topics with highest gap-to-demand ratio from research [1][2]. |
| **engagement** | min_hooks_per_chapter: 1, min_tension_markers: 2 [3] | **Format/platform selection**: High-engagement scores -> audiobook-first (Audible/Spotify). Engagement composite_weight: 0.35 (rerank) [3] drives title prioritization. |
| **safety_compliance** | medical_claim: 0.6, promotional: 0.5, clinical_language: 0.5 [3] | **Locale compliance**: China Minor Protection Law requires safety >= 0.95 [4]. Japan/Korea stigma avoidance maps to clinical_language_threshold. Marketing_compliance_weight: 0.2 [3]. |
| **emotional_coherence** | arc_deviation_warn: 0.3, arc_deviation_fail: 0.6 [3] | **Brand consistency**: Ensures adapted titles maintain emotional arc across locales. Critical for zh-CN/ja-JP where cultural reframing changes surface language but must preserve therapeutic trajectory. |
| **somatic_precision** | min_somatic_words_per_chapter: 3, min_density: 1.5/100w [3] | **Persona match**: First Responders and RNs require highest somatic density. Gen Z titles need accessible somatic language. Cross-reference with persona research for body-based vs cognitive framing. |
| **listen_experience** | pass >= 0.55, TTS max_sentence_words: 35, ideal: 8-25 words [3] | **TTS optimization per platform**: Audible (professional narrator voice), Spotify (conversational), Ximalaya (Mandarin TTS requires shorter sentences). Problematic patterns: long words, parenthetical chains, semicolons [3]. |
