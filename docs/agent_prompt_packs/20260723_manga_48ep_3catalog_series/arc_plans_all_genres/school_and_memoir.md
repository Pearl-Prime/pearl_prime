## calm_student_school

Genre: school | Primary topic: anxiety

**Genre fit note:** direct fit — brand name literally contains "school," and
`school` is this brand's established genre per the catalog; no reassignment.

**Escalation model note:** `school_coming_of_age.md` has no numbered "§7
series-planning" section (unlike the memoir bible). The 4-arc escalation below
is derived instead from its **Three-act skeleton** (Wound → Turn → Renewal,
per ep_001) plus the "Teacher-mode vessel" and "Panel craft" guidance,
scaled up from a single-episode beat to a school-year-quarters structure —
each arc repeats the Wound→Turn→Renewal cycle at rising social stakes,
matching the bible's instruction to keep the self-help doctrine embedded in a
peer/club ritual, never a named brand teacher.

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012):** New term. **Wound** — placement exams, seating
charts, and a first group project activate the body's alarm before any
lesson does; panels open on hands, throat, and posture (per Panel craft),
never a caption naming "anxiety." **Turn** — a quiet upperclassman or a
club-room ritual (not a teacher, never named as doctrine) lets the guard
slip for one beat. **Renewal** — one small kept act: staying a minute
longer in the club room, asking one real question instead of the safe one.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024):** Rising social exposure. **Wound** escalates from
private dread to public visibility — tryouts, a presentation, a lunch-table
reshuffle — where being watched and sorted is now unavoidable. **Turn** —
the peer vessel from Arc 1 becomes a recurring relationship (a club, not a
friend-group rescue — the bible flags "instant friend-group salvation" as an
anti-pattern), so the ease is modeled repeatedly, never lectured. **Renewal**
— the kept act becomes a habit rather than a one-off: showing up to the same
door again and again.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036):** The whole-cohort event. **Wound** peaks at a
school-wide stage — festival, tournament, or exam board — where comparison
to peers and unspoken family expectation bleed into the performance moment;
this is the highest-stakes version of the Arc 1 wound, same body-first
craft, larger room. **Turn** — the protagonist, without ever naming a
doctrine, offers the same quiet ease to a newer, more visibly anxious
underclassman — the vessel role passes forward. **Renewal** — a kept act
sustained across the multi-day event itself, not just a single corridor.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048):** Graduation / leaving the building. **Wound** shifts
from performance-pressure to the diffuse dread of an unscheduled future —
no more seating chart to be sorted by. **Turn** — a final club-room or
hallway ritual closes the loop from Arc 1's opening space. **Renewal** —
per the bible's "close on motion rather than a moral," the arc (and series)
ends on the protagonist walking on, not on an adult-voice lesson or a
therapist-style resolution.

## legacy_builder_memoir

Genre: memoir | Primary topic: self_worth

**Genre fit note — REASSIGNMENT FLAG:** brand name literally contains
"memoir," a strong direct fit for this exercise's genre. However, per
`ASSIGNMENT_MATRIX.tsv` this brand's **established** catalog genre is
`psychological_thriller`, not memoir — existing rows there (en_US/ja_JP/zh_TW)
spec a different 4-arc shape ("obsession seed → spiral → exposure →
reckoning," craft bible `psychological_thriller.md`), and en_US already has a
`REUSE_EXISTING` chapter_scripts dir (listing-only, title *the_file_labeled_
enough*, author helen_frost) built against that thriller shape. **This
document's memoir-genre plan below is a full-coverage-exercise
reassignment, not a replacement of the live thriller assignment** — it does
not overwrite, rename, or author into the existing thriller-track directory.
Flagging per instruction so downstream dispatch does not silently merge the
two structures.

**Escalation model note:** `josei_adult_memoir.md` §7 ("Series planning
implications, 48-volume pre-plan") directly recommends the **diaristic-serial
model**: quarterly rhythm across a life window, with a **retrospective
episode every 8** (ep_008, 016, 024, 032, 040, 048) where the narrator rereads
her own earlier entries. Per this document's brief, "escalation" here means
**deepening self-honesty / retrospective clarity across life-quarters**, not
plot melodrama — each arc below runs the bible's §5 per-volume emotional
shape (*Avowal → Attempt → Aftermath → Honest assessment that things are
only a little different*) at a later, more exposed life-stage than the last.
No transformation arc, no epiphany ending (§6 failure mode 1), no romance
resolution standing in for repaired self-worth (§6 failure mode 5).

**Arc 1 (ep_001-012):** Early adulthood, stalled start. **Avowal** — the
narrator states the specific quiet self-worth wound up front, confessional
pre-emption style ("I built things so no one would ask why I hadn't built a
life"), first 1-3 panels, no therapy-manual vocabulary. **Attempt** — she
throws herself into early "legacy" work (a project, a job, a first
ambitious build) as a stand-in for worth. **Aftermath** — the work ships;
nothing internal moves. Retrospective episode at **ep_008**: she already
rereads the arc's own earlier entries, per §7's rhythm, and the honest
assessment lands flat rather than triumphant.

**Arc 2 (ep_013-024):** Building the legacy in earnest. **Avowal** deepens —
the self-worth wound is now explicitly tied to the scale of what she builds,
not just that she builds. **Attempt** — bigger stakes: a signature project,
a promotion, public recognition of the "legacy," pursued as proof of worth.
**Aftermath** — recognition arrives and the wound is untouched by it, which
she notices and names without preaching to the reader (§6 failure mode 9).
Two retrospective episodes fall in this arc per the §7 rhythm — **ep_016**
and **ep_024** — each a season where she rereads the earlier diary and
revises nothing she said, only how she reads it now.

**Arc 3 (ep_025-036):** Midlife rupture. **Avowal** — a loss, estrangement,
or the visible failure/end of the signature "legacy" project forces a
confession she withheld in Arcs 1-2 (per §4's self-interrupting, corrective
register: "I was proud. No — I was afraid of what I'd be without it.").
**Attempt** — she tries, once, to build worth from something that isn't
output — a relationship repaired, a return to someone she avoided —
without it curdling into a romance-fixes-everything beat (§6 failure mode
5 stays off-limits). **Aftermath** — a small honest reversal, not a
breakthrough. Retrospective episode at **ep_032**.

**Arc 4 (ep_037-048):** Late-arc honest settling. **Avowal** — the plainest,
least defended version of the wound yet, closest to the bible's canonical
confessional-opening register (§9). **Attempt** — she keeps building,
because that's who she is, but now without requiring it to prove anything.
**Aftermath / honest assessment** — "only a little different," per §5 — no
epiphany, no cure claimed, the diary simply keeps going. Two closing
retrospective episodes per the §7 rhythm, **ep_040** and **ep_048**, where
she rereads the very first entries from Arc 1 and the series ends on an
unresolved return (the bible's specified chapter-ending convention), not a
resolved thesis about self-worth.
