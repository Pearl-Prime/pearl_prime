**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Late winter into spring. Nia moves into an inherited house that sticks and creaks in specific, repeatable places — a door that only jams when the weather turns, a floorboard that gives underfoot in the exact spot her shoulders tighten. She starts a small notebook of the house's habits alongside her own body's, oiling one hinge at a time rather than renovating anything; the ice on the windows thaws at roughly the pace the notebook fills.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer. The overgrown garden gets worked in small sessions — pulling one bed of roots, mending one length of fence — and her body loosens in the same piecemeal way, never on a schedule she controls. An old friend visits and remarks that the house "feels different," not fixed, just easier to stand in; Nia can't explain why and doesn't try to.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn. The claw-foot tub, unusable since she moved in, finally gets its first proper soak after a slow re-plumbing subplot told entirely through small repair-day panels. A real storm tests the patched roof and the patched parts of Nia both, and both hold — not perfectly, but enough, which the chapter treats as the whole point.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter again, the year completed. The house still creaks in one spot on the stairs, but it has stopped being a warning and become just a floorboard. Nia hosts someone overnight in the house for the first time in the series — a small, undramatic threshold crossed — and the notebook's last entries are shorter, with more blank space than words, which the chapter lets stand as its own kind of ending.

**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn. She waters the herbs out of simple tidiness, not ownership, and keeps using slide templates built by the person she's replacing without changing a line.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter. The herbs come back stubbornly under her care regardless of how she feels about deserving the desk. She replaces exactly one slide in the inherited template with something in her own voice, then sits with the discomfort of that for a whole episode before touching another. A sticky note with her actual name goes up over the old nameplate — temporary, removable, but there.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Spring. The herbs get repotted into a planter she bought herself, moved off the windowsill she inherited and onto one she chose. A new hire arrives at the next desk over, visibly circling the same untended feeling Priya once had, and Priya hands down the herb box with watering instructions written in her own handwriting rather than the one she found taped there originally.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer, a full cycle closed. The mug, the desk, the templates now entirely rewritten are simply Priya's, without ceremony. She mentors the new hire the unglamorous way — matching her pace, not lecturing — and the chapter closes on both windowsills, old and new, holding herbs that are doing fine either way.

## gentle_growth_healing

**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Spring. Sofi is handed a struggling plant by a neighbor moving away — it didn't ask to become hers, and neither did she ask for the responsibility, but she keeps it alive anyway. A community garden plot comes up in the same season's lottery, and she tends it in visible, humble contrast to the tidier plots around hers.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer. The inherited plant produces cuttings; Sofi gives several away without keeping count of who thanks her for it. Her plot's yield is smaller and scruffier than her neighbors' — the chapter lets that sit as simply true, not as a problem needing to be solved before summer ends.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn. A modest, imperfect harvest still gets eaten and shared; a compost pile absorbs everything that didn't take, and nothing about that feels wasted on the page. Sofi starts marking which of this year's efforts she'll repeat, not out of self-criticism, but the way any gardener keeps notes.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter, a full cycle closed. The plot goes dormant and Sofi treats the dormancy as part of its worth rather than an absence of it — an empty bed under frost is still a tended bed. She shows a newer, more anxious gardener around the shed and the compost bins the way she once needed showing, and the plant from block one, still imperfect, is still simply alive on her sill.

## healing_ground_healing

**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn. Rowan begins walking a forest trail near where they lost someone, leaving a small stone or token at particular trees along the way, and signing a weathered trailhead logbook with nothing but a date. The forest doesn't explain itself and Rowan doesn't ask it to; the ritual is repetitive, small, and entirely theirs.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter. Bare branches change which trees hold snow and which don't, and Rowan starts noticing the difference the way you'd notice a person's mood. A small tin kept at the base of one tree collects folded notes over the season — never read aloud on the page, never explained, just accumulated.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Spring. New growth returns to the grove without erasing anything — the same trees that held Rowan's grief all winter now also hold buds, and the chapter treats both as simply true at once. A sapling gets planted near the trail, tended without being named after anyone out loud.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer, a full cycle closed, full canopy overhead. Rowan brings a newer, more raw griever to the trail for the first time and passes on the small rituals — the stone, the logbook, the tin — without turning it into a lesson. The forest still keeps its names quietly; Rowan is simply easier in it now than they were a year ago.

## night_reset_healing
Existing series: `night_reset_healing__hana_quill__en_US__sleep__the_lamp_stays_on_purpose` (REUSE_EXISTING — arc plan feeds authoring into this existing series identity, do not propose a new one)
Demographic: josei | Primary topic: sleep | Genre: iyashikei (seasonal/atmospheric, NOT stakes-escalation)

**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn, nights lengthening. Hana leaves her bedside lamp on deliberately, later than she needs it, as a small anchor against racing thoughts, and folds the same blanket the same specific way each night whether or not it helps her fall asleep faster. The ritual is shown as a ritual, not a fix.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter. On nights the lamp isn't enough, Hana takes short walks under the streetlights near her building instead of lying awake fighting it — an easier relationship with wakefulness itself rather than a harder push toward sleep. A neighbor's porch light, always on, becomes a small landmark on these walks.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Spring. The lamp gets traded for an earlier, gentler wind-down — blankets aired on the balcony in the new season's air, the lamp switched on later and later into the evening as it's needed less. Nothing is declared "solved"; the lamp is simply used differently now.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer, a full cycle closed. Hana finds she's leaving her own porch light on some nights, not for herself but for a newer neighbor she's noticed keeping late, anxious hours — the same gesture, passed forward instead of needed. The bedside lamp is barely mentioned in the final stretch, simply on, simply there, no longer load-bearing.

**Block 1 (ep_001-012, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Autumn. Eli works behind a shop or office door they dread opening to the day's first customer or colleague, and develops a small habit of touching the doorframe before turning the handle — never explained in caption, just repeated. The door itself, not any person on the other side of it, is the object the chapter keeps returning to.

**Block 2 (ep_013-024, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Winter. On the mildest days, Eli starts propping the door open rather than closing it fully between interactions, and the hallway exchanges on either side of it get a beat or two longer than before. The doorframe touch becomes occasional instead of automatic.

**Block 3 (ep_025-036, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Spring. Eli deliberately holds the door for a visibly nervous newcomer the way they once needed it held for them — a small, specific act of hospitality rather than a grand gesture. The door starts appearing in more panels open than shut.

**Block 4 (ep_037-048, ~3 seasonal cycles):** Summer, a full cycle closed. The door gets opened and closed hundreds of ordinary times across the closing block without ceremony or narration — it has become, as the title promises, only a door. The last chapter's final panel is the door standing ajar in a warm draft, nobody paying it any particular attention at all.
