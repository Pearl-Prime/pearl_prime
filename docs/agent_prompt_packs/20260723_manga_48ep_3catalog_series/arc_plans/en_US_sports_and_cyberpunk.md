## focus_sprint_workplace
Existing series: `focus_sprint_workplace__jamie_cruz__en_US__adhd_focus__the_whistle_is_not_the_start` (REUSE_EXISTING)
Demographic: seinen | Primary topic: adhd_focus | Genre: sports_competition

**Arc 1 — Recruit (ep_001-012):** Devon Alaric, 24, works night-shift outbound at a regional fulfillment warehouse where a scanner alarm chirps every few seconds and he has spent two years flinching at all of them equally. A break-room five-a-side futsal league (warehouse vs. the trucking dock, vs. receiving) posts a roster sheet three names short of eligible, and shift-lead Priya Osei — who also captains the warehouse team — recruits him off a single reflexive save he makes on a stray ball during a supply-closet detour. He says yes without deciding to, the way he does everything; the arc ends with his first practice, not a win.

**Arc 2 — Losing Streak (ep_013-024):** The warehouse team loses match after match because Devon jumps the referee's whistle before it finishes sounding — early runs, mistimed presses, a professional foul that costs a semifinal spot in the interdepot cup. Off the court the same pattern costs him: he reacts to every Slack ping and dock-radio call before he's read the whole message, and a shipping error traced to his line puts him on a written warning. Priya starts timing his reactions instead of correcting his footwork, and says only: "The whistle is not the start. Watch what happens before it."

**Arc 3 — Breakthrough (ep_025-036):** Devon stops treating the whistle as the cue and starts reading the setup that precedes it — the goalkeeper's weight shift, the sub coming off the bench, the exact rhythm of his own held breath — winning a scrappy qualifier not by reacting faster but by already being in position when the whistle catches up to him. The same shift shows up at the warehouse: he starts logging the pattern behind the alarms instead of answering each one, and Priya's team quietly earns a bid to the regional interdepot championship.

**Arc 4 — Championship Arc (ep_037-048):** The regional final lands the same week as Devon's performance review — the promotion that would move him off the floor and off the team both hang on the next ten days. The warehouse squad draws the defending champions, a slick corporate-sponsored team fronted by a striker who used to be exactly as reactive as Devon once was and never learned to stop. The series closes on the final whistle of the championship match, not on the scoreline it starts — Devon already moving before the sound finishes, the promotion decision still unopened in his locker.

## adhd_forge_mystery
Existing series: `adhd_forge_mystery__rex_dalton__en_US__adhd_focus__the_forge_hears_three_things` (REUSE_EXISTING)
Demographic: shonen | Primary topic: adhd_focus | Genre: sports_competition

**Arc 1 — Recruit (ep_001-012):** Cassius "Cass" Marrow, 15, gets caught after hours reforging a bent scrapyard chisel on the dead coal forge behind Kettleworth High's condemned shop building — the one piece of equipment the underfunded Forge Club hasn't had the budget to fire in a year. Club captain Odalys Reyes and the club's silent smith-advisor, Mr. Thane, watch him work a piece of scrap into something almost balanced with no training and no patience for the club's usual slow instruction. He's recruited on the spot, over the objections of members who've watched him get bounced from every team sport in school for the same reason: he can't sit still through anything that takes longer than a minute to matter.

**Arc 2 — Losing Streak (ep_013-024):** Kettleworth's four-person Forge Club enters the district youth-smithing qualifiers and Cass wrecks blade after blade — quenching a half-second early because a judge's cough distracted him, overheating a billet because he was watching the scoreboard clock instead of the metal's color. The club places dead last, and the district athletic board sets a one-season deadline: place at regionals or the forge shed gets converted to storage. Thane says almost nothing, but starts making Cass work blindfolded at the anvil, forcing him to find the steel by ear.

**Arc 3 — Breakthrough (ep_025-036):** Thane finally puts words to what he's been teaching: "The forge hears three things. Everything else is just noise wearing a sound." Cass learns to isolate exactly three signals out of the forge's roar — the color-ring of steel at temperature, the hiss of a true quench, the ring of a strike that lands square — and lets the crowd, the clock, and the judges' murmuring fall away as background weather. He forges a clean hunting knife under full qualifier pressure and Kettleworth scrapes into the regional bracket by one placement.

**Arc 4 — Championship Arc (ep_037-048):** Regionals pit Kettleworth against Ashgrove Academy's fully-sponsored forge team, captained by a smith who mocked Cass out of a youth workshop two years earlier for "not having the patience for real craft." The final round is a live-judged ceremonial-blade forge-off broadcast to the whole district, with the shop's survival and Cass's own place on a team for the first time riding on one heat. The arc ends on the last hammer strike ringing true and the crowd noise arriving a half-second late — Cass already knew, before the judges said a word, exactly which sound the metal had made.

## digital_ground
Existing series: `digital_ground__jun_park__en_US__burnout__the_feed_is_louder_than_blood` (REUSE_EXISTING)
Demographic: manhwa | Primary topic: burnout | Genre: sci_fi_cyberpunk

**Arc 1 — System Optimization (ep_001-012):** Jun Osei-Park, 27, is Halcyon Dynamics' youngest lead engineer on "the Ground" — the sensory-overlay grid that feeds the arcology's residents a curated stream of alerts, rankings, and ambient telemetry through their neural ports. He has optimized his own sleep, focus windows, and dopamine cycles with the same tooling he uses on the city's infrastructure, and his cohort treats him as the model of what the Ground makes possible: a body running at full, sustained throughput. The arc ends on his promotion to lead the Ground's next-generation rollout — the reward that will make everything in arc 2 worse.

**Arc 2 — Body/Identity Cost (ep_013-024):** The optimization stack Jun built for himself starts overriding signals it was never meant to touch — his cardiac-alert overlay flags a "false positive" that turns out to be real chest pain he'd trained himself to route around, and a status ping he barely registers turns out to be a friend's message he never actually read. He doubles his dosage of focus-stack instead of slowing down, and the feed — quite literally, per the title — gets loud enough that he stops being able to hear his own pulse under it. The rollout he's leading starts quietly harvesting the same override behavior from every resident wearing the new build, and Jun is the one who signs off on it.

**Arc 3 — Resistance (ep_025-036):** A retired Halcyon engineer who tore out her own port years ago finds Jun after his optimization stack causes a public collapse the company quietly buries, and shows him the unfiltered telemetry: thousands of residents running the same override pattern that nearly stopped his heart. Jun starts smuggling a kill-patch into the next Ground update from inside his own team, risking his career, his clearance, and a direct confrontation with the executive who owns his modification's patents — the system's first real face in this series. The arc ends with the patch live and Halcyon security already tracing it back to his desk.

**Arc 4 — Re-humanization (ep_037-048):** On the run from Halcyon inside the arcology he helped wire, Jun doesn't tear out his own port — he rebuilds his relationship to it, choosing deliberately which signals get priority instead of running every channel at once, the same skill he once sold the company as pure throughput. The final confrontation is not a system he destroys but one he forces to answer to a body again: his own cardiac feed, restored to the top of the stack, audible over everything else for the first time in the series. The last panel closes on a heartbeat reading that finally outpaces the notification counter beside it — quieter than the ending Halcyon wanted, and the only one Jun trusts.
