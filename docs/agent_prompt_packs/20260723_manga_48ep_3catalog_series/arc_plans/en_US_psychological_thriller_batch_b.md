**Arc 1 — Obsession Seed (ep_001-012):** After nearly missing a critical call from exhaustion, EMT Cal Reed starts a private fatigue ledger in his shift-bench notebook — sleep hours, adrenaline crashes, near-miss timestamps — cross-referenced like a case file that will let him predict which shift produces the next mistake before it happens. He skips a standing dinner with his partner Renata to finish a column of numbers. The arc ends when the ledger correctly flags a coworker's error minutes before it happens: proof the system works, which pulls him deeper instead of settling him.

**Arc 2 — Spiral (ep_013-024):** The notebook becomes a covert shift-board in the break room, now cross-referencing colleagues' fatigue tells without their knowledge. Renata starts finding him awake at 3 a.m. re-running columns instead of sleeping; a senior medic, Dobrescu — who ran an identical board fifteen years ago and burned out into early retirement — recognizes the pattern and tries to warn him, not realizing he's describing himself. The board correctly predicts Dobrescu's own collapse, which only proves the method again. It also quietly causes a scheduling call Cal gets wrong. The arc ends on a coworker spotting the hidden board.

**Arc 3 — Exposure (ep_025-036):** The board goes semi-public — a supervisor finds it during a locker sweep, and "Cal's board" becomes station shorthand, half-joke and half-concern, threatening a fitness-for-duty review. Renata gives him an ultimatum: the board or her. The arc ends with Cal standing over the board at the exact moment a real-time entry would resolve whether the system caused, rather than caught, a colleague's mistake — and he doesn't finish the entry.

**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** A mass-casualty multi-shift event puts Cal in a moment where the ledger's prediction contradicts what his hands and instinct are telling him about a patient. He has to choose the page or the person in front of him. He doesn't burn the notebook and burnout isn't declared cured (acceptance_layer stays authored_candidate) — he leaves one shift's entry unlogged, on purpose, for the first time, and the station doesn't fall apart without it. What's materially bigger than arc 1: the whole crew's trust in him as "the one who never misses" is now public, Renata's presence in the final scene is a held-open door rather than a closed one, and his mastery of burnout deepens from managing exhaustion to recognizing which checks are actually keeping him safe versus which ones are the exhaustion talking.

The arc ends with an alarm going off in a genuinely ambiguous moment — she has to choose whether to trust the readout or the actual sensation in her chest — and she hesitates without answering either way.

**Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** A real emergency at the lab forces Cyra to act on what she physically feels while the rig's readout insists she's fine — the two disagree in front of witnesses who need her to move now. **Arc 4 — Reckoning (ep_037-048):** At the memoir's publication event, with a genuinely major accomplishment available to file as the closing proof, Helen has her hand on the next entry and doesn't make it — the file stays half-filled, on purpose, publicly.
