## Notes on imagery philosophy

1. **Specific over generic.** "Mount Kurama with cedar-lined stone steps" beats "a forested mountain." Names land harder than archetypes.
2. **Architectural over figural** when possible. Buildings, scrolls, vajras, kalashas, lanterns — these never have face-leak risk and carry meaning iconographically.
3. **External over internal.** Earths held in cupped hands at heart-level (external) instead of Earths placed where chests should be (internal). Halos around bodies instead of inside them.
4. **One subject focus.** Even crowd shots have a clear primary attention point.
5. **Painterly always.** Oil-painting / Vajrayana-thangka / ink-wash registers always. Never photoreal, never stock, never neon, never CGI-game-asset.
6. **Sacred substance over visual effect.** Pearl-light, sacred geometry, mandala, halo — these are the visual vocabulary. Sparkle, particle-trail, lens-flare, motion-blur — these are not.
