This report presents a comprehensive systems-design blueprint for a deterministic, high-volume social media publishing engine optimized for the domains of self-help, mental wellbeing, personal growth, and practical philosophy. Designed to operate within the technical constraints and platform parameters of the current digital landscape, the system decouples content inputs, visual style policies, brand assets, and platform boundaries into a modular, rule-based rendering architecture.

Modern digital consumption is defined by a significant decay in user focus, with cross-platform studies showing that screen-based attention spans have contracted to a median of 40 seconds, and mobile-first users in the 18–34 cohort average just 6.8 seconds of focus before swipe-away triggers occur. To counteract this rapid cognitive disengagement, this architecture is engineered to achieve visual comprehension within a sub-one-second threshold. It implements a zero-shot, constraint-based rendering pipeline that scales to generate thousands of platform-native assets without requiring manual, case-by-case design intervention.   

The system relies on a structural separation of concerns, modeling publishing assets not as static files, but as dynamic coordinate zones on a standardized grid. Visual output is controlled by a deterministic selection engine powered by a contextual Multi-Armed Bandit algorithm using Bayesian posteriors and dynamic Beta priors. This approach ensures that newly introduced templates undergo controlled exploration without diluting core brand reach or performance baselines.

Trend Analysis & Strategic Preservation
Short-lived visual novelties like loud neon brutalism are declining due to aesthetic fatigue and structural accessibility barriers. In contrast, editorial minimalism, clean diagram cards, and authentic note-app layouts remain highly stable evergreen performers.   

To protect the system from obsolescence when visual trends shift, the composition templates isolate core structural geometries from ephemeral stylistic choices, allowing style sheets to be updated without breaking the underlying rendering logic.

Deliverable 6: Carousel Story-Engine Catalogue
To maximize user dwell time, the rendering engine runs against 12 standardized, slide-by-slide structural frameworks.   

+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                      `carousel_problem_mechanism_solution`                                        |
+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|  [Slide 1]          |  [Slide 2]          |  [Slide 3]          |  [Slide 4]          |  [Slide 5]                |
|  * The Core Hook    |  * Common Failures  |  * Neuro-Mechanism  |  * The Paradigm Shift|  * Practical Execution   |
|  * Pattern Interrupt|  * Emotional Anchor |  * Why You Freeze   |  * Core Truth       |  * Step 1, 2, 3           |
|  * w: <= 12 words   |  * w: <= 20 words   |  * w: <= 18 words   |  * w: <= 15 words   |  * w: <= 22 words         |
+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|  [Slide 6]          |  [Slide 7]          |                                                                       |
|  * Climax Outcome   |  * Clean Outro      |                                                                       |
|  * Core Shift       |  * Call to Action   |                                                                       |
|  * w: <= 16 words   |  * w: <= 10 words   |                                                                       |
+-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------+
Operational Slide Engines
1. carousel_problem_mechanism_solution
Slide Target Range: 5 to 7 Slides   

Slide-by-Slide Mechanics:

Slide 1: Diagnostic Hook. State the core emotional challenge in bold display serif (e.g., "Why you collapse under emotional avoidance"). Text envelope ≤12 words. Visual requirement: Low-contrast ambient image backdrop.   

Slide 2: Common Missteps. Detail the logical mistakes users make trying to resolve this feeling. Text envelope ≤20 words.   

Slide 3: Neuro-Mechanism. Break down the physiological triggers (e.g., autonomic nervous system reaction). Text envelope ≤18 words.   

Slide 4: Paradigm Shift. Highlight the primary conceptual reframe. Text envelope ≤15 words.   

Slide 5: Practical Action. Step-by-step somatic recovery tools. Text envelope ≤22 words.   

Slide 6: Climax Outcome. Illustrate the emotional state reached once these tools are applied. Text envelope ≤16 words.   

Slide 7: Core CTA. Dedicated layout with interactive save elements. Text envelope ≤10 words.   

Visual Continuity & Styling: Minimal progress bar running on the horizontal margin (y:[0.03]) across every card.   

Failure Conditions: Exceeding 25 words on any non-diagram slide.   

2. carousel_myth_truth_application
Slide Target Range: 5 Slides   

Slide-by-Slide Mechanics:

Slide 1: Primary Lie. Display a widely accepted but limiting belief in high-contrast typographic layout. Text envelope ≤10 words.   

Slide 2: Real-World Consequence. Detail why this conventional expectation causes chronic stress or burnout. Text envelope ≤18 words.   

Slide 3: Core Truth. A corrective, liberating re-frame. Text envelope ≤15 words.   

Slide 4: Somatic Application. Simple mental exercises to integrate the truth. Text envelope ≤20 words.   

Slide 5: Direct Outro. Invitation to share with others. Text envelope ≤8 words.   

Visual Continuity & Styling: Slide backgrounds transition from cool, desaturated colors to warm, high-contrast tones.   

Failure Conditions: Pairing high-saturation backgrounds with text overlays that do not pass APCA audits.   

3. carousel_hook_insights_takeaway
Slide Target Range: 6 Slides   

Slide-by-Slide Mechanics:

Slide 1: Broad Hook. Core topic intro (e.g., "5 rules for emotional boundaries"). Text envelope ≤14 words.   

Slides 2–5: Individual Lessons. Present one clear, self-contained lesson per slide using a consistent layout grid. Text envelope ≤15 words per slide.   

Slide 6: Unified Summary. A closing summary paired with save callouts. Text envelope ≤12 words.   

Visual Continuity & Styling: Clean horizontal line transitions running on y:[0.60], sliding forward with each card progression.   

Failure Conditions: Changes in font weights or alignment systems between slides 2 and 5.   

4. carousel_mistake_consequence_correction
Slide Target Range: 6 Slides   

Slide-by-Slide Mechanics:

Slide 1: Common Mistake. Detail a self-sabotaging habit. Text envelope ≤12 words.   

Slide 2: Hidden Consequence. Explain how this behavior drains personal energy. Text envelope ≤18 words.   

Slide 3: Physiological Trigger. Uncover why the nervous system falls back on this behavior when stressed. Text envelope ≤16 words.   

Slide 4: Pattern Interrupt. A quick practice to stop this behavioral loop. Text envelope ≤15 words.   

Slide 5: Actionable Alternative. Behavioral changes to implement. Text envelope ≤22 words.   

Slide 6: Outro. Reflection question with a save callout. Text envelope ≤10 words.   

Visual Continuity & Styling: Contrast colors accent key words (e.g., Crimson on mistake, Jade on alternative).

Failure Conditions: Inconsistent contrast highlights across slides.

5. carousel_before_turningpoint_after
Slide Target Range: 5 Slides

Deterministic Selection Logic
Python
import numpy as np

def calculate_apca_luminance(rgb):
    # Calculates the relative luminance of a color for APCA [cite: 18]
    r, g, b = [val / 255.0 for val in rgb]
    return 0.2126729 * (r ** 2.2) + 0.7151522 * (g ** 2.2) + 0.0721750 * (b ** 2.2)

def apca_contrast(bg_rgb, fg_rgb):
    # Computes APCA contrast scale values
    y_bg = calculate_apca_luminance(bg_rgb)
    y_fg = calculate_apca_luminance(fg_rgb)
    
    if y_bg > y_fg:
        contrast = (y_bg ** 0.56 - y_fg ** 0.62) * 190.0
    else:
        contrast = (y_bg ** 0.65 - y_fg ** 0.56) * 190.0
        
    return abs(contrast)

def execute_selection_pipeline(request, asset_index):
    # Matches content, templates, and imagery dynamically
    platform = request["platform"]
    brand_house = request["brand_house"]
    topic = request["topic"]
    
    # 1. Filter out recently used templates to prevent visual fatigue
    eligible_templates = [
        t for t in asset_index.templates 
        if platform in t.platforms and t.recency_penalty_index < 0.2
    ]
    
    valid_candidates = []
    for template in eligible_templates:
        # Match images based on CLIP vector similarity
        image_candidate = asset_index.query_clip_match(template, topic)
        bg_rgb = image_candidate.get_dominant_color_at_coordinates(template.text_zones)
        fg_rgb = brand_house.palette.text_primary
        
        # 2. Keep candidates that pass APCA contrast standards [cite: 53]
        if apca_contrast(bg_rgb, fg_rgb) >= 60.0:
            valid_candidates.append((template, image_candidate))
            
    if not valid_candidates:
        # Fallback to typographic block layout if no images pass contrast audits
        return asset_index.fallback_typographic_variant(brand_house)
        
    # 3. Thompson Sampling draws from Beta posterior parameters
    winning_score = -1.0
    winning_match = None
    
    for template, image in valid_candidates:
        alpha = template.performance_prior_alpha
        beta = template.performance_prior_beta
        draw = np.random.beta(alpha, beta)
        
        if draw > winning_score:
            winning_score = draw
            winning_match = (template, image)
            
    return winning_match

Pre-Rendering Automated Checks
Dimensions Verification: Check that target canvas outputs align with platform dimensions (1080×1350 px for Instagram portrait feeds). On discrepancy: Template Reselection.   

Text Envelope Bounds: Check if total string length fits the designated text card zones. On failure: Automatic Repair (run line rebreaking logic) or Template Reselection.

Semantic Consistency Check: Verify CLIP similarity index score is ≥0.22. On failure: Image Reselection.   

Recency Safety Check: Verify the selected template family has not been used within the last 5 posts to prevent repetition. On failure: Template Reselection.

Post-Rendering Process Checks
APCA Perceptual Contrast Audit: Verify text overlays meet contrast thresholds (Lc≥60 for headers). On failure: Automatic Repair (inject a translucent 15% dark background plate or gradient fade under the text zones).   

Safe Zone Boundary Fit: Verify no text overlays sit within the top 250px or bottom 250px of stories to prevent UI overlap. On failure: Hard Failure (block publication, flag for human review).   

Line-Wrap Audit: Identify awkward word wraps or single-character lines. On failure: Automatic Repair (inject a non-breaking space \u00A0 between the final two words to prevent orphans).   

Video Timing Checks: Check if spoken subtitles exceed 260 words per minute. On failure: Template Reselection (switch kinetic layouts back to meditative loops).

Visual Accessibility Parameters
Perceptual Color Contrast: Contrast metrics are calculated using the Accessible Perceptual Contrast Algorithm (APCA) to ensure optimal readability under real-world viewing conditions.   

The system blocks the export of any asset where text contrast falls below Lc≥60 for headers and Lc≥75 for body copy. Text-on-image designs use soft dark background overlay plates to guarantee contrast compatibility.   

Readability-by-Construction Layouts: Standard text blocks use highly readable sans-serif body fonts like Proxima Nova or specialized open-interior fonts like Atkinson Hyperlegible. Left-aligned text layouts preserve natural tracking shapes.   

Body copy is limited to 30 to 75 characters per line to reduce reading fatigue, paired with generous line-height spacing ratios (≥1.5× font size).   

Motion Pacing Safety: To protect viewers with vestibular disorders or motion sensitivity, vertical videos exclude flashing or rapid transitions.   

Subtitles use a consistent vertical center safe-zone alignment, with word-by-word highlight subtitles limited to a comfortable reading pace below 260 words per minute.   

Structured Screen-Reader Context: Visual components are paired with dynamic, programmatically generated alt-text fields that describe the image subject and include all overlaid text directly within the metadata layer.   

Ethical Design Guardrails
Validation of Wellbeing Claims: The words-bank validation engine screens copy variants against clinical mental health dictionaries. Objective assertions (e.g., "This technique cures anxiety") are automatically replaced with reflective prompts (e.g., "A somatic practice to help regulate nervous system activity").

Rejection of Fear-Based Hook Tactics: The selection engine flags and blocks clickbait hooks that leverage user anxiety or shame to drive engagement (e.g., "If you don't do this, your healing is failing").

Verified Wisdom Sourcing: Quote assets must contain verified attribution metadata; the rendering script blocks any quotation block that lacks verified source profiling.
