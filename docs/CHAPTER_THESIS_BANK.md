# CHAPTER THESIS BANK

**Canonical thesis sentences for each chapter intent × engine combination**

For arc generation, theme development, and writer reference. Every chapter in the book needs a thesis — a claim someone could argue with, not a direction to move. This document maps all 20 chapter intents across the 7 engine types with specific, one-sentence theses.

*Last updated: 2026-03-06*

---

## DOCUMENT STRUCTURE

This bank contains:
1. **First Half (Intents 1–9)**: The foundational cycle that appears once per arc
2. **Second Half (Intents 10–20)**: The deeper 11-intent sequence that replaces repetition
3. **Thread Sentences**: The forward-pull language for each intent (for INTEGRATION section)
4. **Quick Reference Tables**: Engine × intent lookup

---

## PART I: FIRST HALF (9 INTENTS)

These intents form the basic arc of recognition and somatic work. All 9 appear once per master arc, followed by the second-half sequence.

### INTENT 1: ESTABLISH_MASK

**Definition**: The reader's protective strategy is named and recognized — it has a shape and a history.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your constant surveillance keeps you performing when you're already running on empty. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | The hypervigilance you've built is so familiar you can't feel it anymore. |
| **Shame** | You learned to disappear into usefulness before anyone could find fault. |
| **Grief** | You've been still and small to honor what was lost, and that stillness became your identity. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"The thing you do—the checking in, the early mornings, the mental ledger of who needs what—didn't appear suddenly. It built slowly, each small act of supervision stacking into a permanent posture. Your body knows it as much as your mind does. You check because checking is how you prove you're worthy of the space you take up. And you've been checking so long that you've forgotten what it felt like not to."

---

### INTENT 2: EXPOSE_COST

**Definition**: The hidden price of maintaining the strategy is made visible — what it costs to keep the mask in place.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your body is paying the price of the strategy your mind thinks is keeping you safe. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | The protection you've built is expensive and you're the only one paying. |
| **Shame** | Usefulness is slowly consuming the parts of you that have nothing to offer. |
| **Grief** | Honoring loss through absence means you're also absent from your own life. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"The alertness—it protected you once. When you were young, when the world required you to sense the shift in a room before anything happened. Now it costs you sleep. It costs you the ability to sit at a table without cataloging exits. It costs you the friends who got tired of explaining themselves. The system that kept you alive is now keeping you from living."

---

### INTENT 3: DESTABILIZE_STRATEGY

**Definition**: The strategy itself is revealed as the problem — it's not working the way the reader believes it is.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | All your monitoring hasn't actually prevented the collapse you fear. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your preparation has prepared you for nothing that actually happens. |
| **Shame** | Disappearing has never made you safe, only made you disappear. |
| **Grief** | Stillness isn't honoring what was lost; it's letting loss define the rest. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"You thought if you stayed vigilant, if you caught the problem early, managed the load, stayed ahead—you could prevent the thing you fear. But the thing you fear isn't something external you can monitor. It's already here. It's in your chest every morning. The watching didn't prevent it. The watching is it."

---

### INTENT 4: REVEAL_HIDDEN_BELIEF

**Definition**: The core belief running underneath the pattern surfaces — the conviction that's been driving everything.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You believe your value lives in your usefulness, nowhere else. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You believe the only safety is total knowledge, total control. |
| **Shame** | You believe if anyone saw the real you, you wouldn't be chosen. |
| **Grief** | You believe your love is measured in how much you've been willing to forfeit. |

**Prose Example (Shame engine, self-worth topic)**:
"Underneath every time you diminished yourself, there's a single conviction: if I'm too visible, I'll be rejected. Not because you did something wrong, but because of what I am. So you learned the angles. The way to take up less room. The way to be useful enough that your mere existence could be tolerated. And you've been living from that belief for so long that you thought it was the truth."

---

### INTENT 5: CONFRONTATION

**Definition**: The moment of clear seeing — where the reader can't pretend they haven't understood what they understand.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You see now that the system you built is turning against you. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You can no longer deny that your fear is your own creation. |
| **Shame** | The invisibility you've maintained is a choice, not a destiny. |
| **Grief** | You're aware now that you're the one who chose to stop living. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"There's a moment where you recognize that the danger you've been protecting against isn't in the world. It's the protection itself. The scanning. The preparation. The catastrophizing. You built it so carefully, and now you're trapped inside it. And you know—really know—that you put yourself there. The moment you can't unknow that is the moment everything shifts."

---

### INTENT 6: SOMATIC_REPAIR

**Definition**: The body holds what the mind tried to manage — and the body has its own path to healing.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your nervous system knows the truth before your mind will admit it. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your body can learn to trust before your thoughts catch up. |
| **Shame** | Feeling is the path back to yourself when thinking got you lost. |
| **Grief** | The body grieves differently than the mind; it holds what can't be said. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"There's a reason you keep coming back to this: the tightness in your chest, the way sleep won't come, the small tremor when you're supposed to be calm. Your mind has been arguing that these are just side effects, acceptable losses in the pursuit of being reliable. But your body isn't arguing. It's speaking plainly. And it's been speaking for a long time. The somatic work begins when you stop translating what your body is saying and just listen."

---

### INTENT 7: RELATIONAL_SHIFT

**Definition**: The pattern lives in and affects relationships — how the reader shows up in relation to others is being revealed.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your control strategy makes genuine connection impossible; they feel managed, not met. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your need for reassurance is actually a need to be believed about your fear. |
| **Shame** | You've been relating from a place of apology, and they've learned to accept it. |
| **Grief** | Your withdrawal has convinced people that you prefer to be left alone. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, boundaries topic)**:
"The thing about always being the one who's watching, managing, staying ahead—it changes what's possible between you and another person. They begin to feel like objects of your concern rather than people with their own autonomy. They learn they're not safe to need something from you. They learn you can't actually receive. And slowly, 'I'm taking care of it' becomes a wall instead of a gesture."

---

### INTENT 8: EMBODIED_IDENTITY

**Definition**: Identity is being revised through the body, not the mind — the person you thought you were is shifting.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You are not the efficient, untiring thing you've been trying to be. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You are not your anxiety; you are the one anxiety is happening to. |
| **Shame** | You are not small; you are the one who learned to make yourself small. |
| **Grief** | You are not an absence; you are a person who was living in one. |

**Prose Example (Shame engine, self-worth topic)**:
"The person you thought you were—the careful, unobtrusive one, the person who doesn't ask for much, who disappears into helpfulness—might not actually be who you are at all. What if that was just what you built to survive in a particular house, with particular people, in a particular time? What if there's someone else underneath—someone bigger, louder, more present—who's been waiting to exist?"

---

### INTENT 9: GROUNDED_REFRAME

**Definition**: A new frame holds more truth than the old one — a different way of understanding what's true about the reader.

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your body's depletion is not weakness; it's information about what was unsustainable. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your anxiety isn't a character flaw; it's a sensitivity that needs different care. |
| **Shame** | Your need to be seen isn't arrogance; it's necessary to being human. |
| **Grief** | Your pain is evidence of what mattered to you; don't call it weakness. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"What if the anxiety isn't a problem you created through weakness? What if it's a nervous system that perceives signals others miss—one that learned to do that in order to keep you safe? The frame changes everything. Instead of 'I'm broken and need to be fixed,' it becomes 'I have a particular sensitivity that's been pointed at the wrong things.' The sensitivity isn't the problem. The targeting is."

## PART II: SECOND HALF (11 NEW INTENTS)

These intents deepen the work beyond the first-half cycle. They appear after the foundational 9 intents and replace any repetition.

---

### INTENT 10: WITNESS_WITHOUT_FIX

**Definition**: Seeing the pattern without immediately trying to change it — the ability to notice without correcting.

**Phase**: Mid-book deepening

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You can observe the urge to manage without acting on every impulse. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | The anxiety can be present without requiring you to solve it. |
| **Shame** | You can see the impulse to disappear without disappearing. |
| **Grief** | Loss can be real without requiring you to become smaller in response. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"This is harder than it sounds. Not 'I'm managing my urge to check'—that's still doing something. But genuinely noticing: here is the impulse to scan, to verify, to stay ahead. And not moving. Not fixing. Not trying to reframe. Just: I can see this. This is happening. I don't have to do anything about it right now. That pause—that's where something new becomes possible."

---

### INTENT 11: THE_COST_NAMED

**Definition**: The specific thing the reader has been losing, named precisely — not vague depletion, but concrete loss.

**Phase**: Mid-book deepening

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You've lost the ability to rest without guilt, and rest is what you need most. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You've lost the capacity to be surprised by joy because you're always braced. |
| **Shame** | You've lost the chance to be ordinary, and ordinariness was where safety was. |
| **Grief** | You've lost the companion who understood your language before any words were spoken. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"It's not that you're tired, though you are. It's that you've lost the ability to anticipate something good without immediately scanning for the catch. Every celebration arrives with a shadow. Every moment of lightness comes with the wait for the other shoe. You've lost the thing animals have—the ability to be fully present in a moment of safety because you know what safety feels like in your body. You can't remember that anymore."

---

### INTENT 12: THE_OLDEST_VERSION

**Definition**: Where the pattern started — not as excuse, but as information that changes how you relate to it.

**Phase**: Mid-book deepening

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | That vigilance came from somewhere real; it was once the smartest thing you could do. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your fear learned in a house where things actually happened without warning. |
| **Shame** | You disappeared because the cost of being seen was higher than the cost of hiding. |
| **Grief** | You learned to be still from someone who needed you to be; stillness was service. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"When you were small, if you paid attention—if you caught the shift in tone, the tightening around the eyes—you could sometimes prevent the explosion. Your vigilance wasn't invented in a vacuum. It was created by a nervous system learning to read a dangerous environment. It kept you safe. It was brilliant. And now that environment is gone, but the system is still running, still convinced that vigilance is what keeps you alive. Understanding where it came from doesn't fix it, but it changes your relationship to it."

---

### INTENT 13: THE_BODY_AFTER

**Definition**: What the nervous system does when the acute threat has passed — the strange territory of settling.

**Phase**: Mid-book deepening

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your body doesn't know what to do with the space where urgency used to be. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Calm is unfamiliar to your nervous system; it almost feels like danger. |
| **Shame** | The absence of judgment feels like a trap; you're waiting for the other person to notice you're present. |
| **Grief** | The loosening in your chest will feel like betrayal before it feels like relief. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"Calm is a country you don't speak. When the body stops bracing, there's a kind of emptiness that most people would call peace. You call it exposure. Your nervous system has been in overdrive for so long that rest feels like vulnerability. As if the moment you stop scanning, something will happen. There's a disorientation in actually being safe—a strangeness to believing that the moment you're in right now isn't a setup for something worse."

---

### INTENT 14: WHAT_YOU_PROTECTED

**Definition**: The thing the pattern was built to keep safe — what vulnerability would cost you.

**Phase**: Mid-book deepening

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | Your vigilance protects everyone around you from ever having to need you. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Your fear protects you from the grief underneath—the thing you're actually afraid of. |
| **Shame** | Your invisibility protects you from the possibility of being truly rejected. |
| **Grief** | Your stillness protects others from the burden of your aliveness. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, boundaries topic)**:
"If you stopped managing, stopped staying ahead, stopped being reliable—what would happen? That's the question your vigilance answers before you even ask it. Your mother wouldn't unravel. Your family wouldn't collapse. Your partner wouldn't realize they don't need you. Or would they? The surveillance isn't really about preventing disaster. It's about preventing abandonment. It's about ensuring you'll never be disposable. And that's what you're protecting—the belief that if you stop being essential, you'll be discarded."

---

### INTENT 15: THE_PRICE_OF_KNOWING

**Definition**: Seeing it clearly means the reader can no longer claim ignorance — the particular weight of knowing.

**Phase**: Late book

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You can no longer unsee that you're building your own collapse through vigilance. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | Awareness that your fear is self-generated is a burden you can't put down. |
| **Shame** | You've seen yourself disappearing and can't pretend it's necessary anymore. |
| **Grief** | You know now that your stillness is a choice, and that means you can unchosen it. |

**Prose Example (Shame engine, self-worth topic)**:
"There's a particular exhaustion in seeing yourself clearly. Before you understood—you could say it was just how things were. You were small because that was your nature. You were unobtrusive because that was kindness. But you've seen it now. You've watched yourself diminish in real time, and you've understood why. The thing about seeing is you can't claim ignorance anymore. You can't say 'I don't know how to be different.' You know. You know exactly. Which means the choice to stay small is now knowingly made."

---

### INTENT 16: MOVING_WITHOUT_CERTAINTY

**Definition**: Taking action before the internal conflict resolves — acting while still uncertain.

**Phase**: Late book

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You must stop managing before you feel safe; safety comes after the stopping. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You move toward what you need while the terror insists it's not safe yet. |
| **Shame** | You take up space while part of you still insists you shouldn't be visible. |
| **Grief** | You live while grief is still fresh, before it becomes safe to live. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"The waiting is the trap. You think: when I feel ready, when the anxiety is less, when I'm certain it's safe—then I'll move. Then I'll speak. Then I'll try. But the anxiety will not ask permission. It will not diminish because you've been good. You move while the fear is still there. You speak while your heart is still racing. You step into the territory that terrifies you, not after you've felt calm, but while you're trembling. And somehow, that's where you discover you're more capable than anxiety said you were."

---

### INTENT 17: THE_RELATIONAL_TRUTH

**Definition**: What others have been experiencing in the reader's presence — how the pattern lands for them.

**Phase**: Late book

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | They've felt controlled by your management; they've learned to mistrust your care. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | They've learned that reassuring you is an endless job with no exit. |
| **Shame** | They've experienced your absence and learned not to expect your presence. |
| **Grief** | They've adjusted to you being unavailable, and that adjustment has become permanent. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, boundaries topic)**:
"This is the hard part. It's not just that the pattern costs you—it costs them. They feel the checking, the management, the way you're always half-attending to something about them that needs to be fixed. They've learned not to be spontaneous around you because spontaneity means you have to adjust your plan. They've learned that your love comes with an agenda. They've learned that to be with you, they have to accept being managed. And the worst part: they might not even realize it yet. But they feel it. They feel it every time."

---

### INTENT 18: THE_NEW_SELF

**Definition**: The person being built through this is already present in small moments — the emerging identity.

**Phase**: Closing

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | The person who can rest without earning it is already showing up in brief moments. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | The you that isn't afraid is flickering into existence in moments you're not watching for it. |
| **Shame** | The visible version of you—the one who doesn't apologize for existing—appears when you're not guarding. |
| **Grief** | The person living fully is already present in the moments when pain doesn't silence you. |

**Prose Example (False Alarm engine, anxiety topic)**:
"You've had them—those moments where something made you laugh and you forgot to scan the room. Where someone spoke and you believed them instead of searching for the hidden meaning. Where you were so absorbed in what was happening that anxiety couldn't get your attention. They're brief. They feel almost accidental. But they're not an aberration. They're the direction. The you that isn't consumed by fear is already real. It's just small still. And your job right now is to notice those moments, protect them, and let them slowly take up more space."

---

### INTENT 19: CARRYING_FORWARD

**Definition**: Not healed. Differently oriented. What integration actually means — the pattern continues but you're not possessed by it.

**Phase**: Closing

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You'll manage your energy differently now; not because you've stopped caring, but because you've learned to care for yourself. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | The fear will still arrive, but you'll know it's not the truth, and that changes everything. |
| **Shame** | The impulse to disappear will still be there; you'll choose visibility anyway. |
| **Grief** | The grief doesn't leave; you become someone who can carry it without letting it carry you. |

**Prose Example (Watcher engine, burnout topic)**:
"This isn't a before-and-after story where vigilance goes away. It doesn't. You don't unsee what you've learned to see. But you can hold it differently. You can say: yes, there are things that need attention. And I cannot be the one to give it all the time. I need rest more than the world needs my exhaustion. You learn to manage—not everything, just what's yours. Not because you've healed, but because you've shifted orientation. You're no longer running from collapse. You're running toward sustainability. And that changes what you can carry."

---

### INTENT 20: THE_OPEN_HAND

**Definition**: Holding the old pattern loosely — what is released, what is kept, what becomes possible.

**Phase**: Closing

| Engine Type | Thesis Sentence |
|---|---|
| **Watcher** (burnout) | You release the need for perfect control and keep the ability to affect your own life. |
| **False Alarm** (anxiety) | You release the belief that safety requires knowing everything and keep the wisdom you've earned. |
| **Shame** | You release the invisibility and keep the sensitivity that taught you to be careful. |
| **Grief** | You release the paralysis and keep the love that made you still. |

**Prose Example (Shame engine, self-worth topic)**:
"There's a way to let the old strategy go without discarding what it taught you. You can release the need to disappear while keeping the sensitivity that made you aware of others' needs. You can stop apologizing for your presence while honoring the careful attention that shaped you. What you held in your hands before—the invisibility, the usefulness, the smallness—those you can open. Let them fall. But the thing underneath—the capacity to sense, to attune, to care about something beyond yourself—that you keep. That was never the problem. It was only ever pointed at the wrong thing. Now you can point it at your own life."

## THREAD SENTENCES

**Forward-pull language for INTEGRATION sections.** These are the quiet, unresolved closes that invite the reader into the next chapter. One or two per intent.

### FIRST HALF THREAD SENTENCES

**Intent 1: Establish_Mask**
- "But there's something underneath the watching, something that might want something different."
- "You know what the pattern is now. The harder question is: what would you be without it?"

**Intent 2: Expose_Cost**
- "And still, every day, you pay."
- "The protection is expensive. You're beginning to understand you can't afford it forever."

**Intent 3: Destabilize_Strategy**
- "The strategy was supposed to keep the worst from happening. The worst is happening anyway."
- "Everything you built to prevent collapse is building the collapse instead."

**Intent 4: Reveal_Hidden_Belief**
- "A single conviction has been running your life, and you've never questioned it until now."
- "What if you're not worthless without your usefulness? What if that's the lie you need to stop believing?"

**Intent 5: Confrontation**
- "You can't unknow this. You can't pretend you don't see it. Everything changes now."
- "The moment you see it clearly is the moment you become responsible for what you do next."

**Intent 6: Somatic_Repair**
- "Your body has been speaking. You're finally learning to listen."
- "The healing begins when you stop thinking your way through and start feeling your way through."

**Intent 7: Relational_Shift**
- "The people around you are beginning to realize they don't have to be managed. The question is whether you can let them be unmeshed from your control."
- "You've been relating from a particular shape. What becomes possible when you soften that shape?"

**Intent 8: Embodied_Identity**
- "The person you've been trying to be is not who you are. Who are you when you're not trying?"
- "There's someone underneath the strategy. And they're bigger than you thought."

**Intent 9: Grounded_Reframe**
- "The story changes when you change the frame. Everything you thought was weakness might be something else entirely."
- "You're not broken. You're not wrong. You're someone whose sensitivity is pointing at the wrong things."

### SECOND HALF THREAD SENTENCES

**Intent 10: Witness_Without_Fix**
- "The pattern is still there. You're learning to let it be there without chasing it."
- "This is a quieter kind of power—not controlling the pattern, but not being controlled by it."

**Intent 11: The_Cost_Named**
- "You've named it now. The absence is real and it has a shape."
- "What you've lost matters. And maybe—maybe—there's a way to get some of it back."

**Intent 12: The_Oldest_Version**
- "It came from somewhere real. That doesn't change what you need to do now, but it changes how you think about it."
- "Your young self was brilliant. She kept you safe. She doesn't have to run the show anymore."

**Intent 13: The_Body_After**
- "There's an emptiness that looks like peace. You're learning to believe it's real."
- "Calm is a foreign country. You're slowly learning its language."

**Intent 14: What_You_Protected**
- "You were protecting something. You finally understand what. The question is what it costs to keep protecting it."
- "The pattern wasn't random. It was built to keep something safe. Now you know what, and you can decide if it's worth the price."

**Intent 15: The_Price_of_Knowing**
- "You can't unsee yourself. The only way out is through."
- "Ignorance is gone. Knowledge is a weight. But you're stronger than the weight."

**Intent 16: Moving_Without_Certainty**
- "You don't feel ready. You move anyway. That's where it starts."
- "The certainty will never come. You're learning to move in spite of that."

**Intent 17: The_Relational_Truth**
- "They've been adjusting to you for a long time. Now you're learning to adjust to them."
- "The pattern affected them in ways you're only now beginning to see. That seeing is the beginning of change."

**Intent 18: The_New_Self**
- "They're small moments still. Brief flickers. But they're real. And they're multiplying."
- "The person you're becoming is already here. You're just making room for her to be visible."

**Intent 19: Carrying_Forward**
- "Not healed. Integrated. You're the same person, but you know something different now about what you can carry."
- "The pattern doesn't disappear. You become someone who can live alongside it without being crushed by it."

**Intent 20: The_Open_Hand**
- "You're learning to hold things loosely. The past can rest in an open hand."
- "There's something on the other side of release. You're about to find out what."

## NOTES FOR IMPLEMENTATION

### For Arc Generation
- Intents 1–9 form the core cycle; they appear once per arc
- Intents 10–20 should be generated for the second-half sequence
- Each chapter should be assigned: `chapter_intent`, `thesis`, `engine_type`, `topic`, `phase`
- Thesis sentences are non-negotiable; they anchor chapter development

### For Writers
- Use the thesis as your true north—every section should serve the claim
- Thread sentences are the quiet close; they create forward-pull into the next chapter
- The prose examples show how a thesis can be expanded into story-level development
- Intents are the *what*; thesis sentences are the *claim*

### Engine Types (Master Arc Architectures)
1. **Watcher** — Hypervigilance, monitoring, control strategy (burnout)
2. **False Alarm** — Constant threat detection, bracing, preparation (anxiety)
3. **Shame** — Invisibility, usefulness, small-self strategy (shame, self-worth)
4. **Grief** — Stillness, absence, honoring-through-withdrawal (grief, loss)
5. **Spiral** — Rumination, cycling, logic-traps (obsessive patterns)
6. **Overwhelm** — Capacity exceeded, shutting down, flooding (sensory/emotional overload)
7. **Comparison** — Relative positioning, inadequacy, status-checking (comparison culture, social media)

*Note: This bank currently covers theses for Watcher, False_Alarm, Shame, and Grief engines. Spiral, Overwhelm, and Comparison theses should be generated using the same structure.*
