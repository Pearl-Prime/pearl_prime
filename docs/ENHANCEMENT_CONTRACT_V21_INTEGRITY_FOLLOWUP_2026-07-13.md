# Enhancement Contract V2.1 Integrity Follow-Up

Status: implemented as a runtime integrity layer over the merged enhancement-contract proof.

This follow-up closes the missing bundle gap: V2.1 is now represented by code, focused tests, config declarations, and an operator-readable receipt rather than by an off-repo apply script.

## What Is Enforced

- Optional accents retain the working-prior doctrine: 5-7 accent chapters, hard max 8, 7-9 total accents, hard max 10, max 2 per chapter.
- Proof and embodiment surfaces do not consume optional-accent capacity: `TROUBLESHOOTING`, `CITED_EVIDENCE`, `EXTERNAL_STORY`, and `AUTHOR_DISCLOSURE` are tracked separately.
- External stories require a function tag plus source/citation/truth metadata.
- Cited evidence requires citation and verification metadata.
- Callback returns require a real prior plant, a return function, and semantic development metadata.
- Parables must be carried by a story-like surface.
- Author disclosure and author commentary remain distinct classes.

## What Stays Advisory

- Supported optional underfill is a warning, not a hard fail.
- Preferred placement misses remain advisory when a legal fallback was used.
- Repeated analogy/metaphor fingerprints are warnings, not automatic rejects.

## Why This Exists

The V2.1 memo is a working-prior system, not a claim of exact empirical bestseller counts. This patch makes the system honest: hard constraints fail closed, creative priors remain visible, and the proof artifact can show which enhancements actually rendered.
