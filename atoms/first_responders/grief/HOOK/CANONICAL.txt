

## HOOK v01
---

---
The child on the scene stays with you different. Not like a call you process and move past. This one lives in your chest and you go back to the station and nobody talks about the child and that's the grief. The grief that doesn't get a debrief. The grief that doesn't get a funeral. Just the watching. Just the carrying.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You lose a crew member and the service comes and everyone shows up and it's formal and real. But losing the person you used to work with who's still alive, who transferred out, who retired, who quit, that's a different kind of gone. You pass the station where they used to be and the grief is private and unnamed.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The call that replays. Years later and you're still running it. Still seeing the moment where things went one way and you know they could have gone another way if. That if is grief. It's not closure. It's the weight of the thing you could have changed but didn't.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Witnessing shapes you. Watching suffering without being able to fix it marks you different. That marking is grief hiding in the uniform. It's not dramatic. It's just the slow understanding that the things you see don't get unseen. They get carried.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The veteran crew member who takes early retirement and suddenly they're gone from the station and nobody's trained you how to grieve someone who's still alive. The loss is real. The absence is real. But the funeral never comes.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You get folded back into readiness before the grief finishes happening. The next call comes and you move into response before you process the last loss. That's the design. That's also the scar. The accumulation of unfinished grief.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The community you thought you were part of changes. People leave. The crew shifts. The station that felt like home starts to feel like a place you're passing through. That loss is real and you're supposed to just adjust and move on.

## HOOK v08
---

---
Losing faith that things can get better is a kind of grief. The innocence where you thought the job was rescue work and then understanding it's also witnessing irreversible things. That's a loss. That's grief.

## HOOK v09
---

---
The part of yourself that could come home and be present with the family got left somewhere on scene. You're here but you're not here. That piece is gone. That's grief wearing the shape of numbness.

## HOOK v10

---
The calls with kids. Years later you still know their names. That's grief. The world moved on. You didn't. You're still holding something that the world let go of.

## HOOK v11

---
The partner who quit because the job was breaking them and you see their point but now you're carrying the load they left behind and that's not anger exactly. It's complicated. It's loss shaped like resentment.

## HOOK v12

---
The debrief happens and then it doesn't happen. The call ends and the body's moving but the mind's still in it and there's no protocol for sitting with that. So you carry it. That carrying is grief.

## HOOK v13

---
You lose the part of yourself that believed this work would stay meaningful. That belief's gone. You're still doing the work but the meaning that made it worth the cost got away somewhere.

## HOOK v14

---
The station changes. People move on. The crew you knew isn't here anymore. New people come in. It's the same building but it's a different station and you're grieving a place while you're still standing in it.

## HOOK v15
---

---
The body you had before the job is gone. The nervous system that could rest. The sleep that was actually rest. The shoulders that didn't carry weight. You mourn those things without calling it mourning. You just notice they're not coming back.

## HOOK v16
---

---
Losing someone to the job happens slow. You watch them break. You see them trying to hold on. And then they're gone and the station moves on and the grief has nowhere to go so it lives in you.

## HOOK v17

---
The call you still think about at random moments. The outcome that haunts you. The thing you wonder if you could have changed. That wondering is grief. That's the conversation you're still having with yourself years later.

## HOOK v18
---

---
You lose the innocence where you thought sadness had a timeline. You thought if you survived something, you'd process it and move on. But some losses don't work that way. They just settle in and become part of the landscape of your chest.

## HOOK v19

---
The crew member dies and the funeral's big and everyone's there and you all mourn together. But the ones who leave quietly, who quit, who can't do it anymore, those losses are private and smaller and somehow deeper.

## HOOK v20

---
Your identity was tied to the job and now you're injured or aging out and you're losing the structure that made you know who you were. That's grief. It's identity loss. It's becoming a stranger to yourself.

## HOOK v21

---
The shift that used to feel like home now feels like a museum of all the people and versions of yourself that are no longer here. That loss is quiet. That's grief.

## HOOK v22

## HOOK v23

---
The person you were before years of calls is gone. The softness. The capacity to be untouched by suffering. That person's dead. You're different now. That's grief and you never got to say goodbye to that version.

## HOOK v24

---
The children you've seen and the outcomes you know. Years later those faces are still clear. You're carrying them and you don't know how to put them down. That's grief. That's the weight that doesn't have a name.

## HOOK v25
---

---
Losing the belief that the work matters in the way you thought it mattered. That belief was the fuel. Now you're running on routine instead of faith and that shift is a loss. That's grief.

## HOOK v26

---
The friend you had on the crew is gone and nobody talks about the friendship that ended. It just ended. Like you can compartmentalize the grief the way you compartmentalize the calls. You can't. It's still there.

## HOOK v27
---

---
The station can't hold the weight of the accumulated loss. So the loss gets pushed into individuals and you carry it silently. That silence is the deepest part of the grief. The thing that can't be spoken.

## HOOK v28
---

---
Years on the job and you realize the price was higher than you knew. More losses. More changes. More pieces of yourself that didn't make it to retirement. That realization is a kind of grief.

## HOOK v29

---
The victims you remember and the ones you forget feel like betrayal. You remember some and not others and you don't get to choose. That inequity is grief. That's the randomness of what sticks.

## HOOK v30
---

---
The future you imagined when you started doesn't exist. The one where the work stayed meaningful. Where the crew stayed the same. Where you stayed intact. That future died and you're living in the different one. That's grief.
