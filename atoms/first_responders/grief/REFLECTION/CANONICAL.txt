

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Grief in first responders doesn't follow the normal cycles. You don't get to mourn. You get the call and the next call and the call after that. The losses just stack up. Unprocessed. Unmoaned. Just absorbed into the baseline of what you carry.
I lost people on my calls. Some I never knew. Some I'd met once and then had to try to save. Some I failed. The grief doesn't get a funeral. Doesn't get acknowledged. Just becomes the weight you carry around forever.
The mechanism is called cumulative grief. It's what happens when you absorb other people's losses repeatedly without processing them. When the deaths stack up faster than you can mourn. When the loss becomes ambient. Just part of the background.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The job teaches you to not feel the loss. To not stop to mourn. To move on to the next call. The protective mechanism says: if you stop to grieve, you might not be ready for the next patient who's still alive. So you compress the grief. You swallow it. You keep moving.
The problem is that grief doesn't disappear. It just goes underground. Becomes an undercurrent. Becomes the thing you're not allowed to feel but feel anyway in the middle of the night.
