## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Press your heels down. Feel the ground push back.

Grief lives in the chest. Place one hand there now.

Notice the weight under your palm. Do not name it. Do not fix it.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six.

The weight does not have to leave. It only has to be acknowledged.

Keep your hand on your chest for thirty more seconds. Stay with what is there.

That is enough.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Find a wall. Press your back flat against it.

Let the wall hold you. Stop holding yourself upright alone.

Think of one person you have lost. One name only.

Say the name out loud. Quietly. Just once.

Notice where your body tightens when you say it. Jaw. Throat. Chest. Hands.

Breathe into that exact spot. Four counts in. Four counts out.

The name does not disappear when the shift ends. It stays. That is not weakness.

Release the wall. Carry the name forward.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Sit down. Both hands on your thighs, palms up.

Open hands mean the body is not bracing. Stay open.

Grief you have packed down does not dissolve. It waits in the body.

Scan from your throat to your gut. Where does it sit right now.

Put one finger on that spot. Press gently. Breathe in for five counts.

Do not analyze what you find. Do not rank it against worse days.

Breathe out slow. Six counts. Release the finger pressure.

You acknowledged it. That counts.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Go somewhere with no radio. No dispatch. No voices.

Sixty seconds. That is all this takes.

Close your eyes. Picture the face of someone you grieve. Hold it steady.

Notice your breathing. It has already changed. Let it.

Do not swallow the tightness in your throat. Let it exist without action.

Breathe in for four. Out for eight. Repeat three times.

Open your eyes. The face stays with you. That is not a problem to solve.

Carry it differently. Not buried. Present.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Take your gloves off. If you are not wearing gloves, unclench your hands.

Grief compresses into the fists first. Open them now.

Look at your palms. These hands have done hard things.

They have also failed to do what no hands could do. Both are true.

Breathe in through the nose. Count to four.

Let the out-breath go long. Six counts. Eight if your body wants it.

Close your hands gently. Not gripping. Just resting.

You are allowed to grieve what those hands could not change.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Sit in your vehicle. Engine off. Do not go inside yet.

Two minutes. The house can wait.

Think of the loss that sits closest to the surface today. Do not choose the safe one.

Place your right hand on your sternum. Feel your heartbeat.

Your heart has been working through this entire shift. It knows.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for one. Out for five.

Repeat four times. Stay with the heartbeat under your hand.

Go inside when the breath is steady. Not before.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Stand outside. Feel whatever the air is doing. Cold. Humid. Still. Wind.

Let it touch your face. Do not turn away from it.

Name one loss you have not spoken aloud to anyone. Keep it internal.

Notice what happens in your chest when you name it. Tightening. Hollow. Both.

Breathe in through your nose. Count four beats. Breathe out through your mouth. Count six.

The air keeps moving regardless. You move with it.

Three more breaths. Slow. Full.

You do not have to explain this grief to anyone today.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Drink a full glass of water before this starts. Grief dehydrates the body. That is physiology.

Now sit. Spine straight. Not rigid. Supported.

Think of the call that still sits wrong. The one that replays.

Do not narrate it. Just feel where it lands in the body. Gut. Shoulders. Behind the eyes.

Press both palms flat on your thighs. Push down slightly. Feel the resistance.

Breathe in for four. Out for six. Keep the pressure in your palms.

The replay is not a flaw. It is the body trying to process what the shift could not.

Stay with the breath. Two more rounds. Then release.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Loosen your jaw first. Grief locks there before anywhere else.

Let your mouth open slightly. Release the back teeth.

Now the shoulders. Drop them one inch. Then another.

Think of who you lost before you could say anything useful. A name. A face.

Do not reach for the memory. Let it surface on its own terms.

Breathe in for four counts. Feel your ribcage expand sideways.

Out for six. Slow. Controlled. Like you are managing a scene.

You are not managing this. You are feeling it.

One more breath. Then return.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Take off your boots if you can. If not, press your feet hard into the floor.

Feel the boundary between your body and the ground. That boundary is real.

Grief blurs edges. This exercise restores one.

Think of the loss that arrived without warning. The one that had no protocol.

Notice your breath. It has gone shallow. Breathe into the belly now. Four counts in.

Hold for two. Out for six. Let the belly drop fully on the exhale.

Your feet are still on the ground. That is not nothing.

Repeat the breath twice more. Stay grounded.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Find a chair. Sit all the way back in it. Let the chair take your weight.

You do not have to hold your own posture right now. Ninety seconds.

Bring to mind someone who died on your watch. Or near it. Or because of the job.

Do not justify the grief. Do not measure it against protocol or outcome.

Put one hand on your own forearm. Press lightly. Feel your own pulse if you can find it.

Breathe in for four. Out for eight. Keep the hand on your arm.

The pulse is still there. Yours. Present.

That person mattered. This breath is proof you remember.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Step away from the screen. All screens. Thirty seconds minimum.

Stand. Roll your shoulders back once. Only once.

Grief that gets no space becomes noise in the body. You know that noise.

Identify where the noise is loudest right now. Neck. Gut. Behind the sternum.

Press two fingers to that spot. Breathe in for four counts directly into the pressure.

Hold for one count. Out for six. Release the finger pressure on the exhale.

The noise does not disappear. It shifts register.

Quieter is enough. Return when ready.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Do this at end of shift. Before the drive. Before the phone.

Sit. Both feet down. Hands loose in your lap.

Run through the shift once. Not the whole thing. Only what hurt.

Do not analyze it. Do not rate your performance. Just locate it in the body.

Breathe in for four counts. Feel the ribcage lift.

Out for six. Let the ribcage fall completely.

Repeat four times. Each exhale, let one piece of the weight exist without being solved.

Grief is not a malfunction. It is the record of what mattered.

Drive home. Carry it honestly.
