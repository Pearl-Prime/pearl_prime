---
Rivera reaches the breaking point. The shame pattern at peak somatic healing intensity has stripped everything back to bone. Chest cracking. Hands shaking. The old frame is not bending. It is shattering. Rivera watches the pieces fall and does not try to reassemble them. The frame told a story about why this pain was the price of the work. The story was wrong. The pain is real. The first direction that does not lead back to the same station, the same silence, the same acceptance of the unacceptable.

Morales tells his crew he needs a minute after a bad call. In front of everyone. His face warms. The old shame says: weakness. Morales walks to the bay door. Breathes for sixty seconds. Returns. The crew does not look at him differently. One of them nods. The minute was not weakness. It was modeling. Morales took the minute he needed and the crew saw that taking a minute is allowed. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/somatic_healing/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Patel identifies the shame's enforcement mechanism in first responder culture. The culture permits physical injury but not emotional injury. A broken bone gets sympathy and sick leave. A broken spirit gets silence and suspicion. The shame is not personal. It is institutional. The mechanism: the culture rewards stoicism and punishes vulnerability. Patel has been following a script written by the institution, not by her own values. Her face warms as the mechanism becomes visible. The shame is a cultural product, not a personal failing.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/somatic_healing/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
O'Brien sees the shame's generational transmission. His captain learned it from his captain. The silence, the stoicism, the punishment of vulnerability — it passes through the ranks like an inherited disease. The mechanism: shame is not felt. It is taught. It is modeled. The new recruit learns it from the veteran's silence. The veteran learned it from the captain's disapproval. The captain learned it from the culture's mythology of the unbreakable hero. O'Brien's face burns. He is not carrying his own shame. He is carrying decades of institutional shame that was handed down like equipment.
