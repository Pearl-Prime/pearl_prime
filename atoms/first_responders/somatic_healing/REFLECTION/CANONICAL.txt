mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm learning to teach about somatic healing in this work—the recognition that you can move through calls and function cognitively while your nervous system is encoding everything in your body as threat.
The mechanism is called occupational trauma encoding—experiences from the field that get encoded not just as memories but as nervous system activation, muscle memory, and protective somatic patterns.
What I'm learning is that I can debriefs the call. I can process it cognitively. I can do the psychological work. But my nervous system is still in the state it was activated into during the call. My body still remembers the threat. My muscles still hold the readiness. My nervous system is still encoded with the need to respond.
The somatic encoding lives in a different system than the cognitive understanding. I can know intellectually that the call is over. But my nervous system doesn't know. My body still thinks the danger is present.
The mechanism is also called occupational dissociation—the separation between the mind that does the work and the body that holds the responses. I learned to function despite what my nervous system was experiencing. I learned to think through the activation. I learned to respond while my body was in protection mode.
The somatic healing is starting to require that I come back to my body. That I notice what's held there. That I learn to tolerate the sensations that the calls encoded. That I help my nervous system understand that the danger has passed, even though my body still believes it hasn't.
