## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Press both feet flat to the floor. Feel the floor push back. That push is real. Now press your palms against your thighs. Hold that pressure. Count to five. Release slowly. Your body is here. The call is over. Scan from feet to crown. Name one thing each body part touches. Floor. Chair. Air on forearms. That contact is the present moment. Nothing else is required right now.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Find something cold. A water bottle. A metal railing. A window. Press it against the back of your hand. Hold it there. Feel the temperature travel up your wrist. That sensation is your nervous system coming back online. Breathe out through your mouth. Let the jaw drop loose. Cold. Breath. Ground. Do this three times before you walk through your front door.

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Stop walking. Plant your feet. Look down at them. Name the color of your shoes. Say it out loud. Now look up and name three objects at eye level. Say each one. Your voice in your own ears matters here. It signals safety to your brainstem. One more breath. Slow exhale. You are the one doing the naming. That is enough.

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Sit on the edge of the seat. Both feet on the floor. Roll your shoulders back once. Let them drop. Feel where your back meets the chair. Now press your tongue to the roof of your mouth. Hold it. This activates the vagus nerve. Count to six. Release the tongue. Breathe normally. Do it again. Twice is enough to shift the system.

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Unclench your hands. Open them wide. Fingers spread. Hold the stretch for four counts. Close them gently. Not a fist. A soft close. Repeat this three times. Hands carry the shift from the last scene. This motion begins to discharge it. Finish with palms resting open on your legs. Open hands signal the brain that the threat has passed.

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Find the pulse in your wrist. Press two fingers there. Feel the beat. Count ten beats. Just count. Nothing else. Your heart is working. It kept working through all of it. Ten beats. That rhythm is yours alone. Now take one slow breath to match it. Exhale twice as long as the inhale. The count is over. Put your hand down.

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Splash cold water on the inside of your wrists. Both wrists. The skin there is thin. The blood vessels are close. The cold travels fast. Stand at the sink for thirty seconds. Let the water run over your hands. Watch it. Just watch it. That is all the task is. Water. Wrists. Thirty seconds. Dry your hands slowly. Walk out.

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Sit or stand. Place one hand on your sternum. Press lightly. Feel the bone beneath. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six. Keep the hand on the sternum throughout. Feel the chest rise and fall under your palm. That movement is involuntary. Your body does it without your permission. It kept breathing through everything. Let it lead now.

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Take off your gear. Set it down deliberately. Do not drop it. Place it. This distinction matters to the nervous system. Deliberate movement signals the shift from operational to off-duty. Now rotate your wrists slowly. Both directions. Three rotations each. Shake your hands loose at the side. Gear off. Wrists rotated. Hands shaken. Three steps. The body begins to register the transition.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Clench every muscle from your feet upward. Calves. Thighs. Abdomen. Chest. Fists. Hold it all. Count to five. Release everything at once. Let it fall. Feel the difference between clenched and released. That difference is what the body needs to recognize. Tension is not permanent. It ends. Do this once. Sit in the release for ten full seconds before moving.

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Look at your hands. Turn them over. Palms up. These are your hands. Not the scene's hands. Yours. Name something they did today that was not the job. Turned a key. Held a cup. Scratched your neck. One thing. Small. Yours. Now close your hands gently around that thought. These hands belong to your life. Both things are true at once.

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Stand outside before you go in. One minute. No phone. Feel the air temperature on your face. Notice if it is moving or still. Listen for the furthest sound you can hear. Then the closest. Far. Close. Far. Close. This is not mindfulness. This is range calibration. Your threat-detection system ran wide all shift. Bring the range back in before you enter your home.

---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Yawn. Force one if you have to. A real yawn, jaw wide open. The body uses yawning to regulate brain temperature and shift arousal states. Force a second one. They chain. Let the eyes water if they do. Three forced yawns resets the alert level more than most breathing exercises. It feels wrong. Do it anyway. Jaw wide. Three times. Then breathe normally.
