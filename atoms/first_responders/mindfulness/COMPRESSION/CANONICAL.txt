## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Presence isn't held, it's returned to. The mind drifts toward unresolved calls — normal, constant, not a flaw. The cost isn't error; it's half-presence: technically there, actually nowhere, faces and details lost. The fix isn't willpower, it's a cheap, repeatable cue at every threshold — a breath, a word, a hand on the rig door — that marks the last call closed and this one started. Repetition trains the return itself to get faster and cost less, the way any reflex sharpens with use. Measure shifts by returns made, not calm felt.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Multitasking on a shift isn't parallel work — it's fast switching with a hidden tax, paid in noticing. Split attention misses the subtle signal that only continuous attention catches. The reframe: stop aiming for a focused shift, aim for a fast return, treating every catch-and-come-back as a rep. Attach the return to something already done at every call — gloving up, a radio check — so it needs no decision under pressure. The goal isn't serenity; it's accuracy, attention on what's real right now, stress included.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
A looping worry or comment isn't defeated by suppression — suppression is its own hidden task, and it makes the loop louder. The fix is parking: name it as real, give it a specific later, then redirect to one concrete thing in the actual scene. Expect to repeat this many times per call; that repetition is the mechanism working, not evidence of failure. Presence, meanwhile, isn't a switch that's on or off — it's a rubber band that stretches and snaps back, and the only trainable part is how cheap the snap-back gets with reps.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Half-presence is invisible in the moment and obvious in hindsight: a shift where the work got done and nobody who did it was ever fully there. It isn't fixed by trying harder to focus, which is too vague under real load. It's fixed by closing each call before opening the next — one sentence, silently finished, half a step past the rig door — so attention stops leaking forward. The responders who seem steady aren't undistracted; they've simply returned so many times the motion costs them almost nothing anymore.
