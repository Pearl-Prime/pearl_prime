Courage is not the absence of fear. That's not how the physiology works. Courage is the activation of response despite fear. It's the nervous system capable of both terror and action simultaneously.
That's the mechanism. That's what the job trains into you. Not fearlessness. The capacity to be afraid and move anyway. To feel the thing and do the thing at the same time.
I ran into a burning building while terrified. My hands were shaking. My heart was racing. My nervous system was screaming that this was not safe. And I ran into the building anyway.
That's not fearlessness. That's courage. That's the capacity to override the protective mechanism of fear with the decision to act.
