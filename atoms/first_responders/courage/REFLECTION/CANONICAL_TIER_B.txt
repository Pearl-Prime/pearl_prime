

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Courage isn't fearlessness. That's not how the physiology works. Courage is the capacity to override the protective mechanism of fear with action. Your nervous system can be activated and terrified and still moving. That's the mechanism that gets trained into responders.
I ran into a burning building while my hands shook. My heart was racing. My body was screaming that this was dangerous. And I ran in anyway. That's not bravery in the abstract sense. That's the nervous system trained to approach instead of avoid.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The protective mechanism doesn't try to make you fearless. It reorganizes your nervous system so that fear becomes something you can move with instead of something that paralyzes you. On the job, this is essential. You feel the fear and you act anyway.
Off the job, that same capacity is still there. People ask where the courage comes from. The answer is that it's built into your nervous system now. The approach response has replaced the avoidance response.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
You don't feel yourself becoming courageous. It's invisible. You just gradually develop the capacity to move despite fear. To function despite doubt. To show up despite being terrified.
One day someone calls you brave and you're surprised. When did that happen? You weren't paying attention. The mechanism just rewired itself gradually until approach became automatic.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The automatic mechanism is called approach behavior. Most nervous systems default to avoidance when threatened. Move away. Escape. Protect.
The job retrains this. Your amygdala learns that moving toward is sometimes the safer option. That action is the only way to actually protect. Off the job, that mechanism is still locked in place.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I've been thinking about how courage gets built in the nervous system. It's not a decision. It's a trained response. The job teaches your autonomic nervous system to activate and move toward instead of away.
The protective mechanism gives you the capacity to hold both terror and action at the same time. That's what courage actually is. Not the absence of fear. The presence of directed action despite it.
