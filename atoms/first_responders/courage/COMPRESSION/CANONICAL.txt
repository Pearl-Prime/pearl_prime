## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Feel the weight of your boots. Press down harder. That contact is real.

Name one thing your hands are touching right now. Say it out loud.

You showed up today. That took something. Notice where in your chest that lands.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four. Out for four.

Courage does not feel heroic from the inside. It feels like this. Ordinary. Tired. Present.

You are still here. That counts.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Your jaw is tight. Unclench it now.

Roll your shoulders back once. Let them drop.

Think of one call this week that cost you something. Do not analyze it. Just let the weight of it sit in your chest for ten seconds.

Ten. Nine. Eight. Count down silently.

Now exhale. Long and slow. Longer than feels necessary.

Courage is not the absence of that weight. It is carrying it and showing up anyway. You already did that. Name the shift in your body when you hear that.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Sit back in your seat. All the way back.

Feel the chair hold you. You do not have to hold yourself right now.

Think of one moment this month where you chose to act when everything in you said stop. Do not explain it. Just locate it.

Put one hand on your sternum. Press lightly.

That moment lives here. In the body. Not in the report.

Breathe into your hand. Four counts in. Six counts out.

Courage already happened. This breath is the proof.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Stand up if you can. If not, press your spine straight.

Feel your own height. Take up the space you actually occupy.

Name the last time you walked into something dangerous on purpose. Say the word out loud: dangerous.

Notice what your throat does with that word.

Breathe in through the nose. Count to five. Exhale through the mouth.

Courage lives in the throat and the chest and the legs that moved forward anyway. You know what that feels like. Your body remembers.

Let it remember. Right now.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Close your eyes if the space is safe.

Put both hands palm-down on your thighs. Feel the warmth there.

Think of one person you protected. Not a statistic. One face, one name, one moment.

Hold that image for eight seconds. Do not rush past it.

Now breathe. In for four. Out for four.

Most days nobody names what you did as courage. That does not change what it was.

Open your eyes. Stay here another ten seconds before you move.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Notice your breathing right now. Do not change it yet.

Is it shallow. Is it held. Is it fast.

Now slow it down deliberately. In for three counts. Out for six.

Think of the last shift you finished when you had nothing left. You finished it anyway. That is the fact.

Put one hand on the back of your neck. Feel the tension there.

That tension is stored effort. It is stored courage. It is not weakness.

Exhale again. Longer this time. Let the neck soften one degree.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Look at your hands.

These hands have done things most people will never do. Do not look away from that.

Breathe in slowly. Count four beats. Hold two beats. Release six beats.

Now ask your hands to relax. Uncurl the fingers. Open the palms.

Courage is not only the moment of action. It is also this. Staying soft when hardness wants to win.

Let your hands rest open on your lap for thirty seconds. Do not close them.

Thirty seconds. Start now.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Feel your feet. Both of them. Socks, boots, floor beneath.

Tap your right heel twice. Then your left. Alternate three times.

This grounds the nervous system. It is not a trick. It is biology.

Now bring one specific moment to mind. A moment you chose courage over retreat. One moment. Specific.

Let it land in your chest without commentary.

Breathe in for four. Out for four. Repeat twice.

You do not need to perform bravery. You have already practiced it. Your body knows the difference.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Drop your chin to your chest for five seconds. Feel the stretch in the back of your neck.

Lift your head back to neutral.

Think of one thing you did this week that nobody saw. No camera. No commendation. Just you and the moment.

That happened. It was real.

Breathe in through the nose. Let the belly expand first, then the chest. Out slowly.

Courage without an audience is still courage. Especially then. The body that carried it through deserves a breath.

Give it one more. Slow. Full. Yours.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Press your back molars together gently. Then release completely.

Do that twice more. Clench. Release. Clench. Release.

Notice where else in your body you are holding tension right now. Name the location out loud.

Breathe into that location. Direct the breath there. Three slow cycles.

Now bring to mind one shift you almost did not make it through. You made it through.

That is not luck. That is built capacity. Courage is built, not born.

Your body built it. Feel that for ten seconds before you move on.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Sit with your weight even across both hips.

Adjust until you feel balanced. Take a moment to find it.

Think of the dispatcher who stayed calm on a call that was anything but. Think of the firefighter who went back in. Think of the medic who kept working.

Now recognize: you are in that category. You belong in that list.

Breathe in for four counts. Out for four.

Courage does not announce itself. It shows up in the body as steadiness under pressure.

Find that steadiness now. It is already in you. Locate it.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Put one hand over your heart. Press until you feel your own pulse.

Wait for three beats. Count them.

Your heart kept beating through every hard call. Every bad night. Every moment you held it together for someone else.

It is still beating now.

Breathe in slowly. Let the exhale be twice as long as the inhale.

Courage is not the absence of fear. It is the heart that keeps going anyway. You felt that firsthand.

Keep your hand there for twenty more seconds. Let that be enough.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Unclench your fists. If they were not clenched, notice that they almost were.

Take one slow breath before you do anything else.

Think of one person on your crew or your team who showed up when it mattered. Picture their face.

Now recognize that they saw the same thing in you.

Breathe in for four. Hold for two. Out for six.

Courage in this work is rarely singular. It moves between people. It holds the line together.

You are part of that line. Feel the weight of that in your chest. Let it be a weight worth carrying.
