## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Notice the weight in your heels. That weight is real. The debt number in your head is a projection.

Name one bill. Just one. Write it on paper or say it aloud. Give it a number and a due date.

Now breathe out slowly. Count to five on the exhale.

The bill exists. The spiral does not have to. You handle one call at a time on shift. Handle one number right now.

Put the paper down. Return to your body. You are not behind. You are locating the problem.

That is the job.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Your jaw is clenched. Unclench it now.

Financial anxiety in first responders often runs on the same loop as threat scanning. Your nervous system treats an overdraft notice like an incoming call. It is not.

Press your back into your chair. Feel the support. You are not in the field right now.

Pick one financial fact. Not a fear. A fact. What is your account balance at this exact moment. Write that number down.

Facts are dispatchable. Fears are not.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six.

You have handled worse with less information. Start with what is true.

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Sit down. Both hands on your thighs.

Feel the pressure of your palms against your legs. Stay there for three full seconds.

Now name the financial fear loudest in your head right now. Not all of them. The loudest one.

Say it plainly: "I am afraid that blank." Fill the blank. Speak it or write it.

Naming it does not make it real. It already felt real. Naming it makes it specific.

Specific problems have edges. Spirals do not.

Breathe in slowly. Breathe out slower.

You locate threats for a living. Locate this one. Give it a shape, a number, a timeline.

Then set it down.

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Feel your shoulders. They are probably high.

Drop them. Deliberately. Let them fall.

You have been carrying financial dread the same way you carry gear — constantly, without noticing the weight.

Put one hand on your sternum. Feel your chest rise and fall. Stay there.

Now ask yourself one operational question: what is the single next financial action available to me today. Not this month. Today.

It does not have to be large. It has to be real.

Write it down. One sentence. One action.

Breathe out fully before you move.

The dread does not disappear. But it loses authority when you have a next step.

Move toward the step.

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Unclench your hands. Open them flat.

Look at them for three seconds. These hands have managed actual emergencies. They know how to work under pressure.

Financial chaos feels like an emergency because your system is trained to treat uncertainty as threat. It is not the same threat.

Ground yourself first. Press your feet into the floor. Notice the surface.

Now write down the number that scares you most. The debt total. The shortfall. Whatever it is.

Look at it. Do not look away.

Numbers do not attack. They inform.

Breathe in for four. Out for four.

You do not have to solve it today. You have to stop running from it today.

That is enough.

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Stop the scroll. Put the phone face-down.

Financial anxiety feeds on more information. You do not need another article about inflation right now.

Place both feet on the floor. Notice the temperature of the room. Breathe once, fully.

Write one true sentence about your finances. Not a fear. A fact. "I owe blank dollars on blank." Or "I have blank in savings."

One sentence. One fact.

Your training runs on accurate information. Give your nervous system something accurate to work with.

Breathe out slowly. Four counts.

The spiral is not data. The sentence is.

Return to the sentence whenever the spiral starts. It is your anchor point.

Hold it.

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Notice your breathing. It is shallow.

Slow it down. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for six.

Do that twice before reading further.

Financial anxiety in high-stress careers compounds because there is never a clean handoff. The shift ends. The worry does not.

So create a handoff now.

On a piece of paper, write: "Financial concerns I am holding." List them. Two minutes. Do not edit.

Then fold the paper. Set it somewhere physical and visible — not your phone.

You have logged the call. It is on record. You do not need to hold it in your body anymore.

Breathe out.

The list holds it now.

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Both hands on the table. Feel the surface.

Cold or warm. Smooth or rough. Notice it.

Now: the financial noise in your head has been running on loop. Loops are exhausting. You know this.

Interrupt the loop with one concrete action. Not a plan. An action you can finish in the next ten minutes.

Transfer five dollars to savings. Log one expense. Open the bill you have been avoiding and read the total.

Small. Concrete. Completable.

Breathe in for four. Hold for two. Out for four.

Completion signals safety to your nervous system. Your system needs that signal right now.

Do the small thing. Finish it fully.

Then breathe again.

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Sit somewhere quiet. Close the door if you can.

Place one hand on your chest and one on your stomach. Notice which one rises first when you inhale.

Breathe into your stomach. Three times. Slow.

You have been managing financial fear the same way you manage everything on shift — push it down, keep moving, deal with it later. Later keeps arriving.

Name one financial decision you have been avoiding. Say it aloud.

Now ask: what is the cost of continuing to avoid it. Not money. Energy. Sleep. The tension in your chest right now.

Breathe out fully.

Avoidance has a price. Facing it has a price too. Choose the one that stops compounding.

You already know which one that is.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Feel the chair beneath you. Real. Solid.

You are not in danger right now. Your body does not know that yet. Tell it.

Say aloud: "I am safe right now. This is a money problem. Money problems have solutions."

Repeat it once more. Flat. Steady.

Now write the three financial numbers that matter most to you right now. Income. Debt. What you need this month.

Three numbers. No commentary.

Look at them. Breathe in for four. Out for four.

Numbers on paper are not the same as numbers in your chest. Moving them outside your body changes the pressure.

You have assessed worse scenes with less clarity.

Start here. Start now.

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Put your back against something solid. Wall. Chair. Whatever is behind you.

Feel it.

Financial dread is a full-body experience for people in high-stress work. The cortisol from the shift does not drain cleanly. It attaches to the next available threat. Tonight that threat is your bank account.

Breathe out first. Long exhale. Four counts minimum.

Now: what is one financial truth you know for certain today. Not a fear. Certainty only.

"My rent is paid through blank." "I have blank in checking." One true thing.

Hold that truth in your chest where the dread was sitting.

Breathe in slowly.

Certainty is a tool. Use it.

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Loosen your grip. On whatever you are holding.

Now loosen the grip inside your chest. Deliberately.

You carry financial anxiety the same way you carry hypervigilance — as protection. But it is not protecting you. It is exhausting you.

Pick one financial task you have been postponing for more than two weeks. Name it exactly.

Set a timer for ten minutes. Open the thing. Do not finish it. Just open it.

Starting is the hardest part for people who are already running on empty.

Breathe in for four. Out for six.

Ten minutes. One task. Just open it.

You do not have to solve everything tonight. You have to move the needle one mark.

That is all.
