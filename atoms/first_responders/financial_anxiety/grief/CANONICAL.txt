---
Nowak remembers when overtime felt like a bonus, not a necessity. When the extra shifts were for vacations, not mortgages. His chest aches. He grieves the version of his career where the pay was enough. The frame shifts: the pay did not change. The costs did. The grief is not about his earning. It is about the economy that made his earning insufficient. His jaw unclenches slightly. The financial anxiety is real. But the target of his grief needs to shift from himself to the structural reality. He is not failing. The math changed.

---
Rivera reaches the breaking point. The grief pattern at peak financial anxiety intensity has stripped everything back to bone. Chest cracking. Hands shaking. The old frame is not bending. It is shattering. Rivera watches the pieces fall and does not try to reassemble them. The frame told a story about why this pain was the price of the work. The story was wrong. The pain is real. The price was manufactured by a system that confuses endurance with character. Rivera stands in the wreckage of the old explanation. Something new is visible in the gap. Not a solution. A direction. The first direction that does not lead back to the same station, the same silence, the same acceptance of the unacceptable.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/financial_anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Patel sees how grief operates in a culture that does not permit it. The department has no ritual for ongoing grief. There is the funeral. There is the memorial. Then there is silence. The grief continues but the container ended. Patel's chest is heavy. The mechanism: the institution processes grief as an event, not a condition. The grief becomes unauthorized after the official period. So it goes underground — into insomnia, irritability, isolation. The mechanism is not the grief itself. It is the absence of a container that allows the grief to exist in the open.

O'Brien sees the grief's institutional suppression. The department has no language for ongoing grief. The words available are: move on, stay strong, honor the memory. Each one closes the conversation. The grief has no sanctioned expression after the funeral. The mechanism: the institution's vocabulary creates the suppression. Without words, the grief goes into the body — chronic pain, sleep disruption, emotional flatness. O'Brien's chest is heavy. The mechanism is linguistic. The body holds what the language cannot express.
