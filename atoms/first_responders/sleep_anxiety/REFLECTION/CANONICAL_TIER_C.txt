

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
My sleep is interrupted constantly. On-call schedules. Emergencies that happen in the middle of the night. Your body learns that sleep isn't reliable. That it can be interrupted. The mechanism is called sleep-interruption-conditioning.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that even when I'm supposed to be off-duty, I sleep lightly. Like I'm still ready. Still alert. The mechanism is called perpetual-readiness-sleep.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of sleep anxiety that comes from knowing you might be called out. Even when you're off. So there's no true rest. Your nervous system stays activated. The mechanism is called on-call-sleep-dysregulation.

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---

## REFLECTION v05
---

voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my sleep anxiety that's about the job itself. I know that sleep deprivation affects performance. So I'm anxious about not sleeping enough before a shift. The mechanism is called performance-anxiety-around-sleep.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---

---

mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
