## HOOK v01
---

---
You got home from the shift two hours ago. You should be tired. Instead you're wired from the call at 9:15 PM—the one where you weren't sure if you did everything right. Your hands won't stop moving. Your mind won't stop reviewing.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
It's 2 AM and you're still at the kitchen table in your uniform. The incident report is done but the scenario isn't. You keep running it differently in your head, faster responses, different angles. Somewhere in one of those versions, you don't have to wonder if you missed something.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The adrenaline from a call can last four hours after the shift ends. You came home two hours ago. Your body is still in the dispatcher's office. Your nervous system hasn't caught up to the fact that the crisis is over.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You close your eyes and see the living room of the last address you responded to. Not the clinical details—the picture on the mantle you weren't supposed to notice. Your brain keeps cataloging what you weren't trained to process.
---
