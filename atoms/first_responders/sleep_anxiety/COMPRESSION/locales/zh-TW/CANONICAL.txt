## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
Your shift ended hours ago. Your nervous system did not get the memo.

Lie flat. Put both hands on your chest. Feel the weight of them.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four. Out for six. The longer exhale signals safety to your brainstem.

Do this three times. Count out loud if the silence feels wrong.

Now name five things touching your body right now. Mattress. Sheet. Pillow. Your own hands. The air on your face.

You are not on scene. You are here.

Stay with that for thirty seconds. Let the body catch up to what you already know.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
The radio is off. The call is closed. Your jaw is still clenched.

Unclench it now. Let your teeth part slightly.

Drop your shoulders away from your ears. Notice how far they traveled without you knowing.

Press both feet flat to the floor. Feel the floor push back. It holds you.

Breathe out first. Empty the tank completely. Then let the inhale come on its own.

Repeat that four times. Out first. Then in.

Your body ran hot for twelve hours. It needs a manual downshift, not willpower.

This is the downshift. Stay here two more minutes.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
You keep replaying the last call. The brain does this. It is not weakness.

Sit up straight. Both feet on the floor.

Make fists with both hands. Squeeze hard for five seconds.

Release. Feel the blood return to your fingers.

That release is the signal. Do it twice more.

Now place one hand on your sternum. Feel your heartbeat directly under your palm.

Count ten beats. Just count. Nothing else.

The counting interrupts the loop. Your prefrontal cortex re-engages when you give it a simple task.

Ten beats. Then let your eyes close.

You did what you could. The night is done.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Three AM. Ceiling staring back at you. Thoughts running hot.

Roll to your side. Curl your knees toward your chest slightly.

This position lowers cortisol faster than lying flat. Use it.

Breathe into your belly, not your chest. Put a hand on your stomach to check.

Feel it rise. Feel it fall. Four counts in. Six counts out.

Your chest breathing kept you sharp on shift. Belly breathing tells your body the threat is gone.

Say it once, out loud or in your head: the shift is over.

Repeat the breath. In for four. Out for six.

Do this five times. Let the ceiling blur.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
You are tired but wired. Both at once. That is the hypervigilance tax.

Stand up. Go to the sink.

Run cold water over both wrists for thirty seconds. Count the seconds.

The cold activates the vagus nerve. Your heart rate drops. This is physiology, not magic.

Dry your hands. Walk back to bed slowly.

Sit on the edge. Look at one fixed point across the room.

Breathe out through your mouth. Long and slow. Like fogging a mirror.

Do that four times. Then lie down.

The wired feeling will not vanish instantly. It will soften. That is enough to start.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Your eyes are closed but your ears are still scanning. Every sound registers as a potential call.

Name the sounds in the room right now. Clock. Furnace. Your own breath.

Label each one: not a threat.

Say it plainly. Clock. Not a threat. Furnace. Not a threat.

This is not a ritual. It is a neurological interrupt. The threat-detection system needs explicit data.

Now breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven.

Feel your ribcage expand sideways. Not just front to back. Sideways.

Do this four times. Let the scanning slow.

You are off the grid. Nothing needs you right now.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---
Shoulders tight. Stomach coiled. Mind three calls ahead of your body.

Sit on the edge of the bed. Spine straight.

Press your palms together in front of your chest. Push hard. Hold for eight seconds.

Release. Shake your hands out loosely at your sides.

That muscle tension needed somewhere to go. Now it has gone.

Close your eyes. Picture the room you are in. Not the scene. This room.

Name three objects in it. Lamp. Dresser. Door.

Breathe normally for one full minute. Just breathe. No counting.

Let your hands rest open on your thighs, palms up.

Open hands signal the brain: nothing to hold. Nothing to fight.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---
The adrenaline has nowhere to go. It sits in your chest like static.

Get on the floor. Lie face down.

Feel the full weight of your body pressing into the ground. Let it press.

This is called prone grounding. The full-body contact activates the parasympathetic response faster than any breath technique.

Stay there for sixty seconds. Count them.

Breathe slowly into the floor. Feel your stomach compress against it with each inhale.

After sixty seconds, roll to your back.

Stare at the ceiling. Notice your heartbeat is slower.

That is your body responding. It always responds. Give it time and contact.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---
You cannot turn the dispatcher brain off. It keeps triaging. Even now.

Give it one final task. Then release it.

The task: scan your body from feet to crown. One pass. Slow.

Feet. Calves. Knees. Thighs. Hips. Stomach. Chest. Shoulders. Neck. Face.

At each point, ask: tight or loose. That is all. No fixing.

Just notice. Tight or loose.

When you reach the top of your head, the scan is complete. The task is done.

Breathe out fully. Let the dispatcher clock out.

Nothing else needs sorting tonight. The scan found you. You are here. That is the whole report.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---
The bedroom feels wrong. Too quiet or too loud. Neither feels safe.

Pick one wall. Look at it.

Find the corners where it meets the ceiling and the floor. Trace the full edge with your eyes. Slow.

Top left corner. Across. Top right corner. Down. Bottom right corner. Across. Bottom left corner. Up.

One full rectangle. Breathe once between each corner.

Your visual cortex just mapped a contained space. Your nervous system registers: edges exist, space is bounded, you are inside it.

Do the rectangle twice more.

Now close your eyes and picture it. The four corners still there.

You are inside something solid. Rest in that.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---
Two hours of broken sleep. You know the shift starts again in five.

Stop calculating. The math makes it worse.

Lie down. Put one hand on your forehead. Feel the warmth of your palm against your skin.

That warmth is yours. You made it.

Breathe in for four counts. Let your hand rise slightly as your chest fills.

Out for six. Feel your hand settle.

Do not think about the next shift. The next shift does not exist yet.

This breath exists. This hand exists. This warmth exists.

Four in. Six out. Three more times.

Let the calculations go dark. Just this. Just now.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---
Your body saved lives today. Now it does not know how to stop saving.

Sit in a chair. Both feet flat. Back supported.

Grip the armrests or your own thighs. Hold firm for five seconds.

Release completely. Let your arms go heavy.

That contrast between grip and release is the message. You held. Now you can let go.

Breathe in through your nose. Slow. Feel your nostrils flare slightly.

Out through your mouth. Long. Audible if you need it to be.

Repeat four times. Each exhale, let your grip on the day loosen one notch.

You are not abandoning the job. You are maintaining the equipment. The equipment is you.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---
The call you keep seeing behind your eyelids. It happened. It is not happening now.

Open your eyes. Look at your hands.

These are your hands in this room at this hour. Not on scene.

Turn them over. Look at the backs. Then the palms.

Breathe in for four counts while you look.

Out for six. Keep looking at your hands.

Do this three times. Let the image behind your eyes compete with what is actually in front of you.

Now press your palms flat to your thighs. Feel the pressure. Feel the warmth.

This is the ground. You are on it.

Here. Solid. Done.
