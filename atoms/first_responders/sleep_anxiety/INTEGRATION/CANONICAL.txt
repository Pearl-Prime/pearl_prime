## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body is still in crisis mode."
---
You're home. The shift is over. The incident is done. But your nervous system doesn't know the difference between "shift over" and "threat over." You're lying down and your heart is still running at call speed. Your hands are still alert. Your breathing is sharp, ready, waiting.

Four hours since the call ended. You're in your own bed. Safe. But your body is still at the scene, still in that adrenaline state, still believing something is wrong right now.

Notice what's actually happening: you're lying down. The room is quiet. The door is locked. Nobody needs you right now. Your body will downregulate when it's ready. You can't force it. You can't convince it. It's still doing its job — just for a threat that ended hours ago.

The shift is finished. Your alarm doesn't know.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Sleep is stolen from the hardest shift."
---
That call cost everything — your attention, your training, your presence. You gave it all. The patient needed it. You delivered.

But the cost doesn't stop when the call ends. Your nervous system is still paying. The adrenaline doesn't have an off switch. You lie down to recover and instead you're lying awake, still in crisis mode, still vigilant, still unable to downregulate.

So sleep doesn't happen. Recovery doesn't happen. Your body was supposed to restore itself after that intensity and instead it's still running the protocol. The shift took eight hours. The aftermath is taking the night.

You don't get to turn off. Not yet. Maybe not for hours. The work extracts more than just the time you're paid for.

That's what you're losing tonight.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if time is what it is?"
---
You're fighting the adrenaline. Trying to force sleep. Trying to convince your body it's safe. Trying to downregulate through sheer will. And the more you try, the more your body knows something's wrong.

But what if you stopped fighting it? What if you just lay there, awake, and let your nervous system process the call at its own pace? What if sleep comes when it comes, and until then you're just awake, and that's okay?

What if the trying is what's keeping you stuck?

What if you gave your body permission to be in crisis mode until it decides it's not?
