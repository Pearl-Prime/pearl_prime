You've made entry on scenes that would freeze most people. You've performed under conditions that don't exist in any training manual. And somewhere in the back of your head, a voice says you got lucky. That voice isn't humble. It's wrong. Luck doesn't sustain a career. Skill does.

--- variant: v02
Old frame: imposter syndrome means you're not qualified. Flip it. In this work, imposter syndrome often means you're competent enough to see how much can go wrong and honest enough to be unsettled by it. The real imposters never question themselves.

--- variant: v03
You compare your inside to everyone else's outside at the station. Your doubt versus their confidence. But you don't see their doubt. You only see the performance. Theirs is as practiced as yours.
