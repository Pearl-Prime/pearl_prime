## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Plant both feet flat on the floor. Press down hard. Feel the floor push back. That pressure is real. Now name three things your hands touched in the last hour. Say them out loud. You did that. You were present for that. The doubt in your head is not evidence. Your actions today were real. You showed up. That counts.

---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Put one hand on your sternum. Feel it rise. Hold that breath for four counts. Release. The person who doubts their competence is the same person who just did the job. Incompetent people do not worry about being incompetent. Notice that. Say your rank or role out loud. Own it for ten seconds. Then get back to work.

---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Stop moving. Stand still for thirty seconds. Let the noise settle. Now think of one call you handled this year that required real skill. One. Hold it. Your nervous system does not fake competence under pressure. It either performs or it does not. Yours performed. That performance was yours. Nobody else ran that call. You did.

---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Sit down. Roll your shoulders back once. Feel your spine straighten. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for six. Do that twice. Now say this aloud: I trained for this. I showed up. I did not quit. Those three facts are not opinions. They are your record. Your record does not lie.

---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Press your thumbnail into your index finger. Hold that small pressure. Stay there. The feeling that you do not belong is not a report from the outside world. It is noise from inside a tired brain. Name one person who trusted your judgment this week. Just one. That trust was not an accident. It was earned. Release your thumb.

---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Look at your hands. Turn them over. These hands have done the work. Count five things they have done on the job. Say each one. Do not rush. When you finish, notice what is in your chest. That tightness is not proof of fraud. It is proof you care about doing it right. Caring is a competency.

---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Take one slow breath. Let it go. Now locate where the doubt lives in your body. Chest. Throat. Gut. Put a finger there. Press lightly. That sensation is stress. It is not truth. Stress and truth feel identical inside a hypervigilant system. They are not the same thing. Breathe out. Drop your shoulders. Move on.

---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Stand up if you are sitting. Take three steps. Stop. You just made a decision and executed it. That is what you do all shift. Small decisions. Large ones. You execute. The voice that says you are faking it has never run a scene. You have. Trust the record over the voice. Always the record.

---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Close your eyes for ten seconds. No longer. Behind those eyes, picture your last successful shift. Not perfect. Successful. See one moment where you held it together when it mattered. Open your eyes. That moment happened. It is documented somewhere, in a report, in a radio log, in someone's memory. It is real. You made it real.

---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Press your back against a wall or chair. Feel the solid surface behind you. That contact is support. Now say this once: I have survived every hard day so far. That is a one hundred percent success rate. The doubt that follows competence is called the impostor cycle. Knowing its name weakens its grip. Breathe. Step forward.

---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Clench both fists tight. Hold for five counts. Release. Feel the blood return. Your body just demonstrated control. You regulated that. Now think of the newest person on your crew. What do they watch you do. They watch because you know things they do not yet know. That observation is not random. It is calibrated. They see what you dismiss.

---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Find something cold. A water bottle. A metal surface. A window. Press your palm against it. Stay there for five seconds. Cold is real. Present. Grounding. Now say the year you completed your training. Say the number of years since. That is not luck. That is sustained performance over time. Luck does not sustain. Skill does.

---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Take a breath. Let it out slow. Longer out than in. Do that once more. The shift you just worked required knowledge you built over years. Nobody handed it to you. You built it call by call, drill by drill, mistake by mistake. That is not the biography of a fraud. That is the biography of a professional. You are that professional.
