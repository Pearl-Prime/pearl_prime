

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I made it through the academy. I passed all the tests. I'm doing the job. And yet there's this part of me that doesn't believe I've really earned this. That feels like I got lucky. That other people are more natural at this than I am. The mechanism is called earned-achievement-not-trusted.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is that I can see other people's competence clearly. I can trust their judgment. I can count on their skills. But when I look at myself, I doubt. The mechanism is called asymmetric competence perception.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of imposter feeling that comes from moments of uncertainty on the job. There are always situations where I'm not sure what the right call is. And in those moments, I wonder if I should be making the call at all. The mechanism is called uncertainty-as-illegitimacy.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that my imposter syndrome is worse when I'm still relatively new. But it doesn't fully go away as I get more experienced. The mechanism is called persistent-imposter-feeling.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that when something goes wrong, I immediately assume it's my fault. My error. My judgment call. My responsibility. Even when it's part of a larger system. The mechanism is called catastrophic-self-blame.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my imposter syndrome that comes from knowing this job is dangerous. That people's lives literally depend on what I do. So if I'm still learning, if I'm still not completely certain, maybe I shouldn't be here. The mechanism is called high-stakes-imposter-syndrome.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've learned that talking about these doubts is difficult. There's a culture of confidence. Of trust in your own decisions. Admitting doubt feels like admitting weakness. The mechanism is called culturally-enforced-confidence.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is how my imposter syndrome sometimes manifests as over-preparation. I have to be absolutely certain. Have to know everything. Because the alternative is admitting that I might not be ready. The mechanism is called perfectionism-as-imposter-compensation.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about the training in this job that can feed imposter syndrome. You're taught what could go wrong. You're trained for worst-case scenarios. And then you're supposed to feel confident that you can handle them. But the training itself can create doubt. The mechanism is called training-induced-anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that imposter syndrome is more active after difficult calls. Calls where things didn't go the way I wanted. Where I wonder if I did everything right. The mechanism is called outcome-dependent-self-doubt.
---
