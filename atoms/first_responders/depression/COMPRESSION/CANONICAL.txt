## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Press down. Feel the floor push back.

Name three things your hands touched in the last hour. Say them aloud, one at a time.

Now locate the weight in your chest. Don't fix it. Just find it.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six.

The heaviness doesn't need solving right now. It needs locating.

Do this once more. Feet down. Breath out. Weight found.

That's enough for now.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Sit down. Not in your vehicle. Inside, in a chair.

Let your back touch the backrest fully.

Notice where your body braces. Jaw. Shoulders. Gut. Pick one spot.

Soften it by ten percent. Not all the way. Ten percent.

Say this once, out loud: "I'm off shift."

Your nervous system won't believe it immediately. Say it anyway.

Breathe out slowly. Let the exhale go longer than feels natural.

Repeat the phrase. Repeat the breath.

Two rounds. That's the whole exercise.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Find a wall. Stand with your back flat against it.

Feel the solid surface behind your shoulder blades.

Close your eyes for ten seconds. Count them.

When the gray feeling sits in your chest, don't name it as weakness. Name it as load.

You carry load. Load is weight. Weight can be set down temporarily.

Breathe in for four. Out for eight.

Open your eyes. Look at one fixed point across the room.

Hold that point for five seconds.

The load is still there. So are you.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Put one hand on your sternum. Press lightly.

Feel your heartbeat or your breath. Either one.

If the numbness has moved in today, acknowledge it directly. Say: "Numb is here."

Don't push it out. Don't explain it.

Breathe in slowly. Let your chest rise against your hand.

Hold for three counts.

Breathe out. Let your hand fall with your chest.

Do this four times. Count each one.

Numbness doesn't leave on command. Contact with your own body interrupts the drift.

Four breaths. Hand on chest. That's it.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Sit somewhere you won't be watched.

Put both palms face-down on your thighs.

Feel the fabric or skin under your hands.

If the darkness came back today, don't argue with it. Just notice it arrived.

Take one slow breath in. No count. Just slow.

Let it out through your mouth.

Press your palms down harder for three seconds. Release.

Again. Press. Release.

The pressure gives your body something real to register. That registration matters.

Three more rounds. Press. Release. Breathe.

Then stand up. Move to the next thing.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Go to a room alone. Thirty seconds is enough.

Stand still. Let the silence land.

If the flat feeling has been with you all day, don't perform energy you don't have.

Put your hand on the back of your neck. Hold it there.

Breathe in through your nose. Four counts.

Feel your hand. Feel the warmth.

Breathe out. Six counts.

Your body ran hard today. The flatness is a response, not a flaw.

One more breath. Hand still on neck.

Then go back. You don't need to be fixed first.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Sit on the edge of your bed. Before you lie down.

Both feet on the floor.

Take stock of your body from feet to crown. Ten seconds. Just scan.

Where is the tension stored right now. Find one location.

Don't release it yet. Just know where it lives.

Breathe in for four. Direct the breath toward that location.

Breathe out for six. Imagine the tension has somewhere to go.

Repeat twice more.

Depression lives partly in a body that never gets to discharge. This is discharge.

Three rounds. Scan. Breathe. Locate. Out.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Drink something warm. Hold the cup in both hands.

Feel the heat transfer through your palms.

If today felt like moving through concrete, that's a real physical state. Name it: "Heavy day."

Two words. That's the whole naming.

Now breathe in slowly while holding the cup.

Let the warmth be the only thing you track for ten seconds.

Breathe out.

You don't need to process the shift right now. You need to land.

Finish the drink. Both hands. Slow breath.

Landing counts as progress.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Step outside. Thirty seconds. Bare minimum.

Feel the air temperature on your forearms.

Cold or warm doesn't matter. Temperature is information. Your skin is receiving it.

If the emptiness has been loud today, let it be loud outside instead of inside.

Breathe in. Let your lungs fill completely.

Hold for two counts.

Breathe out through your mouth. Slow.

Look at the horizon or the farthest point visible.

Hold that view for five seconds.

Come back inside.

You moved your body through space. That matters more than it sounds.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Clench both fists. Hard. Hold for five seconds. Count them.

Release completely.

Notice the difference between clenched and released. That gap is real.

Your body knows the difference between held and let go. It just rarely gets the chance to demonstrate it.

Breathe in for four counts.

Clench again. Hold. Release on the exhale.

If the low feeling has been constant this week, this isn't a cure. It's a signal to your nervous system that release is possible.

Three rounds total. Clench. Hold. Release. Breathe.

Then unclench your jaw. You forgot about that one.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Find a chair with armrests. Sit fully back.

Grip the armrests. Not tight. Just contact.

Feel the solidity under your hands.

If you've been running on hollow for days, your body needs something fixed to hold.

Breathe in. Four counts.

While you breathe, press your feet into the floor simultaneously.

Armrests. Floor. Both at once.

Breathe out. Eight counts. Slow.

Repeat three times.

You are in contact with solid surfaces. That is not nothing.

Solid. Grounded. Still here.

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Before you open any screen, do this.

Stand up. Roll your shoulders back once. Just once.

Feel where your chest opens when you do.

Take one breath into that open space.

If the gray has been sitting on you since you woke up, it doesn't need your attention right now. It needs your body to move first.

Exhale fully. Let the last bit of air out.

Roll your shoulders back one more time.

Breathe in. Stand taller by one centimeter.

That shift is real. Small and real.

Now open whatever you need to open.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Sit somewhere quiet. Elbows on knees. Head down.

Let yourself be small for sixty seconds. No performance.

If the weight has been accumulating shift by shift, that accumulation is real. It has mass.

Breathe in. Don't straighten up yet.

Let the exhale go long.

After four breaths, raise your head slowly.

Look straight ahead.

Say one true thing about today. Not a report. One true thing. Out loud.

Then sit with what you said for ten seconds.

You named something real. That breaks the silence depression uses to grow.

Four breaths. One true thing. Ten seconds.

Done.
