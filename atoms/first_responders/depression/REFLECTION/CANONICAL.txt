

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Depression in first responders is not sadness. It's flatness. It's the absence of feeling rather than the presence of bad feeling. It's the slow draining of color from the world until everything looks the same shade of gray.
I didn't recognize it as depression because I thought depression was crying. Thought it was pain. But what I experienced was numbness. The job got less interesting. People got less interesting. My own life got less interesting.
The mechanism is called anhedonia. It's the loss of capacity to experience pleasure. Not because you're sad about losing pleasure. Because the capacity itself has been dialed down so far that nothing activates it anymore.
Years on the job dampens the dopamine system. Dampens the reward pathways. Teaches your nervous system that pleasure is not reliably available, so why invest in seeking it.
