--- variant: v01
You used to feel something after every call. Now you feel nothing, and you've been telling yourself that's experience. Rename it. That's not experience — it's your system shutting down non-essential functions to keep running. Burnout in a first responder isn't about being tired of the job. It's about the job consuming more than it returns.

--- variant: v02
The old playbook says push through. You've been pushing through so long you forgot there was a pace that didn't require pushing. Burnout isn't a motivation problem. It's a structural failure — and the failing structure isn't you. It's the system that runs on your willingness to keep going.

--- variant: v03
Everyone sees the operator who shows up every shift, gear ready, game face on. Nobody sees the person underneath who stopped caring about the outcome three months ago and hasn't told anyone. That flatness isn't apathy. It's your body pulling the emergency brake because nobody else was going to.
