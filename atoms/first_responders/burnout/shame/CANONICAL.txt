## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/burnout/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kim identifies the shame's enforcement mechanism in first responder culture. The culture permits physical injury but not emotional injury. A broken bone gets sympathy and sick leave. A broken spirit gets silence and suspicion. The shame is not personal. It is institutional. The mechanism: the culture rewards stoicism and punishes vulnerability. Kim has been following a script written by the institution, not by her own values. Her face warms as the mechanism becomes visible. The shame is a cultural product, not a personal failing.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Jackson sees the shame's generational transmission. His captain learned it from his captain. The silence, the stoicism, the punishment of vulnerability — it passes through the ranks like an inherited disease. The mechanism: shame is not felt. It is taught. It is modeled. The new recruit learns it from the veteran's silence. The veteran learned it from the captain's disapproval. The captain learned it from the culture's mythology of the unbreakable hero. Jackson's face burns. He is not carrying his own shame. He is carrying decades of institutional shame that was handed down like equipment.
