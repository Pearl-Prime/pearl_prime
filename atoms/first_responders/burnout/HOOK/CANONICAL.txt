You took the vest off. The vest is in the locker. The vest is not the issue. The issue is that something in your shoulders did not come off with it. The bracing is still in your trapezius at 9 PM on a Tuesday in your own kitchen. The call from this morning was routine. The bracing is not from that call. The bracing is from years of calls layered into the tissue, and the tissue does not file by date.

## HOOK v32
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: first_responders
---
Your spouse asks how the shift was. You say it was fine. Fine is the only word you have. The shift had a code. The code went the way codes go. You filled out the report. You handed off. Fine is the surface. Underneath is a thing you have not yet found language for after sixteen years, and your spouse is not the person you are going to find it with, not because they would not listen, but because you do not want to make them carry what you have been carrying.

## HOOK v33
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: first_responders
---
You do not call it burnout. You call it tired. You call it shift work. You call it the job. Burnout is a word from offices. What you have does not have a clean name. What you have is a body that has been on alert for so many continuous years that the alert is now the floor, not the ceiling, and the floor is what you are trying to take a vacation from. The vacation does not work because vacation does not change the floor.
