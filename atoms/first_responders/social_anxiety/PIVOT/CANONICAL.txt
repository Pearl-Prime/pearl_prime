--- variant: v01
You can walk into a fully involved structure fire without hesitation. Put you in a room full of civilians at a neighborhood barbecue and your nervous system fires the same alerts. The operational you has a protocol for every scenario. The social you doesn't, and the absence of protocol reads as threat.

--- variant: v02
Old frame: you're antisocial. New frame: you spent all your social bandwidth on high-stakes interactions at work. The discomfort at the party isn't a personality flaw. It's a capacity issue — you gave everything to the crew and the calls. There's nothing left for small talk.

--- variant: v03
At the station, roles are clear — officer, medic, driver, crew. Off scene, roles are ambiguous, and your brain reads ambiguity the same way it reads an unknown hazmat. Social anxiety in a first responder is often just operational awareness deployed in the wrong environment.
