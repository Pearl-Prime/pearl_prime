

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
There's a disconnect between how I am at work and how I am in social situations. At work, I'm confident. I know what I'm doing. I'm in charge. But in a social setting, there's anxiety. The mechanism is called context-dependent-confidence.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that I have trouble relaxing socially. I'm always a bit on alert. A bit ready. Like I might be needed. The mechanism is called perpetual-readiness.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of social anxiety that comes from exposure. I've seen things. Experienced things. And I'm worried that people won't understand. That they'll judge. That I won't be able to relate to normal concerns. The mechanism is called experience-based-alienation.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---

mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
