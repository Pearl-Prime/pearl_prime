## EXERCISE v04
---

---
The replay loop just started. Do not argue with it. Do this instead. Look at any object in the room. Describe it out loud in five sentences. The color. The shape. The texture. The weight if you can estimate it. The age. The loop cannot run a description and a replay at the same time. Five sentences. Then stop. If the loop comes back, pick another object. Do not negotiate with the replay. Replace the channel.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sitting up at 0300, replaying the call. Put both feet on the floor. Press the heels down until you feel the calves engage. Hold for a count of ten. Release. Now press the palms together at chest level. Hard. Hold for a count of ten. Release. Now press both hands flat onto the thighs. Hold for a count of ten. Release. The body is processing the call through the muscles. The mind cannot run the loop and the squeeze at the same time. Three rounds. Then lie back down. The loop may return. If it does, do another round. Do not try to sleep on top of the loop. Discharge the loop, then sleep.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
You are off shift and the prediction loop has started about tomorrow's shift. Do this. Take a piece of paper. Write down the three calls you are predicting. One sentence each. Read the three sentences back. Now write underneath: "These have not happened. Tomorrow's calls will be tomorrow's calls. I do not have to pre-think them." Read that line out loud. Fold the paper. Put it on the counter. Walk away from the counter. The thinking is in the paper now. It does not have to be in the head until tomorrow. If the loop returns, point at the paper. The paper is doing the holding tonight.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
The post-call debrief in your head is running its third lap. Stop it for sixty seconds with this. Hum. Mouth closed. Make a low hum that you can feel in your chest and throat. Hum on the exhale. Inhale through the nose. Hum on the next exhale. Six rounds. The vagus nerve responds to the hum the way it responds to slow breath. The debrief cannot run while the hum is running. After six rounds, lie still for thirty seconds. The debrief may return. It may not. Either way, the body got the message that you have an off switch.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Two-minute mental file-away. Sit. Close your eyes. Imagine the call in front of you as an actual paper file. The file has a cover. The cover has a date. Look at the cover. Now imagine a metal filing cabinet. Open the drawer marked with this year. Place the file inside. Close the drawer. Hear it close. The file is not gone. It is filed. You can pull it back out tomorrow if you need to. Tonight it stays in the drawer. Open your eyes. The drawer is closed. The file is filed. The mind is not currently holding it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Walk it off. Literally. Put on shoes. Step outside if it is safe, or pace a hallway if it is not. Walk at a normal pace for two full minutes. Do not check the phone. Do not loop the call. The legs are doing the work right now. Notice the right foot, then the left foot, then the right foot. Each step counts as one. Count to a hundred steps. The loop cannot reliably run during counted walking. Hundred steps. Then come back. The body has burned off some of the loop chemistry through the legs.
