## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You made the call in real time."
---
The call was fifteen minutes long. You've been analyzing it for three days. The decision you made at the scene, split-second, under real pressure — you're rewinding it now like you had an hour to think about it instead of ten seconds.

Your hands know the difference. They're shaking a little when you think about the moment. Your sleep is different. Your stomach tightens when the memory lands. Your body is still in the moment even though the moment is done.

The call is over. Your hands are steady right now. You're sitting still. The decision was made then. It was the right call or it wasn't. You can't change it now.

Notice your feet. You're still here. The patient is where they are. The call continues without you analyzing it.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The replay costs you every time."
---
You made a split-second decision in high pressure. The kind of decision you're trained to make. The kind that might have been perfect or might have been slightly different if you'd had more time. You'll never know, because you didn't have more time.

But now you're giving yourself unlimited time to second-guess it. Days of analysis. Hours of running scenarios. The conversation with your partner where you're talking through "what if I had." The sleep that won't come because your brain is still in crisis mode.

The call lasted fifteen minutes. The aftermath has been three days. The decision costs nothing now — the replay costs you. The doubt costs you. The hours spent analyzing a moment that's finished costs you.

The call was what it was. This analysis is a choice.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if you made the right call anyway?"
---
You're running the scenario again. The vitals you got. The information you had. The decision you made. But you're doing it differently now — you're adding information you didn't have then. You're calling other medics in your head. You're imagining how it might have gone differently.

But what if the information you had in the moment was enough? What if the decision was solid and you've just been turning it over so many times that it feels wrong now?

What if second-guessing isn't the same as failure?

What if the moment was exactly what it was — uncertain, high pressure, and still a call you made with everything you knew?

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your crew knows what you can do."
---
Your partner was there. Your crew was there. They saw the call. They saw you make the decision under pressure. They saw you move through it. They didn't see someone panicking or unprepared. They saw someone doing the job.

What they can't see is how much you're turning it over now. How much you're doubting what they watched you do competently. They don't know you're replaying it because they don't need to. They already know you. They already trust the calls you make.

You're the only one questioning you right now. Everyone else who was there already moved on.

Your crew's faith is steadier than your doubt.

This call is behind you. You're still here, still moving forward, still doing the work. The doubt comes and goes. The work keeps happening.

You've survived the uncertainty before. You're surviving it now.
