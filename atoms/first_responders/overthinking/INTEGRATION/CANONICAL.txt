mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You made the call in real time."
---
The call was fifteen minutes long. You've been analyzing it for three days. The decision you made at the scene, split-second, under real pressure — you're rewinding it now like you had an hour to think about it instead of ten seconds.

Your hands know the difference. They're shaking a little when you think about the moment. Your sleep is different. Your stomach tightens when the memory lands. Your body is still in the moment even though the moment is done.

The call is over. Your hands are steady right now. You're sitting still. The decision was made then. It was the right call or it wasn't. You can't change it now.

Notice your feet. You're still here. The patient is where they are. The call continues without you analyzing it.

reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The replay costs you every time."
---
You made a split-second decision in high pressure. The kind of decision you're trained to make. The kind that might have been perfect or might have been slightly different if you'd had more time. You'll never know, because you didn't have more time.

But now you're giving yourself unlimited time to second-guess it. Days of analysis. Hours of running scenarios. The conversation with your partner where you're talking through "what if I had." The sleep that won't come because your brain is still in crisis mode.

The call lasted fifteen minutes. The aftermath has been three days. The decision costs nothing now — the replay costs you. The doubt costs you. The hours spent analyzing a moment that's finished costs you.

The call was what it was. This analysis is a choice.
