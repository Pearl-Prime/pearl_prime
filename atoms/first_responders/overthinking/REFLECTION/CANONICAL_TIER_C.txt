

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I replay calls. The decisions I made. The things that happened. The things that could have happened. The mechanism is called call-rumination—the repetitive mental review of emergency responses.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that I'm particularly likely to overthink calls where the outcome was bad. Where someone was hurt or died. Where I wonder if I could have done something differently. The mechanism is called outcome-focused-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of overthinking that happens when there's uncertainty about what happened. When I'm not sure if I did the right thing. When other people have different opinions about how I handled it. The mechanism is called uncertainty-driven-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that the overthinking is sometimes about protecting myself from future mistakes. Like if I think about it enough, analyze it enough, I'll prevent a future error. But analysis doesn't actually prevent anything. The mechanism is called preventive-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is how overthinking can be misunderstood as conscientiousness. Like I'm a careful person because I ruminate about my work. But actually it's anxiety disguised as care. The mechanism is called anxiety-as-conscientiousness.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my overthinking that's connected to the culture of the job. We debrief. We talk about calls. And sometimes that becomes rumination. Going over it again and again without reaching resolution. The mechanism is called professional-rumination-norm.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that I overthink most during the quiet times. When I have space to think. When I'm not in the immediate crisis. The mechanism is called crisis-following-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I'm realizing is that some of my overthinking is about trauma. The calls stick with you. And your mind wants to process them. But the processing doesn't always lead anywhere productive. The mechanism is called unprocessed-trauma-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about the unpredictability of the job that feeds overthinking. I can't control what calls come in. So my mind tries to prepare by thinking through possibilities. But the possibilities are endless. The mechanism is called uncontrollable-threat-rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've learned that overthinking is sometimes a way of maintaining control in a situation where I actually have limited control. The mechanism is called control-seeking-rumination.
