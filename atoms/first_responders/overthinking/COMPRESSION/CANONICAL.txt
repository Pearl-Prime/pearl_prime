## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
Your jaw is clenched. Notice that first.

Drop it. Let the back teeth separate.

Now breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold for two. Out through your mouth for six.

Your brain ran the scenario forty times. It cannot run it forty-first and get a different answer.

Name one thing your hands can touch right now. Press your palms flat against it.

That surface is real. The loop is not.

Stay here for thirty seconds. Count them.

When the count ends, the loop ends with it.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
Stand up from wherever you are.

Plant both feet flat on the floor, hip-width apart.

Feel the floor push back. It always does.

Your mind replayed the call. It will replay it again if you let it run unchecked.

Give it a task instead. Name five things in the room that are red or orange.

Say them out loud. Your own voice interrupts the spiral better than silence does.

Now roll your shoulders back once, slow.

The call is logged. The report is filed. Your body is here, not there.

Breathe out long. Longer than feels necessary.

Done.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
Stop moving.

Sit down if you are standing. Put your back against something solid.

Feel that solid thing behind your spine. Press into it slightly.

You have been running the decision tree since the shift ended. Every branch leads to the same knot.

Pick up a cold object. A water bottle, a phone, a set of keys.

Hold it in your dominant hand. Squeeze once.

Cold and pressure travel faster to the brain than thought does. Use that.

Breathe in for four counts. Out for four counts. Repeat three times.

The decision was made. The moment is closed.

Your nervous system needs a signal that the threat is over. You just gave it one.

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Both hands on your thighs. Press down.

Feel the weight of your arms through your palms.

You have been in your head since you got home. The uniform is off but the shift is still running.

Name the room you are in. Say it aloud.

Name one sound you hear right now. Not from the call. From here.

Breathe in slowly. Let your belly move first, then your chest.

Hold for two counts at the top.

Release it all at once through your mouth.

The loop feeds on silence and stillness. Break one of them.

Stand up. Walk to the nearest window. Look out for twenty seconds.

You are back. Stay back.

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
Drink something cold right now.

Feel it move down your throat. Track it.

That sensation is your anchor. Your mind drifted back to the scene. Your body is here in this kitchen, this break room, this car.

Tap your left knee three times with your right hand.

Now your right knee three times with your left hand.

Crossing the midline interrupts the spiral. That is not a theory. That is physiology.

Breathe in for five counts. Out for seven.

Repeat twice.

The overthinking is not weakness. It is a trained brain doing its job past the end of the job.

Tell it: shift over. Mean it.

Then drink again.

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Unclench your hands.

You did not notice they were clenched. They were.

Open your fingers wide. Hold them spread for five seconds.

Now close them slowly, no tension this time.

Your mind is running the what-ifs. Every dispatcher knows that loop. Every medic knows that loop.

It does not resolve. It only exhausts.

Press the soles of your feet into the floor. Both feet. Full contact.

Say the date out loud. Say the time.

Those two facts are a location. You are in the present, not the call.

Breathe out first. Then in. Reversing the order slows the heart rate faster.

Three times. Then stop.

You are done for now.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---
Sit on the edge of a chair or bed. Not leaning back.

Elbows on knees. Head hanging loose.

Let gravity take the weight of your skull for ten seconds.

Count them.

You have been carrying that weight upright for hours, running the incident on a loop.

Let it drop.

After ten seconds, slowly raise your head. Vertebra by vertebra.

At the top, take one long breath. In through the nose. Out through the mouth.

The scenario did not change on replay forty-three. It will not change on forty-four.

Your job right now is recovery. That is not optional. That is operational readiness.

Sit back. Let the chair hold you.

Stay there two minutes.

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---
Put one hand on your sternum.

Press gently. Feel your heartbeat or your breath, whichever comes first.

That rhythm is involuntary. It kept going through the whole shift without your help.

Your thinking brain is not in charge of everything.

Notice that.

Now breathe in for four counts, hold for four, out for four. Three full cycles.

While you breathe, name the last meal you ate. Not the call. The meal.

What did it taste like. Where were you sitting.

Concrete details pull the brain forward in time.

You are not still on scene. Your hand on your chest proves it.

Pulse is steady. Breath is steady.

The loop can stop here.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---
Walk to a sink.

Turn on the cold water.

Hold both wrists under it for thirty seconds. Count them out loud.

Cold water on the wrists drops the core temperature slightly and interrupts the adrenaline cycle. Use the biology.

While you count, look at the water. Only the water.

When thirty seconds end, turn off the tap.

Dry your hands slowly. Feel the towel.

Now stand still for fifteen seconds and do nothing.

The mind will try to restart the replay. Let the thought arrive. Do not follow it.

You followed it long enough.

Breathe once, deep.

Walk away from the sink. The loop stays there.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---
Lie flat on your back if you can. Floor is better than bed.

Arms at your sides. Palms up.

Feel the floor under your shoulder blades, your hips, your heels.

Four points of contact. Name them.

Your nervous system spent the shift scanning for threats. It is still scanning. That is not a malfunction.

But the scan needs a boundary.

Breathe in for four counts. Let your belly rise. Do not force your chest.

Out for six counts. Slow.

Repeat four times.

On the fourth exhale, say out loud: clear.

That word is a signal. You used it on shift for a reason.

Use it now for yourself.

Clear.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---
Stop talking to yourself about it.

Not forever. Right now, for three minutes.

Set a timer.

While the timer runs, do one physical thing only. Wash a dish. Fold something. Lace your boots or unlace them.

Keep your hands moving. Keep your eyes on the task.

When the mind pulls back to the call, redirect it to your hands. What do your hands feel. Texture. Temperature. Weight.

Three minutes is short. It is enough to break the loop's momentum.

When the timer ends, sit down.

Press your back flat against the chair.

Breathe in through the nose, out through the mouth, four times.

The call will still be there if you need to process it.

Right now you need the break more.

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---
Clench every muscle in your body at once.

Fists, jaw, shoulders, thighs, feet. Everything.

Hold for five seconds.

Release all of it at once.

That discharge is physical. The body held the shift in tension. It needed a signal to let go.

Now breathe in slowly for five counts.

Feel the difference between clenched and released. Stay in the released state.

Your mind ran every version of what happened and what could have happened. That is what a trained brain does under stress.

The training worked. The shift is over.

Name one thing you will do in the next hour. One small thing.

That is the next mission.

Only that.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---
Find a wall.

Put your back flat against it. Full contact from shoulders to hips.

Feel the wall. It is not moving.

You have been in motion, mentally, since the call ended. The wall is a counterforce.

Press into it once, hard, for three seconds.

Release.

Breathe in for four counts. The wall is behind you. Nothing is behind you.

Out for six counts.

Your brain flagged a hundred variables tonight. That is the job. The job is done.

Repeat this once: I am off the clock.

Say it to the wall if saying it to yourself feels wrong.

Press your palms flat against the surface beside you.

Cold. Solid. Present.

Stay here sixty seconds. Then go eat something warm.
