## PERMISSION_GRANT v01
---

---
Overthinking arrives wearing the uniform of competence. It looks like preparing. It looks like checking. It looks like doing the work right. Underneath the uniform is a nervous system that has not been allowed to set the call down. Set it down.

You are allowed to leave the call on scene. The truck pulled away. The patient went where they were going. The chart is filed. The call is done. The replay you are running in bed is not the call. It is the cost of having driven the truck. You are allowed to charge the cost without paying it again every night.

You are allowed to not have a better version of what you did. The mind is built to find the better path. Sometimes there is no better path. Sometimes you did the thing the way it could be done, given what was on the screen at the time. You are allowed to let "given what I had then" be a complete answer.

You are allowed to stop reviewing the radio traffic in your head. The radio said what the radio said. You do not have to re-listen to it at 0300. The recording, if there is one, will say what the recording says. The replay in your head is not the recording. It is a story about the recording, and you are allowed to stop telling it to yourself.

You are allowed to let the rookie figure some of it out alone. You did. The figuring is part of the training. The mind that is running a thousand variations of "what if I had warned them about X" is not training the rookie. It is exhausting itself. You are allowed to teach what you know and let the gap be the gap.

You are allowed to be uncertain about a judgment call. The judgment was a judgment. It was not an answer. Other competent people would have called it different ways. You are allowed to not know with certainty whether you got it right, and to also keep working, and to also sleep tonight.

You are allowed to interrupt the loop. Stand up. Walk to the kitchen. Drink water. The loop does not stop because you understood it. The loop stops because you stood up. The body has its own way of breaking the cycle and you are allowed to use it.

You are allowed to be the consciousness watching the loop, not the loop itself. The replay runs. The doubt arrives. And somewhere underneath, awareness keeps watching. The watching is older than the job. The watching is the part the loop has never been able to occupy.

These are not affirmations. They are descriptions of the rights a body comes with after twenty years of running calls. Read them slow. The body will catch up to the words.

## PERMISSION_GRANT v02
---

---
The job teaches a particular kind of thinking. It rewards depth, contingency, prediction. The thinking, on shift, is a survival tool. Off shift, the same thinking does not know how to stop. Set it down for an hour.

You are allowed to think less than the maximum. The maximum is for scene work. The kitchen table is not scene work. The shower is not scene work. The first hour after waking up is not scene work. You are allowed to think the smaller, simpler thought at the table, without losing your edge on the next call.

You are allowed to not solve every contingency in advance. The next call has not happened. The contingency you are pre-thinking may not be the contingency that shows up. The thinking is real labor and the labor has limits. You are allowed to ration it.

You are allowed to forget some of what happened. Not the important things. The small things. The exact words. The exact order. The exact route. The system remembers what it needs to remember. You do not have to hold all of it consciously to remain competent.

You are allowed to not debrief everything to yourself. Not every call needs an internal after-action report. Some calls finished when the shift ended. The internal debrief that runs anyway is not making the next call cleaner. It is just running.

You are allowed to talk to one person you trust, even if the station culture says you don't. The crew handles its way. You can have another way for some of it. A spouse. A therapist. A friend who was on the job and is out now. One outlet that is not the head. The outlet does not violate the crew. It supplements it.

You are allowed to enjoy a day off without auditing it. The day off does not need to be optimized. It does not need to be productive. It does not need to be evaluated at the end of the day for whether you used it correctly. The day off is allowed to just be a day.

You are allowed to outgrow the version of yourself that thought the thinking was helping. Sometimes it was. Sometimes it just felt like control. You are allowed to know the difference now, in this body, in this house, after this shift.

These are the standing permissions. Read them slow. The body knows what to do with them.

## PERMISSION_GRANT v03
---

---
The overthinking has a logic. It thinks it is doing protective work. The work it is doing has a cost. Both can be true. You are allowed to acknowledge the cost without dismantling the logic. The system is doing what it was built to do; you do not have to pay the bill every night.

You are allowed to let an unanswered question stay unanswered. The mind hates open loops. It will try to close every one, even the ones that have no available close. You are allowed to leave the loop open and walk away. The loop will still be there tomorrow. It will be there in ten years. That is fine.

You are allowed to be wrong about your own performance and find out later. Maybe you handled that call better than you remember. Maybe worse. The replay in your head is not the truth. It is one version. You are allowed to suspend the verdict until something other than your own anxious replay weighs in.

You are allowed to stop running the radio call back. The radio is one input. The radio is not the call. You can let go of the exact words the dispatcher used. The call happened in the world, not in the radio.

You are allowed to need help organizing the mind. A therapist is not a confession. A therapist is a tool. The tool is good at what the head cannot do alone, which is hold the data while you sort it. You are allowed to use the tool the same way you use a halligan or a stethoscope — because it does the job the bare hand cannot.

You are allowed to be done thinking about the bad call. There is a point at which more thinking does not produce more understanding. The diminishing returns are real. You are allowed to recognize that point and walk away from it.

You are allowed to be a person who runs calls and also a person who reads novels and also a person who falls asleep watching bad TV. The job is not all of you. The overthinking is the job leaking into the parts of you that were not built for it.

These are not affirmations. They are the standing rights. Read them. The body will work them in.
