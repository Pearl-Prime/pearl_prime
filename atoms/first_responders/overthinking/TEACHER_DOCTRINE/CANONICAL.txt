

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v01
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: post_call_replay
weight: standard
carry_line: "The replay loop is not asking what happened. It is asking what could have gone wrong and did not. The system is trying to catch a future it cannot reach by interrogating the past."
---
Here is the mechanism. The brain you bring to the job has a feature that, on the job, is useful. It runs scenarios. It plays out the alternatives. It asks what would have happened if you had turned left instead of right, gone in first instead of second, called for backup at the door instead of the porch. On scene, this feature saves lives. Off scene, in bed at 0300 with the call already three days old, the same feature does not know it is supposed to be off. It keeps running. It treats the past call as a live problem. It looks for the path through that would have made it cleaner, safer, faster. The call already happened. The outcome already settled. The brain does not care. It is built to find the better path, and it will keep looking even when there is no path left to find. What changes when you see this is that the loop keeps running but you stop arguing with it. You start to recognize it as a tool that was built to run in one context, firing in another. The 0300 replay is not new information. It is the same data, played again, by a system that does not know how to stop playing.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v02
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: prediction_loop
weight: standard
carry_line: "The thinking is not trying to solve the next call. It is trying to imagine every possible version of the next call so that no version arrives unprepared. The system cannot win that race."
---
Here is the mechanism. The mind, on this job, becomes a predictor. It learns to take a small input — the address on the screen, the tone of the dispatcher's voice, the first detail through the radio — and forward-project it into the most likely thirty seconds, the most likely sixty seconds, the most likely outcomes. This is part of how training becomes competence. The prediction runs fast and the body gets ready before the body knows why. The problem is that the predictor does not have an off switch. Three hours after the shift, two days into a long off-stretch, sitting in a chair doing nothing in particular, the predictor is still running. It generates scenarios for tomorrow's shift, the next bad call, the kind of run you have not had yet but the brain knows is statistically due. It is not lying. The statistics are real. But the prediction is consuming the body now, for an event that has not occurred and may not occur in the form predicted. What changes when you see this is that the prediction keeps generating. You cannot dismantle the predictor. But you start to notice that the body is paying the cost of futures that have not arrived. Naming the loop is how you stop paying twice for the same call.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v03
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: training_gap
weight: standard
carry_line: "Training covers most of what shows up. The overthinking lives in the gap between most and all. The gap cannot be closed by thinking harder about it."
---
Here is the mechanism. The training is excellent. It is also finite. It covers the cases that show up most of the time, by design, because that is what training is good at. The cases at the edge — the ones where the protocol does not quite fit, where the call is a hybrid of two categories, where the judgment is real — those cases exist. Every shift has the possibility of one. The mind knows this. It does not know which call will be the edge case. So it goes looking. It tries to pre-think the edge cases. It tries to build a tree of contingencies so deep that no edge case can surprise it. The tree does not finish growing. The edge cases are, by definition, the ones the protocol does not anticipate, which means the tree is trying to anticipate things that resist anticipation. The overthinking is the tree, growing on its own time, with no end-state. What changes when you see this is that you stop expecting the tree to finish. You start to recognize the overthinking as the cost of working at the edge of a body of training, not as evidence that your training is inadequate. The training is fine. The edge is just the edge.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v04
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: solo_processing
weight: standard
carry_line: "The crew does not process. You process alone. Alone processing turns into looping. The loop is not pathology. It is what processing looks like with nowhere to put it down."
---
Here is the mechanism. There is real work the mind has to do after a hard call. Make sense of it. File it. Put it where the rest of the calls go. This work has a name in the literature — it gets called processing — and the work is real, regardless of whether you have a word for it. The station culture has rules about where this work happens. It does not happen at the table. It does not happen with the rookies. It does not happen out loud, mostly, except in the dark joke that closes the topic before it opens. So the work goes inward. It happens in the truck on the way home. It happens at 0300. It happens in the shower. It happens in the head. Alone. Without another voice. Without anyone to say "yes, that was bad" or "no, you handled it." The processing keeps running because nothing has marked it as complete. The loop you are inside is processing without an outlet. What changes when you see this is that the loop is not malfunctioning. It is doing the work. It is just doing the work in the only place the culture allows the work to happen, which is alone. Naming this is the start of choosing whether the culture rules apply at home too, or only at the station.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v05
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: outcome_doubt
weight: standard
carry_line: "The outcome was good and the body does not believe it. The body has been trained to assume the worst until proven otherwise. Proof takes longer to install than fear."
---
Here is the mechanism. The body, on this job, learns a particular kind of math. It learns that the cost of assuming the call went well, when it did not, is higher than the cost of assuming the call went badly, when it did not. This math kept the next call sharp. It also taught the body that good outcomes are provisional until confirmed three times over, and that even confirmed they are still suspect because the next bad outcome will arrive at random. So the call ends. The patient lives. The fire is out. The radio confirms. And the body keeps running the loop — what did I miss, what is delayed, what will the chart show in the morning — because the body does not yet trust the good outcome. It is doing the math it was trained to do. The doubt is not a personality flaw. It is the protective lean of a system that has been calibrated by years of stakes. What changes when you see this is that the doubt keeps arriving. You do not have to argue with it. You can just name it: this is the trained doubt firing on a confirmed-good outcome. The doubt is real. The outcome is also real. Both can be true at the same time, and naming both keeps you from collapsing one into the other.
