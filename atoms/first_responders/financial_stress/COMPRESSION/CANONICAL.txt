## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Feel the floor push back. That pressure is real.

Name one bill. Just one. Write the number down.

Now write what you earn this month. One number.

The gap between those two numbers is information. Not emergency.

Breathe out slowly. Count to six.

The debt existed before this moment. It exists after. You are not the debt.

Put the paper down. Return to the floor under your feet.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Your hands are tight. Open them. Spread all ten fingers wide.

Hold that for four counts.

Now write one financial fact you have been avoiding. One sentence only.

Read it out loud. Hear your own voice say the number.

That number does not change your rank. It does not change your record.

Close your hands. Release them again.

The fact is smaller now. You made it speakable.

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Sit down. Both hands on your thighs.

Notice the weight of your legs. That weight is yours.

Financial pressure narrows vision the same way a scene does. Your brain reads both as threat.

You are not on a scene. Name the room you are in.

Now name one expense you control. One. Not all of them.

Write it down. Circle it.

Control lives there. Start there.

---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Jaw tight. Shoulders up near your ears. Notice both.

Drop your shoulders. Let your jaw fall open slightly.

Say the amount out loud. The number you have been carrying in your chest.

Hear it in the air. Outside your body now.

Take one breath in for four counts. Out for six.

That number is a problem. Problems have steps.

Write step one. Only step one.

Put the pen down.

---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Right now your nervous system reads financial stress as danger. It is wrong. Correct it.

Press your back into your chair. Feel the support.

Name the month. Name the year. Say both aloud.

You are not behind on a call. You are behind on a bill. Different.

Write one number you owe. Write one number you have.

Breathe. Four in. Four out.

The distance between those numbers is a math problem. Math has answers.

---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Both feet down. Count to three before you look at the screen.

One. Two. Three.

Open the account. Look at the balance. Do not look away.

Say the number. Out loud. Flat voice.

Now close the screen.

That number did not stop your heart. You are still here.

Write one action. One thing you can do before Friday. Specific.

Fold the paper. Put it in your left pocket.

Carry the action. Not the dread.

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Your shift just ended. The financial noise starts now. That pattern is predictable.

Predictable means you can prepare for it.

Sit before you check anything. Thirty seconds.

Feel the seat under you. Feel your own exhale.

Now open one statement. Read only the total due. One number.

Write it on a notepad. Old school. Paper.

Beside it write the due date.

That is the whole task. Date and amount. Done.

Close the statement.

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Take your hands off the keyboard. Put them in your lap.

Financial decisions made in a hypervigilant state cost more than the bill.

Feel your pulse. Find it at your wrist. Count five beats.

You are regulated enough to read one number.

Open the document. Find the largest balance. Write it down.

Now write one payment. Any amount. Something real.

Circle it.

That circle is a choice. Choices move things.

---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Stand up. Walk to a window.

Look at something at least thirty feet away. Hold your gaze there for ten seconds.

Your eyes just shifted out of close-threat mode. Your brain follows.

Come back to the table.

Write the three words that describe your financial situation right now. Not a paragraph. Three words.

Read them. They are a diagnosis. Diagnoses lead to treatment.

Write one next step. Underneath the three words.

That step is your shift assignment. Work it.

---

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Cold water. Run it over your wrists for ten seconds. Do this now.

Dry your hands. Sit down.

The financial problem feels like the worst call you ever ran. It is not.

Name the difference out loud. Say it.

Now write one number you owe. Write it large.

Write one resource beside it. A union rep. A credit union. A payment plan.

Draw a line connecting them.

Resources exist. You just made one visible.

---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

You compartmentalize for a living. Use it here.

Create one box. Label it: money. This week only.

Write what goes in the box. One debt. One number. One due date.

Close the box. Not forever. Until Thursday.

On Thursday, open it. Read the number again.

Between now and Thursday, the number does not live in your chest. It lives on the paper.

Breathe out. Long and slow.

The paper holds it now.

---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Chest tight. That is the signal.

Press one hand flat against your sternum. Feel the pressure.

Say the number you are afraid to say. The balance. The total. Out loud.

Hold your hand there. Feel your own heartbeat under your palm.

Still beating. The number did not stop it.

Write the number down. Date it.

Dated numbers are history in progress. History changes.

Put the paper somewhere you will see it Friday.

---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

You run toward what others run from. Financial shame makes you run from the numbers.

Turn around.

Open the statement. Look at the total. Read it once.

Now read it again. Same number.

Numbers do not escalate. They sit. They wait.

Write the number on a notepad. Beside it write: manageable or not yet manageable.

Either answer is true. Either answer is a starting point.

Breathe in for four. Out for four.

You have a starting point now.
