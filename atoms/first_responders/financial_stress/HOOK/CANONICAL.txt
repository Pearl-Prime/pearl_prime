

## HOOK v01
---

---
The pay doesn't move but the bills do. Rent. Insurance. Medical. The things you see on shift end up on your credit card because the salary's designed for someone who doesn't have your body yet. The irony's sharp. You spent years making people whole. Now you're breaking yourself trying to afford to stay in one piece.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Overtime used to be extra. Now it's required mathematics. Without it, your family's underwater. With it, you're underwater in a different way. Your body's tired. Your nervous system's running on fumes and overtime pay. The money's real. The cost is real too and it doesn't show up in the bank statement.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The pension used to be something. A promise of stability. Now you watch the pension funds and watch the horizon and understand that you're working until you can't work and the promise is getting smaller every year. You're locked in. The job's all you have. Leaving means losing the one shot at a future that might hold.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The disability talk is getting serious at the station. Knee. Back. Shoulder. Everyone's carrying something that might not make it to retirement. And if it doesn't, there's no plan. No income. No structure. So you work hurt because hurt is free. Healing costs money you don't have and time you can't afford to take.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The household runs on your salary like an engine on fumes. One injury away from bankruptcy. One medical bill away from losing the house. One pension cut away from the life you promised your family. You show up every shift knowing the math. Knowing that safety is a luxury you're one crisis away from not being able to afford.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The job was supposed to be the stable thing. The thing that held the family when everything else shifted. Now the family's holding you because the salary doesn't move and the costs keep moving and there's no way to make the math work except by working until you break.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You earn enough to be trapped. Not enough to be comfortable. The sweet spot where you can't quit but you can't breathe either. The money's there every two weeks and it's gone before you understand where. The bills are the job now. The job is just the thing that pays the bills.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Years in and your actual hourly wage is down. The job hasn't given raises that match the inflation. The cost of living in a city where the stations are has tripled. But the salary moved once five years ago and hasn't moved since. You're making less every year in real terms and the work's getting harder.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
Sick days don't pay like they used to. Time off costs money. Vacation's fine if you don't take it. Medical leave means less of the paycheck you're already using to cover medical. The system's designed so that taking care of yourself is a financial luxury item. So you don't. You work through it. You break through it. You pay later.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
The crew talks about it different now. Used to be you'd talk about calls. Now you talk about how you're covering bills. Who's picking up extra shifts. Who's dipping into retirement savings for the dental work they can't afford. The job's becoming secondary to the financial survival it's supposed to provide.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
Your partner's working now too. Two full-time incomes and you're still at the edge of being okay. If one of you gets hurt, the whole thing collapses. If the pension changes, it's over. You're built for a crisis you can't respond to. That's a different kind of fear than the calls bring.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
The pay cut happens slowly. Cost of living increase every year. Salary increase never. Compound that over years and you're making a portion of what you thought you'd be making when you signed up. But you can't leave. You're in it too deep now. The pension's the only future you have left.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Disability isn't something that happens to other people anymore. It's something you're counting on not happening to you because if it does, there's no plan. No income. No structure. Just debt. So you work hurt. You work damaged. You work until something gives completely because giving up means giving up everything.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The house is tied to the salary. The car is tied to the salary. The family's survival is tied to the salary. So the salary owns you. It owns your time. It owns your body. It owns your nervous system. You can't say no to shifts because no shifts means no income means losing everything that makes you feel like you've built something.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
The medical bills from work injuries don't qualify as work injuries because they happened on your time off. So you pay them. The preventive care your body needs costs money you don't have. So you skip it. By the time something gets serious, it's much more expensive. But you're already broke so you just get worse.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
Every crew member's doing the math. How many years until the pension. How much more can the body take. Can you make it to the number or will something break first. It's not morbid. It's practical. You're working with variables and a deadline and stakes you can't afford to lose.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
The bonus used to exist. Benefits used to improve. Contracts used to get negotiated. Now the contracts get longer and the benefits shrink and the bonus is a conversation that doesn't happen anymore because there's nothing to discuss. You're grateful to have a job that pays anything.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
Your kids ask about college and you have to do the math in your head and realize you can't afford it without taking on debt they'll spend years paying off. The job was supposed to be the pathway to stability. Instead it's the thing holding you hostage in a place where stability is always three months away.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The injury's coming. You can feel it. Everyone can feel it. But you can't take the injury because the financial structure doesn't allow for it. So you keep pushing. Keep working. Keep holding pieces of yourself together with duct tape and overtime pay hoping you can make it to the pension before you actually break.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
The crew's aging into something nobody wants to talk about. Healthcare costs going up. Bodies wearing down. Injuries piling up. Retirement age still fifteen years away. The math doesn't work but the alternative is worse so everyone keeps working. Keeps sacrificing. Keeps waiting for something to change that isn't going to change.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You're making thirty percent less than your counterpart in the next city over. The cost of living is the same. The work is the same. The pay's just different and there's no explanation and no appeal. You're locked into it because moving means starting over and starting over means losing the seniority that's the only thing keeping you from being even more behind.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The emergency fund got drained by an actual emergency. Now the next emergency is just debt. The budget's been cut so many times there's nothing left to cut except the thing you need to live on. So the debt grows. The stress grows. The work becomes less about helping people and more about not drowning.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
The pension's underfunded and everyone knows it. The promises that made the low pay acceptable are the promises that might not be kept. But you're already in it too deep to get out. So you work. You wait. You hope the math works out better than it looks like it will.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
Overtime's becoming mandatory. The schedule's written so you can't avoid it. The department's cut to skeleton crew so if you say no to extra shifts, someone else has to pick them up and that someone's your crew and you won't do that to them. So you say yes. Every time. You're trapped in a system that's designed to extract every hour you have.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
The new hire's complaining about the salary and you remember when you cared about stuff like that. Now you're just trying to explain how to make the money work. How to live on less. How to arrange your life around what you're paid instead of around what you actually need. He'll learn. They all learn.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
Medical debt is a thing you don't talk about. Dental debt. The thing you haven't fixed because it's not emergency and you can't afford the payment plan. Your body's broken on the job and you're paying out of pocket to fix it because the coverage is a joke. So the body doesn't get fixed. You work around it.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The second job's becoming the third shift. Some guys are taking security gigs on their days off. Some are driving. The department doesn't officially acknowledge this. But everyone knows. The salary's so low that needing another income is just normal. That's not a side hustle. That's survival.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
The wife had to cut hours because the kids needed someone around. Now you're the only income and the income's designed for someone without those costs. The math never worked but now it's actively broken. So you work more. She works less. The family shrinks to fit the budget.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The year the pension got frozen, that's when it became clear this wasn't going to be what they promised. The money you sacrificed years of your life for is disappearing into math you can't control. But you're already in it. The only way out is through and the through is a lot longer than it looked from the beginning.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
You're fifty years old making less than you made at thirty-five because raises don't happen but life expenses do. Retirement's a concept that applies to other people in other jobs. For you it's a theoretical age you'll hit if your body lasts that long. So you keep working. The money keeps disappearing. The future keeps moving further away.
---
