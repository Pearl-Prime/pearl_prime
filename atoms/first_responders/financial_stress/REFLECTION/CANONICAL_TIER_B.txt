

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Nobody talks about the financial stress. They talk about the danger and the mental health part. But they don't talk about the fact that you're doing the most important work your community needs and you can't pay your mortgage. The mechanism is called the worth-earnings gap. It's the disconnect between the actual value of what you do and what the system will pay you for it.
I spent years making less than electricians. Less than contractors. Less than people who did work that mattered less. The work was existential. The pay was not.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The protective mechanism doesn't let you escape the reality. The job teaches you to focus on the work. Don't think about money. Don't let the financial piece distract. So you don't. You show up for less money than the work deserves.
But the mortgage is still due. The kids still need school supplies. The car still needs repairs. And the paycheck doesn't cover it.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
You don't feel the financial stress building until the bill comes and you realize you've been living off credit. Until you get the notice. Until the car breaks down.
The invisibility is that you've been so focused on the work that you haven't been paying attention to the financial reality. Haven't been acknowledging that the math doesn't work.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The automatic response to the worth-earnings gap is to work more. To pick up extra shifts. To do side work. To squeeze more hours out of an already exhausted body so you can close the gap. The mechanism treats every hour as tradeable for money.
Off the job, that same mechanism is running. You're still trading rest for money. Still working yourself to exhaustion.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I've been thinking about what the worth-earnings gap does to the nervous system. How it creates a chronic state of anxiety. How it teaches the body to be afraid of the future. How it makes rest impossible because rest costs money you don't have.
The protective mechanism is the automatic activation of work. The system says: you can't afford to stop. You have to keep going.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The mechanism that underpays responders is elegant. It uses purpose as a substitute for money. It uses your commitment to the work as an excuse to underpay you. The mechanism is telling you that you shouldn't ask for more because the work is important.
But the mortgage doesn't care about purpose.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I didn't realize the financial stress was affecting everything until my therapist asked me when I last took a day off without thinking about money.
I couldn't answer. Not because I was too busy. Because I was always thinking about the mortgage. The insurance. The car repair. The medical bills. The invisibility was that the financial stress had become background radiation.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The protective mechanism keeps you accepting inadequate pay by saying the alternatives are worse. It says the work is purpose. It says you should be grateful. It says asking for more is selfish.
The mechanism is so entrenched that you start to believe it. Start to think that you shouldn't ask for more.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The automatic response to underpayment is to keep working. To keep showing up. To keep accepting that the math doesn't work and you'll figure it out somehow. Years pass. The gap doesn't close. The stress doesn't decrease.
The mechanism just keeps running. Keep working. Keep sacrificing. Keep accepting inadequacy.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The worth-earnings gap creates a specific kind of trauma. You know what you're worth. You know what the system is willing to pay. You know the difference is meant to be absorbed by your sacrifice.
So you sacrifice. You work more. You trade health for money that still doesn't add up. The protective mechanism says this is necessary. The mechanism is making sure you stay trapped.
---
