

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Nobody talks about the financial stress. They talk about the job and the danger and the mental health part. But they don't talk about the fact that you're doing the most important work your community needs done and you can't pay your mortgage.
The mechanism is called the worth-earnings gap. It's the disconnect between the actual value of what you do and what the system is willing to pay you for it. You save lives. You respond to the worst. You sacrifice your time and your health and your nervous system.
And the paycheck doesn't match the cost.
I spent ten years on the job making less than the electricians at the station. Less than the contractors. Less than people who did work that mattered less. The work was existential. The pay was not.
