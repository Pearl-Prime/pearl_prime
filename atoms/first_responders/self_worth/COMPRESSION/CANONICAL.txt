## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Place both feet flat on the floor. Press down. Feel the ground push back. That resistance is real. You are real. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for four. Your worth does not depend on the last call. It does not depend on what you saw, what you did, or what you could not prevent. It exists before the shift starts. It exists after the shift ends. Press your feet down again. Say your name out loud. That person — the one who answered — has value that no dispatch log measures.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Put one hand on your sternum. Feel it rise and fall. That movement costs you nothing. It belongs to you. Breathe in slow. Let your jaw go loose. Your value was not issued with your badge. It did not arrive with your certification. It predates every incident number ever assigned to your name. Breathe out. Let your shoulders drop one inch. The dispatcher who held the line, the medic who ran the call — that person carries worth the radio never confirms. Feel your chest again. Still moving. Still yours.

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

Sit back in your chair. Let it hold your full weight. Stop bracing. Breathe in for four counts. Out for six. Your worth is not a performance review. It is not a save rate. It is not what the lieutenant said in debrief. Notice where your hands rest. Let them be still. Breathe again. You entered this work because something in you decided other people's lives mattered. That decision came from worth already present. It did not create your worth. It expressed it. Let the chair hold you. You do not have to earn this breath.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Stand up. Plant your feet shoulder-width apart. Feel your weight distribute evenly. This is your body, outside the gear, outside the role. Breathe in through your nose. Count to four. Out through your mouth. Count to five. The person who stands here right now is not defined by the worst moment on any shift. Not by the child you could not reach. Not by the fire that moved faster than the plan. Breathe again. Roll your shoulders back once. You are a person first. That sequence matters. Person first. Everything else follows.

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Close your eyes if that feels safe. If not, soften your gaze at the floor. Breathe in. Notice the temperature of the air entering your nose. That sensation is present-tense. You are present-tense. Your worth is not archived in old run reports. It does not live in the calls you replay at 0300. It lives here, in this breath, in this body that showed up again. Breathe out slowly. Open your eyes. Look at your hands. These hands responded. These hands also deserve rest. Both things are true at the same time.

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Press your back against a wall. Feel the solid surface behind your spine. You are supported. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for one. Out for four. Say this once, out loud or in your head: I am more than my last call. Say it again. Notice if your chest tightens or releases. Either response is information, not judgment. Breathe once more. The dispatcher who talked someone through the worst night of their life — that person deserves the same steadiness they gave a stranger. Press into the wall. Let it hold you. Breathe out.

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Unclench your jaw. Do it now. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out for five. Your worth does not require a critical incident to justify it. It does not require a commendation. It does not require anyone's gratitude. Notice your neck. Let it lengthen slightly. You have spent years reading threat levels in every room you enter. Read this room. No threat. Breathe again. The hypervigilance that keeps others safe does not need to audit your own value. Let your face go soft. You are allowed to exist without justifying the existence.

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Take off your watch if you are wearing one. Set it down. Breathe in. You are not on the clock right now. Your worth does not accrue by hours worked or bodies stabilized or fires knocked down. It does not compound with overtime. It does not subtract when a call goes wrong. Breathe out for six counts. Feel your feet. Feel the floor. Pick up your watch again if you want it. But know this: the person who existed before you ever put on a uniform still lives inside this body. Breathe once more. That person has always been enough.

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Sit on the edge of a bed or a chair. Elbows on your knees. Let your head hang forward slightly. Breathe in. Feel the stretch across the back of your neck. Hold the breath for two counts. Release it slow. You carry weight that most people never see. Not the gear. The other kind. Breathe again. Your worth is not measured by how much you carry without complaint. It is not measured by how fast you re-enter civilian life and act like nothing happened. Lift your head. Look straight ahead. You are allowed to be a person in recovery. That takes more strength than the call did.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Press your palms together in your lap. Feel the warmth between them. That warmth is your body, still functioning, still present. Breathe in for four counts. Out for four. Your identity is not the radio traffic. It is not the incident command structure. It is not the last thing the sergeant said about your performance. Breathe again. Spread your fingers. Look at them. These hands have done difficult things. They have also held a coffee cup on a quiet morning. Both versions of these hands belong to the same person. That person has worth in both moments. Breathe out. Let your hands rest open.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Walk to a window. Look out. Pick one object outside — a tree, a parked car, a streetlight. Focus on it. Breathe in. Your nervous system has spent years scanning for what is wrong. Scan for what is stable. The object is still there. You are still here. Breathe out. Your worth does not live in crisis. It does not require emergency conditions to activate. It exists in the ordinary moment you are standing in right now. Breathe in again. The firefighter who stands at this window, off-shift, unremarkable, tired — that person matters. Not because of the last run. Because they exist. Breathe out.

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Lie down if you are able. Flat on your back. Arms at your sides. Feel the surface beneath you. Breathe in through your nose. Let your belly rise. You have spent years sleeping light, alert to the next tone. Right now, no tone is coming. Breathe out fully. Let your legs go heavy. Your worth does not require you to be ready. It does not require vigilance. It does not require proof. Breathe in again. You are a person who has given a great deal. That giving came from something inside you that has value. Breathe out. Let the surface hold you. You do not have to earn the rest.

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Cup your hands around a warm mug or press them flat on a table. Feel the temperature or the texture. Stay there. Breathe in for four counts. Out for five. The EMS provider who ran three calls back to back and never debriefed any of them — that person still has worth. The dispatcher who heard the worst and stayed on the line — still has worth. You. Right now. Off the clock, out of the role, uncertain what to do with the quiet. Still have worth. Breathe again. Press your hands down. The table is solid. You are solid. Breathe out slow.
