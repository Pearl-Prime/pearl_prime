

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Your value becomes the badge. That's what nobody tells you when you start the job. You think the job is something you do. But it becomes something you are. Your identity gets fused with the role.
I spent fifteen years being a firefighter first. Everything else was secondary. My worth was measured by my performance on shift. My value was determined by how good I was at the job. Off shift, I didn't know how to be a person. I was just waiting for the next shift to start so I could have value again.
The mechanism is called role-dependent identity. It's what happens when you spend enough time in a role that it becomes fused with your sense of self. You stop being a person who does the job and become the job incarnate.
---

---

voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The mechanism is called status-based self-worth. Your sense of value is determined by your status in a hierarchy. On the job, you have a clear role. A clear value. A clear place.
Off the job, that hierarchy doesn't exist. And your nervous system doesn't know how to measure your worth anymore. The automatic response is to look for another hierarchy. To find another context where your value can be measured.
