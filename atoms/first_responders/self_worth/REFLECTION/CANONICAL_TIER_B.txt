

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Your value becomes the badge. The job tells you this without saying it. You think the job is something you do. It becomes something you are. Your identity gets fused with the role. I spent years being a firefighter first. Everything else was secondary. My worth was measured by my performance on shift. Off shift, I didn't know how to be a person.
The mechanism is called role-dependent identity. It's what happens when you spend enough time in a role that it becomes fused with your sense of self.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The protective mechanism tells you that your value is conditional. That it depends on performance. That if you're not on shift, you're not particularly valuable.
I watched someone good disappear into this. By the time they noticed, they'd completely lost themselves. The badge had become the only thing that made them real.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
You don't notice that your identity has become fused with the role until something happens. Until you leave. Until you retire. Until you realize you don't know who you are without the badge.
The invisibility is that it feels normal. Everyone at the station has the same thing. Everyone's worth is tied to performance.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The automatic response to role-dependent identity is to pour everything into the role. To make yourself indispensable. To prove your value through the work.
Off the job, you don't know how to measure your worth any other way. The automatic response is to look for another role. Another hierarchy.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I've been thinking about what happens when sense of self gets tied to a job. How it makes you vulnerable to that job. How it makes leaving impossible.
The protective mechanism is that you need the job to feel like you have value. Without the badge, you're nobody. So you stay.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The mechanism that ties your worth to the job is the job's way of keeping you committed. Of keeping you focused. Of making sure you show up even when it costs you.
If your sense of self is fused with the job, you won't leave. You can't leave. Leaving would mean disappearing.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I didn't realize my sense of self had been absorbed into the job until I was injured and off shift for months. Suddenly I had no value. No role. No way to measure my worth.
It was terrifying. I spent months not knowing who I was. The invisibility was that I'd been disappearing into the role so gradually.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The automatic response to having worth tied to a job is to stay in the job no matter the cost. Because leaving means losing your sense of self. Means becoming nobody.
The mechanism keeps you trapped.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The protective mechanism that ties your sense of worth to the badge is locking you into a cage. Is making sure that you can never step away. That you can never question. That you can never change.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
Your identity collapses into the role until there's nothing left underneath. Until you're just the badge. Until being off the job means being nobody.
The mechanism has succeeded in making the job irreplaceable. The cost is your self.
