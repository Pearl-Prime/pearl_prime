--- variant: v01
Your worth got wired to the badge somewhere between the academy and your third year on the job. On-duty, you matter. Off-duty, you're not sure. That's not self-worth. That's a transaction the profession installed, and the terms are terrible.

--- variant: v02
Old metric: you're worth what you can do on scene. New metric: you're worth something on the days you don't respond to a single call. On the days you sit on the couch. On the days you contribute nothing operational. The worth doesn't require a deployment.

--- variant: v03
You know the victims you serve have worth regardless of what happened to them. Apply that same standard to yourself. Your worth isn't conditional on your last save, your last commendation, or your last shift without error.
