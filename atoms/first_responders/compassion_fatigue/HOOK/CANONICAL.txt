

## HOOK v01
---

---
You remember when every call mattered. When you got home and thought about the person you helped. Now you get home and you don't think about anything. The numbness isn't peace. It's what happens when you've seen too much.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The job requires you to care. You've started pretending to care. You're still showing up, still doing the work, but something inside has shut down.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Your partner asks if you're okay. You say yes. You mean no. But no requires feeling something about the answer. You're too tired to feel.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You've answered calls that used to break you. Now they're just calls. Efficiency has replaced empathy. You're faster. You're colder. You're not sure which version of you is real anymore.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The fresh recruits still care. You watch them care and you remember what that was like. Then you remember why you stopped. Caring costs something. You're bankrupt.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The compartmentalization that kept you functional is now keeping you disconnected. You're good at the job. You're terrible at being human about it.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You've seen suffering. So much suffering. It's worn grooves in your capacity for pain. The grooves are getting deeper. The compassion is getting thinner.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
The people you help don't get better. Or they do but you don't know because you're gone. The lack of resolution has cost you the ability to stay invested.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
You're running on autopilot. The body knows what to do. The heart has stopped asking why.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Compassion fatigue feels like being broken without the drama of breaking. You're just... less.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
You can't cry about the calls anymore. You could if you tried. You don't try. You just move to the next call.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
The distance between you and the people you help has grown too wide. You keep them at arm's length because getting close costs too much.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
You're going through the motions. The work gets done. The people get helped. You're watching yourself do it like you're outside your own body.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The stories used to stick with you. Now they slide right through. You hear them and they disappear. Nothing sticks. Everything slides.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
Your crew doesn't talk about the hard stuff anymore. Everyone's learned to numb out together. The station is quiet in a way that isn't peaceful.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
The exhaustion isn't sleep-tiredness. It's soul-tiredness. You don't know how to rest from being tired of human suffering.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
Cynicism is cheaper than compassion. You've become cynical. It costs less.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You remember why you took the job. The reason doesn't feel real anymore. You're still here for a different reason now. Habit. Money. Obligation. Not purpose.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The good calls don't feel good anymore. The bad calls don't feel bad. Everything is flat. The flattening feels like sanity but it's erosion.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
You've learned to not care too much. Then you learned to not care at all. There's no middle ground where you live anymore.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
Your empathy is a limited resource and you've spent it. The overdraft notices are coming but you're not reading them.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The people at the scene need you to feel something. Anger. Urgency. Care. You're empty. You do the work from an empty place.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
The hardening that protected you is now suffocating you. The wall you built is keeping everything out, including yourself.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
You used to bring the work home and process it. Now you bring it home and ignore it. The ignoring is catching up to you.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
The job stripped something from you. You're not sure you can get it back. You're not sure you want to try.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
You're present for the people you help but absent for the people you love. The emotional energy went to the job. There's nothing left for home.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The numbness is protecting you. It's also destroying you. You know both things and you're letting it happen anyway.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You came into this job with a heart. Years later, you're heart-tired. The job has used up your softness and it's not coming back.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
People keep asking you to care about their emergency. You care. You just don't feel it anymore. The feeling burned out somewhere between the fiftieth and the five hundredth call.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
The work still needs doing. You're still doing it. But the connection between you and why you do it has been severed. You're operating on momentum alone.
---
