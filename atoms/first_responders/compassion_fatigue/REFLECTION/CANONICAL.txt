I remember the first call where I felt something break. It was a child. Car accident. And I remember feeling the weight of it. Feeling the impossibility of it. Feeling like the world had cracked and I could see something underneath that I couldn't unsee.
I thought that feeling was the job. Thought that was what it meant to care. To do this work. To be the person who shows up when things are worst.
Fourteen years later, I realized that feeling had changed. Transformed into something else. The weight was gone. The impossibility was just logistics. The crack in the world had sealed itself and I couldn't see anything underneath anymore.
That's the mechanism. That's compassion fatigue. It's the slow degradation of your capacity to feel other people's pain. It's the automatic response your nervous system develops to protect itself from unbearable empathy.

Your compassion is a resource. That's what people don't understand. It's not unlimited. It's not automatic. It's a capacity that gets depleted by use.
