

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Compassion is a resource and it gets depleted. That's the mechanism. You start the job feeling everything. Then gradually the feeling dials down. By year five, you're assessing scenes instead of grieving them. By year ten, you're so numb that bad calls feel like logistics problems.
The protective mechanism is your nervous system's way of saying: I can't keep absorbing this much suffering. So it creates distance. Turns down the empathy. Makes suffering into problem-solving. You look at human pain and feel nothing.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I watched someone I respect become mechanical. Still showed up. Still did good work. But the light had gone out. The empathy had been dialed so far down that they could witness suffering without flinching.
The protective mechanism had protected them by numbing them completely. Your nervous system is constantly outputting empathy, presence, care. It gets depleted. So the system protects itself by refusing to engage.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
You can't feel the numbness coming because it's so gradual. First calls don't touch you the way they used to. Then you notice you're thinking about logistics instead of the person. By the time you realize it, the invisibility is complete.
You're completely numb and you've convinced yourself it's professionalism. The mechanism is running so smoothly that nobody sees it. You look like someone who has it together.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The automatic mechanism that creates compassion fatigue is the nervous system's way of protecting itself from overload. Each call uses up empathy. Each scene depletes presence. After hundreds of calls, the system says: I have to dial this down or I will break.
So the dial goes down. The empathy becomes mechanical. Off the job, you're still numb. Still distant.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I've been thinking about what happens to your capacity for empathy when you absorb other people's suffering repeatedly. How the protective mechanism gradually replaces feeling with logic. How suffering becomes a problem instead of a tragedy.
The protection is elegant. It keeps you functional. The cost is that you become a hollow thing. You show up. You do the work. But there's nobody home anymore.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The numbness comes so gradually that you think you're just becoming more professional. More skilled. More able to handle the work. The invisibility is that your nervous system is progressively numbing you so that you can continue without breaking.
You're calling it professionalism. But what's happening is that the emotional response has been dialed so far down that human suffering has become statistics.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I remember the first person I couldn't save. Remember the devastation. The weight of failure. Then the next call came and I had to move on. The mechanism wouldn't let me stay with the grief.
Years later, that loss is just one of many. Just one voice in the accumulation. The protective numbing has made each death feel less real than it should.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The job requires you to be present to human suffering. To witness it. To touch it. But you can only witness so much before your nervous system says enough.
The protective mechanism pulls back. Creates distance. Dials down the empathy response. Not to be cruel. To protect you from breaking. Off the job, you're walking around with the dimmer switch still turned down.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
The automatic dimmer switch doesn't flip off. You just gradually dial it down. Call one, empathy is at ten. Call five hundred, empathy is at five. Call a thousand, empathy is at one.
Your nervous system is automatically regulating. Protecting. Creating distance so you don't break. Off the job, you're walking around completely numb.
---

---
