Jake hears a dispatch. His body doesn't activate anymore. The fatigue has emptied him. A false alarm or a real call—he moves the same way. His hands go through motions. His heart isn't available. He notices he doesn't care if it's real or false. The detachment is becoming visible to him. His colleague asks: you okay? Jake says: fine. But the fatigue is showing in his indifference.
