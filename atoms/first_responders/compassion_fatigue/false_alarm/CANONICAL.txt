Jackson hears a child laugh at the grocery store and his body activates. Not with joy. With dread. The laugh sounds like a child on a call — a call from three years ago. His chest locks. His hands go cold. The false alarm is his compassion fatigue cross-wiring normal sounds with clinical ones. The frame shifts in the cereal aisle: the child is shopping with a parent. Chen hears a sound at home — a crash from the garage. Her body launches into response mode. Heart rate doubled. Hands ready. Then she pauses. Holds the doorframe. Checks: is this a real emergency or is this my training? She walks to the garage at a normal pace. The cat knocked over a paint can. She picks up the can. Her heart rate is still elevated but her response was measured. The false alarm fired. She chose not to obey it. The choosing is the identity: someone who can feel the alarm and select the response.

Morales wakes up at 3 AM in his own bed, heart slamming, body ready for a call that is not coming. The false alarm is total. His nervous system is in the fire. He lies still. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v06

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Gutierrez maps the false alarm's conditioning. Every tone drop trained her nervous system to expect the worst. After hundreds of calls, the worst-case pathway is the strongest neural pathway. The pathway fires before thought engages. The mechanism is not fear — it is training. Her body was trained to activate on every stimulus. The training was effective. Too effective. It eliminated the delay between stimulus and response that would allow assessment. Her chest tightens. The false alarm is not a malfunction. It is an over-trained response. The muscle is too strong.

Patel sees the false alarm's deepest function: it has replaced intuition. He no longer has a gut feeling that distinguishes between real threats and false ones.
