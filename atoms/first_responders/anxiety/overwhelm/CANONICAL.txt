Marcus runs calls from 8am to 2am. Four bodies. Three overdoses. One elderly person who fell and couldn't get up. His chest is tight by shift end. His throat won't open. The overwhelm isn't about today. It's about today plus yesterday plus the last month. His hands shake on the steering wheel. He needs to go home. He can't drive.

Marcus stops trying to manage the overwhelm alone. He goes to the department counselor. He says: I'm full. There's no space. The counselor listens. Doesn't try to fix. Marcus realizes: the overwhelm is the point. He's not supposed to be fine with this much. His body is telling him the truth. The truth is: I'm running more calls than I have capacity for.
