## EXERCISE v02

---
Name five things you actually see. Three things you hear. Two things you feel. Breathe once after each. Ground in now.

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Cold water on your wrists. Hold for ten seconds. Feel the shock in your system. Your nervous system resets. Breathe.

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
You are off shift. The keys are in the bowl. Stand by the front door. Touch the locked deadbolt once. Out loud, say: "Locked. Door is locked." Touch the lit hallway light. Out loud, say: "Lit. Hallway is lit." Touch the chair you sit in to take your boots off. Out loud, say: "Home. I am home." Three points, three confirmations. Then take the boots off. The check is done. Do not check again until morning.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
The 0300 wake-up has arrived. Do not fight it. Sit up. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press the heels down hard. Hold for five counts. Release. Press again. Hold for five counts. Release. Press a third time. Hold ten counts. Release. The body just got the message that the floor is solid. Lie back down. Breathe in for four. Hold for four. Breathe out for six. Repeat until the body returns to sleep, or until 0330, whichever comes first. Either is fine.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
You are in the cab between calls. The last call was rough. The next one is unknown. Twenty seconds. Breathe in through the nose for a count of four. Hold the top for a count of two. Breathe out through pursed lips for a count of six. The pursed-lip exhale slows the heart. Three cycles. Hands on the wheel the whole time. Then put it in drive. The reset is done.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
You are in a public place and the scan started without you authorizing it. Do not fight the scan. Let it finish one lap of the room. Exits noted. Hands noted. Body posture noted. Now name three things in the room that are ordinary. The pepper shaker. The kid in the booth. The waiter's apron. Each one out loud or in your head. Each one is allowed to be just what it is. The scan was the training. The naming is yours. The naming is what brings the room back to the room.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
You are at the dinner table. The conversation is normal. Your jaw is locked. Without changing your face, do this: feel the back teeth. Let the back teeth separate by one millimeter. Let the tongue rest on the floor of the mouth. Take one slow breath in through the nose. Let the shoulders drop on the exhale. Nobody at the table will notice. The body will. Repeat once every ten minutes for the rest of the meal. You stay in the conversation. The jaw learns it is allowed to be off duty too.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Post-shift, in the parking lot, before you drive. Put both hands on the steering wheel. Squeeze. Hold for ten counts. Release. Notice the blood return to the fingers. Squeeze again. Hold for ten counts. Release. The body is dumping the shift through the hands. Now breathe in through the nose for four. Out through the mouth for eight. Five cycles. The cab is the airlock between the shift and the rest of the night. Do not skip the airlock. Then drive.
