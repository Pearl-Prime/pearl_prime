

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v01
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: false_alarm
weight: standard
carry_line: "The hypervigilance that kept you alive on shift will not turn off because the shift ended. The body does not read clock-out as safe."
---
Here is the mechanism. Your nervous system is older than the job. It was built to scan, to predict, to fire fast and ask questions never. The job loaded it. Twelve years on. Twenty years on. The system learned, in a way it cannot un-learn at will, that the next call could be the bad one. It learned that the kitchen fire at 0237 is real. It learned that the routine traffic stop is the one that goes sideways. It learned by doing, and the learning lives in tissue now, not in thought. So when you come home and lock the door and your hand reaches for the light switch again, the system is not malfunctioning. It is doing exactly what twenty years of input taught it to do. The locked door is, to the system, a kitchen that may still be smoldering somewhere you cannot see. What changes when you see this clearly is small but real. The door is still locked. The hand still reaches. But you start to recognize the reach as old training, not as evidence that something is wrong tonight. The vigilance becomes information about your nervous system, not information about your house. That gap is where the rest of your life gets built.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v02
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: shift_rotation
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your circadian system never agreed to this schedule. The anxiety at 0300 is partly the job and partly a body fighting a clock it cannot win against."
---
Here is the mechanism. Your body has an internal clock. It runs on a roughly twenty-four-hour cycle, anchored to light, to meal timing, to the rhythm of sleep and wake. It was built over a long evolutionary timeline that did not anticipate twelve-hour rotating shifts, twenty-four-on, forty-eight-off, the swing from days to nights and back again every week. The clock does not adapt the way the schedule asks it to. It cannot. So you eat at three in the morning and your gut is confused. You try to sleep at noon and your cortisol is wrong. The anxiety that wakes you on the third day of a swing, the one that arrives at 0300 with no specific content, is not weakness. It is a body that was running on the wrong fuel for a week, trying to sleep against a chemistry that is telling it to be awake. What changes when you see this is that the anxiety is still there. The 0300 wake is still there. But you stop interpreting it as a sign that something is wrong with you, and start recognizing it as the predictable cost of a job that asks the body to do something it was never built to do. That recognition does not fix the schedule. It does separate the anxiety from the self.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v03
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: cumulative_load
weight: standard
carry_line: "Each call leaves a deposit. Twenty years of deposits is not a stack of memories. It is a tuning of the nervous system that does not require recall to keep firing."
---
Here is the mechanism. People outside the job ask whether you can remember the calls. They are asking about narrative memory — the kind that has a story, a date, a face you can describe at dinner. That is one kind of memory. There is another kind, older and lower, that does not bother with narrative. It tunes the system. Each call deposits something — not a story but a calibration. The body learns what a stairwell with a wet floor smells like at 0300. It learns what the radio sounds like before bad news. It learns the shape of a particular kind of silence on scene. After twenty years, you do not have to remember any of it for the calibration to keep firing. The system hears a similar shape — a radio chirp, a smell, a silence — and responds before thought arrives. That is not a flaw. That is what the body was built to do with repeated input. What changes when you see this is that the response is no less real, but it stops feeling like you are losing your mind. It is the deposit doing what deposits do. Naming the calibration as calibration does not erase it. It does keep you from interpreting it as evidence that something is wrong with you, today, this minute, in a kitchen that is fine.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v04
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: threat_scanning
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your eyes still sweep the room. The sweep was trained on scenes. It does not know how to stop at a restaurant."
---
Here is the mechanism. The eye sweep is a trained behavior. It started as a tactical scan — exits, hands, raised voices, body posture, who is behind you, who is between you and the door. It saved lives. It probably saved yours, more than once. The training was deep, repeated, reinforced. Tens of thousands of hours of doing the sweep in environments that required it. The system did not learn to discriminate between a stairwell on a domestic and a restaurant booth on a Tuesday. It learned the behavior. It learned that the cost of not sweeping was higher than the cost of sweeping. Now you are at a birthday dinner and your eyes are still sweeping. You are tracking exits. You are reading hands. The training does not care that the room is friendly. It is running the only program it knows. What changes when you see this is that the sweep keeps happening. The exits keep getting mapped. But you stop reading the sweep as a verdict on the room — that something here is dangerous — and start reading it as a trained behavior firing in a context that does not require it. The sweep is information about your training history. It is not information about the birthday dinner.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v05
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: station_silence
weight: standard
carry_line: "The culture taught you not to bring it home and not to bring it up. What does not get processed in the open processes in the dark at 0300."
---
Here is the mechanism. The station culture has rules that were built for good reasons in conditions that required them. You do not bring it home. You do not unload on your partner. You do not name the bad call by name at the dinner table. The crew handles it the crew way — the dark joke, the quick story, the move on. The rules kept the next call functional. They also taught the nervous system that the bad input goes inward, not outward. Twenty years of inward is not zero. The system does not delete what it has no place to put. It stores it. It pressurizes. It looks for the seams. The 0300 wake-up where nothing in particular is wrong, the chest tightness on the way to a routine call, the irritation with a partner who did nothing — these are not random. They are pressure finding a seam. What changes when you see this is that the culture rules stay (they have their reasons), but you start to see the storage as a real thing happening in real tissue, requiring a real outlet. Naming the pressure is not the same as breaking the rule. It is just acknowledging the cost of the rule, in a place that is yours.
