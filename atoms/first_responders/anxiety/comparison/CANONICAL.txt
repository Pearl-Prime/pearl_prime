Kyle watches Dex in the station. Same call that just came in. Kyle's heart is elevated. His hands feel shaky. Dex is reading. Casually reading. Like the call was nothing. Kyle thinks: he's been doing this longer. He's fine with it. So I should be fine with it. But Kyle's not fine. His stomach is tight. His throat is closing. He's comparing himself to Dex and losing. The comparison tells Kyle something must be wrong with him. Everyone else is handling the thing that's destroying his nervous system.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/anxiety/comparison/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kyle talks to Dex. Not about anxiety. Just: how do you stay so calm? Dex looks at him. He says: I'm not calm. I just don't show it. I go home and I'm a wreck. I have a partner who helps me process. I've been doing this for fifteen years. I earned the ability to seem calm. Kyle realizes: Dex is not actually unbothered. He's just older. The comparison collapses. What Kyle thought was effortless performance is actually practiced presence. That changes things. Kyle's anxiety hasn't changed. But the comparison has lifted because he knows Dex's baseline too.

COST_TYPE: internal

Sofia asks the other firefighter directly: are you actually this calm or are you pretending? The firefighter laughs. She says: pretending. This job makes me anxious as hell.
