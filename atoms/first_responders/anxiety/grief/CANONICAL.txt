Dex has transported seventeen bodies this month. He doesn't think about that number. But his body knows. His chest is tight even on slow shifts. His hands shake slightly. It's not about any individual body anymore. It's the accumulation. The grief has nowhere to go. There's no ceremony. The next call comes. The next body. The grief stacks without processing. He carries the weight without naming it. His anxiety is because the grief is pressing against him with no release valve.
