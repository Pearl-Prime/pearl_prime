You can run into a burning building but you can't sit still for five minutes. That's not weakness. That's a nervous system that forgot how to stand down.
I spent eight years on the job before I understood that. Thought I was just wired different. Thought the guys who went home and stayed home were soft or weren't built for the work. Now I know better. That's the mechanism. Your autonomic nervous system—the part that doesn't ask permission—gets recalibrated by repetition and stakes. You condition it without knowing you're doing it. Every call, every scene, every moment when you had to go from zero to a hundred, your body took note. It built its own memory. It built its own logic.
And that logic says: stay vigilant. Never completely relax. The moment you drop your guard is the moment the next call comes in.

There's something called the sensitization effect. After repeated exposure to threat or stress, your nervous system becomes more sensitive to stimuli that remind it of that threat. The threshold gets lower. It takes less to trigger a full response.
