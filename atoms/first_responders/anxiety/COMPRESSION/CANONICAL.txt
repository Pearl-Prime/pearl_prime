## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Stop moving. Plant both feet flat on the floor.

Press your heels down. Feel the ground push back.

Your nervous system reads that pressure as safe ground.

Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six.

The longer exhale activates your brake system. Do it twice more.

Now name three things your hands can touch right now.

Touch them. One at a time. Stay with each for three seconds.

You just interrupted the threat loop. That is enough.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Your jaw is clenched. Unclench it now.

Let your tongue drop from the roof of your mouth.

These are where the body stores the shift.

Inhale through your nose for four counts. Slow.

Exhale through your mouth like you are fogging glass. Six counts.

Do that three times. Count every single count.

Now roll your shoulders back once. Let them fall heavy.

Heavy is the goal. Heavy means the armor came off.

Stay here for thirty more seconds. You earned it.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

You just came off a hard call. The body does not know it is over.

Put one hand on your sternum. Press lightly. Feel it rise and fall.

That rhythm is yours. It did not stop.

Breathe in for five counts. Breathe out for five counts.

Match the rhythm. Repeat four times.

Now say your name out loud. Quietly is fine.

Hear it land. That is you, here, now. Not there.

The call is filed. This moment is separate.

Let your hands go slack. Both of them. Now.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Scan your body from the top of your skull downward.

Notice where you are braced. Shoulders. Gut. Thighs.

Pick the tightest spot. Breathe directly into it.

Inhale for four. Imagine the breath reaching that exact place.

Exhale for six. Let that spot soften one degree.

One degree is enough. Do not force it further.

Repeat twice more with the same spot.

Now open your hands wide. Spread every finger.

Hold that for five seconds. Then release.

Open hands signal safety to a closed system.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Sit back in your seat. All the way back.

Let the chair hold you. Stop holding yourself up.

Feel the difference. That is your body releasing one layer.

Breathe in for four counts through your nose.

Out for eight counts through your mouth. Slow and steady.

The eight-count exhale matters. Do not shorten it.

Do this four times. Count out loud if the room allows.

After the last exhale, close your eyes for ten seconds.

Just ten. Notice what the dark feels like without a threat in it.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Both feet on the floor. Right now.

Press them down like you are rooting into concrete.

Look at one fixed point in the room. Hold it.

Breathe in for four. Do not move your eyes.

Breathe out for six. Keep the point locked.

Your visual system and your breath are working together now.

Do three more cycles. Same point. Same counts.

Now blink slowly. Let your gaze soften.

The threat is not in this room. Your body is learning that.

Give it thirty seconds to catch up.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Cold water. Get some if you can.

Run it over your wrists for fifteen seconds.

Cold interrupts the adrenaline cycle at the skin level.

If no water is available, press your wrists together hard instead.

Now breathe. In for four. Out for four. No holds.

Equal counts calm a system that is oscillating too fast.

Do six cycles. Every count matters.

After the last exhale, shake your hands loose at the wrists.

Shake them like you are flicking water off.

That releases the grip the body stored without your permission.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

You are still in response mode. The call ended. Your system did not.

Stand up if you are sitting. Plant your feet wide.

Wider than your hips. Solid base.

Breathe in through your nose for five counts.

At the top, hold for two counts. Then out for seven.

That ratio tells your vagus nerve to slow the engine.

Repeat three times. Do not rush the exhale.

Now drop your chin to your chest. Hold for five seconds.

Lift it slowly. Look straight ahead.

You are back in your own body. Stay here.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Notice the sound closest to you right now. Name it.

Now the sound farthest away. Name that one too.

You just used your auditory system to anchor to the present.

Breathe in for four counts. Let the sounds continue around you.

Breathe out for six. Do not silence anything.

The sounds are evidence that you are here, not there.

Do three more breath cycles. Keep listening.

Now press your back teeth together lightly. Then release.

Feel the jaw soften. That softness is available to you.

It does not cost anything. Use it.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Right hand on your left forearm. Press firmly.

Feel the warmth of your own grip. Stay with it.

This is called self-contact. It activates a calming signal.

Breathe in for four counts while you hold the grip.

Breathe out for six. Do not let go yet.

Repeat the breath twice more. Same grip. Same counts.

Now slowly release. Notice the warmth your hand left behind.

That warmth is real. Your nervous system registered it.

You do not need to explain this to anyone.

Do it again whenever the pressure builds.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Stop talking. Stop scrolling. Stop explaining the shift to anyone.

Thirty seconds. Just yours.

Press your back against a wall. Feel the full surface.

Skull, shoulders, lower back, heels. All of it touching.

Breathe in for four counts. Let the wall hold the weight.

Breathe out for six. Push back slightly with your shoulders.

The resistance is grounding. Push and breathe twice more.

Now close your eyes for ten seconds.

Behind your eyelids, there is no call, no radio, no report.

Just ten seconds. Take them.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Gravity is available to you right now.

Sit down. Let your weight drop into the seat completely.

Stop managing how you sit. Let it be heavy.

Breathe in for four counts. Feel the seat beneath you.

Breathe out for six. Sink one degree further.

Do not brace. Do not straighten. Just land.

Three more cycles. Each exhale, give the seat more weight.

After the last breath, place both palms flat on your thighs.

Press down gently. Feel the warmth of your own hands.

You are not on the scene. You are here.
---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Your eyes have been scanning for threats. Give them a rest.

Find a soft surface in the room. Fabric, carpet, anything muted.

Let your gaze rest there. Soft focus. Not searching.

Breathe in for four counts while you hold the soft gaze.

Breathe out for six. Do not sharpen your eyes.

Soft eyes tell the brainstem the perimeter is clear.

Do four cycles. Keep the gaze loose the entire time.

Now blink three times slowly. Let your eyes close on the third.

Hold the dark for five seconds.

Open slowly. Stay soft. The scan can wait.
