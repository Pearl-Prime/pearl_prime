## PERMISSION_GRANT v01
---

---
The job comes with rules. Some written. Most not. Do not bring it home. Do not let them see you shaken. Earn the rest by surviving the shift first. These rules kept the next call functional. They were not designed to keep you whole.

You are allowed to be off-duty in your own house. The vigilance does not have to follow you in. The body that swept the stairwell can set the sweep down at the door. Not perfectly. Not always. But the permission to set it down is yours.

You are allowed to need recovery time the same way the engine needs maintenance. Twelve hours on, then more hours on, then a debrief, then home — the body has not finished. It does not finish on cue. Recovery is not a luxury. It is part of the work cycle. Lie down without earning it again.

You are allowed to feel something about the bad call. The crew jokes have their place. They are not the only language. The thing inside that the joke covered is also real. You are allowed to name it to yourself, even if you never name it out loud, even if you only ever write it down once and burn the paper.

You are allowed to want a normal nervous system in a room full of civilians. The job trained you out of one. The training cost you something. Wanting it back is not weakness. It is the body asking for what it was designed to have before the work loaded it.

You are allowed to not be the calm one tonight. Someone else can hold the line for an hour. The crew has carried you in other ways. Letting them carry you here too is not a failure. It is the trade you have already been making, in the other direction, for twenty years.

You are allowed to outlive the job. Many do not. The statistics on this are not a secret. The choice to stay alive past the badge is a real choice and you are allowed to make it. The body deserves more than the post-retirement collapse the previous generation accepted as the cost of the work.

You are allowed to be the consciousness watching the alarm, not the alarm itself. The system fires. The chest tightens. The eyes sweep. And underneath all of it, awareness keeps watching. That awareness is older than the job. The job did not touch it. It is still yours.

These are not affirmations. They are accurate descriptions of the rights a human body comes with, after the badge and during the badge and after the badge is set down. Read them again. Read them slow. Let the body hear them, one permission at a time.

## PERMISSION_GRANT v02
---

---
Anxiety arrives wearing the job's clothes. It does not announce itself as anxiety. It announces itself as scanning, as planning, as the fifth time checking the side door. Set the disguise down for a minute. The thing underneath has a name.

You are allowed to call it what it is. Not stress. Not just tired. Not a bad day off. Anxiety is the word. Using the word does not change your status on the job. It just stops you from negotiating with a thing whose name you refuse to say.

You are allowed to ask for the help you give other people every shift. You wrap the panicking civilian in a blanket. You sit with the one who lost the kid. You drive the one who cannot drive themselves. The asking goes the other direction too. Letting it go the other direction is not a failure of the role. It is what the role would tell anyone else to do.

You are allowed to take a day. Not earned. Not justified. A day. The earth does not stop because you took a day. The crew does not collapse. The shift moves. The day is yours and you do not need a written reason to take it.

You are allowed to do the breath work without anyone seeing. In the cab. In the locker room. On the toilet at the firehouse if that is what is private. The breath does not require a yoga mat. The yoga mat is not the point. The breath is the point and it is available in any chair you can find.

You are allowed to outgrow the lonely-warrior story. The body was built to settle in the presence of other steady bodies. The crew is a kind of company. Your partner at home is another kind. A therapist is another kind. None of these are weak. They are all places the nervous system was designed to settle, and the system has not changed since they invented it.

You are allowed to feel the thing without acting on it. The chest tightens. The plan starts running. You do not have to follow the plan. You can notice it, name it, and stay in the chair. The feeling does not become a verdict because you felt it. It becomes a verdict only if you act on it as if it were one.

These are not affirmations. They are the standing permissions every body comes with. Read them slow. The body knows what to do with them, even if the mind takes longer to agree.

## PERMISSION_GRANT v03
---

---
There is a version of yourself that the job built. Tactical. Decisive. Sweeping the room before sitting down. That version is real and it kept people alive. It is also not the only version. There is room for another one underneath it, and that one needs permission to exist.

You are allowed to be soft inside the hard shell. The shell stays. It has to. You did not get to the third decade without the shell. But the inside of the shell does not have to match the outside. Soft tissue lives there. It is allowed to be soft.

You are allowed to not be on. The radio is in the locker. The shift is over. The eyes can stop sweeping for an hour. They will sweep again tomorrow. Tomorrow does not need tonight's vigilance to function. Tonight can be off.

You are allowed to grieve a call from ten years ago. The system does not file by date. The grief shows up when the grief shows up. It does not need a recent prompt. The 2014 stairwell can wake you up in 2026 and the grief is still valid. The timeline of grief is not the timeline of the calendar.

You are allowed to like things that have nothing to do with the job. Gardening. A bad movie. A boring podcast. A walk that goes nowhere. The personality the job built is not the only personality you have. There are other ones, older and quieter, still in there.

You are allowed to be done with overtime. The money is real. The cost is also real. The cost shows up in the body, in the marriage, in the sleep, in the things you cannot quite name at the table. Choosing the cost is a real choice. Choosing against it is also a real choice. Either one is yours to make.

You are allowed to not have the answer for the rookie. The rookie wants the answer because the rookie is twenty-four and the world has not loaded them yet. You do not have to pretend. Saying "I don't know yet, ask me in five years" is a real answer. It is the more honest answer. The rookie will be okay.

These are not affirmations. They are descriptions of the rights you came with, before the job, during the job, and after. Read them once a week. Let the body work them in slow.
