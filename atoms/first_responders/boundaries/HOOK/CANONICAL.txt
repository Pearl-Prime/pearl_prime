

## HOOK v01
---

---
You're the one who never says no. The extra shifts get filled by your name. The mutual aid requests come to you first. You can run into burning buildings but you can't set limits on your own availability.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your phone is always on. Always. In the shower. At dinner. At midnight. It's not a phone anymore. It's a leash you volunteered for.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Everyone at the station knows they can ask you. They've learned. You'll cover the shift they need covered. You'll take the call they don't want. You'll be there.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The cost of being needed is that you're never not available. You're never actually off-duty. Even when you're home, you're on call.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You've missed family time for shifts that technically someone else could have taken. But they didn't. They came to you because you never say no.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The other responders lean on you. That's earned their trust. It's also cost you time with people who lean on you in different ways.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You volunteer for every extra shift because the money helps and because saying yes feels stronger than saying no.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Your partner is used to you canceling plans. They've learned to book things you might not be there for. They've learned to assume you'll be working.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The captain knows you'll pick up whatever needs picking up. The chief knows. The whole station knows. You've become the backup plan for everyone else's schedule.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
You're the problem-solver. Someone needs coverage, they call you. Something needs doing, they ask you. You've never learned to say it's not your responsibility.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
Standing down means someone else has to stand up. You can't let that happen. So you stand. You keep standing.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
Your boundaries got thin somewhere between the job and home. Now you don't know where the work ends and where you begin.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Being the strong one means being available. Being available means you've given up the right to be unavailable. That's the deal you made without saying it out loud.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The dispatch came in at two AM. You were awake anyway. You're always awake now because there might be a call.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
Your family scheduled around your shifts and then they scheduled around your extra shifts and then they stopped scheduling because they couldn't predict you anymore.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
Everyone depends on you. That dependency feels like strength until the moment you realize you're dependent on being depended on.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
The station is your home. So staying late at the station is staying home. That's how you justify the hours. That's how you justify not leaving.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You can't rest because resting means letting someone down. Someone might need something and you're not there.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The line between duty and availability blurred somewhere around year three. You don't remember when you stopped seeing a difference between them.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
Your days off are scheduled but not guaranteed. There's always something that could pull you in. And you always answer.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You're the one who shows up. You've made yourself into the one who always shows up. Now you can't not show up.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The job demanded sacrifice. You sacrificed. Then the job kept demanding and you kept sacrificing and somewhere along the way you forgot that sacrifice has limits.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
Your phone goes off and you answer. Every time. The Pavlovian response is complete. Sound equals response. There is no choice anymore.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
Being indispensable looks like strength. It feels like pressure. It's both.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You've taken shifts that made your own health worse because your crew needed them taken. The crew is family. Family comes first. Even when it costs you.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
There's no such thing as a real day off. There's the day you're not officially on shift and the day you're not pulling your weight. You've learned the difference.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The guilt of saying no is bigger than the exhaustion of saying yes. So you keep saying yes.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You're the reliable one. Reliability is just another word for someone who's given up boundaries.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The job needs you. Your family needs you. Your friends need you. You're not enough for all of it. But you're trying.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
You can't turn off being a first responder. So you never turn off. You're on-duty at home, on-duty with your family, on-duty in your own head.
---
