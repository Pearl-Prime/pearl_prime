## COMPRESSION v01
---

---

Plant both feet flat on the floor. Press down hard. Feel the floor push back. That resistance is real. You are not in the last call. You are here. Name three things you can touch right now. Say them out loud. Your nervous system needs sound, not silence. Breathe out first — a long, slow exhale. Then let the inhale come on its own. Do this twice. The shift does not need to be large. Small is enough.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---

Unclench your jaw. Do it now. Notice how long it had been locked. Roll your shoulders back once, slowly. Let them drop. You carried that tension through the whole shift. It does not need to travel home with you. Press your palms together. Hold for five seconds. Release. That deliberate pressure tells your body: the emergency is over. Breathe normally. Stay with that for thirty seconds before you move.

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---

You just crossed the threshold. Work stays behind that door. Say it plainly: "I am off duty." Not to anyone else. To your own chest. Feel the words land. Change one physical thing — shoes off, jacket hung, water poured. The ritual matters. Your brain needs a signal, not a lecture. Drink the water slowly. Taste it. Cold or warm, it does not matter. This moment belongs to you alone.

---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---

Sit down. Not on the edge. All the way back. Notice the chair holding your full weight. You do not have to hold yourself up right now. Scan from your feet to your skull. Find the tightest place. Breathe directly into it — not around it. Hold three seconds. Let go. That spot is where today stored itself. Releasing it is not weakness. It is maintenance. Do the scan one more time.

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---

Before you walk into your house, stop. Stand outside for sixty seconds. Look up. Sky, ceiling, whatever is above you. Wide vision. Not tactical. Let your eyes go soft. Hypervigilance narrows the field. This deliberately widens it. Notice the edges of your vision without chasing them. Your body will begin to downshift. Sixty seconds. Then go inside. The people in there need the version of you that just breathed.

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---

Put your hand on your sternum. Press lightly. Feel your heartbeat under your palm. Count five beats. Just count. Nothing else. This is not meditation. This is a reset. Your heart has been running fast. Acknowledge that. Five beats, counted. Now breathe out through your mouth — long, audible, done. One more time. Hand still on chest. The contact matters. Skin on bone. You are locating yourself in your own body.

## COMPRESSION v07
---

---

Name the last call out loud. One sentence only. Not the details. Just: what it was, where, done. Speaking it closes the loop your brain keeps reopening. Now say: "That is logged." Use those exact words. Your mind responds to finality. It needs to hear the case is closed. Drop your hands to your sides. Open your palms facing forward. That posture signals safety to your autonomic system. Hold it for ten seconds.

---

## COMPRESSION v08
---

---

Drink something cold. Hold the glass with both hands. Feel the temperature move through your palms. Swallow slowly. Track the cold traveling down. This is sensory interruption. It pulls attention out of the replay loop and into the present body. Set the glass down deliberately. Hear it contact the surface. That sound is now. You are not in the call. You are in a kitchen, a break room, a car. Here. Now.

---

## COMPRESSION v09
---

---

Tighten every muscle from your feet to your thighs. Hold for four counts. Release all at once. Feel the difference between contracted and loose. Your body forgot what loose felt like. Do the same from your hands to your shoulders. Four counts. Release. Notice what drops. This is progressive discharge — not relaxation, discharge. Shift is over. The body needs to physically complete what adrenaline started. Two rounds. That is enough.

## COMPRESSION v10
---

---

Step outside. Find one sound that has nothing to do with work. Wind. A dog. Traffic that is not a call. Listen to it for thirty seconds without analyzing it. No threat assessment. Just sound. Let it be neutral. Your threat-detection system runs constantly. This interrupts it on purpose. Thirty seconds of non-tactical listening recalibrates the baseline. Go back inside when the thirty seconds are done. Not before.

---

## COMPRESSION v11
---

---

Splash cold water on your face. Do not rush it. Stand at the sink for a full minute. Feel the water temperature. Feel the towel. Rough or soft — notice which. Your senses have been locked onto threat data for hours. Redirect them to texture, temperature, ordinary sensation. This is not about calming down. It is about switching channels. The emergency channel has been on all day. Switch it. You know how.

---

## COMPRESSION v12
---

---

Write one sentence. Paper, phone, whatever is close. Write what you are carrying right now. Not a report. One honest sentence. "I am tired and the last call is still with me." Something like that. Read it back once. You named it. Named things lose some of their weight. Fold the paper or delete the note. The act of naming and releasing is the whole exercise. Thirty seconds total. Do it now.

---

## COMPRESSION v13
---

---

Stand at a window. Look at the farthest point you can see. Hold your gaze there for twenty seconds. Distant focus releases the close-range muscle tension behind your eyes. You have been scanning near-field threat space for hours. This is the physical opposite. Twenty seconds. Then let your gaze go soft — not focused anywhere. Just open. Feel your forehead unclench. That is the reset point. The rest of the evening starts from here.
