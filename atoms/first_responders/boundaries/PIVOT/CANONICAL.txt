--- variant: v01
The job taught you to be available. Always on, always ready, first on scene. The problem is that skill followed you home. You're always on for everyone — the crew, the family, the neighbor who knows you're an EMT. Boundaries aren't a dereliction of duty. They're the difference between a career and a crash.

--- variant: v02
Old frame: the good responder gives everything. New frame: the good responder knows the difference between a call they should take and a call they're taking because saying no feels like abandoning post. Off-duty is supposed to mean off-duty.

--- variant: v03
You can set a perimeter at a scene without hesitation. Setting a perimeter around your own time, energy, and emotional capacity? That's the scene you were never trained for. And it's the one that's costing you the most.
