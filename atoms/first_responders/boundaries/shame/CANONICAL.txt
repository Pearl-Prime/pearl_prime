## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Chen says no to a call-back request and immediately feels the shame flood. Real firefighters do not say no. Real firefighters show up. His face burns in the empty kitchen. The shame says the no makes him weak. His chest tightens. The frame shifts when he notices the shame only fires on no. Every yes — even the ones that damaged his health, his marriage, his sleep — produced no shame. The shame is directional. It punishes boundaries and rewards self-destruction. His jaw unclenches. He keeps the no. The shame can burn. The boundary holds.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Morales leaves a shift on time for the first time in four years. She walks past the crew still working. Her face burns. The shame is a furnace. Leaving on time feels like abandonment. Her chest constricts. She gets in her car. Drives home. Walks through her front door while there is still daylight. Her daughter looks up from the couch and says: Mom, you're home early. The shame is still burning. But her daughter's face is sunlit. The frame shatters: the shame punishes presence at home. Her daughter rewards it. She sits on the couch. The shame and the daughter occupy the same moment. She chooses who to listen to.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Patel tells his crew he needs a minute after a bad call. In front of everyone. His face warms. The old shame says: weakness. Patel walks to the bay door. Breathes for sixty seconds. Returns. The crew does not look at him differently. One of them nods. The minute was not weakness. It was modeling. Patel took the minute he needed and the crew saw that taking a minute is allowed. The shame wanted him to perform invulnerability. He performed honesty. The honesty is the identity now.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
O'Brien tells her crew she is going to therapy. In the kitchen. At breakfast. Out loud. Her face is warm. The shame is present — a first responder admitting she needs help. She says it clearly and does not soften it with a joke. The crew is quiet for three seconds. Then one of them says: who is your therapist? I need one too. The shame expected isolation. It got company. The announcement is not easy. But it is honest. The honest is the identity now.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Vasquez identifies the shame's enforcement mechanism in first responder culture. The culture permits physical injury but not emotional injury. A broken bone gets sympathy and sick leave. A broken spirit gets silence and suspicion. The shame is not personal. It is institutional. The mechanism: the culture rewards stoicism and punishes vulnerability. Vasquez has been following a script written by the institution, not by her own values. Her face warms as the mechanism becomes visible. The shame is a cultural product, not a personal failing.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/first_responders/anchored/boundaries/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kim sees the shame's generational transmission. His captain learned it from his captain. The silence, the stoicism, the punishment of vulnerability — it passes through the ranks like an inherited disease. The mechanism: shame is not felt. It is taught. It is modeled. The new recruit learns it from the veteran's silence. The veteran learned it from the captain's disapproval. The captain learned it from the culture's mythology of the unbreakable hero. Kim's face burns. He is not carrying his own shame. He is carrying decades of institutional shame that was handed down like equipment.
---
