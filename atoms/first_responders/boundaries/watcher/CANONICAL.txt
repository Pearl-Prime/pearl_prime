O'Brien sees the watcher's deepest paradox: it makes him less safe. The constant scanning depletes the attention needed for actual threat response. The mechanism: the watcher is a net negative. It consumes more safety than it produces. The vigilance is not protective. It is expensive. It costs more than the threats it theoretically prevents.
