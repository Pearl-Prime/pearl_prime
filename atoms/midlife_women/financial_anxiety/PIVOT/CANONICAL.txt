## PIVOT v01
---

---
The retirement gap is real, and the anxiety about it is real, and the anxiety is also running scenarios that treat uncertainty as certainty — projecting worst cases as if they are locked in. The gap exists in the present. The catastrophe lives only in the future-running brain. They require different responses.

## PIVOT v02
---

---
She deferred financial independence because the system was arranged to make that seem like the reasonable choice, and because she was doing the invisible labor that made everyone else's financial advancement possible. The gap in her retirement savings is not evidence of failure. It is evidence of how the math worked when she was working on everyone else's behalf.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
She cannot fix fifteen years of financial decisions in a single quarter. She can take one clear-eyed look at what is actually in front of her — not the catastrophized version, the actual version — and make one decision from that. The anxiety wants to plan everything at once. The path forward requires only the next step.
