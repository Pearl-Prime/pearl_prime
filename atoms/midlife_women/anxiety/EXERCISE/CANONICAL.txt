

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit down. Place both feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. The scan of everyone's needs can wait six seconds. Repeat five times.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Put your phone face down. Set it down fully, not just turned over. Breathe in for four counts. Out for six. Your mother's next call is not owed an instant answer. Repeat six times.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Press your palm flat against your sternum. Feel your own heartbeat under your hand. Count ten beats. This heart has been racing for other people's emergencies all week. Let it just beat for you now.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Name three sounds in the room. Name two things you can touch. Name one thing you can smell. This pulls you out of the loop of what could go wrong with your kids, your parents, your job.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Unclench your hands. Spread your fingers wide. Hold for four counts. Release. Your hands have been braced for bad news since your mother's diagnosis. Let them open instead.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Stand in a doorway. Press both palms into the frame. Push for five counts. Release. The dread in your chest is not today's emergency. It is the accumulated weight of monitoring everyone at once.
---
