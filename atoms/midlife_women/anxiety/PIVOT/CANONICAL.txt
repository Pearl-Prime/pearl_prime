## PIVOT v01
---

---
Her nervous system learned vigilance when it was necessary, when children were small and helpless and someone had to stay alert. The children are grown now, but the body never received that memo. What fires in her at 3am is not a warning — it is old software running on new hardware.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The anxiety is not evidence that something is about to break. It is evidence that she has been the load-bearing wall for a very long time and her body knows how much is resting on her. That is not a crisis. That is information.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Perimenopause changes the cortisol curve — she is not imagining it, and she is not losing her mind. The body that protected her family for twenty years is recalibrating. The hypervigilance that looks like anxiety is the body asking, finally, to be tended.
---
