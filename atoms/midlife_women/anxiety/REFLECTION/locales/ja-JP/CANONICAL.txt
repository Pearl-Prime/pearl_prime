## REFLECTION v01
---

voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic

What I notice is that the anxiety no longer requires a reason. It used to. Twenty years ago there was always a specific trigger I could identify: the difficult conversation ahead, the deadline, the uncertainty about a particular outcome. I could name the source and, in naming it, locate it outside myself. Now the mechanism runs independent of event. I wake at 3am and the cortisol is already there, sharp and purposeful, organizing my body for a threat that isn't in the room. My children are asleep down the hall. My husband is beside me. Nothing has happened. And yet the system is fully activated, running its scan, searching for the reason it knows must exist.

What I have come to understand is that perimenopause is not the source of the anxiety. It is the amplifier of an anxiety that was always running beneath the surface, at a level I could manage, a level that looked like conscientiousness and productivity and high standards. The hormonal fluctuations dropped the suppression threshold. The mechanism that was always there became louder. The anxiety that I spent decades channeling into achievement is now audible as itself.

The automatic quality is what I am examining. The nervous system does not ask permission. It does not wait for my rational assessment of whether the threat is real. It fires, and the firing has a physical signature: the chest tightening, the shallow breath, the particular alertness behind the eyes that means sleep is over. By the time I notice the anxiety, it has already organized my body's response. I am already in the preparatory state for something that isn't coming.

What I am learning is that this is not a disorder of perception. The mechanism is operating exactly as it was designed. A nervous system that learned, somewhere early on, that vigilance was the appropriate baseline — that rest was a vulnerability and preparation was the only safety — does not easily surrender that learning. Perimenopause stripped away the hormonal buffer that had been muffling the alarm. What remains is the mechanism itself, visible now in a way it was not before, asking to finally be seen.

## 反省 v02

その代償は睡眠の年月です。緊急時の急性不眠症ではなく、夜間を昼間の警戒心の延長線上と捉えて蓄積された睡眠不足です。私は何かが悪いから目覚めているわけではありません。私の体は暗闇の安全を完全には信じていません。睡眠中もスキャンは続きます。夢は管理すべき問題や話題、忘れてしまったことを扱う行政的な質を持っています。至る所で休息すら労働の一形態となっています。

またその代償は自分の体との関係でもあります。不安が身体的に現れ始めた私の後半の三十代で、私は体を信頼しなくなりました。体の症状を不安によるものと解釈し続けたからです。胸の張りは軽視しました。心拍の乱れはストレスの一環として扱いました。顎の緊張は歯科医がぎごくについて尋ねるまで気づきませんでした。私は体からの信号を無視することで、すべてを動かし続けることができました。

失ったのは単純な動物的な知識でした。恐怖を感じているときと安全であるときを区別する能力です。そのメカニズムは長年自動的に運転されており、簡単に違いを感じることができませんでした。常に一定程度の警戒心がありました。それが普通に感じられました。稀に訪れる警戒心の欠如は異様でさえ、まるで安らぎをまだ獲得していないかのように感じられたのです。不安の代償はただの無駄な夜や緊張した肩だけでなく、長い間自分の内面状態から切り離され続けたこと、曖昧になった経験を通じて機能し続けることでした。
