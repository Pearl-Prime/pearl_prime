## PIVOT v01
---

---
This version of depression is quiet, high-functioning, invisible from the outside. It doesn't look like inability — she is managing everything — it looks like the absence of something that used to be there. Aliveness. Interest. A sense that the next day held something worth reaching toward. The absence of suffering is not the same as the presence of wellness.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
She lost herself gradually, through years of prioritizing everyone else's interior life over her own. The depression is not a sudden break. It is the endpoint of a long slow subordination of the self — what happens when a person's own needs, pleasures, and ambitions are deferred long enough to become unfamiliar.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
She keeps it together. That is both true and the problem. Keeping it together is a performance she gives every day that costs something she can no longer name. The depression lives in the gap between the performance and the interior — in the exhaustion of maintaining a version of herself that doesn't match what she actually feels.
---
