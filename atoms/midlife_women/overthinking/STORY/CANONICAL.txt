## STORY v01

IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 1
---
Diane has been thinking about the conversation she needs to have with her supervisor for six weeks. She has mapped the different responses her supervisor might have and planned her reply to each one. She has considered timing, setting, framing. The conversation is seventeen minutes long in her head and has twenty-two branches. She has not had it. She keeps adding branches.

## STORY v02
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
---
She is good at decisions in her professional life — clear, fast, well-reasoned. She froze entirely when asked what she wanted to do with the next chapter of her career. Her brain, which had spent twenty years optimizing for family logistics and organizational complexity, had no algorithm for this. She kept generating information and analysis and deferring the decision until she had more data, which was a way of not deciding that felt like rigor.

## STORY v03
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
---
She rehearsed the conversation with her mother four times in the car before she went in. She had mapped her mother's response, planned her own, anticipated the guilt, prepared her counter. Then her mother opened the door and said something completely unexpected and Diane stood there completely unscripted for a moment and the moment was fine. Better than fine. She drove home thinking about how much time she spent preparing for versions of things that didn't happen.

## STORY v04
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 1
---
Her therapist gave her a rule: twenty minutes maximum on any problem that exists only in the future. Set a timer. Think through it once, clearly, write down what you conclude, and then close it. She thought this was too simple for the complexity of her actual life. She tried it anyway. Fourteen days in, she had not solved any problems differently, but she was sleeping better. The analysis was the same. The time she spent running it was not. She told her therapist it felt like she had given her brain permission to stop.
