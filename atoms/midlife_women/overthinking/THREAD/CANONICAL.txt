The overthinking is the planning brain without a clear directive — it built a life for your children, your parents, your household, your employer, and now it is pointed at yourself and doesn't know what question it is supposed to be answering.

## THREAD v02

---
Underneath most overthinking loops at midlife is a want that hasn't been said out loud. Naming the want — even quietly, even to yourself — tends to slow the loop considerably.

---
The confusion about what you want is real and it is also temporary. It lifts as you make small experiments — small choices that are only yours — and your own preferences slowly come back into focus.
