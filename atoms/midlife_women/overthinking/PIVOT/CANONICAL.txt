## PIVOT v01
---

---
Her planning brain is skilled and has kept people safe and accounted for — and it is also running analysis on problems that don't exist yet and may never exist. The brain cannot distinguish between a genuine strategic problem and anxiety generating scenarios to solve. Both feel equally urgent. Only one of them requires action tonight.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The rumination is not problem-solving. Problem-solving reaches a conclusion. Rumination returns to the same place it started. She has circled this question enough times to know she cannot think her way to the answer — which means the brain's insistence on more analysis is not going to help. The thinking has already found the edges of what thinking can do.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
She built her analytical capability in service of other people's needs. It is good at logistics, at risk, at second-order effects. It is less practiced at the questions it is being asked now: what do I want, what do I need, what is enough for me. Those are not problems the planning brain solves. They are questions a different part of her knows how to answer.
---
