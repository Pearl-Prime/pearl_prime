## PIVOT v01
---

---
Her worth has been legible to her for decades only through her function — what she produced, whom she cared for, how well she performed her roles. Intrinsic worth — worth that doesn't depend on output — is not a concept the system she grew up in taught her clearly. What she is being asked to find now is not something she failed to build. It is something she was never given permission to claim.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
She discounts what she does because she does it easily — but "easily" is the wrong measure. She does it easily because she is genuinely good at it, and she is genuinely good at it because she has worked for twenty years and is skilled and capable and experienced. Ease is evidence of competence, not its absence.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The woman who deflects compliments, minimizes her achievements, and apologizes before she speaks is not humble. She is protecting herself from the disappointment of being seen and found wanting. That protection made sense once. At this point in her life it is costing her more than the risk it was designed to prevent.
