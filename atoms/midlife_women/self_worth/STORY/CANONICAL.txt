Diane's mother is ill and her teenager is struggling and her performance review is coming and her husband needs more than she has. She is managing all of it. She adds herself to no list. Not the "needs attention" list, not the "deserves rest" list, not the "something good should happen to this person" list. She is efficient at managing herself out of her own consideration.

Her daughter asked her to name three things she liked about herself and Diane said she was a good mother and a hard worker and then stopped and realized both of those were about her function, not her. She tried again. She couldn't find the answer quickly. She had been so identified with her usefulness for so long that she couldn't locate herself beneath it. Her daughter, who was sixteen, looked at her with something that was not pity but was in that direction.

She received the award at the professional association dinner and she stood at the podium and said something gracious and deflecting and sat down before the applause was fully over. Her colleague leaned over and said she should have stood there longer. Diane said she hates being the center of attention. Her colleague said she knew, and also that people who deserved recognition should let themselves receive it, and also that they should probably talk about this at some point. Diane said sure and knew she wouldn't bring it up.

## STORY v04
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
STORY_TYPE: character_study
STORY_TYPE_SOURCE: heuristic_v1
---
She started small: she stopped apologizing before she spoke in meetings. Just stopped. Let her sentences begin without the ritual diminishment. No one commented. The room did not tilt. She moved to the compliments next — received them without rebuttal, just said "thank you" and kept her face neutral and sat with the discomfort of being seen. It took months before it felt natural. What she noticed first was that her colleagues started listening differently. What she noticed second was that she was listening to herself differently too.
