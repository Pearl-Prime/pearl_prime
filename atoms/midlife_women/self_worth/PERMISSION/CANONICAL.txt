


## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You are allowed to have worth that is not located in what you do for other people. That worth exists. It existed before you were useful to anyone.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You are allowed to feel disoriented when the service structures fall away — when the children leave, when the role changes, when the usefulness you built your identity around becomes less needed. The disorientation is real, and it is pointing toward something important.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You are allowed to be in the middle of learning to locate your worth inside yourself and not have it figured out yet. This is a long project. You do not have to arrive by a particular date.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---

---
You are allowed to want recognition for who you are, not only for what you have done. That is not neediness. That is a basic human requirement that was legitimately unmet.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---

---
You are allowed to stop performing your worth and start inhabiting it. The performance kept you safe. It also kept you at a distance from the thing itself.
---
