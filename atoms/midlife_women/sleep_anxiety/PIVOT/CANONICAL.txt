## PIVOT v01
---

---
The 3am wake is a perimenopause signature — the cortisol curve shifts, body temperature shifts, and the brain that was trained for vigilance reads the sudden alertness as alarm. She is not failing to sleep. Her biology changed and the vigilance system activated without an actual threat. The thoughts that arrive are not urgent. They feel urgent because her nervous system just flooded with the hormone that says act.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Sleep hygiene will not fix a sleep architecture that perimenopause is actively reorganizing. She is not doing sleep wrong. She is sleeping in a body whose hormonal environment has changed in ways that directly affect wake cycles, temperature, and anxiety. This is medicine, not discipline. Trying harder will not work here.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The thoughts that come at 3am feel like problems to be solved because the brain is already activated. They are not more urgent at 3am than they were at 3pm. They feel more urgent because she is alone in the dark and her cortisol is up and there is nothing between her and the thing she's been not-thinking about during the day. The dark is not making them bigger. The day was making them smaller.
