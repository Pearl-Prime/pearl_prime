## PIVOT v01
---

---
Compassion is not an infinite resource. It is a capacity with limits, and those limits are not a measure of how much she loves the people in her life. When it runs empty it requires the same inputs as any depleted system: rest, intake, time outside of giving. The woman who has nothing left to give is not selfish. She is overdrawn.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The numbness she feels when someone needs her is not coldness and it is not indifference. It is a protection response from a system that has been absorbing others' pain without reprieve for too long. The body chose distance to survive. That is not a failure of love.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
She became the family's emotional center because she was capable, and because no one else stepped in, and because she believed it was her job. It was never actually her job. It was a role she filled by default that became a role by obligation that became a role by identity — and now she has to figure out what she is when she is not the person holding everything together.
---
