

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit outside your parent's room after a visit. Breathe in for four counts. Out for six. You gave real attention in there. Let this breath belong only to you now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both hands over your heart. Press gently. Count to eight. This is the part of you that keeps caring even when the caring has no reserve left. Let it rest under your own hands.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Wash your hands slowly after a caregiving task. Feel the water's temperature change. Count to twenty. This ordinary act marks the end of one task before the next one begins.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit and let your face go slack. Unclench your jaw. Let your eyebrows drop. You have been performing steady concern for hours. Let your face stop performing for these ten counts.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Step outside for sixty seconds. Feel the air on your skin. Name one thing that is not a task. This is not a break you have to earn.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Place one hand on your stomach. Breathe until the hand rises more than your chest. Repeat six breaths. Compassion fatigue lives in the gut as much as the heart. Give it this attention.
---
