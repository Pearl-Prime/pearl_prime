COST_INTENSITY: 2

STORY_TYPE_SOURCE: heuristic_v1
---
Diane used to cry at the things her mother told her — the loneliness, the fears, the small daily losses of aging. Now she listens and files. Her mother says she doesn't feel heard and Diane doesn't know what to tell her. She is hearing every word. She is documenting, categorizing, problem-solving. The part that used to feel alongside her mother has been offline for some time now. She doesn't know when it stopped.

Her teenage daughter came home crying and Diane sat with her and said the right things and held her and the whole time there was a part of her that was watching herself do it, noticing the absence of the feeling she used to have when her children were in pain. She loves her daughter. She is certain of this. But the love is somewhere under glass now — she can see it, she just can't reach it.

## STORY v04
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
STORY_TYPE: character_study
STORY_TYPE_SOURCE: heuristic_v1
---
Diane told her therapist she thought she was becoming a bad person. Her therapist asked what evidence she had for that. Diane listed it: she doesn't feel things the way she used to, she resents calls she used to welcome, she is going through the motions of caring for people she genuinely loves. Her therapist said that was compassion fatigue, not moral failure. Diane cried for the first time in four months. Not because it was good news. Because it meant she wasn't broken. The capacity was still there. It was just buried.
