## PIVOT v01
---

---
She performed social ease for so long that she didn't notice what it cost. The performance was good — she was charming, warm, capable in groups — and the performance required her to be a particular version of herself reliably and on demand. What looks like social anxiety now may be the withdrawal of a system that finally ran out of performance budget. She is not becoming anxious. She may be becoming more honest.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The social withdrawal is not permanent damage. It is information: her nervous system needed more recovery between exposures than the schedule allowed, and it started taking the recovery unilaterally because she wasn't giving it. The solution is not to push through and rebuild the performance. It is to find out what genuine, non-performing presence feels like and build from there.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Competence and ease are not the same thing. She has been competent in social situations for decades — it required effort she made invisible through practice. The effort that used to be invisible is now visible to her nervous system, which is demanding acknowledgment. She is not losing social capability. She is being asked to relate to it differently.
