


## THREAD v01
---

---
The social anxiety surfacing at midlife is often the anxiety that was always there, held down by the energy required to perform competence and ease. As that energy depletes, the underlying experience becomes more available — more honest, and harder to ignore.
---

## THREAD v02
---

---
The performance served you: it opened doors, sustained relationships, kept you legible in professional and social contexts. But it also meant you spent decades in rooms that cost more than they gave. Midlife is a reasonable time to revise that arrangement.
---

## THREAD v03
---

---
What you are building toward is not the absence of social anxiety but the presence of social situations that are actually worth attending — where the version of you that shows up is closer to real, and the cost is proportionate to the connection.
---
