


## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You are allowed to grieve your mother while she is still alive. The loss of who she was — before dementia — is a real loss. It does not require her death to be legitimate.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You are allowed to mourn the marriage you thought you had. The grief is about the version you believed in, and that version mattered to you. You do not have to pretend the marriage was always exactly this.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You are allowed to grieve your own body — the one before perimenopause, before the metabolism changed, before sleep became unreliable. You are allowed to miss it without that missing meaning you are vain or ungrateful.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---

---
You are allowed to hold more than one grief at the same time. Midlife loss is rarely singular. The layers do not cancel each other out. They all count.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---

---
You are allowed to grieve the version of yourself you thought you'd become by now. She was real enough that losing her hurts. That is a legitimate loss.
---
