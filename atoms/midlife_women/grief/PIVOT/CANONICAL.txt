## PIVOT v01
---

---
Midlife grief doesn't always have a clean event. Sometimes it is the slow accumulation of the person her mother used to be, disappearing by degrees. Sometimes it is the body she lived in before. Sometimes it is the marriage she thought she was building, or the version of herself she believed she'd become by now. These losses are real, and they don't get a funeral, and they require grief anyway.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
She grieves in the margins of a full life — in the car, in the shower, in the brief quiet before someone else needs something. This is not insufficient grieving. This is grieving the way midlife allows: in the time that remains after everything else, which is still grieving, which still counts.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The grief and the love exist simultaneously and that is not a contradiction. She loves her mother and she grieves her mother. She loves the body she's in and she grieves the body she had. She loves her life and she grieves some of what she thought it would be. Both can be true. The grief doesn't invalidate the love. It is evidence of it.
