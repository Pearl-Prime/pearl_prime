


## THREAD v01
---

---
The layered grief of midlife — the mother, the marriage, the body, the self you expected to become — does not follow a sequence. It arrives sideways, in ordinary moments, and it asks to be felt without a clear timeline for when it should be finished.
---

## THREAD v02
---

---
Each loss you are carrying has its own texture and its own timeline. They do not have to be resolved together or at the same pace. You are allowed to grieve one today and another one next year.
---

## THREAD v03
---

---
Grief at midlife has a particular quality: you are old enough to feel the permanence of what is gone and young enough to feel the cost of carrying it forward. That combination asks for a different kind of tending.
---
