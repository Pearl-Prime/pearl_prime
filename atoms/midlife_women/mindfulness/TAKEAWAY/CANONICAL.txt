## TAKEAWAY v01
---
---
Presence was never about never leaving the room. It's about how fast you find your way back.
---

## TAKEAWAY v02
---
---
Half-presence is the most expensive option in the meeting — you pay for two places and get neither.
---

## TAKEAWAY v03
---
---
The catch is the skill. The drift was never the problem.
---

## TAKEAWAY v04
---
---
A worry you write down stops needing to be carried silently, ninety seconds at a time.
---

## TAKEAWAY v05
---
---
You don't need a mind that never wanders. You need a shorter gap before you notice it has.
---

## TAKEAWAY v06
---
---
Composure and presence are not the same system — you can have one without the other, and only you will know the difference.
---

## TAKEAWAY v07
---
---
One breath, inserted before the sprint toward the notification, is often the whole intervention.
---

## TAKEAWAY v08
---
---
A fear written fully once needs to run in the background far less than one only half-acknowledged all week.
---

## TAKEAWAY v09
---
---
Returning badly, over and over, is still returning. It counts.
---

## TAKEAWAY v10
---
---
Nobody claps for the meeting where you caught yourself nine times and came back nine times. It was still the better meeting.
---

## TAKEAWAY v11
---
---
The gap between leaving and noticing is the only number actually worth tracking.
---

## TAKEAWAY v12
---
---
You were never supposed to hold the whole room and the whole worry at once. Something was always going to be a little absent — the practice is choosing what returns first.
---
