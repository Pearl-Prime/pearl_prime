That night, Marisol doesn't tell anyone about the flat hand on the table, the small return she practiced four times in one meeting. But at pickup, her daughter looks up at her in the car and says, "You're not on your phone," like it's a fact worth reporting. Marisol hadn't planned on that being the noticeable part. Tomorrow, she thinks, she'll try it in the meeting that usually loses her worst — the Friday pipeline review.

## THREAD v02

---
Dana's ten-second hourly check-in — "I am in the third-floor conference room, my feet are on the carpet" — starts to feel less absurd by the second week, and one Thursday she catches herself using it unprompted, mid-drift, in a call with her mother instead of a client. She doesn't mention this to anyone yet. She's curious, though, whether it works the same on the calls that actually scare her.
