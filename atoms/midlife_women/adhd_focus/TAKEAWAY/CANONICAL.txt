## TAKEAWAY v01
---
---
You don't cross the gap between deciding and doing with more willpower. You go around it with a step small enough that it was never really a decision at all.
---

## TAKEAWAY v02
---
---
Importance doesn't get your attention. Salience does. Make the task look new again, and your brain will finally look back.
---

## TAKEAWAY v03
---
---
The blank page was never the obstacle. The idea that you had to fill it alone, perfectly, first try, was.
---

## TAKEAWAY v04
---
---
Your internal clock won't warn you in time. A physical object in your path will.
---

## TAKEAWAY v05
---
---
You don't need a bigger memory. You need fewer things trying to fit inside it at once.
---

## TAKEAWAY v06
---
---
Avoidance charges interest. Say the task's real size out loud, and watch the debt disappear.
---

## TAKEAWAY v07
---
---
Half of what feels hard is just an outdated picture you never went back to check.
---

## TAKEAWAY v08
---
---
A plan only you know about is a plan only you can quietly break. Tell one other person and it holds.
---

## TAKEAWAY v09
---
---
An infinite pile can't be started. A shrinking, countable one can.
---

## TAKEAWAY v10
---
---
The task rarely needs the perfect conditions you assigned it. It usually just needs a pen.
---

## TAKEAWAY v11
---
---
Stop scheduling the calm early version of yourself who never shows up. Schedule the real one, and protect that hour instead.
---

## TAKEAWAY v12
---
---
Shame never got the call made. A little visible, almost-funny data about your own avoidance usually does.
---
