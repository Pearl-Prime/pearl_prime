## PIVOT v01
---

---
Midlife courage is not the dramatic version. It is the slow, unglamorous act of choosing herself after decades of choosing everyone else first — in the conversation with her partner, in the conversation with her employer, in the conversation with the self she has been deferring for twenty years.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
She stayed in the situations that were too small for her because staying felt like responsibility and leaving felt like selfishness. That story served other people well. It cost her considerably. Courage at this stage is not recklessness — it is the refusal to trade another twenty years for safety.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
She has already survived the hard things: the miscarriage, the layoff, the parent's decline, the marriage that almost didn't make it. She has evidence she is more resilient than she believes herself to be. The fear she feels about the next step is real, but it is not information about her capacity. It is information about how much this matters to her.
