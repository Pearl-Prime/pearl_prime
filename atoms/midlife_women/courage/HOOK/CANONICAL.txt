Diane is at the kitchen table at 9 PM, hands flat on the placemat, voice steady — at forty-nine she is saying the thing in her marriage she has not said for eleven years.

At forty-nine, Diane said the thing in the marriage that she had been not-saying for eleven years, and the marriage survived, and she has not stopped thinking about what else she has been not-saying.
