--- variant: v01
You've optimized the schedule, the career, the home, the relationships. Everything has a system except the part where you check if you're actually okay. The ground you're standing on was built by efficiency, not honesty. Anchoring means finding the difference.

--- variant: v02
Stability for you has always meant keeping all the plates spinning. What if real stability means letting one fall and discovering you survive it? The anchoring you need isn't about doing more. It's about being more -- specifically, more honest about what all the doing is protecting you from.

--- variant: v03
Everyone sees someone who has it together. You see the spreadsheet of invisible effort that keeps the illusion intact. Real grounding starts where the performance of togetherness ends. It starts with admitting the foundation is made of tasks, not roots.
