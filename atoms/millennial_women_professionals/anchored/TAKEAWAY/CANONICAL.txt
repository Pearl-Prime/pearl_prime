--- variant: v01
Stability that depends on everything going right isn't stability. It's a house of cards maintained by your willpower. The takeaway is that real grounding requires you to stop equating 'having it together' with being okay. You can be okay even when things are messy. That's the ground you need to find.

--- variant: v02
You don't have to earn the right to feel stable. Stability isn't the reward for doing everything perfectly. It's something you build by being honest about what you need -- not what everyone else needs from you.

--- variant: v03
The anchoring you've been doing was anchoring to roles: the reliable one, the capable one, the one who handles it. What happens when you anchor to yourself instead of your function? That's a different kind of ground. Softer, scarier, and actually real.
