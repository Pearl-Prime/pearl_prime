

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
You function. You show up. You do the things. And underneath, there's a flatness that no achievement seems to touch. This is the particular cruelty of depression in high-functioning people. You're supposed to be getting better because your external life looks fine. Job is fine. Relationships are fine. You're fine. Except you're moving through the world like you're underwater. Everything takes twice as much effort. Everything produces half as much satisfaction. You're not crying in the bathroom. You're not unable to get out of bed. You're just not there. The pleasure centers have dimmed. Food tastes like texture. Friends feel like obligations. Projects feel like tasks. This version of depression doesn't announce itself. It whispers that you're ungrateful or tired or just not trying hard enough.
---

There's something you notice about yourself. You can function at a high level while experiencing almost nothing. You're excellent at the performance of engagement. You laugh at jokes you don't find funny. You discuss things you don't care about. You do your work with precision. Inside, it's like watching yourself from a distance. There's a disconnect between what you're doing and what you're feeling, and that disconnect has become normal. This is the mechanism that allows depression to hide. By maintaining functionality, you're actually allowing it to deepen. No one intervenes. You don't have to face it. So it lives inside you, taking what it needs, and you keep producing.

The flatness isn't sadness. It's the absence of something. For a while, you thought you were just burned out. That a vacation would fix it. That accomplishing the next goal would restore the capacity for joy. But joy doesn't return because depression isn't an absence of good things. It's an absence of the ability to feel good things. This is what makes it invisible. You look fine. You are fine. You just don't feel anything about it. The protective coating of functionality is also keeping you from recognizing how much damage is being done.
