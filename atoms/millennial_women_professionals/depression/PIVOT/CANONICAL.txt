--- variant: v01
High-functioning depression in professional women looks like a decorated career and a curated life. It looks like 'she handles everything so well.' The depression hides behind the handling. It's in the flatness after the meeting, the emptiness behind the achieved goals, the way nothing feels like enough even though the evidence suggests it should.

--- variant: v02
You achieved the career, the partner, the apartment, the vacation. You checked every box. The depression is what lives in the space between the checked boxes and the feeling you were told would come with them. Nobody warned you that you could do everything right and still feel nothing.

--- variant: v03
The depression doesn't announce itself when you're busy. It waits for the quiet. The commute. The shower. The five minutes between putting the kids to bed and opening the laptop again. In those gaps, the flatness rushes in. You're not failing. You're finally noticing what's been there all along, hidden by the noise of constant doing.
