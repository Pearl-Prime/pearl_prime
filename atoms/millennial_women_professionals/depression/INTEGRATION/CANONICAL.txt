

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I am still breathing. That's where I'm starting. Not with joy or motivation or purpose. Just: I'm breathing. My lungs are working. My heart is beating. This body is continuing even though my mind has left the building. That's something. That's the whole thing right now. Just continuing. Not thriving. Not feeling. Not living fully. Just continuing. Each day that I continue is a day I didn't give up. That counts. My therapist tells me that surviving depression is an achievement. It sounds impossible, but I'm learning to believe her.
I am here and that is enough.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I can feel the heaviness in my limbs. It's real. It's not lazy. It's not weakness. It's depression using my body as a language. It's saying: everything is too heavy. Everything requires too much. My legs don't want to move because my mind doesn't see the point of moving. That's how depression works. It talks to me through my body. It makes everything heavy. The coffee cup. The phone. The decision about what to eat. Everything is loaded. I'm learning to work with the heaviness instead of fighting it. To move slowly. To do less. To let things be heavy while I continue anyway.
Depression speaks in the language of weight.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
What if depression is telling the truth? What if things aren't meaningful and I'm just human and everything does end? What if my brain is right that nothing matters? I don't know how to answer this. But I'm learning that even if nothing matters, I can choose to matter to myself. Even if meaning is created, not discovered, I can create it. Maybe meaning isn't the point. Maybe the point is just to not leave. To stay. To be present for my own life even if I don't understand why.
Maybe meaning comes from staying.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I can feel my body getting slightly less heavy. Not light. But slightly less heavy. That's progress. Small progress. Almost invisible progress. But it's there. I notice it in the morning when I don't dread getting up quite as much. In the afternoon when I manage one small action. In the evening when I don't feel quite as far away from myself. Depression taught me to notice small things. To celebrate small changes. To understand that progress isn't the same as transformation. Small is enough.
Small progress is still progress.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
I'm learning to treat myself like someone I love who is struggling. Not to push myself to feel better. Not to say I'm being silly for being sad. Just to sit with myself in the sadness. To let myself be tired. To not demand that I perform wellness. If a friend were depressed, I would never tell her to just think positive thoughts. I would sit with her. I would let her be as she is. I'm learning to do that for myself. To be my own gentle companion in this darkness.
I'm learning to be myself, now.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of depression is that I'm living a small life. A life where I do the minimum. Where I don't pursue dreams. Where I just exist in my apartment and go to work and come back home. That's the price depression is asking. Some days the price is too high and I can't pay it. Some days I can't get out of bed. But I'm learning that even a small life is still a life. Even existing is valuable. Even surviving is a form of living. The person I was before depression—that person is still in here. She's just very quiet right now. But she's still here.
I am still here and I persist.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
What would it take for me to want to be alive? That's the question depression asks. And I don't have the answer. But I'm learning that I don't need the answer. I just need to keep breathing while I figure it out. I don't need to feel full of life. I don't need to feel happy about being alive. I just need to not leave. To stay. To show up for myself even when every cell in my body is saying: what's the point? The point is that I'm here. The point is that I keep going. That's enough for now.
Staying is the answer, for now.
