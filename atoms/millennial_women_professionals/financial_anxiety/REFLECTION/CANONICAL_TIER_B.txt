

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I understand the mechanism now. There's a reference point updating operating beneath the rational spreadsheets and conscious intention. The mechanism is reference point updating, which researchers have documented meticulously: we feel the loss of money more intensely than we feel the gain of it. My nervous system is calibrated to threat, even when the threat no longer exists. I can watch myself do this. The account balance goes up by thousands, which objectively is significant and positive news. But the threat-detection system immediately recalibrates. The new safe threshold is higher. The baseline shifts. The anxiety adjusts to match the new number. It's automatic. I'm not consciously moving the goalpost. My system does it without my deliberate consent. There's a pattern wired in that says whatever number I have is the minimum and it's still not quite enough. It's like my nervous system is configured to never fully believe in safety. Understanding the mechanism intellectually doesn't change the experience.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The mechanism is aspiration treadmill. My system learned that each increase in resources becomes the new baseline for safety. Money arrives. Relief floods in. Then the recalibration happens almost immediately. The nervous system says: this is the new safe number. Anything less than this is now catastrophic. The anxiety doesn't dissolve. It just reattaches to a higher value. I can watch this happening in real-time. The paycheck deposits. The relief arrives. The threat-detection system quiets for a moment. Then it shifts its baseline upward. The new number becomes the minimum. The fear becomes attached to this higher threshold. What's devastating is that I can see this mechanism operating. I understand adaptation. I understand how our nervous systems work. And none of that understanding changes the fact that the anxiety persists, just at a higher numerical level.
