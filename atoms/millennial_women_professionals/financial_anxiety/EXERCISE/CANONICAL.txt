## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your financial anxiety is catastrophizing about the future.
The future isn't here. The present is.
Write down your actual financial situation today.
Income. Essential expenses. What's left.
This is the truth. Not the fear.
Your fear is that you'll become destitute.
But your current situation doesn't support that narrative.
You've managed before. You'll manage now.
Numbers are more trustworthy than your anxiety.
Check the numbers when panic hits.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You have competing financial priorities.
Kids, retirement, car, house. All feel urgent.
But you can't solve everything simultaneously.
Write down what's actually pressing right now.
Not eventually. Right now.
Pick one priority. Just one.
Give that one your focused attention.
The others will wait. They won't disappear.
Anxiety wants to solve everything at once.
You're choosing the one thing that matters today.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You're anxious because you have no financial plan.
Plans don't need to be perfect.
They need to be clear and honest.
Write down: What are you saving for?
What can you cut without suffering?
What's actually non-negotiable?
One honest financial picture is better than spiraling.
Numbers beat anxiety. Always.
Look at your actual situation quarterly.
Not weekly. Quarterly. Give yourself that space.
