

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I can feel my body coming back to me. After years of giving it away in the service of other people's needs, I'm remembering that I live here. That this body has needs too. That it gets tired. That it has limits. I sat alone for three hours yesterday and didn't feel guilty. My body was the center of my own attention. That's not selfish. That's survival. My body is teaching me: you matter as much as the people you care for. You deserve the care you give. I'm learning to listen.
My body is leading me home.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
Compassion fatigue is what happens when I extend myself so far beyond my capacity that I lose contact with myself entirely. It's not that I care too much. It's that I don't care about myself at all. I give and give and give until there's nothing left. But I was never the infinite resource I thought I was. I'm finite. I have limits. Honoring those limits isn't a failure of compassion. It's the prerequisite for sustainable compassion. I can't help anyone if I'm empty. So I'm learning to say: I'm full. I need to refill myself. That has to come first.
Emptiness isn't the same as generosity.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I'm learning to be the person for myself that everyone depends on me to be for them. Patient. Present. Attentive. Believing in their wholeness. When I talk to people I care about, I make space for their feelings. I don't minimize their pain. I don't rush them toward solutions. I just hold space. I'm learning to hold space for my own pain the same way. To sit with my own exhaustion without judgment. To believe that I'm whole even when I'm broken. To let myself be tended to. Maybe not by others. But by myself. I'm learning to be my own sanctuary.
I'm becoming my own safe place.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
I am still here. That's what matters now. Not how much I'm helping. Not how many people's lives I'm changing. Just: I'm still here. I haven't disappeared completely. My presence exists. I can feel it in my body: a small spark of myself. That's the whole goal right now. To stay alive. To stay present. To not give myself away entirely. Everything else—the helping, the advice, the emotional labor—can come later. First I need to come back to myself. I'm learning that coming back to myself is the work.
I'm still here and that's enough.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
What if my worth isn't tied to my usefulness? What if I can stop helping and still be valuable? What if my existence alone is enough? I don't believe this yet. My whole life has taught me that I exist to serve. But the belief is starting to crack. I'm testing it. Resting without earning it. Being present without performing help. Taking care of myself without justifying it. What if the world doesn't need me to be useful in order to continue spinning?
Maybe I'm allowed to just be.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
I can feel the weight lifting. Not completely. The fatigue is still in my bones. But there's a lightness starting to emerge underneath. It happened when I said no to someone. When I protected my own time. When I chose myself. My body knew before my mind did. My body was desperate to come home to itself. I'm listening to that desperation now. I'm taking it seriously. My body is telling me: refill. Rest. Remember that you exist. I'm listening.
My body won't be ignored anymore.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of emptying myself for others is that I have nothing left for my own life. My own dreams. My own becoming. The people I help will eventually be fine. But if I disappear, no one will miss the version of me I never got to become. That's the real cost. Not the exhaustion. Not the resentment. But the unlived life. The dreams I set aside. The person I'm not becoming because I'm too busy keeping everyone else afloat. I'm learning that I can't save other people at the expense of my own survival. That's not noble. That's erasure.
I'm taking my life back for myself.
