

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
You started in this field because you wanted to help. That impulse is still there, but now it lives alongside a bone-deep exhaustion that no amount of rest seems to address. You care for people all day. You hold space for their pain. You offer solutions. You follow up. And at the end of the day, you're depleted in a way that sleep doesn't fix. The system you're running is designed to give more than it receives. Help flows outward. Refill comes from nowhere. So gradually, the reserves empty. You notice you're less patient. Less present. Less able to generate the compassion that used to come naturally. This isn't a personal failure. It's the predictable outcome of a structure that treats your empathy as an infinite resource.
