

## HOOK v01
---

---
You are everyone's listening place. Your chest receives and receives and receives. Nothing comes back in return.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your friend texts crisis details. You respond with care, suggestions, validation. Three months later, she doesn't ask how you're doing. Your stomach learns to expect one direction.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The junior at work trusts you with everything. Your shoulders carry her stress. When you need something, her availability mysteriously doesn't expand.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You've become the one who holds space. Your hands stay open even when they're empty. The depletion is starting to feel like a character flaw.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Everyone comes to you. Your throat stays soft. Nowhere in that arrangement is there someone who receives your vulnerability the way you receive theirs.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You're tired in a way that sleep doesn't fix. Your chest holds the accumulated weight of other people's problems. Your own stays unnamed.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The emotional labor is invisible until one day your hands shake and nobody notices. Your nervous system has been working overtime. The system treats it as your baseline.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You check on her, then her, then her. Your jaw tightens when you realize: nobody's checking on you. The asymmetry becomes clearer every time you notice it.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
You give what you have left. Your feet are still moving. The energy was never meant to flow only outward like this.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Everyone depends on your steadiness. Your shoulders stay level so they can fall apart. The cost of being the stable one is that nobody steadies you.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
She called you at 2 AM last month. Last week you had a bad day and she was busy. Your chest knows the exchange rate now.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
You've become numb to your own needs. Your breath stays shallow. This might be what burnout feels like before the collapse.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
At work you're the support system. Your team leans on you constantly. When you mentioned your own challenge last week, they pivoted to logistics. Your throat tightens at the memory.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
You're holding space for three crises that aren't yours. Your hands feel full. Your own weight just keeps getting heavier and heavier.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
The dynamic is so established now it feels like obligation. Your stomach sinks. You can't remember the last time someone offered care without you asking.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
You show up for them through their relationship chaos. Your chest stays open. The silence when you need support lands like specific abandonment.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
Everyone calls you because you're strong. Your jaw learned that "I'm not okay too" feels like selfishness. Strength has become the wall you hide behind.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You've become the translator of everyone's emotions. Your hands gesture their feelings while your own calcify. The asymmetry is starting to feel structural.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
She vents about work and you listen for an hour. Your feet have been standing this whole time. When you mentioned your job stress, the conversation lasted three minutes. Your shoulders register the mathematics.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
You care so much it's become a vulnerability. Your breath gets thin when you think about what happens if you stop. Your whole network might collapse without your holding.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
The cost of being available is that you're available to your own depletion. Your chest feels it arriving. Soon you might not have anything left to give.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
Everyone assumes you're fine. Your hands look busy. Your nervous system knows the truth: you stopped being fine months ago.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
You give in proportion to how much they ask, not how much you have. Your stomach empties itself daily. The gift they don't know is a gift becomes resentment you can't voice.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
The reciprocal care you've been waiting for still hasn't appeared. Your jaw clenches slightly. You're starting to see it as the arrangement, not as an oversight.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You hold everyone's complexity. Your throat stays open for their story. Your own complexity has nowhere to land because you're the container, not the contained.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
The emotional labor is how you show love. Your hands are always moving toward them. Somewhere in that gesture, you disappeared.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
You said yes to another request. Your shoulders dropped further. The depletion is becoming the water you swim in.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
Everyone leans. Your chest has learned not to expect the lean to go the other direction. This is what exhaustion tastes like.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
You're the shoulder everyone cries on. Your breath steadies theirs. The silence where someone steadies you has stretched into years.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
The giving is so normalized you can't see it's killing you. Your feet barely lift off the ground anymore. You're already thinking about how to give more before collapse.
