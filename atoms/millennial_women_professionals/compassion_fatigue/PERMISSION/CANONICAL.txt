--- variant: v01
You're allowed to be tired of caring. You're allowed to say 'I don't have it right now' without that making you a bad woman, a bad partner, a bad colleague, a bad person. The compassion fatigue isn't a character deficiency. It's an energy deficit. You have permission to honor it.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to redirect the care toward yourself. Not after everyone else is taken care of. Now. Before the meeting. Before the dinner. Before the next person asks for something. You're allowed to put your own oxygen mask on even when nobody else's mask has fallen yet.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to feel resentful without being a resentful person. The resentment is information about an inequitable distribution of emotional labor. You have permission to feel it, name it, and use it as data for what needs to change -- without making it mean something about your character.
