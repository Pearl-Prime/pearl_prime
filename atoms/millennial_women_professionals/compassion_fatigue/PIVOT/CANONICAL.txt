--- variant: v01
You've been the listener, the supporter, the one who remembers everybody's thing. At work and at home, you're the emotional infrastructure nobody acknowledges and everyone depends on. Compassion fatigue is what happens when the infrastructure is never maintained. You're not heartless. You're depleted by a role you were socialized into and never given the tools to sustain.

--- variant: v02
The resentment you feel when someone needs something from you isn't petty. It's your system flagging an overdraft. Compassion fatigue in women who carry dual loads -- professional and personal -- isn't optional burnout. It's the inevitable result of a world that treats your empathy as a communal resource.

--- variant: v03
You used to care naturally. Now caring takes effort, and the effort makes you feel guilty because caring is supposed to be who you are. Compassion fatigue doesn't change who you are. It reveals what happens when who you are gets consumed by what everyone else needs you to be.
