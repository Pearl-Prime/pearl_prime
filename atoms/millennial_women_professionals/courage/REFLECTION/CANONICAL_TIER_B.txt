

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Courage looks like something external until you realize it's actually the calculation of what you'll lose if you stay silent. Every time you think about speaking up, about saying the thing you actually believe, your mind immediately generates the social cost. What will people think. Will they question your competence. Will there be consequences at work. Will you be labeled as difficult. The mechanism is so fast you barely notice it's running. You've learned to measure courage against risk, and the risk always seems immense. Other people seem to speak up effortlessly, as if the social calculation doesn't factor into their decisions. But you know better. You measure every word against potential outcomes. This has kept you safe. It has also kept you small.
---
