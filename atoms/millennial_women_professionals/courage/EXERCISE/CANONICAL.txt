

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Stand in front of your mirror. Look at yourself. Say what you need to say. Say it out loud. Your voice should be steady. Not apologetic. Not small. Steady. Practice saying it. Say it five times. Hear yourself saying it. Your voice is your tool. Use it. After five times, you're ready.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Write down the thing you're afraid to do. Write it down. Now write down what the worst case is. Really write it. Now ask yourself: Can I survive that. The answer is yes. You can survive it. So do the thing. Fear is lying about what will happen. Write the truth. Act on the truth.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Do something small that scares you. Go to a coffee shop alone. Have lunch alone. Take a class. Something small. When you do it, your body learns that fear is not dangerous. Fear is just a feeling. Do small scary things daily. Build the muscle. Then bigger scary things will feel possible.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Imagine yourself six months from now. You did the scary thing. You did it. What are you glad about. Write it down. Imagine how it felt. Write it down. Now come back to now. You're not there yet. But you can get there. You can do this. Imagine the you that's already brave.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Draft the email that scares you. Write it all the way through. Don't edit. Just write. Say everything. When you're done, read it. Did you say what you needed. If yes, send it. If no, edit and send. Don't let it sit. Don't revise forever. Just send it. The fear will be worse than the thing.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Call the person you're afraid to call. Right now. Don't wait. Don't plan. Just call. You'll survive the call. The person will survive your honesty. Call. Your voice will shake. That's okay. That's courage. Courage is not confidence. Courage is doing it scared.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Stand in your car. Feel your feet on the floor. You're here. You're solid. You're doing this. Whatever this is. Put your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is strong. Your body is ready. Now go do the thing. Your body has your back.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Make a list of people who have already been brave. What did they do. How did they do it. Now you're doing what they did. You're brave like them. You're capable like them. You're going to survive this like they survived. Name three brave people. Say their names. Channel them. You've got this.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
You're scared. That's the signal that you should do it. Fear is your GPS. Fear says: You're on the right track. So do the thing you're afraid of. Don't wait until you're not scared. You'll wait forever. Do it scared. Scared is fine.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Put on something that makes you feel powerful. Wear it. Feel it. Now do the scary thing. Your clothes are part of your armor. Use them. Feel strong. Then act strong. Then you are strong.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Write down everything you want to say to someone. All of it. No filter. Say all the hard things. Now read it. Now pick the three most important things. Those three. Say those three. Not everything. Just the three that matter most. Courage is being brave with your words. Say the three things.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Ask yourself: What's the worst that could happen. Write it. Now ask: What's the most likely thing that will happen. Write that. Now ask: What do I want to happen. Write that. You've written three possibilities. The worst is unlikely. The most likely is fine. What you want is possible. So do it. Focus on what you want.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Your body is telling you no. That's normal. That's fear. Fear feels like: Chest tight. Hands shaking. Throat dry. These are fear sensations. They're not danger sensations. Your body is practicing the energy it will need. So thank your body. Say: Thank you for preparing me. Then do the thing anyway. Your body will be fine.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Do one small brave thing every day. Call someone. Ask something. Say something. Try something. Every day. Build the muscle. After thirty days, courage will feel normal. After sixty days, it will be a habit. Start now. Do one thing.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Before you do the scary thing, take ten deep breaths. In for four. Hold for four. Out for four. Do this ten times. Your nervous system will calm down. You'll feel more clear. Then do the thing. Breathing first. Then courage. Then action.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Write a mantra. A thing you'll say to yourself. Something true. Mine is: I can do hard things. Mine is: I am brave. Mine is: Fear is not facts. What's yours. Say it out loud. Say it five times. Write it on your hand. Carry it with you. When you're scared, say your mantra.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tell someone what you're about to do. Say it out loud to a person. The person doesn't have to do anything. They just have to hear you. Saying it to a person makes it real. Makes it possible. Tell them. Then do it. The person will be proud. You'll be proud.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Think about a time you already did something brave. Think about it. How did you do that. You were brave before. You can be brave again. You've done hard things. This is just another hard thing. You already know how.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Your fear is telling you that you'll fail. Ask yourself: So what if I fail. Write it down. Write what happens if you fail. Then ask: Can I recover from that. The answer is yes. You can recover. You've recovered from things before. So fail. Recover. Move on. You'll be fine.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Stop waiting for the right moment. There is no right moment. The moment is now. Do the thing now. Don't wait until you're ready. You'll never be ready. Ready is not a real thing. Ready is procrastination wearing a nice outfit. So do it now. Not later. Now.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Make a decision. All the way. Not a maybe. A yes or a no. A yes means you're doing it. A no means you're not. Which is it. Once you decide, you're calmer. Once you decide, you can prepare. So decide. All the way.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Stand up. Put your shoulders back. Stand tall. Posture creates confidence. Your body creates your mind. So stand like you're brave. Stand like you can do this. Your body will believe it first. Your mind will catch up.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
You're terrified. Good. That means it matters. If it didn't matter, you wouldn't be scared. So your fear is proof that you care. That's good. Use the fear. Let it fuel you. Fear is energy. Use the energy.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Before sleep, imagine yourself doing the brave thing. Imagine it going well. Imagine yourself being proud. Imagine yourself surviving. Imagine yourself on the other side. Imagination is practice. You're practicing success. Sleep on the image. You'll dream it. You'll wake up more ready.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
You have done brave things before. You have survived hard things before. You know how to do this. You're not learning. You're remembering. Remember what you already know. You are brave. You've always been brave.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Write down what courage looks like in your body. For me, it's: Straight spine. Open chest. Clear voice. Write yours. Now create that body. Stand that way. Speak that way. When you're in that body, courage is easier. Your body teaches your mind.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
You don't need permission. You don't need approval. You don't need anyone to say it's okay. It's already okay. You're allowed. Say it out loud: I'm allowed. I'm allowed to try. I'm allowed to fail. I'm allowed to be brave. Say it until you believe it.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Think about who you want to be. Think about her. Think about what she would do. Now be her. For today, be her. Wear her confidence. Speak her truth. Move through the world like her. She's brave. So you're brave.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Pick a date. Put it on your calendar. On that date, you will do the thing. You will not postpone. You will not delay. You will do it. Having a date makes it real. Makes it possible. Pick the date. Put it on your calendar. You're committed now.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
You're about to do something brave. Before you do it, acknowledge it. Say: I'm about to be brave. Say it out loud. Hear yourself. You're not pretending. You're doing it. You're brave. Go do it.
