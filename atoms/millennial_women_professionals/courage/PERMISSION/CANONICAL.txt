--- variant: v01
You're allowed to be soft. In a world that rewards women for being tough, competent, and unflappable, you have permission to be moved, affected, and vulnerable. Softness isn't the opposite of strength. It's the part of strength that got edited out.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to be courageous about the small, private things. Admitting you're lonely. Acknowledging you're overwhelmed. Telling someone the truth about how you're doing. You don't need to storm a boardroom. You need to be honest in your living room.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to let courage look like quitting. Quitting the committee, the extra project, the relationship that drains you, the standard you can't meet. Not every act of courage is additive. Sometimes the bravest thing is subtraction.
