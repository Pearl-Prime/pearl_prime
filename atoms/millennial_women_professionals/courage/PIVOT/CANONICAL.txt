--- variant: v01
You've been courageous in ways that don't get celebrated. The courage to show up at a table where you're the only woman. The courage to advocate while knowing advocacy costs you more than it costs your male colleagues. Now there's a different kind of courage needed: the kind aimed inward, at the feelings you've been too busy being strong to feel.

--- variant: v02
Strength has been your armor. The courage now isn't in putting the armor on. It's in considering, for the first time, what it would feel like to take it off. Not at work. Not for anyone's benefit. Just for you, in private, to see what's underneath the competence.

--- variant: v03
You've been told your whole life that women can do anything men can do. Nobody mentioned the cost of doing it while also doing everything women are still expected to do. The courage you need isn't about doing more. It's about the radical act of doing less and withstanding the internal voice that says less makes you less.
