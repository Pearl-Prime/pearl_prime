

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I did the thing I was terrified of and my hands are still shaking. That's the discovery: I did the brave thing and I'm still scared. Courage isn't the absence of fear. It's action with fear present. It's speaking while your voice shakes. It's asking while your heart races. It's doing it anyway. I've been waiting for the fear to go away before acting. It never goes away. So I'm learning to move with the fear. To let the shaking hands type the email. To let the racing heart carry me through the conversation. My body is teaching me that fear is not a stop sign.
Fear is just information, not instruction.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I am still here after doing the thing. Everything I imagined would happen didn't. The catastrophe I predicted stayed in my imagination. The actual event was much smaller. Much more survivable. My body learned something: I can survive the thing I'm afraid of. I can survive failure. I can survive rejection. I can survive my own courage. The knowledge changes something. It's not that I'm suddenly brave. It's that bravery is now possible. Because I have proof that I survived it before.
I have proof that I survive.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
What's the cost of staying small? What's the cost of never asking? Of never speaking? Of always playing it safe? I'm learning that the cost is my own life. The unlived portions. The risks untaken. The versions of myself I never get to become because I was too scared. That cost is larger than the risk of failure. The risk of failure is temporary. The cost of not trying is permanent. I'm learning to ask: what will it cost me if I don't try?
The cost of safety is everything.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
My body is stronger than my fear. I know this now. When I was shaking, I was still standing. When I was scared, I was still speaking. My body kept going even though my mind was screaming stop. That's the definition of courage. Not fearlessness. But moving forward despite the fear signal. My body is proving this to me repeatedly now. Each time I do something brave, my body shows me: look, you're fine. You're still here. You're capable.
My body is braver than I knew.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I'm learning to be the person for myself that brave people are: someone who takes action despite fear. Someone who shows up even when it's hard. Someone who tries even when the outcome is uncertain. When I think about people I admire, they're not fearless. They're just unwilling to let fear dictate their choices. I'm learning to be unwilling too. To decide that my dreams matter more than my comfort. That becoming myself matters more than staying small.
I'm becoming the hero of my own life.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of courage is discomfort. Temporary discomfort. Fear. Uncertainty. The possibility of failure. These are real costs. But they're finite. The cost of not being brave is infinite. It's a lifetime of wondering what could have been. Of regret. Of smallness. I'm learning that the temporary cost is worth it. That discomfort is a small price for freedom. The people I see living fully—doing what they want, becoming who they want—they're not fearless. They've just decided the cost of fear is worth paying.
Courage costs less than regret.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
After doing the brave thing, I notice that I'm still breathing. My heart is still beating. I'm still alive. The fear didn't kill me. The possibility I tried for didn't consume me. The risk didn't swallow me whole. I'm here. Intact. Changed. But here. Each brave thing I do teaches my nervous system: this is survivable. This is what growth feels like. This is how you become.
I survived my own courage.
