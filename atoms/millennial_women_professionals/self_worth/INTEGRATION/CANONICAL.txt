

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I can feel myself occupying space in my own body. That's new. Usually I'm here but not present. Visible but invisible. But today, when someone spoke to me, I noticed I was actually there. My eyes were making contact. My body was responding. I wasn't disappearing. That's progress. Small progress. The kind of progress no one but me would notice. But it's real. It's the beginning of being visible to myself. And that changes something.
I am occupying space in my own life.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
What if my invisibility isn't actually protection? What if the cost of being invisible is that I disappear? What if people can't hurt me if they can't see me, but that also means they can't value me? I'm learning that invisibility isn't safety. It's loss. Loss of presence. Loss of recognition. Loss of the ability to have impact. What if being visible is worth the risk of being judged?
Maybe visibility is its own form of power.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I am still here. That fact alone is something. I do things. I accomplish things. No one acknowledges them, but I do them. The accomplishment doesn't disappear just because it's invisible. The work was still done. The value was still created. Even if no one sees it, it happened. I'm learning to see it. To acknowledge it. To say: I did this good thing and it matters even if no one is watching.
I'm learning to witness myself.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of invisibility is that I feel like I don't matter. Like my life isn't real. Like I'm playing a role instead of living. But that's the cost of choosing invisibility. The cost of not claiming space. The cost of waiting for others to give me permission to exist. What if I just exist anyway? What if my existence doesn't require validation? I'm learning to exist without permission. To take up space. To be visible just because I'm here.
My existence is not up for approval.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I'm learning to be the person for myself that I am for everyone else: someone whose work I notice. Someone I celebrate. Someone I make visible. When a friend does something good, I acknowledge it. I tell them. I make sure they know they matter. I'm learning to do that for myself. To be my own friend. To say: you did a good thing. You matter. You're visible to me at least.
I'm becoming my own witness.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I'm learning that recognition doesn't create value. I already have value. Recognition just reveals the value that's already there. So waiting for recognition is waiting for something that doesn't change the fundamental truth: I'm valuable. I was valuable before anyone acknowledged it. I'm valuable right now even though no one is clapping. The clapping would be nice. But it's not required. I'm learning to be valuable to myself.
My value doesn't depend on visibility.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
What if I could see myself the way others see people they love? With kindness. With attention. With the assumption that everything I do matters? What if I could believe in myself as much as I believe in the people I care about? I'm learning to try. To look in the mirror and see someone worth knowing. Someone worth celebrating. Someone who matters. It's slow work. But it's working.
I'm learning to matter to myself.
