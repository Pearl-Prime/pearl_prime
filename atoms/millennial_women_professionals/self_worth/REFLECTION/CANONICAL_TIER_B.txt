

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Your sense of your own value has become inseparable from productivity. You are what you do, and what you do is measured in output. Email sent. Project completed. Meeting attended. Problem solved. When you're producing, you know who you are. When you're resting, you begin to disappear. Rest feels like proof that you're not essential. That the work doesn't actually require you. That you're replaceable. So you don't rest. You work in ways that keep you indispensable. You take on extra projects. You become the person who knows the system. You stay late. You answer messages on weekends. The mechanism is efficient. Productivity equals worth equals safety. But the cost is that you only feel real when you're working, and even then, only if the work matters enough. Ordinary work doesn't count. It has to be impressive work. Challenging work. The kind of work that proves you're not ordinary.
---

---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a voice in your head that measures your worth minute by minute. Did that interaction matter. Was that meeting productive. Did you move the needle. When you're working toward something visible, toward metrics and outcomes, you feel like you exist. But when you're just being, just existing without producing, the worth drains away. You're not sure if this voice belongs to the world or to you. Probably both. The world definitely taught you to measure yourself this way. But you've internalized it so completely that you can't tell where the external pressure ends and your own drive begins. The mechanism serves you in specific ways. You're accomplished. You're recognized. But you're also exhausted because your sense of self is built on a foundation that requires constant maintenance.
