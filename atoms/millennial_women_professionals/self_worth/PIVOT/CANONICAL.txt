--- variant: v01
You've achieved the career, the title, the salary, and it still doesn't feel like enough. Because the metric for 'enough' keeps moving. Your self-worth got wired to achievement so early that now achievement and worth are the same circuit. When the achievement pauses, the worth collapses. That's not a personal failing. It's a wiring problem.

--- variant: v02
You learned early that your value was tied to being useful, competent, and low-maintenance. High-achieving and easy to work with. The self-worth issue isn't that you don't see your value -- it's that you've defined your value so narrowly that anything outside of performance feels worthless. Rest feels worthless. Needs feel worthless. That's the problem.

--- variant: v03
The comparison trap hits different for women. You're not just comparing yourself to peers -- you're comparing yourself to an impossible standard that's equal parts professional excellence, effortless personal life, and graceful self-care. Your self-worth is being evaluated against a composite that no single human being can embody. The first step is seeing the rigged game.
