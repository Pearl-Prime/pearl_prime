--- variant: v01
The takeaway is that your worth was never the problem. The measurement system was. You've been evaluating yourself against a composite standard that combines career achievement, domestic perfection, physical appearance, and emotional availability. No human being can max out all four. The game is rigged.

--- variant: v02
You don't need to build more self-worth. You need to stop letting external metrics drain what's already there. The takeaway is that self-worth isn't something you earn through accomplishment. It's something you protect from a world that's constantly trying to make it conditional.

--- variant: v03
The takeaway is simple and hard: you are allowed to matter outside of your usefulness. Your worth doesn't depend on what you do for others, what you achieve at work, or how well you manage the impossible. You matter because you're a person. That's the whole sentence.
