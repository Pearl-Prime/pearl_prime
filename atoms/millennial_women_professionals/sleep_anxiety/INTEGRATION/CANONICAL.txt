

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: STILL-HERE
You're lying in the dark and the thought arrives: the thing you're afraid of. You notice it. And instead of fighting it, you stay with it for a moment. You're still here in your body, in the bed, in this specific moment. The fear is present. And so are you. You're not fixed by the fear. You're adjacent to it. That small distinction—presence alongside the fear rather than fusion with it—changes the neurological response. The fear doesn't have to move for you to rest.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: BODY-LANDED
You're lying down and your nervous system is saying: "Not safe. Not safe. Alert." But your body is saying: "I'm tired. I need rest." Listen to your body. Let it be the authority. Your nervous system is trying to protect you from something imagined. Your body is reporting what's actually true: you're safe enough, and you're exhausted. Land in the body. Trust what it's telling you. The anxiety is real. The danger is not.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
What would you tell a friend who texted you at 2am saying they can't sleep and they're worried about tomorrow? You wouldn't tell them to problem-solve harder. You'd probably tell them: "Your body is tired. Let it rest. Tomorrow will be fine. You've handled hard days before. You can handle it." You have access to this wisdom about others. The work is extending it to yourself without judgment.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST-VISIBLE
Every moment spent trying to control tomorrow through nighttime thinking is a moment not spent recovering. You cannot prepare your way into resilience. You can only rest your way into it. The trade-off is clear: anxiety now for imagined control over tomorrow, versus rest now for actual resilience tomorrow. The rest actually works. The anxiety doesn't.
