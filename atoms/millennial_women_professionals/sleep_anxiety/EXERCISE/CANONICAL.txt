## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're in bed but your mind is at work.
Your brain hasn't downloaded from the day.
Give yourself permission to think about tomorrow.
Seriously. Spend five minutes planning tomorrow.
Write down three things you need to do.
Now put the list down.
Your brain's job is handled. It can rest now.
You've given your concern actual attention.
Now sleep can come.
Your nervous system needs completion before rest.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're anxious about sleep itself.
Worried you won't sleep enough. Worried you can't rest.
This anxiety is the thing keeping you awake.
Stop measuring. Stop analyzing your sleep.
Your only job is comfort, not sleep.
Adjust your pillow. Adjust your blanket.
Make yourself as cozy as possible.
When you do that, sleep follows naturally.
You're not hunting sleep. You're creating comfort.
Sleep is a side effect of comfort.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Feel your body from head to toe.
Where's tension? Your shoulders? Your jaw?
Consciously release what you're holding.
Take five slow breaths.
In for four counts. Out for four counts.
Your nervous system will shift toward rest.
You don't need a full hour of wind-down.
You need intentional release.
Tell yourself: work is finished for today.
Tomorrow belongs to tomorrow. Tonight belongs to rest.
---
