--- variant: v01
The takeaway is that the sleep anxiety isn't a nighttime problem. It's a daytime problem that waits for nighttime to present itself. The audit that keeps you awake is the emotional processing your day never had room for. Better sleep starts with a different kind of day, not a different kind of night.

--- variant: v02
You can't productively think your way to sleep. The takeaway is that the mind running scenarios at midnight is doing the emotional labor the day didn't allow. Sleep comes when the system trusts that the labor can wait -- and that trust is built during the day, not at bedtime.

--- variant: v03
The takeaway is that the sleep anxiety is your body's honest review of a life that isn't leaving enough room for you. The solution isn't a better wind-down routine. It's a life that doesn't require winding down from quite so much.
