

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Open your banking app. Look at the total debt. Write it down. The real number. Not an estimate. The real number. Now breathe. You just named it. You can face what you can name. Keep the number. Don't hide it. You can look at it. You can survive looking at it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Make a spreadsheet of all your debts. List every single one. Interest rates. Minimum payments. Everything. When you see it all, it becomes manageable. Right now it's a nightmare in your head. On a spreadsheet it's just numbers. Numbers you can work with.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Take one action on debt. Call a credit card company. Negotiate the interest rate. Just call. One phone call. After the call, you've done something. You've created movement. That matters. You're not stuck. You're moving.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Write down your monthly income. Write down your monthly expenses. The difference is either positive or negative. You need to know the truth. Calculate it. Write it. Now you can plan. You can't plan from ignorance. Now you know.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Find one area to cut. One category where you're spending without thinking. Coffee. Streaming services. Clothes. Find one. Cut it for one month. See how it feels. You just created money. You have agency. You can change things.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
List three skills you have that could make money. Teaching. Writing. Organizing. Design. What can you do. Write three. Now research if you could do any of these as a side gig. You don't have to do it. But know that you could. You have options.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Look at your spending from last month. Really look. You probably spent more than you thought. You probably spent on things you didn't need. That's okay. Don't judge. Just notice. Awareness is the first step. You're aware now. You can change it.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Negotiate one bill. Internet. Insurance. Phone. Call and negotiate. Say: I've been a good customer. Can you lower my rate. Probably they'll lower it. You just saved money. You have power in these conversations. Use it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Cancel one subscription you don't use. Do it right now. You don't need it. Delete it. You just saved money every month. It's small but it's real. Small money is real money. Keep doing this.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Check your credit score. Go to a free website. Look at your score. Write it down. Track it. When you improve it, you'll feel powerful. You're measuring progress. Track it.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Write down one financial goal. Not a big goal. A small goal. Save $500. Pay down one card. Get a side gig. One goal. Write it. Make it real. Now you can work toward it.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Look at your most expensive monthly expense. Is it necessary. Really necessary. Can you reduce it. Move to a cheaper place. Get a cheaper car. Reduce something big. One big reduction changes everything.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Write down your financial values. What matters most. Your home. Your family. Your health. Write three values. Now look at your spending. Does your spending match your values. If not, something needs to change. You're in control of that change.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Create a zero-based budget. Every dollar has a job. Food. Rent. Debt. Transportation. Give every dollar a purpose. When you do this, you feel in control. You're not at the mercy of money. You're directing it.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Research one way to increase your income. Ask for a raise. Get a promotion. Side gig. What's possible for you. Research one option. You don't have to do it yet. But know what's possible. You have agency.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Write down one lie you believe about money. I'll never be free. I'm bad with money. I deserve to struggle. Write the lie. Now write the truth next to it. Read the truth. Believe the truth. Start challenging the lies.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Find one community resource. A free financial counselor. A free workshop. Free budgeting apps. Find one. You don't have to use it yet. But know it exists. You're not alone in this. Help exists.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Review your subscriptions list. How many are active. How many do you use. Cancel the ones you don't. This is money you forgot about. Money you're giving away. Stop giving it away.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Make a list of your fixed expenses. Things you can't change. Rent. Car payment. Insurance. Write them. Add them up. This is your baseline. Now look at everything else. Everything else is flexible. You can change it.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Write down three things you want to buy. Now write how long you'd need to save for each. Two months. Six months. A year. Now pick one. Focus on one. You're creating intention instead of impulse.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Track your spending for one week. Write down every single purchase. You'll be shocked. You're probably bleeding money on small things. Now you know. Now you can stop.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Create a debt payment plan. Not for everything. For one debt. Figure out how much extra you can pay. Put it in writing. You're making progress. You're moving toward freedom. This matters.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Research one program that might help. Student loan forgiveness. Debt consolidation. Relief programs. What exists for your situation. Write down one program. You might qualify. You might not. But you'll know.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Write down your financial wins. Times you paid something off. Times you saved. Times you chose not to spend. Write them. See them. You have agency. You've exercised it. You can do it again.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Set up automatic transfers to savings. Even $25 a month. Set it up. Money goes to savings before you see it. You can't spend money you don't see. Build savings automatically. You have power over this.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Call one creditor. Explain your situation. Ask about hardship programs. Many creditors have them. You might get relief. You won't know if you don't ask. Ask. That's courage.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Write down what would change if you had no debt. How would you feel. What would you do. Write it. This is your future. This is possible. You can get there. You just need a plan.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Find one way to increase your paycheck. Overtime. Promotion. Side gig. What's available. Research one. You might do it. You might not. But you have options. You're not trapped.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Make a list of free things you enjoy. Parks. Libraries. Community events. Write ten. Now do one every week. You don't need money to have a good life. You're learning that.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Write down your financial story. How you got here. What you learned. What you need to change. Write it. You're not a victim of money. You're navigating it. That's agency. That's real.
