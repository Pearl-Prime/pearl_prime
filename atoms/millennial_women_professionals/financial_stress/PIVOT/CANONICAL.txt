--- variant: v01
You're carrying financial stress while earning a salary your younger self would have been thrilled about. The gap between 'I should be fine' and 'I'm not fine' is where the shame lives. Student loans, care costs, the invisible tax of being the family member everyone assumes can help because you 'have a good job.' Financial stress in high-achieving women often hides behind the appearance of success.

--- variant: v02
The financial stress gets compounded by the emotional labor of managing it alone. The budgeting, the calculating, the quiet panic that you can't share because admitting financial struggle contradicts the image of the woman who has it all figured out. The stress isn't just the money. It's the isolation of carrying it invisibly.

--- variant: v03
You did everything you were told to do -- the degree, the career, the promotions. The financial stress wasn't part of the deal. The gap between what you were promised and what you're living is its own kind of betrayal. Acknowledging that betrayal isn't bitterness. It's clarity about a system that sold you a contract and changed the terms.
