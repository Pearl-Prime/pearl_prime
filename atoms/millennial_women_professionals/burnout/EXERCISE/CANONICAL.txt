## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're running on empty and pretending you're fine.
Stop. Your body knows the truth.
Close your work calendar for the rest of the week.
Just look at what you've actually committed to.
Notice: you're not a superhuman.
Block one hour for rest this week.
Actually write it in. Treat it like a meeting.
When that time comes, don't work.
Your rest isn't selfish. It's maintenance.
You'll work better when you're not depleted.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're burned out but don't know how to stop.
You don't need a vacation. You need a boundary.
Set a work cutoff time today.
Email doesn't get checked after 6pm.
Slack doesn't get answered after 6pm.
Tell your team this boundary clearly.
Most of them will be relieved you set it.
They're burned out too.
Your boundary gives permission for their boundaries.
This is leadership, not abandonment.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Notice one thing you're doing out of obligation.
Not out of actual desire or responsibility.
You do it because you think you should.
Stop doing that thing this week.
Notice what happens. Probably nothing catastrophic.
Your "should" list is exhausting you.
Start asking: Do I actually want this?
Not "should I do this." Actually want.
Your energy is a finite resource.
Spend it on what actually matters to you.
