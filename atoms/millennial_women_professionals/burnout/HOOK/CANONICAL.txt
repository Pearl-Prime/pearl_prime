

## HOOK v01
---

---
You're still answering emails at midnight because no one else will, and you've convinced yourself this is leadership, this is being reliable, this is what it takes to be enough.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The running list in your head never stops: projects, deadlines, being competent, being likable, being the one who doesn't fall apart, being the one they can count on.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
You've optimized everything—sleep schedule, meal prep, workout timing—yet you're still too tired, and the fact that you can't logic your way out of that exhaustion feels like a personal failure.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Burnout doesn't announce itself when you're the kind of person who researches everything and still won't admit you're breaking, who treats tiredness like a project to solve.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You keep waiting for permission to rest, and since no one gives it, you keep running, knowing somewhere that this is unsustainable but unable to be the person who names it.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Your therapist mentioned burnout three sessions ago, but you were too busy defending how much you're actually managing to hear what she was saying.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The moment your body made decisions your brain couldn't override—that's when you realized the system had been running past empty for longer than you'd admitted.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You're performing fine so convincingly that even you forgot it was a performance, that underneath is someone running on fumes and calling it discipline.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The spreadsheet of everything you're responsible for has no empty cells, and you've started making peace with that being permanent.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Burnout arrived so quietly, between the promotion and the project and the trying to prove you deserved both, that you almost missed it.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
You've read the research on burnout, the studies, the warning signs—and you still couldn't admit you were in it until your body made the choice for you.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
The anxiety about not being enough has finally been replaced by something worse: the certainty that you will always be running to keep up.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
You're cycling through the same three productivity systems because surely the right framework will let you do all of this without falling apart.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The thing about being emotionally literate is knowing exactly what's happening to you and feeling completely helpless to stop it anyway.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
You stopped being able to differentiate between tired and broken somewhere around month eight, and now you're not sure which one you are.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
Success looks like this: doing more, proving more, being more—until the day it looks like nothing at all, just the weight of it.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
The guilt about wanting less, about not wanting the thing you fought so hard to get, that's the real exhaustion right there.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You've become the person who apologizes for not being able to work while sick, as if that's somehow a personal shortcoming.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
Burnout wearing a professional blazer, speaking in complete sentences, showing up on time—that's the kind of burnout nobody sees until you do.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
The awareness that you're doing damage to yourself by staying is somehow less motivating than the fear of what happens if you don't.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You keep setting boundaries in theory while systematically honoring none of them, and you know this is the problem and still you cannot stop.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The person you are at work and the person you are at home have become strangers, and you're not sure which one is real anymore.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
You rationalize your exhaustion the way you rationalize everything: it's a phase, it's temporary, it's fine, you're fine, you're handling it.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
The pressure to stay competent while being overwhelmed has become so automatic that you can't separate the ambition from the survival anymore.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You're burnt out and over-functioning at the same time, and this paradox is precisely what keeps you from admitting either one.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
The system doesn't reward slowing down, so you speed up instead, and call it commitment instead of desperation.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
Burnout isn't always a collapse—sometimes it's just the slow disappearance of the person you thought you'd be.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You've mastered the art of looking like you have it together while having less and less of anything together.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The moment you realized you don't remember the last time you enjoyed your work is the moment you stopped being able to lie about how you're doing.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
Burnout when you're the person who fixes things is the loneliest kind, because you're still expected to fix this too.
---
