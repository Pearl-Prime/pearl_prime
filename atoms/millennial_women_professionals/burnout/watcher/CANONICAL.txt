At the task board at 7 pm. Nora notices the split — one part of the self performs while another part judges without mercy. Her hands ball into fists at her sides. The knuckles whiten. For months the frame has held: the watching is necessary because without it everything falls apart. Now Nora watches the frame buckle under evidence it cannot metabolize. Her body registers the shift before her mind can catch up. The chest opens half an inch. Something loosens at the base of her spine. The muscles that held the old posture begin to release. The watching is the interference itself — the monitor is the source of the static, not the cure. The old story does not end with a conclusion. It ends with a silence. Nora stands in that silence. Her hands open at her sides.

The frame that held her world in place tilts on its axis. Priya does not fall. She stands in the vertigo and lets the room reorient around her new position. Layla catches the pattern mid-cycle and holds still long enough to see the entire architecture. Nora pauses while sitting at her desk long after everyone leaves. Her hands go completely numb. Pins and needles race up both forearms. The watcher has run for hours without interruption. Now she sees the machinery laid bare. The watcher creates a permanent split between performer and judge, and the judge never once approves — the observation itself is what contaminates the performance. Nora traces the loop in real time: trigger, activation, the relief that never actually arrives, then the trigger again. Her stomach clenches as she maps the circuit from inside it. Seeing the mechanism does not stop the gears. But it strips the mechanism of its authority. Nora watches the loop turn without feeding it another round.
