

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I can see it happening gradually. The mechanism running underneath is attention residue. I've read the research about psychological depletion—how resources drain like fuel from a tank that wasn't designed to run on empty indefinitely. But reading about the framework is different from watching it reshape your own system. The numbness arrives before the exhaustion. The first sign isn't dramatic collapse. It's a quiet withdrawal from things that used to matter. The presentation for tomorrow that would have kept me energized three months ago now feels like one more task on an infinite list. The accomplishment that meant something last year gets filed away as just part of the job. What's insidious is that the mechanism is automatic. My system learned long ago that productivity equals worth. So when I feel depleted, the solution becomes more production. One more thing. One more achievement. The mechanism doesn't question itself. It just keeps generating until the generating stops feeling like choosing and starts feeling like the only way my system knows how to run.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The numbness is what I notice first. Not the dramatic kind of exhaustion where you have a breakdown and then recover. The creeping kind, where one day you realize you can't remember the last time you felt enthusiastic about anything, including the things you built your career around. The mechanism is allostatic load, and it started as protective. My system learned that if I could just do enough, prove enough, push hard enough, I could earn the safety I needed. Security. The sense of being enough. So I configured myself for high performance. I became the kind of person who takes on projects, who stays late, who pushes through the resistance. For a while this felt good. It felt like agency. It felt like control. But protection has a limit. When the demand becomes infinite and I'm a fixed resource, the mechanism that was supposed to protect me becomes what's destroying me. I'm still pushing, still producing. But the fuel is gone.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
I'm watching the pattern emerge. The mechanism I'm running is ego depletion, and it's automatic. I notice the shift happens gradually. First the numbness. Then the inability to access enthusiasm. Things that would have kept me energized months ago now feel obligatory. The accomplishment gets filed away as part of the job. What's strange is that I understand how this works intellectually. I understand the framework of resource depletion. I can explain the mechanism to other people. But understanding doesn't interrupt the automatic process. My system learned that productivity equals worth. So producing more became the solution to every problem, including the problem of depletion itself. I generate one more thing, and one more, and one more, until the generating stops feeling like choosing and starts feeling like running. The creeping kind of exhaustion where you realize you haven't felt enthusiastic about anything in months.

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
The mechanism is attention residue combined with learned productivity associations. I understand the research about how attention isn't truly divisible. When we shift between contexts, some cognitive resource remains stuck in the previous context. So I arrive at each new task already partially depleted. But that's not the whole mechanism. Underneath is the learned association: productivity equals worth. My system was trained on that equation. So when I feel anything like depletion coming, instead of resting, I produce more. It's automatic. I'm not consciously choosing this response. My system learned that more output was the solution to every problem. More work became the way I signaled my own value. The numbness arrives gradually. The enthusiasm drains slowly. One day I realize I can't remember when I last felt genuinely excited about something. The mechanism keeps running.
---
