--- variant: v01
You're not burned out because you can't handle it. You're burned out because you've been handling it -- all of it -- at a standard that would be unsustainable for anyone, and you've been doing it while making it look effortless. The effortlessness is what burned you out. That performance has a cost that nobody sees because you've been absorbing it perfectly.

--- variant: v02
The burnout doesn't feel like burnout. It feels like 'I just need to get through this week.' You've been getting through this week for three years. At some point, survival mode stopped being temporary and became your entire operating system. That's not resilience. That's a woman who was never given permission to stop.

--- variant: v03
You can't optimize your way out of burnout. You already tried -- the morning routine, the meal prep, the time blocking. The burnout isn't a scheduling problem. It's a structural one: the structure of your life requires more of you than any human being should give, and you've been filling the gap with willpower instead of questioning the structure.
