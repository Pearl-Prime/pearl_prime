Heat climbs through her sternum and spreads across her collarbones. Zara is replaying the comment from the all-hands at 2 AM when she feels the siren fire in a quiet, safe room. The operating assumption — the body never lies about threat — meets a fact it cannot absorb. Her jaw unclenches. Not relief. Disorientation. The frame that held her world in place tilts on its axis. Zara does not fall. She stands in the vertigo and lets the room reorient around her new position. The alarm is signal without source — electricity without event. She does not grasp for a replacement frame. She breathes into the space where the old certainty stood and finds the space survivable.

Leah reconstructs the conversation word by word in bed. Her legs buckle. Leah catches the doorframe before going down. She feels the body hits full emergency protocol with nothing to flee from. The frame that has held everything in place — if the sensation says danger then danger is here — strains under the weight of what Leah sees. For one long second the old architecture holds. Then it cracks. Leah feels the fracture travel through her chest. The story that organized every decision, every reaction, every judgment stops organizing. The body remembers what the present does not contain. Leah does not reach for a replacement. She stands in the gap where the certainty lived, breathing into the new and terrifying openness.
