

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I can name this. I understand the framework. Here's what's happening: The mechanism is recursive thought generation. My mind creates a thought—did I say something weird in that meeting?—and instead of letting it pass, the system starts processing. It branches. It generates scenarios. It rehearses different ways the interpretation could go wrong. This is automatic. I'm not choosing to overthink. My system has learned that if I think through all the possibilities, if I can anticipate all the ways something could go wrong, I can prevent it.
So when a trigger arrives, the thinking starts automatically. The loop activates. What's strange is that I'm aware of it looping. I can watch myself generate scenario after scenario and feel completely unable to interrupt the process. The awareness that I'm overthinking doesn't stop the thinking. It just adds another layer: now I'm thinking about the fact that I'm thinking. And somewhere deep in the recursive stack, there's a belief operating: if I think hard enough about this, if I examine enough variables, I can achieve control over something fundamentally uncontrollable.
