--- variant: v01
The overthinking is doing double duty: processing the actual decision and processing how the decision will be perceived. The takeaway is that when you strip away the second layer -- the perception management -- the decisions get simpler. The complexity was never in the choice. It was in the audience.

--- variant: v02
The takeaway is that the overthinking is a form of labor. Unpaid, unrecognized labor. You're doing cognitive and emotional work that your peers aren't doing because the stakes of getting it wrong are different for you. Naming that labor is the first step toward questioning whether all of it is necessary.

--- variant: v03
You don't overthink because you're indecisive. You overthink because you were taught that women's mistakes cost more. The takeaway is that the pattern was adaptive. It protected you. And now it's consuming you. The skill isn't in stopping it -- it's in choosing when it's worth the cost.
