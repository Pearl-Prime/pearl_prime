## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/millennial_women_professionals/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Her throat closes around the words. The vocal cords tighten. Camille is sitting in the new office that still feels borrowed when she sees the colleague's promotion announcement. The operating assumption — standing relative to others is the only metric that counts — meets a fact it cannot absorb. Her jaw unclenches. Not relief. Disorientation. The frame that held her world in place tilts on its axis. Camille does not fall. She stands in the vertigo and lets the room reorient around her new position. The ladder is a construction, not a landscape. She does not grasp for a replacement frame. She breathes into the space where the old certainty stood and finds the space survivable.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/millennial_women_professionals/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Accepting the title she fought for and now doubts she deserves. Dana sees the ranking arrives and the number is undeniable. Her vision narrows to a single point. The periphery dissolves. For months the frame has held: the ladder determines value. Now Dana watches the frame buckle under evidence it cannot metabolize. Her body registers the shift before her mind can catch up. The chest opens half an inch. Something loosens at the base of her spine. The muscles that held the old posture begin to release. The scoreboard tracks a game nobody consented to play. The old story does not end with a conclusion. It ends with a silence. Dana stands in that silence. Her hands open at her sides.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/millennial_women_professionals/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Reading the promotion email for the fourth time. The base of her skull throbs. The tension radiates forward into her forehead. The comparison pushes with full force against her sternum. Ren closes the ranking tab and opens the project that matters. The body shakes with the effort. The choice holds anyway. This is not willpower overriding sensation. This is ownership absorbing sensation. Ren moves from the new ground she has claimed. Her hands tremble and her hands act. Both facts are true simultaneously. The identity is not a concept. It lives in her muscles, in the steadiness of her spine, in the way her feet stay planted while the old pattern screams for retreat.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/millennial_women_professionals/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Reading the promotion email for the fourth time. Sweat breaks along her hairline. Her shirt collar dampens. The comparison operates in plain view for the first time. Ren catches the pattern mid-cycle and holds still long enough to see the entire architecture. The measuring instrument calibrates itself to find deficiency, discarding accomplishments that do not fit the verdict. The mechanism persists because it disguises itself as protection — it wears the costume of something necessary. Ren watches the loop close and reopen. Her body holds the tension of seeing without intervening. The seeing does not resolve the pattern. But it makes the pattern visible, and visibility changes everything.
