

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I can name the mechanism. The mechanism at work is automatic evidence reframing, and it's so invisible because I've stopped noticing it operating. I accomplish something, and my brain automatically interprets it not as evidence of competence but as evidence that I've somehow fooled people. The pattern runs like this: I accomplish X. The automatic thought arrives: but anyone could have done X. Or, I only succeeded because the circumstances were favorable. Or, they were just being nice in their feedback.
Or, next time they'll discover the truth about my actual capabilities. It's invisible because it's embedded in how I process information. I'm not consciously choosing to distrust my own evidence. My system has learned to distrust it. Any success gets reinterpreted through a lens that confirms the underlying belief: I don't actually belong here. I'm actually quite good at what I do. The results prove this. But the mechanism runs underneath, automatically converting any data point into proof that I'm the exception.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The mechanism is invisible because it operates at the level of automatic thought. I succeed at something significant. My brain reframes the success as luck or favorable circumstances or other people being kind. This isn't a conscious belief I choose. It's how my system automatically processes evidence of competence.
I'm actually quite good at my work. I can see this in the results. I can see it in how people respond to what I produce. But underneath the demonstrated competence is this automatic mechanism that says: this doesn't count as evidence of ability. Any success becomes proof that I've managed to fool people. Every accomplishment gets qualified with caveats. Every recognition gets processed as generosity rather than accurate feedback. I watch people around me who seem to carry their competence easily. They name their accomplishments without caveats. They accept praise without qualification. They seem to believe they deserve their position. I envy this so intensely.
