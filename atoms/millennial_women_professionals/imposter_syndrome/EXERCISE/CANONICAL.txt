## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're in a meeting and feeling like a fraud.
Everyone else seems to know what they're doing.
They don't. They're all slightly faking it.
But you're here because you've earned it.
Name one specific project you led successfully.
One where results were measurable.
That happened. You did that.
Imposter syndrome makes you doubt evidence.
So you have to present evidence regularly.
Write down your professional wins quarterly.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Someone praised your work and you minimized it.
"Oh, I just got lucky." "It was timing."
Stop. That's imposter syndrome talking.
Say instead: "Thank you. I worked hard on that."
Let the compliment land.
You don't have to downplay your wins.
Downplaying keeps you stuck in self-doubt.
Accept the praise. Believe it partially.
That's the middle ground between arrogance and fraud.
Eventually, you'll believe it more.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You're doubting whether you deserve your position.
But you've been there for how long?
If you were truly incompetent, they'd have noticed.
Companies move slowly but they do move.
The fact that you're still here is evidence.
You weren't a mistake. You were a choice.
Someone saw something in you.
You've proven them right through your work.
Imposter syndrome is lying about that.
Trust the evidence more than the feeling.
