--- variant: v01
The imposter syndrome is lying about your capabilities, but it's telling the truth about your environment. The takeaway is that feeling like a fraud in a system that wasn't designed for you isn't delusional. It's perceptive. The work is separating what's true about the system from what's untrue about yourself.

--- variant: v02
The takeaway is that you'll never feel like you 'belong' by achieving more. Belonging isn't earned through performance. It's felt through acceptance -- and the acceptance you need most is your own. No promotion delivers that.

--- variant: v03
You don't have to resolve the imposter syndrome to succeed. You already proved that. The takeaway is that you can stop trying to fix the feeling and start examining the conditions that created it. The feeling is downstream of a reality worth questioning.
