--- variant: v01
You're allowed to be there. In the room, at the table, in the role. You have permission to stop rehearsing your justification for occupying space. You belong there because you're there. Full stop. The imposter syndrome can stay, but it doesn't get a vote.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to accept the compliment, the promotion, the recognition without immediately discounting it. You don't have to deflect. You don't have to attribute it to luck or timing. You're allowed to say 'thank you' and mean it.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to be both excellent and uncertain. Imposter syndrome thrives on the belief that real competence comes with certainty. It doesn't. You have permission to be unsure and still be the right person for this. Certainty was never the requirement.
