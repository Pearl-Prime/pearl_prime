--- variant: v01
The anxiety isn't a sign you're failing. It's the cost of carrying expectations designed for two people by one person who was told she could handle it. The takeaway isn't to manage the anxiety better. It's to question the load that's producing it.

--- variant: v02
You don't need to eliminate the anxiety. You need to stop wearing it as proof of how much you care. The caring was never in question. The question is whether the anxiety is helping you care or burning out the circuits that allow caring to happen.

--- variant: v03
Naming the anxiety is the first thing you've done for yourself in a while that isn't on someone else's behalf. The takeaway is that this awareness -- this moment of seeing the pattern -- is itself a form of self-care. Not the candle-and-bath kind. The structural kind.
