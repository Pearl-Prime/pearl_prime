

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
I said no today and my body immediately felt lighter. That's the signal I was missing. When I say yes to everything, my shoulders live by my ears. When I say no, I can feel my shoulders drop. The cost of yes is physical. I can measure it in tension. I've been paying this cost for so long I thought it was normal to carry this much weight. But my shoulders know better. They're teaching me. When someone asks for something, I can check in with my shoulders: are you willing to carry this? If they tense, the answer is no. My body is smarter than my guilt.
My shoulders know what my guilt hides.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
What changes when I believe that my time belongs to me? What shifts when I stop borrowing my own time to give to other people? I don't know yet. I'm testing it out. Saying no when I want to say no. Protecting my evenings. Not answering texts during dinner. The world hasn't ended. But something in me is shifting. There's more of me here. I'm not spread so thin that I disappear. What if I could keep this? What if I could live in a body that says no without explaining?
Belonging to myself is an experiment.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I am here and I am whole. I don't need to break myself into pieces to satisfy everyone. I can say no and still be a good person. I can prioritize myself and still love people. I can have boundaries and still have relationships. These things don't cancel each other out. The guilt I feel when I set boundaries is old programming. It's telling me that my worth is conditional on my usefulness. But I'm learning that worth is not conditional. I exist. That's enough. The people who leave when I set boundaries were leaving anyway. They were using me.
My boundaries protect my wholeness.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of saying yes to everything is that I have nothing left for myself. No energy for my own projects, my own rest, my own becoming. I've been running at the expense of my own life. The people I'm helping aren't going to help me become myself. That's not their job. That's my job. And I've been outsourcing it. Trading my own development for someone else's comfort. That math never worked. It worked for them. It doesn't work for me.
The price of endless yes is yourself.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I'm learning to be the person for myself that I am for everyone else. The one who says: you can rest. You don't have to earn your place here. You don't have to perform usefulness. You're enough just as you are. When I say this to others, they relax. But I never say it to myself. I'm learning to be my own safe person. To tell myself: you can say no. You're allowed to prioritize yourself. Your boundaries are not mean. They're necessary. They're how you stay alive.
I'm becoming the friend I needed.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I can feel the difference when I say no and mean it. My body is congruent. My shoulders relax. My voice is steady. I'm not performing anything. I'm just here, intact, whole. This is what integrity feels like in my body: the alignment of what I say and what I mean and what I need. I've been fragmented for so long—saying yes while meaning no, smiling while resenting, performing while disappearing—that I forgot what wholeness felt like. I'm remembering. Boundary by boundary, no by no, I'm coming back to my body.
My body rewards the truth I tell.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of not having boundaries is that I lose track of where I end and other people begin. I don't know what I want because I'm too busy managing what everyone else needs. I don't know who I am because I'm whoever everyone needs me to be. My boundaries aren't walls. They're the lines that let me exist as a person instead of disappearing into the service of others. They're how I stay myself. Every no is an act of self-definition. I'm learning who I am by learning what I won't do.
Boundaries are how I become visible.
