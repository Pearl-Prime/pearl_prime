--- variant: v01
The boundary isn't the hard part. The hard part is the identity shift that comes with it. You've been the woman who says yes. Becoming the woman who says no requires mourning the version of yourself who got love by being limitless. That mourning is the real work.

--- variant: v02
Boundaries don't make you less caring. They make your caring sustainable. The takeaway is that unlimited availability is not generosity. It's self-erasure with good PR. The boundary is the first thing you've built that's actually for you.

--- variant: v03
The guilt after setting a boundary is not evidence you did something wrong. It's the withdrawal symptom of a people-pleasing pattern that was running your life as a hidden background process. The guilt will fade. The boundary will remain. One of those things serves you.
