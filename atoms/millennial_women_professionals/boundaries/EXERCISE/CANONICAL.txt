

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Someone just asked you for a favor. Your first instinct is yes. Stop. Pause. Don't say yes yet. Take a breath. Count to five. Now ask yourself: Do I want to do this. Is this my responsibility. Will I resent this later. If the answer to any is yes, you must say no. Your voice should be: I can't do this. Don't explain. Don't apologize. I can't do this. The word no is a complete sentence. Don't add anything to it. Hang up. Send the message. You're done.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're in a group chat and someone is making a demand. Don't respond immediately. Close the chat. Don't look at it. Leave the phone in another room. Wait one hour. When you come back, read it again. Now you're not reactive. Now you can decide. If your answer is no, type it out. Type: I'm not available for this. Don't give reasons. No reasons. Just no. Send it. Put the phone down. Don't wait for a response. Boundaries don't require agreement.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Your boss asks you to take on a project. You're at capacity. Instead of saying yes, say this: Let me check my capacity. I need to verify I can do this well. Don't say yes in the moment. Go back to your desk. Check your schedule. Realize that you can't. Send an email: I've reviewed my capacity and I can't take this on right now. Don't apologize. Not now is enough. Send it. Your boss will find someone else. You're not responsible for finding them.

## EXERCISE v04

---
