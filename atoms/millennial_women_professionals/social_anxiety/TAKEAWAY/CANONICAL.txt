--- variant: v01
The social anxiety is information, not a character flaw. It's telling you that the difference between your professional self and your personal self has grown so wide that bridging it feels exhausting. The takeaway is that the gap needs attention, not the anxiety.

--- variant: v02
The takeaway isn't that you need to be more social. It's that you need social contexts that don't cost you performance energy. The anxiety is highest in settings where you feel obligated to be 'on.' Finding spaces where you can be 'off' is the actual solution.

--- variant: v03
You're not antisocial. You're depleted. The takeaway is that social anxiety in high-performing women is often a resource problem, not a personality problem. When you have something left to give, connection feels good. When you don't, it feels threatening. The variable isn't the people. It's your reserves.
