Priya stands at the happy hour she almost skipped three times. The muscles in her back seize from the base of the skull downward. She opens the feed and finds the milestone post. The frame that has held everything in place — standing relative to others is the only metric that counts — strains under the weight of what Priya sees. For one long second the old architecture holds. Then it cracks. Priya feels the fracture travel through her chest. The story that organized every decision, every reaction, every judgment stops organizing. The metric measures nothing real — it is a machine for producing inadequacy. Priya does not reach for a replacement. She stands in the gap where the certainty lived, breathing into the new and terrifying openness.

Pressure mounts behind her eyes like a wave building against a wall. Camille walks into the networking event alone. The comparison runs its program at full speed. Camille watches with the detachment of someone reading their own diagnostic. The mechanism reveals itself in full: the comparison operates with surgical precision, choosing evidence of shortfall while ignoring every counter-example. Entering the room where every conversation is already in progress. Priya feels the ground tilt under her feet. Vertigo without motion. The comparison operates in plain view for the first time. Priya catches the pattern mid-cycle and holds still long enough to see the entire architecture. The ranking system uses rigged criteria — it selects only data that confirms inferiority and discards everything else. The mechanism persists because it disguises itself as protection — it wears the costume of something necessary. Priya watches the loop close and reopen. Her body holds the tension of seeing without intervening. The seeing does not resolve the pattern. But it makes the pattern visible, and visibility changes everything.
