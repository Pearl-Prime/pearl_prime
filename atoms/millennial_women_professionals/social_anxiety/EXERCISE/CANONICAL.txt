## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're about to enter a work social event.
Your nervous system is ramped up.
Before you go in, ground yourself.
Feel both feet on the floor.
Press them down deliberately for five counts.
Feel your body's weight. You're solid.
You're allowed to just exist in the room.
You don't need to be charming or brilliant.
You just need to show up and be human.
That's enough. Actually, it's more than enough.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're at the event and feeling self-conscious.
This feeling is your nervous system misfiring.
Not an actual threat. Just a false alarm.
Set one small intention: listen to one person.
Ask them something real. Let them talk.
You don't need to perform.
Listening is actually more interesting than talking.
People feel seen when someone listens.
You'll make a better impression this way.
And you'll feel calmer because you're not performing.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You feel like you don't belong in the room.
This is imposter syndrome meeting social anxiety.
But you were invited. You belong here.
Find one person who seems approachable.
Say one genuine thing.
Maybe it's a question. Maybe it's a comment.
One real contribution to one conversation.
That's your win for the event.
Forget networking or impressing anyone.
One authentic moment is success.
---
