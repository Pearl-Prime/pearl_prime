## Engine Mechanics

### SHAME
The self-shrinking mechanism. Body becomes smaller. Gap between who she is and who she thinks she should be.
- **Body signals**: Chest collapses inward, shoulders round forward, throat closes, hands pull back
- **Cost**: Identity, internal
- **Intensity**: 2-3

### FALSE_ALARM
Nervous system fires on non-threats. The meeting reads like danger. Email triggers threat cascade.
- **Body signals**: Chest tightens, throat constricts, hands go cold, breath shallows, jaw clenches
- **Cost**: Internal, opportunity
- **Intensity**: 3-4

### OVERWHELM
The cup overflows. Too many inputs, body can't process fast enough. Not weakness—math.
- **Body signals**: Chest heavy, throat closes, stomach drops, hands shake, vision narrows
- **Cost**: Internal, opportunity
- **Intensity**: 3-4

### COMPARISON
Measuring self against curated external. Comparison creates the gap. Gap creates longing or shame.
- **Body signals**: Chest tightens, shoulders droop, jaw clenches, stomach twists, hands cold
- **Cost**: Social, identity
- **Intensity**: 2-3

### SPIRAL
The thought finds its twin. Each thought summons the next. Spiral accelerates.
- **Body signals**: Chest racing, throat tight, hands trembling, shoulders tense, stomach churning
- **Cost**: Internal, social
- **Intensity**: 3-4

### WATCHER
The observer self. Double consciousness: experiencing + narrating simultaneously.
- **Body signals**: Chest feels distant, throat disconnects, hands feel separate, watching from ceiling
- **Cost**: Identity, opportunity
- **Intensity**: 2-3

### GRIEF
What is lost or never was. Grief for the version unlived.
- **Body signals**: Chest aches deeply, throat heavy, hands feel empty, shoulders sag, weight settles
- **Cost**: Identity, social
- **Intensity**: 2-3

## Content Standards

### Required
- Third person present tense
- Precise body sensations (mandatory: chest, throat, hands, shoulders, jaw, stomach)
- Short, declarative sentences
- Character names from cast
- Realistic professional/personal scenarios
- Cost types vary: internal, social, identity, opportunity
- Progression through identity stages

### Prohibited
- Therapy language ("healing," "journey," "processing")
- Affirmations or inspirational framing
- Terms: "brave," "courageous," "growth mindset," "toxic," "trauma response," "self-care," "boundaries" (as advice), "empower"
- Abstract philosophical language
- Future-focused hopeful narratives
- Generic women's empowerment language

### Characteristics
- Unsentimental
- Precise sensory detail
- Nervous system language (not psychological jargon)
- Recognition of structural barriers (not personal failings)
- Honest about costs
- Clear cause-effect relationships
- Multi-generational awareness
