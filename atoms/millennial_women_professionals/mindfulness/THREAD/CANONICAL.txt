## THREAD v01
---
---
Jae starts keeping a private tally, just a tick mark on a sticky note each time he catches himself drifting and comes back in a meeting. By Thursday there are eleven tick marks and, oddly, he feels better about the week than he has in a month — not because he was more focused, but because he can finally see the returns instead of only feeling the drifts. He doesn't tell anyone about the sticky note. It's not for anyone else.

## THREAD v02
---
---
After the meeting where she finally asked her manager to repeat something instead of faking it, Sofia notices a small, unexpected side effect: she's less tired at the end of the day. She can't quite explain why until it hits her — performing attention while actually being elsewhere took more energy than either being present or admitting she wasn't. The admission was the cheaper option all along.

Kevin says, half-joking, "so I stop cheating on the meeting I'm already in." His teammate laughs, then quietly does the same thing to their own monitor the next day.

She can't close the doorway. But she's gotten fast at noticing when she's walked through it, and faster still at walking back.
