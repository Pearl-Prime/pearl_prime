## INTEGRATION v01
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one recurring meeting — the daily stand-up, the 1:1, whichever one your mind treats as background noise for a second screen — and give yourself exactly one job in it: notice the first time you drift, and come back once. Not the whole meeting, not every drift, just the first one. When you catch it — mid-sentence, mid-nod, wherever — don't narrate why you left or apologize internally. Just put your hands flat on the desk or feel your feet on the floor, and look at the actual person talking, not your own thumbnail, not the notification stack in the corner. That's the entire assignment. If you catch a second drift later in the same meeting, treat it as a bonus rep, not a requirement. The goal isn't a spotless meeting — that was never available to anyone, including the people who look the most "engaged" on your team. The goal is one clean return, done on purpose, that you can actually remember afterward. If it works, you'll know by a small, specific tell: you'll be able to describe what was actually said in that meeting, later, to someone else, in your own words, instead of reconstructing it from your notes or guessing. That's the whole proof. Carry that one rep into the meeting after it, and the one after that — not by forcing every meeting to be perfect, but by trusting that the return, repeated, is the only version of presence that was ever actually buildable.
