--- variant: v01
The grief is real even when the loss is ambiguous. You don't need a funeral to validate what you lost. The takeaway is that women's grief often gets dismissed because it doesn't fit the standard categories. Lost opportunities, abandoned dreams, eroded relationships -- those are real losses that deserve real mourning.

--- variant: v02
The takeaway from grief isn't a lesson. Not yet. The lesson comes later, if it comes at all. Right now, the takeaway is just: this hurts, and the hurt is valid, and you don't have to convert it into growth or meaning on anyone else's timeline.

--- variant: v03
You've been grieving while performing. The takeaway is that the performance doesn't cancel the grief. It just delays it. And delayed grief compounds. The sooner you let yourself feel it -- without a productivity wrapper around it -- the sooner it can move through instead of settling in.
