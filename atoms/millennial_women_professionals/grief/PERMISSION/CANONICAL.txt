--- variant: v01
You're allowed to grieve without being strong about it. Without the graceful mourning, the eloquent lessons, the 'I'm processing it well.' You have permission to grieve messily, angrily, slowly, and without a narrative that makes other people comfortable.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to grieve ambiguous losses. The career path not taken. The relationship that faded. The version of yourself you left behind. These don't need death certificates to be real. You're allowed to mourn what you traded away.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to take time for grief without justifying it as self-improvement. Grief doesn't need to produce growth to be valid. You have permission to be sad for as long as you're sad, without packaging it into a story about resilience.
