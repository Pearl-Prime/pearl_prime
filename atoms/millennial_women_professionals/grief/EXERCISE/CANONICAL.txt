

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Set aside time to grieve. Not random moments. Scheduled grief. Wednesday evening. Saturday morning. Put it on your calendar. When grief time comes, feel it. Don't try to fix it. Just feel. The feeling gets smaller when scheduled. Chaos around grief makes it larger.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Create a space for the dead person. A shelf. A drawer. A corner. Put things there. Photos. Letters. Objects they loved. This person has a place. The place says: You matter. You still matter.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Write a letter to the dead person. Say everything. Rage. Love. Confusion. Say it all. Then read it. Then burn it or keep it. The writing is the work. Do it.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Share a memory. Tell someone something you remember. How they laughed. What they said. How they moved. Keep them alive in story. Stories are how the dead live. Keep telling them.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Light a candle. For one hour. Sit with the candle. Think about the person. You don't have to do anything. Just sit. The candle is a ritual. Ritual helps grief. Do this weekly.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Make a playlist of songs the person loved. Listen to it. Hear them in the music. They live in music. They live in memory. Keep them alive through song.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Do something the person taught you. Cook their recipe. Use their technique. Your hands are their hands. Your actions are their legacy. You're keeping them alive through action.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Say their name out loud. Don't avoid it. Say it. Hear yourself saying it. The person is real. Their name is real. Say it every day. Keep them real.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Make a donation in their name. Money. Time. Something that matters. You're creating meaning from death. You're honoring them by changing the world a little. Do this.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Sit in their absence. Don't try to fill it. Sit in the empty space. This is what grief is. This is what loss is. The space is proof they were real. Sit in it.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Create an object that represents them. A painting. A piece of writing. A garden. Something made. Creation helps grief. Creation is alive. The object keeps them alive.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Visit a place that meant something to them. Or you together. Don't hide from it. Go. Feel the grief. Let the place hold you. The place remembers them too.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Ask someone about them. Ask a friend. A family member. Ask them to tell a story. Listen. They live in other people's memories too. Keep collecting memories. The person grows in your collection of memories.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Let yourself cry. Don't be strong. Don't be fine. Cry. Hard. Long. Your tears are love moving through your body. Tears are the language of grief. Speak it. Cry.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Look at a photo. Really look. Study their face. See them. They were real. They existed. The photo is proof. Look at it until you feel the grief move. Then close it. You've done the work.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Write about what you miss most. Not the death. What you miss. Their laugh. Their advice. Their presence. Write what you miss. The missing is love. Keep the love alive.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tell someone how you're grieving. I'm sad today. I'm angry today. I'm missing them. Name it. Don't pretend you're fine. Someone needs to know the real you. Tell them.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Make a meal from their favorite ingredients. Or their favorite dish. Eat it slowly. Taste them in the food. They're in taste and memory. Taste them. Remember them.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Create a ritual for hard days. Anniversary. Birthday. Just hard days. Do something that honors the person. Light a candle. Call a friend. Write. Do the ritual. Rituals carry us through hard days.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Pick a star. Or a place. Say: That's yours. You're keeping them in the sky. You're keeping them in the world. Your person is part of the world now. They live in your designation. That matters.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Write down how they changed you. What you learned. How they shaped you. Their impact lives in your body. Your actions carry their legacy. Write it. See it. Honor it.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Feel the anger. At them. At God. At the universe. Anger is grief too. Let yourself be angry. The anger means you cared. The anger is love. Feel all of it.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Create space in your life for grief. Don't try to move on too fast. Grief takes time. Years. A lifetime. Give it time. The time doesn't heal. The time teaches you to live with it.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Wear something of theirs. If possible. A shirt. A scarf. Their smell is a comfort. Their physical presence is a comfort. Wear them. They're close. That helps.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Plan something in their honor. A party. A donation. A trip. Something that celebrates them. They're gone but not forgotten. The action proves they're not forgotten. Do it.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Say goodbye differently. Not once. Every time. When you remember them, say goodbye. It's a ritual. It's a way of loving. Say goodbye. Say see you later. Say I miss you. This is the conversation now.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Plant something in their memory. If possible. A tree. Flowers. Something alive. Something that grows. They're in the growth. They're in the alive thing. Plant it.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Accept that grief doesn't end. It changes. It softens. But it doesn't end. That's okay. The grief is your love continuing. The grief is them still being in your life. Accept it. Welcome it.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Share their story. Tell people who didn't know them. Keep their story alive. They lived. They mattered. Keep saying that. Keep telling the story. That's your job now. That's how you love them.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Breathe them in. Close your eyes. Remember them completely. Their full person. Not just the absence. The whole person. Breathe them in. They're part of you. They always will be. Breathe.
---
