--- variant: v01
You're grieving something nobody gave you a funeral for. The version of your life you planned. The career trajectory that shifted. The relationship that ended or never started because there was never enough time. Women's grief often gets filed under 'that's just how it is.' It's not. It's a loss, and you're allowed to call it one.

--- variant: v02
The grief sits in the gap between what you chose and what you gave up to choose it. The promotion that cost the relationship. The caregiving that cost the career momentum. The optimization that cost the spontaneity. Every achievement has a shadow cost, and nobody taught you to grieve what you paid.

--- variant: v03
You're mourning something you can't name because it was never fully yours to begin with. The ease your male colleagues seem to have. The option of being ambitious without the invisible surcharge. The grief isn't about what you lost. It's about what you were never given -- and the exhaustion of building it yourself.
