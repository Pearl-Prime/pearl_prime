

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I can feel my grief in my chest. It's a physical weight. Not metaphorical. Actual. It lives where my heart is. And I'm learning to let it stay there instead of trying to move it. The urge is always to fix the grief, to resolve it, to make it smaller. But grief isn't meant to be fixed. It's meant to be carried. I'm learning to carry it. To move through my days with this weight in my chest and know that the weight is how the person stays close.
My grief is my love continuing.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
What if grief never ends? What if it just becomes part of who I am? What if the person stays with me not as they were but as an absence that shapes everything? I'm learning to stop expecting grief to resolve. To stop waiting for the day when I won't cry about them. That day isn't coming. And that's okay. The grief is the proof that they mattered. The sadness is the evidence of love.
The grief proves the love was real.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
I am still here even though they're not. That's what I'm sitting with. The injustice of continuing. Of having more days. Of living a life they don't get to live. Sometimes the grief is less about missing them and more about the unfairness of being alive when they're not. I don't know how to make peace with that. I'm learning to live with the injustice. To not require myself to understand why I'm still here. Just to be grateful that I am.
I'm here and they're not, both true.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The person is gone and that fact doesn't get smaller. Every day I wake up and remember again. There's no adaptation. The grief doesn't soften. But my body is learning that this is sustainable. That I can feel this loss and still make coffee. That I can miss them and still go to work. That I can be grieving and living at the same time. They're not mutually exclusive. My body is learning to hold both.
Grief and living are not opposites.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: MECHANISM
---
I'm learning to be the person for myself that this loss requires. Someone who honors memory. Someone who tells the stories. Someone who doesn't try to move on too fast. Someone who understands that carrying them forward is how they stay alive. I'm learning to be patient with my own grief. To not judge myself for how long it's taking. To see my grief as loyalty.
My grief is how I love them.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: COST-VISIBLE
---
The cost of loss is that I will carry this forever. The cost is that some days will surprise me with sadness. The cost is that I can't go back. I can only move forward with them as a ghost. I can only live a life that includes their absence. That's what I'm paying for having loved them. And I would pay it again. That's what grief teaches: that the cost of loss is worth the fact that they existed.
Loss is the price of love.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
What if I'm supposed to feel this sad? What if the sadness is the right response to losing someone? What if my body is honoring them correctly by refusing to move on? I don't need to get over this. I just need to live with it. To let them live inside my ongoing life as a memory, a story, an influence. To understand that they're here as an absence. That absence is a form of presence. That grief is attention. That continuing to love them is how they stay.
The dead live on in our grief.
---
