Chen declines an extra shift. Maria waits for the fallout. The charge nurse reassigns the shift to another nurse. Nothing breaks. No icy silence. No passive-aggressive comments. The feared catastrophe doesn't materialize. Yet Maria feels confused, as though she's witnessed an anomaly. The fear lives deeper than logic. Chen is just luckier, or braver, or the rules don't apply to Chen the same way they apply to Maria.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The mechanism is this: If I set limits, people will feel abandoned, and relationships will be damaged. The proof against this mechanism is everywhere. Chen sets limits. Relationships persist. Keisha sets limits. The family dynamic improves. Tomas sets limits. He's integrated into the unit. Yet Maria's mind cannot absorb the proof. The mechanism is not based on logic. It's based on a deep belief about her own nature: that she is responsible for others' feelings, and that her worth is measured by her ability to meet unlimited needs. Against this belief system, the evidence of colleagues thriving with boundaries feels like an exception that proves the rule.
