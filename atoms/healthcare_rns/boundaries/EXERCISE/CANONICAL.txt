## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Someone just asked you to do something extra.
Before you say yes, pause for five seconds.
Actually pause. Don't immediately commit.
Ask yourself: Do I have capacity?
Not "should I help." Do I have capacity?
Say the answer out loud quietly.
If it's yes, say yes. If no, say no.
You're allowed to say: I don't have time.
You're allowed to say: I'm at capacity.
This isn't unkind. This is honest.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You just agreed to something you don't want.
Stop. It's not too late.
Go back to that person if possible.
Say: I realized I'm at capacity right now.
I can't take this on properly.
Your original yes doesn't define you forever.
It's okay to re-evaluate in real time.
Boundaries aren't fixed. They adjust.
Your job is your job. Theirs is theirs.
You can care and still say no.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before your shift, write down your scope.
What are you actually responsible for?
What belongs to someone else?
Keep this written somewhere you can see it.
When someone asks for something, check your list.
Is it on your list?
If not, it's not your job.
You don't have to explain why you're saying no.
A kind no is enough.
Boundaries protect you so you can care better.
---
