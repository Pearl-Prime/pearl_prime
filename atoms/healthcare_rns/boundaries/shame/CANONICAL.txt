---
Keisha declines an extra shift. She states it matter-of-factly. Maria feels a visceral reaction. Not anger. Not confusion. Shame. A shame that settles into her chest and radiates outward. Keisha is doing something Maria cannot imagine doing. The shame is not about Keisha. It's about the gap between who Maria is and who Keisha is. Keisha is someone who knows her limits. Maria is someone who doesn't. Or worse, Maria is someone who knows her limits and violates them anyway. The shame colors the observation. It makes Keisha's boundary-setting feel like a rebuke.

Alicia finds Maria in the break room. She sits down and tells Maria about her own shame around boundaries. How she felt broken. How she thought she was the only one who struggled.
