I want to name something about depression that looks completely different in someone whose job requires them to function, and that difference matters. In other contexts, depression looks like what people expect depression to look like: the inability to get out of bed, the cessation of self-care, the visible withdrawal from activity. But the nurse with depression looks fine. She goes to work. She does the tasks. She provides adequate care. She is present. The mechanism is the compartmentalization of depression into a space that is separate from function. The depression is running in the background but the forefront is occupied by the job. The depression is there but the performance is also there.
