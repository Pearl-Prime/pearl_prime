

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You moved through the shift numb."
---
The day happened and you were there for it and you weren't there. The watcher was watching. The movements were automatic. The words came out. The assessment was correct. The distance between you and the work was complete. That's not laziness. That's depression. The body still moves. The mind doesn't follow. You feel your legs underneath you. Your breathing is shallow. This flatness is not you failing the work. This is the work you're doing from inside a smaller, quieter version of yourself. The depression isn't the absence of will. It's the presence of distance. You're showing up. The numbness shows up with you.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The cost is watching from outside."
---
You clock in and you're present and you're also not there. The dissociation is a tool. It lets you work when the feeling is too heavy. But it has a price. You drive home and can't remember the drive. You sit with your family and can't locate the warmth. You look at the work you chose — the meaning you came for — and it looks flat. Like all meaning has been drained out of it. The institution doesn't see this. The depression is invisible when you're functioning. You're charting. You're present at report. You're managing the patients. Nobody can see that you've left yourself behind somewhere between arrival and the first patient room.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You showed up when showing up felt impossible."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
There were weeks when the thought of putting on scrubs felt insurmountable. When calling in felt easier than facing the parking garage. When the work that used to feel like calling now felt like obligation with no reason behind it. You showed up anyway. Not because you felt good. Not because you found meaning in it. Just because you made the commitment and breaking it required energy you didn't have. The depression didn't stop you. It just changed how you moved through it. The work happened. You happened. Neither with the lightness you remember, but they happened.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Is the numbness you or the depression speaking."
---
The question is: when you look at the work now and feel nothing, is that the work failing you or is it the depression speaking. Is the flatness real information about the career or temporary distortion. You can't tell from inside it. The depression is a good liar. It tells you the meaning is gone and you should believe it. But it also tells you everything is hopeless and sometimes that's the depression talking, not truth. The real question isn't whether you should stay. It's how to make any decision when the lens you're looking through is this dark.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "She still thinks she's just tired."
---
She's been showing up numb for months. She tells you she's just tired, just needs vacation, just needs to push through. You can see what she can't yet: the distance. The way she's watching herself work instead of doing the work. The flatness that has moved in and unpacked. She's not tired. She's depressed. But she doesn't know yet, because depression is very good at disguising itself as fatigue. You could tell her. She wouldn't believe you.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The cost is the self watching from outside."
---
Depression costs visibility to yourself. You can see your hands work but not the person doing it. You can move through a shift and then have no memory of the meaning in it. The dissociation is protective and it's also a loss. You keep functioning and you lose the sense of functioning. The watcher becomes the primary consciousness and you become the thing being watched. That's the cost that nobody measures: not the absence of work, but the absence of you inside the work. The cost is that you're there and not there at the same time.
---
