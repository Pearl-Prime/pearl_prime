## PERMISSION v01
---
---
You are allowed to lose your attention forty times a shift and still call yourself present, as long as you keep coming back.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---
---
You are allowed to measure the shift by how many times you returned, not by how calm or focused you felt the whole way through.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---
---
You are allowed to park a hard thought instead of resolving it before you walk into the next room.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---
---
You are allowed to feel scattered on a short-staffed day and still be doing the practice correctly.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---
---
You are allowed to use a two-second cue instead of waiting for a calmer shift that may never come.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---
---
You are allowed to forget the habit for three rooms and pick it back up on the fourth without starting over.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---
---
You are allowed to be stressed and present at the same time — one does not have to wait for the other.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---
---
You are allowed to not remember every detail of every room, as long as you keep practicing landing in the next one.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---
---
You are allowed to treat a returned attention as a win even when the shift itself never calms down.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---
---
You are allowed to let today's return feel effortful; the ease comes from the reps, not from getting it right the first time.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---
---
You are allowed to give a hard comment or worry a placeholder instead of carrying it into every room for the rest of the shift.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---
---
You are allowed to start this practice on your worst shift instead of waiting for an easier one.
---
