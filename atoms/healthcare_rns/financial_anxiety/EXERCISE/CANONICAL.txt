## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Financial anxiety spirals into catastrophe scenarios.
Stop spiraling. What's actually true right now?
You have this job. This paycheck exists.
Write down what you actually have today.
Not what you might lose. What's here.
Is there food? Is there shelter?
Today's basics are covered. That's the truth.
Tomorrow is a different problem for later.
Your job is managing right now.
Breathe. The catastrophe is not happening now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're panicking about money between shifts.
Pause. What's one urgent money thing?
Write it down. Only one.
The other worries can wait.
Your brain is trying to solve everything simultaneously.
Pick one. Only one gets attention now.
Set a specific time to handle just that one.
Not "whenever you remember." A specific time.
Until then, you're not allowed to think about it.
Your anxiety loses power when you compartmentalize.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Check your actual bank balance right now.
Not your estimate. The actual number.
Write it down. Feel it instead of imagining it.
Is it catastrophic or manageable?
Usually, the real number is better than your fear.
Your anxiety exaggerates. Numbers don't.
When panic hits again, remember this number.
You have proof. You can survive this.
Repeat: I can work with what I have.
This is true. Stay with the truth.
---
