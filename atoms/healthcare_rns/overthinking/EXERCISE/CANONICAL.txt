## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're spiraling about something that happened.
Your mind is replaying it endlessly.
Stop. Come back to your senses.
Name five things you can see right now.
Name three things you can touch.
Name two things you can hear.
Your mind was in the past.
Your body is in the present.
The present moment is safe.
Your spiral was just your nervous system misfiring.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're overthinking a conversation or interaction.
You can't change what already happened.
Overthinking doesn't rewrite the past.
It just steals peace from right now.
Feel your feet on the floor.
Press them down deliberately.
Feel your hands. Squeeze them into fists.
Release. Feel the difference.
This grounds you in the present.
The moment that's happening now is fine.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Your mind is creating worst-case scenarios.
This is what anxiety does automatically.
You don't have to believe the story.
Notice the thought: that's my anxiety talking.
Feel your body breathing right now.
In for four. Out for six.
This breath is real. The scenario is imagined.
Watch the thought like a cloud passing.
You don't have to grab it or fight it.
Just let it drift away.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
The chart-replay is running again. Get a piece of paper. Write down three things you actually did chart at end of shift. Specific entries. Real ones. Vital sign times. The medication administered. The note about the family conversation. Now write underneath: "The chart is closed. The next nurse has it. My version of the chart is not a chart, it is a worry. The chart is closed." Read that line out loud. Fold the paper. Set it on the nightstand. The chart-replay is on the paper now. The chart-replay is no longer in your head's bandwidth tonight. If the replay returns, point at the paper.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
The audit just flagged something at 0300. Do this. Sit up. Feet on the floor. Press the heels down for ten counts. Release. Place one hand on your sternum. Out loud, say: "The shift is closed. The chart says what the chart says. The audit found what audits find. The audit is not the verdict. The shift was worked. I worked it. The audit can wait until morning." Do not negotiate with the audit. Just state the boundary. Lie back down. The audit may continue running. The boundary stands regardless. Sleep is allowed to happen on top of an open audit. The audit is just background noise tonight.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
The hand-off replay is on its third lap. Stop. Do this. Get up. Go to the kitchen. Make a cup of warm water with lemon if you have lemon, or just warm water. While the water heats, count the tiles or the seams in the counter or the cabinet pulls. Twenty count. The counting fills the bandwidth the replay was using. Drink the warm water slow. Notice the temperature in the throat. Notice the warmth in the chest. When the cup is empty, go back to bed. The replay may try to restart. If it does, count something else. The mind cannot run a count and a replay in the same lane.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
The disagreement with the doctor is replaying again. Try this. Imagine the disagreement is a small object — a folder, a stone, a piece of paper. Hold the object in your hands. Look at it. Say in your head: "This is the disagreement from [date]. I am holding it now. I am about to set it down." Place the imagined object on a shelf — high, low, doesn't matter. Step back. The disagreement is on the shelf. You did not throw it away. You set it down. If it calls you back, you can pick it up tomorrow. Tonight it stays on the shelf. Lie back down.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
The outcome-uncertainty replay starts. The "what happened to that patient." Try this. Get a piece of paper. Write the patient's first initial and the date you cared for them. Write one sentence about what you saw and what you did. One sentence. Now write: "After [date], this patient was cared for by other competent clinicians. The story continued without me. The not-knowing is part of the job." Read that line out loud. Fold the paper. Save it or burn it. Either is fine. The point was the writing, not the keeping. Lie back down.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
The self-audit is louder than usual tonight. Try this. Walk to the bathroom. Run cold water over your wrists for thirty seconds. Then warm water for thirty seconds. Then cold again for fifteen. The temperature shift resets some of the vagal tone the audit is exploiting. Dry the wrists. Look in the mirror. Out loud, say: "The shift was twelve hours. The shift was worked. The audit can have ten minutes tomorrow morning if it still has anything to say." Then go back to bed. The audit has been given an appointment. It does not have to keep speaking through the night.
