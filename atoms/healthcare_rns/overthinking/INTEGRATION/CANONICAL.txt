## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Did you miss something in the chart?"
---
You're replaying the shift. The patient. The chart. The medications. The moment where you said something and they said something and you're trying to remember if you explained it clearly or if they understood or if you missed something that would have changed the outcome.

Your shoulders are tight. Your hands feel it. Your body is still at work even though the shift ended hours ago. Your nervous system is still in clinical mode.

You made the calls. You gave the care. You did the assessments. The shift is done. But your brain is still moving through the room, still checking the chart, still wondering if you missed it.

Notice your hands right now. Still. The shift is over. The patient is stable or they're not, and it won't change by replaying it.

Your hands are finished. Your brain doesn't know.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You never stop auditing yourself."
---
You gave the right medication. You checked the vitals. You reported the changes. You did the shift. But you're still auditing it, still reviewing the moments, still checking the chart in your memory.

This costs you. Not the work — you did the work. The endless self-audit. The part of you that never clocked out. The hours after the shift where your brain is still in the room, still checking, still wondering if you missed something.

Every single shift ends this way. The work ends. The second-guessing doesn't. So you go home exhausted not from the work, but from the hours of re-checking you can never finish.

That's what the job costs you beyond the hours you're paid for.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if you caught what mattered?"
---
You're replaying the shift looking for what you might have missed. But what if you didn't miss it? What if you got the important things and the patient is stable because you did your job?

What if the chart is actually fine and you're just in nurse mode, where missing something is the catastrophe that never stops getting checked?

What if you were competent and the shift went the way it was supposed to go?

What if there is nothing to find?

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "They would tell you if something was wrong."
---
Your patient would let you know. Your team would flag it. Your supervisor would catch it. Everyone on the unit is moving past that shift. Nobody else is replaying it.

But you are. You're the only one still in that room auditing the moment. Everyone else has moved on to the next shift, the next patient, the next thing.

They don't see you still checking. They just see a nurse who did the work. They don't see the endless audit running in your head.

You're the only one still there.

---

The patients you treated are still here. The ones who recovered, recovered. The ones who didn't — that wasn't one missed thing, that was a system.

You're still showing up to the next shift. You're still capable. You're still doing the work. The anxiety about missed things has been there your whole career and so has your competence.

The shift is behind you. You're still here, still moving forward, still the nurse everyone trusts.

That's what you do.
