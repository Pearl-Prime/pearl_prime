--- variant: v01
You can walk into a room of crashing patients and take charge without hesitation. Put you in a room of strangers at a social event and your nervous system fires the same alarms. The clinical version of you has a protocol. The social version doesn't — and the absence of protocol is the trigger.

--- variant: v02
The old frame says social anxiety means you're shy. You're not shy. You're someone who spends twelve hours performing competence and has nothing left for performing normal. The social discomfort isn't about social skills. It's about capacity.

--- variant: v03
At the hospital, the roles are clear — nurse, patient, physician, family. Outside the hospital, the roles are ambiguous, and your hypervigilant brain reads ambiguity as threat. Social anxiety in a nurse is often just clinical assessment skills deployed in the wrong theater.
