

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm around people all day at work. Patients. Families. Colleagues. And I'm performing the entire time. I'm the competent nurse. The compassionate provider. The person who has it together. And then I come home and I don't want to see anyone. I don't want to perform anymore. But the performance doesn't stop. I'm still monitoring. I'm still managing my presentation. The mechanism here is called exhaustion-based social withdrawal—the way social anxiety deepens when the performance is required by the job and there's no escape from it.
The caring profession requires constant emotional performance.
