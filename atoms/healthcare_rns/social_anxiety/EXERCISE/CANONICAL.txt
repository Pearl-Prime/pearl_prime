## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're about to enter the break room.
Your nervous system is ramping up anxiety.
Before you go in, ground yourself completely.
Feel both feet on the floor.
Press down for five solid counts.
Feel the earth holding you up.
You're safe in the break room.
These are colleagues. They're not threats.
Take one breath. Walk in.
You don't need to perform. Just exist.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You're with people and feeling self-conscious.
Notice: they're not scrutinizing you.
They're thinking about their own stuff.
Stop trying to say the right thing.
Try listening instead. Ask one question.
Let someone talk. You just listen.
Listening is a skill. You have it.
You don't need to be entertaining.
Being present is enough.
Your anxiety will quiet when you listen.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Social anxiety says everyone's judging you.
This is your nervous system misfiring.
Feel your feet. Feel your hands.
Your body is safe right here.
Set one small social goal: say one real thing.
Or: ask one genuine question.
Or: be kind to one person.
One small, achievable win.
Forget performing. Focus on that one thing.
You'll surprise yourself with what you can do.
---
