## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body wakes in clinical mode."
---
It's 3am. You're awake. You haven't worked since Tuesday. It's Thursday. Your circadian rhythm doesn't know the difference anymore. Your body wakes in clinical mode — alert, ready, waiting. Your nervous system thinks someone needs you right now.

But nobody needs you. You're home. The house is quiet. Your body is still on shift schedule even though the shift is done.

You lie there for an hour. Your mind is moving through scenarios. Your hands know the routine. Your brain is running the protocol even though there is no patient, no call bell, nothing to respond to.

Notice your breath. Just breathing. The house is still. Nobody is calling. Your body will settle when it's ready.

The shift ended. Your alarm doesn't know.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're working nights even on your days off."
---
You sleep four hours. You wake at 3am. You lie awake until 5am. You go back to sleep. You wake up tired. That's how the days off feel. Your circadian rhythm is destroyed. Your body thinks it's on shift time even when it's not.

So the recovery days aren't actually recovery. You're sleeping worse on days off than you do during shifts because your body is in the gap between schedules and it doesn't know what time it is.

Every week you lose sleep to a schedule that's not actually happening. The cost is cumulative. It's weeks and months and years of terrible sleep. Your body is exhausted because it won't downregulate.

That's what the night shift is costing you even when you're not working.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if you just let the waking happen?"
---
You wake at 3am and you fight it. You try to force sleep. You lie there frustrated that you're awake when you should be sleeping on your day off. The fighting keeps you more awake.

But what if you just woke? What if you got up, made tea, read, just existed awake until your body decided to sleep again?

What if the forcing is what's keeping you stuck?

What if you stopped trying to sleep and just let your nervous system process the shift time until it settles?
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "People think you sleep fine on days off."
---
Your friends think you're resting on your days off. Your family thinks you're recovered by the time you're back at work. But you know the nights. You know the 3am waking. You know the terrible sleep that doesn't feel like recovery.

But nobody sees it. They just see someone who has days off. They don't see the compromised sleep. They don't see the cost accumulating.

You show up to work looking fine and you're exhausted from the days off too. Nobody knows the schedule is ruining your sleep all week long.

Your exhaustion is invisible to them.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body has survived years of this schedule."
---
Years of shift work. Years of 3am wakings. Years of circadian rhythm chaos. Your body is still here. You're still functioning. You're still showing up to shifts and doing the work even though your sleep is compromised.

You've never actually broken from this schedule. It's been impossible to fix and you've just kept going anyway.

You're still working. You're still showing up for patients. The terrible sleep has been there your entire career and so has your competence.

The night shift hasn't stopped you. The broken rhythm hasn't stopped you. You just keep showing up even though the cost to your own body is real.

You're still here. Still capable. Still moving forward on terrible sleep.

That's what you do.
