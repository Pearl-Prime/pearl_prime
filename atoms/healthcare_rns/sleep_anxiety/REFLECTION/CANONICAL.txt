

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
Shift work has destroyed my relationship with sleep. I work nights and my body is no longer sure when it's supposed to sleep or be awake. There's confusion. There's misalignment. There's my body trying to sleep at 3 p.m. when the world is awake and then being alert at 2 a.m. when I need to be sleeping. The mechanism here is called circadian rhythm disruption—the way my internal clock has become completely uncoupled from the external world. And unlike people with normal jobs, I can't just fix this by having a consistent schedule. The schedule is what's broken. The job requires me to work nights. So my body's reasonable attempt to adapt to that schedule is what's creating the insomnia.
Night shift has made it so I don't know when I'm supposed to sleep anymore.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I've realized is that the sleep anxiety for night-shift workers is unique. It's not about losing control (though that's part of it). It's about the fundamental impossibility of having a healthy sleep schedule when your job doesn't allow it. I can't fix this through good sleep hygiene. I can't fix this through meditation. I have to fix it by changing my fundamental life structure. Which I'm not going to do because this job is my income. So I'm locked into a situation where I know what's causing the problem and I can't address it.
My body is fighting against what my schedule requires.
