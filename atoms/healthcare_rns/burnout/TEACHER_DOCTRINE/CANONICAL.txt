

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v01
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: false_alarm
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your alarm system has been firing for the full twelve-hour shift and has not been allowed to come down between three back-to-backs. The reset window has been deleted from your week."
---
Here is the mechanism. Your stress response is not designed to run continuously. It is designed to fire, do its job, and reset. The reset is supposed to happen on the walk to your car after shift, in the hours before the next twelve, on the day off. The reset window is what your nervous system was built around. In a twelve-hour shift environment with three back-to-backs and a short turn before the next stretch, the gap is removed. The alarm has been at active load through every call light, every monitor, every code, every family conversation, every chart check, every short staffing, for the entire shift. What was a useful alarm becomes a stuck signal. Your body keeps producing the chemistry it would produce in front of a real threat, only now the threat is the next call light, then the next chart, then the next admission, then the next shift. The alarm does not care that not every call light is a code. It only knows you have not let it come down. This is what burnout looks like in a nurse's body before it has a name. You are still hitting your assessments. You are still showing up to handoff. But the recovery curve that used to bring you back by your next day off does not bring you back anymore. A stuck alarm does not need more shifts. It needs the conditions that allow a reset, which is closer to nothing than to more.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v02
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: spiral
weight: standard
carry_line: "The patient-replay loop runs the shift forward into what could have gone worse. The forecast borrows guilt from outcomes that did not happen."
---
Here is the mechanism. After a hard shift, the prediction loop runs backward instead of forward. It takes the assessment you charted, the med you pulled, the call you made to the resident, and runs them through every variation. If you had caught the change in vitals five minutes sooner. If you had pushed back harder on the order. If the patient had crashed on your watch instead of the next shift's. The loop arrives at a worse outcome and your body responds as if the worse outcome happened. You drive home from a shift where every patient lived and your chest is tight because the version where one did not is running on a side channel of your nervous system. The loop is not lying. It is doing what it does. The problem is that it does not know when to stop, and it treats each invented variation as evidence about your competence, even though every variation past the first is invented. The guilt is borrowed from an outcome that never happened. What helps is noticing the loop itself. Not arguing with it. Just naming the links. The patient stabilized. The shift ended. The loop is running. When you can see the loop in slow motion, you also see that it is a loop, not a verdict on your nursing. The shift still happened. The replay still runs. But you stop paying guilt for outcomes you did not produce.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v03
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: comparison
weight: standard
carry_line: "The charge nurse who looks unbothered after the same shift is not the full picture. The comparison engine reads their composure as data about your fitness for the floor."
---
Here is the mechanism. The comparison engine in you was tuned for a small unit whose internal states you could read. It tracks relative position by reading visible signals, who carries the shift on their face, who is laughing in the break room, who is calm at handoff. It did not evolve for a culture where the visible signal of competence is to show none of the cost. You walk into the break room after a hard assignment and the charge nurse is laughing about something else, and the engine runs a fast subtraction. They are fine. You are not. Therefore something is wrong with your fitness for the floor specifically. What the engine cannot see is what is happening underneath their composure. You do not see the charge who has not slept right in months. You do not see the senior nurse who cries in the parking lot after every code. You do not see the colleague who is carrying moral injury from a patient who died six weeks ago and never said a word about it. You see surfaces, and the engine treats surfaces as full pictures. The comparison takes their visible composure as evidence and your visible reaction as a personal verdict on whether you belong in the job. Both readings are wrong. The shift cost everyone on it something. Some of them just have more reps at hiding the cost.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v04
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: overwhelm
weight: standard
carry_line: "A six-patient assignment with three on telemetry and two new admits is not six tasks. It is six predicted threats holding partial attention for the full twelve."
---
Here is the mechanism. Your working memory has a small budget. Maybe four things at once, maybe five on a good day. A nursing assignment does not respect that budget. Six patients, three on telemetry, two new admits with no chart history yet, one teetering on a wean, a family in the hall who wants to talk, a pharmacy callback that has not come, a doctor returning a page during your med pass, each of these is something the nervous system has flagged and not yet closed. It is not six tasks. It is six predicted threats holding partial attention for the entire shift, draining the same supply you need for the patient at the bedside in front of you. This is not a discipline failure. It is a capacity question. The unit is doing exactly what it was built to do under current staffing, which is to demand attention from every direction at once. Your nervous system is doing exactly what it was built to do, which is to hold the demand open until each one is resolved. The friction you feel by hour nine is the gap between an attention budget that has not changed in fifty thousand years and an assignment density that has been growing since the last contract negotiation. Naming this is the start of asking for what would actually close even one of them.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v05
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: shame
weight: standard
carry_line: "The conviction that the rest of the unit is not this depleted is the part of nursing culture that keeps everyone equally depleted and equally hidden."
---
Here is the mechanism. Nursing burnout shame has a specific shape. It is the conviction that the rest of your unit is handling the same assignment without coming apart, and that your reaction to the last hard shift is a private deficiency you must hide from your charge, your colleagues, your manager, and especially the new grads watching how the experienced nurses do it. So you hide it. You decline the question about how the shift sat with you with a fine. You skip the debrief or you go but say nothing real. You cry in the car or in the bathroom but never on the floor. The hiding is the part that closes the loop. Because nobody on your unit sees how the shift landed on you, you also do not see how it landed on them. Each of you, separately, concludes that you are the only one. Each of you, separately, performs okay-ness for the others. Shame survives in the gap that hiding builds. Naming the mechanism does not change the staffing ratio, but it interrupts the part where you treat the depletion as evidence of a defect rather than as the predictable response of a caregiving nervous system that has now run thousands of twelves without the recovery windows it was designed around.
