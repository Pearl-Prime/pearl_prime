---

---
Maria is in the break room at 3 AM, sandwich untouched, eyes on the clock — she has four hours left on the stretch and her chest feels like she is watching herself from the hallway.

## HOOK v02
---

---
You clock in and your body does the motions before your mind arrives — gowning, scanning, charting — and somewhere in the third hour you realize you have not actually felt anything since the alarm went off.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
A code is called and the old electricity does not come; you move correctly, you are good at this, but the part of you that used to care has gone quiet in a way that frightens you more than the monitor ever did.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
In the supply room you stand for a second too long with your hand on the shelf, not getting anything, just standing, because standing still is the only thing today that has not been asked of you by someone else.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Your friend texts asking how work is and you stare at the screen, because the honest answer — I am still here but I cannot find myself in it — is not something a thumbs-up emoji can hold.

## HOOK v31
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: healthcare_rns
---
You charted for forty-seven minutes after the shift ended. Your shift was twelve hours. The forty-seven minutes was unpaid. The forty-seven minutes was also the difference between the patient who needs to be safe tomorrow and a tomorrow you do not want to think about. You finished the charting at 8:14 PM. Your supervisor will not see the forty-seven minutes on any report. Your body will see it tomorrow at 6 AM when the alarm goes.
---

## HOOK v32
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: healthcare_rns
---
You held the family's hand at the bedside at 3:47 AM. The family went home at 5:12 AM. You did not go home. You had four more hours of shift. Within twenty minutes you were starting an IV in a room three doors down on a patient who needed your full attention and competent affect, and you produced both, and the producing of both is the part of this job that nobody can see and nobody can pay for and the part your body has begun to file.
---

## HOOK v33
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: healthcare_rns
---
You used to know why you became a nurse. You had a clean sentence about it. The sentence still exists. You can still say it out loud. The sentence does not produce the feeling it used to produce. The sentence and the feeling have come unhooked, and the unhooking happened sometime around the second wave, and you have not yet figured out how to either re-hook them or grieve the unhooking.
