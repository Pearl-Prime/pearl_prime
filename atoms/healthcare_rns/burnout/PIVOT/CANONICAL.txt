--- variant: v01
You've been told burnout is what happens when you stop caring. That's backwards. Burnout is what happens when you care at full capacity for years in a system that treats your caring as an infinite resource. The caring didn't fail. The math did.

--- variant: v02
The old narrative says push through. But you've been pushing through for so long that you forgot what it felt like to walk at a normal pace. Burnout isn't a speed problem. It's a structural one — and the structure isn't your character.

--- variant: v03
Everyone sees a competent nurse handling her assignment. Nobody sees the person underneath who stopped feeling anything about it three months ago. That numbness isn't laziness. It's your nervous system pulling the emergency brake.
