

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/financial_stress/spiral/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora has a shift shortened due to unit census. The twelve-hour shift becomes ten hours. The reduction is forty dollars. The forty dollars was allocated to the electric bill. The electric bill will now be late. The late payment carries a fee. The fee converts the shortened shift into debt. Nora calculates forward: the fee means the next billing cycle starts worse. The next billing cycle worse means the financial margin is smaller. Smaller margin means another dropped shift becomes catastrophic. She works the shortened shift and calculates the months forward with each patient interaction. The work is happening. The calculation is also happening. The calculation is becoming the dominant experience.

---
Aisha's bank account has one hundred and thirty-seven dollars. The rent is due in five days. The rent is nine hundred dollars. The one hundred and thirty-seven dollars is the entire gap between having housing and not having housing. Aisha understands that nothing can go wrong. She cannot buy gas without money from an unexpected place. She cannot eat out. She cannot spend anything that is not absolutely necessary. The five days are a countdown. The countdown is to find enough money to close a gap that is almost eight hundred dollars. She goes to work and the work is still the work. But the work is also the five-day countdown.

## RECOGNITION v03

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Claire has a student loan debt of eighty-five thousand dollars. The monthly payment is her largest expense after rent. The payment does not change the principal significantly. The payment is most interest. The calculation is clear: at this rate, she will be paying the loan until she is in her late fifties. The calculation builds a chain: this payment → another thirty years → another thirty years of living with this constraint → the constraint constrains everything. She cannot buy a house. She cannot take a lower-paying job that might be more fulfilling. She cannot leave the urban market where the hospital is, even though the cost of living is destroying her. The student loan is not just a financial obligation. The student loan is a structural constraint on her future.

COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4

---
Tomás calculates his salary against what he could make as a travel nurse. The difference is significant. The difference is two hundred thousand dollars a year more over five years. The calculation arrives after a particularly brutal shift. The shift has worn him out. The calculation becomes attached to the worn-out feeling. The weariness + the calculation creates a loop: I am worn out because I'm not paid enough to not be worn out. The loop becomes a spiral: if I stay, the weariness will compound. If I leave, I abandon my commitment to the institution. If I stay and abandon myself, I break. The spiral does not have an exit. It only has directions.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora's spiral is becoming automatic. Any financial setback triggers the full calculation without conscious effort. A shift is canceled: that means this bill is late. A bill is late: that means interest or a fee. Interest or a fee: that means next month is worse. Next month worse: that means I need to pick up extra shifts. Extra shifts: that means childcare costs or exhaustion that affects the next month's work. The chain runs instantly. By the time she has the thought about the canceled shift, she is already six months forward in the calculation, six months worse. The work is exhausting. The calculation of the consequences of not working is more exhausting.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/financial_stress/spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nora's charge nurse notices the pattern. Nora is visibly stressed after every financial setback. The charge nurse says: "I notice the shifts that get canceled seem to really affect you." Nora tells the truth: the canceled shifts create a cascade. The charge nurse asks: "What if we looked at what actually happens versus what you think will happen?" The question creates a space. Nora realizes that she calculates disaster before she has information. The calculation feels like planning. The planning feels like control. But the planning is preventing her from having information about what actually will happen.

## TURNING_POINT v03

---
Claire meets with a financial planner. The planner asks: "What if you stopped treating the debt as a life sentence?" Claire realizes that she has organized her life around the assumption that the debt is permanent and unpayable. The planner shows Claire alternative paths: loan forgiveness programs, income-driven repayment that actually changes the payoff timeline, geographical moves that might improve her financial situation. ## EMBODIMENT v01

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nora practices stopping the spiral at step one. When a shift is canceled, she names it: "This is one shift. I'm calculating six months forward. Let me check what I actually know." She writes down the facts: this shift is canceled. The next shift is still scheduled. The bills are still the same. She stops the calculation at fact. The spiral still arrives. But the practice of stopping at fact creates a choice. She can run the spiral or she can look at the facts. The choice is becoming available.
