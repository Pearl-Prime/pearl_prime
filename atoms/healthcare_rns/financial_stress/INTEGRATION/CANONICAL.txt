

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your shoulders know about the debt."
---
You're standing at the start of another shift and your body is already tired. Not from the work you're about to do but from the work you've already done to cover the hours that make the math work. Your shoulders are tight. Your jaw is clenched. The numbers ran through your head on the drive in. Student loans. Rent. The car needs work. The calculation of how many shifts to break even. This isn't acute stress. This is the baseline. The body lives in the financial alarm all the time. You feel your feet on the floor and underneath that, the weight of debt. This tightness isn't failure. It's accurate information: you're working in an underpaid profession and the cost is distributed through every muscle. That's not dramatic. It's structural.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The cost is years added to your life."
---
The overtime is necessary. The union negotiated the wage, the institution keeps the wage low, and you pick up the extra shifts to make the salary functional. That's the math every working nurse is doing. The cost is hidden in years. You'll work five extra years to pay off loans before someone else in your cohort takes their first real vacation. You'll pick up shifts when you're tired because the margin is thin. You'll skip the therapy you need because the copay is too much. You'll stay in the job longer than you want because the pension matters. The institution gets your hours. Your life adjusts around the wage. The cost isn't measured in money. It's measured in years.
