Tomás receives three admissions in two hours. Standard ICU turnover. Each admission is a packet: assessment, orders, drip calculations, family notification, documentation. Three packets simultaneously is a volume his brain can handle sequentially but not in parallel. The admissions arrive in parallel. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora maps the overwhelm's structure. It's not the tasks—she can do each task competently. It's the simultaneity. Six patients producing six streams of data, six sets of needs, six potential emergencies. Her brain can process one stream at full fidelity. Every additional stream reduces the fidelity of all streams. At six streams, each one gets roughly seventeen percent of her attention. The seventeen percent catches most things. The things it misses are the things that keep her awake at night. The overwhelm isn't about volume. It's about the fidelity loss that volume produces.

Tomás discovers the overwhelm creates a prioritization hierarchy that erases the non-urgent. Acute needs get attention. Chronic needs get deferred. Emotional needs get nothing. His patient in Room 2 needs a medication adjustment—acute. His patient in Room 4 needs pain reassessment—chronic. His patient in Room 6 needs someone to listen—emotional. The hierarchy ensures Room 6 waits. Room 6 always waits. The patient in Room 6 is scared and alone and their nurse is competent and overwhelmed and the overwhelm decides what kind of care gets delivered.

Claire identifies the moral distress inside the overwhelm. The overwhelm isn't just cognitive overload—it's the experience of providing less care than she knows is needed. She sees the gap between actual care and optimal care on every shift. The gap is not her fault. The gap exists because the system assigns two laboring patients to one nurse. But the gap feels personal. Her body holds the gap as though she chose it. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 2

---
Derek tracks the wall's arrival time over ten shifts. Shift 1: hour ten. Shift 5: hour nine. Shift 10: hour eight. The wall is moving earlier. The trend is clear and concerning. His capacity is shrinking because the recovery between shifts doesn't fully restore it. Each shift depletes to the wall. Each recovery restores to slightly below the previous starting point. The deficit accumulates. The wall will keep moving earlier unless the recovery changes or the load decreases. Neither is in Derek's control. ## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nora writes down her six patients' priorities on a notecard. Not the full assignment—just the one thing each patient needs most right now. Six lines. Six priorities. The notecard reduces six streams to six items. The items can be sequenced. The sequencing creates order inside the overwhelm. The overwhelm is still present—six patients is still too many—but the notecard gives her brain a structure to hold the volume. She works the notecard from top to bottom. The overwhelm runs alongside the list. The list doesn't cure the overwhelm. The list makes the overwhelm navigable.

## TURNING_POINT v02
---

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Tomás asks for help. Three words to the charge nurse: "I need help." The words cost more than they should. The overwhelm said: asking means you can't handle it. He asks anyway. The charge nurse takes the Room 6 admission. Tomás has two admissions instead of three. Two is manageable. The asking didn't reduce his competence. The asking restored his capacity. He completes the two admissions with full fidelity. Room 6's patient gets a nurse who can give them full attention. The help improved care for everyone. The overwhelm said asking was weakness. The outcomes say asking was judgment.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
CALLBACK_ID: sound_triage
CALLBACK_PHASE: setup
---
Aisha puts in earplugs during charting time. Not in patient rooms—at the station, during the twenty minutes of documentation. The earplugs reduce the sound environment by about forty percent. The forty percent reduction produces a disproportionate relief. The hundreds of micro-triage decisions drop to dozens. Her charting improves—fewer errors, faster completion. The earplugs are a boundary between her and the unit's ambient demand. The demand doesn't stop. Her exposure to it pauses for twenty minutes. The twenty minutes of reduced input produce an hour of restored capacity.

## TURNING_POINT v05
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Derek identifies the wall and walks toward it differently. Hour eight approaches. He feels the saturation building. Instead of pushing through with diminishing returns, he takes five minutes. Break room. Water. No phone. No charting. Just five minutes of reduced input. The five minutes don't eliminate the wall. The five minutes move it from hour eight to hour nine. One hour of restored capacity. The five-minute investment returns sixty minutes. The math is absurd in its efficiency. He's been running twelve hours without a cognitive break. The break was always available. The overwhelm said breaks were a luxury. The math says breaks are infrastructure.

COST_TYPE: internal
