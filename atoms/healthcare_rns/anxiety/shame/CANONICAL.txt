---
Nora misses a lab value. Not a critical one—a trending potassium that shifted from 3.6 to 3.4. Within normal limits both times. Clinically insignificant. She catches it on the next check. No harm. But the miss lives in her body as evidence. Evidence of what, she can't articulate precisely, but the shape of it is: you should have caught it. The "should" carries more weight than the clinical reality. The clinical reality is: nothing happened. The shame reality is: something almost happened because of you. The almost is where the shame lives. The almost is larger than the actual.

Tomás asks the attending to repeat an order. The attending repeats it without irritation. Standard clarification. But Tomás's face heats. The heat is shame—not for asking, but for needing to ask. Five years in the ICU and he should know the standard dosing for this medication. He does know it. He asked because the order was unusual—a non-standard dose for a specific clinical reason. Derek makes a medication error. He catches it before administration—scans the barcode, the system flags the wrong dose, he corrects it. The system worked. The safeguard worked. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora maps the shame's logic. The shame says: good nurses don't miss things. The clinical reality says: all humans miss things, which is why systems have redundancies. The shame operates on a model of nursing that requires perfection. The hospital operates on a model that expects errors and builds safety nets. The shame's model has no safety nets because in the shame's model, needing a safety net is the failure. She works inside two systems simultaneously—one that accounts for human limitation and one that punishes it. The hospital designed the redundancies. The shame designed the punishment.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Tomás realizes the shame scales with witnesses. Asking the attending in an empty hallway: moderate shame. Asking the attending during rounds with four residents watching: maximum shame. The clinical value of the question doesn't change. The number of eyes changes. The shame is an audience-dependent phenomenon. It measures exposure, not competence. He's equally competent in both scenarios. He's equally ashamed only when no one watches. The shame needs observers to achieve full intensity. Without observers, the shame is a whisper. With observers, the shame is a verdict.

Aisha discovers the shame has a memory system that only stores failures. Six years of competent care: thousands of correct assessments, hundreds of caught deteriorations, dozens of lives directly preserved by her skill. The shame's archive contains: the time she missed a fever trend, the time she hesitated during a rapid response, the time she misprogrammed a pump and caught it. Three entries in six years. The three entries carry more emotional weight than the thousands of correct actions. The shame's filing system is rigged. Successes go to a folder she never opens. Failures go to a folder that opens itself.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v04
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Claire identifies the shame's deepest layer: it converts uncertainty into identity. A tracing she can't read isn't a difficult tracing—it's proof she isn't good enough. A decision she struggles with isn't a complex decision—it's evidence of her limitations. The shame takes every moment of clinical uncertainty and files it under "who you are" instead of "what you're facing." The distinction matters enormously. A difficult tracing is a feature of the situation. A deficient nurse is a feature of the person. The shame consistently misfiles the situation as the person. The misfiling is the mechanism.
