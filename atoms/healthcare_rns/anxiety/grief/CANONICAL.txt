

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/grief/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Nora finds her old nursing school clinical journal in a box. The entries describe patients with full narrative—their stories, their fears, the way the light hit the room during an evening assessment. She reads three entries and something compresses in her chest. The entries were written by a nurse who had time to notice light. The nurse charting at this desk notices medication times, lab trends, discharge criteria. Both are Nora. The one who noticed light isn't dead—she's buried under seven years of increasing patient loads and decreasing minutes per interaction. The compression in her chest is the weight of what got buried.

## RECOGNITION v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3

---
Tomás watches a nursing student sit with a dying patient. The student holds the patient's hand. No charting. No task. Just presence. The student has one patient. Tomás has four. He remembers having one patient—the clinical rotation where presence was the assignment. The memory produces a feeling he can't schedule into his current shift: longing for the version of nursing where sitting was the intervention. His current version requires movement. Movement is efficiency. Efficiency is survival. Somewhere between the student's one patient and his four, the sitting became a luxury the system stopped affording him.

Aisha remembers the first time she made a child laugh during a procedure. Her second month on the unit. She'd prepared a puppet show with gloves—drew faces on the fingers, created voices. The child forgot the IV for thirty seconds. Aisha hasn't made a puppet in four years. The preparation time doesn't exist in her current schedule. The puppets weren't efficient. The puppets were nursing in a form the efficiency model doesn't recognize. She passes the supply closet where she used to get the gloves and the passing produces a small, specific sadness for a practice that was hers and is now extinct.

Claire attends a retirement party for a nurse who started the same year she did. The retiring nurse gives a speech about why she loved the work. Claire listens for the love in her own body. Derek reads his nursing school application essay. The essay describes wanting to make a difference in people's lives during their most vulnerable moments. The language is earnest and unprotected. He reads it at the nurses' station between medication passes and the distance between the essay and the station is measured in something he can't quantify. Not time—three years isn't long. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nora identifies what the grief is mourning: not the past, but a specific capacity that the present consumed. The capacity to be curious about patients as people, not as clinical data sets. She still cares about her patients. The caring is professional—efficient, boundaried, outcome-focused. The curiosity was different. The curiosity wanted to know who the patient was before they were a patient. The curiosity was trained out by time constraints that rewarded speed over depth. The training was necessary for survival. The survival cost the curiosity. The grief is the recognition that the survival mechanism and the lost capacity can't both fit in a twelve-hour shift.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Tomás maps the grief to specific relationships. His first year, he knew patients' names for weeks after discharge. He asked about their grandchildren. He remembered their preferences. Now patients are room numbers that resolve to names when he checks the chart. The transition from names to numbers wasn't callousness. The transition was cognitive triage—the brain can hold four patients' personal details or four patients' clinical details, not both. He chose clinical because clinical keeps them alive. The choice was correct. The choice also erased something. The grief is the erasure, and the erasure was the price of correct prioritization.

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Aisha realizes the grief intensifies with competence. The more skilled she becomes, the more efficiently she processes each interaction, and the less room remains for the parts of nursing that brought her to the profession. The puppet shows were inefficient and meaningful. The efficient version of Aisha completes an IV insertion in ninety seconds with minimal distress. The puppet-show version took four minutes and produced a laugh. The system rewards ninety seconds. The grief mourns four minutes. The competence and the grief grow together because the competence is what eliminated the space the grief is mourning.

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Claire discovers the grief is about the relationship between her and nursing, not about nursing itself. She still believes in the work. She still shows up. The grief isn't disillusionment—disillusionment is about the thing being worse than expected. The grief is about the relationship changing shape. She used to be in love with nursing. Now she's in a long-term partnership with it—committed, reliable, occasionally resentful. The partnership is sustainable. The love was not. The grief is the recognition that sustainability required the love to evolve into something that doesn't feel like love anymore. The evolution was necessary. The loss inside the evolution is real.

COST_TYPE: internal

---
Nora asks a patient about their garden. Not during assessment—after it. Thirty seconds of non-clinical conversation. The patient's face changes. Not dramatically. The eyes soften. The voice shifts from patient-reporting to person-telling. The patient describes tomatoes. Nora listens. The curiosity was waiting for thirty seconds of non-clinical space. The space is small. The curiosity fills it immediately. Thirty seconds isn't enough to restore what was lost. Thirty seconds is enough to prove the capacity still exists.
