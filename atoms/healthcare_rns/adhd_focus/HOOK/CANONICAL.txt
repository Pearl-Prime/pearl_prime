## HOOK v01
---
---
The discharge paperwork sits three clicks deep in the EHR, untouched since noon. Med pass got done. Vitals got done. The wound care in-service reminder popped up twice and got swiped away both times. Now it's 6:47, report starts at 7, and the folder is still closed.
---

## HOOK v02
---
---
Priya has four charts open in her head and zero open on the screen. She knows exactly what she needs to document — the fall risk reassessment, the family callback, the wound photo — and she has typed none of it. Her cursor blinks in an empty note field.
---

## HOOK v03
---
---
The mandatory in-service module has a due date of "end of pay period." That phrase has meant nothing to Marcus for six pay periods running. He closes the tab again to answer a call light that isn't urgent, because a call light is a shape he can finish.
---

## HOOK v04
---
---
The med cart is stocked, the 1400 pass is done, and somehow it's 1522 and Priya still doesn't know it. She looks at the clock like it lied to her. Behind her, the discharge summary she meant to start at 1400 hasn't been touched.
---

## HOOK v05
---
---
Three admission charts are open in three browser tabs. Denise has read all three top-to-bottom twice. She has typed nothing into any of them, because she can't decide which one is "the real one" to start with, and the decision itself has become the whole task.
---

## HOOK v06
---
---
The overhead page for a code blue on 4 cuts through everything — Priya is moving before she's finished the thought. Twenty minutes later she's back at the station, adrenaline gone, and the incident note she needs to write feels like it's behind glass she can't reach.
---

## HOOK v07
---
---
Marcus has re-read the same line of the wound care policy four times. He is not confused by the words. He is somewhere else entirely, and his eyes are just walking across the page without him.
---

## HOOK v08
---
---
The whiteboard says room 14 needs a turn at 1500. It is 1518. Denise saw the board at 1502 and thought "in a minute," and the minute became sixteen, not because she forgot, but because nothing about walking to room 14 pulled harder than the conversation she was already in.
---

## HOOK v09
---
---
Report is over. The floor is quiet for the first time in nine hours. Priya sits at the station with her badge in her hand and cannot make herself stand up, even though standing up is the only thing between her and going home.
---

## HOOK v10
---
---
The incident report form has eleven fields. Marcus has filled in three. He opened it forty minutes ago. Nothing about the other eight fields is hard — he just hasn't been able to make his hands start on field four.
---

## HOOK v11
---
---
Denise's phone buzzes with a text from her sister. She reads it, feels the pull to respond, and three minutes later she's still holding the phone, the med she was walking to give now sitting on the cart where she set it down to "just check this real quick."
---

## HOOK v12
---
---
The family member at the nurses' station has been waiting eleven minutes for an update Priya intended to give her at minute two. Priya isn't avoiding her. She genuinely lost the thread between "I'll go talk to her" and now, twice.
---

## HOOK v13
---
---
Six patient charts need afternoon reassessments. Marcus picked the loudest one first — the patient who buzzed — not the one actually due first. By the time he notices the order was backwards, two are now late.
---

## HOOK v14
---
---
The float pool text came in at 1830: report at a unit she's never worked. Denise read it, understood it, and then spent forty minutes doing everything except finding out where that unit even is.
---

## HOOK v15
---
---
Priya's badge reel has a sticky note on it that says "CHART NOW NOT LATER." She wrote it herself, this morning. It is now 1900, charting is not done, and she cannot remember reading the note even once all shift.
