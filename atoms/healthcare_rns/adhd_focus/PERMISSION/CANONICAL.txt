## PERMISSION v01
---
---
You are allowed to ask for report to be repeated slower, in writing, without treating it as a failure to listen the first time.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---
---
You are allowed to set an alarm to tell you what time it is, instead of trusting a clock in your head that was never reliable for a twelve-hour shift.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---
---
You are allowed to shrink a task down to its literal first motion and count that as a real, sufficient start.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---
---
You are allowed to hide the rest of the pile and work on exactly one chart, one sheet, one tab at a time.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---
---
You are allowed to ask a coworker to check in on you by name instead of relying on guilt to get a task done.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---
---
You are allowed to read something out loud, even quietly at a nurses' station, if silent reading has stopped working.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---
---
You are allowed to lean on a form's own structure instead of trying to organize a chaotic memory in your head first.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---
---
You are allowed to open a dreaded task just to check its size, with no obligation yet to finish it.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---
---
You are allowed to write a floating intention down the moment you notice it recurring, instead of trusting yourself to remember it a third time.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---
---
You are allowed to explain a task to someone else as your way into finally doing it yourself.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---
---
You are allowed to use a two-minute timer as permission to only touch a task, not finish it.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---
---
You are allowed to keep a running list of the small tricks that work for you and use them without shame, every single shift.
