## THREAD v01

---
The sticky note on Priya's badge reel — "CHART NOW NOT LATER" — outlives that one shift. She starts writing a new one every morning, in the break room, before she clocks in, and stops needing to read it by the third week, because by then the habit underneath it has already taken over.

## THREAD v02
---
---
Marcus never takes the paper cutout off his monitor. New hires ask about it. He tells them the truth: it's not about compliance, it's about tricking his own attention into noticing something boring, and half of them go make their own.
---

## THREAD v03
---
---
Denise's index card system spreads to two other nurses on nights after she mentions it at a shift huddle, almost apologetically, like she's confessing a shortcut. Within a month it's just how report gets taken on that unit.
---

## THREAD v04
---
---
The charge nurse who taught Marcus to hide the pile and hand him one sheet at a time never calls it a system. She just keeps doing it, quietly, for whoever freezes at a stack of anything, and nobody notices it's a technique until they need it themselves.
---

## THREAD v05
---
---
Priya keeps reading her notes out loud at the station long after that one stuck shift, quietly, out of habit, and stops being embarrassed about it around the same time she stops needing twenty minutes to write what used to take her twenty minutes to fail at.
---

## THREAD v06
---
---
The thirty-minute silent alarm becomes such a fixed part of Denise's shift that she notices its absence more than its presence — the one time she forgets to set it, she loses forty minutes again, and resets it before lunch without a second thought.
---

## THREAD v07
---
---
Marcus explains the wound care policy to three more new hires over the following month, and each time, some other stalled module gets finished the same week, almost as a side effect he never quite plans for.

## THREAD v08
---
---
The HR form Denise dreaded for four days becomes a running joke between her and her coworker — "ninety seconds," they say to each other now, whenever either of them stalls in front of something that turns out to be smaller than it looked.

## THREAD v09
---
---
Priya starts asking, out loud, "which bridge haven't I tried yet" — first just to herself, then loud enough that a coworker overhears and starts asking it too, and it becomes a small shared phrase at the station that neither of them remembers naming.

## THREAD v10
---
---
The code blue documentation scaffold — read the form's own order aloud, fill it one line at a time — gets taught to every new nurse who runs their first code, passed down the same casual way Priya learned it, with nobody crediting where it started.

## THREAD v11
---
---
Marcus's two-minute timer trick outlasts the module it was built for. He uses it on charting, on emails, on the discharge summaries he used to stall on for an hour, and it works roughly the same percentage of the time, which surprises him less each time it happens.
---

## THREAD v12
---
---
Denise keeps a blank sticky note pad clipped to her badge reel permanently now, not because she plans to use it every shift, but because the option being right there turns out to be half of what makes her actually reach for it when the moment comes.
