

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The heaviness is real. Keep going."
---
You walked out of that room and the weight landed in your chest. The patient coded. You ran the code. The family was there watching. The code stopped. The patient remained unresponsive. The family made the decision. You extubated. You stayed with the body until the end. Now you're in the break room and your body is heavy. The fatigue is bone-deep. The grief is sitting on top of your sternum like a stone. You still have an hour left on your shift. You feel your feet underneath you. Your chest still moves. This heaviness is accurate grief, not failure. The work required your presence. Your presence is costing you. You carry both at the same time.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The cost is cumulative and unmeasured."
---
This is not your first patient death. You can count the ones from this year. The elderly man from February. The child from April. The complicated case in July. The one you think about when you're driving home. The names accumulate. The grief accumulates. The institution counts patients admitted and discharged. It doesn't measure the grief in the nurses who were there for the journey. The cost is distributed across your body. It lives in the way certain rooms feel. It lives in the patients who remind you of the ones you lost. It lives in the silence you keep to protect the people you care for outside of work. Nobody counts this. You count it. Your body counts it. The cumulative weight is the cost.

That doesn't mean they don't matter. It means you learned to hold them without being broken by them. The grief moves differently now. It comes and sits and moves through you and you keep working. That showing up through grief is what nurses do. You're still here, grieving and working in the same moment.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "How much grief can one person hold."
---
The real question isn't whether this death is sad. It is. The real question is how much cumulative loss a person can carry while still functioning. How many patient deaths before the stone in your chest gets heavier than your capacity to carry it. How many names you'll remember before the remembering becomes unbearable. You're still carrying them now. The question is just there, in the quiet, after each loss: how much more. Nobody has the answer. The answer comes later, when you hit your limit or you don't.
