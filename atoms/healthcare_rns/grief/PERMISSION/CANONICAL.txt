--- variant: v01
You are allowed to grieve patients who weren't yours. The ones you overheard about, the ones in the next room, the ones whose families you watched from the hallway. Proximity to death counts. You were proximate.

--- variant: v02
You are allowed to cry about a patient you lost ten years ago. Grief doesn't follow a chart. It follows its own timeline, and that timeline just found an opening.

--- variant: v03
You are allowed to mourn the accumulated losses without naming each one. The weight is real even when the list is too long to read.
