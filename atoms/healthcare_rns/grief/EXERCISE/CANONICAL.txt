## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You lost a patient. The grief is real.
You don't need to analyze it or fix it.
Feel it in your body right now.
Where is the heaviness? Your chest?
Put your hand there. Just hold it.
Cry if you need to. That's allowed.
Your tears aren't weakness. They're humanity.
You cared. Now you're grieving. That's right.
Don't rush this. Let yourself feel.
The pain means you were truly present.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Find a quiet corner. Sit. Be still.
Remember one good moment with this person.
Not their death. A moment they lived.
Something real. Something human. Something true.
Hold that memory. Let it be enough.
Don't try to make it mean something.
It just means: they were here.
You were part of their story.
They changed you by existing. That's real.
Let the grief exist without explanation.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You witnessed something profound. Honor that.
You don't have to be okay about it quickly.
Grief doesn't have a timeline.
Let yourself feel it all shift.
Feel your body holding this loss.
Feel your heart recognizing that they mattered.
You were brave enough to care fully.
That's what grief is underneath.
It's the proof that love was real.
And real love deserves real grief.
---
