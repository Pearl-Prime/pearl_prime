--- variant: v01
You were trained to manage death clinically. Time of death, notify family, prepare the body, take the next admission. Nobody trained you for the grief that doesn't fit in the chart. The grief that comes home with you and sits in the kitchen while you eat dinner.

--- variant: v02
The old frame says nurses deal with death differently — that you get used to it. You don't get used to it. You get skilled at postponing the grief. But postponed isn't processed, and the backlog is starting to show.

--- variant: v03
Every loss you've witnessed at the bedside gets filed under professional experience. Rename that file. Some of those losses are personal. Some of those patients became people to you. And people-grief needs a different protocol than patient-grief.
