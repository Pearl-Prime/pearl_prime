## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You need to do something that scares you.
Before you do it, ground yourself.
Feel both your feet on the floor.
Press them down deliberately and hold for five counts.
Feel the stability under you.
Name the fear out loud. Don't minimize it.
That's real. Your fear is valid.
Now: you can feel the fear and do it anyway.
Courage isn't the absence of fear.
It's moving forward despite what you feel.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Before the difficult moment arrives, take three breaths.
Breathe in strength. Breathe out the doubt.
Remember: you've done hard things before.
You didn't die. You didn't fail completely.
You survived. You learned. You kept going.
This is just one more hard thing.
Your nervous system knows how to handle hard.
Say out loud: I can do hard things.
Say it like you believe it.
Because you've already proven it's true.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Identify the smallest courageous action right now.
Not the biggest thing. The smallest step.
Maybe it's speaking up. Maybe it's asking.
One tiny brave action. That's all.
Do that one thing before you do anything else.
Notice: you did it. You didn't die.
The fear lessens when you actually move.
Momentum builds when you take small steps.
You don't need to be fearless.
You just need to be willing.
---
