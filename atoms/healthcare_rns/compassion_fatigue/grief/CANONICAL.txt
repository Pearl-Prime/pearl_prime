Maria used to cry at patients' deaths. She would sit in her car afterward, shoulders shaking. That was five years ago. Now she charts the death, notifies the family, helps the next patient. The tears don't come. She notices this absence the way you notice a room after the furniture has been removed. Something vital is missing. The capacity that defined her as a nurse, the ability to feel deeply and be moved, has quietly evacuated. She doesn't grieve the patient. She grieves the part of herself that could grieve.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/healthcare_rns/anchored/compassion_fatigue/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The mechanism is: To survive the pain of this work, I must become numb. The numbness protects. It allows function. But the mechanism has a hidden cost. The numbness doesn't discriminate. It numbs the pain and it numbs the love. It numbs the suffering and it numbs the meaning. The mechanism operates through a death in life. Maria continues to move through her days while mourning the self that could feel. This grief has no outlet because acknowledging it would mean questioning the mechanism that protects her.
---

COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 2

---
The mechanism proves itself through the gap between who the nurses used to be and who they are now. Dominique remembers calling parents after their child's death. She would speak slowly, giving them time to understand. Now she makes the calls efficiently, completing the task. Both versions of Dominique are skilled. But only one carried the belief that the call mattered beyond information delivery. The mechanism has convinced her that this loss of meaning is necessary. The price of survival is the sacrifice of purpose.
