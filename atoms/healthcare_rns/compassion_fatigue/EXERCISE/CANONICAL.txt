## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You've absorbed a patient's pain into yourself.
This happens. It doesn't mean you're weak.
Put both hands on your own chest.
Feel your own heart beating under your hands.
This is your body. Not theirs.
You are a separate person from your patients.
You can care AND have boundaries.
Feel your own breath moving in and out.
Their pain is real. Your separateness is real too.
Hold both truths at once.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Find a mirror if there's one available.
Look at your own face for ten seconds.
This is you. This is your body.
Not your patient's body. Not their suffering.
Feel your own hands. Wiggle your fingers.
These are your hands. They're yours alone.
Press your hands on your thighs deliberately.
Feel the boundary between your skin and yours alone.
You can't fix their pain by absorbing it.
You're more helpful when you stay yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Notice where you're holding their pain in your body.
Your chest? Your shoulders? Your stomach?
One hand on that spot. Breathe there.
Say quietly: This belongs to them.
I cannot carry this for them.
I can witness. I can help. I can't absorb.
Feel the difference between compassion and fusion.
Shake out that part of your body gently.
Feel yourself returning to yourself.
Set the weight down. It was never yours to carry.
---
