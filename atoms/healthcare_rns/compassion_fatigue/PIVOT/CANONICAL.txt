--- variant: v01
You were told compassion is unlimited. It isn't. Compassion runs on a reserve that nobody at the hospital is tracking, and yours hit empty sometime between the third death this month and the family who couldn't stop thanking you for trying.

--- variant: v02
The old frame calls it burnout. But this isn't about being tired of working. This is about being tired of caring and the shame that comes with admitting that. Compassion fatigue is the cost of empathy in a system that treats empathy as free labor.

--- variant: v03
You used to feel the weight of every patient's story. Now you chart the story and feel nothing. That shift isn't cold — it's protective. Your nervous system decided that feeling everything at full volume would destroy you, so it turned the volume down.
