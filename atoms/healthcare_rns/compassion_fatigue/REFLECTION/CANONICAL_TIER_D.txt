

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: D

I want to name something that I see in nurses who have worked in high-acuity settings for many years, and it is sometimes mistaken for callousness but is actually something different. It is the protective shutdown of empathy. After you have held the hand of enough dying patients, after you have heard enough families receive bad news, after you have seen enough suffering that you could not prevent, something in the system that processes connection and feeling becomes less responsive.

I want to name something about the specific form of fatigue that comes from emotional labor that is never reciprocated. You are providing emotional support all shift.
