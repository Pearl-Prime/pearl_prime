--- variant: v01
Your worth got wired to your productivity somewhere between nursing school and your third year on the floor. When you're useful, you feel valuable. When you rest, you feel worthless. That's not self-worth — that's a transaction your profession installed.

--- variant: v02
The old metric measures your value by patient outcomes, peer approval, and how many shifts you can handle without breaking. New metric: you are worth something even on the day you call out sick, even on the day the patient outcome is poor, even on the day you contribute nothing clinical.

--- variant: v03
You know your patients' worth isn't conditional on their behavior or their diagnosis. Apply that same standard to yourself. Your worth isn't conditional on your last shift, your last save, or your last performance review.
