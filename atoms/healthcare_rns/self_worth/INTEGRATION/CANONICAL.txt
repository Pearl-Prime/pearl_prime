

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You exist outside of the scrubs."
---
You took off the scrubs and the person in the mirror wasn't clear. For twelve hours you were defined: the competent one, the expert, the person everyone needed. Now you're home and nobody needs anything and the person standing here has no definition. The chest tightens. The question arrives: who am I when I'm not working. Feel your hands. Your breath. Your feet on the floor. These are yours whether you're in scrubs or not. The identity that gets built entirely around the care you give will feel empty when the giving stops. That's not failure. That's what happens when the work becomes the self. The person you are without the work doesn't disappear. But she becomes unfamiliar.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The cost is losing yourself to your role."
---
For years you've been building an identity made of competence and giving. The system rewards this. The profession demands it. The people around you praise it. But underneath, something is hollowed out. The self outside of care-giving has gotten smaller. The hobbies are secondary. The relationships are organized around your availability. Your worth is tethered to your output. The cost is that when the work changes or stops, the self that remains is underdeveloped. The person you could have become if you weren't constantly pouring into other people's lives is a shadow. That's the cost that accumulates slowly: the erosion of self into role.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You became more than what you gave."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You remember defining yourself completely through the work. Year one was all work, no boundaries between you and the role. Somewhere around year five, you started to notice the cost. The parts of yourself that had gone dormant. The interests that disappeared. The version of you that existed before the work. Building a self that the work feeds but doesn't define required intentional effort. You didn't wake up differently. You made choices to invest in the person you are when nobody needs anything from you. That's not self-discovery. That's reclamation.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would you want if nobody watched."
---
The real question isn't what you should do or be. It's what you'd want if nobody was measuring your worth. If there was no one to impress with your competence or your sacrifice. What would you choose just for you. You don't have to answer this. The question is just there, in the quiet time, when you're not performing care. It's asking whether the self you've built is actually what you want or whether it's what the profession asked for.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "She's disappeared into the work too."
---
She's been a nurse for seven years. If you ask her who she is, she'll tell you about her patients, her unit, her accomplishments at work. She won't tell you about hobbies or dreams or the person she could become if the work wasn't all-consuming. She's disappeared into the role so completely she doesn't remember there was a before. You see it because you've been there. You know what it costs to rebuild the self once it's been entirely absorbed.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The cost is the self you never developed."
---
The profession takes the years you could have spent discovering who you are outside of giving and redirects them into giving. The cost isn't dramatic. It's quiet. It's the hobbies you never took up, the relationships you never developed, the version of you that would have emerged if the work wasn't the organizing principle of your life. That's not unique to nursing. It's the cost of any profession that asks for your whole self. The cost is the alternate life you never get to live.
---
