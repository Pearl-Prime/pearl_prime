---
Chen makes an error in patient care. It's caught before harm occurs. She did the right thing by catching it. Yet the shame arrives. Her face flushes. She feels seen in her inadequacy. The error proves something she already suspected: she's not good enough. The shame is not about this specific mistake. It's the feeling that this mistake proves something fundamental about her worthiness.
