## EXERCISE v01
---

---
A patient trusted you with their life today.
They didn't know you felt like a fraud.
They trusted what they saw. Your competence.
Name one skill they witnessed you use.
You didn't make up that skill.
You trained. You learned. You practiced.
Imposter syndrome is a liar.
You might have doubt, but you have competence.
Look at what you actually do.
You save lives. You can't luck into that.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You made a mistake or didn't know something.
This means you're human. Not a fraud.
Every nurse has moments of not knowing.
The difference between fraud and learning?
Frauds hide. You're asking questions.
Frauds pretend. You're being honest.
Your willingness to learn is proof you belong.
Imposter syndrome makes you a better nurse.
It keeps you humble. It keeps you sharp.
That's not weakness. That's professionalism.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Write down three things you did right this shift.
Not small things. Real things.
You caught something. You comforted someone.
You stayed calm when things got hard.
These weren't lucky. These were skill.
Read your list when doubt creeps in.
Your doubt is loud. Evidence is louder.
You survived nursing school. You passed boards.
You're still working. You're trusted with vulnerable people.
That's not accident. That's competence.
