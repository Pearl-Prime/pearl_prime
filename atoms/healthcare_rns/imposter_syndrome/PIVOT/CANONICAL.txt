You passed boards. You earned certifications. You've kept people alive under conditions that would break most professionals. And still, some part of you walks onto the unit believing today is the day they find out. That belief isn't humility. It's a glitch running on outdated data.

--- variant: v02
The old frame says imposter syndrome means you're not good enough. Flip it. Imposter syndrome in nursing often means you're good enough to see how much you don't know — and honest enough to be unsettled by it. The real imposters never worry about being one.

--- variant: v03
You compare your inside to everyone else's outside on the unit. Your internal doubt versus their external competence. But you don't see their internal doubt. You only see the performance. And yours is just as convincing from where they're standing.
