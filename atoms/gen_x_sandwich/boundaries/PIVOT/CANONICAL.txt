## PIVOT v01

---
You were told that good daughters and good parents have no limits. That boundaries are selfish. But a sandwich with no edges is not a sandwich -- it is a mess. The reframe: every boundary you set is a load-bearing wall. Remove it and the whole structure buckles.

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says saying no to your aging parent or your teenager makes you the bad guy. The new frame: you have been saying yes to everyone else's comfort by saying no to your own survival. That math only works until it doesn't.

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You think boundaries push people away. But what is actually happening is their absence has been pulling you apart. You cannot pour from a pitcher that someone keeps knocking off the counter. The limit is not rejection. It is architecture.
