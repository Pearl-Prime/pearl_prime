

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've been doing this work for two decades, and some mornings I still wake up convinced that today is the day they find out I don't actually know what I'm doing. The imposter mechanism isn't about confidence. It's about the gap between what you were taught to expect of yourself and what you feel capable of delivering. I can see the track record of what I've accomplished, and I can simultaneously believe it's all accident or luck or the right timing. The mechanism feeds itself: you work harder to prove you're not a fraud, and that effort becomes evidence that you must be fraudulent. I teach my children to be confident, and I watch them absorb my doubt instead.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
Two decades in and I still believe today is the day they find me out. That gap between credentials and confidence never closes. The mechanism is that I see evidence of competence and read it as luck. I see accomplishments and believe they're accident or perfect timing or the right person at the right moment. What I can't do is trust my own judgment about my own work. Credentials exist somewhere. Confidence exists nowhere. The gap reads as luck or accident or perfect timing coincidence. The mechanism is that simple. You see evidence and read it as fraud.
