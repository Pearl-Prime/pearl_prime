## PIVOT v01
---

---
You feel like a fraud at work because half your brain is on your mother and the other half is on your kid. The old frame says that makes you less committed. The new frame: you are running three full-time roles on the processing power of one human brain. Imposter syndrome in the sandwich is not about lacking skill. It is about splitting attention across impossible demands and judging yourself by the standard of people carrying one.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old story says real professionals leave home at the door. You cannot leave home at the door because home follows you -- the mid-meeting text about dad, the school call during the presentation. Feeling like a fraud is not evidence that you are one. It is evidence that the definition of competence was written by someone with fewer plates spinning.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You look at colleagues who seem to have margin, focus, bandwidth. What you do not see is whether they are also managing medication schedules and college applications and their own disappearing sense of self. The pivot: the imposter feeling is not a verdict on your ability. It is the tax on doing invisible labor that nobody counts.
---
