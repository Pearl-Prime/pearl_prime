Carol's mother doesn't answer the phone at 4 p.m. Carol calls again. Still no answer. Carol's mind immediately enters the spiral. Her mother doesn't answer at 4 p.m. means something's wrong means Carol should have called earlier means Carol is failing means Carol's mother is suffering because Carol didn't call means Carol should have called at 3 p.m. means Carol is a terrible daughter. The spiral has activated. One missed call has become evidence of Carol's inadequacy as a caregiver. Her shoulders are tight. She's in the loop now.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Carol's spiral is self-perpetuating. She calls her mother to check on her. Her mother says, "You're checking so much. Do you think I'm in trouble?" Carol's concern has now activated her mother's anxiety, which means Carol's response to missing a call has generated an actual problem. Carol spirals again. She shouldn't have called because calling made her mother worried. But not calling would mean she doesn't care. Carol is trapped. The mechanism is that the spiral creates the very problem it fears. Carol's anxious vigilance is making her mother anxious. One missed call has become a chain of events: mother anxious, Carol more vigilant, mother more anxious, Carol more vigilant. Carol's chest is tight all the time now.
