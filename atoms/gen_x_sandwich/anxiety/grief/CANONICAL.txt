She's grieving her mother in real time while her mother is still alive. She's losing her mother before she dies. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_x_sandwich/anchored/anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Paula's mechanism is that her anxiety about grief is preventing her from grieving. She's anxious about how much it will hurt. So she stays in low-level anxiety rather than moving through the grief. But the mechanism is that this prevents actual processing. Paula is stuck in anxiety about a loss she can't prevent. Her mother will die. Paula will grieve. Paula can't stop this. But Paula's anxiety about the grief is making her numb. She's not fully present with her mother because she's too busy being anxious about what's coming. Her throat is perpetually closed. The mechanism is that Paula's fear of the emotion is preventing the emotion from moving through her.

## TURNING_POINT v01

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3

---
Paula has a day with her mother where her mother is lucid. Paula realizes this day might not come again. Paula chooses to grieve it even while it's happening. She tells her mother: "I'm so grateful for today." Paula cries. Her throat releases. The turning point is that Paula decides to feel the grief rather than stay anxious about it. Her chest opens slightly.
