## PIVOT v01
---

---
Your self-worth got wired to usefulness a long time ago. As long as you are needed -- by the kids, by your parents, by the office -- you matter. The crack in that frame: the day the kids leave and the caregiving ends, who are you? The new frame: you are not what you carry. You existed before the sandwich closed around you.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says your value is in what you provide. You provide everything. And you have never felt less valuable. That is the paradox of the sandwich generation -- the more you give, the more invisible you become to yourself. The pivot: worthiness was never about output. It just got buried under everyone else's needs.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You measure your worth in crises averted, schedules kept, and people held together. By that measure you are extraordinary. By the measure of your own joy, your own desires, your own name spoken without a request attached -- you have gone missing. The reframe: self-worth is not a performance review. It is the part of you that matters when nobody needs anything.
---
