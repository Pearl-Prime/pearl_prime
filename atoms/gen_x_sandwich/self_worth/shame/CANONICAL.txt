BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Pam stands in the middle of the pattern and sees it. The exposure and hiding runs hot—legs brace. Pam has carried this story about self worth for so long the weight became invisible. Now the weight announces itself. Goosebumps erupt across Pam's skin. The old frame says: endure. Push through. Keep performing. But the frame cracks. Pam watches the crack spread. The story that held everything together—the one about being strong enough, disciplined enough, good enough—stops making sense. Not because it was wrong. Because it was incomplete. Pam feels the incompleteness in the body. Something shifts behind the sternum. The old story doesn't fit the evidence anymore. Pam stands in the gap between what was believed and what is true.

---
Peak. Goosebumps erupt across Keith's skin. The exposure and hiding mechanism runs so hot it melts its own tracks. Keith's shoulders brace. The self worth becomes total. There is no outside. No perspective. No distance. Just the raw force of it. Keith is inside the center of the storm and the center is silent. In that silence, the old frame stops. Just stops. Like a clock that runs out. Keith waits for the old story to restart and it does not. The silence stretches. In the absence of the old frame, Keith notices what remains: a body, breathing. A floor, holding weight. A present moment with no narrative attached. The exposure and hiding pattern cannot operate without a story to run on. Keith stands in the gap.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2

Peak intensity strips everything to structure. Scott's skin prickles. The self worth hits maximum. The exposure and hiding pattern runs at capacity. Scott's ribs compress. In the crisis, the mechanism becomes naked. Scott sees it without narrative, without justification, without the story that makes it make sense. Raw machinery. The exposure and hiding pattern takes the pain of self worth and converts it into deeper isolation. The conversion is lossy. More energy goes in than comes out. The deficit accumulates in the body. The pattern costs more than it saves.
