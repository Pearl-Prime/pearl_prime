

## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C5
The generation after you expects you to be fine. You've always been fine. So you have to be fine. But fine is increasingly fictional. Fine is exhaustion pretending to be capacity. Fine is burnout performed well enough that nobody intervenes. That's the generation you are. That's where you're dying.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C1
The job is one shift. Then you call your mother to coordinate her cardiologist. Then you pick up your kid from the high school. Then you remember the deck is due tomorrow. The day is a relay race where you are every runner. Nobody else is showing up because nobody else has been positioned to. The position became the job over time, without a hire announcement.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C3
You have been the one who handles things since you were a teenager. The training was good — you handle things. The training was also a trap. You handle things because you handle things, even now, even when the things are too many. The competence became a load-bearing wall in everyone's expectations. Removing it would require an architectural conversation nobody is having.
