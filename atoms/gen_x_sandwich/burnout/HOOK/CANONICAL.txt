You drove to your mother's apartment at 7:42 PM to refill her med organizer because she had called you and the call had been the third time she had called this week about the same thing. You got there. You refilled the organizer. You explained the organizer for the eleventh time. You drove home. By the time you walked in your daughter was already in bed and your husband had eaten dinner without you. You stood in the kitchen at 9:18 PM with a paper plate and the leftovers and a body that had been on the clock since 6 AM in three different houses.
