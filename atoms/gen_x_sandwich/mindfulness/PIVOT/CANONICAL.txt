## PIVOT v01
---
---
You think presence means staying focused for both your parent and your kids all day. Try this instead: aim only for a faster return each time you notice you've left. A dozen fast returns beats one long, unnoticed drift.
---

## PIVOT v02
---
---
You think the goal is to stop your mind from going to the other generation's needs. Try this instead: let it go, notice sooner, and come back. The going was never the problem — the not-noticing was.
---

## PIVOT v03
---
---
You think carrying every worry about both generations with you proves you care. Try this instead: close each conversation before opening the next. One person, fully, beats two people at half-attention.
---

## PIVOT v04
---
---
You think you need to feel calm before you can be present. Try this instead: aim for accuracy, not calm — point your attention at what's real right now, stress included.
---

## PIVOT v05
---
---
You think a looping worry has to be resolved before you can focus on the next thing. Try this instead: park it with a specific time to revisit, then redirect back to the person you're actually with.
---

## PIVOT v06
---
---
You think drifting a dozen times in one day means you're failing at this. Try this instead: count the returns, not the drifts. A dozen returns is a dozen reps, not a dozen failures.
---

## PIVOT v07
---
---
You think presence requires extra time you don't have. Try this instead: attach one breath to something you already do at every interaction — picking up the phone, walking through a door.
---

## PIVOT v08
---
---
You think being scattered is the same as being busy. Try this instead: notice the difference — busy is a lot to manage; scattered is managing it all at forty percent. Do one thing fully, then the next.
---

## PIVOT v09
---
---
You think you have to wait for a lighter caregiving load to practice this. Try this instead: start on the hardest day you've got. That's the day where the return actually matters most.
---

## PIVOT v10
---
---
You think forgetting the cue for a few conversations means the habit failed. Try this instead: just pick it back up at the next one. Missed reps don't erase the ones that landed.
---

## PIVOT v11
---
---
You think a fast, automatic answer to a hard question is efficient. Try this instead: stop for half a second before you answer anything vulnerable, whether it's your parent or your child asking.
---

## PIVOT v12
---
---
You think the drifting itself is the failure. Try this instead: the mind wandering toward an unresolved worry is normal information, not a flaw. The only real miss is not noticing you left.
---
