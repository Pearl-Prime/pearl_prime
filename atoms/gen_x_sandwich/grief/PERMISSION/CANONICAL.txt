## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You are allowed to grieve someone who is still alive. Watching a parent become someone you do not recognize is a loss, and it deserves to be mourned even while you are still showing up for them every day.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You are allowed to miss the life you thought you would have. The one where your parents were healthy and your kids still needed bedtime stories and you had a weekend that belonged to you. That version was real to you. Its absence is a real loss.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You are allowed to cry about this even if nobody died. Grief in the sandwich is cumulative -- small losses stacked on each other until the pile is taller than any single event. You do not need a reason the world recognizes. Your body already does.
---
