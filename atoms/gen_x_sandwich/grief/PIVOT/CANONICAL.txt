## PIVOT v01
---

---
You think grief requires a funeral. But you have been grieving for years -- the parent who no longer remembers your name, the teenager who no longer needs you the way they used to, the version of yourself that had plans that did not revolve around other people. Sandwich grief is not one loss. It is a slow accumulation of leavings.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says you should be strong for everyone. Mom needs you steady. The kids need you present. So the grief goes underground and comes out sideways -- as irritability, as insomnia, as that hollow feeling in the parking lot after the care facility visit. The new frame: grief does not wait for permission. It leaks through whatever cracks it can find.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You are mourning someone who is still alive. You are mourning a version of your life that never arrived. These are real losses that do not fit on a sympathy card. The pivot: stop waiting for the grief to be big enough to count. It already counts. It has been counting for years.
---
