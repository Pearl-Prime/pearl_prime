## PIVOT v01
---

---
Bedtime is when the load you carried all day comes due. The house is quiet but your brain is not -- replaying the conversation with the doctor, the look on your teenager's face, the bills you moved around. The old frame says you have a sleep problem. The new frame: you have a daytime problem that only gets loud at night.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You did everything right today. Everyone was fed, medicated, supervised, and fine. And now you are lying in the dark with your heart racing. Sleep anxiety in the sandwich is not about tonight. It is about tomorrow, and the day after, and the indefinite stretch of days where you are the only one who cannot call in sick.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame says sleep is rest. For you, sleep is the one window where you are not in control -- and for someone whose family depends on their control, that window feels dangerous. The pivot: the anxiety is not about sleep. It is about what happens to everyone if you let go for eight hours.
---
