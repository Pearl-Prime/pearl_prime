## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The only quiet left is the problem time."
---
It's 11pm. The house is finally quiet. Everyone is asleep. You're the last one awake and now the noise starts. Not out loud. Inside. Your nervous system is finally still enough to hear everything it's been holding.

The doctor appointment your mom needs that you have to schedule. The school form your teenager didn't mention until tonight. The mortgage question you haven't addressed. The pills your mom needs to refill. Your own boss's email that's still sitting unanswered.

Your body is tense. Your mind is running lists. This is the only time you have to think and so you think about everything at once. All the impossible logistics. All the things that slip through because you're too busy managing the day-to-day to actually plan.

Notice your shoulders. You're sitting up. Your jaw is tight. The quiet time became worry time.

The list is real. The night is real. Both are here.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Sleep is the tax on keeping everyone afloat."
---
You have one hour of quiet left before sleep. One hour where you get to be alone. So naturally, that's when all the unsolved problems arrive.

The dental work your parent keeps postponing. The conversation you haven't had with your teenager about boundaries. Your own work that's piling up because you're tired. The doctor appointment you've been trying to schedule for two weeks.

You're losing sleep to the logistics of other people's lives. Your parent's needs, your kid's needs, your job's needs. By the time everyone is asleep, you're too wired to rest.

So you don't sleep. Or you sleep badly. Or you sleep and wake up at 3am with the full list running.

That's the cost of the sandwich. The night is when you finally have time to see it all.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if this needs less of you?"
---
You're running the list again. The doctor appointment, the form, the pills, the email. But what if some of this could wait? What if your teenager could handle their own form? What if your mom's pills are actually fine for another week?

What if you're solving problems that aren't actually your job to solve?

What if the list is partly real and partly the anxiety of feeling responsible for everyone?

What if you stopped right now and went to sleep and nothing catastrophic would actually happen?
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "They're all asleep. They have no idea."
---
Your parent is sleeping. Your teenager is sleeping. Your partner is sleeping. Everyone else has moved on to rest.

But you're awake, running lists, solving problems for all of them. You're bearing the weight of the logistics while they recover.

They don't know about the list. They don't know about this hour. They just know they got fed, their needs were met, their lives continued. They sleep fine.

You're the only one carrying the weight of keeping it all moving.

Your exhaustion is invisible to them because you've made sure they don't have to see it.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You've carried every impossible night before."
---
Six months ago you were awake at midnight running the same list. Three months ago, another night, different items but same weight. The sandwich has been doing this to your sleep for years.

And you're still here. The doctor appointments got scheduled eventually. The forms got handled. Your parent is still alive. Your teenager is still okay. Your job is still happening. You're still moving through it even though you haven't slept well in years.

The anxiety was right about the weight. It's been wrong about whether you could keep carrying it.

You're still showing up. Still managing. Still the person everyone else can rest because they know you're awake and handling it.

This is what you do. This is what you've always done.
---
