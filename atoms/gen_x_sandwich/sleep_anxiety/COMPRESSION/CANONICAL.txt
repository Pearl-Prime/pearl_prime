
## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C2
You are the household alarm system for two generations. Your phone sleeps next to your face. The body sleeps in the same posture you used during your kids' baby years and during your parents' first hospital stay — vigilant, light, ready. The vigilance does not turn off because you are tired. It turns off when the watchfulness is no longer needed. It is still needed.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C3
Hormonal shifts in midlife rewrote your sleep architecture. Cortisol patterns that worked in your 30s do not work in your 50s. The body that powered through the parenting years is now metabolizing differently. You are not failing at sleep. You are sleeping in a body that has changed the rules without sending you the new rulebook.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C5
Try this: protect a 30-minute pre-sleep window where the phone is in another room. Not the bedroom. Another room. The reflex to check is rooted in years of being the one who picks up. The body can learn that the room is now a place where the phone does not reach. That learning takes weeks. It is worth weeks.
