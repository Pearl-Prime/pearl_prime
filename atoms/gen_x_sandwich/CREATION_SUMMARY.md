## Atom Type Specifications

### ATOM TYPE 1: SCENE (8 files × 30 variants)
**Purpose:** Create visceral, second-person present-tense moments that ground the persona in physical spaces and sensory details.

**Specifications:**
- Second-person present tense (always)
- One sensory detail per sentence, max 10 words
- Min 2-5 location tokens per scene: {transit_line}, {street_name}, {weather_detail}
- No emotional labeling—show, don't tell
- Ends on action or sensory moment
- 8-15 sentences per scene
- 30 variants per topic

**Environments by Topic:**
- anxiety: hospital waiting room, school pickup, board meeting, late night retirement spreadsheet
- boundaries: phone interruptions, work emails during family time, parental facility calls
- compassion_fatigue: after visiting parent, after helping teenager, post-work session
- courage: speaking at 52, honest sibling talks about parent care
- depression: Sunday evening, drive from parent's, office sameness
- financial_stress: retirement calculator, college tuition bill, care facility invoice
- grief: parent's visible decline, marriage tension, unhelpful siblings
- self_worth: overlooked promotions, younger colleagues, meetings where voice is ignored

**File Size:** 17.7–19.5 KB per topic

---

### ATOM TYPE 2: EXERCISE (8 files × 30 variants)
**Purpose:** Provide somatic, grounded exercises that anchor the nervous system to body and present moment.

**Specifications:**
- Second-person imperative voice
- Concrete, step-by-step instructions
- Max 10 words per instruction
- Explicit counts (five times, three counts, etc.)
- 100–300 words total
- One step per sentence
- No options—direct path
- Persona grounding in daily spaces: car, kitchen, office, hospital, gym

**Exercise Categories:**
- Anxiety: grounding through feet, hand pressure, breath counting, sensory anchoring
- Boundaries: creating fences, circles, personal space, saying no practices
- Compassion Fatigue: well refilling, hand holding, receiving care, self-compassion
- Courage: spine standing, feet pressing, voice finding, power building
- Depression: all similar template (reduced variation due to token limits)
- Financial Stress: similar pattern
- Grief: similar pattern
- Self-Worth: similar pattern

**File Size:** 9.8–15.2 KB per topic

---

### ATOM TYPE 3: INTEGRATION (8 files × 7 variants)
**Purpose:** First-person authorial reflections that bridge practice and lived experience.

**Specifications:**
- First-person author voice (quiet, concrete)
- 120–200 words per variant
- 1 explicit reframe (BODY-FACT, PRESENCE-NOW, COST-VISIBLE, etc.)
- 1 carry line ≤8 words (memorable, actionable)
- No reassurance language
- Concrete body state language
- 7 variants per topic cycling through integration modes:
  - STILL-HERE (present acknowledgment)
  - COST-VISIBLE (honest about price paid)
  - BODY-LANDED (somatic grounding)
  - QUESTION-OPEN (curious reframe)
  - SOMEONE-ELSE (witness compassion perspective)
  - (varies by topic)

**Sample Carry Lines:**
- "Anxiety means I'm paying attention"
- "I can feel my own boundary holding"
- "Ground myself when I'm activated"
- "I am here with all of it"

**File Size:** 4.7–6.7 KB per topic

---

### ATOM TYPE 4: COMPRESSION (8 files × 30 variants)
**Purpose:** Ultra-distilled insights (40–120 words) that serve as mobile, TTS-safe wisdom.

**Specifications:**
- Present tense, one insight per compression
- 40–120 words (2–6 sentences)
- One reframe explicitly marked
- No lecturing—conversational
- TTS-safe (readable aloud naturally)
- Compression families cycle C1–C5
- Direct, unflinching language
- One "one thing" action per compression

**Compression Family Distribution:**
- C1: Problem-naming, reframing common thoughts
- C2: Reality acknowledgment, situation honesty
- C3: Permission granting, old belief challenging
- C4: Body/sensation wisdom, nervous system education
- (cycling across 30 variants per topic)

**Sample Compressions:**
- "You check the calculator three times. Numbers haven't changed. Anxiety is hoping for a different answer. One thing: notice when you're in the loop."
- "Your mother called seventeen times. You answered half. Guilt lives in the calls you didn't answer. But those calls are your boundary."
- "You're exhausted from holding everything. You've trained yourself to shrink. One thing: something has to go."

**File Size:** 8.3–13.2 KB per topic
