## PIVOT v01
---

---
You call it planning. Thinking ahead. Being responsible. But at 2 a.m. your brain is running simulations on your mother falling, your teenager driving, your retirement vanishing -- and none of them have solutions, only loops. The reframe: this is not thinking. It is your nervous system trying to control what cannot be controlled by rehearsing every disaster in advance.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says overthinking means you care. You do care. But the mental hamster wheel at midnight is not care -- it is the mind trying to outrun uncertainty by running every scenario twice. The pivot: you cannot think your way to safety when you are the safety net for two generations.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You have been told to just stop worrying. As if the brain that tracks medication schedules, emotional temperatures, and financial projections for an entire extended family has an off switch. It does not. The new frame: the overthinking is not the problem. The problem is that your situation genuinely requires more processing power than one mind has.
