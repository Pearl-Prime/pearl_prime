

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
There's a loop in my mind that won't shut off. I run the conversation again. I second-guess what I said. I imagine the other person's judgment. I rewind and revise and replay until the original moment is completely lost beneath the layers of my thinking about it. The mechanism is that overthinking feels like thoroughness. Like preparation. Like the thinking itself is productive. But really, it's just looping. The same thoughts, different angles, no new information, no resolution. Just the certainty that I missed something important and if I think about it enough, I'll find it.
