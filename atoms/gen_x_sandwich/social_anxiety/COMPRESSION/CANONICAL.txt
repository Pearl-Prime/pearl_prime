

## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C5
Try this: Before the next social moment, ask 'What do I want to know about this person?' instead of 'What will they think of me?' The performance anxiety doesn't mean you're doing it wrong. It means the stakes registered. They're real to you.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C1
Your friend group from your 20s scattered. Your work network is mostly transactional. Your kids' parents are friendly but not friends. You realize you have not had an unhurried conversation with someone outside your immediate family in a long time. The social muscle atrophied without you noticing. Now using it feels effortful.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C3
Every social commitment competes with the care commitments. Lunch with an old friend means a missed window for your mom's call. Dinner out means missing your kid's actual life update. The math is exhausting. The exhaustion gets read as social anxiety. The cause is the calendar. The system was not designed for this.
