

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
There's a mechanism that activates whenever I'm in a social situation. It's like a performance mode that turns on automatically. I become more polished, more careful with my words, more aware of how I'm coming across. I watch myself from outside my body, monitoring the impression I'm making.
The mechanism is so automatic now that I don't even notice it activating most of the time. It's just how I am in public. Composed, appropriate, carefully managing the persona that I'm presenting to the world.
For people my age, this mechanism probably developed early. We learned that the world wasn't safe, that people could hurt you if you showed too much of yourself, that the best strategy was to be careful about what you revealed. So we learned to perform. We learned to watch ourselves from outside and manage the image we were projecting.
The teaching here is about recognizing that the performance isn't actually voluntary anymore. It's automatic. The social anxiety has wrapped itself so completely around my social self that I can't distinguish between who I actually am and who I'm performing as.
What happens if you stop performing? That's the question I'm sitting with.
