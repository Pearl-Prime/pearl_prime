## PIVOT v01
---

---
You used to be social. Now the thought of a dinner party feels like another performance on a stage you did not audition for. The old frame says you have become antisocial. The new frame: you have been performing competence and togetherness for two generations and you have nothing left for an audience that wants small talk.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Social anxiety in the sandwich does not always look like fear. Sometimes it looks like relief when plans get canceled. Sometimes it looks like sitting in the car for ten minutes after arriving. The pivot: you are not broken. You are a person whose social battery is being drained by caregiving before you ever reach the party.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame says connection heals. And it does -- but only if the connection does not require you to perform being fine. You avoid people not because you dislike them but because the gap between how you are doing and what you are willing to say out loud has become too wide to bridge at a barbecue.
---
