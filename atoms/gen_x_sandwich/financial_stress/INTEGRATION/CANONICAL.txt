

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT

My mother called seventeen times this week. I answered maybe half. The calls I didn't answer sit in my chest like small stones. Guilt. Obligation. Fear that I'm failing her.

But I'm learning to notice: the guilt is information, not truth. My body knows the difference between a real emergency and a routine call. My nervous system can feel when I'm being needed versus when I'm being tested. I'm learning to trust my own signals.

When I said no to visiting this weekend, my mother was upset. I felt the upset. I sat with my own discomfort instead of jumping to fix hers. My jaw stayed tight. My shoulders stayed high. But I didn't cave. I stayed in the boundary.

The boundary held. She's still okay. I'm still okay. But different somehow. Smaller in some ways. Larger in others. My body knows I set it. My body knows I kept it.

**Carry line: I can feel my own boundary holding.**
