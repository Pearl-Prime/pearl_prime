## PIVOT v01
---

---
You think courage means doing the hard thing without flinching. But you flinch every day -- at the specialist's report, at the tuition bill, at the silence from your teenager -- and you do the hard thing anyway. The reframe: courage in the sandwich is not the absence of fear. It is showing up with shaking hands and a full calendar.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says courage is a single bold act. Your courage is quieter and more relentless -- the Tuesday night when you held your mother's hand and your daughter's homework in the same hour and did not let either one see you cry. That is not small courage. That is the kind they do not write stories about because it never gets a day off.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You have been waiting to feel brave before you act. But the bravest thing you do is wake up into a life that asks for more than one person can give and decide -- again -- to give it anyway. The pivot: stop looking for courage in grand gestures. It has been living in your Monday mornings this whole time.
