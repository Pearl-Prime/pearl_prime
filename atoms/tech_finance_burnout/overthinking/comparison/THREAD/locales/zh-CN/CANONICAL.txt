The shift is from **debugging** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the comparison trap, you stop treating your anxiety as a system error that needs to be patched out. You stop spending cognitive resources trying to logically disprove the feeling of illegitimacy.

Instead, you begin to hear what the trap is actually saying. It isn’t saying, *"You are inadequate."*
It is saying, *"I am trying to keep you safe from exposure."*

The comparison trap is not a judge; it is a **guard**. It compares you to others not to humiliate you, but to calibrate your performance so that you remain invisible enough to be safe, yet competent enough to be retained. It is an outdated survival strategy that believes if you can just find the perfect metric where you fit in, you will finally be allowed to stop performing.

Hearing it means acknowledging: *"I see you. You are working hard to protect me from the shame of being found out. But we are not in danger right now."*

This doesn’t make the feeling disappear instantly. But it changes the relationship. You are no longer fighting yourself. You are no longer trying to optimize away a part of your psyche that is terrified. You are allowing the fear to exist without letting it drive the car.

The "next room" is the space where you can feel the anxiety without needing to resolve it. It is the realization that **you can be anxious and still be legitimate.** The two are not mutually exclusive. The anxiety is a sensation; the legitimacy is a fact. They can coexist.
