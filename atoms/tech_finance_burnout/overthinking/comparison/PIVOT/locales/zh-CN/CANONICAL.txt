### The Missing Piece: The Termination Condition

### Why this might resonate (and where it might resist)

1.  **It attacks the "Safety = Vigilance" belief:** You believe that if you stop looking for errors, you will be exposed. PIVOT v04 argues that *continued looking* is the error. It redefines "stopping" not as negligence, but as **system efficiency**.

3.  **Force the output** when the timer hits zero, regardless of how you feel.
4.  **Observe the aftermath:** Did the system crash? Were you exposed? Or did the world continue?

Your nervous system expects a catastrophe when you stop the self-monitoring. The goal of this experiment is not to prove you are "good enough," but to collect data that **the catastrophe does not happen**. This helps update the "outdated or irrelevant data" your threat-detection system is using.
