The shift is from **defense** to **presence**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the overthinking, you stop treating your grief as a malfunction in your production system. You stop trying to debug your humanity.

In that stillness, the noise of the "sprint"—the urgent need to prove worth, to secure the next vesting cliff, to validate your existence through output—begins to fade. What remains is not a problem to be solved, but a witness to be acknowledged.

The "next room" is quieter. It is less about *doing* and more about *being*. It is where you might finally hear what the grief has been trying to say all along: that you are allowed to exist without earning it. That the loss was real, and the pain is valid, not because it makes you stronger or smarter, but simply because it happened to you.

It is the transition from managing the symptom to honoring the wound.
