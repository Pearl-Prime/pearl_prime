

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The mechanism is vigilance-based sleep prevention. I lie down and the system automatically treats sleep as a performance metric. How many hours do I have? Are they going to be enough? Am I successfully entering sleep or failing? The monitoring is automatic. I'm not consciously creating the anxiety. The system learned that tomorrow is important, therefore tonight's sleep is critical, therefore I must ensure success through vigilance.
And the vigilance is the exact thing ensuring failure. The mechanism is elegant in its self-defeat. The anxiety about sleep prevents sleep, which confirms that sleep is fragile and requires monitoring, which continues the anxiety. The loop is airtight. The divided attention, the monitoring, the part of my system that's running diagnostics instead of resting, is exactly why nothing works. The mechanism keeps running because it's configured to believe that stopping the monitoring equals losing control.
