You grieve the sleep you used to have — effortless, deep, restorative. Before the on-call rotations, before the 3am pages, before your nervous system learned to stay half-awake for Slack.

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The grief in sleep anxiety is for the rest you can't access. You're tired enough to sleep and wired enough not to. The gap between exhaustion and rest is where the grief lives.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Underneath the sleep anxiety is mourning for the person who could close their eyes without running tomorrow's scenarios. That person existed before this job. You miss them at 2am.
