### 1. Why "Shaking Hands" and "Cold Water" Work for You
You mentioned that your anxiety manifests physically (shoulder tension, chest tightness, stomach knots) and that you tend to live in your head ("pre-child self archived," over-analysis).
*   **Somatic Interruption:** Your mind is a loop of rumination. You cannot "think" your way out of a nervous system that is stuck in fight-or-flight. You must *feel* your way out. Cold water and shaking hands are **bottom-up** regulatory techniques. They bypass the cognitive critique ("Am I doing this right?") and directly signal safety to the vagus nerve.
*   **Boundary Physicalization:** For someone who feels their identity is completely merged with their duties (nurse/parent), physically feeling the boundary of your skin (hands, chest) helps re-establish where *you* end and *others* begin.

### 2. Reframing "Selfishness" as "Systemic Maintenance"
Your "good parent metric" and professional training likely label taking time for yourself as "selfish" or "negligent."
*   **Reframe:** You are not abandoning your duty; you are **decontaminating**. In nursing, you wouldn't reuse a contaminated glove. Emotionally, you are currently wearing "contaminated gloves" (absorbed stress from coworkers/family). Washing hands/shaking off stress is not self-indulgence; it is **infection control for your psyche**. If you don’t do this, you bring that contamination home to your children and partner, fueling the cycle of guilt.

### 3. Integrating These into Your Day (Low-Friction Strategy)
Given your time poverty and mental load, do not add these as "new tasks." Attach them to existing transitions:

*   **Transition 1: Leaving Work (The Airlock)**
    *   *Before* you get in the car or step onto public transport: **Exercise v02**. Shake your hands. Say silently: *"That was their shift. This is my body."* Visualize leaving the hospital’s emotional weight at the door. Do not carry the "team problems" into your home.

*   **Transition 2: The Bathroom Break (The Reset)**
    *   When you feel the chest tightness or stomach knots rising during a shift or while parenting: **Exercise v03**. Go to the sink. Use cold water. Feel the temperature. Remind yourself: *"My hands are mine. This moment is mine."* This breaks the automaticity of the anxiety loop.

*   **Transition 3: The Morning/Evening Anchor**
    *   **Exercise v01**. Place hands on chest. Breathe for *you*, not for the patient, not for the child, not for the aging parents. Just for the biological organism that is *you*. This counters the "structural emptiness" by filling the vessel with your own presence, even if just for 30 seconds.
