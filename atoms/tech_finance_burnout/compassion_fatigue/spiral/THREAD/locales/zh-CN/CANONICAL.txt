The shift is from **management** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the spiral, you stop treating your exhaustion as a mechanical failure that needs repair so you can return to service. You stop viewing the fatigue as an obstacle to your availability.

Instead, you begin to hear the spiral not as noise, but as a signal. It is the only part of you that is still telling the truth. While your professional persona performs certainty and availability, the spiral is expressing the reality of your depletion.

Hearing it means acknowledging that the "flatness" and the "binary thinking" are not defects in your character, but protective adaptations. They are the nervous system’s way of saying: *I cannot process any more nuance because I am surviving.*

In this next room, the goal isn't to dissolve the spiral. It’s to understand what it is guarding. It is likely protecting you from the grief of realizing that your constant availability did not save the system, nor did it make you safe from abandonment. It is protecting you from the terrifying possibility that if you stop performing, you might cease to exist in the eyes of others—and perhaps, in your own.

To hear it is to ask: *What is the cost of this protection?* And more importantly: *What is the pain it is keeping at bay?*
