COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
After mentoring the third junior developer this week who is struggling. Kiran walks into the room where the absence lives loudest. The muscles in his back seize from the base of the skull downward. For months the frame has held: putting down the weight means it never mattered in the first place. Now Kiran watches the frame buckle under evidence it cannot metabolize. His body registers the shift before his mind can catch up. The chest opens half an inch. Something loosens at the base of his spine. The muscles that held the old posture begin to release. Carrying the weight is not the same as honoring the memory — the memory survives without the ache. The old story does not end with a conclusion. It ends with a silence. Kiran stands in that silence. His hands open at his sides.

COST_TYPE: identity

---
Marcus moves through after mentoring the third junior developer this week who is struggling. The muscles in his back seize from the base of the skull downward. The grief arrives — hot, familiar, insistent, demanding the old response. This time Marcus sets the weight down with both hands and lets the memory stand on its own. Not as strategy. Not as technique. As identity. The sensation remains in his body — chest tight, breath shallow, adrenaline still circulating. Marcus acts through it. The acting is not despite the sensation. It is through the sensation and alongside it. The action carries his name. This is who Marcus is now. Someone who moves with the fire still burning.

## EMBODIMENT v06

Listening to another team member describe their own burnout. The floor drops. Yuki grabs the edge of the nearest surface with both hands. The grief pushes with full force against her sternum. Yuki holds the memory open-handed, without gripping, and lets it exist without the ache. The body shakes with the effort. The choice holds anyway. This is not willpower overriding sensation. This is ownership absorbing sensation. Yuki moves from the new ground she has claimed. Her hands tremble and her hands act. Both facts are true simultaneously. The identity is not a concept. It lives in her muscles, in the steadiness of her spine, in the way her feet stay planted while the old pattern screams for retreat.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/compassion_fatigue/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nolan pauses while listening to another team member describe their own burnout. A band of tension cinches his ribs. Each breath costs effort. The grief has run for hours without interruption. Now he sees the machinery laid bare. Carrying the pain has become the last remaining thread to what was, and setting it down feels like severing that thread. Nolan traces the loop in real time: trigger, activation, the relief that never actually arrives, then the trigger again. His stomach clenches as he maps the circuit from inside it. Seeing the mechanism does not stop the gears. But it strips the mechanism of its authority. Nolan watches the loop turn without feeding it another round.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06

COST_INTENSITY: 5

---
Listening to another team member describe their own burnout. Nausea rolls through like a rogue wave. Aiden grips the counter to stay vertical. The grief operates in plain view for the first time. Aiden catches the pattern mid-cycle and holds still long enough to see the entire architecture. Carrying the pain has become the last remaining thread to what was, and setting it down feels like severing that thread. The mechanism persists because it disguises itself as protection — it wears the costume of something necessary. Aiden watches the loop close and reopen. His body holds the tension of seeing without intervening. The seeing does not resolve the pattern. But it makes the pattern visible, and visibility changes everything.
