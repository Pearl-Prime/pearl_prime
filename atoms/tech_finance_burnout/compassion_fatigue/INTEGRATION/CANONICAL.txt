

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: TANK-FACT
---
My compassion tank is at thirty percent. I know this because I can feel it. My jaw is tight. My hands are reluctant. When someone asks for help my first instinct is to hide. That's the body reporting: empty. I'm not ignoring it anymore. I'm protecting the remaining thirty percent. I'm not trying to refill it immediately. I'm just not draining it further. My body is giving me information. I'm listening.
Carry: My tank level shows in my body.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: TRADE-FACT
---
Every time I help someone there's a cost. To me. To my sleep. To my margin. It's not free help. I'm paying in coins I don't have. I see that now. Not as judgment. Just as fact. I can choose to pay it. Some of the time. But I can't always afford it. The cost is real.
Carry: Care has a price I'm paying.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: ENDURANCE-FACT
---
I said no to someone today. They were sad. I felt the sadness I couldn't fix. And I'm still here. The sadness didn't destroy me. Their disappointment didn't make me collapse. I can feel something without being responsible for fixing it. That's new.
Carry: I can witness pain I don't fix.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: DEPENDENCE-FACT
---
What if people I help are becoming too dependent on my help? What if my availability has made them less resourceful? I'm wondering about that. Not blaming myself. Just wondering. Maybe my most compassionate act is to create space for their own solutions.
Carry: Dependency can masquerade as compassion.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: WITNESS-FACT
---
I watched someone set a mentoring boundary. They said: one hour per week. They set a time. When time ended they said: that's our time for this week. The mentee adapted. The boundary didn't destroy the relationship. It structured it. I can do that.
Carry: Boundaries can hold relationships too.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: DEPLETION-FACT
---
My body is telling me something clear: I'm depleted. Not tired. Depleted. There's a difference. Tired gets sleep. Depletion gets recovery. Stops. Margin. I feel it in my bones. In my slowness. In my inability to be present. I'm listening.
Carry: Depletion sounds like this inside.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: CAPACITY-FACT
---
I have limits. I'm noticing them. Not fighting them. When my compassion tank says empty I believe it. I don't push through. I stop. And somehow I'm still okay. The people I stop helping find other resources or manage differently. I'm not actually irreplaceable. That's both sad and freeing.
Carry: Irreplaceability was a heavy false identity.
