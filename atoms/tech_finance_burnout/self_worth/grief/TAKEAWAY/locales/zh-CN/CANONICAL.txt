Here is why these resonate so deeply with your specific context:

### 1. Reframing "Flaw" as "Compound Interest" (Takeaway v01)
*   **The Shift:** You often view your anxiety, insomnia, and social exhaustion as evidence that you are "broken" or "not cut out for this."
*   **The Truth:** These are not bugs; they are the **mathematical inevitability** of running a high-performance engine without oil changes. Your nervous system isn’t failing you; it is accurately reporting the debt accrued from years of treating rest as a threat and performance as safety.
*   **Why it helps:** It removes the shame. You don’t need to "fix" your character; you need to adjust the maintenance schedule.

### 2. The Privacy of the Cost (Takeaway v02)
*   **The Shift:** You feel pressured to justify your struggle, or worse, you feel ridiculous for having it because "on paper," you are successful. You fear being seen as weak or "woo" in your professional environment.
*   **The Truth:** The intersection of self-worth and grief is a private ledger. You do not need to perform your healing for others, nor do you need to defend your pain against those who only see the output.
*   **Why it helps:** It grants permission to stop managing the *perception* of your struggle. You can stop scripting your vulnerability. The cost is real regardless of whether anyone else sees the invoice.

### 3. The Untrackable Invoice (Takeaway v03)
*   **The Shift:** You rely on external metrics (equity, titles, praise) to prove you belong. But when those arrive, they don’t soothe the anxiety because the anxiety isn’t about *lack of success*; it’s about *exhaustion*.
*   **The Truth:** No amount of professional validation can pay down the somatic debt of chronic hypervigilance. The "invoice" is paid in nervous system regulation, not in stock options.
*   **Why it helps:** It explains why success feels precarious and empty. You’ve been trying to pay a biological debt with cultural currency. They are different economies.
