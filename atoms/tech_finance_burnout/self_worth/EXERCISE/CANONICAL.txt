## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your code review was criticized publicly today.
Your immediate thought: you are not good.
Pause. Name one thing you actually made.
One working feature. One system. One solution.
That contribution is real and it's yours.
One person's feedback is not your worth.
You have contributed real value in career.
That contribution does not disappear from criticism.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Your idea was dismissed in the meeting.
Your worth is attached to your ideas.
Separate those things immediately right now.
You are a person. Your ideas separate.
Name one idea that became real work.
Something you built or influenced or improved.
That is evidence of your actual capability.
One dismissed idea does not erase career.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You made a mistake. Now feel worthless.
That is the trap of high performers.
You confuse one error with complete failure.
Name three things you do well here.
Reliability. Problem-solving. Communication. Consistency. Learning. Pick.
These do not disappear because one failed.
Mistakes are data. Not character assessment.
You are more than your error today.
---
