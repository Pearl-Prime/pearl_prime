These three iterations capture the mechanics of your internal critic with terrifying precision. They illustrate how your analytical mind, which is a asset in your work, becomes a weapon against your selfhood when applied to your worth.

Here is an analysis of why these pivots resonate so deeply with the patterns we’ve identified, and where the "glitch" in the logic lies:

### The Mechanism of the Spiral

**PIVOT v01: The Totalizing Leap**
*   **The Logic:** Critical Feedback → Inadequacy → Worthlessness.
*   **The Pattern:** This is the **inherited belief system** in action. It bypasses the professional context entirely. Because you equate *professional competence* with *personal legitimacy*, a critique of code is interpreted by your nervous system as a critique of your right to exist or belong. It’s not just "I made a mistake"; it’s "I am a mistake."

**PIVOT v02: The Retroactive Erasure**
*   **The Logic:** Current Failure → Past Invalidated → Future Collapsed.
*   **The Pattern:** This reflects the **imposter syndrome** that interprets success as luck. If this one piece of feedback is true, then all previous successes were anomalies or deceptions. It turns your career into a "house of cards" because you never internalized the foundation as *yours*. You are waiting for the wind to blow, and this feedback is the gust.

**PIVOT v03: The Compound Interest of Shame**
*   **The Logic:** Current Event + Archived Failures = Unbearable Weight.
*   **The Pattern:** This is the **hypervigilant cataloging**. Your brain keeps a ledger of every perceived misstep, social awkwardness, or error. When a new trigger occurs, it doesn't stand alone; it activates the entire archive. You aren't reacting to one code review; you are reacting to ten years of accumulated "evidence" that you are secretly unqualified.

### The Core Distortion

The common thread across all three pivots is the **collapse of context**.

In reality:
*   A code review is a **technical artifact**. It is about syntax, logic, efficiency, or style. It is objective, mutable, and separate from the self.

In your internal experience:
*   A code review is a **moral verdict**. It is subjective, fixed, and central to identity.

Your nervous system is treating a **data point** (a comment on a pull request) as a **threat to survival** (exposure as a fraud). This is why the spiral feels so physical and exhausting—it is a full-body stress response to a cognitive event.

### A Counter-Pivot (For Observation, Not Immediate Belief)

If the current spiral is:
> *Feedback → I am flawed → I was always flawed → I am nothing.*

A somatic, reality-based counter-pivot might look like this:
> *Feedback → My nervous system is spiking → This is a familiar false alarm → The code is separate from me → I can edit the code without editing my worth.*

You don't have to believe this yet. The goal right now is simply to **name the mechanism**. When you feel the spiral start, can you identify which Pivot you are running? Is it v01 (Totalizing), v02 (Retroactive), or v03 (Compound)?

Naming it creates a tiny gap between the trigger and the reaction. In that gap, there is a choice—even if the only choice initially is to breathe and wait for the chemical surge to pass.
