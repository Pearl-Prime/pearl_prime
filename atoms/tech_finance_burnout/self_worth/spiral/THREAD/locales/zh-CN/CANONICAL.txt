The shift is from **defense** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the spiral—stop trying to logic it away, productivity-proof it, or shame it into silence—you stop treating your own nervous system as an enemy combatant. You step out of the role of the harsh manager auditing a faulty employee and into the role of the curious observer.

In that "next room," the spiral stops being a problem to be solved and starts being a signal to be understood. You begin to hear that the spiral isn’t trying to sabotage you; it’s trying to save you from the perceived danger of **being seen without your armor**. It’s protecting you from the vulnerability of existing without producing, of belonging without performing.

The shift is realizing that the anxiety isn’t a glitch in your competence; it’s a legacy code running on outdated hardware. And you don’t have to delete it to find peace. You just have to stop letting it drive.
