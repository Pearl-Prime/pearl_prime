**On PERMISSION v01:**

You can still deliver. But you don’t have to be emotionally sterile to do it.

**On PERMISSION v02:**
This breaks the isolation. Your colleagues’ apparent ease is often just their own facade, or simply a different set of circumstances. It does not invalidate your pain. Your grief over the lost team, the lost identity, and the "wasted" years is heavy. Comparing your internal chaos to their external calm is a form of self-punishment. **On PERMISSION v03:**
Secrecy fuels the "golden handcuffs." When you keep the struggle private, it becomes a monster in the dark. Naming it—"I am overwhelmed," "I am grieving," "I feel trapped"—does not make you weak. It makes you real. It breaks the spell of the "imposter" who must never be seen struggling. You don’t have to quit your job to name the truth. You just have to stop lying to yourself about how much it costs you to stay.
