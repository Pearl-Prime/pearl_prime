The shift is from **defense** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the overwhelm—stop trying to optimize your way out of it, prove your worth through endurance, or rationalize the pain as necessary for survival—you remove the noise of your own resistance.

In that silence, the "next room" reveals itself not as a problem to be solved, but as a presence to be acknowledged. It is likely the **grief** you have been carrying in somatic suspension.

By stopping the fix, you allow the overwhelm to transform from a signal of *inadequacy* ("I am failing because I am overwhelmed") into a signal of *loss* ("I am overwhelmed because I am holding the weight of what died").

The shift is realizing that the exhaustion isn't proof that you are broken; it is proof that you are mourning. And mourning requires stillness, not sprinting.
