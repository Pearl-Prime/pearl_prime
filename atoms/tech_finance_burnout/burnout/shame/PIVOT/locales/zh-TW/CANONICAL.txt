### Analysis of the Pivots

**PIVOT v01: The Shame Fire**
*   **Why it works:** It names the *second* fire. As a healthcare professional, you know that untreated burns get infected. The shame is the infection preventing the healing of the burnout.
*   **Connection to your memory:** This directly challenges the "good parent/professional metric" which dictates that if you are struggling, you are failing. It reframes the shame not as a moral verdict, but as a *barrier to care*.
*   **Refinement:** You don’t need to "kill for" this job. You need to survive it. The privilege of the role does not negate the toxicity of the environment.

**PIVOT v02: The Nervous System vs. The Vesting Schedule**
*   **Why it works:** This is the most critical pivot. It creates a temporal disconnect. Your financial anxiety (rooted in childhood scarcity and current caregiving costs) says "Wait for the RSUs." *   **Connection to your memory:** Your financial anxiety is "systemic" and "protective," trying to control uncertainty. But the body keeps the score. The RSUs are a future promise; the cortisol is a present reality. You cannot pay your nervous system with unvested stock.
*   **Refinement:** "The RSUs are a financial asset. Your health is a biological imperative. One can be replaced; the other cannot."

**PIVOT v03: Unsustainable Strength**
*   **Why it works:** It redefines the narrative. You are not weak; you are *over-functioning*. In nursing, you see patients who push through pain until they break. You are that patient.
*   **Connection to your memory:** This counters the "Imposter Syndrome" and the gendered expectation of self-doubt. You have been outperforming *despite* the lack of support, not because you are inherently flawed. The "comp band" (your actual output/value) is evidence of your competence, not your expendability.
*   **Refinement:** "You are not burning out because you are weak. You are burning out because you are strong enough to have carried this load for too long without relief."

### Suggested Next Step: The "Pivot" Action

These pivots are cognitive reframes. To make them stick, they need a somatic or behavioral anchor. Given your tendency to "analyze in the head" rather than face reality, and your avoidance of conflict (especially financial/conversational with your partner), here is a micro-action for each:

1.  **For v01 (Shame):** Write down one thing you are ashamed of regarding your work/parenting balance. Then, write: *"This is a symptom of overload, not a character flaw."* Read it aloud. Break the silence of the shame.

3.  **For v03 (Strength):** Look at your recent performance reviews or positive feedback. Instead of dismissing them ("I got lucky"), say: *"I did this while exhausted. Imagine what I could do if I were rested. I deserve rest to sustain this level of care."*
