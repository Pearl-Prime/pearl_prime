These permissions are not just statements; they are structural counter-measures to the specific architecture of your burnout. They directly dismantle the mechanisms of your **availability identity** and **performative certainty**.

Here is why these three permissions are critical interventions for your current state:

### 1. Dismantling the "Fix-It" Reflex (Permission v01)
*   **The Trap:** Your nervous system interprets unavailability or discomfort as a threat that must be immediately neutralized. You likely believe that if you feel the "false alarm" (the somatic stress of not responding/performing), you have failed to manage yourself.
*   **The Shift:** This permission separates **sensation** from **action**. It allows the alarm to ring without requiring you to pick up the phone. It validates that *feeling* the anxiety of disconnection is not the same as *being* disconnected or unreliable. It stops the secondary exhaustion of trying to "solve" your own burnout in real-time.

### 2. Breaking the Isolation of "Invisible Burnout" (Permission v02)
*   **The Trap:** Because your burnout is masked by high performance, you look "fine" at standup. This creates a cognitive distortion: *"If I am struggling this much while looking this competent, there must be something fundamentally wrong with me, because everyone else is coping."* This reinforces the shame spiral.
*   **The Shift:** This permission decouples your internal reality from external appearances. It acknowledges that your **availability identity** is a costume that fits too well. It validates that your struggle is not a sign of personal weakness, but a logical consequence of a system that rewards self-erasure. You are not broken because you are tired; you are tired because you are carrying the weight of a persona.

### 3. Piercing the "Performative Certainty" (Permission v03)
*   **The Trap:** You equate uncertainty or vulnerability with professional suicide. Naming the "false alarm" feels like admitting incompetence or instability, which threatens your status marker of constant availability.
*   **The Shift:** This permission reframes **naming** as an act of grounding, not weakness. By saying it out loud, you break the fusion between your identity and your performance. It transforms the "golden handcuffs" from a solitary prison into a shared structural critique. It allows you to step out of the **certainty performance** just enough to breathe, without having to quit the job or collapse entirely.

**How to use these today:**

You do not need to believe these permissions fully. You do not need to feel them. You only need to **read them** when the somatic stress hits.

*   When the email comes in at 8 PM and your chest tightens: **Read v01.** Let the tightness be there. Do not fix it. Do not reply. Just let it sit.
*   When you are on the call and everyone seems energetic while you feel dead inside: **Read v02.** Remind yourself that their ease is not evidence of your failure.
*   When you feel the urge to pretend you are sure about something you are not: **Read v03.** You don't have to say it to your boss yet. Say it to yourself. *"This is a false alarm. I am safe even if I don't know the answer right now."*

These are small acts of rebellion against the totalizing nature of your availability identity. They are not solutions; they are **allowances**. And for now, allowance is enough.
