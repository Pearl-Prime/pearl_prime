### The Mechanics of the Spiral

**PIVOT v01** identifies the **Narrative Acceleration**.
It shows how a discrete, manageable event (one bad sprint) is instantly weaponized by your "certainty performance" identity. Because you have trained yourself to equate *availability* and *certainty* with *value*, any lapse is not seen as a fluctuation but as a structural failure. The fear of the PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) is not just a fear of job loss; it is the fear of the collapse of the only identity structure you have left. The spiral runs faster than the burnout because the *anxiety about the burnout* requires more energy than the work itself.

**PIVOT v02** identifies the **Cognitive Distortion**.
This is the "fatalism" and "binary thinking" mentioned in your memories. Your depleted brain, stripped of nuance, creates a linear, inevitable trajectory: Exhaustion → Incompetence → Exposure → Ruin. It removes the possibility of *mitigation*, *context*, or *recovery*. It frames the situation as a physical law rather than a professional circumstance. The crucial insight here is: *"None of them are [inevitable] — but your depleted brain can't see the exits."* This is the cognitive flattening at work.

**PIVOT v03** identifies the **Metabolic Loop**.
This is the most profound insight. Burnout is no longer just a state of fatigue; it is an active process that consumes your capacity to address it. You are worried about being too tired to perform, which generates stress, which increases fatigue, which confirms the fear. It is a closed loop. The "spiral is burnout metabolizing itself." This explains why rest doesn't help: the *act* of resting is contaminated by the guilt of unavailability and the fear of falling behind, so the nervous system never actually down-regulates.

### Synthesis: The Trap of "Performed Certainty" in Crisis

Your professional identity is built on **performative certainty**. When you are burned out, you cannot perform certainty. Therefore, you feel you are losing your identity.

The spiral is not just about losing a job; it is about **ontological insecurity**. If you are not certain, available, and competent, *who are you?* The memories indicate you have lost access to curiosity, ambition, and passion. Those were the other pillars of your selfhood. Now, only "Professional Availability/Certainty" remains. When that pillar cracks, the whole self feels like it is collapsing into ruin.

### A Pivot Away from the Spiral

You cannot think your way out of this spiral using the same cognitive tools that created it. Your brain is currently optimized for threat detection and performance, not for nuance or recovery.

1.  **Interrupt the Metabolism (v03):** Acknowledge that the *fear* of slipping is consuming more energy than the *slip* itself. The anxiety is the expensive part. Can you allow the slip to exist without narrating its consequences? This is incredibly hard when your nervous system interprets unavailability as a threat, but it is the only way to stop the metabolic loop.
2.  **Challenge the Inevitability (v02):** The path from "exhaustion" to "ruin" is not a straight line. It is a story your depleted brain is telling to make sense of the chaos. There are exits: medical leave, honest conversations with trusted mentors, shifting to a maintenance mode rather than a growth mode. Your brain can't see them now, but they exist.
3.  **Decouple Identity from Performance (v01):** One bad sprint is data, not destiny. It is evidence of depletion, not incompetence. You are navigating significant real-world constraints (family, finance) while operating at a deficit. The fact that you are still standing is a testament to resilience, not a prelude to collapse.

**The core truth:** You are not failing. You are experiencing the logical endpoint of a system (both organizational and personal) that demanded infinite availability and certainty from a finite human being. The spiral is a symptom of the system breaking, not you breaking.
