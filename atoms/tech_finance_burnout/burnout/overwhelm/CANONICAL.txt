## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
A band of tension cinches his ribs. Each breath costs effort. Raj is at the IDE at 11 PM on a Friday with no one else online when he feels a new request arrive and the entire system tilt. The operating assumption — everything is equally urgent and nothing can wait — meets a fact it cannot absorb. His jaw unclenches. Not relief. Disorientation. The frame that held his world in place tilts on its axis. Raj does not fall. He stands in the vertigo and lets the room reorient around his new position. The pile is made of individual items that can be addressed one at a time. He does not grasp for a replacement frame. He breathes into the space where the old certainty stood and finds the space survivable.

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Dev stands in the fluorescent hum of the empty office at midnight. Cold sweat sheets across his back. His shirt clings to the skin. He feels the system hits total capacity and no new input can be processed at all. The frame that has held everything in place — falling behind means failing entirely — strains under the weight of what Dev sees. For one long second the old architecture holds. Then it cracks. Dev feels the fracture travel through his chest. The story that organized every decision, every reaction, every judgment stops organizing. Sequence exists even when the panicking mind denies it — one thing, then the next. Dev does not reach for a replacement. He stands in the gap where the certainty lived, breathing into the new and terrifying openness.

Aiden stares at the same ticket for the third consecutive day. The muscles in his back seize from the base of the skull downward. The overwhelm arrives — hot, familiar, insistent, demanding the old response. This time Aiden puts everything down except the next single action and begins there. Not as strategy. Not as technique. As identity. The sensation remains in his body — chest tight, breath shallow, adrenaline still circulating. Aiden acts through it. The acting is not despite the sensation. It is through the sensation and alongside it. The action carries his name. This is who Aiden is now. Someone who moves with the fire still burning.

Marcus pauses while staring at the same ticket for the third consecutive day. His throat closes around the words. The vocal cords tighten. The overwhelm has run for hours without interruption. Now he sees the machinery laid bare. The system conflates volume with urgency, treating everything as equally critical when triage is the actual need. Marcus traces the loop in real time: trigger, activation, the relief that never actually arrives, then the trigger again. His stomach clenches as he maps the circuit from inside it. Seeing the mechanism does not stop the gears. But it strips the mechanism of its authority. Marcus watches the loop turn without feeding it another round.
