## TAKEAWAY v01
---
---
Presence was never a place you arrive and stay. It's a rep you run every time you notice you left, and the rep is the whole game.
---

## TAKEAWAY v02
---
---
The mind that wanders fifty times and returns in two seconds beats the mind that wanders five times and stays gone for twenty minutes. Speed of return is the metric.
---

## TAKEAWAY v03
---
---
Half-presence charges you full price for both rooms and pays out in neither. Full switches beat a permanent smear.
---

## TAKEAWAY v04
---
---
Caring about the outcome and monitoring the outcome feel like the same motion. They aren't. You can keep one and drop the other.
---

## TAKEAWAY v05
---
---
Your body knows you left before your mind admits it. The held breath and the climbing shoulders are the alarm — use them.
---

## TAKEAWAY v06
---
---
Nobody at the table can measure your internal focus. They can only clock the half-second delay. Fix the visible signature first.
---

## TAKEAWAY v07
---
---
The residue doesn't clear because you changed rooms. It clears because you closed the loop on purpose before you walked in.
---

## TAKEAWAY v08
---
---
A thought showing up isn't the failure. Chasing it into a full rehearsal is. Let it land, and don't pick up the thread.
---

## TAKEAWAY v09
---
---
The quiet kind of attention is a different skill than your trading-floor focus, not a weaker version of it. Train it like you trained the other one.
---

## TAKEAWAY v10
---
---
Unbounded checking leaks all day for no return. A bounded window contains the exposure and still gets the job done.
---

## TAKEAWAY v11
---
---
You are not failing every time your mind drifts to the desk. You are only failing if you never bring it back.
---

## TAKEAWAY v12
---
---
One rep, done honestly, is the whole assignment. Not the perfect dinner. Not the flawless meeting. Just the one catch, the one return.
---
