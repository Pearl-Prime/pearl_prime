## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Mechanism: half-presence charges full cost in two rooms and delivers benefit in neither — you don't get the market edge from constant checking and you don't get the room you physically occupy either, because your attention was never fully in it. The fix isn't suppression; you can't stop trade-thoughts from arising, and trying to guarantees you quit. The fix is shrinking the return time: notice the drift, name it once, silently, and re-anchor to one concrete sensory detail in the actual room. Presence is a rate of return, not an achieved state — measured by how fast you come back, not by whether you ever stop leaving.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Mechanism: attention residue means switching rooms doesn't clear the previous room from your processing — a portion keeps running in the background, tax­ing whatever you do next, until you deliberately close the loop. Fix: a specific closing ritual before the transition — name the open item out loud, park it with a concrete detail ("waiting on redline, 9am"), then let the first twenty minutes of residue be expected, not alarming. Vague closure doesn't register as a boundary to your own brain. Specific closure does.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
Mechanism: a trained vigilance system built for real market risk doesn't have a setting for "this room has no exposure" — it fires the same alarm at your kid's bedtime as it does at an open position, because it pattern-matches on unmonitored, not on actual stakes. Fix: consciously flag pre-identified low-exposure windows out loud — "this window has no real exposure" — as an accurate override, not wishful thinking. The reflex updates slowly on new data, the same way any pattern-matcher does, not on a single decision to relax.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Mechanism: caring about an outcome and monitoring it feel identical from the inside, so people assume dropping the monitoring means abandoning the caring. It doesn't — monitoring is a habit riding on top of caring, not a requirement of it. Fix: ask, in the actual moment your mind drifts, "can my attention right now change this outcome?" If the honest answer is no, the monitoring is pure cost, anxiety dressed as diligence. You can still care fully. You just don't have to also watch.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
---
Mechanism: your body registers the drift into work-mode before your conscious mind catches up — shortened breath, climbing shoulders, a set jaw — all firing before you've registered the thought that triggered them. Fix: use the physical tell as the alarm instead of trying to catch the slippery thought itself. One deliberate long exhale and dropped shoulders makes it physiologically hard to stay fully in the market headspace — the two states don't coexist well, so the body's return can pull the mind's return with it.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---
---
Mechanism: a thought about the deal arriving in your mind is involuntary and untrainable; chasing that thought into a full mental rehearsal is a separate, later step, and that step is the actual trainable moment. Most people conflate arrival with pursuit and conclude they have no choice point at all. Fix: notice the thought land, acknowledge it once ("that showed up"), and decline to follow the thread it's offering. It will return. Let it. Each declined chase weakens the habit of chasing.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---
---
Mechanism: multitasking between the room and the market isn't parallel processing — it's rapid switching with a re-orientation cost at every switch, degrading both the conversation and the market-tracking simultaneously, the same tax you already understand from running two live positions at once. Fix: do full switches instead of a diffuse smear — completely in the room, then completely at the check, then completely back. Diluted everywhere loses to fully present somewhere, every time it's tested.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---
---
Mechanism: unbounded attention to work leaks constantly across every room you occupy with no offsetting benefit, since most stray checks accomplish nothing actionable in the moment. Bounded attention — a pre-committed check window — contains the same exposure to a schedule instead of letting it run through every dinner and bedtime. Fix: pre-commit to specific check windows, and treat any urge outside them as a cue to return to the room, not a cue to act. The urge weakens as the reward stops arriving on demand.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---
---
Mechanism: rehearsing your next Slack reply while someone talks to you runs on the same processing capacity you need to actually hear them, so you lose both — a half-formed draft you'll rewrite anyway, and a conversation you weren't present for. Fix: let the reply wait until you're actually reading the message in full. Nothing is gained by drafting mid-conversation; something is lost every time, guaranteed, by not being where you currently are.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---
---
Mechanism: nobody around you can measure your internal attention level — they can only detect its behavioral signature: the late response, the flat eyes, the answer to a question that wasn't asked. Fix: manage the visible signal directly — hold eye contact through the sentence's end, pause one full second before responding, ask one specific follow-up that proves you tracked the actual content. Practicing the outward behavior tends to pull the internal state along with it faster than waiting to feel present first.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---
---
Mechanism: the sharp, adrenaline-assisted focus built for a live trade is a different skill from the slow, undramatic attention a dinner requires — not a weaker version of the same thing, a genuinely separate rep that's had almost no practice because the day only rewards the loud register. Fix: treat quiet attention as an unbuilt skill, not a character flaw, and drill it deliberately in low-stakes recurring windows — a commute, a walk — the same disciplined way you'd learn any new system before taking it live.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---
---
Mechanism: drift frequency is mostly irrelevant to how present you actually seem to others; return speed is what determines it. Fifty drifts with two-second returns reads as more present than five drifts with twenty-minute absences. Fix: stop counting or judging the drifts themselves. Count the returns. Each one is a completed rep, and repetition of return — not elimination of wandering — is the entire trainable mechanism behind presence.
---
