## THREAD v01
---
---
Priya starts keeping a private count on her phone's notes app: not drifts, just returns — the moments she caught herself gone and came back anyway. By Friday the number is higher than she expected, and for the first time it feels like evidence of something instead of a confession.

## THREAD v02
---
---
Marcus mentions the parent-teacher conference to his manager, half-joking, and his manager admits he does the exact same thing in his own kid's games. They start, without really deciding to, checking in on it once a week over coffee — not about work at all, just: did you catch yourself this week, did you come back.
---

## THREAD v03
---
---
Dana keeps the "breathe out" cue from her instructor and starts using it herself, silently, whenever she notices she's drafting in her head instead of being wherever she actually is. It becomes a private phrase, hers now, unconnected to the yoga room it came from.
---

## THREAD v04
---
---
Theo tells his girlfriend, a week later, about the phone-in-the-bag rule, and she tells him she'd noticed the difference before he said anything — she just hadn't wanted to name it in case naming it made him defensive. Now they name it together, casually, whenever one of them drifts.
---

## THREAD v05
---
---
Renata's father asks her, weeks after the hospital, whether she still keeps her phone in the bag by the door when she visits people who matter. She says yes. She doesn't tell him it took his one sentence — "you can go, if you need to" — to make her notice she'd been leaving the whole time.
---

## THREAD v06
---
---
Kai's mother starts asking him to explain his job again, on purpose now, once a month, and he's noticed he actually listens for the flatness in her face that tells him he's drifted — it's become his early warning system for every conversation, not just hers.
---

## THREAD v07
---
---
Yuki keeps the laptop bag in the car now instead of courtside, a small rule that costs her nothing and that her niece has never once mentioned noticing, which Yuki takes as the actual proof it worked.
---

## THREAD v08
---
---
Adrian's "I drifted, I'm back" becomes a phrase his wife picks up too, for her own moments, and it stops being an admission of failure between them and starts being just something people say, the way you'd narrate stepping out of a room and back in.
---

## THREAD v09
---
---
Sam tells his father, much later, that the two minutes of silence in the pew were the first time in months he hadn't been running a scenario. His father says he'd guessed as much, and that it's the only part of the whole visit he remembers clearly.
---

## THREAD v10
---
---
Ling's son starts saying "you're not even here" less often, and one evening, catching her drift herself before he has to say it, she wonders if he's noticed the change or just stopped expecting to have to point it out.
---

## THREAD v11
---
---
Devon and his date end up talking, honestly, about the late laugh — she tells him it's the most honest thing anyone's said to her on a first date in a year. He doesn't know yet if there's a second date. He knows he'll catch the late laugh faster next time, wherever it happens.
---

## THREAD v12
---
---
Omar's hand still moves toward his pocket sometimes, three months of habit not undone in one Saturday, but he's gotten faster at catching it mid-motion, and his nephew, still wobbling, still falling, has started looking for him before he falls now instead of after.
