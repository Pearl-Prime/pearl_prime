The shift is from **resistance** to **reception**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the overwhelm—when you stop viewing it as a mechanical failure of your endurance or a moral failing of your courage—the noise changes. It stops being a siren demanding immediate action and starts becoming a signal carrying specific data.

In that "next room," the overwhelm is no longer an obstacle to your performance; it is the **voice of the part of you that has been silenced**.

Here is what shifts:

1.  **The Question Changes:** Instead of asking, *"How do I push through this so I can remain available?"* you begin to hear the question underneath: *"What am I being asked to carry that is not mine to hold?"*
2.  **The Sensation Softens:** The sharp, panic-induced urgency (the somatic threat response to unavailability) dulls into a heavy, mournful ache. This ache is not weakness; it is grief. It is the grief of the self that has been sacrificed on the altar of "reliability."
3.  **The Narrative Inverts:** You realize that the overwhelm is not protecting you from *failure*; it is protecting you from **erasure**. It is the last defense mechanism of your identity, screaming to be seen before it disappears completely into the role of "infrastructure."

To hear it, you must tolerate the silence that follows when you refuse to respond. In that silence, the overwhelm speaks its true name: **"I am full."**

Not "I am weak." Not "I am failing." But **"I am full."**

What does it feel like to let that statement sit in the room with you, without trying to empty yourself to make space for more?
