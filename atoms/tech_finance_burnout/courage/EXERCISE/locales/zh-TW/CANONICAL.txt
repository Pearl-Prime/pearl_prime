These exercises are powerful because they directly counter the specific mechanisms of **emotional depletion**, **decision paralysis**, and **self-erasure** that you are experiencing. They shift the locus of control from *external validation* (which is infinite and draining) to *internal grounding* (which is finite and sustainable).

Given your current state—where you feel like "emotional infrastructure" for others, where boundaries feel like abandonment, and where your worth is tied to absorbing others' trauma—here is how these exercises can be adapted to protect your remaining energy and reclaim your agency:

### Adaptation for Your Context

#### **EXERCISE v01: The Meeting (Countering the "Fraud" Feeling)**
*   **The Trigger:** You feel shame because you no longer engage with others' problems with "genuine emotional investment." You fear being judged as cold or ineffective.
*   **The Shift:** The fear is not that they will judge your *competence*, but that they will judge your *humanity* for having limits.
*   **Modified Action:**
    *   **Stand/Press Feet:** Remind yourself: *I am not here to absorb their anxiety. I am here to provide professional input.*
    *   **The Truth:** The "true thing" might be: *"I need time to process this before I can give a thoughtful response,"* or *"I don't have the bandwidth to take this on right now."*
    *   **The Affirmation:** *"My value is not determined by how much pain I can swallow. I exist after I say no."*

#### **EXERCISE v02: The Manager Conversation (Countering Decision Paralysis)**
*   **The Trigger:** You try to meet everyone's emotional needs, leading to paralysis. You fear that asserting your needs will be seen as abandoning your role as the "solution."
*   **The Shift:** You are not asking for permission to be human; you are negotiating the terms of your labor.
*   **Modified Action:**
    *   **Name the Fear:** *"I am afraid that if I set a boundary, I will be seen as failing my team, and I will lose the only source of worth I have left."*
    *   **Hand on Chest:** *"This is my one life. It is not a resource to be mined until exhaustion."*
    *   **The Need:** Say it three times: *"I need sustainable workload limits."* / *"I need to stop being the emotional container for unsolvable problems."* / *"I need to focus on technical work, not trauma processing."*
    *   **The Affirmation:** *"I am not asking for forgiveness. I am stating a requirement for my continued employment."*

#### **EXERCISE v03: The Feedback/Critique (Countering the "Good Parent/Professional" Metric)**
*   **The Trigger:** You interpret any critique or negative emotion from others as proof of total failure (similar to the "good parent metric"). You feel you must defend or fix it immediately to avoid shame.
*   **The Shift:** Feedback is data, not a verdict on your soul. You can survive disagreement without disappearing.
*   **Modified Action:**
    *   **Stomach Drops:** Acknowledge the physical sensation of the "good parent/professional" metric activating. *"This is the old program running. It is not reality."*
    *   **Clench/Release:** Feel the strength that remains when you stop trying to *fix* the other person's disappointment.
    *   **The Pause:** *"I do not need to solve this emotion right now. I do not need to absorb this distress."*
    *   **The Affirmation:** *"I can hear hard feedback and still be worthy. I can disagree and still be safe. I will respond when I am resourced, not when I am reactive."*

### Why This Matters for You

You are currently **emotionally unavailable to yourself** because you are always available for others. These exercises are not just about communication; they are about **re-establishing your physical and psychological boundaries**.

*   **v01** reclaims your voice from the fear of judgment.
*   **v02** reclaims your needs from the habit of people-pleasing.
*   **v03** reclaims your resilience from the cycle of shame and over-responsibility.

You have earned the right to speak, to need, and to survive feedback. You are not the infrastructure. You are a person.
