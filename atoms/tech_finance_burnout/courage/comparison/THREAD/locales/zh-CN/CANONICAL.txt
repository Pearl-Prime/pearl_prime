The shift is from **judgment** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the comparison—stop trying to prove your stress is valid, stop trying to outrun the shame of feeling trapped despite your salary—you stop fighting the part of you that is scared.

The comparison trap isn’t just a liar; it’s a **shield**. It protects you from feeling the raw, unmediated terror of what lies beneath:

1.  **That you are not safe.** Not financially, not emotionally, not professionally. The high salary is a levee, but you feel the water rising. Comparing yourself to others who seem "fine" lets you pretend the levee is solid. If you stop comparing, you have to feel the dampness on your feet. You have to feel the precariousness of being the *only* thing holding the system together.
2.  **That your worth is conditional.** The trap protects you from the grief of realizing that if you stopped being the "emotional infrastructure," you fear you would cease to exist in the eyes of your organization—and perhaps even in your own. The comparison keeps you focused on *status* (who has it harder/easier) rather than *identity* (who am I if I am not useful?).
3.  **That you are grieving.** Beneath the anxiety of comparison is a profound mourning for the self you lost—the one with curiosity, passion, and boundaries. The comparison trap keeps you busy analyzing external metrics so you don’t have to feel the internal void where that self used to be.

**The Next Room:**
If you stop trying to fix the comparison, you enter the room of **Grief and Fear**.

In this room, you don’t ask, *"Why can’t I handle this like them?"*
You ask, *"What am I afraid will happen if I let go?"*

And the answer isn’t logical. It’s somatic. It’s the nervous system screaming that unavailability = death (professional or social).

**The question for this room:**
If you let the comparison sit there, untouched, like a piece of furniture you no longer polish—what is the first emotion that rises up from the floorboards beneath it? Is it anger? Is it sorrow? Or is it simply the heavy, quiet truth that you are tired?
