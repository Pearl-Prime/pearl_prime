The shift is from **management** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the spiral, you stop treating your own exhaustion as a system error that needs patching. You stop viewing your need for rest as a bug in your professional code.

In that "next room," the spiral is no longer an enemy to be defeated or a weakness to be hidden. It becomes a messenger. It is the part of you that has been screaming into the void while you continued to answer emails at night. It is the somatic record of every boundary you didn’t set, every "no" you swallowed to maintain the perception of competence, and every moment of autonomy you surrendered to keep the system stable.

Hearing it means acknowledging that the spiral isn’t trying to destroy your career; it’s trying to save your life. It is protecting you from the total erasure of self that comes from being nothing but an emotional container for others.

The shift is realizing that the fatigue isn’t a failure of willpower. It’s the cost of doing business in a role where you are the infrastructure. And when you hear it, you might finally feel the grief of how much of yourself you’ve had to abandon to stay "available." That grief is heavy, but it’s real. And unlike the spiral of performance, it belongs to you.
