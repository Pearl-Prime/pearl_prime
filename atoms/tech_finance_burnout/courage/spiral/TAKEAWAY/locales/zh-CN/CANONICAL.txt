**1. The "Spiral" is a Feature, Not a Bug**
You have interpreted your burnout, anxiety, and inability to disconnect as personal weaknesses or moral failings. You have been running a production system at 100% capacity with zero downtime for patches or updates. The "spiral" is the system’s way of forcing a shutdown because you wouldn’t voluntarily schedule one. It is not a character flaw; it is biological and psychological inevitability.

**2. The Cost is Non-Negotiable**
Takeaway v02 and v03 highlight the asymmetry of your situation. You are trying to pay for emotional and physical depletion with financial instruments (RSUs, salary). But the currency doesn’t match. No amount of vested stock can buy back the neural pathways rewired by hypervigilance, nor can it restore the capacity for joy that has been eroded by constant emotional labor. The "invoice" you are receiving now—in the form of somatic stress, emotional flatness, and exhaustion—is real, even if your brokerage account says you are "winning."

Stepping back from that role feels like abandonment because you’ve trained yourself and those around you to believe your value lies in your availability.
