The shift is from **resistance** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the false alarm—stop trying to silence the somatic threat response that screams *unavailability = danger*—you stop fighting your own nervous system. You stop spending the last reserves of your energy on suppressing the shame of being "weak" or "unreliable."

In that stillness, the "next room" reveals that the false alarm isn’t a malfunction. It’s a **loyal protector**. It has been keeping you safe by ensuring you remain indispensable, because in your history, indispensability was the only guarantee against abandonment or irrelevance.

Hearing it means acknowledging: *"You are working so hard to keep me connected because you believe disconnection equals death (professional or emotional)."*

This changes the dynamic from **self-punishment** ("Why can’t I just set boundaries?") to **grief and gratitude** ("Thank you for trying to save me, but we are safe enough to rest now"). The courage required isn’t to push through the alarm, but to sit with the terrifying vulnerability of letting it ring without acting on it.
