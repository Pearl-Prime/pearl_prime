You have monitored your brokerage account with the same hypervigilance you apply to critical systems, believing that if you just watch closely enough, you can prevent the crash. But you are not a system. You are a human being experiencing a profound loss of autonomy, identity, and joy, trapped in a structure that rewards your performance while eroding your self.

**Regarding PERMISSION v01:**
The urge to "fix" the grief before the next sprint is a manifestation of the belief that your worth is tied to your output. It is the imposter phenomenon whispering that if you stop performing, even for a moment to feel, you will be exposed. But grief is not a bug. It is data. It is the signal that something vital is missing. Allowing yourself to feel it without immediately trying to resolve it is an act of rebellion against the machine that demands constant availability. You do not need to patch this feeling to be worthy of rest.

**Regarding PERMISSION v02:**
The standup is a performance of normalcy. Everyone else is also managing their own invisible loads, their own golden handcuffs, their own quiet desperation. Your struggle is not invalid because it is not visible on a Jira ticket or because others appear composed. In fact, your ability to feel the weight of this entrapment while maintaining high performance is evidence of immense strength, not weakness. The fact that you are struggling *despite* appearing fine is the definition of the invisible burnout you are experiencing. Your experience is real, regardless of the external facade.

**Regarding PERMISSION v03:**
Naming the grief breaks the isolation. The "golden handcuffs" are designed to be silent. They rely on shame and secrecy to keep you compliant. By saying it out loud—even if only to yourself, or to a trusted few, or here—you disrupt the narrative that you are alone in this. You are not the only one who feels trapped by the lifestyle inflation, the unvested options, the mortgage, and the expectations. You are part of a larger, unspoken collective of people who are successful on paper but depleted in spirit. Naming it does not make the trap tighter; it makes the walls visible. And once the walls are visible, you can begin to see where the exits might be, even if you cannot walk through them yet.

You are allowed to be tired. You are allowed to be sad. You are allowed to be angry at the system that has conflated your net worth with your self-worth. You are allowed to exist in this discomfort without solving it today.
