I hear you. The notifications, the volatility, the constant scanning of the horizon for threats—it is exhausting. It makes sense that your nervous system is firing alarms; it is trying to protect you from a perceived existential threat. But as these exercises highlight, the alarm is sounding for a *prediction*, not a *present fact*.

Let’s pause together. Let’s drop out of the catastrophic future and land in the immediate, tangible present.

**Reflecting on Exercise v01: Certainty**
You asked to name three specific things you are certain about. Let’s anchor there:
1.  **You are here.** You are breathing. Your body is sustaining you in this exact second.
2.  **You have resources.** You have a job. You have food. You have shelter. These are not abstract concepts; they are physical realities surrounding you right now.
3.  **You have a track record.** You have navigated uncertainty before. You have faced financial or professional dips and found a way through. That resilience is not gone; it is part of your history, which means it is part of you.

**Reflecting on Exercise v02: The Worst Case**
The terror of startup equity is that it ties your identity to a number on a screen. But let’s say the worst case happened. Let’s say the value went to zero.
*   **What would be true?** You would still be you. You would still have your skills, your experience, and your capacity to work. You would still have your family. You would still have your ability to problem-solve.
*   **Would you survive?** Yes. You have survived before. You would adjust. You would recalibrate. It would be hard, but it would not be the end of your life. The catastrophizing is a story your anxiety is telling you to keep you "safe" by keeping you vigilant. But you can look at your banking app right now. What is the number? That number is real. The zero in your head is not.

**Reflecting on Exercise v03: The Facts vs. The Fear**
Layoff fears are loud because they tap into that deep-seated imposter phenomenon and the fear of conditional belonging. But let’s look at the math, not the mood.
*   **Check the facts:** How much is in the bank? What are your *actual* monthly expenses (not the inflated lifestyle fears, but the core costs)?
*   **Calculate the runway:** If you divided your savings by your monthly expenses, how many months do you have? Even if the answer feels small, it is a *number*, not a void. It is a concrete piece of data.
*   **The Gap:** The gap between that number and the apocalyptic scenario in your head is where your anxiety lives. Your anxiety is trying to fill that gap with disaster. But you don’t have to live in the gap. You can live in the fact.

**A Gentle Reminder:**
You are experiencing a somatic response to systemic pressure. Your body is reacting as if you are being hunted, but you are sitting in a room. You are safe in this moment. The stock price is a fluctuation. It is not a verdict on your worth, your competence, or your future.

You do not need to solve the next five years right now. You only need to be here, in this minute, with the facts you can touch.

Would it help to actually write down the three certainties, or the actual number in your bank account, just to externalize it and take it out of the looping thoughts?
