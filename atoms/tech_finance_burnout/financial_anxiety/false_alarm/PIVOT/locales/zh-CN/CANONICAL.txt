The "all-clear" signal is rejected because the underlying belief isn't about *current* solvency; it’s about *future* catastrophe.

**The Pivot:**
Stop trying to convince your anxiety that you are safe. It won’t listen. Instead, acknowledge that the checking is a compulsion, not a calculation.

*   **Old Action:** Check portfolio → Receive "Fine" → Wait for relief → Relief fails → Check again sooner.
*   **New Action:** Notice the urge to check → Name it ("This is hypervigilance, not due diligence") → Do not check → Sit with the unresolved tension for 12 minutes.

The goal isn’t to feel better. The goal is to break the loop where financial data is expected to perform emotional labor it cannot do. You are absorbing the distress of an uncertain future by micromanaging the present numbers. The numbers will always be "fine" until they aren’t, and no amount of checking will change that binary.
