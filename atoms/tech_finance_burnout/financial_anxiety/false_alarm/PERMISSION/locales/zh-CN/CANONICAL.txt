You can sit with the discomfort of the unvested options or the mortgage pressure without needing to "solve" your life before Monday morning. The anxiety is data, not a directive.

This cuts through the isolation of the "imposter" narrative. When you look around at standup and see others seeming "fine," your internalized belief system interprets this as evidence of your own defectiveness ("They belong; I am faking it"). This permission validates that their external calm does not negate your internal reality. Secrecy is the mortar in the walls of the golden handcuffs. Naming it—"I am feeling a false alarm about my financial security right now"—breaks the spell. It moves the experience from a shameful secret to a shared human condition. It reminds you that the "threat" your body is sensing is often a phantom generated by past conditioning and systemic pressure, not an immediate existential danger.
