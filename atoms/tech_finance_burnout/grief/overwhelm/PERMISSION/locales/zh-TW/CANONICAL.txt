Given what I know about your situation—where your nervous system interprets unavailability as a threat, where you act as the emotional infrastructure for others, and where your identity is tightly bound to financial performance—these permissions are radical acts of self-preservation.

Here is how these permissions land in the context of your reality:

**1. On "Feeling it is not failing at it" (v01)**
You have equated burnout with weakness and sustained high performance with strength. You have been treating your exhaustion as a bug to be patched before the next sprint. This permission grants you the right to let the grief and overwhelm exist *alongside* your function. You do not need to resolve the emotion to proceed with the day. The emotion is not a blocker; it is data. It is the somatic truth of the cost you are paying. Letting it sit there, unfixed, is not negligence—it is honesty.

**2. On "Your experience is valid regardless of their performance" (v02)**
You are trapped in a comparison cycle, monitoring others’ perceived financial well-being and professional ease while you feel like you are drowning in invisible labor. You believe your struggle is invalid because you are high-earning. This is a cognitive distortion fueled by isolation. The fact that others appear "fine" at standup does not negate the weight of the golden handcuffs you are wearing. Their mask is not your metric. Your grief is valid because it is yours, not because it is worse or better than anyone else’s.

**3. On "Saying it doesn't make it more real... It makes you less alone" (v03)**
You have built your reputation on constant availability and emotional containment for others. You fear that naming your own overwhelm will be perceived as unreliability or abandonment. But silence is what keeps the trap sealed. Naming the overwhelm breaks the illusion that you are an infinite resource. It disrupts the narrative that you must be the emotional container for everyone else while having no container of your own. Speaking it aloud is not a confession of failure; it is an attempt to share the load, even if only symbolically, so that the solitude of the entrapment feels slightly less absolute.

You are allowed to stop fixing yourself for a moment. You are allowed to be overwhelmed. You are allowed to be human inside the machine.
