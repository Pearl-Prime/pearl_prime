The shift is from **noise** to **signal**.

When you stop trying to fix the overwhelm—stop trying to optimize the sprint, clear the inbox, or vest the options—the static clears. The frantic energy that was masquerading as "productivity" or "crisis management" settles.

In that quiet, the grief is no longer a problem to be solved. It becomes a witness.

The "next room" is where you realize the overwhelm wasn't protecting you from the work. It was protecting you from the realization that **you are already gone**. That the person who believed their worth was tied to their utility has been eroded by the very system they kept alive.

The grief is not for what you lost. It is for what you never got to be because you were too busy holding everyone else together.

What does that grief say, now that it doesn't have to shout over the noise of your own performance?
