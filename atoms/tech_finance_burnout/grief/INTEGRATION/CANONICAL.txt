

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PRESENCE-FACT
---
My chest is tight when I remember the company. The culture that was. The team that scattered. The tightness is the grief sitting in my physical form. I'm not trying to release it. I'm just feeling where it lives. In my chest. In my throat. I can be here with it.
Carry: Grief lives in my body now.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: SURVIVAL-FACT
---
The loss happened. I'm still here. Not recovered. Not fine. Still here. Still working. Still existing in the aftermath. That's more than I expected during the initial shock. Just surviving the loss is an accomplishment.
Carry: Surviving the loss is full achievement.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: REALITY-FACT
---
The cost of the loss is real. One team I loved. One culture I believed in. One future I imagined. Those things are gone. And I'm noticing: I can see the cost clearly because the loss is real. Not imaginary. Not exaggerated. This clarity helps.
Carry: Real loss deserves to be acknowledged.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: MEANING-FACT
---
What did I learn from what was lost? What did that culture teach me? What did that team show me about myself? I'm asking these questions. Not to fix the loss. To honor it. To let it mean something.
Carry: Meaning doesn't replace, it honors.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: COMMUNITY-FACT
---
Others are grieving too. The same team. The same loss. We're not together anymore but we're grieving together. That connection still exists. The loss created a bond. I'm not grieving alone even though the team is scattered.
Carry: Shared grief creates invisible community.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: MOVEMENT-FACT
---
When grief is too heavy I move. I walk. I stretch. I get the grief out of stationary positions. Moving doesn't make me forget. It makes the heaviness less concentrated. My body needs to carry grief in motion sometimes. Not still.
Carry: Grief moves better than stillness.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: INTEGRATION-FACT
---
The loss is integrated now. Not gone. Not erased. Just part of my story. I carry it forward. I'm not the person I was before the loss. I'm the person who survived it. That's who I am now.
Carry: Loss is part of my story now.
