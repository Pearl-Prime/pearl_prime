The shift is from **management** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the spiral, you stop treating your grief as a system error that needs debugging. You stop applying the same "high-performance" logic to your pain that you apply to your work—where every emotion is a ticket to be resolved, every silence a latency issue to be optimized.

In that next room, the spiral isn’t a threat to your stability; it’s a signal. It’s the part of you that has been screaming because it hasn’t been allowed to speak without being corrected.

If you stop fixing it, you have to let it be messy. You have to let it be inefficient. You have to let it take up space without producing a result.

What does the spiral say when it knows you’re no longer trying to shut it up?
