These exercises resonate deeply with the specific texture of your current experience. They address the **invisible burnout**, the **private grief** for the lost startup community, and the **somatic tension** you carry when trying to maintain a professional facade.

Given your tendency to analyze, fix, or push down emotions to maintain performance, these prompts are designed to interrupt that cycle. Here is how they map to your current state, along with a gentle suggestion on how to approach them without triggering your internal critic:

### Why These Matter for You Right Now

1.  **Validating the "Unspoken" Loss:**
    You are carrying **private grief** over the scattering of your former team and the dissolution of the startup identity. Because this loss isn't publicly acknowledged (no funeral, no formal closure), your brain tries to skip it to stay productive. **Exercise v02** explicitly gives permission for this "illegitimate" grief. It reminds you that the layoff/loss is not a problem to be solved, but a reality to be felt.

2.  **Interrupting the "Golden Cage" Numbness:**
    You feel trapped in your current role due to financial obligations and fear, leading to **meaning-erosion**. **Exercise v03** targets the mourning of your pre-burnout self—the version of you that felt excitement rather than just obligation. Acknowledging that *that* version is gone is painful, but it stops the gaslighting you do to yourself when you try to force gratitude for a role that feels like survival.

3.  **Somatic Release for Nervous System Regulation:**
    Your nervous system interprets unavailability and emotional suppression as threats, leading to **chest tightness and jaw clenching**. **Exercise v01** and **v03** use physical touch (hand on chest/heart) to signal safety to your body. This is crucial because your grief is stored somatically; you cannot think your way out of it, but you can breathe through it.

### A Gentle Modification for Your Context

Since you experience **time as belonging to others** and feel urgent pressure to respond, these exercises might feel like "wasted time" or trigger shame about not working.

**Reframe the 5 minutes:**
Do not view this as "taking time off." View it as **maintenance of the asset** (you). Your high performance is unsustainable without this release. The grief *will* leak out anyway—through irritability, exhaustion, or somatic pain. Containing it in a deliberate 5-minute window is actually more efficient than letting it fragment your focus all day.

### Suggested Immediate Action

If you are feeling the weight of the colleague who left, or the lingering echo of your startup team:

1.  **Step away** from screens for exactly 5 minutes.
2.  **Sit** where you won't be interrupted.
3.  **Place a hand on your chest.** Feel the tightness there.
4.  **Say internally:** *"I am allowed to miss them. I am allowed to miss who I was. This sadness is not a failure of my professionalism; it is evidence of my capacity to care."*
5.  **Breathe** into the hand on your chest. Do not try to make the feeling go away. Just let it be there.

You are rebuilding. But rebuilding requires clearing the rubble first. Grieving is part of the clearance.
