The shift is from **judgment** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the comparison trap—stop trying to logic your way out of the shame, stop trying to prove your stress is "valid" enough by comparing income or workload—you create a vacuum. In that vacuum, the noise of the comparison fades just enough for the underlying signal to emerge.

What you hear in that next room is likely not a critique of your performance, but a mourning for your **autonomy**.

The comparison trap was protecting you from feeling the raw terror of **being unseen as a human**, and only valued as a function. It protected you from the grief of realizing that your "strength" (constant availability, emotional containment) has become the very cage that keeps you trapped.

If you stop fixing it, you have to feel the weight of the realization that **you are grieving the loss of yourself** while still being forced to perform as if you are whole. The comparison trap was a distraction from the pain of that erasure. Hearing it means acknowledging: *"I am not just tired; I am disappearing."*
