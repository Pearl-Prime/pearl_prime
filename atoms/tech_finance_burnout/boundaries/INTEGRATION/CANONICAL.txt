

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BOUNDARY-FACT
---
My phone is in another room. I can't see it. I can't reach it. And I notice: I'm fine. My hands aren't shaking. My breath is normal. I expected catastrophe. No catastrophe arrived. The urgent thing either got handled without me or it's still waiting. My job didn't disappear. My value didn't vanish. I just created distance. Physical distance. It's remarkable how physical distance changes my nervous system. I'm not being unreachable. I'm just choosing when I reach. The boundary is concrete. The boundary is a room. That's all.
Carry: Boundaries are just physical distance.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: CHOICE-FACT
---
I said no to someone today. I felt the guilt immediately. Real guilt. My chest tightened. But I said it anyway. And now I notice: the world didn't break. The person found someone else. The work still happened. I was the one I was protecting. Not the company. Not the project. Me. My time. My energy. The guilt wants me to believe saying no was selfish. But I see now: saying yes when I'm empty is also selfish. It's just selfish toward my future self. This guilt is the price of boundaries. It's not a sign I'm wrong. It's the cost. I'm paying it anyway.
Carry: Guilt is the boundary's natural enemy.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: TIME-FACT
---
Five p.m. came. I closed my laptop. I left work. Not perfectly. Not without worry. But I left. And now it's seven p.m. and I'm still fine. The panic about all unfinished things hasn't destroyed me. I'm here. I'm breathing. I'm separate from work. The boundary held. Not because it was easy. Because I held it. Even while uncomfortable.
Carry: I can be uncomfortable and boundaried.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: POWER-FACT
---
What if I have more power than I think? What if the company needs me less than my anxiety suggests? What if other people are actually fine without my constant availability? I'm not asking these as reassurance. I'm asking them as genuine questions. I'm noticing evidence. People handle problems without me. Deadlines slide and projects adapt. I'm necessary but not irreplaceable. That's not depressing. That's liberating.
Carry: I'm important but not omnipotent.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: WITNESS-FACT
---
I watched someone set a boundary today. They said they were leaving at five p.m. No one else was leaving. They left anyway. The world continued. I realized: I can do that. It's possible. I thought boundaries were selfish. I thought they were rude. But I watched a kind person set a boundary. So boundaries must be compatible with kindness. That changes what I thought I knew.
Carry: Kindness and boundaries coexist.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: SENSATION-FACT
---
My shoulders are down. Not because I stopped worrying. But because I stopped working. My body knows the difference between work hours and personal hours. When I honor that boundary physically my body honors it. My posture changes. My breathing slows. My nervous system recalibrates. The boundary isn't theoretical. It's embodied.
Carry: My body remembers what my mind forgets.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: SELF-FACT
---
I'm still here. Still employed. Still myself. After setting boundaries. The catastrophe I imagined didn't happen. My identity didn't crumble. I'm actually more intact now. Less fractured. The boundary protected my wholeness. Not my reputation. My actual self.
Carry: Boundaries protect integrity, not image.
