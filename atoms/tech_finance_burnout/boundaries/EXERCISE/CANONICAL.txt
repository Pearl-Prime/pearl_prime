## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Someone asks you to add work to today.
Pause. Count to five before you speak.
Clarify: what exactly is being asked here?
Is this urgent? Is this your sprint?
Name your current capacity in one sentence.
Say: "I will confirm by tomorrow morning."
This is not rejection. This is clarity.
Write the request down so you remember.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You want to say yes automatically always.
Notice that urge without acting on it.
Your "yes" has a real cost to you.
What specifically are you agreeing to perform?
What else will drop if you do this?
Ask one clarifying question before committing time.
Your limits protect the work and you.
Say: "Let me think and come back."
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
A sprint demand just landed in slack.
First: breathe. Do not respond right now.
What do you actually want to say?
Can this wait until morning standup meeting?
Your default yes was trained into you.
You can choose differently this time today.
Check your calendar. Name three current commitments.
Is there room? Only then agree to this.
