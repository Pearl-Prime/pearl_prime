Let’s adapt these slightly to fit your current capacity, acknowledging the **somatic reality** of your burnout and entrapment:

### Adapted Approach for Your Current State

**1. Acknowledge the Somatic Spike, Not Just the Thought**
When the code review feedback hits, your nervous system likely triggers a threat response (tightness, shallow breath, hypervigilance). Before you try to "name one concrete thing," pause for 10 seconds. Notice the physical sensation. Say internally: *"This is my nervous system reacting to perceived threat, not evidence of actual danger."* You don’t need to fix the feeling; just name it. This reduces the secondary shame of *having* the reaction.

**2. Lower the Bar for "Evidence"**
In Exercise v01, instead of needing to recall a major system you built, can you name **one line of code** or **one small decision** that was correct? It doesn’t have to be impressive. It just has to be true. The imposter syndrome demands you be *exceptional* to be safe. Counter it with *adequacy*. "I wrote this function. It works. That is enough for this moment."

**3. Use Externalized Evidence (Reduce Cognitive Load)**
Your brain is depleted. Trying to pull three things from memory (Exercise v02) requires executive function you may not have right now.
*   **Pre-load this:** Keep a simple text file open called `facts.txt`. When you solve a bug, ship a feature, or get a thank-you, paste it there.
*   **In the meeting:** If you freeze, look at the file. Read one line. You don’t need to generate the proof in real-time; you just need to retrieve it. This bypasses the "blank mind" caused by stress.

**4. Reframe the "Senior Review" (Exercise v03)**
The terror says: *"They will discover I am incompetent."*
The reality of your situation (financial entrapment, high performance despite burnout) suggests: *"They rely on me because I deliver."*
Instead of listing five wins, list **one current constraint** you are managing. Example: *"I am navigating X technical debt while delivering Y."* This shifts the frame from "proving worth" to "managing complexity," which is what senior roles actually do. It aligns with your actual experience of holding the system together.

### A Gentle Reminder
You are not trying to *feel* confident. You are trying to **act** in alignment with reality despite the lack of feeling. The "flatness" means the emotional reward for doing these exercises won’t come immediately. That’s okay. Do them mechanically, like a script. The goal is not to feel better instantly, but to prevent the spiral from consuming the rest of your day.
