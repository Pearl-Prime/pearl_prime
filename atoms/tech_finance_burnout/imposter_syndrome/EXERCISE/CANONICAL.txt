## EXERCISE v01
---

---
The code review feedback triggered the imposter belief.
You are about to spiral into shame.
Stop. Name one concrete thing you have built.
A feature. A system. A solution that works.
Did your work help people or solve problems?
Yes or no. The answer is evidence.
The criticism is feedback, not your worth.
Your competence is demonstrated in actual code.

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
You are about to speak in a meeting.
Your brain: you do not belong here.
Counter: name three things you know deeply.
Technologies. Decisions. Problems you have actually solved.
You earned your seat through work and skill.
The discomfort is growth, not fraud signal.
Speak your expertise. You have earned this.

## EXERCISE v03

---
Senior review is in two hours from now.

Projects shipped. Bugs fixed. Skills you have built.
Evidence. Not feeling. Not story. Actual evidence.
Your contributions are real and verifiable fact.
You were hired for actual demonstrated skill.
Bring this evidence to the review with you.
