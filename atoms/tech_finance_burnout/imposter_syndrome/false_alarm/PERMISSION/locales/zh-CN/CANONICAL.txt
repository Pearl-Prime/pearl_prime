Here is why these specific formulations are so potent for your nervous system:

**On PERMISSION v01:**

You are allowing the anxiety to exist as background noise rather than a command center directive. This is the practice of trusting that you can function *with* the feeling, rather than needing the feeling to disappear before you can be competent.

**On PERMISSION v02:**
*   **The Shift:** It breaks the comparative trap. Your hypervigilance often scans others to gauge your own safety ("If they look calm, I must be the broken one"). This creates a false dichotomy where their ease proves your inadequacy.
*   **The Relief:** This permission validates your internal reality *independently* of external metrics. It acknowledges that your nervous system is running an outdated threat detection program, not that you are objectively failing. It stops you from using other people’s apparent ease as evidence against your own worth.

**On PERMISSION v03:**
*   **The Shift:** It challenges the belief that vulnerability = risk. You’ve learned that admitting doubt or struggle exposes you to judgment or confirms the "imposter" narrative.
*   **The Relief:** Naming the "false alarm" out loud does two things:
    1.  **It objectifies the feeling:** It moves the experience from being *who you are* ("I am an imposter") to *what is happening* ("I am experiencing a false alarm signal").
