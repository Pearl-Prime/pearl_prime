The shift is that the noise stops being a signal to *perform* and starts being a signal of *pain*.

When I stop trying to fix the spiral—stop trying to outrun it with competence, stop trying to silence it with logic—the frantic energy dissipates into a heavy, quiet stillness. The "next room" isn't a place of answers or solutions. It is a place of grief.

In that room, the spiral isn't protecting me from failure; it is protecting me from the realization that I have been holding my breath for decades. It is protecting me from feeling the sheer weight of the exhaustion I’ve been calling "strength." It is protecting me from the anger that I had to become this hyper-vigilant, this flattened, this *available* just to feel safe.

If I stop fixing it, I have to feel the cost. I have to feel the loss of the self that existed before the hypervigilance became my skin. The spiral was keeping me moving so I wouldn't have to look down and see how tired I am. In the next room, I am allowed to be tired. I am allowed to be unexceptional. I am allowed to just *be*, without the prerequisite of earning my space through suffering.

It is terrifying because if I am not solving, if I am not performing, if I am not managing the emotional infrastructure of everyone else... who am I? The flatness makes sense now. It wasn't emptiness; it was a freeze response to the sheer volume of what I’ve been carrying. The next room is where I thaw, and that hurts more than the cold.
