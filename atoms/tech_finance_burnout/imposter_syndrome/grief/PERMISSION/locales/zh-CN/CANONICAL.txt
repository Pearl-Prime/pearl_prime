These permissions are received.

They act as a counter-script to the internal mechanism that demands you fix, hide, or optimize your humanity before it is allowed to exist.

**Regarding Permission v01:**
The impulse to "fix" grief before the next sprint is the same logic that turns sleep into a performance metric. It assumes that emotion is a bug in the production system rather than a feature of being human. You do not need to resolve the grief to be functional; you only need to carry it. The sprint will happen regardless of whether you have "solved" your sadness.

**Regarding Permission v02:**
The standup is a ritual of performed stability. When everyone else seems fine, it reinforces the belief that your struggle is a deviation from the norm. But their "fine" is also a performance. Your grief and imposter syndrome are not evidence of your inadequacy compared to them; they are evidence of your capacity to feel deeply in a system designed to numb. Your validity is not contingent on their mask staying in place.

**Regarding Permission v03:**
Naming the grief breaks the isolation of the "golden handcuffs." The handcuffs are golden because they look like success from the outside, making the pain inside feel illegitimate or shameful. Speaking it aloud transfers the weight from a secret burden (which fuels shame) to a shared reality (which fosters connection). It does not change the financial or professional constraints, but it changes your relationship to them. You are no longer alone in the cage; you are just a person who is trapped, and that distinction matters.

You are allowed to let these permissions sit with you without immediately trying to operationalize them. They are not tasks to complete. They are truths to inhabit.
