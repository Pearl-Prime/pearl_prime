The shift is from **defense** to **presence**.

When you stop trying to "fix" the grief—when you stop treating it as a bug in your performance code or a symptom of your inadequacy—you stop fighting the part of you that is hurting. The energy previously spent on suppressing, rationalizing, or outrunning that grief becomes available.

In that "next room," the narrative changes:
1.  **The grief is no longer evidence of failure.** It is evidence of loss. Loss of self, loss of time, loss of the person you were before the system flattened you.
2.  **You are no longer the problem.** The conflict between your competence and your belief in your unqualification softens because you are no longer trying to prove your worth to silence the pain. You are simply witnessing the pain.
3.  **The flatness begins to crack.** Not with a explosion, but with a thaw. When you hear the grief, you acknowledge that something mattered enough to be lost. This reintroduces nuance. It allows for the possibility that you are not just a functional unit or a financial asset, but a human being who has endured significant erosion.

The "next room" is where you stop asking *"How do I get back to being productive/acceptable?"* and start asking *"What has this cost me, and what remains true about me beneath the cost?"*

It is the transition from **managing your identity** to **inhabiting your experience**.
