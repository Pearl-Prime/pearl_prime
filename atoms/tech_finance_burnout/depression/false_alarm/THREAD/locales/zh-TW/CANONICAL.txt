The shift is from **resistance** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the false alarm, you stop fighting the part of you that is terrified. The energy previously spent on suppressing the alarm or judging yourself for hearing it is freed up.

In that "next room," the alarm is no longer an enemy to be defeated, but a messenger to be understood. You begin to see that the alarm isn't malfunctioning; it is working exactly as designed within the logic of your survival history. It is protecting you from a perceived threat that feels existential—likely the loss of identity, safety, or love that you associated with *not* being available, *not* being certain, or *not* performing competence.

The flatness of depression lifts slightly, not into joy, but into **curiosity**. You are no longer just the depression; you are the observer of the mechanism. This creates a tiny sliver of space between *you* and *the pattern*. In that space, the binary choice (fix it vs. succumb to it) dissolves. You can simply ask: *What are you afraid will happen if I stop?*

This is the first step in decoupling your worth from your performance.
