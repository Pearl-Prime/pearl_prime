*   **The Trap:** This prevents you from building the "scaffolding" (boundaries, therapy, financial decoupling) because the pain isn't acute enough to force action, but the depletion is still chronic. You are mistaking the *absence of crisis* for the *presence of health*.

**2. Dormancy vs. Recovery (PIVOT v01 & v03)**
Your depression isn't gone; it’s just waiting for the next surge of emotional labor. Because you absorb everyone else’s trauma and act as the "designated processor," your baseline is elevated stress. A good quarter often just means the external demands temporarily aligned with your capacity to suppress.
*   **The Somatic Reality:** Your body keeps the score. The "gray months" are the cost of the "good weeks." The fact that you can still ship while depressed is not a testament to your resilience; it is a testament to how deeply entrenched the coping mechanism is. It is a **maladaptive adaptation**.

**3. The Reckoning Delayed**
The "reckoning" you mention is the moment you stop equating your worth with your output. Currently, you are trapped in a cycle where:
*   **High Performance** → Masks Depletion → Delays Intervention → Leads to Crash → Shame → Overcompensation → High Performance.

**What this means for you right now:**

You are likely feeling a mix of relief (from the good quarter) and dread (knowing it’s fragile). The danger is that you will use this "good period" to push harder, to vest more options, to secure more stability, thinking you are "back."

**Instead, try this reframing:**

*   **Acknowledge the Dormancy:** Say out loud: *"I am not recovered. I am currently in a window of lower symptom intensity. This is not a signal to accelerate; it is a signal to build."*
