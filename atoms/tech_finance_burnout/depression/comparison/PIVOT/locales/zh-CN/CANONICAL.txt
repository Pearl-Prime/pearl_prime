These pivots are sharp, necessary corrections to the narrative your brain is currently running. They dismantle the false equivalence between your internal state and your VP’s external presentation.

Here is why these reframes matter, specifically for your situation:

**1. The Visibility Bias (PIVOT v02)**
You are comparing your *internal experience* (the flatness, the exhaustion, the somatic stress) with their *external output* (energy, decisiveness, presence). This is a category error. You cannot see their internal cost. In high-comp environments, "performing around" depression is a specialized skill set, not a sign of health. Your VP may be running on the same adrenaline-and-cortisol fuel that is burning you out, but they may have different coping mechanisms, different support structures, or simply a different threshold for dissociation. Their ability to perform does not invalidate your pain; it just means their mask is holding—for now.

**2. Depression as Data, Not Defect (PIVOT v03)**
This is the most critical pivot. You have internalized the belief that your reaction (flatness, depletion) is a personal failure of resilience. But if the situation is structurally unsustainable—constant emotional labor, financial entrapment, hypervigilance, lack of autonomy—then **depression is the accurate response.** It is your nervous system’s way of saying, *"I cannot continue to process this level of threat and demand without shutting down non-essential functions."* The flatness isn’t a bug; it’s a feature of a system protecting itself from total collapse. You aren’t broken; you are responding logically to an illogical load.

**3. The Title Trap (PIVOT v01)**
Titles do not immunize against neurochemistry. The pressure that flattens you is the same pressure that drives them, but you are experiencing the *cost* of it while they are displaying the *result* of it. By assuming they are immune, you isolate yourself in your suffering. You assume you are the only one who feels this way, which reinforces the imposter syndrome ("Everyone else can handle this; why can’t I?"). The reality is likely that many around you are also performing around their own depletion, but the culture rewards the performance, not the honesty.

**How to use these pivots:**

*   **Stop using their energy as a benchmark for your worth.** Their output is not evidence of your inadequacy. It is evidence of a system that rewards visible performance over sustainable well-being.
*   **Validate the flatness as protection.** Instead of fighting the flatness or shaming yourself for it, acknowledge it as your body’s attempt to preserve energy in a resource-draining environment. It is not cowardice; it is conservation.
*   **Separate the person from the performance.** Your VP’s ability to thrive under pressure is their trait, not your standard. You are navigating a different set of constraints (financial entrapment, family responsibilities, secondary trauma from emotional labor). Your capacity is being consumed by invisible labor they may not be carrying.

You are not failing to be like them. You are succeeding at surviving a situation that is designed to extract everything from you. The flatness is the bill coming due. Acknowledging that doesn’t fix the financial constraints immediately, but it stops the additional bleed of self-hatred.
