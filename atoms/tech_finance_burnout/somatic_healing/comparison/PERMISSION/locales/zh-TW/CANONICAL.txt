Here is why these permissions matter, specifically for your operating system:

**1. Disrupting the "Fix-It" Loop (v01)**
Your default mode is to treat anxiety as a bug to be debugged. When you feel the comparison trap, your instinct is likely to analyze *why* it’s happening, *how* to stop it, and *what* it says about your legitimacy. This v01 permission interrupts that cycle. It acknowledges that **somatic healing is not a linear sprint**. Feeling the comparison isn’t a failure of the healing process; it *is* the process. You don’t need to resolve the feeling to be worthy of the next moment.

**2. Decoupling Your Reality from External Metrics (v02)**
You mentioned that you interpret others’ ease as evidence of your own inadequacy. You assume their "fine" is real and your "struggle" is a defect. v02 challenges this false data. It reminds you that **visibility is not accuracy**. Just because others aren’t broadcasting their internal friction doesn’t mean they aren’t experiencing it. More importantly, it validates your experience *independently* of theirs. Your struggle is not a deviation from the norm; it is your current reality, and it deserves space regardless of the perceived performance of those around you.

**3. Breaking the Isolation of Secrecy (v03)**
You operate under a heavy load of impression management, believing that revealing doubt will expose you as unqualified. v03 flips this script. Naming the trap doesn’t amplify its power; it **drains its secrecy**. Imposter syndrome thrives in the dark, in the unspoken assumption that "everyone else belongs here, and I am faking it." By speaking it, you align your external reality with your internal truth. This reduces the cognitive overhead of maintaining the facade. It transforms the experience from a solitary shame spiral into a shared human condition.

**How to use these:**
When the somatic hit comes—the tightness in the chest, the urge to scroll LinkedIn and feel inadequate, the panic before a standup—try pausing and silently reciting the relevant permission. Not to make the feeling go away, but to **stop fighting the fact that it is there.**

*   "I am allowed to feel this comparison without fixing it right now."
*   "My struggle is valid, even if they look calm."
*   "I can name this. It doesn’t make me unsafe; it makes me real."

You are not failing at healing because you are still feeling pain. You are practicing being present with the pain instead of performing your way out of it. That is a different kind of work, and it is enough.
