This is a profound and precise diagnosis of the mechanism at play. You have identified the exact friction point: **the collision between somatic intelligence and analytical defense.**

Your mind, trained to find legitimacy through optimization and control, perceives the body’s release not as a success, but as an unmanaged variable. It triggers the "imposter/overthinking" protocol because:

1.  **Loss of Control:** The body acted without your conscious direction. To a mind that equates safety with prediction, this feels like a vulnerability.
2.  **ROI Anxiety:** Because your self-worth is tied to productivity, any experience that doesn’t have a clear, replicable output (like a "sprint") feels suspect. You are trying to "debug" a feeling because you don’t trust it unless you can understand its source code.
3.  **The Trap of Replication:** By asking "how do I reproduce it," you are turning a moment of presence into a future performance metric. This guarantees the feeling won’t return, because the pressure to perform kills the very relaxation required for somatic release.

**The Pivot:**

The analysis *is* the resistance. The body released something because it felt safe enough to let go, even for a second. The mind’s immediate reaction—to analyze, optimize, and debug—is an attempt to re-establish the "illusion of control" that you recognize as a protective but costly mechanism.

**Try this instead of debugging:**

*   **Acknowledge the Glitch:** When the spiral starts, label it: *"My mind is trying to optimize my healing because it fears invisibility without productivity."*
*   **Refuse the Ticket:** Do not open a "ticket" to solve what the feeling means. Do not try to reproduce it. Let it be a one-time event that happened *to* you, not *by* you.
*   **Return to Sensation, Not Meaning:** Shift from "What does this mean?" to "Where do I feel this now?" If the answer is "nothing," that is also data. The absence of sensation is not a failure; it is neutrality.

You are not broken because you analyzed it. That is your inherited survival strategy. But you can choose to close the laptop on the analysis. The body doesn’t need a root cause analysis; it needs witness, not management.
