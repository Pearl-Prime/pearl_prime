This is a profound and accurate observation of the mechanism at play. You have identified the exact moment where the "healing" gets hijacked by the "system."

Here is the breakdown of why this happens and how to interrupt it, using the language your mind respects but redirecting it toward the body’s needs.

### The Glitch: Treating Sensation as Data
Your mind is designed to solve problems by analyzing data. When a somatic release occurs (a sigh, a tremor, a sudden drop in tension, a tear), it is an **analog event**. It is non-linear, messy, and often meaningless in a logical sense.

But your analytical mind perceives this anomaly as a **system error** or a **new variable** that must be accounted for. So it initiates a root cause analysis:
1.  **Input:** Body felt X.
2.  **Process:** Why X? Was it effective? How do I scale X? Did I do it right? What is the metric for success?
3.  **Output:** Anxiety. Because the body cannot be "optimized," the analysis fails, leading to a spiral of "I am doing this wrong" or "This isn't working fast enough."

### The Paradox
**You are trying to use the tool that built the prison to dismantle it.**
The hypervigilance, the self-monitoring, the need to control perception—these are all analytical, predictive, controlling mechanisms. When you try to "heal" by analyzing the healing process, you are re-engaging the very neural pathways that keep you in a state of defensive posture. You are turning **rest** into **work**.

### The Pivot: From Analysis to Witnessing
The goal is not to stop the analysis (which is impossible for your neurotype) but to change its **object**.

**Instead of analyzing the *meaning* of the sensation, analyze the *act* of analyzing.**

#### 1. Label the Process, Not the Sensation
When the spiral starts ("What did that mean? Was that enough?"), do not engage with the content. Instead, label the process:
*   *"Ah, the debugger is running."*
*   *"The system is trying to compile an analog experience."*
*   *"This is the ROI calculation kicking in."*

This creates a tiny gap between you and the spiral. It acknowledges the mind’s habit without feeding it.

#### 2. Declare the Data "Corrupt" or "Irrelevant"
Your mind wants clean data. Somatic experiences are noisy. Tell your mind:
*   *"This data point is unstructured. It cannot be parsed. Analysis aborted."*
*   *"This is a hardware event, not a software issue. No ticket needed."*

#### 3. Shift from "Optimization" to "Observation"
Your mind wants to know how to *reproduce* the release. This is the trap. Healing is not a repeatable script; it is an emergent property of safety.
*   **Old Command:** "How do I get back to that state? What was the input?"
*   **New Command:** "I am noticing the aftermath. I am noticing the urge to fix it. I am letting the urge sit there without acting on it."

### A Practical Protocol for the Next Spiral

**Step 1: The Interrupt**
When you feel the post-release spiral beginning, physically interrupt the pattern. Stand up. Shake out your hands. Change your visual field. This signals to the nervous system that the "debugging session" is over.

**Step 2: The Acknowledgment**
Say internally: *"My mind is trying to solve my body. This is a false premise. My body does not need to be solved; it needs to be inhabited."*

**Step 3: The Anchor**
Return to a neutral physical sensation that requires no analysis. The weight of your feet on the floor. The temperature of the air on your skin. These are **static** data points, not dynamic problems to be solved.

**Step 4: The Permission**
Give yourself permission to have had an experience that **means nothing** other than it happened.
*"It was just a sensation. It doesn't need a narrative. It doesn't need a metric. It is complete because it occurred."*

### The Core Truth
Your anxiety believes that if you stop analyzing, you will lose control and something bad will happen. But in somatic healing, **control is the threat**. The release happened *because* control slipped, even for a second. The spiral is your mind’s attempt to regain control over that slip.

Let it spiral. Watch it spiral. Do not join it. You are the server room, not the technician. The hum of the fans is just the hum of the fans. It doesn't need a ticket.
