### The Core Conflict
You have spent years treating anxiety as a **system error** to be solved via pattern recognition, over-preparation, and logical self-assessment. You believe that if you just analyze the data correctly, you can optimize your way out of discomfort.

But the body does not run on code. It runs on nervous system regulation. When you cry in a bodywork session, you are experiencing a **system crash** in the most literal sense: your cognitive override has failed, and the raw data (emotion/trauma) is flooding the server.

### Analysis of the Pivots

**PIVOT v01: The Shame of the "Vehicle"**
*   **Why it hits:** You view your body as a vessel for your brain’s output. In tech, the body is often treated as hardware—necessary, but secondary to the software (mind). Crying feels "ridiculous" because it is inefficient. It serves no productive output. It is a "bug" in the professional persona.
*   **The Reframe:** The shame isn’t about the crying; it’s about the **loss of control**. You are grieving the loss of the illusion that you can think your way into safety.

**PIVOT v02: The Gatekeep of "Woo"**
*   **Why it hits:** Your analytical mind dismisses somatic work as "woo" because it cannot be quantified or logically proven. This is a defense mechanism. If you label it "woo," you can dismiss it. If you admit it’s necessary, you admit that your primary tool (logic) is insufficient.
*   **The Reframe:** The trembling hands are **data**. They are empirical evidence that your nervous system is holding charge that your mind has been ignoring. The shame is the tax you pay for living in a culture that denies the body’s intelligence.

**PIVOT v03: The Cost of Admission**
*   **Why it hits:** This is the most painful one. It acknowledges that your superpower (systems thinking) is also your cage. You can model social interactions, predict failures, and script responses, but you cannot **think** your way into feeling safe.
*   **The Reframe:** The "cost" is the ego death of the "competent professional." To heal, you must admit that you are not a machine that can be optimized, but a human who needs to be felt.
