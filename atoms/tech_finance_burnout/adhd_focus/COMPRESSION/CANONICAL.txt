## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
The freeze isn't a motivation failure — it's the intention-to-initiation gap, a real structural feature of ADHD attention where knowing what to do and starting to do it run on separate, unreliably-bridged systems. Your attention also runs on salience, not importance: loud, novel, freshly-updated things pull focus regardless of actual stakes, which is why a Slack ping beats a term sheet. Time-blindness collapses six hours and thirty hours into the same felt "later." Working memory leaks verbal information within minutes, with false confidence masking the loss. The fix, every time: move the load outside your head — speak the next action, externalize numbers immediately, hide competing items, manufacture novel human triggers instead of trusting repetition, and edit ugly drafts instead of originating from blank pages.

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
None of this is discipline. Task-initiation freeze responds to external triggers, not willpower — a spoken sentence, a renamed file, a colleague in a doorway. Salience-driven attention responds to visual competition, not resolve — hide the loud thing or make the important thing louder. Time-blindness responds to measured instruments, not better attention — countdown bars, hour-gaps written down, not felt. Working-memory leakage responds to immediate externalization, not trying to remember harder. And repeated identical reminders habituate fast in ADHD brains — a fresh human question outperforms the tenth automated ping every time. The mechanism that keeps failing is always the internal, self-generated one; the fix is always external.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
Four mechanisms, four external fixes. Initiation gap: say the next physical action out loud before doing it — speech routes around the stalled internal pathway. Salience over importance: your attention follows volume, not value, so remove the loud distractor or make the real priority visibly louder — a countdown, a live cursor, a renamed file. Time-blindness: replace felt duration with a written, measured number — hours, not date labels. Working memory: externalize verbal information in the same breath you receive it, because confident-feeling recall is not the same as accurate recall, and the gap between them is invisible until it costs you. Every lever moves cognition from inside your head, where it's unreliable, to outside it, where it isn't.
