## PERMISSION v01
---
---
You are allowed to build yourself an external trigger — a spoken sentence, a renamed file, a colleague's check-in — instead of waiting to will yourself across the gap every single time.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---
---
You are allowed to close the tab instead of trying to out-focus it; removing the competitor is not weaker than resisting it, it's the version that actually works.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---
---
You are allowed to stop trusting your felt sense of six hours versus thirty, and write the number down instead, without treating that as a failure of memory or care.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---
---
You are allowed to assume you'll forget the number from the hallway in ten minutes, and write it down immediately, without waiting to see if this time is different.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---
---
You are allowed to abandon the file that's failed you seventeen times and start fresh somewhere new, without treating that as giving up on the task.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---
---
You are allowed to manufacture your own deadline pressure — a teammate's ping, a visible countdown — for the tasks nobody else is chasing you about yet.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---
---
You are allowed to hide ten out of eleven checklist items just to finish the one in front of you, without calling that avoidance.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---
---
You are allowed to rename a file something ridiculous, pin it to your taskbar, and treat that as a real fix rather than a gimmick beneath the seriousness of the work.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---
---
You are allowed to ask "what does this actually need, right now, specifically" before touching a task, instead of assuming you should already know how to start it.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---
---
You are allowed to ask someone to hand you rough, ugly material to edit instead of insisting you generate the polished version from nothing yourself.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---
---
You are allowed to mute an automated reminder that's stopped working on you, and ask a person to check in instead, without calling that a failure of your systems.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---
---
You are allowed to say the next physical action out loud before doing it, even alone at your desk at 2 a.m., without it meaning anything about your competence.
---
