Ryan's phone pings. Slack notification. His nervous system launches. His chest tightens, his hands go cold, his breath shortens. He reads it. It's a colleague asking about documentation. But the culture of constant async communication has trained his system to stay on high alert. Anything could be urgent. Everything could be a problem.

Ryan starts keeping a log. Ping arrives, alarm fires, he checks the urgency. He discovers that in forty pings, two were actually urgent. The ratio is real. His system is crying wolf constantly. He starts separating the sensation from the interpretation. The ping arrives. The alarm fires. He notes: "alarm fired, actual urgency: low." He does this for two weeks.
