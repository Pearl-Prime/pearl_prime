The shift is from **defense** to **witnessing**.

When you stop trying to fix the alarm, you stop treating your own nervous system as a malfunctioning machine that needs repair. You stop fighting the sensation of urgency. This creates a small, critical space between the *signal* (the anxiety) and the *compulsion* (the immediate emotional labor or rumination).

In that space, the "false alarm" reveals itself not as a lie, but as a **misdirected loyalty**. It is protecting you from feeling:

1.  **The grief of the "pre-child self"**: The alarm keeps you busy managing others' crises so you don’t have to feel the loss of the identity, creativity, and partnership that were archived when you became a parent. If you stop sprinting, you might have to look at what was left behind.
2.  **The terror of irrelevance**: Your worth is tied to being the "emotional infrastructure." If you aren’t absorbing trauma, who are you? The alarm protects you from the void of having no immediate utility to others.
3.  **The anger of systemic betrayal**: The alarm frames everything as *your* failure to manage (the "good parent metric"), which keeps the focus on your internal adjustments. If you stop fixing, you might feel the raw rage that the system is broken, that the costs are unsustainable, and that you were never meant to carry this alone.

Hearing it means asking: *"What are you trying to keep me from seeing if I stop running?"*

The next room is not about solving the anxiety. It is about **grieving** what the anxiety has been keeping at bay.
