Ryan opens his todo list. Forty-three items. He has three days left in the sprint. The numbers don't add. His stomach sits somewhere behind his ribs. He has built a sprint velocity that assumes he is not human. He is not sleeping enough to maintain it. He is not pausing between tasks. The context switching is constant—production issue, then new feature, then code review, then design meeting. His head feels pressure from all sides. He stares at the list. The pressure increases. Each item is real. Each item matters. His capacity is finite. The gap between the two facts is making his chest tight.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Ryan's overwhelm has become his baseline. He is no longer operating at capacity. He is operating past capacity. His nervous system is in a permanent state of "there is not enough time and you are behind." Nothing he does feels complete because he is always moving to the next thing. His quality is dropping. He notices. His anxiety spikes. He works harder to compensate. The harder he works, the less time he has for each task, the more incomplete everything feels. The mechanism has closed into a loop. Overwhelm is generating more work through mistakes and rework. That more work is intensifying the overwhelm.

Ava has been overwhelmed for so long that her perception of normal has shifted. She thinks back to a time when she had less on her plate and she cannot remember what that felt like. ## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Ryan sits with his list and he starts deleting. Not things that will get done—things that need to not exist. He crosses them off without doing them. His anxiety spikes. But he does it anyway. Four items deleted. His list is now thirty-nine items. Still too much. But six items fewer that he was never going to do anyway. His chest releases a fraction. He is claiming that his capacity is finite and his time is real. The items that stay are the ones that actually matter. The sprint might not finish everything. He might be fine with that.
