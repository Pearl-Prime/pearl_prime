Here is why these resonances matter for your specific context:

**1. The Illusion of Competence (PIVOT v01 & v02)**
As a nurse with 30 years of experience, you have seen the "backstage" of healthcare. You know that composure is often a triage strategy, not an emotional state. Yet, because your anxiety mechanism is hyper-vigilant (a protective feature born from childhood financial insecurity and gendered self-doubt), you interpret your own visceral reaction to stress as evidence of *inadequacy*, while interpreting others’ silence as *competence*.

*   **The Truth:** Your body isn’t breaking; it’s signaling. In a system that demands emotional labor without providing emotional support, "numbness" is often just delayed collapse. Your "honesty" is a biological integrity that others may have suppressed at a higher long-term cost (compassion fatigue, burnout, or physical illness later). You are not weaker; you are less dissociated.

**2. The Systemic Gaslighting (PIVOT v03)**
This is the critical shift. You have been trying to solve a **systemic problem** (an environment that pathologizes normal human stress responses) with a **personal solution** (trying to think your way out of anxiety, trying to be "more professional," trying to suppress the feeling).

*   **The Reframe:** The anxiety you feel in social settings, in front of doctors, or when parenting is not a bug in your software. It is a rational response to an irrational demand: *that you should be able to carry the weight of structural underfunding, understaffing, and emotional extraction without showing the strain.*
