

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I learned early that my job was to see threats before they arrived. My brain became a prediction engine, running worst-case scenarios in constant background. The market drops and I calculate portfolio impact. The team goes quiet and I scan for the crisis nobody mentioned. Uncertainty isn't neutral anymore; it's a signal.
You train your nervous system to treat not-knowing as danger. That unread Slack might be disaster. The high-priority email definitely will be. This isn't caution anymore. It's constant anticipatory dread that keeps you ahead at work but exhausts you at home.
I was valuable for this. I caught problems early. I escalated before meetings. My compensation reflected the value. But the cost compounds invisibly. Every moment has threat assessment running underneath. Rest becomes impossible because resting means something could happen while you're not predicting it.
The alarm evolved past its purpose. It doesn't distinguish between real danger and routine uncertainty anymore. A delayed response from your manager feels existential because the prediction engine treats all unknowns equally. The mechanism that made you valuable became the thing that won't let you sleep.
