Ryan looks at pictures from college. Ten years ago. He was thin. He had time. He read books. He climbed mountains. That person feels like someone else now. His chest is heavy. There is an anxiety underneath the heaviness. He has lost something. He does not know how to name it. The person he was is gone. The person he is now works seventy-hour weeks. He has no time for mountains. He has no time for books. He has become the job. The anxiety is the recognition that he chose this and the job did not turn out to be enough.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/tech_finance_burnout/anchored/anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Ryan's anxiety about the grief has intensified. He is anxious about the fact that he is anxious about the loss. He cannot admit the loss because it might mean the job was not worth the trade. But he also cannot ignore the loss. So the anxiety increases. He is split between two truths: the job has consumed him and the job defines him. He cannot be both the victim and the architect. The mechanism is: the grief is being converted to anxiety because grief requires acknowledgment and he cannot acknowledge it.
