

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
My shoulders are tight. Right now. Not someday. Right now. There's a specific tension at my trapezius. Where the headphones press. This is real. This is the only thing. The only thing I can change. My mind wants to catastrophize. Wants to spiral. But my shoulders? They can drop. My hands can unclench. My jaw can release. I'm not waiting for the catastrophe to resolve. I'm not waiting for Slack to quiet down. I'm not waiting for the standup to end. I'm here. With my shoulders. With my breathing. With my body that's exactly here. And it's not catastrophic. It's just tense. And tense is changeable. Not manageable. Changeable. This is not the future. This is now.
Carry: My shoulders belong to me.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: BREATH-FACT
---
The notification sound played. My nervous system jumped. And it's still jumping. Still moving. Still activated. I didn't stop it immediately. And that's okay. The activation is happening. My heart rate is elevated. My hands are slightly shaking. I'm not breathing normally. I'm breathing quickly. This is what activation looks like. In my body. This is not dangerous. This is just activation. And I'm still here. Still sitting. Still able to read Slack. Still able to respond. The activation doesn't mean collapse. The activation is information. My body is telling me: you've been triggered. Not: you're in danger. There's a difference. I'm learning the difference. Right now.
Carry: Activation has a body, not a timeline.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: CHOICE-FACT
---
I can see what the anxiety costs. Not abstractly. Concretely. It cost me thirty minutes of focus time. It cost me one meal skipped. It cost me two hours of sleep. Those are real costs. To real things. My body. My attention. My rest. The anxiety isn't free. It has weight. It has consequences. And I can't pretend it doesn't. But I also notice: I chose some of this. I could have closed the performance review doc. I could have closed Slack. I could have stopped checking the metrics. I did those things anyway. Not because the anxiety forced me. Because I was following anxiety's instruction. I can see that now. The cost is visible. The choice is visible.
Carry: I'm not trapped by anxiety's instructions.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: CURIOSITY-FACT
---
What if the anxiety has something to teach me? Not what if I can fix it. Not what if it goes away. What if I just listen to what it's saying? My anxiety is saying: you need to be prepared. You need to be ready. You need to check one more time. You need to make sure. What if that's not pathological? What if that's just my system being cautious? I'm not asking myself to be happy about it. I'm asking: what is this actually protecting me from? What does my body think it needs to do? There's something underneath the anxiety. Not underneath the anxiety. In it. I'm curious about that.
Carry: Anxiety has information I haven't heard yet.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: SENSATION-FACT
---
My stomach is tight. It feels like a knot. Specifically at my solar plexus. I can feel it even now. Writing this. The tightness doesn't respond to logic. Telling myself I'm fine doesn't unknot it. Reassuring myself doesn't release it. But moving helps. Breathing helps. Pressure helps. Physical things help. My body isn't irrational. It's just not responsive to rational argument. It's responsive to physical change. So I'm not fighting my stomach. I'm just shifting weight. Just stretching. Just breathing into it. The tightness will loosen or it won't. But I'm not waiting for it to be perfect to keep moving.
Carry: My body speaks a physical language.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: WITNESS-FACT
---
I watched someone else have anxiety today. I saw their shoulders tense. I saw them check their phone repeatedly. I saw them take shallow breaths. And I didn't think they were weak. I didn't think they were pathological. I thought they were a person responding to real stress. And now I know: that's me too. I'm not special in my anxiety. I'm not special in my activation. I'm having the normal human response to an abnormal system. That person I watched? They seemed fine despite the anxiety. They were still here. Still functional. Still moving. And so am I.
Carry: My anxiety is ordinary. My survival is remarkable.
