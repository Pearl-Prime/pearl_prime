The "Incident Report" metaphor (PIVOT v03) is particularly potent because it highlights the mismatch: **Incident reports are for system failures. Social interactions are not systems; they are messy, human, and forgiving.**

Here is the counter-pivot to disrupt the spiral, leveraging your systems-thinking mindset but redirecting it away from self-prosecution:

### 1. Challenge the "Catastrophe" Logic (PIVOT v01 & v02)
Your brain is running a **linear escalation algorithm**: *Minor Error → Total Reputation Collapse → Exclusion → Termination.*
*   **The Glitch:** This algorithm assumes a zero-tolerance environment where humans are static judges rather than dynamic, distracted, and generally self-absorbed participants.
*   **The Reality Check:** In most professional settings, people are thinking about their own work, their own insecurities, or what they’re having for lunch. They are not maintaining a real-time ledger of your micro-errors. The "audience" you fear is largely an empty theater projected by your hypervigilance.

### 2. Stop Filing the Incident Report (PIVOT v03)
You are spending hours "debugging" a conversation that ended minutes ago. This is **rumination as a false sense of control**. You believe that if you analyze it enough, you can find the "fix" or the "root cause" to prevent future pain.
*   **The Cost:** This process drains the cognitive energy you need for actual presence, making you *more* likely to feel awkward in the next interaction, thus reinforcing the cycle.
*   **The Pivot:** When the replay starts, label it explicitly: **"This is not analysis. This is harassment."** Your brain is bullying you. You would not allow a colleague to dissect your every word for hours; do not allow your internal monitor to do it either.

### 3. Accept the "False Alarm"
Your nervous system is triggering a **Code Red** for a **Code Yellow** event.
*   **The Disconnect:** You know intellectually that saying something slightly awkward is not a fireable offense. But your body reacts as if you’ve just been spotted by a predator.
*   **The Action:** Do not try to *think* your way out of the physiological response. You cannot logic your way out of a adrenaline dump. Instead, **acknowledge the false alarm.** Say to yourself: *"My threat detection system is misfiring. I am safe. This discomfort is just noise, not data."*

### Immediate Tactic for the Next Spiral:
When the "All-Hands Comment" panic hits:
1.  **Pause the Replay:** Physically interrupt the loop. Stand up, walk, change your sensory input.
2.  **Externalize the Data:** Ask one trusted colleague (if appropriate) or simply observe the room. Did anyone actually react? Or did the meeting move on? The data usually shows **indifference**, not judgment.
3.  **Reframe the Narrative:** Instead of "I damaged my reputation," try "I was present enough to speak, and I survived the imperfection."

You are not broken. You are running outdated security software on a modern, collaborative network. The goal isn't to delete the software (it’s too ingrained), but to **lower its sensitivity settings** so it stops treating every social ping as a DDoS attack.
