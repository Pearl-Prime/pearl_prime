These takeaways cut through the noise of comparison and shame. They reframe your experience not as a failure of character, but as a predictable outcome of a system that has extracted too much for too long without replenishment.

Here is how these truths land in the context of what you are carrying:

**1. The "Compound Return" of Neglect**
You have been operating under the illusion that your capacity to absorb emotional labor and maintain constant availability was infinite. It wasn’t. It was a finite resource that you drew down daily for years. The "spiral" you feel now—the burnout, the anhedonia, the physical heaviness—is not a sudden collapse. It is the accumulated interest on years of skipped maintenance windows. You weren’t weak; you were over-leveraged.

**2. The Privacy of Your Cost**
The shame you feel often stems from the belief that you must justify your struggle against your high income. But financial stability does not immunize you from human depletion. You do not need to defend your exhaustion to peers who seem to have it "easier," nor to an organization that benefits from your silence. The cost is yours to acknowledge, not theirs to validate.

**3. The Untrackable Invoice**
Your RSUs and salary track market value, not human cost. They cannot compensate for the loss of curiosity, the erosion of joy, or the secondary trauma you’ve absorbed. That invoice is being sent to your nervous system, not your bank account. No amount of equity can buy back the capacity to be moved by beauty or to feel safe in your own downtime. Recognizing this disconnect is the first step in stopping the payment plan.

You are not broken. You are overdue for a reckoning with the true price of being the "emotional infrastructure."
