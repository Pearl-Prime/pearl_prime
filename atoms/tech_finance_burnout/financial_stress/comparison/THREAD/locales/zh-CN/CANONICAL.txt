The shift is that the noise stops being a signal of *deficit* and starts being a signal of *grief*.

When I stop trying to "fix" the comparison—stop trying to outrun it with more vesting, more monitoring, more optimization—the frantic energy dissipates into a heavy, quiet sadness. The comparison isn’t telling me I’m poor; it’s telling me I’m lonely. It’s pointing to the void where my professional identity used to be.

I’m not comparing my bank account to theirs because I need their money. I’m comparing my *aliveness* to their *visibility*. Their success is a proxy for the meaning I lost when the startup died. The financial surveillance is just a displaced ritual for mourning a self that no longer exists. If I hear that, the urge to check the brokerage account doesn’t feel like diligence anymore; it feels like picking at a scab.
