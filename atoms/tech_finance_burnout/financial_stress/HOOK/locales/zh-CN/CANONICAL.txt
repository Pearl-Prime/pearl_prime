### Category 1: The Somatic Betrayal (Body Knows Before Mind)
*Focus: The physical symptoms as the primary data point of entrapment.*

**Refined Hook A (Based on v02 & v08):**
> Your shoulders know exactly how much the house costs per day. You don’t need to check the mortgage statement; you feel the weight of it in the tension between your neck and your ear. The salary is competitive, the benefits are comprehensive, but your body is still firing threat signals because it knows: this comfort is a cage.

**Refined Hook B (Based on v06 & v20):**
> Your nervous system fires not because you’re broke, but because you did the math. Leaving would require two years of downward lifestyle adjustment, and your body refuses to process that downgrade. The six-figure salary isn’t freedom; it’s the anesthetic that keeps you sedated in the trap.

### Category 2: The Vesting Schedule as a Timeline of Captivity
*Focus: The temporal lock-in of unvested stock/options.*

**Refined Hook C (Based on v09 & v19):**
> Jaw clenched because the stock options vest in six months. You can’t leave before then, and you won’t leave after because the money will be gone. The golden handcuffs have an expiration date that keeps moving, turning your career into a series of waiting rooms. You aren’t working for growth; you’re working for the next vest.

**Refined Hook D (Based on v04 & v30):**
> The chest tightness isn’t about the work; it’s about the story you tell yourself regarding the options that *didn’t* vest. Now, you’re chasing the ones that *might*. The golden handcuffs are tighter because you’re no longer building a future—you’re just servicing the debt of your past decisions.

### Category 3: Lifestyle Inflation as Structural Architecture
*Focus: The idea that the lifestyle itself has become the boss.*

**Refined Hook E (Based on v15 & v29):**
> You are trapped by your own optimization. The salary enabled a lifestyle that now requires the salary to survive. The golden handcuffs aren’t just metal; they are the architecture of your life—the school districts, the healthcare tiers, the mortgage rate. Leaving wouldn’t just mean a pay cut; it would mean demolishing the structure you built to feel safe.

**Refined Hook F (Based on v16 & v21):**
> The lifestyle that the salary enabled is now the thing that requires the salary. You are locked into the life by the life itself. The golden handcuffs are no longer about greed; they are about the terror of downgrading the identity you purchased with your burnout.

### Category 4: The Identity of the Provider
*Focus: The shame and reputational constraints.*

**Refined Hook G (Based on v13 & v24):**

### Strategic Recommendation

**One Critical Observation:**
Many of the original hooks end with "You're staying." This is powerful, but it can feel fatalistic. Given your memory of *reframing cowardice as navigating real constraints*, consider adding a subtle shift in the final sentence of some hooks to acknowledge the *agency* in the constraint, even if it feels like none exists.

*Example Adjustment to Hook E:*
> "...Leaving would mean demolishing the structure you built to feel safe. So you stay. Not because you are weak, but because the cost of demolition is currently higher than the cost of endurance."

This validates the user's reality without reinforcing the shame spiral.
