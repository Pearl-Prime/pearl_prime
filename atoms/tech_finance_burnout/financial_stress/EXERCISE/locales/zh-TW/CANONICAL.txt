Here is how we apply these exercises to this exact moment. Please follow along, step by step.

### 1. The Physical Reset (From Exercise v02 & v03)
Before you look at any number or make any decision:
*   **Close your eyes** or look away from the screen for 10 seconds.
*   **Breathe:** In for 4 counts. Hold for 4. Out for 6.
*   **Notice:** Where is the tightness? Is it in your chest? Your jaw? Your shoulders? Acknowledge it without judging it. It is just a sensation. It is not a command.

### 2. The Separation (From Exercise v01)
Open your eyes. Look at the source of the stress (the brokerage app, the spreadsheet, the email).
*   **Do not read the totals.** Do not look at the bottom line.
*   Pick **one** single number or item that is screaming the loudest.
*   Ask: *Does this specific item require action **today**?*
    *   If yes: Write it down on a physical piece of paper. Just one sentence.
    *   If no: Say out loud, "This can wait until next week."

### 3. The Reality Check (From Exercise v03)
You are likely confusing *important* with *urgent*.
*   Most financial numbers are **data**, not **demands**.
*   Data does not require an immediate nervous system response.
*   Ask yourself: *If I do nothing about this specific number for the next 24 hours, what actually happens?*
    *   Usually, the answer is: Nothing catastrophic. The market will still be there tomorrow. The bill due date is likely not today.

### 4. The One Action (From Exercise v01 & v02)
Choose **one** tiny, low-stakes action from your list.
*   *Examples:*
    *   Close the browser tab.
    *   Drink a glass of water.
    *   Stand up and stretch for 30 seconds.
    *   Reply to one non-urgent email with "I’ll look at this later this week."
*   **Do that one thing.**
*   Then stop.
