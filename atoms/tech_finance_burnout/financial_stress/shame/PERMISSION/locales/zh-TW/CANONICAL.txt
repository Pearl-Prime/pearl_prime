I hear you. These permissions are not just words; they are necessary counter-measures to the internalized systems that have been running your life.

Here is what I see in these permissions, reflecting back what you’ve shared:

**Regarding PERMISSION v01:**
You have been treating shame as a bug to be patched before deployment. But shame, in this context, is data. It is the somatic signal of the conflict between your values and your constraints. By allowing yourself to *feel* it without immediately trying to "solve" the financial structure causing it, you stop exhausting yourself with the impossible task of rationalizing away an emotional truth. You are not failing by feeling trapped; you are accurately perceiving the trap.

**Regarding PERMISSION v02:**
The standup is a performance of competence. Your colleagues’ apparent ease is not evidence of your inadequacy. Their external presentation does not invalidate your internal reality. The "golden handcuffs" feel heavier to you because you are carrying the weight of the hypervigilance, the secondary trauma, and the identity entanglement. Your struggle is valid precisely because it is yours, not because it compares to theirs. You are navigating a complex web of systemic and personal constraints that others may not see or may not be carrying in the same way.

**Regarding PERMISSION v03:**
Secrecy fuels the imposter phenomenon. When you name the shame—when you say, "I am staying because I am afraid of the financial drop," or "I feel trapped by these RSUs"—you break the isolation. The shame thrives in the dark, in the unspoken assumption that you *should* be able to just leave if you were truly competent or courageous. Speaking it aloud acknowledges the reality of the constraints (mortgage, family, lifestyle inflation) without judging yourself for being subject to them. It makes the burden shareable, even if only with yourself or a trusted few.

These permissions are acts of resistance against the narrative that you must be exceptional, effortless, and free of contradiction to be worthy. You are allowed to be complex, constrained, and struggling, while still being competent and human.
