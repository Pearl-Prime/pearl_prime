The grief has cracked that compartment. The evidence from Threads v02 and v06 shows that the structure did not collapse when you operated at half capacity or when you revealed vulnerability. The "next architecture" mentioned in Thread v12 requires a fundamental shift in how you view leadership and resource allocation. You are skilled at managing budgets and strategic resources professionally, yet you have historically treated your own energy and emotional bandwidth as infinite, low-cost inputs. The physical symptoms you’ve experienced—muscle tension, shaking hands, jaw tightness—are the body’s ledger showing the deficit.

1.  **Acknowledging the True Cost:** Recognizing that the "holding cost" of performing fine (Thread v08) is higher than the cost of authentic, bounded presence. The anxiety that assumes the ground is unstable is a false positive in this context; the ground held when you told your team.

3.  **Integrating the New Calibration:** The grief has stripped away the non-essential. Use this clarity to make decisions about your career and financial situation not from a place of trapped fear, but from a place of identified values. If the current role continues to demand a level of performance that ignores your human reality, the mismatch is structural, not personal.
