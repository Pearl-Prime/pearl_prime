

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: CULTURE-LOSS
---
The company we worked for doesn't exist anymore. It was restructured into efficiency and the soul went away somewhere in the process. We used to know every person on the team. We knew their kids' names. Friday afternoons were actually slack time where people talked. Now we have OKRs and sprints and a cadence that leaves no space for the human part. My team is the same people physically but something fundamental about the culture is gone. The reframe isn't that change is inevitable—it's that what we lost was actually valuable and I'm allowed to grieve it. My chest is tight when I think about where we were. My throat closes when someone new joins and I realize they'll never know what this place used to feel like.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: TEAM-DISSOLUTION
---
The team I built was restructured. Three people I hired and developed and believed in were scattered across different divisions. One left the company entirely. The reframe isn't that this is just business—it is business, and it's also the loss of something I actually created. I invested years in building that team's capability and trust. Now they report to different people. My hands feel empty when I think about it. The work we did together is still valuable, but it doesn't exist as a coherent thing anymore. What I built got torn apart and distributed, and I'm supposed to move forward as if nothing happened.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: IDENTITY-SHIFT
---
I was the person who took care of the team. That was core to how I saw myself. Now I'm managing up more than down, and that identity is slipping. The people I built relationships with have dispersed. The energy I put into creating psychological safety is being applied to a team that's different now. My shoulders carry this. The physical sensation is heaviness. I'm tired in a way that's not about hours—it's about the loss of purpose. The reframe is that I built my sense of self on something that was temporary, and now I'm grieving the loss of that reflection. I don't know who I am without that team.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: REPLACEMENT-ERASURE
---
We hired new people to fill the spots of the old team. They're fine. They're competent. They're not my people. The reframe isn't that I should love them the same way—I don't, and that's OK. It's that the old team members felt like mine because I built them up from where they were. These new people started at the top of their trajectory. I'm grieving that specific relationship of investment and growth. My hands are restless when I think about how quickly the old dynamic evaporated. The new team is good. It's just not mine.
