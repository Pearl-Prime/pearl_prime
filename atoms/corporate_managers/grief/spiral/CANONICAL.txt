David grieves and spirals: grief means he made wrong choice means he should go back means he should quit. His chest is heavy. Sarah grieves and spirals: grief means she can't accept the role means she should leave. Her hands are cold. Kevin grieves and spirals: grief means he's stuck means he should exit. His shoulders are tight. Anthony grieves and spirals: grief means he's not cut for leadership means he should leave. His throat is tight. Tanya grieves and spirals: grief means she chose wrong means she should quit. Her stomach is heavy. Grief becomes exit planning. David stops the spiral: "Grief is normal. It doesn't mean I should quit." His chest unclenches. Sarah interrupts: "Grieving doesn't mean I can't stay." Her hands warm. Kevin breaks in: "Grief is real. Staying is still right." His shoulders release. Anthony pauses: "Grief doesn't determine my choice." His throat releases. Tanya stops: "Grief doesn't mean I leave." Her stomach settles. Grief is integrated, not life-determining.

David spirals about the loss. If this person left, others will leave. If others leave, the team collapses. If the team collapses, David's leadership fails. If leadership fails — the spiral converts one departure into total professional annihilation in four steps. Hands grip the desk. Then David catches loop three and writes it down. One person left. That is the fact. Everything after the fact is fiction. The frame cracks — the spiral is not processing the grief. It is avoiding the grief by replacing it with catastrophe. Catastrophe is easier than sadness. Predicting doom is easier than feeling loss. Fists open The grief waits underneath the spiral, patient and real.

David hits the floor of the spiral — the place where even the spiral cannot continue because the body has no fuel. In the silence at the bottom, something shifts. The catastrophe did not arrive. Hours of prediction and the predicted future did not materialize. David sits in the gap between the spiral's reality and actual reality and the distance is infinite. The frame shatters — the spiral was not preparing for catastrophe. It was manufacturing catastrophe inside David's body while the actual world continued normally. Hands grip the desk. The old way is no longer survivable because the spiral consumed the body it was supposed to protect.

David maps the spiral's structure around grief on paper. The first thought is reasonable: a concern about a real situation. The second thought adds interpretation. The third adds projection. The fourth adds certainty. By the fifth, the concern has become a conviction of doom. Hands grip the desk. Each loop adds one thing: certainty. The spiral does not add information. It adds confidence to the same fear. By loop five, David is certain about a catastrophe that loop one merely wondered about. Face burns. The pattern persists because certainty feels like knowledge. The spiral produces the feeling of knowing something terrible, and the feeling is so convincing that David treats the feeling as evidence.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David has been spiraling about grief for so long that the spiral has consumed the subject it was processing. David can no longer distinguish between the original concern and the spiral's elaboration of it. The concern and the catastrophe have merged. Chest crushes. David sees the full machinery: the spiral does not process problems. It metabolizes them into fuel for more spiraling. Each problem that enters the spiral is converted into material for the next loop. The spiral feeds on its own output. Hands shake. The pattern persists because the spiral has become the primary activity. David spirals instead of acting. The spiraling feels like work — effortful, exhausting, productive-seeming. But it produces nothing except more spiraling. It is a machine that manufactures its own input from its own output, a perpetual motion engine of suffering that will run until the body cannot sustain it.
