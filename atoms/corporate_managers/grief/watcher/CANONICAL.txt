## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David scans the team for the next departure. Someone will leave next. Someone always leaves. The watcher monitors engagement levels, meeting attendance, email response times — looking for the signal that the next loss is approaching. Chest crushes. David is so focused on preventing the next loss that the current loss goes unprocessed. The grief about the person who left sits in David's chest, unattended, while the watcher hunts for the next threat. The frame cracks — the watcher is not protecting David from grief. It is distracting David from grief. Surveillance replaces mourning. Chest opens slightly The person who left deserves to be missed, not replaced by a monitoring protocol.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's vigilance about grief reaches a breaking point. The watcher has been running at full capacity for so long that David cannot remember what it feels like to be in a room without scanning it. Face burns. Chest crushes. Then the watcher fails. It misses something real — a genuine issue that was hiding in plain sight while the watcher was busy analyzing phantom threats. The failure is catastrophic for the watcher's credibility. It was scanning everything and caught nothing that mattered. The frame shatters: the watcher is not effective. It is comprehensive. And comprehensiveness without accuracy is just exhaustion with a purpose. Hands shake. David realizes the watcher must be retired and replaced with something targeted, specific, and rest-compatible. The current system is destroying the person it was built to protect.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah enters a room related to grief and the watcher activates — scanning faces, reading tones, mapping threats. Sarah notices the scan and redirects attention to the actual content of the interaction. Chest is tight but held The watcher runs at low volume in the background. Sarah does not suppress it. Sarah does not obey it. Sarah lets it scan while focusing on the conversation, the decision, the actual work. Hands stay on the table, warm Sarah is a person who is aware without being consumed by awareness. The watcher watches. Sarah lives. Both happen. Sarah is louder.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah faces maximum uncertainty in grief. The watcher wants total surveillance — monitor everything, trust nothing, scan every signal. Sarah chooses targeted awareness instead. Identifies three things that matter. Monitors those. Releases the rest. Chest hollow. The uncertainty remains. The watcher screams about everything Sarah is not monitoring. Sarah holds the discomfort of not knowing. The three things that matter are tracked. Everything else is noise. Hands grip the desk. The outcome proves the approach: targeted awareness caught the important signals. Comprehensive surveillance would have drowned them in noise. Sarah is a person who can live with uncertainty — not by monitoring it away but by choosing what deserves attention and letting the rest exist unscanned. Body is tired but present The watcher is not retired. It is leashed. Sarah holds the leash.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David measures the watcher's cost within grief. Approximately thirty to forty percent of cognitive resources go to surveillance — reading faces, interpreting silences, monitoring tonal shifts, tracking body language. Chest crushes. The remaining sixty percent handles actual work. David has been operating at sixty percent capacity and comparing the output to peers operating at one hundred percent. The deficit is not ability. It is bandwidth. Hands shake. The pattern persists because the watcher feels like a professional skill — attentiveness, awareness, emotional intelligence. But it has crossed from skill to compulsion. The watcher no longer enhances performance. It drains it. The protective logic is that stopping the surveillance means missing threats. But the surveillance catches phantom threats and misses nothing real.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David's watcher has been monitoring grief for so long that David cannot imagine existing without it. The watcher is not a behavior anymore. It is a component of David's identity. Being watchful feels like being David. Chest tight. David sees the full architecture: the watcher has merged with the self. Removing it would feel like removing a limb. David does not know who David is without surveillance. Hands cold. The pattern persists because the watcher is no longer a strategy. It is a personality trait — built from decades of practice until it became structural. It is not something David does. It is something David is. And changing what you are requires a different kind of work than changing what you do. David's eyes are wet. The watcher promised safety. It delivered a life lived in permanent observation of a world that might have been safe all along.
