## TURNING_POINT v01

---
David is drowning in accumulated losses. The colleague who retired. The project that was cancelled. The team member who transferred. Each loss is small. Together they are an avalanche. Throat closes. David cannot grieve one loss because six others demand attention simultaneously. Then David picks up one loss — just one — and sits with it. The retired colleague. Remembers her laugh. Feels the absence specifically. The grief moves. One loss, felt fully, creates space for the next. The frame cracks: grief is sequential, not parallel. The overwhelm came from trying to mourn everything at once. Mourning works one absence at a time.

---
David's body shuts down from grief overload. Not metaphorically. The nervous system hits its circuit breaker. Vision grays. Hands go numb. Thoughts stop forming. Jaw clenches. David sits in the shutdown and cannot move. The overwhelm exceeded the system's capacity and the system chose collapse over continued operation. In the collapse, a brutal clarity arrives: the body drew the boundary the mind refused to draw. The frame shatters — David was never capable of carrying this much. The belief in infinite capacity was the lie that broke the system. Stomach drops. The body's refusal is not weakness. It is the most honest communication David's nervous system has ever made. David cannot carry everything. The body proved it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David examines the overwhelm's structure within grief. It is not a feeling — it is a cognitive failure. The brain cannot hold more than four to seven active concerns simultaneously. David is carrying twelve. The excess does not queue — it cascades. Each concern triggers the next and the cascade feels like drowning. Shoulders lock. David sees the machinery: the overwhelm is a bandwidth problem disguised as a workload problem. The workload might be manageable with a system. Without a system, the workload becomes emotional noise. Throat closes. The pattern persists because building a system requires cognitive bandwidth that the overwhelm has already consumed. The solution requires the resource the problem has depleted.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/grief/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David sees the overwhelm's deepest mechanism within grief. The overwhelm is not about the volume of work. It is about the belief that David must hold all of it personally. Every task, every decision, every problem belongs to David. Vision narrows. The belief predates the role, predates the career, predates the profession. It was installed in a childhood where being needed meant being safe. Chest hollow. The pattern persists because the overwhelm is identity-level: David believes that being overwhelmed IS being valuable. Without the overwhelm, David fears being dispensable. The overwhelm is not a side effect of the work. It is the evidence David offers the world that David matters. Reducing the overwhelm would require dismantling the equation between suffering and worth. That equation is the oldest thing David owns.
