## EXERCISE v01
---

---
The reorg announcement lands. Your chest tightens. Stop. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press down. Feel the ground hold you. Name one thing you have lost. Say it aloud. Then name one thing still yours. Breathe out slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Before your next skip-level meeting, sit still. Notice where your body braces. Jaw. Shoulders. Gut. Choose one spot. Soften it deliberately. This is grief wearing a business suit. Acknowledge it. Then walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Write the name of the role you used to hold. Or the team. Or the boss who understood you. Write it on paper. Set the pen down. Read it once. This matters. Fold the paper. Put it away.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
After a performance review, find a private space. Sit. Place one hand on your sternum. Press lightly. Notice what tightens beneath your palm. Stay there for thirty seconds. Do not explain the feeling. Just locate it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Your team shrinks again. Someone leaves. Before you send the all-clear email, pause. Close your laptop. Name what you are losing. Say it to the empty room. Then open the laptop. Write the email.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Stand at your desk. Uncross your arms. Let them hang. Think of one person no longer on your team. Say their name once, quietly. Notice what shifts in your throat or chest. That shift is real. Let it be there.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Set a two-minute timer. Write without stopping. Begin with: The job I thought I had was. Do not edit. Do not pause. When the timer ends, stop writing. Read nothing yet. Breathe. Return to it tomorrow.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
During your next one-on-one, notice the moment you perform certainty you do not feel. After the call ends, sit back. Exhale fully. Say to yourself: I am carrying something heavy. That is accurate. That is enough.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Identify one expectation the reorg erased. A promotion. A project. A path. Write it in one sentence. Read it. Cross it out slowly. This is not defeat. This is an honest accounting. Set the pen down.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Grief lives in the body. Right now, scan from your shoulders to your hips. Find the tightest point. Breathe into it. Count four counts in. Hold four. Out for six. Repeat three times. Return to your desk.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Before the all-hands where the new structure gets announced, find a bathroom stall. Stand. Press your back against the wall. Feel the wall solid behind you. Say once: I do not know what comes next. Now go in.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
At the end of this workday, do not check email one final time. Instead, sit with the lights off for sixty seconds. Let the day's losses surface. Do not solve them. Just let them exist in the dark. Then leave.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Think of your former manager. The one who left. Or was let go. Picture their face. Notice where that image lands in your body. Chest. Throat. Behind the eyes. Place your hand there. Press gently. Thirty seconds. Release.
---
