## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before your next skip-level, write down one decision you made this quarter. Write the outcome. Write it in full sentences. This is your record. It exists independent of what anyone thinks of you.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Your chest tightens before performance review season. Notice that. Place one hand flat on your sternum. Press lightly. Breathe out slow. Your worth existed before this cycle started. It exists now.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Name one person on your team who grew this year. Write their name. Write what you did that helped. Read it back aloud. That outcome required you.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
The reorg announcement dropped. Your jaw is tight. Unclench it deliberately. Stand up from the chair. Plant both feet on the floor. Your competence did not get reorganized. It travels with you.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
After the next meeting where you stayed silent, stop. Do not leave the room yet. Name one thing you understood that others missed. Say it to yourself quietly. That knowledge was present. So were you.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Write the phrase: "I delivered this." Fill in what follows. Be specific. Use numbers if you have them. Do not qualify it. Do not soften it. Read it once. Close the notebook.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Your manager's approval did not arrive today. Notice where you feel that in your body. Shoulders. Throat. Gut. Name the location out loud. Then name one thing you did well today that required no approval to be true.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Pull up last quarter's goals. Find one you hit. Highlight it. Do not scroll to what you missed. Stay on the line you hit. Count to five. That line is yours.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Before the skip-level starts, sit still for thirty seconds. Feel the chair under you. Feel your feet. You are a person, not a performance. Enter the room as the first thing.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Someone above you took credit. Your throat tightens. Do not send the email yet. Write it. Save it as a draft. Then write one sentence: what you built. Keep that sentence. Delete nothing.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Ask one person on your team a direct question today. Listen to their full answer. Do not redirect. Do not fix. Notice: they trusted you enough to answer. That trust is data about you.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
The hierarchy feels unstable. Write three things you know how to do that no reorg removes. Not titles. Skills. Concrete ones. Read them. These are portable. These are yours.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
At the end of today, before you close the laptop, finish this sentence in writing: "Today I held the line on." One sentence. No list. Shut the screen. That sentence is enough.
