

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: RATING-REALITY
---
I got "needs improvement" on my performance review. In a company where the top rating is "exceeds expectations," that lands you below average. I know my work is good. My team's numbers are strong. I delivered on every commitment. And yet: needs improvement. The reframe isn't that the review is wrong—it's that my value in this place is being measured by something I didn't understand. My chest tightened reading it. My hands were cold. For a week I questioned whether I'm actually competent at this or whether I've been faking it the whole time. The review says one thing. My nervous system heard something else. Your worth here is in doubt.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: EXPECTATION-FAILURE
---
My headcount was reduced. I was supposed to manage a team of seven and they're reducing me to five. That's the company's way of saying "you don't need as many resources to do this job." Translation: you're not valuable enough to warrant a bigger org. The reframe isn't that budget cuts are random—they often are business-driven. It's that I was expecting to grow and expand and instead I'm being contracted. My sense of trajectory shifted. This was supposed to be the beginning of something bigger. Instead it's a signal that they're rightsizing what they think I can actually handle. My hands know the difference between "we're cutting costs" and "we don't think you need this much."

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: SELF-EVIDENCE
---
I can feel it in my nervous system: I'm not the person I thought I was. I'm not secure enough, not confident enough, not visionary enough. The evidence is accumulating. The review, the head count reduction, the promotion that went to someone else. My gut is interpreting this coherently: you overestimated yourself. My shoulders are pulled inward. My breath is shallow. The body doesn't distinguish between real evidence and the story I'm telling about the evidence. It just registers: threat to self-image. I'm now actively doubting my core competence, not just in this moment, but fundamentally.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: ACCUMULATED-VERDICT
---
It's been three years since the review. The promotion didn't materialize. The headcount didn't come back. I've built something good in my current role but I'm past the point of growth here. The reframe isn't that I should move on—I've thought about it. It's that staying in a place where I know the verdict is "good enough but not great" has eroded something fundamental about how I see myself. I'm capable. My work is solid. But I'm not exceptional here. That gap between what I thought I was and what I'm being told I am lives in my chest. I'm learning to occupy the smaller version of myself. The cost is knowing I've accepted it.
