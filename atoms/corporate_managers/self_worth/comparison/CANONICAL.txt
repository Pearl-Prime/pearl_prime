## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David watches a peer handle self-worth with apparent ease. The comparison fires hot — David struggles while others seem to navigate the same terrain effortlessly. Chest tight. Hands cold. The gap between David's experience and the peer's performance feels like proof of deficiency. Then something cracks open. David catches a glimpse behind the peer's composure — a trembling hand, a tight jaw, a voice that almost breaks. The ease was armor. The frame shifts: David was comparing a private struggle to a public performance. Chest loosens The comparison mechanism was measuring the wrong dimension. Both people carry the weight. One hides it better.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's self-worth reaches a breaking point. The comparison has been running for so long and so hard that the body refuses to continue. Hands grip the desk. Face burns. David stands in the wreckage of a metric system that collapsed under its own weight — measuring against others until the measurement consumed all the energy that should have gone to the actual work. Then the person David has been comparing against reveals their own collapse. The carefully maintained exterior crumbles. David's chest cracks open. The comparison was between two performances of adequacy, not between adequacy and inadequacy. Both were performing. Both were breaking. The frame shatters because the person who seemed to have it all together was held together by the same kind of wire. Nobody wins the comparison because nobody was competing. They were all just surviving.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah encounters the familiar trigger — a peer's success, a visible achievement, the data that usually fires the comparison engine. The comparison activates. Chest tight. Sarah notices it, acknowledges it, and returns attention to the work in front of them. The peer's achievement does not determine Sarah's value. Sarah's value comes from the work Sarah does, the decisions Sarah makes, the life Sarah has built. Chest stays open The comparison still fires. Sarah no longer builds an identity around its reports. Sarah is a person who does their own work well. The peer does their own work well. Both exist. Neither diminishes the other.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The maximum test arrives. Sarah's self-worth situation reaches its most intense moment alongside a peer who is handling the same situation differently and, by every visible measure, better. The comparison fires at full intensity. Jaw clenches. Sarah feels it all — the inadequacy, the envy, the deficit — and acts anyway. Acts from Sarah's own understanding, Sarah's own values, Sarah's own way of handling this. The outcome is not the peer's outcome. It is Sarah's outcome. Imperfect. Real. Earned. Stomach drops. Sarah stands in the aftermath with the comparison still running its calculations and the calculations no longer matter. Sarah did the work. The work stands on its own. It does not need to be better than anyone else's to be valid. Hands are warm The comparison has been permanently reframed: information, not identity.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David catches the comparison operating in real time within self-worth. The comparison fires before the thought completes — automatic, pre-rational, faster than analysis. Chest crushes. David watches the machinery: a neutral data point enters the system and exits as a deficit. The peer's action becomes evidence of David's inadequacy. The conversion happens in milliseconds. Hands shake. The protective logic is visible now: if David tracks who is better, David can never be surprised by falling short. But the tracking IS the falling short. The comparison creates the deficit it claims to measure. The pattern persists because the comparison feels like assessment when it is actually assault.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/self_worth/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David lies awake at night and the comparison runs its full program within self-worth. Every peer. Every gap. Every dimension on which David falls short. The comparison has become autonomous — it needs no trigger, no input, no event. It generates its own material from memory and projection. Chest tight. David sees the full architecture in the dark: the comparison is not a response to external data. It is an internal program that creates its own data. It selects the peers. It chooses the dimensions. It constructs the gaps. The entire comparison is self-authored. Hands cold. The pattern persists because the comparison feels like it is about other people when it is entirely about David's relationship with David's own adequacy. Other people are just the raw material. The deficiency is pre-existing. The comparison just gives it a face.
---
