Sarah sits with seventeen financial obligations on her kitchen table. Mortgage statement. Car loan. Student loan. Credit card. Insurance. Utilities. Tuition. Each one is a piece of paper demanding money she has. She has the money. The problem is not the money. The problem is the seventeen pieces of paper creating seventeen simultaneous demands on her attention. Her chest is concrete. Then she stacks them by due date. The stack reorganizes from chaos to sequence. Only three are due this week. The frame cracks. The overwhelm was spatial — all the bills on the table at once. The reality is temporal — three this week, four next week, the rest after. She can pay three bills. She cannot face seventeen. Same money. Different arrangement. Her hands warm as she writes three checks.

莎拉坐在廚房餐桌前，面對著十七項財務義務。房貸對帳單。車貸。學貸。信用卡。保險。水電費。學費。每一張紙，都在要求她付出她手上其實有的錢。錢，她是有的。問題不在於錢。問題在於，這十七張紙，同時對她的注意力提出了十七項要求。她的胸口像水泥一樣沉重。接著，她把這些帳單按照到期日排序疊好。這疊紙，從混亂重新排列成了順序。這星期只有三張要到期。那個框架就此裂開。這份不堪負荷，原本是空間性的——所有帳單同時攤在桌上。而現實，其實是時間性的——這星期三張，下星期四張，其餘的往後。她可以付得起三張帳單。她沒辦法同時面對十七張。同樣的錢。不同的排列方式。她的雙手在寫下三張支票時，漸漸回暖。
