Linda looked at her credit card statement this morning. The mortgage payment is due. The car insurance just went up. Her daughter needs new glasses. Her mother's medical bills are coming again. All of it hits at once. Not the individual bills. Linda can handle individual bills. It's the convergence that makes her chest tight. Too many things demanding money at the same time. She makes good money. Should be fine. But "should be fine" and "fine" are different things. She has enough income to cover everything but not enough margin to breathe. She's spending the next three weeks mentally shuffling bills. What can wait? What's essential? The shuffling is exhausting. The money is there. The security somehow isn't.
