David checks his company's stock price before breakfast. His compensation is partly equity and every dip feels personal. His chest tightens. He watches the pre-market numbers and constructs a financial narrative from the ticker. Down two percent — is the company struggling? Up one percent — temporary relief. He has been doing this for three years. Then he calculates: his daily stock check has never changed his financial outcome. Not once. The stock vests on a schedule. The price at breakfast does not affect the price at vesting. He is monitoring something he cannot influence on a timeline that does not matter. The frame cracks. The watcher is consuming his mornings for zero financial benefit. His jaw loosens. He deletes the stock widget from his phone.

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's company announces restructuring and the watcher goes to maximum financial alert. He monitors every internal communication for signals about compensation changes. He tracks his manager's calendar for meetings with HR. He refreshes the company's earnings call transcript seventeen times. His body is locked in financial surveillance mode — chest tight, hands cold, jaw grinding. Three months pass. No compensation changes. No layoffs. The restructuring affected a different division entirely. The watcher spent three months monitoring a financial threat to his income that never existed. His chest cracks. He lost three months of his life to financial vigilance about something that was never going to happen. The frame shatters: the watcher does not distinguish between relevant and irrelevant financial signals. Every organizational change triggers the same financial threat response. He has been financially vigilant about things that have nothing to do with his finances. The watcher is blind to relevance.

## EMBODIMENT v01
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David checks the stock price once a quarter, on vesting day. The rest of the time, the widget is gone. His chest still tightens during earnings season — the watcher wants data, wants signals, wants to scan. He lets it want. He does his work. The stock does what the stock does. His compensation follows the schedule regardless of his surveillance. His hands are warm on his keyboard. He is a person who manages his financial information intake instead of being consumed by it. The watcher fires during market drops. He does not check. The drop is priced in by the time he would see it anyway. His financial life is managed by structure, not by vigilance. The structure works better.

## EMBODIMENT v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_stress/watcher/embodiment/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The market crashes. Twenty percent in a week. David's retirement account drops by six figures overnight. The watcher lunges for the trading app. David does not open it. He calls his financial advisor. The advisor says: "Stay the course. This is temporary." David stays the course. The watcher screams for three weeks. David does not open the app for three weeks. By week four, the market recovers half the loss. By quarter's end, most of it is back. David's inaction — the hardest thing the watcher has ever endured — was the correct financial decision. The watcher said surveillance would save him. Restraint saved him. His hands are warm. He is a person who can sit through a financial storm without watching. The watching would have produced panic-selling. The not-watching produced recovery. He trusts the not-watching now. The watcher has been permanently demoted from financial advisor to noise.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_stress/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David traces the watcher's financial surveillance pattern. It monitors three categories: income security (is his job safe), expense stability (are costs rising), and market performance (are his investments growing). Each category generates multiple daily checks. Income security: scanning for signals at work. Expense stability: reviewing bank transactions. Market performance: checking portfolio values. His chest is tight. The watcher runs three surveillance operations simultaneously, twelve hours a day. The pattern persists because financial security has three dimensions and the watcher monitors all three continuously. One dimension calming creates anxiety about another. Secure income does not calm expense anxiety. Low expenses do not calm market anxiety. The watcher can always find a dimension to worry about.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_stress/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David's financial watcher has damaged his health. The constant cortisol of financial surveillance — chest tight twelve hours a day, jaw clenching during market hours, hands cold during earning season — has produced measurable physical effects. High blood pressure. Grinding teeth. Chronic tension headaches. His doctor says: "Your stress is creating medical bills that are adding to your financial burden." The pattern has become literally expensive. The watcher's financial surveillance is generating medical costs that worsen his financial position. His chest aches with the recognition: the watcher is not free. It runs on his body and his body is sending invoices. The medical bills from stress-related conditions now exceed any financial loss the watcher has ever prevented. He is paying more for the surveillance than the surveillance could ever save. The pattern persists because the watcher does not account for its own costs. It monitors his finances while destroying his health and his health bills destroy his finances and the watcher monitors the destruction and generates more stress. He is the patient and the disease.
