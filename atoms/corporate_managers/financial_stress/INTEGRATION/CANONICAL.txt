

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: MARKET-MISALIGNMENT
---
My salary is $140K and the market is hiring for this role at $170K. I know this because I looked. I know because someone at another company who does my job told me over coffee. The reframe isn't that I should feel grateful for what I have—it's that I'm being underpaid and I can't do anything about it without risking the stability that feels like the only thing holding together my life right now. My stomach tightens when I think about it. I have kids' tuition coming. The mortgage is fixed. The difference between $140K and $170K is the difference between OK and fine, and I'm stuck at OK. The equity didn't vest. I'm not leaving because leaving is expensive and I don't have a cushion.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: LATERAL-COST
---
The lateral move sounded good: same level, different division, broader exposure, more responsibility. What it actually meant was relocating our family which cost $40K in moving expenses, schools had to change, my wife's job was flexible but not remote. We're OK—we're not broke. But we're not comfortable anymore. The buffer we had evaporated. The reframe is that lateral moves aren't free. They look like sideways steps on paper but they're actually sideways and down financially. My jaw is tight when I think about the timeline to rebuild that cushion. Five years, maybe. I'm 47 years old and I reset my timeline.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: INVISIBLE-PRESSURE
---
Nobody knows I'm worried. I present financial reports to my team. I make decisions about resource allocation. I look stable from the outside. Inside, I'm calculating: If I got laid off tomorrow, how long would we last? Six months, maybe seven if we cut discretionary spend. My hands shake slightly when I think about that timeline. A colleague just got a severance package and my first thought wasn't "that's generous," it was "is that enough?" The reframe is that financial stress at this income level is invisible. I'm not supposed to be afraid. I have healthcare. I have a steady paycheck. But the math is tighter than it looks, and I'm acutely aware of it. My body carries this constant low-level dread.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: CHOICE-CONSTRAINT
---
I can't leave. That's the actual constraint. The market gap between what I'm being paid and what's available elsewhere is real, but leaving costs: new hire bonuses don't happen at this level, relocation would disrupt my family again, we'd lose institutional knowledge and relationships. It's rational to stay. It's also a trap. The reframe is that I'm not free to move, which means I'm not free to negotiate here either. I'm stuck. My hands know this. I go to sleep calculating numbers and wake up still calculating. The financial stress isn't about being poor. It's about feeling trapped.
