## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your chest tightens before the budget call. Press both feet flat on the floor. Name one number you control. Write it down. That number is your anchor. Everything else is noise until the call ends.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Pull up your bank account. Look at the actual balance. Say the number aloud. Do not round it. Do not soften it. Now write one expense you cut this week. One. Stop there.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before the reorg announcement lands, breathe in for four counts. Hold for four. Out for four. Your salary exists right now. Name that fact. Repeat it once. Then open the email.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Write "financial pressure" at the top of a blank page. Below it, draw a line. Left side: what you control. Right side: what you do not. Fill the left side only. Ignore the right.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Your jaw is clenched. Unclench it. Now list your fixed monthly obligations. Numbers only. No commentary. Total them. Compare that total to your current income. That gap is the real problem. Everything else is projection.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Set a timer for three minutes. Write every financial fear in your head. Do not edit. When the timer stops, stop writing. Circle one item that has a concrete next action. Do only that action today.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Before your skip-level meeting, notice your shoulders. Drop them. Remind yourself your paycheck cleared this week. Say that fact silently. Now walk in. The money fear and the meeting are separate problems.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Pick one bill due this month. Open it. Read the exact amount. Do not estimate. Write a payment date beside it. Close the tab. Your nervous system calms when vague becomes specific.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Stand up. Shake your hands loose at your sides. Breathe out hard through your mouth. Sit back down. Now name one financial decision you delayed this week. Schedule it for tomorrow morning. Ten minutes. Done.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Your performance review cycle starts soon. Separate the money fear from the performance fear. Write them on two separate lines. Address one today. Leave the other for tomorrow. Mixing them doubles the dread.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Notice where stress lives in your body right now. Chest. Stomach. Throat. Place one hand there. Breathe into that spot for five slow counts. Then open your budget spreadsheet. Body first. Numbers second.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Write down your monthly take-home pay. Underline it. That number is a fact. Now write your worst-case financial fear. Look at both lines. Measure the actual distance between them. Most fears live in that gap, not in reality.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Before your next one-on-one with your director, drink a full glass of water. Swallow slowly. Your financial anxiety and your manager's opinion are two different threats. Name them separately. Walk in carrying only the one that belongs in that room.
---
