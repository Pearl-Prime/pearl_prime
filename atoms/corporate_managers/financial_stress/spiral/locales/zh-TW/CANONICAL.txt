莎拉 spirals about the car repair estimate. $3,200. The spiral begins: if the car costs this much now it will cost more later, which means she should buy a new car, but a new car means payments, and payments mean less savings, and less savings means retirement delays, and delayed retirement means working until seventy, and working until seventy means — she catches loop five. A $3,200 repair became working until seventy in four logical-sounding leaps. She writes the number on paper: $3,200. Her emergency fund covers it with room to spare. The frame cracks. The spiral took a solvable problem and projected it into a life catastrophe. The repair is $3,200. Not $3,200 plus working until seventy. Just $3,200. She authorizes the repair. Her hands are steady. The spiral protests that she should think bigger. She refuses to think bigger than the actual number.
