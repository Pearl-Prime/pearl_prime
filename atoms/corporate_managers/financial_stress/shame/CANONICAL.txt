Kevin notices the shame surfaces most intensely around specific financial actions: using a coupon, checking the price before ordering, declining a social invitation because of cost. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v07

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin realizes the financial shame is generational. His father was ashamed. His grandfather was ashamed. Three generations of men who could not talk about money without the heat rising in their faces. Kevin's financial shame is not personal. It is inherited. It was installed before he could choose it. He carries his grandfather's shame about the Depression, his father's shame about the factory closure, his own shame about the business failure. Three generations of financial trauma wearing the same mask. His chest is hollow. The pattern persists because it is older than he is. Fighting his own shame is fighting his father's and his grandfather's simultaneously. The shame is not about his financial decisions. It is about being a man in a lineage of men who were taught that money problems equal manhood problems. He did not choose this equation. He inherited it. The recognition does not dissolve the shame. But it relocates it — from personal failing to cultural wound. The wound is still his to carry. But knowing it is inherited changes how he carries it.
