## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David makes a mistake in handling imposter syndrome and the shame ignites before the consequences arrive. Chest tight. Face burning. The shame does not wait for judgment — it provides its own. David is defective. David should have known better. David is the kind of person who makes this kind of mistake. Then someone witnesses the mistake and responds with: "That happens to everyone." The frame cracks. The shame predicted exile. The witness provided normalization. Fists open The mistake is a mistake. The shame tried to make it an identity. The witness reminded David it is an event.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David is exposed in the context of imposter syndrome. Fully, publicly, undeniably. The thing the shame was guarding — the perceived deficiency at the center — is visible to everyone. Shoulders lock. Throat closes. David waits for annihilation. The room continues. The world does not end. People see the flaw and respond with humanity instead of exile. The shame's prophecy — that exposure equals destruction — detonates against evidence. David stands in the full light of being seen as imperfect and the light does not burn. It illuminates. The frame shatters because the shame lied about the consequences. The consequences are bearable. The shame insisted they would not be. Everything David hid, every performance of competence, every mask — all to prevent a moment that turned out to be survivable. Vision narrows. The shame's power depended on David never testing its predictions. The predictions were just tested. They failed.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah makes a visible mistake in the context of imposter syndrome and does not hide it. Announces it. Names it. Proposes the fix. Hands grip the desk. The shame fires — face warm, stomach tight. Sarah lets it fire. The shame is a reflex, not a verdict. The mistake is corrected. The team moves on. Nobody died. Nobody was exiled. Jaw is loose Sarah is a person who can be wrong without being defective. The distinction lives in practice now, not just in understanding.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The most public failure arrives in Sarah's imposter syndrome domain. Visible. Documented. Discussed. Sarah's shame fires its nuclear program — face burning, chest imploding, the desire to disappear. Hands cold. Sarah does not disappear. Stands in the visibility. Addresses what happened with honesty. Does not perform composure. The voice cracks. The hands shake. The words come out imperfect and true. Jaw clenches. The room responds to truth, not to performance. Sarah is a person who failed publicly and remained publicly present. The shame says this should have destroyed Sarah. Sarah is not destroyed. Sarah is more visible, more real, more trusted than before the failure. Jaw is sore but intact Shame predicted annihilation. Sarah produced authenticity.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David catches the shame's trigger mechanism around imposter syndrome. The shame does not respond to actual failure — it responds to the possibility of being perceived as failing. Vision narrows. A meeting where David might not know the answer. An email that might contain criticism. A review that might reveal a gap. The shame fires on "might." It does not wait for evidence. It preempts evidence by generating its own conviction of inadequacy. Chest hollow. The pattern persists because the shame is prophylactic — it punishes David before the world can, creating the illusion that self-punishment prevents external punishment. But self-punishment does not prevent anything. It just arrives first.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David traces the shame about imposter syndrome to its origin. The shame was not born in this role or this career. It was born in a moment — specific, remembered, formative — when a younger David was seen as insufficient and believed it. The professional shame is a professional costume on a personal wound. Hands grip the desk. Every activation at work is the same activation from that original moment. The boss's frown is the parent's disappointment. The missed target is the failed test. The professional review is the childhood judgment. Face burns. The pattern persists because the shame is pre-professional. It arrived before the job. It will outlast the job. Fighting it at the professional level is fighting the symptom. The wound is underneath, in a younger body, in an earlier room, where someone who mattered said or implied that David was not enough. David's eyes are wet. The career just gave the old wound a new vocabulary.
---
