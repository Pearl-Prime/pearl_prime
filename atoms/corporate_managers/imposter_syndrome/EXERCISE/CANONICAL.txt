## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before the skip-level meeting, your chest tightens. Press both palms flat on the desk. Count four slow breaths. Name one decision this quarter that held. That decision is real. Walk in carrying it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Pull up your last performance review. Find one specific strength listed. Read it aloud. Not in your head. Aloud. Your voice makes it concrete. That assessment came from someone above you. It is evidence, not flattery.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before the reorg announcement lands, write three things your team built this year. Actual deliverables. Not feelings. Numbers if possible. Keep that list open on your screen. When doubt spikes, read line one.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Someone challenges your call in the meeting. Your jaw tightens. Do not defend yet. Breathe once through the nose. State the decision and the reason in one sentence. Short. Factual. Then stop talking.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
After managing up today, notice where tension lives in your body. Shoulders. Throat. Gut. Name the location out loud. Place one hand there. Breathe into that spot for four counts. Release the hand. Move.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Write the name of one person you promoted or developed. Below it, write what they can do now that they could not do before you managed them. That outcome belongs to your leadership. Own it fully.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
The performance cycle opens. Before you read your ratings, list three problems you solved this year without being asked. Write them by hand. Slow down. Let the pen finish each word. Then open the review.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Your team looks to you for certainty during the reorg. Stand up from your chair. Both feet on the floor. Roll your shoulders back once. Speak one true thing you do know. One is enough.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Identify the last time a senior leader took your recommendation. Write the date and the outcome. Tape it near your monitor. When the fraud feeling hits, look at it. That moment already happened.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Before a difficult one-on-one with a direct report, breathe out fully. Empty. Then inhale. Ask yourself one question silently: what does this person need from me right now. Answer it. Enter the room with that answer only.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Count the decisions you made this week that no one reversed. Three. Five. More. Each one is a vote of operational trust. Write the number down. Circle it. That number is not nothing.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
After the skip-level ends, find a private space. Sit down. Notice your heartbeat. Let it slow. Replay one moment in that meeting where you spoke clearly. Hold that moment for ten full seconds. Then stand.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Your calendar fills with meetings you did not request. That is not punishment. Count how many people needed you today. Write the number. Beside it write: they came to me. Read both lines once. Close the notebook.
