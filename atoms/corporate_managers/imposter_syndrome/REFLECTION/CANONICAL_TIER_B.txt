

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I got promoted because someone believed in me before I believed in myself. Now I carry that debt. Every success feels like it's borrowed from the person who took a chance on me. The mechanism is that you're always playing a role someone else cast you in. Performing competence rather than actually competent. I'm responsible for hiring and developing talent. I see the imposter syndrome in others instantly. They're overqualified, genuinely skilled, but caught in the same loop of self-doubt. What I don't know how to do is immunize them against it, because I can't even cure it in myself.
