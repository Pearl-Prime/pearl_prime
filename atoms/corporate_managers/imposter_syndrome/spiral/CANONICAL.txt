---
David questions his abilities and spirals: I'm not qualified means people will discover means I'll be fired means I've failed. His chest is heavy. Sarah questions herself and spirals: I'm a fraud means someone will find out means I'll be exposed means I've failed. Her hands are cold. Kevin questions abilities and spirals: I'm not cut for this means I should quit before getting fired. His shoulders are tight. Anthony questions competence and spirals: I don't belong means the board will find out means I'm done. His throat is tight. Tanya questions herself and spirals: I'm a fraud means I'll be exposed means I'm out. Her stomach is heavy. Spirals generate exit narratives. David stops the spiral: "I question myself. That's normal. I'm still competent." His chest unclenches. Sarah interrupts: "I doubt myself. I'm not a fraud." Her hands warm. Kevin breaks in: "Questioning doesn't mean disqualified." His shoulders release. Anthony pauses: "Self-doubt doesn't determine my value." His throat releases. Tanya stops: "I'm not a fraud." Her stomach settles. Self-doubt doesn't need to spiral into exit.

David enters the spiral about imposter syndrome. First loop: the situation is concerning. Second loop: the situation is dire. Third loop: the situation is irreversible. It was decorating the situation with fear until the fear became the situation. Jaw releases The fact is manageable. The fiction was not.
