## EXERCISE v01
---

---
The reorg email sits unopened. Put both feet flat on the floor. Press down. Feel the floor hold you. Open the email now. Read only what is written. Not what it might mean.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Your skip-level is in two hours. Write one sentence: what your manager actually said last week. One sentence. Fact only. Set the paper down. Breathe out fully. Return to that one sentence.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
The spiral starts. Grip your pen. Hard. Feel the pressure in your fingers. Release it slowly. Name the last task you completed today. That task happened. It was real.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
You are rehearsing the performance review conversation again. Stop mid-sentence. Stand up. Walk to a window. Name one thing outside it. Come back to your chair. Sit down differently than before.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Your team is watching you for signals. Take one slow breath through your nose. Count to four. Hold for two. Out for four. Your face settles. They read your face.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Write down the worst outcome you keep imagining. One line. Now write what you control in that outcome. Circle only the controllable part. Look at the circle. Work from there.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
The meeting replays. Again. Press your back into your chair. Feel where the chair pushes back. Name that pressure. Stay there ten seconds. The meeting is over. This pressure is now.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Before the next calendar block opens, close your laptop screen. Look at your hands. Open and close them once. Ask: what do I know for certain right now. Write that. Only that.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
You are building a case for a decision not yet made. Stop typing. Put your palms flat on the desk. Feel the surface temperature. Cold or warm. Name it out loud. Start one task instead.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
The team uncertainty is loud in your chest. Place one hand there. Don't move it. Breathe into that pressure three times. Now name one person on your team. Think of one thing they do well.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
You are running three conversations in your head simultaneously. Pick one. Write its name at the top of a page. Close the other two. Deal with the one you wrote. Only that one.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Caught between managing up and managing down. Plant your elbows on the desk. Rest your head in your hands. Ten seconds. Lift your head. Name the next person you speak to. Prepare only for them.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
The review cycle closes Friday. Write today's date. Look at it. Count the days remaining on your fingers. Put your hand down. Open the document in front of you. Edit one line. Then another.
