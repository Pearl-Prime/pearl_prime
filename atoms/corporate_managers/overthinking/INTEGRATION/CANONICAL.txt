## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The conversation already happened."
---
You're replaying the meeting again. The words you said when you told her the project was shifting. The pause before she answered. The email she sent after. You're watching it frame by frame like it's still happening, still changeable. Your shoulders know the difference — they're tight, pressing down into the chair. Your jaw too. Both braced like the moment's still open.

It's not. The meeting is done. Three days behind you. What's still happening is the replay. That's a different event — one that lives in your nervous system, not in the conference room.

Notice your feet on the floor. The meeting ended. The decision stands whether you replay it or not. She's moved on. She's probably written three emails since. You're the one replaying it. That's real. And it's happening now, not then.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Nobody gets paid for this analysis."
---
Count the hours you've spent replaying that one conversation since Tuesday. Not the actual conversation — the replay. The versions where you said it differently. The ones where you caught her expression. The scenario where she's thinking less of you. The loop runs on its own time.

This costs something. Not the decision itself. The constant re-auditing. The part of your attention that never left the room. The emails you wrote slower because part of you was still standing in front of her. The decision you second-guessed in the shower at 6am.

It's hours. It's attention. It's sleep that didn't settle because your brain was still managing the moment. None of this appears in your productivity metrics. None of it shows in what you actually delivered.

What you delivered is still what happened. The replay cost you, not them.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if it doesn't matter to her?"
---
You've analyzed her face for three days now. The pause, the word choice in the email, the tone shift. You're building an entire story about what she thinks of you based on a reaction that lasted two seconds.

But what if she was just processing? What if she was thinking about her own afternoon, her own deadlines? What if she's forgotten about the meeting entirely and moved to whatever's next?

You don't actually know what happened inside her. You only know what happened inside you — the replay, the analysis, the story you built about her story about you.

What if the pause meant nothing at all?
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "She's probably forgotten by now."
---
If someone watching you from outside could see what's happening right now, they'd see a manager who made a clear decision in a meeting. A difficult decision, yes. Communicated directly. Then moved on to the next thing.

They'd also see someone who came back to that moment four times today. Who's analyzing the same two-second pause like it contains data it doesn't. Who's wearing the conversation in their shoulders while the person being thought about is probably thinking about something else entirely.

She made a face. You made a story. The story has been running longer than the conversation did.

She's probably already forgotten.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You made the right call. You're still here."
---
Three months ago you were replaying a different conversation. You survived that one. You survived the one before it. Every difficult conversation you've had as a manager has moved downstream — people adjusted, projects continued, the company kept running.

The anxiety of the moment felt permanent then too. This one does now. But you're still here. The organization is still here. The people you manage are still here, still working, still moving forward in ways that had nothing to do with the moment you're replaying.

You made the call that needed making. It was imperfect. It was real. You're still the manager. The team is still there. The work keeps moving.

The decision was then. You are now.
