
## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C2
Every decision you make affects someone's career. You feel the weight of it. So you think it through. Then you think it through again. The rumination is not a personality trait. It is the cost of carrying outcomes you cannot fully control for people who are trusting you to control them anyway.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C1
The decision was made on Tuesday. By Thursday you are still rerunning the conversation in your head. Looking for the move that would have preempted the pushback. The replay is not learning. It is the part of you that wanted no friction trying to invent a version of the past where the friction did not happen.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C5
Try this: when the loop starts about a decision already made, write down the inputs that were available at the time. Not the outcome. The inputs. Most rumination is the present judging the past with information the past did not have. Naming the inputs returns the decision to the moment it was actually made in.
