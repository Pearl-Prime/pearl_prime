## SCENE v01
---
---
The calendar invite lands at 8:47 a.m. Skip-level. No agenda. Your stomach drops before your brain catches up. You set your coffee down. The ceramic clicks against your desk. Around you, keyboards keep moving. No one else checks their phone. You open the invite again, read the same four words, close it. Your jaw tightens. You roll your chair back an inch, then stop. Nowhere to go.

## SCENE v02
---
---
Marcus stands at the whiteboard in the empty conference room. Reorg chart. His handwriting. Three boxes crossed out in red marker. He caps the pen slowly. His own name sits one level lower than yesterday. The fluorescent light hums above him. He traces the new reporting line with one finger, not pressing, just following. The marker squeaks faintly under his touch. He steps back. Stares.
---

## SCENE v03
---
---
The performance review portal opens at midnight. Priya refreshes it at 12:04 a.m., sitting at her kitchen table in the dark. The screen brightens her face. Exceeds expectations — three of her direct reports. Meets expectations — her. She reads her rating twice. The refrigerator kicks on behind her. She closes the laptop. Sits in the hum and the dark.
---

## SCENE v04
---
---
You take the long route back from the all-hands. Down the stairwell, not the elevator. Your hand drags along the painted cinder block. Cold. Rough. Your VP just announced a restructuring. Effective Q2. Your team's function unnamed. Three flights down, you stop on the landing. Fluorescent light flickers once. You breathe through your nose. Your chest doesn't fully expand.
---

## SCENE v05
---
---
Devon's one-on-ones keep getting moved. Third week running. He opens the Slack thread, types "no worries," deletes it, types "understood," sends that instead. He pushes back from his desk. His shoulders sit high, near his ears. He doesn't lower them. The open office moves around him — someone laughs by the kitchen. Devon looks at his screen. The cursor blinks.
---

## SCENE v06
---
---
The feedback form asks you to rate your own performance. Scale of one to five. You hover over four. Your hand doesn't move. Down the hall, your director's door is shut. It's been shut since Tuesday. You click three. Stare at it. Change it back to four. Your throat feels tight and dry. You hit submit before you change it again.
