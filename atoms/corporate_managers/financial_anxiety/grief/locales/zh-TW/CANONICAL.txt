These narratives offer a direct map for your own journey:

*   **Validating the "Ghost" Narrative:** Like David, Sarah, and Kevin, you may be grieving a version of your career or financial trajectory that didn’t happen (e.g., the pure engineer path, the faster wealth accumulation). This aligns with your work on **distinguishing genuine vs. imagined suffering** and **protecting your interiority**.

### Key Takeaway for Your Practice
The transition from **Mechanism Proof** to **Embodiment** is not about eliminating grief. It is about **changing the relationship to grief**.

*   **Before:** Grief is a signal that something is wrong with *me* or my *future*. It demands I fix the past (which is impossible), so I freeze.
*   **After:** Grief is a signal that I valued something deeply. It invites me to honor that value by building wisely with what I *actually* have. I move.

For you, this might mean acknowledging the grief over the "pure engineer" identity or the "financial security" you expected, and then asking: **"What does a financially wise, emotionally sustainable life look like for the person I am *today*, not the person I planned to be?"**

The body knows the answer. When the plan matches the reality, the hands warm up.
