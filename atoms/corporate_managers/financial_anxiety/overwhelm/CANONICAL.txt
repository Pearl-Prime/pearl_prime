## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah faces the financial overwhelm: mortgage, car payments, tuition, insurance, retirement contributions, emergency fund, college savings. Every number demands attention simultaneously. Her chest is concrete. She cannot prioritize because every financial obligation feels equally urgent. Then her financial advisor says something simple: "Which of these, if missed, would cause immediate harm?" The question cuts through the noise. Mortgage. Insurance. Everything else has a grace period. The frame cracks. The overwhelm treated every financial obligation as equally urgent. They are not. Some are immediate. Some are deferrable. Some are aspirational. Her shoulders drop. She is not facing one massive financial problem. She is facing six different problems at six different urgency levels. She can sequence them. The overwhelm dissolves when the priorities become visible.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah loses her job and the financial overwhelm becomes existential. Every number turns red simultaneously. She sits at her kitchen table with bills spread out and her body goes into freeze. Chest locked. Vision tunneled. Cannot think. Cannot plan. Cannot even cry. The overwhelm has exceeded her nervous system's processing capacity. Then her sister sits down next to her and picks up one bill. Just one. And says: "Let us start here." The frame shatters. Sarah was trying to solve all of the financial crisis simultaneously. Her sister modeled solving it one bill at a time. The overwhelm was the product of scope, not severity. Each individual bill is addressable. The collection of bills is paralyzing. Sarah picks up the next bill. Her hands shake. But she is moving. One bill at a time. The overwhelm did not lift. She just stopped trying to hold all of it at once.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah reviews her finances monthly instead of daily. She has a system: one hour, first Saturday, all accounts. Between reviews, she does not check. Her chest tightens between reviews — the old overwhelm wants constant attention. She lets it want. The monthly review tells her everything she needs to know. The numbers are organized. The priorities are clear. She makes one adjustment per month. Her hands are steady on the spreadsheet. She is a person who manages finances with a system instead of being managed by financial anxiety. The overwhelm still surges when unexpected bills arrive. She adds them to next Saturday's review. They wait. She lives. The financial overwhelm is contained in a box one hour wide and one day per month. The rest of her time belongs to her.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The genuine financial emergency lands — medical bill, job change, market downturn hitting simultaneously. Sarah opens her system. She triages. Medical bill: payment plan. Job change: severance covers three months. Market: do not touch, wait for recovery. She makes three decisions in thirty minutes. Her chest is tight. Her hands are cool. But she is functioning. The old Sarah would have frozen under the simultaneous weight. This Sarah has a system and the system works even when her nervous system does not. She calls her financial advisor. She updates her budget. She sleeps. Not well. But she sleeps. The financial overwhelm is real and managed. She is a person who navigates financial emergencies with structure instead of drowning in simultaneous panic.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah sees the pattern: financial overwhelm comes from treating every financial obligation as equally urgent. Her brain presents all financial data simultaneously — mortgage and grocery budget and retirement contributions and insurance premiums — with equal weight. The overwhelm is a display problem, not a financial problem. Her actual finances have structure. Her brain refuses to see the structure. Her chest is heavy. The pattern persists because the overwhelm creates urgency that prevents the sorting that would relieve the urgency. She cannot organize her finances because the disorganization is too overwhelming to face. She needs to organize in order to stop being overwhelmed. The loop is perfect.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah wakes at 3 AM and every financial number she knows runs through her brain simultaneously. Mortgage: $2,847. Car payment: $412. Insurance: $890. Tuition: $4,200. Groceries: $1,100. The numbers stack without context — no income figure, no timeline, no prioritization. Just raw numbers piling up. Her chest is crushed under the weight of numbers that, organized, are manageable. At 3 AM they are just numbers and the numbers add up to doom. She sees the full architecture: the overwhelm is a presentation problem masquerading as a financial problem. By day, with a spreadsheet, these numbers tell a story of a managed life. At 3 AM, without structure, they tell a story of imminent collapse. The pattern persists because her nervous system processes financial data without the cognitive tools that make it intelligible. Raw data plus fear equals overwhelm. Organized data plus reason equals a budget. She has both. The overwhelm only shows her the first version.
---
