## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before the skip-level meeting, stop. Place both hands flat on your desk. Feel the surface. Cold or warm. Hard or soft. That desk is real. The catastrophic reorg story is not. Name one actual fact about your budget today. One fact only.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Write your team's headcount on paper. The real number. Not the rumored cuts. Circle it. Set the pen down. Breathe in for four counts. Out for six. Return to the circled number. That is what exists right now.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Pull up last quarter's performance data. Read the first line aloud. Your voice, not your fear's voice. Notice your jaw. Unclench it. Read the number again. Anchor to what already happened. Not what the next review cycle invents.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Stand up from your chair. Press both feet into the floor. Feel your weight. Steady. Name the three budget line items you control directly. Say them out loud. Slow. Each word lands before the next one starts.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Open your calendar. Find one meeting this week you actually own. Not a status update. A decision. Write the word MINE next to it. Close the calendar. Breathe out completely. You have authority somewhere. Start there.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
When the reorg rumors hit your chest, stop moving. Sit down. Put one hand over your sternum. Feel the pressure there. Breathe into that hand for four counts. The pressure is information. It is not a verdict. Stay for thirty seconds.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
List the five people on your team by first name only. Say each name once. Slowly. These are real people. Present. Not headcount in a spreadsheet. Feel the difference in your body between those two framings. Notice it. Do not explain it.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Before opening the email marked urgent from your director, exhale first. Fully. Empty. Now open it. Read the first sentence only. Stop. Breathe in for four counts. Read the second sentence. One sentence at a time. Your nervous system stays regulated. The email gets processed.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Take your monthly budget number. Write it by hand. Not typed. The physical act of writing slows the spiral. Look at what you wrote. Draw a box around it. That box is the boundary of what is real today. Stay inside it.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
After a performance review conversation, find a wall. Press your back against it. Feel the wall hold you. Solid. Breathe out. Name one thing that went well in that meeting. One specific moment. Not a general feeling. A moment.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
When managing-up anxiety spikes before a senior leadership call, look at your hands. Open them. Close them. Open again. Your hands are here. Your voice will work. Say your first agenda point aloud right now. Once. Hear it land.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Check your actual salary deposit from last month. The real number in the account. Confirm it arrived. It did. Write the date it landed. That date is evidence. Evidence outweighs speculation. Set the phone down. Shoulders drop. Breathe once.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
At the end of a high-uncertainty day, sit in your chair before leaving. Do not stand yet. Count three things your team delivered this week. Actual deliverables. Concrete. Say the word done after each one. Done. Done. Done. Then stand.
