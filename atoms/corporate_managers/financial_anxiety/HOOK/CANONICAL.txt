

## HOOK v01
---

---
Marcus is in his home office after midnight, spreadsheet glowing, fingers frozen on the mortgage line — the model says fine and his chest says the floor could drop any quarter.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Marcus refreshes the brokerage tab for the fourth time before the market even opens, coffee going cold, the number unchanged — but the refreshing is the only thing that quiets the hum behind his ribs.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The team hits its targets and the bonus letter still hasn't landed; Marcus reads the same Slack thread three times, calculating mortgage against severance, planning for a layoff that exists only in his head.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
His daughter asks for soccer cleats and Marcus says of course, then sits in the car in the driveway for ten minutes doing the grocery-versus-savings math he swore he'd stop doing.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Payroll clears at 9 a.m. and by 9:04 Marcus has already moved it three places in his mind — the card, the loan, the emergency fund that never feels like enough to deserve the name.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Marcus runs a forty-person budget without blinking, then opens his own checking account and feels the cursor hover, unwilling to look, as if the personal number might bite in a way the company's never does.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The quarterly review goes well; on the drive home Marcus is already rehearsing how he'd explain a salary cut to his wife, a conversation that isn't happening, for a job he isn't losing.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
He keeps a spreadsheet of every fixed cost color-coded by severity, and updating it at midnight feels like control — right up until he closes the laptop and the floor tilts again.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
Marcus watches a colleague pack a desk into a banker's box and spends the rest of the afternoon pricing his own runway: months of expenses, divided by the savings, equals a number that never sits still.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
The mortgage statement arrives and Marcus pays it instantly, early, the way you flinch from a hot stove — then lies awake recomputing how many of these he could cover if the income stopped tomorrow.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
His son leaves the lights on in three rooms and Marcus feels the irritation spike out of all proportion, because it isn't the electric bill, it's the whole invisible ledger pressing on the back of his neck.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
Marcus gets the raise he asked for and the relief lasts about a day before the goalpost slides; now the question isn't whether he can cover the house, it's whether he could cover the house and the what-ifs.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
He stands in the kitchen at 6 a.m. with the credit-card app open, telling himself the balance is fine, that he's being ridiculous, while his thumb refreshes it one more time anyway.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The 401(k) statement shows a good year and Marcus reads only the line that dropped, the way the eye finds the one bruise in the mirror, and the rest of the page might as well be blank.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
Marcus signs off on a million-dollar department spend in a meeting, then sits in his car and hesitates over a fourteen-dollar lunch, the two numbers somehow weighing exactly the same in his hands.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
A vague rumor about restructuring moves through the floor and by lunch Marcus has built an entire severance timeline, mortgage included, for an event no one has confirmed and probably won't.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
He promises his wife they'll book the trip this year, then quietly pushes it another quarter, telling himself it's prudence when really it's the same fear wearing a sensible coat.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
Marcus knows the emergency fund covers six months and still feels the cold drop in his stomach every time a real bill lands, because the number in the account never argues with the number in his chest.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The performance bonus posts and within the hour Marcus has it spent on paper three different cautious ways, none of which involve actually enjoying it, all of which involve bracing for the bottom.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
He opens the mortgage amortization schedule the way other people open bad news, scrolling to the balance, doing the years-remaining math at midnight as if the number might have moved since yesterday.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
Marcus catches himself calculating his net worth in a meeting that has nothing to do with money, the figures running underneath every sentence like a second conversation he can't turn off.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
His kid's tuition invoice arrives and Marcus pays it in full, on time, then spends the evening rebuilding the entire household budget around a job loss that lives only in the worst ten minutes of his day.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
The market dips four percent and Marcus feels it in his jaw before he sees it on the screen, the body keeping its own running tally of every dollar he's afraid to lose.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
Marcus tells his direct report not to worry about the reorg, that her job is safe — advice he is completely unable to take himself as he drives home recomputing his own runway.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
He has the income, the title, the savings, and still wakes at 3 a.m. to the same arithmetic, as if the fear were a fixed monthly cost the spreadsheet forgot to list.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
Marcus declines the offsite dinner he can easily afford, then sits with a strange shame about it, knowing the saying-no had nothing to do with the price and everything to do with the dread.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The bonus is generous and Marcus's first thought is how fast it could be gone, the relief and the bracing arriving in the same breath, neither one willing to let the other finish.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
He keeps the household running on a model that assumes the worst, and the assuming feels responsible right up until he notices he hasn't felt safe in any quarter, good or bad, for years.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
Marcus reads the layoff headline about another company and feels his own stomach drop, plotting the months of cushion, the cost of the house, the exact shape of a fall that isn't coming.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
Late at night he closes the spreadsheet, certain for one quiet second that the numbers are fine — and then the certainty drains out of him before he's even reached the top of the stairs.
---
