### Synthesis: What This Means for Your Pivot

You are not dealing with two separate problems (work burnout and financial anxiety). You are dealing with **one unified nervous system response** to perceived instability, manifested in two domains.

**The Core Pattern:**
> **Hyper-vigilance → False Positives → Over-Functioning → Somatic Exhaustion → Inability to Trust Safety**

**Reframing the Recovery:**
Since you recognize that "recovery requires more than rest or conventional wellness approaches" (from your memories), consider this:

1.  **Stop Trying to Solve the Feeling with Data:** More checking (v06) or more planning (v12) will not soothe the body. The body needs **evidence of safety**, not more information. This means practicing *non-checking* as a somatic exercise. Can you sit with the uncertainty of not knowing the balance for 24 hours? That is the rep that builds trust.
2.  **Decouple Identity from Provision:** You carry the financial load like the emotional load (v10). Ask yourself: *If I stopped carrying the weight of everyone’s safety, who would I be?* Your identity is tied to being the **Stabilizer**. Recovery requires experimenting with being **Vulnerable** or **Unavailable** in small, safe doses.
3.  **Address the Architectural Loop (v09):** Is there a way to reduce the dependency? Not necessarily by quitting, but by **diversifying the source of safety**. This could mean building non-financial reserves (community, skills, health) so that the job is not the *only* pillar holding up your sense of self-worth and survival.
4.  **Name the "Older Threat" (v04):** What is the childhood root of this financial terror? Was there actual scarcity? Or was there emotional scarcity masked as financial prudence? Understanding the origin helps the body realize: *That was then. This is now. The threat is not current.*
