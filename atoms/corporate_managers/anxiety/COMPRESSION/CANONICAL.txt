## COMPRESSION v31
---
compression_family: C1
---
The reorg email hits your inbox and your body knows before your mind does. Chest tightens. Hands cool. Breath shortens. You have not read the email. Your nervous system has already decided what it contains. By the time your eyes process the words, your body has already lived through the layoff, the awkward goodbye, the box of personal items. The email is about a new reporting structure. Your body does not care. Your body already survived the catastrophe. The fact that the catastrophe did not happen is irrelevant to the cortisol now circulating in your bloodstream.
---

## COMPRESSION v32
---
compression_family: C2
---
You prepare for the quarterly review the way other people prepare for surgery. You gather data. You anticipate questions. You rehearse answers to questions no one will ask. You build contingency plans for scenarios that exist only in your imagination. The preparation is not about readiness. The preparation is about control. If you prepare enough, the anxiety tells you, nothing can surprise you. The anxiety is wrong. Something always surprises you. And the surprise undoes all the preparation in a single second.
---

## COMPRESSION v33
---
compression_family: C3
---
Your direct report seems distant this week. Not hostile. Just slightly less engaged. Your mind immediately writes the screenplay. She is leaving. She got an offer. She is already checked out. By Thursday she will give notice. By next month you will be backfilling the role and explaining the attrition to your VP. Your direct report is not leaving. Your direct report has a cold. The spiral does not distinguish between disengagement and nasal congestion.
---

## COMPRESSION v34
---
compression_family: C4
---
You read the same Slack message four times. Not because it is complex. Because each reading generates a new interpretation. The first reading is factual. The second reading finds subtext. The third reading finds threat. The fourth reading confirms the threat that the first three invented. You have spent twelve minutes on a message that required ten seconds. The anxiety converts simple communication into elaborate decoder puzzles.
---

## COMPRESSION v35
---
compression_family: C5
---
You know the exact number of open positions on your team. You know the hiring pipeline. You know the budget. You know the timeline. And still your mind runs the scenario where none of them fill and the work collapses and your team burns out and the attrition cascades and the VP asks what happened. You have the data. The data says the hires are on track. The anxiety says the data is lying.
---

## COMPRESSION v36
---
compression_family: C1
---
The performance review cycle opens and your anxiety treats it as an indictment rather than a process. Every self-assessment question becomes a trap. Rate your impact. Describe your development areas. The questions are standard. Your answers are careful. Not honest. Careful. Because honesty might reveal the thing you suspect: that you are not performing at the level the role requires. The review is a mirror. The anxiety insists the mirror will show someone insufficient.
---

## COMPRESSION v37
---
compression_family: C2
---
A peer gets praise in the all-hands. Public recognition. Applause. Your first feeling is not happiness for them. Your first feeling is the mathematical calculation: their recognition reduces the available pool of recognition for you. The recognition is not finite. You know this. The anxiety operates as though visibility is a zero-sum resource and someone else's win is your deficit.
---

## COMPRESSION v38
---
compression_family: C3
---
You schedule buffer time between meetings not for preparation but for recovery. The recovery is from the anxiety of the previous meeting. The anxiety of whether you said the right thing. Whether your contribution was valuable. Whether the silence after your comment was respect or discomfort. The buffer is not a scheduling technique. It is a decompression chamber between exposures.
---

## COMPRESSION v39
---
compression_family: C4
---
The budget got cut. Not your budget. A different team's budget. And your first thought was: we are next. The first cut is the precedent. The precedent becomes the pattern. The pattern reaches your team. By the end of the thought, you have already restructured your team in your mind, identified who you would have to let go, rehearsed the conversations. The budget cut was three floors away. Your anxiety brought it home in thirty seconds.
---

## COMPRESSION v40
---
compression_family: C5
---
You lie awake at night replaying the decision you made about the product roadmap. The decision was sound. Your team supported it. Your VP approved it. The data supported it. And still at 1 AM your mind is constructing the version where the decision was wrong and the product fails and the failure is traced back to this moment, to this choice, to you. The anxiety does not care about consensus. The anxiety only cares about the possibility of being wrong. And the possibility of being wrong is always present, which means the anxiety is always present.
---

## COMPRESSION v41
---
compression_family: C1
---
You have become the manager who over-communicates. Not because you value transparency. Because silence from your team triggers catastrophe thinking. If you do not hear from someone by 10 AM you assume they are disengaged. If the standup is quiet you assume morale is collapsing. The over-communication is monitoring disguised as leadership. You are not communicating. You are scanning.
---

## COMPRESSION v42
---
compression_family: C2
---
The CEO sends a company-wide email. Your name is not mentioned. You scan the email for implications about your department. You find none. The absence of mention becomes its own data point. Not mentioned means not visible. Not visible means not valued. Not valued means vulnerable. The email was about a new benefits policy. Your anxiety made it about your job security.
---

## COMPRESSION v43
---
compression_family: C3
---
You have started keeping a private document of your accomplishments. Not for performance reviews. For the moments at 3 AM when the anxiety says you have contributed nothing. The document is a fact sheet against the fiction your mind generates. You should not need a document to prove to yourself that you are competent. The fact that you do is the clearest evidence of how far the anxiety has embedded itself.
---

## COMPRESSION v44
---
compression_family: C4
---
Someone laughed during your presentation. A brief laugh. Probably at a joke you made intentionally. Your anxiety is not sure it was the intended laugh. Your anxiety thinks the laugh might have been at you rather than with you. The presentation had forty-seven slides. The laugh was on slide twelve. You cannot remember slides thirteen through forty-seven because the anxiety spent the remaining thirty-five slides analyzing the laugh.
---

## COMPRESSION v45
---
compression_family: C5
---
The thing you are most afraid of has already not happened a thousand times. The layoff that did not come. The project that did not fail. The feedback that was not devastating. The client that did not leave. A thousand non-events that your body experienced as events. A thousand catastrophes your nervous system survived that never existed outside your own thinking. The cost is a thousand recoveries from nothing.
---
