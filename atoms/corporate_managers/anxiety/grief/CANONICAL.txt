## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/grief/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David stands at his old mentor's retirement reception. The mentor who hired him, who shaped his leadership, who believed in him before anyone else did. The mentor is leaving. David's chest is tight. His throat closes. He has been carrying the anxiety about this departure for months — the loss of the one person who understood what he was trying to build. But standing here, watching his mentor laugh with people David now leads, something shifts. The mentor is not disappearing. The mentor's influence is already inside David's decisions, his management instincts, his way of building trust. The grief is real. The loss is real. But the frame that says the loss makes him less capable — that frame cracks. His mentor's departure does not undo what David already internalized. His shoulders drop. The grief stays. The anxiety about the grief begins to separate.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/grief/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's best team member dies. Car accident. Tuesday morning meeting slot goes permanently empty. David's body shuts down. His chest is hollow. His hands are ice. He sits in his office and the grief is so total it obliterates the anxiety underneath it. For weeks he has been anxious about losing people — worried about retention, about engagement, about whether his leadership is enough to keep them. Now the loss is real and absolute and has nothing to do with his leadership. It has nothing to do with performance reviews or career trajectories. Someone is simply gone. The old story — that he should be anxious about loss because loss reflects on him — breaks apart completely. This loss does not reflect on him. This loss just is. His body shakes. The frame that connected grief to personal inadequacy cannot survive contact with actual grief. Something deep shifts. He stops trying to prevent loss and starts learning to carry it.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07

COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5

The company restructures. David's division is folded into another leader's organization. Ten years of building — gone in one announcement. David sits in the meeting and his body registers the full impact. Chest hollow. Hands numb. But he does not shatter. He has learned to grieve without drowning. He calls each team member individually. He tells them the truth. He absorbs their anger and fear without deflecting it. His voice cracks on the fourth call. He lets it crack. He is not performing resilience. He is grieving openly while still functioning. The old David would have converted this grief into anxiety about his career, his reputation, his future. This David lets the grief be grief. His body shakes after the last call. He sits in the empty conference room and feels the weight of ten years ending. He feels it all the way down. And he is still here. Still himself. The loss did not erase him.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David notices the pattern: every time someone leaves his team, the grief triggers anxiety about his own adequacy. Maria left for a promotion elsewhere. His first thought was not "I will miss her" but "what does her leaving say about my leadership." His chest locks. His jaw clenches. He sees the machinery — how grief gets hijacked by self-evaluation. The loss of a person becomes evidence of a deficiency. The sadness gets converted into self-doubt before he can even feel it cleanly. His hands are cold. The protective logic is clear: if he makes every departure about himself, he stays vigilant about retention. But the cost is that he never actually grieves. He just worries. And the worrying prevents the grieving, which means the grief accumulates, which means the anxiety intensifies. The system feeds itself.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David stands at the window after the third resignation in two months. His body is vibrating. His vision blurs. He cannot tell where the grief ends and the anxiety begins — they have fused into a single state of dread. He sees, with terrible clarity, why the pattern persists. He was taught that good leaders do not lose people. Every departure is a failure. Every absence is an indictment. The grief is not allowed to be grief because grief implies acceptance and acceptance implies he is okay with losing people. The system demands he convert every loss into a problem to solve. His chest is crushed. He grips the windowsill. The understanding arrives in his body, not his mind: he has been so afraid of loss that he cannot feel loss. The anxiety shields him from the grief, and the grief, unfelt, poisons everything. His eyes sting. The pattern persists because feeling the actual grief would mean admitting he is not in control of who stays.
