伊蓮娜 stares at the calendar invite: "Skip-level with VP." It’s a routine check-in, or it’s supposed to be. But her stomach knots because she hasn’t shipped the feature update on time. The delay was due to a vendor issue, not her team’s performance, but explaining that feels like making excuses. She rehearses the explanation in her head, stripping away any hint of defensiveness, polishing it until it sounds like objective fact. Her hands are cold on the mouse. She checks her Slack status to make sure it says "Available," not "Away," as if visibility equals competence. She realizes she’s holding her breath. She exhales sharply, but the air doesn’t reach her lungs. The shame isn’t about the delay; it’s about the possibility that the VP will see the delay as a pattern, and the pattern as a character flaw. She is already guilty before the trial begins.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v08
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
伊蓮娜 sits in the skip-level. The VP asks, "How’s the timeline looking?" 伊蓮娜’s throat closes. She hears the question as an accusation. She launches into the prepared explanation, too fast, too detailed. She cites the vendor emails, the contract clauses, the mitigation steps. She is proving her innocence rather than answering the question. The VP nods, looking slightly confused by the intensity. 伊蓮娜 sees the confusion and interprets it as disappointment. She stops talking abruptly. The silence stretches. She wants to apologize for talking too much, but apologizing would admit weakness. So she sits in the silence, vibrating with the effort of appearing calm. The mechanism is clear: she is managing the VP’s perception of her competence instead of managing the actual project status. The distance between them grows, not because of the delay, but because of her defense against being seen as imperfect.

## TURNING_POINT v08
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
伊蓮娜 catches herself mid-explanation. She stops. She takes a breath that actually lands in her diaphragm. "Actually," she says, her voice slower, "the timeline is slipped by two weeks. I’m frustrated by it. The vendor missed their deadline. Here’s our new plan to recover." She waits. The VP doesn’t frown. The VP doesn’t judge. The VP says, "Two weeks is tough. What do you need from me to help with the vendor?" 伊蓮娜 blinks. The catastrophe she predicted—the exposure of her inadequacy—didn’t happen. Instead, a problem appeared, and a resource was offered. The shame recedes, not because she’s perfect, but because she’s real. The connection with the VP shifts from performer-audience to partner-partner. Her shoulders drop. The air returns to her lungs.

## EMBODIMENT v08

COST_TYPE: internal

---
伊蓮娜 walks out of the skip-level and doesn’t replay the conversation. She doesn’t analyze her tone or wonder if she sounded confident enough. She knows the timeline is slipped. She knows the plan. She knows she asked for help. That’s enough. She goes back to her desk and sends a brief update to her team: "Timeline slipped two weeks due to vendor. New plan attached. Let’s discuss at standup." No apology. No over-explanation. Just the facts. Her hands are warm. She feels present in her body, not floating above it watching herself perform. The shame is still there, a quiet hum in the background, but it no longer drives the car. She is driving. The mistake is part of the work, not a reflection of her worth. She opens the next task. She begins.
