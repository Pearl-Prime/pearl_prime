## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before the performance review, sit down. Plant both feet flat on the floor. Press your heels down hard. Feel the ground push back. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Your work exists. It happened.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Pull up the org chart. Look at your name. Close the tab. Stand up. Roll your shoulders back once. Feel your spine lengthen. Say one decision you made this week that helped your team. Say it out loud.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Find a pen. Hold it in both hands. Feel the weight. Breathe in slowly. Write one thing your team delivered this quarter. One thing. Set the pen down. That result did not disappear because of a reorg.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Before you walk into the skip-level, stop in the hallway. Press your feet into the floor. Feel your shoes grip. Take one full breath. Name your three direct reports silently. You know these people. You know this work.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Open a blank document. Type the words: I managed this. List two problems you solved this month. Read them back slowly. Close the document without saving. The evidence lives in you, not the file.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
When the anxiety spikes, grip the edge of your desk. Both hands. Press your palms down hard. Count five things you can see in the room. Name them quietly. Stay with the last one until your breath slows.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Before the all-hands reorg call, find your chair. Sit back fully. Let the chair carry your weight. Place one hand on your sternum. Feel your chest rise. Breathe out completely. Empty. Then breathe in. You are still here.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
After a difficult managing-up conversation, go to a window. Look at something far away. Let your eyes soften. Take three slow breaths. On each exhale, drop your jaw slightly. Let the tension fall out of your face.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Write your name on a piece of paper. Write your team's names beneath it. Look at the list. This is real. Fold the paper once. Put it in your pocket. Carry that into the next meeting.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
When your inbox fills with uncertainty, close the screen. Put both hands on your thighs. Press down. Feel your legs under your palms. Breathe in for four counts. Name one thing your team needs from you today. One thing only.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Stand up from your desk. Walk to the nearest wall. Press your palm flat against it. Feel the solidity. Say one decision you made this week that protected your team. Say it once. Return to your chair slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Before you read the performance feedback, set the document aside. Sit straight. Roll your neck left, then right. Feel the stretch. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for eight. Pick the document back up. Read one line at a time.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
When the team asks questions you cannot answer, pause. Do not fill the silence immediately. Place your feet flat. Feel the floor. Take one breath. Then say exactly what you know. Stop there. Silence is not failure.
---
