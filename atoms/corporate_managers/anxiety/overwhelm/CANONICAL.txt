## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah has forty-seven unread emails, three overdue deliverables, and a team member in crisis. Her chest is a fist. Her vision tunnels. She opens her laptop and stares at the inbox and her fingers freeze over the keyboard. The overwhelm is total — every task demands attention and no task can receive it. Then something shifts. She closes the laptop. The gesture is small and radical. She picks up a pen and writes three words on a sticky note: the one thing. Not the forty-seven things. The one thing she can do in the next thirty minutes. Her hand shakes writing it. The frame that says she must hold everything simultaneously — that frame cracks. She cannot hold everything. She never could. The belief that she should is the source of the paralysis, not the volume of work. Her chest opens one fraction. She does the one thing.

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah collapses at her desk. Not metaphorically. Her body gives out. Her vision grays. Her arms go limp. She sits with her forehead on the keyboard and cannot move. The overwhelm has exceeded her nervous system's capacity to sustain it. A colleague finds her. Asks if she is okay. Sarah opens her mouth and what comes out is: "I cannot do all of this." The words hang in the air. She has never said them before. She has always believed she should be able to do all of it. The collapse breaks the frame completely. Her body has voted. The old story — that capacity is limitless if you just try harder — is no longer survivable. Her body refused to carry it. She sits up slowly. Her hands tremble. Everything changes from this point. Not because she wants it to change, but because her body will no longer permit the alternative.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah opens her calendar and counts: fourteen meetings in a single day. She scheduled them all. She said yes to every request. Her chest is concrete. Her hands are numb. She sees the pattern with hot clarity. The overwhelm is not happening to her. She is building it. Each yes adds a brick to the wall that traps her. The protective logic is visible: saying yes means she is indispensable, and indispensable people are safe. But the safety is an illusion. She is drowning in the evidence of her own indispensability. Her throat closes. The pattern persists because saying no feels like risking her value. She would rather be overwhelmed and valued than available and uncertain. Her jaw aches from clenching. The understanding does not reduce the overwhelm. It shows her the architecture.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah cannot remember the last day she did not feel behind. The overwhelm has become her baseline. She wakes behind. She sleeps behind. Her chest never fully unclenches. At 1 AM she lies in bed and sees the entire system: she takes on more because more proves her worth, the more becomes unmanageable, the unmanageable becomes shame, the shame drives her to take on more to compensate. The loop is perfect. Airtight. Self-fueling. Her heart pounds in the dark. She sees why the pattern never resolves — resolution would mean accepting that she is enough without the volume. That her value does not scale with her output. That doing less does not mean being less. Her body resists the understanding. Every muscle says: if you stop, you disappear. The pattern persists because the alternative feels like annihilation.
