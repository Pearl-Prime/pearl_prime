## TURNING_POINT v06
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David receives a routine notification and the alarm fires at full intensity — as if sleep anxiety has reached crisis level. Stomach drops. Shoulders lock. The body does not distinguish between signal and noise. Every input related to sleep anxiety triggers the same emergency response. David reads the actual content: nothing urgent, nothing threatening, nothing that requires the cortisol flooding through the system. The frame cracks. The alarm has been treating every piece of information as an emergency for so long that David forgot what proportional response feels like. Chest eases The alarm is not calibrated to reality. It is calibrated to fear.

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/sleep_anxiety/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's body reaches maximum sleep anxiety activation with zero external trigger. No event. No email. No conversation. The alarm fires from sleep, from rest, from nothing. Chest crushes. David lies in the dark and the alarm runs its full emergency program against an empty room. This is the moment the frame shatters — the alarm does not need reality. It generates its own material. It fires because firing is what it does. The alarm is not a response system. It is a production system. It manufactures emergencies from silence. Hands shake. David sees the full architecture in the dark: no amount of safety will satisfy a system that creates its own threats. The alarm will fire from a beach, from a bed, from a life that is objectively fine. The old way — trying to make life safe enough for the alarm to stop — is no longer survivable because safe enough does not exist in the alarm's vocabulary.

## EMBODIMENT v05
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah receives the trigger and the alarm fires. Jaw clenches. Sarah pauses. Checks the facts. The facts are neutral. The alarm is lying. Sarah returns to work. The alarm fades within two minutes. Sarah did not chase it. Did not build a catastrophe. Did not reorganize the day around a phantom threat. Chest holds Sarah is a person with an overactive alarm who no longer obeys it. The alarm fires. Sarah checks. Sarah decides. The alarm does not decide anymore. Sarah decides. The distinction is the new identity.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/sleep_anxiety/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The genuine sleep anxiety crisis arrives. Real threat. Real stakes. Sarah's alarm fires at its usual volume — the same volume it fires for neutral emails and parking lot memos. Sarah acts from the facts, not from the alarm. Makes decisions. Manages consequences. Navigates the real crisis without the false alarm's additions. Throat closes. Afterward, Sarah sits in the quiet and the alarm runs its residual program. Sarah lets it run. Sarah navigated a real emergency by trusting reality over the alarm. The alarm was not needed. The facts were enough. Hands eventually warm Sarah is a person who has proven, under maximum pressure, that the alarm is not necessary for survival. Survival comes from clarity, not panic. The alarm is legacy software. Sarah's operating system has been upgraded.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/sleep_anxiety/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David tracks the alarm's behavior around sleep anxiety. It fires at the same intensity for genuine concerns and trivial irritants. A significant deadline and a routine email produce identical physiological responses — same cortisol, same chest pressure, same cold hands. Chest tight. David sees the machinery: the alarm has no calibration mechanism. It runs a single program at a single volume for all inputs. The pattern persists because the alarm's inability to distinguish intensity means David is always at maximum alert. Hands cold. There is no resting state. Every stimulus is emergency-grade. The body never recovers because recovery requires a period of non-emergency and the alarm never permits one.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/sleep_anxiety/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David's alarm has been running about sleep anxiety for so long that the body has adapted to crisis as baseline. Normal cortisol levels are elevated. Resting heart rate is high. The body has restructured itself around perpetual emergency. Jaw clenches. David sees the full machinery: the alarm is not a temporary response to temporary threats. It has become a permanent state that the body treats as normal. The pattern persists because the alarm has rewritten the body's definition of normal. Stomach drops. Recovery is not about stopping the alarm. It is about teaching the body what normal feels like again — a process that requires undoing years of physiological adaptation to a state of constant threat. The alarm is not running. The alarm has been built into the architecture.
