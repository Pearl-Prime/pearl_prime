## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your mind replays the skip-level meeting. Stop. Sit up straight. Press both feet flat on the floor. Feel the floor push back. That pressure is real. The replay is not.

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Write down every unresolved work item. One per line. Draw a single line through each one. Say aloud: done for tonight. Close the notebook. Set it across the room.

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four. Out for six. Repeat three times. Your nervous system responds to this. Your inbox does not.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Name the worst fear keeping you awake. Write it in one sentence. Underneath, write one thing you control tomorrow. Focus lands there now.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Lie flat. Clench both fists tight. Hold for five counts. Release completely. Notice the difference in your hands. Move to your jaw. Clench. Release. Move down your body.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Set a physical boundary. Place your phone face-down outside the bedroom. Do this now, before you lie down. The boundary is the signal. Sleep starts with that signal.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Think of one direct report. Name something specific they handled well this week. Hold that detail. Your team exists. The reorg does not exist yet.

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Sit on the edge of the bed. Roll your shoulders back slowly. Three full rotations. Feel the pull across your chest. That tension lived there all day. Release it now.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Pick the single most important task for tomorrow. Write it on a separate card. Put the card by your keys. Everything else waits. One task. One card. Done.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Your jaw is tight. Open your mouth wide. Hold for three counts. Close slowly. Do this twice. Tension stored in the jaw belongs to the day. Leave it there.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Lie on your back. Place one hand on your chest. Place one on your stomach. Breathe until only the lower hand rises. Chest stays still. Count ten breaths this way.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Write down the performance review anxiety in one line. Next to it, write the date it resolves. Until that date, it is scheduled. It is not tonight's problem. Close the page.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Stand up. Walk to a different room. Drink a full glass of cold water slowly. Feel it move down your throat. Return to bed. Your body reset. Begin there.
