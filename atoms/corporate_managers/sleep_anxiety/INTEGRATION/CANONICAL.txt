## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your chest is still breathing. It's 1:43am."
---
The alarm runs at 1:43am. You're in bed. The meeting is tomorrow. Not today — tomorrow. Your nervous system doesn't recognize the difference. It's running the scenario like it's happening now. Your heart's doing the math, your breath is tight, your hands know something's wrong.

But nothing's wrong right now. The meeting doesn't start for nine hours. You're horizontal. The sheets are clean. The alarm is running on future time, not present time.

Notice what's actually happening: you're breathing. The room is dark. The clock says 1:43. You're still in bed. The meeting is nine hours away. Your body is safe right now. The alarm is just early.

It fires on schedule whether you sleep or not.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Sleep is the cost this time."
---
You're awake at 1:43am running through tomorrow's performance reviews. The one where you have to tell someone their project isn't moving fast enough. The one where you have to justify the decision to pull someone from the team. The room is dark. The rehearsal is fully lit.

You will do this tomorrow. You will be capable. You will likely handle it fine. But right now you're paying the cost of that capability — the sleep you're not getting, the hours your body was supposed to spend recovering. Instead it's cycling through scenarios, checking angles, preparing for the worst.

This happens every night before a hard meeting. You've never actually been unprepared. You've never actually said the wrong thing. But you're the one who doesn't sleep before it happens.

The meeting will go. Your body will be tired. That's the trade.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if you let it be tomorrow's problem?"
---
You're lying there rehearsing lines you'll probably change when you actually deliver them. Building arguments that might not be necessary. Predicting reactions that might not come. Preparing for a conversation that doesn't exist yet.

But what if the preparation happened in real time instead? What if you let yourself be tired for this one and just trusted tomorrow-you to figure it out?

You know yourself. You know how to have hard conversations. Tomorrow's you will be present. Tomorrow's you will adjust. Why does tonight's you need to solve a problem that doesn't exist until tomorrow?

What if sleep mattered more than perfect preparation?

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "They have no idea you didn't sleep."
---
The person sitting across from you at 2pm tomorrow won't know that you've been awake since 1:43am. They won't be able to tell. They'll see a manager who's present, who listens, who makes a clear decision about their work.

What they can't see is the night you're losing right now. The cost you're paying in real time while they sleep, while their nervous system rests, while they get to approach tomorrow fresh.

You'll show up tomorrow as someone who didn't sleep. You'll be a little less sharp, a little more tired, a little more reactive. And they'll never know why. They'll just think you're normal.

Your body knows. That's enough.

You're still here. Still managing. Still showing up.
