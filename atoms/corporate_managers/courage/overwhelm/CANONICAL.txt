Sarah faces seven situations that require courage simultaneously. Report the ethics violation. Challenge the budget allocation. Confront the underperforming team member. Push back on the timeline. Advocate for her team's resources. Question the strategic direction. Address the culture problem. Each one demands bravery. Together they demand something impossible. Her chest is locked. Her hands are cold. The overwhelm of so many courage-requiring moments paralyzes her on all of them. Then she picks one. Just one. The ethics report. She files it. Her body shakes. But one thing is done. The frame cracks: she cannot be brave about everything simultaneously. But she can be brave about one thing today. The overwhelm was treating courage as all-or-nothing. It is not. It is one act at a time. Her shoulders drop. Tomorrow she picks the next one.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah breaks down because the courage required exceeds her capacity. The organization needs someone to speak truth at every level — to the board, to the CEO, to the culture, to the systems — and she cannot be that person alone. She is one body. Her chest cracks. Her hands shake. She has been trying to be the sole voice of conscience in a system that requires an army. The frame shatters: individual courage cannot fix systemic problems. She has been overwhelming herself by taking on a structural challenge with personal bravery. She needs allies. She needs shared risk. She needs courage to be distributed, not concentrated in one person. Her body sags with the relief of releasing the sole burden. She picks up the phone and calls a peer. "I need help carrying this." The words are the bravest thing she has said in months.

The systemic crisis demands collective courage. Sarah does not try to carry it alone. She convenes a group. She shares the risk. She speaks the truth and asks others to speak theirs. The room fills with voices that were individually silenced by the same overwhelm Sarah felt. Together, the courage is enough. The board hears six voices instead of one. The systemic problem gets a systemic response. Sarah's chest is tight with the vulnerability of shared action. Her hands tremble. She is not the hero. She is the organizer of heroes. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/courage/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah sees the overwhelm-courage trap: the more situations require bravery, the less brave she feels about any of them. The cognitive load of holding seven courageous acts in queue depletes the emotional resources needed to execute even one. Her chest is heavy. The pattern persists because courage requires emotional reserves and overwhelm depletes emotional reserves. The system creates its own impossibility. Her hands are cold. She needs to be brave but she is too overwhelmed to be brave and the overwhelm comes from needing to be brave about too many things. The circle is perfect and suffocating.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah realizes the overwhelm has converted courage into paralysis across her entire life. She cannot speak up at work because the queue of things requiring speech is too long. She cannot set boundaries at home because the accumulated unset boundaries are too many. She cannot advocate for herself because the list of unadvocated needs stretches back years. Her chest is crushed. The pattern persists because every delayed act of courage adds to the overwhelm and the increased overwhelm makes every remaining act harder. She is buried under the weight of all the brave things she did not do. Each unsaid truth is a brick in the wall. The wall is so high now that individual acts of courage seem futile against it. Her hands lie in her lap. The pattern persists because she needs systemic change and only has individual courage and the gap between them grows wider every day she is silent.
