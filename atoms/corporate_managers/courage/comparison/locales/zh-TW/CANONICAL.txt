### Application to Your Current Work:

You are working to distinguish between genuine and imagined suffering. These stories suggest a diagnostic question for those moments of somatic tension (tight chest, cold hands):

> **"Am I feeling this tension because the situation is genuinely dangerous/unethical, or because I am comparing my natural response to someone else's performed response?"**

If it’s the latter, the path forward isn’t to force yourself to act like the other person (which leads to the paralysis in *Mechanism Proof*), but to ask: **"What does courage look like *for me*, in this body, right now?"**

This aligns perfectly with your new model: activating compassion (for self and others) only when genuinely needed, and recognizing that your "quiet, precise, well-timed" courage is not a deficit, but a distinct and necessary leadership asset.
