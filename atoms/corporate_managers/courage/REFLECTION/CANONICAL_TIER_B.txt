---
You see the problem clearly. The process is inefficient. The decision is wrong. The timeline is unsustainable. You know this because you have the information and the experience to see it. And you feel the impulse to speak up.
Then you calculate. Speaking up means challenging the director's decision. It means suggesting that the senior leader's direction is misguided. It means creating friction in a system where your credibility depends on being known as a reliable team player.
This isn't cowardice. This is how your nervous system is trained in hierarchical systems. You've learned that the cost of being right can be higher than the cost of being quiet. You've learned that visibility can become vulnerability.
So you don't speak. You do what's asked. You work around the problem where you can. You warn your team privately about what's coming. You perform agreement while internally disagreeing.
This isn't a character failure. This is a rational calculation in a system where speaking up has real costs and no institutional protection for people who name problems.
