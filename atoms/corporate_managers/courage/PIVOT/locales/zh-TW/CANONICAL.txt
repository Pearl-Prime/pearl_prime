Here is how these pivots map onto the work you are doing:

### 1. The Illusion of Protection (PIVOT v04, v08)
You’ve recognized that shielding your team or rescuing them from failure is often a mechanism to manage *your own* discomfort, not their growth. This is the **false positive** in action: you perceive their struggle as a threat to your competence or their safety, when it is actually necessary data for their development.
*   **The Pivot:** Stop scanning for distress to fix it. Start allowing friction to exist so you can distinguish between *suffering* (which needs compassion) and *struggle* (which needs space).

### 2. Consensus as Avoidance (PIVOT v02, v11)
You noted that you manage consensus because conflict feels like failure. This is the **inherited leadership model** speaking: the idea that a good leader keeps the peace. But as you’ve observed, this creates systemic burnout because unresolved tensions accumulate as emotional labor.
*   **The Pivot:** Recognize that disagreement is not a breach of relationship; it is a clarification of reality. When you agree with the VP to avoid "career suicide," you are paying with your interiority. The cost is higher than the risk.

### 3. The Embodied Truth (PIVOT v06, v07)
Your body knows the difference between pragmatic and afraid. This is crucial to your view of burnout as an **embodied challenge**. You cannot think your way out of this; you have to feel your way through it. The "impossibility" of setting a boundary is a somatic signal of old survival patterns, not a logical assessment of the situation.
*   **The Pivot:** Trust the bodily sensation of fear as a indicator that you are approaching a genuine boundary, not a danger. The fear is the compass.

### 4. Authority Through Vulnerability (PIVOT v09, v10)
You are redefining leadership presence. Admitting "I don’t know" or making the hard choice without full consensus shifts the locus of authority from **omniscience** (which is exhausting and impossible) to **integrity** (which is sustainable).
*   **The Pivot:** Stop trying to appear competent to protect yourself. True authority comes from owning the weight of the decision, not from pretending the decision is easy.
