## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before the skip-level meeting, sit upright. Both feet flat on the floor. Name the one thing you will not hide today. Hold it in your chest. Walk in carrying it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Write the honest performance note. Read it once. Don't soften the last sentence. Submit it. Step away from the screen. Breathe out fully.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Say the reorg concern out loud. Alone first. Hear your own voice say it. Notice where your jaw tightens. Unclench it. Now you know what is real.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Before managing up today, write one true thing you need. Not a request. A fact. Keep it under ten words. Bring it to the meeting.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Stand before your team. Name the uncertainty directly. One sentence. No qualifiers. Watch their shoulders drop. Let that land.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Identify the feedback you have delayed. Set a five-minute timer. Write the first sentence only. Stop when the timer ends. Send it tomorrow.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Before the performance review, press both palms flat on the desk. Feel the surface. State your actual assessment aloud. Don't revise it yet. That version is the truth.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Notice the question you swallowed in the last meeting. Write it down now. Decide if it needs an answer. If yes, send it today.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Pick the team member you have been avoiding. Schedule fifteen minutes. Write one sentence describing what you owe them. Read it before you walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Before the reorg announcement, breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six. Do this three times. Then open the door.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Locate the decision you keep deferring. Name the actual risk. Write it in one clause. Put a deadline on it. Commit to that date now.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
In your next one-on-one, speak the hard observation first. Not last. Not softened. First. Feel your sternum. Stay upright through the silence that follows.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
After the difficult meeting, sit still for sixty seconds. Don't check your phone. Let the discomfort exist without fixing it. That discomfort is evidence of courage.
---
