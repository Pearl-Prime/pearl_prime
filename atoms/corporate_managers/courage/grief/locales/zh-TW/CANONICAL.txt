### 1. The Somatic Archive of Courage
Your model identifies a critical blind spot in conventional leadership development: **Courage is treated as a renewable resource, but Grief is treated as waste.**

*   **Mechanism Proof v02/v03** reveals that the body keeps a ledger. When David’s chest stays heavy or Kevin’s shoulders stay tight, this is not just "stress"; it is **unprocessed loss**.
*   In your earlier reflections, you noted that burnout is often an "automatic and invisible part of leadership style." Here, you make it visible: The "heaviness" is the accumulated weight of past truths told that resulted in personal loss.
*   **Key Insight:** The inhibition isn't fear of the *current* moment; it’s the body’s protective reflex against *re-experiencing* the grief of previous costs. This explains why high-performing leaders often become quieter over time—not because they care less, but because their "grief debt" has compounded.

### 2. The Double Bind of Grief (Mechanism Proof v03)
This is the most psychologically acute section. You identify the **trap of the two griefs**:
1.  **The Grief of Action:** Losing status, safety, relationships, or identity because you spoke truth.
2.  **The Grief of Inaction:** Losing oneself, one’s integrity, and one’s sense of purpose by staying silent.

> *"He is trapped between the grief of acting and the grief of not acting. Both griefs are real. He is trapped... There is no painless option. There is only which grief he can live with."*

This aligns with your exploration of **distinguishing genuine vs. imagined suffering**. Here, the suffering is entirely genuine. The "false positive" of hyper-vigilant leadership would be to try to *avoid* this grief entirely (by scanning for distress to fix, or by staying safe). Your model argues that **mature leadership is the capacity to choose which grief is bearable.**

### 3. From Inhibition to Integration (Turning Point & Embodiment)
The shift from **v01** to **v03** demonstrates the move from **Systemic Sacrifice** to **Self-Claimed Identity**.

*   **v01 (Inhibition):** Grief *prevents* action. The leader is paralyzed by the somatic memory of loss.
*   **v02 (Turning Point - Micro):** Grief is *present* but action happens anyway. The frame shifts from "Grief means stop" to "Grief is the cost of impact." Kevin realizes his courage had collective value even if it had personal cost.
*   **v03 (Embodiment - Identity):** Grief is *integrated*. Kevin doesn’t just "push through"; he **discloses** the grief. By telling his new team, *"I was fired... I will do the same here,"* he transforms his vulnerability into authority.

This mirrors your work on **redefining leadership presence through boundaries and self-awareness**. Kevin is no longer performing competence despite internal struggle (the "protective function" you noted earlier). He is **leading from the struggle**. His authority comes *from* the fact that he has paid the price and survived.
