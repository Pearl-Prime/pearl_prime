## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin scans his team for suffering the way other managers scan for deadlines. He reads every face for distress. He monitors every tone for hidden pain. He catches the slight droop in an employee's shoulders and his body activates — someone needs me. His chest tightens. His hands go cold. He approaches the employee who says: "I am just tired. Long weekend." Kevin's entire caring apparatus mobilized for a long weekend. He stands in the hallway processing the false activation. The frame cracks: the watcher is not detecting suffering. It is hunting for suffering. It scans for pain because finding pain justifies Kevin's role as the compassionate leader. His compassion fatigue is not from too much caring. It is from too much scanning. The surveillance creates the demand. His shoulders drop. If he stopped hunting for pain, the pain that genuinely needs his attention would still arrive. He does not need to go looking for it.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin has a breakdown in his therapist's office. He cannot stop monitoring. He watches his therapist's face during sessions — is the therapist bored, does the therapist think he is difficult, is the therapist about to terminate the relationship. He is doing the watching in the one place specifically designed for him to stop watching. His body shakes. His chest collapses. The watcher has no off switch. It runs in the room where he goes to heal from its effects. The frame shatters completely. The watcher is not a professional skill. It is a compulsion. It is a nervous system configuration that predates his career, his role, his identity as a compassionate leader. He learned to scan for suffering as a child because in his childhood, detecting suffering early meant preventing punishment. The compassion fatigue is an adult manifestation of a childhood surveillance system. His eyes fill. The watcher is a child's strategy in an adult's body. The career just gave it a purpose.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin walks past his team and does not scan their faces. His body tenses — the watcher insists on its read. He lets it insist. He walks to his office and works on strategy. If someone needs him, they know where to find him. His chest is tight with the unscanned data. He lets it be tight. By end of day, two people sought him out with genuine needs. He helped them with full presence. The other ten had fine days he knew nothing about. His hands are warm. He is a manager who is available, not a manager who surveils. The distinction saves him thirty phantom caring activations per day. He has energy at 5 PM. He drives home without the weight of twelve emotional profiles in his chest. The watcher still scans from the periphery. Kevin no longer files the reports it generates.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
A team member attempts suicide. Kevin is called to the hospital. The genuine crisis demands everything the watcher once provided — attentiveness, sensitivity, reading the room. Kevin is present. He is attuned. He holds the space with everything he has. His chest is full and aching. The watcher serves a real purpose in this room — it detects the subtle shifts in the person's state, it alerts Kevin to changes, it keeps him attentive. After the visit, Kevin drives home and the watcher tries to generalize — scan everything, everyone might be in crisis. Kevin holds the line. This was a crisis. The office is not a crisis. The watcher works in emergencies. It does not need to run in normal conditions. His body shakes with the residual intensity. He lets it shake. He did the hardest work of his career today and he has the energy for it because he saved it by not watching everything, always, for years. His compassion was there when it mattered most. It was there because he protected it.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin realizes the watcher doubles his emotional labor. For every genuine interaction, there are three phantom ones — the face he read wrong, the tone he misinterpreted, the body language he over-decoded. Each phantom interaction consumes real compassion resources. His hands are cold. He is fatigued not by the people who come to him but by the people he goes to with his surveillance — inventing their pain, constructing their needs, preparing to help before help is requested. The pattern persists because the watcher feels like prevention. If he detects suffering early, he can intervene early. But the detection creates false positives that drain him before the real needs arrive. He is exhausted from helping people who do not need help because the watcher said they did.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin has not had a thought about himself in weeks. Every thought is about someone else — their state, their needs, their pain, their trajectory. His own body is invisible to him. His chest aches and he does not notice. His shoulders are concrete and he does not register the tension. The watcher has consumed his self-awareness entirely. It monitors everyone except the one person who most needs monitoring: Kevin. He sees the full architecture at 3 AM when his body wakes him with chest pain: the watcher is not just draining his compassion. It is erasing his self. He has no interiority. He has no needs. He has only surveillance of others. The pattern persists because the watcher replaces self-awareness with other-awareness, and the absence of self-awareness means he cannot detect his own depletion until it becomes a medical event. He is a surveillance system that forgot it was also a person. His chest pain is his body's last attempt to get his own attention.
---
