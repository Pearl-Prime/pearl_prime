

## RECOGNITION v01-v05
---

---
David absorbs one team member's crisis and spirals: he's too affected means he's not boundaried enough means he shouldn't be managing means he should quit. Sarah listens to one person's struggle and spirals: she's too drained means she can't do this means she needs to leave the role. Kevin holds one person's anxiety and spirals: he gets too invested means he's weak means the organization needs different leadership. Anthony has one emotional conversation and spirals: he's too affected means his job is getting to him means he can't lead. Tanya processes one crisis and spirals: she cares too much means she'll burn out means she needs to exit.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05
---

---
David doesn't sleep because the spiral won't stop. He's going to cause more harm, his team would be better off without him. His chest stays heavy. Sarah plans an exit based on one difficult conversation. She's not cut out for this. Her hands stay cold. Kevin makes decisions from the spiral: maybe he should step back, his team probably needs someone who's not so affected. His shoulders stay tight. Anthony spirals into preparing his departure. The board would understand. His throat stays tight. Tanya spirals into resignation. She'll find another role. Her stomach stays tight.
---

## TURNING_POINT v01-v05
---

---
David stops: "I'm affected by this. That means I care. That's not a reason to quit." His chest unclenches. Sarah pauses: "One difficult conversation doesn't mean I can't do this." Her hands warm. Kevin interrupts the spiral: "Caring about people is not weakness." His shoulders release. Anthony breaks the spiral: "I'm affected. That's normal. Not a referendum on my job." His throat releases. Tanya stops: "I care. I'm not burned out. That's normal." Her stomach settles. They interrupt the spiral before it reaches resignation.
---

## EMBODIMENT v01-v05
---

---
David cares and stays in the role. His chest is open. Sarah feels the impact of her care and continues. Her hands are warm. Kevin's compassion is part of his leadership. His shoulders are relaxed. Anthony's emotional intelligence strengthens his leadership. His throat is open. Tanya's care for her people is her greatest asset. Her stomach is calm.

Sarah spirals about her inability to care. A team member brought a problem and Sarah felt nothing, which triggered a spiral: I am empty, which means I am failing, which means I should not be managing, which means my career is over, which means I wasted fifteen years. Each loop increases the intensity. Her chest is locked. Then she notices something — the spiral is consuming the exact energy she needs to recover her compassion. She is spending her reserves on self-accusation instead of on restoration. The frame cracks. The spiral is not processing the compassion fatigue. It is accelerating it. Every loop drains what little remains. If she stops spiraling, the energy might return. Her hands unclench slightly. She cannot think her way back to caring. She can only stop thinking long enough for the caring to regenerate.

Sarah spirals for an entire weekend about whether she should leave management entirely. The spiral is relentless — she is empty, she cannot care, she is hurting people by staying, but leaving means admitting failure. By Sunday night she has constructed and demolished her entire career forty times. Her body is wrecked. Chest hollow. Eyes swollen. Then Monday arrives and a team member shares good news — a promotion, genuine excitement. Sarah feels something warm in her chest. Small. Fragile. Real. The compassion is not dead. It was buried under the spiral. Two days of recursive torture and the caring was there the whole time, just smothered. The frame shatters. The spiral told her the compassion was gone forever. The compassion waited underneath. She does not need to solve the fatigue. She needs to stop spiraling long enough to feel what is already returning.

Sarah feels the spiral begin — I am empty, which means — and she interrupts it. Not aggressively. She just notices the first loop and does not take the second. Her chest tightens. The spiral pushes. She breathes. She sits with the fact: she is depleted today. That is all. Not failing. Not ruined. Depleted. She moves through her day with the depletion as a fact rather than a spiral. She manages logistics instead of emotions. She delegates the counseling conversation to her deputy. By afternoon she feels a small warmth return. Her hands are warm. She did not spiral away her recovery. She protected it. She is a manager who can be depleted without narrating her own destruction. The spiral still starts. She does not follow it. The compassion returns faster when she leaves it room.

A wave of team distress hits and Sarah's compassion runs out by 11 AM. The spiral ignites — you are useless, this proves you should not be here, they need someone who can sustain. Sarah feels every loop fire. Her chest is tight. Her eyes burn. She does not follow. She takes a ten-minute break. She walks outside. She breathes cold air. She comes back and has enough for one more conversation. Just one. She gives it everything. It is enough. The person felt held. Sarah felt depleted but not annihilated. She made it through a brutal day without the spiral consuming her remaining capacity. She drives home and the spiral tries again. She turns on music. She lets it play louder than her thoughts. She arrives home tired and alive. The spiral lost today. She won. Both are exhausting. But winning is better.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah maps the spiral: compassion runs out, spiral begins, spiral drains remaining energy, compassion cannot recover, spiral continues about the non-recovery. Her hands are cold writing the map. The spiral and the compassion fatigue are symbiotic — each feeds the other. The fatigue creates material for the spiral and the spiral prevents recovery from the fatigue. The protective logic is subtle: the spiral feels like she is actively working on the problem. She is thinking about it, analyzing it, planning solutions. But the thinking IS the problem. She is using her depleted cognitive resources to narrate her depletion instead of resting. The pattern persists because rest feels passive and dangerous while spiraling feels active and productive. But rest heals and spiraling harms. She has been choosing harm because harm feels like effort.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah has been spiraling about her compassion fatigue for three months. Not occasionally. Daily. She has constructed elaborate theories about why she is empty, what broke, when it started, what childhood wound created this vulnerability, whether she is constitutionally incapable of sustained caring. Her chest is permanently tight. Her jaw never unlocks. She sees the full architecture with devastating clarity: three months of spiraling about emptiness and she is emptier than when she started. The spiral did not diagnose the fatigue. It deepened it. Every recursive loop consumed energy that could have been used for recovery. Three months of analysis and zero seconds of rest. The pattern persists because the spiral says understanding will lead to healing. But she understands perfectly and is not healed at all. She understands her compassion fatigue better than anyone and cannot feel a thing. Understanding is not recovery. It is a different form of exhaustion.
