## EXERCISE v01
---

---
After your next performance review conversation, stop. Find a wall. Press your back flat against it. Feel the solid surface. You are separate from their outcomes. Breathe out. Let the wall hold you.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Before a skip-level meeting, sit. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press down. Count four slow seconds. Your body is here. The hierarchy is not inside you.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
When your team's fear starts living in your chest, name it aloud. Say: this is not my fear to carry. Say it again. Then unclench your jaw.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
At the end of a reorg announcement day, go somewhere private. Shake your hands loose at the wrist. Hard. Ten seconds. Drop what you absorbed. Walk away.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
After managing up and down in the same hour, sit in your car. Do not check your phone. Place one hand on your sternum. Count five exhales. Only five.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
When someone brings you their uncertainty, listen fully. Then, after they leave, press your fingertips together. Feel the pressure. You are distinct. You remain.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Before your next one-on-one, write one sentence. Write: I am not responsible for fixing their fear. Read it once. Leave the paper on your desk. Enter the meeting.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
After a difficult cascade conversation, find a window. Look at something distant. Hold your gaze there for thirty seconds. Let your eyes soften. Come back to your own body.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
When the team's anxiety fills a room, notice where it lands in you. Stomach. Shoulders. Throat. Name the location. Do not fix it. Just name it. Exhale once.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
At the close of a high-pressure review cycle, write three things that belong only to you. Not your team. Not your boss. Yours. Read them back. Slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
After absorbing a direct report's distress, stand up. Roll your shoulders back. Once. Feel your spine lengthen. Say nothing. This posture is yours alone.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
When you leave a difficult all-hands, do not debrief immediately. Walk to a bathroom. Run cold water over your wrists. Count to eight. Feel the temperature. Return.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Before you send the next reassuring email you do not fully believe, pause. Notice the tension in your hands above the keyboard. Set them in your lap. Breathe once. Decide from there.
---
