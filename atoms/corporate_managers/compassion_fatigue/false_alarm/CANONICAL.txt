

## RECOGNITION v01-v05
---

---
David has one difficult conversation with a team member and spirals into believing he's compassion fatigued. His chest tightens. Sarah checks in on someone and immediately feels emotionally drained and assumes it's compassion fatigue. Her hands are cold. Kevin listens to one person's problem and spirals into feeling completely used up. His shoulders tense. Anthony has one emotional conversation and thinks his capacity is gone. His throat tightens. Tanya processes one person's anxiety and spirals into believing she's totally depleted from empathy. Her stomach feels hollow. Their nervous systems treat one difficult conversation like systemic depletion.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05
---

---
David starts changing how he manages based on false alarm. He pulls back his compassion because he thinks he's already at capacity. His chest stays tight. Sarah starts setting unnecessary boundaries before actually hitting her limit. Her hands stay cold. Kevin distances himself from his people preemptively. His shoulders stay tight. Anthony becomes guarded in conversations. His throat stays tight. Tanya stops being present with her team's emotions. Her stomach stays tight. They're all creating distance based on false depletion alarms.
---

## TURNING_POINT v01-v05
---

---
David checks: "Am I actually depleted or just tired from one conversation?" One conversation isn't compassion fatigue. His chest unclenches. Sarah distinguishes between empathic engagement and depletion. One check-in isn't exhaustion. Her hands warm. Kevin realizes one difficult conversation is normal, not a sign he's used up. His shoulders release. Anthony recognizes that difficult conversations don't deplete his actual capacity. His throat releases. Tanya distinguishes between emotional engagement and depletion. Being present with one person's emotions isn't compassion fatigue. Her stomach settles. They calibrate their alarms to actual exhaustion, not normal emotional labor.
---

## EMBODIMENT v01-v05
---

---
David cares without assuming depletion. His chest is open. Sarah engages without premature boundaries. Her hands are warm. Kevin listens without pulling back. His shoulders are relaxed. Anthony stays present in conversations. His throat is open. Tanya engages fully with her team. Her stomach is calm.
---

Kevin manages HR and every email from an employee triggers the false alarm — someone is suffering, someone needs him, someone is about to break. His chest locks on every notification. His body treats every message as a crisis of human pain. He opens an email about vacation scheduling. Not suffering. Just logistics. But his body was prepared for suffering. He has been treating every human contact as a potential wound. His frame cracks: the false alarm has made him unable to receive neutral communication from the people he manages. Everything is filtered through the lens of someone who needs saving. His shoulders drop. Not every email is a cry for help. Not every meeting is a crisis. His compassion fatigue is partly driven by the alarm treating everything as an emergency of caring. He has been depleted by phantom suffering, not just real suffering.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin has a panic attack when his phone rings. Not because of who is calling. Because his body has learned that phone calls mean someone needs emotional labor. His heart pounds. His chest collapses. The call is from the building manager about a water heater repair. Kevin is on the floor hyperventilating about a water heater. The absurdity breaks the frame. His nervous system has generalized compassion fatigue into a full-body alarm response to any human contact. He cannot distinguish between a call that requires emotional labor and a call about plumbing. His body has decided that all human interaction is exhausting. The old frame — that he is always needed, always required to care — has expanded until caring and contact are fused. He lies on the floor breathing and the water heater caller has hung up. His body needs to relearn that contact does not always require compassion. Some calls are just about pipes.

## EMBODIMENT v02

COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5

---
Kevin receives an email from a team member and pauses before opening it. His alarm fires — prepare to care, prepare to hold pain. He opens the email. It is a status update. He reads it and responds factually. No emotional labor required. His chest settles. He is learning to check the content before mobilizing the compassion. Not every interaction demands his empathy. Some demand his attention, his decision-making, his logistical brain. He is a manager with compassion AND a manager with other capacities. His hands are warm on the keyboard. The alarm still fires at contact. He no longer assumes the alarm is correct. He checks. Most of the time, the caring machine is not needed. He saves it for when it is.

## EMBODIMENT v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The genuine crisis arrives. A team member is in real distress — family emergency, needs support. Kevin's alarm fires and for once it is accurate. He moves into caring mode with intention. He listens. He holds space. He helps arrange leave. His chest is full but not crushed. He gave real compassion to a real need and it did not destroy him because he has not been spending his reserves on false alarms for months. He has energy for this moment because he saved it. After the conversation, he is tired but functional. He sits quietly for ten minutes and then returns to work. His hands are warm. He is a person who can care deeply when it matters because he learned to stop caring reflexively when it does not. The distinction is the architecture of a sustainable career in managing human beings.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin counts his alarm responses for one week. Forty-seven times his body activated for potential emotional labor. Of those, three required actual compassion. Forty-four were false. His chest is tight reading the count. The compassion fatigue is not from the three genuine needs. It is from the forty-four false activations. Each false alarm costs emotional energy — cortisol, tension, the mobilization of caring muscles. His hands are cold. The pattern persists because his body learned to treat all human contact as demanding. The alarm does not distinguish between someone sharing good news and someone in crisis. Both trigger the same preparation. He is exhausted not from caring but from preparing to care, constantly, for everyone, about everything.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/compassion_fatigue/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin realizes he has started avoiding people. Not meetings — people. He takes the long route to avoid the break room. He emails instead of walking to someone's desk. He closes his door and pretends he is in a call. His chest is tight with the recognition: the false alarm has made him afraid of his own team. Every face is a potential demand for empathy. Every conversation is a potential draw on reserves he does not have. His hands grip his desk. The pattern persists because the alarm has trained him to associate human proximity with depletion. The caring reflex fires on proximity alone. He does not need to actually care about someone for the caring to cost energy — he just needs to be near them and the system activates. The compassion fatigue has metastasized from his work into his relationship with being around other humans. His office door is closed. His isolation is the pattern's final product.
