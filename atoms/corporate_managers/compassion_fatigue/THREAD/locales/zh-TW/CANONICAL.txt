### The Next Step: Integrating the "Receiving" Muscle

You have named the asymmetry (v04). You have set the boundary (v12). Now, the hard part begins: **practicing the receipt of care without guilt or deflection.**

Your system is likely wired to interpret receiving as:
1.  **Weakness** (I should be able to handle this).
2.  **Burden** (I am taking energy from someone else).
3.  **Debt** (Now I owe them).

**Question for reflection:**
In the past week, when an opportunity to *receive* support arose (a colleague checking in, a team member solving a problem without your input, a moment of rest), what was the immediate physical or emotional sensation? Did you lean into it, or did you reflexively redirect it back to work or others?

That reflex is the old circuit. The pause before you react is the new design.
