## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David watches his peer present at the leadership summit — energized, sharp, radiating the kind of passion David used to have. His chest is heavy. His hands are cold. The comparison fires: she is thriving and he is burned out. He is the one who cannot keep up. Then his peer mentions, casually, that she took a three-month sabbatical last year. That she burned out in 2023 and rebuilt from zero. David's frame cracks. His peer is not thriving because she is better. She is thriving because she stopped. She allowed herself to collapse and recover. David has never stopped. The comparison was between someone who rested and someone who refused to rest. His shoulders drop. The burnout is not evidence of his inferiority. It is evidence that he never gave himself what his peer gave herself: permission to break.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David collapses at work. Panic attack in the stairwell. His body refuses to climb to the third floor. He sits on the concrete steps and cannot move. His chest is locked. His vision is gray. He thinks about his peer upstairs, presenting, leading, alive with purpose. He is on a stairwell floor unable to stand. The comparison reaches maximum intensity and then something beneath it gives way. He is not failing to be his peer. He is failing to be a person with a functioning body. The comparison is irrelevant. His nervous system is broken. No amount of comparing himself to someone healthier will make him healthy. The frame does not just crack — it becomes absurd. He is measuring career trajectories while sitting on a concrete step unable to breathe. His body is asking him to stop and he is thinking about who is ahead. The absurdity breaks something open. He stops comparing and starts listening to the stairwell under him, the breath in his lungs, the basic fact that he needs help.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David returns from medical leave. He walks into the office and his peer is still thriving. The comparison flickers. He notices it and sets it down. He is not here to match her pace. He is here to work at a pace his body can sustain. His chest is open — not wide, but not locked. He takes on less than he used to. He leaves earlier. He does not check his peer's output against his own. His hands are warm on his keyboard. He is a leader recovering from burnout, working at a sustainable rhythm, producing quality work within limits he set. The comparison still arrives in his peripheral vision. He does not turn his head to look at it directly. His peer thrives. He recovers. Both are valid current states.

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David is offered the promotion he spent years comparing himself against others for. He has the energy for it now. His body is rebuilt. He looks at the job description and asks a question he never asked before: do I want this? Not — do I deserve this, am I good enough for this, will this close the gap. Do I want this. His chest stays open while he considers. He decides no. The role requires the kind of all-consuming pace that broke him. He declines. His hands are steady writing the email. No shame. No comparison. He is not behind the people who took this role. He is somewhere else entirely — in a life he can sustain, with work he can do well, in a body that carries him without breaking. The comparison has no hold. David is not running anyone's race. He is walking his own pace on his own road. His jaw is loose. His breath is even. He goes home on time.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David scrolls through LinkedIn and counts the peers who seem energized. Four posts about new initiatives. Two about promotions. One about a keynote speech. David's chest collapses. He cannot imagine having the energy for any of these things. He has just enough energy to get through his day. The comparison illuminates the mechanism: he measures his depleted state against other people's peak performance. He compares his worst to their best. His hands are cold. The protective logic is visible — if he tracks who has more energy, he can identify what is wrong with him and fix it. But the tracking is itself draining. Every comparison costs energy he does not have. The pattern persists because the comparison pretends to be diagnostic when it is actually parasitic. It feeds on the energy reserves it claims to be measuring.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David cannot get out of bed. The alarm rings and his body does not respond. He lies in the dark thinking about everyone who got up this morning and went to work with energy. His CEO. His peers. The new hire who radiates enthusiasm. David's chest is a stone. He sees the full machinery of the pattern: the comparison has been draining his reserves for years. Every time he measured himself against someone with more energy, the measurement cost energy. Every time he pushed harder to match someone else's pace, the pushing depleted him further. The comparison did not diagnose his burnout. It accelerated it. He has been burning out faster because he has been running someone else's race at his own body's expense. His eyes are open in the dark. His body will not move. The pattern persists because the comparison demands he match a pace that is destroying him, and the destruction makes the gap wider, which makes the comparison more urgent. It is a machine designed to consume the person operating it.
