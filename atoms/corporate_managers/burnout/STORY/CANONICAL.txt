This is what management burnout looks like at home, Pearl said. Not a fight. Not an estrangement. She had been generous about them. She was now, gently, naming what they were.

The work is not to figure out what he wants to do this weekend. The work is to notice that the supply that produces wanting is also the supply that runs the team, and the supply is finite, and the team has been getting all of it, and the marriage has been receiving the residue. The residue is not enough. The wife knows. The body knows. The org chart does not know and has no incentive to ask.

She was in the lobby because she did not have the bandwidth for the bar. Her body had used all available reserves to hold a presence through eight hours of high-stakes peer interaction, and the reserves did not refill in the forty minutes between the end of the day and the start of the dinner.

She was on her phone. She was not reading anything. She was holding the phone because holding the phone was a culturally accepted way of being alone in a hotel lobby. The phone was a perimeter. The perimeter was what she could manage.

What this means, Pearl said, is that the manager-role you have been running all day comes off, when it comes off, in a particular way. It does not come off in a single decision. It comes off in a slow, post-day collapse of the surface area you had been maintaining. The collapse needs space. The hotel lobby was Yuki's space. The phone was the wall.

The wall was not a failure. The wall was Yuki's nervous system taking the only post-load recovery available to it in a setting that had been engineered for continuous high-empathy peer interaction. The dinner would have been more of the same. Her body had filed the dinner as "additional load." Her body had moved her to the lobby. Her body had given her the phone as cover.

This is what late-stage management burnout looks like at offsites. Not absence. Not visible withdrawal. A person in the lobby with a phone, looking like she is doing something, doing in fact the only thing that is keeping her capable of returning to the room in the morning.
