## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin used to love this work. He remembers the feeling — arriving early because the problems excited him, staying late because the solutions fascinated him. Now he arrives depleted and leaves more depleted. His chest is heavy with a specific grief: the loss of the person who cared. The passion is gone. Not reduced. Gone. He sits in a strategy meeting that would have energized him three years ago and feels nothing. Then something shifts. He realizes the grief is not about the work changing. The work is the same. He changed. The burnout consumed the part of him that lit up. His frame cracks: he is not grieving the job. He is grieving himself. The version of Kevin who loved this work still exists somewhere underneath the exhaustion. The recognition lands in his sternum like a stone. He cannot get that person back by working harder. He can only get that person back by stopping.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin finds a notebook from his first year in the role. His handwriting was different — quick, excited, full of exclamation points and underlines and arrows connecting ideas. He reads his own enthusiasm and his chest cracks open. His hands tremble holding the notebook. This person is unrecognizable. This person had ideas and energy and belief that the work mattered. Kevin has been running on fumes and obligation for two years. The grief hits like a wall. He is mourning himself. Not a colleague who left. Not a project that failed. Himself — the living, interested, alive version who got consumed by the machine. The old frame — just push through, it will come back — shatters against the notebook. It will not come back on its own. The burnout did not temporarily misplace his passion. It killed it. He sits at his desk holding evidence of who he used to be and the distance between that person and this one is the full measure of what he has lost.

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin is rebuilding. Slowly. He took the leave. He rested. He is back in the role with reduced scope and guarded energy. The grief is still present — he misses the person who bounded into work with ideas. That person has not returned. But a different person has emerged: someone who works with care instead of passion, with intentionality instead of fire. His chest aches when he remembers the old energy. He lets it ache. He does not chase the feeling. He works from where he is, not from where he was. His hands are warm on the keyboard. He is slower. He is also more precise. The grief taught him that passion consumed without boundaries becomes burnout. He carries the loss and works anyway. Not the same way. A sustainable way. The grief is part of who he is now. He does not try to outrun it.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
A new challenge arrives that genuinely interests Kevin. His chest responds — a small warmth he has not felt in two years. His eyes widen slightly. His hands reach for a pen. The interest is real but fragile. The old Kevin would have thrown himself at it — all hours, all energy, all consuming. New Kevin feels the interest and pauses. He protects it. He approaches the challenge with measured energy, guarding the spark instead of burning it. The grief transforms in that moment — from loss into lesson. He lost himself once by giving everything to the work. He will not lose himself again. The interest stays alive because he does not smother it. He works on the challenge for two hours and stops. His hands are warm. His chest is open. He goes home and the interest is still there the next morning. He did not consume it. He tended it. The person who loved work is not back. Someone wiser is here instead.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin realizes the grief has a function: it keeps him tethered to the old self. Every morning he mourns the energy he used to have, and the mourning consumes the little energy he has now. His chest is heavy. His hands grip his coffee cup. The pattern is visible: the grief about burnout makes the burnout worse. He spends emotional resources lamenting his lost capacity instead of using his remaining capacity. The protective logic is subtle — if he keeps grieving, he stays connected to the person he was. If he accepts the loss, he becomes the burned-out version permanently. His throat tightens. The grief persists because accepting it would mean accepting that the old Kevin might be gone for good. And that acceptance feels like a second death.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin sits in his car in the parking lot unable to go inside. Not because of the work. Because of the distance between who he is and who he was. The building looks the same. The role is the same. He is not the same. His chest is hollow. His hands are limp on the wheel. He sees the full architecture: the burnout killed his engagement and the grief about the dead engagement prevents recovery. He cannot heal because he is too busy mourning. He cannot mourn effectively because he is too burned out to feel fully. He is trapped in a half-grief — too exhausted for full mourning, too bereaved for full functioning. The pattern persists because recovery requires energy he does not have, and the grief consumes the energy that trickles back. He is a system that uses its repair resources to mourn the need for repair. His eyes close. The parking lot is quiet. He sits with the full weight of a person who cannot fix what is broken because the broken is all he has left to fix with.
