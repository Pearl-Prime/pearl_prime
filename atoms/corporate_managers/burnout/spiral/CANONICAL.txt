

## RECOGNITION v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David feels depleted one day and spirals: tired means burned out means his role is wrong means he should leave. Sarah is exhausted and spirals: too much work means everything is broken means the team can't function. Kevin is tired and spirals: he can't keep up means his leadership is failing means the whole organization is in trouble. Anthony is worn down and spirals: hard quarter means he's finished means the board knows. Tanya is depleted and spirals: she's burned out means she shouldn't be VP means she needs to leave. Their chests/hands/shoulders/throats/stomachs are tight. The spiral turns temporary fatigue into identity crisis.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David doesn't sleep because the spiral won't stop: he's going to quit and then what, he has kids, he failed at the role. Sarah makes decisions from the spiral: she should restructure everything, her team is broken, everything needs to change immediately. Kevin acts from the spiral: he should step back, he shouldn't be leading, everything is falling apart. Anthony feeds the spiral: his days are numbered, the board is waiting, he needs to prepare his exit. Tanya spirals into planning: she'll leave, move to something else, this was the wrong role all along. None of them are addressing the actual fatigue. They're all responding to the catastrophe the spiral created.
---

## TURNING_POINT v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David stops the spiral: "I'm tired. That doesn't mean I should quit." Sarah interrupts: "I'm exhausted. I need rest. That's all." Kevin breaks in: "Hard week doesn't mean everything is broken." Anthony pauses: "I'm worn down. That's normal. Not a referendum on my job." Tanya stops: "I'm tired. I need to recover. That's not an exit cue." They all set boundaries on the spiral. Fatigue is information, not identity threat. They address the fatigue instead of responding to the catastrophe story.
---

## EMBODIMENT v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David rests and the spiral stops. Sarah takes time off and the catastrophe dissolves. Kevin gets sleep and the organization is still standing. Anthony takes a break and clarity returns. Tanya recovers and she still wants the role. They all distinguish between fatigue and fundamentally wrong. They address the fatigue and move forward.

Sarah is spiraling about whether her burnout means she should leave the company or just the role or just the team or maybe change industries entirely. Each option triggers another loop of catastrophic analysis. Her chest is locked. Her hands grip her skull. She has been spiraling for three hours and has not made a decision — she has made forty decisions and reversed all of them. Then she stops. Not because the spiral resolves. Because she is too exhausted to continue it. The burnout is so deep that even the spiral runs out of fuel. She sits in the silence and something shifts: the spiral was pretending to solve her burnout but it was spending her last reserves of cognitive energy. The frame cracks. She does not need to solve anything right now. She needs to stop thinking. The spiral told her that stopping was dangerous. Her body is telling her that not stopping is fatal.

## TURNING_POINT v03

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah spirals herself into a dissociative episode. She has been running the burnout loop for so long — should I stay, should I go, what if I stay and get sicker, what if I go and regret it — that her brain simply disconnects. She stares at her office wall and cannot feel her hands. Cannot feel her face. The spiral overloaded her system and her system shut down. She sits in the numb silence and the spiral has nowhere to go because she cannot think at all. Her chest is absent. Her body is a shell. In the numbness, something breaks through: the spiral will consume her entirely if she lets it. It will spiral until there is nothing left to spiral with. The old frame — that thinking harder will solve the burnout — is gone. Her brain voted by leaving. She sits in the quiet and for the first time in months, the silence is not waiting to be filled with another loop. It is just silence. Empty. Still. She breathes. The breath is the first thing she has felt in hours.

COST_INTENSITY: 5

---
Sarah feels the spiral begin — the familiar rev of what if, and then, but maybe — and she sets it down. Not easily. Her chest tightens. The spiral protests. She picks up a pen and writes: I am burned out. I do not need to solve it today. Her hand shakes writing it. The spiral insists there is urgency. She disagrees. The burnout took years to build. It will take time to dismantle. She does not need to figure out her entire career trajectory in one sitting. She needs to get through Tuesday. Her jaw loosens. She gets through Tuesday. Wednesday arrives and she gets through that too. She is not the person who spirals about burnout anymore. She is the person who carries burnout one day at a time. The spiral still starts its engine. She lets it idle. She does not drive it. She drives herself. Slowly. Forward.

Sarah makes the decision. She takes the leave. The spiral lunges — what if this ruins your career, what if you cannot come back, what if you are making the biggest mistake of your life. She feels every loop fire simultaneously. Her chest is tight. Her hands are cold. She walks out of the building anyway. The spiral runs its full program and she walks through it like walking through rain. It lands on her. It does not stop her. She gets in her car and drives home at 2 PM on a Wednesday. The spiral says this is the end. Her body says this is the beginning. She parks in her driveway and sits in the quiet car and the spiral finally, slowly, winds down. Not because it resolved. Because there is nothing left to spiral about. She made the choice. The choice is done. Her chest opens one centimeter. She walks inside her house in the middle of a workday and the house is quiet and the quiet is the opposite of the spiral. She stands in her kitchen and breathes.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah maps the spiral's relationship to her burnout. The burnout creates a problem: she is exhausted, depleted, unable to function. The spiral takes the problem and turns it into a maze: she is exhausted, which means she is failing, which means she should change something, but what if the change makes it worse, and what if she cannot recover, and what if this is permanent. Her hands are cold. Each loop adds complexity to a simple truth — she is tired and needs to rest. The spiral cannot tolerate simple truths. It elaborates, decorates, catastrophizes. Her chest is tight. The pattern persists because the spiral substitutes for action. As long as she is thinking about the burnout, she feels productive about it. But the thinking IS the exhaustion. She is spending her depleted energy on recursive analysis instead of on recovery. The spiral eats the medicine.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah has been spiraling about her burnout for four months. Not occasionally. Daily. The spiral is the first thing that activates in the morning and the last thing running at night. Her chest is permanently tight. Her vision is narrowed. She has not made a single decision about her career in four months because the spiral consumes every decision with counter-arguments and catastrophes. She sees the full machinery: the spiral is not processing her burnout. It is preventing her from processing it. Every time she approaches a simple truth — I need to stop — the spiral wraps it in complexity until the truth is buried. The spiral persists because it creates the illusion of engagement with the problem. She feels like she is working on her burnout when she is actually feeding it. Four months of thinking about exhaustion and she is more exhausted than when she started. The spiral does not metabolize the problem. It reproduces it. Her eyes are dry. Her body is a husk. The spiral runs inside the husk because that is all it knows how to do.
