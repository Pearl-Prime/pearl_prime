

## RECOGNITION v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David feels depleted one day and spirals: tired means burned out means his role is wrong means he should leave. Sarah is exhausted and spirals: too much work means everything is broken means the team can't function. Kevin is tired and spirals: he can't keep up means his leadership is failing means the whole organization is in trouble. Anthony is worn down and spirals: hard quarter means he's finished means the board knows. Tanya is depleted and spirals: she's burned out means she shouldn't be VP means she needs to leave. Their chests/hands/shoulders/throats/stomachs are tight. The spiral turns temporary fatigue into identity crisis.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David doesn't sleep because the spiral won't stop: he's going to quit and then what, he has kids, he failed at the role. Sarah makes decisions from the spiral: she should restructure everything, her team is broken, everything needs to change immediately. Kevin acts from the spiral: he should step back, he shouldn't be leading, everything is falling apart. Anthony feeds the spiral: his days are numbered, the board is waiting, he needs to prepare his exit. Tanya spirals into planning: she'll leave, move to something else, this was the wrong role all along. None of them are addressing the actual fatigue. They're all responding to the catastrophe the spiral created.
---

## TURNING_POINT v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David stops the spiral: "I'm tired. That doesn't mean I should quit." Sarah interrupts: "I'm exhausted. I need rest. That's all." Kevin breaks in: "Hard week doesn't mean everything is broken." Anthony pauses: "I'm worn down. That's normal. Not a referendum on my job." Tanya stops: "I'm tired. I need to recover. That's not an exit cue." They all set boundaries on the spiral. Fatigue is information, not identity threat. They address the fatigue instead of responding to the catastrophe story.
---

## EMBODIMENT v01-v05 (COMBINED FOR BREVITY)
---

---
David rests and the spiral stops. Sarah takes time off and the catastrophe dissolves. Kevin gets sleep and the organization is still standing. Anthony takes a break and clarity returns. Tanya recovers and she still wants the role. They all distinguish between fatigue and fundamentally wrong. They address the fatigue and move forward.
