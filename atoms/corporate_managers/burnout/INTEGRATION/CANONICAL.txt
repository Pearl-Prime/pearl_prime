## INTEGRATION v08
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body has been speaking. You stopped listening."
---
The headache that arrives by 3 PM. The shoulder pain that wakes you at night. The stomach that rejects lunch. These are not random. These are the body's performance reviews. The body has been documenting the cost of the workload in the only language it has: sensation. The headache is the body saying: the cognitive load exceeded capacity four hours ago. The shoulder pain is the body saying: the tension you are carrying is not yours alone. You have been treating these signals as inconveniences to manage. They are information. They are the most honest feedback you will receive today. The body does not politic. The body does not optimize for your feelings. The body reports what is true.
---

## INTEGRATION v09
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would you protect if you could only protect one thing."
---
Not the project and the people and the timeline and the budget and the stakeholders and the roadmap and the team morale and the hiring pipeline. One thing. If you could only protect one thing, what would it be. The question is not theoretical. The question is diagnostic. Because you have been trying to protect everything and the result is that nothing is fully protected. Least of all yourself. The burnout lives in the refusal to prioritize. In the belief that everything matters equally. Everything does not matter equally. Something matters most. Name it. Protect it. Let the other things receive the attention they can get rather than the attention they demand.
---

## INTEGRATION v10
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: standard
carry_line: "The manager who stepped back is still standing."
---
The peer who took the demotion. The one who asked for a smaller scope. The one who said: I need a role that does not consume my entire life. People talked. People judged. People assumed weakness. Two years later, that person is the most effective manager in the organization. Not because the role is smaller. Because the person is larger. Rested. Present. Thinking clearly for the first time in years. Strategic instead of reactive. The person who stepped back did not lose. The person who stepped back recovered the cognitive capacity that burnout had stolen. The step back was not retreat. It was repair.
---

## INTEGRATION v11
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Rest is not reward. Rest is infrastructure."
---
You have been treating rest as something you earn after the work is done. The work is never done. So rest never arrives. The reframe is structural: rest is not a reward. Rest is the foundation that makes the work possible. Without rest, the decisions degrade. The patience thins. The empathy contracts. The creativity disappears. You are not resting to be lazy. You are resting to be functional. The infrastructure of your leadership includes sleep, recovery, and stillness. When you skip the infrastructure, the building stands for a while on momentum. Then the building collapses. You are the building. Rest is the foundation. The foundation is not optional.
---

## INTEGRATION v12
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The team absorbs what you do not process."
---
The burnout you are carrying does not stay with you. It radiates. Your team reads your energy. When you are depleted, your patience shortens. When your patience shortens, your feedback becomes blunt instead of developmental. When your feedback becomes blunt, your team's psychological safety decreases. When safety decreases, risk-taking stops. When risk-taking stops, innovation dies. The cost of your burnout is not just your cost. It is the cost your team pays in the form of a manager who is running on empty. They cannot tell you this. They manage around it. They protect you from additional problems. They absorb the overflow. The burnout has distributed itself across the people who depend on you.
---

## INTEGRATION v13
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What did you stop doing that kept you whole."
---
Before the burnout became total, there were things that kept you regulated. The morning run. The Sunday cooking. The phone call with the friend who makes you laugh. The fifteen minutes of nothing between meetings. These were not luxuries. These were regulation tools. The burnout did not start when the workload increased. The burnout started when the regulation tools were surrendered to the workload. The workload grew and the recovery shrank and the gap between demand and capacity widened until the gap became the burnout. What did you stop doing. Name it. Consider whether the thing you stopped is the thing that would have prevented the state you are in now.
---

## INTEGRATION v14
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Five minutes of stillness is not wasted time."
---
Sit still for five minutes. Set a timer. Do nothing. The discomfort will arrive immediately. The discomfort is the burnout talking. The burnout says: you cannot afford five minutes. You have emails. You have a meeting in twenty minutes. You have a deliverable. The burnout has trained you to equate stillness with irresponsibility. The stillness is not irresponsible. The stillness is the minimum maintenance requirement for a human nervous system under sustained load. Five minutes. Not to solve anything. Not to plan. Not to reflect productively. Five minutes to exist without producing. The discomfort you feel during those five minutes is the measure of how far from yourself you have traveled.
---

## INTEGRATION v15
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your direct report sees what you cannot."
---
The person on your team who said: You seem different lately. They said it carefully. They said it with the particular caution that people use when they are telling their manager something the manager does not want to hear. You seem different. Meaning: you seem diminished. Meaning: the person I started working for had energy and ideas and warmth. The person I work for now has task lists and deadlines and a flatness that is starting to feel permanent. Your team sees the burnout before you do. Your team lives with the consequences of your depletion before you acknowledge them. The observation from your report is not a complaint. It is a mirror. Look at it.
---

## INTEGRATION v16
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The burnout took the joy first."
---
You used to enjoy solving problems. You used to enjoy the complexity of organizational dynamics. You used to enjoy the moment when a plan came together. The enjoyment left quietly. You did not notice its departure because the work continued. The work does not require enjoyment. The work requires execution. And you can execute without joy for a long time. The cost is not visible in the output. The cost is visible in the experience. The days pass and they are competent and they are empty. The emptiness is not depression. The emptiness is the absence of the engagement that made the work meaningful. The burnout took the meaning and left the mechanics. You are running on mechanics. Mechanics can sustain a career. Mechanics cannot sustain a person.
---

## INTEGRATION v17
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would you tell your direct report if they described your symptoms."
---
Imagine your best direct report sits down in a one-on-one and describes exactly what you are experiencing. The exhaustion. The flatness. The inability to rest. The physical symptoms. The emotional numbness. The loss of interest in things they used to love. What would you tell them. You would tell them to take time off. You would tell them to see a doctor. You would tell them that no project is worth their health. You would tell them that the organization will survive without them for a week. You would tell them these things with genuine concern because you are a good manager. Now tell yourself. The advice you give others is the advice you need. The compassion you offer your team is the compassion you withhold from yourself. The standard you hold for their wellbeing is the standard you violate for your own.
---

## INTEGRATION v18
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The exhale is longer than the inhale. The system begins to reset."
---
One breath. Let the exhale be longer than the inhale. Three counts in. Five counts out. This ratio activates the parasympathetic nervous system. The system that repairs. The system that restores. The system that the burnout has suppressed for months. You do not need a meditation retreat. You do not need a wellness program. You need one breath where the exhale exceeds the inhale. One moment where the body is told: you can repair now. The repair does not happen in one breath. But the signal does. The signal says: the emergency is paused. For five counts. The pause is enough for the system to remember what repair feels like. Do it again. The second breath builds on the first. The system remembers more.

## INTEGRATION v19
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The organization survived every departure."
---
Every person who left your organization. Every manager who burned out. Every director who resigned. Every VP who was restructured out. The organization survived each departure. The meetings continued. The products shipped. The revenue came in. The organization is designed to survive the departure of any individual. This is not a critique of your importance. This is a liberation. The weight you are carrying, the sense that everything depends on you, is not true. The organization will continue. The question is whether you will continue. The organization has a plan for your departure. Do you have a plan for your survival.

## INTEGRATION v20
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You are allowed to be finite."
---
You are a finite being in a system that behaves as though human capacity is infinite. The system will continue to ask for more because the system does not measure the cost to the person. You measure the cost. You feel the cost. And you have been treating the cost as a personal failure rather than a structural reality. You are not failing because you are burned out. You are burned out because you are finite and the demand is not. The integration is this: you are allowed to be finite. You are allowed to have limits. You are allowed to reach the end of your capacity and say: this is the end. Not the end of your commitment. Not the end of your competence. The end of what one person can carry. That end is not weakness. That end is honest. And honest, right now, is the bravest thing you can be.
