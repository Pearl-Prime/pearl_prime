## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah is burned out and the false alarm keeps firing anyway. Her body is depleted but her alarm system runs at full power. She gets an email about a routine policy change and her body responds like a termination notice. Chest locked. Hands ice. She stares at the email — it is about parking lot construction. Her exhausted body is running emergency protocols for parking lots. The absurdity cracks the frame. She is too tired to function and simultaneously too activated to rest. The false alarm does not care about her energy levels. It does not pause because she is depleted. It fires from empty reserves. She sees it: the alarm is part of the burnout. It is burning fuel she does not have. Every false alarm drains a tank that is already empty. Her shoulders sag with a different weight — not the alarm's weight but the recognition that the alarm is accelerating her collapse.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah has a panic attack at her desk. Heart racing. Cannot breathe. Certain she is dying. She calls 911. The paramedics find nothing wrong. Her heart is healthy. Her lungs are fine. She is lying on a stretcher in her office lobby and she is not dying. She was dying from a parking lot email and a budget review and a Slack message and a calendar invite — from the accumulated false alarms that her burned-out body could no longer absorb. The paramedics leave. Sarah sits in her office chair and the frame is gone. There is nothing left to crack. The alarm said she was in danger and her body believed it so completely it simulated cardiac arrest. She is burned out and the false alarm is using her remaining energy to create fake emergencies. The old way ended on a stretcher. She needs a different way. Her hands shake. Her chest is raw. She starts by closing the laptop.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah receives the urgent email. Her alarm fires. Chest tight. Hands cool. She reads the email. It is a schedule change for next week's meeting. She sets it aside. The alarm continues for thirty seconds and fades. She does not engage. She does not build the catastrophe. She is a burned-out manager slowly rebuilding, and part of rebuilding is not spending her depleted energy on false emergencies. Her hands warm on her coffee cup. She does the next task with the energy she saved by not chasing the alarm. By end of day she has done less work than she used to but she has done it from presence, not panic. Her body notices the difference. She is tired but not destroyed. Depleted but not demolished. The false alarm fires and she lets it pass. She is not the alarm. She is the person underneath it, slowly recovering.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah is rebuilding from burnout when the real crisis arrives. Division restructuring. Her role changes. Genuine uncertainty. The alarm fires — loud, hot, consuming. Her chest locks. Her hands freeze. But something is different. She checks the alarm against reality: this time the signal matches. Real threat. Real uncertainty. She responds with appropriate concern instead of panic. She asks questions. She makes contingency plans. She does not catastrophize. She acts. Afterward she sits in her car and trembles — not from false alarm but from genuine stress properly processed. The distinction is everything. She felt the alarm, checked it, found it real, and responded proportionally. She did not waste energy on projection. She did not add phantom threats to the real one. She handled an actual crisis with the reserves she saved by not handling a thousand fake ones. Her body is exhausted but clean. This is what healthy activation feels like. She had forgotten.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah realizes the false alarm is a primary driver of her burnout. She spends mental energy on every email, every meeting invite, every Slack notification — not processing the content but processing the threat. Her body treats every input as a potential emergency. She calculates: fourteen false alarms per day, each lasting ten to twenty minutes of elevated cortisol. That is three to four hours of emergency response to non-emergencies. Every day. Her chest is tight. Her hands tremble. The burnout is not from the work. It is from the alarm system the work triggers. She sees the protective logic: if she stays in emergency mode, she is always ready. But perpetual readiness IS the exhaustion. The alarm does not cause burnout on top of work. The alarm IS a significant portion of the burnout.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah cannot tell the difference between tiredness and terror anymore. They feel the same. Her body runs the alarm and the alarm feels like fatigue and the fatigue triggers more alarm. She is in a loop where burnout feeds the false alarm and the false alarm feeds the burnout. Her chest never opens. Her hands are permanently cool. She lies in bed unable to sleep because her body is both exhausted and activated — too tired to function, too alarmed to rest. She sees the full architecture in the dark: the alarm was supposed to protect her from threat. Instead it allied with the burnout to create a state where she can neither work nor rest. Both systems are running simultaneously, draining her from both ends. The pattern persists because the alarm makes rest feel dangerous and the burnout makes vigilance impossible. She is trapped between two systems that are destroying each other and destroying her in the process. Her body has become the battlefield.
---
