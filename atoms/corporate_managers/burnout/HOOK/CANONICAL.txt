## HOOK v31
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: corporate_managers
---
The 1:1 with your direct report ran four minutes long. You stayed warm the whole time. You asked the right follow-ups. You used the word "absolutely" in the right places. The 1:1 ended and you stood up and walked to the kitchen and the moment you were alone in the kitchen something in your chest dropped a foot. Not because the conversation was hard. The conversation was fine. The drop was the cost of the four extra minutes of warmth you had to manufacture while running on a tank that emptied three weeks ago.
---

## HOOK v32
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: corporate_managers
---
Your team had a good quarter. You said so in the all-hands. You used the word "proud." Your team smiled. Underneath the smile, on your side, was a sentence you could not say in the room: I am the only person here who knows what this quarter cost. The team sees the numbers. The team does not see the manager who absorbed the friction so the numbers could happen. You absorbed it. The absorbing was your job. Nobody is going to thank you for it because nobody saw it.
---

## HOOK v33
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: corporate_managers
---
You realized at 11:14 PM, halfway through a Slack reply you did not want to send, that you have not been a person to yourself in a long time. You have been a function. A manager-function. A point of contact. The version of you that used to read fiction on the subway, the version that used to laugh at her own jokes in the kitchen — that version got requisitioned for the org chart somewhere around month nine, and the requisition came with no return date.
