## PERMISSION_GRANT v01
---

---
Burnout, in the middle stratum, arrives wearing a stack of unspoken rules. Hold the line for the team below. Look composed for the team above. Take no time you have not earned. Never let the depletion show in the leadership meeting. Be the one who is fine when nobody else is. These rules are not company policy. They are noise the role accumulated. Set them down.

You are allowed to be depleted and still hit your numbers. The body keeping a quiet tally underneath the deliverables does not mean the deliverables are wrong. The numbers and the depletion are both real at the same time.

You are allowed to need rest your title does not include. The title is a description of accountability, not a description of capacity. Capacity is a body fact. The body needs what the body needs.

You are allowed to stop performing energy you do not have. The composure you carry into the leadership meeting is not the role. The role is the work. The composure was an accumulation, and you are allowed to set it down without setting the work down.

You are allowed to let one of your direct reports see that you are human. The fear that vulnerability collapses your authority is mostly a story the role tells itself. The team has seen the cost on your face for months. Naming it does not break the team. Hiding it forever does.

You are allowed to take the vacation without justifying it as a planning issue. The vacation is the reset window the calendar deleted from your year. It does not need a strategic reason. Recovery is the reason.

You are allowed to admit, to yourself first, that the workload is not sustainable. Saying so does not make you a worse manager. Refusing to say so for one more quarter is what made you the manager you no longer recognize.

You are allowed to be the consciousness watching the burnout, not the burnout itself. The alarm fires. The chest tightens. The composure clicks into place. And somewhere behind all of it, awareness keeps watching. That awareness is the seat. That awareness is the part of you that survives the quarter, regardless of how the quarter goes.

These are not affirmations. They are accurate descriptions of the rights every middle-stratum nervous system comes with. Read them again. Read them slowly. Let the body hear them, one permission at a time.
