## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David tells his wife he is burned out. The words leave his mouth and the shame ignites. His face is fire. His hands grip the kitchen counter. Good leaders manage their energy. Good husbands do not bring this home. Good men do not break. His wife looks at him and says: "I know. I have known for a year." The shame predicted shock, disappointment, diminished respect. His wife already knew and stayed. His chest cracks. The frame shifts: the shame assumed his burnout was hidden. It was not hidden. It was visible and his wife was waiting for him to name it. The shame said naming it would destroy his image. Naming it destroyed nothing. The gap between shame's prediction and his wife's response is the crack in the frame. His hands release the counter. He is still ashamed. But the shame has been wrong about the consequences.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David weeps in his office. Door closed. Shoulders shaking. He has nothing left. The shame is so intense it creates its own weather — he is ashamed of being burned out and ashamed of crying and ashamed of being ashamed. Three layers of shame stacked on exhaustion. His body shudders. Then his door opens. His assistant sees him. The moment of maximum exposure. David cannot hide. He is undone and someone is witnessing it. His assistant says: "Can I get you some water?" Not judgment. Not horror. Water. The simplest kindness in the face of his total collapse. David takes the water. His hands shake holding the cup. The frame detonates. The shame said being seen in breakdown would be catastrophic. Being seen in breakdown was met with water. The old story — that burnout means he is defective and being seen as defective means being destroyed — cannot survive a glass of water. His body continues to shake. But the shame has lost its prophecy.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David tells his team he is reducing his hours. His face warms. The shame says: they will see you as weak. He says it anyway: "I have been running at a pace that is not sustainable and I am adjusting." His voice is steady. His hands are on the table. His team nods. One person says: "Thank you for modeling that." David is not ashamed of having limits. He is a leader who names his limits publicly. The shame fires and misses. It hits the old David — the one who would have worked until collapse rather than admit depletion. This David is different. He has been burned out and he is not pretending otherwise. His jaw stays loose. The shame still visits. He no longer invites it to stay.

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/shame/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David applies for a role one level below his current title. The shame is enormous — he is stepping down, admitting defeat, publicly acknowledging that he could not handle the bigger role. His chest burns filling out the application. His hands shake. He submits it. He gets the role. He starts Monday with a manageable workload, a team of four instead of fourteen, and a body that can breathe. The shame howls for a week. Then it quiets. David comes home with energy. He plays with his kids. He sleeps through the night. He rediscovers the person who existed before the burnout consumed everything. The shame said stepping down would erase him. Stepping down rebuilt him. He is a person who chose his health over his title. The shame calls that weakness. His body calls it the first wise decision he has made in five years. His body wins the argument.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David notices the double bind: the burnout makes him feel deficient and the shame about feeling deficient prevents him from addressing the burnout. He cannot rest because resting means admitting he needs rest. He cannot slow down because slowing down means admitting he cannot sustain the pace. His chest is concrete. The shame stands guard at every exit from burnout. Every recovery action is shamed before it can be taken. His hands are cold. The protective logic is brutal: if he stays burned out but performing, he maintains his image. If he stops and recovers, the stopping reveals the weakness. The pattern persists because the shame makes every healing action feel like a confession of failure. Recovery requires the very vulnerability the shame is designed to prevent.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/burnout/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David is burned out to the point of clinical depression. His doctor says: take leave. David's first thought is not about his health. It is about what people will think. His chest is hollow. His eyes are flat. He is so depleted he can barely dress in the morning and his primary concern is his professional image. He sees the full architecture of the pattern in the doctor's office: the shame is more powerful than the burnout. He will endure depression, physical collapse, the dissolution of his health before he will endure being seen as someone who needed to stop. The shame will let him die at his desk rather than be seen leaving it. His hands lie in his lap. The pattern persists because the shame treats survival as less important than image. His body is begging to stop. His shame will not let him. He is caught between a body that needs rest and a shame system that treats rest as a moral failing. The doctor waits for his answer. David sits in the chair, too ashamed to save his own life.
---
