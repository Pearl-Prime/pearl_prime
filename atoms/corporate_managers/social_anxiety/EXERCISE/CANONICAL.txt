## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before the skip-level meeting, sit upright. Place both hands flat on your thighs. Press down. Feel the pressure return. Breathe in for four counts. Out for four. You are not on trial. You are reporting facts.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Your manager just walked in unexpectedly. Stop typing. Plant your feet on the floor. Notice three things you can see. Name them silently. Your body settles before your mind does. Let it settle first.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before a performance review conversation, write one sentence. Write what you actually did this quarter. One specific result. Read it aloud. That sentence is your anchor. Return to it when the room gets loud.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
In the reorg meeting, your jaw tightens. Notice that. Unclench it deliberately. Drop your shoulders one at a time. Left, then right. You need your full attention now. Tension steals it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
After a difficult all-hands, find a private space. Stand up straight. Press your back against a wall. Hold for ten seconds. Feel the solid surface. That is real. The spiral in your head is not.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Before you send that upward email, stop. Read it once for facts. Read it again for fear. Remove every word that apologizes for existing. Send the factual version.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Your team looks to you for certainty you don't have. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Out for six. Do this twice before you speak. Steadiness starts in your body, not your script.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
You are about to enter a room where your status feels unclear. Pause outside the door. Roll your shoulders back once. Lift your chin two inches. Walk in at your normal pace. Slow is not weak.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
After a meeting where you stayed silent, don't punish yourself. Write down one thing you observed clearly. One accurate read of the room. That is data. Use it next time.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
During a tense one-on-one, your chest constricts. Name it without judgment. Tight. Just that word. Then take one full breath. Ask your next question from that breath, not from the tightness.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Before a presentation to senior leadership, stand alone for ninety seconds. Feet shoulder-width apart. Arms uncrossed. Breathe normally. Your nervous system reads posture as signal. Give it a steady one.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Your team member challenges you publicly. Pause. Count to three silently. Feel your feet. Respond with one sentence only. One. Brevity signals control. Rambling signals panic.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
At the end of a high-stakes day, sit down before you check messages. Place one hand on your sternum. Feel it rise and fall three times. The day ended. You are still here. Now check messages.
---
