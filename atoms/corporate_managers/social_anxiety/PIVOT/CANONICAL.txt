


## PIVOT v01
---

---
You manage people for a living and the team happy hour makes your palms sweat. The management skill and the social fear run on separate tracks.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The networking event isn't about connections. It's about a body that learned social exposure is where judgment happens.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You're fine in the meeting because the meeting has structure. Remove the structure and your system doesn't know where to stand.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Small talk with the VP feels like a performance review conducted in the elevator. Your body can't tell the difference.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
You rehearse the casual conversation before the casual conversation. The rehearsal is the anxiety. The conversation is usually fine.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
The team lunch invitation triggers a calculation: who will be there, what to say, how to exit. The calculation takes more energy than the lunch.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
You're socially competent at work because competence is a role you play. The person behind the role dreads the unscripted moments.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
The anxiety isn't about the people. It's about the version of yourself that exists when there's no agenda to hide behind.
---

## PIVOT v09
---

---
After the office party, you replay every conversation for ninety minutes. The replay is your system auditing for social mistakes it can't undo.
---

## PIVOT v10
---

---
You declined the invitation because the energy for social performance wasn't available. The declining feels like relief and shame simultaneously.
---

## PIVOT v11
---

---
The conference panel makes you nauseous. Not because of the content. Because of the exposure.
---

## PIVOT v12
---

---
You're excellent one-on-one. In groups, the excellence disappears because the audience multiplies the vulnerability.
---
