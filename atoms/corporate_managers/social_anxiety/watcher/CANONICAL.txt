COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David measures the watcher's cost within social anxiety. Approximately thirty to forty percent of cognitive resources go to surveillance — reading faces, interpreting silences, monitoring tonal shifts, tracking body language. Chest crushes. The remaining sixty percent handles actual work. David has been operating at sixty percent capacity and comparing the output to peers operating at one hundred percent. The deficit is not ability. It is bandwidth. Hands shake. The pattern persists because the watcher feels like a professional skill — attentiveness, awareness, emotional intelligence. But it has crossed from skill to compulsion. The watcher no longer enhances performance. It drains it. The protective logic is that stopping the surveillance means missing threats. But the surveillance catches phantom threats and misses nothing real.
