David feels anxious in a meeting and spirals: I'm failing socially means people see me means I'm exposed means I'm done. His chest is heavy. Sarah feels awkward and spirals: I'm not handling this means people judge means I don't belong. Her hands are cold. Kevin feels tense and spirals: I'm failing at leadership means the team sees means they don't respect me. His shoulders are tight. Anthony feels out of place and spirals: I don't fit means executives know means I'm not qualified. His throat is tight. Tanya feels uncomfortable and spirals: I'm failing at presence means people notice means I'm out. Her stomach is heavy. Social moment becomes existential crisis. David stops: "I felt anxious. That's just anxiety." His chest opens. Sarah interrupts: "I felt awkward. People didn't judge." Her hands warm. Kevin breaks in: "Tension doesn't determine competence." His shoulders release. Anthony pauses: "Discomfort doesn't determine fit." His throat opens. Tanya stops: "Social anxiety is just anxiety." Her stomach calms. Anxiety stays anxiety.

David enters the spiral about social anxiety. First loop: the situation is concerning. Second loop: the situation is dire. Third loop: the situation is irreversible. Fourth loop: everything connected to the situation will also fail. Jaw clenches. Stomach drops. By loop five, David has constructed a complete catastrophe from an incomplete data set. Then David writes down loop one: the situation is concerning. That is the fact. Loops two through five are fiction. The frame cracks — the spiral was not analyzing the situation. It was decorating the situation with fear until the fear became the situation. Jaw releases The fact is manageable. The fiction was not.

The spiral ran continuously for hours, building catastrophe upon catastrophe, consuming cognitive resources until there was nothing left. Chest hollow. David hits the floor of the spiral — the place where even the spiral cannot continue because the body has no fuel. In the silence at the bottom, something shifts. The catastrophe did not arrive. Hours of prediction and the predicted future did not materialize. David sits in the gap between the spiral's reality and actual reality and the distance is infinite. The frame shatters — the spiral was not preparing for catastrophe. It was manufacturing catastrophe inside David's body while the actual world continued normally. Hands grip the desk. The old way is no longer survivable because the spiral consumed the body it was supposed to protect.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David has been spiraling about social anxiety for so long that the spiral has consumed the subject it was processing. David can no longer distinguish between the original concern and the spiral's elaboration of it. The concern and the catastrophe have merged. Chest crushes. David sees the full machinery: the spiral does not process problems. It metabolizes them into fuel for more spiraling. Each problem that enters the spiral is converted into material for the next loop. The spiral feeds on its own output. Hands shake. The pattern persists because the spiral has become the primary activity. David spirals instead of acting. The spiraling feels like work — effortful, exhausting, productive-seeming. But it produces nothing except more spiraling. It is a machine that manufactures its own input from its own output, a perpetual motion engine of suffering that will run until the body cannot sustain it.
