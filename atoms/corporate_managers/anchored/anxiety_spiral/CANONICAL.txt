MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
David's engineer makes a mistake. The mistake creates a small bug. The small bug propagates through the system. David's stomach drops immediately into catastrophe thinking. One small mistake becomes evidence that he hired poorly. Hiring poorly becomes evidence that he lacks judgment. Lacking judgment becomes evidence that he shouldn't be VP. The spiral accelerates. It's been two minutes since he learned about the bug and he's already questioning his fundamental competence. His chest is tight. The spiral is faster than his ability to fact-check it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David doesn't stop the spiral. He feeds it. He starts reviewing his other hires. Some of them look less stellar now. He starts assessing his hiring criteria. Maybe they're not rigorous enough. He starts wondering if he should have gone to a different school, if he should have worked at a different company, if he should have different judgment entirely. The spiral has consumed the original bug. The bug is solved. But the spiral continues because the spiral isn't about the bug. It's about David's fundamental fear that he's not competent. The bug is just permission to finally admit it.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety_spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David notices he's spiraling and interrupts it. "This is spiral thinking," he says to himself. "One bug is a bug. It's not evidence about my hiring judgment." He forces himself to look at the actual facts. His team has delivered solid work for three years. One bug in the context of three years of solid work looks like what it is: a bug. His chest unclenches slightly. The spiral is interrupted by naming it. He addresses the bug as a technical problem, not as a referendum on his competence.
---
