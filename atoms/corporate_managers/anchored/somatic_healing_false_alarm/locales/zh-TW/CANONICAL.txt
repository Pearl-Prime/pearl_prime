### Psychological Concepts Illustrated

1.  **Health Anxiety/Hypochondria:** The tendency to interpret normal bodily variations as signs of serious illness.
2.  **Nocebo Effect:** Negative expectations leading to worsened outcomes (increased tension, perceived severity).
3.  **Mind-Body Connection:** How thoughts (catastrophizing) directly impact physical states (muscle tension, vasoconstriction causing cold hands), and how changing thoughts can reverse these effects.
4.  **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) Principles:** Identifying irrational thoughts, challenging them with evidence (doctor's visit, learning about stress), and observing the resulting change in emotional and physical state.
5.  **Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) / Mindfulness:** Allowing sensations to exist without judgment or struggle ("Symptoms stay as symptoms"), reducing suffering caused by resistance and fear.
