David has financial stress and spirals: money stress means insecurity means failure means family is at risk. His chest is heavy. Sarah has financial stress and spirals: money stress means bad planning means she's incompetent means disaster. Her hands are cold. Kevin spirals: money stress means he's not managing well means failure. His shoulders are tight. Anthony spirals: money stress means bad strategy means incompetence. His throat is tight. Tanya spirals: money stress means poor planning means disaster. Her stomach is heavy. The spirals prevent actual financial action. David interrupts: "I have financial stress. That means I need a plan, not that I'm failing." His chest unclenches. Sarah interrupts: "Financial stress needs addressing, not judgment." Her hands warm. Kevin breaks the spiral: "Financial stress is real. I need help." His shoulders release. Anthony pauses: "I need financial strategy." His throat releases. Tanya stops: "Financial stress means I need action." Her stomach settles. When they address the actual financial situation instead of the spiral, stress resolves.
