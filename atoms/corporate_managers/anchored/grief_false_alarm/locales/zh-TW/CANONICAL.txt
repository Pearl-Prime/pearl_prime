Here’s a breakdown of the psychological mechanism at play in your v01-v05 sequence, which could be useful for refining or expanding the model:

### Core Insight: The Spiral vs. The Release
The text contrasts two states:
1.  **The Spiral (Identification):** A temporary, normal human feeling (nostalgia, missing a role) is misinterpreted as a permanent, catastrophic truth (grief, crisis, displacement, wrong career). This triggers a somatic stress response (tightness, coldness).
2.  **The Release (Recognition):** The individual *recognizes* the feeling for what it is—a transient emotional state—without attaching a permanent identity or life-altering consequence to it. This leads to somatic relief (unclenching, warming, releasing).

### Why This Works (The "Embodiment" Aspect)
The model effectively links **cognitive reframing** with **somatic awareness**. It doesn’t just say "they felt better"; it shows the physical release that accompanies the mental shift. This is crucial because:
*   **Emotions are embodied:** Stress manifests physically (tight chest, cold hands).
*   **Release is physical:** Relief is often felt as a physical letting-go (unclenching, warming).
*   **Recognition interrupts the loop:** By naming the feeling accurately ("this is nostalgia," not "this is grief"), the brain stops amplifying the threat response.
