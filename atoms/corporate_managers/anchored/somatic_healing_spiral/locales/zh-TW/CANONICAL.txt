This text outlines a cognitive-behavioral and somatic framework for managing health anxiety or panic responses. It illustrates the cycle of **catastrophizing physical sensations** and the subsequent relief found through **grounding somatic practices**.

Here is a breakdown of the pattern observed in **RECOGNITION-EMBODIMENT v01-v05**:

### The Spiral Cycle (Catastrophizing)
Each individual experiences a neutral or mild physical sensation, which triggers a negative cognitive interpretation, leading to an emotional spiral:

| Person | Physical Sensation | Cognitive Distortion (The "Meaning") | Emotional/Physiological Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **大衛** | Tension / Heavy chest | "I’m broken → Body failing → Need help" | Escalated anxiety |
| **莎拉** | Tightness / Cold hands | "I’m damaged → Body wrong" | Shame/Fear |
| **凱文** | Achiness / Tight shoulders | "Something serious → Falling apart" | Dread |
| **安東尼**| Tightness / Tight throat | "Heart problem → Emergency" | Panic |
| **譚雅** | Discomfort / Heavy stomach | "Serious condition → Crisis" | Alarm |

**Core Mechanism:** *Body sensation becomes emergency.* The mind interprets normal physiological fluctuations as signs of imminent danger or structural failure.

### The Resolution Cycle (Somatic Intervention)
Each individual interrupts the spiral by engaging in a gentle, embodied action, which leads to physiological regulation and cognitive relief:

| Person | Somatic Practice | Physiological Shift | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **大衛** | Breathes with tension | Chest opens | Tension passes |
| **莎拉** | Moves with tightness | Hands warm | Tightness releases |
| **凱文** | Stretches | Shoulders relax | Tension decreases |
| **安東尼**| Practices breathing | Throat softens/opens | Calm returns |
| **譚雅** | Moves | Stomach calms | Tension releases |

**Core Mechanism:** *Somatic practice stops spirals.* By staying with the sensation and moving/breathing through it rather than resisting or catastrophizing, the nervous system regulates, and the perceived threat dissipates.

### Key Takeaways for Application
1.  **Recognition:** Identify the initial sensation without judgment (e.g., "My chest feels heavy," not "My heart is failing").
2.  **Interruption:** Notice the spiral ("This means I’m broken") and label it as a thought, not a fact.
3.  **Embodiment:** Engage in a simple, gentle physical action (breath, stretch, movement) directed *at* the sensation with curiosity rather than fear.
4.  **Observation:** Notice the natural shift in the body (warmth, softening, opening) as evidence that the body is self-regulating.

This model emphasizes that **sensations are not emergencies**—they are data. The emergency is created by the narrative attached to the sensation, not the sensation itself.
