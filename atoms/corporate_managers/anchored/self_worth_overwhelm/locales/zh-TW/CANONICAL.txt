This text outlines a somatic and psychological framework linking **overwhelm** directly to the collapse of **self-worth**, and demonstrating how regulating the nervous system (reducing overwhelm) restores a sense value.

Here is a breakdown of the pattern observed in **RECOGNITION-EMBODIMENT v01-v05**:

### Core Thesis
> **Overwhelm destroys self-worth. Removing overwhelm restores worth.**

### Key Observations for Application

1.  **Somatic Anchoring:** Each individual has a unique bodily "anchor" for their distress (chest, hands, shoulders, throat, stomach). Recognizing this specific physical sensation is the first step in recognition.
2.  **Intervention Variability:** While the goal is the same (reduce overwhelm), the methods vary slightly:
    *   *Self-regulation:* David, Sarah, Kevin, Tanya ("addresses," "manages," "reduces").
    *   *Co-regulation:* Anthony ("gets support").
3.  **Non-Linear Recovery:** Note the language of recovery:
    *   *Returns:* David, Anthony (suggests a full restoration).
    *   *Stabilizes:* Sarah, Tanya (suggests finding equilibrium).
    *   *Recovers:* Kevin (suggests a process of healing).
4.  **Physiological Correlates:**
    *   **Heavy/Cold/Tight:** Signs of sympathetic overactivation or freeze response (constriction, poor circulation, muscular armor).
    *   **Open/Warm/Relaxed/Calm:** Signs of parasympathetic activation (safety, connection, flow).
