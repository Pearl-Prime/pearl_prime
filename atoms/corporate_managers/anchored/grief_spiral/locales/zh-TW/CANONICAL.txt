This is a powerful and concise narrative arc illustrating the psychological concept of **emotional integration** versus **reactive avoidance**.

Here’s a breakdown of the structure and its therapeutic/philosophical implications:

### Core Pattern
1.  **Trigger:** A shared experience of grief (likely due to a difficult collective decision, loss, or transition).
2.  **Somatic Spiral:** Each character experiences grief not just emotionally, but *physically* (embodiment), which immediately triggers a cognitive distortion: *"This feeling means I made a mistake/I don’t belong/I must escape."*
3.  **The Turning Point:** One character (大衛) interrupts the automatic narrative with a meta-cognitive statement: *"Grief is normal. It doesn't mean I should quit."*
4.  **Collective Regulation:** The others follow suit, reframing their relationship to grief.
5.  **Resolution:** The physical symptoms release as the cognitive distortion is corrected. Grief is allowed to exist *alongside* commitment, rather than dictating action.

### Key Themes
- **Embodiment:** The text wisely links emotional states to specific physical sensations (chest, hands, shoulders, throat, stomach). This grounds the abstract emotion in tangible reality.
- **Cognitive Defusion:** The characters learn to separate the *feeling* of grief from the *meaning* they assign to it. Grief is data, not a directive.
- **Leadership & Vulnerability:** 大衛’s intervention isn’t about fixing the grief, but about normalizing it. This models healthy leadership—acknowledging pain without letting it derail purpose.
- **Collective Resilience:** The shift happens interpersonally. 大衛 starts it, but the group co-regulates. Their individual releases reinforce each other.

### Why This Works
- **Brevity:** The repetitive structure creates a rhythm that mirrors the spiral, then the breaking of it.
- **Specificity:** Each character has a unique somatic marker and cognitive fear, making them distinct despite the shared experience.
- **Hopeful Realism:** It doesn’t say the grief disappears. It says grief is *integrated*. They still grieve, but they stay.
