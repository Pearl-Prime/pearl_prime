This is a concise and powerful vignette illustrating the paradox of insomnia: **the act of monitoring one’s inability to sleep perpetuates wakefulness, while releasing that monitoring allows sleep to return.**

Here’s a breakdown of the psychological and physiological mechanisms at play in your text:

### Core Insight: The Observer Effect in Insomnia
- **"Watching prevents sleep."**  
  This mirrors the concept of **hyperarousal** and **performance anxiety** around sleep. When individuals monitor their sleep state ("Am I asleep yet?"), they activate cognitive and sympathetic nervous system responses that are incompatible with sleep onset.

### Key Therapeutic Principles Embedded
1. **Paradoxical Intention / Acceptance:**  
   Sleep often returns when the *effort* to sleep is abandoned. This aligns with Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT) and Paradoxical Intention techniques used in CBT-I (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia).

2. **Somatic Awareness & Release:**  
   Each character’s tension is localized (chest, hands, shoulders, throat, stomach), highlighting how anxiety manifests physically. Relief comes not from *fixing* the body, but from *releasing the mental grip* on the situation.

3. **Sleep Hygiene as a Grounding Tool:**  
   David’s use of "sleep hygiene" suggests structured, non-judgmental actions (e.g., dim lights, no screens) that shift focus from *outcome* (sleep) to *process* (restful behavior).
