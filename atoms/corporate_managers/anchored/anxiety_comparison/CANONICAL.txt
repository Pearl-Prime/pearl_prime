

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety_comparison/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
David's peer got promoted to SVP three years ago. David is still VP. The peer's team seems happy. The peer's promotions come through faster. The peer talks about their sabbatical. David goes home and opens his laptop after dinner. His hands tense. Something is wrong with his trajectory. Something is less than. His team performs well but not excellently. His relationships are good but not glowing. His strategic thinking is solid but apparently not visionary. The comparison activates before the evidence. His chest tightens at the gap between himself and the person doing better.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David actually looks at his peer's success and realizes it's built on different choices. The peer took a risky promotion earlier. The peer moved from engineering to operations. The peer was in the right place when the company needed someone with that profile. David made different choices and they've led to a solid career with a strong team. The comparison was treating different paths as same/better/worse when they're just different. His chest unclenches slightly. He stops running toward the peer's finish line and starts looking at his own trajectory. The anxiety of comparison drops when the comparison disappears.

## TURNING_POINT v03

BAND: 3

COST_TYPE: internal

---
Carl asks the new VP directly: "What do you see about how we operate here?" The new VP says: "You've built deep institutional knowledge and trust.
