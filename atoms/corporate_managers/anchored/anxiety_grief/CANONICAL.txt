

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety_grief/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
David was good at engineering before he became VP. He was the person who solved the hard problems. Now he solves management problems. The shift was supposed to be upward mobility. It feels like loss. He doesn't get to code anymore. He doesn't get to solve elegant technical problems. He manages people who solve elegant technical problems. His chest is heavy. The thing he was good at is now done by people he manages. The grief is quiet and persistent.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety_grief/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David acknowledges the grief instead of denying it. "I loved engineering," he says out loud. "I'm not doing that anymore. That's real." The acknowledgment creates space. He stops trying to find ways back and instead looks at what managing engineering actually offers. He can influence the problems being solved. He can shape the technical culture. He can develop people who are good engineers. The engagement is different, not nonexistent. His chest unclenches slightly. The grief is still there but it's not driving his anxiety anymore.
---

## TURNING_POINT v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/anxiety_grief/turning_point/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah tells her manager about the grief. "I miss doing the work," she says. Her manager nods. This is normal. Sarah's manager felt it too. The acknowledgment is relieving. Sarah's not the only one grieving the transition. Everyone makes this trade. The grief becomes normal instead of shameful. She can be a good manager and also acknowledge that she misses the work. Both things can be true. Her stomach settles slightly.

His shoulders release slightly. He's not grieving the loss as much once he accepts what he's gaining.
