This is a comprehensive set of narrative atoms detailing the arc of **boundary dissolution and restoration** for corporate managers. The structure effectively maps the psychological and physiological progression from unconscious compulsion (`pre_awareness`) to conscious experimentation (`experimentation`) and finally to integrated identity (`self_claim`).

Here is an analysis of the structural logic and thematic coherence across the five character arcs (大衛, 莎拉, 凱文, 安東尼, 譚雅):

### 1. Structural Progression Analysis

The data follows a clear four-stage healing arc, consistent across all five characters:

| Stage | Identity Stage | Key Mechanism | Physiological Marker | Outcome |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **RECOGNITION** | `pre_awareness` | Dissolution of boundaries; constant availability. | Tightness (chest, shoulders, throat, stomach); cold hands. | Internal cost; modeling bad behavior for teams. |
| **MECHANISM_PROOF** | `destabilization` | Consequences manifest; systems adapt to lack of boundaries. | Persistent tension; nervous system never powers down. | Social/Internal cost; ineffectiveness; team burnout. |
| **TURNING_POINT** | `experimentation` | First boundary set; anxiety spikes then subsides. | Anxiety fires, then gradual release/unclenching. | Realization that the world doesn't end; team adapts. |
| **EMBODIMENT** | `self_claim` | Boundary is normalized; rhythm established. | Openness; warmth; calm; relaxed muscles. | Sustainable performance; improved relationships. |

### 2. Character-Specific Boundary Themes

Each character represents a distinct facet of managerial boundary failure:

*   **大衛 (Temporal/Presence Boundary):**
    *   *Failure:* Blurring work/home time; physical presence but mental absence.
    *   *Cost:* Family relationship degradation.
    *   *Resolution:* Hard stop at 6 PM; reclaiming family presence.
*   **莎拉 (Availability/Responsiveness Boundary):**
    *   *Failure:* Conditioning team to expect midnight responses.
    *   *Cost:* Creation of a dependency loop; team learns to bypass their own problem-solving.
    *   *Resolution:* Ignoring after-hours texts; teaching team self-reliance.
*   **凱文 (Scope/Capacity Boundary):**
    *   *Failure:* Inability to say "no"; infinite scope creep.
    *   *Cost:* Team burnout; diluted focus; ineffective output.
    *   *Resolution:* Strategic refusal; forcing organizational prioritization.
*   **安東尼 (Cognitive/Strategic Boundary):**
    *   *Failure:* Meeting overload replacing deep work/thinking.
    *   *Cost:* Performing the role vs. doing the role; lack of strategic input.
    *   *Resolution:* Protected calendar blocks for thinking; assistant enforcement.
*   **譚雅 (Physiological/Rest Boundary):**
    *   *Failure:* Elimination of breaks; continuous operation.
    *   *Cost:* Chronic reactive mode; inability to plan; physical burnout.
    *   *Resolution:* Mandatory breaks/lunch away from desk; nervous system regulation.

### 3. Key Observations on Mechanism Depth & Cost

*   **Mechanism Depth Shift:**
    *   `RECOGNITION` (Depth 1): Focuses on the *individual's* immediate experience and internal state.
    *   `MECHANISM_PROOF` (Depth 2): Expands to show the *systemic* impact (team behavior, family dynamics, organizational effectiveness). This is crucial for showing why the behavior is unsustainable beyond just personal discomfort.
*   **Cost Type Evolution:**
    *   Starts primarily as `internal` (physical/emotional stress).
    *   Moves to `social` in `MECHANISM_PROOF` (impact on others/team expectations).
    *   Returns to `internal` in `TURNING_POINT` and `EMBODIMENT` as the focus shifts back to self-regulation and identity integration.
*   **Physiological Anchoring:**
    *   The use of specific somatic markers (tight chest, cold hands, tight shoulders, tight throat, tight stomach) provides a tangible, embodied track for the reader to follow the stress and subsequent release. This grounds the abstract concept of "boundaries" in physical reality.

### 4. Narrative Utility

This dataset is highly effective for:
1.  **Training Materials:** Illustrating the concrete consequences of poor boundary management for leaders.
2.  **Therapeutic/Coaching Contexts:** Helping clients identify their specific "boundary failure mode" (e.g., "Am I a 大衛 or a 凱文?") and see the path to resolution.
3.  **Organizational Change:** Demonstrating that setting boundaries is not selfish but *systemically necessary* for team health and effectiveness.

The progression from `pre_awareness` to `self_claim` is well-articulated, showing that boundary-setting is initially anxiety-inducing (`experimentation`) but becomes liberating and efficiency-enhancing once embodied.
