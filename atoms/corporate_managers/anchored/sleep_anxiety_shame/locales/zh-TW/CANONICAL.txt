This is a powerful vignette illustrating the shift from **shame-based suppression** to **recognition and embodiment**.

Here’s a breakdown of the psychological and somatic mechanics at play in **RECOGNITION-EMBODIMENT v01-v05**:

### 1. The Shame Loop (v01–v04)
Each character experiences insomnia not just as a physical state, but as a **moral or professional failure**:
- **大衛:** Leadership identity → "Good leaders manage stress." → *Shame: I am failing as a leader.*
- **莎拉:** Self-expectation → "I should be able to rest." → *Shame: I am broken.*
- **凱文:** Resilience identity → "I should be resilient." → *Shame: I am weak.*
- **安東尼:** Role identity (CFO) → "CFOs sleep." → *Shame: I am unprofessional.*
- **譚雅:** Control identity → "I should manage this." → *Shame: I am out of control.*

**Somatic Manifestation:**  
Shame doesn’t just live in the mind; it locks into the body:
- Chest heavy (大衛)
- Hands cold (莎拉)
- Shoulders tight (凱文)
- Throat tight (安東尼)
- Stomach heavy (譚雅)

This creates a **feedback loop**: Insomnia → Shame → Somatic tension → Increased arousal → Worse insomnia.

### 2. The Recognition Shift (v05)
The turning point is **naming the experience without judgment**:
> *"大衛 says: 'I have sleep anxiety. That's real.'"*

This is **recognition**. It moves the problem from *"I am failing"* to *"I am experiencing a real phenomenon."* This depersonalizes the shame and allows for action.

### 3. Embodiment & Resolution
Once the shame is removed, the body can respond to care rather than criticism:
- **大衛:** Chest opens → Acceptance.
- **莎拉:** Hands warm → Support received → Safety.
- **凱文:** Shoulders relax → Direct address → Release of burden.
- **安東尼:** Throat opens → Seeking help → Voice restored.
- **譚雅:** Stomach calms → Addressing anxiety → Grounding.

### Core Insight:
**"Sleep addressed improves everything."**  
But more precisely: **"Shame removed allows sleep to be addressed."**

The vignette demonstrates that **embodiment** (noticing where shame lives in the body) and **recognition** (naming the reality without judgment) are prerequisites for effective intervention. You cannot fix a problem you are ashamed to admit you have.
