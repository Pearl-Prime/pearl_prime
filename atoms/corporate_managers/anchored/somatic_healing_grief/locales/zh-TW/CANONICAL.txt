This text outlines a somatic framework for understanding and processing grief, mapping specific physical manifestations of emotional pain to targeted bodily interventions. It follows a clear **Problem (Embodiment of Grief) → Solution (Somatic Practice) → Result (Integration/Release)** structure.

Here is a breakdown and analysis of the **RECOGNITION-EMBODIMENT v01-v05** model presented:

### 1. The Core Premise
> *"Grief manifests in body."*  
> *"Somatic practice integrates grief."*

The text asserts that grief is not purely psychological but is held physically. Therefore, resolution requires physical engagement, not just cognitive processing.

### 3. Key Observations

- **Specificity of Location:** Each person holds grief in a different anatomical region (chest, hands/extremities, shoulders, throat, stomach). This reflects real-world somatic therapy principles where trauma/emotion localizes differently based on individual physiology and history.
- **Intervention Matching:**
  - **Breath** is used for core/internal constriction (大衛’s chest, 安東尼’s throat).
  - **Movement** is used for stagnation/coldness (莎拉’s hands, 譚雅’s stomach).
  - **Stretching** is used for muscular armor/tension (凱文’s shoulders).
- **Progression from Constriction to Flow:**
  - *Before:* Heavy, cold, tight, closed, knotted.
  - *After:* Open, warm, relaxed, open, calm.
- **Integration:** The final line emphasizes that the goal is not just relief, but *integration*—making the grief part of the lived experience without it causing physical distress.
