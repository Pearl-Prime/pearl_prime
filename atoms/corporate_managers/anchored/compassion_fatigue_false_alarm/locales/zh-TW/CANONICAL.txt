### Core Insight: The False Alarm of Acute Stress
The central theme is **misattribution**. High-empathy individuals often interpret the immediate somatic markers of stress (tightness, coldness, tension) as evidence of long-term depletion. This triggers a protective but counterproductive withdrawal, which actually prevents the natural resolution of the stress response.

### Application Takeaway
For leaders and caregivers:
1.  **Name the Sensation:** When you feel tightness, coldness, or tension after a difficult interaction, pause.
2.  **Check the Scale:** Ask, "Is this one event, or is this a pattern of chronic exhaustion?"
3.  **Stay Present:** Resist the urge to withdraw prematurely. Allow the discomfort to pass through rather than building walls against it.
4.  **Recalibrate:** Trust that your capacity is larger than one difficult moment.

This framework transforms compassion from a finite resource that can be "used up" into a renewable practice grounded in somatic awareness.
