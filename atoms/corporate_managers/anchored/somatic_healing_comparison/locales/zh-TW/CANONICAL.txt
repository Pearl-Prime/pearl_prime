Here’s a breakdown of the psychological and somatic dynamics at play in **RECOGNITION-EMBODIMENT v01-v05**:

### 1. The Trigger: Comparison as Somatic Threat
Each character experiences a physical tightening when they observe someone else who appears more relaxed or capable. This reflects a common psychological pattern:
*   **Perceived Deficiency:** Seeing another’s ease highlights one’s own discomfort, creating a sense of “wrongness” or inadequacy.
*   **Sympathetic Nervous System Activation:** The body responds to this perceived social/physical threat with tension (tight chest, cold hands, tense neck/throat, tight stomach).
*   **Feedback Loop:** The tension reinforces the belief that something is wrong, which creates more tension.

### 2. The Turning Point: Recognition
The line *“Comparison proves 大衛's body is wrong”* is the pivotal moment of insight. It names the destructive mechanism: **comparison is not a neutral observation; it is a judgment that harms the self.**

### 3. The Response: Embodiment Practices
Each character engages in a specific, grounded practice to return to their body—not to fix it, but to *inhabit* it:
*   **大衛 (Chest):** Breath → Opens the chest. (Breathwork directly impacts thoracic tension.)
*   **莎拉 (Hands/Shoulders):** Stretching → Softens and warms. (Movement increases circulation and reduces muscular guarding.)
*   **凱文 (Neck/Shoulders):** Movement → Loosens. (Dynamic motion breaks static tension patterns.)
*   **安東尼 (Throat):** Breathing → Opens. (Conscious breath relaxes the laryngeal and pharyngeal muscles.)
*   **譚雅 (Stomach):** Release → Calms. (Letting go of abdominal bracing activates the parasympathetic nervous system.)

### 4. The Core Truth: “Healing his own body beats comparing.”
This final line is the thesis. It asserts that:
*   **Healing is internal:** It comes from attending to one’s own sensations, not from measuring against others.
*   **Agency lies in action:** Each person takes a small, concrete step to care for their body.
*   **Comparison is a distraction:** It pulls attention outward and away from the present-moment experience where healing occurs.
