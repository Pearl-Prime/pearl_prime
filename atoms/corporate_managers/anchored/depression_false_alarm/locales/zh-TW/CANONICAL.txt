### Core Theme: The Danger of Diagnostic Overreach
The central conflict is not the presence of negative emotions (sadness, low energy, disengagement), but the **interpretive framework** applied to them. By labeling transient states as "clinical depression," the characters trigger a stress response that reinforces the very symptoms they fear.

### Phase-by-Phase Breakdown

#### 1. RECOGNITION v01-v05 (The Mislabeling)
*   **Cognitive Error:** Equating *state* (temporary) with *trait* or *disorder* (chronic/clinical).
*   **Physiological Reaction:** The body responds to the *threat* of the label. Thinking "I am depressed" is perceived by the amygdala as a danger signal, triggering sympathetic nervous system activation (fight/flight/freeze).
    *   *大衛/Chest:* Anxiety/constriction.
    *   *莎拉/Hands:* Poor circulation due to stress vasoconstriction.
    *   *凱文/Shoulders:* Defensive posturing/tension.
    *   *安東尼/Throat:* Globus sensation (anxiety-induced tightness).
    *   *譚雅/Stomach:* Gut-brain axis disruption (nausea/cramping).

#### 2. MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05 (The Self-Fulfilling Prophecy)
*   **Behavioral Consequence:** Because they believe they are clinically ill, they act as if they are incapacitated.
    *   *大衛:* Treats a bad day like a crisis → prevents natural recovery.
    *   *莎拉:* Makes major decisions from a place of panic → creates actual life disruption.
    *   *凱文:* Changes everything → destabilizes his routine, causing more fatigue.
    *   *安東尼:* Steps back → isolates himself, reducing social buffering.
    *   *譚雅:* Prepares for major changes → anticipatory anxiety worsens the flatness.
*   **Result:** The physiological symptoms persist ("stay tight," "stay cold") because the perceived threat remains active. The "false alarm" becomes a real disruption.

#### 3. TURNING_POINT v01-v05 (Cognitive Reappraisal)
*   **Intervention:** Reality testing and differentiation.
    *   *Key Distinction:* "One bad day" vs. "Depression." "Low motivation" vs. "Clinical Anhedonia."
    *   *Action:* They check the criteria. They calibrate their internal alarms. This is a form of **Cognitive Defusion** – separating the thought ("I am depressed") from the fact ("I am having a hard week").
*   **Physiological Shift:** As the cognitive threat diminishes, the parasympathetic nervous system engages (rest/digest).
    *   *Unclenches, Warms, Releases, Settles.* The body signals safety.

#### 4. EMBODIMENT v01-v05 (Integration & Resilience)
*   **New Normal:** Acceptance of emotional variability without pathologizing it.
    *   *大衛:* Has bad days, but they are just days. Chest open = ability to breathe deeply and remain present.
    *   *莎拉:* Low motivation is data, not destiny. Hands warm = readiness for action when appropriate.
    *   *凱文:* Low energy is a signal to rest, not a sign of collapse. Shoulders relaxed = no need for defensive posturing.
    *   *安東尼:* Disengagement is occasional. Throat open = ability to communicate needs.
    *   *譚雅:* Flat periods are part of the cycle. Stomach calm = trust in the body's baseline.

### Key Takeaway for Application
This model demonstrates that **mental health literacy** must include **nuance**. Understanding the difference between:
*   Sadness vs. Depression
*   Fatigue vs. Chronic Fatigue Syndrome/Burnout
*   Worry vs. Generalized Anxiety Disorder

...is crucial. When we mislabel normal human experiences as pathological, we create unnecessary suffering. The path to well-being involves **accurate labeling**, **tolerance of discomfort**, and **trust in the body's natural regulatory systems**.
