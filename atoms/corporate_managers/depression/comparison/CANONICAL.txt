David watches his colleagues animate through a strategy discussion and he cannot feel a thing. They are energized, debating, alive. He is grey. The comparison has always been about performance — who is ahead, who is behind. Now the comparison is about capacity to feel. His peers can care about this meeting. David cannot. His chest is hollow. Then his peer mentions, quietly afterward, that she started antidepressants last year. She is animated now because she got help. The frame cracks. David has been comparing his untreated depression to his peer's treated one and calling the gap a character flaw. His hands are cold. She did not out-perform him. She out-healed him. She asked for help and he interpreted his refusal to ask as evidence of his inferiority. His shoulders drop. The comparison was always between someone who sought help and someone who was too proud to seek it.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David cannot get out of bed. His peer texted about an exciting new project. David stares at the text and feels nothing — not envy, not interest, not even the comparison's usual sting. The depression has consumed even the comparison. He is too flat to measure himself against anyone. He lies in bed and the absence of the comparison's energy is its own revelation. His chest is stone. The comparison required energy. It required caring about who was ahead. The depression ate the caring and the comparison starved. In the silence where the comparison used to run, David hears something else: he is sick. Not behind. Not inferior. Sick. The frame shatters with the simplicity of the diagnosis. The comparison was treating a medical condition as a character deficit. He does not need to catch up to his peers. He needs to see a doctor. His eyes are dry. He picks up the phone. Not to compare. To call for help.

David is in treatment now. He walks into the office and his peer is still more energized, more animated, more visibly alive. The comparison flickers. David lets it pass. He is recovering at his own pace. His chest is not open but it is not locked. He does his work with the energy he has. He does not measure it against anyone else's output. His hands are cool but not cold. He is a person in recovery from depression who is working within his current capacity. The comparison says he should be further along. Recovery says he is exactly where he needs to be. He believes recovery over the comparison today. Tomorrow might be different. Today is enough.

## EMBODIMENT v03

---
David feels interest in his work for the first time in two years. Small. Fragile. Real. A spark in his chest that was not there yesterday. He does not compare the spark to anyone else's fire. He protects it. He works on the thing that interests him for one hour and the spark holds. His hands are warm. He is not behind his peers. He is not ahead. He is a person who lost the ability to care and is slowly, carefully, learning to care again. The comparison is irrelevant to this process. No one else's trajectory matters. David's spark matters. He tends it the way he would tend a plant — carefully, patiently, without comparing it to a forest. His chest is warm for the first time in months. He lets the warmth be enough.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David sees the comparison-depression feedback loop. The depression makes him flat and slow. The comparison measures his flatness against others' energy and declares him deficient. The declaration of deficiency deepens the depression. The deeper depression makes him flatter. The comparison runs again and the gap is wider. His chest is heavy. The pattern persists because the comparison does not know he is depressed. It measures output without accounting for the illness reducing the output. It is measuring a sick body against healthy bodies and calling the difference failure. His hands are numb. The pattern persists because the comparison is the worst possible tool for evaluating depression — it guarantees he will always fall short.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David realizes the comparison accelerated his depression. For years he compared himself to peers and the comparison confirmed what the depression whispered: you are less than, you are falling behind, you are inadequate. The comparison gave the depression evidence. The depression gave the comparison fuel. They collaborated. His chest is crushed. He sees the full architecture in his therapist's office: the depression said he was worthless and the comparison proved it. Every peer's success became proof of David's illness. Every comparison was a confirmation of the depression's thesis. The pattern persists because the comparison and the depression are allies. They tell the same story from different angles. The depression says: you are fundamentally insufficient. The comparison says: look at the evidence. Together they are airtight. His eyes fill. He has been fighting a war on two fronts and both fronts were inside him.
