

## RECOGNITION v01-v05

---
David feels flat and depressed. His chest is heavy. The shame fires: good leaders don't get depressed. He should be able to handle this. Sarah feels hopeless about her role and the team. Her hands are cold. The shame is immediate: she's supposed to stay engaged. Kevin feels disconnected from his work. His shoulders are tight. The shame: real leaders don't feel this way. Anthony feels empty about accomplishments. His throat is tight. The shame: he should feel satisfied. Tanya feels numb about what used to energize her. Her stomach is tight. The shame: she should still care.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01-v05
---

---

Kevin distances from his team because connection is too hard. His shoulders stay tight. Anthony goes through the motions without feeling. His throat stays tight. Tanya covers the numbness with activity. Her stomach stays tight. Depression compounds because they're all hiding it.

---

Kevin cannot make himself care about the quarterly review. The depression has flattened his engagement. The shame fires immediately: good leaders do not stop caring. Something is wrong with him. He should be able to feel something about numbers that affect his team. His face burns. His chest is both empty and burning — the depression's void filled with the shame's heat. Then his therapist asks: "What if the not-caring is a symptom, not a choice?" The frame cracks. Kevin has been shaming himself for a symptom of illness the way someone might shame themselves for coughing. The depression reduced his capacity to engage. The shame treated the reduction as a moral failure. His jaw loosens slightly. He is not a bad leader for feeling nothing. He is a sick leader who needs treatment, not judgment.

Being seen in depression was met with water and quiet company. The frame shatters: the shame has been forcing him to perform wellness inside an illness. The performance is exhausting the sick body further. The shame is making the depression worse by demanding he hide it.

Kevin's depression lifts enough to feel things again. The first thing he feels is the residual shame — months of it, stored and waiting. He sits with it. He does not run. He does not perform recovery. He lets the shame surface and examines it: every moment he hid, every lie about being fine, every performance of wellness. The shame is real. He was ashamed of his illness. He is no longer ashamed of having been ashamed. The distinction is subtle and everything. He was a person who was sick and afraid and hiding. Now he is a person who was sick and is recovering and is learning to stop hiding. His chest is clear for the first time in months. His hands are warm. The depression may return. The shame will try to return with it. Kevin knows what shame looks like now. He will recognize it faster next time. He will name it sooner. He will not let it force him to hide for as long. He is not cured. He is aware. Awareness changes everything.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin traces the shame-depression loop. The depression arrives. The shame fires: you should not be depressed, something is wrong with you, good leaders do not feel this way. The shame does not help the depression. It converts the depression into a character flaw. The conversion makes the depression heavier because now it carries both the illness and the shame about the illness. His chest is double-weighted. The pattern persists because the shame is faster than the depression. The depression moves slowly — it settles in over weeks. The shame fires instantly the moment the depression is detected. The shame has first response. It frames the depression as failure before the depression can be recognized as illness. His hands are cold. The shame is the doorman. Everything the depression brings is labeled inadequacy before it even enters the room.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin realizes the shame has been blocking treatment for years. Every time he considered therapy, the shame said: you should be able to handle this alone. Every time he considered medication, the shame said: strong people do not need pills. Every time he considered telling someone, the shame said: they will see you differently. The shame guarded every exit from depression and turned him back. His chest is empty. He sees the full architecture: the shame is not a response to depression. It is the prison guard. It keeps him inside the illness by shaming every attempt to leave it. The pattern persists because the shame has convinced him that seeking help is more shameful than being sick. So he stays sick. He stays silent. He stays in the dark because the shame says the light is worse than the dark. His eyes are wet. The shame is killing him slowly and he has been too ashamed to stop it.
