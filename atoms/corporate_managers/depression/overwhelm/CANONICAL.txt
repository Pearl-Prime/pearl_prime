## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah is depressed and overwhelmed and the combination is paralysis. The depression says nothing matters. The overwhelm says everything matters. She sits at her desk unable to act because the two systems cancel each other out. Her chest is concrete. Then she recognizes the stalemate. The depression and the overwhelm are not the same thing. They are separate conditions producing opposite instructions. She has been obeying both and therefore doing nothing. She picks one instruction: the overwhelm says this email matters. She answers it. The depression disagrees. She answers it anyway. Her hands are shaking. The frame cracks — she does not need both systems to agree. She needs to pick one action and override whichever system objects. The paralysis was not from the depression or the overwhelm. It was from the conflict between them. She answered one email. The act of doing one thing breaks the stalemate.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah's depression lifts slightly and the overwhelm floods in. The depression was a dam. Behind the dam: every email she did not answer, every project she let slide, every relationship she neglected during the dark months. The backlog is real and enormous. Her chest seizes. She cannot face it. She considers letting the depression return because the flatness was easier than this. Then she sees the frame: the overwhelm is not new. It was always there. The depression just hid it. Now she has to face both the depression's residue and the overwhelm it concealed. The frame shatters. Recovery is not just about the mood lifting. Recovery is about facing what the mood buried. Her body shakes. She sits with the enormity. She does not retreat into flatness. She stays with the overwhelm because staying means she is recovering. The overwhelm is the cost of getting better. She is willing to pay it.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah faces the backlog with a system. She triages. She deletes what no longer matters. She delegates what others can handle. She does three things per day — not thirteen, not thirty, three. Her chest is tight but her hands are moving. The depression is present. The overwhelm is present. She works between them like walking a path between two walls. Neither wall falls. Neither closes in. She moves forward. By week's end, the backlog is smaller. By month's end, it is manageable. She did not conquer the overwhelm. She reduced it one action at a time while carrying depression in her other hand. Her body is tired. Her identity is different now: she is a person who can function inside limitation. Not thrive. Function. Functioning inside limitation is its own kind of strength.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The organization goes through another crisis and the overwhelm reaches levels that would have triggered Sarah's depression before. She feels the pull toward flatness — the temptation to shut down, go numb, let the depression handle it. She resists. She calls her therapist. She adjusts her boundaries. She tells her team she needs help. She does not go flat. She stays overwhelmed and functional and honest. Her chest is tight. Her hands shake. She makes decisions under pressure without the depression consuming her. By the end of the crisis, she is exhausted but present. Not grey. Exhausted. The distinction matters. She chose to feel the overwhelm instead of letting the depression protect her from it. Feeling the overwhelm was harder. Feeling the overwhelm was also living. She chose living.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah sees the mechanism: the overwhelm triggers the depression and the depression enables the overwhelm. When the demands exceed her capacity, her body shuts down. The shutdown is the depression arriving as a circuit breaker. But the shutdown does not reduce the demands. It just delays them. When the depression lifts, the demands are larger because they accumulated during the shutdown. The pattern persists because the depression is a pause button, not a solution. It pauses the overwhelm and then the overwhelm plays at higher volume when the pause ends. Her chest is heavy. She has been cycling between overwhelm and depression for years — each one handing off to the other, neither resolving.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah lies in bed unable to move. The depression has won today. She thinks about her inbox — two hundred unread emails. The thought triggers a wave of overwhelm so intense that the depression deepens in response. She sees the full loop: thinking about the overwhelm triggers the depression and the depression makes the overwhelm unaddressable. She cannot fix the overwhelm because the depression immobilizes her and she cannot fix the depression because the overwhelm gives it fuel. Both systems are in equilibrium — a terrible, stable equilibrium where nothing changes. Her hands are limp. The pattern persists because breaking one system without breaking both is impossible. Addressing the overwhelm requires energy the depression does not permit. Addressing the depression requires reducing the overwhelm that the depression prevents her from reducing. She is a closed system. Nothing enters. Nothing leaves. She lies in bed and the equilibrium holds.
---
