### 1. The Mechanism is a Closed Loop, Not a Linear Problem
**MECHANISM_PROOF v05 & v06** clarify why conventional "wellness" fails here. You are not dealing with two separate issues (stress and mood); you are dealing with a **mutually reinforcing circuit**.
*   **The Circuit Breaker:** Depression acts as a protective shutdown when overwhelm exceeds capacity.
*   **The Accumulation:** Because the shutdown doesn't reduce the external demands, the backlog grows.
*   **The Trap:** When the depression lifts slightly, the accumulated backlog triggers *more* overwhelm, which triggers *deeper* depression.
*   **The Equilibrium:** As noted in v06, this is a "stable equilibrium." It feels terrible, but it is predictable. Breaking it requires disrupting *both* sides simultaneously, which feels impossible because each side disables the tool needed to fix the other.

### 2. The Turning Point is Cognitive Dissonance, Not Relief
**TURNING_POINT v06** is the critical pivot. The breakthrough wasn't that the depression left or the workload decreased. The breakthrough was the realization that **the paralysis was caused by the conflict between the two systems, not the systems themselves.**
*   Depression says: *Nothing matters.*
*   Overwhelm says: *Everything matters.*
*   Result: *Stasis.*
*   **The Hack:** You don't need them to agree. You only need to pick one instruction (e.g., "This email matters") and override the objection of the other system. This is the move from passive victim of the loop to active operator within it.

*   When the depression (the dam) breaks, the flood (overwhelm) returns.
