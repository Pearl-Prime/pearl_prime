### 2. The False Positive of Engagement (Takeaways 03, 04, 06)
"Performing engagement while feeling nothing" is the ultimate **false positive**. You are signaling care and presence, but the internal signal is flat. This creates a dangerous disconnect:
*   **The Risk:** As you noted in your memories, this leads to **imagined suffering** vs. **genuine connection**. If you are a "ghost version" of yourself in one-on-ones, you cannot accurately read your team’s needs, nor can they trust your feedback.
*   **The Shift:** By activating compassion **only when genuinely needed**, you stop the continuous scan. This allows you to be fully present when it matters, rather than partially present all the time.

"The depression used your responsibility as leverage." This is a critical insight into the **systemic roots of burnout**. You inherited a model where **self-sacrifice = good leadership**. Stopping feels like betrayal because the system rewards overextension.
*   **The Shift:** You are redefining leadership presence through **boundaries**. Recognizing that "passion doesn't survive when everything is redirected to survival" allows you to protect your **interiority**. Recovery isn’t just rest; it’s reclaiming the capacity for anticipation without heaviness.
