## TURNING_POINT v01-v05
---

---
David stops the spiral: "I'm depressed. That's not the same as broken." His chest unclenches. Sarah interrupts: "Feeling numb doesn't mean I should quit." Her hands warm. Kevin breaks in: "Disconnection is a symptom, not permanent." His shoulders release. Anthony pauses: "Emptiness is depression, not reality." His throat releases. Tanya stops: "Flatness is treatable." Her stomach settles. They all address the depression underneath, not the spiral above it.

Sarah spirals about her depression. She is depressed, which means she is failing, which means she is a burden, which means her team is suffering, which means she should resign, which means she will lose her income, which means she will lose everything. Each loop adds weight to a body already crushed. Her chest is concrete. Then she notices: the spiral is the only thing with energy. The depression has flattened everything except the spiral. The spiral runs on anxiety fuel even when the depression has consumed all the joy fuel. The frame cracks. The spiral is not processing her depression. It is the one system that still works in a depleted body, and it is using its remaining energy to make things worse. If the spiral stopped, the energy it consumes might be available for something else. Her hands unclench slightly.

Sarah spirals about her depression for seventy-two hours straight. She does not sleep. She does not eat well. She spirals through the night and through the day and through the next night. By the third morning she is so depleted that the spiral stops — not because she resolved anything but because her body has no more fuel. She lies in the silence and something shifts. The spiral consumed her last reserves and in the empty aftermath, the depression is just depression. Not spiraled depression. Not catastrophized depression. Just: she is sad. She is flat. She is unwell. Without the spiral adding layers, the depression is simpler. Terrible but simple. The frame shatters: the spiral has been making the depression complex when the depression is actually straightforward. She is sick. She needs help. That is all. Her body rests for the first time in three days.

The worst depression episode returns. Sarah's mood drops to the floor. The spiral lunges — this proves you will never recover, this proves treatment is not working, this is permanent. Sarah feels every loop fire. Her chest is heavy. Her hands are cold. She does not follow. She calls her therapist. She adjusts her medication. She tells her team she needs a lighter week. She acts inside the depression without spiraling about it. The spiral screams. She acts. By week's end, the episode is lifting. The spiral's prediction — permanent, untreatable, terminal — was wrong again. Sarah recovers. The spiral lied. She survived the depression by refusing to let the spiral turn it into a catastrophe. The depression was hard enough without the spiral's additions. She managed the hard without the additions. Her chest opens. She breathes. She is still here.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah maps the spiral-depression interaction. The depression provides the content: hopelessness, worthlessness, flatness. The spiral provides the architecture: if I am hopeless now then I will always be hopeless and if I am always hopeless then nothing will work and if nothing works then I should give up. The depression says: I feel bad. The spiral says: you will always feel bad. The spiral takes a temporary state and makes it permanent. Her hands are cold. The pattern persists because the spiral converts episodes into prognoses. A bad week becomes evidence of a bad life. A flat morning becomes proof of a flat future. The spiral's contribution to the depression is not more sadness — it is the elimination of hope. The depression might lift on its own. The spiral makes sure she does not believe it can.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah has been spiraling about her depression for so long that the spiral IS the depression now. She cannot distinguish between feeling depressed and thinking about feeling depressed. The two have merged into a single state of recursive suffering. Her chest is a loop. Her thoughts are a loop. She is depressed about being depressed and spiraling about spiraling. She sees the full machinery from inside it: the spiral metabolized the depression into fuel and now runs on it. The depression provides hopelessness and the spiral converts hopelessness into evidence of permanent hopelessness and the permanent hopelessness deepens the depression. The system is self-sustaining. It needs no external input. She is a closed loop of suffering that generates its own energy. Her eyes are flat. The pattern persists because the spiral cannot run out of fuel when it runs on depression and the depression cannot lift when the spiral constantly reinforces it. She is a perpetual motion machine of pain.
