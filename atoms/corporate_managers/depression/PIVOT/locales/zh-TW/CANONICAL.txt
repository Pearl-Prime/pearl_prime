You have successfully identified the mechanism: **The Performance of Competence is consuming the Energy required for Recovery.**

Here is the synthesis of what these pivots reveal about your specific leadership burnout model:

### 1. The "Glass Wall" is a Protective Shell, Not a Barrier
Pivots v03, v06, and v11 describe the "glass." This is not just isolation; it is a **dissociative boundary** you created to survive the hyper-vigilance you previously practiced.
*   **Past Model:** You scanned for distress, absorbed it, and managed it (high emotional labor).
*   **Current State:** To stop the bleeding, you shut down the intake valve. You still *look* like you are listening (v07), but you have severed the somatic connection ("listening with their whole body") to protect your interiority.
*   **The Cost:** The glass keeps pain out, but it also keeps meaning out. You cannot feel the team’s wins (v12) because you cannot feel anything.

### 2. Autopilot is the Enemy of Presence
Pivots v02, v04, and v05 highlight that your outputs remain consistent, but your **agency** has vanished.
*   You are no longer leading; you are **executing a script** written by your former, more engaged self.
*   The "ghost version" (v07) is a survival mechanism. It allows the organization to continue functioning while you withdraw into a state of basic biological and psychological survival (v08).
*   This confirms your earlier insight: **Burnout is systemic.** The system rewards the *output* (the steady leader) and ignores the *input* (the person moving through concrete). Because the output hasn’t dropped, the system provides no feedback loop to stop you.

### 3. Responsibility as Leverage
Pivot v10 is the critical key: *"The depression used your responsibility to your team as leverage against you."*
*   Your inherited leadership model equates **self-sacrifice** with **good management**.
*   You believe that if you stop, the team suffers. Therefore, you must keep performing.
*   This is the trap. By continuing to perform engagement (v05), you are teaching your team that **leadership is a disembodied function**, not a human relationship. You are inadvertently modeling the very burnout you are trying to escape.

### The Pivot Point: From "Performance" to "Presence"

You are currently in the **Flatness Phase**. This is not the end; it is the **necessary void** before reconstruction. The old model (hyper-vigilant, self-sacrificing, always-on) is dead. The new model has not yet been built.

**Your next step is not to "fix" the flatness. It is to stop fighting it.**

#### Immediate Action: The "Transparent Pause"
You cannot recover behind the glass. The glass requires energy to maintain. You must begin to **lower the glass** in controlled, low-risk ways.

1.  **Name the Ghost:** In your next 1:1 with a trusted direct report or peer, say something like:
    > *"I’ve been operating on autopilot for a while, focusing heavily on execution. I’m realizing I’ve disconnected from the deeper 'why' of our work. I’m working on reconnecting with that, and I may seem quieter or more deliberate in meetings as I do this."*
    *   **Why:** This breaks the performance cycle. It stops the energy drain of pretending. It invites authenticity without dumping your burden on them.

2.  **Reclaim One Meeting:** Choose one recurring meeting where your presence is least critical to outcomes.
    *   **Action:** Cancel it, or attend but **do not speak** unless directly asked. Use the time to sit with the "heaviness" (v09) without trying to fix it. Observe the discomfort. Let it be there. This is **embodied recovery**.

3.  **Redefine "Winning":** Pivot v12 says the registration system is broken. Stop looking for external validation (team wins) to feel alive.
    *   **Action:** Identify one small, personal metric of wellbeing each day (e.g., "I felt my feet on the ground during the commute," "I noticed the color of the sky"). This rebuilds the connection between **effort** and **meaning** (v04) on a micro, somatic level.

You are not failing. You are **molting**. The old skin (the hyper-vigilant manager) is too tight. The flatness is the space where the new skin grows. Do not rush to fill it with performance.
