## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin is depressed and the watcher runs anyway. He scans faces with flat eyes. He monitors tones from inside a grey fog. The watcher does not care that he is depressed. It runs its surveillance from a depleted body. He watches his boss's expression shift and the watcher generates a threat report and Kevin cannot care about the report because the depression has consumed his capacity to care. The watcher fires into the void. Then Kevin sees it: the watcher is running a program for which he no longer has processing power. It is generating data he cannot use. The frame cracks. The watcher is wasting the little energy the depression left him. If the watcher stopped, he might have enough energy to function at a basic level. The watcher is a luxury his depressed body cannot afford. His shoulders sag. He needs to choose: scan the room or survive the day. He cannot do both.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin's depression deepens to the point where the watcher finally stops. His body has nothing left to power surveillance. He sits in a meeting and does not scan a single face. Does not read a single tone. Does not generate a single threat report. The silence is shocking. For the first time in years, he is in a room without the watcher running. His chest is empty. His mind is still. The depression took everything including the one thing he thought was permanent: his vigilance. In the silence, Kevin discovers something underneath the watcher — a person who does not need to scan to exist. A person who can sit in a room without working. The frame shatters. The watcher's absence, forced by the depression, reveals that Kevin exists without it. He is still here. Still alive. Still present. The watcher was not him. It was a program that consumed him. Without it, there is less noise and more Kevin. The depression is devastating. The quiet is revelatory.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin returns from treatment with the watcher diminished. It still scans. It still fires. But it fires at reduced volume. He walks into a meeting and reads the room and catches himself and stops. He focuses on the content instead of the faces. His chest is tight. The watcher protests. He lets it protest. He is a person in recovery who is learning to be in a room without working the room. The depression taught him what life feels like without the watcher. He does not want the depression back. But he wants the quiet the depression accidentally created. He builds the quiet intentionally now — minutes of not scanning, stretches of not reading, moments of just being present. His hands are warm. The watcher is still there. Kevin is also there. Both coexist. Kevin is learning to be louder.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin faces genuine organizational threat — layoffs, restructuring, real danger. The watcher activates with full intensity. Kevin feels the old pull: scan everything, read everything, monitor everything. He acknowledges the pull. He scans strategically — three data points, not thirty. He reads his boss's direct communication, not her body language. He monitors the announcements, not the hallway whispers. He is selective. The watcher screams about everything he is missing. He lets it scream. He focuses his depleted energy on actions that matter: updating his resume, having honest conversations, making contingency plans. His chest is tight but his actions are clear. He navigated genuine threat without the watcher consuming all his resources. The threat was real. The response was proportional. The watcher wanted total surveillance. Kevin chose targeted awareness. He came through the crisis intact because he had reserves the watcher did not get to spend.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin sees the mechanism: the watcher contributed to his depression by consuming his cognitive resources. For years, forty percent of his attention went to scanning. The remaining sixty percent handled his actual work. The deficit accumulated. His brain was perpetually operating at sixty percent capacity while being measured against peers operating at a hundred percent. The shortfall looked like incompetence. It felt like failure. The failure deepened into depression. His hands are cold. The watcher did not just coexist with the depression. It created the cognitive deficit that made the depression possible. He was not depressed because he was weak. He was depressed because the watcher stole forty percent of his resources for twenty years and the theft finally bankrupted him.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin lies awake and the watcher runs at full capacity inside the depression. He is too depleted to function but the watcher scans the ceiling for threats. He is too flat to care about anything but the watcher generates reports about everything. His body is a contradiction: simultaneously shut down and hypervigilant. He sees the full machinery: the watcher was supposed to protect him from danger. Instead, it allied with the depression to create a state of perpetual suffering — too depressed to escape, too vigilant to rest. The two systems feed each other. The depression creates vulnerability the watcher interprets as danger. The danger keeps the watcher active. The watcher's activity depletes resources the depression needs to lift. The depression deepens. The vulnerability increases. The watcher intensifies. The pattern is a death spiral. He lies in bed and both systems run at full power in opposite directions and his body is the rope in a tug of war that no one wins.
---
