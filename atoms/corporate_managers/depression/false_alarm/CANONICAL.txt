

## RECOGNITION v01-v05
---

---
David has a bad day and thinks he's depressed. His chest tightens. One bad day means depression? Sarah feels unmotivated one day and spirals into clinical depression. Her hands are cold. Kevin has low energy and thinks something is seriously wrong. His shoulders tense. Anthony feels unengaged for a week and assumes depression. His throat tightens. Tanya has a period where nothing feels exciting and spirals into depression. Her stomach is tight. They all escalate temporary mood states into clinical conditions.

---

## TURNING_POINT v01-v05

---

Tanya recognizes: temporary flatness isn't depression. Her stomach settles. They calibrate alarms to actual criteria.

Sarah sits in the grey flatness of depression and her alarm fires anyway. Her body is depleted, her mood is absent, and the alarm still insists something terrible is about to happen. She feels nothing except the alarm telling her to feel afraid. Her chest is both hollow and tight — an impossible combination. Then she realizes the alarm is firing into a vacuum. There is no specific threat. There is no identifiable danger. The alarm is running its program inside a depressed body that cannot even produce the anxiety the alarm demands. The frame cracks. The alarm and the depression are fighting over the same body. The depression says: nothing matters. The alarm says: everything is dangerous. She is caught between apathy and terror. The alarm was supposed to protect her but it is tormenting a person who can barely function. Her shoulders sag. The alarm is not helping. It is harassing an exhausted system.

It just fires. She lies on the floor and the alarm runs its program and the depression holds her down and she is trapped between two systems that do not care about each other or about her. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah maps the alarm's behavior during depression. The depression says: nothing matters, stay still, disengage. The alarm says: everything is dangerous, stay alert, activate. She is receiving contradictory instructions from her own nervous system. Her body is both deadened and hypervigilant. Her chest is the battlefield where these two systems wage war. The pattern persists because neither system can overcome the other. The depression cannot fully flatten the alarm and the alarm cannot fully override the depression. She is trapped in the crossfire. Her hands are cold. She sees why recovery is so hard: it requires addressing two conditions that operate by opposing mechanisms. Treating the depression does not silence the alarm. Treating the alarm does not lift the depression. They are independent systems occupying the same body and neither will yield.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah realizes the alarm contributed to her depression. For years the alarm kept her in a state of constant emergency. The emergency depleted her cortisol system. The depletion created the conditions for depression. The alarm caused the depression and now fires inside the depression it created. Her chest is crushed. She sees the origin story: she was not always depressed. She was alarmed. The alarm ran for so long and so hard that her body collapsed into depression as a shutdown response to unbearable activation. The depression is her body's circuit breaker. The alarm blew the fuse and the depression is the result. The pattern persists because the alarm does not recognize its own role in the depression and continues to fire, which continues to deplete, which continues the depression. She is caught in a causal loop where the alarm creates the condition it then worsens. Her eyes are dry. The alarm is not just comorbid with the depression. It is its ancestor.
