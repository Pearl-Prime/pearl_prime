## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David sits with the grief that feeds his depression. He lost his professional identity when the reorganization stripped his scope. He grieves the leader he was — the one with the big team, the strategic role, the seat at the table. The depression wrapped around the grief like ivy, converting loss into flatness. He stares at his diminished calendar and feels nothing. Then a former team member calls to thank him for mentoring her. The warmth is small and genuine and it cuts through the flatness. His chest responds — not with joy but with recognition. The grief is about what he lost. The depression is about losing the ability to feel the loss. Her call reconnects the wire. He can feel the grief now. It is terrible. It is also alive. The frame cracks: the depression was not just sadness. It was his body's attempt to not feel the sadness. The grief underneath is enormous and real and feeling it is the beginning of something.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David grieves and the grief opens the depression like a key. He cries at his desk — really cries, for the first time in a year. He cries for the career he lost, the identity that was stripped, the team he built and had taken away. His chest cracks open. The crying is violent and ugly and the most alive he has felt in months. The depression kept the grief locked away and the locked grief kept the depression in place. Now the grief is flowing and the depression's grip loosens. Not dissolves. Loosens. He can feel things. The things he feels are terrible. But feeling terrible is still feeling. The frame shatters: the depression was not a separate condition from the grief. It was the wall his body built to contain the grief. The grief was always the core. The depression was the defense. He cries until his chest is empty and then he sits in the emptiness and the emptiness is lighter than the depression. Something shifted. Not healed. Shifted.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David carries his grief openly now. He talks about the loss — the role, the team, the identity. He does not perform recovery. He says: "I am grieving and I am working and both are happening simultaneously." His chest aches. The depression is present at the edges. But the grief is flowing instead of dammed, and the flow keeps the depression from solidifying. His hands are warm. He is a person who lost something real and is feeling the loss instead of going numb. The grief is the more painful option. It is also the more alive option. He chose alive. His jaw is sore from the effort but his eyes are clear. He sees his new role for what it is — different, smaller, still meaningful. The grief is about what was. The work is about what is.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David mentors a younger manager who is going through a similar loss. He shares his grief openly. He says: "I lost my team and my identity and my purpose and I rebuilt from the loss." His voice is steady. His chest is full. The grief has become a resource — not because it is comfortable but because it is honest. The younger manager says: "Nobody told me leaders lose things." David says: "We all lose things. The difference is whether we grieve or go flat." His hands are warm. He chose to grieve. The grieving rebuilt him. Not the same. Different. More honest. More present. More capable of sitting with someone else's loss because he sat with his own. The depression is in the past. The grief is ongoing. He carries it openly and it makes him a better leader than the title ever did.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David sees the mechanism: the ungrieved loss became depression. He lost his role and instead of grieving, he went flat. The flatness protected him from the pain of loss but also prevented him from processing it. His chest is heavy with stored grief. The pattern persists because the depression functions as a grief substitute — instead of feeling the loss, he feels nothing. Feeling nothing is less painful than feeling the loss. But feeling nothing also prevents healing. The grief needs to flow to be processed. The depression stops the flow. He is stuck. His hands are cold. The protective logic is clear: if he stays depressed, he never has to feel the full weight of what he lost. The depression is the grief's anesthesia.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/depression/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David realizes he has been depressed since the loss and the depression is the grief refusing to be felt. The grief sits behind the depression like a lake behind a dam. Every time the depression lifts slightly, the grief leaks through and the pain is so intense that the depression rebuilds the dam. He is caught in a cycle: depression lifts, grief surfaces, grief is too painful, depression returns to block it. His chest is crushed. The pattern persists because the grief is enormous — not just the current loss but every loss he never processed. The dam is holding back years of ungrieved departures, deaths, disappointments, diminishments. If the dam breaks, the flood might be unsurvivable. So the depression keeps the dam. And the dam keeps the grief. And the grief feeds the depression. He is a person whose body chose numbness over drowning. His eyes are dry because the tears are behind the dam too.
---
