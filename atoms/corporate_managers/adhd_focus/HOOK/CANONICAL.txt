## HOOK v01
---
---
The deck is due at nine. It is now seven-forty. You have known about this deck for eleven days. You open the file, see the title slide, and something behind your eyes goes flat and static, like a screen that powers on but never loads. You know exactly what slide two needs. You can describe it out loud. Your hand rests on the trackpad and does not move. The cursor blinks in the empty text box, patient, indifferent, and you sit there watching it blink, fully aware, fully stuck.
---

## HOOK v02
---
---
Your manager Slacks: just need that headcount memo by EOD, no rush. You read it three times. Each word is simple. The whole thing is maybe four hundred words you could write in your sleep. You alt-tab to your calendar, then to email, then back to the blank doc, then to a tab about a conference you will not attend. An hour passes the way water leaves a sink, quiet and total. The memo is still four hundred words you have not begun.
---

## HOOK v03
---
---
Calibration is Thursday. You have six people to rank and a form with six boxes. You sit down to do it Monday. You sit down to do it Tuesday. The boxes are still empty Wednesday at four, and now the not-doing has its own weight, a low hum under your sternum, because every hour you did not start has made starting feel more like confessing.
---

## HOOK v04
---
---
The expense report is fourteen line items. Fourteen. You have done a thousand of these. You open the portal, and your whole body becomes a held breath. You straighten three pens on your desk. You refill a water bottle that was full. You answer a Slack that did not need answering. The portal sits open behind everything, login timed out, asking you, again, to begin.
---

## HOOK v05
---
---
Priya has eight tabs open and cannot tell you which one matters. Each is genuinely urgent. The QBR prep, the skip-level notes, the budget reconciliation, the offer letter that's been waiting two days. Her brain reaches for the most important one and comes back with nothing, just a smooth wall where the ranking should be, and so she does the eighth tab, the small one, because it's the only one her hand will actually move toward.
---

## HOOK v06
---
---
You told yourself you would write the performance review the moment your calendar cleared. It is clear now. A perfect, gleaming forty-five-minute block, no meetings, door effectively shut. And you are reading about a kitchen renovation. You are not interested in kitchens. You watch yourself read about kitchens and you think, with real bewilderment, what is wrong with me, and the cursor in the other window keeps its slow, blinking time.
---

## HOOK v07
---
---
The thing about the reorg announcement is that it has to go out today, and everyone is waiting, and you have written the first sentence nine times. Each version is fine. None of them lets you write the second sentence. You stand up. You sit down. The stakes are high and somehow that does not make starting easier, it welds your hands to your sides, and you understand for the first time that pressure is not the same as momentum.
---

## HOOK v08
---
---
Marcus knows what the right call is on the vendor contract. He has known for a week. He could say it in one sentence. Instead he keeps the email in drafts, opening it on his phone in the elevator, at lunch, at red lights, reading his own clear recommendation and not pressing send, as if sending were a different and far heavier act than knowing.
---

## HOOK v09
---
---
Your one-on-ones are scheduled. Your one-on-ones are prepped. Your one-on-ones do not exist as actual prep, only as the intention of prep, and now Dana is at your door, on time, and you have the talking points fully formed in your head and absolutely nothing on the page, and you smile and say give me one sec and the one sec contains all your panic.
---

## HOOK v10
---
---
There is a task so small you keep skipping it: forward one email to Legal. Two clicks. You have not done it in three days. It travels with you, this two-click task, heavier than the strategy doc, because the strategy doc at least feels worthy of the resistance and this does not, and the smallness of it is exactly what makes the not-doing feel like proof of something.
---

## HOOK v11
---
---
The all-hands slide is half built. You were in it, genuinely in it, fingers moving, ideas landing, and then a Slack ping pulled your eyes for four seconds and when you looked back the thread was gone. Not the file, the thread, the live wire of attention that had been running through you. You stare at the half-slide trying to find your way back in and the door it came through has closed.
---

## HOOK v12
---
---
Quarter-end is Friday and your part of the close is a single spreadsheet you have opened and not touched every day this week. You can see it now, the tab, the little green icon, mocking you with how ready it is. You are not lazy. You worked eleven hours yesterday. You just could not make your hands go toward that one specific green icon, and you still can't, and Friday keeps coming.
---

## HOOK v13
---
---
Lena's calendar says focus block in soft gray, eleven to noon. It is 11:52. She has spent the block deciding which of four equally important things to start, and the deciding ate the doing, and now there are eight minutes left and a familiar warmth of shame climbing her neck, because to anyone watching she simply sat at her desk and did nothing, and that is not what happened, and she cannot explain what did.
---

## HOOK v14
---
---
The budget cut means you have to tell someone their role is changing, and you have to draft the talking points first, and you have opened the talking-points doc forty times. Each time, your eyes slide off the blank page like water off glass. You are not avoiding the hard conversation. You would have the conversation right now. You cannot begin the document that has to come before it.
---

## HOOK v15
---
---
Your inbox has a flagged email from your skip-level, eleven days old, marked with your own little orange flag like a flare you fired and then ignored. Every morning you see the flag. Every morning your stomach does a small drop. Every morning you decide today, and the deciding is real, you mean it, and then noon comes and the flag is still orange and you are somehow more tired than if you had done it.
---

## HOOK v16
---
---
It is the simplest possible first step: open the doc and type the project name at the top. That's all. Just the name. You have been not-typing the name for twenty minutes, and the strange part, the part you'd never say in calibration, is that you can feel exactly how good it would feel to start, you can taste the relief on the other side, and the wall between you and it is made of nothing you can name and you cannot walk through it.
