## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Initiation freeze is not laziness; it is a missing first action. Your system cannot grab a task described as a goal, only as a verb. Name the smallest physical move, anchor it to a visible timer, and the wall you called willpower becomes a single step you can take.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Time-blindness means the passage of time does not register internally the way it does for others. Deadlines feel abstract until they arrive as surprises. The fix is not more discipline but more visibility: a clock in your sightline, a countdown on the second monitor, a date in writing. Externalize the sense you lack.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
Dopamine-driven prioritization steers you toward what is interesting, not what is important, because salience drives your focus. Fighting this directly fails. Instead, engineer salience onto the dull-but-urgent task with timers, deadlines you can see, and small wins up front, so your attention system finds reasons to stay.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
The knowing-doing gap is mechanical, not moral. You can fully understand what to do and still not start, because intention and initiation run on separate machinery. Stop sentencing your character for a wiring fact. Build the bridge with named actions and external anchors, not with another round of self-blame.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
---
Working memory in an ADHD-style system empties the instant something louder arrives, so a single Slack ping can erase an hour of assembled context. The defense is to externalize relentlessly: paper, lists, sticky notes, written deadlines. Stop trusting your head to hold the thread, and give it a surface that holds it for you.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---
---
Distractibility is a weak filter, not a weak will. Your attention orients hard toward whatever is most salient in the moment, by design. You cannot command the filter, but you can reduce the noise: close the channel, cover the tabs, hard-block the hour. Engineer the environment your focus cannot self-regulate against.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---
---
Starting is the entire job. The gap is never between minute four and minute forty; it is between not-doing and doing. Set a timer absurdly short, put your hands on the keys, say the next action aloud, and cross the line. Momentum is built from the first verb, not summoned from intent.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---
---
A blank page gives your system nothing to push against, which is why generating stalls while editing flows. Write the worst first sentence on purpose, talk the idea into a voice memo and transcribe it, or list the easy parts first. Convert the empty document into something you can attack, then attack it.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---
---
Body-doubling borrows activation you cannot generate alone. A silent colleague on a call, both of you grinding through stalled work, supplies the external structure your focus needs. It is not a crutch or a confession; it is a deliberate use of social presence to start tasks that refuse to start in isolation.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---
---
Novelty hijacks focus, so any disruption, a reorg, a new initiative, a fresh ping, can swallow a productive afternoon whole. This is orientation, not failure. When the new arrives, name one concrete task, finish it, and let completion restore traction before you decide what genuinely matters next.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---
---
Concrete beats important when initiation is the bottleneck. The most important task is often the least startable because it is abstract. Reach instead for the most concrete thing on your list, finish it, and let the small win supply the activation the hard task could not generate cold. Order by startability, not priority.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---
---
An attention difference is not a deficiency in standards. You can want focus intensely and still not land it, because wanting and regulating are governed by separate systems. The work is not to feel worse about the gap but to scaffold across it, with timers, anchors, and named actions, until doing becomes reachable.
---
