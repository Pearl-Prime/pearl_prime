## THREAD v01
---
---
You scheduled the hard one-on-one for noon, then watched it slide to Thursday, then next week. It was never about the person. The unscripted opening had no first action your system could grab, so it kept finding nothing to start with. You notice the dread lifts the moment the first line exists.

## THREAD v02
---
---
You promised the deck by Friday and meant it with your whole chest. Friday came and the file was still empty, and the gap stung. The intention was real; the bridge from intention to first keystroke is the part your wiring does not pave on its own. You sit with that, quietly.

## THREAD v03
---
---
Three reopened tabs, a half-typed reply, the QBR clock ticking, and somehow no forward motion for an hour. It looks like avoidance from outside. Inside, it is an attention system with no filter, pulled toward whatever is loudest. You watch the pattern without naming it a failure this time.
---

## THREAD v04
---
---
The interesting initiative pulled your whole week while the urgent one starved. Your boss noticed the imbalance before you did. It was not poor judgment; salience steered you, the way it always does when nothing engineers the dull task to stand out. You notice how reliably the bright thing wins.
---

## THREAD v05
---
---
You felt the afternoon vanish after the reorg announcement, productive intentions and all. Novelty had swallowed your focus whole and left the to-do list untouched. There was no failure of will in it, just a system that orients hard toward the new. You sit with how much the news cost you.
---

## THREAD v06
---
---
You owed the performance reviews for two weeks and reopened the doc daily without writing a word. The wins were easy to recall; the structured paragraphs refused to load cold. It was retrieval, not caring, that stalled you. You notice the document opens easier once the first easy line is down.
---

## THREAD v07
---
---
You set the deadline in your head and trusted yourself to feel it approaching. The day arrived as a surprise it had no business being. Time does not register for you the way it does for others, and the calendar only helped once it was visible. You notice the quiet of that gap.
---

## THREAD v08
---
---
The Slack ping pulled you mid-sentence and the thought was simply gone, unrecoverable. You blamed your concentration. But the thread lived in working memory, and working memory empties the instant something louder arrives. You watch how a single interruption erases an hour of assembled context, and say nothing.
---

## THREAD v09
---
---
You called the all-hands prep avoidance, but the truth was thinner and stranger: the blank document gave your system nothing to push against. Talking produced words instantly; the page produced none. It was never reluctance, only the absence of a handle. You notice how the freeze dissolves the moment you speak.
---

## THREAD v10
---
---
You finished the easy task first and felt vaguely guilty about it, like you'd dodged the real work. Then the harder one started moving in its wake. The small win had supplied the activation the big one couldn't generate alone. You notice momentum is something you build, not something you summon.
---

## THREAD v11
---
---
You watched yourself reopen the same email four times across the morning, each pass leaving it unanswered. It was not the content; it was the missing first line. The reply existed everywhere except as a place to begin. You notice the whole wall was one sentence wide, all along.
---

## THREAD v12
---
---
You judged your scattered morning as a lapse in standards, the way you always do by reflex. But the standards were intact; the regulation was the gap. Wanting to focus and being able to land focus are governed by different machinery. You notice the judgment, and let it pass without joining it.
