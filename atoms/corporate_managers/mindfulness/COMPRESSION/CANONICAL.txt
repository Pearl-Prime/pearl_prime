## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Mindfulness for a manager is not calm; it is accuracy. The racing mind narrates threats that have not arrived and meetings not yet held. Returning attention to now strips the narration and leaves the actual data, the actual person, the one real decision in front of you.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Your attention points one direction at a time. Every thread you hold half-open is a thread you are half-absent from. Presence is simply choosing the one in front of you and letting the rest wait, not because they vanish, but because divided attention serves none of them.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
The inbox, the dashboard, the next reorg are infinite and ungovernable. This message, this number, this conversation are finite and yours. Mindfulness is shrinking your field to what you can actually act on now, then acting, instead of drowning in the sum of everything.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Between every meeting there is a seam. Skip it and the day becomes one unbroken obligation that frays you. Honor it with a single breath and you hand the last meeting back to itself, arriving at the next as someone who is here, not someone still mid-sentence elsewhere.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---
---
The mind treats imagined outcomes as present emergencies. Dread of the review, the rating, the QBR question is the body bracing for a future not happening now. Notice the bracing, name it as thought, and return to the inputs you actually have, which is all you can ever work with.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---
---
Noticing without fixing is the whole move. You catch the scatter, the spiral, the blame story starting to write itself, and you do not fight it. You simply let it be seen, which loosens its grip, and you bring attention back. The catching and returning is the practice itself.
---

## COMPRESSION v07
---
---
Full attention is the most expensive thing you give and the easiest to spend absently. Fifteen minutes of real presence with one report outweighs an hour of half-listening. Mindfulness is making the spend deliberate: this person, this problem, now, with nothing else in the room.
---

## COMPRESSION v08
---
---
You will leave the present a hundred times a day, pulled by a ping, a dread, a draft forming mid-conversation. This is not failure. The practice is not staying; it is returning. Each time you notice you have left, you are already most of the way back. Come back, again.
---

## COMPRESSION v09
---
---
A red metric triggers a story before it triggers analysis. The story feels like thinking but is reflex wearing its clothes. Mindfulness inserts a breath between the trigger and the tale, long enough to look at the number plainly and ask what it shows before deciding what it means.
---

## COMPRESSION v10
---
---
Borrowed urgency, from an anxious VP, a buzzing channel, a loud all-hands, pulls your attention out of your own footing. You over-explain, you race, you fill silence. Presence is feeling your feet, saying the headline, and letting the pause stand, because urgency you did not generate is not yours to carry.
---

## COMPRESSION v11
---
---
The corridor, the elevator, the walk to the room, these in-between moments are where most of your working life actually happens, and the rehearsing mind skips them entirely. Notice the steps, the light, the breath. The seams are not waiting; they are the fabric you keep tearing past.
---

## COMPRESSION v12
---
---
Mindfulness for managers is not a retreat from the work; it is the condition for doing it well. Scattered attention manages the meetings in your head. Returned attention manages the one in the room. The whole discipline is bringing yourself, repeatedly, to where the work actually is.
---
