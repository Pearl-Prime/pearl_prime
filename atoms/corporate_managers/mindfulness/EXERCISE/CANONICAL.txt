## EXERCISE v01
---
---
Before your next 1:1, stop at the door. Feel both feet on the floor. Take one slow breath in, longer breath out. Drop the prep notes for three seconds and just notice this person is about to be in the room. Then walk in. You are not arriving from the last meeting. You are arriving here.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---
---
Mid-inbox, your jaw is tight. Pause on the open message. Unclench the jaw, let the shoulders fall an inch. Read the email once, slowly, as if you have time. You do, for this one. Reply to this one only. The other forty are not in this moment with you.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---
---
In a QBR, your mind sprints to the slide you fear. Set both palms flat on the table. Feel the cool surface. Breathe out the rehearsal. The number on screen is the number. You can only respond to what is being said right now, not the question you imagine is coming.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---
---
Before a hard message to your skip-level, take ninety seconds. Hands off the keyboard. Name one true thing about the situation, plainly, in your head. Breathe. Then write that one true thing first. Clarity lives in the present, not in the spiral of what they might think.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---
---
Walking to the all-hands, you are already drafting the Q&A in your head. Notice the corridor. The light, the floor, the distance shrinking. Three steps, three breaths. Let the room be a room you walk into, not a verdict you walk toward.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---
---
Your calendar shows a fifteen-minute block. Treat it as fifteen minutes, not a gap to cram. Close the laptop lid halfway. One breath. Ask what this quarter-hour is actually for. Do that one thing. The full hour you wish you had is not arriving; this fifteen is here.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---
---
A Slack ping pulls your eyes mid-thought. Before you click, finish the sentence you were thinking. Exhale. The ping will still be there in four seconds. Notice that the urgency was yours, not the message's. Then choose to open it, or not.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---
---
Before delivering a rating in a review, sit for a moment with the person, not the form. Soften your gaze. One slow breath. They are a whole working year, not a number. Speak the rating, then stay present for what lands on their face. Do not rush past it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---
---
Reorg news just dropped and your chest is racing. Press your feet down hard, then release. Three breaths, counting the out-breath longer. Notice: nothing has changed in this exact second except your attention. Decide what is true now, before deciding what to fear.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---
---
Between back-to-back calls, do not open the next deck yet. Stand. Roll your shoulders once. Look at something more than ten feet away for one breath. You are handing the last meeting back to itself. The next one has not started. Let this seam exist.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---
---
Managing up to an anxious VP, you feel the pull to over-explain. Plant your feet. One breath before you speak. Say the headline, then stop. Let silence sit. Presence is staying in the pause instead of filling it with reassurance you have not yet earned.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---
---
Your KPI dashboard is red and your mind leaps to blame. Hands on the desk. Breathe out the verdict. Look at one number, just one, and ask what it is actually telling you today. The story you were about to tell is not data. The number, right now, is.
---
