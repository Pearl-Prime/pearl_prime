The conference room. Glass on three sides. Your reflection faint over the parking lot. The VP is mid-sentence and you are watching your own hands flat on the cool table, and for one beat you are only that, hands on table, breath in chest, the hum of the projector. ---
Elevator, sixth floor, doors closing. Eight seconds of nobody needing anything. You watch the numbers count down and feel the small drop in your stomach as the car descends, and notice your shoulders are up by your ears again. You do not force them down. You just see them. Outside, past the lobby glass, a gray {weather_detail} you have not looked at all day. Six. Five. Four. The day waits on the other side of the doors. For now, just the drop, the shoulders, the gray.

## SCENE v03
---
---
Inbox open. Cursor blinking. Forty-one unread and your jaw has already set, teeth lightly together, a clench you did not order. You feel it now that you are looking. The screen glow is cold. The chair holds you. Nothing in the inbox has actually happened to you yet, and your body is already braced against all of it. You unclench the jaw, one millimeter, on purpose. The unread count does not change. But you are here for it now, instead of nowhere.

## SCENE v04
---
---
The 1:1. Laptop lid down, on purpose, and the desk looks bare and strange without the glow. Sara is talking. You feel the itch in your right hand, the phantom reach for the keyboard that is not there. You let the itch be an itch. Her voice fills the space the screen used to fill. For the first time in a while you can hear the small catch in it, the thing under the words. The itch fades. You lean in.

## SCENE v05
---
---
Standup, eleven faces in a grid. Someone is giving an update and your attention has slid sideways into your own to-do list. You catch it. You bring it back to the grid, to the actual voice, mid-word. You missed a sentence. You let yourself have missed it instead of covering. The voice keeps going and you are here for the rest of it, the breath in your chest steady, the to-do list quiet for now in its drawer.

## SCENE v07

---

## SCENE v08
---
---
Slack pings. Your hand is already moving. You catch it in the air, the reach, halfway to the phone, and you let your hand hover there for one breath. The ping is just a sound. The phone is just a rectangle. In the quarter-second of the hover, you are the one deciding, not the reflex. You let the breath finish. Then you choose, on purpose, and the choosing feels strange and roomy, like a door you did not know was a door.

## SCENE v09
---
---
Hallway between meetings. {street_name} traffic faint through the window. You have ninety seconds and the old instinct says fill them, check something, manage something. Instead you walk slowly and feel your feet land, heel then sole, heel then sole, the carpet firm beneath. The hallway is just a hallway. Your next meeting is not here yet. For ninety seconds there is only walking, and feet, and the faint traffic, and a quiet that you forgot was allowed.

## SCENE v10
---
---
Your reflection in the dark monitor at 6:50, office half-empty. Nobody needs anything. The quiet is almost loud and your mind, with nothing to manage, starts inventing things to manage. You watch it do that. You do not believe it. You feel your back against the chair, your feet on the floor, the cooled air on your hands. The inventing slows. The quiet stops being loud. You sit in it for one more minute before you stand, and the minute is yours.

## SCENE v11

---
The doorway of the 10am. One breath, taken on purpose, before you walk in. Feet under you. Shoulders dropping the half-inch they have held since dawn. The room is full of faces and agendas and you are about to enter it, but for this one breath you are only in the doorway, only breathing, only here. ## SCENE v12
---
---
Video call, your own face in the corner, and you keep watching it, managing it. You click it off. Gone. The relief is immediate and physical, a small loosening behind the eyes. Now there is only the speaker, the actual person, the actual words, and the room of your attention is suddenly roomy with the self-monitoring gone. You feel your shoulders, unwatched, drop on their own. You hear the meeting fuller than you have in months.
