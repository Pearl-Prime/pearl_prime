## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

COST_TYPE: internal

---
Watch what overwhelm actually does to attention, mechanically. Attention is not infinite. It is a narrow beam, and it can rest on one thing or scatter across many, but it cannot do both. When the load exceeds capacity, when there are eleven live threats and one of you, the beam stops resting and starts strobing, flicking object to object, never settling long enough to actually see. From the inside this feels like thinking about everything. It is closer to thinking about nothing, very fast. Your chest stays braced because the body reads the strobe as danger and keeps the alarm half-on. And the cost is precise and hidden: the person in front of you, the cue in their voice, the thing they almost said, all of it falls into the gaps between flickers. You are not absent because you stopped caring. You are absent because caring about everything at once is mechanically identical to being present for none of it.
