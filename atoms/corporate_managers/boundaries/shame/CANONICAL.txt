## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin tells his team he is taking a real vacation. Two weeks. No email. No Slack. His face burns as he says it. The shame fires immediately: good leaders do not disconnect. Good leaders are always available. He watches his team's faces. They look relieved. One person says: "Finally." Kevin's chest tightens. The shame predicted judgment. His team delivered permission. The frame cracks — his boundary is not selfishness. His team has been waiting for him to model what they all need. The shame insisted that setting limits would expose him as uncommitted. His team sees setting limits as leadership. His jaw loosens. The old story — that boundaries are shameful — cannot survive his team's relief.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin breaks down in a meeting. Not a single tear. A full collapse. His voice cracks. His hands shake. He says: "I cannot sustain this pace." The shame is so intense he considers walking out of the building and never coming back. His face is on fire. His stomach is acid. He has just shown everyone that he is not strong enough. His boss leans forward and says: "None of us can sustain this pace. Thank you for saying it." The room shifts. Other people exhale. Kevin's collapse was not weakness. It was the thing everyone needed someone to say. The shame predicted exile. The room provided solidarity. Kevin sits in the aftermath shaking. The old frame — that admitting limits means admitting deficiency — broke against the reality of a room full of people who felt the same way and were too ashamed to say it. He said it. The shame did not kill him. It set something free.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin says no in a meeting. His face warms. The shame fires — faint, familiar, automatic. He does not chase it. He says: "My team is at capacity. We cannot take this on without dropping something else." His voice is even. His hands are steady on the table. He is not ashamed of his limits. He is reporting them as facts. The distinction rewires the room. His director nods and reassigns the project. Kevin walks out with his shame humming in the background and his integrity intact in the foreground. He is a leader who names capacity without apologizing for it. The shame still says he should be able to do more. He lets the shame say it. He does not believe it anymore.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin applies for a lateral move. Less scope. Less prestige. Better hours. The shame is volcanic — he is stepping backward, he is admitting he cannot handle the bigger role, he is publicly downgrading. His face burns filling out the application. His hands tremble signing it. He submits it anyway. He walks into the interview and says: "I want a role that I can sustain." The interviewer does not flinch. Kevin gets the role. He starts Monday with a smaller team and a lighter calendar and a body that can breathe. The shame still whispers: you could not handle it. Kevin hears it. He responds from a different place: I chose not to be destroyed by it. He arrives home at 5:30. His daughter is still awake. He sits on the floor and plays. The shame fades to background noise. His daughter's laughter is louder.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin catches the shame-boundary loop in action. He considers blocking his Friday afternoons for strategic thinking. The shame fires: people who block their calendars are protecting themselves, not the team. He overrides the shame and blocks the time anyway. Within an hour, someone requests a meeting for that slot. Kevin feels the shame intensify — see, they need you, you are being selfish. He keeps the block. His chest is tight. He sees the machinery: the shame uses other people's requests as evidence that his boundaries are wrong. Every meeting request becomes proof that he should have been available. The pattern persists because demand is infinite and shame interprets every unmet demand as personal failure. His hands are cold. He cannot set a boundary without the shame presenting it as a moral deficiency.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin lies awake calculating what people think of him after he left early for his son's game. He replays every face in the office as he walked out at 4 PM. The shame constructs a narrative: they noticed, they judged, they are comparing his hours to theirs. His chest is crushed. His heart pounds. He sees, in the dark, the full machinery of the pattern: the shame is not about the boundary. It is about being seen as the kind of person who has boundaries. And that kind of person, in the shame's architecture, is a person who does not care enough. The shame converts every boundary into a character flaw. Every act of self-preservation becomes evidence of selfishness. His jaw aches. The pattern persists because the shame arrived before the career — it was built in childhood, in a house where rest was laziness and limits were weakness. The career just gave it a corporate vocabulary. The shame is older than his title. It will outlast it.
---
