凱文 leaves his laptop in the office on a Friday. He does not take it home "just in case." The shame spikes—a sharp, cold needle in his gut. *What if there’s an emergency? What if they need you?* He walks to his car without looking back. The weekend is quiet. No ping of notifications. No blue light in the dark. On Monday, he returns to a desk that is exactly as he left it. The world did not end. The team did not collapse. He realizes the emergency was never real; it was a phantom generated by the shame to keep him tethered. He sits down, opens his notebook, and begins to work. Not from fear, but from choice. The boundary is no longer a wall he builds against others; it is the floor he stands on.
