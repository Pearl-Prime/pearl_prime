

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
My calendar has become someone else's. I'm three tabs deep in a meeting about a meeting, shoulders tight at my ears, knowing my daughter's recital ended an hour ago and my wife handled the pickup alone. The reframe isn't that I'm giving too much—it's that I'm not the one deciding where the line is. Someone else is. My chest knows this. The time between 6 and 8 PM stopped being mine sometime in November. I can feel the weight of it in my jaw, the way I clench my teeth watching email notifications at 9:47 PM. The boundary isn't something to set with words. It's something to set with time. The calendar reflects who really decides.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: TRADE-OFF-CLARITY
---
The promotion came with a new kind of poverty. I'm making more money and have less time with the people I chose. That's not a reframe—that's a deal I made without reading the fine print. My body lives in the cost now: the way I rush through dinner, the stiffness in my neck from back-to-back calls, the fact that I can't remember the last time I read something that didn't have a business purpose. The boundary isn't about "work-life balance"—that phrase erases the truth. I traded life for work. My hands know this. I need to know what I'm actually protecting, and what I'm willing to lose to protect it.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: ATTENTION-CURRENCY
---
My attention is the resource I'm out of. Not time—everyone is out of time. But the quality of my presence, the capacity to actually listen, to think something through instead of just react, to be here instead of elsewhere: that's gone. My eyes are on my phone at my kid's soccer game. My mind is on the email I didn't send. The reframe isn't to feel guilty about this—it's to see it clearly. I've spent my attention like currency, and I'm bankrupt. The boundary that matters isn't about saying no to meetings. It's about where I spend the only thing I actually have: this moment, right now. The line I need to draw is against myself.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: CHOICE-RECOGNITION
---
I used to think boundaries were about being assertive, drawing lines, holding people to them. Now I see: I'm not boundaried. I'm just resentful. I say yes to things and feel angry about it later. That's not a boundary—that's a trap I'm building for myself. My hands are tight on the steering wheel during my commute, my breath shallow when I read slack notifications. The reframe is that every boundary is a choice about what I'm willing to let go of. I can't have it all. I haven't accepted that yet. But my body already knows the cost of pretending I can.
