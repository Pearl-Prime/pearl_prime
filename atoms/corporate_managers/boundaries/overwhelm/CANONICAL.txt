## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah has eighteen direct reports. She meets with each one weekly. Her calendar is wall-to-wall. Her chest never unclenches. She carries everyone's problems alongside her own. Then one week she cancels all her one-on-ones. Not strategically. She just cannot face another hour of absorbing someone else's stress. The week passes. Her team does not collapse. Two people solve their own problems. One delays a decision until next week. Nobody spirals. Sarah sits with the data: her constant availability was not preventing overwhelm. It was creating it. She was the bottleneck. Her frame shifts — what if boundaries do not reduce her impact but increase her team's capacity? Her shoulders drop. The overwhelm is not about volume. It is about her refusal to let anyone else carry weight.

---
Sarah's body stops cooperating. Migraines. Three in one week. She cannot look at a screen. Cannot attend meetings. Cannot answer emails. The overwhelm has exceeded her physical capacity and her body has enforced the boundary she refused to set. She lies in a dark room and the work continues without her. Projects move. Decisions get made. Her team adapts. The revelation is devastating: she is not as necessary as her overwhelm told her she was. Her chest cracks. The old frame — that she must be involved in everything or everything falls apart — shatters against the evidence of a week where everything held without her. Her body drew the boundary her mind refused. The old way is literally no longer survivable. Her body voted. She lies in the dark and begins to understand that overwhelming herself was never about the team's needs. It was about her own.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah counts her commitments. Thirty-seven active projects. Twelve committees. Eighteen direct reports. Her chest is concrete. She accepted every single one. Nobody forced them on her. She sees the machinery: saying yes prevents the discomfort of saying no. The overwhelm is not imposed. It is invited. Each yes feels safe in the moment — proof of value, proof of importance. The accumulation is the catastrophe. Her hands are numb. The pattern persists because each individual yes is painless and each individual no feels dangerous. But the aggregate yes is destroying her. She cannot see the cost of one more yes because the cost is distributed across everything. Her body is paying the total. Her mind is approving line items.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah forgets her daughter's birthday. Not the date. The party. She is in a meeting about Q3 planning when her phone buzzes with a photo from her husband — their daughter blowing out candles. Sarah was supposed to be there. She promised. The overwhelm ate the promise. Her stomach drops through the floor. Her vision blurs. She sees the full architecture with sickening clarity: the overwhelm does not just consume her time. It consumes her life. Every commitment she accepted at work was a boundary she failed to set with her family. Every yes to a project was a no to a birthday, a dinner, a bedtime story. The pattern persists because work rewards overwhelm with status and importance. Her daughter does not reward absence with anything except sadness. Sarah sits in the meeting and cannot breathe. The cost is not exhaustion. The cost is her daughter's face she is not seeing. The cost is real and irreversible and happening right now.
