---
David works until 9 PM because his team is working until 9 PM. The boundary between leadership and crisis management has dissolved. He's available all the time. His phone buzzes at dinner. He checks his email after his kid goes to bed. The boundary that should exist—between work and life—doesn't. His chest is tight even in moments that should be restful because the work presence is constant. He's not protecting his own space. He's modeling that no one gets protected space.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries_overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David sets a boundary: no work email after 6 PM. His anxiety fires immediately. What if there's a real emergency? His team needs him. His chest constricts. But then he actually sees that there are no emergencies. The emergency doesn't come. His family gets him back. His kids notice. His spouse notices. The boundary is hard but the effect is immediate. His nervous system starts to power down because it knows there will be some time when work isn't available.
---
