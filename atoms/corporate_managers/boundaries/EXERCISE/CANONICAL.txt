## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your skip-level meeting ends. Your chest tightens. Write down one thing your manager asked that felt like overreach. Name it exactly. Do not soften the language. Read it back. That discomfort is information.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
A direct report brings you their team's problem. Stop. Place both feet flat on the floor. Ask: is this mine to solve. If the answer is no, say so aloud. Practice the sentence before the next meeting.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before the reorg announcement lands, write your three non-negotiables. Not preferences. Non-negotiables. Keep the list under your keyboard. Read it before every senior conversation this week.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Someone above you adds scope without removing scope. Pause. Breathe out fully. Write the trade-off in one sentence. Send that sentence upward before accepting the work.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Notice the tightness. Name it. Set a two-minute timer. Write every task currently on your plate that belongs to someone else. Count them. That number is your starting point.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Your calendar fills without your input. Block one hour tomorrow. Label it: Protected. Do not explain the label to anyone. Hold it once. Then hold it again next week.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
After your next performance review conversation, sit down. Write one expectation your manager stated that you did not agree to. Underline it. Decide before you leave the building what you will say.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Someone escalates past you to your team. Feel the heat in your face. Let it settle. Then send one sentence to your team: bring those requests to me first. Send it today.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Before your next one-on-one with leadership, write down the ask you plan to deflect. Say your deflection out loud. Time yourself. Keep it under fifteen seconds. Shorter is stronger.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
A colleague forwards you a crisis at 9 PM. Do not open the attachment. Write one sentence response. Set it to send at 8 AM. Your boundary lives in that delay.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Identify one recurring meeting that drains without producing decisions. Write the meeting name. Write one word describing what it costs you. Send a decline with that cost stated plainly.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Your team looks to you for certainty you do not have. Stand still. Unclench your jaw. Say exactly this: I do not have that answer yet. Notice the room does not collapse.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
At end of day, scan your task list. Find one item you absorbed from someone else's urgency. Move it to their queue. Write their name next to it. Do not move it back.
---
