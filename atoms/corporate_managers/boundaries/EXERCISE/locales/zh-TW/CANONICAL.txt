### 🛡️ The Executive Boundary Protocol  
*Based on Exercises v01–v13*

#### I. Physical & Emotional Grounding  
*Use when you feel reactive, tight, or overwhelmed.*

- **v05: The Inventory Pause**  
  *Trigger:* Chest tightness or mental clutter.  
  *Action:* Set a 2-minute timer. Write every task on your plate that belongs to someone else. Count them. That number is your starting point for delegation.
- **v02: The Floor Check**  
  *Trigger:* A direct report brings you a problem.  
  *Action:* Plant both feet flat. Ask: *“Is this mine to solve?”* If no, say so aloud. Practice the sentence beforehand.
- **v12: The Uncertainty Stand**  
  *Trigger:* Team looks to you for certainty you don’t have.  
  *Action:* Unclench jaw. Say: *“I do not have that answer yet.”* Notice the room does not collapse.

#### II. Verbal Boundaries & Deflection  
*Use in meetings, reviews, and upward conversations.*

- **v09: The 15-Second Deflection**  
  *Trigger:* Preparing for a 1:1 with leadership.  
  *Action:* Write down the ask you plan to deflect. Say it out loud. Keep it under 15 seconds. Shorter is stronger.
- **v07: The Disagreement Record**  
  *Trigger:* After a performance review.  
  *Action:* Write one expectation your manager stated that you did *not* agree to. Underline it. Decide what you will say before leaving the building.
- **v01: The Overreach Name**  
  *Trigger:* After a skip-level meeting feels invasive.  
  *Action:* Write down one thing asked that felt like overreach. Name it exactly. Do not soften. Read it back. Discomfort is information.

#### III. Structural & Calendar Defense  
*Use to protect time and scope.*

- **v06: The Protected Hour**  
  *Trigger:* Calendar fills without input.  
  *Action:* Block one hour tomorrow. Label: *“Protected.”* Do not explain. Hold it once. Then hold it again next week.
- **v11: The Meeting Decline**  
  *Trigger:* Recurring meeting that drains without decisions.  
  *Action:* Write the meeting name. Write one word describing its cost (e.g., *“Focus,”* *“Energy”*). Send a decline stating that cost plainly.
- **v04: The Trade-Off Sentence**  
  *Trigger:* Scope added without removal.  
  *Action:* Breathe out. Write the trade-off in one sentence. Send it upward *before* accepting the work.

#### IV. Team Protection & Delegation  
*Use to shield your team and reset ownership.*

- **v08: The Escalation Reset**  
  *Trigger:* Someone escalates past you to your team.  
  *Action:* Let the heat settle. Send one sentence to your team: *“Bring those requests to me first.”* Send it today.
- **v13: The Urgency Return**  
  *Trigger:* End-of-day task scan.  
  *Action:* Find one item you absorbed from someone else’s urgency. Move it to their queue. Write their name next to it. Do not move it back.

#### V. Strategic Clarity & Timing  
*Use during transitions and off-hours.*

- **v03: The Non-Negotiables List**  
  *Trigger:* Before a reorg or major change.  
  *Action:* Write three non-negotiables (not preferences). Keep under keyboard. Read before every senior conversation.
- **v10: The 9 PM Boundary**  
  *Trigger:* Crisis forwarded at 9 PM.  
  *Action:* Do not open attachment. Write one-sentence response. Schedule send for 8 AM. Your boundary lives in the delay.
