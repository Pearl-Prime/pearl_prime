These are powerful, concise reflections on **boundaries, burnout, and self-preservation** in professional (and personal) contexts. They read like modern aphorisms for the overworked, the people-pleaser, and the high-performer who has forgotten how to stop.

Here’s a synthesis of the core themes across these 12 takeaways:

### 🧱 **The Cost of Missing Boundaries**
- **v01 & v04**: Boundaries aren’t just protective—they’re *economic*. Not setting them costs you more (in energy, resentment, time) than the discomfort of setting them.
- **v10**: Without clear endpoints, responsibility becomes infinite. Ambiguity is exploitable.

### 🚪 **Availability vs. Accessibility**
- **v02 & v09**: Being “available” (always on, always open) is not virtue—it’s vulnerability. Accessibility implies choice; availability implies absence of filters.
- **v12**: Closing doors (literal or metaphorical) isn’t rejection—it’s self-definition.

### 🔄 **The Cycle of Overfunctioning**
- **v03 & v08**: You trained others to depend on you by never saying no. Now you’re trapped in a system where you’re the default solver, even when no one checks in on *you*.
- **v05 & v11**: Managing without boundaries turns you into a conduit for others’ stress. Their urgency becomes your physiology.

### 💔 **The Hidden Trade-Offs**
- **v06 & v07**: Every “yes” to external demands is a “no” to your own needs. Weekend checks aren’t dedication—they’re hypervigilance. Your nervous system pays the price.

---

### 🔑 **Key Insight**
> **Boundaries are not barriers to connection—they are the architecture of sustainable contribution.**  
> Without them, you don’t serve others; you disappear into their needs.
