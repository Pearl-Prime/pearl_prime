## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David sets a boundary — he will not check email after 7 PM — and the watcher immediately intensifies. What is happening in his inbox. What are people saying. What decisions are being made without him. His body is on high alert. Chest tight. Jaw locked. He sits on his couch and the scanning has nowhere to go. There is nothing to scan. The watcher runs its program against the blank wall of his living room. He watches it spin. Something shifts — the watcher is not responding to actual events. It is responding to the absence of surveillance. The boundary created a gap in coverage and the watcher cannot tolerate gaps. His shoulders drop slightly. The frame cracks: the watcher does not protect him from threats. It protects him from not knowing. And not knowing, after 7 PM, is actually fine. Nothing has ever happened after 7 PM that could not wait until morning. The watcher disagrees. David holds the boundary anyway.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David goes on vacation and turns off his phone. Fully off. For the first time in twelve years. His body goes into freefall. Heart pounding. Vision sharp. Hands cold. The watcher has lost all its data streams. It screams inside him — danger, danger, you cannot see what is happening, something will go wrong and you will not know. He stands on a beach and his nervous system is in a boardroom. The gap between where he is and where his body thinks he is breaks the frame wide open. He is safe. He is on a beach. And his watcher is running a full threat scan on ocean waves. The absurdity lands in his chest like a fist. He has been running this program everywhere, always, about everything. The watcher does not know how to stop. It does not know the difference between a boardroom and a beach. His eyes fill. The old way is no longer survivable because he cannot even stand on sand without scanning for danger. He has to choose: the watcher or his life. He cannot have both.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David sits in a meeting and holds his boundary: he does not scan the room. He listens to the words. He watches the presentation. He does not decode facial expressions or analyze tonal shifts. His chest tightens — the watcher insists he is missing something. He lets it insist. He stays with the content. The meeting ends. He does not know what the CFO was thinking. He does not know if the VP was bored. He does know the strategic plan and his role in executing it. His hands are warm. He is a leader who pays attention to the work, not to the weather in the room. The watcher runs in the background like a retired engine. It hums. It does not drive. David drives. The boundary between his attention and the watcher's demands is his new identity.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The layoffs are real. David's role survives but three of his peers are gone. Maximum uncertainty. Maximum threat density. The watcher lunges for every data point — who is left, who has influence, where the next cut might fall. David feels the pull. He does not follow it. He meets with his remaining team. He tells them what he knows and what he does not know. He does not pretend the uncertainty is resolved. He holds the not-knowing without scanning for hidden signals. His chest is tight. His jaw aches. But he is present with his team instead of absent in surveillance. After the meeting, a team member says: "You are the only leader who seems actually here right now." David's eyes sting. He chose presence over protection. The watcher screams that he is exposed. He is exposed. And his team is held. The boundary between vigilance and presence is the hardest thing he has ever maintained. It is also the thing that makes him the leader his team needs.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David realizes the watcher undermines every boundary he sets. He blocks time for strategic thinking. The watcher spends that time scanning for what he is missing while he is not in meetings. He sets a boundary around lunch. The watcher spends lunch monitoring Slack for subtext. He leaves at six. The watcher replays every conversation from the day, analyzing them for threat signals he missed by leaving. His chest is concrete. The pattern is clear: every boundary creates a surveillance gap, and the watcher fills every gap with anxiety. He cannot rest inside any boundary because the watcher converts rest into risk. His hands grip the steering wheel. The pattern persists because the watcher treats every boundary as a security breach. Time off is not recovery. It is an unmonitored interval that requires post-hoc surveillance. The watcher makes boundaries impossible by making them more stressful than working.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David sets a boundary and then watches himself violate it within the hour. He said no weekend emails. He is checking his phone at 8 AM Saturday. Not because he chose to. Because his hand picked up the phone before his brain registered the decision. His chest drops. The watcher has automated. It does not need David's permission. It runs at the autonomic level — below thought, below choice, below boundaries. He sets the phone down. His hand reaches for it again. He watches his own hand betray his own decision. The recognition is devastating: the watcher is not a thought pattern he can manage with discipline. It is a nervous system configuration. His body was trained to scan. The training predates his career. It predates his job. The pattern persists because it lives in his muscles, his reflexes, his automatic reaching hand. No boundary can hold against a system that operates below the level where boundaries are set. His eyes fill. He needs something deeper than a decision. He needs his body to learn a different way of being in the world.
---
