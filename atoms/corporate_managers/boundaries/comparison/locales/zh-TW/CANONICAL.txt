### Narrative Arc Summary: The Cost of Boundless Identity

**1. The Mirror (Pre-Awareness & Destabilization)**
Rachel’s journey begins not with introspection, but with observation. She watches a peer leave at 5:00 PM (`RECOGNITION v01`, `TURNING_POINT v06`). Initially, this triggers a defensive comparison: Rachel equates her own exhaustion with dedication and views her peer’s boundaries as a lack of commitment or an unfair privilege.

*   **The Crack:** The realization that the peer’s output is equal in quality but lower in cost (`MECHANISM_PROOF v05`) destabilizes Rachel’s core belief: *If they can do it without suffering, am I just inefficient? Or worse—am I unnecessary?*

**2. The Crisis (Turning Point)**

*   **The Shift:** The comparison flips. The peer is no longer a rival to be judged, but a model of survival. Rachel realizes her lack of boundaries is not heroism; it is self-destruction. The "old story" (boundaries = laziness) becomes literally fatal.

**3. The Embodiment (Self-Claim)**
Recovery is not intellectual; it is behavioral. Rachel begins to enact boundaries not because she feels ready, but because she must survive.
*   **Micro-Action 1:** Leaving at 5:30 PM (`EMBODIMENT v05`). She feels the guilt ("chest tightens"), observes the judgment of colleagues, but chooses the act anyway. She redefines herself: *I am a manager who delivers excellence within bounded hours.*

She distinguishes between *actual urgency* and *performative availability*. The boundary is no longer borrowed from her peer; it is owned by Rachel.
