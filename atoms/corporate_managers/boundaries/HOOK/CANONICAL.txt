

## HOOK v01
---

---
Your team messages you at 7 PM because they know you respond. Your manager messages you at 11 PM for the same reason. The phone in your hand at midnight isn't optional.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The dinner table conversation about your day becomes a therapeutic debriefing. Your family knows your direct report's performance issues before they know how you are.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
You've told your manager you need focus time. He scheduled a meeting during it anyway. Your calendar is now evidence that your time isn't actually yours.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The boundary you set with your team lasted three days. When the exception became the rule, you never reset it. Now you're available all the time.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Your manager asks for a "quick call" at 4:55 PM on Friday. The call runs 47 minutes. Your weekend starts after your nervous system settles down.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You know your job isn't 40 hours. You've accepted that. What you haven't accepted is that nobody acknowledges it. The unpaid hours are invisible.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The Slack channel is marked optional but your boss is in it, posting at all hours. You don't read it optional.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You blocked vacation on your calendar and your team is still asking if you'll "just quick check email." You haven't actually taken a vacation in four years.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The responsibility that belongs to your boss has migrated to your desk. You're managing both your work and the work above you.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Your team needs something and your life has other things. One of them always loses. You're the one absorbing the consequence.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
The person above you has no boundaries and now they're your template. You're starting to believe that's what leadership looks like.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
You said no to a project and felt immediate dread. The dread meant you eventually said yes. Your no was never actually no.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Your direct report is texting you personal problems on Sunday. The boundary you should set feels like abandonment.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The work that spills into your evening was never officially assigned. It's just what you thought of and couldn't not do.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
You're managing a team across time zones. The boundary between work and off-work no longer exists. You're always someone's working hours.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
The promotion came with the understanding that you'd be more available. More available to more people for more things. The time to say no was before you accepted.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
Your manager treats your calendar like a suggestion. The boundary isn't that you can't be interrupted. It's that you think you should enforce it.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
The emotional labor of managing is unlimited. The budget to take it on is the hours nobody counts.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
You check email before bed because someone might need something. Your brain stays partially at the office all night.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
Your kid asked you to be present at dinner and you were. Your body was there. Your attention was still in the 4:15 PM meeting.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
The expectation that you'll absorb chaos for your team is never stated and always assumed. You've absorbed so much there's nothing left.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
You've been the person who can handle anything. Now you're the person people turn to when things fall apart. The boundaries between helper and dumping ground disappeared.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
Your first direct report calls with a personal crisis at 9 PM. You answer because that's what you do. You don't know how to be unavailable to people who need you.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
The weekend messages from your boss arrive as questions disguised as conversations. By Monday morning they feel like assignments.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You're expected to be the bridge between the executive team and your people. The bridge burns from traffic.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
Your vacation email says you'll respond when you return. You've already responded to three things from the airport.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The company culture says "work-life balance" and then celebrates the person who works through the night. The contradiction is in your body.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You've managed to set a boundary once. You watched the person react badly and decided boundaries weren't worth the friction.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The time that was supposed to be yours got absorbed into someone else's need. This happens so often it no longer feels like loss.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
You're on your own island of availability in a sea of people who need things. Your edges have dissolved.
