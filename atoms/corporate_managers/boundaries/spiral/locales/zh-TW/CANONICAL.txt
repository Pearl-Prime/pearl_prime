Based on the provided story atoms, here is a synthesis of the **Boundaries Spiral** mechanism, structured by its progression from unconscious reaction to embodied identity.

### Core Mechanism: The Anti-Boundary System
The central insight across these atoms is that the "spiral" is not merely an emotional reaction to failure; it is a functional **anti-boundary system**. Its primary purpose is not to protect the individual from external consequences, but to prevent the individual from setting internal limits. It achieves this by making the *act* of setting a boundary feel more dangerous and painful than the state of being overwhelmed.

---

### Phase 1: Recognition (Pre-Awareness)
**State:** Identity is fused with external data. There is no boundary between event and self.
**Cost:** Internal, Intensity 2-3.

In this stage, a single data point triggers a catastrophic identity threat because the psychological container (boundary) is absent.

*   **大衛 (Criticism):** One comment $\rightarrow$ Systematic failure. *Somatic marker:* Chest tightens.
*   **莎拉 (Customer Interaction):** One bad interaction $\rightarrow$ Team failure $\rightarrow$ Personal failure as a leader. *Somatic marker:* Hands cold.
*   **凱文 (Underperformance):** One person struggling $\rightarrow$ Organizational collapse. *Somatic marker:* Shoulders tense.
*   **安東尼 (Variance):** One forecast error $\rightarrow$ Job security threat. *Somatic marker:* Throat tightens.
*   **譚雅 (Metric):** One bad number $\rightarrow$ Leadership invalidation. *Somatic marker:* Stomach hollow.

**Key Dynamic:** The spiral consumes energy *before* analysis can occur. The individual is no longer processing feedback; they are catastrophizing identity.

---

### Phase 2: Mechanism Proof (Destabilization)
**State:** The spiral accelerates because there is no friction to stop it. The narrative generates its own evidence.
**Cost:** Internal/Social, Intensity 3-5.

Here, the cost shifts from immediate shock to sustained dysfunction. The spiral begins to drive behavior, replacing clarity with fear-based reaction.

*   **Loss of Containment:** 大衛 spends four hours reviewing decisions instead of addressing the specific critique. 凱文 questions his entire leadership capability based on one report.
*   **Decision Corruption:** 莎拉 starts managing from fear. The spiral generates strategy. 安東尼 prepares mentally for firing instead of analyzing the variance.
*   **The Anti-Boundary Architecture (莎拉 v06/v07):**
    *   **Pattern Recognition:** 莎拉 maps her history. Every attempt to set a limit (leaving early, delegating, vacation) was met with a spiral intense enough to abort the boundary.
    *   **The Trap:** The spiral makes the *exit* (setting a boundary) feel worse than the *cell* (being overwhelmed).
    *   **Lack of Calibration:** The system treats every boundary as existential. Saying "no" to a report review triggers the same catastrophic intensity as saying "no" to the CEO. This prevents habit formation because no boundary ever feels "safe" or "easy."

---

### Phase 3: Turning Point (Experimentation)
**State:** Introduction of cognitive boundaries. The individual interrupts the cascade by redefining the data.
**Cost:** Internal/Social/Opportunity, Intensity 2-4.

The shift occurs when the individual inserts a verbal or cognitive frame that separates the *event* from the *identity*.

*   **The Interrupt:**
    *   **大衛:** "This is feedback about one situation. It's not about my fundamental competence."
    *   **莎拉:** "One difficult interaction is data. It's not evidence of systemic failure."
    *   **凱文:** "One person's struggle is a management conversation, not organizational failure."
    *   **安東尼:** "Variance is variance. It's not about job security."
    *   **譚雅:** "One metric is data about that metric. It's not about my leadership."

*   **The Evidence Gap (莎拉 v06/v07):**
    *   莎拉 realizes the spiral writes a story and calls it prediction.
    *   **Crucial Experiment:** She asks for help/set a boundary. The spiral predicts annihilation. Reality delivers support/neutrality.
    *   **Insight:** "The spiral stole six hours from her life to prevent a boundary that turned out to be safe." The distance between the predicted catastrophe and the actual outcome breaks the old frame.

---

### Phase 4: Embodiment (Self-Claim)
**State:** The boundary is maintained despite the spiral's presence. The individual learns to let the spiral "idle" without driving it.
**Cost:** Identity, Intensity 1-5 (High intensity endured, low long-term cost).

In this final stage, the spiral may still start, but it no longer controls the outcome. The individual develops the capacity to endure the discomfort of the spiral without obeying its commands.

*   **Somatic Shift:**
    *   **大衛:** Chest stays open. Feedback is just feedback.
    *   **莎拉:** Hands warm. She stays with the fact: "I set a boundary. Period." She lets the spiral run on fumes and quiets it by not engaging.
    *   **凱文:** Shoulders relaxed. People management is distinct from systemic judgment.
    *   **安東尼:** Throat open. Variance is analyzed, not feared.
    *   **譚雅:** Stomach calm. Metrics are data points, not verdicts.

*   **Advanced Embodiment (莎拉 v07):**
    *   莎拉 tells her VP she is stepping down from a committee.
    *   **The Test:** The spiral lunges at full intensity (career suicide, irrelevance).
    *   **The Action:** She does not reverse the decision. She does not argue with the spiral. She drives home while it burns itself out.
    *   **The Result:** She arrives home. The world is intact. She has proven she can survive the spiral without obeying it.
    *   **New Identity:** "She is someone who can endure the spiral without obeying it."
