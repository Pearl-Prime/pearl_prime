## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah says no to a project and the spiral starts immediately. What if they think she is not committed and then what if they assign it to someone else and that person does it better and then what if Sarah becomes the person who says no while others say yes and then — she catches the fourth loop. She writes it down. The spiral has run the same script every time she considers a boundary: no leads to rejection leads to replacement leads to irrelevance. She stares at the paper. She said no two hours ago. None of the predicted consequences have arrived. The spiral wrote a story and called it prediction. Her chest loosens. The frame cracks: the spiral does not prevent bad outcomes. It prevents boundaries. Every time she considers a limit, the spiral manufactures consequences vivid enough to stop her. The spiral is the boundary's enemy. She sees it now.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah spirals for six hours about whether to tell her boss she needs help. The spiral constructs an elaborate catastrophe: asking for help means admitting weakness, admitting weakness means losing credibility, losing credibility means losing her role. By hour six she is shaking, nauseous, unable to eat. Then she calls her boss because she physically cannot hold the weight alone anymore. Her boss says: "Thank you for telling me. Let us redistribute." The spiral predicted annihilation. Reality delivered support. Sarah stands in the gap between prediction and outcome and the distance is infinite. Her chest cracks open. Six hours of torture for a thirty-second conversation that went well. The spiral stole six hours from her life to prevent a boundary that turned out to be safe. The old frame cannot survive this evidence. The spiral was never protecting her from consequences. It was protecting her from boundaries.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah sets a boundary and the spiral starts its engine. She feels the first loop: what if this means — she stops it. Not by force. By choice. She does not follow the "what if." She stays with the fact: she set a boundary. Period. Her chest tightens. The spiral pushes harder. She breathes and does not engage. The spiral runs for a few minutes on fumes and then quiets. Her hands are warm. She is a person who sets boundaries without spiraling about them. The distinction is her new identity. The spiral still starts. She no longer drives it. She lets it idle and walks away. Her jaw is loose. The boundary stands. The spiral spent itself. Sarah goes home with energy she did not have when she let every boundary launch a six-hour catastrophe session.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sarah tells her VP she is stepping down from the cross-functional committee. The spiral lunges — career suicide, reputation damage, professional irrelevance. Sarah feels every loop fire at once. Her chest is tight. Her hands are cold. She stays in the conversation. She says: "My bandwidth does not support this commitment and I want to be honest about that rather than deliver poorly." The VP accepts it. Sarah walks to her car and the spiral runs at full intensity for ten minutes. She lets it run. She does not reengage. She does not call back and reverse the decision. She drives home while the spiral exhausts itself. By the time she parks, her breathing has normalized. The spiral told her the world would end. She is parked in her driveway and the world is intact. She is someone who can endure the spiral without obeying it. The boundary holds. The spiral burned. She survived both.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah realizes every boundary she has ever considered triggered a spiral. She maps them: the time she considered leaving early (spiral about perception), the time she thought about delegating a project (spiral about being unnecessary), the time she almost took a vacation (spiral about things falling apart). Every boundary attempt was followed by a spiral intense enough to stop the boundary. Her hands are cold. She sees the architecture: the spiral functions as an anti-boundary system. It does not protect her from consequences. It prevents her from setting limits. The pattern persists because the spiral is more uncomfortable than the lack of boundaries. She would rather be overwhelmed and boundary-less than endure the spiral that comes with setting a limit. Her chest aches. The spiral has kept her imprisoned by making the exit feel worse than the cell.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sarah wakes at 4 AM spiraling about a boundary she set yesterday. She told a colleague she could not review their report this week. Thirteen words. The spiral has spent eight hours constructing an elaborate narrative about what those thirteen words mean about her as a person, a professional, a human being. Her body is exhausted. Her chest is raw. She lies in bed and sees the pattern with brutal clarity: the spiral does not process her boundary. It punishes it. Every limit she sets receives the same penalty — hours of recursive self-torture. The pattern persists because the punishment is reliable. Every boundary, no matter how small, triggers the same intensity of spiral. Saying no to a report review generates the same catastrophic thinking as saying no to the CEO. The system has no calibration. It treats every boundary as existential. Her eyes are wet. The spiral persists because it makes every boundary equally unbearable, which means no boundary is ever easy, which means she can never build the habit of having them.
---
