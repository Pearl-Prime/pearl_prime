## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David sets a boundary with his team — no weekend Slack messages — and immediately feels the loss. The constant availability was connection. It was how he knew his people were okay, how they knew he cared. His chest aches. His hands grip his phone at 8 AM Saturday and there are no messages. The silence is grief. He is mourning the version of himself who was always reachable, always needed, always central. But standing in the silence, something shifts. He notices he slept through the night for the first time in months. His shoulders are not at his ears. The boundary took something but it also returned something. The frame cracks: what he is grieving is not connection. It is the illusion that constant availability IS connection. His team can reach him Monday. They will be fine. He is the one who needs to learn to be fine with the quiet.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David's best performer tells him she is leaving because he has no boundaries. She says: "You work all the time and it creates pressure for everyone to work all the time. I cannot live like this." David's stomach drops. His face burns. The loss is immediate and total — she is his best person and she is leaving because of him. Not because of the work. Because of him. He sits in his office after she leaves and the grief is enormous. He built his identity on being the always-available leader and that identity cost him the person he most wanted to keep. The frame does not crack. It detonates. His boundarylessness was not generosity. It was a demand disguised as availability. His body shakes. The old story — that having no limits shows commitment — just destroyed the thing it was supposed to protect.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
David walks past his office at 6 PM and keeps walking. He feels the pull — the desk, the inbox, the unfinished threads. The grief whispers: you used to be needed here at this hour. He acknowledges the whisper. He keeps walking. His chest aches but his feet move toward the parking lot. He is not the always-available leader anymore. He is the leader who trusts his team to hold things overnight. The grief of that identity shift is real. He lost something. He also gained something — the walk to his car, the drive home in daylight, the door opening to a family that sees him. His hands are warm on the steering wheel. He carries the grief of the old identity and the weight of the new one simultaneously. Both are true. He chose the one that keeps him whole.

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
A team member calls at 11 PM with a genuine problem. David answers. He helps. He sets the boundary after: "Tomorrow we build a plan so this does not land on either of us at eleven at night." His voice is steady. His chest is clear. He is not the person who refuses to help. He is not the person who helps without limit. He is the person who helps and then builds the structure so the help is not needed again at this hour. The grief for who he used to be — the hero who absorbed every crisis — passes through him. He lets it pass. He is building something more sustainable than heroism. His body knows the difference. He sleeps after the call. The old David would have stayed up building the plan tonight. This David trusts the morning. His breath is even. The grief is carried, not denied.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David notices that every time he sets a boundary, grief arrives dressed as guilt. He blocks his calendar for lunch and feels guilty about the meeting he could have taken. He leaves at six and feels guilty about the email he could have sent. His chest is tight. The pattern becomes visible: the grief is not about the boundary. The grief is about the identity the boundary threatens. Being available at all times means being indispensable. Being indispensable means being safe. Every boundary chips away at indispensability. His hands are cold. The protective logic is clear: if he grieves every boundary, he will eventually stop setting them. The grief functions as a retrieval mechanism — pulling him back to the old pattern. The pattern persists because the grief is louder than the relief.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David sits at his kitchen table on a Saturday morning with nothing to do. No messages. No fires. No team needing him. The silence is unbearable. His chest is hollow. His hands fidget. He opens his phone three times and puts it down. He is grieving the version of himself who was always needed, and in the grief he sees the full architecture: he erased his boundaries not for his team but for himself. The availability was not generosity. It was a way to never face the emptiness of a Saturday morning with nothing to prove. His eyes sting. The pattern persists because boundaries create space, and space creates silence, and silence forces him to sit with who he is without the work. And who he is without the work is someone he has never met. The grief is the sound of meeting a stranger who lives in his own body.
---
