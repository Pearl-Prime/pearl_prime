Based on the provided story atoms, here is a synthesized narrative arc for **David**, tracing his journey from unconscious projection of grief to the embodied integration of a new leadership identity.

### Phase 1: The Unconscious Projection (Pre-Awareness)
**Status:** *Identity Stage: Pre-Awareness | Cost: Internal*

David grieves the loss of his direct connection to the work and the visceral engagement he felt before becoming VP. However, he lacks the boundaries to contain this grief. Instead of processing it internally, he projects it onto his team. He demands they mirror the intensity he has lost, creating unreasonable expectations for appreciation and engagement.

*   **Somatic Marker:** His chest is tight.
*   **Team Impact:** The team feels responsible for managing David’s unresolved emotions. They are not free to engage with the work on their own terms; they are performing to soothe his loss.
*   **Mechanism:** David is trying to engineer the experience he lost by controlling his team’s emotional output. The boundary between his personal grief and his professional leadership has dissolved.

### Phase 2: The Destabilization (The Crack in the Frame)
**Status:** *Identity Stage: Destabilization | Cost: Internal/Opportunity*

As David attempts to set initial boundaries, he encounters resistance not from his team, but from his own psyche. He realizes that his "always-on" availability was not an act of generosity, but a defense mechanism against feeling irrelevant or empty.

*   **The Saturday Silence (TP v06):** David enforces a no-weekend-Slack rule. At 8 AM on a Saturday, he grips his phone, aching for the connection of being needed. The silence forces him to confront a truth: he wasn’t grieving connection; he was grieving the *illusion* that constant availability equated to worth. He sleeps through the night for the first time in months, realizing the boundary took something (the dopamine of urgency) but returned something (rest).
*   **The Guilt Loop (MP v06):** Every boundary triggers guilt. Blocking lunch or leaving at 6 PM feels dangerous because his identity is tied to indispensability. The grief functions as a retrieval mechanism, pulling him back to the safety of being overwhelmed.
*   **The Hollow Morning (MP v07):** Sitting at his kitchen table with nothing to do, David faces the unbearable silence. He realizes he erased boundaries to avoid meeting the stranger inside himself—the person who exists without the validation of crisis management.

### Phase 3: The Detonation (Crisis of Identity)
**Status:** *Identity Stage: Destabilization | Cost: Identity*

The cost of his boundarylessness becomes undeniable and external.

*   **The Resignation (TP v07):** His best performer quits. She tells him, *"You work all the time and it creates pressure for everyone to work all the time. I cannot live like this."*
*   **The Impact:** David’s stomach drops. His face burns. The grief is enormous because he lost the person he most wanted to keep *because* of his lack of boundaries. His identity as the "committed, always-available leader" detonates. He sees clearly now: his boundarylessness was a demand disguised as availability. It destroyed the very thing it was supposed to protect.

### Phase 4: Experimentation (Setting the Boundary)
**Status:** *Identity Stage: Experimentation | Cost: Internal*

David begins to consciously separate his grief from his leadership actions. He acknowledges that his loss is his to process, not his team’s burden.

*   **The Declaration (TP v01):** He sets a clear internal and external boundary: *"My grief about what I lost is mine to process. It's not my team's responsibility."*
*   **The Shift:** His chest unclenches slightly. By containing his grief, he gives his team the freedom to find their own engagement. He stops trying to engineer their experience and starts trusting their autonomy.

### Phase 5: Embodiment (Self-Claim)
**Status:** *Identity Stage: Self-Claim | Cost: Identity (Integrated)*

David integrates the grief into a new, sustainable leadership identity. He no longer denies the loss, but he no longer lets it dictate his behavior.

*   **Walking Away (Embodiment v06):** At 6 PM, David walks past his office. The grief whispers that he used to be needed here. He acknowledges the whisper but keeps walking. He accepts the ache in his chest as the price of wholeness. He gains the drive home in daylight and the presence of his family. He carries both the grief of the old identity and the weight of the new one.
*   **Sustainable Help (Embodiment v07):** When a team member calls at 11 PM with a genuine crisis, David answers. He helps. But afterward, he sets a structural boundary: *"Tomorrow we build a plan so this does not land on either of us at eleven at night."*
    *   **The Difference:** The old David would have stayed up all night building the plan, fueled by heroism and anxiety. The new David trusts the morning. His breath is even. He sleeps. He is not the hero who absorbs every crisis; he is the leader who builds systems so crises don’t recur.
*   **Final State:** His chest is open. His hands are warm. He has transformed from a manager projecting his grief into a leader who holds his own history while making space for his team’s future.
