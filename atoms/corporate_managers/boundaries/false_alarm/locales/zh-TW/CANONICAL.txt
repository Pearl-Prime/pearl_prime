Based on the narrative arc provided in the `story_atoms`, here is the synthesized progression of Kevin’s journey from **Pre-awareness** through **Destabilization**, **Experimentation**, and finally to **Self-Claim**.

### **Phase 1: The Noise (Pre-awareness)**
*Status: High Band, High Internal Cost, Low Mechanism Depth*

Kevin exists in a state of constant, low-level panic. The signals are indistinguishable from noise.
*   **The Triggers:** Slack notifications, calendar invites, and "the quiet that means something."
*   **The Sensation:** A pervasive sense that everything is urgent. The "false alarm" is not yet recognized as false; it is accepted as reality.
*   **The Cost:** Internal exhaustion. The body is always braced for impact, but there is no specific enemy—only the ambient threat of being unavailable.

### **Phase 2: The Crack (Destabilization)**
*Status: Peak Internal Cost, Mechanism Revealed*

The pattern becomes visible because the cost becomes unsustainable.
*   **The Revelation (v05):** Kevin traces the logic. The alarm isn't protecting his schedule; it's protecting his *existence*. It equates "boundary" with "replacement."
*   **The Horror (v06):** The hospital scene. The alarm fires while he is physically incapacitated. This is the moment of absolute clarity: **The alarm has no compassion for the host.** It will demand labor from a dying body. The mechanism is exposed as parasitic, not protective.

### **Phase 3: The Test (Experimentation)**
*Status: High Opportunity Cost, High Stakes*

Kevin begins to act against the alarm. This is the most dangerous phase because the alarm predicts catastrophe.
*   **Micro-Test (v06):** He sets a small boundary (no meetings after 4 PM). The body reacts with severe physical symptoms (chest seizure, cold hands). **Result:** Nothing happens. The world does not end. The prediction fails.
*   **Macro-Test (v07):** He says "No" to the CEO. The alarm predicts career suicide. **Result:** The CEO validates him. The gap between the *predicted* catastrophe and the *actual* reward is so wide that Kevin’s old framework collapses. He realizes the alarm was lying about the nature of reality.

### **Phase 4: The New Normal (Self-Claim)**
*Status: Identity Shift, Mechanism Depth 4*

Kevin does not silence the alarm; he changes his relationship to it.
*   **The Practice (v05):** Dinner time. The phone rings. The alarm fires. Kevin feels the shake, but he chooses to eat. He checks facts, not feelings. He is no longer the person who answers; he is the person who *decides*.
*   **The Integration (v06):** The real emergency happens (2 AM production down). The alarm fires, but this time it is **accurate**. Kevin answers. He solves the problem. He sleeps.
    *   **Key Distinction:** Old Kevin had no boundary (everything was an emergency). New Kevin has a flexible boundary (he distinguishes between *alarm* and *emergency*).

### **Summary of the Mechanism**

| Stage | Alarm Signal | Reality | Kevin's Response |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pre-awareness** | "Everything is urgent." | Mostly noise. | Obey immediately. Exhaustion. |
| **Destabilization** | "If you stop, you die." | He is in a hospital. | Recognizes the lie. Horror. |
| **Experimentation** | "They will fire you." | They validate him. | Tests the boundary. Frame breaks. |
| **Self-Claim** | "This is an emergency." | Sometimes yes, sometimes no. | Checks facts. Responds to reality, not prediction. |

**Final State:** Kevin is not "cured" of anxiety. The alarm still fires. But he has installed a filter between the signal and the action. He has moved from **reacting to prediction** to **responding to reality**.
