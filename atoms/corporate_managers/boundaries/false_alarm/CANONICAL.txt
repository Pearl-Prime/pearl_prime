## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin sets a boundary — no meetings after 4 PM — and his body immediately fires an alarm. His chest seizes. His hands go cold. The alarm says: someone will need you and you will not be there. Someone will be angry. Someone will question your commitment. He holds the boundary for one day. Nobody notices. No crisis emerges. No anger arrives. The alarm fired at maximum intensity and predicted consequences that did not materialize. His jaw loosens slightly. The frame cracks: the alarm is not predicting real reactions. It is predicting the worst-case scenario and presenting it as certainty. Kevin kept the boundary and the world did not collapse. The alarm was false. His shoulders drop. He keeps the boundary for a second day.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kevin says no to the CEO. Directly. In a meeting. "I cannot take on another initiative this quarter." The alarm detonates. His vision tunnels. His heart hammers. His body is certain he has just ended his career. The CEO nods and says: "Good. I need leaders who know their limits." Kevin cannot process the response. His nervous system prepared for catastrophe and received validation. The gap between prediction and reality is so wide his frame collapses entirely. He sits in the meeting shaking. The alarm insisted that any boundary would be punished. The CEO rewarded it. Everything Kevin believed about the cost of saying no — every false alarm that kept him from setting limits — all of it built on a prediction that was just proven wrong at the highest possible stakes. His chest breaks open. The old way cannot hold.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kevin's phone rings during dinner. His chest tightens — the old alarm. He checks the caller: his director with a non-urgent question. He lets it go to voicemail. His hands shake. The alarm fires: what if it was actually urgent, what if they needed you, what if this is the one time it matters. Kevin eats his dinner. He returns the call at 8 AM. The director does not mention the delay. Kevin is a person who protects his evenings. The alarm still fires but Kevin no longer obeys it. He checks the facts, not the feelings. The boundary holds because he holds it, not because the alarm stops. His jaw stays loose through dessert. He is not the person who answers every call. He is the person who decides which calls to answer.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The actual emergency arrives. Production system down. Kevin's phone rings at 2 AM. His alarm fires — but this time the signal matches the noise. He answers. He works. He solves the problem by 4 AM. The boundary bent for a genuine emergency. It did not break. The distinction is everything. Old Kevin had no boundary at all — every call was an emergency, every buzz was a crisis. New Kevin has a boundary that flexes for real emergencies and holds for false ones. His body knows the difference now. He goes back to sleep at 4:30 AM. Not easily. But he sleeps. Tomorrow the boundary resets. He is someone who can distinguish between alarm and emergency, who responds to reality instead of reacting to prediction. The alarm still fires. He checks it against the facts. The facts determine his response. The alarm does not.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin traces the false alarm pattern around boundaries. Every time he considers setting a limit, the alarm fires the same sequence: they will think you are not dedicated, they will find someone who says yes, you will be replaced. His chest locks on the word "replaced." He sees the machinery: the alarm treats every boundary as a threat to survival. Not a threat to his schedule. A threat to his existence in the organization. His hands are cold. The protective logic is clear: if he never sets a boundary, he never risks rejection. But the absence of boundaries is the thing actually destroying him. His body aches. His sleep is broken. His relationships are strained. The alarm prevents the cure by presenting the cure as the disease. Setting a limit feels dangerous because the alarm says it is dangerous. The alarm persists because it has never been tested against reality.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/corporate_managers/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kevin is in the emergency room with chest pains. The doctor says it is stress. Kevin's phone is in his hand. He has eleven unread messages. He is lying on a hospital bed and the alarm says: you need to respond. They will think something is wrong if you do not respond. His hands grip the phone. He sees the full horror of the pattern: he is in a hospital and the alarm is telling him to work. The false alarm does not care about his body. It does not care about his health. It only cares about the perceived threat of being unavailable. He is unavailable because he is in a hospital and the alarm STILL fires. His eyes sting. The pattern persists because it has no floor. There is no level of crisis personal enough to override it. The alarm will fire from a hospital bed, from a funeral, from his own collapse. It has no compassion for the body it inhabits. The recognition is devastating and precise.
---
