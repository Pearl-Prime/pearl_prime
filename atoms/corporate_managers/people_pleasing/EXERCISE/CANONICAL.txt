## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit upright in your chair. Both feet flat on the floor. Notice the pressure in your heels. Think of the last request you agreed to without wanting to. Name it out loud. One word. Now say: "I agreed because I feared the cost." Say it again. Slower. Feel where your chest tightens. That tightness is data. Write down what the actual cost was to you. Keep the paper.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Open your calendar right now. Find one meeting you accepted to avoid friction. Not because it serves your work. Block thirty seconds. Breathe out fully. Say the meeting name aloud. Then say: "This is a people-pleasing choice." No judgment. Just naming. Write the word "optional" next to it. Decide nothing yet. Naming comes first.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Stand up. Shake out your hands. Roll your shoulders back once. Think of your most recent performance review conversation. Find the moment you softened a truth. Locate that moment in your body. Chest. Throat. Gut. Press two fingers there. Hold for four counts. Breathe in through your nose. Exhale through your mouth. Say: "I edited myself." Say it without rushing.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Take out a blank page. Write the name of one direct report at the top. Below it, write the last piece of feedback you withheld from them. Write the real version. Uncensored. Read it back silently. Notice your jaw. Is it clenched. Release it. Now write one sentence: what withholding that feedback cost them. Fold the page. Keep it visible on your desk today.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Before your next skip-level meeting, stop outside the door. Place one hand on your sternum. Feel your heartbeat. Count four beats. Ask yourself: what do I actually need this person to know. Not what is safe. What is true. Say that true thing quietly, once, before you enter. Your body heard it. That matters.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Set a two-minute timer. Write every commitment you made this week that originated from fear of disapproval. Not workload. Fear. List them fast. Do not edit. When the timer ends, count the items. Say the number aloud. Then say: "This is the size of my people-pleasing this week." No shame. Exact size. Circle the one with the highest cost to your team.
