--- variant: v01
Eighteen months of runway sounds like a number. It feels like a countdown clock bolted to your chest. Financial anxiety in founders isn't about the money -- it's about the identity collapse that happens if the money runs out. You're not afraid of being broke. You're afraid of what being broke would prove about you.

--- variant: v02
You check the bank balance the way other people check their pulse. Not for information -- for reassurance that you're still alive. The financial anxiety has fused with your survival instinct until every dollar spent feels like a piece of yourself leaving. That's not financial literacy. That's financial hypervigilance wearing a spreadsheet as camouflage.

--- variant: v03
Your investors see burn rate. You feel burn. Every expenditure passes through a filter that converts dollars to days-until-death. The anxiety isn't irrational -- your company could actually fail. But the way it lives in your body, the way it follows you to dinner and into sleep, that's not strategic concern. That's your nervous system treating a balance sheet like a predator.
