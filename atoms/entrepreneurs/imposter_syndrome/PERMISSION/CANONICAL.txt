--- variant: v01
You're allowed to feel like a fraud and keep going. The imposter syndrome doesn't need to be resolved before you can be effective. You have permission to build the company while feeling uncertain about whether you should be the one building it. Most founders do exactly this.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to not know what you're doing. That's not imposter syndrome lying to you. That's the truth of building something new. The permission isn't to feel confident. It's to act without confidence when confidence isn't available.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to admit the imposter feeling out loud. Not as a humble brag. Not as a relatable founder moment. As an honest disclosure of what it actually feels like to carry the gap between how you appear and how you feel. That honesty is allowed to be uncomfortable.
