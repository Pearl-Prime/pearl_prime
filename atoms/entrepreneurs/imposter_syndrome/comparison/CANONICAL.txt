## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sasha sits in a founder roundtable listening to a peer describe their growth metrics. Everything Sasha has built feels small. Fraudulent. His chest constricts. He compares his honest struggles to their curated highlights and the comparison produces a verdict: they belong here, he does not. His hands are cold under the table. The frame shifts when the peer finishes and privately asks Sasha for advice on retention — the one thing Sasha does well. The comparison had no category for selective excellence. It only measured total performance against a fiction. His shoulders release. He is not an imposter. He is a specialist in a room of generalists.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Wei reads a profile of a founder her age in a business magazine. Same industry. Different trajectory — the trajectory that looks like what success is supposed to look like. Her stomach is a stone. Her vision narrows. She has been measuring her legitimacy against other people's press coverage for three years. The comparison fires so hard her hands go numb. The frame ruptures completely: the magazine did not profile that founder because they are better. It profiled them because they are photogenic and quotable. The comparison was between Wei's actual company and someone else's marketing. Her jaw unlocks. The imposter is not her. The imposter is the version of entrepreneurship she has been comparing herself to.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Nadia sits at the founder dinner and hears the revenue numbers again. The comparison fires. Nadia lets it fire. Watches it land in the chest, tighten the hands, begin the calculation. Then Nadia speaks: 'Our retention rate is what I'm proudest of.' The comparison wanted silence. Nadia gave it a counter-metric. Not defensively. From a settled place. The chest is tight but the words are clear. Nadia is not comparing anymore. Nadia is reporting from a company that has its own measures. The comparison runs in the background. Nadia operates in the foreground.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Tomás is in the room with the founder whose success used to destroy him. The comparison fires at full intensity. Revenue. Team size. Press coverage. All of it. Tomás's chest is tight. Hands cold. The comparison runs its full program. And Tomás stays. Not frozen. Present. He speaks about his company from his company's reality, not from the comparison's ranking. The comparison is screaming. Tomás's voice is calm. The two coexist. The comparison no longer runs Tomás. Tomás runs the company while the comparison does whatever it does in the background. The background is where it belongs.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya maps the comparison's data pipeline. It takes a single metric — revenue, headcount, press mentions — strips all context, and outputs a ranking. The ranking has only two positions: ahead and behind. Priya is always behind because the comparison selects the person who is ahead in that specific metric. The composite competitor does not exist. No one leads in every metric simultaneously. Priya's chest tightens as the mechanism becomes clear: the comparison is rigged. Not by malice. By architecture. It was built to find inadequacy. That is its only function. His hands unclench as he sees the wiring. The machine works perfectly. It just builds the wrong thing.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Dante sees the comparison's deepest layer: it is not comparing companies. It is comparing worth. Every metric gap between Dante's company and someone else's is converted into a worth gap between Dante and that person. Revenue is not revenue. It is a measure of human value. Headcount is not headcount. It is a proxy for how much the world respects you. Dante's chest breaks open. The mechanism is total: the comparison has fused business performance with personal identity. There is no gap between the company's numbers and Dante's sense of self. They are the same data stream. Separating them is the work.
---
