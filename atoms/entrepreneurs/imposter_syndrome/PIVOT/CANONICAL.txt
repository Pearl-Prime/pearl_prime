--- variant: v01
You raised the round, built the team, shipped the product -- and you're still waiting for someone to check your credentials. Imposter syndrome in founders is particularly cruel because the role literally has no credentials. You made the job up. That's what entrepreneurship is. The feeling of being a fraud is baked into doing something no one handed you a manual for.

--- variant: v02
Every time someone calls you CEO, a part of you flinches. Not because you're not doing the job -- you clearly are -- but because the gap between how competent you appear and how uncertain you feel has become its own full-time position. You're running the company and running from the feeling that you shouldn't be, simultaneously.

--- variant: v03
The pitch deck says 'visionary founder.' The inner monologue says 'they're going to find out.' What they'd find out, if they looked, is someone doing an extraordinary thing while feeling ordinary. That's not a contradiction. That's what courage looks like from the inside.
