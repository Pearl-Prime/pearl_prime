## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Ava gives a keynote and stumbles on a statistic. Thirty seconds of recovery. The audience does not notice. But Ava notices. His face burns for the rest of the talk. His hands shake behind the podium. The stumble confirms what imposter syndrome has been insisting: he does not know his own material well enough to stand on this stage. The frame shifts after the talk when three attendees reference the exact section where he stumbled — they found it compelling. The shame erased their response. The imposter syndrome only recorded the error and deleted the impact. His chest releases. The stumble was human. The talk was effective. Both are true.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nadia is asked a direct question by a journalist: what qualifies you to lead this company? Her throat closes. Her hands go white. Every credential she has earned disappears from memory. Her mind goes blank — pure, exposed, white-noise blank. The shame is not about the question. It is about the silence where her answer should be. Three seconds of silence. Five. She starts to answer and her voice cracks. The frame shatters: the silence was not ignorance. It was shame blocking access to her own history. The shame does not believe her qualifications are real, so it locks them away under pressure. She forces a breath. Starts again. The second answer is quieter. More honest. More real than any polished version would have been.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Priya presents the real numbers at the board meeting. No sanitizing. No editing. The numbers are not good. The room is quiet. Priya's face is warm but the voice is steady. The shame says: they will see you as a failure. Priya says the numbers aloud anyway. The saying is the identity. A founder who presents real numbers, even the bad ones. The board asks questions. The questions are practical, not damning. The shame expected execution. The reality is a working meeting. Priya's hands unclench under the table.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Dante is asked point-blank at a public event: 'Did your last company fail?' The shame detonates. Face hot. Hands ice. Every cell wants to disappear. Dante answers: 'Yes.' One word. The word hangs in the air. The audience does not flinch. Dante continues: 'And this is what I built from the wreckage.' The shame wanted the yes to be the end. Dante made it the beginning of the next sentence. The audience leans in. The shame recedes behind the story. Not gone. Behind.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Lila identifies the shame's core logic: visibility equals vulnerability. Every act of being seen — presenting numbers, sharing struggles, asking for help — routes through the shame processor, which tags it as exposure and triggers the hide response. The shame does not evaluate the content of the visibility. All visibility is dangerous. His face warms as he sees the mechanism. The shame treats a board presentation and a confession of failure identically: both are visibility, both trigger hiding. The mechanism has no granularity. His chest loosens slightly. The shame is not wise. It is indiscriminate.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/imposter_syndrome/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus identifies the shame's terminal architecture: it has become the foundation of his decision-making. Every choice passes through the shame filter: will this expose me? Every action is evaluated not for its merit but for its risk of visibility. The shame has become the executive function. Marcus's face is burning. The mechanism is complete: he does not make decisions. The shame makes decisions. He executes them. The decisions protect the shame, not the company. Not the person. The shame.
---
