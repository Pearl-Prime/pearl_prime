--- variant: v01
The imposter feeling isn't going away with more success. If anything, more success gives it more material. The takeaway is that you can feel like a fraud and still be the right person for this. Both things can be true. Competence and insecurity share office space in most founders.

--- variant: v02
You're waiting for the moment you finally feel qualified. That moment doesn't exist. Nobody feels qualified to do something nobody else has done before. The imposter syndrome isn't a bug -- it's the standard emotional tax on doing original work.

--- variant: v03
The secret every founder keeps: everyone feels this way. The ones who seem confident have just learned to act while feeling uncertain. You don't need the feeling to leave. You need to stop letting it have veto power over your decisions.
