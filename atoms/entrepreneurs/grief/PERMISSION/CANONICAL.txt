--- variant: v01
You're allowed to grieve without pivoting. Not every loss needs a silver lining or a lesson extracted. You have permission to be sad about something that ended without immediately converting the sadness into fuel for the next thing.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to grieve at your own pace, which might be slower than startup speed. Grief doesn't optimize. It doesn't iterate. It moves in circles and revisits what you thought was resolved. That's not failure. That's how it works.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to grieve something that nobody else considers a loss. The version of the company that only existed in your mind, the relationship that shifted when you started this, the person you were before the founder identity took over. Those losses are real even if they're invisible.
