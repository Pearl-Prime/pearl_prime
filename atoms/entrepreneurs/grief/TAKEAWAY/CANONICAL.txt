--- variant: v01
You're allowed to grieve the thing that failed, the partnership that ended, the version of the company that only existed in your mind. 'Learning experience' is what other people call your loss to make themselves comfortable. You don't have to make your grief comfortable for anyone.

--- variant: v02
Grief doesn't follow a sprint cycle. It doesn't have milestones or a clear exit. The founder instinct to process it efficiently, extract the lesson, and move on is the very thing that keeps it stuck. Some things need to be mourned at their own pace, not on your product roadmap.

--- variant: v03
The loss you're carrying is real even if nobody else saw it. Especially if nobody else saw it. The version of you that believed in that thing, invested in that person, banked on that future -- that version deserves acknowledgment, not just a retrospective.
