--- variant: v01
Nobody sends flowers when a startup dies. Nobody holds a service for the version of your life that existed before you bet everything on this. Founder grief is disenfranchised -- you're expected to pivot, not mourn. But something died and you haven't been allowed to bury it properly.

--- variant: v02
You lost the co-founder, or the deal, or the version of the company you actually wanted to build. Everyone says 'that's startup life.' As if naming it normalizes the hole it left. Grief in entrepreneurship gets rebranded as learning experience before you've even finished feeling the loss.

--- variant: v03
The thing you're carrying isn't a lesson. It's a loss. The partnership that ended, the product that failed, the future you'd already started living in your mind. Founders are supposed to be forward-looking, but you're mourning something that was never allowed to exist long enough to be properly missed.
