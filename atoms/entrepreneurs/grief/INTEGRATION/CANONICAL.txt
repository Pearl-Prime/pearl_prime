

## INTEGRATION v01
---

---
I wrote them a letter and burned it. Watched the words go to ash.
All the anger. All the gratitude. All the missing.
The burning didn't erase them. It released the grip.
Carry line: Grief shared with fire becomes lighter.
I'm lighter now.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---

---
I told the team one story about them.
The story made them real instead of memory.
The real version hurt less than the absence.
Carry line: Grief honored is grief that can integrate.
I'm honoring them by continuing.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---

---
I realized I'd been trying to be both of us.
Filling the gap with more of me.
But the gap wasn't meant to be filled. It was meant to be acknowledged.
Carry line: I can't be them, only me continuing.
I'm continuing as myself alone.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---

---
I looked at the original vision we created together.
I mourned that it didn't become what we imagined.
Then I mourned less when I looked at what actually became.
It's not what we planned. It's good.
Carry line: The actual path has its own beauty.
I'm walking it now.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---

---
I stopped expecting them to respond to calls.
I stopped turning around in meetings expecting them to be there.
The absence was real and then it became just absence.
Not absence-of-them but just absence.
Carry line: Grief becomes manageable when it becomes honest.
I'm managing it now.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---

---
I hired someone new. It wasn't replacement.
It was expansion into what comes next.
The new person won't be them but they're good.
Carry line: Continuation is not betrayal, it's survival.
I'm continuing their belief by surviving.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
I sat in the office where we built it together.

I felt the full weight of what was.

And then I felt the weight of what I'm building now.

The grief didn't disappear. It became part of the story.

**Carry line: Grief is the price of having loved.**

I paid that price gladly.
