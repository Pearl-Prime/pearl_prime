## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/overthinking/spiral/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Dante begins with a product question: should we add this feature? The question spawns a competitive analysis. The analysis spawns a market study. The study spawns a pricing review. The pricing review spawns an existential question about the company's direction. His chest is compressed. Four hours of spiraling from a feature question to an identity crisis. The frame shifts when he writes the original question on a sticky note and puts it on his monitor: should we add this feature? The answer takes ten minutes once he separates it from the spiral. Yes. Ship it. His jaw releases.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/overthinking/spiral/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Lila is trapped in a recursive loop about the company name. Should she rebrand? The question leads to brand perception research, which leads to customer interviews, which leads to market positioning, which leads back to: should she rebrand? She has completed the circle four times. Her neck is frozen. Her hands are cramped. The spiral has consumed a month. The frame ruptures: the name is fine. The spiral is not about the name. The spiral is about the anxiety underneath the name — the fear that the company is one wrong decision from failure. Every question becomes that question. She stops researching the name. Sits with the fear. The fear is one thing. The spiral made it into forty things.
---

BAND: 4

COST_INTENSITY: 5

---
Ren notices the spiral starting. The thought hooks into the next thought, which hooks into the next. Ren names it aloud: 'Spiral.' The word is a speed bump. The spiral does not stop but it loses velocity. Ren picks up a pen and writes the original question — the one before the spiral hijacked it. The question is simple. The answer takes five minutes. The spiral wanted five hours. Ren gave it five minutes and a name. The spiral will return. Ren knows what to do when it does.

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/overthinking/spiral/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sasha feels the spiral build to peak speed. Every thought chain is running simultaneously: finance, product, team, market, self-worth, mortality. The spiral is a centrifuge. Her chest is compressed. Her vision narrows. She does not fight the centrifuge. She sits in the center of it. Closes her eyes. The spiral spins around her. She is still. The stillness is not the spiral stopping. It is Sasha finding the motionless point inside the spin. The point exists. She found it before. She finds it now. The spiral loses its center when Sasha claims it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/overthinking/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Wei catches the spiral's propagation method. It does not generate new content. It takes one thought and copies it, adding a slight variation with each copy. The original thought: the pitch might fail. Copy one: the pitch will fail. Copy two: the pitch will fail and the company will suffer. Copy three: the company will suffer and people will lose jobs. Each copy adds one link. The chain grows because each link creates the next. Wei's chest tightens. The spiral is not creative. It is a copier with an increment function. The original thought is still just: the pitch might fail. Everything after it is duplication.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/overthinking/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Ava sees the spiral's deepest truth: it is a substitute for feeling. Every rotation converts emotion into thought. Grief becomes analysis. Fear becomes scenario planning. Anger becomes strategic assessment. The spiral takes raw emotional material and processes it into cognitive product. Ava's chest is tight. The mechanism: the spiral is not maladaptive thinking. It is emotional avoidance disguised as intellectual engagement. She is not thinking too much. She is feeling too little, and the thinking fills the gap.
---
