Your brain isn't trying to solve the problem. It's trying to prevent the feeling that comes with making the wrong choice. Overthinking is emotional insurance disguised as intellectual rigor. The premium is your presence, your energy, and your ability to act when action is what's actually needed.

--- variant: v02
No amount of thinking will make uncertainty go away. Uncertainty is the job description. The overthinking is an attempt to make entrepreneurship feel controllable, and it works -- except it controls you in the process.

--- variant: v03
The decision you made three days ago was probably fine. The twelve scenarios you've run since then haven't improved it. They've just kept you from being available for what's actually happening now. The takeaway: your brain's quality control department needs a scope reduction.
