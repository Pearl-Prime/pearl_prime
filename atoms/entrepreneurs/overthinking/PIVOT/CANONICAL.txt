--- variant: v01
You've run the scenario twelve times. The board meeting. The product launch. The conversation with your co-founder. Your mind treats every decision like a term sheet that needs seventeen rounds of revision. Overthinking in founders looks like thoroughness from the outside. From the inside, it's a hamster wheel disguised as a strategy session.

--- variant: v02
The decision was made three days ago. You're still rehearsing alternatives in the shower, at dinner, at 3 AM. Your brain has confused preparation with protection -- as if thinking hard enough about what could go wrong will prevent it from happening. It won't. But it will prevent you from being present for what's actually happening.

--- variant: v03
Your competitive advantage was supposed to be thinking fast. Now you can't stop thinking at all. Every fork in the road becomes a decision tree that branches until you're paralyzed. The overthinking isn't making you smarter. It's making you slower and calling it strategic.
