I've noticed the pattern in myself and in most of the business owners I've worked with: the moment the revenue dips, the nervous system moves. Not consciously. The drop triggers an ancient alarm system that reads financial uncertainty as a survival threat. I call this the revenue alarm. The alarm isn't illogical. The revenue alarm is automatic. The alarm doesn't listen to that data. But there's something in naming it, in recognizing that this is the revenue alarm—not a prophecy, not a rational assessment of actual danger, just an old system doing what it was designed to do. You're still standing in the vulnerability of the revenue dependency. That remains true.
