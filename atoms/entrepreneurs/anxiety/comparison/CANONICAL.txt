---
Alex is on Twitter at 6 AM. His former competitor just announced Series A funding. Nine figures. His stomach drops. His chest tightens. He scrolls back through the timeline—the company was founded only two years before his. The founder's story is almost identical to his: same market, same problem, same energy. But they got capital. They got velocity. They're growing at a rate that makes his growth look anemic. His jaw clenches. He looks at his own numbers. Respectable growth. But not like that. Not Series A growth. His hands are cold. He can feel the gap between where they are and where he is. He spent forty minutes looking at that founder's accomplishments before he turned to his own work. The forty minutes had a taste of envy underneath the analysis.

Maya has a coffee with another consultant. The consultant casually mentions that she's raised her rates significantly. Maya feels her stomach drop. She's been at her current rates for two years. The consultant is charging roughly double. Maya immediately wonders: am I underpricing? Am I demonstrating insufficient confidence in my value? Her hands grip her coffee cup. But also: how does she afford to charge double? Surely clients won't accept that. Or maybe they do. Maybe Maya's clients would too, and she's just not confident enough. The comparison settles into a low hum of anxiety. Her shoulders tense. She starts second-guessing everything about her pricing strategy, her value positioning, her market positioning. The comparison takes root.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex starts obsessively tracking his competitors. He has alerts on every announcement. He's doing financial modeling to estimate their burn rate, their growth rate, their runway. He's looking at their hiring to understand their product direction. His chest is tight. He's spending hours analyzing people rather than building his own thing. But he can't stop. Every comparison reveals a gap. He's slower. He's further behind. He's less VC-backed. He's less well-positioned. His hands are cold. The comparison has become a monitoring system. He's tracking the gap constantly, which means the gap is always in his awareness, which means his baseline mood has shifted toward a kind of persistent anxiety. He's not building forward. He's building to catch up to someone else's vision.

Alex catches the comparison operating in real time within anxiety. The comparison fires before the thought completes — automatic, pre-rational, faster than analysis. Chest crushes. Alex watches the machinery: a neutral data point enters the system and exits as a deficit. The peer's action becomes evidence of Alex's inadequacy. The conversion happens in milliseconds.
