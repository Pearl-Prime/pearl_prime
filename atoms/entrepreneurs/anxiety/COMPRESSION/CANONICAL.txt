

## COMPRESSION v16
---

---
compression_family: C1
You're trying to solve uncertainty by gathering more information. The information reveals more uncertainty. The loop is self-perpetuating. Certainty is not an outcome. It's a myth you've been chasing. At some point, you have to move despite not knowing. Moving while uncertain is the only way forward. The knowing comes after the moving, not before.
---

## COMPRESSION v17
---

---
compression_family: C2
Your anxiety is trying to prevent something bad. It's not actually preventing anything. It's just making the waiting worse. The bad thing either will or won't happen. Your anxiety doesn't change the odds. What it changes is your present moment. You're trading your now for a maybe. The trade is always a loss.
---

## COMPRESSION v18
---

---
compression_family: C3
You've survived every difficult moment that's happened to you so far. One hundred percent survival rate. Your anxiety is predicting a moment you can't survive. But you've never actually met a moment like that. You're fighting a future that doesn't exist while damaging your present that does. Fight what's actually here instead.
---

## COMPRESSION v19
---

---
compression_family: C1
The shame about your anxiety makes the anxiety worse. You're anxious about being anxious. The second layer of judgment is the real problem. Anxiety is a physical response. It's not moral. It's not a failure. It's your nervous system being cautious. You can be cautious without being ashamed. The removing of shame is half the healing.
---

## COMPRESSION v20
---

---
compression_family: C2
Your perfectionism is anxiety's best friend. Each one demands the other. You believe that if you're just perfect enough, bad things won't happen. Perfection is impossible and it wouldn't protect you anyway. Bad things happen to perfect people. The only antidote is acceptance of imperfection. The acceptance is terrifying. The acceptance is also true.

## COMPRESSION v21
---

---
compression_family: C3
You're comparing your inside experience with other people's outside presentation. Their outside looks calm. You have no idea what their inside feels like. Everyone is anxious sometimes. The people who look fine are often having invisible crises. You're not uniquely broken. You're just honest about it. The honesty might feel like weakness. It's actually strength.
---

## COMPRESSION v22
---

---
compression_family: C1
Your body knows the difference between real danger and psychological anxiety. Real danger requires action. Psychological anxiety requires observation. You're treating psychological anxiety like real danger. You're running, hiding, preparing. None of these things stop the anxiety. Sitting with it while it passes is the only thing that works. The sitting is the hardest action.
---

## COMPRESSION v23
---

---
compression_family: C2
You're maintaining anxiety by avoiding the thing you're anxious about. The avoiding keeps the threat alive in your mind. The threat becomes larger in the avoiding. Facing it directly is the only way to make it smaller. The facing will be uncomfortable. The comfort of avoiding is temporary. The discomfort of facing is the path forward.
---

## COMPRESSION v24
---

---
compression_family: C3
Your anxiety is giving you information about what you care about. You care about being successful. You care about being seen. You care about not failing. This caring is actually good. The anxiety is just the flip side of caring. You could numb the anxiety by not caring. Or you could accept the anxiety as proof that something matters. The mattering is worth it.
---

## COMPRESSION v25
---

---
compression_family: C1
The checking compulsion is a loop. You check to get relief. The relief is temporary. The temporary ends and you check again. You've trained your body that checking is the solution. But checking is the addiction. Breaking the loop requires enduring one moment of discomfort without checking. The one moment breaks the pattern. The pattern breaks in a day. Try it.
