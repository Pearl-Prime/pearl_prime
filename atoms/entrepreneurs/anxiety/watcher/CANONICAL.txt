

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/watcher/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex is in a meeting with his team. He's talking about the quarterly roadmap, projecting confidence. But there's a part of him watching himself. Observing: is this convincing? Are they believing this? Do I sound like I know what I'm doing? His chest is tight. He's performing confidence while privately uncertain. The performance is necessary. If he shows doubt, the team doubts. If the team doubts, they leave. So he performs. But the performance requires a watcher inside him, constantly evaluating how the performance is landing. His jaw is tight. The watcher never rests. Even in moments designed to be leadership, part of his attention is on monitoring how he's being perceived. It's exhausting.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/watcher/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex notices the watcher during a team meeting. He's talking, and he notices he's watching himself talk. He says out loud: "I notice I'm performing confidence right now because I'm uncertain about something." The team responds positively. They ask questions. They engage differently. His chest loosens. He discovers that his honesty is more connecting than his performance ever was. The watcher protests. It says: this is dangerous, you're revealing weakness. But the actual response is: his team trusts him more.
