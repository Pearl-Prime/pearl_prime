

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
My shoulders live somewhere near my ears. My jaw is permanently clenched. My stomach produces acid at the thought of a meeting. I've outsourced my entire emotional experience to my body, and the body is exhausted.
This is the somatic healing mechanism: the reality that the body holds what the mind refuses to process. The fear that would be overwhelming if felt directly gets compressed into muscle tension. The grief that would incapacitate if acknowledged gets stored in the chest. The shame that would paralyze if faced gets locked in the belly.
The automatic response is to ignore the body. To push through the signals. To treat physical sensation as noise rather than information. And for a while, this works. You keep moving. You keep functioning. You keep the business running despite what your body is screaming.
But the body doesn't forget. It holds. It accumulates. It stores the somatic memory of every emotion you decided was too much to feel. Every sensation you decided was inconvenient. Every signal you decided to ignore.
And one day, the body stops accepting the silence. It demands to be heard. And the mechanism that moved all feeling into the body now has to find a way to process what the body is holding.
What happens when you realize your entire nervous system has become the message you refused to deliver to yourself?
