--- variant: v01
You can't pour from an empty cup is a cliche because it's true and because founders keep trying to disprove it. Compassion fatigue is the receipt for every withdrawal made from an account you forgot to fund. The work now is accepting that refilling it isn't selfish. It's operational.

--- variant: v02
The numbness toward people you used to care deeply about isn't personal failure. It's resource depletion. Your compassion ran out of runway because nobody -- including you -- treated it as a finite resource that requires intentional replenishment.

--- variant: v03
Caring again starts with giving yourself permission to stop caring for a moment. Not permanently. Not callously. Just long enough to remember what your own needs feel like before they're overwritten by everyone else's.
