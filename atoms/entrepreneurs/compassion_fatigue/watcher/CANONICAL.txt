---
path: The emotional shutdown of running the operation
BAND: 2
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex closes the laptop at 9 PM. Feels nothing. Not relief, not fatigue, nothing. The business survived another day. The feeling that used to arrive with survival — pride, adrenaline, something — does not arrive. Alex notices the absence only in passing. Names it "just tired." Moves to the next task. The shutdown has already started. Alex does not clock it yet.

BAND: 2

## MECHANISM_PROOF v04
---
path: The guilt of being untouched when others are breaking
BAND: 4
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
A cofounder breaks down in the hallway — real tears, real collapse. Alex watches, calculates next steps, feels nothing rise in his own chest. Should feel something. Concern. Grief. Anything. Instead: a checklist. Cover the meeting. Send flowers. Adjust the roadmap. Alex registers the absence of feeling as its own kind of alarm. Guilt arrives late, secondhand — guilty about not being able to access guilt directly.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: The team that used to believe
BAND: 3
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
The team used to light up in all-hands. Now they nod, take notes, leave on time. Alex's old move: work harder to re-earn the belief, personally, alone, at 2 AM. This time Alex asks three people directly what changed. The answer is not about Alex's effort. It is about clarity they stopped getting eighteen months ago. Alex writes one clear memo instead of working one more weekend. Belief does not return that day. Something in the room loosens anyway.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: The watching instead of living
BAND: 3
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex catches himself watching his own life like a dashboard — metrics up, metrics down, sentiment green, sentiment red — instead of living inside it. Old move: watch harder, catch the dip before it happens. This time Alex closes the laptop mid-afternoon and takes an actual walk, no phone, no scanning. Nothing collapses. The dashboard is still there when he opens it back up. He notices, for eleven minutes, what it felt like to be a person instead of a monitor.

## TURNING_POINT v05
---
path: The stranger who used to be your friend
BAND: 3
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF1
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
A friend from before the company calls. Alex realizes, mid-conversation, he has nothing to say that isn't about the business. The friend used to be someone Alex told everything. Now Alex cannot remember the last real thing he told anyone. Old move: end the call quickly, blame busyness. This time Alex says it plainly: "I don't know how to talk about anything but work anymore, and I don't like that." The friend says: "Then let's talk about that." They do, for an hour. It is the first uncalculated conversation Alex has had in months.

BAND: 1
SEMANTIC_FAMILY: SF3

Alex sits through a leadership meeting and notices, cleanly now, which parts are real and which are performance. He says what is true instead of what is expected. One answer is "I don't know yet." This is what the meetings feel like now — fewer words, no performance, more true.

---
Alex scans for threats related to compassion fatigue constantly. Every meeting is a minefield of potential signals. Every silence is suspicious. Every change in routine is a warning. Throat closes. Vision narrows. The watcher runs its protocol and Alex is exhausted from monitoring a threat landscape that may not contain threats. ## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex's vigilance about compassion fatigue reaches a breaking point. The watcher has been running at full capacity for so long that Alex cannot remember what it feels like to be in a room without scanning it. Face burns. Chest crushes. Then the watcher fails. It misses something real — a genuine issue that was hiding in plain sight while the watcher was busy analyzing phantom threats. The failure is catastrophic for the watcher's credibility. It was scanning everything and caught nothing that mattered. The frame shatters: the watcher is not effective. It is comprehensive. And comprehensiveness without accuracy is just exhaustion with a purpose. Hands shake. Alex realizes the watcher must be retired and replaced with something targeted, specific, and rest-compatible. The current system is destroying the person it was built to protect.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Maya faces maximum uncertainty in compassion fatigue. The watcher wants total surveillance — monitor everything, trust nothing, scan every signal. Maya chooses targeted awareness instead. Identifies three things that matter. Monitors those. Releases the rest. Chest hollow. The uncertainty remains. The watcher screams about everything Maya is not monitoring. Maya holds the discomfort of not knowing. The three things that matter are tracked. Everything else is noise. Hands grip the desk. The outcome proves the approach: targeted awareness caught the important signals. Comprehensive surveillance would have drowned them in noise. Maya is a person who can live with uncertainty — not by monitoring it away but by choosing what deserves attention and letting the rest exist unscanned. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex measures the watcher's cost within compassion fatigue. Approximately thirty to forty percent of cognitive resources go to surveillance — reading faces, interpreting silences, monitoring tonal shifts, tracking body language. Chest crushes. The remaining sixty percent handles actual work. Alex has been operating at sixty percent capacity and comparing the output to peers operating at one hundred percent. The deficit is not ability. It is bandwidth. Hands shake. The pattern persists because the watcher feels like a professional skill — attentiveness, awareness, emotional intelligence. But it has crossed from skill to compulsion. The watcher no longer enhances performance. It drains it. The protective logic is that stopping the surveillance means missing threats. But the surveillance catches phantom threats and misses nothing real.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex's watcher has been monitoring compassion fatigue for so long that Alex cannot imagine existing without it. The watcher is not a behavior anymore. It is a component of Alex's identity. Being watchful feels like being Alex. Chest tight. Alex sees the full architecture: the watcher has merged with the self. Removing it would feel like removing a limb. Alex does not know who Alex is without surveillance. Hands cold. The pattern persists because the watcher is no longer a strategy. It is a personality trait — built from decades of practice until it became structural. It is not something Alex does. It is something Alex is. And changing what you are requires a different kind of work than changing what you do. Alex's eyes are wet. The watcher promised safety. It delivered a life lived in permanent observation of a world that might have been safe all along.
