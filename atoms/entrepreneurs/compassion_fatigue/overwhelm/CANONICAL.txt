## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex faces compassion fatigue from every direction simultaneously. The inbox. The meetings. The decisions that stack without resolving. Face burns. Chest crushes. The overwhelm has reached the point where even triage feels impossible — which fire to fight first when all of them are burning at the same intensity. Then Alex does something radical: drops everything for ten minutes. Steps outside. Breathes cold air. Returns and picks one thing. Just one. The frame cracks — the overwhelm was demanding simultaneous solutions to sequential problems. Shoulders drop One problem at a time. The rest wait. They have been waiting this whole time.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex's body shuts down from compassion fatigue overload. Not metaphorically. The nervous system hits its circuit breaker. Vision grays. Hands go numb. Thoughts stop forming. Jaw clenches. Alex sits in the shutdown and cannot move. The overwhelm exceeded the system's capacity and the system chose collapse over continued operation. In the collapse, a brutal clarity arrives: the body drew the boundary the mind refused to draw. The frame shatters — Alex was never capable of carrying this much. The belief in infinite capacity was the lie that broke the system. Stomach drops. The body's refusal is not weakness. It is the most honest communication Alex's nervous system has ever made. Alex cannot carry everything. The body proved it.
---

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Maya faces a heavy day of compassion fatigue demands and works within limits. Three priorities. Clear boundaries. Delegated tasks. Vision narrows. The overwhelm whispers that more should be happening. Maya checks the results instead of the feelings. The results are solid. The work is good within the limits set. Chest stays open Maya is not everything. Maya is something specific, focused, and effective. The overwhelm cannot compete with evidence. The evidence says the limits work.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Everything converges at once. Maximum compassion fatigue demands from every direction. The old pattern reaches for Maya — carry it all, drown in it all. Maya refuses. Triages. Delegates. Says the words: "I cannot do all of this and I need help with these specific things." Chest crushes. The help arrives. The load distributes. The crisis is managed — not by Maya alone but by a system Maya built by admitting limits. Hands shake. Maya survives the maximum overwhelm scenario not through heroic individual effort but through the architecture of asking for help. The identity is clear: Maya is a leader who builds capacity in systems, not a martyr who absorbs everything personally. Body is exhausted but intact

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex examines the overwhelm's structure within compassion fatigue. It is not a feeling — it is a cognitive failure. The brain cannot hold more than four to seven active concerns simultaneously. Alex is carrying twelve. The excess does not queue — it cascades. Each concern triggers the next and the cascade feels like drowning. Shoulders lock. Alex sees the machinery: the overwhelm is a bandwidth problem disguised as a workload problem. The workload might be manageable with a system. Without a system, the workload becomes emotional noise. Throat closes. The pattern persists because building a system requires cognitive bandwidth that the overwhelm has already consumed. The solution requires the resource the problem has depleted.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/compassion_fatigue/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex sees the overwhelm's deepest mechanism within compassion fatigue. The overwhelm is not about the volume of work. It is about the belief that Alex must hold all of it personally. Every task, every decision, every problem belongs to Alex. Vision narrows. The belief predates the role, predates the career, predates the profession. It was installed in a childhood where being needed meant being safe. Chest hollow. The pattern persists because the overwhelm is identity-level: Alex believes that being overwhelmed IS being valuable. Without the overwhelm, Alex fears being dispensable. The overwhelm is not a side effect of the work. It is the evidence Alex offers the world that Alex matters. Reducing the overwhelm would require dismantling the equation between suffering and worth. That equation is the oldest thing Alex owns.
---
