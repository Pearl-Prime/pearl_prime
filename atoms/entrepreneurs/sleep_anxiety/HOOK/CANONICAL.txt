

## HOOK v01
---

---
The business doesn't sleep. So you don't sleep either. You're mentally on call 24/7 for problems that might emerge from any direction. The on-call status is incompatible with rest.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You're lying in bed and your mind is working on tomorrow's presentation, next week's fundraising, next quarter's strategy. The work follows you everywhere including the place where you're supposed to be resting.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The anxiety isn't about sleep. It's about the business. But the business anxiety arrives at night when you should be off. You haven't built an off-switch.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Lying awake running scenarios about the business. Market shifts. Competitive threats. Team risks. Each scenario generates more scenarios. By 3am, you've catastrophized an entire empire built on speculation.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The business is your identity so business anxiety is identity anxiety. You can't put work problems aside because putting work aside means putting yourself aside.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You've learned to run on fumes. Poor sleep is normalized in startup culture. You're doing what everyone does. You're also destroying yourself in the process.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The isolation at night is intense. You can't talk to the team about being worried. You can't show them the anxiety. So you carry it alone into the darkness. The darkness amplifies it.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Your body is tired but your mind is running. The mismatch is uncomfortable. You're caught between the two. Neither one is getting what it needs.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
You told yourself you'd scale back. You told yourself you'd sleep better once things stabilized. Things never stabilize enough. Your sleep never gets better.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
The dread about tomorrow—whether it's a big meeting or just the usual operations—arrives before bedtime. The dread is your nervous system saying: threat ahead. Which makes sleep impossible.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
You're trying to lead from a place of exhaustion. Exhaustion makes everything harder. Every decision feels more consequential. Every risk feels more risky. You're making decisions on worse data.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
The sleep anxiety is partly the company's anxiety bleeding into your body. You're the container for the stress. Nobody else is holding it. So you're always holding it, even at night.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
You've romanticized the sleepless nights. Founders who don't sleep are working hard. Working hard is necessary. So you're equating sleep loss with dedication. It's not. It's just sleep loss.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The business is in your head at night. There's no escape. You've built something that occupies your full consciousness. The price is sleep.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
You're trying to control something—the business, the future, the outcomes—by staying awake. The staying awake doesn't create control. It creates exhaustion. But it feels like it's doing something, so you do it anyway.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
The anxiety about fundraising, growth, competition, execution, hiring—all of it arrives at night. During the day you can manage it with action. At night, there's no action available. So the anxiety just sits there.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
You're lying in bed and your body is signaling: I need rest. Your mind is signaling: the business needs work. The conflict keeps you awake.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
The nights are when you feel the weight of what you've built and what you're responsible for. The weight is real. It's crushing sometimes. Sleep doesn't come when you're being crushed.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
You know what would help: accepting that you can't control everything. But that acceptance feels like giving up. So you stay awake maintaining the illusion of control.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
The sleep anxiety is partly grief. Grief about what you've sacrificed to build the business. Grief about the person you were before the company consumed you. Grief about the life that's no longer available.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You're successful. You've built something real. And you're exhausted. The exhaustion is not a sign of failure. It's a sign that the load is unsustainable. But admitting that feels like admitting defeat.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The business problems are real but they don't need solving at 2am. They're not more solvable in the middle of the night. But your system doesn't understand that. It activates anyway.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
You're running yourself into the ground and calling it sacrifice. It might be necessary sacrifice. But it's still ground underneath you giving way.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
The isolation of entrepreneurship is real. You make decisions alone. You carry the consequences alone. You're awake alone at night thinking about them alone. The aloneness is part of what prevents sleep.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You've built a life where rest feels like irresponsibility. Peace feels like complacency. Anything other than grinding feels like failure. So you never rest.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
The business will continue without you for eight hours of sleep. This fact doesn't calm your nervous system. Your system has been convinced that constant vigilance is required. It's not. But it believes it is.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
You're lying awake and part of you knows this is unsustainable. Another part is convinced this is necessary. The conflict keeps you awake.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
The anxiety about the business is sometimes easier to hold than the anxiety about yourself. So you focus on the business while lying awake. You avoid the deeper fears by staying on the surface of work.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
You've normalized exhaustion as a status symbol. Entrepreneurs who sleep are not hungry enough. This belief is destroying you. It's also false.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
The nights are when the business feels most fragile. During the day, it's solid and real. At night, it becomes this delicate thing that could crumble any moment. The night vision of the business is not accurate. But sleep doesn't come when you're in night vision.
---
