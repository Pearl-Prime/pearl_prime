

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I lie down and the business comes with me. Not as a thought I choose to have. As an automatic system that activates the moment my body stops moving.
The sleep anxiety mechanism isn't insomnia, though it produces insomnia. It's the nervous system's refusal to downregulate. It's the belief that rest is the moment when the company will collapse. That sleep is the gap in surveillance that will allow disaster to strike.
So the mechanism keeps you awake. Keeps you available. Keeps you vigilant through the dark hours while your body begs for rest.
The automatic response is to accept the wakefulness. To get up and work. To be productive in the insomnia. To treat the hours of sleeplessness as additional working time that nobody else has access to. And for a while, this feels like you're using the insomnia productively.
But the insomnia isn't a resource. It's the mechanism expressing its belief that you can't afford to sleep. That the company needs you awake. That darkness is dangerous.
And your body, running on insufficient rest, becomes slower. Less creative. Less resilient. Everything you're trying to protect by staying awake is being destroyed by the sleep deprivation required to stay awake.
What happens when you're trying to protect the company by destroying the one thing the company actually needs—which is you in a functional state?
