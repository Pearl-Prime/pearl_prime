## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Lila gets into bed and the tasks arrive. Not as a list — as a weight. Every unfinished item presses on his chest. His breathing shallows. His shoulders creep toward his ears. The overwhelm that he managed during the day floods the nighttime with everything he deferred. The frame shifts when he realizes the bed is not where the tasks belong. They followed him because he never gave them a container. His jaw unclenches. He picks up a notebook. Writes three items. Closes the notebook. The tasks are in the container now. His chest opens. Not much. But enough.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Marcus's mind plays tomorrow's schedule on repeat. Every meeting, every deliverable, every conversation she needs to have. The replay is not planning — it is rehearsing anxiety. Her body is rigid under the covers. Her hands are fists. She has been lying here for two hours and her heart rate has not dropped below 90. The frame ruptures: tomorrow will happen whether she rehearses it or not. The rehearsal is not preparation. It is the overwhelm's nighttime shift. She exhales and lets tomorrow be a stranger. She will meet it in the morning. Her fists open slowly. Not completely. But they open.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Sasha opens the inbox: forty-seven messages. The overwhelm presses on the chest. Sasha counts three that are urgent. Forty-four that are not. Sasha opens the first urgent message. Not all forty-seven. Not the overwhelm's preferred method: everything at once. One message. Sasha has built the practice of subtraction — removing the non-urgent to see the urgent. The overwhelm protests. Sasha works the three. The forty-four wait. They are still there when the three are done. They are manageable now.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Wei is in the hardest week of the year. Everything is due. Everything is urgent. Her chest is a vise. Her hands are numb. The overwhelm is at peak intensity. She does not try to solve the overwhelm. She does the next task. Then the next. The overwhelm is the weather. The tasks are the path. She walks the path in the weather. She does not wait for the weather to change. The weather does not change. She changes her relationship to walking in it. Each completed task is a step. The overwhelm is still the sky. She is still walking.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Ava sees the overwhelm's architecture. It does not respond to actual volume. It responds to perceived simultaneity — the belief that everything must happen at once. Twenty tasks spread across a month are manageable. The same twenty tasks perceived as simultaneous are paralyzing. The overwhelm converts sequential demands into parallel ones. Ava's chest compresses. She sees the conversion happening in real time: the task list does not say 'all at once.' Her mind does. The overwhelm is an editing error. Her mind removes the timestamps and presents every deadline as now.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/sleep_anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nadia sees the overwhelm's terminal stage: it has become the operating system, not the error message. She does not experience overwhelm as a state. She experiences it as reality. The world is too much. Always. The overwhelm is not a response to conditions. It is the lens through which all conditions are perceived. Nadia's chest is compressed. The mechanism: the overwhelm installed itself as the default filter. Every input is pre-overwhelmed before it reaches her. She does not get overwhelmed by things. She receives things through overwhelm.
---
