--- variant: v01
Your valuation went up. Did you? Somewhere along the way, your self-worth got listed on the same ticker as your company. Good quarter, you're enough. Bad quarter, you're nothing. That's not a business model for a human being. That's a hostage situation with a cap table.

--- variant: v02
You built something from nothing and you still feel like nothing. The self-worth problem isn't that you lack evidence of your value -- it's that no amount of evidence sticks. Every achievement has a half-life of about twelve hours before the old narrative reasserts itself: you're only as good as your last sprint.

--- variant: v03
Strip away the title, the company, the team -- who are you? If that question makes your stomach drop, that's the signal. Your identity has been running on borrowed equity from the business. Self-worth that depends on the startup surviving isn't self-worth. It's a convertible note with your humanity as collateral.
