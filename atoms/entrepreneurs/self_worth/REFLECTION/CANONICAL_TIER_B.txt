

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I want to name something that happens specifically to self-employed people whose business model is based on their own time or expertise: the mechanism that measures self-worth through recurring revenue. I call this the MRR identity. MRR is monthly recurring revenue. It's the amount of money you can reliably expect to come in each month from recurring contracts or subscriptions. For many consultants, coaches, service providers, and knowledge workers, MRR becomes a metric that carries weight beyond its financial meaning. The mechanism is automatic. You internalize the metric. The mechanism is automatic because it's been reinforced by the entrepreneurial community. But the mechanism has collapsed the distinction completely. Your worth is now your revenue. Your expertise is what generates the revenue. Your attention and energy and skill are directly converted into money. So the fusion between the metric and the self feels more justified than it might for other business models. But the fusion is still a mechanism. And mechanisms can be named and examined.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
There's something protective about the MRR identity mechanism. It's protective against the terror of being worthless. If you can measure your worth through the revenue you generate, then you always have an answer to the question: am I valuable? You have a number. The protection works partly because the number is verifiable. It's outside yourself. Nobody can argue with it. The MRR is real. Which makes the identity feel more real. The mechanism is using the external validation of the metric to substitute for internal sense of worth. But the protection is fragile. Because if your worth is dependent on the metric, then every fluctuation in the metric becomes a fluctuation in your worth. The mechanism has created a system where your self-esteem is held hostage to your revenue.
