## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Ava attends a founder dinner and listens to revenue numbers that dwarf his own. Each number lands like a punch to the sternum. His hands are cold around his water glass. He shrinks in his chair. The comparison is converting their success into his worthlessness. The frame shifts when someone asks about his customer retention rate — the highest in the room. The comparison had no category for quiet excellence. It only measured loud growth. His chest opens slightly. His worth is not their metric. His metric is his.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nadia reads a Forbes list and counts the founders younger than her who have accomplished more. The count is seven. Her stomach drops. Her hands are ice. She has reduced her entire career to a ranking against strangers in a magazine. The comparison has stripped every achievement down to its relative position. The frame shatters: she is measuring her worth against people who did not build what she built, serve who she served, or survive what she survived. The list cannot contain her. Her worth was never on it.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Priya sits at the founder dinner and hears the revenue numbers again. The comparison fires. Priya lets it fire. Watches it land in the chest, tighten the hands, begin the calculation. Then Priya speaks: 'Our retention rate is what I'm proudest of.' The comparison wanted silence. Priya gave it a counter-metric. Not defensively. From a settled place. The chest is tight but the words are clear. Priya is not comparing anymore. Priya is reporting from a company that has its own measures. The comparison runs in the background. Priya operates in the foreground.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Dante is in the room with the founder whose success used to destroy him. The comparison fires at full intensity. Revenue. Team size. Press coverage. All of it. Dante's chest is tight. Hands cold. The comparison runs its full program. And Dante stays. Not frozen. Present. He speaks about his company from his company's reality, not from the comparison's ranking. The comparison is screaming. Dante's voice is calm. The two coexist. The comparison no longer runs Dante. Dante runs the company while the comparison does whatever it does in the background. The background is where it belongs.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Lila maps the comparison's data pipeline. It takes a single metric — revenue, headcount, press mentions — strips all context, and outputs a ranking. The ranking has only two positions: ahead and behind. Lila is always behind because the comparison selects the person who is ahead in that specific metric. The composite competitor does not exist. No one leads in every metric simultaneously. Lila's chest tightens as the mechanism becomes clear: the comparison is rigged. Not by malice. By architecture. It was built to find inadequacy. That is its only function. His hands unclench as he sees the wiring. The machine works perfectly. It just builds the wrong thing.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus sees the comparison's deepest layer: it is not comparing companies. It is comparing worth. Every metric gap between Marcus's company and someone else's is converted into a worth gap between Marcus and that person. Revenue is not revenue. It is a measure of human value. Headcount is not headcount. It is a proxy for how much the world respects you. Marcus's chest breaks open. The mechanism is total: the comparison has fused business performance with personal identity. There is no gap between the company's numbers and Marcus's sense of self. They are the same data stream. Separating them is the work.
---
