## TURNING_POINT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/turning_point/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Ava hesitates before sending the invoice. His rate is fair. His work is excellent. But something in his chest says the amount is too high. That he is overcharging. That they will see the number and realize he is not worth it. His face burns. His hands hover over the send button. The frame shifts: the hesitation is not about the invoice. It is about the belief that his work is inherently less valuable than he claims. He presses send. The client pays the same day without negotiation. The shame was never about the number. It was about the worthiness behind the number.
---

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nadia is asked to speak at a conference about her journey. She freezes. Her stomach drops. Standing on a stage and claiming her story has value feels physically dangerous. Her throat closes. Her hands go numb. The shame says: who are you to tell people anything? She almost declines. The frame shatters when the organizer sends the audience feedback from her bio submission: 73 percent said her topic is the one they are most excited about. The shame did not allow for the possibility that people want to hear from her. She accepts the invitation with shaking hands. The shaking is not weakness. It is the shame losing its grip.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Priya presents the real numbers at the board meeting. No sanitizing. No editing. The numbers are not good. The room is quiet. Priya's face is warm but the voice is steady. The shame says: they will see you as a failure. Priya says the numbers aloud anyway. The saying is the identity. A founder who presents real numbers, even the bad ones. The board asks questions. The questions are practical, not damning. The shame expected execution. The reality is a working meeting. Priya's hands unclench under the table.
---

## EMBODIMENT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/embodiment/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Dante is asked point-blank at a public event: 'Did your last company fail?' The shame detonates. Face hot. Hands ice. Every cell wants to disappear. Dante answers: 'Yes.' One word. The word hangs in the air. The audience does not flinch. Dante continues: 'And this is what I built from the wreckage.' The shame wanted the yes to be the end. Dante made it the beginning of the next sentence. The audience leans in. The shame recedes behind the story. Not gone. Behind.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Lila identifies the shame's core logic: visibility equals vulnerability. Every act of being seen — presenting numbers, sharing struggles, asking for help — routes through the shame processor, which tags it as exposure and triggers the hide response. The shame does not evaluate the content of the visibility. All visibility is dangerous. His face warms as he sees the mechanism. The shame treats a board presentation and a confession of failure identically: both are visibility, both trigger hiding. The mechanism has no granularity. His chest loosens slightly. The shame is not wise. It is indiscriminate.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/self_worth/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus identifies the shame's terminal architecture: it has become the foundation of his decision-making. Every choice passes through the shame filter: will this expose me? Every action is evaluated not for its merit but for its risk of visibility. The shame has become the executive function. Marcus's face is burning. The mechanism is complete: he does not make decisions. The shame makes decisions. He executes them. The decisions protect the shame, not the company. Not the person. The shame.
---
