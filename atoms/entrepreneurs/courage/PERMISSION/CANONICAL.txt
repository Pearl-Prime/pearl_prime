--- variant: v01
You're allowed to be afraid. Full stop. Not 'afraid but pushing through.' Just afraid. You have permission to feel the fear without it being a prompt to act, perform, or prove something. Sometimes the bravest thing is letting the fear exist without making it useful.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to be vulnerable without turning it into a leadership moment. Not every honest feeling needs to be a LinkedIn post about authentic leadership. You're allowed to feel something and not share it, not leverage it, not optimize it for brand building.

--- variant: v03
You're allowed to need courage for the small things. The phone call. The honest conversation with your partner. The moment of admitting 'I don't know.' Courage isn't only for the dramatic pivots. You have permission to be brave in the mundane, un-pitchable moments.
