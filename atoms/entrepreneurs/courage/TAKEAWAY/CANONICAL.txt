--- variant: v01
The next courageous thing isn't a bigger bet, a bolder pitch, or a harder conversation with your board. It's the terrifyingly small act of admitting that you're a human being running a company, not a company running as a human being.

--- variant: v02
Real courage in entrepreneurship has nothing to do with risk tolerance and everything to do with truth tolerance. Can you tolerate the truth of what you're actually feeling underneath the founder identity? That's the bet that changes everything.

--- variant: v03
You've proven you can be brave in boardrooms. The frontier now is being brave in the moments between them -- the ones where there's no audience, no pitch, no reason to perform. Courage in private is the kind that actually heals.
