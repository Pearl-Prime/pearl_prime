

## INTEGRATION v01
---

---
I raised my prices and only two clients left. I expected ten to leave.
My hands shook when I hit send on the email. But my body knew something: I deserved more.
The clients who stayed said they didn't care about price. They care about results.
The ones who left found someone cheaper. Good. That was the point.
Carry line: Courage means accepting who stays.
I'm still here.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---

---
I said no to the acquisition offer. Walking away from millions felt insane.
But staying independent felt like the only sane thing.
My body was clear. My mind kept calculating. My body won.
Now, two months later, the business is growing in directions I control.
That growth is slower but it's mine.
Carry line: Slow ownership beats fast exit.
I can live with slow.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---

---
I fired the biggest client. The money hurt for three weeks.
Then it stopped hurting. The relief was so profound I knew I'd made the right call.
I was working for someone who made me less of who I wanted to be.
Firing them was choosing myself.
Carry line: Freedom is worth the financial hit.
I'm finally free to be good.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---

---
I pivoted the company. The old vision died.
I mourned it. I really did.
Then I built something new and realized the old vision was holding me back.
Courage isn't always moving forward. Sometimes it's moving sideways.
Carry line: Pivot is not failure, it's evolution.
I'm evolved now.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---

---
I sent the price increase email to my smallest client.
They said yes immediately. No negotiation. Just yes.
I'd been undercharging them for years out of fear.
Fear is a terrible pricing advisor.
Carry line: Fear sells you short in money and respect.
I'm charging what I'm worth.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---

---
I closed the product line that wasn't working.
The sunken cost was real. The grief was real.
But the time it freed up was more real.
I built something better with that time.
Carry line: Closure is brave, not giving up.
I'm closing things I'm ready to close.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
I asked for investment even though I was terrified.

They said no.

I was devastated for two days. Then I realized I don't need their money.

I can grow without them.

The courage wasn't in the asking. It was in surviving the no.

**Carry line: No is information, not judgment.**

I'm learning to use that information.
