--- variant: v01
The startup world has a word for what you're feeling: 'founder funk.' It sounds temporary. It sounds manageable. It sounds nothing like the weight that makes getting out of bed feel like a Series A negotiation with gravity. Depression in founders hides behind productivity metrics and 'just a rough quarter' narratives until it can't anymore.

--- variant: v02
You used to care about the roadmap. Now you're going through motions sophisticated enough to fool your board but not yourself. The numbness isn't strategic detachment. It's what happens when a system designed to feel gets overridden by a system designed to perform for so long that feeling becomes a deprecated feature.

--- variant: v03
Everyone asks about the company. Nobody asks about you -- and you've made sure of that. The depression sits in the gap between the founder everyone needs you to be and the person you actually are at the end of the day when the laptop closes and there's nothing left to optimize.
