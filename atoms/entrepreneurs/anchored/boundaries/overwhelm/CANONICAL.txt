

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex works until 2 AM because a client emailed an urgent question at 11 PM. He could wait until morning. The question isn't actually urgent. But he jumps anyway. His chest is tight. He doesn't have boundaries between work time and personal time. The business doesn't respect his sleep schedule because he doesn't enforce that respect. His hands are cold. He tells himself that being responsive is good service. It is good service. But it's also destroying any space he has to recover. His shoulders are elevated. He hasn't thought about what time is actually his. Everything is potentially work-time if a client reaches out.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex sets a hard rule: email shuts down at 6 PM. He doesn't check it. Not until morning. It's difficult at first. His anxiety spikes. But by week two, something shifts. He's sleeping better. His mind has space to process other things. Miraculously, nothing catastrophic happens because he didn't respond to the 11 PM email. The client waits until morning. The world doesn't end. His chest loosens slightly.
---
