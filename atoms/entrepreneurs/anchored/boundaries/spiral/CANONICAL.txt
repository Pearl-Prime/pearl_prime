

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/spiral/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex receives a critical client feedback. He immediately spirals. The feedback means the client is unhappy. Unhappy clients leave. If they leave, revenue drops. His chest tightens. Without a boundary between work-time and personal-time, the spiral follows him home. He's lying in bed thinking about how to fix the feedback. He can't escape it. His hands are cold. The spiral lives in the spaces where he should be recovering. His nervous system never resets because the boundary-less work follows him everywhere.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex creates a boundary: work-time is 6 AM to 6 PM. Personal time is his. This is strictly enforced. When the spiral tries to follow him home, he notices it. He says: this is personal time, I'll think about this tomorrow. The spiral protests. He tolerates the protest and doesn't engage. By week two, something shifts. He has time to recover. The spiral is still there during work. But it has space to decompress during off-time. His sleep improves.
---
