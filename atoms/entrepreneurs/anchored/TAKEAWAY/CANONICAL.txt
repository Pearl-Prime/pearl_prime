--- variant: v01
Stability doesn't come from building bigger. It comes from being willing to stand still long enough to feel what's actually underneath you. The most grounded founders aren't the ones with the best plans. They're the ones who stopped mistaking motion for foundation.

--- variant: v02
You can't anchor yourself to the business and call it stability. The anchor has to be something that survives if the business doesn't. Finding that thing is the work nobody puts on a product roadmap but every founder eventually needs to ship.

--- variant: v03
Real anchoring requires you to stop performing steadiness and start actually feeling it. That means tolerating the wobble. The shaking isn't a sign you're falling apart. It's a sign you're finally standing on your own legs instead of the company's.
