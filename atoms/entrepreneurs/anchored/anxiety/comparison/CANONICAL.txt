

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/comparison/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex is on Twitter at 6 AM. His former competitor just announced Series A funding. Nine figures. His stomach drops. His chest tightens. He scrolls back through the timeline—the company was founded only two years before his. The founder's story is almost identical to his: same market, same problem, same energy. But they got capital. They got velocity. They're growing at a rate that makes his growth look anemic. His jaw clenches. He looks at his own numbers. Respectable growth. But not like that. Not Series A growth. His hands are cold. He can feel the gap between where they are and where he is. He spent forty minutes looking at that founder's accomplishments before he turned to his own work. The forty minutes had a taste of envy underneath the analysis.

## RECOGNITION v02
---

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Maya has a coffee with another consultant. The consultant casually mentions that she's raised her rates significantly. Maya feels her stomach drop. She's been at her current rates for two years. The consultant is charging roughly double. Maya immediately wonders: am I underpricing? Am I demonstrating insufficient confidence in my value? Her hands grip her coffee cup. But also: how does she afford to charge double? Surely clients won't accept that. Or maybe they do. Maybe Maya's clients would too, and she's just not confident enough. The comparison settles into a low hum of anxiety. Her shoulders tense. She starts second-guessing everything about her pricing strategy, her value positioning, her market positioning. The comparison takes root.

## RECOGNITION v03

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2

---
Carlos attends a restaurant owner meetup. One of the owners mentions their revenue number casually. It's double what Carlos is doing. The owner is operating a single location, same as Carlos. No fancy concept. No special position. Just competent operations. Carlos nods appropriately while his internal state shifts. He's working harder than this person, or at least feels like he is. He's doing more hours. He's more personally invested. And yet the financial outcome is half. His throat tightens. He starts questioning everything about his operation. Is he underpricing? Is his market weaker? Is he just less competent? The comparison spirals. He drives home thinking about what's wrong with his business.

## RECOGNITION v05

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Omar sees a competitive product get mentioned positively in a blog post he follows. The product is solving similar problems, targeting a similar market. The reviewer called it "elegant" and "intuitive." Omar's jaw clenches. His product is solving the problem too. But maybe not as elegantly. Maybe not as intuitively. He opens his product and suddenly he's seeing all the rough edges. He's comparing his product, in beta, to a competitor's product, which is presumably further along. He's comparing his metrics to their metrics, but doesn't actually know their metrics. He's building a narrative anyway. His shoulders tense. The comparison creates pressure to do something, anything, to differentiate further. He's not making a strategic decision. He's reacting to the gap between his perception of his product and his perception of a competitor's product.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex starts obsessively tracking his competitors. He has alerts on every announcement. He's doing financial modeling to estimate their burn rate, their growth rate, their runway. He's looking at their hiring to understand their product direction. His chest is tight. He's spending hours analyzing people rather than building his own thing. But he can't stop. Every comparison reveals a gap. He's slower. He's further behind. He's less VC-backed. He's less well-positioned. His hands are cold. The comparison has become a monitoring system. He's tracking the gap constantly, which means the gap is always in his awareness, which means his baseline mood has shifted toward a kind of persistent anxiety. He's not building forward. He's building to catch up to someone else's vision.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya has started pricing based on what others are charging, not what her work is worth. She's trying to match the market rate of people who are further along. She's undercutting slightly to be competitive. Her margin is smaller. Her status is lower. Her hands shake. She's abandoned her own pricing logic and substituted someone else's reference point. Now she's competing on price against people she doesn't fully understand. People who might have different cost structures, different market positions, different pricing strategies. She's making decisions based on comparison rather than clarity. Her throat is tight.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/comparison/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex takes a hard look at his comparison pattern. He realizes he's comparing his year two to a competitor's year four. He's comparing his self-funded bootstrap to someone who raised capital. He's comparing his chosen market to their chosen market. Every comparison is apples-to-oranges. More importantly, he realizes: his business is growing. It's not Series A growth. But it's healthy growth. His chest loosens. He unfollows the competitor. He stops tracking the gap. He refocuses on his own metrics, his own progress, his own vision. The anxiety doesn't disappear immediately. But it has less fuel when he stops comparing.
---

## TURNING_POINT v02
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/comparison/turning_point/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Maya decides to anchor her pricing to her value, not to what others charge. She thinks about the results she delivers, the problems she solves, the outcomes her clients experience. She raises her rates. A few clients push back. Most accept. Some actually appreciate the positioning shift toward higher value. Her hands warm. She realizes that pricing based on comparison kept her small. Pricing based on value allows her to actually serve the kind of clients she wants to serve. The comparison loses power when she stops using it as a reference point.
