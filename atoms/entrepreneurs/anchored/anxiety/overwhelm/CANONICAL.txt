

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex sits at his desk at 11 PM on Wednesday. He's wearing multiple hats simultaneously: handling a customer complaint, reviewing contractor invoices, updating the marketing calendar, responding to investor email. His chest is tight. His shoulders are elevated. He has no HR person. No ops manager. No executive assistant. There's just him and a growing pile of responsibilities that don't consolidate into single roles. His throat feels closed. An hour ago, he was writing marketing copy. Before that, fixing a server issue. Before that, on the phone with a key vendor. His brain never settles. It's always context-switching. The context-switching feels like competence—look at everything I can do—but it also feels like drowning in slow motion. His hands are cold. He has no delegation structure. Nobody can take anything off his plate because the plate is him. The business exists in the gap between what he does and what gets done. He can feel this clearly at 11 PM on a Wednesday when his nervous system is finally quiet enough to acknowledge it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex has stopped sleeping well. His mind keeps cycling through everything that hasn't been done. His chest tightens at 3 AM. He wakes up thinking about the thing he forgot yesterday. The overwhelm is creating a second overwhelm: anxiety about how much there is to do. He's started making mistakes. Simple operational oversights. Nothing catastrophic yet. But he can feel the margin for error shrinking. His cognitive capacity is diminishing under the weight of everything he's holding. His hands are cold. His breath is shallow. He hasn't had a full day off in months. Even on weekends, he's thinking about the business. His mind never fully disengages.

Maya is making worse decisions as fatigue accumulates. She's underbidding projects because she doesn't have mental capacity to think through pricing. ## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex writes down everything he's responsible for. The list is forty-three items. He realizes that one person working forty hours per week cannot sustainably manage forty-three responsibilities. He's not failing. The structure is failing. This shift in perspective is enormous. He's been interpreting overwhelm as personal inadequacy. He's realizing it's a resource problem. He starts categorizing: what must he do, what could be delegated, what could be eliminated, what could be delayed. His chest loosens slightly. Just mapping it creates distance from the overwhelm.
---
