

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/spiral/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex missed a monthly revenue target. The number is slightly down from last month. His chest tightens immediately. The narrative starts: the business is failing. If the business is failing, then his investors will see it. If his investors see it, they'll lose faith. If they lose faith, they'll pull capital. If they pull capital, the runway becomes nonexistent. The catastrophe spirals. From one missed metric, he's now at existential collapse. His hands are cold. His jaw clenches. He's constructing a logical chain: metric miss equals company failure equals personal failure equals total loss. The spiral takes maybe three minutes to construct. His nervous system is in emergency. His mind is cycling through bankruptcy, team layoffs, returning to employment, explaining to his family that he failed. All from a single datapoint. His stomach drops.
---

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/anxiety/spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex breaks the spiral by deliberately stopping the cascade. When a metric miss happens, he writes down the spiral: metric miss equals business failure equals investor loss equals collapse. He looks at it. He realizes: none of these steps are inevitable. A metric miss could mean: this one variable didn't perform as expected. That's it. He starts asking: what's one thing that could have caused this? What's one thing I could adjust? His chest loosens. He's interrupting the automatic catastrophization. He's not denying the miss. He's just refusing to extrapolate it into total collapse. The spiral still tries to start. But he has a tool now: the awareness that his mind does this.
---

## TURNING_POINT v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Maya talks to a therapist (or another founder, or someone who has perspective). She describes a business problem and watches herself spiral it into identity destruction. The other person stops her. They point out the cascade. They ask: is any one of these steps inevitable? She realizes: no. A project problem doesn't necessarily mean her whole business is broken. Her value doesn't evaporate based on one event. Her choice to be self-employed isn't invalidated by a single difficulty. Her hands warm. She starts catching the spirals earlier. She interrupts the cascade.
