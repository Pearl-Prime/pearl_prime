--- variant: v01
You're allowed to not have it figured out. You're allowed to be the founder who doesn't know where the ground is yet. Stability is something you build from honesty, not from performing confidence. You have permission to wobble.

--- variant: v02
You don't have to be the rock for everyone else right now. You're allowed to prioritize finding your own footing before supporting others. That's not abandonment. That's structural engineering. You can't hold the building up if the foundation is cracking.

--- variant: v03
You have permission to admit that the things you built to feel stable aren't actually making you feel stable. That admission isn't failure. It's the architectural review that every honest founder eventually does -- the one where you check whether the structure is sound or just tall.
