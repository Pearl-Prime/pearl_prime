

## HOOK v01
---

---
The business is you and you are the business, so burning out feels like the business failing, which feels like identity death, which is why you can't stop even when you're breaking.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You built something that requires everything from you and now it's consuming everything from you and you can't separate from it because separating means losing it.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The isolation you mistook for independence is actually burnout wearing a founder's label. No one checks on you because you said you were fine. You've been fine for so long you forgot what breaking looks like.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The risk you took masked the fear underneath, and now you're exhausted from maintaining both: the business and the performance of being unfazed by it.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You're always on because being off means the thing falls apart. So you can never be off. You've built yourself a beautiful cage.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The passion that got you here has calcified into obligation. But you can't admit it because the passion was supposed to be infinite. It wasn't. It's finite. You're out.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Every decision affects others, so every decision carries weight, so you never rest because rest means risk. You've made your own burnout mandatory.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You're waiting for the break-even point where it feels like success. But success looks like more work, not less. So the finish line keeps moving and you keep running.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The business is growing which means everything is growing: stress, responsibility, stakes. Growth that doesn't include your own well-being is just acceleration toward breaking.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
You can't afford to be burned out because the business can't afford it. So you're performing fine while actual fine is a memory.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
The people who work for you are depending on you, which means you can't fail, which means you can't rest, which means you're building toward inevitable failure anyway.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
You've sacrificed everything that makes life worth living for the promise that the business will eventually allow you to live. But it won't. It just expands to fill all the space.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
The dream of ownership was supposed to feel like freedom. Instead it feels like you're owned by your own creation.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
You sold yourself the story that burnout is the price of success. You're paying that price. You're not sure success is coming.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
The business needs you available at 2 AM. So you stay available at 2 AM. And 3 AM. And every night. Because what if something breaks and you're not there.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
You're not allowed to have bad days because bad days affect the business. So you have bad days alone, secretly, and then return to pretending.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
The risk is intoxicating until it isn't. Until you're past the point of exhilaration and into pure sustained panic disguised as drive.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You measure yourself in revenue and users and growth metrics. None of those metrics measure you. But you've forgotten there's anything else to measure.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The startup phase was supposed to be temporary. But temporary became permanent and now you're running on fumes and calling it hustle.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
You made the choice to do this, which means you can't complain about it, which means the suffering is silent, which means it's destroying you invisibly.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
The pressure to scale is infinite but your capacity is finite. You're operating at the intersection of those two truths and it's killing you.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
You're not supposed to rest. Rest is privilege. Rest is for people who haven't made the commitment you've made. So you don't rest and you don't admit it.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
The business is your identity, your income, your validation. Losing it would mean losing everything. So you're holding on with everything you have. Everything you have is not enough.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
You're building your own prison and calling it ambition. The bars look golden until you realize you can't leave.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
The loneliness of leadership is that no one can help because the responsibility is uniquely yours. So you carry it alone. And it's getting heavier.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
You said you'd never work for anyone else, which means you're working for yourself now, which might be worse.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
The story about loving what you do is true until it isn't. Until you're burnt out and you're not sure when the transition happened.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You're supposed to be inspired. You're supposed to wake up eager. You're waking up terrified. And you can't tell anyone because they're relying on your confidence.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The myth is that founders have unlimited energy. The reality is that founders are running on borrowed energy and it's coming due.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
You built something, and now it's building you into something you don't want to be. But you don't know how to step back because stepping back feels like betrayal.
---
