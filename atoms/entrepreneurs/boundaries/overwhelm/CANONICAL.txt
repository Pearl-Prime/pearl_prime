## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex examines the overwhelm's structure within boundary-setting. It is not a feeling — it is a cognitive failure. The brain cannot hold more than four to seven active concerns simultaneously. Alex is carrying twelve. The excess does not queue — it cascades. Each concern triggers the next and the cascade feels like drowning. Shoulders lock. Alex sees the machinery: the overwhelm is a bandwidth problem disguised as a workload problem. The workload might be manageable with a system. Without a system, the workload becomes emotional noise. Throat closes. The pattern persists because building a system requires cognitive bandwidth that the overwhelm has already consumed. The solution requires the resource the problem has depleted.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex sees the overwhelm's deepest mechanism within boundary-setting. The overwhelm is not about the volume of work. It is about the belief that Alex must hold all of it personally. Every task, every decision, every problem belongs to Alex. Vision narrows. The belief predates the role, predates the career, predates the profession. It was installed in a childhood where being needed meant being safe. Chest hollow. The pattern persists because the overwhelm is identity-level: Alex believes that being overwhelmed IS being valuable. Without the overwhelm, Alex fears being dispensable. The overwhelm is not a side effect of the work. It is the evidence Alex offers the world that Alex matters. Reducing the overwhelm would require dismantling the equation between suffering and worth. That equation is the oldest thing Alex owns.
