--- variant: v01
The boundary you need most isn't with your team, your investors, or your customers. It's with the part of you that believes your worth is measured by your availability. Until that boundary exists, every other one will feel like betrayal.

--- variant: v02
Setting a boundary doesn't mean you care less. It means you've finally done the math on what caring without limits actually costs. The ROI on unlimited availability is always negative -- it just takes founders longer to read that particular balance sheet.

--- variant: v03
Boundaries are the infrastructure you forgot to build. You designed systems, processes, and org charts for everything except protecting the person at the center of all of it. That's not an oversight. That's a founding assumption that needs to be challenged.
