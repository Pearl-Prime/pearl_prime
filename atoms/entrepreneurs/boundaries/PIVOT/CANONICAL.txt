--- variant: v01
You built a company with no walls around your own time, energy, or identity. The open-door policy isn't generosity -- it's a terms sheet where you gave away all the equity in yourself. Boundaries aren't bureaucracy. They're the only infrastructure that keeps the founder from becoming the product.

--- variant: v02
Every Slack ping feels like it could be the one that matters. So you answer them all, at all hours, from every location. You're not building culture. You're training your team to expect a founder who has no edges. The first boundary you need isn't with them. It's with the version of you who thinks disappearing into work is the same as commitment.

--- variant: v03
Your investors have term sheets. Your employees have contracts. Your customers have SLAs. You have nothing protecting your time, your rest, or your right to be unreachable. The business has boundaries everywhere except around the person keeping it alive.
