## TURNING_POINT v06
---

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex receives a routine notification and the alarm fires at full intensity — as if boundary-setting has reached crisis level. Stomach drops. Shoulders lock. The body does not distinguish between signal and noise. Every input related to boundary-setting triggers the same emergency response. Alex reads the actual content: nothing urgent, nothing threatening, nothing that requires the cortisol flooding through the system. The frame cracks. The alarm has been treating every piece of information as an emergency for so long that Alex forgot what proportional response feels like. Chest eases The alarm is not calibrated to reality. It is calibrated to fear.

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex's body reaches maximum boundary-setting activation with zero external trigger. No event. No email. No conversation. The alarm fires from sleep, from rest, from nothing. Chest crushes. Alex lies in the dark and the alarm runs its full emergency program against an empty room. This is the moment the frame shatters — the alarm does not need reality. It generates its own material. It fires because firing is what it does. The alarm is not a response system. It is a production system. It manufactures emergencies from silence. Hands shake. Alex sees the full architecture in the dark: no amount of safety will satisfy a system that creates its own threats. The alarm will fire from a beach, from a bed, from a life that is objectively fine. The old way — trying to make life safe enough for the alarm to stop — is no longer survivable because safe enough does not exist in the alarm's vocabulary.

## EMBODIMENT v05
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Maya receives the trigger and the alarm fires. Jaw clenches. Maya pauses. Checks the facts. The facts are neutral. The alarm is lying. Maya returns to work. The alarm fades within two minutes. Maya did not chase it. Did not build a catastrophe. Did not reorganize the day around a phantom threat. Chest holds Maya is a person with an overactive alarm who no longer obeys it. The alarm fires. Maya checks. Maya decides. The alarm does not decide anymore. Maya decides. The distinction is the new identity.
---

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
The genuine boundary-setting crisis arrives. Real threat. Real stakes. Maya's alarm fires at its usual volume — the same volume it fires for neutral emails and parking lot memos. Maya acts from the facts, not from the alarm. Makes decisions. Manages consequences. Navigates the real crisis without the false alarm's additions. Throat closes. Afterward, Maya sits in the quiet and the alarm runs its residual program. Maya lets it run. Maya navigated a real emergency by trusting reality over the alarm. The alarm was not needed. The facts were enough. Hands eventually warm Maya is a person who has proven, under maximum pressure, that the alarm is not necessary for survival. Survival comes from clarity, not panic. The alarm is legacy software. Maya's operating system has been upgraded.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex tracks the alarm's behavior around boundary-setting. It fires at the same intensity for genuine concerns and trivial irritants. A significant deadline and a routine email produce identical physiological responses — same cortisol, same chest pressure, same cold hands. Chest tight. Alex sees the machinery: the alarm has no calibration mechanism. It runs a single program at a single volume for all inputs. The pattern persists because the alarm's inability to distinguish intensity means Alex is always at maximum alert. Hands cold. There is no resting state. Every stimulus is emergency-grade. The body never recovers because recovery requires a period of non-emergency and the alarm never permits one.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/entrepreneurs/anchored/boundaries/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex's alarm has been running about boundary-setting for so long that the body has adapted to crisis as baseline. Normal cortisol levels are elevated. Resting heart rate is high. The body has restructured itself around perpetual emergency. Jaw clenches. Alex sees the full machinery: the alarm is not a temporary response to temporary threats. It has become a permanent state that the body treats as normal. The pattern persists because the alarm has rewritten the body's definition of normal. Stomach drops. Recovery is not about stopping the alarm. It is about teaching the body what normal feels like again — a process that requires undoing years of physiological adaptation to a state of constant threat. The alarm is not running. The alarm has been built into the architecture.
---
