

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT

I said no to a project last week. A real no. Not a soft maybe.

The guilt lasted three days. But something else lasted longer—the relief.

I still have time in my life. Margins exist. Breathing room. My chest doesn't feel permanently tightened anymore.

The project would have filled those margins. The project would have made me available at midnight again. The project would have cost me the three hours I've started protecting for myself.

Saying no feels like loss until you realize it's actually restoration.

My body knows the difference. My shoulders stay down now. My sleep is better. I'm not lying awake calculating how to fit one more thing.

**Carry line: Boundaries protect the life I claim.**

I'm claiming my life back.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PATTERN-BREAK

Friday is supposed to be mine. That was the rule I set.

Last Friday, a client called. "Quick call about Monday's deliverable?" I almost said yes. The word was forming.

Then I remembered: This is my Friday. This is my boundary.

I said: "I'm not available Fridays. I'll call you first thing Monday."

They said: "Oh, okay then."

That was it. They didn't fire me. They didn't think I was unavailable. They just adjusted to my rhythm.

By protecting Friday, I've protected my entire week. I show up Monday more effective because I actually rested.

The boundary isn't selfish. It's leadership.

**Carry line: Protected boundaries create protected teams.**

My team is watching me hold the line.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: PROOF-COLLECTION

I've been tracking what happens when I enforce boundaries. Nothing catastrophic.

Clients adapt. Deadlines move. Teams recalibrate. The world continues.

More importantly: I continue. I'm still here. I'm more functional. I'm less angry.

I used to worry that boundaries would cost me my business. What's actually happening is that boundaries are saving my business. Because they're saving me.

A depleted founder makes bad decisions. A protected founder makes good ones.

**Carry line: Boundaries aren't luxury, they're infrastructure.**

Every boundary strengthens the operation.

## INTEGRATION v07
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: THRESHOLD-AWARENESS

I know when I'm running on empty. It's not a vague feeling. It's specific.

When I skip lunch three days in a row. When my voice gets smaller. When I stop asking questions and just start solving.

These are my threshold signals.

Now, when I notice them, I stop. I add a boundary. I protect something.

It's not always easy. But it's easier than rebuilding from collapse.

I'm learning to be my own guardian. To notice before it breaks.

**Carry line: The threshold is where I turn around.**

I'm turning around earlier now.
