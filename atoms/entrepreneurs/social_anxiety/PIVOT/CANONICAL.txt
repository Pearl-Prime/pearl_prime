--- variant: v01
You can pitch a room of fifty investors but ordering coffee with a stranger makes your chest tight. Social anxiety in founders is selectively invisible -- it hides behind the performing version of you that's been trained to be 'on' in professional settings. The anxiety isn't about people. It's about the gap between the founder mask and the face underneath it.

--- variant: v02
Networking events feel like performance reviews where everyone is grading you simultaneously. You've gotten good at the small talk, the handshake, the confident opener. So good that nobody knows each interaction costs you something. The social battery isn't low because you're introverted. It's low because authenticity and performance are running on the same circuit and it's overloaded.

--- variant: v03
You cancelled the dinner again. Told them you had a work thing. The truth is simpler and more embarrassing: being around people without a business reason feels exposing. When you're not 'the founder,' you're just... you. And you've spent so long avoiding that person that meeting them in public feels genuinely unsafe.
