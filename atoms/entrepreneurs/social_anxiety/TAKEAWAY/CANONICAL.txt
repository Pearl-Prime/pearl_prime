--- variant: v01
The anxiety in social situations isn't about the situations. It's about the distance between who you are and who you've been performing as. In professional settings, the founder mask handles everything. In personal ones, there's no script. That's what's actually terrifying.

--- variant: v02
You've built a company and you can't relax at a dinner party. That's not a contradiction -- it's a data point. The social anxiety is telling you that your comfort zone has been narrowed to contexts where you have a role. Without the role, you feel exposed. That's worth paying attention to.

--- variant: v03
The cancelled plans, the avoided calls, the friends you haven't seen in months -- those aren't scheduling conflicts. They're the cost of a social battery that only charges in professional mode. The personal connections need a different kind of energy. One you haven't been generating because you didn't know it was depleted.
