
## REFLECTION v01
---
family: current
voice_mode: direct
mechanism_emphasis: naming
---
Compassion fatigue is not a character flaw. It is a resource problem. When you have been giving consistently without replenishing, the well empties. The emptying is physiological before it is moral. Your nervous system, not your values, is running the shortage.

The guilt you feel about having nothing left is real and misplaced. You are not a worse person than you were in September when the warmth came easily. You are a person who has been spending faster than you have been earning. The math is not a verdict. It is a budget.

Naming the depletion does two things. First, it separates you from it — you are not the emptiness, you are a person experiencing a period of emptiness, which is different. Second, it makes the problem legible. Legible problems can be addressed. Unnamed problems simply persist, wearing the face of personal failure.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: origin
voice_mode: gentle
mechanism_emphasis: pattern
---
You probably learned early that being the person who shows up was a good way to matter. The friend who answered the phone. The sibling who absorbed the family pressure. The student who held together the group project when others dropped their pieces. These roles carried reward. They also carried a cost that no one tallied.

Now you are in a semester that has added an item to the register every week. Friend in crisis. Roommate struggling. Family calling. The tally ran past your reserves and your body started rationing. The warmth narrowed. The presence thinned. The care became technical.

This is not betrayal of who you are. This is your body enforcing a boundary your mind would not. The recovery is not dramatic. It is incremental. It requires giving yourself the same quality of attention you have been extending outward.

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: practice
voice_mode: steady
mechanism_emphasis: intervention
---
Compassion fatigue has a physical signature. The chest feels less open when someone shares a hard thing. The voice gets quieter. The body adds a half-second of delay before responding. These are not personality changes. They are battery readings.

Before the next emotional conversation, place one hand on your sternum and breathe in. Notice whether the inhale lands fully or stalls. A stalled inhale is the gauge telling you that the well is shallower than usual, and the next conversation is going to draw from it anyway.

Reading the gauge does not stop the depletion. It changes what you do with it. Sometimes you proceed and accept that the conversation will leave you flatter than it found you. Sometimes you notice that the gauge is below the line where presence is even possible, and you protect yourself or the other person from a hollow exchange. Either choice is informed instead of automatic. That difference is the work.
