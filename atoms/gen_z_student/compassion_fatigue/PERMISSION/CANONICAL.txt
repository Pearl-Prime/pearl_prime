## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to stop being the therapist for your friend group. Put your phone down. Feel the weight leave your palm. Other people's pain is real, and it does not belong inside your ribcage. Your job is to be a friend, not a crisis line. You cannot give from a tank that is already cracked.

You have permission to say "I can't right now" when a roommate begins to spiral. Feel your chest before you answer the next call. Notice whether it is already tight. That tightness is information. A full body is not a selfish body. It is the only body capable of showing up tomorrow.

You have permission to feel nothing after the fifth difficult conversation this week. Emotional numbness is not coldness. It is the nervous system enforcing a boundary your mouth refused to set. Press your palm flat against your sternum. Feel the slow beat. Your heart still works. It is resting, not broken.

You have permission to close your door and not open it for an hour. The hall, the group chat, the communal kitchen — all of it will exist when you return. Your door does not abandon anyone. It gives your nervous system back to you for sixty minutes. The math is simple. You cannot regulate someone else from an empty account.

You have permission to stop checking in on everyone. Your shoulder muscles have been holding other people's stories for months. Roll them back. Feel the blade pinch and release. The person you worry about most is not currently in your arms. They are somewhere else, breathing. So are you.

You have permission to grieve the role without giving it up entirely. You took on the caretaker position because you are capable and someone needed it. That was real. And the weight is real. Place your hand on your collarbone. Feel the breadth of it. You are allowed to mourn the version of yourself who had more to give.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to receive support instead of only giving it. Sit back in your chair. Feel the backrest hold your spine. You are allowed to be held. Your friends' problems are not more important than yours by default. Your pain counts in the same accounting system. Let someone carry it for once.

You have permission to mute the distressing group chat overnight. Reach for your phone. Find the notification settings. Mute it until 9 a.m. Your unconscious mind has been running a background process on everyone else's crises. Muting is not abandonment. It is letting your nervous system sleep.

You have permission to stop performing okayness while internally depleted. Drop your shoulders. Feel what is actually there. Chronic emotional labor costs as much as physical labor and leaves less visible evidence. You do not owe anyone a calm face when your body is quietly underwater.

You have permission to tell a friend that their situation is beyond what you can hold. Feel your jaw before you speak. Notice whether it is braced. The truest kindness you can offer a struggling friend is honesty: "I love you and I'm not resourced for this right now." That sentence protects both of you.

You have permission to feel resentment toward someone you love. Resentment is not a character defect. It is the signal that a need went unmet for too long. Place your hand on your stomach. Feel the hot tightness there. Name it without judgment. Resentment named can be worked with. Resentment denied becomes bitterness.

You have permission to be angry that you absorbed everyone's hardest semester while yours went unwitnessed. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground. Your anger is evidence of what you gave without reciprocation. That anger is proportionate. It is pointing toward the right door.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to step back from a friend who only contacts you in crisis. Feel the pattern before you respond next time. The knot in your stomach when their name appears is data. You are allowed to love someone at a greater distance. Proximity is not proof of care.

You have permission to prioritize your own mental health appointment over a friend who needs you to listen right now. Close the conversation. Open your calendar. The appointment took three weeks to schedule. Your friend will find a way through this hour. You need to find a way through this semester.

You have permission to stop explaining yourself to people who require constant emotional processing. Your hands have been wringing themselves for weeks over relationships that drain the same amount regardless of what you put in. Press them flat on your thighs. Feel the stillness. Some relationships are not broken. They are simply more expensive than you can currently afford.

You have permission to feel compassion for someone without absorbing their suffering into your body. Touch your collarbone. Feel the line of bone across your chest. That bone marks where you end and someone else begins. Compassion crosses that line in both directions. Suffering does not have to.

You have permission to feel burned out on caring. The student who always checks in, always shows up, always remembers everyone's hard days — that student also has a nervous system with a capacity threshold. Burnout means the threshold was crossed, not that the care was fake. The care was real. The tank is empty. Both are true.

You have permission to rebuild slowly instead of bouncing back fast. Set your phone face down. Feel the table hold it. Recovery from compassion fatigue is not a weekend reset. It is weeks of redirecting your attention inward before you have anything sustainable to offer outward. Slow is not weak. Slow is how the tank refills.
