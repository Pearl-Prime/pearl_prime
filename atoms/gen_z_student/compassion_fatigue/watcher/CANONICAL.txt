
## RECOGNITION v01

Dani sits with a crying friend and watches herself perform empathy. She says the right things. She hands over a box of tissues. She nods at the right intervals. She is good at this. She is also watching from a remove, noting her own responses from the outside: there goes the hand on the shoulder, there goes the "I'm so sorry," there goes the careful listening face. The friend doesn't notice. The performance is perfect. What Dani notices, in the back row of herself, is that the warmth she used to feel during these moments has been replaced by a kind of technical accuracy. She is doing care without feeling it. The watching self catalogues this neutrally. The cost is what she finds when she gets home and the performance ends: nothing. Just a flat empty room where something warm used to live.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya volunteers on the crisis line on Thursday evenings and has done so for a year. Tonight she handles three calls in four hours. She is professional. She stays present. She follows protocol and adapts and closes each call with appropriate care. Afterward she sits in the volunteer room and watches herself sit in the volunteer room. She can see her own posture—shoulders slightly forward, hands folded on the table—and the image looks like a person who is fine. The person who is fine is not how she is inside. Inside is the watching, and the watching is noting that none of the three calls moved her the way they used to, and the not-moving frightens her more than the calls themselves. She cycles home in the dark and her legs work automatically while her mind keeps scanning for the feeling that used to be there. It is not there tonight. She doesn't know if it will come back.

## TURNING_POINT v03

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Arjun steps back from the student advocacy committee for one semester. He has been the person who shows up—to every meeting, every training, every community event—for two years. He sends the email to the committee chair and then sits at his desk and watches himself in the aftermath. He expected to feel relieved. What he feels instead is the specific quiet of having removed a weight that had also been a definition. The committee was how he knew himself as someone who cared about more than his own grades. Without the committee on his calendar, he watches himself have unstructured time for the first time in months and does not know what to do with it. He opens a book he bought last summer that has nothing to do with advocacy or school. He reads six pages. His hands hold the book steadily. The watching self is still there, still narrating. But the narration has less urgency than it did in the meeting room. That's a small difference. Small differences are all he's working with.
