## PIVOT v01
---

---
The exhaustion you feel after holding space for everyone is not weakness. It is a receipt — proof of what you gave. The question is not whether to stop caring. It is whether to start including yourself in the group you care for.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You became the person everyone calls because you were good at it. That is a real thing to be proud of. It is also a role that will hollow you out if it never turns around. Your availability has been treated as infinite. It is not.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Your shoulders are carrying weight that was never meant to be permanent storage. Other people's pain was supposed to move through you — witnessed and released, not stored in muscle. What you call empathy has become architecture. The next step is renovation, not demolition.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
The friend group needed a safe container, and you became it. That is a generous and exhausting thing to do for two years. Containers need to be emptied before they can be refilled. You are not being selfish by putting yourself down for a while. You are doing maintenance.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
Compassion fatigue does not mean the compassion ran out. It means the delivery system is overwhelmed. The care is still there, behind the numbness, behind the irritation. It needs a different route out — one that starts with you, not with whoever is currently in crisis.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
The roommate who cries at 1 a.m. does not know how much the 1 a.m. visits cost you. They are not being malicious. They are drowning and grabbing the nearest rope. You are allowed to lower your rope and walk to higher ground. That is not abandonment. That is survival.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
You learned early that staying attuned to other people's emotional states kept things safer. That skill served a purpose. In a dorm with four people's worst semesters, it is running day and night without a break. The skill needs a power schedule, not a constant feed.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
The chest heaviness after another late-night conversation is not a sign that you are doing it wrong. It is a sign that your body is paying for something. Notice the cost. The cost is information about what needs to change, not evidence that you should feel guilty for having limits.
---
