## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to grieve on a different timeline than everyone expects. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept you. Grief has no deadline, no due date, no extension request. Your body sets the pace. Trust the body.

You have permission to laugh during grief. Place your hand on your belly. Feel the muscles that contract for laughter and for sobbing. The same anatomy produces both. Joy does not cancel sorrow. They share a mouth, a diaphragm, a set of ribs.

You have permission to fail the exam you studied for through tears. Grip your pen. Feel the barrel between your fingers. A grade earned while grieving measures nothing about your intelligence. It measures the impossible conditions under which you sat down and tried.

You have permission to keep their photo as your lock screen. Press your thumb to the screen. Feel the glass warm under your touch. The dead do not need you to erase them from your daily view. Their face on your phone is a memorial that fits in your pocket.

You have permission to talk about them in present tense. Feel your tongue move behind your teeth. "They love that song." "They hate mornings." The grammar of love does not require past tense. Your mouth carries them forward in verb form. Let it.

You have permission to ask for an extension without explaining the full story. Place your hand on your desk. Feel the wood. "I am dealing with a loss" carries enough information. Professors hear the weight in those six words. You do not owe the details to anyone holding a gradebook.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to be furious at the person who died. Press your fists into the pillow. Feel the resistance. Anger at the dead is grief in a different costume. The love did not vanish. It rearranged into rage. Both are real. Both belong.

You have permission to sleep twelve hours and still feel exhausted. Roll over. Feel the heaviness. Grief metabolizes through the body. The fatigue is not laziness. It is the system processing a loss the schedule will not pause to acknowledge.

You have permission to forget for an hour and feel guilty about it. The forgetting is not betrayal. The mind cannot hold acute grief continuously. The breaks are mercy. The guilt is just love checking in.

You have permission to wear something they would have liked. Touch the fabric. Feel its weight on your shoulders. Wearing the shirt is not pretending they are still here. It is carrying them forward in an object the body recognizes.

You have permission to cry in the middle of the cafeteria. Lower your fork. Let it land. The tears are not a scene. They are a body responding to a stimulus your roommates cannot see. People who matter will not need an explanation.

You have permission to take a different route across campus to avoid the place that triggers the memory. Cross at the other corner. Feel your feet make the new turn. The avoidance is not weakness. It is conservation. Take the long way until the short way is bearable again.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to mark the anniversary alone. Sit in your dorm. Light a candle. Watch the wick catch. Public ritual is one option. Private ritual is another. Both honor the person. Choose the one your body can hold this year.

You have permission to tell a new acquaintance about them the first time they come up. Speak the sentence. Watch the listener absorb it. Hiding the loss accumulates a small lie inside every introduction. Tell the truth at the start.

You have permission to keep their voicemail saved without listening to it. Lock the phone. Set it down. The voicemail is an artifact, not a homework assignment. Sometimes its existence is enough. Listening when you are ready will be a different day.

You have permission to stop attending the support group when it stops helping. Walk past the door. Feel your feet keep moving. Groups have phases. Your phase may be different from theirs now. Leaving is not regression. It is a body knowing what it needs next.

You have permission to ask for a deadline extension six months after the death, not just six weeks. Email the professor. Type the date. Grief does not honor the syllabus calendar. The professor cannot grade you back to normalcy. Most will extend if asked plainly.

You have permission to laugh at the funeral memory the family shares. Feel your shoulders shake. Hear the laugh leave your throat. The dead were funny. Remembering them as funny is fidelity, not betrayal. The laugh keeps them dimensional.
