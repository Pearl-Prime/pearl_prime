## SCENE v01
---

---
The phone call came during Tuesday's lecture. You stepped into the hallway. The fluorescent lights buzzed overhead. Your back hit the cinderblock wall and slid down until you sat on the linoleum. Your knees pressed against your chest. Your phone stayed warm against your ear long after the voice stopped speaking. Feel the wall behind you. It holds your weight when your legs refuse.

Your roommate plays music across the room. A song comes on that belonged to the person you lost. Your breath catches mid-inhale. Your ribs lock. Your eyes burn. Press your hand against the mattress. Feel the springs push back. The song fills sixty seconds of air. The memory fills your entire chest. Both will pass through you. Neither destroys you.

The dining hall serves their favorite food. You stand in line staring at the tray of mac and cheese. Your throat closes. Your hands grip the tray until your knuckles go white. The person behind you bumps forward. Your feet carry you past the station. Feel your shoes grip the tile. Your body navigates this room on autopilot. Autopilot is not failure. It is survival engineering.

Finals week arrives and grief sits in the empty chair beside you in the library. Your textbook blurs. Your highlighter cap stays on. Your hand rests flat on the page. Feel the paper under your palm. The words wait. Grief does not respect academic calendars. Your body sits here between two impossible demands: perform and mourn. Sitting in this chair meets both halfway.

You open your phone to text them. Your thumb types three letters before the memory lands. They will not reply. Your stomach drops through the floor. Press the phone against your thigh. Feel its flat surface against your leg. The impulse to reach out proves the bond was real. The silence that follows proves the loss is real. Both truths coexist in the same hand.

The shared apartment fills with your roommates' laughter. You sit on your bed with the door open. The sound reaches you through a thick pane of glass that no one else can see. Your hand touches the doorframe. Feel the wood grain under your fingertips. Laughter exists on the other side. Grief exists on this side. The doorframe holds both rooms together.
