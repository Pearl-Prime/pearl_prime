## HOOK v01
---

---
You walk into the lecture hall Monday morning. Third row, aisle seat — that was their spot. The chair sits empty. Your chest folds inward like wet cardboard. The professor starts talking about supply curves. Your lungs forget their rhythm. Nobody in this room knows your ribs just became a cage.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The group chat still shows their name. Green dot gone. Your throat closes around words you never typed fast enough. Three dots that never arrived. The notification sound still fires through your nervous system like voltage. Your body keeps checking a door that locked from the other side.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Midterms do not pause for funerals. The registrar has no checkbox for "my person died." Your stomach drops through the floor every morning at 7 AM — the time you used to get that text. Your GPA becomes a number you carry in the same hands that carried a casket last Tuesday.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Your roommate asks why you keep the overhead light off. You cannot explain that darkness feels closer to where they went. Your hands grip the comforter at 2 AM. The dorm walls hold sound like a confession booth. Grief turns a twin XL into a raft on black water.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Everyone on campus moves forward. Backpacks swinging. Coffee in hand. Your jaw locks when someone laughs near the spot you last saw them. Grief in your twenties has no script. No elder pulls you aside. Your body carries the funeral home smell into organic chemistry lab.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The semester keeps scrolling. Deadlines stack. Your shoulders carry textbooks and the weight of someone who will never graduate. You sit in the dining hall alone, fork frozen mid-air. The empty tray across from you holds more truth than any condolence card.
---
