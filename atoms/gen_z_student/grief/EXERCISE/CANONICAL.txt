## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Open your phone. Find the last message from them. Do not read it yet. Place your phone face-down on your chest. Press both hands over it. Feel the weight of the device against your sternum. Breathe into the pressure point. Three breaths. Now turn the phone over. Read the message once. Place your hands back on your chest. Let the words land in the body that held them first.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand up. Walk to the nearest doorway. Stand in the frame. Press your palms against both sides — left hand on the left frame, right hand on the right. Push outward like you are trying to widen the opening. Hold for ten seconds. Release. Step through. This is the practice: pressing against the structure that holds you, then choosing to move forward. Repeat with the next doorway. Your legs carry grief and still walk through every threshold.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Sit somewhere private. Set a timer for two minutes. Place one hand on your chest and one hand on your throat. Hum one low note. Not a song. Just a vibration. Feel it travel from chest to throat and back. The hum proves your body still produces sound even when words fail. Let the vibration be the only language for two minutes.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit in a chair. Let your spine round forward until your chest rests on your thighs. Arms hanging. Head heavy. Stay folded for thirty seconds. Then roll up slowly — one vertebra at a time — until your shoulders sit over your hips. This is not posture correction. This is your body practicing collapse and recovery in the same minute. Repeat once.
