## PIVOT v01
---

---
Your chest did not break. It absorbed an impact designed for someone with decades more practice. That compression you feel is not weakness. It is your sternum holding the full weight of love.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Your hands keep reaching for the phone. That reflex is not dysfunction. Your nervous system built a circuit for connection, and the circuit still fires. The reaching proves the bond existed in your body, not just your memory.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The words jammed in your throat do not mean you failed to speak. Grief fills the vocal channel first. Your throat chose silence because the truth was too large for a single sentence.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
That hollow pit in your stomach sits there because your body registered the absence before your mind could. The gut grieves faster than the brain. Trust the organ that knew first. It is trying to digest the undigestible.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
Your legs feel heavy because they carried you through a week that should have stopped you entirely. The heaviness is not collapse. It is the weight of someone who kept walking when the ground disappeared.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Your shoulders climbed toward your ears the moment the news arrived. They have not come down. That elevation is not tension. It is your body bracing for the next blow in a world that already delivered the worst.
---
