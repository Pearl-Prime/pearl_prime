The loss split your timeline into before and after. Before, the campus felt navigable. After, every hallway holds a shadow. Your feet still walk these halls. Press your heels down. Feel the floor. The hallway did not change. You changed. And you keep walking through changed.

People say "let me know if you need anything" and your mouth says "thanks" while your body screams for something no one can give. Your jaw holds that scream as tension. Unclench it now. Let your lips part. The thing you need does not exist in any offer. Grieve that too.

Guilt rides alongside grief. You laugh at a joke and guilt punches your ribs. Your smile drops. Your hand goes to your stomach. Feel the clench. Laughter does not betray the dead. It proves the living still have breath to spare. Spend it.

The semester marches forward. Deadlines stack up. The world did not stop when your world stopped. Your hands still turn pages. Your fingers still type. Touch the keyboard. Feel the keys under your fingertips. These hands grieve and produce simultaneously. That is not cold. That is extraordinary.

You carry the loss into rooms where no one knows. The lecture hall. The campus bus. The coffee shop. Your backpack holds books. Your chest holds absence. Place your hand on the strap. Feel the weight pull on your shoulder. You carry both loads across the same campus. That strength goes ungraded and unnoticed. It is still strength.

Time does not heal. Time provides distance. Your body uses that distance to build new neural pathways around the wound. Touch your temple. Feel the bone underneath. The brain rewires itself around loss the way a river reroutes around a boulder. The boulder stays. The water learns.
