## THREAD v01
---

---
The loss split your timeline into before and after. Before, the campus felt navigable. After, every hallway holds a shadow. Your feet still walk these halls. Press your heels down. Feel the floor. The hallway did not change. You changed. And you keep walking through changed.

People say "let me know if you need anything" and your mouth says "thanks" while your body screams for something no one can give. Your jaw holds that scream as tension. Unclench it now. Let your lips part. The thing you need does not exist in any offer. Grieve that too.

Guilt rides alongside grief. You laugh at a joke and guilt punches your ribs. Your smile drops. Your hand goes to your stomach. Feel the clench. Laughter does not betray the dead. It proves the living still have breath to spare. Spend it.

The semester marches forward. Deadlines stack up. The world did not stop when your world stopped. Your hands still turn pages. Your fingers still type. Touch the keyboard. Feel the keys under your fingertips. These hands grieve and produce simultaneously. That is not cold. That is extraordinary.

You carry the loss into rooms where no one knows. The lecture hall. The campus bus. The coffee shop. Your backpack holds books. Your chest holds absence. Place your hand on the strap. Feel the weight pull on your shoulder. You carry both loads across the same campus. That strength goes ungraded and unnoticed. It is still strength.

Time does not heal. Time provides distance. Your body uses that distance to build new neural pathways around the wound. Touch your temple. Feel the bone underneath. The brain rewires itself around loss the way a river reroutes around a boulder. The boulder stays. The water learns.

## THREAD v02
---

---
The absence follows you into spaces the person or thing never occupied. The lecture hall has never held this loss and still you carry it there. Your backpack holds notebooks. Your chest holds the absence. Set the backpack down and feel the brief lightening. The absence stays. But the backpack is the part you can put down.

You stopped eating in the dining hall because the food stopped mattering. Your taste buds have not reported accurately since the loss. Touch your tongue to the roof of your mouth. Feel the textured surface. The food will matter again. The texture comes back before the taste. Start with texture.

The grade on the assignment represents a self who could focus. You became a different self the day the loss arrived. Your hands completed assignments your mind could not supervise. Touch your hands. They carried the work when the mind was absent. They can be trusted with the next assignment too.

Social withdrawal feels like protection and functions as isolation simultaneously. Both things are true. Your body needs quiet. Your nervous system also needs the presence of other regulated bodies. Identify one person who does not require you to explain the grief. Sit in proximity to them. Feel their warmth. That is enough.

Grief and guilt visit together in the early phases. The guilt asks what you could have done differently. Your stomach contracts around the question. The question has no useful answer — only an exhausting one. Press your hand flat on your stomach. Feel the contraction. Let it pass without answering. The question is a trap the grief set.

Your body will find its way back to the things it loved. Music will matter again. The smell of certain foods will return with memory rather than nausea. Touch your ears. Feel them. They are waiting. The world is still producing sound. You will hear it fully again. Not today. But the capacity is intact.

## THREAD v03
---

---
Grief after a relationship ends carries different shame than grief after a death. The culture does not provide ceremony for it. Your body grieves the same biological attachment circuits regardless of the cause. Pressing your hand to your chest does not distinguish between categories of loss. Grief is grief. Your body does not require cultural permission to be accurate.

First-year losses hit differently because you have not yet built a campus support system dense enough to absorb them. The loss arrives before the roots are deep. Feel how lightly you are currently tethered to this place. That lightness is not failure to adapt. It is timing. Roots take time. Grief arrived early.

You expected to feel better by now. The expected timeline was shorter than the actual one. Your nervous system is still processing. Touch your breastbone. Feel the unfinished quality there. The body does not grieve on a schedule. "By now" is a story the grief is using to produce shame. Ignore the timeline. Follow the process.

Some of the people in your life have never lost the thing you lost and cannot fully reach you. That gap is real. It is not abandonment. It is a limit in their nervous system, not a limit in your worth. Sit in that gap without filling it with pretend understanding. Accurate aloneness is less damaging than false company.

Physical memorialization of loss is not morbid. The photo on your desk, the playlist, the object that belonged to what you lost — these are sensory anchors that allow controlled access to the grief. Touch the anchor object if you have one. Feel its specific weight and surface. Controlled access is safer than the grief arriving unannounced. You chose the timing.

The grief that stays longest is the grief that does not get witnessed. Your story needs a listener — not a solver, not a fixer. A listener. Touch your throat. Feel it hold. That holding is the untold story compressing. Find one person, one hour, one telling. The body loosens what the voice releases.
