## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
The fourteen drafts never improve the message. They feed the fear. Each revision delays the discomfort of pressing send and inflates the cost of pressing send. The loop disguises avoidance as effort. Breaking the loop means sending at draft three, tolerating the pulse spike, and discovering the catastrophe lives only in the rehearsal. The reply arrives. Normal. The body learns what the mind resisted.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
Overthinking steals time and returns nothing. Forty minutes perfecting a seven-word text. Two hours replaying a four-second comment. A full night rehearsing a ten-minute presentation already memorized. The currency is sleep, presence, and creative energy. The exchange rate is brutal: hours of anguish for seconds of imagined judgment that rarely materializes. The cost hides because the mind calls it preparation.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
The body knows before the mind labels it. Tight jaw signals the loop has started. Shallow breath confirms it. Locked shoulders sustain it. Calming the body interrupts the thought faster than arguing with the thought interrupts the body. One slow exhale — longer out than in — drops the alarm. The concern remains. The emergency dissolves. From that calmer baseline, the send button stops looking like a detonator.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Every spiral follows the same architecture. A single thought tips into repetition. Repetition generates false urgency. False urgency blocks action. Blocked action confirms the original fear: something is wrong. The loop is self-sealing. The exit point sits at the first revolution — the moment the thought recurs for the second time. Catching it there, labeling it as a loop instead of a problem, breaks the seal before the architecture locks.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
The overthinking mind casts itself as the center of every scene. The friend's short reply, the professor's blunt email, the stranger's glance — all interpreted as reactions to the protagonist's flaw. Expanding the frame introduces other explanations: distraction, exhaustion, a bad day unrelated to any text received. The chest opens when the spotlight dims. The story holds more characters than one.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Action at eighty percent teaches the nervous system more than analysis at one hundred percent ever delivers. The imperfect message sent and met with a normal reply rewrites the internal forecast. The rehearsed catastrophe compared against the lived outcome produces a gap. That gap — between what the mind predicted and what actually arrived — is the raw material of change. Each send deposits evidence. The evidence accumulates. The loop weakens.
