
## RECOGNITION v01

Luz rewrites the email to her professor seven times before sending it. The email is: "Hi Professor Chen, I wanted to confirm the due date for the final paper—is it December 12th or 13th?" She rewrites it because the first version sounds too casual, the second too formal, the third the comma placement is wrong, the fourth sounds presumptuous as though she hasn't read the syllabus. She knows she has read the syllabus. The syllabus is unclear. She sends the seventh version at 11 p.m. The professor responds in twelve minutes: "December 12th. Good question, the syllabus was ambiguous." Luz stares at the reply. Forty minutes of overthinking, seven drafts, for a reply that took twelve minutes and confirmed what she suspected. She closes her laptop. Tomorrow she has a twenty-page paper due on December 12th. She spent forty minutes on an email about the date.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kofi spends two hours deciding which section to add-drop into for statistics recitation. There are three sections with open seats. He has built a spreadsheet comparing times, TA ratings from the course evaluation portal, walking distance from his other classes, and projected conflict with his work schedule. The spreadsheet has seventeen rows. His roommate asks what he's working on and Kofi says "just scheduling stuff." He makes the decision, submits the form, and then spends another thirty minutes confirming he made the right choice. The section he chose is fine. All three sections were fine. The two hours were not fine. He opens his actual homework. His palms are damp. His neck is stiff from hunching over the spreadsheet. The statistics recitation starts next Tuesday. He chose the Tuesday section. The Tuesday section is fine.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/overthinking/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Remy sets a two-minute timer for the decision. He is trying to decide whether to go to office hours or study at the library. It is a small choice. He knows it is a small choice. He also knows that without the timer, he will still be deciding in forty minutes and miss both options. The timer runs. He picks office hours. He goes. The TA explains two things he had been confused about for a week. He walks out with his notes corrected. His chest is still tight—the timer felt like an act of force against his own brain, and the residue of not-having-thought-it-through stays in his shoulders. He sits on the steps outside the TA's building and tries to locate what he would have lost by choosing the library. He can't locate it. The cost of the not-overthinking is only the anxiety of not being sure. The benefit is already in his corrected notes.
