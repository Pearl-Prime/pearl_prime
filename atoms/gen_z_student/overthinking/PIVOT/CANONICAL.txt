## PIVOT v01
---

---
The loop runs on one assumption: the first interpretation is the true one. It never is. The brain grabs the worst-case reading and locks it in before the evidence arrives. That grab is the mechanism. Name it and the grip loosens.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Fourteen drafts of the same text produce zero new information. Each rewrite feeds the anxiety, not the clarity. The first version carried the meaning. Everything after served the fear. Recognizing the difference changes the whole game.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The body registers the spiral before the mind names it. Tight jaw. Shallow breath. Locked shoulders. Those signals arrive as data, not destiny. They mark the moment the loop started, and that moment is also where it can stop.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Overthinking disguises itself as preparation. It promises a perfect version of the sentence, the email, the moment. That version never arrives. The promise itself is the trap. Action at eighty percent beats paralysis at a hundred.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
The mental replay edits the past into a courtroom. Every word becomes evidence. Every pause becomes a verdict. But the original moment held none of that weight. The weight arrives later, manufactured by a brain trained to prosecute itself.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Reading someone's tone through a screen is guesswork dressed as certainty. The period instead of the exclamation point. The "haha" instead of "hahaha." The brain fills gaps with threat because threat kept ancestors alive. It does not keep inboxes safe.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
The cost of the loop hides inside the loop. Hours vanish. Sleep dissolves. Conversations rot in drafts. The mind calls this thoroughness. The body calls it exhaustion. One of them is telling the truth about the price.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
Every spiral has a first revolution. A single thought that tips into repetition. Catching it on the first pass takes practice, not perfection. The goal is not to stop thinking. The goal is to notice the moment thinking stops serving and starts consuming.
---
