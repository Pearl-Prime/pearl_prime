## SCENE v01
---

---
The text message sent forty minutes ago. No reply. You read it again. Your chest tightens. Your eyes scan each word for the mistake, the wrong tone, the sentence that offended. Your thumb scrolls up, down, up. Your neck cramps from hunching over the screen. The dining hall dinner grows cold on the tray. Your fork sits untouched. Your brain runs the conversation through seventeen different interpretations. Your temples pulse.

The professor returns the essay. B-plus. Your hands grip the edges of the paper. Your jaw locks. The B-plus warps inside your skull — what did you miss, which paragraph failed, where did the argument weaken. Your eyes skim the comments but do not read them. Your brain already built its own critique, longer and harsher than anything written in red ink. Your shoulders curl around the paper. Your stomach tightens into a knot.

Ava sits on the campus bus replaying the study group from two hours ago. She said something about the reading. Everyone went quiet. Her face flushes. Her hands grip her backpack straps. She rewrites the sentence six ways in her head. Each version sounds worse. Her teeth clench. The bus passes four stops. She notices none of them. Her body rides the bus. Her mind stays trapped in a room she already left.

You lie in bed at midnight, covers pulled to your chin. The conversation with your advisor replays on loop. Your fists ball against the sheets. You chose the wrong words. Your advisor paused too long before responding. Your chest constricts. You flip to your side. The replay restarts from the beginning, sharper now, louder. Your forehead creases. Your eyes squeeze shut but the scene projects on the inside of your eyelids.

The group chat discussion ended an hour ago. You typed three messages, deleted two, sent one. Now you dissect the one you sent. Your thumb hovers over the screen. Your throat tightens. The message sits there, read by four people, responded to by none. Your stomach clenches. You open Instagram instead. Scroll for six minutes. Return to the chat. Still no reply. Your ribs compress. The loop resets.

The career fair ended at 4 PM. You shook hands, exchanged names, smiled. Now you sit in the library at 7 PM reconstructing each conversation. Your shoulders hunch. Your pen taps the desk in rapid bursts. You said "um" too many times at the third booth. Your handshake at the fifth booth was too limp. Your chest sinks. Your back rounds. Three hours passed. The reconstruction produces nothing usable. Your body spent the energy of a full workday reviewing events that already ended.
