## EXERCISE v01
---

---
THE TWO-MINUTE SEND RULE. Open the drafted message. Set a phone timer for two minutes. Read the message once. Fix only factual errors — a wrong name, a wrong date. When the timer ends, press send. Close the app. Place the phone screen-down. Feel the pulse in your fingertips and let it settle without checking for a reply.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
BODY SCAN INTERRUPT. When the rewriting loop starts, stop typing. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press your palms against the desk surface. Name three physical sensations: the chair against your back, the temperature on your skin, the weight of your jaw. Hold each for five seconds. Return to the message only after the breath drops below your collarbones.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
WORST-CASE COMPLETION. Write the catastrophic outcome in full: "They will read this message, think I am annoying, never speak to me again, and tell everyone." Read it out loud. Notice how the full sentence sounds when it hits the air instead of looping inside the skull. Rate its actual likelihood from one to ten. Write the number next to the sentence.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
THE SINGLE-DRAFT CHALLENGE. Choose one low-stakes message per day — a reply to a meme, a reaction in a group chat. Type it once. Send it without rereading. Track the outcome in a notes app: date, message sent, actual consequence. After seven days, read the log. Count how many catastrophic responses arrived. Let the number speak.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
ATTENTION LABELING. When the replay loop fires — the party comment, the text tone, the email phrasing — say internally: "My mind is replaying." Do not argue with the replay. Do not solve it. Label it and redirect attention to one physical task: pouring water, tying a shoe, pressing a key. The label breaks the trance for three seconds. Three seconds is enough to start.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
THE NINETY-SECOND TIMER. Sit with the anxious thought without acting on it for ninety seconds. Hands in your lap. Phone on the desk. Feel the urge to draft, revise, or scroll for reassurance. Let the urge peak. Adrenaline metabolizes in sixty to ninety seconds when no new fuel enters the system. After the timer, notice the intensity dropped. That drop is the data.
