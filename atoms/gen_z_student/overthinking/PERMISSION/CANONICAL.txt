## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to send the text and put the phone in a drawer. Your message does not require surveillance. The words left your fingers. They travel on their own now. Your thumb deserves rest. Your eyes deserve a different view. Let the conversation breathe without you watching.

You have permission to accept the B-plus without an autopsy. The grade stands. Your body spent more energy analyzing it than earning it. Close the essay. Stack it in a folder. Walk to the window. Your lungs need air more than your brain needs another review.

You have permission to say the imperfect thing in the study group. Your stumbled sentence carried meaning. The pause that followed was processing, not judgment. Your flushed face cooled within minutes. The group moved on. Your body holds permission to move on too.

You have permission to stop replaying the conversation at midnight. Your pillow is not a review desk. Place your hand on your chest. Feel the heartbeat. That rhythm belongs to right now, not two hours ago. The replay loses power when your body returns to the present room.

You have permission to leave the career fair debrief at ten minutes. Extract one lesson. Write one follow-up action. Close the mental file. Your shoulders earned their release. Three hours of reconstruction serves your anxiety, not your career. Ten minutes serves both.

You have permission to eat your dinner while it is still warm. Your fork waits. Your food waits. The unread reply does not change the taste of the meal in front of you. Set the phone face down. Pick up the fork. Chew slowly. Your jaw unclenches when it has a new job to do.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to flip a coin for the small decision. Pull the quarter from your pocket. Feel its weight. The coin will be wrong fifty percent of the time and correct fifty percent. The mind that has been deliberating for forty minutes is doing no better. Spend the energy on something else.

You have permission to name the loop out loud the moment it starts. Speak: "I am running it again." The naming does not stop the loop. It changes who is watching it. The watcher was the part of you you wanted to access. She arrived as soon as you spoke.

You have permission to make a decision with imperfect information. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept the choice. Most decisions you face will never have complete information. The seeking is what burns the day. Decide on what is available.

You have permission to delete a draft and start over. Highlight the text. Press delete. Watch the screen go blank. The previous version was practice. The next version is the one. The loop wants you to refine. Sometimes refinement is just hiding behind editing.

You have permission to set a timer for the deliberation. Open the clock. Set fifteen minutes. When the alarm rings, choose. The timer is not a guess. It is a structural intervention against a brain that does not know how to stop on its own.

You have permission to send the imperfect email. Type. Reread once. Press send. The reread cycle that follows would have caught typos at a cost of sixty-five extra minutes. The recipient does not penalize typos. The recipient values speed.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to put the phone in the kitchen drawer at 10 PM. Close the drawer. Walk back to your bedroom. The phone is the rumination's primary input device. Removing the device from the room reduces the loop's available data.

You have permission to take notes by hand for the class that triggers the most overthinking. Pick up a pen. The slower input rate prevents the brain from generating the fifth-order doubts that the laptop encourages. The notes will be shorter. The body will be calmer.

You have permission to schedule the difficult thinking for a fixed window. Open the calendar. Block 3 PM to 4 PM. The thinking gets the slot. The other twenty-three hours do not. This is not avoidance. It is containerization.

You have permission to interrupt your own loop with a cold-water face-rinse. Walk to the sink. Turn the cold tap. Splash. The vagus nerve responds to the temperature drop. The thinking pauses for thirty seconds. The pause is real, biological, and free.

You have permission to tell a friend "do not let me revisit this conversation tonight." Sit on their couch. Speak the request. The friend can hold a small accountability boundary. The brain that returns to the topic later will hit a wall the friend is helping you build.

You have permission to journal a decision and then close the journal. Press the cover shut. Set it on the shelf. The page held the thinking. The decision was made on the page. Reopening it tomorrow is a relapse, not a continuation.
