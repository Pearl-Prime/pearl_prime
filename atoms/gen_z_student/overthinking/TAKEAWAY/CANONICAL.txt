## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Overthinking disguises itself as problem-solving. Your brain insists the replay serves a purpose. It does not. The seventeenth analysis of a sent text produces zero new information. Your jaw clenches harder with each loop. Recognizing the loop as repetition — not insight — breaks the circuit at the body level.

Your mind replays the past to prevent future pain. The intention is protective. The execution is destructive. Your shoulders hunch around a conversation that ended hours ago. The body pays the bill for the mind's endless review. Redirect the energy. One action in the present tense outweighs a thousand replays.

The silence after your text means nothing. Your brain fills the gap with worst-case narratives. Your chest tightens around fiction. Other people eat dinner, shower, charge dead phones. The absence of a reply is not the presence of rejection. Your throat loosens when you accept the information gap.

Overthinking shrinks your world to the size of one thought. Your eyes unfocus. Your food grows cold. Your body occupies space your mind abandoned. Re-entering the body — feeling the chair, tasting the food, hearing the room — expands the world back to actual size. One sensation breaks the trance.

Perfection drives the replay loop. Your brain searches the conversation for the ideal version you failed to deliver. That ideal version does not exist. No sentence survives seventeen rounds of editing. Release the draft. Your words landed close enough. Your body relaxes when good enough replaces flawless.

The energy spent reconstructing the career fair equals the energy of a full workout. Your body burned cortisol for three hours producing nothing actionable. Overthinking is metabolically expensive and informationally bankrupt. One written note captures more value than three hours of mental replay. Write it down. Close the file.

## TAKEAWAY v02
---

---
Overthinking increases metabolic load without generating useful output. Your brain consumes the same energy processing the same loop the fourth time as it did the first. Place your hands on your knees. Feel the warmth of your palms transferring to the fabric. That warmth is a real-time sensory input the loop cannot generate. Follow the warmth. Leave the loop.

Decision paralysis is overthinking applied to choice. The body freezes while the mind runs more scenarios. Place one finger on the object of the decision — the application, the form, the email. Feel the edge of the paper or the temperature of the glass. Contact with the physical object reduces the mind's abstraction. The decision becomes smaller when you touch it.

Rumination mistakes repetition for investigation. Your mind returns to the same interaction or mistake expecting that one more review will yield new information. It will not. Pinch the skin on the back of your hand lightly. Feel the brief sensation. Each repetition feels like the first. So does each rumination. The pinch teaches the loop nothing. Neither does the replay.

The overthinking brain requires a manual interrupt, not a logical argument. Argument feeds the loop. Physical displacement breaks it. Stand up. Walk to a different room. Feel your feet change surfaces. The nervous system receives new sensory input. New input requires processing. The loop competes with the new input and loses.

Overthinking about relationships — what the text meant, what the silence signaled — activates the attachment nervous system. Your body prepares for social threat. The shoulders rise. The stomach tightens. The threat is imagined but the body's preparation is real. Press your back against a wall. Feel the solid support. The wall behind you is real. The threat in the text is uncertain.

Sleep is overthinking's peak performance window. The distracted mind is not available. The thinking brain accelerates. Write one sentence on paper before lying down: the decision or situation you are holding. Feel the pen on the page. The external record allows the internal processor to partially release the file. Your brain monitors less when the information is stored outside it.

## TAKEAWAY v03
---

---
Overthinking predicts catastrophic social outcomes based on incomplete data. Your brain generates the worst interpretation of ambiguous situations and presents it as the most likely. Notice when the interpretation arrived already formed. Place your thumb on the pulse point on your wrist. Feel the steady beat. The prediction is not evidence. Your pulse is evidence. Start there.

The overthinking loop is not thinking. Thinking generates new information. The loop replays existing information without transformation. Feel the difference in your chest between engaged problem-solving — which carries a slight forward lean, a sense of movement — and rumination, which carries heaviness and repetition. They feel different. Track the sensation, not the content.

Every overthinking session has an entry point. A comment someone made. A grade returned. A silence that lasted too long. Find the entry point. Touch your chest at the moment of entry. The overthinking is the body's attempt to regain control over a moment that felt uncertain. You cannot retroactively control the moment. You can release the attempt.

Perfectionism and overthinking share the same nervous system signature. The body will not release the work, the decision, the plan, because release feels like acceptance of imperfection. Exhale longer than your inhale. Feel the body soften on the extended exhale. That softening is practice for releasing imperfect things into an imperfect world. Start with the breath.

The person you are overthinking about is not thinking about you at this frequency. Statistical likelihood: they processed the interaction and moved on within minutes. Your nervous system allocated disproportionate resources to a moment that occupied their attention briefly. This is not a moral failure. It is an overactive social threat detector. Note the discrepancy. Recalibrate.

When the overthinking involves a real decision, a deadline creates a container. Set a physical timer. Feel the device in your hand before you set it. When the timer ends, commit to whatever the best current option is. The decision made under time constraint is almost always as good as the decision made after three more hours of loop. The loop improves nothing. The timer closes it.
