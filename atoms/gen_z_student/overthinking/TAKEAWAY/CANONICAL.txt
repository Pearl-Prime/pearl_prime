## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Overthinking disguises itself as problem-solving. Your brain insists the replay serves a purpose. It does not. The seventeenth analysis of a sent text produces zero new information. Your jaw clenches harder with each loop. Recognizing the loop as repetition — not insight — breaks the circuit at the body level.

Your mind replays the past to prevent future pain. The intention is protective. The execution is destructive. Your shoulders hunch around a conversation that ended hours ago. The body pays the bill for the mind's endless review. Redirect the energy. One action in the present tense outweighs a thousand replays.

The silence after your text means nothing. Your brain fills the gap with worst-case narratives. Your chest tightens around fiction. Other people eat dinner, shower, charge dead phones. The absence of a reply is not the presence of rejection. Your throat loosens when you accept the information gap.

Overthinking shrinks your world to the size of one thought. Your eyes unfocus. Your food grows cold. Your body occupies space your mind abandoned. Re-entering the body — feeling the chair, tasting the food, hearing the room — expands the world back to actual size. One sensation breaks the trance.

Perfection drives the replay loop. Your brain searches the conversation for the ideal version you failed to deliver. That ideal version does not exist. No sentence survives seventeen rounds of editing. Release the draft. Your words landed close enough. Your body relaxes when good enough replaces flawless.

The energy spent reconstructing the career fair equals the energy of a full workout. Your body burned cortisol for three hours producing nothing actionable. Overthinking is metabolically expensive and informationally bankrupt. One written note captures more value than three hours of mental replay. Write it down. Close the file.
