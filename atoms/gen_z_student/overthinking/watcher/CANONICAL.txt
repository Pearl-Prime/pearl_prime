Selin watches herself overthink. She is in the middle of choosing which section of the library to study in and she can see, from the outside, that this is not a meaningful choice. She observes herself weighing the ambient noise level on the third floor against the lighting on the second floor against the outlet availability near the window seats. She observes herself doing this for six minutes. The watching self finds this faintly absurd. The watching self does not have the ability to stop the deliberating self. She ends up on the third floor where she started. She opens her laptop. The deliberation took seven minutes and she is in the same place she would have been in the first thirty seconds. She notes this. She sets a timer for herself for next time. Her timer is in her phone. Her phone is now in her hand. She has begun thinking about whether the timer will work.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Arjun is in a conversation with a classmate about summer internship plans. He is also, simultaneously, watching himself have this conversation. He notes the cadence of his own responses, measuring whether he's saying too much or too little. He observes the classmate's expressions for signs of disengagement. He wonders if he's being too eager or not enthusiastic enough. He responds to a question the classmate asks and then analyzes his response before the sentence is complete. The classmate says "that sounds really cool" about Arjun's summer plans. Arjun smiles and says thanks and the watching self immediately runs a check: did that sound genuine, was it the right amount of enthusiasm, did the classmate mean it. He walks away from the conversation having successfully participated in it and having no idea whether he enjoyed it. His hands are in his pockets. His neck is tight. He liked the classmate. He thinks.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/overthinking/watcher/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kofi is running at 6 a.m. and for about ninety seconds the watching stops. He isn't monitoring his pace or his form or what he'll say to his thesis advisor tomorrow. He is just moving, just breathing, just cold air and pavement. The watching resumes. He notices it resume—the familiar dual consciousness returning, the back row filling up again. He keeps running. The ninety seconds were real. They are already past but they were real. He gets back to the dorm and stretches on the floor and the watching is already running its commentary on his stretching. He accepts it. He will go back tomorrow at 6 a.m. For the ninety seconds or the thirty seconds or however long it gives him. The cost of the watching is that it never fully stops. The value of the ninety seconds is that it sometimes pauses, briefly, long enough to remember what it feels like to be inside his own life.
