
## RECOGNITION v01

Priya checks her bank balance after the housing deposit hits and the number is smaller than she calculated. She stares at it. She recalculates. The number stays the same. Her chest tightens. She opens a spreadsheet she maintains with her monthly expenses and runs the columns. If she works all three scheduled shifts at the campus café this month, she can cover the shortfall—but that assumes no unexpected costs, no medication copays, no bus fares she didn't plan. The assumption is fragile and she knows it. Her hands feel cold against the laptop. She has three weeks until rent is due. She closes the spreadsheet. She opens it again. The numbers are still the same numbers. She closes it. She opens her financial aid portal to check whether she missed anything. She knows she didn't. She checks anyway, twice, and the checking costs time she was supposed to use for the reading that's due at midnight.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus is in the student café with two friends who are deciding where to eat for the end-of-year dinner. They're discussing a restaurant he has checked and cannot afford. He watches the conversation run while his chest fills with the specific pressure of having to either spend money he doesn't have or explain why he can't. Both options cost something. He says "I'm actually pretty tired, I might sit this one out." His friends look mildly surprised and say okay. He walks back to the dorm. The social cost of the exit sits in him alongside the financial math he ran in his head at the table. He microwaves ramen in the dormitory kitchen and eats alone by the window. His friends send a photo from the restaurant. He gives it a heart. The dinner looks nice. The heart costs nothing. The evening costs him his participation in an ordinary thing that his bank balance made unavailable.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_anxiety/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Selin makes an appointment with the financial aid office. She has been avoiding this for two months because making the appointment means naming the problem out loud to someone in an institutional setting. She sits across from a counselor and lists her expenses. The counselor listens without expression and runs some calculations and explains three options she didn't know existed: an emergency bridge fund, a work-study adjustment, a textbook loan program. None of them fully solves the problem. Together they reduce it. Selin writes the information down on a notepad she brought because she knew she wouldn't retain it if she was anxious. She thanks the counselor and walks out into the campus corridor. Her chest is still tight. The numbers are still difficult. She has three fewer unknowns than when she sat down, which is different from being okay but is also different from where she started. She puts the notepad in her bag. She'll look at it when she gets home.
