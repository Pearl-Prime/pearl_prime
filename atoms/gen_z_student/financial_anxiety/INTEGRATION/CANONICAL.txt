
## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: financial_grounding
reframe_type: spiral_to_sequence
---
This week, you build a floor instead of a forecast. The floor is the minimum viable number: what do you need to cover the non-negotiables this month? Rent, food, transportation to work. Write only these. Everything above the floor is margin. Everything below it is the actual emergency, which may not be where you are.

When the anxiety activates — bank notification, group dinner invitation, unexpected bill — name the event without the cascade. "The disbursement is late" is a sentence. "The disbursement is late and therefore I will lose my apartment and fail out of school" is a spiral. Practice stopping at the sentence.

At the end of the week: identify one thing you did that addressed the actual situation rather than the feeling it generated. You called the financial aid office. You picked up the extra shift. You cancelled the subscription. Note it. The action is evidence that you are capable of working on the problem, not just feeling it. Collect this evidence. It becomes the counter-argument when the spiral accelerates.
