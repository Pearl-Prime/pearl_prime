Remy receives an email about a new course fee for his major's senior practicum. Seventy-five dollars. The spiral starts before he reaches the end of the sentence. If he pays the fee, he's short for groceries. If he's short for groceries, he'll have to ask his mother. If he asks his mother, she'll worry. If she worries, his father will find out and call. If his father calls, there will be a conversation about whether school is worth it. If there's that conversation, Remy will spend three days recovering from it. He has moved from seventy-five dollars to the viability of his education in forty seconds. His pulse is elevated. His hands are on the keyboard but not typing. The email is still open. The fee is due in two weeks. He doesn't know if the catastrophe chain is accurate. He also cannot stop running it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Dani's car needs a repair. The mechanic says two hundred dollars and her chest seizes. Two hundred dollars is three of her campus job shifts. She is standing in the mechanic's parking lot and the spiral runs: if she pays for the repair, she can't cover the credit card minimum. If she misses the minimum, there's a late fee. If there's a late fee, her credit score dips. If her credit score dips, the apartment she needs to lease next fall might not approve her. The mechanic is waiting for her decision. Her mouth is dry. She approves the repair because the alternative—no car, no way to get to her off-campus work—is worse than the cascade. She drives back to campus and sits in the parking structure for ten minutes with her forehead against the steering wheel. Her hands grip the wheel and the spiral is still running. The car is getting repaired. None of the rest of it has happened. Her body doesn't know the difference.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_anxiety/spiral/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kofi writes out the spiral on paper instead of letting it run in his head. He starts with the actual fact: his student loan disbursement was two weeks late. He writes what he knows: he has enough in his account for the next fourteen days. He writes the first what-if and then stops and marks it as unknown. He writes the second what-if and marks it as unknown. He keeps going until he has five actual knowns and nine unknowns. Then he looks at the unknowns and marks the ones he can research today. He can research two of them. He emails the financial aid office and his landlord. His chest is still tight. The spiral has not stopped running. But it has been interrupted by the exercise, slowed by the act of externalizing it onto paper where it takes up less space than it does in his body. His hands are steadier by the time he folds the paper. He still doesn't know what the next two weeks look like. He knows what the next two hours look like, which is more than he had.
