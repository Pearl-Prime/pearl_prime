
## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Open a blank document and write the actual number: what you have, what you owe this month, when each payment is due. Put the numbers outside your body where they can be looked at rather than felt. The act of writing them does not make them larger. It makes them finite. Finite problems have edges. Problems with edges can be approached.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Identify one financial unknown that is generating the most anxiety right now. Write it as a yes/no question: "Will the disbursement arrive before rent is due?" Then write the two possible answers and what you would do in each case. The branching turns a spiral into a decision tree. Decision trees have endings.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Find the one thing in your current financial situation that you have some control over. One shift you could pick up. One subscription you could cancel. One conversation you could have with the financial aid office. Take that one action today. Not to solve everything. To move from passive worry to active contact with the problem.
