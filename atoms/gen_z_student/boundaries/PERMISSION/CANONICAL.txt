## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to let the phone ring. Place it face-down on the desk. Feel the vibration against the wood. The call will go to voicemail. Voicemail holds the message until you choose to retrieve it. Your attention is not on-demand infrastructure.

You have permission to eat your meal while your friend talks. Pick up your fork. Feel its weight in your hand. Listening and eating happen simultaneously. Nourishing yourself during someone else's story is not selfish. It is basic.

You have permission to close your laptop at eleven p.m. even if the group project partner is still typing. Press the screen down. Feel the hinge resist and then give. Your sleep matters more than their procrastination. The deadline belongs to every member equally.

You have permission to lock your bedroom door in the shared apartment. Turn the latch. Feel the click. That sound is not hostility. It is a body declaring the minimum space it needs to restore itself. Every living thing needs a boundary between itself and everything else.

You have permission to change your mind after saying yes. Place your hand on your sternum. Feel your breath move beneath it. Consent is ongoing. A yes from Tuesday does not obligate Saturday. Your present self outranks your past agreement.

You have permission to disappoint someone who expects too much. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept your weight without conditions. Disappointing someone by being honest costs less than destroying yourself by being convenient.
