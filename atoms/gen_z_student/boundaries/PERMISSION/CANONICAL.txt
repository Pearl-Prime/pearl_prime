## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to let the phone ring. Place it face-down on the desk. Feel the vibration against the wood. The call will go to voicemail. Voicemail holds the message until you choose to retrieve it. Your attention is not on-demand infrastructure.

You have permission to eat your meal while your friend talks. Pick up your fork. Feel its weight in your hand. Listening and eating happen simultaneously. Nourishing yourself during someone else's story is not selfish. It is basic.

You have permission to close your laptop at eleven p.m. even if the group project partner is still typing. Press the screen down. Feel the hinge resist and then give. Your sleep matters more than their procrastination. The deadline belongs to every member equally.

You have permission to lock your bedroom door in the shared apartment. Turn the latch. Feel the click. That sound is not hostility. It is a body declaring the minimum space it needs to restore itself. Every living thing needs a boundary between itself and everything else.

You have permission to change your mind after saying yes. Place your hand on your sternum. Feel your breath move beneath it. Consent is ongoing. A yes from Tuesday does not obligate Saturday. Your present self outranks your past agreement.

You have permission to disappoint someone who expects too much. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept your weight without conditions. Disappointing someone by being honest costs less than destroying yourself by being convenient.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to take the headphones out and put them back in mid-conversation. Touch the bud. Feel the click. Your ears are not on-call infrastructure. Music is recovery. The friend can wait twelve seconds.

You have permission to eat the leftovers your roommate said were communal. Open the fridge. Lift the container. Feel its weight. Communal food has a half-life. Day three is yours by attrition. Eat without negotiation.

You have permission to refuse to drive everyone everywhere. Set your keys on the counter. Hand them to nobody. Your car is your bedroom on wheels. The default driver was an assumption you can revoke.

You have permission to leave the group project meeting at the agreed end time. Stand up. Pack your laptop. Walk out. The meeting can run long without you. Your scheduled hour was the contract. The contract closes when the clock says.

You have permission to log out of the shared Google Doc when you are done. Click the tab. Close it. Feel the room get quieter when the cursor stops blinking. Watching others edit is not collaboration. It is surveillance you imposed on yourself.

You have permission to leave the family group chat on mute. Open settings. Tap mute. Feel the phone go silent without dropping the connection. Mute is not exit. It is rate-limiting. The messages survive without your real-time attention.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to say "that does not work for me" without offering an alternative. Press your tongue to the roof of your mouth. Release it. The no is complete. The next step belongs to the person whose plan you declined.

You have permission to end a friendship that costs more than it gives. Walk to the window. Look out. Friendship is not a contract that auto-renews. The body knows which connections drain it. Listen to the body.

You have permission to tell your parent you cannot talk every day. Press the call button. Speak the sentence. Your relationship survives a reduced cadence. Your nervous system stops bracing for the daily check-in. Both improvements happen at once.

You have permission to say "I am not your therapist" to a friend who keeps unloading. Place your hand on your chest. Breathe. Care is not the same as capacity. Naming the limit protects the friendship from the resentment building underneath.

You have permission to ask your roommate to keep their music below a specific decibel. Knock on their door. Speak the request. Comfort costs decibels. The conversation is short. The peace lasts until they move out.

You have permission to cancel a commitment because you are tired, not because you are sick. Type the message. Press send. Tiredness is a real reason. The lie about being sick is the part that erodes you. Tell the truth in plain words.
