## HOOK v01
---

---
Her roommate's friends pile into the dorm at 11 p.m. on a Tuesday. Four bodies on the futon, someone's speaker blasting from the dresser. Her jaw clenches around the word she never says. She pulls her textbook closer, shrinks into the wall, and studies through the noise with her molars grinding.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The group chat lights up: "Can someone share the Econ notes from Thursday." His shoulders climb to his ears before he finishes reading. He took those notes. He always takes those notes. His chest constricts around the knowledge that saying no will cost more than the two hours of rewriting his shorthand into something shareable.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
She lends her meal swipes to a floormate who forgot his card. Third time this month. Her stomach folds as she watches her balance drop. She smiles and says it is no big deal. Her throat tightens around the lie. She eats ramen in her room at midnight because the swipe count no longer covers dinner.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
His project partner assigns him three extra sections the night before the deadline. His neck cords tight. He opens his mouth to push back and the words dissolve on his tongue. He stays up until 4 a.m., shoulders welded to his ears, finishing work that belongs to someone else while his own essay rots in a neglected tab.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Her best friend asks to borrow forty dollars for a concert ticket. Her ribs compress against her lungs. She has forty dollars. She also has rent due Friday. Her hand reaches for Venmo before her brain finishes calculating the cost. Her fingers tremble on the send button. She taps it anyway.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The floor group plans a Saturday night out. He needs to study. He opens his mouth to decline and his chest locks — a steel door slamming shut on the word no. He pictures their faces if he stays behind. His jaw tightens. He closes the textbook, pulls on a jacket, and walks out carrying a smile that weighs more than the door he left open.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Her roommate's boyfriend sleeps over for the fifth consecutive night. The room shrinks. She presses against the wall in her twin bed, shoulders rigid, breathing shallow through her nose so she makes no sound. Her own space dissolved three weeks ago and she swallowed the protest before it reached her teeth.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Someone tags him in a group project presentation slot he never agreed to. His stomach drops through the library chair. He reads the message twice, jaw locked, pulse hammering against his collarbone. He types "sounds good" with hands that shake. Delete would cost a friendship. Enter costs only him.
