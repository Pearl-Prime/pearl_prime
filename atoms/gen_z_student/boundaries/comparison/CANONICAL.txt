
## RECOGNITION v01

Kofi watches his roommate decline a group project without apology. "I've got too much this week," the roommate says, and the person on the other end of the call says "no worries" and that's the end of it. Kofi sits with this. He has never once said no to a group project without spending three days convincing himself the request was unreasonable before he could answer. The comparison is immediate: the roommate lives in a different relationship to his own limits. He makes them visible and they don't collapse him. Kofi writes his limits in his head in invisible ink and then apologizes for having them anyway. His chest tightens. He turns back to the assignment he said yes to last week—the one that takes four hours he doesn't have. He types his name at the top of the document and cannot start.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/boundaries/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Dani volunteers to cover a classmate's presentation slot because the classmate texted at midnight and Dani was awake and said yes before she fully processed the ask. She now has three days to prepare a presentation she didn't plan for, on top of the biochemistry midterm she had planned for. Her jaw is tight the next morning. She runs the comparison in the dining hall: her friend at another table said no to the same request from a different classmate, said "I can't swing it this week," and was eating breakfast normally instead of stress-planning a presentation. The difference between them is not character or work ethic. The difference is that her friend can hold her own capacity as a fact, and Dani holds hers as a negotiating position that always loses. Her oatmeal gets cold.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/boundaries/comparison/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Priya says no to a study group request for the first time this semester. The word comes out and she watches the other person's face for signs of damage. There are none. They say "okay, no problem" and leave. Priya sits with what's left. Her chest is tight in a way that surprises her. She expected relief and got something more complicated—guilt, and underneath the guilt, the faint outline of something that might be sovereignty. She compares the feeling to watching others say no: they always looked like they felt less, not more. She feels both. She opens her notes and studies for an hour with her phone face-down. The hour is uninterrupted. Her focus holds better than it has in weeks. She doesn't know what to do with that evidence yet. She only knows that the tightness in her chest is different from the tightness she carries when she says yes to things she can't afford.
