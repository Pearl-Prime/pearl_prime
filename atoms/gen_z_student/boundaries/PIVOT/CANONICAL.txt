## PIVOT v01
---

---
That clench in your jaw every time someone asks for something is not rudeness trying to surface. It is a boundary your body drew before your mouth found the courage.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Saying no does not subtract you from the group. It tells the group where you actually stand. People who leave when you stop performing availability never occupied your corner in the first place.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Your chest constricts because your body already knows the answer. The work is letting your voice catch up to what your ribcage has been screaming for semesters.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Generosity without a bottom is not generosity. It is self-erasure with a friendly face. Your shoulders carry the weight of every favor that should have been a negotiation.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
The open door felt like community. Now it feels like occupation. Naming that shift does not make you ungrateful. It makes you accurate about what your nervous system can afford.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Every time you swallow a no, your throat stores the cost. That tightness has a ledger. Boundaries do not close doors — they install locks that you control from the inside.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
A boundary is not a wall built against people. It is a fence built around the space your body needs to breathe. Fences have gates. You hold the key.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
Your body flinches before the request finishes because it already knows the pattern: ask, comply, collapse. Interrupting that pattern starts with one honest sentence spoken out loud.
---
