## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Place both hands flat on your desk. Press down until your arms straighten. Feel the surface push back against your palms. Hold for ten seconds. That resistance is a physical rehearsal for the boundary your voice has not practiced yet. Your body learns the shape of pushback through contact before it learns the words. Repeat before the next conversation where someone asks you for something.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Unclench your jaw right now. Open your mouth one inch and let your tongue drop to the floor of your mouth. Hold it there for fifteen seconds. Breathe through the gap. Your jaw stores every swallowed no from this semester. Releasing it once does not erase the backlog, but it teaches the muscle that unclenching is an available position. Practice before you check the group chat.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Stand with your back against your dorm room door. Press your shoulder blades into the surface. Feel the door solid behind you. This is your room. This is your threshold. Hold the position for twenty seconds and breathe. Let your body memorize what it feels like to occupy your own space without apology. Step away from the door carrying that posture into the hallway.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Write one sentence on paper — not your phone — that starts with "I need." Not "I think I might need." Not "Sorry but." Strip every cushion word. Read it aloud to your empty room. Hear your own voice state a requirement without apology. Your throat will resist. Let it resist and speak anyway. The sentence does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist outside your body.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Squeeze your fists tight for seven seconds. Hold. Now open them slowly, palms up on your thighs. Notice the difference between gripping and releasing. Gripping is what your body does when it braces to absorb someone else's demand. Releasing is what your body does when it decides the demand can wait. Practice the release five times before your next social obligation.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Set a timer for two minutes. Sit in silence with the door closed. No phone. No music. No input. Let your shoulders descend. Let your chest expand without earning the expansion through productivity. This is practice for the radical act of taking space without justifying it. When the timer ends, notice that the world continued. Nothing collapsed because you disappeared for one hundred and twenty seconds.
