## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
A boundary is not a wall. It is a door with a handle on your side. Grip any handle near you right now. Feel the metal or plastic. You choose when it opens. You choose when it closes. The door serves both functions equally.

Saying no does not subtract you from the relationship. Press your tongue against the roof of your mouth. Feel the pressure. "No" uses the same mouth as "I love you." Both sentences live in the same throat. Both deserve air.

Resentment builds where boundaries dissolve. Place your hand on your stomach. Feel the tension stored there. That knot formed from every yes your body meant as no. Each honest no loosens one loop of the knot.

Other people's reactions to your limits belong to them. Plant your feet on the floor. Feel the ground hold steady. The floor does not apologize for being solid. Solidity is its purpose. Yours too.

Exhaustion is the body's final boundary. Touch the skin under your eyes. Feel the delicate tissue. When you override every earlier signal, fatigue speaks last. Honor the whisper before it becomes a collapse.

A favor given freely and a favor given from guilt feel different in the body. Place one hand on your chest. Notice whether the sensation is open or tight. Openness signals choice. Tightness signals obligation. Your chest knows which one drives the yes.
