## SCENE v01
---

---
Your roommate borrows your charger again without asking. You watch them plug it in while you sit on your bed, phone at twelve percent. Your stomach tightens. Your teeth press together. Your hands ball into fists against the comforter. Feel the fabric bunch under your fingers. The word "hey" sits in your throat like a stone. The stone has weight. Speak it or feel it sink deeper.

The group project partner texts at midnight asking you to finish their section. Your phone glows on the nightstand. Your chest floods with heat. Your fingers hover over the keyboard. Press your palms flat on the mattress. Feel the sheets under your hands. You did your part. The screen can wait until morning. Morning arrives with the same answer: no.

Your friend vents for forty minutes in the dining hall. Your food sits untouched, getting cold. Your shoulders ache from leaning in. Your jaw has locked from nodding. Place your fork in your hand. Feel its weight. Picking up the fork is not abandonment. It is the body saying: I also need to eat.

The professor assigns another revision on a paper you already rewrote twice. Your back stiffens against the lecture hall seat. Your eyes burn. Your fingernails dig into your notebook cover. Feel the cardboard bend under the pressure. Effort has a limit. That limit lives in your body before your mind admits it.

Your parents call during study hours for the third time this week. The phone buzzes against the library desk. Your ribs contract. Your hand reaches for it automatically. Pause. Feel your fingers curl around the phone's edges. Love does not require immediate availability. Press decline. Your thumb makes the boundary your voice cannot.

A classmate copies your answers during the exam. You see them glance at your paper. Your neck heats up. Your arm tenses. Shift your body. Angle your paper away. Feel your elbow create distance. Your muscles drew a line before your mind finished calculating the social cost.
