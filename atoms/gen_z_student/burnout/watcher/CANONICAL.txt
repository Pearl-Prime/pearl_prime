
## RECOGNITION v01

Marcus watches himself go through the motions of being a student. He observes himself attend lectures, take notes, eat in the dining hall, return to the dorm. The activities are present but the person inside them has receded. He exists at a slight remove from everything he does, watching from somewhere behind his own eyes. He does not feel distressed about this. He doesn't feel much of anything, which is its own kind of alarm. He registers the alarm without feeling it. He sits in Introduction to Political Theory and watches himself turn a page. The professor makes a point that would have interested him a year ago. Marcus watches himself not be interested. He files this under "later" and moves on to the next observation. The list of things filed under "later" is getting long.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Remy watches himself having dinner with his friend group. He says the right things at the right moments. He laughs when laughter is appropriate. He asks a question that shows he has been paying attention. From the outside he is fully present. From inside—where he lives—he is across the room watching himself perform presence. His friends are talking about spring break plans and he hears the words and responds but doesn't feel connected to any of it. His chest is flat. His hands are warm around his mug. He observes himself nodding. When he gets home he lies on his bed and the performance ends and nothing else starts. Just the flat expanse of post-semester burnout, which he is watching with the same detached attention. He is not okay. He watches himself not being okay. The gap between the watching and the feeling is getting wider.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/burnout/watcher/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Yuki is in the campus wellness center waiting for her first counseling appointment. She watches herself sit in the plastic chair, hold the intake form, write her name in the top left box. She has been a watcher of her own experience for so long that even this act—seeking help—arrives with commentary. She observes herself being brave or being smart or finally doing the thing. She observes herself observing. The counselor calls her name and Yuki stands and walks in and sits across from a person who is looking at her with simple attention, no comment. The watcher goes quiet for a moment. Just a moment. Yuki's throat tightens. She hadn't expected the quiet to feel like grief—the gap where the commentary stopped, the unfamiliar stillness of someone just looking at her without interpretation. She presses her palms to her thighs to keep her hands still. The quiet lasts three seconds before the watcher resumes its post. But she felt it. The three seconds cost her the whole rest of the armor she had built for the appointment.
