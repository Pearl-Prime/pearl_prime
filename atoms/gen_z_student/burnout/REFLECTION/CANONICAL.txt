## REFLECTION v01
---
family: identity_performance
voice_mode: direct_second_person
mechanism_emphasis: identity_erosion
---
You built a self out of output. GPA, dean's list, scholarships — each achievement added a brick and you called the tower "you." The structure felt permanent. It felt earned.
Then the output stopped. The cursor blinked on a blank page. Your fingers locked. Your chest hollowed. And the tower swayed.
Burnout did not steal your motivation. It revealed that the motivation ran on a fuel source — external validation — that depletes under sustained load. The straight-A student who cannot write a single paragraph sits inside a body that spent months converting anxiety into performance. The conversion rate dropped. The anxiety remains.
Your worth never lived in the GPA. It lived in your capacity to learn, adapt, and rest. Rest got cut first. Then adaptation. Then learning collapsed under its own weight. The order matters. Rebuild in reverse: rest first, then adapt, then learn. The grades follow. They do not lead.

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: depletion_mechanics
voice_mode: observational_third
mechanism_emphasis: nervous_system_overload
---
The nervous system runs on a budget. Every deadline, every all-nighter, every caffeine-powered sprint withdraws from the same account. The balance drops. The body sends warnings — tight jaw, shallow breath, cold hands. The student ignores the warnings because the semester does not accept overdraft notices.
Depletion operates on a delay. The crash arrives weeks after the spending. Week twelve feels like the problem. Week four held the cause — the night sleep dropped below five hours and stayed there. The cascade ran silently through the body until the visible systems failed: focus gone, memory fragmented, emotional range flattened to a narrow band between numb and irritable.
Understanding the delay matters. The student who blames week twelve misses the structural cause. The system overspent in September. It collapsed in November. Recovery addresses the spending pattern, not the collapse.

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: social_masking
voice_mode: direct_second_person
mechanism_emphasis: mask_cost
---
You say "just tired" and the conversation moves on. The mask works. It costs you everything.
Every performed smile pulls energy from a reserve already running dry. Every "I'm fine" adds a layer between your actual state and the people who might help. The gap widens by the week. On one side: the competent student the world sees. On the other: the person who cried in the shower and cannot name why.
The mask exists for good reason. Vulnerability in academic spaces carries risk. Admitting struggle feels like admitting failure. The calculation makes sense on paper. In practice, the mask consumes more energy than the honesty would cost.
One real sentence to one trusted person — "I am not okay and I do not know why" — does not fix burnout. It breaks the isolation that accelerates it. The mask protects from judgment. It also blocks every exit route.

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: perfectionism_mechanics
voice_mode: observational_third
mechanism_emphasis: standard_gap
---
The perfectionist sets a standard. The exhausted brain fails the standard. The perfectionist raises the standard to compensate. The brain fails harder. The loop tightens like a ratchet — each cycle clicks one notch further from functional.
Fourteen drafts of one paper. Eleven rewrites of one introduction. The student measures each version against an ideal that exists only as an internal metric, untethered from any external requirement. The professor asked for three pages. The student demands three flawless pages and the word "flawless" carries a definition that shifts upward with every attempt.
Perfectionism under burnout becomes self-punishment disguised as ambition. The skipped meals. The guilt over rest. The conviction that struggle proves insufficient effort rather than insufficient resources. Rewriting the standard — from flawless to complete, from exceptional to adequate — feels like surrender. It functions as survival.

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: emotional_numbing
voice_mode: direct_second_person
mechanism_emphasis: circuit_breaker_response
---
You got the news and felt nothing. The scholarship advanced. Your body stayed flat. Your face manufactured a smile and your cheeks ached from the effort. That absence of feeling terrifies more than any anxiety did.
The numbness is not apathy. It is protection. Your nervous system hit a threshold — too many stressors, too little recovery — and tripped a breaker. Emotions require processing resources. The system ran out of resources and shut down the expensive operations first. Joy, excitement, connection — gone. Basic function — eating, walking, attending — preserved on minimal power.
The instinct says push harder, feel harder, force the caring back. The instinct is wrong. The breaker tripped for a reason. Forcing it back triggers a deeper shutdown. The path out runs through reducing the load until the system restores capacity on its own. You do not restart a blown fuse by running more current through it.

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: structural_critique
voice_mode: observational_third
mechanism_emphasis: systemic_demand
---
The semester asks for a twelve-week sprint and calls it normal. Fifteen credit hours. Part-time work. Extracurriculars that pad the resume. Sleep compresses. Meals shrink. Social life narrows to study groups and group chats. The student who collapses at week twelve did not fail the system. The system demanded what no body can sustain.
The structural lens matters because the individual lens produces shame. "I should handle this" ignores that "this" exceeds human operating parameters. The student working closing shifts and morning lectures runs a schedule that would trigger labor protections in any regulated industry. The university calls it personal responsibility.
Recognizing the structural cause does not remove the personal experience. The locked hands still lock. The hollow chest still hollows. But naming the system shifts the weight from "I am broken" to "the demand exceeds the supply." That shift — from character flaw to resource mismatch — opens a different set of solutions. Not "try harder." Instead: redistribute the load.
