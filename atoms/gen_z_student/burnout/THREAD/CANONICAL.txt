---
Your body chose conservation. The next chapter teaches you how to refuel without waiting for the semester to end. You build micro-recovery into the cracks of the schedule. Five minutes here. Ten minutes there. The tank fills in small pours. The engine restarts.

The boundary between you and your output dissolved. Ahead, you rebuild it. The next chapter separates identity from productivity using a body-based practice that takes three minutes daily. Your chest learns to carry selfhood independent of your grade. The fusion breaks.

The library marathon proved that hours do not equal output. The coming chapter introduces energy-based scheduling. You learn to match task difficulty to body-state. Hard work fills your peak hours. Recovery fills your valleys. The schedule follows your biology, not your calendar.

The flat affect and empty chest tell the truth about your reserves. Next, you learn which activities deposit energy and which withdraw it. The chapter ahead maps your personal energy economy. You stop spending blindly and start budgeting by sensation.

The unread messages represent connections your depleted body could not maintain. The next chapter shows you how to keep one thread alive during burnout. Not eight threads. One. One person. One check-in. The minimum viable connection sustains you until the reserves rebuild.

You ate the bread roll. The stomach remembered. Ahead, you rebuild the fuel chain link by link. The next chapter covers the three inputs your burned-out body needs most: food on a schedule, sunlight before noon, and ten minutes of movement that does not serve a grade. Simple. Non-negotiable. Restorative.

## THREAD v02
---

---
The vocabulary shifts now. Burnout stops being a description and becomes a signal your body sends on purpose. The next chapter teaches you to read the signal earlier — at the first dulling of interest, the first forced laugh, the first morning you do not want to open your laptop. Early signals are cheaper to respond to.

You spent reserves that took months to accumulate. The recovery timeline surprises most students: it is longer than expected, and it is non-linear. Ahead, you learn to track your recovery with body data instead of academic performance. Your energy levels report the truth before your grades do.

The academic identity fused with the exhausted identity. "I am a burnout case" replaced "I was a high-functioning student who ran dry." The next chapter rebuilds a pre-burnout identity that does not require high functioning to remain intact. Your name does not change with your output. That work starts next.

Your campus has more burnout support than most students find because they stop looking when they are burned out. The coming chapter maps what exists — reduced-load options, incomplete grades, counseling services, peer programs — and gives you language that makes the door easier to open when you are depleted.

The return to work after burnout requires a different system than the one that produced the burnout. Your old system ran on adrenaline, guilt, and comparison. The next chapter introduces a system that runs on recovery capacity, stated limits, and deliberate pacing. New architecture requires rebuilding from the foundation.

You will not be burnout-susceptible forever. Recovery builds new stress-response patterns that are more durable than the ones that broke. The next chapter introduces you to three practices that graduate students use to maintain output without accumulating debt. The habits are sustainable. Your body was not designed to burn.

## THREAD v03
---

---
Structural causes produced your burnout alongside personal ones. The workload was real. The expectations were real. The culture that normalized seventy-hour weeks was real. Touch your shoulders. Feel the institutional weight still stored there. The next chapter names the structural contributors so you stop carrying the full cost as personal failure.

The burned-out student often has the highest compassion for others and the least for themselves. You checked on your friends during your own depletion. You submitted work while your system was in crisis. Roll your neck slowly. Feel the tightness. The next chapter turns some of that care inward. It is not a betrayal of your nature. It is its extension.

Community changes burnout recovery at the cellular level. Social connection produces the same neurochemicals as rest for some bodies. But burned-out bodies often cannot sustain the social load required for community. The coming chapter finds the minimum viable connection — one person, one format, one frequency — that your depleted nervous system can maintain.

Your creativity returns before your productivity does. The flat, mechanical output of burnout recovery gives way to genuine interest weeks before full capacity returns. The next chapter teaches you to protect that early creative re-emergence from the academic machine that will immediately try to harvest it. The spark is fragile. Protect it.

Physical illness follows burnout as the body's immune system finally processes what the performance schedule suppressed. Your body spent months holding the infection at bay so the grades could happen. Now it settles the biological debt. The coming chapter explains this physiological sequence so you do not interpret illness as a new problem. It is the last chapter of the old one.

You are the expert on your own burnout pattern. No one else has lived in your body for this semester. The next chapter teaches you to write your own burnout protocol — the early signals, the specific depleting inputs, the specific restoring ones. Your protocol is not transferable. It is yours. Document it while it is fresh.
