## SCENE v01
---

---
The alarm fires at 7 AM for the third consecutive week. Your arm swings to silence it. Your body stays flat. Your eyelids open but nothing ignites behind them. The ceiling looks the same. The day looks the same. Your legs swing over the bed because gravity pulls them, not because you chose to move. Your chest holds a dull weight, not sharp like anxiety — thick, heavy, gray. You stand. You do not arrive.

The laptop opens to fourteen tabs. Your eyes scan none of them. Your hands rest on the keyboard without typing. The cursor blinks. Your jaw hangs slightly open. Your shoulders slump forward, rounding your upper back into a permanent curve. The coffee sits cold beside the trackpad. Three hours passed. The word count reads the same number it showed at noon. Your body occupies the desk chair. Your motivation left the building.

Priya walks into the dining hall and stares at the food line. Her tray stays empty. Her arms hang. Her appetite disappeared two weeks ago. She picks a bread roll and a glass of water. Sits alone. Her phone shows eight unread messages. Her thumb does not move. Her face holds a flat expression — not sad, not angry, just absent. Her back presses against the chair like it needs the support to stay upright.

The group project meeting starts at 3 PM. You join on mute, camera off. Your screen shows their faces. Your eyes drift to the wall behind the laptop. Someone asks your opinion. Your mouth opens. The words come flat, stripped of inflection. Your hands do not gesture. Your body sits perfectly still in the desk chair. The meeting ends. You close the laptop. You do not remember what the project was about.

Friday night. Your roommates leave for a party. The door closes. Silence. You lie on the couch in the shared apartment. The TV remote sits within reach. Your arm does not extend. Your legs stretch long. Your breathing runs shallow, slow, not from calm but from depletion. The ceiling fan rotates. You watch one blade, then lose track. Your body feels too heavy for the cushion. Sleep does not come. Wakefulness does not sharpen. You float in the middle, empty.

The library closes in twenty minutes. You sit at the same table you occupied eight hours ago. Your highlighter dried out an hour ago. Your notes trail off mid-sentence. Your neck aches from the permanent forward tilt. You close the textbook. Your hands move through the motions — pack bag, zip pocket, stand, walk. Each action runs on residual programming, not intention. The night air hits your face outside. You feel nothing. Your skin registers temperature. Your brain registers nothing.
