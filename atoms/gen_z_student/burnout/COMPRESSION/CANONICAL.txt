## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
Burnout locks the body before it reaches the mind. Hands freeze above keyboards. Eyes slide off screens. The chest hollows. The student who built an identity on performance discovers that identity collapses when the output stops. The blank page does not reflect laziness. It reflects a nervous system that spent its budget in September and hit zero in November. Recovery starts with the body — unclench the jaw, drop the shoulders, feel the desk under the palms. Rest is not a reward for finishing. Rest is the foundation the finishing stands on.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
Week twelve breaks the student who sprinted since week one. The caffeine still hits but the focus never follows. Fourteen drafts of one paper. Eleven rewrites of one sentence. The perfectionist ratchet tightens with every failed attempt. The standard rises as the capacity drops. The gap between expectation and output becomes the source of the pain. Lowering the standard from flawless to complete feels like surrender. It functions as the only viable exit. Existing work beats imaginary perfection.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
The mask says "just tired." The body tells a different story — clenched jaw, cold hands, hollow sternum. Every performed smile drains a reserve already running dry. The gap between the presented self and the actual self widens by the week. One honest sentence to one trusted person does not fix burnout. It breaks the isolation that accelerates it. The mask blocks judgment. It also blocks every exit route. Vulnerability costs less than the performance of invulnerability.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
The numbness arrives last and stays longest. Good news lands flat. Messages go unanswered. The face manufactures smiles that ache in the cheek muscles. This is not apathy — it is a circuit breaker tripped by a system that ran out of processing power. Emotions cost resources the body no longer holds. Forcing the feeling back triggers deeper shutdown. The path runs through reducing load until the system restores itself. No one restarts a blown fuse by increasing the current.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
No one sprints for twelve weeks and wonders why the legs buckle. The academic semester demands exactly that sprint. Fifteen credit hours, closing shifts, thesis drafts in the gaps between. Sleep compresses to fragments. The student who collapses did not fail the system — the system demanded what no body sustains. The structural lens shifts the weight from "I am broken" to "the demand exceeds the supply." That shift opens different solutions. Not "try harder." Instead: redistribute the load.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Performance and worth fused early — first dean's list, first scholarship letter framed on the wall. The weld held for years. Now every blank page attacks the foundation. The student who cannot write a paragraph sits inside a body that spent months converting anxiety into output. The conversion rate dropped. The anxiety remains. Separating output from identity begins with one recognition: the capacity to rest, adapt, and learn held the worth all along. The grades followed. They never led.
