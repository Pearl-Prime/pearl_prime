## STORY v01
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: identity_erosion
IDENTITY_STAGE: performer_collapse
COST_TYPE: academic_and_relational
COST_INTENSITY: high
---
Jordan sits in the library basement at 2 a.m., third Red Bull going warm beside her laptop. Her fingers hover above the keyboard and refuse to drop. She came to this university on a full scholarship. Straight A's since freshman year. The identity of "the one who handles it" glued itself to her bones before she turned nineteen.
Now the glue dissolves. Her eyes drag across the screen, catching nothing. The essay prompt stares back — seven hundred words on urban policy. She wrote three thousand words on this topic last semester without blinking. Tonight the cursor blinks and her mind empties.
Her phone buzzes. Study group asking where she is. Her throat closes. She cannot tell them she sat here for four hours and produced a title and a period. The hollow space behind her sternum widens. She presses her palms flat against the desk and feels the cold laminate but nothing else.
The scholarship review sits two weeks out. Her GPA dropped a full point this semester. She skips the gym now. Skips meals without noticing. Her roommate asked last week if she felt okay and Jordan said "just tired" while her jaw clenched hard enough to ache.
She closes the laptop. Opens it. Closes it again. Her hands tremble. The performer who never missed a deadline stares at a blank page and meets a stranger. The body that carried her through twelve weeks of sprinting locks its joints and stops.

## STORY v02
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: depletion_spiral
IDENTITY_STAGE: dissociation_onset
COST_TYPE: cognitive_and_physical
COST_INTENSITY: severe
---
Marcus wakes at eleven and cannot remember the day. Tuesday or Wednesday — both feel identical. He rolls off the dorm mattress and his knees buckle. The room spins. He ate last at — he cannot recall. A granola bar wrapper sits on the desk. That counts.
He opens his laptop. Seventeen tabs from last night still glow. Organic chemistry notes. A half-written lab report. Three YouTube videos paused on frames he does not recognize watching. His eyes slide across the tabs like a hand across wet ice. Nothing grips.
He texts his mother "doing great" and his fingers feel like they belong to someone else. The lie sits in the chat like a stone. She sends a heart emoji. His chest compresses.
Midterm grades posted yesterday. He did not check. The notification badge glows orange on the portal app and he swipes it away. His pulse stays flat. Three months ago a bad grade sent adrenaline crashing through his ribs. Now the signal fires and his body does not answer.
His lab partner knocks at the door. Marcus stays silent. The knocking stops. He pulls the blanket over his head and his breathing slows to something mechanical. The ceiling above him disappears. The semester continues on the other side of the door. Marcus stays on this side, limbs heavy, vision narrowing to the dark fabric pressed against his face.

## STORY v03
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: social_withdrawal
IDENTITY_STAGE: mask_fracture
COST_TYPE: relational_and_emotional
COST_INTENSITY: high
---
Priya laughs at the right moments during the group project meeting. Her cheeks ache from the effort. The mask holds — barely. She nods, takes notes, volunteers for the conclusion section. Her pen moves but the words it writes mean nothing.
After the meeting she walks to the bathroom and locks the stall door. Her hands press flat against her thighs. The laughter drains out. Her face drops into something blank and heavy. She breathes through her mouth. The fluorescent light hums above the tile.
Three weeks ago she loved this major. Linguistics lit her brain up. She annotated readings for fun, messaged classmates about theory at midnight. Now the textbook sits unopened on her desk, spine still crisp. The passion evaporated like water on hot pavement — she watched it go and felt nothing.
Her best friend notices. "You seem off." Priya's shoulders tighten. She says "just busy" and changes the subject. The truth — that she cried in the shower this morning for no reason she can name — stays locked in her sternum.
She returns to the library. Opens the laptop. The conclusion section needs three hundred words. She types one sentence and her vision blurs. Her fingers go cold. The girl who once filled pages now stares at the screen and the screen stares back, equally empty. The mask cracks a little wider each day and Priya presses her palm against it, holding the pieces together with raw will.

## STORY v04
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: chronic_overextension
IDENTITY_STAGE: collapse_point
COST_TYPE: physical_and_academic
COST_INTENSITY: severe
---
Kai stands at the bus stop at 7 a.m., backpack cutting into his shoulders. He works closing shifts at the restaurant four nights a week. Classes fill the mornings. The thesis eats the gaps. Sleep lives in the cracks between — two hours here, ninety minutes there.
His hands shake holding the coffee cup. He watches the tremor and notes it like a fact about someone else. The bus arrives. He climbs the steps and his knees protest. He sinks into a seat and his spine molds against the plastic like it plans to stay forever.
In lecture he takes notes but later the pages hold only fragments. Half-words. Lines that trail off the edge of the margin. His professor pulls him aside after class. "Your last paper didn't sound like you." Kai nods. His throat locks. It did not sound like anyone. He assembled it from scraps at 4 a.m. between the restaurant and dawn.
His body sends warnings he registers but cannot obey. Headaches that start behind the eyes and spread to the jaw. A twitch in his left eyelid that runs for days. He drops eight pounds without trying. His jeans hang loose and he tightens his belt another notch and keeps walking.
The thesis defense sits six weeks out. He opens the document on the bus and reads his own words like a foreign language. His stomach clenches. The boy who took on everything to prove he belonged here now carries a weight his frame cannot hold. His shoulders curve forward. The bus rolls on.

## STORY v05
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: perfectionism_trap
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_worth_collapse
COST_TYPE: emotional_and_cognitive
COST_INTENSITY: high
---
Sana rewrites the introduction for the eleventh time. Each version looks worse than the last. Her chest tightens with every delete. The cursor blinks. The dorm room shrinks.
She earned a 3.9 GPA last year. Her parents framed the dean's list letter. It hangs on the kitchen wall back home — she knows because her mother photographs it monthly. That letter now feels like a contract she signed in blood. Every assignment carries the full weight of the family name.
Her drafts pile in a folder labeled "trash." Fourteen attempts at a single paper. She reads each one and finds only failure. The standards she carries demand perfection and her exhausted brain delivers fragments. The gap between expectation and output widens by the hour.
She stops eating lunch. Her stomach growls during afternoon lecture and she presses her fist against it. The hunger sharpens something — not focus, but punishment. She deserves the discomfort. The paper remains unwritten.
At midnight she sits on the dorm floor, back against the bed frame, laptop balanced on her knees. Her eyes burn. Her fingers hover. She types a sentence that meets none of her standards and hits submit anyway because the deadline passed twenty minutes ago. The relief does not come. Her hands drop to the carpet. Her chest empties. The girl who built her worth on flawless output submits garbage and feels her foundation crack beneath her.

## STORY v06
---
MECHANISM_DEPTH: emotional_numbing
IDENTITY_STAGE: apathy_lock
COST_TYPE: existential_and_social
COST_INTENSITY: severe
---
Devon stops caring on a Thursday. He pins the exact moment: 3:47 p.m., sitting in the campus coffee shop, staring at a notification that his scholarship application advanced to the final round. His body produces nothing. No spike. No warmth. The news that would have launched him off his chair a month ago lands flat in his chest like a coin on concrete.
He tells his roommate. His voice comes out level and detached. His roommate cheers. Devon mimics the shape of a smile and his face muscles ache from disuse.
He attends classes. Takes notes. Eats meals. Each action runs on automation while something essential idles beneath the surface, engine running, wheels disconnected. His philosophy professor lectures on existential dread and Devon watches the words land on the class like weather. He feels nothing — not agreement, not resistance, not even boredom.
His girlfriend texts. He reads the message. His thumbs sit motionless on the screen. Three dots appear and vanish on her side — she types, deletes, types again. He locks the phone and sets it face down. The guilt he expects does not arrive.
At night he lies on top of the covers, fully dressed, shoes still on. The ceiling holds no answers. His breathing stays even. The semester runs forward on its track and Devon lies perpendicular to all of it, body present, interior vacant. The numbness sits in his bones like cement. It sets harder each day and he watches it harden and does nothing to stop it.
