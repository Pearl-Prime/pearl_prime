## PIVOT v01
---

---
The body sends the signal before the mind accepts it. Locked hands, hollow chest, eyes that slide off screens — burnout speaks through flesh first. The semester does not pause. But the body already did.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Exhaustion is not laziness wearing a mask. The student who cannot type, who stares at blank pages for hours, runs a system drained past its reserves. The engine did not quit. The fuel ran out. That difference changes everything.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Burnout strips identity down to scaffolding. The high-achiever who built a self on output discovers that self collapses when the output stops. Recognizing the collapse is not weakness. It is the first accurate reading of the damage.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
The numbing arrives last and stays longest. A student who feels nothing about a grade, a deadline, a message from someone they love — that student crossed a line the body drew months ago. The flatness is not peace. It is a circuit breaker tripped to prevent total failure.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
Performance and worth fused somewhere in the first semester and no one noticed the weld. Now every blank page attacks the foundation. Separating output from identity does not happen through willpower. It starts with noticing the fusion exists.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
The mask cracks in the bathroom stall, on the bus, in the thirty seconds between closing the laptop and pretending to be fine. Those cracks are data. They measure the distance between the presented self and the actual self. That distance is the cost.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
No one sprints for twelve weeks and wonders why their legs buckle. The academic semester demands exactly that sprint and calls the buckling a personal failure. The structure broke the runner. Name the structure.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
Recovery does not begin with catching up on assignments. It begins with the body. Unclench the jaw. Release the shoulders from the ears. Put the palms flat on a surface and feel the temperature. The nervous system needs proof that the emergency ended.
---
