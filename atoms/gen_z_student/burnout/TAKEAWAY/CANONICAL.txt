## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Burnout is not laziness wearing a different mask. Your body burned through its reserves. The empty feeling in your chest signals depletion, not deficiency. A car with no gas does not have a motivation problem. It has a fuel problem. Your tank ran dry. Refueling comes before driving.

The flatness you feel replaced the anxiety that preceded it. Your nervous system spent weeks in overdrive. Now it downshifted into conservation mode. The numbness protects you from further depletion. Your body chose survival over productivity. Honor the choice. Rest is not retreat.

Burnout erases the boundary between you and your tasks. You became the assignment, the deadline, the grade. Your identity fused with your output. When output drops, selfhood drops with it. One sentence rebuilds the boundary: you are not your GPA. Your body exists beyond your transcript.

Eight hours at the library produced less than one hour of actual work. Presence without capacity is performance, not productivity. Your body sat in the chair to prove something to yourself. The proof failed. Quality hours require rested bodies. Leave the library. Recover. Return sharper.

The unread messages pile up because your social battery died alongside your academic battery. Burnout drains both. Your isolated body is not antisocial. It runs triage. Energy goes to breathing, eating, basic functioning. Replies require surplus energy you do not currently hold. The messages wait. You heal first.

Your appetite disappeared because your body stopped investing in non-essential functions. Digestion takes energy. Burned-out systems conserve. The empty tray in the dining hall signals system-wide shutdown, not a food preference change. Eat something small. A bread roll starts the engine. Your stomach remembers its job when you give it a task.

## TAKEAWAY v02
---

---
Recovery from burnout does not feel like recovery at first. It feels like more emptiness. The resting body expects relief and finds flatness instead. Touch your forearm. Feel the skin, the warmth underneath. That warmth is metabolic activity continuing quietly. Your body works on restoration even when you cannot feel it working.

Sleep changes during burnout. The exhausted body cannot always enter deep rest. You lie down and lie awake. The nervous system stays elevated even when the schedule stops. Cool the room. Feel the temperature drop on your forearm skin. Physical cooling signals safety to an overheated nervous system.

The return of appetite is an early indicator that your body is exiting burnout. Notice the first real hunger sensation. It arrives in the stomach, a hollow ache you remember from before everything went flat. That signal means your body is reinvesting in non-essential functions. It chose to heal.

Burnout produces a specific kind of loneliness. You are surrounded by people and feel nothing for them. The disconnection is physiological, not relational. Your nervous system conserved social energy alongside academic energy. Sit near, not with, people. Feel their proximity without obligation. The circuit reconnects slowly.

Exercise during burnout recovery requires reduction, not elimination. The burned-out body cannot sustain the pre-burnout training load. A ten-minute walk replaces the forty-minute run. Feel the difference between movement for survival and movement for performance. Survival movement asks almost nothing of you. That is the right level right now.

The work will return to feeling meaningful. This is not motivational rhetoric. It is a physiological fact about burnout recovery. The nervous system returns to baseline when the load decreases. Interest, curiosity, and drive are downstream of baseline. Protect baseline first. Everything else follows from there.

## TAKEAWAY v03
---

---
Burnout prevention requires catching the early signals before they compound. The first signal is a slight decrease in how much you care. A task you enjoyed becomes neutral. Notice the first neutral. Touch your sternum. That shift from engaged to flat is the body's first warning. The warning is easier to respond to than the collapse.

Overcommitting begins in the calendar but lands in the body. Your nervous system does not track events as separate items. It processes them as accumulated load. Open your calendar for the week. Feel any tightening in your chest as you scan it. That tightening is your body reading the load. Trust the reading.

The student who never cancels plans and never says no is not thriving. Their body is managing. Managing produces burnout on a predictable timeline. Feel the difference between genuinely wanting to attend and attending because canceling feels wrong. One fills your energy reserve. One drains it.

Rest that feels guilty is rest that does not restore. Your nervous system needs permission to downregulate fully. Lying on your bed while mentally listing what you should be doing activates, not rests, the system. Place both palms flat on your thighs. Feel the warmth transfer. Tell your body it did enough today. Believe it enough to let the breath slow.

The semester's end does not automatically end burnout. The nervous system stays activated by habit. You finish finals and still feel the urgency with no exam attached. Give your body two weeks of deliberate decompression. Feel the absence of alarm. The body learns that the threat cycle ended. It begins to reset.

Saying "I am burning out" out loud to someone is not weakness. It is an early intervention. Your voice box carries the information your behavior is hiding. Speaking the state aloud lets another person help carry the load. Two nervous systems managing a crisis are more stable than one running alone.
