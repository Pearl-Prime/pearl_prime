Kofi opens his laptop for the fourth time this evening and closes it again without writing anything. He used to love this subject. He remembers sitting in the library sophomore year, genuinely absorbed, reading three chapters ahead because he wanted to know what happened next. That version of himself is theoretical now, a photograph from a time before three consecutive semesters of overload. He sits at his desk and feels nothing toward the assignment except a low-grade reluctance he can't name. His chest is not tight. His heart rate is not elevated. He is just flat. The flatness is a new kind of loss—not dramatic, not acute, more like arriving at a room where something used to live and finding it empty. He doesn't know when the curiosity left. He only knows it isn't there anymore.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya sees a first-year student in the hallway, reading a course packet with her forehead creased and her pen moving fast across the margins. Priya recognizes the posture. That was her two years ago—genuinely engaged, annotating because the ideas mattered. Now she annotates because the professor checks and she needs the grade. The recognition hits her in the sternum. She has lost something she didn't notice leaving. She stands in the hallway an extra moment and watches the first-year turn a page, and the loss sharpens into something specific: she misses the person who studied because the subject was alive to her. That person is still her name, still her body, but is increasingly unreachable. She walks to class with her bag over one shoulder. The course packet stays unread in her bag. She takes notes mechanically for fifty minutes and does not remember a single thing the professor said.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/burnout/grief/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Marcus takes a Saturday afternoon and goes to the museum. Not for a class. Not for any reason. He stands in front of a large painting for six minutes without looking at his phone. His mind is quiet in a way it hasn't been since August. He notices this. He also notices that the noticing makes him slightly sad—that a museum on a Saturday is the only place in four months where his nervous system unclenched. He sits on a bench near the painting and stays for twenty minutes. He misses whoever he was before a university education turned into a series of deliverables. That person had time to stand in front of paintings without it feeling like recovery from something. He leaves the museum and the weight comes back gradually on the walk to the bus stop, settling onto his shoulders like something that lives there now. He is glad he went. The gladness costs him nothing this time, which feels unfamiliar.
