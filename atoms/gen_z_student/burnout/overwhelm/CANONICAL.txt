
## RECOGNITION v01

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2

Dani stares at her to-do list and feels nothing. Not stress, not urgency—nothing. The assignments sit in their columns like objects that no longer carry weight. She has been here before but never this far in. She tries to manufacture urgency by reading her syllabi. The deadlines don't land. She opens a blank document for the essay that's due Thursday and types her name. Her hands stop. Her brain is simply not available. She closes the laptop. She is not tired in a way that sleep fixes. She has slept eight hours three nights running and woken up just as empty. The to-do list will be there when she reopens the laptop. The capacity she needs to work through it has gone somewhere she cannot locate.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/burnout/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Selin's advisor asks for a draft of her senior thesis chapter by Friday. It is Tuesday. She has forty pages of notes and zero paragraphs. She sits in her advising appointment and nods and says she'll have something by Friday. Outside the office, she stands against the wall in the hallway. Her eyes sting. She is not sad. She is just completely out of fuel and someone just asked her to run further. The request is reasonable. The timeline is compressed but not impossible for someone with functional capacity. She doesn't have functional capacity. She has been running on fumes since October. She doesn't know how to say this. She walks to the library and opens her notes and reads the same page for forty minutes. The page doesn't turn. Her pen doesn't move. Friday arrives in her mind like a wall.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/burnout/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Arjun emails his professor to ask for a one-day extension. He types the email four times before sending it. He gives a factual reason without catastrophizing: he is behind on sleep, his other deadlines overlapped, one more day would allow him to submit work he can stand behind. He hits send and his chest seizes. He waits. The professor responds in three hours: "Of course, no problem." Arjun reads the reply and sits with it. He got the thing he needed. His body registers this as a near miss rather than a solution. His shoulders release slowly over the next twenty minutes. He opens the essay. The words come slower than they used to—he is still running on depleted reserves. But they come. He writes two paragraphs before his hands need a break. Two paragraphs are more than he has written in three weeks. He saves the document. The cost of having asked for what he needed was the four drafts, the chest seize, and the three hours of waiting, none of which will stop next time.
