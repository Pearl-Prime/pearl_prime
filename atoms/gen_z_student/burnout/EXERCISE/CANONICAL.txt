## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Close the laptop. Place both palms flat on the desk. Feel the surface temperature — cold, warm, smooth, rough. Name the texture out loud. Hold the position for thirty seconds. Notice the jaw. Unclench it. Drop the shoulders two inches. Breathe through the nose for four counts. This is not productivity. This is the ground floor beneath it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Open the notes app. Set a timer for three minutes. Write one sentence: "Right now my body feels ___." Fill the blank with a physical sensation — tight chest, cold fingers, heavy eyelids. Not an emotion. A body fact. Write three more body facts. Read them back. This inventory costs nothing and returns the first honest data of the day.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Stand up from the desk. Plant both feet flat. Press them into the floor and feel the pressure under the arches. Shake both hands at the wrists for fifteen seconds — fast, loose, no control. Let the arms drop. Notice the tingling in the fingertips. That tingling is the nervous system resetting. Sit back down only when the tingling fades.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Pick one assignment. Not the hardest. The smallest. Set a timer for twelve minutes. Write badly on purpose. Misspell words. Use incomplete sentences. Break every standard the perfectionist holds sacred. When the timer ends, stop. Read nothing. Close the document. The draft exists now. Existing beats perfection every time.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Text one person the truth. Not the whole truth — one sentence of it. "I'm running on empty this week." Send it. Put the phone face down. The vulnerability sits in the sent message like a stone dropped in water. Let the ripples move. Do not chase a reply. The act of sending broke the isolation. That is the entire point.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Lie flat on the floor. Not the bed — the floor. Feel the hard surface press against the shoulder blades, the spine, the back of the skull. Set a timer for five minutes. Breathe into the belly. Count each exhale. Stop at ten. Start over. The ceiling holds nothing. The floor holds everything. Five minutes of gravity doing the work the body forgot to request.
