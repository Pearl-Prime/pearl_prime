
## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Write your three non-negotiable monthly expenses. Just three: the ones that cannot be late, cannot be skipped, cannot be reduced. Every other item in your budget is secondary to these three. Separating the non-negotiable from the negotiable makes the anxiety smaller. The non-negotiables are covered or they aren't. The negotiables are where you have room.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Track your spending for three days without judgment. Not to fix anything — to see what's actually happening. Record each purchase in a note on your phone, just the amount. At the end of three days, look at the list. Some of the spending will surprise you. Some won't. Seeing it clearly is different from feeling it in the dark.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Identify one resource on your campus you haven't used yet: the food pantry, the textbook lending library, the emergency fund, the shift board for extra work hours. You are not obligated to use it. You are just noting that it exists. Resources you know about can be accessed. Resources you don't know about cannot.
