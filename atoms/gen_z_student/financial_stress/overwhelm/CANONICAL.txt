
## RECOGNITION v01

COST_TYPE: internal

Marcus works three jobs: morning campus café shifts, weekend data entry for an administrative office, and tutoring two high schoolers on Thursday evenings. His week is structured to the hour. There is no slack in it. On Tuesday his tutoring client cancels with no notice and he loses forty dollars. He sits in his dorm that Tuesday evening with his budget spreadsheet open and recalculates the month. The numbers work out. They always just barely work out. His jaw is clenched. His shoulders are up near his ears. He has a problem set due Thursday and a midterm next week and he has been studying at midnight because the days are for working and the evenings are for catching up. He puts the spreadsheet away. He opens the problem set. His body holds the week's weight in the muscles between his shoulder blades. The problem set is in front of him. The shoulder blades don't loosen.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya is on her third consecutive night of four hours of sleep. The financial stress has been low-grade for two semesters, but this week it compounded: her hours at the campus bookstore were cut, her textbook for organic chemistry was a hundred and twenty dollars she didn't have until payday, and her scholarship requires maintaining a 3.2 GPA while she is currently running on nothing. She sits in her 8 a.m. lecture with her notes open and her eyes burning. She can feel the words not processing. The professor makes a point that she will need for the exam. She types it out. She doesn't know if typing is the same as understanding right now. Her body is at capacity in a way she doesn't have language for. Not exhausted, not sick—just spent, the way a phone looks when the battery says four percent and every function slows down to conserve what's left.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_stress/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Selin asks her campus supervisor for more hours. She rehearsed the ask four times before walking into the office. She gives a reason that is true and partial—she'd like to increase her savings buffer. Her supervisor checks the schedule and offers two additional hours per week starting next month. Selin thanks him and walks out. She wanted three hours. Two is something. Her chest is still tight. More hours mean less time for her thesis chapter, which is already behind schedule. She walks to the library and sits down with both the expanded work schedule and the thesis draft open on her screen. She looks at both. She does not know how to make these two things fit. She knows she has to try. She opens the thesis and writes one sentence that she doesn't delete. It is not a good sentence. It is a sentence. The work schedule starts next month. The thesis chapter is due in six weeks. She writes a second sentence.
