
## RECOGNITION v01

Remy's laptop breaks during midterms. The screen goes dark. He stares at the dark screen. The spiral runs before he can reach for his charger: repair will cost three hundred dollars. He doesn't have three hundred dollars. He'll have to use campus computers. Campus computers are all taken during midterms. He'll fall behind. He'll fail the exam. He'll lose his scholarship. He'll have to leave school. He'll move home and his brother will know he couldn't do it. He is sitting on his dorm bed with a broken laptop and has arrived at his own failure before he has looked at the charger. He plugs in the charger. The screen turns on. The battery had simply died. He sits with the still-running spiral while the login screen loads. His pulse doesn't slow for another fifteen minutes. The laptop is fine. The fifteen minutes are gone.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kofi looks at his bank statement on a Saturday night and sees that his account is forty-seven dollars lower than his spreadsheet projected. He retraces the last two weeks of spending and cannot find the discrepancy. The not-finding starts the spiral: if there's a discrepancy he didn't account for, there might be others. If there are others, his spring semester budget is wrong. If his spring semester budget is wrong, he might not be able to pay May rent. If he can't pay May rent, he'll have to give thirty days notice in April. It is currently November. He is planning for an emergency in May based on a forty-seven dollar discrepancy he hasn't explained yet. His hands are cold against his phone screen. He checks his transactions three more times. He finds the missing charge at 1 a.m.: a monthly subscription he forgot about. He cancels it. The spiral doesn't end. It just runs out of original material and starts repeating.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_stress/spiral/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Dani builds a worst-case-scenario budget on a Sunday when the spiral is running loudest. She is not solving her problems. She is building a floor. She calculates what she would need if the tutoring client cancels, if her hours get cut, if the unexpected expense arrives—the one that always arrives. The floor number is lower than the spiral claimed. She can survive worse than she has been afraid of. Her chest doesn't fully release. The spiral is an emotional mechanism, not a logical one, and the logic doesn't turn it off. But the floor is there now, on paper, with real numbers. When the spiral returns—tonight, tomorrow, next week—it will run past the floor eventually and she will recognize the floor. She folds the paper and puts it in her desk drawer. Her hands are steadier than when she started. The next bad week is still coming. She knows what the worst looks like now, and the worst is survivable, which is not the same as fine but is something she didn't have before.
