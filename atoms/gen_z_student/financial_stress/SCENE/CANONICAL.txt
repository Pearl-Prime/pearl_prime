
## SCENE v01
---

---
You are working a closing shift at the campus café and your section of the organic chemistry reading is still waiting at home. Your apron is on. The mop bucket is in the corner. A classmate texts a question about the problem set that's due tomorrow. You dry your hands and type back what you know. You don't mention where you are.

You sit in the financial aid office waiting room with your bank statement on your phone. The waiting room has plastic chairs and a number dispenser. You have been here twice this semester. The woman at the desk knows your name now. You sit with your bag on your lap and your shoulders rigid. You have all the documentation. You have done everything right. You are still here, waiting, for a problem that should not be this hard.

The end of the month arrives the same way it always arrives: suddenly. You lay the bills out on your desk in the order they're due. The electric. The rent. The minimum on the credit card. Your phone shows your checking balance. You move the numbers around looking for a sequence that works. The sequence works this month, by fourteen dollars, assuming nothing unexpected happens. You hold very still.
