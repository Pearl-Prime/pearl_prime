
## RECOGNITION v01

Arjun tells his study group he can't make the library café session because he has a shift. This is true. What he doesn't say is that the shift is at the campus dining hall, not the tutoring center job he told them about, because the dining hall pays more and he needed the extra twelve dollars. The distinction shouldn't matter. It matters to him. He is wiping down tables at 6 p.m. when his study group sends a photo from the café. He looks at it and his throat tightens. Not from jealousy exactly—from the gap between the version of himself they see and the one wiping down tables in a school-issued apron. He finishes his shift. He studies at midnight. He does not fall behind. The tightness doesn't go away because the grades don't go away either. Both things are true and only one of them is visible.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_stress/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nadia receives the financial aid notification that her Expected Family Contribution has been recalculated. Her aid decreases by eight hundred dollars for the spring semester. She is in the library when she reads this. She sits very still. The number runs through her body before she can process it. Eight hundred dollars she doesn't have, eight hundred she'll have to find, eight hundred that means calling her parents or taking more work-study hours or applying for emergency funds at the financial aid office, which means sitting across from someone and explaining her family's finances to a stranger while trying not to feel like her right to be here is conditional on her ability to solve this quietly. She closes her laptop. She presses her fingertips together under the table. Around her, people are studying normally. The library is silent. The silence costs her nothing. The recalculation costs her everything it touches.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/financial_stress/shame/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Luz applies for the campus emergency fund. She has needed to do this for two months. The application asks her to describe her financial hardship in three hundred words. She writes the three hundred words without softening them. She describes the medical bill, the reduced family support, the gap between her aid and her rent. She submits it. She sits in the financial aid office hallway for a few minutes after. Her face is hot. She has been managing alone because asking felt like proof of inadequacy. The application is not proof. It is a document. It is paper. Her face is still hot when she stands up. The fund may or may not be available. She will find out in a week. She has done the thing she was avoiding and her body is running the shame response as though she has been seen doing something wrong. She presses her palms to her thighs and stands. She has not done anything wrong. The walk back to her dorm is longer than she remembered.
