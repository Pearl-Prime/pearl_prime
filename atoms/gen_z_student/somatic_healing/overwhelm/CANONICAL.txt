
## RECOGNITION v01

Nadia discovers during finals week that she has been holding her breath. Not always—in concentrated bursts, when reading difficult material or checking grades. She catches herself mid-hold and exhales and for two seconds her shoulders drop. She didn't know her shoulders were up. She checks: yes, they have been near her ears for an unknown stretch of time. She is sitting at the library desk and her back aches and her neck is stiff and her hands have been pressing the desk harder than necessary. She has been living in this body all semester without fully inhabiting it. She does the exhale again deliberately. Her sternum softens. The softening lasts six seconds before the tightness returns. Six seconds is more than zero. She makes a note in her phone: "breathe." She looks at this note. She has written it before.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus is in a yoga class his roommate dragged him to. He has not done yoga before. The instructor says to scan your body from feet to crown. Marcus does this and finds: tight calves from a week of no walking beyond classroom distances, a locked left hip flexor he wasn't aware of, shoulders that resist the instruction to release toward the floor, a jaw he has to consciously open because it has been clenched without his permission for some part of the day. He is lying on a mat in a room that smells like rubber and he has just taken an inventory of a body he has been treating like a vehicle for carrying his head between classes. The inventory isn't comfortable. The tight hip doesn't release. His shoulders don't fully drop. But the scan happened. He knows now where everything is. This is more information than he had when he walked in.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/somatic_healing/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Kofi takes five minutes between classes and sits on a campus bench in the sun. He has been in back-to-back obligations since 8 a.m. and it is now 1:30 p.m. He has not stopped moving. He sets his bag down and sits and puts his hands on his knees and just breathes. The sun is warm on his face. His shoulders begin to descend in increments. His jaw unclenches by degrees. He does not have his phone out. He is not reviewing notes. He is just occupying a bench in the sun for five minutes. When he stands up, the weight comes back—the afternoon is full, the obligations are real. But he goes back into it with his muscles slightly less locked and his breathing slightly deeper than when he sat down. The five minutes didn't solve anything. They put him back in his body before the next several hours of being out of it.
