## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Stand up. Inhale and shrug your shoulders as high as they go — ears and shoulders should try to touch. Hold for five seconds. Squeeze hard. Now exhale and drop them. Let the arms hang dead. Feel the blood rush into the space between neck and shoulder. Repeat three times. The shrug teaches the body the difference between holding and releasing. Most students only know the holding. Give the shoulders the full cycle.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place your fingertips on your cheekbones. Slide them down to the jaw hinge — the spot just in front of your ears. Press gently. Open your mouth slowly until you feel the hinge move under your fingers. Close slowly. Open again. Five times. The jaw joint moves through its full range while your fingers monitor the tension. Notice where the hinge catches. Breathe into that spot.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Lie on the floor or sit reclined. Place a book on your belly — a real book with weight. Inhale through your nose and push the book toward the ceiling with your belly. Exhale and let the book sink. Ten breaths. The book provides feedback the body cannot fake. If the book rises, the diaphragm is working. If it stays flat, the breath is still stuck in the chest. Train the rise. Ten breaths a night rebuilds the pattern.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Stand up from your chair. Step your right foot forward into a lunge. Drop the left knee toward the floor. Push your hips forward until the front of the left hip stretches. Hold for thirty seconds. Switch sides. The hip flexors shortened during your study session. This stretch returns them to a length the body recognizes as functional. Two minutes undoes four hours of sitting. Do it between classes.
