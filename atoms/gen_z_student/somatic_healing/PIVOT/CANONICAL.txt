## PIVOT v01
---

---
Your shoulders climbed toward your ears because the semester demanded a brace position. That tension is not dysfunction. It is a body doing its best impression of armor. The armor served you. Now you can set it down one millimeter at a time.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Your jaw clenches because your nervous system treats deadlines the same way it treats danger. The clench is not a flaw. It is a survival response misfiled under "academic pressure." The jaw can learn the difference. Teach it.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Your breath lives in the upper chest because shallow breathing kept you alert for every notification, deadline, and social scan. That pattern protected you. But the diaphragm still works. Place your hand below your ribs. Ask the breath to visit the lower floors.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Your hips locked because the chair became your habitat. Eight hours of ninety degrees rewired the default setting. The body adapted to the position it spent the most time in. It will adapt again. Stand up. The hips remember range even after semesters of compression.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
Your hands grip the phone, the pen, the laptop edge because tension flows downhill. The stress in your shoulders travels through your arms and pools in your fingers. Open the hands. Spread the fingers wide. Let the tension drain.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Your stomach churns before class because the gut reads your environment faster than the brain does. That churn is not anxiety performing. It is the body's earliest warning system doing its job. Acknowledge the signal. The stomach settles when it knows someone is listening.
---
