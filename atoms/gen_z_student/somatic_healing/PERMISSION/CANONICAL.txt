## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to stretch in the middle of a lecture. Extend your legs under the desk. Feel your hamstrings lengthen. Point your toes. Flex them back. Your body does not need to wait for a break to claim basic movement. Motion belongs to you in every room.

You have permission to yawn without covering it. Open your jaw wide. Feel the hinge stretch. Your body sends oxygen to your brain through the yawn. That function predates every social rule about politeness. Let the jaw open. Let the air arrive.

You have permission to leave the desk and lie on the floor. Lower yourself down. Feel the carpet or tile against your back. Your spine decompresses against a flat surface in ways a chair never provides. The floor is not a place of defeat. It is a tool for recalibration.

You have permission to shake. Stand up and shake your hands at your sides for ten seconds. Feel the blood rush to your fingertips. Animals shake after stress to discharge adrenaline. Your body holds the same animal wisdom. Let the shaking move through your arms, your shoulders, your torso. Release what the muscles trapped.

You have permission to cry as a physical release, not just an emotional one. Let the tears come. Feel the pressure behind your eyes dissolve. Your lacrimal glands flush cortisol through tears. Crying is a biological detox your body initiates when the chemical load exceeds capacity. Let the system work.

You have permission to say "my body needs a break" without explaining further. Press your hand against the desk. Feel the surface. Stand up. Walk out. The explanation lives in your tight shoulders, your aching back, your shallow breath. The body's testimony needs no verbal supplement.
