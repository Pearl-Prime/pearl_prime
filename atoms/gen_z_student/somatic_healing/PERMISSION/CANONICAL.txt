## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to stretch in the middle of a lecture. Extend your legs under the desk. Feel your hamstrings lengthen. Point your toes. Flex them back. Your body does not need to wait for a break to claim basic movement. Motion belongs to you in every room.

You have permission to yawn without covering it. Open your jaw wide. Feel the hinge stretch. Your body sends oxygen to your brain through the yawn. That function predates every social rule about politeness. Let the jaw open. Let the air arrive.

You have permission to leave the desk and lie on the floor. Lower yourself down. Feel the carpet or tile against your back. Your spine decompresses against a flat surface in ways a chair never provides. The floor is not a place of defeat. It is a tool for recalibration.

You have permission to shake. Stand up and shake your hands at your sides for ten seconds. Feel the blood rush to your fingertips. Animals shake after stress to discharge adrenaline. Your body holds the same animal wisdom. Let the shaking move through your arms, your shoulders, your torso. Release what the muscles trapped.

You have permission to cry as a physical release, not just an emotional one. Let the tears come. Feel the pressure behind your eyes dissolve. Your lacrimal glands flush cortisol through tears. Crying is a biological detox your body initiates when the chemical load exceeds capacity. Let the system work.

You have permission to say "my body needs a break" without explaining further. Press your hand against the desk. Feel the surface. Stand up. Walk out. The explanation lives in your tight shoulders, your aching back, your shallow breath. The body's testimony needs no verbal supplement.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to leave class early when your stomach hurts. Stand up. Walk to the door. The stomach is a signal the lecture cannot decode. Honoring the signal is a faster route to the diagnosis than waiting through 40 more minutes.

You have permission to drink water during a quiet exam. Lift the bottle. Sip. Hydration is a biological function. The proctor cannot ban metabolism. Hydration improves cognition. Drink.

You have permission to wear the brace on your wrist even if it looks medical. Strap it. Tighten. The wrist is talking. Not wearing the brace is the silence that makes the conversation last longer. Wear it. Look medical. Heal sooner.

You have permission to skip the workout when you are pre-period and the body says rest. Stay in the chair. Feel the support. The cycle is real. Pretending it is not real is the part that keeps the body fighting itself. Honor the cycle. The workout will be there next week.

You have permission to use the elevator in the building you have been taking the stairs in. Press the up button. Feel the wait. Stairs were a self-imposed test. The test was costing more than it was teaching. Take the elevator without explanation.

You have permission to sit in the corner of the cafeteria with your back against the wall. Slide into the chair. Feel the wall behind your shoulder blades. Some bodies regulate better with one less direction of input. Your back to the wall halves the social bandwidth required.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to ignore the posture lecture from your relative and slouch. Lower your shoulders. Round your back. The slouch is a body finding a temporary configuration that lets it rest. Posture rules optimize for someone else's image of you. Your body knows when to straighten.

You have permission to ask for a wheelchair on a campus tour even if you can walk. Sit. Feel the seat. Stamina is finite. Spending it on a 90-minute tour leaves none for the actual day. The wheelchair extends the day. Use the tool.

You have permission to tell your dance instructor your back is hurting and sit out a number. Walk to the bench. Sit. The instructor would rather have you for the rest of the semester than push you out of class permanently. The honest pause protects the longer arc.

You have permission to ice the injury during the lecture. Pull the ice pack from the bag. Apply it. The professor will not stop class to ask. The ice is medicine. The lecture is information. Both can happen at once.

You have permission to nap on the lawn between classes. Lay down on the grass. Feel its texture. The lawn was always available. The norm of not-napping was social, not legal. Norms can be revised by individual choice. Nap.

You have permission to take the slow walking route across campus. Feel each step land. The fast route would have saved four minutes. The slow route saves the lower-back tension that the rushed gait would have caused. Pick the route the body prefers.
