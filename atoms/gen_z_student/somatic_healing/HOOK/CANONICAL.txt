## HOOK v01
---

---
Your shoulders touched your ears during freshman orientation. They never came back down. Four semesters later, your neck has forgotten what neutral feels like. The trapezius muscles run a permanent shift — no breaks, no PTO. You carry a backpack on top of a body already loaded with invisible weight.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your jaw clenches during lectures you enjoy. It clenches during exams. It clenches while scrolling at midnight. The masseter muscle does not distinguish between academic pressure and doomscrolling — it reads all inputs as threat. You wake up with teeth marks on the inside of your cheek. The jaw works overtime without clocking in.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The library chair owns your lower back. Eight hours in the same position. Hips locked at ninety degrees since September. Your lumbar spine curves into the shape of the seat, and the seat has no interest in your anatomy. You stand up and the body unfolds like a letter that was folded too long.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You forgot how to breathe below the collarbone. Stress moved your breath upward — into the throat, the upper chest, the shallow territory. Your belly has not expanded on an inhale in weeks. The diaphragm filed a missing persons report. Nobody answered.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Your thumbs ache from a screen grip that started in middle school. Your wrists carry a decade of scrolling in their tendons. The hands that type essays and text friends and swipe through feeds have not been fully open — fingers extended, palms flat — since you cannot remember when.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Your stomach sends signals you learned to override. Hunger dismissed during study sessions. Nausea ignored before presentations. The gut speaks in a dialect of churns and drops and knots, and you respond by reaching for caffeine instead of food. The stomach stopped expecting a reply.
---
