Remy wants to apply to present at a student conference. The application is two paragraphs. He opens it. His thoughts start accelerating: what if his work isn't original enough, what if the reviewers think he's overreaching, what if he gets in and then the actual talk goes badly, what if his advisor finds out he applied without telling her, what if he doesn't get in and feels stupid for trying. The cascade runs faster than he can interrupt it. He closes the application. His pulse is elevated. His shoulders are tight. The application is two paragraphs about preliminary research findings and he has found fourteen reasons not to write them in four minutes. He opens his email and stares at unread messages for a while instead. The conference deadline is in nine days. He closes his laptop.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/courage/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Arjun tells his parents he wants to change his major from pre-med to public health. He has been constructing this conversation in his head for four months, running every version of how it could go wrong. He calls on a Sunday afternoon. His father is quiet for a long time. His mother asks questions he has answers for. The conversation does not go the way any of his spiral versions predicted—not the disownment version, not the tears version, not the "we worked too hard for this" version. His father says he needs time to think. His mother says she loves him. They hang up. Arjun sits on his bed. His chest is pounding. His hands are shaking from the adrenaline. He has spent four months in the spiral and the actual conversation was different from all of it. The fear remains. The conversation happened anyway. The relief he expected hasn't arrived yet. What's there instead is the raw aftermath of having done something real.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/courage/spiral/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Dani moves through the spiral differently for the first time. She's been offered a study abroad spot she applied for as a long-shot and has four days to decide. The spiral starts immediately: can she afford it, what if she fails the language requirement, what if she falls behind, what if she gets lonely. She notices the spiral starting. She doesn't stop it—she can't—but she sets a rule: she will let the spiral run for twenty minutes and then she will call her mother. The twenty minutes run. The spiral produces eighteen discrete catastrophic outcomes. She calls her mother. Her mother asks three questions about logistics. They talk for thirty minutes. Dani makes a list of actual unknowns separate from the spiral's inventory. The actual unknowns are three. She has answers for two of them. The third she can research. Her chest is still tight when she hangs up. She opens the acceptance form anyway. Her hands don't stop shaking while she fills it out. She submits it. The spiral is still running but she has outrun it on the page.
