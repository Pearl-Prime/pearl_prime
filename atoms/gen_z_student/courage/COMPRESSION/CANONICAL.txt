## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
The raised hand is the smallest act of exposure on a college campus and the one that costs the most. Your hand weighs ounces. Your chest makes it weigh tons. Every silent lecture reinforced the deal: stay invisible, stay intact. But the hand that rises — trembling, reluctant, uncertain of the answer — rewrites the contract between chest and arm. One raise does not cure the fear. It updates the data. The chest learns that visibility did not destroy you. The hand files the evidence. Next class, the raise comes one second faster.

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
Speaking in a room full of peers activates every social surveillance system your body built since middle school. Your throat narrows. Your jaw clamps. The words form behind your teeth and press against the enamel like passengers against a locked train door. Courage in this body does not sound like a TED talk. It sounds like a voice cracking on the second word of a sentence in a Tuesday afternoon seminar. The crack is not failure. It is the sound of a throat opening under protest. The jaw aches afterward because it held the gate and then let it go.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
Walking into the counseling center requires the same legs that walked past it forty times. Your stomach clenches at the door because entering means the struggle became official. The body protects the narrative — "I am fine" — by redirecting the legs away from any building that challenges it. The day your legs carry you through that door, the stomach will revolt. Let it. The stomach adjusts. The legs remember the new route. By the third visit, the walk feels like walking. The courage was only required once.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
The send button on a vulnerable email sits six inches from your hands and six miles from your comfort zone. Your face heats at the thought of someone reading your unfiltered truth. Your hands hover. The cursor blinks. Courage in the digital age is a single click that converts a private struggle into a shared one. Your face will burn. Your hands will go cold after the click. Both sensations prove the message mattered. Nobody trembles over a message that carries no weight. The trembling is the body measuring what the words cost you.
