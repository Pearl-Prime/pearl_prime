## SCENE v01
---

---
The professor opens the floor for questions. Your hand lifts two inches off the desk and stalls. Your pulse drums in your wrists. Every eye in the lecture hall faces forward. Your throat dries. Your fingers tremble. Push your hand higher. Feel your shoulder engage. The air above your head holds nothing dangerous. Your arm rises through its own resistance and stays.

You stand outside the advisor's office. The door is closed. Your stomach flips. Your feet shift on the hallway tile. Your knuckles hover an inch from the wood. Feel the air between your fist and the door. That inch holds every excuse to turn around. Knock. Feel the impact travel through your knuckles into the wood. The door absorbs the sound and becomes an entrance.

The campus org holds elections. Your name sits on the ballot you submitted at two a.m. The meeting room fills with people. Your legs bounce under the table. Your hands grip your knees. Feel the bone under your palms. When they call your name, stand. Feel your weight shift from chair to feet. The room watches a person who chose to be seen. That person is you.

Your essay argues against the professor's published position. You click submit and your finger hovers over the trackpad. Your chest tightens. Your jaw sets. Press your fingertip down. Feel the click register. The file uploads. Your argument exists outside your laptop now. It occupies space in someone else's inbox. Disagreement delivered with evidence is not disrespect. It is scholarship.

The difficult conversation waits in the shared apartment kitchen. Your roommate sits at the table. Your hands sweat. Your voice feels thin. Place your feet hip-width apart. Feel the tile hold each foot. Breathe in through your nose. Open your mouth. Speak the first sentence. Your voice shakes. Let it shake. Steady voices and shaking voices carry the same words. The words matter more than the tremor.

You walk into the campus counseling center for the first time. The waiting room smells like carpet cleaner. Your leg bounces. Your hands grip the armrests of the chair. Feel the plastic under your fingers. You filled out the form. You drove or walked or bused here. Every mile was a decision repeated. Your body delivered you to this chair. The hardest step already happened beneath your feet.
