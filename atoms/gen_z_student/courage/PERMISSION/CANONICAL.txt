## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to raise your hand without a perfect answer. Lift your arm now. Feel the shoulder joint rotate. Your hand in the air signals willingness, not mastery. The classroom belongs to the curious, not just the certain.

You have permission to walk into a room where you know no one. Place your hand on the doorframe. Feel the wood. Your body enters rooms the same way regardless of who occupies them. One foot, then the other. The mechanics of arrival do not require a social invitation.

You have permission to change your major, your path, your mind. Press your palms flat on your desk. Feel the surface that held your old plans. The desk does not care what you study. It holds whatever you place on it. So does your future.

You have permission to tell someone "that hurt" without softening the sentence. Touch your sternum. Feel the bone. Your pain is not a negotiation. Reporting it accurately costs less than burying it permanently. Let the sentence stand without a disclaimer.

You have permission to try and fail publicly. Grip the edge of your chair. Feel the frame hold steady. Public failure embarrasses. Private stagnation erodes. Embarrassment fades in days. Erosion takes years. Choose the shorter pain.

You have permission to be the only one who thinks differently in the discussion. Press your feet hard into the floor. Feel the ground hold. Consensus is comfortable. Dissent is generative. Your singular perspective adds a dimension the room lacks. Occupy that dimension.
