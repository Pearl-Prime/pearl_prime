
## RECOGNITION v01

Yuki tells her seminar class she doesn't understand the Foucault reading. The room has twelve students and the professor and the words come out while everyone is looking for someone to speak. She says "I got lost halfway through the panopticon section—I'm not sure I followed the argument." The professor says "That's useful, let's unpack it." The class spends twenty minutes unpacking it. Three other students admit they were also lost. Afterward Yuki walks to the dining hall and sits alone with her tray and replays the moment. She told twelve people she didn't understand something. Her stomach is tight. The moment was fine—useful, even—and her body still registers it as something she owes penance for. The shame doesn't update on outcomes. She said "I don't know" out loud and the walls didn't fall. She eats her lunch and the shame remains anyway.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/courage/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kofi tells his study group that he bombed the midterm. He has been pretending for a week that he's fine—changing the subject when grades came up, logging off the group chat when the results thread went active. Finally someone asks directly and he says "I didn't do well, honestly." No one says anything terrible. Two people say they struggled too. One person offers to swap notes before the final. The conversation moves on. Kofi sits with what's left. He spent a week protecting a piece of information that people mostly met with empathy. The protection cost more than the disclosure. His jaw unclenches slowly over the next hour. The shame is still present—not as sharp, but still resident in his chest, still running its claim that a bad grade is a verdict on what he is rather than a result of what happened. The notes swap is Tuesday. He will show up.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/courage/shame/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Priya withdraws from her advanced literature seminar. She has been in the class for five weeks and is genuinely over her head. She has tried. She has read everything twice, attended every office hours. She is not keeping up. She initiates the drop and stands in the registrar's office waiting for the administrative judgment that isn't coming—just a form, a signature, a changed schedule. She walks out into the November cold. Her chest is tight in a way that feels like she has failed publicly, even though no one saw this, no one knows, and the decision was reasonable. The shame doesn't require witnesses. She walks toward her dorm knowing she made the right call and feeling like she didn't. The course that replaced the seminar fits her actual level. She sits in the first class and understands every sentence the professor says. The understanding is real. The tightness in her chest doesn't fully leave for the rest of the week.
