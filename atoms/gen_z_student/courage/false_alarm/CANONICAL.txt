
## RECOGNITION v01

Luz stands outside the professor's office door for three minutes before knocking. The ask is simple: she wants to know if she can switch her presentation date. The professor has office hours for exactly this kind of request. Her body treats the knock as a significant risk anyway. Her pulse is elevated. Her hands are slightly cold. She knocks. The professor opens the door and says "Hi, come in." Luz asks the question. The professor checks a spreadsheet and says "Sure, move to Thursday." That's it. Luz thanks her and walks back down the hallway with her heart still going and a small surprised feeling underneath the relief. She had prepared for a different outcome—not consciously, but her body had been braced for something that didn't come. The hallway is exactly the same as when she walked up it. The ask cost more than it was worth.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus raises his hand during the seminar discussion to push back on a reading interpretation. His voice is steady. The argument is sound. He heard it come out of his mouth and thought, that was okay. His professor engages with the pushback seriously, adds a complicating angle, and moves on. A classmate nods at him afterward. None of the things he feared happened—no one looked annoyed, the professor didn't dismiss him, no one challenged him aggressively afterward. He sits with this in the campus café after class. His palms are still slightly damp. His throat is still slightly tight. His body ran a full threat response for a moment of intellectual engagement that was supposed to be the normal activity of a seminar student. He drank an entire coffee waiting for a consequence that didn't come. He spent the consequence budget on nothing. The seminar resumes Thursday.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/courage/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nadia applies for the competitive research fellowship without telling anyone. She fills out the application over four evenings, revises the personal statement six times, asks her advisor to review it in a nervous email that takes her twenty minutes to send. The advisor responds the same day with edits and encouragement. Nadia submits the application on the final day before the deadline. Her hands shake when she hits submit. She closes her laptop and sits with the fact that she did it. The outcome is unknown. The fellowship is selective. She might not get it. But the act of applying—the thing she spent three weeks convincing herself she wasn't qualified enough to do—turns out to have been survivable. Her chest is tight. Her stomach still holds the fear. The fellowship is still real and so is the rejection possibility. But she did it, and the doing cost exactly as much as she had in her account today.
