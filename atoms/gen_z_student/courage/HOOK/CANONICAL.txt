## HOOK v01
---

---
You're in lecture with the answer assembled three seconds ago, hand in your lap like a brick — the professor is still waiting and your throat will not open.

You know the answer. Your brain assembled it three seconds ago. Your hand stays in your lap like a brick. The professor scans the room. Your throat fills with cement. Thirty students sit in silence. You sit in a silence that has your name stitched into it. The hand stays down.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The presentation loads on the projector. Your name glows on the title slide. Twenty-five faces rotate toward you. Your chest locks. Your stomach descends three floors. The cursor blinks. Your mouth opens. The first word tastes like metal. Being seen costs the body something no grade can repay.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Office hours. The door stands open. The professor sits twelve feet away. Your legs stall at the threshold. Your hands grip the backpack straps like a parachute harness. Asking for help requires crossing a distance the body measures in vulnerability, not feet. You stand in the hallway. The door stays open. Your legs refuse the invitation.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The group project meeting starts. Four people talk. You hold a better idea in your mouth like a coin under your tongue. Your jaw locks. Your shoulders pull inward. Smaller body, smaller target. Speaking up in a group of peers costs more than speaking up to a professor. The coin stays hidden.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Career fair. Recruiters in pressed shirts. Your resume trembles in your hand. Your chest tightens at the booth you actually want. Your face rehearses a smile that your nervous system did not authorize. Approaching a stranger with your future on a single page turns the chest into a drum.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You typed the email three times. Deleted it twice. The cursor pulses in the "To" field. Your hands hover above the keyboard. Your throat dries even though talking is not required. Sending a message to a professor — requesting an extension, reporting a struggle — turns your fingers into witnesses against you.
