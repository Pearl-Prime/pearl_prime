## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit in a chair. Place both hands on your thighs. Slowly lift your dominant hand — straight up, past your shoulder, above your head. Full extension. Hold for five seconds. Notice the shoulder tension. The chest exposure. The vulnerability of an open palm facing the ceiling. Lower the hand slowly. Repeat with the other hand. This is the rehearsal. Your arm practices the motion so the classroom version costs less.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Open a voice memo app. Press record. State your name and one thing you believe about your field of study. Stop recording. Play it back. Listen to the sound of your own conviction leaving your throat and entering a room. Delete the recording or keep it. The point is not the product. The point is hearing your throat commit to a sentence out loud.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Find a doorway. Stand three feet away. Look at the threshold. Walk toward it at half speed. Notice when your legs want to accelerate through the opening. Override the rush. Cross the threshold at the same slow pace. Stop on the other side. Turn around. Cross again. Slowly. Courage is not speed. It is the willingness to feel every inch of the crossing without sprinting past the discomfort.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Write one sentence on a piece of paper that you have never said out loud. Fold the paper. Hold it against your chest with both hands. Feel the sentence pressed against your sternum. Your stomach may churn. Your chest may tighten. Hold the paper there for thirty seconds. Then open it. Read it once. The sentence exists outside your body now. That is the smallest unit of courage.
