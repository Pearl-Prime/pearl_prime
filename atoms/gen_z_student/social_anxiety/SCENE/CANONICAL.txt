## SCENE v01
---

---
The dining hall roars with noise. You stand at the entrance holding your tray. Every table looks full. Your heart hammers against your ribs. Your fingers grip the tray edges until your knuckles go white. Your feet feel bolted to the tile. Press your heels down. The floor holds. Pick one empty chair. Walk toward it. Your legs know how to carry you through noise.

The professor asks the class a question. You know the answer. Your hand stays in your lap. Heat climbs your neck and reaches your ears. Your tongue presses against the roof of your mouth. Your thighs press together under the desk. Feel the pressure of your legs meeting. You are contained. The answer lives inside your chest whether you speak it or not.

The campus bus stops and a group of classmates boards. They wave. Your stomach flips. You pull out your phone, pretending to read something urgent. Your shoulders curl forward. Your breath goes shallow. Press your back against the seat. Feel the vinyl push into your spine. One wave back costs three seconds. Your hand already knows the motion.

Your roommate invites friends over. Laughter fills the shared apartment. You stay in your bedroom, door cracked, listening. Your chest feels tight. Your jaw clenches. Place your hand on the doorframe. Feel the wood grain. The door opens both ways. You choose when to step through. No one tallies your entrance or absence.

A study group forms after class. Someone asks if you want to join. Your throat closes. Words pile up behind your teeth. Your fingers grip your notebook spine. Feel the spiral wire press into your thumb. Nod once. Your body answers before your mind finishes panicking. Follow your feet. They walk toward the group without waiting for permission from your fear.

The library is packed during finals. Every table has people. You need a seat. Your palms sweat against your laptop bag. Your eyes dart from table to table. Your feet slow. Press your toes into the soles of your shoes. Feel the pressure spread across five points. Choose the nearest open chair. Sit down. Your body occupies the chair now. The library accepts you by default.
