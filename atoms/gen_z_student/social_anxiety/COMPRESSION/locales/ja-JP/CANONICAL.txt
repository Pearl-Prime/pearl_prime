## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
The stomach drops at the dining hall door. That drop is not a verdict — it is a signal. Signals carry information, not commands. Name the drop. Walk through the door. Eat something. Leave when finished. The meal plan does not refund avoidance. Show up with a tight stomach and eat anyway. Comfort arrives after the tray, not before it.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
The face flushes before the mouth opens. The flush predicts nothing — it narrates the body's old story about danger in rooms where no danger lives. One sentence, spoken rough, costs less than forty-five minutes of post-class replay. Say it ugly. Say it fast. The seminar room forgives imperfection faster than the mind forgives silence.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
The phone is not a life raft. It is a habit with a screen. Put it away for ten minutes. Stand with empty hands at a party and feel the discomfort without numbing it. The room does not require performance. It barely requires presence. Stay ten minutes without the screen. Let the hands hang. Boredom replaces panic faster than expected.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
The voice thins in groups because the stakes inflate. One meeting, twelve people, a million imagined judgments. Shrink the audience to one. Talk to the person next to you, not the table. One sentence to one person. The thin voice handles a single listener. Start there. Scale later. Contribution is not volume — it is showing up and adding one honest thought.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
Avoidance buys relief and charges interest. Every skipped meal deposits comfort now and withdraws confidence later. The dining hall is a room with food in it. Not a stage. Not a courtroom. Enter with the stomach tight and sit down. The body learns safety from repetition, not reassurance. Ten meals in, the drop still arrives. It arrives smaller.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Set a departure time before arrival. Sixty minutes. Ninety at most. The open-ended party triggers open-ended dread. A bounded visit fits inside a plan, and plans calm the nervous system more than courage does. Show up, stay the time, leave clean. No invented emergencies. No Irish goodbye. Walk out the front door. The face flushes on exit too. Let it. You earned the parking lot air.
