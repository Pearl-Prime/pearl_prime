## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Anxiety predicts catastrophe. Your body reports reality. Press your feet into the floor. The ground holds. No audience watches your every move. The dining hall cares about food, not your entrance.

Silence in a group does not equal failure. Place your hand on your thigh. Feel the muscle underneath. Listening requires the same presence as speaking. Your body participates before your voice does.

The fear says everyone notices. The truth says everyone focuses on themselves. Touch your collarbone. Feel the ridge of bone. You occupy far less of other people's attention than anxiety insists.

Avoidance feels like safety. It functions as a shrinking room. Grip the edge of your chair. Feel the solid frame. Staying in the room — even silent — keeps the walls from closing tighter.

Your nervous system treats a crowded lecture hall like a threat. It learned that response. It can learn a different one. Place both palms flat on your desk. Feel the cool surface. Safety lives in surfaces, not in escape routes.

One awkward moment does not brand you. Press your thumb into your opposite palm. Feel the pressure point. Moments pass. Bodies forget. The only person replaying that interaction at two a.m. is you.
