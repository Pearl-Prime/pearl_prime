## HOOK v01
---

---
The dining hall doors stand open. Noise pours out like a wall. Your stomach drops three floors. You scan for a familiar face, find none, and your thumb already opens DoorDash. Forty dollars to avoid walking through a doorway. The meal plan charges you anyway.

## HOOK v02
---

---
The group chat says "library, second floor, 7pm." You arrive at 7:14 because on-time feels desperate. Your voice thins the moment you pull out the chair. Three people already divided the slides. You nod at a plan nobody explained to you. Your mouth opens, closes, opens again. Nothing lands.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Bass thumps through the apartment door. Your hands grip your phone so hard the case creaks. You text your friend "here" and wait ninety seconds on the landing. No reply. The door opens when someone else leaves. You slip in sideways. Nobody notices. That should feel like relief. It does not.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The professor asks the room a question you know cold. Your face flushes before a single word forms. Heat crawls up your neck. Someone else answers — wrong. You sit there holding the right answer like a live grenade. The moment passes. Your jaw unclenches five minutes later.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You rehearse the sentence before you reach the table. "Mind if I sit here." Four words. Your hands grip your phone like a shield. The walk across the dining hall stretches into an open field with no cover. You sit at an empty two-top instead. Four words, unspoken, burn in your throat.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Your roommate said "just come, it'll be chill." Your stomach drops when you see forty people crammed into a living room. Chill meant something different to her. You stand near the kitchen counter and pour a drink you do not want. Holding a cup gives your hands a job. That is the whole strategy.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Discussion section. Twelve people. The TA calls your name. Your voice thins to a wire. The answer lives fully formed in your skull but exits your mouth in fragments. Classmates stare at their laptops. The TA moves on. You spend the next hour replaying six seconds of silence on a loop.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You typed the best section of the group paper. Everyone knows it. The professor asks who wrote Part Three. Your face flushes crimson. You raise one finger instead of speaking. Credit arrives, but your body treated recognition like a threat. Praise and panic wore the same outfit today.
