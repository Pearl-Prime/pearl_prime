Kofi says the wrong person's name at a party. He has met this person twice and tonight he called her Julia when her name is Jess. She corrected him kindly. He said "sorry, of course, Jess." The conversation continued. He left the party an hour later and on the walk home the replay started: saying the wrong name, the moment she corrected him, his apology, her face. He is two blocks from his dorm and replaying it. He is in his dorm room and still replaying it. He brushes his teeth and the replay runs. He lies down and the replay runs. He knows the interaction was minor. He knows Jess almost certainly isn't thinking about it. His body doesn't know this. The shame has rendered a minor social error into a verdict about who he is in rooms with other people, and the verdict runs on loop at midnight in his chest.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya laughs at something in seminar that no one else laughed at. It wasn't a joke. The professor made a wry observation and Priya thought it was funny and laughed and the room didn't. The laugh hung in the air for one second before the professor continued. Priya's face is hot. She looks at her notes and writes something that makes no sense so her pen has something to do. For the next forty minutes she does not look up from her paper. When the seminar ends she packs up quickly and walks to her next class. On the walk she runs the loop: what she laughed at, the silence that followed, her face. By the time she reaches the building her shoulders are rigid against her collar. She sits in her next class with her pen ready and the laugh still hot in her chest, still the thing she cannot stop measuring the room's one second of silence against.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Arjun goes back to the student organization meeting after the embarrassing exit last month. He had stumbled through a presentation in October and left immediately after, too humiliated to make conversation. He has been avoiding the room. Tonight he sits in it again. His chest is tight walking in. He says hi to the person who was sitting next to him in October. The person says hi back normally, without any indication that October is loaded. The meeting proceeds and Arjun takes notes and doesn't present anything tonight. He stays until the end. He says goodbye to two people. Outside he stands in the cold for a moment and notices that his jaw has unclenched slightly. The shame from October is still there—it will run its invoice again in the bathroom at midnight. But he sat in the room where it happened. The room is just a room. That's something he couldn't have believed two weeks ago.
