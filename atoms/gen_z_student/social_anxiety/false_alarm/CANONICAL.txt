
## RECOGNITION v01

Marcus asks a classmate a question after lecture. Just a question—"did you catch what she said about the second theorem?" His pulse is elevated before he opens his mouth. The classmate says "oh yeah, I think she meant..." and explains it normally and that's the whole interaction. Marcus says thanks and they both go separate ways. He stands outside the lecture hall in the November cold and runs the debrief his body insists on: it was fine, the classmate wasn't annoyed, he asked clearly, the answer was helpful. The debrief takes three minutes. The interaction took forty seconds. He spent three minutes analyzing an exchange that required none of it, processing a threat that was never present. His hands are in his jacket pockets. His pulse is back to resting. He has another class in twelve minutes.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Luz walks into the campus club meeting she has been avoiding for four weeks. The room has twelve people. A few heads turn when the door opens. Her throat constricts. Her hands are cold. She takes a seat near the door in case she needs to leave. The meeting starts. It is a normal meeting: someone reports, someone proposes, there's a vote on something logistical. No one scrutinizes her. No one asks the new person to introduce herself. The meeting ends in forty-five minutes and two people say "good to see you" on the way out. She walks to her dorm and her neck aches from the tension she carried the whole meeting. The ache is real. The threat was not. She spent forty-five minutes in a state of high alert at a meeting about scheduling a fundraiser. She will go back next week. Her body will probably do the same thing. She will go back anyway.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/social_anxiety/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Selin introduces herself to a new seminar cohort at orientation. Eight people, a round table, each person says their name and where they're from and one thing about why they're in the program. Her turn is fifth. She hears the first four people and notices her chest tightening with each one, the dread mounting as she approaches. When her turn comes she says her name, her undergraduate school, and something true about her research interests. It comes out right. No one looks at her strangely. The person after her goes and the moment passes. Selin breathes carefully for the next twenty minutes while orientation continues. At the end, one of the eight people comes over and says they work on adjacent topics and would she want to grab coffee sometime. She says yes. Her pulse spikes again. She schedules coffee for Thursday. The cost was all that dread for thirty seconds of introduction that worked fine. The benefit won't be visible until Thursday, but it's already in the calendar.
