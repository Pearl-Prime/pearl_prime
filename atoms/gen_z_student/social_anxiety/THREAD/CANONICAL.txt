The pattern started before college. A younger version of you learned that visibility meant vulnerability. Your shoulders still round forward to shrink the target. Roll them back now. Feel the muscles stretch. You outgrew that classroom, but the posture stayed.

Every avoided conversation builds the case that avoidance works. Your body stores that evidence as tension in your jaw and tightness in your chest. Unclench your jaw. Let your lips part. The case for avoidance weakens every time you stay.

Social media trained you to curate before connecting. Real rooms do not allow edits. Your stomach knots at the difference. Press your hand against your belly. Feel the knot. Unedited presence is not a flaw. It is the only way humans actually meet.

The fear says you will say the wrong thing. The thread beneath that fear says being wrong means being rejected. Touch the back of your neck. Feel the vertebrae. Your spine held you upright through every wrong answer you ever gave. It will hold through the next one.

First the hallway felt dangerous. Then the group chat. Then the classroom. Anxiety expands its territory when you give ground. Plant your feet. Feel them press down. Reclaiming one room reclaims the pattern.

You rehearse conversations before they happen. You replay them after. Your temples hold that mental labor as pressure. Press your fingertips to your temples. The conversation already ended. Your body still runs it like a background app draining your battery. Close the tab.
