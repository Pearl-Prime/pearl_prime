## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to sit in the dining hall alone without pretending to be busy. Set your phone face-down. Place your hands on the table. Feel the surface. A person eating a meal is a complete scene. No props required.

You have permission to leave a party early without inventing an excuse. Stand up. Feel your weight shift to your feet. Your body knows when it has had enough. Honor that signal. The door exists for departures too.

You have permission to say three words instead of thirty. Press your tongue to the roof of your mouth. Release it. Short answers carry the same validity as long ones. Brevity is not awkwardness. It is efficiency.

You have permission to let a conversation land badly and still walk away whole. Touch the back of your hand. Feel the tendons shift. One stumbled sentence does not fracture you. Your body keeps moving past the moment even when your mind replays it.

You have permission to decline a group invitation without guilt. Place your hand on your chest. Feel your breath move under your fingers. Solitude chosen is not isolation suffered. Your nervous system knows the difference.

You have permission to show up and say nothing. Grip the strap of your backpack. Feel the weight on your shoulder. Presence does not require performance. Standing in the room counts. Sitting in the room counts. Breathing in the room counts.
