Arjun watches his classmate move through the welcome event with ease. The classmate introduces herself to faculty, holds a drink, laughs at something, circles back to a conversation—all without the visible effort Arjun feels in his own body every time he opens his mouth. Arjun is standing near the drinks table because the drinks table has an obvious reason to be there. His chest is tight. His hands hold his cup in a grip tighter than necessary. He watches the classmate move again and the comparison runs: she is not calculating everything, she is just doing it. He is calculating everything and it still costs this much. He does not know how to not calculate. He smiles at someone who makes eye contact. The smile takes effort. From outside it probably looks like a normal smile.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nadia goes to the department mixer because missing it feels worse than attending. She stands against the wall and counts the people who look comfortable. Thirteen people look comfortable. She looks comfortable, she thinks, from outside—she knows how to hold her body in the right configuration. Inside, her pulse is running at event pace, not standing-still pace. She watches a classmate tell a story that makes three people laugh. The comparison measures everything: the classmate's ability to hold attention, her posture, the way she moves her hands. Nadia's hands are not moving. They are holding her drink. She smiles at the right moments. She says something to the person next to her and it lands fine and costs her the same amount it always does, which is more than it looks like. She stays for forty-five minutes, which feels like six hours, and leaves before anyone proposes going somewhere afterward.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/social_anxiety/comparison/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Yuki attends office hours in a group session for the first time. She has been going to individual sessions where it's just her and the TA. Group sessions involve other students asking questions out loud and she has been avoiding them. She sits in the back. Two students ask questions in the first ten minutes. Both questions are good questions. Both questions are things Yuki also wondered. Neither student seemed to experience the asking as a cost. Yuki watches this and the comparison runs its full argument: their comfort is native, hers would be performance. Then she raises her hand. Her voice is controlled. She asks about the proof they got wrong on the last problem set. The TA says "good question" and explains it. Yuki writes down the explanation. Her hands are steadier by the end of the session than when she arrived. The cost of having asked is the elevated heart rate she carried for the three minutes before she raised her hand.
