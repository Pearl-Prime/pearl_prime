## SCENE v01
---

---
Your roommate lands the internship. Your feed fills with congratulations. You sit on your dorm bed, laptop open to the same rejection email you read four times. Your throat tightens. Your hands press flat against your thighs. The mattress holds your full weight. You are still here, still breathing, still real in this room.

The group chat blows up with plans. Everyone pairs off for the dining hall. Your phone stays face-down on the library desk. Your ribs ache from holding yourself still. Your feet press into the carpet under the study carrel. The floor does not move. You do not need an invitation to exist in this building.

The professor hands back essays. You flip yours over. A C-plus stares back. The student beside you grins at her A. Your jaw locks. Your palms flatten against the wooden desk. The grain presses into your skin. One grade does not carve the final shape of your life.

You scroll through stories of classmates studying abroad. Your shared apartment feels smaller. The kitchen counter holds last night's ramen bowl. Your bare feet touch cold tile. Your spine straightens against the chair back. This floor, this meal, this life belongs to you without apology.

The campus bus fills with laughing groups heading to the football game. You ride alone, earbuds in, backpack on your lap. Your shoulder blades press into the vinyl seat. Your breath fogs the window glass. Solitude is not a verdict. Your body takes up space on this bus the same as anyone.

The lecture hall empties after class. You stay behind, pretending to pack your bag. The silence lands heavy on your sternum. Your notebook presses against your chest. Your shoes grip the concrete step. You stood up this morning. You walked here. That act of arrival counts for something solid.
