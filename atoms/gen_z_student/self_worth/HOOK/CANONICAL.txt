## HOOK v01
---

---
You open Instagram at 1 AM. A classmate just landed a Google internship. Your chest sinks. Your thumb keeps scrolling. Each post shrinks the thing inside you that once felt solid. Your ribs tighten around something that used to breathe freely.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The dorm mirror catches you mid-scroll. Shoulders curled inward, jaw locked. You look smaller than you remember. Somewhere between orientation week and now, you started measuring yourself in other people's highlight reels. Your throat tightens. You keep scrolling anyway.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Between classes, you check LinkedIn. Three people from your high school already have job titles that sound important. Your stomach drops. Your shoulders round forward like they want to fold you in half. The phone feels heavier than it did thirty seconds ago.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You sit in lecture and your phone buzzes. Another acceptance post. Another celebration you watch from the outside. Your chest caves. Your breathing goes shallow. The professor keeps talking but the words dissolve before they reach you. You shrink inside your hoodie.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The group chat lights up — someone got into med school. You type "congrats!!" while your throat closes. Your hands go cold. You lock the phone and press it face-down on the desk. The surface of your skin buzzes with something close to shame.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Late night. Blue light. Your roommate sleeps while you catalog proof that everyone else figured it out already. Your chest tightens one post at a time. Your jaw clenches. You close the app. Open it again eleven seconds later. The loop owns you now.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You rehearse your accomplishments in the bathroom mirror. They sound thin out loud. Your shoulders drop. The face staring back looks unconvinced. You list them again, louder this time. Your voice cracks on the third one. The mirror does not argue back.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
A friend posts a study-abroad photo from Tokyo. Your stomach folds inward. You close the app and stare at your ceiling. The water stain above your bed maps the exact shape of everything you have not done yet. Your chest aches with inventory.
---
