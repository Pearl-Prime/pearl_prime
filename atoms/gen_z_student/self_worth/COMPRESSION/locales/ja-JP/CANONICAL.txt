## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
The comparison loop runs on a simple engine: scroll, absorb, subtract. Each post you consume performs quiet arithmetic on your self-image. Your chest tightens. Your shoulders curl. The feed never runs out of evidence that you fall behind. But the equation contains a fatal error — it treats someone else's achievement as your deficit. Your lungs expand the same way they did before you opened the app. Your worth never decreased. Your attention just pointed at the wrong scoreboard. Close the tab. Breathe from the belly. The real math restores itself in the exhale.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
You trained your nervous system to wait for a notification before it releases satisfaction. The achievement happens. The chest stays tight. The warmth only arrives when the like count crosses a threshold your brain set without your permission. This is dependency wearing the costume of ambition. Your body knows the difference. When you sit with an accomplishment in silence — no post, no story, no metric — the warmth surfaces from your own belly instead of your phone. It arrives slower. It stays longer. It belongs to you in a way the notification never did.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
The mask fits so well that you forgot you built it. You perform confidence in seminar and competence in group chats while your stomach hardens underneath. The impostor feeling is not a flaw in your character. It is your body signaling that the distance between who you show and who you are grew too wide. Your throat catches. Your hands tremble. These are not symptoms of fraud. They are symptoms of a nervous system stretched across a gap. Close the gap by one honest sentence. Say "That was hard for me." Feel your shoulders drop. The relief arrives before the other person even responds.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Somewhere you absorbed the idea that success is a pie and other people already took your slice. That belief sits in your body as contraction — ribs tight, breathing shallow, shoulders braced. Your posture literally shrinks to match the narrative. But success never operated on scarcity. Your classmate's internship did not consume the last available unit of worth. Your lungs still hold the same air. Your hands still open the same way. Stand up. Feel your spine lengthen. The expansion your body produces when you drop the scarcity story is the truest metric you own.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
Perfectionism disguises itself as high standards. Your body knows the truth. High standards open the chest and lean toward the work. Perfectionism locks the jaw and braces for verdict. You rewrite the paragraph a sixth time. Your neck locks. Your appetite disappears. The gap between your output and your imagined standard generates a physical toll that no grade can repay. Shift the measurement. Not "Is this flawless" but "Did I show up honestly." Feel your shoulders drop the instant the question changes. That drop — two inches of released muscle — is the sound of a standard that actually serves you.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
You used to know what you liked before the feed told you what to admire. The erosion happened one post at a time. Your interests bent toward what performed well. Your goals shifted toward what photographed clearly. Your stomach tightened each time your authentic impulse conflicted with the curated version. Now the tightness is so constant you mistake it for personality. It is not. Underneath the curation, your original preferences still pulse. They live in the moments you forget to perform — laughing at something dumb, rereading a passage no one assigned, cooking a meal that exists only as flavor in your own mouth. That is the self the algorithm never indexed. It is still whole.
