## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to close the laptop and stop comparing. Press your palms against your knees. Feel the warmth transfer. Your body does not need another data point to justify its presence in this dorm room tonight.

You have permission to celebrate a B-minus. Place your hand on your chest. Your heart does not stutter at average. It beats steady through every grade. Let that rhythm stand as your real report card.

You have permission to rest without earning it first. Lean back in your chair. Let your spine accept the support. Rest is not a reward for productivity. It is a biological fact your body demands and deserves.

You have permission to take up space in the dining hall alone. Plant your elbows on the table. Feel the surface hold your weight. A solo meal is not a failure. It is a person feeding themselves with dignity.

You have permission to unfollow accounts that shrink you. Grip your phone with both hands. Feel its edges. This device serves you. You do not serve its algorithm. One tap reclaims your attention and your peace.

You have permission to say "I did enough today" before the list is done. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept your full weight. Enough is not a finish line. It is a decision your body makes when the muscles say stop.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to be photographed and not look at the picture. Smile. Hear the click. Walk away from the phone. The photo lives. You do not have to audit it. The audit is a tax on the body that just performed for the camera.

You have permission to accept the gift without immediately calculating the return. Open the box. Hold the contents. The gift is information about the giver, not a debt. Reciprocation, if it comes, will arrive on its own timeline.

You have permission to choose the easier task when you have a choice. Pick up the worksheet. Set down the textbook. Easier is not a weakness category. It is a calibration. The body that finished the easy task can choose the hard one tomorrow with more reserves.

You have permission to be loved by someone who does not believe in the things you achieved. Sit with them. Hold their hand. The achievements were tools, not credentials for being lovable. The hand holding yours is responding to the part of you that exists before the achievements.

You have permission to take up the seat at the front of the class. Walk down the aisle. Sit. The front-row seat does not require pre-qualification. The seat exists. You exist. The two facts can meet without permission from anyone else in the room.

You have permission to be tired without justifying the tiredness. Lay your head on your arm at the desk. Close your eyes for a minute. The tiredness is a body state. State-of-the-body does not require evidence.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to be ordinary at something you used to be exceptional at. Touch the keyboard. Feel the keys. The high school identity that came from one skill does not have to define college. Let the skill be one of many things you do, not the only thing that defines you.

You have permission to take a personal day from your own ambitions. Lie on the couch. Feel the cushion. Ambition without recovery becomes a treadmill. Your body steps off. The treadmill keeps running without you. That is fine. It will be there tomorrow.

You have permission to enjoy a hobby you are bad at. Pick up the guitar. Hit a wrong chord. Hear it. The hobby is not a future career application. The hobby is a place where worth is decoupled from output. Both can be true: the chord is wrong and you are okay.

You have permission to refuse to rank yourself against your siblings. Stand in the kitchen. Press your palm flat. The family scoreboard is not a structure your nervous system has to maintain. Drop the comparison. Notice that the room does not collapse.

You have permission to say "I do not know what I am good at yet." Sit at the table. Look up at the ceiling. The not-knowing is honest. The fake answer would have aged badly. Your skills are still forming. Forming things do not have a final label.

You have permission to walk into a room and not perform. Cross the threshold. Sit down. The room contains other bodies. You are one of them. The performance was a habit, not a requirement. Try the room without it. Notice what shifts.
