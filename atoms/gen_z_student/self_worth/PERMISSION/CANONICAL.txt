## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to close the laptop and stop comparing. Press your palms against your knees. Feel the warmth transfer. Your body does not need another data point to justify its presence in this dorm room tonight.

You have permission to celebrate a B-minus. Place your hand on your chest. Your heart does not stutter at average. It beats steady through every grade. Let that rhythm stand as your real report card.

You have permission to rest without earning it first. Lean back in your chair. Let your spine accept the support. Rest is not a reward for productivity. It is a biological fact your body demands and deserves.

You have permission to take up space in the dining hall alone. Plant your elbows on the table. Feel the surface hold your weight. A solo meal is not a failure. It is a person feeding themselves with dignity.

You have permission to unfollow accounts that shrink you. Grip your phone with both hands. Feel its edges. This device serves you. You do not serve its algorithm. One tap reclaims your attention and your peace.

You have permission to say "I did enough today" before the list is done. Press your feet into the floor. Feel the ground accept your full weight. Enough is not a finish line. It is a decision your body makes when the muscles say stop.
