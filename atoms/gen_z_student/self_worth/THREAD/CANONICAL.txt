## THREAD v01

---
Every semester starts with a blank planner and the same silent question: am I enough for this. Your body answered that question this morning when your lungs filled without permission. Your feet hit the floor before your mind approved. Trust the body that carries you to class.

The story started long before college. Somewhere a younger version of you decided worth required proof. Your shoulders still carry that old weight. Roll them back now. Feel the muscles release. The assignment to prove yourself expired years ago.

First it was grades. Then followers. Then internships. The goalpost moved every time you reached it. Your jaw holds that pattern as tension. Unclench it now. Let your teeth part. Worth does not live at the next milestone. It lives in your next breath.

Other people's timelines look like a highway. Yours feels like a dirt road. Press your thumb into your opposite palm. Feel the pressure build and release. Every road leads somewhere. Speed does not determine destination.

You learned to measure yourself by output. Papers written. Events attended. Connections made. Your hands know a different truth. Open them. Look at the lines. Those palms held books, held friends, held your own face when you cried. That record matters.

The inner critic speaks in the voice of every authority figure who graded you. It sounds permanent. Place your hand on your belly. Feel it rise. That breath does not need a letter grade. Your body runs on oxygen, not approval.
