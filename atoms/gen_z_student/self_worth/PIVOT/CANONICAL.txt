## PIVOT v01
---

---
The scroll shows you everyone else's story. It never shows you yours. Your chest holds something the feed cannot measure. That thing still breathes. It waited for you to stop comparing long enough to notice.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Your worth never lived inside a notification. It lived in your ribcage the whole time — steady, unfiltered, indifferent to the algorithm. The ache you feel is not proof of failure. It is proof you still care.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The comparison feed runs on one lie: that someone else's achievement subtracts from yours. Your lungs expand the same way they always did. The math never worked. Your body already knows this.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Put the phone face-down. Feel your feet on the floor. The tightness in your chest loosens one degree. That single degree holds more truth than a thousand posts. Start there. Start with what your body remembers without a screen.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
You built a courtroom in your skull and appointed Instagram as the judge. Fire the judge. Your shoulders drop an inch when you stop performing for an audience that never actually watched. The relief is physical and immediate.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Other people's timelines belong to other people. Your spine straightens when you stop carrying their milestones as your failures. The weight lifts. Your chest opens. You walk differently without that borrowed heaviness.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
The throat tightens because you forgot: achievement is not a ranking. It is a direction. Your direction exists even when the feed buries it. Look away from the screen. Feel your own pulse. That rhythm is yours alone.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
You scrolled for evidence of your own inadequacy and the algorithm delivered. It always delivers what you search for. Search for something different now. Your chest already loosened reading that sentence. Trust the loosening.
