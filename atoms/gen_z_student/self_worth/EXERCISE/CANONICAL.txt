## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Put your phone face-down on the nearest surface. Place both palms flat beside it. Press your feet into the floor. Take one breath that fills your belly, not just your chest. Hold for four seconds. Exhale until your shoulders drop. Name one thing you did today that required no audience. Say it out loud. That is yours. The feed cannot touch it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand in front of a mirror. Drop your shoulders away from your ears. Unclench your jaw. Name three things your body did today without anyone watching — walked to class, digested breakfast, kept your heart beating. Place one hand on your chest. Feel the heartbeat. That rhythm never checked a single notification. It kept going regardless. Let that register.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Set three alarms throughout the day. When each alarm sounds, write down the last comparison you made. Note where your body held the tension — jaw, chest, stomach, shoulders. Then write one fact about yourself that the comparison erased. Read the three facts before bed. Notice your breathing shift as you read them. The facts outlast the comparisons every time.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Do one thing today that produces zero content. Cook without photographing. Study without posting. Walk without tracking. When you finish, sit still for sixty seconds. Place your hand on your belly. Notice the warmth that arrived without an audience. That warmth is satisfaction in its original form — before the feed taught you to export it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit on the edge of your bed. Close your eyes. Start at your jaw — unclench it. Move to your shoulders — drop them. Move to your chest — let it expand. Move to your stomach — soften it. Move to your hands — open them. Each release point stored a comparison you absorbed today. Name the comparison as you release the muscle. Let both go at the same time.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
The next time you accomplish something that matters to you, do not post it for twenty-four hours. Sit with the accomplishment in your body. Notice where the pride lives — chest, shoulders, spine. Write down how it feels without translating it into a caption. After twenty-four hours, decide if the posting still matters. Most of the time, the body already got what it needed.
