## THREAD v01
---
---
You scheduled the hard email to your advisor for your break between classes, then watched it slide to after dinner, then to tomorrow, then to "this week sometime." It was never about the advisor. The email had no visible first sentence your system could grab, so it kept sliding to wherever the pressure was lowest. Tonight, write the first sentence down before you open the email, and notice how much less it slides.

## THREAD v02

---
You keep meaning to update the shared group doc every Sunday, and every Sunday it's still six sections behind by the time you go to sleep. It was never about not caring about the project — you think about it all week. The doc has no natural entry point, so your brain treats all six sections as one enormous decision instead of six small ones.
