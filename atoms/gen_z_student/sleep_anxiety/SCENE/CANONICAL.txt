## SCENE v01
---

---
The clock reads 2:47 AM. Your roommate's breathing runs slow and even three feet away. Your eyes sting. Your body aches for sleep but your mind sprints. Your jaw clenches against the pillow. Your shoulders pin themselves to the mattress. The ceiling holds no answers. You count the hours until the alarm. Four. Three and a half. Your stomach knots around each subtraction.

You close the laptop at midnight. The blue light afterimage burns behind your eyelids. Your head hits the pillow. Your neck refuses to release. Your legs twitch once, twice. The reading assignment replays in fragments. Your heart beats harder than it should for a body lying still. You flip to your left side. Your right. Your back. Each position holds tension in a different joint.

Kai lies in the dorm bed, earbuds in, white noise playing. His eyes stay open. His fists ball against the sheets. Tomorrow's 8 AM exam sits in his chest like a cinder block. His breathing hitches. His ribs lock. The white noise drones but his thoughts outpace it. He watches the ceiling fan rotate. Each revolution marks another minute lost. His throat dries.

Your phone screen reads 3:15 AM. You already checked it at 2:50 and 3:02. Each check sends a jolt through your wrists. The brightness stabs your pupils. Your hand shakes putting it back on the nightstand. Your body temperature spikes under the blanket. You kick one leg free. Your skin prickles. The quiet room amplifies your heartbeat into a drumline behind your sternum.

The shared apartment falls silent by 1 AM. You lie on the futon in the common room. The streetlight cuts a stripe across the ceiling. Your back aches against the thin cushion. Your eyelids flutter but refuse to seal. Your mind replays the conversation from dinner — the wrong word, the awkward pause. Your stomach churns. Your toes curl. The futon springs creak with every shift.

You tried the breathing exercise. You tried the body scan. You tried counting backward from three hundred. Your eyes burn with dry exhaustion. Your limbs feel heavy but your skull buzzes. The dorm hallway echoes with someone laughing at 1:30 AM. Your fists clench. Your teeth grind. Sleep sits just past a wall you cannot climb. Your chest holds the paradox: too tired to stay awake, too wired to fall asleep.
