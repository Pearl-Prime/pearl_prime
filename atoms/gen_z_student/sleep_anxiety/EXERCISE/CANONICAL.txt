## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Lie on your back. Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly. Breathe in through your nose for four counts — send the air down so only the belly hand rises. Hold for two counts. Exhale through your mouth for six counts. Feel the belly hand drop. Repeat five times. Notice your jaw — unclench it deliberately between each cycle.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Sit on the edge of your bed. Plant both feet flat on the floor. Press your feet down hard for five seconds — feel the tension climb through your calves and thighs. Release all at once. Notice the flood of warmth. Repeat with your hands gripping the mattress edge — squeeze for five seconds, release. Do three rounds. Lie down only when your arms feel heavy.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Put the phone face-down. Close your eyes. Clench every muscle in your body — fists, jaw, shoulders, stomach, legs, feet — hold for ten seconds. Count each second. Release everything at once. Feel the contrast between tension and release in your limbs. Breathe normally for thirty seconds. Repeat twice. The heaviness that follows is your body shifting out of guard mode.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Press your tongue flat against the roof of your mouth. Hold for ten seconds. Release. Notice the instant softening in your jaw and throat. Now press your palms together in front of your chest — hard, like pushing two walls together. Hold for ten seconds. Release. Let your hands fall to the mattress. Track the warmth spreading through your fingers and wrists.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Name five things you feel on your skin right now. The sheet against your ankles. The pillow under your neck. The air on your face. The weight of the blanket on your chest. The fabric of your shirt on your shoulders. Speak each one as a flat statement — no judgment, no story. This anchors your nervous system to the present room, not the projected tomorrow.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Place both hands on your ribcage, fingers spread. Breathe in slowly and feel your ribs expand sideways into your palms. Exhale and feel them contract. Focus only on the mechanical movement — ribs opening, ribs closing. Count ten breath cycles. Your hands give your brain a concrete sensation to track, pulling attention from the thought spiral into the body's rhythm.
