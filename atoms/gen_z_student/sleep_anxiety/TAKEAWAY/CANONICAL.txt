## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Sleep resists force. The harder you chase it, the faster it runs. Your clenched jaw and rigid shoulders signal your nervous system to stay alert. Releasing the effort to sleep is the first step toward actually sleeping. Let your body lie still. Stop commanding it to shut down.

The clock check costs you more than it tells you. Each glance fires cortisol through your bloodstream. Your pupils constrict. Your heart rate spikes. Turn the phone face down. The time changes nothing about your situation. Your body needs darkness, not data.

Your bed became a battlefield because you fight there nightly. Your nervous system tagged the mattress as a stress zone. Lying in bed awake for twenty minutes trains your brain to associate bed with wakefulness. Get up. Sit in a chair. Return only when your eyelids droop.

The exam exists tomorrow. It does not exist right now. Your body lies in a safe bed in a warm room. No predator stalks the hallway. Your nervous system runs old threat software. Update it. Press your palms against the cool sheet. Feel the fabric. You occupy the present tense. Sleep lives here.

Exhaustion and alertness coexist in an anxious body. Your limbs sag while your brain fires. This paradox has a name: hyperarousal. Your sympathetic nervous system overrides your fatigue. One extended exhale — six counts out — activates the braking system. The override releases. Heaviness wins.

Rest counts even without sleep. Your body repairs in stillness. Your muscles release tension while lying still. Your heart rate drops in the dark. Removing the demand for unconsciousness removes the performance pressure. You rest. That is enough. Sleep follows when the pressure lifts.
