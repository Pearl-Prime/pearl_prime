## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to get out of bed. Lying awake for an hour punishes your body and trains your brain wrong. Stand up. Walk to the chair. Sit in dim light. Your bed is for sleeping, not for suffering. Return when your eyelids pull heavy. Not before.

You have permission to skip the 6 AM alarm after a rough night. One late morning does not destroy your semester. Your body needs recovery. Set the alarm for the class you cannot miss. Let everything else bend. Sleep debt demands repayment. Honor the debt.

You have permission to tell your roommate the light bothers you. Your sleep environment belongs to your health. One honest sentence protects hours of rest. Your tight jaw at 1 AM costs more than a brief conversation at noon. Speak the need. Your body thanks you.

You have permission to stop trying to sleep. Release the effort. Let your body lie flat and heavy. Close your eyes because they feel tired, not because you owe unconsciousness to tomorrow's schedule. Sleep is not a performance. You do not earn it through effort. You allow it through surrender.

You have permission to feel angry at 3 AM. The frustration of sleeplessness is legitimate. Your fists clench. Your teeth grind. Name the anger. It burns hot for thirty seconds and then dims. Anger at insomnia is not insomnia. It is a feeling. Feelings pass. Sleep follows.

You have permission to need help with sleep. Visiting the campus health center about insomnia takes one appointment. Your body deserves professional support. Asking for help with sleep is not weakness. It is maintenance. You service a car before it breaks down. Your body deserves the same attention.
