## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to get out of bed. Lying awake for an hour punishes your body and trains your brain wrong. Stand up. Walk to the chair. Sit in dim light. Your bed is for sleeping, not for suffering. Return when your eyelids pull heavy. Not before.

You have permission to skip the 6 AM alarm after a rough night. One late morning does not destroy your semester. Your body needs recovery. Set the alarm for the class you cannot miss. Let everything else bend. Sleep debt demands repayment. Honor the debt.

You have permission to tell your roommate the light bothers you. Your sleep environment belongs to your health. One honest sentence protects hours of rest. Your tight jaw at 1 AM costs more than a brief conversation at noon. Speak the need. Your body thanks you.

You have permission to stop trying to sleep. Release the effort. Let your body lie flat and heavy. Close your eyes because they feel tired, not because you owe unconsciousness to tomorrow's schedule. Sleep is not a performance. You do not earn it through effort. You allow it through surrender.

You have permission to feel angry at 3 AM. The frustration of sleeplessness is legitimate. Your fists clench. Your teeth grind. Name the anger. It burns hot for thirty seconds and then dims. Anger at insomnia is not insomnia. It is a feeling. Feelings pass. Sleep follows.

You have permission to need help with sleep. Visiting the campus health center about insomnia takes one appointment. Your body deserves professional support. Asking for help with sleep is not weakness. It is maintenance. You service a car before it breaks down. Your body deserves the same attention.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to move your bed to the other side of the room. Push the frame. Feel the floor scrape. The new orientation may not fix sleep. It interrupts a pattern your body has memorized. Interruption is the first step of anything new.

You have permission to read a boring book in bed instead of scrolling. Open the textbook. Feel its weight. Boredom is a sleep aid the phone cannot offer. The textbook makes good on its promise. The phone keeps you up by design.

You have permission to wear earplugs every night, not just on noisy ones. Pinch the foam. Roll it. Insert. The earplugs are equipment, not surrender. Some bodies sleep better with reduced auditory input. Yours might be one. Try them every night for a month.

You have permission to eat dinner at 6 PM instead of 9 PM. Walk to the dining hall earlier. Eat. Late dinner triggers digestion that competes with sleep. The earlier meal is a small lever. Pull it.

You have permission to shower in the morning instead of at night. Step into the stall. Feel the water. Some bodies wind down faster without the temperature swing of a hot shower at bedtime. Move the shower. Notice if sleep changes.

You have permission to keep a notebook by the bed instead of using the notes app. Pick up the pen. Write the sentence. The notebook does not light up. The writing happens without the glow. Same content. Different effect on the nervous system.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to ask the campus health center about sleep medication. Walk to the appointment. Sit in the waiting room. Sleep medication is a category that includes everything from melatonin to prescription. The appointment establishes which one your body might benefit from.

You have permission to nap during the day even if it might affect tonight's sleep. Lie down. Set a 25-minute timer. The night is not guaranteed. The 25 minutes are. Take the recovery available now.

You have permission to skip the morning exam-prep routine for one extra hour of sleep. Turn off the alarm. Roll over. The hour you would have spent flashing through cards is not the hour the exam will be won or lost. The rested brain outperforms the exhausted brain even with less prep.

You have permission to stop tracking your sleep on the wearable. Take the watch off. Set it on the dresser. The data did not improve sleep. It added one more variable for the brain to monitor. Remove the variable. Notice what happens.

You have permission to declare the night a loss at 3 AM and stop trying. Sit up. Turn on a soft light. Read for an hour. The night will end. The day will start. Forcing sleep that is not coming costs more than accepting the loss and managing tomorrow.

You have permission to talk to your roommate about going to bed earlier. Sit on the floor of the common room. Speak the request. The roommate may not have known. The conversation gives them information they did not have. Some adjustments only require asking.
