
## RECOGNITION v01

Yuki lies down at 11 p.m. She has nothing urgent tomorrow—a 10 a.m. class, no early commitments. Her body decides this is when to review everything. Her midterm grade from three weeks ago. A conversation with her mother that felt slightly off. Whether she submitted the right version of her paper. The review is exhaustive and involuntary. She watches it run. None of the items are new. None of them have changed since she last reviewed them at 3 a.m. Her heartbeat is elevated against the mattress. Her hands are fists at her sides. By midnight she has solved nothing and slept nothing. She opens her phone and reads an article about sleep hygiene and the blue light makes it worse. She puts the phone down. The review begins a second loop.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nadia has a 9 a.m. presentation and falls asleep at 1 a.m. after three hours of trying. She wakes up at 3 a.m. and her first thought is: presentation. Her second thought is: what if something goes wrong. The next ninety minutes are spent rehearsing scenarios that didn't happen and won't. She is awake at 5 a.m. looking at her phone, calculating: four hours of sleep, the presentation is in four hours, if she sleeps now she'll be groggy. She lies very still and tries to stop calculating. The calculating continues. Her neck is rigid against the pillow. Her legs are heavy. She is exhausted and awake simultaneously, which is its own kind of cruelty. At 6 a.m. she stops trying and makes coffee. The presentation goes fine. Her voice shakes slightly at the start. It steadies by the third slide. She comes home and sleeps for three hours at noon.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/sleep_anxiety/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Luz puts a notebook next to her bed for the night-thoughts. When a review item surfaces at midnight, she writes it down and closes the notebook. This is the rule she made. The idea is that her brain can let go of the item because it's been recorded. Some nights this works. Tonight she writes four items and then lies still and the items continue surfacing. She writes them down anyway. After twelve items the surfacing slows. She doesn't know if the notebook is working or if she just ran out of review material. She doesn't care. Her shoulders are slightly looser at 1 a.m. than they were at 11 p.m. She closes the notebook. Her breathing slows. Sleep comes at 1:15 a.m., which is later than she wanted and earlier than last week. She wakes at 7 a.m. with the notebook still on the nightstand. The items are still there, still real, still waiting. But she is rested enough to carry them.
