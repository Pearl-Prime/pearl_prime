## HOOK v01
---

---
Your phone screen burns white against the dark ceiling at 2:47 AM. Your jaw locks tight. Your thumbs scroll through nothing — TikTok, Instagram, TikTok again — because stopping means facing the silence. Your chest drums a rhythm no one asked for. Tomorrow's alarm sits six hours away, mocking you.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The group chat died two hours ago. Your roommate breathes slow and even three feet away. Your eyes sting from the blue light but you keep scrolling because putting the phone down means lying alone with your heartbeat pounding against the mattress. Your neck aches from the same hunched position since midnight.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
You set the phone face-down on the nightstand. Fourteen seconds pass. Your hand reaches back for it. Your pulse kicks up the instant the screen goes dark, and your stomach tightens into a fist. The ceiling becomes a projector for every undone assignment, every unanswered text. You pick the phone back up.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Three alarms set. Sleep tracker open. Melatonin dissolving under your tongue. Your shoulders stay welded to your ears. Your eyes track the ceiling fan in circles while your brain replays that thing you said in seminar six hours ago. Your fingers find the phone again. The clock reads 3:12 AM.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Your roommate's white noise machine hums through the dark. Your body lies flat but every muscle grips the mattress like you're falling. Your throat tightens around nothing. You open Safari and type "how to fall asleep fast" for the fourth night this week. The screen sears your retinas. You keep reading.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The dorm hallway goes quiet around 1 AM. That's when your ribs start to feel too small. Your breath shortens. Your legs twitch under the comforter. You flip the pillow to the cold side, then flip it back. Your phone glows on the nightstand and you grab it like a life raft made of glass.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You close your laptop at midnight and your body hums with a tired that lives behind your eyes but refuses to reach your brain. Your heart thuds against your sternum. Your hands ball into fists under the blanket. The dark room amplifies every thought into a siren. You reach for the phone.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Lights off. Eyes open. Your jaw clenches so hard your molars ache. Your chest constricts around each breath like someone cinched a belt across your ribs. The dorm radiator clicks. Your roommate shifts in their bed. You lie rigid, watching the minutes on your phone climb past 2 AM, past 3, past the point of rescue.
