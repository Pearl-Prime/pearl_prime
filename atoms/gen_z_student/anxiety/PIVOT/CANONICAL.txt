## PIVOT v01
---

---
That tightness in your chest is not a flaw in your character. It is your nervous system doing what it learned to do. And learning can be redirected.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Anxiety lies about deadlines, about people, about your own competence. It speaks in certainties. The truth is quieter and lives in your body, not your spiraling thoughts.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Your jaw is clenched right now. Notice it. That noticing — that sliver of distance between you and the tension — is where everything changes.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
The panic says you need to fix everything tonight. Your body says it needs one breath that reaches below your collarbone. Start there.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
You built an alarm system so sensitive it fires in the dining hall, in the library, in bed. That system kept you safe once. Now it needs recalibration, not destruction.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Every time your shoulders hit your ears before class, your body is telling you a story about danger. You get to decide whether that story is current.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
Anxiety shrinks when it has a name. Call it the chest thing, the spiral, the 2 a.m. lock. Naming it pulls it out of your bones and into the room where you can see it.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
The assignment is not the enemy. The group chat is not the enemy. The enemy is the gap between what your body feels and what you let yourself say about it.
---
