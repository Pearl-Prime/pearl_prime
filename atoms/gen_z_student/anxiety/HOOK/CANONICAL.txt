## HOOK v01
---

---
The syllabus drops at 11 p.m. and her jaw locks before she reads a single word. Three papers, two exams, a group project with strangers. Her throat closes around the dining-hall coffee she just swallowed. The semester starts tomorrow. Her body already lives in its worst week.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
His thumbs hover over the group chat. Fourteen unread messages about the presentation due Friday. His stomach folds in on itself, a fist tightening below his ribs. He locks the phone, sets it face-down on the dorm desk. The notification light pulses against the wood like a second heartbeat he never asked for.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
She sits in the back row of the lecture hall and her pulse drums in her ears louder than the professor's voice. A cold line of sweat tracks down her spine. Attendance counts for participation. Participation means speaking. Her tongue presses flat against the roof of her mouth and stays there.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The library closes in forty minutes. His vision blurs over the same paragraph he read six times. A tremor starts in his left hand, subtle enough that no one at the neighboring table notices. His chest compresses. The essay needs a thesis. His brain offers static.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Her roommate sleeps three feet away, breathing slow and even. She stares at the dorm ceiling, fingers gripping the blanket edge until her knuckles ache. Tomorrow holds an 8 a.m. exam she studied twelve hours for. Her body refuses to believe the preparation counts.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
He opens the grade portal and his shoulders climb to his ears before the page loads. His breath catches high in his chest, stuck behind his collarbone. A spinning wheel on the screen. His entire GPA narrows to a single loading icon and the blood draining from his face.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The dining hall hums with voices. She holds her tray, scanning for an open seat, and her arms lock rigid at the elbows. Everyone sits in clusters. Her throat dries. She picks the table nearest the exit, sets the tray down with stiff fingers, and eats facing the door.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
His advisor sends a one-line email: "Let's discuss your progress." Four words. His stomach drops through the library chair. He reads the sentence again, hunting for tone, finding none. His neck muscles cord tight. He drafts a reply, deletes it, drafts another. The cursor blinks on an empty field.
---
