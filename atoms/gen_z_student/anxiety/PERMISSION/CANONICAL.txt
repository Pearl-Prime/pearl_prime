## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to leave the library when your chest tightens. No assignment outweighs your nervous system screaming for a reset. Step outside. Feel air on your skin. Let your shoulders drop. The work stays. Your body comes first.

You have permission to ignore the group chat for one hour. Your phone does not own your pulse. Set it face down. Place your hand on your sternum. Breathe into the palm. The notifications wait. Your heartbeat matters more.

You have permission to say the deadline feels impossible. Speaking the pressure out loud deflates it. Your jaw unclenches when the words leave your mouth. Silence protects no one. Your voice breaks the seal on the pressure valve.

You have permission to sit with the anxiety and do nothing about it. Not every alarm requires a five-paragraph essay. Rest your hands in your lap. Let the wave crest. It falls on its own. Doing nothing is a valid, powerful response.

You have permission to ask for an extension. Your professor sees students struggling every semester. One email costs you two minutes. The relief lasts hours. Your worth does not decrease when you request more time. Your honesty increases it.

You have permission to feel anxious and still show up. Courage is not the absence of shaking hands. It is walking to the podium with shaking hands. Your legs carry you forward. The trembling does not disqualify you. It proves you care.

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You have permission to put your forehead on the desk for a full minute. Press the bone against the wood. Feel the cool surface absorb heat from your skin. The body needs a rest position the chair cannot give. The professor will not call on you face-down. Use the minute.

You have permission to leave your camera off in the Zoom class. Reach for the camera button. Tap it. Feel your shoulders drop two inches. Your face does not owe daily public broadcast. Your attention to the lecture is the actual contract.

You have permission to hand in a draft instead of a final. Open the document. Save a copy named "draft." Submit the copy. Your draft submitted on time outperforms the perfect version that never arrives. Done is the threshold. Not perfect.

You have permission to take an anxiety medication exactly as prescribed without explaining yourself. Hold the pill in your palm. Feel its weight. Brain chemistry is a fact. The medication is maintenance. You service a body. Nothing more.

You have permission to skip the optional study session. Open the calendar. Delete the event. Feel the box on the screen disappear. The optional was always optional. Optional means you choose. Choose your nervous system tonight.

You have permission to walk out of the exam to the bathroom and breathe. Stand up slowly. Walk to the door. Turn the handle. The bathroom is a regulation station inside the test. Two minutes there. Three breaths. Return.

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You have permission to choose a major because it does not spike your anxiety, not because it impresses your parents. Press your palm flat on the registrar's form. Feel the paper. Your nervous system is information. The major your body can survive is the major you can graduate from.

You have permission to drop a class after the add/drop deadline if your body is showing physical symptoms. Walk to the dean's office. Feel the floor under your shoes. The deadline serves the registrar's calendar, not your spinal column. Ask for the late drop. Most schools have a procedure.

You have permission to take a semester off. Stand in the courtyard. Feel your feet on the pavement. Time off is not a derailment. It is a pause that lets your body rebuild capacity for the work ahead. Some students return better than they left.

You have permission to tell your therapist you are scared, not just stressed. Sit on the couch. Feel the cushion accept your weight. Stress is a category insurance will pay for. Fear is the truth underneath. Both deserve the appointment time.

You have permission to stop taking on caretaking roles in your friend group. Lock your phone. Leave it on the desk. Walk to the kitchen. Your friends will redistribute. The group will not collapse without you. Your nervous system gets a quieter week.

You have permission to define success as making it through this semester without a hospital visit. Place your hand on your chest. Feel the heartbeat. The bar set by other students does not account for what your body has been carrying. Set the bar your own physiology can clear.
