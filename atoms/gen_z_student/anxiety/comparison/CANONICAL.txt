Selin opens Instagram at 11 p.m. when she should be reading for her sociology midterm. A classmate posted a photo from a leadership retreat Selin didn't know existed. Forty people in a mountain lodge, matching hoodies, tagged professors in the caption. The comparison runs before her brain catches up: they were invited. She was not. Her stomach tightens. She scrolls back through the semester—every seminar she spoke in, every office hours she attended, every extra reading she printed. The resume of trying sits in her chest like dead weight. She puts the phone face-down on her desk. The photo stays in her vision anyway. Her sociology pages blur. The words stop carrying meaning. She reads the same paragraph four times. The question underneath every sentence: what do those forty people have that she doesn't. Her neck aches from holding still. The midterm is in eight hours.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/anxiety/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus checks his grade on the organic chemistry exam before leaving the building. Seventy-one. He sits on a bench outside the science hall and opens the course GroupMe. Kofi got an eighty-nine. Min-Ji got a ninety-two. Three other people post scores in the eighties. Marcus stares at his seventy-one. He studied the same chapters, used the same practice tests, attended the same review sessions. The gap between his score and theirs measures itself in his chest as something that goes beyond points. His jaw clenches. His hands feel cold despite the warm afternoon. He replays the exam in his head, the questions he second-guessed, the mechanisms he misread. The replay doesn't explain Kofi's eighty-nine. It only sharpens his own seventy-one. By the time he reaches his dorm, the number has grown to fill the entire room. He sits at his desk and cannot remember what subject he was supposed to study next.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/anxiety/comparison/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Priya stops refreshing her peer's LinkedIn profile and sets her phone down on the library table. She has checked it four times in two hours. The classmate added two new internship lines and a conference presentation. Priya has one part-time campus job and an unfinished research assistant application. Every time she refreshes, the gap between their profiles expands in her stomach. She picks up her pen. The internship applications she has been avoiding sit in a browser tab she hasn't closed in three days. She opens the first one. Her shoulders are tight against her ears, her molars pressing together. Her fingers hover over the keyboard and the anxiety spikes—not because she's about to fail, but because starting means she might have to measure herself against something real instead of something imagined. The cursor blinks in the text box. Her hands stay cold. The comparison hasn't stopped running. She starts typing anyway, which costs something she doesn't have a name for yet.
