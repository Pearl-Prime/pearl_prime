
## RECOGNITION v01

Dani opens the email from her professor with her hands already shaking. The subject line says "Quick note about your paper." She reads the first sentence three times before her vision stops blurring. The professor wants to cite her analysis in an upcoming lecture. It is a compliment. Her nervous system doesn't register this. Her pulse still hammers at ninety and her armpits are damp and her phone slips in her grip. She sets it on her desk and breathes through her nose, counting to four. Her body ran the fear response before her brain could read the second sentence. This has happened before. She knows it will happen again. The email is still open on her screen, genuinely good news, while her ribcage acts like she is standing at the edge of a cliff. Her hands stop shaking after three minutes. The afternoon is already spent.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Arjun walks into his statistics recitation and the TA says "Can I talk to you after class?" His stomach drops through the floor. He spends the next fifty minutes not hearing a word. His pen moves across the page but records nothing. He replays every recent assignment, every forum post, every homework submission—looking for the thing he did wrong. His neck is locked in one position. When recitation ends and he walks to the front, the TA says she wants to nominate him for a departmental award. Arjun says thank you and walks out of the building and sits on the steps with his head in his hands. He wasted fifty minutes in a full anxiety response to something that wasn't a threat. He has a statistics problem set due tonight and retained nothing from the recitation he just attended. The award feels far away. The lost hour is immediate.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/anxiety/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Yuki gets a calendar notification for a meeting with her academic advisor and her chest seizes. She was supposed to schedule this meeting herself two weeks ago. She forgot. The anxiety scaffolds immediately: the advisor will be annoyed, she'll be put on probation, she'll lose her scholarship. She presses her palms flat on her desk to stop them trembling. She looks up the email chain. The advisor sent a gentle reminder last week. The tone is warm. The meeting is a standard check-in. Yuki reads it four times and the evidence doesn't stop the physical response. Her heart rate stays elevated. Her stomach remains tight. She knows intellectually that this is a routine appointment. Her body insists it's a verdict. She confirms the meeting time and stares at the confirmation screen while her diaphragm slowly loosens. The cost is not the meeting. The cost is the hour between the notification and the loosening, which she will not get back for studying.
