## SCENE v01
---

---
The group chat blows up at 11 PM. Three people dropped the project. Your stomach clenches hard beneath your ribs. The cursor blinks on an empty Google Doc. Your jaw locks. Your shoulders climb toward your ears. The deadline sits fourteen hours away. Your fingers hover above the keyboard, trembling. The dining hall empties around you, trays clattering, but the noise stays distant. Your chest holds something heavy, solid, immovable.

The professor posts final grades at midnight. Your thumb freezes over the notification. Heat floods your face. Your throat tightens into a fist. The dorm room shrinks. Your roommate sleeps three feet away, breathing easy. You sit cross-legged on the mattress, spine rigid, eyes locked on the screen glow. Every muscle in your neck pulls taut. The number loads. Your ribcage forgets how to expand.

Mia opens her laptop in the library basement. The loan balance stares back. Her hands go cold. Her stomach drops like a missed stair. She presses her palms flat against the desk. The fluorescent lights hum overhead. Her jaw grinds. Her breath turns shallow, quick, trapped high in her chest. The spreadsheet columns blur. She blinks hard. Her shoulders hunch forward, curling around the ache in her sternum.

The campus bus lurches forward. Your phone buzzes with a voicemail from your advisor. Your pulse slams into your wrists. You grip the metal pole. Your knees lock. The bus sways and your stomach tilts. Other students scroll their feeds, relaxed, loose-shouldered. Your teeth press together. The voicemail notification sits unopened. Your thumb hovers, pulls back. Your chest compresses around each breath like a vise.

The lecture hall holds three hundred people. The professor calls your name for the presentation. Your legs go hollow. Your hands shake at your sides. You stand. The walk to the podium stretches long. Your throat dries. Your tongue sticks to the roof of your mouth. The projector throws blue light across your face. Your heartbeat drums behind your eyes. Every seat holds someone watching.

You sit in the shared apartment kitchen at 2 AM. The rent email arrived six hours ago. Your shoulders bunch around your ears. Your stomach acid climbs. The fridge hums. The counter holds three empty mugs and a stack of unopened textbooks. Your fingernails press crescents into your palms. Your jaw aches from clenching. The number in the email does not change no matter how long you stare.
