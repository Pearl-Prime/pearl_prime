## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Drop your shoulders. Right now, actively push them away from your ears. Hold for five seconds. Feel the space that opens between your neck and your trapezius muscles. That space did not exist thirty seconds ago. You built it with one deliberate movement. Repeat three times before you open your textbook.

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both feet flat on the floor under your desk. Press down through your heels until you feel the ground push back. Hold that pressure for ten seconds. Your nervous system reads contact with the ground as safety. Let your legs carry the weight your chest has been holding since you sat down.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Open your mouth slightly and let your tongue drop from the roof of your mouth to the bottom. Hold it there. Breathe through the gap between your teeth. Your jaw carries the tension your brain refuses to name. Releasing it does not solve the assignment, but it gives your body one less battle to fight while you work.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Inhale for four counts through your nose. Your belly pushes outward against the waistband of your pants. Hold for four counts. Exhale for six counts through your mouth. The exhale is longer than the inhale on purpose — it activates the branch of your nervous system that slows everything down. Do four rounds before the lecture starts.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Squeeze both hands into the tightest fists you can make. Hold for seven seconds. Feel the burn climb from your fingers through your forearms. Now release. Let your hands fall open on your thighs, palms up. The contrast between gripping and releasing teaches your muscles the difference between alarm and rest. Your body learns through contrast, not logic.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Press your back flat against your dorm room wall. Slide down until your thighs parallel the floor. Hold for twenty seconds. Your legs will burn. Focus on the burn. Anxiety lives in abstraction — "everything is wrong." The wall sit forces your attention into a single, concrete, physical sensation. When you stand up, your chest will be looser. The body traded one tension for another and came out ahead.
