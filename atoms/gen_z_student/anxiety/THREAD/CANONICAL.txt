Identity and anxiety braided together early. You became "the anxious student" before you had a chance to become anything else. Your hands grip tighter when you identify that way. Open them. Feel the fingers extend. Your anxiety is a visitor with a loud voice. It is not your name.

The academic calendar runs anxiety in predictable cycles: start of semester, midterms, finals, break. Your body knows the cycle and prepares ahead of the schedule. The next chapter maps your personal anxiety calendar so you can pre-position your toolkit before each peak rather than scrambling during it.

Peer support and professional support serve different functions for the anxious nervous system. Your friends can ground you in the moment. A therapist can rewire the pattern over time. Touch your collarbone. Feel the breadth of it. You deserve both. The next chapter covers when each resource belongs in your plan.

Your campus has resources your anxiety has been keeping you from accessing. The next chapter is a practical map: where to go, what to say, what to expect. Your stomach tightens at the idea of walking into a counseling center. That tightening is information. The chapter shows your body that the door is safe to open.

The medication question comes up for many students and belongs in a clinical conversation, not a Google search. Touch your throat. Feel it. The stigma attached to psychiatric medication lives in the body as shame. The next chapter separates the chemistry from the character. Your brain chemistry is not a moral category.

Anxiety and high achievement live together in more bodies than the university will ever tell you. The next chapter introduces you to the high-functioning anxious student's specific trap: the performance that masks the cost. Your body pays the tab even when the grade disguises it. The chapter ahead names the tab and shows you how to start closing it.
