## TAKEAWAY v01
---

---
Anxiety lives in the body first. The chest tightens. The jaw locks. The stomach drops. These sensations arrive before any thought forms. Naming the physical signal breaks the spiral. Your nervous system fires a warning. You do not have to obey it. The sensation passes. Every single time, the sensation passes.

Your body stores the stress your mind refuses to name. Shoulders climb. Fists clench. Breath shortens. The tension accumulates across hours. One deliberate exhale longer than the inhale resets the signal. Your diaphragm drops. Your ribs expand. The alarm fades. Anxiety shrinks when the body softens first.

The anxious thought demands immediate action. It screams urgency. Your pulse obeys. Your muscles brace. But urgency is not accuracy. Pausing for three breaths creates a gap between the alarm and your response. Inside that gap, clarity lives. The body already knows how to return to baseline. Let it.

Anxiety tells you everything matters equally. It flattens priority. Your chest carries the weight of ten deadlines stacked. One truth cuts through: only this hour exists. Choose one task. Place your feet flat on the floor. Feel the ground hold your weight. Begin there. The rest waits.

The clenched stomach is not a prophecy. The racing heart is not evidence. Your body responds to perceived danger, not actual danger. Pressing your palms against a cool surface sends a grounding signal through your nerves. Temperature change interrupts the loop. Your hands register something real. Start there.

You survived every anxious moment before this one. Your track record stands at one hundred percent. The shaking hands still typed. The tight throat still spoke. The hollow legs still walked to the podium. Your body carried you through. It carries you now. Trust the record, not the alarm.
