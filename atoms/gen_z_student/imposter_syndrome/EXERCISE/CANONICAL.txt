## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Evidence Audit. Open your notes app. Create a list titled "Skill, Not Luck." Every evening this week, write one academic action you completed that required knowledge, effort, or judgment. No disclaimers. No hedging. Read the list on Sunday. Notice the tension in your chest as you read it. That tension is the mechanism resisting accurate data.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Exposure Inventory. List three academic situations you avoided this semester — an office hour, a study group, a raised hand. Pick the smallest one. Do it this week. Before you go, place your hand flat on your sternum and notice the physical sensation. After, write two sentences about what actually happened versus what your brain predicted.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Replay Interrupt. The next time your brain loops on something you said in class, set a three-minute timer. When it rings, write down the sentence your brain flagged. Below it, write what the professor and classmates actually did in response. Compare the two. Your jaw will unclench when the evidence contradicts the replay.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Compliment Receipt. The next time a professor, TA, or classmate praises your work, do not laugh, deflect, or minimize. Say "thank you" and stop. Notice your face flush. Notice your ribs tighten. Write down the compliment verbatim within one hour. Reread it before bed. The discomfort is the mechanism losing a data point.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Comparison Correction. Pick one classmate you admire. Write three things they do well. Then write three things you do well in the same course. Force yourself to match the length and specificity of both lists. Your hand will slow on your own list. That friction is the bias showing itself. Finish the list anyway.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Draft Permission. Start your next assignment with the words "This is a rough draft and it does not need to be perfect." Leave that sentence at the top of the document while you write. Delete it before submission. Notice how your shoulders drop when the sentence gives you permission to produce instead of perform. Track whether the final grade changes.
