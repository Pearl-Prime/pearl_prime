The acceptance letter arrived in March. By September, Kai sat in a 200-person lecture hall convinced the admissions office had confused his file with someone else's. His throat tightened every time the professor scanned the room for volunteers. Forty other students raised their hands. Kai pressed his palms flat against his thighs and stared at his notes.

## HOOK v02
---

---
Priya earned a 3.9 GPA last semester. She still opens her grades portal at 2 a.m. expecting to see a revocation notice. Her chest constricts before every email from the registrar. The honors program accepted her, and she treats each new assignment like a countdown to the moment someone discovers she memorized answers instead of understanding them.

## HOOK v03
---

---
Everyone in the seminar reads the same material. Everyone has the same twelve hours before class. But when the professor calls on Diego, his tongue locks against the roof of his mouth. His stomach drops. The insight he rehearsed in his dorm room dissolves. He hears his own voice thin out, and the sentence he delivers sounds borrowed from someone smarter.

## HOOK v04
---

---
Mina types three paragraphs in the group chat, then deletes all of them. Her fingers hover over the keyboard while her study group debates the thesis prompt. She knows the answer. Her ribs tighten around her lungs. She types a fourth version, shorter, hedged, stripped of the confidence she felt ten seconds ago. She hits send and immediately regrets it.

## HOOK v05
---

---
The fellowship interview lasted nine minutes. Jalen walked out and catalogued every answer he gave, tagging each one as proof he did not belong. His jaw clenched in the elevator. His neck stiffened on the bus ride home. By midnight he had built an airtight case that the committee would see through him, and his shoulders still had not dropped from his ears.

## HOOK v06
---

---
Sana raises her hand in a 30-person seminar and her pulse hammers in her wrists. She speaks for eleven seconds. The professor nods. Three classmates scribble notes. Sana spends the next forty minutes replaying those eleven seconds, certain she mispronounced a term, and her face burns each time she remembers the sound of her own voice in that room.

## HOOK v07
---

---
Office hours terrify Amir more than exams. He stands outside the professor's door rehearsing a single question, and his palms slick with sweat. Inside, the professor treats him like a colleague. Amir's stomach clenches. He nods through the explanation, too locked in his own dread to absorb a single word. He thanks her and leaves understanding less than when he arrived.

---
The dean's list notification landed in Thea's inbox on a Tuesday. She screenshot it, then spent Wednesday building a mental spreadsheet of classmates who deserved it more. Her shoulders knotted. Her jaw ached from clenching overnight. Recognition does not quiet the voice that insists she gamed a system built for people with real talent. It amplifies it.
