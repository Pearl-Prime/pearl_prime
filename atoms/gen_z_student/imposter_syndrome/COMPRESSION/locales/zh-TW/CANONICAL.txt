## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
Imposter syndrome treats your achievements as errors and your doubts as data. Your brain built a filter that discards evidence of competence and amplifies evidence of fraud. The filter runs automatically — your stomach drops, your voice thins, your hands shake. You do not need to dismantle the filter today. You need to start logging what it deletes. One honest entry per day rewrites the record your nervous system refuses to update on its own.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
You sit in a room full of students and assume every one of them belongs more than you do. Your chest tightens. Your throat locks. The comparison runs on curated data — their best public moments against your worst private fears. Accurate comparison requires your full record, not the highlight reel your anxiety edits together. Track your contributions the way you track theirs. The ledger balances when the inputs are honest.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
Avoidance feels protective. It functions as evidence. Every office hour you skip becomes a line on the case your brain builds against you. Every fellowship you decline confirms the verdict you already reached. The loop breaks at the action step: show up once. Speak once. Submit once. Your body will send distress signals the entire time. Act through the signals. The alarm recalibrates after the action, not before.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Your brain replays eleven seconds of class participation for forty minutes afterward. It scans for errors the room noticed. It finds none and treats the absence as proof you have not been caught yet. This is hypervigilance aimed at social performance, and its cost exceeds any mistake it pretends to prevent. Label the replay when it starts. Do not fight it. Stop treating its conclusions as fact.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
Perfectionism and imposter syndrome share a border. You rewrite a sentence because it sounds too confident for someone who does not belong. Then you rewrite it because it sounds too cautious. The document never grows. Lower the standard from flawless to finished. A submitted paper that earns a B teaches your nervous system more than an outline that stays pristine in your drafts folder. Hit submit. Let your hands shake.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Recognition triggers the fraud alarm louder than failure does. The dean's list notification tightens your shoulders. The professor's praise heats your face. Your brain insists the evidence is wrong. The practice is small: hold still when someone acknowledges your work. Say thank you. Do not redirect. The discomfort is the mechanism losing its grip on a data point it needs to keep the narrative intact.
