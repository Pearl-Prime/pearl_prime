## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to accept the compliment. Let it land in your chest without deflecting. Your professor meant the words. Your hands did the work. The praise belongs to you. Set down the shield. The compliment is not an attack.

You have permission to not know the answer in the study group. Silence is not evidence of stupidity. Your closed mouth gathers information. Learning requires gaps. Your empty page fills at its own pace. Confusion precedes clarity every single time.

You have permission to celebrate the ninety-four. The score reflects hours of effort your body remembers. Your sore back from studying. Your tired eyes from reading. The two-point gap means nothing. The work means everything. Your body earned this grade.

You have permission to take up space in the room. Your chair holds you. The table has room for your elbows. Spread your notebook open. Uncross your arms. Your body belongs in this classroom exactly as much as every other body present.

You have permission to be a beginner. First-year students do not owe expertise. Your confusion is appropriate. Your stumbling is developmental. Your shaking voice in the presentation proves you stood up and spoke. Beginners begin. That is the entire job description.

You have permission to close LinkedIn. The curated highlight reel of other people's summers carries zero information about your worth. Your thumbs deserve rest. Your stomach deserves peace. Set the phone down. Look at the ceiling. Breathe into your belly. Return to your own lane.
