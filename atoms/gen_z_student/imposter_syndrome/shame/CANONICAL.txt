
## RECOGNITION v01

COST_TYPE: internal

IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Marcus gets accepted into an honors thesis program. He reads the acceptance email in his dorm room and the first feeling is not pride. The first feeling is a cold certainty that the committee made an error. He reads the email again. It has his name. It references his application essay. It is addressed specifically to him. The shame runs underneath the certainty: he doesn't deserve this, and when they figure it out, it will be worse than never having been selected. He replies to the acceptance and then closes his laptop and does not tell anyone for four days. He worries that telling someone will make the error visible. He worries that celebrating will accelerate the discovery. He sits with the acceptance letter in his inbox and treats it like a problem he hasn't solved yet.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/imposter_syndrome/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Dani presents her research at the department symposium and her voice is steady throughout. She answers three questions from faculty. She does not fumble. Afterward, a professor says her data visualization was the clearest in the session. She smiles and thanks him. On the walk back to her lab, the shame offers its counterargument: the professor was being kind, the visualization was adequate not excellent, the other presenters had more longitudinal data, she got lucky with the question she was asked. The praise and the shame run simultaneously. By the time she reaches the lab door, the shame has outrun the praise in volume. She knows this is not accurate. She knows the professor's compliment was real. She writes it down in her notebook anyway, as evidence she has to return to when the shame speaks louder than the data.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/imposter_syndrome/shame/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nadia tells her advisor she has been holding back in seminar discussions because she's not sure her contributions are at the expected level. Her advisor looks at her and says "You're one of the most prepared students I've had this year." Nadia hears it. She also feels the shame continue underneath it, unchanged, like a system running in a background her advisor can't see. She says "I know, but I feel like I'm missing something everyone else has." Her advisor talks for a while about the pattern. Nadia listens. She takes notes. The conversation is real and it matters and the shame is still there when she walks out. The difference is small but precise: she said it out loud. She named the feeling to a person who could see the gap between the feeling and the evidence. The gap hasn't closed. But it has a name now and a witness, which costs her something she was protecting, and which she doesn't entirely regret.
