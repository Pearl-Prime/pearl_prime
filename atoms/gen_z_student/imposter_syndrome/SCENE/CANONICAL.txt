## SCENE v01
---

---
The acceptance letter sits open on the laptop screen. Your roommate hugs you. Your parents text exclamation points. Your hands stay cold. Your stomach holds a stone. The words blur. You read the letter again, searching for the mistake, the clerical error, the wrong name. Your chest tightens around a single thought: they got the wrong person. Your smile stretches thin across clenched teeth.

The study group gathers in the library. Four students toss terms around like breathing. Your throat seals shut. Your pen stops moving. Heat creeps up your neck. Your legs press together under the table, bracing. Everyone nods at something you do not understand. Your ribs contract. You flip to a random page in your textbook, pretending to reference something. Your fingers leave damp prints on the paper.

Jordan checks the grade. Ninety-four percent. His classmate scored ninety-six. His stomach hollows out. He closes the tab. His jaw tightens. The dining hall buzz fills his ears but nothing registers. He stares at the tray. The fork stays still. Two points. His shoulders curl forward. His chest folds around the gap between who he is and who he believes he should be.

The professor says "excellent work" after class. Your face burns. Your eyes drop to the floor. Your hands grip your bag straps. The compliment lands like an accusation. Your pulse quickens. You walk faster down the hallway. The fluorescent lights catch your reflection in the glass door. You look away. Your neck stiffens. The praise sits in your throat, unswallowed.

You scroll LinkedIn in the shared apartment. Everyone interned somewhere impressive. Your thumbs freeze. Your jaw locks sideways. Your stomach acid rises. The screen shows smiling faces in branded shirts. Your chest compresses. You set the phone face-down on the couch. Your breath catches high in your clavicles. The comparison loop fires. Your shoulders climb. Your worth shrinks with each scroll.

The group project assigns you team lead. Your hands go clammy. Your heartbeat thumps in your fingertips. Five people watch the shared screen, waiting for your direction. Your mouth opens. Nothing comes. Your tongue presses against the roof of your mouth. The silence stretches. Your ears ring. Your kneecaps lock. The cursor blinks on the empty agenda doc. Everyone still waits.
