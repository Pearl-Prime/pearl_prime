## PIVOT v01
---
---
You think the goal is to never let your mind wander off during class or dinner. Try this instead: notice you've left, and come back — that's the entire skill, every single time, for the rest of the semester.
---

## PIVOT v02
---
---
You think being distracted means you're bad at focusing. Try this instead: treat the drift as normal and put your energy only into the return — the anchor, the actual sentence, the actual face in front of you.
---

## PIVOT v03
---
---
You think presence is something calm, focused people just have and you don't. Try this instead: they're not drifting less than you — they're returning faster. Practice the return, not the not-drifting.
---

## PIVOT v04
---
---
You think catching yourself distracted is proof the technique isn't working. Try this instead: catching it IS the technique working — you can't return from a drift you never noticed.
---

## PIVOT v05
---
---
You think you need to fix your whole attention span before this semester gets easier. Try this instead: fix the next five minutes. One anchor, one return, repeated. The semester follows from there.
---

## PIVOT v06
---
---
You think multitasking through lecture or dinner gets you more done. Try this instead: pick one thing to actually be present for and notice how much less exhausted you feel afterward.
---

## PIVOT v07
---
---
You think self-criticism after drifting will make you focus better next time. Try this instead: skip the internal lecture entirely and go straight to the anchor. The criticism is just a second drift wearing a disguise.
---

## PIVOT v08
---
---
You think you have to eliminate the pull toward the group chat before you can be present. Try this instead: let the pull exist, and just get faster at noticing it and coming back.
---

## PIVOT v09
---
---
You think being present means holding focus for the whole hour without a single lapse. Try this instead: aim for faster returns, not fewer lapses. Nobody manages the second one anyway.
---

## PIVOT v10
---
---
You think checking your phone mid-conversation is harmless because you're "still listening." Try this instead: notice what you actually remember from the conversation afterward — that's the real test, not how it felt in the moment.
---

## PIVOT v11
---
---
You think today's drift means tomorrow's will be just as bad. Try this instead: each noticed return is a rep. Reps compound, even the ones that felt clumsy.
---

## PIVOT v12
---
---
You think the fix is trying harder to concentrate. Try this instead: give your attention one small, specific anchor to return to — trying harder with nothing to aim at just burns you out faster.
---
