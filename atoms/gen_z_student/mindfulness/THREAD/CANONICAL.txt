## THREAD v01
---
---
Priya keeps her phone face-down through one more week of Psych, and by Friday she notices something she didn't expect — she can predict the professor's next point before he says it, not because she's smarter, but because she's actually been there for all the setup. She mentions this to her roommate almost as an aside. Her roommate asks what she changed. Priya isn't sure how to explain something this small yet.

## THREAD v02
---
---
Marcus starts looking up before answering at the rec desk, just as a habit now, no memory of deciding to keep doing it. A regular says, out of nowhere, "you always seem like you're actually here, it's kind of nice." Marcus laughs it off. Later, walking back to his dorm, he can't stop thinking about how low the bar apparently is, and how easy it turned out to clear.

## THREAD v03
---
---
Devon finishes an entire reading in one sitting for the first time all semester and texts his study group, half-joking, that he thinks he found a cheat code. Nobody responds for an hour. When his friend finally does, it's just: "wait what cheat code," and Devon realizes he doesn't have a clean way to explain "I got faster at coming back" that doesn't sound like nothing.

## THREAD v04
---
---
Aisha tells Ren, mid-conversation, "wait, say that again, I drifted" for what must be the fifth time that week, and instead of it feeling awkward like the first time, it's just become a normal thing they do with each other now. Ren starts doing it back to her. Neither of them names what's happening. It's just how they talk now.
---

## THREAD v05
---
---
Tomas submits the internship application four hours before the deadline instead of forty minutes, cover letter actually finished instead of half-written for the third night running. He doesn't credit any specific technique when his roommate asks how he got it done early. He just says, "I don't know, I stopped rereading the same line for an hour," which is truer than he realizes.
---

## THREAD v06
---
---
Nina notices, three weeks into using the ladle-sound as her anchor, that she recognizes the regulars in the dining hall line now — actual faces, actual small habits, instead of a blur of trays. One of them says "you remembered my order" like it's a big deal. To Nina, it barely registered as an effort at all anymore.

## THREAD v07
---
---
Jae's seminar professor stops him after class one day and says, unprompted, "your comments have gotten sharper this month." Jae almost explains the finger-on-the-notebook thing and decides it would sound too small to be believed. He just says thanks and walks out, quietly aware of exactly what changed and why nobody would guess it from the outside.

## THREAD v08
---
---
Lily's mom, on the next Sunday call, doesn't say anything about the difference, but the call runs twenty minutes longer than usual without either of them trying to end it. Lily notices this on the walk to her next class and can't explain why a phone call, of all things, is what made her feel most like herself all week.

## THREAD v09
---
---
Oscar and Fei get through their next lab in half the usual back-and-forth, no repeated instructions, no confused pauses. Fei says, "good lab today," like it's a small thing. Oscar knows exactly why it went well and doesn't try to explain it, just quietly plans to say the same thing to her before their next session too.

## THREAD v10
---
---
Camille arrives at her first class one Thursday and realizes, walking in, that she remembers the entire walk — the specific crack in the sidewalk, a dog someone was walking, the actual weather. It unsettles her a little, how rare that is, and how unremarkable the walk itself actually was.

## THREAD v11
---
---
Ben's next interview goes long — the interviewer keeps asking follow-up questions, which he later learns is a good sign. He doesn't credit the spiral-catching trick out loud to anyone. He just notices, days later, that he can actually remember the interviewer's face and specific questions instead of a vague blur of anxiety, and that alone tells him something worked.
