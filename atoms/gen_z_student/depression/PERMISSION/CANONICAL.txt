## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You have permission to call brushing your teeth a victory. Grip the toothbrush. Feel the handle in your palm. Depression turned basic hygiene into a mountain. Climbing it counts. Your clean teeth are proof of effort no one else understands.

You have permission to skip the party and feel nothing about it. Press your back into your mattress. Feel the bed hold you. Rest is not failure dressed in pajamas. Rest is the body allocating its limited resources where they matter: survival.

You have permission to eat whatever requires the least effort. Open the granola bar. Feel the wrapper crinkle in your fingers. Nutrition purists can wait. Calories enter your blood and fuel your brain. A granola bar in a depression is a full meal. Accept it.

You have permission to cry without a reason. Let the pressure behind your eyes release. Feel the tears track down your face. Touch your cheek. The wetness is real. The body processes what the mind cannot name. Tears are data, not drama.

You have permission to ask for help in a text instead of a conversation. Type three words: "I am struggling." Press send. Feel your thumb lift off the screen. Three words carry enough signal. The person who receives them will hear the weight. You do not need a speech. You need a bridge.

You have permission to attend class and absorb nothing. Sit in the seat. Feel the chair hold your body. Showing up with a blank mind still counts as showing up. Your physical presence maintains a thread of routine. That thread keeps you stitched to the world when everything else unravels.
