## HOOK v01
---

---
The alarm fires at 8 AM. Your eyelids stay shut. Not sleeping — just refusing. Your limbs press into the mattress like poured concrete. The ceiling holds nothing worth reaching for. Three missed calls blink on the lock screen. Your thumb does not move toward them. The day exists. You do not enter it.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your roommate leaves for class. The door clicks shut. Silence floods the dorm room like cold water. Your chest sits hollow, emptied out from the inside. The shower runs in the hall — someone else starting their day. Your body stays flat. Your jaw hangs slack against the pillow. Nothing pulls.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Dining hall opens at seven. Your meal plan charges whether you eat or not. The thought of standing makes your knees ache before they bend. Your stomach growls but your hands stay tucked under the blanket. Hunger lives in one room of your body. The rest of the rooms locked their doors.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Three lectures today. Organic chemistry, world lit, statistics. Your backpack sits packed by the door from last night's good intentions. Your feet touch the cold floor and stop there. The walk to campus takes eleven minutes. Your legs refuse the first step. Attendance points drain away like bathwater.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The group chat fills with plans. Saturday night, downtown, someone's birthday. Your phone lights up on the nightstand. Your eyes track the messages. Your fingers do not type. Your chest tightens around a hollow center. The screen dims. The screen goes black. You watch it happen.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Campus health center sits four blocks east. You looked up the hours last Tuesday. Your sneakers wait by the door. Your hand reaches the doorknob and stops. Palm flat against the metal, arm locked straight. The appointment form sits open on your laptop. Cursor blinking. Fingers still.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You open the textbook. Page 114, where you left the highlighter. Your eyes scan the same paragraph three times. Nothing sticks. The words move across the page like strangers on a sidewalk. Your jaw clenches. Your temples press inward. The book closes under the weight of your hand.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Night comes and your body finally moves. Two AM energy — restless, wired, pointless. You pace the dorm room in the dark. Your calves burn from twelve hours of stillness. The ceiling fan clicks. Your chest hums with static. Morning will flatten you again. You already feel it gathering.
---
