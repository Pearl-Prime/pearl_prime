## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Lie flat on your bed. Place one hand on your chest. Place the other on your stomach. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. The stomach hand rises. The chest hand stays still. Exhale through your mouth for six counts. Repeat five times. Notice which muscles unclench first. Your jaw. Your shoulders. Your fists. Track the order.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Set a timer for three minutes. Stand up. Walk to the nearest window. Place your palms flat against the glass. Feel the temperature — cold, cool, warm. Count five things visible from this spot. Name them out loud. Your voice activates your vagus nerve. The naming anchors your attention outside the fog. When the timer sounds, return to bed. That counted.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Open your phone. Find one person you trust. Type one honest sentence. Not "I'm fine." Not "all good." One sentence that tells the truth about today. "Today feels heavy." "I stayed in bed until noon." "I need to talk." Press send. Put the phone face down. The sentence exists outside your skull now. That changes the equation.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Fill a glass with cold water. Drink it in one continuous pour. Feel the cold travel down your throat, hit your stomach, spread outward. Your body registers the sensation before your mind interprets it. This is the body's reset — a simple input that demands a simple response. Refill the glass. Drink again. Two glasses. Mark it done.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit on the edge of your bed. Feet flat on the floor. Press your soles down — feel the cold tile or the carpet fibers. Grip the mattress edge with both hands. Squeeze for five seconds. Release. Squeeze again. Release. Five rounds. Your hands remember how to grip. Your feet remember the ground. The body finds its edges before the mind finds its direction.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Take a pen and a scrap of paper. Write three words that describe your body right now. Not your mood — your body. Heavy. Tight. Numb. Cold. Buzzing. Flat. Three words. Flip the paper over. Write one action your body can do in the next sixty seconds. Stand. Stretch. Drink. Walk. Do that one action. The body moves before the mind agrees. Let it lead.
