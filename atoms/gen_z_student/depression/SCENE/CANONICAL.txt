## SCENE v01
---

---
The alarm goes off for the third time. Your dorm room is dark behind the blackout curtain. Your body feels poured into the mattress like concrete. Your eyelids weigh more than your backpack. Your hand finds the phone and silences it. Press your palm flat on the sheet beside you. Feel the cotton against your skin. One hand moved. That hand is a start.

The lecture hall fills around you. Your notebook sits open to the same blank page for twenty minutes. Your pen rests between limp fingers. Words from the professor dissolve before they reach your brain. Press your feet into the floor under your desk. Feel the concrete through your shoes. Your body came to class. Your body carried you here through the fog.

Dishes stack in the shared apartment sink. You stare at them from the couch. Your arms feel like sandbags. Your roommate left hours ago. The quiet hums. Your back presses into the couch cushion. Feel the foam give beneath your weight. The dishes do not require you today. Your breathing requires you today. You are meeting that requirement.

The dining hall serves dinner. You stand in line holding a tray. Everything looks grey — the food, the walls, the fluorescent lights. Your stomach registers hunger but your brain registers nothing. Place the tray on the counter. Feel the metal edge. Scoop something onto the plate. Your hands perform the motion from memory. Memory is enough tonight.

Your friends text about weekend plans. The group chat scrolls with excitement. You watch the messages arrive from your bed. Your thumb does not move. Your chest holds a flat, heavy stillness. Press your fingertips together. Feel the ridges of your fingerprints meet. You are not required to feel excited. You are required to keep breathing. You are doing that.

The shower runs in the dorm bathroom. You sit on the toilet lid, still dressed, listening to the water. Getting in requires standing. Standing requires your legs to push. Your legs feel hollow. Grip the edge of the sink. Feel the porcelain cold against your fingers. Pull yourself upright. One motion. The water does not care how you got here. It hits your skin regardless.
