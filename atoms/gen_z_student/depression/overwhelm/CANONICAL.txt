
## RECOGNITION v01

Selin hasn't opened her email in four days. The number on the inbox badge is forty-seven. She knows some of those emails require action. She knows the longer she waits the worse it gets. She also knows that the inbox represents a world that requires her to function at a level she cannot currently sustain. Her bed is a safer distance from that world. She eats crackers from a box on her desk because the dining hall requires navigating stairs and people. She showers every other day. She keeps her roommate's schedule and arranges to be in bed before her roommate gets home. The management of not being seen is exhausting in its own way. The forty-seven emails become forty-nine. She turns her phone face down. Outside the window it is still light, which means it is earlier than she feels. The day is not over. She would prefer that it were.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Arjun sits in the advising office because his advisor emailed him twice and called his dormitory phone, which nobody does. He sits across from her and she lists his incomplete assignments and missed recitations. The list is eight items. He hears each item land in the room between them without being able to respond to any of them. He knows what the list means. He also knows he has not been able to act on anything in the list for three weeks. He has not been able to explain this to himself, let alone to an advisor. He says "I know, I've been behind." She asks what's going on. His throat tightens. He presses his palms flat on his thighs. The question is kind. He has no answer that fits in this office at this table. He says "It's been a hard stretch." She schedules a follow-up and suggests the counseling center. He takes the piece of paper she gives him. His hands are cold when he walks out into the afternoon.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/depression/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Remy does one thing. Just one. He has been lying in bed deciding which of the overdue tasks to start and paralyzed by the list for eleven days. Today he makes the list smaller. He picks the one with the nearest deadline and opens the file and writes a single paragraph. The paragraph is not good. It is a paragraph. He closes the laptop. His chest is still heavy. He still hasn't opened email in a week. The seventeen other things are still undone. He doesn't know what tomorrow looks like. He has written one paragraph today and his body is not celebrating this. But he also isn't where he was at noon, which was zero paragraphs. He sits on the edge of his bed and eats something, crackers and peanut butter, because he has not eaten today. The paragraph is still in the file. He will open it again tomorrow, or not. He has written it. That part is done.
