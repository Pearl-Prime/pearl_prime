
## RECOGNITION v01

Nadia watches herself smile when a classmate makes a joke. She watches herself agree when someone suggests coffee. She watches herself sit in the café with her hands around a warm mug and nod at the right moments and give back what the conversation asks for. From a distance—from the back row where she actually lives—she looks fine. The classmate leaves. Nadia sits alone and the watching self and the empty self are the same self again. She finishes her coffee. The café is bright and loud. She is glad the classmate thinks she is doing well. She is doing well, in the sense that she is here and dressed and has had a conversation. The gap between that and actually being okay is not something she can explain to someone who hasn't lived in it. She pays and walks out into the afternoon and the watching starts again.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kofi attends his own birthday dinner because his friends planned it and he didn't want to explain why not coming would be easier. He sits at the center of the table and watches himself receive the attention. His friends are warm. They ordered the cake he likes. He says thank you and means it in the technical sense and watches himself mean it. The double layer is exhausting in a way nobody at the table can see. He is here and not here, present and observing, performing "touched by this" while standing at a remove from his own life. He watches himself blow out the candles. He makes a wish as a reflex because the ritual requires it. He doesn't know what he wished for. Something about being able to feel this instead of just observe it. He eats cake and smiles and his friends take a photo. He looks happy in the photo. He looks exactly like someone who is at his own birthday and present for it.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_student/depression/watcher/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Yuki is in her first counseling session and the counselor asks "What would it feel like to not be watching?" and Yuki doesn't have an answer. She has been the watcher for so long that she doesn't know what the watched-self experiences directly. The question sits in the room. She tries to remember a moment when she was just in something rather than observing herself in it. She finds one: age fourteen, swimming in a lake at summer camp, fully underwater, no self-monitoring, just cold and dark and moving. She tells the counselor this. Her voice cracks on the word "lake" in a way she didn't predict. Her throat tightens. The watcher notes the crack, the tightening. Even here—in the act of identifying the watcher—the watcher is watching. She presses her fingertips together in her lap. The session has twenty minutes left. She is still mostly behind herself, still narrating. But the crack in her voice was real and it cost her something she was holding.
