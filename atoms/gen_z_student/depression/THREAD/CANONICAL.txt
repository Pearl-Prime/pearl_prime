## THREAD v01
---

---
The weight arrived gradually. First the mornings got harder. Then the assignments. Then the showers. Your body tracked the accumulation as heaviness in your limbs. Press your palms into your thighs. Feel the muscle underneath. The weight is real. It is not laziness wearing a costume.

You used to care about things that now register as static. Music. Movies. The meal you loved at the dining hall. Your taste buds still function. Your ears still hear. The signal gets intercepted before it reaches the part of your brain that assigns meaning. Touch your ear. Feel the cartilage. The hardware works. The software needs repair.

Depression tells you isolation protects you. It says people drain what little remains. Your shoulders curve inward around that story. Place your hands on your knees. Push your shoulders back. Isolation does not protect. It concentrates the fog into a smaller room with no windows.

The comparison feeds the depression. Everyone else seems to function. They walk to class with purpose. They laugh at lunch. You drag. Your ankles hold the weight of that comparison. Roll your feet in circles. Feel the joint move. Their exterior tells you nothing about their interior. You are comparing your backstage to their front row.

Sleep used to restore. Now it consumes. Twelve hours pass and the exhaustion remains. Your eyelids still droop. Your pillow holds the indent of hours spent horizontal. Place your hand on the pillow. Feel the impression. Over-sleeping is the body's attempt to heal with the only tool it trusts. It needs a wider toolkit. That search starts with one waking hour at a time.

The inner voice narrates every failure in present tense. "You are falling behind. You are losing people. You are wasting your tuition." Press your fingertips to your throat. Feel the pulse. That voice borrows the authority of truth. It speaks only prediction. Your pulse speaks fact. You are here. The rest is undecided.

## THREAD v02
---

---
The flatness has a floor. You have not found the floor yet because you have been falling. But the floor exists. Place your feet on the actual floor right now. Feel it. Real floors have real limits. Yours does too. The free-fall is not permanent.

Getting outside once today was enough. The campus is not asking for heroics. It is asking for one door, one breath, one step past the threshold. Touch the door frame when you leave. Feel the wood or metal. That threshold marks a real crossing. Your body crossed it. That counts.

Your sense of time is distorted right now. An hour feels like a day. A day feels like a month. Your phone clock is more accurate than your body clock at this moment. Check the actual time. Feel your phone in your hand. Ground your position in real time. Depression warps the clock. You can recalibrate.

The part of you reading this is not the most depressed part of you. Somewhere in your nervous system a function remained online that could direct your eyes to these words. Touch your eyes lightly. That function is a resource. Build from the parts that remained.

Someone in your circle would want to know. Not to fix it, just to know. Your instinct says telling them will be a burden. Press your lips together. Feel them. That instinct protects the other person at the cost of your isolation. The burden of not knowing is often heavier than the burden of knowing.

The assignments are still there. The messages are still unread. The life will be waiting when the system comes back online. Your only task right now is the minimum viable version of getting through today. Touch your sternum. Feel it rise and fall. That is today's task. Your body is already doing it.

## THREAD v03
---

---
Depression recruits your intelligence to argue against recovery. The executive function that organizes your coursework now organizes the case for staying in bed. The irony is exact. Your clearest thinking happens in service of your stillness. Touch your forehead. Feel the thinking there. Redirect it: what is one small thing that would constitute enough for today.

The word "help" is harder to form when you need it most. Your throat constricts. Your jaw locks. The sentence that starts with "I'm not doing well" stays in the mouth rather than the air. Swallow once. Feel the muscles contract and release. The sentence can leave in pieces. "I'm struggling" is enough to start.

Every semester produces students who quietly manage serious mental health crises while meeting their course requirements. You are not an outlier. You are in a very large, very silent group. Press your shoulder blades together once. Feel them meet. The silence is a collective habit, not an accurate picture of the room. You are surrounded by people who would understand.

The medication conversation is worth having and is not a defeat. Some brains have persistent chemistry that requires consistent chemical intervention to achieve a neurological baseline that other brains maintain by default. Touch your jaw. Feel it tighten. That tightening is the stigma. The stigma is not medical information. The doctor has the medical information.

Three things that continue to function during depression: your capacity to read, your capacity to feel texture, and your capacity to breathe. Place your hand on a textured surface right now. Feel the small ridges or grain. These three functions are available to you. They do not fix anything. They anchor you. Anchored is a position you can move from.

This semester is not your summary. Your GPA during this episode is not your intellectual ceiling. The student you are under this weight is not the only version of you that will ever enroll in this institution. Touch your collarbone. Feel the breadth of your own shoulders. More semesters follow this one. Different conditions produce different results.
