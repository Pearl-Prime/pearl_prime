## STORY v01
---

---
Maya's alarm fires at 8:07 AM. She stares at the ceiling. Her eyelids hang at half-mast, dry and gritty. Her arms lie flat against the sheets, heavy as waterlogged wood.
She counts the cracks in the plaster. Seven. Same as yesterday. Same as the day before.
Her roommate left for bio lab an hour ago. The room holds the shape of absence — unmade bed, open laptop frozen on a lecture slide, a protein bar torn halfway. Maya's stomach tightens but her hands stay pinned at her sides.
At noon she sits up. The motion takes everything. Her jaw hangs slack. She pulls on yesterday's hoodie and walks to the dining hall. Her sneakers drag the pavement. Each step lands like a decision made against her body's vote.
The dining hall roars with voices. Maya picks up a tray. Sets down a bagel. Sits at an empty four-top near the window. Her chest caves inward — a hollow drum. She tears the bagel into quarters. Eats one piece. Leaves the rest.
Back in her room, she opens the campus health portal. The cursor blinks on the intake form. Her fingers hover above the keyboard. She types: "I feel." She stares at the two words. Her dry eyes burn. She does not finish the sentence. She does not close the tab.

## STORY v02
---

---
Jordan sits in the back row of Intro to Psych. The professor draws a dopamine pathway on the whiteboard. Jordan's pen rests on the notebook, untouched. His shoulders climb toward his ears and lock there.
He knows the material. Studied it last semester when things still moved. Now the words bounce off his skull like rain off a windshield. His fingertips tingle — numb from gripping the desk edge too hard.
After class he walks to the library. Third floor, corner desk, same spot since freshman year. He opens his laptop. The screen glows. Pressure builds behind his eyes, a wall of static. He reads the same email four times. Assignment due Friday. Three days. His hands do not move toward the keyboard.
At 6 PM he packs up. Nothing accomplished. He counts the hours lost — another deposit into a debt that compounds daily.
In the dorm bathroom he stands at the sink. Cold water hits his face. He grips the porcelain edge. His knees lock straight, holding him upright through force, not will. The mirror shows dark circles carved under his eyes.
He dries his face. Walks back to his room. Sits on the bed. Picks up his phone. Puts it down. The night stretches out in front of him like an unlit hallway. He enters it without moving.

## STORY v03
---

---
Priya crosses the quad at 10 AM. Her feet drag through fallen leaves. Each step lands a half-second late, like her body runs on a delay. Students stream past — laughing, rushing, alive. She moves among them as a ghost walks through walls.
Her advisor's office sits at the end of the humanities corridor. Priya stands outside the door. Her throat tightens around words she rehearsed last night. She knocks. The door opens. Dr. Chen smiles. Priya sits down and her breath goes shallow, trapped in the top inch of her lungs.
"How's the semester going." Dr. Chen's voice sounds far away, filtered through water. Priya opens her mouth. Her throat closes. She says, "Fine." The word drops like a stone into a well.
She leaves the office five minutes later. Her eyelids hang heavy as she walks back across the quad. The sun hits her face. She feels nothing from it.
In her dorm bed at 2 PM she pulls the comforter over her head. The fabric creates a dark cave. Her body sinks into the mattress. Her breath evens out but sleep does not come. She lies in the gray space between waking and rest — a place that belongs to neither, that offers nothing, that holds her anyway.

## STORY v04
---

---
Dante sits in his car outside the campus gym. Engine off. Hands on the steering wheel. His gym bag sits in the passenger seat, packed three days ago. His arms hang dead from the shoulders, refusing the pull of the door handle.
He used to bench 185. Used to run the track at 6 AM. Now the parking lot feels like the end of the world's longest hallway.
After twenty minutes he turns the key. Drives back to the dorm. Parks. Walks upstairs. Each leg swings forward like wood — no spring, no rhythm. His chest lies flat behind his ribs, a tire with no air.
He sits on his bed. His temples ache from clenching his jaw all morning. He opens his phone. Scrolls past three texts from his mother. Types nothing. Sets the phone face down.
At 4 PM he searches "campus counseling walk-in hours." The website loads. Monday through Friday, 9 to 5. Tomorrow is Thursday. He screenshots the page. Puts the phone down again.
He lies back. The ceiling stares at him. He stares back. His body does not fight the mattress — it surrenders to it. Tomorrow holds a single task. Walk through that door. His legs do not believe they can carry him there. His thumb saved the screenshot anyway.

## STORY v05
---

---
Suki stands in the dorm kitchen at 7 AM. Her hands wrap around a mug of instant coffee. The ceramic burns her palms but her fingers stay cold underneath, like the heat cannot reach past the first layer of skin.
She pours the coffee down the sink. Her stomach clenches at the thought of food. She grabs her bag — textbook, laptop, charger, the supplies of a student who still attends class. Her spine holds her upright through habit alone.
At the bus stop she stands among nine other students. No one talks to her. She does not expect them to. The bus arrives. She climbs the steps. Sits in the back. Her neck droops forward, chin nearly touching her chest. The campus rolls past the window in a blur of brick and green.
She walks into the lecture hall twelve minutes late. The professor does not pause. Suki finds a seat in the last row. Opens her laptop. The screen glows white. Her hands rest on the keys. She types the date at the top of a blank document. Nothing follows.
Fifty minutes later the class empties. Suki sits in the vacant room. Fluorescent lights buzz overhead. She stares at the date on her screen — the only proof she showed up. Her spine curves forward. Her cold hands close the laptop. She stands. Walks out. The hallway swallows her footsteps.

## STORY v06
---

---
Ren locks the library bathroom stall and sits on the closed lid. His eyes burn — dry and overworked from staring at nothing. He presses his palms against his face. The pressure blocks the light. His neck holds rigid, a steel rod fused to his shoulders.
He stays in the stall for nine minutes. Counts each one on his phone. The tiles reflect fluorescent light in pale green squares. His scalp tingles like static electricity crawling across his skull.
He unlocks the stall. Washes his hands. The soap smells like fake lavender. He walks to the third-floor study lounge. Three students hunch over textbooks. Ren sits in the corner chair — the one facing the wall. His laptop opens to seventeen unread emails. Each subject line reads like a demand from another life.
He closes the laptop. Leans back. His lips go numb, pressed together in a tight line he does not remember forming. The clock on the wall ticks past 3 PM. He arrived at 10. Five hours. Nothing produced.
At 5 PM he walks to the residence hall lobby. The RA waves from the front desk. Ren lifts his hand — a gesture that drains him. He climbs two flights of stairs. Each landing asks his legs a question they barely answer.
In his room he stands at the window. Campus lights flicker on across the quad. His burning eyes watch them without blinking. The night arrives. He lets it.
