## COMPRESSION v01
---

---
The alarm fires. Your body vetoes the day before your eyes open. Limbs press into the mattress — concrete poured overnight. Your chest holds a hollow where motivation used to store itself. The ceiling offers nothing. Your jaw hangs slack. This is not laziness. This is a nervous system that pulled the emergency brake. The brake has a purpose. Learning to read its signal starts the next step. Feet on the floor. One act. Begin there.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---

---
Lectures stack up. Attendance points drain away. Your backpack sits packed by the door — evidence of a version of you that planned to show up. Your legs refuse the walk. Your eyes track the missed-class notifications without blinking. The gap between intention and action widens each morning. Bridge it with the smallest crossing: shoes on, door open, one step into the hallway. The hallway leads forward. Trust the architecture.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---

---
The dining hall roars with voices. Your tray holds a bagel torn into quarters. You eat one piece. Your stomach clenches but your hands withdraw. The empty seat across from you broadcasts a signal only you receive. Hunger lives in the body. Loneliness lives beside it. Both demand a response. Start with the body: finish the bagel. Then lift your eyes. One human face. One nod. Connection begins below language.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---

---
Your phone screen fills with group chats, birthday plans, weekend invitations. Your thumb does not type. Your chest tightens around a hollow center — the social self went offline and left no return date. Isolation feels like protection until it becomes the cage. One reply. Two words. "Sounds good" or "Not tonight" — either one breaks the silence. The silence is the enemy. Any signal beats no signal.
---

## COMPRESSION v05
---

---
Campus health center. Four blocks east. You looked up the hours. You screenshotted the page. Your hand reached the doorknob and stopped. Palm flat, arm locked, body voting no while the mind voted yes. The distance between knowing and going measures exactly one appointment. Call the number. Let the receptionist's voice carry the weight of the first step. Your vocal cords do the work. Your legs follow later.
---

## COMPRESSION v06
---

---
Night flips the script. Two AM energy floods a body that lay still for fourteen hours. You pace the dark room. Calves burn. Skin buzzes. The ceiling fan clicks a rhythm your thoughts chase but never catch. Morning will flatten you again — you feel it gathering like weather. Write down three words about the night body. Write down three words about the morning body. Hand that paper to someone trained to read it. You already wrote the message. Now deliver it.
