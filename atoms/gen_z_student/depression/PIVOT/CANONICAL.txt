## PIVOT v01
---

---
Your body keeps score even when your mind checks out. That heaviness in your limbs carries data. It speaks a language you can learn to read — not fix, not fight, just read.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The flat morning does not make you broken. It makes you someone whose nervous system pulled the emergency brake. The brake exists for a reason. Understanding the reason starts the next chapter.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Staring at the ceiling counts as surviving. Surviving counts as a foundation. Foundations hold weight. The next small act — feet on the floor, water on your face — builds from here.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
Missing the lecture does not erase you from the roster. Skipping the dining hall does not cancel your hunger. Your body still sends signals through the fog. Those signals deserve an answer, even a quiet one.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
The campus health center sits four blocks away. Four blocks. Not a journey — a walk. Your legs carried you farther on worse days. The intake form asks simple questions. Your hand can hold a pen.
---

## PIVOT v06
---

---
Depression lies about time. It says nothing changes. Your chest knows different — it tightened, then loosened, then tightened again. Movement lives inside the stillness. Track it. Write it down. Proof accumulates.
---

## PIVOT v07
---

---
You do not owe the world a performance of wellness. You owe your body one honest response to its loudest signal. Heavy limbs say rest. Hollow chest says connect. Dry eyes say the screen can wait.
---

## PIVOT v08
---

---
The semester does not require your perfection. It requires your presence — partial, heavy, dragging. Show up at forty percent. Forty percent beats zero. Your GPA can recover. First, your body needs to know someone listens.
---
