

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Heaviness is not weakness."
---
Feel what's in your body right now. The weight. The way things that used to matter don't quite reach you anymore. The watching. You're watching your life happen from somewhere inside yourself, like you're observing it through thick glass. Like the thing that usually animates you has gone somewhere else.
This isn't sadness. Sadness is a feeling that moves through. This is something heavier. It's a shutdown. Your nervous system decided that the world was too much and it started protecting you by making you numb. By slowing things down. By putting distance between you and everything that would normally hurt.
Your body isn't broken. It's trying to keep you alive by making you less available to pain. The problem is that when your system numbs the pain, it numbs everything else too. The joy. The connection. The aliveness that you're supposed to feel at this age.
Heaviness is not weakness.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're paying in presence, in realness."
---
Nobody can see the cost. You're showing up. You're getting through. You're doing the required things. And inside, you're completely absent. The parts of you that make you feel alive—they've gone quiet. The interests that used to matter. The excitement about the future. The ability to just exist without analyzing whether you should keep going.
The cost isn't measured in collapsed days or obvious crisis. It's measured in the realness you're losing. The presence. The thing that makes you feel like you're actually inhabiting your own life instead of just watching it from somewhere far away inside yourself.
You're paying in presence, in realness.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Heaviness shifts, not all at once."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
What you feel now is not permanent geology. It will shift. Not because you'll suddenly be happy or because this period will feel different when you look back. But because your nervous system will eventually find a way to come back online. Because you'll get older and your brain will continue developing. Because the things that feel unbearable now will transform into something you survived.
You're in the waiting. The part where nothing changes and you don't believe anything ever will. But change doesn't announce itself. It happens quietly, in the space between one day and the next. You'll look back six months from now and realize that something shifted. Not because the heaviness went away. But because you're still here. Because your system found a way to hold the weight without crushing entirely.
Right now, your job isn't to fix this. It's to stay. To let people in if you can. To reach for help even when it feels pointless. To believe that the thing that feels like it will kill you is actually something your body knows how to survive.
This heaviness moves, even when it doesn't feel like it.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if numbness is trying to protect you."
---
What if the heaviness isn't a sign that something is wrong with you. What if it's your system's best attempt to keep you standing while everything feels unbearable. What if the shutdown is actually a kind of wisdom—your body saying that you don't have to feel everything all at once.
What if you didn't have to be fixed right now. What if you could just be in the heaviness and let your system do what it needs to do to keep you alive until you're ready to feel again.
What if numbness is trying to protect you.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Silence about it costs you more."
---
The part that kills you isn't the heaviness. It's the distance you create by not telling anyone. The way you keep it quiet. The lie that everything is fine when it's not. Every time you hide it, you move further away from the possibility of being seen.
The heaviness is hard enough. But you don't have to carry it completely alone. The cost of pretending is higher than the cost of being honest about what's happening in your body.
Silence about it costs you more.
