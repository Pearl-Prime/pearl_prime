## INTEGRATION v01
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one small moment — sitting down at the dinner table, starting a conversation with a friend, sitting down to play a game — and before you dive in, notice one real thing about it: a sound, a color, a face. Do this every single time you can remember to, not just when it feels important. It will feel small and maybe pointless some of the time. Do it anyway, because those are exactly the moments you're most likely to be somewhere else without noticing.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
---
Tomorrow, whenever you catch your mind wandering away from something — a conversation, your homework, a game — don't feel bad about it. Just notice it, take one breath, and bring your attention back to whatever's actually in front of you. You might do this many times in one day. That's not a sign something's wrong. That's what real practice looks like.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
---
Tomorrow, if a worry or an upsetting thought keeps popping back into your head while you're trying to do something else, don't try to force it away. Just tell yourself, "that's real, I'll think about it later," and bring your attention back to what you're doing right now. Expect to do this more than once. That's completely normal.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
---
Tomorrow, during your hardest or most boring subject in school, try noticing one small detail about the room or the lesson every time you catch your mind drifting — the color of the marker, a word on the board. Use it as your way back, over and over, without judging how many times you need to use it.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
---
Tomorrow, at one meal, put down whatever you're holding — a fork, a phone, a toy — for the first few seconds of a conversation with whoever's there, and actually look at them while they talk. Notice if it feels different from how you usually listen. You don't have to do this for the whole meal, just the beginning.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
---
Tomorrow, the next time someone tugs your sleeve or calls your name and you realize you weren't really there, don't just apologize quickly and move on. Actually stop, look at them, and give them one real moment of your attention before continuing whatever you were doing.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
---
Tomorrow, during a game or activity you enjoy, notice if you catch yourself doing it on autopilot, not really there even though you're clearly playing. When you notice, take one breath and actually look at what's in front of you — the board, the ball, the screen — before your next move.
---

## INTEGRATION v08
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one activity you do every day without thinking — brushing your teeth, walking somewhere, putting on your shoes — and try to notice one real thing about it while you do it: a taste, a sound, a feeling. See if you remember doing it afterward, compared to days when you don't try this.
---

## INTEGRATION v09
---
---
Tomorrow, if you're doing two things at once — homework and talking, a game and a conversation — try picking just one to actually focus on for a moment, and notice if you understand or remember it better than when you were splitting your attention.
---

## INTEGRATION v10
---
---
Tomorrow, instead of trying to feel calm all day, just try to notice, a few times, whether your attention is actually on what's happening right now or somewhere else. If it's somewhere else, gently bring it back, even if you're still feeling worried or annoyed about something.
---

## INTEGRATION v11
---
---
Tomorrow, count how many times you notice your mind wandering and bring it back, just for fun, without trying to make the number lower. A high number isn't bad — it just means you're actually noticing, which is the whole point.
---

## INTEGRATION v12
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one small phrase to say to yourself — like "okay, here" — and use it every time you start something new: a class, a conversation, a game. It will feel a little silly at first. Use it anyway, and see if it gets easier to remember by the end of the day.
---
