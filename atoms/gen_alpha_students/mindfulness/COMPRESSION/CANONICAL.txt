## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Being present isn't staying focused the whole time — it's a return you make over and over, and the returning is the actual skill. Every mind wanders toward things that feel unfinished or more interesting — normal, constant, not a flaw. The cost isn't a disaster; it's missing the small, real moments of a day without noticing. The fix is a cheap, repeatable cue — a breath, a word, one noticed detail — used at the start of each new moment. Repetition makes the return faster and easier, the same way any practiced skill improves. Measure your day by returns made, not by how calm you felt.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Doing two things at once isn't really doing both — it's switching fast between them and doing neither one fully, which is why you can answer someone and immediately forget what they said. The fix is practicing giving one thing your full attention at a time, even briefly, and attaching a small "here now" cue to things you already do — sitting down, starting a conversation — so it needs no extra decision-making in the moment.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
A looping worry doesn't go away by pushing it away — pushing it away is its own hidden effort that makes it louder. The fix is parking it: name it as real, give it a specific later time, and bring your attention back to what's actually in front of you. Expect to repeat this many times; that repetition is the practice working, not a sign of failure.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Half-presence — being physically there but mentally somewhere else — is invisible in the moment and obvious later, when you realize you can't remember a conversation or a chapter you were "reading." It isn't fixed by trying to focus harder, which is too vague. It's fixed by noticing one real detail at the start of each new moment, so your attention actually lands there instead of drifting in unnoticed.
---
