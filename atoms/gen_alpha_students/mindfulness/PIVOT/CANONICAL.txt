## PIVOT v01
---
---
You think you have to stay focused all day to be present. Try this instead: aim for a fast return every time you notice you've drifted. Lots of quick returns beat one long unnoticed drift.
---

## PIVOT v02
---
---
You think the goal is to stop your mind from wandering. Try this instead: let it wander, notice it sooner, and bring it back. The wandering was never the real problem.
---

## PIVOT v03
---
---
You think doing two things at once makes you faster. Try this instead: doing one thing fully, then the next, usually works better than splitting your attention between both.
---

## PIVOT v04
---
---
You think you need to feel calm to be present. Try this instead: you can be worried or excited and still be present. Being present just means your attention is actually here.
---

## PIVOT v05
---
---
You think a worry has to be solved before you can pay attention to anything else. Try this instead: tell yourself you'll think about it later, then bring your attention back to right now.
---

## PIVOT v06
---
---
You think drifting off during class means something's wrong with you. Try this instead: everyone's mind wanders. The only thing that matters is noticing and coming back.
---

## PIVOT v07
---
---
You think being present takes extra time you don't have. Try this instead: attach one second of noticing to something you already do, like sitting down at your desk.
---

## PIVOT v08
---
---
You think nodding along means you were actually listening. Try this instead: notice if your mind was actually there. If not, ask the person to repeat it — that's better than pretending.
---

## PIVOT v09
---
---
You think missing part of your favorite show means you don't like it anymore. Try this instead: your attention just drifted. Notice it, bring it back, and enjoy the rest.
---

## PIVOT v10
---
---
You think being scattered and being busy are the same thing. Try this instead: notice the difference — busy means a lot to do, scattered means doing it all halfway. Do one thing at a time.
---

## PIVOT v11
---
---
You think you should already be good at paying attention all the time. Try this instead: it's a skill you build with practice, one small return at a time.
---

## PIVOT v12
---
---
You think the wandering itself is the failure. Try this instead: wandering is normal. The only real miss is never noticing you left.
---
