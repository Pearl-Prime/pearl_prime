

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm watching my parents worry about money. I see the stress it creates in my household. And I'm absorbing that stress into my own nervous system. Even though I'm not the one responsible for finances. I'm developing financial anxiety before I've even started earning money. The mechanism is called intergenerational financial anxiety transmission.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that I'm thinking about college costs already. And the debt that might come with it. And how that debt might shape my life choices. I haven't even graduated high school and I'm already managing anxiety about money I don't have yet. The mechanism is anticipatory debt anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of financial anxiety that comes from comparing my family's situation to my peers' situations. Some of my friends' families seem to have unlimited resources. And my family has to be more careful. And I'm absorbing the message that there's something wrong with our situation. The mechanism is called comparative financial shame.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm aware that my career options might be limited by financial constraints. That I might not be able to pursue the path I want because of cost. That I might have to choose a more profitable option instead. And this awareness creates a kind of trapped feeling. The mechanism is called constraint-driven anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is that I don't fully understand how money works. But I understand enough to know that it's stressed my family. So there's this anxiety about money that's based partially in reality and partially in my own limited understanding. The mechanism is informed anxiety mixed with knowledge gaps.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my financial anxiety that's about autonomy. I want to not depend on my parents. To be able to support myself. But I'm also aware of how expensive the world is. And how difficult it is to achieve independence. The mechanism is autonomy-independence anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that I'm starting to think about my earning potential already. What jobs pay well. What skills are valuable. I'm trying to position myself for financial security before I've figured out what I actually want to do. The mechanism is called preemptive financial optimization.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I'm realizing is that financial anxiety is affecting my wellbeing in ways I don't fully understand. I'm more stressed. More competitive. Less able to just be a kid. The mechanism is financial stress seeping into all domains of life.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about the knowledge that my future financial security is not guaranteed. That I can't rely on my family. That I have to earn it myself. And I don't know if I'll be good enough at earning. The mechanism is called early financial self-reliance anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that my financial anxiety sometimes comes out as judgment toward others. Toward my parents for not having more. Toward my richer peers for being cavalier about money. The anxiety is finding outlets. The mechanism is anxiety-driven judgment.
---
