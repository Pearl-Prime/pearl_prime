## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/compassion_fatigue/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Nolan identifies the shame's social architecture. It does not come from inside alone. It is built by the audience — real or imagined. Shame requires witnesses. Without witnesses, a mistake is just a mistake. With witnesses, a mistake is a judgment. The mechanism: Nolan's shame is not about what she did wrong. It is about who saw. The seeing is the mechanism. Her face warms. The shame does not operate in private. It requires the gaze. In her room, alone, the wrong answer is nothing. In the classroom, the wrong answer is everything.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/compassion_fatigue/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Amara sees the shame's recursive trap: the shame makes her hide, and the hiding confirms the shame. She does not raise her hand because she might be wrong. Not raising her hand means she does not get corrected. Not getting corrected means she does not learn. Not learning means she falls behind. Falling behind creates more shame. The mechanism is a closed loop: shame creates hiding, hiding creates failure, failure creates shame. Each element produces the next. Amara's chest tightens. The loop has no exit from inside. The exit is outside: raising the hand. Breaking the first link.
