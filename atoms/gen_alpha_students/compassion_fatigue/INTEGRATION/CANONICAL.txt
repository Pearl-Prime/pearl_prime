

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Emptiness after caring is real."
---
Feel what's in your body right now. The flatness. The way you helped a friend through something hard and now there's nothing left of you. The numbness that comes after you've held space for someone else's crisis while your own body sits below the surface of your attention.
You're the person people come to. You listen. You hold it. You don't tell anyone how much it costs. And the cost is real. Your nervous system doesn't differentiate between stress that's yours and stress you're carrying for someone else. It processes both as a threat you need to survive. Which means every time you absorb someone else's pain, your body treats it like you're the one in danger.
The emptiness after isn't weakness. It's the honest aftermath of emotional labor. Your system gave what it had to give. Now there's nothing left to give to yourself.
Emptiness after caring is real.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're tracking pain that isn't on any chart."
---
You know who's struggling. Not because they told you directly, but because you read between the lines. The friend who's quieter than usual. The one whose posts started looking different. The person who used to laugh and doesn't anymore. You see it. You track it. You worry about it. You carry it.
This is the unseen cost of being the person people trust. You accumulate awareness of suffering. You hold other people's private crises. You become responsible for noticing, even though no one asked you to be. Even though you're still figuring out your own stuff.
You're tracking pain that isn't on any chart. You're carrying weight that nobody sees because you carry it so quietly.
You're tracking pain that isn't on any chart.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Care doesn't require your life."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
This season of your life will pass. The friends who need you most right now won't always need you in exactly this way. The crises that feel enormous will resolve or transform. You'll learn that showing up doesn't require giving everything. You'll discover the difference between care and sacrifice.
You're young and you care deeply. That's beautiful. But it doesn't mean you get to disappear into other people's healing. The most important person you'll ever take care of is the one you're living in right now—you. And you get to decide how much of your energy goes out and how much stays home.
Years from now, you'll be glad you learned this early: compassion that costs you your own survival isn't compassion. It's drowning. Real care has a boundary. Real presence happens when you've got enough of yourself left to actually be there.
Care doesn't require your life.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if helping means staying intact."
---
What if you could care about someone and not absorb their pain. What if showing up meant being present without taking on their crisis as your own responsibility. What if being there for someone actually requires that you stay separate from them, that you have clear edges between your nervous system and theirs.
What if the best thing you could do for the people you care about is refuse to disappear into their suffering. Because then you'd actually be able to see them clearly. You'd have energy to listen. You'd be more useful, not less, because you're not drowning.
What if helping means staying intact.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Someone needs you alive, not martyred."
---
The people who love you don't want you to disappear into their problems. They need you whole. They need you present. They need you to have your own life, your own energy, your own reasons to exist that have nothing to do with rescuing them. That's not selfish. That's the only way you can actually help.
Someone needs you alive, not martyred.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED

weight: interrupt_landing
carry_line: "Your body holds its own threshold."
---
Notice where the boundary is. The moment when listening becomes absorbing. When care becomes drowning. When showing up turns into disappearing. Your body knows the difference. It signals when you've given too much. The flatness. The heaviness. The nothing left.
Your body holds its own threshold.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "You pay twice: caring, then recovering."
---
Every crisis you absorb costs you twice. Once when you carry it. Again when you try to recover from it. The emotional weight. The sleepless nights. The part of you that stays vigilant because someone you care about is suffering. Then the rebuilding. The slow return to baseline. The exhaustion of that cycle.
You pay twice: caring, then recovering.
