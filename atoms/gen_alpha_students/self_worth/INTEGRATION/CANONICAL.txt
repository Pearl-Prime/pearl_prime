

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body knows when you're being watched."
---
You feel it in your shoulders. The way you stand differently depending on who's looking. The version of yourself in the group chat that's slightly edited. The laugh you do at lunch that's a little louder than the one that happens when you're alone. You're always performing. Always aware of how you're being perceived. Always checking the feedback from the algorithm and the eyes around you.
Your body adjusts in real time to what it thinks people want to see. Your nervous system is constantly reading the room, translating feedback from likes and follows and comments into information about your worth. And your body responds. It tightens. It performs. It becomes whatever shape will get validation.
The part that's dangerous: you're learning to know yourself through external reflection instead of through your own internal knowing. You're getting trained to trust the algorithm's assessment more than your own sense of what's real.
Your body knows when you're being watched.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're outsourcing your own knowing."
---
Every time you post something and check how many people engaged. Every time you adjust your outfit based on who might see it. Every time you calculate whether something is worth saying based on whether it will get likes. You're giving your sense of what matters to platforms that are literally designed to exploit your attention.
Your worth isn't a number. But you're living in a system that has taught you to measure yourself that way. That has trained you that your value goes up and down based on performance. That has made you believe that who you are needs external validation to be real.
The cost is the part of you that never gets to exist because it's not aesthetically interesting. The thoughts you don't share because they're not performative. The authentic self that's sitting underneath all the curation, probably afraid and definitely tired.
You're outsourcing your own knowing.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Your worth is not a moving target."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Right now, your worth feels very connected to performance. To what people see. To metrics. And that makes sense given the world you live in. But somewhere inside you is something that has value independent of any of that. Something that mattered before anyone was looking. Something that will still matter when the phone is off.
You have time to figure out who you are when nobody's watching. Time to discover that the parts of you that don't perform well online might actually be the most interesting parts. Time to learn that belonging doesn't mean performing. It means being seen by people who like the real thing.
Your worth will feel different in five years. Not because you'll suddenly be confident. But because you'll have lived long enough to see that the stakes aren't as high as they feel now. That the people worth knowing care about who you actually are, not the curated version. That the version of yourself you show online is just a small fraction of what's real in you.
Your worth is not a moving target.
