## TURNING_POINT v06

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nolan stands in the homework pile feeling the pattern of overwhelm pressing against the inside of the chest. The burnout has been building for weeks. Hands cold. Jaw locked. The old story — that this pattern is permanent, that it defines everything — starts to crack. Nolan notices the crack. Does not fill it. Does not repair the old story. Just stands in the homework pile and watches the frame shift. The pattern is still running. But the frame around it is no longer solid. Something moved. Not much. Enough.

## TURNING_POINT v07
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Amara stares at his schedule and counts the free hours in his week: four. Total. Including weekends. He is twelve years old with four free hours per week. His chest compresses. The frame ruptures when he shows his mother the calendar. Her face changes. She did not know. She signed him up for each activity individually. Individually, each was reasonable. Together, they are a sentence. His mother crosses out two activities without asking which ones. The four free hours become twelve. Twelve is not luxury. It is childhood.

## EMBODIMENT v06
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/embodiment/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Zadie looks at the assignment board and feels the overwhelm press against his ribs. Five assignments. Three days. He does not freeze. He picks the shortest one. Completes it in twenty minutes. The board now shows four assignments. The overwhelm lost one item. Zadie picks the next shortest. The practice is not genius. It is subtraction. Each completed assignment is the overwhelm getting smaller. He works the small ones first. The big one is left for last. By then, the overwhelm has lost its reinforcements.

## EMBODIMENT v07

BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Kai faces the biggest assignment of the year. Due in a week. The overwhelm is real: research, outline, draft, revision, presentation. He does not panic. He does not spiral. He opens a blank document and writes one sentence. The sentence is not good. It does not need to be good. It needs to exist. The blank page was the overwhelm's stronghold. The single sentence conquered the stronghold. Kai writes a second sentence. The overwhelm lost the blank page. It still has the deadline. But it lost its best weapon.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v06
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v06.txt
BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Lina identifies the overwhelm's multiplier: context switching. It is not the volume of assignments. It is switching between them. Math brain to reading brain to science brain to social brain. Each switch costs energy. The cost is invisible but cumulative. By afternoon, Lina is not tired from work. He is tired from switching. His chest tightens. The mechanism: the schedule is designed for variety. His brain is designed for depth. The mismatch between the schedule and the brain is the overwhelm.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v07
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/burnout/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v07.txt
BAND: 5
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 5
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Deshawn sees the overwhelm's root: the system was not designed for him. The schedule assumes every student processes at the same speed, switches context at the same speed, and recovers at the same speed. Deshawn does not. His processing speed is different. His context-switch cost is higher. His recovery takes longer. The mechanism: the overwhelm is a mismatch between the system's assumptions and Deshawn's reality. He is not failing the system. The system is failing to accommodate his operating specs. His chest loosens slightly. The overwhelm is structural, not personal.
---
