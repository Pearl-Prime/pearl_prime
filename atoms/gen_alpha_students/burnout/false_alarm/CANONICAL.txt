BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Zoe's body is in crisis about a work email. The email is probably fine. It's marked not urgent. But her nervous system has treated it like a five-alarm fire. Her chest is tight. Her heart is fast. She's reading and rereading the email looking for the threat. There's no threat. The email is about schedule changes. But her body is responding to it like there is. The false alarm is exhausting. She's tired from fighting fires that don't exist. Her hands shake as she reads something benign.

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3

Eli realizes that the false alarms are happening but the disasters aren't. He starts keeping a list. Times he was convinced something bad happened. Times the actual outcome was fine or neutral. ## TURNING_POINT v03

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Mika talks to her therapist about the false alarms. The therapist asks: are you actually in danger right now? Mika says: no. The therapist asks: is your body telling you that you're in danger? Mika says: yes. The distinction is important. The body's alarm system is malfunctioning. It's not because there's actually danger. It's because the system is broken. Knowing it's a malfunction helps. She doesn't have to believe the alarm.
