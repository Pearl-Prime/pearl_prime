

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: B

Your nervous system fires at full intensity before you speak, raise your hand, or try something new. The fear is real. The mechanism treats the possibility of embarrassment the same way it treats actual danger. What you come to understand is that courage is not the absence of fear. Courage is fear alongside action. The mechanism has not learned that both things can be true at the same time. You can be terrified and you can be doing it anyway. You can raise your hand with a shaking voice. You can speak up with a racing heart. You can try despite the terror that something might go wrong. The fear and the courage are not opposites. They are companions. The mechanism might never develop the certainty that it thinks it needs. But the action can still happen.
