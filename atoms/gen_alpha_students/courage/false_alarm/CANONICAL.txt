COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Maya thinks about trying out for the school play. The thought arrives with a body alarm—stomach drops, chest tightens, face warms. The alarm is the same alarm that fires when she's in actual danger: nearly falling, a loud crash, a car swerving. The alarm doesn't know the difference between a stage and a swerving car. Both produce the same hormonal cascade. The play is in three weeks. The audition is voluntary. Nobody is in danger. Maya's body is responding to the idea of performing as though performing is a survival threat. The survival threat response is designed for survival threats. A school play is not one.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/courage/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya notices the alarm fires exclusively on optional opportunities. Required things—tests, class presentations, homework—produce regular anxiety but not the full alarm. Optional things—tryouts, auditions, submissions—produce the full alarm. The difference is exit availability. Required things have no exit: she has to take the test. Optional things have an exit: she can choose not to audition. The alarm fires on the exit, not the activity. The alarm is specifically designed to make her choose the exit. Required activities bypass the alarm because the exit doesn't exist. The alarm is a quitting mechanism, not a danger mechanism.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/courage/false_alarm/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Zoe identifies the alarm's timing strategy. The alarm is loudest at the decision point—the moment of signing up, submitting, or committing. After the commitment is made, the alarm quiets significantly. The alarm is a gate, not a companion. It guards the entrance to new things and becomes largely irrelevant once the entrance is passed. The gate's effectiveness depends on making the entrance feel dangerous enough to prevent passing through. Once through, the danger the alarm projected is rarely present. The alarm invests everything in prevention. The alarm invests nothing in the aftermath because the aftermath usually contradicts the prevention.

---
Tyler discovers the alarm conflates judgment with annihilation. The swim team tryout involves being watched. Being watched produces the alarm's threat response. But the threat response is calibrated for physical danger—the hormones, the heart rate, the tunnel vision are designed for escaping predators. There are no predators at swim tryouts. There are coaches with clipboards and kids in swim caps. The alarm maps the ancient response onto the modern situation. The mapping is biologically efficient and contextually absurd. His body prepares to flee a swim tryout the way his ancestors' bodies prepared to flee predators. The swim tryout and the predator share nothing except the alarm's response.
