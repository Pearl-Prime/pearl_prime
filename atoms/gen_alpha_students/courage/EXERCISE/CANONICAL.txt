

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Plant your feet firmly on the ground. Feel the solid contact. Push down through your heels. Feel the earth push back. This is the foundation of courage. Your feet are not moving. Stand here.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold for four. Breathe out through your mouth for six counts. The longer out-breath activates your brave nervous system. Repeat five times. You are building courage in your breath.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Feel your chest. Put your hand on your heart. Feel it beat. Your heart is working. Your heart is alive. Courage is a heart that keeps beating when scared. Feel yours beat.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Clench your fists. Squeeze hard. Feel the power in your hands. Count to four. Release. Feel the strength release. Make fists again. This is courage. Gathering power. Releasing it purposefully.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Say one true sentence out loud right now. Something you have wanted to say. Say it. Hear it. Your voice works. Your voice is real. That sentence was brave.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Feel your spine. Lengthen it. Sit up tall. Courage stands tall. Slouching is doubt. Feel your full height. Imagine a string pulling you up from the crown of your head. Stand there.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Roll your shoulders back five times. Roll. Roll. Roll. Open your chest forward. You are not turning away. You are facing what is ahead. Open chest means facing it.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Press both palms together in front of your chest. Press hard. Count to three. Feel the power in your body. Release. You can make yourself stronger. You just did.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Look at your own hands. These are the hands of a person who does hard things. These hands can write. These hands can raise. These hands can act. Look at them. Believe them.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Make eye contact with someone. Even if imaginary. Do not look away first. Look steady. This is the face of someone brave. Keep that face. Do not flinch.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Take one deliberate step forward if you can. Feel yourself move into space. That movement is courage. You are not staying back. You are moving forward. Repeat three times. Step. Step. Step.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Shake your whole body for five seconds. Shake like you are cold. Shake hard. Let the shake move through you. Shakiness is okay. You can feel scared and move anyway.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Hum at a low pitch loudly. Make your voice heard. Hum for five seconds. Stop. Hum again. Your voice is stronger than you think. Your voice deserves space. Take that space.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Feel your jaw. Unclench it. Open your mouth. Courage lives in an open jaw. A clenched jaw is holding back. Release it. Let whatever words are there come out.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Press your feet into the ground and stand. Feel all your weight. You are substantial. You are real. You are not floating away. You are taking up space. You are here.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Tense your legs hard. Push your heels down. Count to three. Release. Your legs hold you steady. Your legs say I am staying. I am not running away.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Feel your whole body. From your head to your toes. Feel all of you. You are one complete person. That person is brave enough. That person is enough.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Cross your arms over your chest. Hug yourself. Count to four. Release. That hug is courage. You are holding yourself. You are supporting yourself. You do not need permission.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Feel your breath. Follow it in and out. Your breath is steady. Your nervous system is steady. Even if you are scared, your breath keeps going. Count five breaths. Trust that steady rhythm.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Look in the mirror or imagine your face. This is someone brave. Tell yourself one thing you believe about yourself. Say it. You just told the truth to your own face.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Rub your hands together fast for five seconds. Feel the heat. Press that warmth on your face. Warmth is aliveness. Aliveness is courage. You are warm. You are alive. You are here.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Feel where your bottom touches the seat. Feel grounded. Grounded people can speak. Floating people cannot. Press down. Feel heavy. Feel stable. Speak from that heaviness.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Tap a rhythm on your lap or desk. Tap. Tap. Tap. Steady rhythm. Keep the rhythm going. Your body keeping time is steady. You are steady. Keep tapping. Stay steady.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Feel your back. Imagine it is straight and strong. Your back supports your spine. Your spine holds your whole self. Your back has your back. Trust it.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Say the word courage out loud. Hear yourself say it. Your voice can say brave things. Repeat it three times. Courage. Courage. Courage. You just practiced.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Feel your neck. This is where your head connects to your body. Feel them together. Your thoughts and your body working together. That integration is courage. You are not all head. You are whole.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Wiggle your toes inside your shoes. Feel your small movements. Small movements are still movements. You do not have to be loud to be brave. Wiggle counts. Movement counts.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Touch something warm near you. Your own skin. A warm cup. Feel the warmth. Warm things are alive. You are alive. Alive things can be brave.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Breathe in and imagine filling your whole body with strength. Breathe out and imagine releasing any doubt. Repeat five times. Fill with strength. Release doubt. You are filling yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Feel your hands. Make fists. You are ready. Open your hands. You are releasing ready. Repeat five times. Ready. Open. Ready. Open. You can keep doing the brave thing. Your hands know how.
---
