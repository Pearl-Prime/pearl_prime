Maya has an idea for the science fair. The idea is good—she knows it's good because she researched it and the research supports it. She opens her mouth to tell the teacher and the shame arrives: who are you to have a science fair idea, the smart kids do science fair, you're not a science-fair kid. The "not a science-fair kid" is an identity the shame assigns. The identity has no evidence. Maya gets As in science. Maya reads science books for fun. The identity ignores the evidence and operates on a feeling: the feeling that certain opportunities belong to certain kids and Maya is not one of them. The feeling is the shame's ceiling.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya identifies the shame's identity-ceiling mechanism. The shame doesn't say she can't do science. The shame says she's not a science-fair kid. The distinction is between ability and identity. She has the ability. The shame disputes the identity. The identity ceiling operates above the ability level—she can do the work but can't claim the role. The ceiling keeps her in the doing but out of the being. She does science without being a science person. She does well without being a good student. The doing is permitted. The being is blocked. The ceiling is invisible from the outside—teachers see a capable student. From the inside, Maya sees a capable student who doesn't belong in capable spaces.

Jaden notices the shame only activates on public courage. He draws every day—no shame. He tries new techniques—no shame. He plans ambitious projects—no shame. The shame fires only when the work would be seen by people who might evaluate it. The shame is a visibility gate. Private ambition passes through unchecked. Public ambition triggers the gate. Zoe discovers the shame's private-versus-real distinction collapses under examination. She writes in her journal: essays, stories, observations. She writes for school: essays, stories, responses. MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Tyler maps where the shame learned its ceiling. Elementary school: no ceiling. He joined everything, tried everything, performed in talent shows, ran for class president. Middle school: the ceiling appeared. The ceiling correlates with social awareness—the moment Tyler became aware of how others might perceive him, the shame installed itself as a filter between desire and action. The filter says: before you act, check if you're the kind of person who does this thing. The checking is the shame's primary function. The checking didn't exist before social awareness. Social awareness brought the shame. The shame brought the ceiling. The ceiling prevents the same kid who ran for president from joining the debate team.
