

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited

I have noticed that student grief is often invisible because you are supposed to keep going. Your grandparent dies and you still have to go to school. Your friend moves away and you still have to show up for class. Your future changes and you still have to do your homework. The world does not pause while you grieve.

There is a mechanism called stoic continuation, and what it does is allow you to function while you are broken. The mechanism knows that grief is happening. The mechanism knows that loss has occurred. But the mechanism keeps moving. The mechanism keeps doing.

What I have come to understand is that the stoic continuation is both a gift and a curse. The gift is that you are able to function. The curse is that functioning while grieving is profoundly lonely. No one sees that you are broken because you are still showing up.

What I have come to think about is the way that the visible performance of okay-ness prevents visible grief. The grief becomes private. The grief becomes silent. The grief becomes something that happens in the spaces between classes, in the quiet moments, in the night when you are alone.

The mechanism is allowing you to survive. But the survival is incomplete. The mechanism is allowing you to function, but the functioning is hollow.

You are here, but you are not fully here. You are present, but you are also absent. The mechanism is keeping you moving while your insides are still.

But the mechanism is being rushed. The mechanism is being asked to return to normal. The mechanism is being asked to be okay when the mechanism is not okay. The mechanism is being asked to perform normalcy while the mechanism is grieving.

What I have come to think about is the way that the rushing is creating a kind of unprocessed grief. The mechanism is not having the time to actually grieve. The mechanism is pushing the grief down so that it can function. The mechanism is storing the grief.

And the stored grief does not disappear. The stored grief is there, underneath the functioning, creating a heaviness that does not have a name.
