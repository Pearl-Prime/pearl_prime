

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit down in a safe place right now. You do not have to hide this sadness. Sit. Let your body be heavy. Sadness is heavy. That is okay. Sit with the heaviness.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Feel your chest. This is where grief lives. Your chest is allowed to be tight. You do not have to force it open. Feel your chest. Breathe gently into it.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Cry if you need to. Or let tears be ready. Your eyes are allowed to leak. Tears are honest. Tears are your body telling truth. You do not have to stop them.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Feel where the grief sits in your body. Your throat. Your belly. Your heart. Put your hand there. Let that place be tender. Tenderness is allowed.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Breathe slowly. Inhale for four. Exhale for four. Do not rush the exhale. Let it be slow. Your grief does not need to leave fast. Let it leave at its pace.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Say the name of the person or thing you are grieving. Say it out loud. Hear the sound. Speaking the name honors them. Your voice carries their name. That matters.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Feel your heart. Your heart is broken and it is still beating. That is the work of grief. Your heart does both things at once. Feel that contradiction. That is real.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Notice your body sitting. You are here. The person you are grieving is not. Your body exists in this timeline. That is painful. That is the hardest truth. Your body knows.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Hum a low note. Let the hum be mournful. Let it be sad. Your voice can hold sadness. Hum for five seconds. Stop. Hum again. Your body expressing grief.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Feel your throat. Grief gets stuck there. Swallow. Feel the tightness. You do not have to swallow it down. You can let it be stuck. You can let it hurt.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Press your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. This person who died also had a heart that beat. Your hearts were connected. Feel that connection. That connection still exists.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sit with the empty feeling. Do not try to fill it. Just sit with it. Emptiness is part of grief. You do not have to fix it. You can just sit with empty.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Remember one moment with this person. Just one. Hold it in your mind. Feel it. That moment is yours to keep. They cannot take it. Time cannot erase it from you.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Feel the weight of sadness on your shoulders. Let it be there. You do not have to carry it alone. Cry. Breathe. Let your body feel the weight. The weight is your love. Love is heavy.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Look at something that reminds you of them. Or imagine it. Feel what comes up. Let it come. Do not push it away. Your feelings about them are real.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine them. See them. See their face. See their hands. Hold that image for five breaths. They exist in your memory. Memory keeps them alive in you.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Feel your legs. Plant your feet. Grief makes you want to float away. Your feet keep you here. They keep you in this world. Feel the ground. Stay here even though it hurts.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Tell them something you wish you could say. Say it out loud. They are gone and you still have words for them. Say them. Speak to them. That matters.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Feel any anger about this loss. You are allowed to be angry. Angry that they left. Angry that you hurt. You do not have to be only sad. You can be angry too.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Notice one thing that is the same now as before. The sun. The moon. Gravity. Breathing. Some things did not change. The world kept going. That is weird and it is true.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Feel your belly. Grief makes you feel sick sometimes. That is normal. That is your body responding to loss. Breathe into that place. You can be sick and still breathe.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Ask yourself if you are angry at them. Sometimes grief carries hidden anger. Or at yourself. Or at the situation. Notice what you feel. You can feel multiple things about death.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Feel your hands. Sometimes you want to hold them again. Your hands remember. Let your hands remember. Feel what they want to hold. It is okay to want what you cannot have.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Notice the space where they used to be. An empty chair. An empty place in line. An empty heart space. That emptiness is real. You do not have to fill it immediately.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Talk to them in your mind. Have a conversation. You get to do this alone. No one has to know. They are listening in the way that memory listens. Talk to them.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Feel the shock of their absence every time you remember. Shock is normal. You have to remember over and over. Each time it hurts. That is how loss works. You are not wrong.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Notice a moment today where you felt okay for a second. Not good. Just not sad. Notice that. Then notice the guilt of not being sad. Both things are true. You are allowed.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Feel your face. Does it feel wet. Does it feel tight. Grief changes your face. Your face is showing the truth. Your face does not have to hide. Your face can grieve.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Breathe in and think their name. Breathe out and say their name silently. Repeat ten times. Their name on your breath. Their name living in you. They live in your breath.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Sit. Be still. The loss is here. You are here. Both are true. You do not have to do anything. You do not have to feel better fast. Sit. You are doing enough.
---
