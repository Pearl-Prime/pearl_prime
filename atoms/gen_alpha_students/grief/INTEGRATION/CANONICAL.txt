

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body knows it's gone."
---
There's an empty space inside you. Not the metaphorical kind. The actual absence where something used to be. A presence that was normal and now isn't. The place at the table. The person you'd text. The future you were supposed to have with them still alive.
Your body knows it's gone because your body registered them as part of the world. As part of your system. Now every day you walk through a world where they're missing and your nervous system keeps expecting them. Keeps reaching. Keeps living in the before-time for a moment before you remember. Then you remember again. And it lands in your body like it's the first time.
This isn't something you're supposed to move past quickly. Not at your age. Not with this kind of loss. The ache in your chest. The heaviness. The way certain moments or songs or words hit different now. That's your body telling the truth about what's missing.
Your body knows it's gone.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're living in the after-world now."
---
Everything has a before and after. Before they got sick. Before the accident. Before the thing that ended them. And now you live in the after. In a version of the world that's different because they're not in it. And you're tracking all the ways it's different. All the moments where they should be here. All the future moments that now won't happen.
The cost shows up in the blank spaces. The birthday that comes. The moment you accomplish something and instinctively turn to tell them. The realization that they won't see you graduate, won't meet someone important to you, won't know the adult you're becoming. The future they're not in keeps arriving.
You're living in the after-world now.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Loss changes shape. It doesn't disappear."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Right now, the loss is acute. Every day feels like it confirms that they're gone. But grief transforms. Not because you forget. Not because it stops hurting. But because your system will eventually adjust to their absence. The sharp pain will dull. It will become something you carry rather than something that knocks you down.
A year from now, you might be able to remember them without immediately falling into the acute grief. Three years from now, you'll realize that entire weeks go by where you don't think about the specific day they died. The love won't go anywhere. But the emergency of it will soften. You'll learn how to live in a world where they're missing and you're still here.
Years from now, you'll find unexpected moments where you feel them. In a song. In the way someone else laughs. In the discovery that you have a quality that came from them. And it will hurt and it will also feel like connection. Like they're still part of the person you're becoming, even in absence.
Loss changes shape. It doesn't disappear.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What do you carry that came from them."
---
What if the question isn't how to stop missing them. What if it's what stays. What piece of how they moved through the world lives in you now. What quality or kindness or specific way of being did they teach you just by being alive. What part of how you are in the world is shaped by having known them.
They're gone from the world. But they're not gone from you. They shaped you. They left a mark. What if grief is partly about learning what that mark is. What they gave you that stays.
What do you carry that came from them.
---

---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON

---
You're not alone in missing them. There's someone else walking around carrying this absence. Who loved them differently than you did but loved them just as real. Who has their own moments where something hits and suddenly they're not here. You're both living in the after-world. You both feel the empty space.
Someone else is grieving them too.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "You lost more than one person."
---
When someone dies, you lose the actual person. And you also lose the future with them. The conversations that won't happen. The moments they won't witness. The part of your life they won't be in. You're grieving the person who existed and also the person they would have become and all the things they would have seen you become.
The loss is expansive. It's not just in the past. It reaches into the future too. Into the time that will never include them.
You lost more than one person.
