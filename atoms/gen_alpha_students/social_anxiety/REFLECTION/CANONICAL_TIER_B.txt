

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
There's a moment right before I have to present in class when I feel this activation. My heart rate increases. My hands shake. I'm convinced everyone is going to judge me for what I say. The mechanism is called pre-social threat appraisal—the prediction of negative judgment.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that I'm more anxious about social situations with new people. Or larger groups. Or people I'm trying to impress. The mechanism is called variable social anxiety—anxiety that increases with stakes.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of anxiety that happens in group projects. I'm supposed to collaborate. To share ideas. But I'm worried that my ideas aren't good enough. Or that I'll be judged for not contributing enough. The mechanism is called collaborative social anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that my social anxiety shows up physically. I feel heat in my face. My voice gets quieter. I have trouble making eye contact. And these physical signs make me feel more anxious because I can tell that I'm anxious. The mechanism is called symptom-amplification-through-visibility.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is that I'm more anxious in social situations after I've done something I'm self-conscious about. Like if I think I said something weird at lunch, then I'm more anxious in my afternoon classes. The mechanism is called self-consciousness-based social anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my social anxiety that's about social comparison. I'm comparing myself to my peers. And I'm convinced they're more confident. More articulate. More worth listening to. The mechanism is called comparative social anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that my social anxiety decreases when I'm with people I'm very comfortable with. But those situations are rare. Most social situations feel a bit threatening. The mechanism is called context-dependent social anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I'm realizing is that some of my social anxiety is about authenticity. Like if people really knew me, they wouldn't like me. So there's a performance. And the performance is exhausting. The mechanism is called authenticity-anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about the permanence of social media that increases my anxiety. Something I say could be recorded. Could be taken out of context. Could haunt me. The mechanism is called permanence-anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've learned that my social anxiety sometimes prevents me from having experiences that would decrease anxiety. If I avoid social situations, I don't learn that the feared outcomes don't happen. The mechanism is avoidance-maintained-anxiety.
---
