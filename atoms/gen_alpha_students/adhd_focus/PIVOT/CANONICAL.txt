## PIVOT v01
---
---
You think you're being lazy when you stare at the worksheet without writing. Try this instead: the task just needs its first tiny step named out loud, then your hand can move.
---

## PIVOT v02
---
---
You think forgetting the permission slip means you don't care. Try this instead: your brain just doesn't hold onto plans well without something written down. Write it the second you think of it.
---

## PIVOT v03
---
---
You think the spelling list is taking forever because you're bad at spelling. Try this instead: each word is its own little start. Cross them off as you go and watch the list shrink.
---

## PIVOT v04
---
---
You think you're avoiding the book report because you don't like writing. Try this instead: there are too many ways to start and picking one feels like its own job. Always start with the title, every time.
---

## PIVOT v05
---
---
You think you should already know how to start the big project. Try this instead: give it a made-up finish line, like "just the first drawing," instead of trying to imagine the whole thing at once.
---

## PIVOT v06
---
---
You think the scary group chat message means something bad is happening. Try this instead: read only the first line. The worry is almost always bigger than the actual message.
---

## PIVOT v07
---
---
You call it distraction when you draw instead of doing your homework. Try this instead: your attention goes toward what's loud and interesting. Make your homework louder — a timer, a sticker chart, someone checking in.
---

## PIVOT v08
---
---
You think sitting at your desk with your folder open means you already started. Try this instead: sitting down and starting are two different steps. Add a tiny bridge, like saying the first sentence out loud.
---

## PIVOT v09
---
---
You think problem eight feels harder because you're getting tired. Try this instead: your brain just wants something new. Switch pencils or say the answer out loud to wake your attention back up.
---

## PIVOT v10
---
---
You think losing the folder in your backpack means you're messy. Try this instead: check the one spot you always forget to check. The folder is usually right where it always is.
---

## PIVOT v11
---
---
You think the homework didn't get done because you didn't try hard enough. Try this instead: the task had no visible first step. Write just the title, and the rest tends to follow.
---

## PIVOT v12
---
---
You think the whole hour disappeared because you weren't paying attention. Try this instead: time is hard to feel without a timer. Put one where you can see the numbers count down.
---
