## PERMISSION v01
---
---
You are allowed to write the tiniest, smallest first step of a hard task on a sticky note, instead of trying to figure out the whole task in your head first.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---
---
You are allowed to make your first try at something messy on purpose, because a messy first try is what makes a good final version possible.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---
---
You are allowed to write down or tell someone about anything you need to remember the moment you think of it, instead of trusting yourself to remember it later.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---
---
You are allowed to give a task with no clear ending — like "clean your room" — your own made-up finish line, instead of trying to guess what "done" is supposed to mean.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---
---
You are allowed to read only the first line of a scary message, with no promise to answer the rest of it right away.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---
---
You are allowed to always start a project the exact same way every time, instead of trying to figure out the "best" way to begin each time.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---
---
You are allowed to do your hardest homework first, right when you get home, instead of saving it for when you're already tired from everything else.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---
---
You are allowed to leave the thing you need to do somewhere you'll actually see it, instead of trusting yourself to remember where it is.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---
---
You are allowed to use a timer with numbers you can watch, because it's hard to feel time passing without something to look at.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---
---
You are allowed to say out loud, "I keep doing something else instead of starting," the moment you notice it, instead of feeling bad about it quietly.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---
---
You are allowed to switch to a different pencil, chair, or spot when a repeated task starts feeling boring, instead of forcing yourself to keep doing it exactly the same way.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---
---
You are allowed to treat sitting down with your homework and actually starting your homework as two different steps that each need their own small push.
---
