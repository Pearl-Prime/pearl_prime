COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Zoe's parents sit her down junior year and talk about college costs. They have saved thirty thousand dollars. The cost of college is sixty to eighty thousand dollars per year. The difference is fifty thousand dollars minimum. Zoe is sixteen and she is holding the number in her head: fifty thousand dollars. Her parents explain financial aid and loans and merit scholarships. The explanation is meant to be informative. For Zoe, it is overwhelming. There are too many variables. There are too many numbers. There are too many decisions that she does not have enough information to make. Her chest feels heavy. Her belly feels hollow. Her throat closes. The information is too much and she does not know where to put it.

Maya breaks down in her school counselor's office. She tells the counselor that she is overwhelmed and exhausted and confused and she does not know how to do this.
