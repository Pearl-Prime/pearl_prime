

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body carries the family stress."
---
You know something. You've seen a parent worried about money in a way that lands in your nervous system. You've felt the financial alarm that lives in your house. You've heard the conversations that weren't meant for you. And your body absorbed it. Turned it into its own kind of background threat. Into the knowledge that there isn't enough. That things could shift. That stability isn't guaranteed.
This doesn't come from your own crisis. It comes from living inside a family system where money creates fear. And your nervous system doesn't differentiate—it just registers: not safe, could change, need to be ready. Your body lives with that knowledge. You feel heavier. More watchful. More aware of the cost of things. More afraid of taking up space.
That weight in your body. That's not you being dramatic. That's you absorbing the real stress of the people who raised you.
Your body carries the family stress.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're keeping score of what you can't have."
---
You know what your peers have that you don't. You see it. The casual spending. The clothes nobody worries about. The college talk like it's simple. The ability to do things without calculating cost. And you're counting. You're tracking. You're aware in a way that other kids your age don't have to be.
The cost shows up in small places. In the conversations you don't join because they're about money. In the things you don't ask for. In the way you make yourself smaller so nobody has to spend on you. In the part of you that's always aware of what you cost.
You're keeping score of what you can't have.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Your future is not your parents' past."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You're living with knowledge that most people your age don't have. You understand economic precarity. You know that stability is fragile. You see the system in ways that your peers are protected from. This is difficult knowledge. And it changes you.
But here's what I want you to know: the financial stress you're carrying right now—your family's struggle, the uncertainty, the weight of it—that's the present. It's not a prediction about your future. You have choices ahead that your parents didn't have. You have time. You have skills you haven't even discovered yet. The path forward isn't what the past looked like.
Learning early that resources matter, that you have to be strategic, that nothing is free—those are hard lessons. But they're also tools. The awareness you're carrying will serve you. Not because it feels good now. But because it's teaching you to think differently about what's possible.
Your future is not your parents' past.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if this awareness becomes your strength."
---
What if the thing that feels like a limitation is actually information. What if paying attention to resources, understanding cost, being aware of what things really require—what if that becomes the thing that lets you move forward strategically. What if this difficult knowledge becomes a tool instead of just a weight.
What if you stopped seeing the financial reality around you as evidence that you're destined for the same struggle. What if you saw it as the place where you get to make different choices. Where you get to break a pattern because you're aware of what the pattern costs.
What if this awareness becomes your strength.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Your parents carry this alone."
---
They don't want you to feel this. They're not trying to give you their stress. But it lands because you live in a system where the anxiety is present and palpable. You sense it even when they try to hide it. You carry it even though it's not your weight to carry. And in the quiet moments, you know they wish you didn't have to know what you know.
Your parents carry this alone.

You can set the worry down sometimes.

The alarm that runs because of money steals small pieces of your youth. The lightness. The carefree moment. The ability to just take up space without calculating what it costs.
The alarm absorbs your childhood.
