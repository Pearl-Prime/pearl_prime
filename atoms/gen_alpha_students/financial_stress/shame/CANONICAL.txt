COST_TYPE: social

IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Zade's school announces a trip to the state science competition. The registration is two hundred dollars. His mother asks how much and when he tells her, he sees something shift in her face. Not judgment. Recognition of a cost she cannot absorb right now. His chest tightens. She says "We'll see." The "we'll see" means no. He knows this. He does not say anything. He goes to school the next day and three kids are talking about the trip and what they will pack. Zade listens. He does not mention that he will not be going. His belly feels tight. His hands feel cold. The cost is not just two hundred dollars. The cost is that his mother cannot afford it and the cost is that he knows she cannot afford it and the cost is that he cannot say this out loud. The shame sits in his sternum. The shame is not about the money. The shame is that the money is visible.

Jaden's school is majority wealthy. He is on scholarship. On Fridays, the school has a casual day that costs five dollars. The money goes to a charity. Jaden cannot afford the five dollars. He has decided not to participate. On Friday, he wears uniform while everyone else wears casual clothes. The difference is stark and visible. A teacher asks if he forgot about casual day. His belly drops. He says he did not have cash. The teacher seems not to understand that this is code for "my family cannot afford five dollars." The teacher offers to put it on his account. Jaden says no. The shame of the visible difference is worse than the shame of being called out. He sits in uniform while everyone else sits in jeans and the shame sits in his throat like something that will choke him. The shame is not about five dollars. The shame is that five dollars is visible as a real cost in his life.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/financial_stress/shame/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Zade starts to decline invitations to things that cost money. Parties that require bringing food. Hangouts that involve going to restaurants. Trips to the mall. He makes excuses. He is busy. He does not feel well. He has homework. The excuses are true sometimes. The excuses are also shame-management. He cannot afford to participate in the way that his peers participate. So instead, he does not participate. His friends begin to notice. One friend asks if he is okay. Zade says he is fine. He is not fine. He is becoming increasingly isolated because the isolation feels less shameful than the visibility of his financial situation. His shoulders curve inward. His chest stays tight. The mechanism is that he is trading social connection for the absence of shame. The trade feels necessary.
---

Maya starts to look at her family's finances through the lens of her peers. Her parents make a middle income. No one says anything. Her belly becomes hollow. Her hands stay cold. She is performing the version of a girl from a wealthy family while being a girl from a middle-income family. Jaden begins to understand that the shame about money is also shame about his family. The wealth difference is evidence of class difference. Maya is sitting in her therapist's office and the therapist asks about her social isolation. Maya talks about school. She talks about the trips and the clothes and the restaurants.
