

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic

I have noticed that financial stress in students often arrives silently. You do not have a job that is not paying you. You do not have bills that you cannot pay. You just have an awareness that things cost. That your family is managing resources. That money is not unlimited. That the future has a price tag.

There is a mechanism that is absorbing financial anxiety from the environment. You overhear a conversation about college costs. You notice your parents being stressed about bills. You become aware that money is a thing that runs out. The mechanism is internalizing the scarcity before you have even earned your first dollar.

What I have come to understand is that the financial anxiety is often not your own anxiety about your own money. The financial anxiety is inherited anxiety. The anxiety is coming from the adults around you. The anxiety is the message that money is tight, that resources are limited, that the future is expensive.

What I have come to think about is the way that the inherited anxiety becomes your anxiety. You start to carry it. You start to feel responsible for it. You start to believe that it is your job to manage the financial stress that was never your job to manage.

The mechanism is creating a particular kind of burden. The burden is the awareness that money is a thing that defines your future. The burden is the awareness that money limits possibility. The burden is the awareness that you will have to earn enough to survive, and survival is not cheap.

And the awareness is arriving when you are still young. When you are still supposed to be having possibilities. When you are still supposed to believe that the future is open.
