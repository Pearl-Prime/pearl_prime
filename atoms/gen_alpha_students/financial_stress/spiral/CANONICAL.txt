---
Maya is a sophomore and she begins to understand the cost of college. Her parents earn about seventy-five thousand dollars a year. The cost of a private university is sixty-five thousand dollars per year. The math is visible and immediate. If she goes to a private university, her family will have to take on debt. The debt will take decades to pay back. She is fifteen and she is calculating her family's financial ruin. Her belly feels tight. Her chest feels heavy. She has not even applied yet and she is already failing. She has not done anything wrong and her life is already going to cost her family something that might break them. The spiral begins in the realization that this choice was not going to be her choice. This choice was going to be determined by money.
