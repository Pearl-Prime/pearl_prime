

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit in your chair right now. Feel your feet flat on the floor. Press your feet down hard. Count to three. Release. Press again. Count to three. Release. Repeat five more times. Notice how solid the ground feels. Your body is here. The ground holds you up. Rest.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your eyes where you are. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold that breath for four counts. Breathe out through your mouth for six counts. That longer out-breath calms your nervous system. Do this five times. Feel your shoulders drop. Your body knows what to do.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Look around the room. Find one blue thing. Stare at it for five breaths. Notice its exact shade. Now find something red. Look at it for five breaths. Now something green. Five breaths. Your brain shifts from worry to looking. Worry needs your attention. Looking takes it back.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Make tight fists with both hands. Squeeze hard for three seconds. Feel the tension build. Now release. Let your hands go completely loose. Notice the difference. Repeat three times. Squeeze. Hold. Release. Let go. Your body holds your worry in your hands. Release it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Clench your jaw hard. Count to three. Release. Repeat three times. Feel your jaw loosen. Move your jaw side to side slowly. Open your mouth wide. Your jaw tightens when you worry. Loosening it tells your brain you are safe.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Press the palms of your hands together hard. Count to four. Feel the pressure build. Release. Repeat four times. Push. Feel the strength. Let go. Notice the calm after. Your hands can hold pressure. You can release it.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Tense all your shoulder muscles. Shrug them up toward your ears. Hold for three seconds. Drop them fast. Feel the release. Repeat five times. Up. Hold. Drop. Your shoulders live in your worry. Drop them down. That is safer.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Sit straight. Place one hand on your belly. Breathe slowly. Feel your belly expand when you inhale. Feel it fall when you exhale. Do this for eight breaths. Feel your belly move. Your breath controls your nervous system. Slow breath equals calm body.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Rub your hands together very fast for five seconds. Feel the heat build. Press your warm palms on your face. Feel that warmth. Hold for three breaths. The warmth anchors you. You are here. You are warm. You are real.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Cross your arms over your chest. Squeeze yourself gently. Hold for four breaths. Release. Repeat three times. Hug yourself tight. That pressure feels like safety. You can give yourself that. Repeat when you need it.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Tap your fingertips on your desk or your lap. Make a steady rhythm. Tap for ten seconds. Focus only on the sound. Let your mind follow the rhythm. Your nervous system loves rhythm. Rhythm means everything is okay.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Feel your back against the chair. Press backward hard. Notice how solid the chair is. It holds your whole weight. You are supported. Breathe three times. Feel how supported you are. The chair is not leaving.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Name five things you can see right now. Say them out loud quietly. Be specific. Not just blue. Say navy blue. Not just a desk. Say my wooden desk. Your brain stops worrying when you name real things around you.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Listen for three sounds right now. Stop and listen. Do not make them up. What do you actually hear. Name them quietly. Your ears bring you to now. Worry lives in later. Listening brings you back to now.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Touch something textured near you. A blanket. A jacket. Rough fabric. Feel it slowly. Feel the texture on your fingertips. Rub it for ten seconds. Textures anchor you. Your skin is your present moment.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Roll your neck slowly in one direction. Slow roll. Feel your neck muscles stretch. Go all the way around four times. Then go the other direction. Four times. Your neck holds tension. Roll it out.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tighten your leg muscles by pointing your toes down. Flex hard. Count to three. Release. Repeat five times. Feel your legs get heavy. Heavy legs ground you. Anxiety lives in lightness and floating.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Press your tongue against the roof of your mouth. Press hard. Count to four. Release. Relax your tongue to the floor of your mouth. Repeat five times. Your mouth holds worry. Release it.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Blink slowly and deliberately. Blink five times. Each time, blink like you are taking a small reset. Slow blinks calm your nervous system. You are in control of your blink. Control your blink. Control yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Stand if you can or shift in your seat. Move your weight from one foot to the other. Shift. Shift. Shift. Feel the ground underneath each foot. Feel your balance. Your feet are making contact with earth. That contact is real.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Touch your forehead. Press gently with your fingers. Make small circles. Feel the pressure release. Do this for ten seconds. Your forehead is where you think. Let it relax. Thinking made you tense.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Hum at a low pitch. Let the hum vibrate in your chest. Hum for five seconds. Stop. Hum again. Repeat five times. The vibration is soothing. Your voice is your anchor. Use it.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Feel where your bottom touches the chair. Feel the contact. Press down slightly into the seat. Feel that pressure. You are held. The seat is under you. Repeat the pressure five times. Notice. Press. Feel held.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Pinch the skin on your inner arm gently. Just enough to feel it. Hold for three seconds. Release. Repeat five times. Sensation brings you to your body. Your body is safe right now.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Count each breath you take. Inhale is one. Exhale is one. Count to ten breaths. Start again at one. Do this three times. Counting and breathing together interrupt the worry cycle. Your breath keeps the count.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Wiggle your toes inside your shoes. Wiggle for five seconds. Feel them move. Repeat three times. Your toes are far from your brain. Toes in the present moment. Brain in the past worries.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Make a small noise. A small uh or mm. Make the sound three times. Hear your own voice. You are in your body making sounds. Sounds mean you are present and real.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Look at the back of your hand. Turn it over slowly. Look at the palm. Study the lines. Look at this hand. This is your hand. This is your body. It is here with you.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Breathe in a smell near you. A pencil. Your clothes. Your hand. Smell something real. Smell it for three breaths. Smell brings you to now. Now is not the anxiety future.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Open and close your fists slowly five times. Open. Close. Open. Close. Feel your hands work. Your hands do things. You can make them move. Control is in movement.
---
