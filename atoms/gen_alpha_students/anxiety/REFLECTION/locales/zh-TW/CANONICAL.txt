The person who speaks easily in class is not speaking from a place of having no alarm. They are speaking with the alarm running and have learned, in some way you have not yet, to move while it fires.

The mechanism that reads other people's behavior and concludes they are not feeling what you are feeling is making an inference that the behavior itself cannot support. You can see the behavior. You cannot see the interior. And the inference that ease of behavior means absence of anxiety is not accurate.

What I have come to understand is that the goal is not to eliminate the alarm. The goal is to expand the range of what you can do while the alarm is running. That expansion happens gradually, through exposure, through acting while the alarm fires and discovering that the feared outcome either does not arrive or can be survived when it does.

The mechanism is not the obstacle. The belief that the mechanism must stop before you can move is the obstacle.
