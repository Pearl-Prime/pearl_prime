

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/watcher/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Zoe is at a party but she's not at the party. There's a part of her that's watching her be at the party. She sees herself standing by the wall. She notices that she's not talking much. She monitors whether other people notice that she's not talking much. Her hands are cold. She's split in half. One Zoe is physically present. The other Zoe is observing and critiquing Zoe number one. She watches her face. Is she smiling correctly? Is the laugh too loud? Did that comment sound stupid? The observer is exhausting. It runs constant surveillance. Her own behavior has become a performance she's watching from the audience.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/watcher/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Eli talks to someone he trusts. He describes the feeling of being watched. Of monitoring himself constantly. The person nods and says: yeah, I had that in college. I still do sometimes. The permission to name it is the first step. So Eli starts noticing when the watcher is active. He recognizes the physical sensation. The tight shoulders. The disconnection. He starts speaking the observation out loud: I'm in watcher mode right now. Just saying it makes the watcher a little quieter. It becomes something he's noticing, not something he is.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/watcher/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Mika notices something. The times she's most effective are the times the watcher is quiet. When she's actually focused on the task instead of monitoring herself doing the task. She's never noticed this before. Now that she notices, she starts small. She silences her phone during work. The phone silence helps the watcher be quiet. The focus improves. Her hands steady. The work goes faster. She's learning: the watcher is optional. When the watcher is off, she's actually better at the things the watcher was trying to protect her from.
