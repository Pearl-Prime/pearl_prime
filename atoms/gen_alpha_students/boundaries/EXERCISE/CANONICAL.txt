

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Say the word no out loud three times. Say it firm. No. No. No. Feel it in your throat. Your voice can say no. Your voice belongs to you. Saying no is not mean. It is honest.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand or sit up straight. Feel your spine lengthen. This is boundary posture. Straight spine means I stand for myself. Slouching means I am sorry. Keep your spine straight. Feel your power in it.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Clench your fists. Squeeze hard. Count to three. This is your boundary. Your body is your boundary. It is not soft. It is not permeable. Release. Feel how solid you are.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Press both hands down on the surface in front of you. Press hard. Count to four. You are pushing against something. You are holding your ground. Your hands say no to pushing.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Place your hand on your chest. Feel your heartbeat or your breath. This is your body. This is yours. No one else gets to decide about this body. Breathe four times. Keeping this body safe is your job.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Look someone in the eye. Even if imaginary. Do not look down. Look directly. This is the face of boundary setting. You look steady. You do not flinch. You do not hide.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Breathe in through your nose. As you breathe out, imagine a circle of light around your body. That is your boundary. Breathe in. That light is there. Breathe out. The light holds.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Shake your hands out violently for three seconds. Shake. Shake. Shake. Feel the energy release. Say no while shaking. The movement matches the word. Your whole body says no.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Turn your head slowly to the left. Then to the right. This is no in your body. A gentle head shake. Do this three times. No. No. No. Your neck knows this gesture.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Take a step backward if you can. Feel the space you just created. That space is yours. You moved the boundary. Repeat three times. Step back. Feel the gap grow. That is your right.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Squeeze your jaw. Hold it tight. Count to three. Release. Your jaw tightens before you say no. That is normal. Notice it. Release it. Say what you need to say anyway.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Press your feet flat into the ground. Push hard with your heels. Feel the earth resist. You are rooted. Someone asks for too much. Your feet stay planted. You do not move.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Make a boundary gesture. Put your palm up and forward. Like stop. Hold it. This is a physical boundary. Your hand says do not come closer. Hold for three breaths. You are allowed to hold this.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Feel your shoulders. Are they tense. Bring them up to your ears. Now drop them. Do this three times. Tension means you are holding no inside. When you say no out loud, drop the tension.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Take one slow breath. As you exhale, imagine breathing out all the reasons you should say yes. Breathe in fresh air. Breathe out pressure. Do this three times. Pressure leaves with your breath.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Touch something solid near you. A wall. A desk. A book. Feel how solid it is. That solidity is boundary. You have it inside you. You are not soft. You are substantial.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Cross your arms over your chest gently. This is a boundary pose. You are holding yourself. You are protecting yourself. You do not need to protect yourself from friends. But you can. Your body can do this.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Say no and follow with a full sentence. No and then your reason. No, I cannot. No, I do not want to. No, I already said yes to something else. The word no needs no permission.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Notice your jaw tension right now. Where is it tight. Loosen your jaw deliberately. Open your mouth slightly. Relax your tongue. A tense jaw makes speaking hard. A loose jaw lets truth flow.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Feel your legs. Push them down into the floor or the seat. Feel your legs strong. Your legs carry you away from what you do not want. Feel that power. You can walk away.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Breathe into your belly. Make your belly expand. This breath anchors you in your body. When you say no, stay in your body. Do not float away. Stay in your belly. Breathe there.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Make a fist and tap it gently on the surface near you. Tap three times. I am here. I am solid. I am not disappearing. Your boundary is not rude. It is presence.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Feel the space around your body. Imagine it extending two feet in every direction. That space is yours. No one enters without permission. When someone violates it, feel the violation. Name it.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Take a sip of water or imagine taking one. Swallow. Feel it go down. You just took something in that you wanted. That is consent. You wanted that in your body. You do not want other things in. Say so.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Say no five times in different ways. No. No thank you. I cannot. I do not agree. Not this time. Your voice has options. Your no does not need to sound the same.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Feel your back against the chair. Your back is protected when you sit this way. Someone cannot surprise you from behind. Your back is safe. Your front is open and honest. Sit this way when setting boundaries.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Touch your own cheek. Feel your face. This is you. This is the person saying no. Your face is allowed to show it. Your no-face is honest. Let your face tell the truth.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Hum while saying no. Hum and say no at the same time. Feel the vibration. Your whole body is saying no. Not just your mouth. The hum makes it louder inside you.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Press both palms together in front of your heart. Press together. Count to four. This is your center. Your heart is not available to everyone. Keep it close. Let trusted people in only.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Wiggle your toes. Feel your feet. You are grounded when you set a boundary. The ground does not shake. You do not shake. You stay solid. Your feet keep you steady.
---
