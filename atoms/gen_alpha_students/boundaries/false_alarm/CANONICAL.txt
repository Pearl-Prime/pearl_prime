path: the body says danger when it's safe
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Ava feels her body respond before her mind catches up. Someone asks her to help plan the party. Her chest tightens. Her breath gets shallow. It feels like danger. Her hands start shaking slightly. She's about to say no, but something makes her pause. The request is actually reasonable. The person is kind. There's no threat here. Her nervous system doesn't seem to care about those facts. It's firing like there's an emergency. She agrees to help—what else can she do?—and immediately feels her whole body flood with relief. The relief tells her something was wrong. But now she's confused. Was she actually in danger, or did her body just think she was? She spends the next hour confused and exhausted, not sure why saying yes felt like survival.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: the thirty-second pause
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Ava experiments with something different. Instead of ignoring the false alarm or collapsing into it, she tries to notice it and wait. When someone asks her to do something and the panic fires, she sits with the panic for just thirty seconds. She doesn't act on it. She doesn't say yes. She doesn't panic-communicate. She just sits. The thirty seconds feel long. The nervous system is screaming danger. But nothing bad happens during the thirty seconds. No abandonment. No disaster. Just her nervous system firing and her staying present with the firing. At the end, she can sometimes access her actual yes or no. Sometimes the real answer is different from what the panic suggested. It's a small experiment. Not a fix. But it's creating a small space between the alarm and her response.
