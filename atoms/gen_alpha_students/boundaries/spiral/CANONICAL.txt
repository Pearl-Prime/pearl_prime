

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/spiral/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Zoe gets a B on her chemistry exam. It's not a failing grade. It's not even a bad grade. But in her mind, the equation starts immediately. A B means her GPA might drop. If her GPA drops, the scholarship is at risk. If the scholarship goes, tuition becomes impossible. If tuition becomes impossible, she has to drop out. If she drops out, her career is over before it started. All of this from a single B on a single exam. The spiral is fast and complete. Her chest tightens. She's spiraling forward from today through a future that hasn't happened yet. The math is logical if you don't interrupt it. One grade leads directly to homelessness. Or at least, that's what her nervous system is showing her. She sits in the dining hall surrounded by people but she's living five years in the future.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Zoe catches herself spiraling about a test and stops. She actually stops. Not completely. But she says: okay, I'm spiraling. This is a spiral. I'm not in the future. I'm in the current moment and I got a B on a test. She sits with the actual fact. Not the story. The B itself. What does a B actually mean? It means her grade in that class is a B. That's where the spiral stops if she stops it. She keeps spiraling anyway. But now she knows she's doing it. Knowing makes space. She can't stop the spiral but she can observe it. She can say: this is the story my brain is telling. Not: this is what's actually happening. The distinction is small and enormous. Her chest loosens slightly. She's learning that the spiral is optional, even though it doesn't feel optional.

## TURNING_POINT v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Mika writes down the actual facts when she's spiraling about money. She has seventeen hundred dollars saved. Rent is nine hundred. That leaves eight hundred for food and expenses. If she's careful, eight hundred is enough for a month. She's not going to be homeless tomorrow. When she writes it down, the spiral has to contend with actual math instead of imaginary math. The actual math is tight. The imaginary math is apocalyptic. She keeps the note visible. When the spiral starts, she reads the numbers. The anxiety doesn't disappear. But it becomes manageable. It becomes: this month is tight. Let me work extra hours. Not: I'm going to be homeless because I'm incompetent. The spiral needs vagueness. The numbers don't provide vagueness.

## TURNING_POINT v05
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/spiral/turning_point/micro/v05.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Nia talks to her academic advisor about the month she missed. She went in expecting expulsion. The advisor listens and asks: do you want to be here? Nia says yes. The advisor says: okay. We'll work out a plan. The advisor is treating the situation as a problem with solutions, not a terminal diagnosis. They map out which classes she can still recover in. Which ones need an incomplete. The advisor is telling her: this happened. It's not good. It's also not unrecoverable. The spiral in Nia's head has been: I've ruined everything. The advisor is saying: you missed some classes. Let's fix it. The endpoints are different. The spiral doesn't disappear. But now Nia knows it's not the only story. There's another story where she stays enrolled and recovers.

## EMBODIMENT v02
---
path: story_atoms/gen_alpha_students/anchored/boundaries/spiral/embodiment/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 1
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 1
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Leo now has a rule: he doesn't spiral about social situations after dark. The spiral works best in isolation at night. He learned this about himself. So he tells himself: if you're spiraling at 11 PM, you're tired and your brain is lying. He sleeps on it. Almost every time, the next morning brings perspective. The spiral seems less true. He still feels embarrassed sometimes. But embarrassment is different from the spiral. Embarrassment is proportional. The spiral is hyperbolic. He's learning to recognize which one he's in. His sleep is better. His social anxiety is the same. But he's not feeding it all night with catastrophic projections.

## EMBODIMENT v04

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: self_claim
---
Asha built a spiral-stopping practice. When the spiral starts, she says what she actually knows. Just the facts. I made an error. The error was caught. The patient was fine. Those three things are true. I don't know what this means about my career. That's also true. But I know one thing: I've already learned from this. I'm more careful now. She's learning to separate the known from the unknown. The spiral lives in the unknown. The facts are smaller. Less dramatic. But they're solid. Her hands stop shaking. Her stomach unclenches. The spiral is still accessible but she's not living inside it.
