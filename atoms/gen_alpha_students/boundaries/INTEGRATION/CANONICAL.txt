

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your exhaustion is information."
---
Notice where you're holding tension right now. Your phone is still in your hand, even though you meant to put it down. Your mind is still in the group chat even though you stepped away. Your nervous system is still available—still checking for the next demand, the next notification, the next friend who needs you at 2 AM.
This is what happens when you have no boundary. Your body doesn't get to rest. It gets to keep working. It gets to stay on alert. It gets to hold the weight of other people's needs inside your own system.
Your exhaustion isn't laziness. It's what happens when your nervous system never gets permission to stand down. It's information. Your body is telling you something true: you're carrying something that isn't yours to carry alone.
Your exhaustion is information.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Availability has an invisible price tag."
---
You've been saying yes to everything. The friend who texts at midnight. The group chat. The expectations at home while you're trying to focus on school. The idea that being a good friend means never saying no. The belief that boundaries are selfish.
What you're not counting: the hours that disappear. The homework you rush through. The version of yourself that gets smaller because there's no space that's only yours. The resentment that builds when you've given everything and still aren't enough.
Availability has a price. It just doesn't show up on your calendar. It shows up in your body—in how heavy you feel, in how empty you are by the end of the day, in the way you disappear even when you're in the room.
Availability has an invisible price tag.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Your life is yours to keep."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Five years from now, you won't remember most of the group chat messages. You won't think about the moments when you said yes when you meant no. What you will remember is whether you kept any space that was only yours. Whether you got to be someone other than what was demanded of you.
You're in the season where everyone is pulling—school, family, friends, the algorithm. Everyone has a claim on your attention. But the person whose claim matters most is you. Not in a selfish way. In a foundational way. You can't give from an empty place. You can't show up for anyone else if there's no one home in your own body.
Setting a boundary now is an investment in the person you're becoming. It's the practice of keeping something sacred. It's choosing yourself, not instead of other people, but as part of how you stay real enough to actually be with them.
Your life is yours to keep.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would change if you stopped apologizing."
---
What would happen if you said no without explaining why. If you put your phone down and didn't check it for an hour. If you told the friend that asks too much that you can't meet them there, not because something is wrong with you, but because something is right with you—you're protecting something that matters.
What would change if a boundary didn't feel like rejection. If it felt like clarity instead. If it meant you were choosing to be honest about what you can actually carry, what you actually have room for.
What would the world be if you stopped apologizing for having limits.
What would change if you stopped apologizing.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Someone needs you rested."
---
There's someone who depends on you being okay. Not because you're responsible for their healing—you're not. But because when you're drained, you show up differently. You're less present. Less patient. Less yourself. They notice even if they don't say it.
The boundary you set protects both of you. It means you get to rest. Which means the people who matter to you get you at your actual baseline, not at your worst, stretched-thin version.
Someone needs you rested.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The cost of boundless is burning out."
---
Every time you don't set a boundary, you're accumulating a debt you'll pay later. In resentment. In emptiness. In the parts of yourself that go quiet because there's no room for them. In relationships that feel one-directional because you never said what you actually need.
The cost of boundless is burnout. Simple math. You can't pour from an empty cup. You can't show up for anyone if there's nothing left of you.
The cost of boundless is burning out.
