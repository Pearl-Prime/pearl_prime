

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I can't sleep even when I'm tired. My mind stays active. Thinking about what happened today. Planning for tomorrow. Analyzing interactions. Worrying about tests. The mechanism is called pre-sleep mental activation—the mind refusing to quiet when sleep is needed.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I notice is that I check my phone right before bed. I'm scrolling social media. Checking notifications. And my brain is stimulated. So then I can't sleep even though I'm exhausted. The mechanism is called screen-induced sleep disruption.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of sleep anxiety that comes on school nights. Knowing I have to wake up early. Have to be functional. Have to perform. And that pressure keeps me awake. The mechanism is called performance-anxiety-related insomnia.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that I sleep poorly the night before something important. A test. A presentation. A social event. My body knows something is coming and it prepares by keeping me awake. Which means I face the important thing already depleted. The mechanism is anticipatory insomnia.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is that my sleep is worse when I'm worried about something. And my mind seems to prefer ruminating at night. When I'm supposed to be resting. The mechanism is called nighttime rumination-induced insomnia.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my sleep anxiety that's about FOMO. The fear that something important is happening and I'm missing it. Even though logically I know nothing important is happening at 2am. But the fear is still there. The mechanism is called FOMO-driven anxiety.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that I'm often too tired during the day because I'm sleeping poorly at night. Which makes me less able to focus. Which makes me more anxious about things. Which makes me sleep worse. The mechanism is called anxiety-fatigue-anxiety cycle.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I'm realizing is that my sleep anxiety is partly about the pressure to perform. Sleep is supposed to help me recover so I can be my best. So if I'm not sleeping well, I'm failing at recovery. And the failure creates more anxiety. The mechanism is performance-anxiety-around-sleep.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about social media that seems designed to prevent sleep. I'm exposed to things that upset me. Things that activate my nervous system. And then I'm trying to sleep while activated. The mechanism is called social-media-induced sleep dysregulation.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've learned that my sleep anxiety is connected to my control anxiety. Sleep is something I can't control. My mind won't cooperate. My body won't cooperate. And the loss of control creates anxiety. The mechanism is control-loss-driven sleep anxiety.
---
