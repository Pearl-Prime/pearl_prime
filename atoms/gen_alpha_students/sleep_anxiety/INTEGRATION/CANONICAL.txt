## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The school day hasn't happened yet."
---
It's 1:15am. You're in bed. You're awake. Tomorrow is 1:15am away — that's not a tomorrow, that's right now. Your nervous system is already there. Already in the hallway. Already worried about the test, the conversation, the thing someone said.

But your body is still in bed. Still horizontal. Still seven hours away from the thing you're anxious about.

Your jaw is tight. Your hands are restless. Your mind won't stop building the tomorrow that hasn't happened yet. Your nervous system is trying to prepare for a moment that doesn't exist for seven more hours.

Notice your hands right now. Still. The phone is on the nightstand. Your body is safe. Tomorrow is not happening right now.

Your alarm is just running early.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Sleep is the thing you can't control."
---
You're losing sleep to a day that hasn't happened. The anxiety arrives before the thing that makes it necessary. You've already spent four hours of tonight lying awake building scenarios. Preparing for conversations. Running through the test.

Tomorrow you will be tired. Your body will be slower, your patience shorter, your attention split. You'll need more resources for a hard day and you'll have fewer because your nervous system wouldn't let you rest tonight.

So the test will be harder. The conversation will be more fragile. The tiredness will make everything feel worse.

This is what you're losing while you try to control what you can't control anyway.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if you slept and still handled it?"
---
You're staying awake to prepare. To get ahead. To make sure tomorrow goes right. But what if being tired makes tomorrow harder? What if the preparation you're doing right now is actually making it worse?

What if you slept and just trusted tomorrow-you to handle it? What if you woke up tired but present instead of exhausted and already defeated?

What if the anxiety needs sleep more than it needs preparation?

What if lying awake worrying is the opposite of actually getting ready?

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard

---
At school tomorrow nobody will know that you've been awake since 1:15am. They won't know you spent the night worried. They'll just see someone who's moving through the day like everyone else. They won't see the exhaustion. They won't see the cost.

What they'll see is you taking the test, having the conversation, moving through the day. And they'll think you're fine.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You've survived every day you lost sleep before."
---
Last week you were awake at 2am before the test. The week before that, before the presentation. The month before that, before the thing with your friends. You've lost sleep dozens of times before hard days and you've still shown up. You've still done the thing. You've still survived.

You've never actually fallen apart. You've just convinced yourself you would, and then you didn't.

Tomorrow you'll be tired and you'll still move through it. You'll still take the test. You'll still be there. The anxiety has been right about the difficulty, but it's been wrong about your ability to handle it.

You're still here. Still showing up. Still moving forward even when you haven't slept.

That's what you do.
