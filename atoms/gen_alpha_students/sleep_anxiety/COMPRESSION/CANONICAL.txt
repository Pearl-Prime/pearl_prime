
## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C2
The last thing the brain saw before bed was a screen. The first thing it sees in the morning is a screen. Sleep happens in the gap between. The gap got smaller as the screens got better at holding attention. The body that grew up with this gap does not know what a fully un-screened evening feels like. That unknowing is not personal. It is generational.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C3
Your circadian rhythm is being trained by lights designed to be more interesting than darkness. Melatonin is suppressed by the same blue spectrum your room glows with at midnight. The body cannot tell that the light source is small and personal. To the eye, it reads as sun. The sun should not be on at midnight. The body responds accordingly.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C5
Try this: charge the phone in another room. Not the bedroom. Another room. The argument against doing this is convenience. The argument for doing this is sleep. The body cannot win against the phone in the same room. It was not designed to. Removing the device from the room is the only fair fight.
