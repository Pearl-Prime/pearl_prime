

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I replay conversations with friends. Did I say something weird? Do they think I'm weird? Are they going to tell other people? And these thoughts spiral. The mechanism is called social rumination—repetitive analysis of social interactions looking for threat.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is that I overthink things that happened a long time ago. A comment I made years ago that nobody else probably remembers. But my mind brings it back up. And I analyze it. And I feel embarrassed all over again. The mechanism is called temporal rumination—overthinking about past events that can't be changed.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a specific kind of overthinking that happens when I'm supposed to be doing something I find boring. My mind suddenly becomes very active. Looking for threats. Analyzing past situations. Planning for future problems. The mechanism is called distraction-driven rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I notice that I overthink most about things I care about. School. Friendships. My identity. My future. The things that matter most create the most rumination. The mechanism is called importance-level rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What I'm realizing is that some of my overthinking is actually anxiety trying to solve a problem. Like if I think about it enough, I'll be prepared. If I analyze it enough, I'll prevent bad outcomes. But analysis doesn't actually prevent anything. The mechanism is anxiety masquerading as planning.
---

## REFLECTION v06
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
---
There's a part of my overthinking that's about perfectionism. I'll ruminate about something until I feel like I've figured it out perfectly. But perfect understanding is impossible. So the rumination never actually stops. The mechanism is called perfectionism-driven rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v07
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I've noticed that I overthink more when I'm tired or stressed. My cognitive resources are depleted. And my mind seems to prefer rumination over the kind of clear thinking that could actually help. The mechanism is called resource-depletion rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v08
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
What strikes me is how my overthinking creates more problems. I worry about something. The worry causes stress. The stress makes me less able to focus. And then I have something to worry about for real. The mechanism is worry-creating-reality.
---

## REFLECTION v09
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There's something about having access to information that feeds overthinking. I can research any topic. I can find information about any possible problem. And this feeds the sense that I should be able to think my way to solutions. But I can't. The mechanism is information-abundance rumination.
---

## REFLECTION v10
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm learning that my overthinking is sometimes about avoiding feeling things directly. Instead of feeling sadness, I think about why I'm sad. Instead of feeling fear, I analyze the threat. The mechanism is thought-as-avoidance.
---
