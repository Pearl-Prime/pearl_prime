## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your stomach was tight when you sent it."
---
You sent the text in the group chat three hours ago. Something about the homework assignment. You thought it was funny. Now you're replaying the moment you hit send. The exact words. The way they might land. Whether anyone even laughed.

Your phone is in your hand. Your stomach is tight. You're checking the chat again even though nothing new has happened. Your shoulders are up around your ears like you're braced for something.

But nothing is happening. The text is already sent. You can't change it now. The room is the same room. You're breathing the same breath. Your stomach tightens every time you think about their reaction, but the reaction already happened hours ago or it hasn't happened yet and won't.

Notice your feet on the ground. The text is done. Your body is here now.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "This is hours you're not getting back."
---
Three hours analyzing a text you sent. The tone, the intent, the possible interpretations. You've reread it seventeen times. You've imagined twelve different group reactions. You've mentally written three different follow-up messages to soften it or clarify it or take it back.

This costs you. Not the text. The analysis. The hours of attention that could have gone to homework, to friends, to literally anything else. The night you're spending re-auditing a moment that took six seconds to create.

It's a lot of hours. It's a lot of nervous energy. It's the part of you that won't stop performing even when the audience already knows what you said.

The text was what it was. This anxiety is something you're adding.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if they didn't even notice?"
---
You've been analyzing their silence like it's a message. No heart emoji. No response yet. But what if they're just busy? What if they're not actually upset, not actually thinking less of you, not actually going to bring it up tomorrow?

What if you said something fine and you're the only one worried about it?

What if the group chat moved on and they completely forgot about your text?

What if nobody noticed except you?
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "They already moved to the next thing."
---
The group is already past your text. They're talking about something else now. They're laughing at something new. They've forgotten about the thing you sent three hours ago.

But you haven't forgotten. You're still in the moment. You're still trying to correct it, understand it, fix it. You're still performing. You're still worried.

They probably think you're fine. They probably didn't see anything wrong with what you said. They probably forgot it two minutes after they read it.

They've moved on. You're the only one still there.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You've survived every weird text you sent."
---
Last week you sent something you worried about all day. The week before that, something else. Every single time you've convinced yourself it was so bad and everyone noticed and tomorrow will be disaster. And every single time, nothing happened. People moved on. You're still in the group. You're still invited.

You've never actually damaged it. You've just convinced yourself you did, over and over, and then been proven wrong by time.

This text will be the same. You'll worry tonight and tomorrow and maybe the next day. And then it will be forgotten and you'll remember something else to worry about.

You're still here. Still part of it. Still moving forward.
---
