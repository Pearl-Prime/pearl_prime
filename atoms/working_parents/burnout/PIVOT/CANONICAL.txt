## PIVOT v01
---

---
You think you are tired. Tired has a fix. What you have is the slow erosion of doing two jobs -- one that pays and one that does not -- with no shift change, no weekends off, and no performance review that captures both. Burnout in working parenthood is not dramatic. It is the quiet death of enthusiasm.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says burnout means you need to work less. The new frame: you need fewer roles, not fewer hours. You are the employee, the parent, the partner, the household manager, the scheduler, the emotional regulator. Burnout happens when five jobs live in one body.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Everyone sees productivity. Nobody sees the cost -- the bedtime you missed for the deadline, the deadline you missed for the sick kid, the guilt that runs underneath both like a second operating system. The pivot: burnout is not about capacity. It is about guilt running in the background consuming resources.
---
