

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
My daughter asked me something yesterday and I wasn't there. Not physically. I was sitting at the table. But mentally, I was three hours in the future, running through the presentation I have tomorrow. My body was present. My presence was absent.
This is the burnout mechanism for working parents: the fragmentation of self. The automatic division of attention. The belief that you can be in two places at once if you're just strategic enough about how you distribute your consciousness.
The mechanism says: work requires your best thinking. Parenting requires your presence. You can't give both fully. So distribute the limited attention you have. Take from parenting so you can give to work. Take from yourself so you can give to both.
And you organize your time like a strategist. Like you can hack the system through better scheduling. Like presence is just another allocation problem.
But presence isn't strategic. Presence is whole or it's absent. And the mechanism ensures that you're partially absent from both roles. Not fully at work. Not fully with your kids. Burned out in the middle of the split.
What happens when you've optimized your time so much that there's no time left to actually live in the moments you've scheduled?
