## INTEGRATION v01
---
---
Carry one habit into tomorrow: before your hardest undated task — a form, a call, an email — decide its first physical action tonight, while your judgment is clear and the resistance isn't yet in the room, and write it on a card by the door. Not the whole form. The one field that opens it. When tomorrow's exhaustion settles in, you won't have to decide anything — the deciding is already done, sitting on the card, and you only have to do the small thing it names.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
---
Pick the one task that has stalled the longest and pair it, tomorrow, to a routine your household already runs without being asked — right after teeth brushing, right after the kids' show ends, right after the coffee finishes brewing. Attach the stalled task to that anchor and do the smallest possible unit of it right there, in the anchor's wake. The anchor becomes the trigger your discipline was never going to manufacture alone on no sleep.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
---
Tomorrow, when you catch yourself toggling between three open, equally urgent household tasks, stop and close two of them entirely, not later, immediately. Work only the one left open until it's finished or genuinely blocked. This will feel like dropping the other two. It isn't. It's the difference between one thing actually done and three things half-touched by bedtime.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
---
Before you fall asleep tonight, name one task you've opened and closed more than three times this week without finishing, and write, in one sentence, the smallest physical first move that would start it — not the plan, the move. Put that sentence somewhere you'll see it before the morning rush starts. The goal isn't to finish it tomorrow. It's to prove the freeze breaks at the small step, every time, if the step is small enough.
---
