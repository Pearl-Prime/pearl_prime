Lena has nine household tasks that all genuinely need doing this week, no clean way to rank them, and by Wednesday the sheer number has produced not motion but its opposite, a total stall, because her brain, offered nine equal-weight demands, cannot pick a first one and so picks none. She finally writes all nine on a sticky note, closes her eyes, and points, and the arbitrariness is the entire unlock, because a started task, any started task, generates the momentum a chosen task only promised.
