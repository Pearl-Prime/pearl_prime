## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Initiation freeze at the kitchen counter is not a discipline gap; it's a missing external trigger. You start instantly for a real emergency and stall completely on anything vague or bureaucratic. Manufacture the trigger — a timer, a phone call, a habit-anchor — and the wall you called disorganization becomes a single step you can take.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Responding fast to a crisis and choosing among four equally undated forms are different skills. You have the first. The freeze on the second is not a contradiction in your parenting. It's proof the two draw on different systems.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
The form you've been avoiding is not too big for your organization. It's missing a first move small enough for your depleted attention to grab. Name the checkbox, not the form, and the stall usually breaks.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Moralizing the stall — telling yourself a better parent would have done it already — is both unkind and inaccurate, and shame is a poor fuel for starting anything on four hours of sleep. Structure fixes what shame cannot.
---
