

## HOOK v01
---

---
You grieve the person you were before this. The one with time, with agency, with a sense of self that wasn't defined by someone else's needs. That person didn't die. She disappeared.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your relationship used to be ours. Now it's theirs—the kids'. You're business partners managing operations instead of lovers. The partnership you built together got swallowed whole.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Grief lives in the creative space you used to have. The band equipment in the garage. The novel outline in a drawer. The hobby that wasn't practical and fed your soul. That part of you is dead.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You see your reflection and there's a stranger in the mirror. When did you become the person who doesn't go out, doesn't create, doesn't dream. That version of you is gone.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The grief is quiet because you're not supposed to mourn your own life. You're supposed to be grateful. So you grieve silently while performing contentment.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
Your partner looks at you like they don't know you anymore. You don't know you either. The person you were is a ghost that follows you through the house.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Grief arrives when you realize you can't be both. You can't be the person you were and the parent you're supposed to be. So you chose and now you mourn.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You feel it in your hands. The hands that used to create, to make things, to do things for yourself. Now they're always available for someone else.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The life you planned is not the life you're living. Not worse, not better, just different. And you're grieving the version that never happened while managing the one that did.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Your creative self is shelved indefinitely. It wasn't a thing you chose to give up. It was a thing that got erased in the wake of children. The loss is enormous and nobody talks about it.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
You grieve the couple you used to be. The inside jokes, the spontaneity, the time to just be together. Now there's logistics and co-management and a deep distance in the same bed.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
The grief is complicated because you wanted this. You chose this. The guilt about grieving makes the grief worse.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Your body remembers who you were. The way you moved through the world before. The confidence that's been replaced by constant self-doubt. Your body is mourning.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
You mourn your freedom in small moments. Your solo shower interrupted. Your morning alone gone. Your afternoon with a friend canceled. Freedom is something you used to take for granted.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
The hobbies are gone. The band, the writing, the art, the sport. The thing that made you feel like yourself. That version of yourself is archived.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
You're grieving your partnership while you're still in it. Your partner is here and you're missing them anyway. Missing who you used to be together.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
Grief is the ache when you remember what sleep felt like. Uninterrupted, restorative, yours. Now sleep is a thing that happens in fragments and you're never actually rested.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
Your career became a necessary evil instead of a passion. The ambition you had is something you grieve quietly. You're successful enough and unfulfilled anyway.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The person you were wouldn't recognize the person you've become. And you're mourning that loss while you're busy keeping small humans alive.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
Grief settles in when you realize the shelf you put your dreams on was a permanent storage unit. The book is never getting written. The band is not getting back together. That life is closed.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You mourn your body. The way it used to be, move, belong to you. Now it's a resource for everyone else. You're grieving your physical autonomy.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The grief is loudest at night when everyone is asleep. That's when you remember who you used to be. That's when the loss feels most real.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
Your identity collapsed into Mom and Dad. The person before that is a memory. You're grieving that version even as you're building this one.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
You see your friend who didn't have kids and there's a sharp pang. Not envy exactly. Grief. Grief for the version of your life that didn't require this particular sacrifice.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
Grief is the feeling when you realize this is your life now. Not a phase, not a temporary season. This is the life you're building and the life you're mourning simultaneously.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
The relationship intimacy you had is gone. Not dead, just transformed into something you don't recognize. You're mourning the knowing of your partner that got buried under logistics.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
Your solitude is gone. Your ability to just be alone with your thoughts. Now your thoughts are managed, scheduled, interrupted. You're grieving the person you could be in silence.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
The grief is that you chose this and you'd choose it again and you're still sad about what you lost. The contradiction is unbearable.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
You mourn the version of your partnership that was just about you two. Before the weight of raising humans. When you had time to know each other.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
The person you were before children is a ghost. She visits sometimes, in dreams, in moments of unexpected freedom. You reach for her and she's already gone.
---
