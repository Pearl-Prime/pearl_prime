## PIVOT v01
---

---
You are grieving moments you missed because the meeting ran long. The first word you heard about secondhand. The recital that started without you. The old frame says those are trade-offs. The new frame: trade-offs have two options. You had one -- the one that paid. Calling it a choice does not make it one.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says you should be grateful for what you have. You are grateful. And you are grieving the version of parenthood you imagined before you realized it required a second income just to survive. Both things are true. The pivot is letting them coexist instead of using gratitude to silence the grief.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Working-parent grief is not about one big loss. It is about the daily micro-losses -- the bedtime you missed, the game you watched on video, the conversation your partner had with your child that you heard about at dinner. Each one is small. Together they form a quiet, persistent ache that nobody names.
