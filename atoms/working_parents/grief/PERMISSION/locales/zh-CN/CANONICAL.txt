**1. Validating the "Invisible Loss" (Permission v01 & v02)**
You have described a state of "existing but not being present," where your pre-child self and aspirations were sealed away. Society often dismisses this as a normal trade-off of parenting, but these permissions name it correctly: **it is a loss.**
*   The ache of missing the recital or the unhurried morning is not a failure of your logistics; it is the grief of the life you *did not get to live*.
*   By allowing yourself to mourn the "unhurried mornings," you stop gaslighting yourself into believing that your current exhaustion is simply "what love looks like." It acknowledges that the version of parenthood you imagined was valid, and its absence leaves a real void.

**2. Dismantling the "Gratitude Trap" (Permission v03)**
Your financial anxiety and professional identity are often hidden under the language of being "responsible" and "rational." Similarly, your emotional numbness is often masked by performing the role of the grateful, capable mother/nurse.
*   This permission breaks the binary that says you must either be grateful for your family/job *or* you are ungrateful/complaining.
*   It allows **grief and gratitude to coexist**. You can love your children and value your career while simultaneously grieving the loss of your autonomy, your creative spark, and your ease. This reduces the cognitive dissonance that fuels your overthinking loops.

**3. A Shift from "Failure" to "Love Registering Loss"**
Your "all-responsibility" mode interprets any gap between expectation and reality as a personal defect ("I should have been there," "I should have managed better").
*   Reframing the ache as **"love registering a loss"** shifts the locus from *performance* to *connection*. It suggests that the pain exists because you care deeply about the moments and the self you’ve lost, not because you failed to execute a perfect schedule.
