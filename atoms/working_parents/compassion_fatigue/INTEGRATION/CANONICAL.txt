

## INTEGRATION v01
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
My shoulders don't rise anymore. Not when she cries before school. Not when I'm asked to problem-solve the friendship drama again. The collapse is so complete it feels like nothing.
I used to think depletion meant exhaustion—that wired, wrung-out feeling. This is different. This is the heaviness of a body that's given every available ounce to small humans who needed me while I smiled at the laptop, switched registers, drove carpools. The emptiness isn't laziness or failure. It's the physiological fact of an account drawn below zero.
The reframe: I haven't become less. I've become mathematically accurate—what's left is what's left when you give everything away.
My chest is a room with nothing in it.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST-UNCOUNTED
---
What this costs goes unwritten. The sleep I've lost to her breathing changes at 2 a.m. The creative thought that used to arrive unbidden, now nowhere. The conversation with my partner that's been on pause for months because we're too wrecked to have it.
I show up, functionally. The kids eat. The house doesn't burn down. The work gets done. But the invisible cost—the part of my attention that's permanently allocated to small humans, the part of myself that's always half-listening for crisis—this calculates to something I haven't named until now.
The reframe: Every moment of presence I've given is real. The absence I feel is also real.
Nothing here is wasted or wrong.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
integration_mode: STILL-HERE
reframe_type: QUESTION-REFRAME
---
I sit in the car after drop-off. The engine's running. I'm not crying. I'm not doing anything. My hands are on the wheel.
The old narrative said: you love your children, so this energy exchange is worth it. You signed up for this. But that math had a variable I didn't account for—the idea that I had something infinite to give.
What if the depletion isn't a personal failing but a structural fact. What if the question isn't "why am I empty" but "what would it mean to replenish anything in this schedule."
I put the car in drive.
Some things need naming before they can shift.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
integration_mode: QUESTION-OPEN
reframe_type: TIME-SHIFT
---
Yesterday I was the person who had thoughts. Not consumed thoughts—thoughts that were mine, that wandered, that belonged to something like curiosity. I can almost remember the texture of that.
Now I'm the person who responds. Reacts. Moves from one small emergency to the next. The compassion fatigue isn't that I've stopped caring. It's that caring has become a reflex so automatic I can't distinguish my own heartbeat from the sound of what I'm giving away.
The reframe: Five years ago, I had bandwidth I didn't know I had. What might I discover about my own capacity if I stopped performing the story of having none.
What could I actually want.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
integration_mode: SOMEONE-ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER-PERSON
---
My friend says she's "just not feeling it anymore." She means the patience. She means the game on the floor where you build the tower the exact right way. She means the question about why the sky is blue asked for the seventh time.
I know what she means because it's the same thing I'm experiencing—not a lack of love but a kind of sensory overload that's past the point of management. Her body has the flat, practiced expression I recognize. The way she's learned to smile without moving anything behind her eyes.
The reframe: What we're experiencing isn't weakness. It's the body's accurate report.
We're running on fumes and calling it normal.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
integration_mode: COST-VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST-UNCOUNTED
---
I can trace exactly how much I've paid. The Saturday mornings that were mine, now shared. The projects I thought I'd finish before 40, now filed under "someday." The part of my sexuality that just... switched off because my body has been touched so many times by small people that I'm touched out by evening.
The cost is visible but we don't talk about it. We talk about guilt instead. We talk about balance, about self-care, about making time for ourselves. But these words don't account for what's actually gone—the reserves, the reserves, the reserves.
The reframe: What I've given is choice. What's empty is the refilling system.
Depletion is not a character flaw.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
integration_mode: BODY-LANDED
reframe_type: BODY-FACT
---
My jaw is tight. Has been for months. I notice it when I'm alone and can finally release. The tension is me bracing—against the next request, the next disappointment, the next moment when someone needs me to be more.
This isn't moral exhaustion. This is what happens to a nervous system that's been in mild activation for so long it's forgotten rest. The body keeps the score. Mine is saying: you cannot pour from an empty vessel because the vessel itself is depleted.
The reframe: The body that's tired isn't broken. It's accurate.
I stop fighting what I already know.
