

## HOOK v01
---

---
You give to your children relentlessly. You give to your partner, to your aging mother sometimes, to your boss, to coworkers asking favors. You give until there's nothing left and then you find something to give anyway.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your child is crying and you have nothing inside. No patience, no warmth, no presence—just a hollow person going through the motions of care. You've given away the part of you that actually loves this.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Compassion fatigue is the flatness that arrives after endless giving. You're here but not present. You're listening but not hearing. You're parenting but you're performing it.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Your reserves have been drawn on for so long you forgot what full felt like. Your emotional tank isn't empty—it's corroded at the bottom. Nothing fills it anymore.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You feel it as numbness in your chest. Someone needs you emotionally and you have no access to feeling. You're running on duty, not love. The love is still there, somewhere, buried under the depletion.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The accumulation happens slowly. One conversation where you held emotional space. Then another. Then your child's homework crisis. Then your partner's work stress. Then your own stress that you managed alone. Then your mother's health scare.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
You're numb to your child's excitement because you're exhausted from absorbing their stress. You can't reach joy anymore because you're drowning in everyone else's feelings.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Compassion fatigue makes you irritable at love. Your child wants closeness and you recoil. Your partner reaches out and you flinch. You're touched out, felt out, drained of the capacity to receive connection.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
Your nervous system is depleted. You have nothing left to regulate your own emotions, let alone soothe anyone else's. But they still need soothing and you still have to try.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
You're giving to people who are still asking. Your child asks for presence and you're elsewhere. Your partner asks for patience and you have sharp words instead. The well is dry and they're thirsty.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
The guilt deepens the fatigue. You're not the empathic parent you wanted to be. You're running on fumes and resentment. You hate yourself for it and that makes the fatigue worse.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
Your capacity diminishes with each demand. Someone needs something and you can feel yourself shrinking. Your bandwidth is already spoken for. There's no space left to receive, only to give from a place of depletion.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
You've been poured from so many cups that your own is not just empty—it's bone dry. And someone is always thirsty.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The emotional labor is invisible until it's gone. Then you realize you have nothing left to offer and everyone is still asking.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
You're tired in a way that sleep doesn't touch. Your fatigue is the depletion of the soul, not the body. Rest doesn't reach where the exhaustion lives.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
Compassion fatigue feels like resentment disguised as exhaustion. You're angry at everyone for needing you, angry at yourself for having nothing left to give.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
Your child comes home and needs your attention. You're already depleted from work, from managing the house, from being the emotional holder in your relationship. There's nothing left but obligation.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You perform care instead of feeling it. The motions are there, but the presence is gone. Your kids don't know the difference yet, but you do.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The accumulation is the problem, not any single demand. Each request for your emotional presence adds a brick. You're buried under thousands of small asks.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
Your body tenses when someone asks how you're doing. You don't have the energy to answer honestly. You have the energy to say fine and move on.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You're running on fumes and pretending it's fine. Your partner doesn't know. Your kids don't know. Only you know that the tank is empty and you're still driving.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
Compassion fatigue makes you selfish and you hate it. You want to stay present for your child and you can't. You want to be there for your partner and you're irritated instead.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
The giving never stops, which means the depletion never stops. There's no endpoint where you finally get to rest. Someone is always thirsty.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
You're numb to kindness because you're exhausted from giving it. When someone offers care, you can't receive it. You flinch from gentleness because you have nothing left to give back.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
The fatigue lives in your shoulders, your neck, your chest. It's not a feeling—it's a state of being. You've become so depleted that emptiness is your baseline.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
Your reserves are depleted and you're still expected to give. Your child cries and you have no compassion left, only duty. Your partner needs you and you're running on empty.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
Compassion fatigue is when you can't access your own love anymore. You know it's there, but there's too much rubble on top. The exhaustion has buried what you care about most.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You're irritable at love and that makes you feel like a monster. The fatigue has made you sharp. You react harshly where you want to respond gently.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
The accumulation of emotional labor has calcified somewhere inside. You're hard now where you used to be soft. The depletion has changed your architecture.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
Everyone is still asking. Your child, your partner, your job, your mother. The depletion doesn't make them stop. It just makes you answer from an emptier place each time.
---
