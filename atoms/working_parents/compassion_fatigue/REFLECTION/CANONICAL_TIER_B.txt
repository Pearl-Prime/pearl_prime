Your empathy is a well someone else is always drawing from. You respond to your child's emotions first. You sense your partner's stress and you absorb it. You hold space for others so skillfully that you've forgotten what it means to ask someone to hold space for you.
This is called compassion fatigue. Not because you're bad at caring, but because you've been caring in a system that only takes. No one is taking turns. No one is replenishing you. You're just slowly running dry.
The mechanism is subtle. You don't notice it happening until suddenly you're empty. You're listening to your child's problems and you feel nothing. You're hearing your partner's request and you want to scream. You're not broken. You're depleted.
Structural emptiness. That's what this is. A system that requires constant giving and provides no input.
