## PIVOT v01
---

---
You used to feel everything your child felt. Now the tantrum lands and you feel nothing, or the colleague tears up and you just wait for it to pass. The old frame says you have become cold. The new frame: your emotional bandwidth has been allocated across too many channels, and the signal is degraded. You are not cold. You are oversubscribed.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Compassion fatigue in working parents looks like patience running out by 6 p.m. -- not because you do not love them but because you have been performing empathy in meetings all day and the tank was empty before you walked in the door. The pivot: the problem is not your caring. It is the sequencing.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame says a good parent never runs out of tenderness. The new frame: tenderness is a renewable resource only if it gets renewed. You have been spending it all day on clients, colleagues, and crises, and then expecting a full reserve for bedtime. That is not a character issue. It is a logistics issue.
---
