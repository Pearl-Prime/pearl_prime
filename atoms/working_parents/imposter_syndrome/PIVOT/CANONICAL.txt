## PIVOT v01
---

---
You feel like a fraud at work because your kid's sick day pulls at your focus, and you feel like a fraud at home because the work email pulls at your presence. The old frame says pick one and commit. The new frame: the imposter feeling is not about your inadequacy. It is about occupying two roles that each demand the full version of you.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old story says real professionals leave their personal life at the door. You cannot, because your personal life sends text messages at 2 p.m. about a fever. Imposter syndrome for working parents is not about competence. It is about a workplace designed for someone without a daycare pickup at 5:30.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You compare yourself to the colleague without kids who stays late and the stay-at-home parent who volunteers at school, and you come up short against both. The pivot: you are being measured by two rulers that were made for different people, and the fact that you stand at all between them is the accomplishment.
