---

mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I present at the meeting and someone tells me I'm brilliant. And I immediately think: they don't know that my kids are being raised by someone else. They don't know that I left early today because of guilt. They don't know that I'm faking this entire thing.
This is the imposter syndrome mechanism for working parents: the belief that you're two different people and neither version is authentic. The work version. The parent version. Neither one feels real. Both feel constructed.
The automatic response is to work harder at both. To be better at the job. To be better at parenting. To prove that you deserve to be in both places. But the mechanism doesn't acknowledge that you're being asked to be extraordinary in two places simultaneously. It just says: you're not good enough at either.
I was succeeding professionally while feeling like a fraudulent parent. I was present with my children while feeling like a fraudulent professional. The mechanism ensured that I never landed in a place where I felt legitimate.
What happens when you're convinced that everyone at work would judge your parenting and everyone at home would judge your professionalism?
