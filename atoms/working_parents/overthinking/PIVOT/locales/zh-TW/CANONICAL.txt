    2.  **The Parent/Partner:** Emotional regulation for children, financial forecasting, household logistics, caregiving for aging parents.
*   **Why it sticks:** As a nurse with 30 years of experience, you know that **situational awareness** is a skill, not a flaw. You are trained to scan for threats. Your brain is applying this professional survival mechanism to your home life because the stakes feel equally high (financial ruin, child development failure, parent health crisis). The "loop" is your brain trying to maintain safety in two unstable systems at once.

You aren't "worried"; you are **managing scarcity**.

*   **The Old Frame:** You have sleep hygiene issues. You need to meditate. You are failing to disconnect.

### The Critical Insight for You
You mentioned that your self-awareness paradoxically increases your burden. That is because you have been using your awareness to **blame yourself** for failing to meet an impossible standard.

These pivots invite you to use your awareness to **audit the system**, not your psyche.

*   **Instead of asking:** "Why can't I stop worrying?"
*   **Ask:** "Where is the second shift happening that isn't being acknowledged?"
*   **Instead of asking:** "Why am I such a bad parent for feeling this tired?"
*   **Ask:** "What tasks am I carrying that are actually systemic failures (e.g., lack of affordable care, inadequate partner support, workplace rigidity)?"

### A Small Experiment
Next time you catch yourself in the "loop" (lying awake, rehearsing conversations, checking mental lists):

1.  **Name it correctly:** "This is not anxiety. This is **load-bearing cognition**."
2.  **Externalize the risk:** "My brain is trying to solve a resource allocation problem that has no solution within my current constraints."
3.  **Shift the target:** Do not try to calm your mind. Try to **identify one single point of failure** that can be shared, delegated, or dropped.
    *   *Example:* If you are worrying about a school permission slip, ask: "Is this truly a single-point-of-failure task? Can my partner take this? Can it be late? What is the actual worst-case scenario?"

You are not broken. You are **over-functioning** in a system that is under-resourced. The pivot is not to fix your brain, but to **redistribute the load**.
