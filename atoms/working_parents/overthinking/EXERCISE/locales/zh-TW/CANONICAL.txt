### Why These Work for Your Specific Profile

1.  **Breaking the "Mental Loop" (Exercise v01 & v03):**
    *   Your brain is a "scenario-generation machine," constantly modeling potential failures. Replaying the snap or the hurtful words is your brain trying to *solve* the threat by predicting the next one.
    *   **The Interrupt:** Physical movement (jumping jacks, walking, tactile tasks) forces your nervous system out of the *sympathetic* (fight/flight/freeze) state and into the *parasympathetic* (rest/digest) state. It signals safety to the body, which the mind then accepts.

2.  **Countering "Performative Certainty" (Exercise v02):**
    *   You often feel pressure to have the "right" answer or to maintain control. In an argument, your brain tries to "win" or "fix" it by rehearsing lines.
    *   **The Interrupt:** Asking "What is actually true *now*?" strips away the performance. It grounds you in objective reality (children are safe, argument is over) rather than the subjective narrative of failure or threat.

3.  **Addressing "Shame as Failure":**
    *   You tend to frame hesitation or emotional slips as personal failure/cowardice. These exercises reframe the moment not as a moral failing, but as a *physiological event* that needs a physiological reset.

### Potential Resistance & Adaptations

*   **"I don’t have time to stop."**
    *   *Your Pattern:* Time belongs to others; responsiveness is your identity.
    *   *Adaptation:* These exercises take <60 seconds. Frame them not as "stopping work" but as "maintenance required for continued high performance." Your nervous system *needs* this reset to avoid the "invisible burnout" you experience.

*   **"Moving feels frivolous/indulgent."**
    *   *Your Pattern:* Achievement is a precursor to danger; rest feels like betrayal.
    *   *Adaptation:* Reframe the movement as *data collection*. You are gathering sensory data (feet on floor, breath in lungs) to counteract the false data your anxiety is generating (catastrophe, abandonment).

*   **"I can’t just 'let go' of the argument."**
    *   *Your Pattern:* Unavailability = unreliability; disagreement = personal attack.
    *   *Adaptation:* Acknowledge that the argument *is* over in real-time, even if it’s not resolved emotionally. Say to yourself: *"I am not abandoning the issue. I am pausing the rehearsal so I can address it effectively later."*
