---
family: F1

mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
I'm driving to work and I'm still mentally replaying the conversation I had with my daughter this morning. Did I say it right? Did she understand? What did her tone mean? Am I being the kind of parent she needs?
This is the overthinking mechanism for working parents: the inability to stop processing. The loop that activates in the car. The loop that continues all day at work. The loop that starts again when you get home.
The mechanism doesn't just think about one thing once. It spins the situation. It looks for alternative explanations. It generates new worries based on new angles. It's never satisfied with understanding. It needs to understand from every possible direction.
The automatic response is to accept the spinning. To work through the analysis. To process until you feel like you've understood. But the mechanism doesn't have a finish line. There's always another angle. Always another way to look at it.
I thought once I understood the situation fully, the anxiety would stop. I'd analyze thoroughly, and then I could move on. But the mechanism doesn't work that way. The analyzing becomes the entire activity. Understanding becomes impossible while overthinking is the goal.
What happens when you're trapped in the analysis of experiences you're too fragmented to actually live?
