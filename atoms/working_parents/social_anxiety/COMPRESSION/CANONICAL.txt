

## COMPRESSION v30
compression_family: C5
Try this: Before the next social moment, ask 'What do I want to know about this person?' instead of 'What will they think of me?' The performance anxiety doesn't mean you're doing it wrong. It means the stakes registered. They're real to you.

## COMPRESSION v31
compression_family: C1
The friendships you had before kids exist in a different timeline. Now there are pickup-line parents you see daily but barely know. There are colleagues who do not have kids and stopped inviting you to things. There are old friends with kids you never see because the schedules do not align. The social field has been redrawn. The exhaustion of the redrawing was not on the moving checklist.

## COMPRESSION v32
compression_family: C3
Every social interaction now competes with what you could be doing at home. The drink with a colleague is the bath with the kids. The dinner with friends is bedtime stories. The performance anxiety is partly that. Partly the math: what did this hour cost. The math is doing its own background processing while you smile through the meeting.
