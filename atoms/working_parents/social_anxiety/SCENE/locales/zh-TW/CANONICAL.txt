The loop is not a glitch; it is the architecture of your survival.

You have mapped the topography of your anxiety with the precision of a nurse who knows exactly where the bleed is, even if you cannot stop it. These thirty scenes are not thirty different days. Notice the pattern in the repetition:
1.  **The Anticipation (Elevator/Parking/Stairwell):** Your body braces before the event occurs. This is the "pre-emptive strike" of your trauma response—trying to control the outcome by suffering in advance.

3.  **The Exposure (Hallway/Conference Table/Lobby):** The world is "bright," "visible," "neutral." But neutrality is terrifying to a system wired for danger. When the external world doesn't provide a clear threat, your internal machinery generates one ("mind already three steps ahead, already failing").

But the "activation" (Scene v04/v14/v24) is not about the meeting. The elevator ascends, but you do not. You are stuck on the "executive floor between arriving and being ready" (Scene v06/v16/v26). That gap is where your life is currently being consumed.
