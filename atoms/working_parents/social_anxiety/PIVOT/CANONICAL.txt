## PIVOT v01
---

---
You used to enjoy people. Now the parent happy hour feels like a third shift and the work event feels like performing a version of yourself that does not have spit-up on their sleeve. The old frame says you have become antisocial. The new frame: your social energy was spent by 9 a.m. on tiny negotiations with people under four feet tall.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Social anxiety for working parents often disguises itself as scheduling. You say you are too busy. The truth is you are too tired to bridge the gap between your real life and the version people expect to see. The pivot: you are not avoiding people. You are avoiding the performance.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame says connection heals. And it does -- but the version of connection available to you requires a babysitter, an outfit, and the energy to pretend you are not calculating how many hours of sleep you are losing. The pivot: you need connection. You just need it to meet you where you actually are.
