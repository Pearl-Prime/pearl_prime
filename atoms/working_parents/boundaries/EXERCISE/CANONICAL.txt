## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Your boss messages you. Kids asleep now.
Your impulse: respond to the message right.
Pause. The message will still exist tomorrow.
Your sleep is not a luxury right.
Name what your family actually needs here.
Rest so you can show up tomorrow.
Close the app. The email can wait.
You are not on-call after kids sleep.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Your kid just had a meltdown.
Now they need something. You want snap.
Pause. Breathe. You are overwhelmed, not bad.
This is not an emergency requiring instant.
What do they actually need versus want?
Name one boundary you need to keep.
You can say no. You can wait.
Your limits protect both you and them.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Meeting is scheduled. Kids need pickup same.
Your default: you will figure out how.
Stop. This is not your problem alone.
State the conflict clearly to manager.
Propose a solution: different time or day.
You are asking, not apologizing, sacrificing family.
Your commitment to work is real. Parenting.
Both matter. Both require saying what needs.
