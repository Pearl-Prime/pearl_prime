

## HOOK v01
---

---
Your child needs you endlessly and you let them. Your partner needs you endlessly and you let them. Your job needs you endlessly and you let them, until there's no you left to give from.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You said no once and felt guilt for three days. The guilt was worse than whatever you refused, so now you say yes to everything and resent everyone for it.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
The word "no" tastes like abandonment in your mouth. You learned somewhere that love means endless availability. Now you're available to everyone and invisible to yourself.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Your child climbs into bed at midnight and you don't send them back. Your partner sighs about the work hours and you adjust again. Your boss assumes you'll do it all and you do.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
Boundaries require saying no and watching someone be sad. You can't do it, so instead you say yes and watch yourself disappear. The math works better that way.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You feel it as tightness across your shoulders. Someone needs something and you're already giving before they finish asking. The boundary line was erased so long ago you forgot it existed.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Your child needs 300 things before dinner. Your partner needs your emotional labor. Your job needs your availability after hours. You're a resource people draw from until empty.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You can't say no to your child's need without thinking you're selfish. You can't claim your own time without guilt. You can't disappoint anyone without believing you've failed.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The boundary started as a whisper: I need something for myself. You immediately negotiated with yourself about why that was unreasonable. The boundary never stood a chance.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Your time is not your own and you've agreed to this arrangement. You're the manager of everyone else's lives, which means your own is managed by everyone else.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
Someone is always at the edge of you needing something. A small hand, a text message, an email flagged urgent. You've learned to live fragmented.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
You draw a line and your child's disappointment erases it. Your partner's frustration blurs it. Your job's demands ignore it. The lines you set are written in disappearing ink.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Boundaries mean choosing yourself once and disappointing three people. You've done the math: it's not worth it. So you continue choosing them and disappoint the most important person: yourself.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
Your body holds the boundary your words can't maintain. You're aching, tired, irritated—signals that the floor is gone. But the signals are so constant you think they're normal now.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
You're the person everyone comes to because you never say no. You're proud of this and furious about this in equal measure.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
The boundary violation feels like daily practice now. Someone needs you, you show up, you resent it, you do it again. The cycle is so practiced it's automatic.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
You wanted to raise kids with healthy boundaries. You're modeling something different: a parent with no floor, giving until empty, teaching them that love means erasure.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
Your phone buzzes during the only moment that was yours. You could ignore it. You won't, because someone might need you and that's more important than your moment.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
The resentment builds because the boundary stays down. You're angry at everyone: your child, your partner, your boss, yourself. You're doing this to yourself and you can't stop.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
You feel it first as tension in your neck. The refusal building, the word forming, then the guilt arriving and swallowing the no before it reaches your mouth.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
Someone asks and you say yes before thinking. Your body answers before your mind catches up. You've been saying yes so long it's your default language.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
Boundaries require disappointing people you love. You've decided you can't do this, so instead you disappoint yourself. The math feels easier when you're the only variable.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
Your partner expects your availability. Your child expects your presence. Your job expects your responsiveness. You expect nothing from anyone because you're too busy managing their expectations.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
The floor disappeared so gradually you didn't notice. One concession became two. Two became five. Now you're standing on nothing and wondering how to ask for it back.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
You learned that boundaries are selfish, so you don't have any. You learned that love is endless, so you keep giving. You learned that your needs matter least, so you stopped naming them.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
Your exhaustion is a signal that the boundary is gone. Instead of rebuilding it, you just get more tired. The fatigue becomes your new baseline.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
You know where the boundary should be. You can feel it missing. You can articulate it perfectly—just not to anyone who matters. The words won't form under pressure.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
The guilt arrives before the boundary even exists. You're defensive, anticipatory, already negotiating why someone else's need matters more than your own.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
You set a boundary and then talk yourself out of it within hours. The guilt wins. The need of others wins. You win nothing and call it love.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
Your shoulders drop slightly whenever everyone's needs are temporarily satisfied. You're not relaxed, you're just catching your breath before the next round of demands.
---
