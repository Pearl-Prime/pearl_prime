## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Someone always needs something right now."
---
You said this was your hour. This one hour was supposed to be yours. And then the text came. The request from work. The question from your kid. The thing someone needs right now.

Your body tenses. Your shoulders go up. You're not angry — you're just gone, just immediately responsive, just back in the logic of everyone else's needs.

The hour is still technically happening but you've left it already. You're in the request, in the response, in the thing you're solving. Your body is here but your attention is gone.

Notice your hands. They're still. You haven't actually moved yet. The hour is still here. The boundary is still open.

But you can feel it closing.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You're never actually unreachable."
---
You tried to carve out one hour. You set the boundary. You told everyone. And then someone needed something so you dropped the boundary.

This is the cost of the boundary attempt — the guilt of saying no and then saying yes. The hour is gone. Your own need is gone. Someone else's need is met and you're right back where you were before.

You can't actually have what's yours because someone else's urgent feels more urgent than your need. So the boundary becomes a suggestion. Your hour becomes negotiable.

And you're back to having nothing that's yours.

That's what the broken boundary costs you.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN

weight: standard
carry_line: "What if they could wait one hour?"
---
You're assuming the request is an emergency. But what if it's not? What if it can wait the sixty minutes? What if the person asking can actually handle it or solve it themselves?

What if you're jumping to respond out of habit instead of actual urgency?

What if the boundary you set was actually more important than the thing interrupting it?

What if you just said, "I'll help in an hour when my time is done"?
