## PIVOT v01
---

---
The old frame says boundaries are selfish -- especially for parents. The meeting needs you. The kids need you. The partner needs you. The new frame: every time you skip the boundary, you are borrowing from tomorrow's version of you to fund today's version of everyone else. That loan has interest.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You think saying no at work means you are not committed and saying no at home means you are not present. The pivot: saying no is the only thing that makes your yes mean anything. Without limits, you are not generous. You are depleted.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame: availability equals love. The new frame: the parent who is always available is not more loving. They are more exhausted. And exhaustion is not a parenting strategy.
