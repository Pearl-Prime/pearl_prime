## PIVOT v01
---

---
You think courage is quitting the job or making the big change. But your courage is quieter -- it is the Monday morning when you drop off a crying toddler, walk into a meeting with mascara still intact, and do not let either world see the seam. That is not small. That is relentless.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says brave parents sacrifice everything. The new frame: the bravest thing you can do is refuse to sacrifice yourself. Staying whole in a system that wants all of you is not selfishness. It is the hardest kind of courage.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Courage for working parents is not one heroic act. It is the daily negotiation between what you want to give your kids and what the world demands of your time, resolved imperfectly every single day without anyone handing you a medal. That repetition is the bravery.
---
