So, let us make this concrete. We are moving from the *concept* of courage to the *mechanics* of the request.

**The Request:**
It must be small enough to be safe, but specific enough to break the pattern. It cannot be a grand declaration of change (which triggers your fear of instability). It must be a micro-boundary.

**Who is the target?**
1.  **The Partner:** Not to ask them to "fix" you, but to redistribute the *mental load*. Example: "I am at capacity. I need you to take full ownership of [specific task/decision] this week without my input or reminder."
2.  **The Boss/System:** Not to quit, but to reclaim agency. Example: "I cannot take on [specific extra duty]. I will focus on [core duty] to ensure quality care." (This challenges your imposter syndrome directly by asserting your professional judgment over the expectation of endless availability.)
3.  **The Self:** The hardest request. To stop the "performance of care." Example: "For 15 minutes, I will not be useful. I will sit. I will not solve. I will not plan."

**The Barrier:**
Your mind will immediately generate reasons why this request is selfish, risky, or impractical. This is the "structural emptiness" speaking—the belief that you are only valuable when you are depleted.

**The Next Step:**
Choose **one** request. Write it down exactly as you would say it. Do not analyze its likely outcome. Do not predict their reaction. Just write the words.
