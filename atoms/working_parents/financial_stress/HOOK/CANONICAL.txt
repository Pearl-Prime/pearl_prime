

## HOOK v01

---

---

---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Financial stress lives in your stomach. A constant knot that tightens when the bills arrive. You're earning $100K and somehow you're still behind.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
The spreadsheet shows how underwater you are. Mortgage, childcare, insurance, food, the car that's about to break. There's no category for yourself.

## HOOK v05
---

---
Your spouse works and you work and somehow you still can't afford the life you're supposed to be able to afford. The guilt multiplies because you should be grateful for two incomes.

## HOOK v06
---

---

---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Financial stress is the conversation you're not having. Your partner knows, you know, but you don't say it out loud. The silence makes it worse.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Your child asks for something small and your stomach clenches. Not because you can't afford it, but because you're keeping a running tally of every dollar that leaves your account.

## HOOK v09
---

---
The debt feels like a third child you're caring for. Feeding it, managing it, lying awake at night thinking about it. It's relentless and it doesn't love you back.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
You're making good money and you're stressed about money. The guilt about that stress is its own separate burden. You're supposed to be fine. You're not fine.

## HOOK v11
---

---
Your shoulders tense when you open your email. There's an invoice, a bill, something unexpected. You're bracing for the financial blow before you even read it.

## HOOK v12
---

---
Financial stress is the tension between your partner that nobody names. One of you spent $50 on something unnecessary and now you're both angry. The anger is about money, but it's really about survival.

## HOOK v13
---

---

## HOOK v14
---

---
Your child's medical bills are piling up. Insurance covered what it covered and you're responsible for the rest. You're already underwater and the water is rising.

## HOOK v15

---

## HOOK v16
---

---
Financial stress makes you resentful of your kids. They cost so much. They cost everything. The guilt about that resentment is another expense you can't afford.

## HOOK v17
---

---

## HOOK v18
---

---
Your chest tightens when your partner mentions vacation. You know you need it. You can't afford it. You can't afford not to go either.

## HOOK v19

---
The financial stress is affecting your health. Your body is keeping score. You need a therapist you can't afford, a massage you can't afford, rest you can't afford.

## HOOK v20
---

---
You're lying in bed calculating whether you can retire. The number is terrifying. You do the math again hoping for a different answer. The answer is always no.

## HOOK v21
---

---
Financial stress is the background radiation of your life. It's in every decision, every conversation, every moment of rest that's interrupted by a thought about money.

## HOOK v22

---

## HOOK v23

---

## HOOK v24
---

---

## HOOK v25
---

---

## HOOK v26

---
Your partner wants to talk about finances and you want to disappear. The conversation is always difficult because the numbers are always scary. Avoidance is your strategy and it's working terribly.

## HOOK v29

---
Financial stress is the argument you had with your partner at midnight. Not about the money directly, but about how tired you are of thinking about money.
