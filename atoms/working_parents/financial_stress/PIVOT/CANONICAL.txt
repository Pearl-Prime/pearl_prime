## PIVOT v01
---

---
Financial stress used to mean a bad month. Now it means a permanent calculation running in the background -- what the kids cost, what the job pays, what the gap requires you to sacrifice. The reframe: this is not a willpower problem. It is the arithmetic of a society that charges premium prices for the basics of raising a family.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The old frame says if you just earned more or spent less, the stress would stop. The new frame: you are trying to fund a family on a system built for singles and couples. The stress is not about your financial literacy. It is about a cost structure that never adjusted for the life you are actually living.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You work to provide. You spend to parent. The guilt lives in the gap between those two. The pivot: financial stress for working parents is not about money itself. It is about the impossible choice between investing time or money in your children when both are inadequate.
---
