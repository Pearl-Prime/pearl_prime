

## HOOK v01
---

---
The postpartum depression stayed and you stopped talking about it. Five years later, you're still flat. You're still going through the motions of being the parent you're supposed to be.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
Your child laughs and you feel nothing inside. Not sad, not happy—just a gray space where feeling used to live. You smile anyway because that's what you're supposed to do.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Depression is performing motherhood while feeling nothing. You're feeding them, driving them, loving them, and there's a wall of cotton between you and it all.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Your hands are moving through the day but you're not there. Breakfast, work, pickup, dinner, bedtime—all happening without you. You're witnessing your own life.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The flatness arrived and never left. Joy feels like a lie now. You know it exists somewhere but you can't reach it from where you're standing.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
You're exhausted in a way that has nothing to do with sleep. Your body is heavy. Your thoughts are slow. You're dragging yourself through hours that feel like underwater swimming.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Depression makes you a mother without the warmth. You're present but hollow. You're here but not really here. Nobody knows because you're still doing the work.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Your child tells you about their day and you hear it like it's happening in another room. You respond appropriately but there's no you in the response.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
The gray fog settled after birth and never lifted. It's been so long you forgot what color feels like. Now everything is muffled, distant, happening to someone else.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
Maternal burnout disguised itself as depression. You stopped being able to tell the difference. Now you're just moving through each day because that's what you do.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
Your body aches with a heaviness that isn't physical. It's the weight of performing wellness while feeling nothing. It's the effort of standing upright when gravity feels too strong.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
You're going through the motions so perfectly that nobody sees you're gone. Your children think you're fine. Your partner thinks you're fine. You know the truth and you're keeping it.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
Depression makes the smallest task enormous. Getting out of bed. Getting the kids dressed. Making dinner. These things should be automatic and they require every ounce of energy.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The flatness is worse than sadness. Sadness means you're still feeling. Flatness means you're numb to everything, even the things that should make you happy.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
Your nervous system has learned to function in flat mode. You're efficient, competent, and completely disconnected from your own life. This is what autopilot looks like.

## HOOK v16
---

---
You love your kids and feel nothing about it. The love is in your actions, in your choices, but not in your heart. Your heart is gray.

## HOOK v17

---
Depression is the wall between you and joy. You can see it from the other side. Your kids are happy and you're standing behind glass, watching.

## HOOK v18
---

---
Your child hugs you and you feel the weight of it. Not the warmth, not the love—just the physical heaviness. Your body is here but it doesn't feel like you.

## HOOK v19

---
The fog is so thick now you don't remember what clarity felt like. Everything is muted, slowed down, happening behind glass. You're parenting from underwater.

## HOOK v20
---

---
You're exhausted before you even wake up. The day stretches ahead and it's too much. Everything is too much and you have to do it anyway.

## HOOK v21
---

---
Depression sits in your chest like concrete. You're moving, breathing, functioning, but there's a weight that nothing lifts. Even good moments feel heavy.

## HOOK v22

---

## HOOK v23

---

## HOOK v24

---

## HOOK v25

Maternal depression is invisible because you're still showing up. You're still parenting, still working, still performing the role so well that nobody sees the emptiness inside.

## HOOK v26

---
Your body is here with your children but your presence isn't. You're ghosting through your own motherhood. It's functional and it's heartbreaking.

## HOOK v27

---
The depression lives as heaviness in your limbs. Moving through the day requires effort. Everything is effort. Even rest doesn't touch the exhaustion.

## HOOK v28
---

---
You're numb to the good things and hyperaware of the failures. Depression is selective—it kills joy and amplifies guilt. It's a cruel algorithm.

## HOOK v29

---
The gray fog was temporary when it arrived. You kept waiting for it to lift. Now you're not sure it ever will. Now you're learning to parent inside it.

## HOOK v30

---
Your children won't remember this year as depressed. They'll remember a normal year with a parent who showed up. That's the heartbreak of it: you're disappearing while still being present.
