You read the bedtime book and cannot recall a page of it, because you spent it drafting tomorrow's form. The reading happened; you weren't fully there for it. Notice where your attention actually was, not where your hands were.

## THREAD v02
---
---
You override your own tiredness constantly because the day demands it. Notice that the override has a cost, paid quietly, in moments with your kid that happened without you fully in them.

## THREAD v03
---
---
You've gotten so good at managing the logistics of parenting — forms, appointments, schedules — that you've quietly stopped registering the moments the logistics were supposed to protect. Notice the gap.
---

## THREAD v04
---
---
Your nervous system can't tell a feared phone call from a real one. Notice, once, what happens when you let the actual moment land before you brace for the one that hasn't arrived.
