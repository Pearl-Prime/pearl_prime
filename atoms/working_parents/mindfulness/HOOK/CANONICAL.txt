## HOOK v01
---
---
You are at the kitchen table doing the bedtime reading, your voice going through the words in the right rhythm, your arm around the small warm weight of your kid, and you are not there. You are three tasks ahead, half-drafting tomorrow's daycare form in your head, and your kid says wait, you skipped a page, and you have to admit you don't know, because your body did the reading and the rest of you left the room.
---

## HOOK v02
---
---
Count what's actually running right now, underneath whatever you're doing this second: the school form due Friday, the thing you forgot to say to your partner, the number on the daycare invoice that was higher than you expected, the load of laundry that's been damp in the machine since this morning. All of it drawing current, all day, and you wonder why a single crying jag at 6pm lands like the whole day collapsing at once.
---

## HOOK v03
---
---
Your whole evening is a wall of tasks, back to back, dinner then bath then bedtime then the dishes then the lunches for tomorrow, and you have made a quiet mistake: you think you're present in each one because your hands are doing the motions. Your attention is actually in the seam, the seam between bath and bedtime, already at the next task while your hands finish the current one. This is a chapter about the seams.
---

## HOOK v04
---
---
Your kid is telling you something that matters to them, something about a friend at school, and you catch yourself nodding at the wrong beat, half a second behind, because part of you is still checking the clock for how much bedtime buffer you have left. You drag your attention back. This is the whole practice, smaller than a breath: noticing you left, and choosing to come back. Nobody stays. The coming back is the entire skill.
---

## HOOK v05
---
---
The pediatrician's office calls back with results and your whole chest does the thing before you even answer, a fast flat drumming, and your mind is already three scenarios ahead of whatever they're about to say. It's nothing serious. You hang up and realize you didn't hear the reassurance the first time because you were bracing for news that never came. Where did you go? You were standing right here, and then you weren't.
