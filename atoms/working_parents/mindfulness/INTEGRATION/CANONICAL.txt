## INTEGRATION v01
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one recurring doorway and make it your return: the threshold when you walk in from work, the first minute of bath time, the moment you sit down for bedtime reading. Choose one, and every time you cross it, take one breath and name one true physical fact — the weight of your kid against you, the actual temperature of the room, the sound the water is making. You will forget most days. That's the baseline every exhausted parent starts from. The point isn't to be present the whole evening. It's to install one small, reliable return so that at least once, in the middle of the rush, you actually arrive where you already are.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
---
Carry one habit forward: the next time your kid says watch this or look, give it three full seconds of your actual eyes before you respond, not a glance while still doing something else. Three seconds. You'll feel the pull to multitask through it; that pull is the whole pattern. Do it once tomorrow, on whatever comes — a jump, a drawing, a fact about dinosaurs — and notice what you catch that you would have missed at a half-glance.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
---
Pick the hardest transition in your evening — pickup, or the start of dinner, or bedtime — and set one rule for its first sixty seconds: hands off the phone, attention only on your kid's face. Not the whole evening, just the opening, the part you currently lose to logistics and bracing. Notice what you catch in those sixty seconds that you'd normally miss while still arriving mentally from wherever you were.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
---
Tonight, before you fall asleep, name one moment today you weren't actually present for — a bath, a story, a small thing your kid told you — and give it thirty seconds now, retroactively, replaying it as fully as you can recall. It will feel strange, reaching back for something you half-missed. Do it anyway. It proves the moment was there the whole time, and that you have the capacity to catch it next time, in real time, if you choose to.
