## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Mindfulness for a parent running on no sleep is not calm; it's accuracy. The racing mind narrates the school form, the invoice, tomorrow's logistics — all real, none of it happening right now. Returning attention to the small hand in yours strips the narration and leaves the actual moment, the one you'll actually remember.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
The exhaustion of a full parenting day is not only from the hours. It's from bracing into each task and releasing out of it with no moment actually inhabited in between. Presence doesn't slow the day down. It's the difference between surviving it and having been in it.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
Your kid can tell, without being able to name it, when part of you is somewhere else. The tugging, the repeated question, the story that trails off — these are data, not neediness. They're telling you where your attention actually was.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Noticing without fixing is the hardest move for a parent, because your whole day is fixing. Let the tension at the table just be there for one breath before you try to solve it. It often loosens faster without the fight.
---
