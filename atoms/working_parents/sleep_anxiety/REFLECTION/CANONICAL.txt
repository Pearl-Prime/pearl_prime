I lie down and the day refuses to follow me into bed. The emails at work that need responses. The conversation with my daughter that didn't go right. The presentation I didn't prepare well enough. The schedule I failed to coordinate. The nervous system remains on duty.
This is the sleep anxiety mechanism for working parents: the inability to downregulate. The belief that if you sleep, something important will happen without you. That the moment you're unconscious, the balls you've been juggling will all drop simultaneously.
The automatic response is to stay awake. To review the day mentally. To prepare for tomorrow mentally. To ensure that by the time sleep finally comes, you've processed enough that you might be able to rest.
But the processing doesn't end. The mind doesn't tire of reviewing. The moment you drift off, something surfaces and pulls you back to wakefulness.
I was exhausted while lying in bed. Too tired to sleep. Too alert to rest. The mechanism had me in a state where rest was literally unavailable despite being what I needed most.
What happens when your body is begging for sleep but your nervous system has classified sleep as the moment when disaster might strike?
