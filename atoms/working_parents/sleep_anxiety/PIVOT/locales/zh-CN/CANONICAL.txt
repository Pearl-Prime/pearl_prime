### A Small Experiment for Tonight

Since you recognize the **automaticity** and **inertia** of these thought loops, don’t try to stop them. That requires energy you don’t have. Instead, try **externalizing the debrief**.

1.  **The "Brain Dump" Protocol (5 minutes max):**
    *   Before bed, take a physical notebook (not digital—avoid the scroll trap).
    *   Write down the **top 3 unfinished work items** and the **top 3 parenting "misses"** or worries.
    *   Next to each, write: *"This is noted. It will be addressed tomorrow at [specific time]."*
    *   Close the book. Physically put it away.

2.  **The "Shift Change" Ritual:**
    *   As a nurse, you know the power of handoff. You are currently trying to hold the day shift and the night shift simultaneously.
    *   Create a tiny ritual that signals **handoff to your future self**. Light a candle, wash your face, or say out loud: *"My shift is over. The next shift (tomorrow me) will handle this. I am clocking out."*
    *   This isn’t about solving the problems; it’s about **transferring the custody** of the problems from your present self to your future self, so your present self can rest.

3.  **When the Split Screen Starts:**
    *   Don’t fight the images. Acknowledge them: *"Ah, there’s the work tab. There’s the parenting tab. They are running in the background. I don’t need to click on them. I can let them buffer while I rest."*
    *   Remind yourself: **Rest is not a reward for perfect performance. It is a biological requirement for survival.** You are not earning sleep; you are needing it.

You are carrying a systemic load with individual tools. It’s okay to feel the weight. It’s okay to be tired. The goal tonight isn’t to fix the system; it’s to survive the night with a little more compassion for the person holding it all together.
