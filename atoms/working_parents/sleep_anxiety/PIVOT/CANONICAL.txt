## PIVOT v01
---

---
Bedtime is when both jobs hold their debrief simultaneously -- the work you did not finish and the parenting moments you missed play on a split screen in the dark. The old frame says you have a sleep problem. The new frame: you have a processing problem. The day gives you no time to think, so your brain schedules the session at midnight.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You did everything today. Meetings, meals, homework, bedtime. And now your reward is lying awake rehearsing what you forgot. Sleep anxiety for working parents is not about the night. It is about the day giving you zero margin and the night being the only place the deficit shows up.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The old frame says clear your mind before bed. Your mind holds a dual-track system -- one for the career, one for the kids -- and neither comes with a shutdown procedure. The pivot: the anxiety is not about sleep. It is about being the only person who holds the full picture, and the full picture is too big for the hours you are given.
