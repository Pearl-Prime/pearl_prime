## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "It's finally 10pm and you're running."
---
The kids are asleep. Your partner is asleep. The house is quiet. This is the only moment that's yours. And instead of resting, your mind is running the next day's logistics.

Doctor appointment at 9am. School pickup at 3pm. Dinner — what even is dinner? The work presentation. The email you didn't respond to. Your kid's attitude in the car yesterday, is that normal or is something wrong?

Your body should be settling into sleep. Instead it's still at full activation. Your nervous system is still in work mode even though the work is done. You're lying in bed thinking about tomorrow with the intensity of someone actively living it.

Notice your breath. You're lying down. The house is quiet. Tomorrow is ten hours away.

But your system is already there.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Sleep is the one thing you own."
---
You have maybe six hours before you need to be up. You're spending it running logistics. The cost is simple: you will be tired tomorrow. The tired will make the presentation harder. The tired will make you more snappy with the kids. The tired will make you worse at everything.

But you can't stop running the list because the list is real. The appointment is real. The meal needs to be figured out. The work presentation needs your attention even at 10pm.

So sleep doesn't happen. Recovery doesn't happen. You're paying for the logistics with the one resource you actually needed — rest.

That's what tomorrow is costing you tonight.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if tomorrow handled itself?"
---
You're planning tomorrow like it needs you awake right now to work out. But what if morning-you could handle some of this? What if you could leave the planning until there's light and you can actually think?

What if the logistics don't need to be solved at 10pm?

What if you just slept and trusted yourself to figure it out when the day arrives?

What if the preparation is actually the thing keeping you from being ready?

## INTEGRATION v04

mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard

---
Your kids would rather have you rested than perfectly planned. Your partner would rather have you functioning than running logistics at midnight.

But they don't know what you're doing. They're asleep. They think you're sleeping too. They'll just see a tired version of you tomorrow and they won't know why.

Your exhaustion is the real cost to your family, not the imperfect logistics.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You've run these for years anyway."
---
Every night for years you've done this. Planned the next day at 10pm. Worried through the morning before the appointment. And the appointments happened. The meals got made. The presentation went. The kids survived.

The logistics have never actually required the midnight planning. You've just convinced yourself they do.

You're still here. Still managing. Still showing up even though you're doing it on terrible sleep.

The anxiety about preparation has been constant. The actually handling of it has been constant too. You've never actually needed the midnight planning to make tomorrow work.

You're still showing up. Still the parent. Still managing it all even though you're exhausted.

That's what you do.
