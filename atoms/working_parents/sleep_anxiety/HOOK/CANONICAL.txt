## HOOK v01
---

---
The kids are finally asleep and the house is quiet. This is the first moment of silence all day. Your brain takes this as permission to think about everything it couldn't while you were needed: the email you didn't send, the meeting tomorrow, whether your daughter understood what you said, whether you're handling this right.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You are lying in bed at 11:15 PM running through tomorrow's logistics: lunch boxes, the permission slip, the one thing you said you'd do but didn't, the text your partner sent that you read but haven't processed yet. Your body is physically done but your mind is still managing.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Your three-year-old fell asleep on the couch at 6 PM and won't transfer to bed. You have an early call. You should be sleeping. Instead you're lying awake knowing there's only one outcome to this night and you're choosing to stay alert for it.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You're awake at 2 AM and you realize you can't remember the last time you did something just because you wanted to. Everything is in service of the children or the job or the logistics. The emptiness of that hits you in the dark when you're supposed to be resting.
