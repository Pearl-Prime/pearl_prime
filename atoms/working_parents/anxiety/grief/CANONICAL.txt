Tara decides to try something: at the next school event, she leaves her phone in her car. Just one soccer game. Just ninety minutes. Her hands are twitchy in her lap without it. Her nervous system feels exposed. When her daughter scores a goal, Tara's entire attention is there—she feels it in her chest, a lift, a release she hasn't felt in months. Greg ends the Slack message with: "I'm unreachable until 8:30. Family dinner time." Then he turns off notifications. Completely. His chest feels exposed for the first seven minutes. He's waiting for the catastrophe that will require him. It doesn't come. Instead his daughter actually talks, not the performative update but real words about something that bothers her.
