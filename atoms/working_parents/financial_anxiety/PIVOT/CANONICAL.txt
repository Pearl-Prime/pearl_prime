## PIVOT v01
---

---
The old frame says financial anxiety is about spending habits. Your version is about structural impossibility -- childcare that costs more than rent, a career that demands your time but not your worth, a future that gets more expensive every year you plan for it. The pivot: this is not a budgeting failure. It is a systemic gap wearing a personal mask.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You were told to save. You are saving. And the gap between what you save and what your family needs is not closing. Financial anxiety in working parenthood is the math of trying to invest in your kids and yourself simultaneously when the equation was never designed to balance.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
Everyone has advice. Nobody has your particular math -- the daycare bill that rivals a mortgage, the career opportunity that requires travel you cannot afford, the retirement plan that assumes one fewer dependent than you actually have. The pivot: stop measuring your finances against people without your variables.
