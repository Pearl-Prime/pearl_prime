I want to name a mechanism that's running in your nervous system that directly connects your worth as a person to your performance as a parent. The mechanism of the good parent metric is that your child becomes the mirror in which you see yourself. And if you see yourself as failing in that mirror, then you are failing. You are bad. You are not good enough. The metric connects your child's wellbeing directly to your worth as a human being.
So you're always trying to optimize. You're always trying to be better. You're always trying to reach a state of parenting perfection where you're never impatient, never tired, never frustrated. You're trying to reach a state of parenting that makes your worth undeniable.
But the metric is impossible to satisfy. Because good parenting under conditions of poverty and time poverty and emotional poverty is actually impossible. You can't be patient when you're exhausted.
