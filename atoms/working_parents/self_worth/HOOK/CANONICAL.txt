

## HOOK v01
---

---
Your worth equals your parenting metric now. Good kids equals good parent equals your value as a human. The equation leaves no room for error or humanness.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
You snapped at your child and your worth plummeted. You forgot the permission slip and your value as a mother dropped. Your entire identity is balanced on their wellbeing and your performance.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
Self-worth collapsed the day you became a parent. You're worthy if they're well-behaved, well-adjusted, happy. Any deviation is a referendum on who you are as a person.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
Your entire sense of self is tied to being good enough. Good enough parent, good enough partner, good enough worker. The failing is in the margins. You're trying to fit everywhere and crushing yourself in the process.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
You feel it in your chest when your child struggles. Anxiety about them, guilt about you, shame about your parenting. Your sense of worth goes down when theirs does.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
The guilt runs underneath everything. You're not a good enough mother, partner, worker, daughter. The metric for enough keeps rising. You're always falling short.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
Your identity as a person got consumed by your identity as a parent. You don't know where one ends and the other begins. You're only worth something if you're being worth something.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
You measure your value in small increments. Did the kids eat vegetables. Did you get to work on time. Did your partner feel attended to. These become the metrics of your worth.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
Self-worth used to be something internal. Now it's external, dependent, constantly at risk. Your children's behavior, your partner's satisfaction, your boss's approval. You're a house built on someone else's foundation.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
The guilt is your constant companion. The specific, acute guilt of imperfection. You're not enough and never will be. The voice that says this is your own voice, internalized and relentless.
---

## HOOK v11
---

---
You're performing a version of yourself that's competent, calm, present. That version isn't you. The real you is terrified and exhausted and failing. But the performance has become your worth.
---

## HOOK v12
---

---
Your worth is a currency you spend constantly. On your kids, your partner, your job. You're depleting it daily and the sources of replenishment are empty.
---

## HOOK v13
---

---
You can't separate your parenting from your personhood. A bad day with the kids means you're a bad person. A mistake at work means you're a failure. The boundaries have dissolved completely.
---

## HOOK v14
---

---
The guilt starts before you wake up. You're already calculating where you'll fail today. Already preparing for the moment your inadequacy shows. Your worth is predetermined to be insufficient.
---

## HOOK v15
---

---
Your self-worth lives in their smiles and their accomplishments. When they're happy, you exist. When they're struggling, you're somehow responsible for erasing yourself even more.
---

## HOOK v16
---

---
You're trying to be perfect and the perfection is the point. Not for them, for you. Because if you're perfect, you're worthy. If you're flawed, you're failing.
---

## HOOK v17
---

---
The comparison game is relentless. Other parents doing it better, other mothers who seem to have it together. Your worth shrinks in the space between you and them.
---

## HOOK v18
---

---
You can articulate your child's worth easily. They're worthy because they exist, because they're trying, because they're human. You can't extend the same grace to yourself.
---

## HOOK v19
---

---
Self-worth is something you traded for this. You gave it away when you became responsible for someone else. Now you're trying to function without it.
---

## HOOK v20
---

---
Your hands are doing the work of ten people. Your worth is being measured in how many things you accomplish. You're never doing enough because there's always more.
---

## HOOK v21
---

---
You apologize for taking space. You apologize for your needs. You apologize for being human. Your worth is perceived as an inconvenience to everyone around you.
---

## HOOK v22
---

---
The shame is relentless. You're not good enough at parenting, at working, at partnering. The shame arrives with no warning and stays for hours.
---

## HOOK v23
---

---
Your worth is tied to your productivity. The more you do, the more you matter. Stop doing and you stop mattering. It's an exhausting equation.
---

## HOOK v24
---

---
You can't take a compliment because your internal narrative of unworthiness is louder. Someone says you're doing great and you hear the lie beneath it.
---

## HOOK v25
---

---
Self-worth is the thing you're supposed to build for yourself. But you have no time, no energy, no space to build anything. You're too busy maintaining the structure for everyone else.
---

## HOOK v26
---

---
The guilt about your guilt is its own separate burden. You feel bad and then you feel bad about feeling bad. The layers of shame are crushing you.
---

## HOOK v27
---

---
Your imperfections are evidence of your failure. A raised voice, a forgotten commitment, a tired response. These become proof that you're not enough.
---

## HOOK v28
---

---
You're the problem in every scenario because you're at the center of your own story. If your child is struggling, you caused it. If your partner is frustrated, you failed them. The responsibility is total.
---

## HOOK v29
---

---
Your worth was transferred to your children the moment they arrived. Now you're trying to function in the margins. You're only valuable insofar as you're useful to them.
---

## HOOK v30
---

---
The voice that says you're failing is your own voice. The standards are impossible. The guilt is permanent. Your sense of worth is being rationed out in pieces while you give everything away.
---
