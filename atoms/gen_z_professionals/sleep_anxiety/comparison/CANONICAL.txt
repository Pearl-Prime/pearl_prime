---
Kai is at the gym. A coworker is on the treadmill next to her. The coworker says: I slept nine hours last night, I feel incredible. Kai slept five. Kai is tired. Kai feels the comparison cut in. Coworker is thriving. Kai is hanging on. The difference is sleep. Coworker got nine. Kai got five. The logic feels airtight: coworker has figured out how to rest. Kai hasn't. Therefore coworker is doing something right and Kai is doing something wrong. The comparison is not just about last night. It's about the pattern. Coworker always looks rested. Kai never does. The body tightens. There's a sensation of being insufficient. Of moving slower. Of being less capable than the person next to her.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Jordan is in a meeting where two people are presenting. Both are sharp. Both are compelling. Jordan knows both people also struggle with sleep. Jordan has seen them yawning. Has heard them mention hard nights. But in this meeting, they're performing peak functionality. The mechanism is this: the comparison doesn't happen with the actual person. It happens with the performed version. Everyone performs. Everyone shows their best at work. But when you're sleep-deprived, the performance takes more energy. The cost is higher. So the comparison is comparing Jordan's exhausted self with everyone else's peak self. It's an unfair comparison but the brain doesn't notice the unfairness. It just sees: everyone else is fine. I'm not fine. The body is depleted from the effort of matching a performance that costs them more to produce. The heart is racing with the anxiety of the gap.
