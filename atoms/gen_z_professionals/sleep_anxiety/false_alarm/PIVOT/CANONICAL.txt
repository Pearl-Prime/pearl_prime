--- variant: v01
Your body screams danger but the danger is a notification, a calendar invite, a thought. The old frame calls this overreacting. The new frame: your sleep anxiety alarm was calibrated during a time when the threats were real. Now it fires on everything because it never got the update that the emergency ended. Legacy code, running on new hardware.

--- variant: v02
The false alarm around sleep anxiety isn't proof you're broken. It's proof your protective system is thorough to a fault. It would rather give you a hundred false positives than miss one real threat. The reframe isn't silencing the alarm. It's learning which fires are real and which are the system stress-testing itself.

--- variant: v03
Every spike of sleep anxiety that turns out to be nothing still costs you the same adrenaline as a real event. Your body doesn't do refunds on cortisol. The pivot: the false alarm isn't your personality. It's a nervous system that can't confirm safety long enough to power down that needs recalibration, not judgment.
