## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "It's 2am and you're already failing."
---
You're awake at 2am analyzing whether you're behind. Your peers are probably sleeping. Their careers are probably moving faster. You probably said something stupid in the meeting today that made you look less capable. Your probably aren't getting promoted on schedule.

Your chest is tight. Your hands are moving. Your mind is running the comparison loop and your nervous system believes it's happening right now — the failure, the falling behind, the peers getting ahead.

But it's 2am. The meeting was today. Tomorrow is eight hours away. Your nervous system is running a catastrophe that doesn't exist yet.

Notice your spine against the bed. You're horizontal. You're alive. The bed is still there. The anxiety is real. The catastrophe is not.

It's just 2am.

---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED

carry_line: "This is what early career costs you."
---
You're awake at 2am analyzing your career trajectory. The analysis loop never actually resolves. You fall asleep at 4am and wake up at 6am exhausted and convince yourself you're too tired to be productive today. So you miss the morning the way you missed last week's morning.

The peers you're comparing yourself to? They're probably sleeping. They're getting recovery. They're showing up the next day fresher than you are.

You're losing sleep to a competition that's happening in your head. The comparison is real. The analysis costs you. The exhaustion is real.

Meanwhile your actual career is slower because you can't sleep before the day that's supposed to build it.

That's what the anxiety is costing you.
