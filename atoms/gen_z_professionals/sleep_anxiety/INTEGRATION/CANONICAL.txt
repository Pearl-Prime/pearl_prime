## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "It's 2am and you're already failing."
---
You're awake at 2am analyzing whether you're behind. Your peers are probably sleeping. Their careers are probably moving faster. You probably said something stupid in the meeting today that made you look less capable. Your probably aren't getting promoted on schedule.

Your chest is tight. Your hands are moving. Your mind is running the comparison loop and your nervous system believes it's happening right now — the failure, the falling behind, the peers getting ahead.

But it's 2am. The meeting was today. Tomorrow is eight hours away. Your nervous system is running a catastrophe that doesn't exist yet.

Notice your spine against the bed. You're horizontal. You're alive. The bed is still there. The anxiety is real. The catastrophe is not.

It's just 2am.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "This is what early career costs you."
---
You're awake at 2am analyzing your career trajectory. The analysis loop never actually resolves. You fall asleep at 4am and wake up at 6am exhausted and convince yourself you're too tired to be productive today. So you miss the morning the way you missed last week's morning.

The peers you're comparing yourself to? They're probably sleeping. They're getting recovery. They're showing up the next day fresher than you are.

You're losing sleep to a competition that's happening in your head. The comparison is real. The analysis costs you. The exhaustion is real.

Meanwhile your actual career is slower because you can't sleep before the day that's supposed to build it.

That's what the anxiety is costing you.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: UNCERTAINTY_HELD
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if being behind is actually fine?"
---
You're convinced you're falling behind your peers. But what if you're exactly where you're supposed to be? What if the speed of other people's success has nothing to do with your timeline?

What if you're comparing yourself to a highlight reel while judging yourself by your actual experience?

What if the people you think are ahead are also awake at 2am worried they're behind?

What if "on schedule" is a myth and everyone just gets there differently?
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: EXTERNAL_VIEW
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your colleagues think you're fine."
---
At work, nobody knows that you spent the night awake analyzing your career. They don't see the 2am version. They see the tired version in the morning and they probably just think you had a late night.

They don't know that you're running constant comparison. They don't see the loop. They just see someone working, showing up, doing the job.

The version of you that's capable is the one they see. The version that's panicking is invisible.

You're the only one who knows you're worried.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You've survived every 2am spiral you've had."
---
Last month you were awake at 2am convinced you weren't smart enough. The month before, the conversation where you said the wrong thing. Every single month since you started this job you've had at least one night where you convinced yourself you weren't going to make it.

And you're still here. Still employed. Still moving forward. The spiral was never right about the outcome.

You've survived every catastrophe that happened only in your head at 2am. Every single one.

The anxiety keeps arriving. You keep showing up anyway. You're still building the career even though your nervous system spends half your nights convincing you that you're failing.

You're still here. Still competent. Still moving forward.

That's what you do at 2am. That's what you keep doing.
