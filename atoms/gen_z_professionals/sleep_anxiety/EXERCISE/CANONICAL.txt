## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Put your phone on the other side of the room.
Don't check the time when you wake.
List three things you need to do tomorrow.
Write them down so your brain stops worrying.
Your brain's job is to think. Give it permission.
Now tell your brain: we'll handle tomorrow tomorrow.
Take a slow breath in through your nose.
Hold it for two counts.
Release through your mouth slowly.
Repeat five times with complete focus.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your eyes and scan your body.
Start at your toes. Notice any tension.
Release whatever you're holding without judgment.
Move up through your feet, calves, thighs.
Do the same for your whole body.
When you reach your head, let it feel heavy.
Your bed is supporting all your weight.
You don't have to hold yourself up.
Let gravity do the work.
Your only job right now is to rest.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
You're anxious about tomorrow's sleep, which creates tension.
Stop focusing on whether you'll sleep well.
Focus on your body being comfortable instead.
Adjust your pillow. Adjust your blanket.
Make one small thing feel better.
Then move to the next small thing.
Your job is comfort, not sleep.
Sleep is something that happens, not a performance.
You don't have to earn rest.
Give yourself permission to be awake.
---
