

## HOOK v01
---

---
You killed the lights at eleven. The ceiling is still there at 2:14. You have run the math until the numbers go numb: if sleep came now, you would still land short. Your eyes stay shut. Your brain is sorting slides for a meeting that is not for six hours.
---

## HOOK v02
---

---
The phone is face-down an arm's length away. You will not flip it. You still know you are late. The room is the wrong temperature on both sides of the blanket. Your pulse sounds ordinary and that somehow offends you — like your body did not get the memo that tonight is for power-saving mode.
---

## HOOK v03
---

---
You drank the wind-down tea. You dimmed the screen. You listened to someone whisper about rain through cheap earbuds. The routine scored a hundred on paper. Paper is not where you are living right now. The bed is real. The awake is realer.
---

## HOOK v04
---

---
You are flat. Your thoughts are not — they ladder up through tomorrow, next week, the sentence you wish you had said last Tuesday. Sleep was sold as the off-switch. Someone wired a dimmer wrong. You keep turning it and the room stays bright inside your skull.
---

## HOOK v05
---

---
The clock app promised six hours until the alarm. That was true before you watched it become five, then four. Each glance steals another slice without asking. You are negotiating with time like it owes you fairness. It does not.
---

## HOOK v06
---

---
At nine you were fog-tired. At midnight you are wired. The swap is cruel: the body that needed rest now hums because rest turned into a test you are visibly failing. You lie still on purpose. Stillness is not the same as down.
---

## HOOK v07
---

---
The building sounds empty in the way that says everyone else pulled it off. A fridge clicks on somewhere below. Your own breath is loud in the sheets. The hour sits on you without introduction. You are tired of being the only window still leaking light into the dark.
---

## HOOK v08
---

---
Tomorrow's dry mouth is already rehearsing itself. You are arguing with a future self who will hate this night before the day has opened. The argument happens in the dark where there is no jury, only ceiling and the sound of a neighbor's shower you cannot time to anything sane.
---

## HOOK v09
---

---
You catch yourself composing how you will tell this story — to a partner, a doctor, your group chat. The composing keeps you awake. You notice that. You compose that observation too. The loop has a sense of humor you do not find funny at 3:12.
---

## HOOK v10
---

---
A car passes outside. The stripe of streetlight moves on the wall. For three breaths you pretend you are someone who sleeps through tires on wet asphalt. Then your mind picks up exactly where it left off — same thread, same knot, same refusal to let the day end where the calendar said it would.
---
