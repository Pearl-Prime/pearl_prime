## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v01
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: false_alarm
weight: standard
carry_line: "Lying in bed at 2am with your heart racing is the alarm system firing on darkness, not on a real threat."
---
Here is the mechanism. The alarm system that wakes you at 2 am is doing what it was built to do, in a context that no longer matches the conditions it was built for. In the environment it evolved in, waking up alert in the middle of the night was occasionally useful — a sound at the edge of a camp, a shape moving where it should not. The system fires fast, floods the body with cortisol, and asks you to scan. The scan was supposed to land on something. In your apartment, with the door locked and a Slack window glowing on your nightstand, the scan lands on whatever your mind has been carrying that day. The unanswered message. The 1:1 tomorrow. The OKR you have not started. The alarm did not pick those targets. It just fired, and your mind — needing somewhere to put the alertness — handed it the most pressing item in working memory. This is why the 2 am content feels so urgent and so confused. It is not a real-time threat assessment. It is a stuck alarm reaching for any nearby fuel. What changes when you see this is that you stop solving the content. The content was never the source.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v02
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: spiral
weight: standard
carry_line: "The chain runs: I am still awake, then I will be tired tomorrow, then I will fail the standup, then this is the pattern of my life."
---
Here is the mechanism. The prediction loop has a specific routine for nights. You notice you are still awake at 1:14 am. The loop receives "still awake" and runs it forward. Still awake becomes I will be tired tomorrow. Tired tomorrow becomes I will be slow in the standup. Slow in the standup becomes my manager will notice. My manager will notice becomes the pattern of my last review. The pattern becomes my whole career trajectory. By 1:23 you have built a chain that connects this single sleepless hour to a verdict on your future. Each link past the first is invented, but the body cannot tell. Cortisol has already risen. The bed is now associated with the chain, which means lying down tomorrow will trigger the same loop earlier. This is how sleep anxiety becomes self-reinforcing. The loop produces the wakefulness it forecasts. What helps is naming the chain in slow motion: "Still awake. Tired tomorrow. Slow in standup. Manager notices." Seeing the links does not put you to sleep, but it does separate the first link, which is real, from the rest, which are not yet anything.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v03
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: comparison
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your roommate's screenshot of Slack at 11pm reads like data on what normal people sleep through. The data is wrong."
---
Here is the mechanism. The comparison engine does not turn off at night. It often runs harder, because the inputs are quieter and the interior is louder. You lie in bed and your mind reaches for the version of your peers it has stored from the day. Your college roommate posted a screenshot in the group chat at 11 pm — Slack still open, casually, as if she were unbothered by the late hour. Your standup peer mentioned getting eight hours like it was unremarkable. The engine reads these surfaces as the full picture of how other people relate to sleep. By contrast, your own 1 am awake-ness becomes uniquely deficient. What the engine cannot see is that your roommate may have lain awake until 3, or that your peer may have said "eight hours" while running on five. You have access to your interior and to their surface, and the engine is treating that asymmetry as evidence about you. The 2 am verdict is not a measurement. It is the engine doing the only kind of math it knows how to do, on input it was never supposed to handle.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v04
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: overwhelm
weight: standard
carry_line: "The day's open Slack channels do not close when you put the phone down. Your nervous system holds them open until 3am."
---
Here is the mechanism. Sleep is not a switch. It is a transition that requires the nervous system to register that the day's open loops are safe to release. Slack channels, Notion comments, unsent messages, the meeting tomorrow you have not prepped for — each is a small loop the nervous system has flagged as unresolved. During the day, you can keep them open without noticing the cost. At 11 pm, when you put the phone face down and try to sleep, the loops are still open. The body cannot tell which ones are real obligations and which ones are residue. It holds them all the same way, with the same low-grade alertness. This is why falling asleep can feel like trying to put down something whose weight you only notice when you stop moving. It is not insomnia in the clinical sense. It is your nervous system refusing to release loops it never registered as closed. Naming this matters because it changes the response. You do not need to argue yourself into being tired. You need to find the small ritual that signals to the body that today's open loops are allowed to stay open until tomorrow.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v05
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: shame
weight: standard
carry_line: "The secret is that you cannot do the basic human thing. The secrecy is what keeps it from getting smaller."
---
Here is the mechanism. Sleep shame has a specific shape. It is the conviction that the inability to fall asleep, or to stay asleep, is a private failure at something everyone else does without effort. Other people in your cohort, the shame insists, do not lie awake at 2 am refreshing Slack to check whether their manager replied. They sleep like adults. You, alone, cannot do the basic biological thing your body is supposed to do automatically. So you do not mention it. You do not tell your standup that you got three hours. You do not tell your roommate that you have been waking up at 4. You drink more coffee, perform alertness, and the performance becomes one more loop the nervous system has to hold open. The hiding is the closing of the trap. Because no one knows, no one tells you they also lie awake. You conclude you are uniquely broken at something most of your cohort is also struggling with, in private. Naming the mechanism does not put you to sleep. It does change what the wakefulness is evidence of. Not your defect. A common pattern, hidden into a feeling of singularity.
