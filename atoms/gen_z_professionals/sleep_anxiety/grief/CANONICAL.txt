Kai remembers falling asleep in the library. It was 2am during finals week, the desk was hard, and sleep came anyway. Now at 25, with a normal schedule and a good bed, sleep doesn't come. There's a strange ache in the chest when Kai thinks about this. The body that used to cooperate has stopped. This isn't a problem yet, just an observation. The observation arrives with a heavy feeling. Kai was different then. Younger, but also easier. The ease is gone. It's hard to say when it ended. Kai lies in the dark and remembers falling asleep during lectures. The memory feels like someone else's life.
