--- variant: v01
You're allowed to grieve things that didn't die -- futures that evaporated, relationships that shifted, versions of yourself you had to leave behind. Loss doesn't require a funeral to be real. If it mattered and it's gone, the grief is legitimate.

--- variant: v02
You have permission to feel the grief under your financial stress without solving it. Not every feeling needs a fix. Sometimes the grief just needs to be felt, fully, without you rushing it toward a lesson or a silver lining. The grief will integrate on its own timeline, not yours.

--- variant: v03
It's okay to mourn and function at the same time. The grief doesn't need to stop you in your tracks to be real. You can carry it quietly and still deserve acknowledgment -- from yourself, if from nobody else -- that you're carrying something heavy.
