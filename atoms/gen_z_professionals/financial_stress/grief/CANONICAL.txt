Alex's friend gets engaged. The announcement happens in the group chat. Everyone is excited. Alex scrolls through the messages and feels something in the chest collapse. Not happiness about the engagement. Something else. Alex realizes: she can't afford to be in the wedding. The bridesmaid dress. The bachelorette trip. The gift. The flight to wherever the wedding is. Alex's brain is doing the math faster than Alex wants it to. Alex's chest is tight. This is supposed to be happy. Alex can't afford happy. Alex texts: so excited for you. Alex's fingers shake slightly typing it. Alex feels the lie. Not the lie about excitement but the lie about participation. Alex knows already that the answer is going to be no. The no sits in the throat like something swallowed wrong. Alex will not be in the wedding photos. Alex will miss the bachelorette weekend. Alex will buy the gift from the registry, the smallest one, the $25 option. Alex will attend the wedding alone because bringing a plus-one is an extra cost. The cost isn't money.
