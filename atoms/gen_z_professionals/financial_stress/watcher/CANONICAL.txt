## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/financial_stress/watcher/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The watcher is a promise. The promise is simple: if you monitor the money closely enough, you will prevent the catastrophe. If you know how much you have down to the dollar, you won't overdraft. If you calculate before every purchase, you won't make a mistake. If you review every transaction, you'll catch fraud. If you track every subscription, you won't waste money. The watcher is efficient. The watcher keeps lists. The watcher checks multiple times per day. The watcher has spreadsheets. Alex creates spreadsheets. Three different ones. One for fixed expenses. One for variable expenses. One for the buffer that isn't really a buffer because it's allocated to next month's rent already. The watcher promises: this much data, this much vigilance, and you'll be safe. You'll never be blindsided. You'll never run out. The watcher speaks in if-then logic. If you check the balance, then you'll know what you can spend. If you know what you can spend, then you won't make a mistake. If you don't make a mistake, then you won't fall behind. If you don't fall behind, then you'll be okay. The logic is tight. The logic is airtight. The logic doesn't account for the fact that checking the balance forty times a day doesn't change the balance. Doesn't prevent the overdraft fee that came anyway, six weeks ago, despite the vigilance. Doesn't explain why the catastrophe the watcher is preventing never arrives, yet the watcher still keeps watching.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

Jordan's brain has divided money into categories: necessary money and discretionary money. Necessary is rent, utilities, food. Discretionary is everything else. But discretionary keeps shrinking. A wedding gift is necessary because you're in the wedding party. A car repair is necessary because the car is how you get to work. A therapy copay is necessary because anxiety is physical. The line between necessary and discretionary moves every week. Jordan's shoulders are permanently tightened. The tightening is from the constant recalculation. Will this purchase move the finish line? Will this purchase delay the finish line? What is the finish line? Jordan doesn't know. The finish line used to be: save three months of expenses. Now the finish line is: make it to next month. The watcher doesn't celebrate the small victories. The watcher only counts what's still missing. The watcher says: you still can't afford the flight home. You still can't afford the dental work. You still can't afford the thing that would have been simple if you had money. Jordan's hands shake slightly when opening bills. Jordan's throat closes when friends mention their bonus checks. The watcher has taught Jordan that money scarcity is permanent. The watcher has taught Jordan that the catastrophe is not a future event but a present condition.
