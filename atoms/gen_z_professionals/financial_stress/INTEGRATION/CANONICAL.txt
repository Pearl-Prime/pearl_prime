

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The alarm lives in your chest."
---
You calculated the numbers again this week. Same answer. The salary that felt like it would be enough doesn't move the math forward. The numbers are still sitting below what you need. That knot in your chest — that's the physical record of financial arithmetic that doesn't clear.
Your nervous system isn't overreacting. The calculation is real. You are genuinely in a position of insufficient. The alarm running through your day isn't irrational. It's accurate.
Notice your chest right now. Notice what tension lives there when you aren't actively calculating. That's your body keeping score.
The alarm lives in your chest.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The debt payment is a weekly grief."
---
Count what you owe. The student loan that sits in your account like a permanent tenant. The rent that takes a percentage you were told to be grateful for. The cost of living that no salary seems to catch up to. Each month the arithmetic comes out the same way: you make decent money and it isn't enough.
This doesn't just cost money. It costs a version of your life. The freedom to leave. The freedom to take risks. The freedom to want things that cost money you don't have. The version of yourself that could stop strategizing and just live.
The debt payment is a weekly grief.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You're still here. The math hasn't changed."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Weeks and weeks of this equation. Early on you thought the salary would eventually solve it. Or the raise. Or some event that would shift the ratio. It hasn't. You're further in now and you know the arithmetic isn't resolving because it was set up not to.
That's not failure. That's how it's designed. The system needs you financially precarious enough to stay.
You're still here. The math hasn't changed.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What are you actually being paid for."
---
The question isn't how to make the math work. You already know the answer to that — work more, want less, accept the insufficient. The question is what the financial precarity is actually trading for.
Maybe it's flexibility to stay in your current city. Maybe it's the cost of working somewhere that matters to you. Maybe it's what you bought with the freedom to choose this job at all. Maybe it's simply the price of not having someone else's wealth to borrow.
The precarity isn't just a problem. It's also a choice, constrained as that choice was.
What are you actually being paid for.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "They're calculating the same math."
---
Someone in your field makes the same salary and the math clears for them. You know why — different city or family money or no debt or a partner with income. The fact that you can see that difference means you're tracking who has structural safety and who doesn't. That tracking is painful. It shows you the system isn't meritocratic.
Someone else is making the same money and sleeping at night.
They're calculating the same math.

The moment came to ask for something. You calculated what you needed and you asked for less. Your body knows this. The tightness in your shoulders is the record of knowing what you're worth and offering less out of fear that someone better positioned could take the role.
Your hands can feel that calculation.
You didn't ask for more.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It has compromised years of choice."
---
Trace back how long you've been managing this. The years you didn't pursue something because you couldn't afford the instability. The relocation you couldn't make. The risk you couldn't take because failing meant financial catastrophe. The dreams that required money you didn't have and the choice to stop dreaming. The conversations where you said yes because you needed the money, not because you wanted the work.
That's what financial precarity costs. Not just money. Agency. The life you didn't get to choose.
It has compromised years of choice.
