--- variant: v01
You're allowed to struggle with financial stress and not feel shame about it. The struggle is the experience. The shame is the commentary, and the commentary was written by someone who never struggled publicly. You don't owe anyone a clean version of your process.

--- variant: v02
Permission to be visibly imperfect. The shame says if anyone sees you struggling, they'll confirm what you fear about yourself. But the shame is running a bluff. Most people who see you struggle feel relief -- because they're struggling too and nobody's saying it.

--- variant: v03
The shame says you should have this handled by now. Here's your counter-permission: there is no deadline for healing. There is no age by which you should have figured out financial stress. The timeline the shame imposes was made up by people who never did the work themselves.
