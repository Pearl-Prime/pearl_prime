The mechanism I want to name is the ratio that does not add up. You did the right things. You graduated. You got the job. You are earning what looked like real money when you were in school. But the rent is still too high. The student loan payments are still there. The life that everyone around you seems to be living is still not affordable. The mechanism is the arithmetic that fails. The ratio between what you earn and what you need to live has broken. You followed the formula. You did everything right. But the formula was never actually designed for you. It was designed for someone who bought a house in 1985 at a price that is not the price now. The mechanism is the broken formula that you followed anyway. So you are not failing at math. The math is failing you.
