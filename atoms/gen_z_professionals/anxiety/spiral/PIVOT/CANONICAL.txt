
## PIVOT v01
---

---
The spiral isn't you losing control. It's your mind trying to solve an unsolvable equation by running it faster. More thinking won't crack a problem that thinking created. The old frame says you need more analysis. The new frame: the spiral is what happens when the analysis tool gets applied to things that require presence, not processing.
---


## PIVOT v02
---

---
You didn't choose the spiral. The spiral chose you the moment you tried to think your way out of a feeling. Anxiety lives in the body. Thinking lives in the mind. When you throw the mind at a body problem, it just spins. The reframe isn't "stop thinking." It's recognizing which tool fits which problem.
---


## PIVOT v03
---

---
The spiral feels productive because the mind is working hard. But a hamster wheel also involves effort. The pivot: the spiral isn't problem-solving with extra steps. It's your nervous system's version of buffering -- intense activity with zero forward motion. Recognizing the buffer is the first step off the wheel.
---
