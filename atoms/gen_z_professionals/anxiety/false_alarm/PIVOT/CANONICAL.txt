
## PIVOT v01
---

---
Your body screams fire and you search for smoke. The old frame says the alarm means danger. The new frame: your alarm system was calibrated in childhood and never updated. It is running threat detection on a system that learned to flag everything. The false alarm is not your failure. It is legacy software running in an environment it was not designed for.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The anxiety spike at 2pm is not about the 3pm meeting. It is about every meeting that ever went sideways, compressed into a single anticipatory hit. Your nervous system does not distinguish between past threat and present email. The reframe is not "calm down." It is understanding you are responding to a ghost with real adrenaline — and the ghost is not here anymore.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
You keep bracing for impact that never comes. The old story says you are dramatic. The real story: your system got stuck in the loading screen between safe and not-safe. The alarm fires not because danger is present, but because your body cannot confirm danger is absent. That is not weakness. That is an overworked security system doing exactly what it was built to do.
---

## PIVOT v04
---

---
The false alarm costs the same adrenaline as the real thing. Your nervous system does not issue refunds. The meeting ends fine, the message is neutral, the manager is not upset — and you still feel the aftermath in your wrists and jaw for an hour after. That exhaustion is not irrational. The body spent real resources on a threat that did not materialize. Both things are true.
---

## PIVOT v05
---

---
What you call overreacting is actually your nervous system completing a sequence it started. The alarm fired, the preparation launched, and then — nothing happened. The preparation has nowhere to go. So it stays in your chest, your throat, your hands, waiting for an event that already passed without arriving. The antidote is not calm. It is completion.
