Priya notices the false alarm has a social multiplier. Alone, a mistake is a mistake. In front of others, a mistake becomes exposure. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v04

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 4

---
Maya maps the false alarm's anatomy. Step one: ambiguous signal. Step two: body activation—chest, hands, jaw. Step three: mind races to worst case. Step four: worst case feels like fact. Step five: evidence arrives that contradicts the worst case. Step six: brief relief. Step seven: body stays activated, waiting for the next signal. The cycle takes ten to forty minutes. She runs it six to eight times per workday. The math is brutal: up to five hours daily spent inside an alarm that has no fire. Her performance reviews are excellent. No one sees the cost.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v05

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Dev discovers the alarm has a preparation myth. If he worries enough before a presentation, the presentation goes well. If he doesn't worry, something will go wrong. The superstition is invisible until he names it. The worry feels like preparation. The worry is actually a body state—elevated cortisol, shallow breathing, tight throat—that has nothing to do with the slides. He prepares the slides in an hour. He worries about the presentation for a day and a half. The preparation and the worry occupy different systems. The worry claims credit for the preparation's work.
