
## THREAD v01
---

---
Your alarm system has a pattern — same time, same trigger type, same body response. Mapping the pattern does not stop the alarm. But it gives you something the alarm does not want you to have: a three-second head start on knowing what is happening before your body decides for you.
---

## THREAD v02
---

---
There is a difference between the false alarm and the real thing. Your body does not know it yet, but there is a signal buried in the response that distinguishes them. Finding that signal changes the entire relationship with the alarm — not from fear to calm, but from reaction to recognition.
---

---
Most people try to stop the false alarm. That approach has a specific failure mode. What works instead is counterintuitive, and it starts with something your nervous system already does but you have not named. The next chapter covers exactly that. You may recognize it the moment it is described.
