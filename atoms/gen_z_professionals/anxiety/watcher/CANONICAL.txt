Jordan realizes he can't turn off the watcher during conversations. He tried. Told himself to just be present at the team dinner. For about ninety seconds, he was. Then the watcher rebooted: how's your posture, you've been quiet too long, that joke didn't land, recalibrate. The watcher isn't a choice. The watcher is an operating system. Priya calculates the cost. Every meeting has two tracks: the meeting and the monitoring. The monitoring runs commentary on her own participation—tone, frequency, content quality—which means half her cognitive bandwidth is surveillance and half is contribution. She's operating at fifty percent capacity in every room she enters. The monitoring promises it will improve her performance. The monitoring actually halves it. She could contribute more if she watched less. The watching feels like diligence. The watching is the thing that makes diligence necessary.

Kai notices the watcher intensifies around certain people. With peers, it runs at medium—monitoring but not commanding. Maya discovers the watcher watches the watching. She catches herself monitoring a conversation. Tells herself to stop. Then watches herself try to stop. Then evaluates how well she stopped. MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal

---
Jordan is presenting when he catches the watcher mid-evaluation. The watcher says: that transition was weak. Jordan notices the noticing. For a fraction of a second, he's aware of three layers: the presentation, the watcher, and the awareness of the watcher. The third layer doesn't fix the first two. The third layer introduces a new possibility: if he can see the watcher, the watcher isn't him. It's a process. Processes can be observed without being obeyed. He continues the presentation. ## TURNING_POINT v02

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social

---
Priya tells a friend: "I run surveillance on myself in every meeting." She says it as a joke. The friend pauses. "What does that look like?" Priya describes it: the dual-track, the commentary, the evaluation that never completes. Saying it out loud makes it sound exhausting. From inside, it sounds like responsibility. From outside, it sounds like a job no one assigned her. The friend says: "That sounds like a lot of extra work." The word extra is the turning point. The watcher always presented itself as necessary. Extra implies it might not be.

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Dev tries a new approach to the midnight replay. Instead of examining each interaction, he gives the watcher a time limit. Five minutes. The watcher can review whatever it wants for five minutes. Then it's done. The watcher runs at full speed—standup, code review, lunch conversation, the email he took too long to answer. At five minutes, Dev doesn't stop the watcher. ---
Jordan presents. The watcher activates. He lets it. Doesn't fight the dual-track—lets the presentation and the commentary run simultaneously. The difference: he feeds the presentation and starves the commentary. When the watcher says weak transition, he doesn't respond internally. He moves to the next slide. The commentary doesn't stop.
