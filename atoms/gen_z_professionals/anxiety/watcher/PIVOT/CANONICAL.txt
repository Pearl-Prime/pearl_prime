--- variant: v01
You've been watching yourself from the outside -- monitoring how you sound, how you look, whether you seem okay. The old frame says that's self-awareness. The new frame: the watcher is a surveillance system you installed to prevent rejection. It kept you safe once. Now it keeps you performing instead of living.

--- variant: v02
The watcher in your head runs a constant live-stream: evaluating, scoring, flagging. It calls itself protection. What it actually does is split you in two -- the one living and the one grading the performance. The reframe isn't killing the watcher. It's understanding that the watcher was born in a moment when being watched was the only way to stay safe.

--- variant: v03
Somewhere you learned that if you could see yourself the way others see you, you could stay ahead of their judgment. So you built an internal camera. The camera never turns off. Every interaction is simultaneously lived and reviewed. The pivot: the camera doesn't prevent judgment. It just means you experience everything twice -- once in real time and once through the lens of imagined evaluation.
