## PERMISSION_GRANT v01
---

---
Anxiety arrives with a stack of unspoken rules. Hold it together. Look fine. Do not need anyone. Earn the rest you have not earned yet. These rules are not law. They are noise. Set them down.

You are allowed to not be fine. Not fine is a real state. It does not need to be hidden or performed around. The chest can be tight and the day can still get done. Both are true at once.

You are allowed to feel the doubt. The doubt is not a verdict. It is weather moving through the body. It will arrive. It will rain. It will pass. The body that holds the doubt remains.

You are allowed to need people. The lonely-warrior story is a lie. The nervous system was built to settle in the presence of other steady bodies. Reaching for company is biology, not weakness.

You are allowed to succeed without feeling ready. Readiness is a story the mind tells after the fact. Nobody who ever did anything hard felt ready first. The body shows up. The feelings catch up later, or they do not.

You are allowed to receive good things. Promotion. Praise. Love. A morning that does not hurt. The body may flinch when the good lands. That flinch is not proof you do not deserve it. It is the nervous system adjusting to a wider room.

You are allowed to rest before you have earned it. Rest is not a reward for productivity. Rest is what the body was designed to do between efforts. Earn nothing first. Lie down. The world keeps turning.

You are allowed to be the consciousness watching the alarm, not the alarm itself. The alarm fires. The body tightens. The breath climbs. And somewhere behind all of it, awareness keeps watching. That awareness is the seat. That awareness is the practice. That awareness is the part of you anxiety has never been able to touch.

These are not affirmations. They are accurate descriptions of the rights every human body comes with. Read them again. Read them slowly. Let the body hear them, one permission at a time.
