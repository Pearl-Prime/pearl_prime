Jordan tracks the comparison pattern. It activates on three triggers: social media, peer conversations about career, and his own milestones. The milestones are the cruelest trigger—getting a raise activates the comparison to see if the raise is enough relative to others. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya discovers the comparison selects its targets strategically. She doesn't compare herself to the CEO. She doesn't compare herself to the intern. She compares herself to the people closest to her level—one or two steps ahead. The near-miss comparisons. Close enough to feel achievable. Far enough to feel like failure. The engine chooses targets that maximize the gap sensation. If she surpasses one target, the engine selects a new one slightly further ahead. The finish line moves at the same speed she does. The gap is maintained by design.
