

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit back from your screen. Place both feet flat on the floor. Feel your heels make contact. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for four counts. That is one cycle. Do not rush the holds. Keep the counts even. Complete four cycles. On the fourth exhale, let your shoulders drop before you breathe in again. Return to the screen after the fourth cycle.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place one hand on your belly. Place the other on your chest. Breathe in slowly. Let the belly hand rise first. The chest hand rises second. Breathe out. Let the chest hand fall first. The belly hand falls second. Count five breaths. If the chest rises before the belly, start the count again. Five complete belly-first breaths. Then remove your hands.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for eight counts. Make the exhale twice as long as the inhale. Do not force the pace. Count slowly. If eight feels too long, use six. Keep the out-breath longer than the in-breath. That is the only rule. Six complete cycles. Notice where in the body the exhale releases last.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Put the phone face-down. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press your heels down. Press your toes down. Feel the full sole. Breathe in for four. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Keep the feet pressed. Do not lift them. Four cycles. On the last exhale, release the pressure in your feet. Feel the difference. Pick up the phone after.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Name one thing you can see right now. Stay with it for one full breath. Name one thing you can hear. One breath. Name one thing you can physically feel — the chair, the keyboard, your clothes. One breath. Name one thing you can smell. One breath. Name one thing you can taste. One breath. Do not rush. Five things, five breaths. Then look at the screen again.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Breathe in fully. Fill the lungs completely. Hold for two counts. Open your mouth. Let the air out in one long exhale. Do not control the speed. Just release. Close your mouth. Pause for two counts. Breathe in again through the nose. Sigh out again through the mouth. Four full sigh cycles. Let the sound happen. Do not suppress it.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Close your eyes. Start at the top of your head. Notice the scalp. Breathe once. Move to the forehead and eyes. Breathe once. Move to the jaw. Let it go slack. Breathe once. Move to the neck and throat. Breathe once. Move to the shoulders. Let them drop. Breathe once. Move to the chest. Breathe once. Move to the belly. Breathe once. Move to the hips. Breathe once. Move to the thighs and knees. Breathe once. Move to the feet. Breathe once. Open your eyes.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Notice your jaw. Let it unclench. Let the back teeth separate slightly. Let your tongue rest on the floor of your mouth. Breathe in. On the exhale, let the shoulders drop. Do not push them down. Let the exhale drop them. Breathe in again. On the exhale, let the jaw soften further. Four breath cycles. Jaw soft. Shoulders down. Repeat until both feel different from when you started.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Breathe in naturally. On the exhale, count slowly: one, two, three, four, five, six. Make the exhale last the full count. If it runs out before six, breathe in again sooner. If six is easy, add one count per cycle up to ten. Start at six. Add one count each exhale until you reach your limit. Stay at your limit for three breaths. Then let the breath return to its natural pace.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Breathe in through the nose. On the exhale, hum with your mouth closed. Make the hum last as long as the exhale lasts. Feel the vibration in your chest. Feel it in your throat. Feel it in your lips. Breathe in again. Hum out again. Five cycles. Vary the pitch if you want. Keep the hum continuous. Do not stop the hum before the exhale ends.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Find three places where your body touches something solid. Your feet on the floor. Your back against the chair. Your hands on the desk or your thighs. Name each one. Press into the first point. Feel the resistance. Breathe in. Breathe out. Move to the second point. Press. Breathe in. Breathe out. Move to the third point. Press. Breathe in. Breathe out. Press all three at once. Breathe twice.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sit without leaning back. Feel the base of your spine. Breathe in. On the exhale, feel the weight of your spine stacking from the base upward. Do not straighten aggressively. Let the breath do it. Breathe in again. On the exhale, feel from the lower back through the mid-back to the shoulders. Breathe in. On the exhale, feel from the shoulders through the neck to the base of the skull. Three more breaths. Feel the full length each time.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Place your right hand on your chest, over your heart. Feel the warmth of your own hand. Feel the rise and fall. You may feel a heartbeat. You may not. Do not look for it. Just feel the warmth and the movement. Breathe in slowly. Breathe out slowly. Six complete breaths. Keep the hand still. Keep the pressure light. Remove the hand after the sixth exhale.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
If you are near water: go to a sink. Run cold water over the inside of your wrists for thirty seconds. Feel the temperature. Do not rub. Just hold your wrists under the water. Breathe normally while the water runs. After thirty seconds, shake your hands dry. Return to your seat. Place your feet flat. Take three slow breaths before opening the screen again. If no water is available, press the inside of your wrists together and breathe six times.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Before you open the next notification: stop. Place your feet flat. Breathe in for four. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Notice what is happening in your chest or belly before you open it. Name the sensation in one word. Do not evaluate the word. Just name it. Breathe once more. Then open the notification. Do this practice before the next three notifications you open today.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Feel the weight of your body in the chair. Let yourself be heavy. Stop holding yourself up with muscle. Let the chair hold you. Let your arms rest without tension. Let your belly be soft. Breathe in. On the exhale, release any holding you find in the torso. Breathe in again. On the exhale, release any holding in the face. Four more breaths. Let the chair do the work. You do not have to hold anything right now.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Breathe in fully. Let half the air out. Stop. Hold for two counts. Let the remaining air out. Hold for two counts. Breathe in again. Repeat. Half out, pause, rest out, pause, breathe in. Four full cycles. Keep the pauses. Do not rush through them. The pause is part of the exercise.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Place your hands on your thighs or the desk. Feel the temperature of your own hands. Are they warm or cool? Feel where the warmth starts and stops. Now feel the temperature of whatever they are resting on. Notice the difference. Breathe in. On the exhale, relax your fingers slightly. Feel the temperature again. Five breaths. Keep noticing the temperature. Let it anchor you.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Look at the screen or the wall. Now let your focus go soft. Do not focus on anything specific. Let the edges of your vision widen. Let the center blur slightly. Keep your eyes open. Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Hold the soft focus. Five cycles. Do not squeeze your eyes. Keep them open and unfocused. After the fifth exhale, let your focus return to normal.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Close your eyes. Listen for the nearest sound. Name it. Listen for a sound further away. Name it. Listen for a sound further than that. Name it. One per breath. Continue outward until you reach the limit of what you can hear. Name each sound once. Do not evaluate it. Just name it. When you reach the limit, stay there for two breaths. Then open your eyes.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Breathe in completely. Pause at the top. Do not hold with effort. Just pause before the exhale begins. Let the pause be still. Then exhale. Breathe in again. Pause. Exhale. The pause should feel like stillness, not strain. If it feels like strain, shorten it. Six cycles. Vary the pause length until it feels still rather than held.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Take off your shoes if possible. Place your bare feet on the floor. Feel the texture of the floor against your soles. Press down slowly. Feel the resistance. Lift your heels slightly. Put them down. Lift your toes slightly. Put them down. Press the full foot flat. Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Four cycles. Keep the feet in full contact on each exhale.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Drop your chin toward your chest. Do not force it. Let the weight of your head create the stretch. Breathe in. On the exhale, let the chin drop a millimeter further. Three breaths. Lift your chin slowly back to neutral. Breathe once. Drop your right ear toward your right shoulder. Do not lift the shoulder. Breathe three times. Return to neutral. Repeat on the left side. Three breaths. Return to neutral. Breathe once.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Breathe in. On the exhale, count backward from ten. Ten, nine, eight, seven, six, five, four, three, two, one. Make the exhale last the full count. Count slowly. If you run out of breath before one, breathe in at any point and continue the count on the next exhale. Complete the countdown three times. After the third one, breathe normally for three breaths.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Place your hands on your ribs, one on each side. Breathe in and feel your ribs push outward against your hands. Not forward. Sideways. Feel the expansion laterally. Breathe out and feel them return. Keep your hands on the ribs. Five breaths. Focus on the sideways expansion, not the chest rising. After five breaths, remove your hands and breathe normally for two more.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Breathe all the air out first. Empty completely before you breathe in. Push the last of it out with the belly. Now breathe in. The inhale will come naturally. Breathe out completely again. Wait. Breathe in. The key is the complete exhale before the inhale. Not forcing — emptying. Six cycles. Start each cycle with the exhale, not the inhale.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Scan your body quickly. Find the place with the most tension right now. Do not try to release it. Breathe in and direct the breath toward that place. Imagine the breath reaching it. Breathe out from that place. Five breaths directed toward the tension. Do not force a release. Just breathe toward it. After the fifth exhale, notice if anything has changed. Do not evaluate. Just notice.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Hold your right hand out. Make a fist. Squeeze it for five counts. Release. Let the fingers spread. Feel the blood returning. Breathe in. Breathe out. Repeat with the left hand. Fist for five, release, breathe. Now both hands. Fist for five. Release both at once. Feel the fingers spread. Three breaths with both hands open. Then rest them on your thighs.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Close your eyes. Cover them lightly with your palms. Do not press. Just rest the palms over the eyes. Block the light. Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Keep the eyes closed and the palms in place. Five cycles. Do not think about what is next. Your only task is the breath and the dark. After the fifth exhale, remove your palms slowly. Let your eyes adjust before opening them.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Stop what you are doing completely. Put the phone down. Close the laptop or push back from the desk. Sit with nothing in your hands. Place your feet flat. Breathe in for four. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Do not check anything. Do not plan anything. Six complete cycles. You are not doing anything right now except breathing. After the sixth exhale, stay still for ten more seconds. Then return.
---
