Alex works in user support at a fintech startup. Spends the day answering questions about investments people are terrified about. Each conversation asks Alex to hold someone's anxiety, their financial fragility, their need to believe this app will save them. Alex does. Absorbs it. Cares actually. But at 6 PM, when the company finally quiets, Alex feels something like loss. Not of a person. Of a version of Alex that existed before this job. The version that had energy left over. That could care about a friend's problem without feeling like there was nothing left in the tank. That version is gone. The grief is quiet but it's there: mourning who you were before the work hollowed you out.
