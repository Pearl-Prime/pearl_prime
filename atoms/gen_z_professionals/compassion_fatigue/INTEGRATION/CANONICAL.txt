

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your chest is lighter now."
---
The call ended and something in your shoulders released. That feeling — that sudden drop of weight — is what happens when you stop holding someone else's crisis. For an hour you had their panic in your body. Your nervous system was running their emergency. That's what listening does at close range.
Notice your chest right now. Notice what your breath does when you're not responsible for someone else's answer. This is what resourced feels like. Not after-care or recovery. Just the weight that isn't being held anymore.
The call was real. Their struggle was real. The fatigue you feel now is the price of being someone who can absorb that much.
Your chest is lighter now.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "You gave until you had nothing."
---
Count this week's emotional labor. The friend who needed to talk for two hours while you made dinner. The peer who texted the spiral at midnight. The group chat that is perpetually in low-grade crisis. The colleague who says they're fine and you know they aren't. Each time you created space inside yourself for someone else's feeling.
This doesn't show up anywhere. Not in your calendar. Not in your salary. Not in anyone's gratitude — people rarely get their thanks together when they're drowning.
Your body kept the count. The heaviness in your shoulders. The flatness at the end of the day when everyone has had access to you but no one checked if you were full.
You gave until you had nothing.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You still answered. You were empty."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Weeks of this pattern. Some easier. Some where you had less to give and gave anyway. The difference between early-book exhaustion and late-book exhaustion is that now you know exactly what you're running out of. You can name it. Before, it was just a vague numbness.
The people you supported are still struggling. The world didn't improve because you listened. But something in you knows now what the price of caring this way has been. That knowledge changes how the giving happens, even if it doesn't stop it.
You didn't stop answering. That's what you notice now — you still answered when you had nothing left.
You still answered. You were empty.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would happen if you said no."
---
The question isn't whether people need support. They do. The question is what you believe will happen to you if you stop providing it. Maybe you'll be selfish. Maybe you'll be abandoning them right when they need you. Maybe you'll lose the relationships entirely because the relationships are built on your capacity to absorb their weight.
That last one is the true one sometimes. Not always. Sometimes people love you and want you to have boundaries. Sometimes the system has made you the support person and removing yourself means becoming invisible.
You don't have to answer tonight. The question just needs to stay open.
What would happen if you said no.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "They stopped taking calls. You know why."
---
Someone in your friend group withdrew recently. They don't return texts the same way. They're harder to reach. Your first read was that they got distant. The other read is that they were the person everyone else was supposed to rely on and they decided to stop burning at that temperature.
You know why they did it. You felt the pull right toward their exit. The version of you that's considering disappearing too.
They stopped taking calls. You know why.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: interrupt_landing
carry_line: "You said it. The word stayed."
note: interrupt_landing — use after The Interrupt story, quietest atom in the book
---
The moment you said no, you felt your body brace. Waiting for the consequence. The anger. The abandonment. The evidence that you were selfish. And then nothing came. Or something came that you can hold. Your body is still here. The relationship is still here. You said no and you didn't disappear.
Your hands know this now.
You said it. The word stayed.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It's emptied you for years now."
---
Trace back how long you've been doing this. The friend crisis you absorbed in university. The team member you became responsible for. The pattern of being the one who can hold more. Years. Some of it willingly. Some of it because the system organized itself around your capacity.
That's the cost. Not this week. Across time. Who you would have been if you hadn't spent this energy on other people's emergencies. The projects you didn't start. The relationships you didn't deepen because you were too full of someone else's needs. The version of yourself that had ambitions beyond absorbing.
It's emptied you for years now.
