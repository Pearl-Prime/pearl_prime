Sam works as a therapist at a clinic. All day: people's pain, people's growth, people's healing. Sam shows up and does the work. But at some point, usually around client four or five, something shifts. Sam feels like he's watching himself listen. Like there's a Sam doing the therapy and another Sam floating above it, observing. The observer-Sam notes: you should care more. You should be present. But the watching keeps happening. Sam is there and also not there. He's functioning—doing everything right, saying the right things—but the feeling has gone numb. The watcher engine is activated. The split has begun.
