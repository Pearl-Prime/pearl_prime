

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
After listening to someone's crisis, sit down immediately. You are not responsible for fixing it. Place both feet flat on the floor. Feel the ground. Count to five. Your feet are separate from their pain. Feel this separation. Place both hands on your thighs. Press down. You have a body. Your body is yours. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Breathe out through your mouth for six counts. Do this three times. Your nervous system is separate from theirs. It does not need to absorb their crisis. You have released it back to them.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close the door to your office or workspace. If there is no door, go to a bathroom. You need privacy. Sit or stand. You just spent emotional energy. You are now refueling. Place both hands on your chest. Feel your heartbeat. Count five beats. Your heart belongs to you. Close your eyes. Say: "I have given enough today." Say it twice. Open your eyes. You have told the truth. Your body believes it. You are allowed to be empty now.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
On your lunch break, sit alone. Not at your desk. Not with colleagues. Alone. Place one hand on your belly. Breathe into your belly slowly. Count to four. Feel it expand. Count to four on the exhale. Feel it contract. Do this five times. Your belly is your center. Compassion fatigue lives in the chest and throat. Anchor yourself in your belly instead. Stay here. Breathe here. This is your refuel station.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
After a difficult conversation, stand up. Walk to a window or sink. Run cold water on your wrists. Hold them under cold water for ten seconds. Feel the shock. Feel the clarity. Your nervous system has been hijacked by empathy. Cold water resets it. Pat your wrists dry. You are back in your body. You are separate from them again. Walk away from the sink slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit on your chair. Place both shoulders up toward your ears. Hold for three seconds. You have been carrying someone else's weight. Drop your shoulders. Feel the release. Your shoulders do not need to carry their burden. Repeat five times. Each time, feel the weight leave. Shake your arms gently. Shake out the absorbed emotion. Let it fall to the floor. It belongs there now, not in you.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
At your desk, close your eyes. Count backward from twenty to one slowly. This stops active worry. Count: twenty, nineteen, eighteen. Slow down for each number. When you reach one, open your eyes. Your nervous system has interrupted its empathetic spiral. You have stopped the loop. You are back in the present moment. Look at your desk. You are here, not in their crisis.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
In a bathroom, splash your face with water slowly. Feel the water run down your cheeks. Feel it. This is sensation. This is now. Pat your face dry. Look at yourself. Say: "I am not responsible for their healing." Hear your voice. Believe it. Your voice is proof that you are separate. You are a separate person. They are a separate person. You have released them.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Sit on a transit seat. Place both feet flat on the floor. Feel the floor beneath the vehicle. Feel the movement beneath you. You are moving away from them. Notice this. Feel your spine as the vehicle moves. Movement is change. You are changing locations. Your emotional state will change too. Just move away. Let time and distance heal you. You are moving.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
On your couch, lie down. Place one hand on your chest, one on your belly. This is the vulnerable position. You have given yourself away. Now you receive back. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for six counts. Do this five times. Each breath brings oxygen to you. Each exhale releases their pain that you borrowed. Breathe until you feel lighter.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Stand in your workspace. Slowly turn in a complete circle. As you turn, say: "That was their experience." Keep turning and repeating this. Turn five times. By the fifth turn, you understand: their crisis was theirs. You witnessed it. You did not create it. You cannot solve it. Stop turning. Stand still. Feel the ground. You are still standing. They are still their own person.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Sit upright. Close your eyes. Scan your body from the top of your head to your toes. Notice where you feel tension: jaw, throat, chest, stomach. Count the tense areas. Usually three to five. Now reverse the scan: toes to head. As you move upward, intentionally relax each area. Reach your head. Open your eyes. You have mapped and released the pain you absorbed. You are lighter now.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
After a heavy conversation, go to your bathroom. Fill a glass with cool water slowly. Watch the water fill the glass. Watch it become full. You are like this glass. You have been full of their emotions. Now you fill yourself with water instead. Drink it slowly. Count the swallows: one, two, three. Feel the water hydrate you. You are replenishing your own body. Drink completely.

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
At your desk, place both palms down flat. Press them into the desk firmly. Feel the pressure. Your hands are grounded. You are grounded. Press for five seconds. Release. Do this five times. Each press and release reminds your body: you are here, not there. You are separate. Your hands did not cause their pain. Your hands are clean. Press them together gently. Rest them in your lap.

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Sit in a quiet space. Hum for six seconds. Feel the vibration in your chest. Stop. Hum again. Feel it. The vibration is clearing blocked energy. Your chest held their pain. Humming releases it. Hum five times. Each hum is a small evacuation of borrowed emotion. By the fifth hum, your chest feels lighter. Your voice has healed you. Stop humming. Breathe normally. Feel the silence.

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Shift your weight side to side slowly. Feel your left foot press down. Count to three. Shift to your right foot. Count to three. Do this ten times. Side to side. Left, right, left, right. Your body is learning: you are here, grounded, separate. You have a location. They have a location. These are different. Stand still. Feel the separation in your feet.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
On your couch, lie back. Cover yourself with a blanket or jacket. This is a cocoon. You are safe inside it. You are not responsible for the world outside this cocoon. Feel the fabric on your skin. Feel contained. Feel held. Breathe normally. Stay here for one minute. Just breathe. Just be held. After one minute, uncover yourself slowly. You have replenished. You are ready to move.

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Sit upright. Close your eyes. Say silently: "I release what was not mine to carry." Say it three times. Feel it settle in your body. Say: "I keep what is mine: my rest, my peace." Say it three times. Feel it anchor. Open your eyes. You have separated yourself from them. You have kept yourself separate. Your nervous system knows the difference now.

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
At a sink, wash your hands slowly with warm water. Rub your hands together completely. Count to twenty. Feel the warmth. Feel the movement. You are washing away the weight. Rinse your hands. Count ten seconds. Pat them dry. Look at your clean hands. They are yours. They did not cause harm. They helped. Your hands are clean. They can rest now.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
On transit or in your car, focus on sounds around you. Count five sounds that are not related to your conversation: traffic, voices, doors, music, birds. Just sounds. No meaning. No weight. Listen without interpreting. Your brain has been interpreting their pain. Now interpret nothing. Just sound. Hear five sounds. Let them move through you. They belong to the world, not to you.

Your body continued functioning. You did not absorb their pain so deeply that you stopped. You are resilient. Count ten beats one more time. Feel your resilience. Feel your aliveness.

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Place one hand on your shoulder. Squeeze gently. You have been carrying weight. Now you carry yourself gently. Move your hand to your other shoulder. Squeeze gently. Repeat five times, alternating shoulders. Each squeeze is self-care. Each squeeze says: "I am taking care of me." By the fifth squeeze, your shoulders feel lighter. Your arms know: you are worth care too.

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Sit on your couch. Close your eyes. Think of one moment today that was good: one laugh, one kind interaction, one moment of beauty. Hold this moment. Feel it in your body. Feel the goodness. Stay here for one minute. Your brain tends to hold onto crisis. Make it hold onto goodness instead. Open your eyes. You have reset your emotional memory.

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Stand and shake your entire body. Shake your arms, legs, torso. Shake for ten seconds. Feel the vibration. Feel the release. Compassion fatigue is stagnant. Shaking mobilizes it. Shake for five more seconds. Stop. Feel the stillness after shaking. Feel lighter. Feel mobile. Feel alive. Stand still. You have physically moved the stuck energy. It is gone.

---

---

Tonight you receive rest. Tonight your body replenishes itself. Your body knows how to heal. Let it. Feel your belly rise and fall. Feel yourself becoming whole again. Sleep well.
