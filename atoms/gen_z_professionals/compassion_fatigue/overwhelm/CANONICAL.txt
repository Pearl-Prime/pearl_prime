Priya works at a nonprofit doing education advocacy. Every day brings requests: a school needs help navigating funding, a family needs guidance through special ed, a student needs mentorship, a policy window just opened and needs input, colleagues are struggling and need support, her manager is drowning and needs help carrying it. The requests come from every direction simultaneously. Priya is supposed to be able to say yes to some and no to others. But saying no means a child doesn't get what they need. The overwhelm settles in: there are infinite needs and a finite Priya. She can't say no because someone will be hurt. She can't say yes to all of it because she's already past capacity. The overwhelm is the recognition that there's no right answer.
