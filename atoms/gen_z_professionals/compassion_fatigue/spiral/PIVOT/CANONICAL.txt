--- variant: v01
The spiral feels like problem-solving but it's a hamster wheel. Your mind applies the analysis tool to compassion fatigue, which lives in the body, and gets nothing but spin. The old frame says think harder. The new frame: the spiral is what happens when the wrong tool meets the right problem. You can't debug a feeling with logic alone.

--- variant: v02
The compassion fatigue spiral picks up speed because every loop adds a new "what if" without resolving the last one. It's a tab explosion in your browser -- each one loading, none completing. The pivot isn't closing all the tabs. It's recognizing that the browser itself needs a restart, not more tabs.

--- variant: v03
You mistake the spiral for depth. But going in circles isn't going deeper. The spiral runs the same three fears past the same three scenarios and calls it thorough. Thorough would mean reaching a conclusion. The spiral specifically avoids conclusions because conclusions require action, and action means risking being wrong.
