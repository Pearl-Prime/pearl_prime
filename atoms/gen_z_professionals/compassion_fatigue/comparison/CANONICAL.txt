Maya sits across from the senior clinician in the break room. The senior clinician is present. Actually present. She speaks to patients with something that looks like care. Her hands are steady. Maya watches her and feels her stomach drop. The senior clinician has been doing this for fifteen years. Maya has been doing this for two. And yet the difference Maya sees is this: the senior clinician hasn't depleted and Maya has. This means something is wrong with Maya. Not with the field. Not with the structure. With her. Her jaw tightens. She is fundamentally less suited for care work than the woman across from her.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya doesn't know that the senior clinician works four days a week. That she has a partner at home who handles most household labor. That she stopped doing overnight shifts five years ago. That she's been in trauma therapy for two years. That she's learned to say no to some things. That she has financial resources that allow her to choose her work. Maya only sees the surface presence. The comparison is clean and devastating because it ignores every variable except presence.
