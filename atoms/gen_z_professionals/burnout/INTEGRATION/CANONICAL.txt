Underneath it is a Sunday evening you have not had since you took this job. The depletion does not lift on the first Sunday. The first Sunday is the first deposit in an account that has been empty for fourteen months.

Sunday at 8 PM, close the laptop. One lid. One night. One small no the body can hear.

## INTEGRATION v27

stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Each Friday at 5 PM, write the one thing the burnout took this week."
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: gen_z_professionals
note: closer chapter — named action, ledger practice
---
The chapter opened with a body keeping a ledger that the OKR doc does not track. It closes by handing you the ledger.

Here is the practice. Each Friday at 5 PM, before you close the laptop, open a Notion page called "what burnout took this week." Write one line. Not the work cost. The other cost. The dinner you canceled. The book you did not open. The friend you did not call. The hobby you did not return to.

One line. Not a list. The point is not to fix the cost. What this means is to put the cost on a page where you can see it, because the cost has been invisible and the invisibility is part of what made it sustainable. The system is engineered so the costs do not show up in your performance review. The Notion page is the part of the review the system does not run.

Notice what happens by the fourth Friday. You will start to see a pattern in what burnout has been taking from you. The pattern is not a verdict. The pattern is information. Choose what to do with the information after you have lived with it for a month. For now, write the line. Close the page. The week is over. The ledger is current.

Each Friday at 5 PM, write the one thing the burnout took this week.

carry_line: "What would you do this weekend if your output were already enough."
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: gen_z_professionals
---
The chapter opened with a body running a rate the calendar could not see. It closes with a question you do not have to answer this weekend. The question is this. What would you do this weekend if your output were already enough.

Not "if you had finished everything" — the work-system you live in does not produce finishing as a state. The question is the other one. If the thing you have already shipped were enough. Today. This Sunday. As is. What does the weekend look like.

Sit with the question. Do not solve it. The answer is not yet available because the body cannot yet feel "enough" as a body-state. The body has been calibrated to "more." But the question is the first move. It is the first time in fourteen months you have been asked to imagine a Saturday that is not a recovery from one week and a preparation for the next.

The question is not a plan. The question is a clearing. What would you do this weekend if your output were already enough.
