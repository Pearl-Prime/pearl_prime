COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Sam is working on a freelance project that's running late. The deadline was promised to the client. Sam can make the deadline by working through the weekend. But working through the weekend is what Sam said Sam wouldn't do anymore. So Sam considers not making the deadline. Considers telling the client the truth: I can't deliver on time. Immediately the thought spirals: if I miss the deadline, the client will be upset. If the client is upset, they'll hire someone else next time. If they hire someone else, the income drops. If the income drops, the rent becomes hard to pay. If the rent becomes hard to pay, Sam's housing is at risk. If housing is at risk, everything falls apart. So Sam has to make the deadline. Has to work the weekend. Has to override the boundary that was supposed to protect the burnout from getting worse. The thought loop produces one outcome: work anyway. The spiral doesn't provide solutions. It provides justifications for unsustainable choices. Sam is locked in.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/burnout/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Sam starts to see the pattern. The spiral always starts with a decision point. A moment where a choice exists. Then the spiral jumps three to five steps ahead. Connects the initial decision to financial catastrophe or loss. Then concludes: don't make the choice that would create that scenario. The mechanism is that the spiral skips all the middle steps. Doesn't explore whether the catastrophe would actually happen. Doesn't assess probability. Just assumes the worst-case connection is inevitable. The spiral that says "miss deadline → client leaves → income drops → housing at risk" is missing all the steps where reality intervenes. The client might understand. Might stay for other reasons. Might actually appreciate the honesty. But the spiral doesn't care about those probabilities. It just calculates: if the worst happens, can I survive it? The answer is usually no. So the spiral forces the choice: do the unsustainable thing to prevent the worst case. The mechanism is that spirals prevent changes by catastrophizing the exit.
