## HOOK v31
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: gen_z_professionals
---
You closed Slack at 11:47 PM and the closing did not register as relief. The unread count dropped to zero and your chest did not. You are not behind. You are not even tired in the way the word usually means. What you are is a system that has been outputting at its rated capacity for fourteen months and has begun, very quietly, to forget what it used to want. The promotion is on the calendar for Q3. The salary is fine. Your manager says you are doing well. None of these are the report your body is filing.
---

## HOOK v32
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: gen_z_professionals
---
The pour-over takes four minutes. You set the timer. You stare at the water. The four minutes are the longest four minutes of the morning because for four minutes there is nothing to optimize, nothing to respond to, nothing to ship. The four minutes are also where the thing you have been outrunning since the alarm went off catches up to you. By minute three you are reaching for the phone. The phone has nothing for you. The phone is what saves you from the four minutes.
---

## HOOK v33
---
stage: pearl_writer_stage_3
register: burnout_depletion_identity_erosion_capacity_gap
persona_anchor: gen_z_professionals
---
Your friend asks what you do for fun. You stall. Not because the list is long. Because the list is shorter than it was at twenty-two and you have not yet found a way to say that out loud without it sounding like a failure. The hobbies did not leave at once. They left in the gaps between sprints, one missed yoga class at a time, until the gaps were the shape of your life and the hobbies were a thing you used to do.
