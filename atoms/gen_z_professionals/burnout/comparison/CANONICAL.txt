

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/burnout/comparison/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Priya sits in the break room and watches the senior nurses move through their shift. They look fine. They look efficient. They move like people who aren't depleted. Priya is collapsing by hour six. Can barely stand by hour eight. The comparison circuit activates immediately. Stomach drops. Jaw tightens. The thought arrives clean and clear: they're doing this job and they're okay. I'm doing the same job and I'm broken. The gap between her visible depletion and their apparent sustainability feels like evidence of personal failure. She's been a nurse as long as some of them. The difference is something about her — some weakness, some incapacity, some fundamental lack of whatever it takes to sustain this. The other nurses don't mention being tired. Don't mention struggling. They make jokes and move efficiently and seem fine. Priya is trying to hide that she's shaking. The comparison converts a structural problem — the job is genuinely depleting — into a personal one: I'm not cut out for this.

Must be more capable.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/burnout/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya realizes the mechanism. The other nurses don't mention depletion. But they also don't mention their lives outside the hospital. She knows one of them sees a therapist. Knows another one reduced their hours last year. Knows the senior one had panic attacks early in their career. But none of that is visible. What's visible is the efficient movement through the shift. What's visible is the absence of visible struggle. The comparison circuit fires on visible data only. The invisible depletion of the colleagues doesn't register. The mechanism is that comparison uses public information to assess private internal states. Everyone projects okayness. The people who are actually managing sometimes have invisible support — a partner who handles home, savings that create a buffer, health insurance that covers therapy, work flexibility that the casual observer can't see. But comparing the visible output without the visible support systems feels like proof of personal inadequacy. Priya's visible depletion combined with the assumption that others have no depletion becomes evidence that something is specifically wrong with Priya.

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex starts to see the mechanism. The colleagues posting wins in Slack are posting wins. They're not posting the failures, the restless sleep, the anxiety about sustainability. They're posting the moments where the sprint delivered. Where the career moved. And Alex is comparing the full internal experience of burnout to the curated external moments of success. The mechanism is that comparison uses asymmetric information. Alex has access to all of Alex's internal struggle. Alex has access to none of the colleagues' internal struggle. So comparing Alex's full 24-hour experience of depletion and anxiety to the 60-second Slack post of achievement is a rigged comparison. But it feels real because the evidence is right there. They're moving forward. Alex isn't. But the real variable isn't capacity — it's that they're not posting about their bad days and Alex is living in them.
