COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Kai is trying to decide on a professional development course. The options are clear. The choice is not complex. But Kai's mind is processing multiple factors simultaneously. Cost versus benefit. Time commitment versus current workload. Whether this skill aligns with the current role or positions for a different role. Whether choosing one option forecloses other options. Kai is aware, with some embarrassment, that normal people just decide. They look at the options and pick one. Kai is here, analyzing at depth, and it's been three hours. The shame sits quietly in the recognition: I think too much about simple things. There's something broken in how my brain works. Other people don't do this. The jaw tightens. The shoulders rise slightly. The shame is not about the decision. The shame is about the fact that the decision is generating this much cognitive activity. Normal people would have chosen by now.

Dev is watching this and thinking: how do they just decide like that?
