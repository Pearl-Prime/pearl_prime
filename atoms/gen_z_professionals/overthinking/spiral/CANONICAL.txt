It starts with a what if. Alex is in the shower, not thinking about anything in particular, and then one thought arrives. What if the pull request has a bug. And the what if spirals. What if the bug makes it to production. What if the bug causes downtime. What if downtime gets traced to the PR. His chest tightens. His shoulders lift toward his ears. He's not in the office. He's in the shower at 7am. The thought found him anyway. He knows the spiral is happening. He can't stop it. The warm water hits his back and he's already three steps into a chain of consequences that might not exist.

Kai traces the spiral's anatomy later. One observation enters: her message response time has slowed. That is the first thought. The spiral needs that thought to be evidence of something. Sam tells his friend about the numbers spiral. His friend asks: have you ever run the numbers and found a problem you hadn't already considered? Sam thinks. Honestly, no. He runs the numbers because the anxiety says to. The numbers never add information he didn't already have. His friend says: so the running isn't solving. Sam sits with that.
