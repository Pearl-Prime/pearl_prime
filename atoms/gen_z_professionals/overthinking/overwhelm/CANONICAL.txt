

## RECOGNITION v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/overthinking/overwhelm/recognition/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 1
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Priya is choosing a restaurant for lunch. The lunch break is only thirty minutes. The choice should be simple. But Priya's mind is generating weight. The restaurant has good food, but is the wait time worth it? The other option is faster but the quality is lower. What if Priya chooses the fast option and regrets the lunch quality? What if Priya chooses the good option and runs out of time? What if Priya gets back late and it impacts the afternoon workflow? What if the impact on afternoon workflow causes problems that cascade into the evening? The chest gets heavy. The mind is assigning significance to the lunch choice that the choice doesn't actually carry. Priya is aware that a lunch choice shouldn't generate this much cognitive load. But the load is present. The decision is paralyzed by the weight of the variables. The hands feel cold. The body is in a state of overwhelm over something that shouldn't be overwhelming.

Priya forces a decision. It's arbitrary. Priya picks the closer restaurant. The lunch happens. The lunch is fine. Nothing catastrophic happens.
