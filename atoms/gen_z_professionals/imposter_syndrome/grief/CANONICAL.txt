Sam is three years into the freelance career. Sam should be confident by now. Should know the market. Should have a clear voice. Should trust the process. Sam doesn't. Every project still generates the same not-knowing. Is this right? Is the client going to want something different? Am I actually getting better or just getting faster at this particular thing? Sam watches other creatives who've been working for three years. They seem settled. They seem to know what they do. They seem to own it. Sam is supposed to be that version of Sam by now. The version that existed in Sam's imagination three years ago—the competent creative who trusted the work, who felt the authority of experience. That Sam hasn't arrived. The chest has a small hollow feeling in it. The throat has a slight tightness. This is the grief. Not grief about something that happened. Grief about something that was supposed to happen and didn't.
