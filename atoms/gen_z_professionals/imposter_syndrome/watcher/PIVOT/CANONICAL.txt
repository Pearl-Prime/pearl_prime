--- variant: v01
The watcher monitors your imposter syndrome from the outside -- grading your reactions, scoring your composure, flagging any sign that you look affected. The old frame calls this self-awareness. The new frame: the watcher is a surveillance system you built to stay ahead of judgment. It doesn't reduce the imposter syndrome. It adds a performance layer on top of it.

--- variant: v02
You're simultaneously experiencing imposter syndrome and evaluating how you look experiencing it. The watcher splits you into the one feeling and the one scoring. That split is exhausting because you're running two processes on a system designed for one. The pivot: the watcher was useful when being watched was survival. Now it's just doubling your cognitive load.

--- variant: v03
The watcher whispers: are you handling this correctly? Does anyone notice? Are you performing feeling like a fraud the right way? Here's the reframe: the watcher doesn't protect you from judgment. It just means you judge yourself first, faster, and harsher than anyone else could. That's not awareness. That's preemptive self-criticism running on autopilot.
