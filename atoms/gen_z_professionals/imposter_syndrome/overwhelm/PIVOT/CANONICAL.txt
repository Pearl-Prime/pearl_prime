--- variant: v01
The old frame: your imposter syndrome proves you can't handle it. The new frame: you're handling a workload designed for three people in a system that calls it one job. The overwhelm isn't about your capacity. It's about the gap between what's expected and what's humanly possible. The imposter feeling isn't lying to you about your competence. It's telling you the scoreboard doesn't measure what you actually value.

--- variant: v02
You think the overwhelm means you're failing at feeling like a fraud. It doesn't. It means your input queue is maxed and nobody throttled the incoming. No app performs well with that many background processes. You're not the problem. The architecture is.

--- variant: v03
They sold you productivity tools to manage the overwhelm. But the overwhelm isn't a productivity problem. It's a load problem. Optimizing how you carry too much still leaves you carrying too much. The pivot: stop optimizing the carrying. Start questioning the load.
