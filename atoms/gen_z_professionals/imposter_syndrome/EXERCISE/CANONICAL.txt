## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Stop. You're experiencing imposter syndrome again.
Name one specific thing you built.
Not "I helped on a project."
One concrete deliverable with your name.
Who would that project look like without you?
Worse. It would be worse.
You weren't faking your contribution then.
You're not faking it right now.
Write this down and read it again.
Your brain needs the evidence.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Open your work emails from the past month.
Find one email where someone thanked you.
Find one where you solved something.
Find one where you started something new.
These weren't mistakes. These were real wins.
You didn't luck into these moments.
You actually did the work.
Imposter syndrome is lying.
Screenshot one email or save it.
Look at it when doubt hits.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Think about one skill you've actually learned.
The fact that you had to learn it.
You weren't born knowing it. You got good.
That means you're not a fraud.
That means you can learn anything else.
Tell yourself: I earned this role.
Say it even if it feels weird.
Your brain is just slow to catch up.
Repeat it every time you doubt.
This is how you rewire imposter thoughts.
---
