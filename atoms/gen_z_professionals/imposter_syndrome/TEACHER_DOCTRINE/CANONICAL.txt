## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v01
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: false_alarm
weight: standard
carry_line: "The alarm fires when you hit Send on a deliverable, not because the work is wrong but because being seen is the threat."
---
Here is the mechanism. Imposter syndrome borrows the body's old alarm system and points it at a new shape of threat: visibility. You hit Send on a Notion doc, post the deliverable to a Slack channel, share your screen in a 1:1, and the alarm fires the same way it would fire if you had stepped into a clearing where predators could see you. Chest tightens, breath shortens, attention narrows to anything that could be wrong. The work itself is not the problem. The problem, to the alarm system, is exposure. It does not matter that the deliverable is good. It does not even read the deliverable. It reads the act of being looked at, of placing yourself inside someone else's evaluation window, and it categorizes that act as risk. This is why the dread does not scale with the quality of your work. You can ship an objectively strong analysis and still feel the same hot, sour swallow when you press send. The alarm is not auditing your output. It is responding to visibility. What changes when you see this is that you stop arguing with the alarm about whether the work is good enough. The alarm was never the right judge.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v02
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: spiral
weight: standard
carry_line: "The chain runs: one piece of feedback, then the whole role, then your career, then your worth. Each link is invented past the first."
---
Here is the mechanism. The prediction loop, when it gets imposter content as input, runs a specific chain. Your manager leaves a comment on a Notion doc: "Can we tighten this section?" The loop receives "tighten this section" and runs it forward. Tighten this section becomes I missed something obvious. I missed something obvious becomes I am not strong on the fundamentals. I am not strong on the fundamentals becomes They are starting to notice. They are starting to notice becomes the next round of layoffs becomes the conversation with my parents becomes the resume gap. By the time you finish reading the comment, the loop has built ten links that were not in the comment. Each link is treated as evidence for the next, even though only the first one came from outside you. The rest is interior architecture pretending to be analysis. What helps is naming the chain in slow motion. "Tighten this section. Missed something. Not strong on fundamentals. Layoffs." When you can see the links, you also see how quickly the loop crossed from feedback to existential threat. The 1:1 still happens. The chain stops borrowing certainty from links it invented.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v03
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: comparison
weight: standard
carry_line: "The engine measures your interior against your peers' edited surface and rules you a fraud on rigged math."
---
Here is the mechanism. The comparison engine, in imposter conditions, runs the most asymmetric subtraction it knows how to run. It takes what you have access to about yourself — every doubt, every half-finished draft, every moment of confusion in a meeting — and weighs it against what you have access to about your peers, which is mostly their LinkedIn, their cohort updates, their confident tone in standup. You have all of your interior. You have almost none of theirs. The engine treats this as a fair comparison. It looks at your former classmate's "thrilled to share" promotion announcement and your own midweek confusion about a project scope, and concludes you are behind. It does not have access to your classmate's interior, where there is almost certainly an equivalent confusion that did not make it to the announcement. The engine does not flag the asymmetry. It runs the subtraction anyway. Recognizing the rigged math does not make the feeling disappear. It does change what the feeling is evidence of: not your inadequacy, but the engine's bad accounting on input it should never have been asked to compare.

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v04
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: overwhelm
weight: standard
carry_line: "Holding the secret of being a fraud across every Slack thread is the unpaid second job draining your day."
---
Here is the mechanism. Working memory has a small budget, and imposter syndrome eats into it from a direction most productivity advice does not name. Every meeting, every Slack thread, every shared Notion doc carries a hidden second task: the management of being perceived. You are not just answering the question in standup. You are also auditing your tone, scanning for anything that might reveal what you are sure other people would see if they looked closely enough. Across eight Slack channels, three meetings, and a deliverable, that audit runs in the background continuously. The cost is not visible on the calendar. It does not show up in the deliverable. It shows up in the fatigue at 4 pm that the day's actual work does not explain. This is what makes imposter syndrome so quietly expensive. It does not just feel bad. It runs an unpaid second process in working memory that competes with the work itself for the same scarce attention. Naming the audit is the start of choosing whether to keep paying it.
---

## TEACHER_DOCTRINE v05
---
mode: SECULAR_BRIDGE
doctrine_source: somatic
mechanism: shame
weight: standard
carry_line: "The secret belief is that everyone else belongs and you are the one who slipped through. The secrecy is the proof to yourself."
---
Here is the mechanism. Imposter shame is not the feeling of being underqualified. It is the conviction that you are the one in your cohort who is faking it, and that the others belong in a way you do not. The shame survives because you do not test it. You do not tell your college roommate that you almost cried before your last 1:1. You do not tell your standup peers that you googled a basic term last Tuesday. You hide the doubt, and in hiding it, you remove the only mechanism that would let you find out the doubt is shared. So you sit in a meeting watching everyone nod, certain they all understand something you do not, while several of them are running an identical interior monologue about you. The shame runs on a closed loop. It cannot be falsified, because the falsification would require admitting it, and admitting it feels like the very thing the shame says will get you exposed. Naming the mechanism does not fix the loop, but it interrupts the part where you treat the privacy of the doubt as evidence that you are uniquely defective. The privacy is the symptom. It is also the strategy. Both can change.
