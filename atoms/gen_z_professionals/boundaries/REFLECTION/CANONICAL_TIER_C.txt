

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

The boundary-setting machine in your nervous system was never installed because the environment where you learned to work never required it. You learned responsiveness instead. You learned that being valuable meant being available, that saying yes was the mechanism by which you proved you were serious, that the three-day turnaround was the speed that mattered. So now when you close Slack at 7pm, there is a cost calculated in your body: the cost of not responding, the cost of being the person who didn't, the cost of the small absence that might be noted. This is not paranoia. This is pattern recognition. You have been rewarded for responsiveness so thoroughly that the boundary between working and available has dissolved.

The boundary that is hardest to set is the one that would require someone else to manage without you. Because managing without you means your work environment has to change. Which means accepting that you are replaceable. Which means admitting that the work is not actually dependent on your constant presence. So the boundary remains unset because setting it would require a reckoning with the entire structure.
