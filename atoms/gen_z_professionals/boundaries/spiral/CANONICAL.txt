Kai is composing an email to the VP to say that the Q2 roadmap is unrealistic given the current capacity. It's a reasonable thing to say. The analysis is sound. But the thought comes: if I send this, the VP will think I can't handle the role. If the VP thinks I can't handle it, I'll be off the fast track. The thought chain is building speed. The chest is tight. The breath is shallow. It has produced paralysis. Kai closes the email tab and opens a spreadsheet instead, as if the math of the budget might somehow rearrange itself into possibility.
