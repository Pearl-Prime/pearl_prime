

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Close your laptop with both hands. Feel the screen go dark. Count to three. Place your palms flat on the closed lid. Press down gently. Notice the hardness beneath your hands. Say "work time is finished." Take one breath. Stand up slowly from your chair. Roll your shoulders backward five times. Feel tension leave with each rotation. Walk away from your desk completely. Do not look back at your screen. Sit somewhere else in your home. Feel the weight of your body on a different seat. Take three slow breaths. Your body knows when work has ended.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Put your phone face-down on your desk. Count to ten without touching it. Notice the urge to check it. Let the urge exist without acting. Take one slow breath through your nose. Exhale through your mouth completely. Pick up your phone with your eyes closed. Keep them closed. Power it off fully. Listen to the shutdown sound. Open your eyes. Place it in a drawer. Close the drawer firmly. Your notifications will wait. You will not wait for them. Feel relief in your shoulders.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Sit at your desk chair. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press down through your heels. Count the pressure points: left heel, right heel. Feel the ground beneath you. Put your hands on your thighs. Squeeze your leg muscles for five seconds. Release completely. Do this three times. Feel the boundary between work and rest. Your body is the border. Work ends where your muscle release begins. Stand up. Walk to another room. Your desk is behind you now.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit on your couch or chair. Close your eyes. Scan your body from head to toe. Notice where tension lives: jaw, shoulders, chest. Clench your fists for four seconds. Release. Do this twice more. Your hands remember work tasks. Release them anyway. Move your head side to side slowly. Count four times in each direction. Feel your neck soften. Open your eyes. You have given yourself permission to stop.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Before replying to an after-hours message, stop. Take three breaths. Ask your body: "Is this urgent?" Wait for the answer. Usually it is not. Put the phone down. Wait two minutes. The message will remain unchanged. You do not need to respond immediately. Your rest is non-negotiable. Take one more breath. Set the phone aside.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Stand in your bathroom. Close the door. Splash cold water on your wrists slowly. Count to five while the water runs. Feel the shock and reset. Pat your wrists dry. Look at your face in the mirror. Say one boundary out loud: "I am off work." Hear your voice. Believe it. This is not weakness. This is survival. Leave the bathroom. You have marked the transition.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Sit on your transit seat. Place your feet firmly on the floor. Feel the vehicle move beneath you. You are moving away from work. Notice this movement in your spine. Tilt your head forward slowly. Let your neck release. Count to five. Tilt to the right. Count to five. Tilt to the left. Count to five. Return to center. You are arriving somewhere new.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
At your desk, place one hand on your heart. Feel it beating. Count ten beats. Your heart worked hard today. Place your other hand on your belly. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Exhale for four counts. Do this three times. Your nervous system is listening. It hears: safety. Work is over. You are safe now. Stand and stretch your arms overhead. Feel space open in your chest.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Pick up your work phone or laptop charger. Hold it in both hands. This is the final ritual. Plug it in slowly. Watch it connect. Step away. Do not check your notifications one last time. Resist this. Your discipline is your boundary. Walk to your bedroom or furthest room. Leave the charger behind. You have physically separated yourself from work tools.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
On your couch, lie back slightly. Place one hand on your chest. Feel your breath. Slow it down intentionally. Breathe in through your nose: one thousand one, one thousand two. Breathe out through your mouth: one thousand one, one thousand two, one thousand three. Do this five times. Each breath is a boundary. Work cannot cross a boundary made of calm. Your breathing is the border.

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Sit upright in a chair. Clench every muscle in your body. Face, jaw, shoulders, arms, belly, legs. Squeeze for five seconds. Make fists. Tense everything. Hold it. Release completely all at once. Do nothing for two seconds. Feel the difference. Feel the afterimage of tension. Recognize it belonged to work. You have released it. Do this two more times. On the third release, stay loose.

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Before bed, sit on your mattress. Press your hands into the mattress below you. Feel its support. This is not your desk chair. This is rest furniture. Lie back on your pillow. Feel it receive your head. Count five areas where your body touches the bed. Head, shoulders, back, hips, legs. Feel five points of contact. You are grounded in rest. Your body is no longer in work position.

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
In a bathroom or quiet space, splash cold water on your face. Feel the shock. Count to four. Pat your face dry. Look at yourself. Say: "I set my phone down." Say it twice. Your nervous system needs to hear this. Repeat: "Work waits for me, not the reverse." Feel the statement settle in your body. Your jaw may relax. Your shoulders may drop. This is the somatic evidence of your boundary.

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
At your desk, push your chair back slowly. Stand up. Do not sit back down. Walk in a circle around your workspace. Notice every corner of it. This is the perimeter. You are walking outside of it now. On your third lap, walk toward the exit. Leave that space. Do not return today. You have physically completed work. Your body knows the session has ended. Rest now.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Hold your shoulders up toward your ears. Squeeze them up for three seconds. Drop them completely. Feel the fall. Your shoulders carried work stress. Release it down. Repeat five times. Each time, feel the muscles let go. Shake your arms gently. Shake out the workday. Shake from your wrists, elbows, shoulders. Let your arms move loosely. Feel the vibration of release. Stop shaking. Feel the stillness. This is post-work stillness.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
On your couch, lie on your back. Place your arms at your sides, palms up. This is the vulnerable position. Work requires defense. Rest requires openness. Stay here for ten breath cycles. Breathe in deeply through your nose. Breathe out completely through your mouth. Count each cycle. One, two, three. By ten, your nervous system believes you are safe. Stay here.

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Sit in your chair. Place both palms on your thighs. Press them down firmly. Feel your power. Now relax them completely. Feel the release. Press and release five times. Work requires you to grip. Rest requires you to let go. Notice the difference. On the final release, let your hands rest. Let them be soft. Your hands do not need to work now.

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Unplug your work laptop slowly. Watch the light go out. Count to three. Carry it to another room. Place it on a shelf. Close a door between you and it. You have created distance. Physical distance becomes mental distance. Walk away. Do not look back. Your hands are now free. Feel them swing at your sides. They are not typing. They are resting.

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Stand in front of a window. Look outside. Look at something not on a screen. Count five things you see: tree, sky, car, person, building. Your eyes have stared at screens all day. Give them new input. Let them rest on distant objects. Close your eyes. Feel the light on your eyelids. Keep them closed for five breaths. Open them. Your eyes have reset.

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Sit at your desk one final time for the day. Close your eyes. Mentally scan your day. Find one moment you wish you had handled differently. Hold it without judgment. Say: "I will handle this differently tomorrow." Release it. Find one moment you handled well. Hold it. Say: "I am learning." Release it. Open your eyes. You have processed. You have released. You may leave.

---

Your ears are escaping the office. Close your eyes for one minute. Just listen. Let sound move through you without meaning. Open your eyes. You have crossed the boundary between work and commute.

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
In a bathroom, wash your hands slowly. Feel the water temperature. Warm or cold, notice it. Rub your hands together completely. Count twenty seconds. Count out loud if you must: "one, two, three..." Feel the friction. Feel the soap. You are washing away the workday. Rinse your hands. Pat them dry. They are now clean. They are ready for evening. Not for work.

---

Work lives in your facial tension. Release it. Smile gently without forcing it. Feel the muscles you use. Rest them. Your face does not need to perform now.

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Before sleep, lie in bed. Place one hand on your heart, one on your belly. Feel both. Say silently: "I set a boundary today." Feel the boundary in your body. Say: "Tomorrow I will set another." Feel the promise. Say: "Tonight I rest." Feel the permission. Breathe normally. Count ten breaths. By the tenth breath, your nervous system knows the day has ended. You are safe. Sleep now.
