The real frame: comparison runs a rigged algorithm -- it only imports other people's highlights and compares them to your behind-the-scenes. That's not motivation. That's running someone else's validation app on your hardware. Your worth was never the output. The system that made worth conditional was the one that broke.

You were taught that measuring yourself against others would sharpen your edge. What it actually sharpened was the sense that you're falling behind. When self worth meets comparison, the result isn't clarity. It's a leaderboard you never signed up for, scored by metrics you didn't choose.

--- variant: v03
The comparison engine promises perspective. What it delivers is paralysis. Because when you stack your self worth against someone else's curated version of handling it, you'll always lose. The pivot: stop comparing your unfiltered reality to their exported highlights. Different source data, different conclusions.
