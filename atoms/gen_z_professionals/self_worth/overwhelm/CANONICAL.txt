---
Maya is standing in her apartment and there are too many fronts of not-enoughness active at the same time. Professionally: behind peers. She should have been promoted by now. She's 24 and hasn't hit the career milestones the 24-year-olds in her network have hit.
Financially: precarious. The rent eats 45% of her income. She has $3,000 in savings. This feels unstable and young and unprepared.
Physically: not what she's supposed to be. Wants to work out more, to have the body the other young people at her company seem to have. Wants to sleep eight hours and look rested and vibrant. Wakes up and looks tired.
Personally: hasn't met someone, hasn't built a stable partnership, still navigating the mess of online dating. Watches peers pair off and move into the next life stage.
Each dimension alone would be manageable. But they're all active simultaneously. She's not good enough at work AND financially behind AND physically not optimized AND personally unpartnered.
Her chest is heavy with the weight of too many inadequacies at once.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/self_worth/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya's mechanism is about accumulated verdicts. Each front of inadequacy delivers a verdict: not good enough. The verdicts stack.
When there's one verdict, the system can compensate. But five verdicts simultaneously? The system can't compensate. The system overloads.
The mechanism is: she can't fix career fast enough (timescale: years). She can't fix finances fast enough (timescale: years). She can't fix body fast enough (timescale: months). She can't fix relationship status fast enough (timescale: unknown).
So she's living in a state of unresolvable insufficient-ness across multiple timescales. This creates a kind of paralysis that isn't laziness. It's genuine system overload from too many active problems at once.
When you're behind on five fronts, the motivation to work on any one of them decreases because working on one doesn't solve the fact that you're behind on four others.

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Dev's mechanism is about comparative assessment. Dev is holding up his own life against an imagined standard (or against other people's curated life presentations) and the gap is enormous.
The gap exists across multiple dimensions. It's not just career — it's also personal life, health, relationships, financial stability, side projects, learning.
The brain tries to calculate how to close the gap. But the gap is so large and on so many fronts that there's no viable strategy. You can't close all the gaps.
So Dev goes into a kind of dissociation. Removes himself from trying to close them. This looks like depression or apathy, but it's actually a rational system response to an impossible problem.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/self_worth/overwhelm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Maya is at her limit and something has to give. She decides: I'm going to pick one front to invest in and accept that the others will be behind for a while.
This sounds simple but it's terrifying. Because accepting that the body front is behind feels like accepting she's not taking care of herself. Accepting that the financial front is slow feels like accepting she's not adulting properly.
But she picks the career front and starts investing in that specifically. Proposal responses faster. Meetings better prepared. Takes on visible work.
Within two months, there's movement. Not a promotion, but visible forward motion.
And something unexpected: the stress lessens. Not because the other fronts improved, but because she's no longer trying to improve them all simultaneously.
Her chest loosens slightly.
