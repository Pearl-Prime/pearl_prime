## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/self_worth/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Alex's mechanism is about vertical collapse. The brain takes a specific performance issue and converts it into a general verdict.
The mistake is at the level of: this specific action was imperfect.
But the brain collapses it vertically to: I am imperfect → I am not competent → I should not have this job → I am a fraud.
The collapse happens because the brain is looking for a generalization. If I made a mistake here, and I'm generally a certain type of person, then maybe I'm that type of person across the board.
The mechanism is: one data point (the mistake) gets overgeneralized into a total self-verdict.
And once the verdict is reached, the brain is no longer thinking about the specific task. The brain is thinking about whether to stay in the job.

## TURNING_POINT v01

---
