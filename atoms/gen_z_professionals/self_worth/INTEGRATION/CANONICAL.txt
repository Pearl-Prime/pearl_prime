

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You exist separate from the output."
---
The project shipped. Or it didn't. The metric moved. Or it flatlined. Somewhere in your chest you felt your worth shift with it. That sensation — that tightness that comes with the number changing — is your body registering that something about you just became less real.
But sit with this: the project doesn't contain you. The output is not the same as the person doing the work.
Notice your hands right now. Your breath. These are yours whether the metric is up or down. This is true even when the system has trained you to believe otherwise.
You exist separate from the output.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your self became dependent on metrics."
---
Count what you've traded to become someone whose worth is quantifiable. The ambitions you abandoned because they weren't measurable. The parts of yourself that don't compress into a performance review. The time spent on metrics that don't reflect what matters to you, only what the system can count.
You did this because the metrics made you visible. Made you real in the places that determined what happened next. The cost and the survival logic are both true at the same time.
But your body knows. Every time the metric drops, some part of you disappears.
Your self became dependent on metrics.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You learned who you are without the number."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You're weeks further in than when this book started. You've had moments where the metric didn't confirm you existed. And you're still here. That's the information you carry now — you don't disappear when the number moves.
That doesn't mean the system's evaluation doesn't matter. It does. It just means it doesn't contain you.
You learned who you are without the number.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would you want if nobody measured."
---
The real question isn't whether you're performing well enough. It's what you actually want that has nothing to do with performance. What moves you when there's no metric attached. Who you are when the system isn't watching.
That question is dangerous in an environment built on measurement. Which is why it matters. The system's silence about who you might be outside of productivity is its own kind of violence.
You don't have to answer tonight. Just let it sit.
What would you want if nobody measured.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "They lost the metric and lost their shape."
---
Someone you know had their project cut. The work that made them visible ended. And they became harder to see. Not because they changed. Because the system had organized their worth around that specific visibility. Without it, they seemed to shrink.
That could be you. That might be you next.
They lost the metric and lost their shape.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: interrupt_landing

---
The moment you said out loud that you don't know if you're good enough, something shifted. You expected to disappear. Instead, the admission made you more present. Your body felt heavier, more anchored. That's what happens when you stop performing and start existing.
Your shoulders know the difference.
You admitted it. You felt real.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It's erased years of who you might be."
---
Map backward to when your worth first became metric-dependent. The class system that taught you to be measurable. The salary negotiations where you learned to justify yourself in outputs. The years of building a self that could be counted and discarded the parts that couldn't fit into a profile.
That's the cost. Not this week. Across time. The versions of yourself that never developed because they weren't profitable. The interests that didn't compound. The relationships that couldn't be justified in ROI terms.
It's erased years of who you might be.
