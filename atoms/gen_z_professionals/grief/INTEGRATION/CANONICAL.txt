

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You walked into that meeting anyway."
---
Someone died. Or left. Or something fundamental shifted. And three hours later you had a deliverable due. Your body had to hold both things at the same time — the grief that wanted to flatten you and the professional shape you had to maintain because the work doesn't stop when loss does.
That heaviness in your chest right now. That's not a failure at moving on. That's the weight of carrying grief into a space that doesn't have room for it.
Notice how your body is sitting. What your shoulders are doing. They're holding something you haven't had permission to put down.
You walked into that meeting anyway.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Nobody counted the cost of attending."
---
Count what showing up cost you. The focus that went to holding your face neutral. The work that didn't happen because your hands were shaking. The meeting after the meeting where you had to repeat the explanation of your absence. The energy spent on being professional instead of falling apart.
This doesn't appear anywhere. No one tracks the emotional tax. The productivity metrics don't register that you accomplished something while someone you cared about was disappearing.
Your body knew the cost. That's the only ledger that mattered.
Nobody counted the cost of attending.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "The grief is still here. The project ended."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Weeks have passed since the loss. The deliverable shipped. The crisis resolved. And the grief is still sitting in your chest like something that won't resolve. That's not weakness. That's what grief does — it doesn't organize itself around work timelines.
You're further in now. Not further from the grief. Further through time while carrying it.
The grief is still here. The project ended.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What did you have to stop feeling."
---
The real question isn't whether the grief is valid. It is. The question is what you had to do to continue functioning in a space that required your full professional capacity. Something in you had to pause. Not the grief. Your access to the grief. You had to partition it.
That partition is still there. You can feel it — the grief on one side and the version of yourself that can attend meetings on the other.
That split is real. You don't have to heal it tonight.
What did you have to stop feeling.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "You've grieved in borrowed time for weeks."
---
Look at how long you've been doing this. Carrying loss while the productivity clock ticks. The person who died. The part of yourself that died with them. The version of your career that would have unfolded differently if they were still here. The relationships that would have deepened if they'd lived. All of that is still sitting in your chest.
You weren't supposed to fall apart. So you didn't. You compartmentalized. And that compartment is still there, a locked room inside you.
You've grieved in borrowed time for weeks.
