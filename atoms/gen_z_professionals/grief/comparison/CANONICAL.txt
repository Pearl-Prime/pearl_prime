COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Maya's aunt died. It was sudden. Maya was sad for two weeks and then the sadness stayed, lived in her chest, made her cry at random moments. But Maya's friend's parent died. Has been dead for six months. The friend is functional. The friend goes to work. The friend makes jokes. Maya measures her grief against this and finds it wanting. She thinks: I shouldn't still be crying. I shouldn't still think about it this much. I shouldn't need this much time. The jaw tightens. The throat closes slightly. Maya's sadness becomes something else—shame that the sadness is still there. Maya starts hiding it. Doesn't mention the aunt. Doesn't let the grief show at work. The body knows it's being judged and tightens against it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Maya's grief can't move because Maya has deemed it invalid before it can be felt. The process goes: grief arises → immediate comparison → immediate judgment → grief gets suppressed. The suppress costs energy. The grief doesn't disappear—it goes underground, becomes shame, becomes the belief that something is wrong with Maya for still feeling it. Maya's eyes get heavy. Not from sadness but from the effort of holding down the sadness. The jaw stays tight. The throat stays closed. Other people at work mention their losses and Maya supports them and internally judges: they're handling it better than I did. The comparison is constant. A measuring stick against every emotion. Maya becomes very tired.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/grief/comparison/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Maya cries at work. Accidentally. Someone makes a small comment about their own loss and Maya's eyes fill up before she can stop it. The jaw drops slightly. The throat releases. Maya is terrified—she's revealed the grief. But then her coworker just hands her a tissue and keeps talking. Doesn't make a big deal. Doesn't say anything. Just: here's a tissue, I see you. Maya's body registers: this is safe. The grief that Maya was comparing and judging is just grief. It doesn't have to win or lose against anyone else's grief. It just has to exist. Maya doesn't stop comparing yet but something cracks in the comparison framework.
