Jordan's coworker notices. Not with pity or concern—with recognition. They take Jordan to lunch and don't ask what's wrong. They just sit across from Jordan and eat their sandwich and say, "You don't have to be okay yet." Jordan's eyes get hot. Not from crying, not exactly. From something releasing slightly.
