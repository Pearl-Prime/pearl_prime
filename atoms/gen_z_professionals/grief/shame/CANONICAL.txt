Alex starts hiding the crying. Alex stops mentioning the friend. Alex tells people: I'm fine. I'm just adjusting to the new dynamic. Alex's throat closes. Alex's shoulders tighten. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The shame of grief comes from a simple equation: grief has a duration and the duration is being measured. The duration is being judged. The duration is being compared to what's acceptable. The measurement happens in the culture and the person internalizes it. Alex's grief about the friend is supposed to last two weeks. Maybe three. Not three weeks. Definitely not three months. Alex has been grieving for three weeks and Alex is starting to feel like there's something wrong with Alex's grief. The grief is too much. The grief is too visible. The grief is taking too much space. The grief is also the natural response to the loss of daily intimacy with someone who has been daily for a decade. The grief is appropriate. The measurement is the problem. Alex is grieving and also grieving the judgment from others about the grieving. The grief is being doubled. The shame is the doubled grief. The mechanism is: grief triggers judgment and the judgment triggers shame and the shame makes the grief feel like a problem. Alex's body registers all three things. Alex's chest is tight from the grief and the shame. Alex's chest is collapsed from trying to fit the grief into the acceptable timeframe. Alex's eyes are hard from the surveillance of the grief, from watching to make sure the grief is not showing too much.
