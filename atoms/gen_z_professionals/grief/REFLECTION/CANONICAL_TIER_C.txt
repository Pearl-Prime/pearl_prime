family: F1

The grief that your generation is processing is not grief for something that was. It is grief for something that was supposed to be. The future that was promised. The path that was supposed to work. The adulthood that was supposed to arrive with the right combination of education and effort. This is the mechanism I want to name: the grief for the unmade future. It is a specific kind of loss because what is lost never existed. You do not grieve what you had. You grieve what you were told you would have. The mechanism is that the loss is abstract. But it is also real. You have lost something that only existed as promise.
