

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit in a chair. Leave the chair next to you empty. This empty chair is the absence. Do not look at it constantly. Just know it is there. Sit with the empty space beside you for five minutes. Feel the absence. This is grief. Absence is real. The person is not here. They were. Now they are not. Feel this realness. After five minutes, you have sat with absence. You have acknowledged it. This is an act of love.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Look at a photo of the person you lost. Let yourself look completely. Do not rush. Count the details you see: their eyes, their smile, their clothes. Count ten details. Feel each one. These details are proof they existed. They were real. Your memory is real. Your grief is real. Look until you feel something: sadness, love, pain, gratitude. Feel the feeling. Do not suppress it. Let it live.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place one hand over your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is broken. A broken heart is still beating. Count ten beats. Your heart continues even in grief. This is love in action. Your heart loves someone no longer here. This love is still real. Feel it beating. Feel your grief. Feel your love. They are the same thing.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Go to a quiet space. Sit on the floor or couch. Say the person's name out loud. Say it slowly. Say it twice. Say it three times. Hearing their name aloud makes them real. Your voice just made them present. Your voice has power. You have spoken their name. You have honored them. Say their name one more time. Feel the love in your voice.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
In a bathroom, splash your face with cool water slowly. Feel the water. Feel the wetness. Feel this sensation. Grief makes you feel numb. Sensation is proof you are alive. Pat your face dry. Feel the dripping water. Feel yourself present. You are here. They are gone. You are here grieving. This is your life now. Feel it.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit at your desk. Open a blank document or piece of paper. Write one sentence about the person: something you remember, something you loved, something you miss. One sentence. Close it. You have written their memory. You have made their memory real on a page. They exist in your writing now. They exist in your memory. They are not completely gone.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
On a transit seat, focus on the motion beneath you. The vehicle is moving. You are moving. Grief keeps you still. Motion is proof you are continuing. The world continues. People continue. You continue. Feel the motion. Feel the continuation. Feel this despite the loss. You are still moving. This is living while grieving.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Close your eyes. Count to sixty slowly. This is one minute. One minute of holding space for grief. Count slowly: one, two, three. Do not rush. By sixty, you have held space. You have given time to grief. Time honoring the person you lost. Open your eyes. You have grieved for a minute. You can do this again. Grieving is your love.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
On your couch, lie down. Cover yourself with a blanket. This is a cocoon of grief. Grief requires safety. Grief requires softness. Stay covered for five minutes. Just lie. Just breathe. Just be with the sadness. After five minutes, uncover yourself. You have allowed yourself grief. You have not run from it. You have sat with it. This is brave. This is love.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Stand and place your feet shoulder-width apart. Feel both feet equally. Feel how you are held by the earth. You lost someone. The earth still holds you. Feel this. Feel held by something larger than your grief. The earth does not care about your sadness. The earth just holds. Feel held. Feel supported by something eternal. Feel less alone.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
At your desk, close your eyes. Say silently: "I loved them." Say it five times. Feel each statement. Say: "I miss them." Say it five times. Feel each statement. Say: "They mattered." Say it five times. Feel the truth. You are speaking truth about the person. Your words are prayers. Your words are love. Open your eyes. You have loved them with words.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Pick up a blanket or jacket that reminds you of the person. Hold it. Feel its fabric. This is a physical object they may have touched. Feel the connection. Hold it close. Feel its weight. Weight means realness. Feel the realness. Hold it for one minute. You are holding something that connects you. Connection continues. Love continues. You continue.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
In a bathroom mirror, look at your face. Look at your eyes. You are the person who loved them. You are the person who is grieving. This is your face. This is your grief. Say: "I am here." Say it three times. Hear your voice. You are here. You are alive. You are grieving. All of this is happening. Feel it.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Sit in a chair and place your hands in your lap. Rest them there. Your hands did things with this person: held theirs, hugged them, touched them. Your hands remember. Feel your hands. Feel the memory. Say: "My hands remember." Say it twice. Your hands are keepers of memory. Your body is a keeper of memory. You carry them in your body.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
On a transit seat, hold your phone without checking it. Just hold it. Feel its weight. This person is no longer in your phone. No new messages will come. Feel this absence. Feel how your phone is a location where they are not. Feel the emptiness. This emptiness is grief. Sit with it. The person is not in your phone anymore. They are in your heart. Feel your heart holding them.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Close your eyes. Recall one moment with this person. Remember it completely. The details: what they said, what you felt, what happened. Feel it. Feel yourself in that moment. For this moment, they are alive again in your memory. Feel their aliveness. Feel the moment. After one minute, open your eyes. You have visited them in memory. Memory is a place where they still live.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Stand and hum for five seconds. Feel the vibration in your chest. Your chest is where grief lives. Humming moves it. Hum again. Feel it move. Hum five more times. Each hum is a prayer. Each hum is a sound made by someone alive. Someone alive is humming. You are alive. You are grieving. Both are true.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
At your desk, open a photo of the person on your screen. Let yourself see them. Do not hide from the image. See them completely. See them on your screen. Your screen is a window to their image. Feel the connection. Your eyes can still see them. See them. Let yourself feel whatever comes. Feel it completely.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
In a quiet space, place your hand on the ground or floor. Feel it. The ground is solid. You are less solid now. Grief has dissolved some of your solidity. But you are still here. Your hand is on the ground. The ground holds you. Feel held. Feel this holding as love from the earth. The earth loves you. The person loved you. You are loved.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Sit upright. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press them down firmly. Feel the pressure. Grief wants to take you away. Pressure grounds you. Press for five seconds. Release. Do this five times. Each press says: I am here. I am grieving. I am still here. I am pressed into the earth. I am alive. I am here.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Before sleep, lie in bed. Place both hands on your heart. Say silently: "I loved them." Say it five times. Feel the love in your hands. Feel it in your heart. This love does not disappear. This love continues. The person is gone. The love remains. Feel the love. Feel it as proof the person mattered. Feel it as proof you loved well.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
On your couch, close your eyes. Listen without trying to hear. Just listen. Let sounds come to you. These sounds are the world continuing. The world grieves with you. The world holds you in its grief. Listen to the world. Listen to birds, traffic, silence. The world is alive. You are alive in the world. You continue.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Take a piece of paper. Write their name slowly. Feel the letters form. Feel each letter. Feel the whole name together. You have made their name real with your hand. You have written them. They exist in your writing. Fold the paper slowly. You have contained them. You carry them with you.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Stand in front of a window. Look outside. The world continues without them. This is hard to feel. But the world is continuing. You are continuing with it. Feel the world's continuation. Feel your continuation. Feel both at the same time. The world moves on. You move on. This is survival.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
In a bathroom, splash cold water on your wrists slowly. Count to five. Feel the shock. Shock is sensation. Sensation is aliveness. You are alive. Grief is the price of love. Aliveness is the evidence of having loved someone real. Pat your wrists dry. You are alive and grieving. Both are true. Both matter.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Sit in your chair. Close your eyes. Say the person's name silently. Say it five times slowly. Feel their name in your mind. Feel them. Say: "You mattered to me." Say it three times. Feel the statement. You are speaking to them silently. You are communicating your love. They may not be here. But you are telling them. You are speaking your love.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Place one hand on your shoulder and one on your belly. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for six counts. Do this five times. You are holding yourself. You are comforting yourself. You are being the person who remains. You are taking care of you. Feel this care. Feel this strength. You are the one who continues. You continue for them.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
On a transit seat, watch the people around you. People continue living. Loving. Working. Grieving. Everyone grieves something. You are part of human experience. Feel connected to all grieving people. Feel not alone. Millions grieve. You are one of millions. Feel the community of grief. Feel held by all who grieve.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
At your desk, hold something that belonged to the person or reminds you of them. Hold it in both hands. Feel it. Say: "You are in my memory." Say it twice. Feel the object as a bridge to them. Hold it close to your heart. Keep it near. Let it remind you daily: they lived. They mattered. They were here.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Before sleep, lie in bed. Place your hand on your heart. Say silently: "Grief is love with nowhere to go." Say it three times. Feel this truth. Your love for them is still here. It has nowhere to go now. So it stays in you. In your heart. In your body. In your memory. This is sacred. This is how they live: in your love. Sleep holding this love.
---
