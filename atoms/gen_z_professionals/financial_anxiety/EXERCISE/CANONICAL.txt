## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Open your bank app right now.
Look at the actual number you have.
Not what you owe. What you have.
Write it down or screenshot it.
This is the truth right now.
You're catastrophizing about a future that's not here.
What is one dollar you can control today?
Can you skip one purchase tomorrow?
You don't need to fix everything now.
One small choice is enough for today.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
List three financial things you're worried about.
Pick the one that's actually urgent.
Maybe it's not the one you're spiraling on.
The other two don't need solving right now.
Focus on the one that actually matters.
Set a specific time to handle it.
Not "anytime soon." A real time.
Then stop thinking about the others.
Your anxiety is lying to you.
All three problems don't explode simultaneously.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Write down what you actually earned this month.
Now write down what you actually spent.
The gap between them is what's real.
Not your fear. The actual numbers.
Is the gap scary or manageable?
Look at the answer without judgment.
If it's manageable, you already know that.
Your anxiety wants you to forget.
Remind yourself every time the panic hits.
Facts beat fear every single time.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Before you open the banking app for the third time today, stop. Place both feet flat on the floor. Press your heels down. Feel the floor hold you. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. The number in that app did not change in the last hour. Your nervous system did. Notice your shoulders. Drop them. Then, if you still want to, open the app once. Look. Close it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Phone down. Screen off. Place one hand flat on your chest. Feel the pressure there. That tightness is the rent-due story arriving early. Breathe into that hand for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Do three cycles. On the third exhale, name one bill that is actually due this week. One. Not the imaginary stack. The real one. That is the only number that needs you today.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
When the comparison spiral hits and everyone online looks richer, stop scrolling. Close your eyes. Place both hands over your eyes for ten seconds. Feel the dark. Breathe out completely. Now open your eyes and look at one real thing you paid for this month that you actually needed. Say it out loud. Your money did a real job. Their highlight reel is not your ledger.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Sit down before you check the balance. Both feet on the floor. Hands on your thighs. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for six. Now, open the account. Read the number once, out loud, in your own voice. Not your panic's voice. Yours. Whatever it is, that is the real starting line today. Notice your jaw. Unclench it. The number is information, not a verdict.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Take three slow breaths before you read this. In for four. Out for four. Again. Now, write one fixed cost you have every month. Rent. Phone. One only. Next to it, write the exact date it leaves your account. That date is real. The vague dread of "I can't afford anything" is not. Feel the difference in your chest between the real date and the fog. Stay with the date.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
When the paycheck-to-paycheck panic spikes, find a wall. Press your back flat against it. Feel it hold your full weight. Solid. Breathe out long. Now count, on your fingers, three expenses you covered this month. Rent. Groceries. One more. Say "covered" after each. Covered. Covered. Covered. You are not behind on those. Drop your shoulders. The wall still has you.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Notice your hands gripping the phone right now. Set it face-down. Place both hands flat on your desk or your knees. Feel the surface. Cold or warm. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Do this five times. On each exhale, let one catastrophic money thought leave with the breath. You are not solving your whole financial life in this minute. You are just letting your body know it is not an emergency right now.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Before you panic-buy or panic-cancel anything, run this first. Step 1: put the card down. Step 2: place one hand on your sternum and breathe in for four counts. Step 3: ask out loud, "Do I need this in the next seven days?" Step 4: breathe out for six and wait one full minute before you decide. The urge to act now is the anxiety, not the answer. Let the minute pass. Then choose.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
End of a money-stress day. Sit on the edge of your bed before you lie down. Feet on the floor. Place one hand on your belly. Breathe in and let the belly rise first. Breathe out slowly. Five breaths. Now name one financial thing you handled today, even a tiny one. Paid for lunch. Checked a balance. Say "done." You are allowed to stop tracking for the night. Lie down. The numbers will still be there tomorrow, and so will you.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
When the "I'll never afford it" thought loops, interrupt it physically. Stand up. Press both feet into the floor. Reach both arms overhead. Stretch tall for five seconds. Drop your arms. Drop your shoulders. Breathe out. Now pick one savings number that is real and small. Five dollars. Whatever is true. Move it to a separate account right now while you're standing. One small real move beats one hour of imaginary math.
---
