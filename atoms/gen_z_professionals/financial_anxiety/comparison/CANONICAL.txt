---
Priya is scrolling Instagram when a college peer posts a photo from a vacation. The location is somewhere expensive-looking. The peer is visibly relaxed. Visibly able to afford relaxation. Priya's body recognizes the gap immediately. The peer has enough money to travel. Priya has enough money to pay rent and eat and maybe save a tiny amount. The gap appears as evidence. Not of circumstance. Of failure. The peer got a better job or comes from money or made smarter choices. Priya didn't. The comparison doesn't produce envy exactly — it's heavier than that. It produces shame. A quiet internal knowledge that she's behind. That she's not doing what she should be doing at her age. That the peer has figured something out that Priya hasn't. Her stomach tightens. Her shoulders round forward. The vacation is happening on someone else's account and that account is fuller than hers and that gap feels personal.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/financial_anxiety/comparison/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: identity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Priya begins to understand the mechanism. It's running constantly now. Every time she sees evidence of others' financial stability, the mechanism activates. The peer's vacation, the colleague's apartment, the friend group's casual spending. Each evidence point becomes proof. Proof that she's not doing what she should be doing. Proof that she made wrong choices or came from the wrong place or was born at the wrong time. The mechanism doesn't compare neutral facts. It compares potential. The peer had the potential to save enough for vacation and achieved it. Priya has the same time and resources but didn't achieve it. Therefore Priya failed. The logic is relentless. The mechanism ignores: the peer might not have been paying student loans, the peer might have received help, the peer might have made different choices about housing, the peer might have made more money. The mechanism just sees: peer achieved, Priya didn't, therefore Priya is failing. Her body organizes around this belief. Her shoulders are tight. Her chest is compressed. She's become someone who's failing financially. The mechanism is proving it constantly.
