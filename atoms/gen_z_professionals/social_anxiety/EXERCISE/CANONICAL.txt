## EXERCISE v01
---

---
You're about to walk into a social situation.
Notice the anxiety but don't resist it.
Take one long breath in through your nose.
Hold it for a count of four.
Release completely through your mouth.
Repeat two more times.
You don't need to be perfect in there.
You don't need to have the right things to say.
You just need to show up and be human.
That's literally all anyone expects.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Before you enter, ground yourself in facts.
People are usually worried about their own stuff.
They're not scrutinizing everything you say.
Give yourself one small role in the room.
Ask one person about themselves.
Let them talk. You just listen.
You don't have to be entertaining or brilliant.
Listening is enough. It's more than enough.
When the anxiety spikes, ask another question.
Your anxiety will quiet down.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Set an intention before you go out.
Not "be less anxious" or "be cool."
Set something concrete: "I'll say one real thing."
Or "I'll listen to one full story."
One small, achievable win.
When you get there, look for that win.
Skip trying to be someone you're not.
Be the person who can listen.
Be the person who can be honest.
That's enough for any social situation.
---
