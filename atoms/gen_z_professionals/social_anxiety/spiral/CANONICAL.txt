COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: pre_awareness
---
Alex said something in the standup. It was a comment about code architecture. The comment was reasonable. But now, three hours later, Alex is replaying it. Did the tone sound right? Did people think it was obvious? The replay is automatic. The brain has decided the comment needs to be audited. Alex's chest tightens with each replay. The comment is slightly different each time Alex replays it. In the original, Alex sounded competent. In the replay, Alex sounds defensive. In the next replay, condescending. The replays are generating evidence of failure. Alex didn't say anything actually wrong, but the spiral has decided there might be something wrong. The body responds to the spiral as if the thing actually happened. The neck becomes stiff. The shoulders rise. Alex is living through the mistake repeatedly.

## Mechanism_Proof v05

BAND: 4
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 4
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Marcus is generating scenarios based on no information. The email was sent six hours ago. No response is available. The mechanism is that the absence of information creates a vacuum that the spiral fills. Marcus's brain is generating possible responses. Most of them are negative. Marcus is imagining the client's disappointment. Marcus is imagining the email being forwarded with criticism. Marcus is imagining the client deciding to switch vendors. None of this has happened. All of it feels like it's happening. The body is experiencing the scenarios as real. Marcus's heart is beating slightly faster. The palms are beginning to sweat. Marcus has lost the ability to be uncertain. The uncertainty has been filled with threat.

## Turning_Point v01
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/social_anxiety/spiral/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Alex asks someone directly. Alex says: I made a comment in the standup about architecture. The comment is now being replayed as wrong. Can you tell me if it was actually wrong? The asking is the turning point. Alex is interrupting the spiral with external reality. The person responds: your comment was fine. It was helpful. The response doesn't fully convince Alex, but it introduces doubt into the spiral. The spiral requires certainty. The certainty that the thing was definitely wrong. The external perspective introduces uncertainty into that certainty. Alex's body relaxes slightly. The certainty of wrongness is the only thing that keeps the spiral alive. The moment the certainty is questioned, the spiral begins to lose power. Alex's shoulders drop slightly. The mechanism is reversing.
