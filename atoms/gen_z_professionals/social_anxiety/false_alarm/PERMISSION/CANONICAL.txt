--- variant: v01
Your alarm system going off doesn't mean you're weak. It means you're wired to protect yourself, and the wiring is sensitive. You're allowed to feel the full surge of a false alarm and still recognize, afterward, that you're safe. Recognition after the fact still counts.

--- variant: v02
You don't need to justify the adrenaline. You don't need to explain why a text message made your heart race or why a calendar notification triggered a cortisol spike. Your body has reasons that don't require your conscious approval. Accepting the response without judgment is part of the recalibration.

--- variant: v03
Permission to be tired from alarms that weren't real. The energy was real. The body doesn't care about the source -- it spent the same resources. You're allowed to need recovery from threats that never materialized. The exhaustion is valid even when the danger wasn't.
