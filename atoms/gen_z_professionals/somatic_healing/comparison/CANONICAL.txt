Jordan's scroll through transformation content triggers a specific mental mechanism. The before-and-after becomes a before-and-during-and-forever-after. If the influencer could change in thirty days, Jordan's lack of change in three years becomes evidence of deeper dysfunction. The mechanism produces a secondary layer: Jordan must be resistant to healing. Must be doing something wrong. Must be unsuitable for the simple fixes that work for simple people. The mind compresses the gap between Jordan's current state and the shown state into a sign that Jordan's dysregulation is intractable. The body responds to this thought. The heart rate increases. The stomach knots. The comparison engine has produced proof that Jordan cannot heal.

Maya's failure to relax in the restorative class produces a specific mechanism. The failure becomes a personal failing. The personal failing becomes evidence that she is uniquely dysregulated. Alex asks the yoga instructor what to do about the persistent shoulder tension. The instructor says: "That might not be about yoga." Alex stops expecting yoga to fix something that might not be yoga's territory. She notices that in the moments when she is not comparing herself to the person on the mat next to her, something shifts. The shoulders drop slightly. Not fully, not like the other woman, but different. She begins to notice that comparison is not a fact about her body but a choice she is making. The choice appears as something she could examine rather than something that is happening to her.

Priya notices that on days when she does not make eye contact with Marcus during lunch, her own nervous system behaves differently. She sits at her desk instead of the common area. The jaw loosens. The shoulders lower. Not to the degree she wants, but noticeably. She realizes that Marcus's calm is not a benchmark for her dysregulation; it is a trigger for her comparison engine. She begins to take her break in the office. Her nervous system has a chance to organize itself without the comparison architecture. The turning point is realizing that other people's peace is not evidence of her failure.

Dev realizes during a meeting that when he stops trying to make his body match the baseline of the room, something shifts. He is tense. The room is quiet. His tension does not disappear, but it stops being a problem. It is just what is happening. He speaks during the meeting with the tension present. The words come out sharper. More direct. His tension is not preventing speech; it is informing it. The turning point is not that his body has become calm like the others. It is that his body has become information rather than evidence of failure.
