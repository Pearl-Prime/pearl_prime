Your alarm system has a pattern -- same time, same trigger type, same body response. Mapping the pattern doesn't stop the alarm. But it gives you something the alarm doesn't want you to have: a three-second head start.

--- variant: v02
There's a difference between the false alarm and the real thing. Your body doesn't know it yet, but there's a signal buried in the response that distinguishes them. Finding that signal changes the entire relationship with the alarm.

--- variant: v03
