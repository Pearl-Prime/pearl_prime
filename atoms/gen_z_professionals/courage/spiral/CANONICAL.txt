Jordan maps the spiral's relationship to courage specifically. Routine actions don't trigger the chain. Brave actions trigger it instantly. The spiral has a threshold: if the action involves risk, visibility, or stepping beyond the current identity, the chain activates. Safe repetition passes through unscanned. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 2

---
Priya notices the spiral's predictions never come true. She's kept informal track: seven times the spiral ran, seven times the predicted catastrophe didn't happen. The spiral predicted career destruction seven times. Zero destructions occurred. The spiral's track record is 0 for 7. Despite this, the eighth spiral feels as convincing as the first. The predictions don't need to be accurate to be compelling. They need to be vivid. The vividness substitutes for accuracy. She can see the catastrophe in detail. Detail feels like truth. The feeling overrides the record.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03
---
path: story_atoms/gen_z_professionals/anchored/courage/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v03.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Kai identifies the spiral's speed as its primary weapon. The chain runs so fast that by the time he could evaluate link two, he's already at link five. The speed prevents examination. Each link feels like an inevitable consequence of the previous one, but only because the transition is too fast to question. Slow the chain down—demo to tough question to learning opportunity—and different second links become visible. But the chain doesn't slow down voluntarily. The speed is the mechanism. Without speed, the catastrophe dissolves into probabilities. With speed, the catastrophe feels certain.
