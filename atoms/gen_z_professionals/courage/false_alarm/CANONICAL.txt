---
Jordan maps the alarm's territory. It fires on: applications, pitches, proposals, introductions, anything that puts his name on something visible. It doesn't fire on: routine tasks, expected outputs, things that have been done before. The alarm's boundary is novelty and visibility. Anything new and seen triggers the alarm. The alarm has created a career path that is safe and repetitive. The safe path protects him from the alarm. The safe path also protects him from growth. Growth requires the exact conditions that activate the alarm: new, visible, uncertain. The alarm and growth are structurally incompatible.
