

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before you unmute in a meeting, press both feet into the floor. Feel the ground beneath you. Count to three. Your feet connect you to solid ground. You are not floating. You are not falling. You are stable. Place your hands flat on the table in front of you. Press down. Feel the table support your hands. Now your hands know: support is real. Unclench your jaw. Feel your jaw relax. Your mouth is ready to speak. Open your mouth slightly. You are ready. Unmute now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Sit upright in your chair five minutes before speaking. This is your anchor time. Feel your spine against the back of the chair. Feel how solid it is. Your spine is your courage bone. It holds you upright even when afraid. Feel it supporting you. Now place your hand on your chest. Feel your heart. Your heart is still beating. Your heart is still strong. It does not need permission to keep beating. Feel this. Count ten heartbeats. You are alive. You are capable.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Stand up slowly from your desk. Feel your full height. Usually you are sitting. Standing changes your perspective. Feel the difference. Roll your shoulders backward three times. Feel them open. Your shoulders have been hunched over your desk. Courage requires open shoulders. Raise your arms overhead. Stretch for three seconds. Feel the expansion in your chest. Your chest is now open. Courage lives in an open chest. Drop your arms. You are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Before an uncomfortable conversation, go to a bathroom sink. Splash cold water on your wrists slowly. Count to five. Cold water is a jolt to your nervous system. It wakes you up. It says: you are ready. Pat your wrists dry. Look at your face in the mirror. Make direct eye contact with yourself. Say: "I can do this." Hear your voice. Believe it. Your voice just proved it. Walk out.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit at your desk. Clench your fists under the table. Squeeze them as hard as you can for three seconds. You are gathering your strength. Your hands are gathering courage. Release your fists completely. Do this three times. Each clench and release teaches your body: I have strength inside me. On the final release, let your hands rest loosely. Your body knows where the strength is. You can access it. Now speak.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
On a transit seat before an important interaction, place both feet flat on the floor. The floor of the vehicle is moving you forward. You are moving toward something difficult. This is forward movement. This is courage. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Breathe out through your mouth for six counts. Do this five times. Each breath is slower. Each breath is calmer. By the fifth breath, your nervous system has reset. You are ready to arrive.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Place your hand over your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Count twenty beats. Your heart is beating faster than usual. This is not weakness. This is activation. This is your body preparing you for action. Your body is on your side. Count ten more beats. Feel the rhythm. Feel the activation as support, not threat. Your heart is pumping blood to your limbs. Your limbs will move. You will speak. You will do this.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Before pressing send on a difficult email, pause for five seconds. Place both hands on your desk. Press down. Feel the support. You have written the truth. The truth requires courage. Pressing send is the courageous action. Remove your hands. Shake them gently. Shake out the doubt. Shake out the fear. Now place them back on the keyboard. Your hands are steady. Press send. You have done it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Sit upright. Close your eyes. Tense every muscle in your body for three seconds. Clench your face, shoulders, arms, belly, legs. Make fists. Tense everything. Hold it. Release completely. Do this two more times. By the third release, feel the difference between tension and release. Tension is control. Release is trust. You need both. Trust that you can access your strength. Relax into that trust.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Stand and place your feet wide apart, wider than your shoulders. Feel the stability. This stance is a power stance. Martial artists use this. Politicians use this. You are using this now. Keep your feet wide for thirty seconds. Feel the power in your legs. Feel the groundedness. Now bring your feet to shoulder-width. Feel the difference. You have the wide stance inside you. You can access it anytime.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Place your hand on your jaw. Feel how tight it is. You are clenching. Courage requires an open mouth. Slowly open your mouth wide. Count to three. Your mouth is open. This is the first step to speaking. Close your mouth slowly. Open it again. Do this five times. By the fifth opening, your jaw learns: I can open. I can speak. The path is clear.

---

This strength is beneath you, supporting you. Press down five times. Each press reminds you: I am strong. I am supported. I am capable. Release on the final press. Your strength remains inside you.

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Close your eyes. Recall a moment you were brave. Remember what it felt like. Feel it in your body now. Where did you feel courage? Chest? Belly? Legs? Locate it. Feel it. Breathe into that location. Count four as you breathe in. Count six as you breathe out. Do this three times. You are rekindling the same courage. It is still inside you. Open your eyes. You have accessed it again.

---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Stand in a doorway. Place one hand on each side of the frame. Feel the structure around you. You are in a threshold. You are between the known and the unknown. Feel both. The known is behind you. The unknown is ahead. Press your hands against the frame for three seconds. Release. You have felt the support of the structure. Structures exist to support. Walk through the doorway. ## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Sit upright. Lift your chin slightly. Usually fear drops the chin down. Courage lifts the chin. Hold your chin lifted for ten seconds. Feel the difference in your posture. Feel how your chest opens. Feel how your breathing deepens. Now return your chin to neutral. You have felt what courage posture is. Your body remembers this. Return to chin-lifted posture. Hold it. Walk with this posture into your conversation.

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Before a difficult interaction, tap your chest slowly. Tap once, wait one second, tap again. Tap five times. Each tap says: I am here. I am present. I am brave. Feel the tapping. Feel your presence. Your body is here even if your mind is worried. Your body is brave. Trust your body. Stop tapping. Your chest is now activated. You are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Close your eyes. Say silently: "I have survived every difficult moment before this." Say it three times. It is true. You are still here. Open your eyes. Say: "I will survive this one too." Say it three times. Feel the truth settle. You are a survivor. Survivors have courage. You have it. Say: "I am ready." Open your eyes. You are ready.

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
At your desk, straighten your posture completely. Sit up very straight. Usually you slouch slightly under stress. Straightness is strength. Hold this posture for two minutes. Feel what strength posture feels like in your body. Feel your spine. Feel your chest. Feel your shoulders back. After two minutes, relax slightly. You have felt strong posture. Your body knows it. Return to strong posture for your difficult moment.

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Place one hand on your shoulder. Squeeze gently. Say to yourself: "I support myself." Say it aloud if you can. Your hand is your support. You are supporting yourself. Move your hand to your other shoulder. Squeeze and say: "I support myself." Do this five times, alternating shoulders. By the fifth, you believe it. You are not alone. You are supporting yourself. This is courage.

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Before speaking, take one slow breath. Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out through your mouth for four counts. Do this once. Only once. This single breath is your courage breath. One breath shifts your nervous system. One breath says: I am calm. I am ready. Speak on the exhale of this breath.
