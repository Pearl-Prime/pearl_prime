

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You stayed in your chair."
---
The moment you spoke up in the meeting, your hand started shaking. That tremor isn't failure. It's your nervous system registering that you did something risky. You disagreed with someone who determines what happens to you next. Your body moved before your mind could convince you it was too dangerous.
Notice your hands now. They're still. The meeting ended. You're still here.
That tremor you felt — that was courage moving through a body that's learning it can stay even when it's afraid.
The thing you said is still out there. You can't take it back. Your body knows that it didn't need to.
You stayed in your chair.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Speaking up cost you something."
---
You won't know the full cost for weeks. Maybe the feedback comes in a review. Maybe it comes in who gets invited to the next room. Maybe it comes in the subtle recalibration of how safe you are. That's what speaking up costs in a hierarchy that wasn't designed to hear you.
The thing you said was true. The cost is also real. Both are true.
Your body is registering that you chose the truth and you're still standing. That's not recovery. That's the moment where risk becomes information instead of just fear.
Speaking up cost you something.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You spoke up again. You're still here."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You've done this more than once now. Early on it was paralyzing. The first time you disagreed publicly, you thought it would cost you everything. It cost you something. Not everything. You learned the difference between these.
Now when you speak up, the fear is the same but it lands differently. You have evidence. You have the history of times you were wrong about the consequence. That history sits in your body now.
You're not fearless. You're experienced.
You spoke up again. You're still here.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What are you protecting by staying quiet."
---
The real question isn't whether what you said was smart. It was true. The question is what silence has been protecting you from. Maybe it's the direct attention of someone more powerful. Maybe it's the safety of being invisible. Maybe it's an old belief that people like you aren't supposed to have opinions that matter.
Something kept you quiet before. That something is still there. You moved past it this time. You didn't dismantle it.
The question can stay open.
What are you protecting by staying quiet.

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Someone else heard you. That matters."
---
You won't know who. Someone in the meeting was waiting for someone to say the thing you said. Someone was watching to see if it was possible to disagree with that person and survive. Someone registered that yes, people like you can take up space here.
You didn't have to change the room's mind. You changed something in one person's understanding of what's possible. That's currency in a system built on silence.
Someone else heard you. That matters.

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: interrupt_landing
carry_line: "Your breath came back."
note: interrupt_landing — use after The Interrupt story, quietest atom in the book
---
The second after you spoke, you couldn't breathe fully. That shallow breath was your body protecting itself from the consequence it expected. And then time kept moving. Nothing catastrophic happened in that exact moment. Your lungs remembered how to expand.
Sit with that. Your chest rising fully again.
Your breath came back.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "You've paid the silence price for years."
---
Before you spoke up, how long did you stay quiet. Months. Years. The ideas you didn't share. The questions you didn't ask. The moments you registered something was wrong and you swallowed it instead. All that silence cost you.
Not in promotions necessarily. In presence. In the pieces of yourself you stopped developing because they weren't safe to show. The voice that atrophied from disuse. The confidence that never formed because confidence requires visibility and you learned to be invisible.
You've paid the silence price for years.
