

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: D

The specific kind of courage that is asked of you in the first years of your career is the courage to say nothing. The meeting happens. You see the problem. You have a solution. And the mechanism that stops you is not fear of being wrong. It is the assessment that speaking will cost you more than silence will. So you do not speak. You watch the meeting go somewhere suboptimal.

The courage that is hardest to find is the courage to be ordinary. In a meeting, to say something that is not perfectly polished. To ask a question that reveals you do not know. To admit that you are not sure. Because in a room where everyone is performing competence, the performance is what stands out. And the cracks in the performance are what people notice. So the mechanism is the performance. It requires constant polish.

The mechanism of the strategic silence is that it becomes habitual. You are quiet. That works. So you are quiet again. That works again. And the mechanism embeds itself. Now being quiet is not a strategy. It is who you are. You have become the person who does not speak. And this identity becomes a problem when the environment finally needs you to speak. When the meeting needs your input.
