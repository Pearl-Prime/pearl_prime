Alex starts to see the pattern. Not with thinking but with watching. Every time someone else takes a risk—shares an idea, asks a question in the big meeting, sends that email—the comparison engine activates. But it doesn't activate with just observation. It activates with a specific narrative: they are brave, I am not. They can do this thing, I cannot. And the proof? The proof is that they did it and I didn't. It's circular but the circularity is invisible. It feels like fact. Alex watches the engine work and something shifts: the comparison isn't reflecting reality. It's creating it. Every idea Alex doesn't share becomes evidence that Alex can't share ideas. Every meeting where Alex stays silent becomes evidence of fundamental voicelessness. But the not-sharing came first. The engine reads the not-sharing as proof of the verdict, not as a choice that could be different. The chest tightens with a different kind of recognition. This logic is trapping something. The comparison has looped back on itself: the fear produces the evidence and the evidence proves the fear was justified.
