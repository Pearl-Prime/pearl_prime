The mechanism of depression that arrives in professional contexts is different from the textbook version because it does not look like depression. It looks like competence. You are getting things done. You are showing up. You are producing. But inside, the texture has changed. The thing that used to feel engaging now feels flat. The accomplishment that used to matter now feels hollow. This is the mechanism I want to name: the flatness that coexists with functionality. The system that keeps you performing while removing the sensation that the performance means anything. You are depressed and effective. These are not supposed to be compatible. But they are. And the compatibility is what makes it invisible. Because if you were failing, it would be clear. But you are not. You are fine. And the fineness masks the flatness underneath.
