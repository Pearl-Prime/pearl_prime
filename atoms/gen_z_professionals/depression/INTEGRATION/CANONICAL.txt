

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "You showed up anyway."
---
The day happened and you moved through it like a watcher. Your body was there. Your hands typed. Your face made the shapes that mean professional. And somewhere behind all of that was a you that couldn't feel much of anything.
This isn't failing at being a person. This is what depression does when the external circumstances are technically fine. Everything looks right from the outside. The salary cleared. The project's on track. The LinkedIn profile is acceptable. And inside there's a flatness that doesn't match any of that.
Notice your feet on the floor. Your breath somewhere in your chest. That heaviness you feel — that's depression in your body. Not a failure. A fact.
You showed up anyway.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The numbness cost you presence."
---
Count what the flatness took this week. The conversations you were in but not present for. The things you didn't initiate because nothing felt worth the effort. The sleep that came easier than living through the day. The version of yourself that would have been interested in something, anything, but numbness had other plans.
These costs are invisible. Your productivity didn't drop. You smiled at the right moments. No one's metrics recorded the absence of you.
But you know. Your body knows it was running empty.
The numbness cost you presence.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "The flatness is still here. So are you."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
Weeks and weeks of this. Sometimes it lifts for a day. Sometimes it deepens. You're further in now than you were at the beginning of this book. Not recovered. Further. You know the shape of it better. You can name it without the shame that used to come with the naming.
The career keeps moving. The deadlines don't care that you're watching from somewhere flat and far. That's not a reason to feel worse about it. That's how depression works — it shows you how dispensable you might be, and also that dispensability isn't the same as non-existence.
You're still showing up. That's the whole job description.
The flatness is still here. So are you.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What would it mean if it lifted."
---
The depression feels stable because it's been here so long. It has the weight of something true. And underneath that is a different question: what would have to be true about you if you weren't flat. What ambition or feeling or version of yourself would have to emerge if the numbness broke.
Sometimes the depression is a protection. Not protection from danger. Protection from having to want things. Having to risk things. Having to believe the promise that achievement would make you feel different.
You don't have to answer that tonight. Just let it sit. What would it mean if it lifted.
What would it mean if it lifted.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "They're watching from the same distance."
---
Someone in your org sent a message yesterday that looked completely functional. You know because you've learned to see what functional looks like when there's nothing under it. That person is watching their career from somewhere flat. You can feel it in the phrasing. Professional. Absent.
They don't know you're doing this too. That's part of it. Everyone at the table looks like they're present. Everyone is watching from different distances.
They're watching from the same distance.

Feel that now. Your hands. Your chest. The fact of your existence.
Your body is still here.

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "It's cost you months of feeling."
---
Look back at the stretch of flatness. Not the sadness. The numbness. The time that passed in a blur. The relationships that didn't deepen because you couldn't engage. The things you didn't create because creating requires being present. The version of your career that would have unfolded if you'd been here for it.
Months. Some of them are gone. You can't get them back.
That's the cost depression takes. Not pain necessarily. Absence from your own life.
It's cost you months of feeling.
