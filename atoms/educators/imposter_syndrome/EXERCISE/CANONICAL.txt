

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts. Silently say: I was trained for this. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat six times. The credential is real even on the days it does not feel real.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place your hand flat on your chest. Feel it rise and fall for six breaths. You do not have to feel confident to be competent. The two are not the same thing, no matter how loud the doubt gets.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Press your palms together firmly for a count of five. Release. Press again. Repeat five times. Each press is proof your hands know exactly how to do this work, whether or not your mind agrees today.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. The other teachers down the hall have this same fear. You are not the only one performing certainty.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Roll your shoulders back and let them drop. Count to four. Repeat five times. Notice how much smaller you make yourself when you feel like a fraud. Let your shoulders take up their actual space again.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Stand and press your feet into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Repeat five times. You have stood in front of this class before and it went fine. Your feet remember that, even when your mind forgets.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. Being observed today does not erase every lesson that worked before it.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let it hang loose. Breathe six slow breaths through this softened jaw. The fear of being found out lives in a tight jaw. Loosen the jaw and the fear loses some of its grip.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart. Feel it beating for ten counts. This heart has gotten you through every first year, every hard class, every hallway you doubted yourself in. It is still here.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Name three specific things you did well this week, out loud or silently. Notice how hard it is to list them. Notice you found three anyway. That is the truer count than the doubt gives you credit for.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Clench your fists for four counts. Release and spread your fingers wide. Repeat six times. You are releasing the grip of comparing your insides to everyone else's outsides.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sit back and let the chair hold your full weight. Count to six. You do not have to prove anything to the chair. Let it hold you exactly as competent and exactly as tired as you actually are.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Shake your hands out for ten seconds. Stop. Rest them. Shake again for ten seconds. You are shaking off the residue of a hard meeting or a hard observation, not shaking off your qualifications.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently name one moment this year a student understood something because of you. Repeat with three different moments. The doubt cannot hold all four moments at once.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Place your feet flat and press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Repeat four times. You are standing on the same ground every experienced teacher started on. No one skipped this part.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Cup your hands over your closed eyes. Sit in the dark for ten counts, breathing slowly. In this dark, no one is watching, grading, or comparing. It is only you and your own breath.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tense your whole body for four counts, then release completely. Repeat three times. Notice how much energy the fear of being exposed uses. Let some of that energy go.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six. Repeat this cycle six times. Feeling unqualified is not the same as being unqualified. Let the breath separate the feeling from the fact.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Lean forward, elbows on your knees or desk, and let your head hang for a count of eight. Rise slowly. Repeat three times. You do not have to hold your head high and certain every single second of the day.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly: index, middle, ring, pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. Each tap is a small, undeniable fact: your hand does exactly what you tell it. So does your teaching, more often than the doubt admits.
---
