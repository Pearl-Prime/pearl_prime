

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts, feeling the day still held in your shoulders and back. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat six times. Your body carried you through every hour of standing and speaking. Let it be heard now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both hands on your lower back. Press gently. Count to five. Release. Press again. Count to five. Repeat four times. This is the part of you that stood at the front of the room for hours. Give it this small attention.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Roll your shoulders back slowly. Count to four as they rise. Let them drop. Count to four as they settle. Repeat six times. Feel where the tension sits before it eases, so your body can recognize the difference.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. This breath is not correcting anything. It is simply letting your body know the demand has paused.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Gently roll your ankles in a slow circle, five times one direction, five times the other. Your legs held your full weight all day without complaint. Return the attention now.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Rest one hand on your throat. Feel the vibration if you hum quietly. Hum for six slow exhales. Your voice worked hard today. Let it rest in this soft sound instead.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Clench your fists for four counts, then release completely, letting your fingers hang loose. Repeat six times. Your hands wrote, pointed, gestured, and steadied all day. Let them simply be still now.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Press your feet firmly into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Count to six. Repeat five times. Feel the ground holding your full weight, the way it held you standing at the front of the room.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Tense your whole body for a count of four: legs, stomach, hands, shoulders, face. Release everything at once. Repeat three times. Notice the difference between held and free.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat eight times. The long exhale releases what a full day of talking and standing leaves in the chest and throat.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let your tongue rest low and soft. Let your lips part slightly. Hold this for eight slow breaths. Your jaw held words, patience, and expression all day. Let it fully soften now.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Place your hand on your heart. Feel it beating for ten counts without adjusting your breath. This muscle worked steadily through every hour of the day, unnoticed. Notice it now.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Shake out your whole body for fifteen seconds — arms, legs, shoulders, head. Stop completely. Feel the stillness after. This releases what a day of constant motion leaves behind.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Sit back and let the chair fully hold your weight. Count to eight. Feel your spine finally released from holding itself upright all day.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly: index, middle, ring, pinky. Reverse. Repeat four times. This small, precise movement returns you to your own hands after a day spent responding to everyone else's needs.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Lean forward with your elbows on your knees and let your head hang. Count to eight. Rise slowly. Repeat three times. Let your spine round and release instead of holding "teacher posture" for one more minute.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Cup your palms over your closed eyes. Sit in this small darkness for ten counts, breathing slowly. Your eyes worked all day scanning the room. Let them rest completely now.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six. Repeat this cycle eight times. Your body does not need instructions right now. It only needs this rhythm and permission to follow it.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Push firmly for five counts. Release. Repeat five times. This pressure, and then its release, is a physical echo of setting the whole day down.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Notice where your body still feels tight: jaw, shoulders, feet, hands. Breathe into that one place for four full breaths. You do not have to release everything at once. One place, four breaths, is enough for now.
---
