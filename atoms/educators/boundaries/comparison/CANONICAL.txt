Rachel is leaving school at 5pm on Friday. The sun is still high. Her car is almost empty in the parking lot because most teachers are still inside grading. She passes the windows of other classrooms and sees them bent over papers, active at their desks. Rachel leaves with her ungraded essays. It's not guilt exactly—guilt would mean she'd made a choice and violated it. It's more like she's witnessing what the job looks like and she's leaving early. The other teachers are there doing it right. She's here in her car. The leaving feels slightly wrong even though she knows she's taught all day. The measurement has started without her knowing to measure.
