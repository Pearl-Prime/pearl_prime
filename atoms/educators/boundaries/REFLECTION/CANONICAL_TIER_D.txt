

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: D

What I notice is a particular erosion that happens at the edge of the work day. A student stays after class to talk about something difficult. A parent emails after hours about a concern. A colleague needs something done before tomorrow. The moment arrives where I could draw a line between the work and the rest of my life, and the line does not get drawn. The mechanism I recognize is what I think of as default-to-availability.

---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: D

What I notice is the expectation that exists in educational culture that care means unlimited availability. The teacher who is truly dedicated shows up early. The teacher who really cares stays late. The teacher who genuinely loves their students gives their time generously. The mechanism that this activates is what I think of as the devotion equation. The mechanism is not unique to education. It exists in many helping professions.
