# REFLECTION PRACTICE

## What is reflection?

Reflection is the practice of pausing to observe your own thoughts, feelings, and actions without judgment. It's not about fixing yourself or analyzing what went wrong. It's simply noticing.

When you reflect, you create space between what happens and how you respond. That space is where freedom lives.

---

## Why reflect?

Most of us move through life on autopilot. We react without thinking. We feel ashamed without understanding why. We repeat patterns we don't even recognize.

Reflection wakes you up.

It shows you what you actually believe—not what you think you should believe. It reveals the stories you tell yourself about who you are. And once you see them clearly, you can choose differently.

---

## How to reflect

Find a quiet moment. Ten minutes is enough.

Ask yourself one question:

**What did I notice about myself today?**

Don't answer with "I was bad" or "I should have known better." Just notice what was there. How did your body feel? What stories ran through your mind? What did you want in that moment?

Write it down if you can. Your hand moving on paper often knows things your thinking mind hasn't caught yet.

---

## The practice

Start small. One moment. One question. One honest answer.

Reflection isn't about becoming perfect. It's about becoming awake.
