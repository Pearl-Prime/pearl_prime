Six-forty-seven AM. Rachel rereads the parent email for the fourth time. "I have concerns about Marcus's grade." Nine words. Her chest is tight, pulse quick, body bracing for something. The email doesn't threaten anything. Doesn't demand a meeting with administration. Just nine words. But her body has already built the confrontation, the complaint to the principal, the write-up in her file. She sets down her phone. The nine words sit there, unchanged. Her chest doesn't return to normal. The email has triggered a full cascade of fear response for what is essentially a neutral inquiry. She hasn't even read the second sentence yet.
