Rachel is teaching second period AP Literature. She can see herself doing it: gesturing at the board where the poem is projected, asking a question, waiting for hands to rise. The movement is smooth. Her voice carries. The pacing is right. She can see all of this as though from a position slightly behind her own eyes. She is conducting the lesson perfectly. She is also not inside it. Something in her is observing the performance of conducting the lesson. The students respond to her questions. She hears their answers and watches herself respond to them. The lesson is fine. She is not fine. She has been not-fine since she walked into this classroom.
