


## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You are allowed to feel anxious about the observation even though you've been teaching for years. Experience does not erase the body's response.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You are allowed to not answer the parent email tonight. The child will still be there tomorrow.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You are allowed to close the classroom door and sit in the quiet for five minutes. The quiet is not laziness.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---

---
You are allowed to feel angry that another initiative landed on your desk without anything being removed.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---

---
You are allowed to have a bad lesson and not carry it home as evidence of your inadequacy.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---

---
You are allowed to admit that you cannot meet thirty-two different needs at once. That is a structural problem, not a personal one.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---

---
You are allowed to feel relief when a difficult student is absent. The relief does not make you a bad teacher.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---

---
You are allowed to stop grading at 8pm and watch something that has nothing to do with education.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---

---
You are allowed to need the weekend to actually be a weekend, not two more days of lesson planning.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---

---
You are allowed to feel afraid of the parent conference even though you know the student's situation better than the parent does.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---

---
You are allowed to cry in your car after dismissal. The car is yours. The tears are honest.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---

---
You are allowed to not have the answer for the student who is struggling. Not-knowing does not erase your training.
---

## PERMISSION v13
---

---
You are allowed to set a boundary with a colleague who uses your planning time for venting.
---

## PERMISSION v14
---

---
You are allowed to feel the test scores without letting them define your worth as a teacher.
---

## PERMISSION v15
---

---
You are allowed to want to leave teaching and still love the students. Those are not contradictions.
---

## PERMISSION v16
---

---
You are allowed to feel exhausted by caring. The exhaustion is proof you care, not evidence you're failing.
---

## PERMISSION v17
---

---
You are allowed to stop scanning the room for the next crisis and trust that you will handle it when it arrives.
---

## PERMISSION v18
---

---
You are allowed to ask for help from admin without worrying it will appear on your evaluation.
---

## PERMISSION v19
---

---
You are allowed to do enough instead of everything. Enough is real. Everything is a trap.
---

## PERMISSION v20
---

---
You are allowed to be a person who teaches, not a teacher who occasionally remembers to be a person.
---
