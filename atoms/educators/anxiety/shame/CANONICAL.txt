Anika sits with this truth and it releases something in her shoulders that has been clenched for months. She reads a student evaluation that says the course could be improved and instead of searching for evidence of her inadequacy, she simply notes it. This is feedback. It's useful. It doesn't define her competence. Her partner notices that she is more present at home. Her doctoral students notice that she is genuinely engaged again. Anika has learned that excellence in one domain—her research—doesn't require excellence in another—her teaching format—to justify her place in academia. She can be very good at teaching without being perfect. She can be learning something new without failing. The shame has released its grip.
