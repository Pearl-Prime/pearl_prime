

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible

What I notice is a particular binding of self-worth to the outcomes of teaching. A student succeeds and I feel valuable. A student struggles and I feel inadequate. A class goes well and I feel worthy. A class goes badly and I feel like a failure. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as outcome-dependent-worth.

The mechanism is the way that my sense of value is entirely dependent on things that are not fully in my control. The mechanism has tied my worth to student outcomes, test scores, parent satisfaction, administrative evaluation. The mechanism has made my worth into something external.

What I have come to understand is that this mechanism is extraordinarily powerful because it means that my worth is constantly in question. It is never resolved. It is never secure. Each day brings new data that affects the calculation.

I have noticed that the mechanism often becomes acute in moments of failure. A student does not succeed. A lesson falls flat. A parent is unhappy. The mechanism immediately translates these events into a judgment about my worth.

What the mechanism does is create a situation where my fundamental sense of value is unstable. The mechanism has made my worth a function of external outcomes. When outcomes are uncertain, worth is uncertain.
