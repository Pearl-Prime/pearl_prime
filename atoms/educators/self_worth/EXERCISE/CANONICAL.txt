

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit down. Place your feet flat on the floor. Press down hard. Count to six. You received feedback about your work. Your work is not your worth. Press again and count to six. Your body is separate from the evaluation. Feel the ground holding you.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your eyes. Say silently: "I am more than this evaluation." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. Repeat three times. The feedback is data. It is not your essence. Your essence is vast and ungraded.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Say silently: "My heartbeat does not change based on feedback." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. You are alive. That is the baseline fact.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Make tight fists. Count to three. Release and spread fingers wide. Count to three. Repeat six times. You may be clenching around the criticism. Release it. Your hands are not responsible for the evaluation.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward five times. Let them drop. Your shoulders carry the weight of professional judgment. Set it down. Roll and drop.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Hum as you exhale. Breathe in silently through your nose. Hum on the exhale. Do this eight times. The vibration is your own voice. The evaluation is not. Listen to your own hum.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Press your back against a wall or chair firmly. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You are supported. The evaluation does not remove support.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. Your nervous system is activated by judgment. Calm it. You are safe. The feedback is over.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Tense every muscle in your body. Count to four. Release. Tense and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. You are holding the evaluation in your muscles. Release it. Your body is separate from the grade.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Press your heels down hard. Keep toes light. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat six times. Your feet are rooted. The evaluation cannot shake this.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Clench your jaw. Count to three. Release. Repeat six times. You may have unsaid responses to the feedback. Release the jaw. You do not need to defend yourself to yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Look at your hands. Notice one detail on each hand. A line. A freckle. A callus. These hands did the work. Separately from how the work was evaluated. These hands are real.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Cup your hands over your eyes without touching them. Sit for ten counts. Breathe. The feedback is temporary. Your life is vast. Remove your hands. You are wider than this moment.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip on one hand slowly. Thumb to index. Middle. Ring. Pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. Your mind is sorting small, present facts. Not the evaluation.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Press both feet down. Count to six. Release. Your body is stronger than feedback. Press and plant yourself. Feel the earth hold you.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Shake your hands out hard for five seconds. Stop. Shake them again. Stop. Three times. You may want to grab the feedback and twist it. Shake your hands empty. They can be empty.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Feel the temperature of the room on your face. Breathe in and notice it. Breathe out and notice it. Repeat four times. You are in this room. The evaluation is a document. You are a person.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest firmly. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. You are gathering yourself. You are gathering your own compassion for yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Lean your upper body forward. Let your arms hang. Count to four. Rise slowly. Count to four. Repeat four times. You are moving out of contraction. Rising toward dignity.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. Nasal breathing in activates calm. Mouth breathing out releases the sting. In through nose. Out through mouth.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Place both hands on your thighs. Press them down. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your legs are strong. They carry you into the next day.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Tense your face, forehead, eyes, jaw. Count to four. Release completely. Repeat four times. Your face may contort around the evaluation. Release it. You can face the world with ease.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine one thing you did well this week. See yourself doing it. Count to six. Open your eyes. That well thing is also true. The feedback does not erase it.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Make a tight fist with each hand. Count to three. Release and spread fingers wide. Count to three. Repeat six times. This is the gesture of holding the feedback and releasing it. You hold it and let it go.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Say out loud or silently: "I did my best with what I knew." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. Repeat three times. The feedback cannot change the past. It can inform the future. But your past effort was real.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Press your tongue hard against the roof of your mouth. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your mouth is strong. The feedback is not a judgment of your worth.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Shake your entire body for ten seconds. Let yourself vibrate. Stop. Feel the settling. The evaluation has moved through you. Now you are still.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Place your hand on your belly. Breathe in and feel it expand. Breathe out and feel it settle. Repeat six times. Your belly knows the difference between feedback and truth. Feedback is temporary. You are lasting.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Turn your head slowly left. Breathe in. Breathe out. Turn right. Breathe in. Breathe out. Repeat three times. You are turning toward the next moment. Not away.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Look in a mirror or window. See your own face. Say silently: "You are worthy whether or not." Look at your own eyes. Say it again. Repeat three times. Whether or not the evaluation was positive. Whether or not anyone is watching. You are worthy.
