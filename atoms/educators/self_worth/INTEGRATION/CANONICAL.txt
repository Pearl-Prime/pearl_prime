

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your worth is not in the attendance record."
---
When you're not in the classroom, who are you. This is not a philosophical question for you. It's a practical crisis. The identity that's built over years is tied to what you do. The care you give, the lessons you teach, the students whose names you know, the problems you solve. When school ends for the day, that structure collapses. You're in the kitchen and there's no one who needs anything from you and the silence arrives. Your body doesn't know how to be present in a context where you're not required. The worth that's lived in your usefulness dissolves. You panic a little. You check your email for something that needs attending. You plan something for the next day. The nervous system has learned: you exist when you're productive. When you're not, the question becomes: why am I here. This is not vanity. This is the consequence of building your entire sense of value on what you give.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Tying worth to outcomes costs your inner life."
---
Every student success becomes your success. Every failure becomes your failure. The grade on the test becomes a referendum on your competence. The parent's criticism lands as evidence of your inadequacy. The system is designed this way. Your value is tied to measurable outcomes. So your nervous system vigilantly monitors performance. Did I reach enough students. Did they learn. Am I good enough. The monitoring is exhausting. The work becomes seconded by anxiety about whether the work is good. Your worth is constantly on trial. The internal resource that gets devoted to self-measurement is unavailable for rest or joy or being anything besides productive. The cost is paid in the part of you that could exist for its own sake, not as evidence of competence.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You have survived summers without purpose."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You are here. You have lived through the summers where the classroom emptied and you became unclear to yourself. You have sat in your own house with no one needing anything from you and felt the silence. You have struggled with the question of who you are when you're not teaching. You have watched other people rest and felt guilty for not being needed. You have come back to school in the fall grateful for the return to purpose. You have done this year after year. You haven't quit. You haven't dissolved entirely into the role. Some part of you has persisted in the empty space. The self that exists outside the classroom is smaller and less defined. But it's there. You've kept it alive while the professional identity consumed most of the available energy.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if your worth existed before school."
---
The underlying belief is: I have value because I teach. I have purpose because I am needed. But what if the teaching is something you do, not something you are. What if the worth is not created by the outcomes but already present. What if you could care about the students and the work and still hold a separate truth: I exist. I have value. Not because of what I produce. Not because of what happens in the classroom. Just because I'm here. This is not selfish. This is the only kind of stability that allows sustainable care. The teacher who thinks they're only valuable when giving will eventually have nothing left to give.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The system needs you through service."
---
If teachers believed they had intrinsic worth independent of outcomes, they wouldn't accept underpayment as the price of meaningful work. If teachers had a separate identity outside the classroom, they would prioritize rest and limits. The system needs your worth to be entirely housed in your professional value. It needs you empty without the job. This keeps you compliant.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: interrupt_landing
carry_line: "The panic in summer is information about identity."
---
When the classroom empties and the anxiety arrives, pay attention. Your nervous system is telling you: you've organized your entire worth in one place. That's not stability. That's risk.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Teaching consumed the self that existed beside it."
---
The energy devoted to being valuable in the classroom is energy unavailable for being yourself outside it. The trade happens quietly. By the time you notice, the version of you that exists for its own sake is mostly gone.
