Rachel gets the highest parent satisfaction scores in her department. She reads them and waits for the but. The scores are clean—no but, no caveat, no qualification. The shame produces its own: the survey was easy this year, the parents were being polite, next semester will be different. The scores are on paper. The shame's revision is in her head. Rachel's sense of her own competence is empty.

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3

---
Rachel maps her filing system. Positive: parent scores, student letters, peer compliments—all filed under "they're being nice." Negative: one parent complaint, one rough observation, one lesson that fell apart—all filed under "the real you." The filing system has operated for fourteen years of teaching. Fourteen years of evidence that she's good at her job, consistently filed as exceptions. Fourteen years of rare negative events, consistently filed as truth. The filing system is a machine that produces one output regardless of input: you are not as good as they think you are. The machine doesn't malfunction. The machine functions exactly as the shame designed it.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
James identifies the shame's speed. Positive events: the shame takes thirty seconds to reframe. Negative events: the shame takes zero seconds—instantaneous filing under "proof." The speed differential is the mechanism. Positive evidence requires processing time during which the shame inserts doubt. Negative evidence arrives pre-processed—no doubt, no reframing, no alternative interpretation. The asymmetry means negative evidence always wins the race to belief. One parent complaint reaches James's self-concept faster than twenty parent compliments. The complaint travels at the speed of confirmation. The compliments travel at the speed of doubt.

Michael realizes the shame converts every success into a debt. The curriculum redesign was praised—now the shame says: you've set a standard you'll have to maintain. The maintenance converts achievement from a deposit into a withdrawal. Each success adds to the expectation account. ## TURNING_POINT v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Rachel opens the drawer. Reads the parent scores—all of them, from three years of surveys. Reads them not as individual documents but as a dataset. The dataset shows: consistently high scores, three years running, across four different survey instruments. The shame can dismiss one survey. The shame has difficulty dismissing a three-year pattern across multiple instruments. Patterns are harder to reframe than events because patterns contain redundancy. Each data point confirms the others. She sits with the data. The data says: this is sustained performance, not isolated luck. The shame tries "the surveys are easy." Three different surveys, same result. The shame's explanation has to become increasingly elaborate to account for the data. Elaborate explanations are less convincing than simple ones.
