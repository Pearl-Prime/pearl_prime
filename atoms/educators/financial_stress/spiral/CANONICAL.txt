COST_TYPE: internal

The spiral has a structure. Lisa tracks it: trigger is always small. A bill, a price tag, a comment about money. Then the first calculation. Then the second. Then the tenth. Each calculation produces the next like dominoes falling sideways. The content changes—sometimes it's rent, sometimes it's retirement—but the architecture is identical. Small trigger. Damon notices something: the spiral happens fastest when other people are present. Alone, a bill is a bill. But when a colleague mentions a new car, the bill becomes evidence. Evidence of falling behind. Evidence of wrong choices. The presence of witnesses accelerates the math. Other people's stability makes his instability louder. He can spiral from a coworker's vacation photo to career regret in under thirty seconds. The speed is the mechanism. The audience is the fuel.

Lisa writes down the spiral. Not the content—the structure. Trigger: electric bill. Step 1: bill amount. Step 2: other bills. Step 3: career choice. Step 4: retirement. Step 5: complete ruin. She reads it back. Five steps from a utility bill to total ruin. On paper it looks absurd. In her body it felt inevitable. The absurdity and the inevitability existed at the same time. She keeps the paper. Looks at it the next time the spiral starts. The paper doesn't stop the spiral. The paper makes it visible.
