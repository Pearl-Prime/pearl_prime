What I notice is a particular kind of math that I run continuously. The classroom supplies that are not paid for by the school. The cost of printing materials at home. The cost of professional development that is not funded. The second job. The freelance work. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as perpetual-budget-calculation.

The mechanism is the running addition and subtraction that happens in the background of my mind. I am aware constantly of the gap between what I earn and what things cost. The mechanism is calculating whether I can afford things, what I might have to give up, where the money will come from.

What I have come to understand is that this mechanism never turns off. I am sleeping and the mechanism is running. I am teaching and the mechanism is running. The mechanism has become a permanent background operation.

I have noticed that the mechanism often becomes acute during particular moments. When bills arrive. When something breaks. When school expenses are coming up. The mechanism activates and suddenly the calculation is not in the background, it is in the foreground.

What the mechanism does is create a kind of constant worry beneath everything else. The teaching is happening but the financial concern is always there. The mechanism has made financial survival a permanent part of my consciousness.
