title: When Your Child's Dreams Cost Money You Don't Have
category: financial_anxiety
tags: parenting, money_stress, shame, unmet_expectations

---

你的胸口緊了起來。

你兒子說得很輕鬆：「我想去一個不錯的地方。」

不錯。那個詞在你心裡迴盪。不錯要花錢。很多錢。你沒有那麼多錢。

你兒子假設他的家庭能負擔得起大學。他沒有問過。他只是假設了。而你還沒有告訴他真相——你的家庭負擔不起。

你四十幾歲了。你的財務狀況從三十幾歲到現在沒有改善過。你現在賺的錢，扣除通膨後，和十五年前一樣。十五年。一切都變得更貴了，但你的薪水沒有。

你兒子在構想一個未來。他看著這個未來，看著它閃閃發光，充滿可能性。而你知道你沒有辦法給他那個未來。

你正在教導他。同時你也在想辦法該如何讓他失望。

這兩件事同時發生著，在你的心裡。

---

## THE WEIGHT v01

### SOURCE: internal monologue

The shame is real. Not because you've failed — but because the system has. You did the work. You showed up. You earned a living. And somewhere between then and now, that stopped being enough. That's not a personal failure. That's a structural one. But it doesn't feel that way when your son is talking about "somewhere nice," does it?

你感到羞愧。不是因為你失敗了——而是因為這個系統失敗了。你做了工作。你按時出現。你賺取生活費。但在過去和現在之間的某個地方，那變得不夠了。這不是個人的失敗。這是結構性的。但當你的兒子在談論「一個不錯的地方」時，感覺不是這樣，對嗎？

The worst part? You can't even be angry at your son for assuming. He's supposed to assume. He's supposed to believe his parents can give him things. That's what childhood is. That's what trust is. You can't punish him for that.

最糟的部分是什麼？你甚至不能對你兒子的假設感到生氣。他應該假設。他應該相信他的父母能給他東西。那就是童年。那就是信任。你不能因此懲罰他。

So you carry it alone. The gap between what he expects and what you can deliver. You teach during the day and do the math at night. College costs. Scholarships are competitive. Loans exist, but they're his burden then, not yours now.

所以你獨自承擔著。他期望的東西和你能給他的東西之間的差距。你白天教書，晚上做計算。大學要花錢。獎學金很難競爭。貸款存在，但那時會是他的負擔，不是你現在的。

---

## THE CONVERSATION v01

### SOURCE: anticipation

You haven't had the talk yet. But you're rehearsing it in your head. Over and over.

你還沒有進行那場談話。但你在心裡反覆排練著。一遍又一遍。

How do you tell him? Do you start with "we need to talk about money"? Does that sound ominous? Does it prepare him or terrify him? Do you lead with the numbers — show him the spreadsheet, make it about facts instead of feelings? Do you tell him first, or do you let him apply and then crush the dream when the acceptance letters come?

你怎麼告訴他？你是從「我們需要談談錢」開始？那聽起來不祥嗎？那是為他做準備還是嚇唬他？你是從數字開始——給他看試算表，讓它成為關於事實而不是感受的事？你是先告訴他，還是讓他申請，然後在錄取通知書來時摧毀他的夢想？

There's no good way to do this.

沒有好的方法來做這件事。

But you have to do it anyway.

但你無論如何都必須做。

---

## WHAT NEEDS TO HAPPEN v01

### SOURCE: practical next steps

**First: Stop protecting him from reality.**

**首先：停止保護他遠離現實。**

Not with cruelty. Not with bitterness. But with honesty. He's old enough to understand that families have different resources. He's old enough to know that "nice" costs money. He's old enough to problem-solve with you instead of against you.

不是通過殘忍。不是通過怨恨。而是通過誠實。他已經足夠大，能理解家庭有不同的資源。他已經足夠大，能知道「不錯」要花錢。他已經足夠大，能和你一起解決問題，而不是與你對抗。

When you tell him, don't apologize for being poor. Apologize for not telling him sooner.

當你告訴他時，不要為貧窮而道歉。為沒有更早告訴他而道歉。

**Second: Expand what "nice" means.**

**其次：擴展「不錯」的含義。**

A nice college isn't always the expensive one. A nice college is one where he can thrive. Sometimes that's a state school. Sometimes that's community college first, then transfer. Sometimes that's a school that actually wants him enough to give him money. Those are all nice. Some of them are nicer than the ones with the big names.

一所不錯的大學不一定是昂貴的那所。一所不錯的大學是他能茁壯成長的地方。有時那是州立大學。有時那是先上社區大學，然後轉學。有時那是一所真正想要他——足夠給他錢的學校。那些都不錯。其中一些比那些大名鼎鼎的學校更不錯。

Help him reframe. Help him see that you're not saying no to college. You're saying yes to a different version of it.

幫助他重新框架。幫助他看到你不是在對大學說不。你是在對它的不同版本說是。

**Third: Let him feel disappointed. Then move.**

**第三：讓他感到失望。然後前進。**

When you tell him, he might be upset. He might feel betrayed. He might cry or yell or go silent. That's okay. That's his job — to feel what he feels. Your job is to not make his feelings your punishment. Don't disappear. Don't get defensive. Don't make him comfort you about your own money shame.

當你告訴他時，他可能會不高興。他可能感到被背叛。他可能哭泣或尖叫或沉默。那沒關係。那是他的工作——感受他的感受。你的工作是不要讓他的感受成為你的懲罰。不要消失。不要變得防守。不要讓他安慰你關於你自己的金錢羞愧。

Then, the next day or the day after, you move. You sit down together and you figure it out. What schools are affordable? What scholarships is he eligible for? What can he contribute? What can you contribute? What does the actual path look like?

然後，第二天或第三天，你前進。你一起坐下來，你們一起解決。哪些學校負擔得起？他符合哪些獎學金的資格？他能貢獻什麼？你能貢獻什麼？實際的路是什麼樣的？

Action is the antidote to shame.

行動是羞愧的解藥。

---

## THE HARDER TRUTH v01

### SOURCE: reflection

Here's what you need to know: You are not failing him by being poor. You are failing him only if you hide it and let him crash into reality without warning.

你需要知道的是：你不是因為貧窮而對他失敗。只有當你隱藏它並讓他毫無警告地撞上現實時，你才是對他失敗。

Your son will recover from this disappointment. Millions of kids do every year. They find other paths. They get creative. They build lives that are still good, still full, still theirs.

你的兒子會從這個失望中恢復。每年有數百萬孩子這樣做。他們找到其他路徑。他們變得有創意。他們建造仍然美好、仍然充實、仍然是他們自己的生活。

What he won't recover from, as easily, is the feeling that you lied to him. That you knew and didn't tell him. That you let him dream about a future you couldn't afford.

他不會那麼容易恢復的是感覺你對他撒謊。你知道但沒有告訴他。你讓他夢想一個你負擔不起的未來。

Be honest. Be kind. Be clear.

誠實。善良。清楚。

Then build a plan together. That's what he'll remember — not the money you didn't have, but the way you faced it with him.

然後一起制定計劃。那就是他會記得的——不是你沒有的錢，而是你和他一起面對它的方式。
