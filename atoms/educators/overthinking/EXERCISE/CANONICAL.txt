

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts. Silently say: this thought can wait. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat six times. The parent email will still be there. It does not need to be drafted eleven times in your head right now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both hands flat on your thighs. Press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Press again. Repeat four times. Each press pulls your attention back into your hands, out of the loop replaying in your mind.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. You are allowed to stop reviewing the lesson that already happened. It is over. This breath is now.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Roll your neck slowly to the right. Count to four. Return to center. Roll left. Count to four. Repeat three times each side. Notice how the replaying thought pauses while your neck moves. Movement interrupts the loop.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Name five things you can see right now. Then four things you can hear. Then three things you can touch. This pulls your mind out of the hallway conversation you keep re-running and back into this room.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let your tongue rest low in your mouth. Breathe six slow breaths through this softened jaw. Overthinking tightens the jaw first, before the mind even notices it is spiraling.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Press your feet into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Repeat five times. The floor does not change no matter how many versions of the conversation you replay. Let your feet find that steadiness.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. One slow exhale interrupts a spiral more effectively than trying to think your way out of it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Clench your fists for four counts. Release and spread your fingers wide. Repeat six times. Each release is a physical version of letting go of the thought you cannot stop turning over.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart. Feel it beating for ten counts. Your heart does not overthink. It just keeps its rhythm. Borrow that rhythm for these ten counts.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Sit back and let the chair hold your full weight. Count to six. The decision you are second-guessing already happened. The chair holds you now, in the present, not in the version of the day you keep rewriting.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Shake your hands out for ten seconds. Stop. Rest them. Shake again for ten seconds. You are physically shaking loose the thought that has been looping since the last bell.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently say: I already know what I know. Repeat this eight times. Overthinking asks the same question in new disguises. This phrase closes the loop.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Place your feet flat and press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Repeat four times. Your feet are on this floor, in this room, not still standing in the hallway replaying what you said.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Cup your hands over your closed eyes. Sit in the dark for ten counts, breathing slowly. In this dark there is nothing to analyze. There is only the breath.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Tense your whole body for four counts, then release completely. Repeat three times. Overthinking often lives in a tensed body long before the mind admits it is spiraling. Release the body and the mind can follow.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six. Repeat this cycle six times. You do not need a final answer to the question in your head before you can breathe. The breath does not wait for resolution.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Lean forward, elbows on your knees or desk, and let your head hang for a count of eight. Rise slowly. Repeat three times. Let gravity hold the weight of the thought for a moment instead of your mind holding it alone.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly: index, middle, ring, pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. Follow the counting fully. When your mind drifts back to the replay, bring it back to your fingers.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Hum quietly on a long exhale. Breathe in silently through your nose. Hum again on the exhale. Repeat six times. The vibration gives your mind something simple to follow instead of the thought it keeps circling.
---
