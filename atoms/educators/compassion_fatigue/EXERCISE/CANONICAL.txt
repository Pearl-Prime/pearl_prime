

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit in a chair with your feet flat. Breathe in for four counts. The student's words are not in your body. Breathe out for six counts. You have listened. You have held space. Now you release it. Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Repeat six times. Your empathy has limits. That is wisdom, not failure.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand and press your feet hard into the floor. Count to five. Your feet are here. The student's pain is not your pain to carry home. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat six times. You can be present without absorbing. Presence is enough.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Place your hand on your heart. Feel it beating. Count three beats. Remove your hand. Place it on your belly. Feel the breath. Count three breaths. Remove your hand. Place it on your thigh. Feel the solid contact with the chair. Count three sensations of contact. You are inside your own body, not inside the student's story.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Shake your hands out hard and fast for five seconds. Stop. Let them drop. Shake them again for five seconds. Stop. You are physically releasing what is not yours to carry. The student's trauma lives in the student's body. Your body is separate.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Hum as you breathe out. Breathe in silently through your nose. Hum on the exhale. Do this six times. The vibration reminds you: you are a separate person. You have your own nervous system. It is okay to calm it.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Walk to the bathroom or another room alone. Close the door. Sit. Say silently: "This is not my pain." Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Repeat four times. You can acknowledge the student's pain without owning it.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Press your back against the chair or wall firmly. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You are bounded. You are contained. The student's pain does not leak into you through the spaces in your edges.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Tense every muscle in your body. Count to four. Release. Tense and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. On the final release, feel how much you were holding. Let it rest now.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward slowly. Count to four. Release. Repeat this six times. Your shoulders have carried the weight of their disclosure. Set it down. Your shoulders can be light again.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Look at something in the room that has no connection to the student. A pen. A plant. A wall. Stare at this object for a full minute. Your mind is returning to the present, to what is now. Not what was just said.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Make tight fists. Count to three. Release and spread your fingers wide. Count to three. Repeat six times. This is a gesture of letting go. You held what the student needed. Now you release it.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Cup your hands over your eyes without touching them. Sit in darkness for ten counts. Breathe. This is a container for the moment. You have held it. Now you close it. Remove your hands. The moment is closed.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Hold for one. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Hold for one. Repeat six times. The different pathways calm different nervous system activation. In through nose. Out through mouth.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly on one hand. Thumb to index. Middle. Ring. Pinky. Reverse. Repeat this sequence three times. Your mind is sorting itself. You are separate. Your hand is your hand. Your mind is your mind.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Press both feet down hard. Count to seven. Release. Press and count to seven. Release. Repeat five times. Your feet know the ground. The ground is constant. You are planted here, not in the student's pain.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Clench your jaw. Count to three. Release. Repeat six times. You may have been clenching your teeth through the disclosure. Release the jaw. Release the moment.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tense your face, forehead, eyes, cheeks, jaw. Count to four. Release completely. Repeat four times. Compassion fatigue lives in the face. Release it. Relax your face.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Push them toward each other firmly. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. You are closing your hands. The moment is contained.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Feel the temperature of the air on your face. Breathe in and notice cool air. Breathe out and notice warm air. Repeat this awareness four times. You are sensing the present room, the present moment. Not the past words.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Lean forward slightly, arms hanging. Count to four. Rise to sitting tall. Count to four. Repeat four times. You are bending with the weight. Then standing strong. You can hold both.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Press your heels down hard into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat six times. Your feet are here. The student is in their own body. You are in your own body.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, say "mm" silently in your throat. Count to six on the exhale. Repeat six times. The internal sound is just for you. Your own nervous system. Your own regulation.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Turn your head slowly left. Breathe in. Breathe out. Turn right. Breathe in. Breathe out. Repeat three times. You are turning away from the disclosure. Facing toward your own body, your own needs.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine a clear boundary between you and the student. A line of light. Count to four. Open your eyes. The boundary stands. It is not unkind. It is necessary.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Place your hand on your own shoulder. Feel the solid bone. Feel the muscle. Feel the skin. This is your body. It is separate. It has endured. It is okay now.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Tense your legs and press your feet down. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat six times. Your legs carry your own body. They are not carrying the student's burden.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Shake your entire body for ten seconds. Let your whole frame vibrate. Stop. Feel the settling. You are releasing what is not yours.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat eight times. This biological regulation is for you. You are allowed to regulate yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Press your tongue hard against the roof of your mouth. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. You are reclaiming the small spaces inside your own body.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Rest your head back if you can. Feel support behind your head. Breathe in for four. Breathe out for six. Repeat four times. You have done the work of listening. The student is in their own care now. You are in your own care. Both are true.
