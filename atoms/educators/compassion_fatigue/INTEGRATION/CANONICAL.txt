

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The numbness is not compassion breaking."
---
When you stop feeling the impact of a student's story—the child whose bruises don't register, the family living in a car that becomes background—this is not moral failure. This is your nervous system protecting itself. Compassion fatigue is not a broken heart. It's a protected heart. Your body has learned: there is more suffering here than you can absorb and remain functional. So it closes the door. The flattening you feel, the way stories slide past without landing—this is a survival mechanism. Your capacity to feel the impact was real once. The capacity to keep feeling it without dissolving requires something that the system doesn't provide: time to process, resources to repair, permission to be affected. The numbness is what happens when those don't exist.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Carrying pain accumulates without repair."
---
You cannot witness the broken home situation in how a child sits and then leave it at school. The story stays with you. The sixth grader's disclosure at lunch. The parent's desperation in the hallway. The accumulation happens quietly. There's no ledger. Nobody counts the emotional weight you're holding. The system doesn't budget for processing time. You don't get to close the classroom door and have someone help you metabolize what you've absorbed. Instead, it compounds. Each story lands in a nervous system that's already full. The body keeps a different kind of score than the one in the grade book. The accumulation is real whether or not it's acknowledged.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Numbness does not mean care is gone."
---
The underlying belief is: if I stop feeling the impact, I've stopped caring. But numbness and caring are not opposites. Compassion fatigue is what happens when caring exhausts the nervous system faster than the system allows it to repair. The numbness is not evidence that the care is gone. It's evidence that the care had no container. What if you could acknowledge the limit—I cannot absorb infinite suffering and remain human—without that being failure. What if numbness is information: the system that demands constant witnessing without resources to process it is broken, not you. What if you could be in the classroom with students and know that you are already full, already affected, already carrying what you can carry.
