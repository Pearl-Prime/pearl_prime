# Systemic Compassion Fatigue: A Naming

You're describing something precise that deserves its own language. Here are some framings:

## The Core Mechanism

**Structural Moral Injury** — the specific wound that comes from witnessing systemic harm to vulnerable people while being embedded in the system that produces it, with the knowledge and powerlessness to affect its causes.

This is distinct from:
- Vicarious trauma (absorbing others' suffering)
- Burnout (exhaustion from workload)
- Compassion fatigue (empathic distress)

The injury comes from the *positioning itself*: you are required to see, to care, to be present—and simultaneously positioned in a role that cannot address the root causes. The system *needs* you to witness and manage the human cost of its own operations.

## Alternative Names

**Institutional Witnessing Injury** — the harm of being positioned as a required observer of systemic damage while constrained by the institution itself.

**Complicity Fatigue** — not that you are complicit in intention, but that your daily functioning depends on a system you recognize as harmful to children. The fatigue comes from the gap between what you see and what you're permitted to address.

**Structural Helplessness** — repeated confrontation with preventable harm in contexts where your role is explicitly insufficient to intervene at the level where intervention would matter.

## Why This Matters

Naming this specifically matters because:

1. **It's not your compassion that's the problem.** Standard "self-care" advice misses the injury entirely because the injury isn't caused by caring too much—it's caused by structural contradiction.

2. **It won't resolve through individual resilience.** You cannot think or breathe your way out of a positioning that is, by design, insufficient.

3. **It requires systemic acknowledgment.** The institution must recognize that it has positioned people to witness harm while constraining their ability to address its causes—and that this is a form of harm to its workers.

Does this naming resonate with what you're experiencing?
