

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

What I notice is a particular kind of numbness that emerges after prolonged emotional labor. A student shares something difficult and I respond appropriately. I listen. I validate. I hold space. But there is something absent in my own internal experience. I am going through the motions of compassion while experiencing a kind of emptiness beneath it. The mechanism I recognize is what I think of as compassion-depletion. The system that generates empathetic response is like a well that can be drawn from, and the well has a finite depth. When the drawing continues long past the replenishment, the well begins to run dry. The mechanism has withdrawn the emotional ease that once made this work feel natural.

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: protective
tier_variant: C

What I notice is a particular kind of cynicism that begins to color my thinking about students and families. A student who is struggling begins to seem like they are not trying hard enough. A parent who is involved in their child's education begins to seem like they are micromanaging. A situation that would normally invite compassion begins to invite judgment. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as fatigue-driven-attribution-shift. The mechanism shifts the way I interpret behavior. When I am resourced, I interpret a student's difficulty as a sign that they need extra support. When I am fatigued, the same behavior begins to seem like a sign that the student is not sufficiently motivated.

What I notice is a particular quality of irritability that emerges when I am fatigued. A student asks a reasonable question and I feel an internal flash of irritation. A parent sends a reasonable email and I feel a surge of resistance. A colleague makes a reasonable request and I feel a pull toward saying no. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as low-threshold irritability. The mechanism is the heightened sensitivity to demands when the system is depleted. The threshold for irritation is lower because the capacity to absorb additional demands is lower. Everything that comes toward me registers as a demand, and every demand registers as an irritation.

What I notice is a particular withdrawal from student relationships as the fatigue deepens. Students who used to feel important become people I interact with less. Students who used to invite my engagement become people I maintain a professional distance from. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as relationship-protective-withdrawal. The mechanism has turned the protective withdrawal into evidence of failure.
