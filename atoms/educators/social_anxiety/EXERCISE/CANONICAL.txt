

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts before you walk into the staff room. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat four times. You do not need a script. You only need to walk in and let the room be what it is.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both hands flat on the table in front of you. Press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Press again. Repeat four times. This grounds your attention in your hands instead of in how you think you are being perceived.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes if you can, or soften your gaze. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat six times before the meeting starts. Your nervous system does not know the difference between real danger and a room full of colleagues yet. This breath teaches it the difference.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let it hang slightly open. Breathe six slow breaths. Social anxiety sets the jaw tight before you even walk through the door. Notice it now and let it soften before you speak.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Roll your shoulders back and let them drop. Count to four. Repeat five times. You do not have to make yourself smaller to be in this hallway with other people. Let your shoulders take up their actual space.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Press your feet into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Repeat five times. The ground under you does not change whether the conversation goes well or awkwardly. Let your feet hold that steadiness.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. One slow exhale before a parent conference does more than any rehearsed line.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Clench your fists for four counts. Release and spread your fingers wide. Repeat six times. Each release loosens the urge to over-prepare every sentence before you say it out loud.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart for ten counts, breathing slowly. This heart has gotten you through every hard hallway conversation before this one. It can get you through this one too.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Notice three details in the room that are not the people in it: a chair, a window, a cup. Return your attention to your own breath. You do not have to track everyone's reaction every second.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Sit back and let the chair hold your full weight. Count to six. You do not have to perform relaxed. You are allowed to actually be as tense as you are, and let the chair hold you anyway.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Shake your hands out for ten seconds before you enter the room. Stop. Let them rest. This is not about calming down completely. It is about releasing the sharpest edge of the tension before you walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently name one thing that is true regardless of how this conversation goes. Repeat with two more true things. Social anxiety predicts disaster. This breath returns you to fact.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Place your feet flat and press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Repeat four times. You are standing on solid ground in this staff meeting, not on the version of it your anxiety is imagining.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly under the table: index, middle, ring, pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. No one can see this. It is only for you, only to keep you steady while the meeting continues around you.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Tense your whole body for four counts, then release completely. Repeat three times, ideally before you leave your classroom to enter the hallway. Notice how much lighter you feel walking out.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six. Repeat this cycle six times. You do not have to know exactly what to say before you walk in. The breath steadies you enough to find the words as you go.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Lean forward slightly with your elbows resting on the table. Count to four. Sit back up straight. Count to four. Repeat three times. This small movement gives your body something to do besides brace.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Notice the temperature of the air on your face as you walk toward the staff room door. Breathe in and feel it cool. Breathe out and feel it warm. This keeps you present in the hallway instead of in the imagined conversation ahead.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Rest your palms flat against your thighs under the table. Press gently for a count of five. Release. Repeat four times. This quiet pressure is yours alone, steady and private, for as long as the meeting runs.
---
