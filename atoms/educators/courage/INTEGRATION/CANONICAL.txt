

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Courage and fear occupy the same body."
---
When you speak up in a staff meeting where dissent is discouraged, your hands might shake. Your voice might catch. The body fire that arrives when you contradict authority is not evidence that you shouldn't speak. It's evidence that speaking matters. The alarm response is appropriate to the risk. You're challenging a hierarchy that controls your employment. The fact that your nervous system registers this as threat is not weakness. It's accurate calibration. Courage is not the absence of the body's alarm. Courage is standing in the classroom and telling a truth that costs something to say while your chest is tight. The heat, the tremor, the pressure in your throat—these are the signatures of real courage. They mean you're risking something that matters.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Advocacy in hierarchy carries invisible penalties."
---
When you advocate for a student against the administration, you pay a price that doesn't show in a contract. The shift in how you're perceived. The subtle ways you become the person "not a team player." The door that stays a little more closed. You don't document these costs. They're not formal. Nobody calls it retaliation. It's just the water you swim in: the professional relationship that cools, the opportunity that doesn't materialize, the budget decision that doesn't favor your department. The system is built so that the costs of dissent stay invisible. Nobody says: you questioned the decision, so you're getting this consequence. It just arrives. And you pay it while everyone else stays neutral and safe.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "You have spoken into the silence."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You have raised your hand in a meeting and disagreed with the administrator. You have sent the email pushing back on a policy. You have told the parent the difficult truth they didn't want to hear. You have stood with a student when the system wanted to sort them away. You have done the small thing that felt large. You have stayed in the profession while doing it—not quit, not caved, not gone silent. You have carried the cost of your own speech. The way the room shifted after you spoke. The way the conversation took a different shape. You have lived through that. You're still employed. You're still showing up. The courage happened in your own nervous system while everything stayed the same on the surface. That counts.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if compliance is not the only safety."
---
The underlying logic is: speak up and you endanger yourself. Stay quiet and you're protected. The system is built on that belief. But this binary is not complete. What if the danger of compliance is different but equal—the slow dissolution of integrity, the watching yourself disappear into institutional acceptance, the child who doesn't get advocated for because nobody spoke. What if courage and safety are not opposites. What if staying whole while teaching in a system built on compromise requires a different kind of risk. What if you could be strategic about when to speak and still speak. What if silence is not the same as protection.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The system silences through consequence, not force."
---
The administrator doesn't need to fire you. They need you quiet. The system maintains itself through subtlety. A comment that travels. The opportunity that goes to someone else. The budget request denied. The teacher who stops speaking after the first time the cost became visible. The system has learned: you don't need overt punishment when psychological cost works. Your own desire to belong, to be safe, to not rock the boat—these become the bars of the cage. The system is elegant this way.
