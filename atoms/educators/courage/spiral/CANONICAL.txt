Rachel wants to propose the new curriculum. But if she proposes it, which means it might get rejected. Which means she'll look foolish. Which means people will question her judgment. Which means future proposals will be prejudged. Which means she loses her voice in the department. Which means she becomes irrelevant. She's holding the proposal in her hands. The papers are warm from her grip. Her hands haven't moved in five minutes. She has held the proposal this way for two days. The proposal is solid. She has researched it. The curriculum addresses real gaps. But the chain of consequences in her mind is so clear, so inevitable, that the proposal stays in her hands. Her fingers are slightly cramped. The proposal is supposed to be shared tomorrow. She is thinking about how she could cancel the share. Something that would make sense. Something that wouldn't be a lie.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/educators/anchored/courage/spiral/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Every brave act runs through the machine. Michael enters the action: "speak up in the meeting." The machine processes it. The machine outputs disaster: isolation, judgment, career damage. He enters a different action: "try something new." Same machine. Same output. The input changes. The output stays constant. The machine only has one setting. Michael has learned not to trust the variation in inputs. The variation doesn't matter. The output is always: this will harm you. He has tried to argue with the machine. The machine doesn't listen to argument. The machine runs the calculation and produces the same result every time. Michael is learning to live with the machine running constantly, always predicting disaster, always finding the thread that unravels into ruin.

COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 3

Rachel knows the structure: action → consequence → worse consequence → ruin. The structure is the same for every brave thing. She has learned the shape. The chain always ends at the same place. Exposure. Loss. Regret. The content changes. The destination doesn't. Every act of courage, according to the spiral, leads to the same cliff. Rachel has learned to see the cliff before she takes the action. She can always trace the path from the action to the cliff. The path is invisible to others. But to Rachel it's clear. She sees it. She stops.

COST_TYPE: opportunity
COST_INTENSITY: 4
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
The spiral runs fast. James can build the whole catastrophe in seconds. He's gotten efficient at it. By the time he's considered speaking up, he's already calculated five steps of fallout. Step one: I say the thing. Step two: parent gets upset. Step three: parent tells admin. Step four: it's in my file. Step five: it affects my evaluation. Step six: my reputation. Step seven: my opportunities. The calculation feels like preparation. The preparation is actually paralysis dressed up as foresight. James is not preparing for something that might happen. James is rehearsing something that won't. The foresight never says "go." It only says "don't." By the time he's done calculating, the moment to speak has passed.
