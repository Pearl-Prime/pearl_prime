

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Press both feet down hard. Count to six. Feel the ground supporting you. Release. Your body is strong. Stand again. Press down. Count to six. This ground is beneath you. You are not alone in your stance.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Make two tight fists. Count to five. Release and shake your hands out. Count to five. Repeat four times. Courage is not the absence of tension. It is action despite tension. Tense and release. Tense and move.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. Your nervous system knows you are about to do something hard. Calm it. Breathe in. Hold. Breathe out.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Place your hand on your heart. Feel it beating. Say silently: "I am doing this." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again: "I am doing this." Repeat three times. Your heart knows. Your body knows. Courage is this simple.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward five times. Feel them drop and settle. Your shoulders have carried worry. Set it down. Roll them back. They are strong. They are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit tall. Lengthen your spine upward. Imagine a string pulling from the crown of your head. Count to six staying tall. Release slightly. Repeat five times. Tall spine. Grounded feet. This is the posture of courage.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest firmly. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. This gesture closes and opens. You are gathering yourself. You are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine yourself saying the hard thing. See yourself speaking calmly. See yourself standing still. See yourself finishing. Open your eyes. You have already done this in your body. Your body will remember this rehearsal.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Tense every muscle in your body. Count to four. Release. Tense again and count to four. Release. Repeat four times. Tension is not your enemy. It is your body preparing. Honor it.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Press your heels down hard. Keep toes light. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat six times. Your feet are planted. You will not be moved by the other person's reaction.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Hum as you exhale. Breathe in silently. Hum on the exhale. Do this eight times. The vibration in your body is courage. It is a low, steady frequency. Hold it.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip on one hand, slowly. Thumb to index. Middle. Ring. Pinky. Reverse. Do this three times. Your mind is present. You are not spinning stories about what might happen. You are here, touching what is real.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Stand and look in a mirror or window reflection. See yourself. Say silently: "I am capable." Look at your own eyes. Say it again. Repeat three times. You are already someone who has done hard things. This is the next one.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Cup your hands over your eyes without touching them. Sit for ten counts. Breathe in. Breathe out. This is your private moment. Your own certainty. Remove your hands. You have gathered courage in the dark.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. The nasal pathway activates parasympathetic response. The mouth exhale releases. In through nose. Out through mouth.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Clench your jaw. Count to three. Release. Repeat six times. You may want to speak small things or no things. Release the jaw. You will speak truth.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tense your face, forehead, eyes, cheeks, jaw. Count to four. Release. Repeat four times. Your face may want to contort with anxiety. Release it. Let it be calm and present.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Press your back against a wall or chair firmly. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You are supported. Something holds you. You are not unsupported in this moment.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Feel the temperature on your face. Notice the air. Notice one sound you hear. Notice the ground beneath you. You are surrounded by the present moment. Not the fear. Not the outcome. The present.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Lean your upper body forward. Let your arms hang. Count to four. Rise slowly, vertebra by vertebra. Count to four. Repeat four times. You are bending with the weight. Then standing strong. Both are okay.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Press your palms together and push them toward each other hard. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. This is a gesture of gathering strength. Do this in your chest, in your core.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Tense your legs hard. Press feet down. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat six times. Your legs are powerful. They will carry you into the room. They will stand while you speak.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Make a tight fist with one hand. Hold it. Count to five. Say silently: "I can." Open your hand. Repeat with the other hand. Do this three times total. Courage is this small and this loud.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Shake your entire body for ten seconds. Let yourself vibrate. Stop. Feel yourself still. You have released the fear energy. Now you are clear.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for one. Breathe out for six. Hold for one. Repeat six times. The rhythm is steady. You are steady. You are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. It is fast. That is okay. Your body is alert. Your body is ready. Say silently: "Thank you, body." Breathe in. Breathe out.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Turn your head slowly left. Breathe in. Breathe out. Turn right. Breathe in. Breathe out. Repeat three times. You are turning toward what comes next. Not away.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Press your tongue hard against the roof of your mouth. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your mouth is strong. Your words are strong. You will speak them.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine light in your chest. It is steady and bright. Count to six. Open your eyes. The light is still there. It is your own light. No one can take it.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Place your feet flat on the floor. Press them down. Say out loud or silently: "I am going in. I will do this. Then I will rest." Press your feet down once more. Stand. Walk toward the conversation. Your body knows how.
