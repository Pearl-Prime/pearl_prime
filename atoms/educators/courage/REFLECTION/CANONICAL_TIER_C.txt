

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
tier_variant: C

What I notice is a particular quality of choice that exists every time I close my classroom door. What I am about to teach is what I believe students should learn. What I am about to teach is not necessarily what the administration told me to teach. What I am about to teach is not necessarily aligned with what the test is measuring. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as pedagogical-fidelity-tension. The mechanism recognizes that I have a choice. I can teach what I am told to teach, or I can teach what I believe students should learn. These are not always the same thing. The mechanism knows this with full clarity.

What I notice is the courage required to advocate for a student against the system. A student needs something that the system is not providing. A student is being treated in a way that I believe is wrong. A student is being pushed toward an outcome that does not serve them. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as systemic-advocacy-courage. The mechanism is the willingness to challenge the system even when challenging the system creates risk. What I have come to understand is that this courage is often costly.
