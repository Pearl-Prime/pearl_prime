

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts. You gave everything you had today. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat six times. Your energy is a resource, not a moral test. Running low does not mean you failed.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Push firmly for five counts. Release. Push again for five counts. Release. Repeat five times. You are pushing against the belief that you must always have more to give. You do not.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Let your shoulders drop away from your ears. Count to four as they fall. Lift them back up. Count to four. Drop them again. Repeat five times. Notice how much weight you carry there by the end of a teaching day. Let it go one more time.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Close your eyes. Place one hand on your chest, one on your stomach. Breathe so the lower hand rises first. Do this eight times. Your body has been running on adrenaline since the first bell. This breath tells it the day is closing.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Shake out both hands hard for ten seconds. Stop. Let them hang loose. Shake again for ten seconds. Stop. You are releasing the accumulated tension of standing, talking, and giving for hours without a real break.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit back fully in your chair. Feel it hold your full weight. Count to six. You do not have to hold yourself up right now. The chair is doing that. Breathe out slowly. Let something else carry you for six counts.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Roll your neck slowly to the right. Count to four. Return to center. Roll left. Count to four. Return to center. Repeat three times each side. Your neck holds the strain of a hundred small decisions made today.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. The long exhale is the signal your nervous system needs: the demands have paused, even briefly.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Rest your hands flat on your thighs. Press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Press again. Count to five. Release. Repeat four times. This is not one more task. This is the opposite of a task. This is stopping.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Notice three things you can see right now that have nothing to do with school. Name them silently. Notice two sounds that are not a bell, a voice, or a hallway. Notice one sensation in your body. You are here, not still teaching.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Clench your fists tightly. Count to four. Release and spread your fingers wide. Count to four. Repeat six times. Each release is a small proof that you can put something down and it will not fall apart.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sigh out loud, long and audible, letting your shoulders drop as you do. Repeat four times. This is not dramatic. It is a documented way to tell your body the effort can ease. You do not need to hold the room anymore.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Place your feet flat and press them into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You gave the classroom your feet all day. Give them back to yourself now.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Cup your hands loosely over your closed eyes. Sit in the dark for ten counts. Breathe slowly. This is a small room with no requests in it. When you open your eyes, you re-enter on your own terms.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Tense your whole body for a count of four: legs, stomach, shoulders, hands. Release everything at once. Feel the drop. Repeat three times. Notice how much you were holding without deciding to.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently say one thing you did today that mattered, even small. Repeat this four times with four different things. Burnout narrows the view to what is unfinished. This widens it back out.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let your tongue rest away from the roof of your mouth. Let your lips part slightly. Hold this softened face for six breaths. You have been "on" for hours. This is what "off" starts to feel like.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Stand and press your heels into the floor. Count to five. Rise slightly onto your toes. Count to five. Lower back down. Repeat four times. Your legs held up the whole day. Let this movement thank them and settle them.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart. Feel it beating. Count ten beats without changing your breath. This is the same body that got through the day. It is still working. It deserves ten quiet counts of attention now.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six, hold for two. Repeat this full cycle six times. This pattern is not another obligation on the list. It is the one thing on the list that asks nothing of you but to receive it.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Lean forward with your elbows on your knees or desk. Let your head hang. Count to eight. Rise slowly. Repeat three times. You do not have to sit up straight and capable every second. Let gravity hold some of the weight for a moment.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Tap your collarbone gently with two fingers, alternating left and right, for twenty seconds. Breathe naturally as you tap. This small rhythm is not for anyone watching. It is only for you, only right now.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Notice the temperature of the air on your skin. Breathe in and feel it cool. Breathe out and feel it warm. Repeat this noticing four times. You do not have to think ahead to tomorrow's lesson right now. You only have to notice this air.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Press your back flat against the chair. Count to six. Ease off slightly. Press again. Count to six. Repeat four times. The chair is not going anywhere. Let it hold you as long as you need before you stand up again.
---
