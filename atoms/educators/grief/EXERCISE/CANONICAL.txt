

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit down. Place your feet flat on the floor. Feel the ground beneath you. The person is gone. You are still here. Feel the ground. Count to six. They cannot be brought back by your will. The ground is real. You are still sitting on it.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your eyes. Say their name silently. Let the absence be present. Count to four with their name in your mind. Breathe in. Breathe out. Say their name again. Grief is love with no place to go. Let it stay in your body a moment longer.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart continues. Say silently: "I am still here." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. You survived. Your body is still functioning. That is okay. That does not mean you are over it.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Press your feet down hard into the floor. Count to six. The earth holds the dead too. You are both here. You on the surface. They below. Press down and feel the thickness of the earth. It holds everything.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Tense your entire body. Count to five. Release slowly. Feel the difference between held and released. Grief makes you hold tight. Release it. Your body is allowed to soften.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Cup your hands over your eyes without touching them. Sit in the darkness. Count to ten. This is a private moment. No one is watching. Your grief does not need witnesses. Remove your hands. The darkness is still inside you. That is okay.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Hum as you exhale. Breathe in silently. Hum on the exhale. Do this six times. The vibration is a kind of prayer. Let your body hum the sorrow.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Look at a photo or imagine a specific moment with the person. Do not push the image away. Let yourself see it. Count to six. Then look away. You do not have to hold the image forever. You can hold it and release it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Shake your hands out hard for five seconds. Stop. Shake them again. Stop. Three times. Grief makes you want to grab onto something. Release your hands. They are empty. Let them be empty.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. Your breath is still working. Your body is still working. These small miracles continue.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Place your hand on your belly. Breathe in and feel it expand. Breathe out and feel it settle. Repeat six times. Your belly knows sorrow. Let it sit there. Your body is vast enough to hold grief.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward slowly. Count to four. Release. Repeat six times. Grief adds weight to your shoulders. Let them drop. They can soften.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Clench your jaw. Count to three. Release. Repeat six times. You may have wanted to say something you did not say. Release the jaw. What you meant to say is still true even unsaid.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Press your back against a wall or chair. Feel the support. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You are held. Something solid is behind you.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip slowly on one hand. Thumb to index. Middle. Ring. Pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. Your hands are still here. You are still here. This is a small surprise sometimes.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine the person at peace. Not suffering. Count to six holding this image. Open your eyes. That peace is real even though they are not. It is okay to want that for them.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest firmly. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. You are gathering yourself. You are holding your own heart.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Feel the temperature of the room on your face. Breathe in and notice it. Breathe out and notice it. Repeat four times. You are sensing the present moment. Not the past moment. The present is different.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Make a tight fist with each hand. Count to three. Release and spread fingers wide. Count to three. Repeat six times. This is the gesture of holding on and letting go. You cannot hold the person. You can hold the love. Let your hands show you both.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Press both feet down. Count to six. Release. You are still here on this earth. They are not. This is true. The ground beneath you is real.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. The nasal pathway is grounding. The mouth pathway is releasing. In through nose. Out through mouth.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Lean your upper body forward. Let your arms hang. Count to four. Rise slowly. Count to four. Repeat four times. You are bending with the weight of grief. Then standing tall. You can do both.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Tense your face, forehead, eyes, jaw. Count to four. Release completely. Repeat four times. Grief clenches your whole face. Release it. Your face can be soft even in sorrow.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Say out loud or silently: "I miss you." Let the words be true. Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. Grief is love. Missing is love. These words are a kind of prayer.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Press your heels down hard. Keep toes light. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat six times. Your feet are alive. The person's feet are not. This is the hardest truth.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Shake your entire body for ten seconds. Let yourself vibrate. Stop. Feel the settling. Grief makes you want to shake it away. You cannot. You can acknowledge it. Then settle.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Place both hands on your thighs. Press down. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your legs carry you forward. The person cannot walk anymore. You do. Move forward when you are ready. You can stop anytime.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Look in a mirror or window. See your own face. Say silently: "You are still living." Look at your own eyes. Say it again. Repeat three times. This is not a betrayal. This is continuation.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine the person's love as a light in your chest. It does not go away when they do. Count to six holding this light. Open your eyes. The light is still burning inside you.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Sit quietly for two full minutes. Do not try to feel anything. Do not try to process. Just sit. Your body knows how to grieve. It does not need permission. When the two minutes are done, stand. You have done enough today.
---
