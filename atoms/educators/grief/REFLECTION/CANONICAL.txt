

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited

What I notice is a particular quality of loss that accumulates across a career. Students who disappeared. Students whose trajectories shifted. Students who I thought would make it and did not. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as cumulative-student-loss.

The mechanism is the way that losses accumulate. Each year brings new students. Each year some of those students become students I worried about. Each year some of those students are no longer there. The mechanism has been tracking these losses for years.

What I have come to understand is that the losses are not usually dramatic. A student stops coming to school. A student drops out. A student moves schools. A student takes a different path than the one I envisioned. The individual losses are understandable. The accumulation is something else.

I have noticed that the mechanism often activates at particular moments. The moment when I realize I do not know what happened to a student. The moment when I find out that a student did not graduate. The moment when I see a student years later and they are not the person I thought they would become. The mechanism registers the loss.

What the mechanism does is create the awareness that the impact of teaching is visible in only certain directions. I see the students who succeed and feel some satisfaction. I do not see most of the students who do not succeed. But the mechanism is aware of them anyway.

What I notice is the particular way that the end of the school year can carry grief. The year is ending. The students are moving on. I will not see them again. The mechanism that activates is what I think of as cyclical-ending-grief.

The mechanism is the grief that comes with each ending. The mechanism is knowing that the relationships that have been formed are being dissolved. The mechanism is knowing that the community that has been built is being disbanded.

What I have come to understand is that this mechanism happens every year. The grief is reliable and predictable. The mechanism has learned the rhythm of the grief.

I have noticed that the mechanism often intensifies for students I am particularly close to. The students I have known for longer. The students I have invested more in. The mechanism is particularly acute for the relationships that are deepest.

What the mechanism does is make the ending of each year into a kind of death. The community dies. The relationships end. The mechanism is grieving what has passed.
