James cleans out his classroom at the end of the year. Every year, same ritual: take down the posters, file the assignments, box the supplies. Every year, the same grief. Not for any single student—for the class. The class as an organism: the dynamics, the inside jokes, the way third period finally gelled in February. The organism dies in June. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/educators/anchored/grief/grief/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
Rachel identifies teaching's structural grief: the annual cycle of attachment and release. Build relationships from September to June. Release them in June. Rebuild from September. The cycle produces a specific grief pattern—anticipatory loss starting in May, acute loss in June, numbness in July, tentative attachment again in September. The pattern repeats every year. Fourteen repetitions in Rachel's career. Each class is mourned. The mourning is invisible because the schedule continues. The schedule doesn't pause for grief. The schedule says: new students, new year, start over. The starting over is required. The grieving is not scheduled.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02

BAND: 2
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
James discovers the grief accumulates without a discharge mechanism. Teachers don't have funerals for ended classes. No ritual marks the transition. No ceremony acknowledges that a living thing—the class as a collective—ceased to exist. The absence of ritual means the grief is processed individually, silently, in empty classrooms during the week between school years. James processes alone. Every teacher he knows processes alone. The aloneness is not because they don't share the experience. The aloneness is because there's no structure for shared processing. The structure of teaching includes creation and dissolution. It does not include mourning.

Michael notices the student death grief is layered with professional guilt. Layer one: a person died. Layer two: a person he knew died. Rachel notices that grief about grief surfaces at predictable triggers — a date, a name, a place, a song. The grief is not random. It has architecture. ---
Rachel realizes the grief about grief has been compounding for years. Not one loss but an accumulation — each small grief adding to the pile because none were fully processed. The current grief is not about the current loss. It is about every loss that preceded it. Shoulders lock. Rachel sees the full machinery: each ungrieved loss adds weight to the next. The system has no outlet. Grief enters and never leaves. It accumulates in the chest, in the shoulders, in the jaw. Throat closes. The pattern persists because grieving requires time and space and both are in short supply. The unprocessed grief stays stored, and stored grief compounds into depression, and depression prevents the processing that would relieve the accumulation. The system is a grief warehouse with no exits.
