## TAKEAWAY v01
---
---
Your body was in the room the whole time. Bringing your mind back to join it is the actual job, and it's one you get to practice every single period.
---

## TAKEAWAY v02
---
---
The wandering isn't the failure. Not noticing you wandered is. Notice fast, return faster, and call that a win.
---

## TAKEAWAY v03
---
---
Presence was never a plateau you earn after enough years in the classroom. It's a return you make, period by period, for the rest of your career.
---

## TAKEAWAY v04
---
---
A parent who feels heard remembers it all year. A parent who senses you're elsewhere remembers that too — and emails you about it at 11 p.m.
---

## TAKEAWAY v05
---
---
You don't need an unbroken hour of focus grading thirty essays. You need thirty one-second returns, one per name.
---

## TAKEAWAY v06
---
---
Hallway duty isn't dead time. It's the five minutes where the real thing usually happens, if you're actually standing in it.
---

## TAKEAWAY v07
---
---
The rehearsed defense and the real conversation use the same attention. You only get to spend it once — spend it on the person actually in the chair.
---

## TAKEAWAY v08
---
---
Experienced teachers don't wander less than new ones. They just come back faster. That speed is the whole skill, and it's still trainable.
---

## TAKEAWAY v09
---
---
Ten seconds looking at an unfamiliar room is not wasted time. It's the difference between subbing on autopilot and actually teaching that class.
---

## TAKEAWAY v10
---
---
The unresolved thing at the copier line doesn't need you right now. It needs an appointment. Give it 3:15 and give the copier line back to today.
---

## TAKEAWAY v11
---
---
You can hold the whole forty-five-minute meeting in your head, or you can hold the one sentence that actually mattered. Choose the sentence.
---

## TAKEAWAY v12
---
---
The kid kneeling at the desk beside you doesn't know about your grading stack. Give them the sixty seconds they can actually feel.
---
