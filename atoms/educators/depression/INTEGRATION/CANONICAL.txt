

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Numbness is not the same as peace."
---
The flatness that settles in after years of giving—when you're teaching the lesson but watching it happen from somewhere else, when the student's news doesn't land, when the feedback that used to sting now just lands as sound—this is not resignation. It's depression. Your nervous system has learned: hoping for change exhausts you. So it stops. The watcher in the back of the room is a protective mechanism. Your body has decided: if I don't expect things to shift, disappointment can't reach me. The cost of this protection is presence. The cost is the thinning of joy. You move through the day doing what's required while something in you has gone flat. This flatness is not a character state. It's a symptom. Your nervous system is telling you: this situation has exceeded my capacity to hope about it while remaining functional.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Depression costs presence you can't afford to lose."
---
The teacher you were five years ago had more aliveness. The voice had more color. You got excited about ideas. Now you do the work. You know the material. But the energy that used to propel it is somewhere else. Or nowhere. The system counts on this. An engaged workforce is harder to exploit than a compliant one. Depression is the cost of sustained disappointment in a system that's not designed to hear you. The cost compounds silently. The kids notice when you're distant. They remember the teacher who was present less than the curriculum. The cost is paid by everyone in the room. And it stays uncounted because depression is invisible in the attendance record.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "What if flatness is telling you something."
---
The underlying narrative is: you're depressed, so something is wrong with you. But what if the flatness is appropriate response to an appropriate situation. What if your nervous system is reading accurately: years of giving without reciprocal change, watching kids suffer in systems that aren't built for them, caring about things that the institution refuses to prioritize. What if depression is not malfunction but information. What if it's your body saying: I cannot sustain hope in this condition. What if instead of fixing the depression to return to functioning in the system, the question is: what is the system doing that makes hope impossible to maintain.
