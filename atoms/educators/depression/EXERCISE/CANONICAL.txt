

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before you get out of bed, feel the sheets. Notice the texture. Count to three noticing. Place your feet on the floor. Feel the ground. Count to three. Stand. Your body is here. It made it through the night. Feel that.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand and stretch your arms up slowly. Count to four raising them. Count to four lowering them. Repeat five times. Depression keeps you small and contracted. This movement tells your body you exist in space. You take up room.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Press your feet hard into the floor. Count to six. Feel them push back. Release. Your feet are alive. The ground is solid. Count to six pressing down again. This ground is real.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Shake your hands out hard for five seconds. Stop. Shake them again. Stop. Shake them again. Three times. Your nervous system is sluggish. Shake it awake. Your hands are alive and moving.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit with your feet flat. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. Depression slows your breath. Wake it up. In for four. Out for six. Stay with this rhythm.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Cold water on your face if possible. Or cold water on your wrists and hands. Hold them under for ten counts. Feel the jolt. Your nervous system is waking. Feel the sensation on your skin.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Make tight fists. Count to three. Release and spread fingers wide. Count to three. Repeat six times. Your hands have been inactive. Activate them. Tight. Release. Tight. Release.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward five times. Then forward five times. Feel your upper body move through space. Depression keeps you still. Move. Your shoulders know how.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Stand. Place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Say silently: "I am alive." Breathe in. Breathe out. Say it again. Repeat three times. Your heart is beating. That is all you need today.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Look out a window at something far away. Notice one color. Notice one shape. Notice movement or stillness. You are connecting to the world outside your body. The world exists. You are in it.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Push them toward each other firmly. Count to five. Release. Press and count to five. Release. Repeat five times. Your chest is opening. Blood is flowing.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Tense every muscle in your body. Count to four. Release. Tense and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your muscles are responding. Your body is not broken. It can still do things.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Hum as you exhale. Breathe in silently. Hum on the exhale. Do this eight times. The vibration wakes your nervous system. Hum louder. Your voice is real.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Place your hand on your belly. Breathe in and feel it expand. Breathe out and feel it settle. Repeat six times. Your belly is moving. Your breath is flowing. You are functioning.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Stand tall. Lengthen your spine. Imagine a string pulling from the crown of your head. Count to six staying tall. Release slightly. Repeat five times. Your posture is powerful. Your spine is strong. Hold this alignment.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Clench your jaw. Count to three. Release. Repeat six times. Depression clenches you. Release it. Your jaw can soften. Your face can relax.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Take a walk, even to the mailbox, even around one room. Move your legs. Feel them work. Feel your arm swing. Your body is capable of motion.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Close your eyes. Imagine sunlight on your skin. Feel the warmth. Count to six. Open your eyes. Sun still exists. Even if you cannot feel it right now, it is there.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Touch your thumb to each fingertip on one hand. Thumb to index. Middle. Ring. Pinky. Reverse. Repeat three times. Your mind is sorting itself into small tasks. You are intact.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Press your heels down hard. Keep toes light. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat six times. Your feet are rooted. The earth holds you. You will not fall.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Shake your entire body for ten seconds. Let yourself vibrate. Stop. Feel the settling. You have moved. You have discharged something.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for five counts. Breathe out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. Different pathways activate different nervous system states. In through nose. Out through mouth. Stay with this pattern.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Place both hands on your thighs. Press them down hard. Count to four. Release. Press and count to four. Release. Repeat five times. Your legs are strong. They can carry you through today.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Make a tight fist. Count to three. Open and say the first task you must do. Make a tight fist again. Count to three. Open. Repeat this three times. You are breaking the paralysis into small moments.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Lean your upper body forward. Let your arms hang. Count to four. Rise slowly. Count to four. Repeat four times. You are moving out of heaviness. You are rising.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Look in a mirror or window. See your own face. Say silently: "I am here." Look at your own eyes. Say it again. Repeat three times. Your existence matters. You are not invisible.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Feel the temperature of the room on your skin. Notice where you feel warm. Notice where you feel cool. Your body is sensing. Your body is present. You are here in this room.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Cup your hands over your eyes without touching them. Sit for ten counts. Breathe. Depression narrows vision. Remove your hands. See what is in front of you. The world is wider than the darkness.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Press your back against a wall or chair. Feel the support behind you. Count to six. Release. Press and count to six. Release. Repeat five times. You are held. You are not falling.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Say out loud or silently: "Today I will do one thing." Just one. Feel your feet on the ground. Breathe in. Breathe out. You have survived until now. You can survive one more hour. Then one more. Then one more.
