

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Lie down on your back. Breathe in for four counts. Silently say: tomorrow's lesson is planned enough. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat eight times. The grading will be there in the morning exactly as it is now.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place one hand on your chest, one on your stomach. Breathe so the lower hand rises first. Do this for ten slow breaths. Your body needs this signal at night: the classroom is closed until morning.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat ten times. If tomorrow's worries surface, notice them and let the next exhale carry them back down.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Tense your feet and calves for four counts. Release completely. Move up to your thighs, tense for four, release. Continue through your stomach, hands, shoulders, and face. Feel your whole body settle into the mattress.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat eight times. The longer exhale is a direct signal to your nervous system that it is safe to stop reviewing the day.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let your lips part slightly. Let your tongue rest low and soft. Hold this for six slow breaths. Your jaw carries the unspoken words of the whole day. Let it release them now.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Notice the weight of your body sinking into the bed. Breathe in and feel the mattress rise slightly to meet you. Breathe out and let your legs go heavy. Repeat this noticing six times.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Count backward slowly from twenty, one number per exhale. If a thought about tomorrow interrupts, return gently to the last number you remember. There is no wrong place to restart.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart. Feel it beating slowly for ten counts. It has carried you through every difficult day so far. It will carry you through tomorrow too, whether or not you plan for it right now.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Roll your shoulders back into the mattress. Count to four. Let them settle heavier. Repeat four times. The weight you carried standing in front of the class all day can be set down here.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six, hold for two. Repeat this full cycle eight times. This pattern is not a task to complete well. It only asks you to receive it.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Clench your fists gently for a count of three. Release and let your fingers go completely loose. Repeat five times. Each release loosens the grip your mind has on tomorrow's to-do list.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Notice five sounds in the room, from loudest to quietest. Let each one arrive and pass without following it with a thought. This brings your mind back into this dark, still room.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Breathe in slowly and silently name one thing that went right today, however small. Breathe out. Repeat with three more things. Let the day close on what worked, not only on what is unfinished.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Press your feet gently against the sheets. Count to five. Release. Press again. Count to five. Repeat three times. Your body is fully supported by the bed. You do not need to hold yourself up right now.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Cup your palms loosely over your closed eyes for ten slow counts. Breathe evenly. This is a small dark room with nothing due in it until morning.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Sigh out loud, long and audible, letting your whole body soften as you do. Repeat four times. This tells your nervous system the demands of the day have actually ended.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently release one specific worry about tomorrow, picturing it setting down beside the bed. Repeat with three different worries. They will be there in the morning if you still need them.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Let your eyelids grow heavy without forcing them shut. Breathe naturally and slowly. Notice the darkness behind your closed eyes deepening with each breath. There is nowhere else you need to be.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Hum very quietly on each exhale, barely audible. Breathe in silently through your nose. Repeat this six times. The low vibration is a signal to your body that it is safe to stop being alert.
---
