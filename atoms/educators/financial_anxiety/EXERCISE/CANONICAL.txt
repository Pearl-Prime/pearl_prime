

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit with your feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts, thinking only of the air, not the number in your account. Breathe out for six counts. Repeat six times. Your worth is not the same number as your balance.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both hands flat on the desk in front of you. Press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Press again. Count to five. Release. Repeat four times. Your hands are steady even when the math is tight. Feel that steadiness now.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two. Breathe out for six. Repeat eight times. The bill will still be there after this breath. It will not get worse because you took one minute to breathe.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Roll your shoulders back slowly. Count to four. Let them drop. Repeat five times. Money worry lives high in the shoulders and the jaw. This movement is not a fix for the budget. It is relief for the body carrying it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Rest one hand on your chest, one on your stomach. Breathe so the lower hand moves more than the upper. Do this for ten breaths. You are not behind because you struggle with this. You are managing more than one salary can hold.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Clench your fists for a count of four. Release and spread your fingers wide. Repeat six times. Each release loosens the grip that scarcity puts on the whole body, not just the mind doing the math.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Stand and press your feet into the floor. Count to six. Release. Press again. Count to six. Repeat five times. The ground holds your weight whether or not the paycheck stretched far enough this month. Feel it holding you now.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Out through your mouth for seven. Repeat six times. Notice your heartbeat slowing. The spending-out-of-pocket for classroom supplies is a real cost. This breath does not erase it. It steadies you to face it.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Unclench your jaw. Let it hang slightly open. Breathe six slow breaths through this softened jaw. Financial worry sets the jaw hard without you noticing. Notice it now, and let it go.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Name three things in the room that are not related to money. Look at each one for a few seconds. Notice their color, their shape. You are allowed a minute where nothing here is a bill.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Push firmly for five counts. Release. Repeat five times. You are pushing against the feeling that you should have already figured this out by now. You are still figuring it out. That is allowed.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sit back and let the chair hold your full weight. Count to six. You do not have to hold everything up by yourself in this moment, including the numbers. Let the chair carry what it can.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Shake your hands out for ten seconds. Stop. Let them rest. Shake again for ten seconds. This is not going to solve the budget. It will loosen the tightness that thinking about the budget puts into your hands and wrists.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, silently name one thing that is stable right now, even something small. Repeat with four different stable things. Scarcity narrows your view to what is missing. This widens it back out.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Place your feet flat and press down firmly. Count to five. Release. Press again. Count to five. Repeat four times. Your body is solid and present, separate from the spreadsheet in your head.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Cup your hands over your closed eyes. Sit in the dark for ten counts, breathing slowly. This is a small space where nothing is due, nothing is owed, for just these ten counts.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Tense your whole body for four counts: legs, stomach, hands, shoulders. Release everything at once. Repeat three times. Notice how much of that tension you were carrying before you decided to release it.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Breathe in for four, hold for four, out for six. Repeat this cycle six times. You are one person managing a teacher's salary against a family's needs. This breath will not change the number. It changes how your body holds it.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Rest your hand over your heart. Feel it beating for ten counts without changing your breathing. This heart has carried you through every tight month before this one. It is still working.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Lean forward, elbows on your knees or desk, and let your head hang for a count of eight. Rise slowly. Repeat three times. You do not have to sit up straight and in control of every dollar this exact minute.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Notice the weight of your body in the chair. Breathe in and feel the seat support you. Breathe out and let your legs go heavy and still. Repeat four times. You are supported right now, in this specific minute, regardless of what the account shows.
---
