

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit at your desk before the meeting where you'll present. Place both hands flat on the table. Feel the solid surface. Say silently: I am prepared, not perfect. Breathe in for four counts. Exhale for four counts.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand in the hallway before walking into the boardroom. Roll your shoulders back. Breathe in as you lift your chin slightly. This is not arrogance. It is your body telling your mind you belong in the room you were invited into.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Recall one specific decision you made correctly this month. Not a compliment, a decision. Hold the memory for five counts. This is evidence, not a feeling.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Place a hand over your stomach. Breathe into the space beneath your hand. The tightness there is the fear of being found out. Breathe into it for six counts without trying to fix it.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Before speaking in a meeting, press your feet into the floor. Feel the floor fully supporting both feet. Your credentials are not what is holding you up right now. The floor is. Speak from there.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit and count the years of specific experience that led you to this seat, not in general terms, but a real number. Breathe out as you say the number silently. Numbers do not lie the way the inner voice does.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
In the elevator before a client meeting, breathe in for four counts, hold for four, out for four, hold for four. Box breathing lowers the internal volume of the voice that says you don't belong.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Place both hands on your thighs. Press down for five counts. Release. The chair chose to keep you. The room chose to invite you. Repeat this pressing three times as physical evidence.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Before answering a hard question, pause and breathe in fully before speaking. The pause is not a sign of not knowing. It is the sign of someone who thinks before answering.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
At your desk, write down one thing you know that most people in the building do not. Notice the confidence in that fact as you read it silently. This is not arrogance. It is fact-finding against the fraud narrative.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Stand and breathe in as you stretch your arms wide before walking into the room. Hold for three counts. Lower. This expands the chest that imposter feelings tend to collapse.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Sit and unclench your hands from your lap. Let your palms open, facing up, resting on your thighs. Feel the openness in your palms. Open hands are associated with confidence signaling, even when no one is watching.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Before a performance review, breathe in for four counts through the nose. Exhale for six counts through the mouth with a slight sigh. The longer exhale tells your body the threat is not real danger.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Place your hand on the back of your neck. Apply gentle pressure. Breathe in for four counts. This point holds tension related to being watched or evaluated. Hold for six counts, then release.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
At the end of the day, name one moment when you contributed something only you could have contributed. Notice the specific evidence as you say it. This is the fact the fraud voice omits.
---
