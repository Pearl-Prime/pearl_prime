

## PIVOT v01
---

---
The belief isn't that you're incompetent. The belief is that the proof of your competence is always provisional — one bad quarter, one exposed gap away from being revoked. The imposter feeling isn't about your skills. It's about how permanent you believe your belonging to be.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You didn't get the seat through luck. But some part of your nervous system decided the story of luck was safer than the alternative: that you belong here, and belonging here means you can lose it. The imposter narrative is a hedge against a loss that hasn't happened.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The question isn't whether you've earned the role. The question your body keeps running is whether earned is ever enough — whether some authority will override the earning and take the seat back. That authority isn't in the room. It never was.
---
