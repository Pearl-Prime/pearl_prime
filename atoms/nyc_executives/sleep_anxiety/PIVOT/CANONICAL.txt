

## PIVOT v01
---

---
The 3am wake-up isn't insomnia. It's a nervous system that spent years being rewarded for vigilance and now doesn't know how to distinguish night from threat. Sleep requires safety. Your body isn't running on your calendar — it's running on a threat clock that doesn't respect close-of-business.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
You can't think your way back to sleep because the wakefulness isn't a thought. It's a body-state — cortisol, elevated readiness, the physical preparation for something that isn't coming. The analysis of why you're awake is the same process that's keeping you up.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The crisis ended. Your body didn't get the memo because nobody sends a memo to the nervous system. It learned that waking early meant you caught things. It's still catching things. The things it's catching are scenarios you made up in the dark.
---
