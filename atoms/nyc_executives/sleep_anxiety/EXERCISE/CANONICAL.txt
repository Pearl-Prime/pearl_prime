

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
In bed, place both hands on your stomach. Breathe so your hands rise more than your chest. Count each breath up to ten. If your mind returns to work, start the count over at one without frustration.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Before turning off the light, write down one thing you are still holding from today. Close the notebook. Say silently: this is on paper now, not in me.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Lying down, tense your feet for five counts, then release. Breathe out fully with each release. Move to your calves. Tense, hold, release. Continue upward through your whole body. This is progressive relaxation for the racing mind.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
If you wake at 3am with a work thought, do not reach for your phone. Place one hand on your chest. Breathe in for four counts, out for six counts. Repeat until the thought loosens its grip, not until it disappears.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Before bed, lie flat and place a hand over each side of your ribs. Breathe in and feel your ribs expand sideways. Breathe out and feel them fall. Repeat eight times slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
In the dark, name five sounds you can hear without moving. Notice the quietest one. This grounds you in the room, not in tomorrow's calendar.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Lying in bed, breathe in for four counts, hold for four, out for four, hold for four. Box breathing at night slows your heart rate specifically. Repeat six cycles.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
If tomorrow's meeting is looping in your mind, whisper the single most important thing you need to remember for it. Breathe out as you release the rest. Once said, tell yourself: it is remembered.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Place a pillow or your hand under your lower back. Feel the support. Your bed is holding your full weight right now. You do not need to hold anything else up.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Before sleep, uncurl your toes deliberately. Notice the tension leaving your toes. Let them go fully loose. Move slowly up through your ankles.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
If your jaw is clenched in bed, place two fingers lightly on your chin. Let your jaw drop open slightly. Feel the release in your jaw. Hold for ten counts. Close gently. Repeat once.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Lying down, count your exhales only, not your inhales. Count to ten exhales. If you reach ten and are still awake, that is fine, begin again at one.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Place one hand over your heart in the dark. Feel the beat. Say silently: my heart is doing its job tonight without my help. Breathe normally and let the hand stay there.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
If the to-do list keeps surfacing, picture setting each item down one at a time onto a shelf beside the bed. Breathe out as you set each one down. You are not losing the items, only setting them down until morning.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Before closing your eyes for the last time, take one breath longer and slower than any breath you took today. Let the exhale be the longest part. This is the signal to your whole system that the day is over.
---
