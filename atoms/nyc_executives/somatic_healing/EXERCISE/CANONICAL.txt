

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit at your desk and place both hands on your thighs. Notice you have not felt your legs in hours. Press down gently and feel the muscle beneath your palms. This is your body, still here.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your eyes and scan from your scalp to your feet slowly. Do not fix anything you notice. Simply notice. This is the opposite of the constant fixing your job demands.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place one hand on your stomach. Feel it rise and fall with each breath for ten breaths. This is likely the longest you have paid attention to your own breathing today.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Stand and place your feet hip-width apart. Bend your knees slightly. Feel your weight settle into your heels. This is a body that has been sitting all day remembering how to stand.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Press your palms together in front of your chest. Push gently for five counts. Release. Notice the warmth between your palms. This warmth is generated by you, not by a device.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit and roll your wrists in slow circles, five times each direction. Feel the movement in each joint. Your wrists have been typing and swiping without acknowledgment. This is a thank-you gesture to them.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Place both hands over your ears lightly for ten seconds. Notice the quiet. Your ears have been receiving calls and notifications continuously. This is a small rest for them.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Stand and gently shake your whole body for ten seconds, arms, legs, torso. Stop. Notice the stillness that follows. This discharges stored tension your body has been holding without your knowledge.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Sit and place one hand on your heart, one on your stomach. Breathe so both hands move. Notice which hand moves more. This tells you where your breath has been living today.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Close your eyes and bring attention to your feet inside your shoes. Wiggle your toes. Your feet have been carrying your full weight all day without a single thought from you.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Place your hands on your face. Feel your own temperature. This is a body, not a function. Hold your hands there for five counts.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Stand and stretch your arms overhead, then let them fall loosely to your sides. Let the drop be complete, not controlled. Notice the difference between holding and releasing.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Sit and press your spine gently against the back of your chair. Feel the contact point by point, one vertebra at a time if you can. This is a spine that has been slightly forward all day.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Place a hand over your throat lightly. Breathe in for four counts. Your throat carries every word you speak for others. Give it one breath that is only for you.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Before leaving the office, place both feet flat on the floor and breathe in for four counts, out for six counts, three times. This is the transition of your body leaving work mode, not just your calendar.
---
