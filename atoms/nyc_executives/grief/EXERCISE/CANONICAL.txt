

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit in your chair. You do not have to be productive right now. Place both feet on the floor. Feel the chair holding you. The chair is doing the work. You are being held. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat five times. You can rest in the grief.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Close your office door or bathroom door. You are alone. Sit. You do not have to perform anything right now. Close your eyes. Feel the isolation. The isolation is safe. In this isolation, you do not have to pretend. Sit for six counts. You can be here without pretending.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place both hands on your face. Feel your own skin. This is your face. This face is grieving. This face is allowed to grieve. Hold your hands there for six counts. Breathe in and feel held. Breathe out. Remove your hands. You have held yourself.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit and allow your shoulders to drop forward. Let them hang heavy. Do not straighten them. Hold this posture for six counts. This is the posture of grief. Grief makes you heavy. The weight is real. Breathe into the heaviness. Straighten slightly. Repeat three times. Grief is physical.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two counts. Breathe out for six counts. Hold for two counts. The long exhale is a sigh. Let the sigh come. Repeat five times. The sigh is allowed.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Place one hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is broken. Your heart is still beating. That is not a contradiction. Feel the heartbeat. It keeps going even in grief. Repeat three times. Your heart is broken and beating.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Walk slowly through your office or home. Do not rush. Feel each footfall. One foot. Then the other. Feel the slowness. Grief is slow. Grief is not efficient. Let yourself be slow. Walk for one minute. Slowness is allowed.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Sit and place both feet on the floor. Press your feet down for six counts. Your feet are holding you. You do not have to hold yourself together. Your feet will hold you. Release. Repeat five times. You are being held by the ground.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Close your eyes. Scan your body for the grief. Where do you feel it. Your chest. Your stomach. Your throat. Your legs. Find where the grief lives. Place your hand there. Hold your hand there. Breathe into that place for four counts. You are acknowledging the grief's location.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Hum for the entire exhale. Breathe in in silence. Hum out. The humming is a sound. You are not silent. You are grieving out loud in this small way. Repeat six times. Your grief has a sound.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Sit and allow your body to shake. Do not suppress it. If tears come, let them. Do not wipe them immediately. Feel them. Feel your body moving with emotion. This is grief moving through you. This is allowed. Sit for six counts. You can feel this fully.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. As you hold, picture the person or loss. See them. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat four times. You are allowing yourself to think about what you lost. You are permitted to think about it.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Place your hands in your lap. Open your hands completely. Fingers spread. Let them be vulnerable and open. Hold for six counts. Open hands mean open grief. Open hands mean you are not fighting the feeling. Close your hands slightly. Repeat three times. Open and close. This is the rhythm of grief.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Stand and place your back against a wall. Feel the wall supporting you. You do not have to stand alone. The wall is holding you. Breathe in and feel held. Breathe out. Repeat five times. You are being supported in your grief.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Close your eyes. Listen to the silence. The silence is safe. The silence is where grief lives. The silence will not hurt you. Listen for one full minute. The silence is your companion.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Sit and place both hands on your thighs. Press down for five counts. Feel the weight of your body. Your body is real. Your body is still here. The loss is real. Your body is still here. Repeat three times. Both are true.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for six counts. The long hold on the exhale is rest. Grief is exhausting. Rest is permitted. Repeat five times. You can rest in your grief.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Place one hand on your belly. Feel it rise and fall as you breathe. Your belly is breathing. You are still alive. Your body is still alive. This is not abandonment. This is continuation. Feel your belly breathing five times. You continue.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Close your eyes. Name one memory of what you lost. One specific moment. Say it internally. Stay with that memory for eight counts. Memories are safe. Memories are where the lost thing still lives in your body. You can live in memory.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Sit and tense your entire body. Make yourself small. Hold for five counts. Release and expand slightly. You can make yourself small in grief. You can take up less space. You can also expand. Repeat three times. You have control over your shape in grief.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Breathe in for four counts. The pressure is containment. You are contained. You will not fall apart. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat five times. Containment is allowed. You can be whole while grieving.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
Walk to a window or go outside if possible. Look at something living. A tree. A plant. A bird. The sky. Something that is separate from your grief. Look at it for one minute. The world continues. The world is still here.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Imagine breathing out the heaviest part of the grief. Just the heaviest part. Breathe in again. Repeat five times. Each exhale releases a small piece. You do not have to release it all at once.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Sit and place your feet flat on the floor. Alternate pressing each foot for five counts. Left foot. Right foot. Left foot. Right foot. You are still moving. You are still moving through the world. Movement is allowed in grief.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Place both hands on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is grieving. Your heart is still beating. Your heart is not broken in the way that stops it. Your heart continues. Feel it beat five more times. Your heart is strong.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Close your eyes. Hold your eyes closed for ten counts. The darkness is safe. Grief has a darkness. The darkness will not harm you. Open your eyes. You have survived the darkness. Repeat twice. Darkness is part of grief.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Sit and allow your head to drop forward toward your chest. Feel your neck releasing. Do not force it. Let gravity pull it down. Hold for six counts. Lift your head. The drop and lift is the movement of sorrow. Repeat three times. Sorrow has a rhythm.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Clench your fists at your sides. Hold for five counts. Release and let your hands hang. Repeat five times. Clenching is holding on. Releasing is letting go. You can do both. You can clench and release in grief.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for eight counts. The long hold is stillness. Stillness is permitted in grief. The long hold honors what you lost. Repeat four times. Your stillness honors the loss.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Place both feet on the floor. Place both hands on your lap. Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for four counts. You are alive. The loss is real. Both are true. You are here. You will survive this. Repeat three times. You will survive because you are here. You are in your body. Your body will carry you through.
---
