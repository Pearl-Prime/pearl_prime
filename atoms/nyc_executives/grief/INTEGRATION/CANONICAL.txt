

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your body carries the loss in silence."
---
The funeral was on Thursday. The board meeting was Friday morning. You walked from the service to the office in the same suit. The voice in the meeting didn't break. The competence didn't crack. The loss lived inside while the performance happened outside. The body holds this bifurcation. The grief compressed into the chest. The throat that doesn't expand. The eyes that remain dry in the room and fill with tears in the car. The body knows the cost of maintaining composure while inside you're being unmade. Notice where you're holding it—the shoulders that won't release, the jaw that stays locked, the breath that stays high and shallow. The body is a monument to the grief that the role won't let you speak. This is not stoicism. This is the nervous system learning to contain what can't be expressed in the professional space.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "The grief happens in the margins nobody sees."
---
The wake on Tuesday. The will reading Thursday. The estate management that will take years. These fit around the schedule. The client presentation Monday—that doesn't move. The quarterly close Friday—that doesn't move. The grief gets the time no one notices. The lunch you skip to cry in the bathroom. The early morning that you use to collapse before the day requires competence. The weekend that you spend managing the family logistics that death requires. The cost is paid in the exhaustion of maintaining two calendars: the one everyone sees and the one where you're falling apart. The cost is the isolation of being the executive who's grieving. The person everyone depends on doesn't get to not be okay. The cost is paid in the silence.

The grief becomes something you carry while doing everything else. The familiar and the absence coexist now.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Who gets to mourn while maintaining the façade."
---
The rules of the professional space are clear: show up, perform, maintain. The rules of grief are clear: feel everything, express the loss, let it break you. These exist in complete contradiction. The people around you don't know which version of you they'll get. The version that's intact enough to make decisions. The version that can't. Most of the time you show them the intact one. The cost is the disconnect between who you are and who the system allows you to be. The question isn't whether you should grieve. The question is which self gets to grieve.
