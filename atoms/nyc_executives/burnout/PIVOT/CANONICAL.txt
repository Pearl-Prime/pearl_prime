

## PIVOT v01
---

---
The exhaustion isn't from working too much. It's from working without ever letting the body believe the output was enough. The fuel ran out because it was never replenished — it was just spent faster.
---

## PIVOT v02
---

---
Burnout doesn't announce itself. It lands as the absence of something: the absence of satisfaction, of hunger, of the forward pull that used to be automatic. Your body stopped generating that pull because it stopped believing the destination was safe.
---

## PIVOT v03
---

---
The compensation made burnout feel like a personal failure. If you're earning this much, the body shouldn't be this empty. But the nervous system doesn't negotiate with compensation — it responds to cost, and the cost was continuous.
---
