

## PERMISSION v01
---

---
You are allowed to stop performing wellness while still depleted. The title doesn't require you to be fine.
---

## PERMISSION v02
---

---
You are allowed to acknowledge that the adrenaline kept you running and the bill has now arrived. That is not weakness. That is biology.
---

## PERMISSION v03
---

---
You are allowed to need more than a weekend to recover from a decade of sustained performance output.
---

## PERMISSION v04
---

---
You are allowed to feel nothing where the drive used to be. Numbness is not permanent. It is a symptom.
---

## PERMISSION v05
---

---
You are allowed to step back before the body forces the decision for you. The board presentation that stopped you mid-sentence is not who you have to become.
---

## PERMISSION v06
---

---
You are allowed to find the work that once electrified you now feel flat. That shift is information, not a character failure.
---

## PERMISSION v07
---

---
You are allowed to rest even though the rest feels like you are losing ground. The ground you are losing is already gone.
---

## PERMISSION v08
---

---
You are allowed to admit that you have been compensating — with caffeine, with drive, with the story that you thrive under pressure — and that the compensation is no longer working.
---

## PERMISSION v09
---

---
You are allowed to grieve the version of yourself who could run this way. Something has changed. The grief is appropriate.
---

## PERMISSION v10
---

---
You are allowed to leave before the ambulance call. Burnout does not improve with more of what caused it.
---

## PERMISSION v11
---

---
You are allowed to redefine what performance looks like for a body that has been running at maximum output for years without recovery.
---

## PERMISSION v12
---

---
You are allowed to want something different without knowing yet what it is.
---
