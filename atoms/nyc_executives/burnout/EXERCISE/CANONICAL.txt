

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit at your desk. Close your eyes. Notice how heavy your eyelids feel. Do not fight the heaviness. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for two counts. Breathe out for six counts. The long exhale signals your body it can stop producing for a moment. Repeat five times. You are allowed to feel tired.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand before your next meeting. Roll your shoulders back three times slowly. Feel the stiffness release with each roll. Your shoulders have been carrying tension since your first meeting this morning. Let them drop an inch lower than they were. Notice the difference.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Place both hands flat on your desk. Press down and hold for five counts. Release. Your hands have been typing, gripping a phone, holding a pen for hours without rest. This press is a pause. Repeat three times.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Between calls, walk to the window. Look at something at least fifty feet away. Hold your gaze there for ten counts. Notice the detail furthest from you. Your eyes have been fixed on screens all day. This is a reset for your eyes and your nervous system together.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Sit back in your chair fully. Let your spine touch the backrest. Feel the chair fully supporting your back. You have been sitting at the edge of the seat, ready to move. Let the chair hold your full weight for ten counts. You do not have to be ready right now.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Before your next call, place one hand on your chest and one on your stomach. Breathe so the stomach hand rises more than the chest hand. This is diaphragmatic breathing. Repeat six breaths. Your body has been breathing shallow all day.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
In the elevator, close your eyes for the ride. Count each floor as it passes through your other senses, the small drop in your stomach, the ding, the shift in pressure. This is thirty seconds that belongs to no one else.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
At your desk, unclench your jaw deliberately. Let your tongue rest away from the roof of your mouth. Let your teeth separate slightly. Hold this open, unclenched position for ten counts. Burnout lives in the jaw as much as anywhere.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Stand and stretch both arms overhead. Breathe in as you reach as high as you can without straining. Hold for four counts. Lower slowly. Repeat three times. This is the first time your arms have gone above your shoulders today.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Before you answer the next email, place your feet flat on the floor. Press down through both heels. Count to five. You are allowed to finish this exhale before you start the next task.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
In the bathroom, run cool water over your wrists for fifteen seconds. Feel the temperature travel up your arms. This interrupts the flat, wired feeling burnout produces. Dry your hands slowly.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
At the end of a long call, before opening the next tab, close your eyes for three breaths. In for four. Out for four. Do not check anything during these three breaths. This is the transition your nervous system rarely gets.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Sit and let your hands go completely limp in your lap. Feel the absence of gripping. Your hands do not need to hold anything right now. Hold this looseness for ten counts.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Before your commute home, sit in the car or on the train and place both feet flat on the floor. Breathe in for four counts. Exhale for four counts. Repeat until the vehicle starts moving. You are marking the boundary between office and home.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Lean your head back against your chair for two minutes. Let your eyes close. Set no timer in your mind. Two minutes of doing nothing is not indulgence. It is recovery your body has been asking for all day.
---
