

## PIVOT v01

---
The competence isn't the same as safety. You can perform in rooms where your nervous system is running threat. What you can't do is be in rooms — present, loose, actually there. The performance keeps you upright. It doesn't keep you present.

## PIVOT v02
---

---
The social anxiety didn't disappear when the title arrived. It learned to dress better. The chest-tightening before the partner dinner is wearing Armani now but it's the same calculation it ran in the school cafeteria: where do I stand, who's watching, and what does it cost if I get this wrong.

## PIVOT v03

---
You read a room for status signals faster than anyone else at the table. That's not a skill you learned — it's a nervous system that decided social environments were assessment environments a long time ago. The title changed the environment. The nervous system didn't get the briefing.
