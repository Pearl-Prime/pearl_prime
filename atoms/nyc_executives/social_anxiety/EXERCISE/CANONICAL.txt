

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Before entering the networking reception, stand in the hallway. Place both feet shoulder-width apart. Breathe in for four counts. Exhale for four counts. Notice the floor holding you before you walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
In the elevator before a client dinner, press your palms together in front of you for five counts. Feel the pressure between your palms. Release. This discreet pressure grounds you before you have to make conversation.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Before speaking up in a large meeting, take one breath in through your nose. Let your exhale be slightly longer. This single breath is enough to steady your voice before the first word.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Standing at a reception, hold your drink or a glass with both hands briefly. Notice the coolness of the glass. The grip gives your hands somewhere to be, which frees your attention for the conversation instead of your fidgeting.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Before walking into the boardroom, breathe in as your shoulders roll back once. Let your arms hang naturally at your sides rather than crossed. Open posture changes how your own nervous system reads the room.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
In a group conversation, choose one person to make eye contact with for a few seconds at a time rather than scanning the whole group. Breathe out between exchanges. This reduces the sense of being watched by everyone at once.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Before a presentation, press your feet flatly into the floor and count the pressure under each foot, left heel, left toes, right heel, right toes. Feel the floor under each point. Four points of contact, fully grounded.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
If you feel your face flush in a meeting, place one hand briefly on the back of your neck under the table. Breathe in for four counts. The gesture is invisible; the calming effect is not.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Before a client call where you'll be evaluated, breathe in for four counts, hold for four, exhale for four, hold for four. Complete three cycles before you pick up the phone.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
At a networking event, give yourself permission to leave a conversation after a natural pause. Breathe out as you step away. You do not owe every interaction more time than feels sustainable.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Before walking into a room full of senior executives, name one true fact about why you are there. Breathe in as you hold it silently. This is your anchor if the room starts to feel like a test.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
If your hands are shaking before you speak, press your fingertips together lightly, one hand against the other, for five counts. Feel the contact steady your hands. This is subtle enough to do while holding notes.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
In a Q&A you're nervous about, breathe in fully before answering each question. The room reads the pause as thoughtfulness, not hesitation.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Before a large all-hands presentation, stand backstage or in a private space and shake out both hands for six counts. Stop. Notice the stillness. This clears excess adrenaline before you're seen.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
After a socially demanding event, sit in your car or a quiet stairwell for two minutes. Breathe in for four counts, out for six counts. This is recovery time your nervous system needs after being watched.
---
