Walking into the conference room where the seats are already taken. Elliott walks into the room where the absence lives loudest. Sweat breaks along his hairline. His shirt collar dampens. For months the frame has held: letting go means forgetting what mattered. Now Elliott watches the frame buckle under evidence it cannot metabolize.
