

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The alarm system cannot distinguish between physical threat and social threat. A missed quarterly target produces the same physiological response as a predator at the cave mouth.
The mechanism is the threat-type confusion. The nervous system was designed for acute physical danger, not abstract performance outcomes. Yet it treats them the same way.
High-performers learn to function while the alarm is running. They present steadily while burning internally. This split awareness works. But the alarm never stops. Resources meant for recovery are spent on mobilization.
What I have come to understand is that this creates a person who performs well but never actually rests.
