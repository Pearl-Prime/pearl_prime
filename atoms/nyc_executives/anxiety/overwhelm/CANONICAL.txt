Sunday night. David looks at the week: three client meetings, two board calls, the quarterly review, the team offsite planning, seventeen emails flagged urgent. Each item is manageable. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01
---
path: story_atoms/nyc_executives/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v01.txt
BAND: 2
---
The pile grows faster than it shrinks. David closes three deals; four more open. He clears the inbox; it refills while he's clearing. The ratio never shifts. More comes in than goes out. The working isn't inefficient. The work is infinite.
---

## MECHANISM_PROOF v02
---
path: story_atoms/nyc_executives/anchored/anxiety/overwhelm/mechanism_proof/micro/v02.txt
BAND: 3
---
Everything costs the same. Sarah notices: one email costs what ten used to. One call depletes what a whole day of calls used to. The capacity hasn't changed. The demands have. Or her reserves have emptied without refilling. The math doesn't work anymore.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v03

The easy things are the hardest. Michael can handle a crisis—adrenaline handles crises. But returning a routine call? Scheduling the standard meeting? Those require initiative from somewhere depleted. He can close a billion-dollar deal and can't reply to "Sounds good, let's connect."
