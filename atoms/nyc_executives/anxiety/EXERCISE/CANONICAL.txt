

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit back from your screen. Place both feet flat on the floor. Feel your heels make contact. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for four counts. That is one cycle. Do not rush the holds. Keep the counts even. Complete four cycles. On the fourth exhale, let your shoulders drop before you breathe in again. Return to the screen after the fourth cycle.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand in front of your desk. Place your feet shoulder-width apart. Feel the floor beneath each foot. Clench both fists tightly for six counts. Notice the tension across your hands. Relax your fists completely on the exhale. Shake out both hands for four counts. Let the tension fall to the ground. Repeat this cycle five times. Clench and hold for six. Shake and release immediately after. You are grounding the alarm through your hands.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Sit in your chair. Close your eyes. Scan your face from forehead to jaw. Notice where you hold tension. Place one finger on your temples. Apply light pressure and breathe in. Hold the pressure for four counts. Release on the exhale. Move to your jaw hinges. Apply the same pressure. Breathe in for four counts. Hold. Exhale and release. Repeat this sequence three times. Pressure and breath anchor the nervous system.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit at your desk. Turn to face the window. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for six counts. Breathe out for eight counts. Hold for four counts. This is extended exhale breathing. The long exhale signals safety to your body. Do not count faster than feels natural. Complete five cycles. Rest between each cycle. On the final exhale, open your eyes and notice three things you can see outside.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Stand and place your hands on your desk. Press your palms down firmly. Keep your arms straight. Breathe in as you press. Hold the pressure for five counts. Exhale and lift. Rest your hands. Repeat eight times. Let the pressure create a physical anchor. The desk is solid. Your hands are pressing into something stable. Each press signals that you can push back against the alarm.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Sit with both feet on the floor. Lift your right foot slightly. Press your toes into the ground. Feel the pressure under your toes. Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, release the pressure. Lift the left foot. Press those toes down. Feel the difference. Breathe in for four counts. Exhale and release. Alternate feet eight times. End with both feet pressing down together. This is single-leg grounding.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Walk to the bathroom or closest private space. Splash cold water on your wrists. Do not splash your face. Hold your wrists under cold water. Count to ten slowly. The cold water interrupts the alarm loop. Feel the temperature change. Dry your wrists. Notice the difference in your heartbeat. Return to your space. You have interrupted the physical cascade.
---

---

Repeat this ten times. Clench and hold for three. Relax and hold for three. The jaw is where anxiety lives in your neck. You are teaching it to let go.

---

Your heartbeat does not need to change. You are noticing it without judgment. The hand on your chest is a pressure point. It anchors your attention to something real.

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Sit in your car or at your desk. Close your eyes. Listen for three distinct sounds. Identify the first sound. Breathe in. Identify the second sound. Breathe out. Identify the third sound. Breathe in. Open your eyes. You have anchored to the present moment through sound. This is sensory grounding. The alarm is about what might happen. Sound brings you to what is happening.

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Before the meeting, stand and tense every muscle. Start at your forehead. Squeeze all the muscles in your face. Hold for four counts. Release. Move to your shoulders. Lift them toward your ears. Hold for four counts. Release. Move to your arms, chest, core, legs. Tense each section for four counts. Release completely. Finish with your toes. This is a full-body tension release. Progressive muscle tension teaches your body the difference between activation and rest.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Stand. Feel your feet on the floor. Raise your arms out to your sides. Hold for three counts. Lower your arms. That is one rep. Do eight reps slowly. As you raise, breathe in. As you lower, breathe out. Your body is capable of movement. Movement is evidence of safety.

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Sit. Close your eyes. Count every muscle relaxation deliberately. Start at your toes. Relax them. Count one. Move to your ankles. Relax. Count two. Continue to your shins, knees, thighs, hips. Count each region. Continue through your torso, back, arms, hands, neck, jaw, face. Do not rush. Count thirty distinct relaxations. You are mapping your body as safe.

---
