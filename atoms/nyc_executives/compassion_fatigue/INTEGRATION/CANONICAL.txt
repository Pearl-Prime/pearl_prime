

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "The caretaker's body holds what it doesn't speak."
---
You're holding it. The anxiety of the CFO who's terrified of the audit. The grief of the associate who lost a parent mid-trial. The fear of the junior partner who doesn't know if they'll make it through the evaluation cycle. You absorb these without naming it. You hold the room steady while they process. Your voice stays calm. Your presence becomes their stability. The body registers this work. Not as stress, but as weight. As a low-grade heaviness that lives in the chest. As the fatigue that accumulates from emotional labor that doesn't appear on any worksheet. The body knows it's holding something that isn't yours. Notice where the weight sits. This is not pain. This is the evidence of what you've been carrying.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your tank empties in silence, unrecorded."
---
Nobody tracks the emotional labor. The mentoring of the junior partner who's struggling with the work-life balance you never solved. The client dinner where you listened to their marriage dissolve while eating the appetizers. The emergency call from the team member who panicked over a feedback comment. The feedback conversation with the person who's about to be laid off. The energy it costs to remain steady, resourced, present—that energy comes from somewhere. It comes from your tank. The tank doesn't refill through normal work. It doesn't refill through achievement or compensation. It refills through rest and reciprocal emotional support and being held the way you hold others. Most leaders in your position never receive that. The cost accumulates in the depletion that no conference or vacation fixes.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Still here. Before the weight."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You remember who you were before you became the steady one. Before you learned to absorb other people's anxiety without showing it. Before you taught yourself to be the container that held the space. You were more porous then. You could be affected. You could be uncertain. You could ask for support. The role taught you to stop doing those things. The role required you to be the calm in the storm. Year after year, you became more capable at this. Year after year, you became more alone in it. You are still here. The person you were before is still underneath the person you became. The steadiness is a real skill. It's not the entire you.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Who holds the person who holds everyone."
---
You know the answer to every question. You have the resources. You have the experience. You have the perspective that makes the problem smaller. The question isn't whether you can hold the space for other people's emergencies. The question is who holds the space for yours. The emergencies you don't name. The fear you don't voice. The moments when you need someone to tell you it will be okay, and you need to believe them, not calculate their accuracy. The question isn't whether you're equipped to carry others. The question is whether you've allowed anyone to carry you.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Your mentor runs the same pattern."
---
The partner who taught you everything. Who made it look effortless. Who seemed to have unlimited capacity. Who could hold everyone's crisis and still close the deal. You learned from them. You absorbed their steadiness as a model. What you didn't see then: the solitude of that role. The isolation of being the one everyone needs but no one supports. The way their marriage deteriorated because their partner couldn't access them. The way their body began to give out in the late years—the high blood pressure, the insomnia, the doctor visits they never mentioned. They're running the same pattern. They learned it before you did.
---

## INTEGRATION v06
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: interrupt_landing
carry_line: "Your shoulders release when you ask for help."
note: interrupt_landing — use after The Interrupt story, quietest atom in the book
---
The heaviness shifts. The chest opens. The constant vigilance for other people's needs pauses. You say it: I need something. I'm tired. I can't hold this alone. The response matters less than the act. The act of asking is the moment the weight becomes shared. The body knows the difference between bearing and being borne. One collapses you. One allows you to stand.
---

## INTEGRATION v07
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: heavy
carry_line: "The cost is measured in self."
---
The caretaker loses track of their own needs. The voice that would have opinions disappears. The preferences become unclear. The desires fade. What remains is the reflexive response to what others need. The cost is paid in the dissolution of the boundary between self and role. In the slow forgetting of what you want when there's no one else to hold. In the numbing of your own alarms because you've learned to hear only others'. The cost is your own presence in your own life.
---
