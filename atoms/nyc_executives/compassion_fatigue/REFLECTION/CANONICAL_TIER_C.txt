

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The mechanism I notice is the asymmetrical attentional drain. I hold space for others' uncertainty. I absorb their concerns. I give my problem-solving energy. They receive my attention; I receive periodic acknowledgment.
Over weeks, the output exceeds the input. The capacity to genuinely engage thins. I become mechanical. The work continues but something real has thinned.
The asymmetry is not about time spent. It is about the direction of emotional resource flow. Input and output operate on different schedules entirely.

mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
I have learned to distinguish between short-term depletion and chronic depletion. Short-term is recoverable. I rest; I return to engagement.
Chronic is different. It emerges from sustained imbalance. Year after year of giving more than receiving. The system does not recover because the condition does not change.
This is the mechanism of unsustainable rhythm institutionalization. I adapted to it. Became efficient. But the adaptation became the problem.
I function so well while depleted that the structure never experiences pressure to change.

Depletion is not primarily about how much I do. I have known executives working sixty hours per week who are less depleted than those working forty.

This is the mechanism of directionality in human resource exchange. More time off cannot fix what the structure created.
