

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your worth collapses when the revenue softens."
---
The quarter missed targets. Not by much. Not catastrophically. Just missed. The voice in meetings is smaller. The confidence retreats. The sense of yourself as competent becomes uncertain. You move through the day the same way. You make the same decisions. Your experience hasn't changed. Your judgment hasn't changed. What changed is the external metric. And your sense of who you are shifted with it. This is what it means to have worth that's tethered to output. The body knows this dynamic. The chest that was expansive becomes compressed. The voice that was sure becomes tentative. The stakes were never just about the revenue. The stakes were about the self that the revenue proved existed. When the metric shifts, the self threatens to disappear.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Every achievement is a temporary reprieve from worthlessness."
---
The deal closed and you felt real for a moment. The partnership came through and you felt solid. The performance ratings put you in the top quartile and you felt safe. The relief is always temporary. The achievement gets absorbed into the baseline. The next metric becomes the test. The goal posts move. The reprieve expires. The cost is the endless cycle of achievement and then immediate dread about the next achievement. The cost is the sense that you're only as good as the last deal. The cost is the inability to accumulate worth in any stable way. The achievements don't build a foundation. They're temporary anesthetics for the underlying sense of insufficiency.

weight: light
carry_line: "Still here. The self beneath the metrics."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You were worthy before you made partner. You were a whole person before the title. You had value before the revenue. You knew this once. The person you were before the fusion had opinions that didn't serve career advancement. Had interests that didn't strengthen the brand. Had a self that wasn't entirely devoted to the next achievement. That version feels foreign now. That version feels soft. That version feels insufficient. But that version was you. You are still here. The metrics have changed. The revenue has fluctuated. The titles have arrived and can be lost. What remains constant is the person underneath the performance. You've spent so long building the persona that you've forgotten the person is still there.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Who would you be if the metrics disappeared."
---
Not hypothetically. Concretely. If you woke tomorrow and the partnership was over. The compensation was gone. The reputation was erased. The status was removed. What would remain. What would you want to be. What would matter. The question isn't whether this is likely. The question is whether you know the answer. Because if you don't know who you are without the metrics, you're not safe. Your entire worth is attached to things that can be lost. You don't need the metrics to disappear to understand this. You just need to know what happens inside you if they do.
