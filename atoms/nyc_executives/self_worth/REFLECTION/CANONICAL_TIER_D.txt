

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: D

I have come to understand that there is a particular version of self that emerges when you are not being observed. When there is no audience. When the performance can stop. This self is different from the self that exists in the role. The self in the role is constructed. It is built from the title, the achievements, the recognition. It is the self that the external world sees. The person built a self on a foundation that moves.

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: D

There is a particular vulnerability in people whose sense of self is built primarily around achievement and performance. When achievement is possible and performance is valued, the identity holds. The person knows who they are. But when achievement is not possible or performance is not valued, the identity becomes uncertain. The person is not sure who they are without the achievement. The person is not sure what their worth is when the performance is not happening.

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: D

There is a particular way that comparison can corrode self-worth. In a high-performance context, there are always people who are doing more, achieving more, earning more. The comparison is constant and specific. The person might be doing well by objective standards. But by the standards of the comparison group, the person might be behind. The mechanism is the comparison-worth calibration. The sense of self-worth is not being measured against absolute standards. It is being measured against the performance of peers.
