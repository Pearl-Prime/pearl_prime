

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: C

I have come to understand that there is a particular version of self that emerges when you are not being observed. When there is no audience. When the performance can stop. This self is different from the self that exists in the role. The self in the role is constructed. It is built from the title, the achievements, the recognition. It is the self that the external world sees. But the self that emerges when no one is watching is different. It is quieter. It is less certain. It is not as sure of its own worth. The mechanism is the performance-dependent identity construction. Over time, the identity becomes built around the performance. The achievements. The recognition. The audience.

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: C

There is a particular vulnerability in people whose sense of self is built primarily around achievement and performance. When achievement is possible and performance is valued, the identity holds. The person knows who they are. But when achievement is not possible or performance is not valued, the identity becomes uncertain. The person is not sure who they are without the achievement. The person is not sure what their worth is when the performance is not happening. The mechanism is the identity-foundation specificity. The identity has been built on a specific foundation: achievement and performance. If that foundation is not supporting, the identity destabilizes. If the conditions change, the identity becomes unstable.

## REFLECTION v05
---
family: F5
voice_mode: narrator
mechanism_emphasis: invisible
tier_variant: C

There is a particular way that comparison can corrode self-worth. In a high-performance context, there are always people who are doing more, achieving more, earning more. The comparison is constant and specific. The person might be doing well by objective standards. But by the standards of the comparison group, the person might be behind. The mechanism is the comparison-worth calibration. The sense of self-worth is not being measured against absolute standards. It is being measured against the performance of peers. As long as the person is comparing favorably, the worth is secure. When the comparison becomes unfavorable, the worth is threatened. I have felt this most acutely in moments when a peer achieved something significant.
