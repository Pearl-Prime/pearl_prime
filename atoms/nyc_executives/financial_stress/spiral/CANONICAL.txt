He's lying in bed at night wanting to open the spreadsheet. He doesn't. He sits with the anxiety instead. The anxiety is real. The anxiety is also not the truth. The spiral started as a reasonable concern — wanting to understand his financial position. The spiral has become a compulsion. The compulsion is no longer serving him. He's beginning to break the compulsion. The breaking is painful. The breaking is necessary. The freedom from the spiral is on the other side of the pain. He can't see it yet. But he knows it's there.
