

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The paradox-of-ascent mechanism: as income increases, financial anxiety increases. Lifestyle expands to match compensation. The baseline rises. Anything below feels like deprivation. The result: the executive spends what they earn. The safety margin does not increase with income. They are always one reduction away from feeling failure. The anxiety does not decrease. It scales up with the income. The paradox is that more money creates more anxiety, not less.
---

## REFLECTION v02
---
family: F2
voice_mode: companion
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The identity-income coupling mechanism: the executive has built identity around earning. The external validation of high income has become essential to sense of self. When income is threatened, identity is threatened. The person is not just losing money. They are losing the evidence they are the person they understand themselves to be. If the income ends, the validation ends. Without external validation, the internal sense of identity becomes uncertain.
---

## REFLECTION v03
---
family: F3
voice_mode: mirror
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
The assumption-collapse mechanism: when the executive has had high income for a long time, certain assumptions become invisible. The assumption that they can afford things. The assumption that financial limitations do not apply. These assumptions are so embedded they become transparent. Then something changes. The income is threatened. Suddenly the assumptions are visible. They are revealed as assumptions, not facts. The loss of the assumptions is almost as disorienting as the financial loss itself.
---

## REFLECTION v04
---
family: F4
voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
The proportional-adequacy assessment mechanism: the executive assesses whether their income is adequate to sustain their lifestyle. This assessment is about the relationship between income and lifestyle. As long as the assessment is positive, there is security. When it becomes uncertain, anxiety emerges. The anxiety is not irrational. It is generated by awareness that the current lifestyle requires a specific level of income. If income decreases, lifestyle has to change.
---

---
The reference-point elevation mechanism: when earning high income, the comparison point is not average income. It is the income of peers. There are always people earning more. This creates financial anxiety that is not about absolute need. It is about relative status. The sense of financial adequacy is not based on what is needed. It is based on what peers have. The comparison is always available. The reference point is always moving.
