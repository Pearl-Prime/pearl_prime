

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your chest tightens at the number."
---
Seven figures on the screen. The mortgage. The tuition. The maintenance of the life you've built. The number that should feel manageable given your income. The number that should feel safe. But the body tightens. The jaw clenches. The breath gets smaller. The financial alarm fires regardless of the actual math. The income covers it. The assets support it. The spreadsheet says it's fine. But the body runs a different calculation. The body runs the calculation of "what if." What if the income stops. What if the economy shifts. What if the firm's stability changes. What if the deal flow reverses. The body doesn't trust the spreadsheet because the body remembers when the ground shifted before. The body carries the memory of financial fragility even when the spreadsheet says otherwise. The tightness is the nervous system holding multiple financial futures at once.
---

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Wealth anxiety doesn't quiet with balance."
---
The fear should be gone by now. The savings account is substantial. The investments are diversified. The income is more than sufficient. The net worth has hit numbers you couldn't have imagined ten years ago. And the anxiety persists. The fear of losing it persists. The sense that it's not enough persists. The cost of having built something is the constant fear of losing it. The cost is the vigilance. The cost is checking the portfolio obsessively. The cost is the conversations about market risk that interrupt every meal. The cost is the inability to feel secure because security has been defined as "never having to worry again," which is impossible. The wealthy carry a particular anxiety: the terror of falling. The distance to fall is far. The cost is paid in the exhaustion of keeping up with an income that never actually silences the alarm.

## INTEGRATION v03
---
mode: STILL_HERE
reframe_type: TIME_SHIFT
weight: light
carry_line: "Still here. The crisis that didn't come."
note: STILL-HERE mode — final chapter only, one per book
---
You remember the year when the firm lost a major client. The year when the deal fell through at the last moment. The year when the market correction hit and the portfolio dropped thirty percent. The fear was absolute. The calculations started about what you'd have to give up. The schools. The apartment. The status. The summer place. The identity that the income supported. And you survived it. The income didn't disappear. The market recovered. The firm found new clients. The crisis was real for a moment. And then it passed. You are still here. The life you built remained intact. Not because the danger wasn't real. Because you were more resilient than the fear suggested.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Who are you without the metrics."
---
The numbers in the spreadsheet are substantial. The income is reliable. The financial position is secure. Imagine what happens if all of it disappears tomorrow. Not to panic you. To clarify. To see what you're actually protecting. The life. The identity. The proof that you've made it. The self-worth fused to the numbers. These reveal where the fear actually lives. The fear isn't always about survival. Sometimes it's about what losing the money means about who you are. The real question lives in that gap between the financial position and the internal sense of worth.
---

## INTEGRATION v05
---
mode: SOMEONE_ELSE
reframe_type: OTHER_PERSON
weight: heavy
carry_line: "Your colleague's anxiety mirrors yours exactly."
---
The one who made partner the same year you did. The portfolio that's similar to yours. The lifestyle that matches yours. The same anxieties. The same fears about losing it. The same inability to feel secure. You compare spreadsheets sometimes. You realize you're both running the same calculation. You're both protecting against the same ghosts. The difference in fear doesn't come from the difference in financial position. It comes from something older. Something that money was supposed to fix and never does.

---

---
You have what you thought would be enough. The number that would finally quiet the fear. The income that would finally feel safe. The assets that should finally mean you can rest. And the alarm still fires. The insufficiency still hums underneath. The cost of building wealth when the internal sense of scarcity never healed is the discovery that more money doesn't fix the broken sense of enough. The cost is paid in the endless acquisition. In the constant growth requirements. In the identity that has become the accumulation.
