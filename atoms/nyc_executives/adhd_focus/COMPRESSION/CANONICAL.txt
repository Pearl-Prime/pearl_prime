## COMPRESSION v01
---
---
Initiation freeze at your altitude is not a discipline gap; it is a missing external trigger. You start instantly under real deadline pressure and stall completely on anything vague. Manufacture the trigger — a timer, a stated commitment, a fake call — and the wall you called willpower becomes a single step you can take.
---

## COMPRESSION v02
---
---
Deciding fast under live pressure and choosing among equally-weighted open priorities are different skills. You have the first. The freeze on the second is not a contradiction in your competence. It is proof the two draw on different systems.
---

## COMPRESSION v03
---
---
The task you have been avoiding is not too big for your discipline. It is missing a first move small enough for your attention to grab. Name the verb, not the outcome, and the stall usually breaks.
---

## COMPRESSION v04
---
---
Moralizing the stall — telling yourself you'd start if it mattered enough — is both unkind and inaccurate, and shame is a poor fuel for beginning anything. Structure fixes what shame cannot.
---
