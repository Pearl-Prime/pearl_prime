

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit at your desk. Place your hands on your keyboard. Feel the keys under your fingers. Breathe in for four counts. Now place your hands on the desk beside the keyboard. Feel the empty surface. Breathe out for four counts. This is the transition. Repeat three times. Hands on keyboard. Breathe in. Hands on desk. Breathe out. You are physically releasing the work.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Stand up from your desk. Push your chair back deliberately. Make the motion deliberate and full. Listen to the sound. Feel your legs under you. Walk to the window. Return to your desk. Walk away one more time. You are creating distance. Distance is a boundary. Your legs carry you away from the work.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your laptop slowly. Feel the resistance as you close it. Pause with it half-closed. Breathe in. Close it fully. Breathe out. Place your hand on the closed laptop. Feel the solid surface. Count to ten. You have closed the work. The laptop is closed. It is not open to you.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Sit after you close your laptop. Place both feet on the floor. Press your feet down. Hold the pressure for six counts. Release. This is the end-of-day signal. Your feet are on the ground. The day is complete. Repeat five times. Each press is closure.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Stand and place both hands on top of your closed laptop. Press down for five counts. Breathe in. Release and step away. Breathe out. Repeat three times. You are physically pressing down the work. The work is contained. The work is not expanding into your evening.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
In the bathroom, wash your hands. Feel the water temperature change. Wash each finger deliberately. Count to ten as you wash. This is a ritual. Rituals are boundaries. You are washing off the day.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
At your desk, place one hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Place the other hand on your belly. Feel it rise and fall. Breathe in for five counts. Hold for two counts. Breathe out for five counts. Repeat four times. You are shifting your attention from your work to your body. Your body is separate from your work.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Stand and raise your arms overhead. Stretch for a full count of five. Feel the extension from your feet to your fingertips. Release and drop your arms. Feel the difference. Repeat twice. You are expanding out of the work position. You are taking up space that is not the desk.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Sit. Clench every muscle in your face. Hold for four counts. Release completely. Do this twice. Your face carries the day's tension. Release the face and you release the day. Your face is no longer the work face.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Close your eyes. Listen for five distinct sounds. Name each one internally. A car. The refrigerator. The building settling. A person. Wind. These are the sounds of your home. Not the sounds of your office. Repeat this. You are auditorily relocating.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Walk through your home. Touch five different objects. Feel the texture of each. Count to three on each object. Texture is real. Texture is separate from work. Your hands are in your home. Your hands are not on a keyboard.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Remove your work shoes or change your clothes. Make the action deliberate. Feel the difference in your feet. Feel the difference in your body. This is a boundary signal. Different clothes mean different role. Your body recognizes the change.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Sit and press your back against a chair back. Feel the support. Breathe in for four counts. Shift your weight forward. Feel your feet on the ground. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat five times. You are moving between the work position and the rest position. The movements are distinct.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Stand in your kitchen or home entry. Place your hands on your hips. Breathe in for four counts. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat four times. This is power pose closure. You are claiming the space. The space is yours. The work is not in this space.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. As you exhale, make a sound. A long exhale sound. Repeat five times. Humming out tension. Releasing tension through your voice. Your voice is returning to you.
---

## EXERCISE v16
---

---
Place your phone in another room. Walk to retrieve it. Feel the distance. Walk back. Repeat three times. You are creating physical distance from the connection to work. Distance is a boundary. Your feet are enforcing the boundary.
---

## EXERCISE v17
---

---
Sit and place your hands in your lap. Lower your shoulders away from your ears. Hold for five counts. Breathe in. Shoulders up to ears. Breathe out and lower. Repeat six times. Your shoulders release the tension of holding. Shoulders down mean the day is down.
---

## EXERCISE v18
---

---
Stand. Place both feet on the floor. Close your eyes. Feel the ground under your feet. Breathe in for four counts. Feel yourself sinking into the ground. Breathe out for four counts. Repeat four times. You are rooting into your home. Not your office. Your home ground.
---

## EXERCISE v19
---

---
Press your palms together at your chest. Breathe in for four counts. Press your hands together firmly. Hold for four counts. Breathe out and release. Repeat six times. Hands together mean closure. Separation on the exhale. The work is being released.
---

## EXERCISE v20
---

---
Sit and tense your legs. Squeeze your thigh muscles. Hold for four counts. Release completely. Feel the blood flowing back. Repeat five times. Your legs carry you away from the work. Tense and release means they are yours again.
---

## EXERCISE v21
---

---
Roll your shoulders backward slowly. Count to five as you roll. Feel the muscles releasing. Stop and feel the difference. Repeat three times. Shoulders back mean the work is being shed. You are physically putting the work behind you.
---

## EXERCISE v22
---

---
In the car, grip the steering wheel. Hold for five counts. Release and place your hands in your lap. Repeat four times. The grip is the work holding on. Release is letting go. Your hands know the difference.
---

## EXERCISE v23
---

---
Stand and stamp your feet. Deliberately stamp each foot. Count to four on each side. Feel the impact on the ground. Repeat twice. You are firmly claiming the ground. The ground is not the office. The ground is home.
---

## EXERCISE v24
---

---
Lie down if possible or sit back in your chair. Place both hands on your belly. Breathe in and feel your belly rise. Breathe out and feel it fall. Count to four each direction. Repeat five times. Belly breathing means deep rest. Work breathing is shallow. You are switching.
---

## EXERCISE v25
---

---
Close your eyes. Scan your body from head to toes. Notice any remaining tension. Pause there. Breathe into that spot for four counts. Move to the next area. Repeat from head to toes. You are locating the work in your body and releasing it.
---

## EXERCISE v26
---

---
Sit and straighten your spine. Feel your back against the chair. Drop your head forward slightly. Breathe in. Lift your head. Breathe out. Repeat six times. The forward drop releases neck tension. The lift reclaims your posture. Work is released. You are reclaimed.
---

## EXERCISE v27
---

---
Place a cold object against your wrist. A cold cup. A cold counter. Feel the temperature. Hold for ten counts. The cold is a reset. Cold wakes up the present moment. The present moment is your evening.
---

## EXERCISE v28
---

---
Stand and clench your fists at your sides. Hold for five counts. Open your hands completely. Spread your fingers wide. Hold for five counts. Repeat five times. Clench means grip. Open means release. The work is released from your hands.
---

## EXERCISE v29
---

---
Sit and place your feet flat on the floor. Press your heels into the ground. Hold for six counts. Release. Repeat eight times. Your heels are grounding you into the evening. The day is below your heels. You are pressing it into the earth.
---

## EXERCISE v30
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. Hold for six counts. Breathe out for four counts. Hold for four counts. Repeat five times. The holds are where the nervous system shifts to rest. Each cycle moves you further from work. The final exhale is the end of the work day.
---
