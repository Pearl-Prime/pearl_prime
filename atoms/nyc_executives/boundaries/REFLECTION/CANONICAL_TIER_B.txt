

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: protective
---
Boundaries are about energy, the limited resource that depletes when you use it. In leadership, energy transfer is constant. When you receive someone's needs, you absorb their internal state. When you manage a person, you hold their uncertainty.
The mechanism is the reciprocal energy transfer. The role structure does not account for replenishment. You receive energy-extracting interactions all day, then the system expects you ready for the next one.
I notice this most in late afternoon. The accumulated transfer is visible. You have less patience. You are more reactive. The ability to be present is diminished. And there are still hours remaining.
What I have come to understand is that boundary violation is not about the person violating it. It is about the system that does not account for the cost of constant human output.

voice_mode: guide
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
There is confusion between availability and worth. Being available becomes a measure of value.
If you are available, you are responsive. If responsive, you are serious. If serious, you are worth something. The chain is simple and becomes internalized.
The mechanism is the visibility-value conflation. In an environment where visibility equals value, the constantly present person seems more valuable. The person available for every meeting is the person seen. Visibility becomes a proxy for impact.

What I have come to understand is that conflation of availability with worth is a feature of the environment, not a truth. But it is so embedded that it shapes behavior unconsciously.
