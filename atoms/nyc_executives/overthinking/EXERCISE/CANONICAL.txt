

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
Sit at your desk. Notice the loop repeating the same question. Say silently: this is the fifth time. Breathe in for four counts. You do not need a sixth pass to find the answer. Exhale for four counts.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Place both feet flat on the floor. Press down firmly. The spiral lives in your head. The floor is beneath your feet, not your head. Feel the floor for five counts.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes. Picture the decision as a closed door. Breathe in for four counts. On the exhale, mentally close the door. You can reopen it tomorrow if needed, not in this instant.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Stand and shake out your hands for six counts. The spiral is mental energy with nowhere to go. Shaking gives it somewhere to go. Stop and notice the stillness.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Write the looping thought on paper in one sentence. Look at it. It is smaller on paper than it is in your head. Breathe out as you fold the paper. Set it aside.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Place one hand on your forehead. Breathe in for four counts. Say silently: I have thought about this enough for now. Breathe out for four counts. Lower your hand.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Count backward from twenty by threes, twenty, seventeen, fourteen. This requires enough working memory that the spiral cannot run in parallel. Continue to zero or as far as you get.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
Sit and press your palms together in front of your chest. Push them into each other for five counts. Feel the pressure between your palms. Release. The physical pressure interrupts the purely mental loop.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Before a decision, set a timer for two minutes. Think only until the timer ends. When it rings, act on your best current answer. The spiral thrives on unlimited time.
---

## EXERCISE v10
---

---
Breathe in through your nose for four counts. Hold for seven counts. Exhale through your mouth for eight counts. This ratio is specifically slowing, useful when thoughts are moving faster than your breath.
---

## EXERCISE v11
---

---
Name three physical objects in the room without pausing between them. Notice the detail in each one. This shifts processing from the looping verbal channel to the visual one.
---

## EXERCISE v12
---

---
Place your hand flat on your chest. Feel your heartbeat is steady even while your thoughts are not. The two are not the same system. Breathe in for four counts, noticing the steadiness.
---

## EXERCISE v13
---

---
Stand and walk to a different part of the office, even ten feet. Physical movement changes the loop's context. Notice the new details in the space you walked into.
---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---
Before responding to the email that started the spiral, draft the reply, then close the tab for five minutes. Breathe in for four counts, out for four, five times. Reopen and reread before sending.
---

## EXERCISE v15
---

---
At your desk, tap your left knee, then your right knee, alternating slowly eight times. Feel the alternating rhythm. This bilateral movement engages both hemispheres and tends to interrupt single-track rumination.
---
