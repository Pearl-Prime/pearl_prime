## INTEGRATION v01
---
---
Tomorrow, pick one recurring threshold and make it your doorway back: the elevator ride down after a meeting, the four seconds after a call ends and before the next tab opens, the walk from the conference room to your desk. Choose one, and every time you cross it, take one full breath and name one true sensory fact — the temperature of the air, the weight of your feet, the actual sound in the room. You will forget most of the time. That is not failure; it is the baseline every manager at your altitude starts from. The point is not to be present for the whole day. The point is to install one small, reliable return so that at least once, in the middle of the flood, you actually arrive where you already are.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
---
Carry one habit forward from this chapter: before you open the next tab after a win, however small, give the win three full seconds of felt attention before you move on. Not a mental acknowledgment — an actual pause, hands off the keyboard, eyes off the screen, three seconds of simply letting it be real. You will feel the pull to skip this immediately; the pull is the whole pattern this chapter is about. Do it anyway, once tomorrow, on whatever counts as a win — a good call, a signed page, a problem that resolved. Three seconds. That is the entire practice, and it is enough to start teaching a nervous system that has never been told it's allowed to register arrival.
---

## INTEGRATION v03
---
---
Choose the hardest meeting on tomorrow's calendar and set one rule for its first sixty seconds: hands still, no notes, attention only on the person speaking. Not the whole meeting — just the opening, the part you currently lose to rehearsal and bracing. Notice what you catch in those sixty seconds that you would normally have missed while pre-loading your response. This is not a productivity technique. It is a small, deliberate proof that presence and performance are not in competition, and you will need that proof before you trust the practice with anything bigger.
---

## INTEGRATION v04
---
---
Tonight, before you close your laptop, name one thing that happened today that you did not actually feel happen — a close, a hard conversation, a number on a screen — and give it thirty seconds now, retroactively, before you let the day end. It will feel strange, reaching back for a feeling you already skipped past. Do it anyway. The goal is not to relive the day. It is to prove to yourself that the feeling was there the whole time, waiting, and that you have the capacity to find it even after the fact — which means you have the capacity to catch it next time, in real time, if you choose to.
