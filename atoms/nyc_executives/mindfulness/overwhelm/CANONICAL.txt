Lena has four term sheets open in four tabs and a board call in six minutes, and her mind is not on any of the four, it is on all four at once, a flat static hum where a decision should be. She picks the tab closest to the deadline, not the one that matters most, because closest-to-deadline is the only ranking her flooded mind can still perform. The board call starts. She is present enough to answer, absent enough that she will not remember, an hour later, what she actually said.

Nina sets a rule for herself during the busiest close of her career: one browser tab, one deal, no exceptions, even when three other deals are screaming for attention.
