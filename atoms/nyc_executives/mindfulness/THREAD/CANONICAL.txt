## THREAD v01
---
---
You close a deal and cannot recall a single sensory fact from the hour around it, because you spent it already drafting the next one. The win happened; you were not fully there for it. Underneath the pattern is a simple fact: attention only points one direction at a time, and yours has been pointed at "next" for years.

## THREAD v02
---
---
You override your body's signal — the tight chest, the cold hands — because overriding it wins negotiations. Notice that the override has a cost, paid quietly, for years, in a currency your compensation statement does not track.
---

## THREAD v03
---
---
You have gotten so good at the account of your life — the numbers, the names, who said what — that you have quietly stopped living the parts you're accounting for. Notice the gap between what you can recite and what you actually felt.
---

## THREAD v04
---
---
The feeling and its strategic implication are two different things, and you skip straight to the second every time. Notice, once, what happens when you let the first one land before you convert it.
