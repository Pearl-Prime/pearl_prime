---
David remembers when mornings had a quality to them. Not happiness exactly. Just presence. He would wake up and the day had texture ahead of it. There was anticipation in the body. There was the sense of being available to something. Now his eyes open and the day is flat. It's a sequence of activities. The reorganization happened quietly. He didn't decide to stop experiencing anticipation. The anticipation stopped. The flatness isn't sadness that can be named. The flatness is the absence of the thing that made the day feel real. He goes through the morning routine. Coffee tastes like the concept of coffee. The meetings happen. He participates. Something is missing that he can feel the absence of more clearly than he can feel anything present.

Michael finds old deal memos in the files. He reads his own notes from the margins: "Great outcome!" "Client thrilled." "This is the kind of work that matters." The handwriting is enthusiastic. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

The wave comes while David is reviewing documents. He's reading through a contract and something occurs to him: he misses who he used to be. The person who had energy. The person who existed before the flatness. The missing is sharp and specific. He can see the difference between the person then and the person now. The wave rises and crests. Then it passes. He returns to the contract. The document is still there. The flatness returns. The wave was an interruption in the flatness, a momentary recognition that something is not normal. The flatness is the normal now. The wave told him what's absent. The wave didn't change the absence. He sits with both: the knowledge that he's lost something and the present incapacity to feel it as loss in a way that moves toward resolution.

## TURNING_POINT v01
---

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
David decides to examine what's happening more carefully. He's been organizing the flatness as a given. He begins to question if this is normal. The questioning is the first movement toward something different. He doesn't know what the something different would be. But he knows that the flatness, the organizing of all experience into a gray routine, might not be the baseline of human experience. He starts to pay attention. One morning, he notices a moment where the flatness lifts slightly. Not joy. Just the sense that the day might have something in it. The turning point is when he recognizes that this is not just aging. This is not just the inevitable wearing down that comes from years in high pressure. This is something specific. Something that might be possible to address. He makes an appointment.

## TURNING_POINT v02

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 3
COST_TYPE: internal

---
Sarah tells a colleague in a moment of vulnerability. "I miss being excited about closings. I don't remember when it stopped." The colleague listens. The colleague says: "I've been there. The thing is, it came back eventually. Different but back." The word eventually is news. Sarah had begun to believe the flatness was permanent. The colleague is saying it might not be. Sarah doesn't feel hopeful exactly. But the eventuality changes something. The turning point is the recognition that others have survived this. That the flatness isn't the final state. That something else might be possible on the other side. She decides to ask for help. Not because she feels ready. Because the alternative — continuing in the gray — has become less tolerable than asking.
