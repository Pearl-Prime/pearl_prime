

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: inherited
---
I have come to recognize what I call the invisibility-of-continuity mechanism. The executive continues to deliver results while the internal experience has fundamentally shifted. Work still happens. Deals still close. Teams still function. But the person moving through these events has changed in ways that no one else can see.
What has shifted is not the capacity to perform. It is the absence of felt engagement. The executive is executing familiar patterns while the inner sense of meaning has dimmed. This creates a peculiar asymmetry. The world sees competence. The person experiencing it knows something has gone offline.
The mechanism operates because high-performers have trained themselves to function regardless of internal state. The skill becomes a liability. The ability to continue masking the internal change allows the depression to deepen unseen.
I recognize this most acutely in the gap between what appears to be happening and what is actually true. The visibility of success does not reflect the invisibility of disconnection.
