David watches the team celebrate the quarterly numbers. They're good numbers. He helped produce them. His body is in the room, drink in hand, smile operating correctly. But there is a pane of glass between him and the celebration. He can see it. He can hear it. He cannot feel it. The glass has been there for weeks. Maybe months. He laughs when someone makes a joke. The laugh is accurate. The laugh is also performed.

## MECHANISM_PROOF v01

BAND: 3
MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3
IDENTITY_STAGE: destabilization
---
David tracks his own performance: still hitting targets, still answering emails, still showing up. The function is intact. The person inside the function is not. He can run meetings while feeling nothing. He can close deals while the watcher behind his eyes notes the closing without participating. The gap between output and internal experience is the mechanism. High function masks low connection. The mask is so effective that the depression hides inside competence. No one looks for depression inside results.

Sarah notices the withdrawal is selective. She can perform for clients—bright, engaged, sharp. The performance drains a battery that doesn't recharge. After the client leaves she sits in her office with the door closed. The sitting is not rest. The sitting is collapse. The selective performance proves the depression isn't about ability. ## MECHANISM_PROOF v03

MECHANISM_DEPTH: 2
COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3

---
The watcher position becomes Michael's default. He watches himself eat breakfast. Watches himself commute. Watches himself talk to his kids. The watching creates distance, and the distance creates safety—he can't be hurt by things he's observing from behind glass. But the safety also blocks everything else. Joy arrives and hits the glass. Affection arrives and hits the glass. The glass doesn't discriminate. It keeps out pain and connection with equal efficiency.

Rachel's husband stops asking what's wrong. He used to ask weekly. Then he stopped. She's not sure when. The stopping is information. It means the distance has been normalized. ## TURNING_POINT v01

COST_TYPE: internal
COST_INTENSITY: 3

---
David is watching himself in a meeting when he catches the watching. Not the meeting content—the watching itself. He notices the pane of glass. He notices himself behind it. The noticing creates a third position: the watcher watching the watcher. For a brief moment the recursion cracks something open. He's not in the meeting and not behind the glass. He's somewhere observing the whole arrangement. The arrangement looks exhausting from this third position. The exhaustion is new information.

---
Sarah tells one colleague the truth. Not all of it—a piece. "I've been running on empty for a while." The colleague doesn't fix it. Doesn't offer solutions. Says: "Yeah. Me too, last year." Two sentences. The two sentences create a hairline crack in the glass. Not enough to shatter it. Enough to prove it's glass, not wall. Glass can crack. Walls can't. The distinction matters. She leaves the conversation with the glass still there but now she knows what it's made of.

Michael's daughter grabs his hand in the park. Small fingers, insistent grip. The watcher notes it: child, hand, park. But something else happens too. BAND: 2

COST_TYPE: social
COST_INTENSITY: 2
IDENTITY_STAGE: experimentation
---
Sarah leaves her office door open. A small thing. The closed door was depression architecture—a physical boundary matching the internal one. The open door lets in hallway noise, passing colleagues, casual interruptions. The interruptions cost energy she doesn't have much of. The interruptions also create contact she can't create for herself. She doesn't solve the depression with an open door. She lets the world have a gap through which to reach her. Some days the gap is enough. Some days she closes the door by noon.
