

## INTEGRATION v01
---
mode: BODY_LANDED
reframe_type: BODY_FACT
weight: standard
carry_line: "Your hands shake. You speak anyway."
---
The moment before. The recommendation that contradicts the senior partner. The deal that requires you to be wrong and still move forward. The hire that breaks the pattern everyone else is comfortable with. The body fires the alarm. The adrenaline spikes. The hands shake. The mouth dries. This is courage. Not the absence of fear. Not the comfortable moment. Courage is the shaking hand moving anyway. Courage is the voice that wavers and speaks. The nervous system is lit. The stakes are visible. And you move. Notice where you feel it—the chest that's expanded despite the alarm. The diaphragm that's holding steady. The spine that stays upright. The body knows what courage feels like. It feels like fear with agency. It feels like continuing while every system is signaling threat.

## INTEGRATION v02
---
mode: COST_VISIBLE
reframe_type: COST_UNCOUNTED
weight: standard
carry_line: "Being right doesn't erase what it cost."
---
The recommendation was correct. The deal closed. The hire made the team stronger. The cost of being right doesn't reduce the cost of being visible. The moment when you were wrong, or could have been wrong, in front of people who mattered. The moment when your judgment was under scrutiny. The social penalty of being the one who pushed back. The reputation shifts—some see strength, some see risk, some see someone who doesn't play the game. The political capital you spent. The relationships that became more careful. The moments when people who used to mentor you stopped returning your calls. The cost is paid in the social currency. Being right doesn't refund it.

You've made the courageous choice seven times now. The recommendation that was unpopular. The decision that contradicted the consensus. The moment when you pushed when pushing could have ended things. Some of those moments hurt. Some cost you relationships. Some cost you opportunities that went to the safer person. You're still here. The version of yourself you can live with. The risks you remember now are not the ones that destroyed you. They're the ones that built you.

## INTEGRATION v04
---
mode: QUESTION_OPEN
reframe_type: QUESTION_REFRAME
weight: standard
carry_line: "Courage costs you socially. Unmeasured."
---
The deal succeeded. The recommendation was sound. The hire transformed the team. By every metric that appears on the page, you were right. But the partner who liked you before stopped making eye contact. The mentor who would have advocated for you moved his support elsewhere. The informal power network that could have benefited you shifted. The cost of visible dissent in hierarchical systems is not always immediate. Not always measurable. The question isn't whether your judgment was correct. The question is whether you're willing to pay the social cost of being right in ways that make others uncomfortable.

## INTEGRATION v07

mode: COST_VISIBLE

---
The people who know you as the person who speaks. The person who thinks independently. The person who won't fold. The person who can be trusted to dissent. That reputation is real. It's valuable. It's also separating. People don't relax around you the way they relax around the safe ones. The inner circles that form around shared consensus don't include you.
