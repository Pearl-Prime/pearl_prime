

## REFLECTION v01
---
family: F1
voice_mode: teacher
mechanism_emphasis: automatic
---
There is a specific kind of courage required in a room where everyone is performing certainty. It is not the visible kind. It is the internal kind. The moment when you have to choose whether to say something true when saying it is risky. The room is full of people projecting competence and knowledge. If you introduce uncertainty, you introduce something that does not belong. The mechanism is the certainty-performance norm. In high-stakes rooms, the norm is that you project certainty. So the courage required is to introduce uncertainty. To say "I do not know." The person who speaks truth bears the cost.
