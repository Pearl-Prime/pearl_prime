

## EXERCISE v01
---

---
In the hallway before the room, stand and press your feet into the floor. Feel the solid ground beneath you. Breathe in for four counts. This ground will not move. Breathe out for four counts. You are standing on something real. Repeat three times. Your body is solid. You will not collapse. You will walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v02
---

---
Clench both fists at your sides. Hold for five counts. Feel the power in your hands. Release and shake your hands out. Repeat three times. Your hands have power. Your hands can do what you came to do. Your hands are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v03
---

---
Close your eyes for ten counts. Breathe in for four. Hold for four. Breathe out for four. With your eyes closed, you cannot see their faces. You can only feel your own body. Your body is here. Your body is enough. Open your eyes. You are ready.
---

## EXERCISE v04
---

---
Press your hands together at your chest. Hold them there for six counts. Feel the pressure. The pressure is containment. It is not suppression. Release and let your hands fall. Repeat three times. You are holding yourself steady.
---

## EXERCISE v05
---

---
Place both hands on the wall outside the room. Press firmly. Feel the wall holding your weight. Count to five. Release. The wall is solid. You are solid. You are pressed against something that does not move. Repeat twice. Walk toward the room.
---

## EXERCISE v06
---

---
Stand and place your feet shoulder-width apart. Feel the ground under both feet equally. Shift your weight from foot to foot. Feel the solid ground shift with you. The ground is supporting every movement. Breathe in and feel taller. Breathe out and feel grounded. You will walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v07
---

---
Hum for the entire exhale. Breathe in in silence. Hum out. Repeat four times. Humming activates your vagus nerve. It tells your body you are safe. You are safe. The room is safe. Walk in humming if you need to.
---

## EXERCISE v08
---

---
In the elevator or car, place your hand on your heart. Feel your heartbeat. Your heart is beating. It has not stopped. It will not stop in the room. Your heart knows what to do. Remove your hand. Walk in.
---

## EXERCISE v09
---

---
Close your eyes. Breathe in for four counts. As you exhale, imagine yourself walking into the room calmly. See yourself doing it well. Breathe in again. Repeat three times. You have already walked into the room in your body. Your body knows how to do this.

---

---

## EXERCISE v14
---

---

---

---

---
