## File 1: anxiety/shame/CANONICAL.txt

### RECOGNITION v01 (Alex's experience)
"Alex stands in the office—three desks, two windows, a coffee maker he bought secondhand. The quarterly revenue report sits in front of him. The number is half of what he'd projected to the team. His chest tightens. Nobody knows yet. He hasn't told them. The gap between what he promised and what materialized feels like proof of something fundamental: he doesn't know what he's doing. Other founders post Series A announcements. He's here, doing the accounts payable himself, knowing that if they miss payroll next month, they'll discover exactly who their leader really is. His jaw clenches. The chair creaks under him. He refreshes his email, waiting for the check from his largest client. It's two weeks late. Everything hinges on promises he made about growth trajectories and market opportunities, and now he's watching the market behave exactly as it wishes, indifferent to his narrative. His shoulders round inward. The shame isn't about the numbers. It's that he spent a year convincing everyone—his team, his investors, himself—that he had the vision, the strategy, the ability to build something exceptional. And now he's staring at evidence of the gap."

**Length:** 180 words | **Third-person present** | **Somatic specificity** (chest tightens, jaw clenches, shoulders round)

### MECHANISM_PROOF v01 (Alex caught in pattern)
"The investor call happens on Thursday. Alex has prepped. He's practiced the growth narrative, the market story, the roadmap. But three minutes in, when she asks about churn, he can feel the truth pulling at him. He knows it. Churn is higher than his projection. He can present it as 'market adjustment,' can frame it as 'expected for this phase.' He does. And it works. She nods. They move forward. But after the call, he sits in his car for twenty minutes, staring at nothing. The shame has a specific weight now: he wasn't caught, but he also wasn't truthful. The lie of omission didn't derail the narrative. And that's somehow worse. Because it means he can do this. He can sustain the gap between reality and story. His team is depending on him to be someone who knows the path. His investors are counting on him to have a handle on the fundamentals. His own belief in the company requires him to project certainty. So he lies. Not dramatically. Just enough. And the lie works, which makes it worse, which makes the next lie easier, which makes the shame deeper and more solid and more necessary."

**Length:** 173 words | **Shows mechanism reinforcing itself** | **Somatic tracking:** chest tightens → specific weight → deeper shame

---

### TURNING_POINT v01 (First shift)
"Alex sits with his CFO—technically a fractional CFO he can barely afford, but he finally accepted he needed someone to tell him the truth. They're looking at the numbers together. The CFO says something unexpected: 'The shame you're feeling isn't data. It's just a feeling you're trying to make into evidence.' Alex's first instinct is to reject it. He has evidence. The metrics prove it. But the CFO points something out: the metrics prove the business is smaller than he projected. They don't prove he's a failure. They prove he made an inaccurate forecast. His chest loosens slightly. He spends the next hour understanding the difference between 'the business isn't where I predicted' and 'I'm not competent.' It's not a revelation that dissolves the shame. But it's a crack in the structure. He starts asking a different question: not 'what does this revenue number say about me?' but 'what does it tell me about my market assumptions?' His shoulders drop. It's a small shift. But it's the beginning of learning that the shame isn't the data. The shame is his interpretation, and he can change that."

**Length:** 177 words | **Shows pause and reframing** | **Somatic shift:** chest loosens, shoulders drop

---

### EMBODIMENT v01 (Integration)
"Alex stops checking his email first thing in the morning. He reads the quarterly reports differently now. The number that is smaller than he predicted is allowed to be true without meaning something about him. He can stand in the office—three desks, two windows, the coffee maker—and know that the business is exactly the size it is, and he's exactly the person he is, and they're not the same thing. His chest stays open. He doesn't perform certainty he doesn't feel. In investor calls, he gives honest forecasts with honest confidence intervals. The investors like him more for it, not less. The team trusts him more when he doesn't pretend to know things he doesn't. He has discovered that running a business doesn't require being the person he was ashamed of not being. It requires being the person who can actually see what's happening and respond appropriately."

**Length:** 161 words | **Shows embodied choice** | **Somatic baseline:** chest stays open, warm integration

## File 23: courage/overwhelm/CANONICAL.txt

### RECOGNITION v01 (Initial awareness)
"Alex sits at his desk at 11 PM on Wednesday. He's wearing multiple hats simultaneously: handling a customer complaint, reviewing contractor invoices, updating the marketing calendar, responding to investor email. His chest is tight. His shoulders are elevated. He has no HR person. No ops manager. No executive assistant. There's just him and a growing pile of responsibilities that don't consolidate into single roles. His throat feels closed. An hour ago, he was writing marketing copy. Before that, fixing a server issue. Before that, on the phone with a key vendor. His brain never settles. It's always context-switching. The context-switching feels like competence—look at everything I can do—but it also feels like drowning in slow motion. His hands are cold. He has no delegation structure. Nobody can take anything off his plate because the plate is him. The business exists in the gap between what he does and what gets done. He can feel this clearly at 11 PM on a Wednesday when his nervous system is finally quiet enough to acknowledge it."

**Length:** 178 words | **Character-specific context:** founder doing everything | **Somatic tracking:** tight, elevated, closed, cold

## File 40: financial_stress/spiral/CANONICAL.txt

### TURNING_POINT v03 (Learning containment)
"Carlos creates a boundary: family time is phone-free. Work problems stay at the restaurant. He's fully present with his family. His daughter notices. She engages more. His wife notices. She engages more. His shoulders drop. He's learning to compartmentalize. The spiral lives in the restaurant. But it doesn't follow him home. His legs ache less when he's not carrying the mental load into dinner. His back hurts less when he's not tensed against the business problems. His chest opens. He's learning that containing something doesn't mean solving it. It means creating space where the spiral can't reach. His daughter tells him about her soccer game. He actually hears it. His wife laughs and he actually receives it. The boundary isn't perfect. The spiral still tries to follow him. But he's learning that he can redirect it. He can say: this is family time, the restaurant can wait. The restaurant can actually wait."

**Length:** 171 words | **Shows specific behavioral boundary** | **Somatic progression:** shoulders drop, legs ache less, chest opens

## File 60: self_worth/spiral/CANONICAL.txt

### EMBODIMENT v01 (Integrated choice)
"Alex has sovereignty over spiral. Comparison is part of life. But the mechanism doesn't run him. His hands are warm. His shoulders are relaxed. He's learned agency. He responds instead of reacts. He's claimed the space where choice lives. He makes decisions from clarity, not from catastrophized interpretation. A missed metric is data. A comparison to a competitor is information. A moment of doubt is a moment, not a life direction. His jaw is relaxed. His chest is open. The spiral still visits. But it no longer colonizes his entire nervous system. He notices it, observes it, and chooses what to do with it. His team responds to his clarity. They don't mobilize constantly around catastrophized interpretations anymore. The company moves faster because energy isn't consumed by emergency response to false alarms. His chest is open and his hands are warm. He's made peace with all of it."

**Length:** 163 words | **Shows full integration** | **Somatic baseline:** warm hands, relaxed shoulders, open chest, relaxed jaw
