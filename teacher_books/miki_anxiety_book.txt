A Note on the Teachings of Miki

I was not a direct student of Miki. I encountered their work through books, talks, and publicly available teachings. What follows is not an official interpretation of Miki's work — it is an application. I have done my best to honor the integrity of the original teachings while translating them into practical guidance for the challenges you may be facing.

Miki's understanding of anxiety reshaped the way I see this subject. Their approach — rooted in Japanese mindfulness; nature-based awareness; forest bathing; simplicity as practice — offers a lens that goes beyond conventional advice. It speaks to something deeper: the patterns beneath the surface, the quiet mechanisms that keep us stuck, and the often-overlooked pathways toward genuine relief.

This book applies Miki's teachings to the specific experience of gen alpha students navigating anxiety. It does not replace the teacher's original work. Where I have adapted exercises or frameworks, I have done so with care and transparency. Any simplification is mine, not theirs.

If something in these pages resonates with you, I encourage you to go to the source. Seek out Miki's own words — their talks, their writings, their direct teachings. What I offer here is a bridge, not a destination. The real work lives in the original.
