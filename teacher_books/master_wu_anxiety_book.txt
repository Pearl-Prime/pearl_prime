A Note on the Teachings of Master Wu

I was not a direct student of Master Wu. I encountered their work through books, talks, and publicly available teachings. What follows is not an official interpretation of Master Wu's work — it is an application. I have done my best to honor the integrity of the original teachings while translating them into practical guidance for the challenges you may be facing.

Master Wu's understanding of anxiety reshaped the way I see this subject. Their approach — rooted in Taoist; Wu Wei (effortless action); yielding as strength; water philosophy — offers a lens that goes beyond conventional advice. It speaks to something deeper: the patterns beneath the surface, the quiet mechanisms that keep us stuck, and the often-overlooked pathways toward genuine relief.

This book applies Master Wu's teachings to the specific experience of gen alpha students navigating anxiety. It does not replace the teacher's original work. Where I have adapted exercises or frameworks, I have done so with care and transparency. Any simplification is mine, not theirs.

If something in these pages resonates with you, I encourage you to go to the source. Seek out Master Wu's own words — their talks, their writings, their direct teachings. What I offer here is a bridge, not a destination. The real work lives in the original.

What it does not know — yet — is that the program can be interrupted. Not by force. Not by willpower. By a different kind of attention. The kind you will practice in these 20 chapters.

You do not need to understand this now. You just need to keep listening.
