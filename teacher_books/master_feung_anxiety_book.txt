A Note on the Teachings of Master Feung

I was not a direct student of Master Feung. I encountered their work through books, talks, and publicly available teachings. What follows is not an official interpretation of Master Feung's work — it is an application. I have done my best to honor the integrity of the original teachings while translating them into practical guidance for the challenges you may be facing.

Master Feung's understanding of anxiety reshaped the way I see this subject. Their approach — rooted in Chinese energy medicine; Qigong; meridian healing; stillness as strength — offers a lens that goes beyond conventional advice. It speaks to something deeper: the patterns beneath the surface, the quiet mechanisms that keep us stuck, and the often-overlooked pathways toward genuine relief.

This book applies Master Feung's teachings to the specific experience of gen z professionals navigating anxiety. It does not replace the teacher's original work. Where I have adapted exercises or frameworks, I have done so with care and transparency. Any simplification is mine, not theirs.

If something in these pages resonates with you, I encourage you to go to the source. Seek out Master Feung's own words — their talks, their writings, their direct teachings. What I offer here is a bridge, not a destination. The real work lives in the original.

Introduction

Your body already knows what anxiety feels like. It is the jaw tension before a meeting. The chest tightness when you open your laptop. The thing where you cannot sleep but you also cannot stop scrolling.

This book is not going to explain anxiety to you. You already get it. What it is going to do is give you a way to work with it that does not require more willpower, more discipline, or more self-improvement content.

Just practices. Short ones. And a way of seeing what is already happening in your body so it stops running the show.

In the Qigong tradition, there is a teaching about the river. The river does not struggle against the stone. It does not fight. It simply moves, and in moving over centuries, it wears the stone smooth.

The river does not decide to be patient. Patience is its nature. It flows at the speed it flows. If the channel narrows, the water deepens. If the banks widen, it spreads. It follows the shape it is given.
