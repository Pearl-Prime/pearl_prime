At the six-month physical, the doctor presses along Cal's neck and stops. "How long have you had these?" she asks, thumbing the ropes of muscle. Knots the size of marbles, both sides, cemented. Cal says he didn't know they were there. She says the body keeps the post you assign it, even when you go home. He says yeah and buttons his shirt. But at the light on the way back to the station he catches himself with the wheel in a death grip at a red, nothing coming, and he makes his hands open.
