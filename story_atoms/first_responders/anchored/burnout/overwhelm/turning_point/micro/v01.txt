A retired medic Reyes barely knows sits down in the bay and does not ask if he is okay. The man just says, low, "The ones we keep re-running, we ran them right. That is why they stay." Reyes says something about the coffee and gets up. But in the truck, doing the checklist, his hands stop at the pediatric bag and do not open it a second time. He sits there with them still in his lap, and for once the shoe does not blink.
