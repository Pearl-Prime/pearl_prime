His niece is telling him about a dead classroom hamster, six years old and using her whole body to grieve it, and Odie feels his face do the truck thing — the unhook, the gray — at a kid over a hamster. She stops mid-sentence. Studies him. Says, "Uncle Odie, are you even in there?" He says of course, buddy, and gets her a popsicle. But driving home he catches his own eyes in the rearview, parked on the far wall, and can't remember choosing to put them there.
