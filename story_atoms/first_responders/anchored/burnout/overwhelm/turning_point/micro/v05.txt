A retired captain doing station visits watches Iker laugh late at his own joke and doesn't smile back. He says, quiet, over by the coffee, "The funny's the last thing to go and the first thing you miss. You're not slow. You're carrying the whole run in your body." Iker says something to move it along and pours the coffee. But in the truck that night he notices he's read the exits twice already and can't remember doing it once.
