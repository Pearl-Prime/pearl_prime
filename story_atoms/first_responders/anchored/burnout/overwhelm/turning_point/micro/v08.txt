A newer medic rides shotgun and says, mid-nothing, "I stopped hearing the tones in my sleep after I quit sleeping with the scanner on." Sena keeps her eyes on the road. She has slept with the scanner on for eleven years; she did not know it was a choice. That night she leaves it in the kitchen instead of on the nightstand. She lies down. Her feet stay under the blanket a whole minute longer than usual, and she notices the minute.
