Gray's daughter is telling him about the dog that died and she stops mid-sentence. "You do the same face for this as you do for the news," she says, not mean, just looking at him. He starts to say he is listening, which is true, and stops, because the face is what she means, not the listening. He says something about the traffic and lets it drop. But at the sink that night he catches his reflection trying the face on, moving nothing, and his throat goes tight for a reason he can't file.
