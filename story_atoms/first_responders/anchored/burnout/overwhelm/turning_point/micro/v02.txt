His daughter draws the family. In her picture Hollis is a stick figure standing apart, beside a door she has labeled EXIT in careful letters. "That's you watching," she says, and moves on to coloring the dog. Hollis pins it to the fridge like the others. But he stands in front of it longer than he means to, coffee going cold in his hand, and something he'd held at attention for years goes loose in his chest for a second, and he doesn't reach to catch it.
