The engine is off and the eleven-year-old is still in the cab with her. This time Lux says it into the quiet, low, just to the windshield: that one's staying in the truck tonight. The call does not leave. But she names the door — the porch, the light, her wife's shape in the kitchen window — and she puts one boot on the driveway before the whole shift can follow her in. Not clean, not done. Just the first foot down while the scan is still running.
