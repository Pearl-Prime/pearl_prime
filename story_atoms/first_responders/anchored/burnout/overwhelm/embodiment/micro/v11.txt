The rookie comes off the same pediatric call with his jaw set wrong, and Gray does not hand him the checklist. He sits down on the bumper next to him, close, not talking, the way nobody sat with Gray twenty years back. After a while he says, "That one was bad. You're allowed to say it was bad." His own chest is still flat and he knows it. But he stays on the bumper past the point of usefulness, not fixing the kid, just refusing to be the far-off thing he became.
