It's his nephew's birthday and Hollis is down on the floor, his back to the open doorway, helping thread a bead onto a shoelace. He knows the exit is behind him the way you know a clock is on the wall — it's there, he doesn't turn. A kid tips the whole cup of beads and Hollis laughs, actually laughs, sweeping them up with both hands. He glances at the door once. Then back to the small fingers waiting for the next bead, and he stays.
