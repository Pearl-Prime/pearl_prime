The tones drop for a real call and Sena is up, gear on, moving — but at the rig door she stops, plants one hand flat on the cold metal, and takes the single breath the newer medic taught her before climbing in. It is four seconds. Dispatch is still talking. She goes anyway, hands steady on the wheel. Halfway to the scene she realizes her jaw isn't clenched, and she can't say when it let go.
