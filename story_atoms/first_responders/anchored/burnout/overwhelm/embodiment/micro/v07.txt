Cal is on the couch after a bad shift, a pediatric call he will not describe. He notices his shoulders climbing and does not fight it. Instead he lies flat on the living room floor, the way the doctor showed him, and lets the ground take his weight for the first time in years. He does not feel fixed. His jaw is still tight. But his back is on the floor and the floor is holding, and he stays there long enough to feel one shoulder, just one, come down half an inch.
