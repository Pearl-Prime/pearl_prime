The pediatric call clears and Priyanka is in her car with the key turned. The radio comes on, some ordinary song, and her hand lifts toward the dial the way it always does. This time she leaves it. She pulls out of the bay with the music low and lets it be there, one small sound she didn't erase. At the second light she texts her partner: rough one, you around later. She doesn't wait for the answer before she drives, but she sent it, and the radio stays on the whole way.
