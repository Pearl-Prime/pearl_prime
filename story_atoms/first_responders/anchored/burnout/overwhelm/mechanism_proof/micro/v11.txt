The tones drop for a full arrest and Gray is already reading the map before his heart rate moves. Twenty years ago this call put a wire through him. Now the dispatch info lands flat, filed, and his pulse stays where it was over the coffee. He does the work clean — better than clean, the newer guys watch his hands. On the ride back someone cracks the old joke about the gurney and Gray laughs on the exhale, a beat late, matching the sound he knows the joke is supposed to make.
