Cal's daughter slams a cabinet in the next room and he is on his feet with his weight forward, scanning the doorway for the threat. It is a five-year-old wanting a cup. His pulse takes ninety seconds to level. Later, in bed, his wife's arm drapes across his chest and he flinches like it landed on a wound. He lies rigid, jaw set, running the exits of his own bedroom. He tells himself he will relax when the next tone drops, and the next tone drops, and he does not.
