Her daughter is telling a story about a girl at school and Sena is nodding at the right places, but under the table her thumb is pressed to the inside of her own wrist, counting. Sixty-two. She does this now without deciding to, in the grocery line, at the dinner table, checking a pulse that is only ever hers. The story ends. She missed the middle. She says that's amazing, honey, and does not know what was amazing.
