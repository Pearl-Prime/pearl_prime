The debriefs are where Marsh disappears. He runs them clean — asks each man how he's carrying the bad ones, watches for the flat stare, sends two to the counselor and calls it leadership. When it circles to him he says the captain's fine and moves the meeting on. He has started sleeping in the recliner by the watch desk so he can hear the tones a half-second sooner. Three shifts running he has driven home and sat in the driveway, engine ticking, unable to name why he cannot go inside.
