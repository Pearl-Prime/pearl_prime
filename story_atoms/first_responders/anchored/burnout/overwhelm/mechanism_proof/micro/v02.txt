At the grocery store Hollis clocks the ceiling sprinklers, the propane cage out front, the woman blocking the fire lane with her cart. He has stopped going to movies; the theater is a box with two doors and four hundred people. His son asks why they always sit in the back at church. Hollis says he likes the view. The scan runs while he sleeps now — he wakes at 3 a.m. having already mapped the bedroom, the drop from the window, the kids' rooms down the hall.
