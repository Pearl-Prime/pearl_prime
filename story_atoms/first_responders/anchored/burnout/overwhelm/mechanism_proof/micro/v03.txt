Dana keeps the volume one notch too high now. In the break room the microwave beeps and her thumb twitches toward a talk button that isn't there. She has started narrating her drive home in a flat dispatcher cadence — northbound, quarter mile, no visible — to fill the quiet, because the quiet is where the silent calls live. Her partner asks about her day. Dana says busy. She does not say she can still hear the 4:52 line, hissing, waiting, going nowhere.
