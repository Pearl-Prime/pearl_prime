Lux has been parked in the driveway for nineteen minutes with the engine off. The porch light is on. Dinner is somewhere behind that door. But she is still running the eleven-year-old from the third call, the way his sock came off when they moved him, and whether the tourniquet was high enough. Her own street is quiet. Her eyes keep going to the neighbor's dark upstairs window, checking it for smoke that is not there. The keys are in her hand and she does not reach for the door.
