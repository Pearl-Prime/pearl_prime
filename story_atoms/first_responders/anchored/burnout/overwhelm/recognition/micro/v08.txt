Sena is off shift, lying down at 3:40 a.m., and the ceiling fan ticks once on its slow turn. Her feet are already on the floor before she knows she has moved, hand reaching for the boots that aren't there. No tones. The pager on the dresser is dark. She stands in the dark holding the shape of a call that didn't come, then lowers herself back down and waits for the fan to tick again.
