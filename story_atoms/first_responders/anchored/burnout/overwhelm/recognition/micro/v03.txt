Dana's headset is warm from eight hours of other people's worst days. A caller two hours ago gave an address, then the line went to that specific hush — not hung up, just gone. Dana dispatched the units, logged the disposition, took the next call. Now, mid-sentence with a woman reporting a fender-bender, Dana catches herself pressing the earpiece harder into her skull, listening past the woman for a sound that ended at 4:52 p.m.
