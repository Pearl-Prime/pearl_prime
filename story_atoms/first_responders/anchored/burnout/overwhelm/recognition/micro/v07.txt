Cal is off shift, standing in his own kitchen, and the smoke detector chirps its low-battery warning. He is across the room and reaching for the counter before he registers moving. His hand has already found the fire extinguisher bracket. There is no fire. There is a nine-volt battery dying in a plastic box on the ceiling. He puts the extinguisher down and notices his shoulders are still up around his ears, the way they ride during a working call, and he cannot remember the last time they came down.
