The call is a pediatric arrest and Odie is doing compressions, and across the small body he watches the senior medic's face for what to do with his own. Renner's jaw is loose. His eyes are parked somewhere on the far wall. So Odie lets his own face go there too — unhooks it, sets it down, aims it at the same middle distance. His hands keep the rhythm. On the outside he is a smooth gray nothing, and he notes, distantly, that it worked on the first try.
