It's his daughter's sixth birthday and Hollis is standing where he can see both doors. Twenty-two kids, one narrow hallway to the kitchen, a table of paper plates six feet from the candles on the cake. He counts the exits without deciding to. When his wife hands him the camera he takes it, but his eyes go back to the sliding door, the window latch, the distance to the driveway. He is smiling in the photos.
