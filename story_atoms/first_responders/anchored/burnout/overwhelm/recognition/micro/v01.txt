Reyes is restocking the rig at 3 a.m., counting pediatric airways into their pouch. He counts them twice. The station is quiet, which is when it starts — a Tuesday call, a booster seat, a shoe with a light in the heel that blinked while he worked. His thumb runs the pouch zipper closed and open, closed and open. Someone laughs in the bay and it lands somewhere he cannot reach. He counts the airways a third time.
