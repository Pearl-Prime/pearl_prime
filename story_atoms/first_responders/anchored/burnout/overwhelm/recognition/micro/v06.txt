Priyanka's rig is back in quarters and the paperwork is done. The others are still in the bay, retelling the pediatric call, laughing the way they do to make it fit somewhere. She signs the run sheet, initials the narcotics log, and is in her car before anyone says her name. She turns the key and the radio comes on mid-song. Her hand goes to the dial and kills it. She drives the whole way home inside that specific quiet, watching the road, cataloguing nothing, telling herself she is just tired.
