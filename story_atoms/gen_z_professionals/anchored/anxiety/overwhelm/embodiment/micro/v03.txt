The compensation review memo lands at 4:47pm on a Friday. Daniel reads
the subject line, marks it unread, and then opens it anyway. The language
is careful: "market adjustment," "current economic conditions," "valued
contribution." He finds his number three paragraphs in. He sits with it.
Last year he would have sent his manager a follow-up. This year he is
doing a calculation that does not involve follow-up — a different
calculation, about what this number means relative to the rent increase
and the loan payment and the number in his savings account that has not
grown in four months. He closes the email. He does not mark it unread
again.
