Jordan sets a timer on her phone for twenty minutes. Not a Pomodoro. She has tried Pomodoros. This is different — a stop timer, not a start timer. When it goes off she is done, whether or not the paragraph is finished.

The grant section is due Friday. It is Tuesday. She has one section drafted out of four.

She opens the document. She writes. She does not open a new tab to verify the number she just cited. She writes a bracket instead: [CHECK LATER]. She keeps going.

The timer goes off. She puts the phone down and counts the tabs at the top of her browser. Thirteen. She had twenty-two this morning.

She saves the document. She does not read what she wrote. She closes the laptop and makes tea and comes back and opens it again, but that is a different twenty minutes.
