The question from the first chapter finally gets its answer on an unremarkable Thursday. Who is she without the overwhelm — she used to fear the answer was nobody: a slower worker, a person with no edge. Priya spends an ordinary week's evidence against that old fear. Monday, a teammate's panic knocks in a thread; she feels it ask to move in, and it does not get past the doorway, and the reply she sends back is kinder for it. Wednesday she presents — heart quick, hands steady enough — and the quickness reads as energy now that it is not fighting a war on a second front. Thursday she leaves at 5:40 with the laptop closed, the close-out done, and nothing following her down the stairs. The edge she was afraid of losing turns out to have been mostly noise; what remains when the noise drains is not less of her but more — attention with nowhere it has to leak. She is not finished. Some mornings the phone still wins, some meetings still echo into the evening, and she logs those as weather and leaves them logged. But the person she spent years protecting with all that vigilance is, it turns out, sturdier than the vigilance ever believed. On the last flight of stairs she stops for one breath she does not need, just to feel the building hold still. She was in there the whole time, waiting for the alarm to end.
