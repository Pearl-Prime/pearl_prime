The practice that survives is almost embarrassingly small. After she closes the laptop on Thursday, Priya sets a two-minute timer before she opens the fridge — not to fix her anxiety, just to feel her feet and name one sound in the room. No app counts it. No streak updates. She misses Friday and does it again Monday without apologizing to herself, which turns out to be the actual skill — the practice was never in danger from the missed day, only from the story that a missed day means the end. By the third week her shoulders drop one inch before dinner on two of five nights. That is not a transformation montage. It is evidence her body can learn a closing ritual that does not require a login. The abandoned apps asked for identity change; this asks for one repeatable seam in a day she already owns. On the bad Thursday — the one with the 6pm escalation — she does it anyway, exhausted, standing in front of a fridge she does not open for the full two minutes, and when the timer ends nothing is fixed but something is finished. She keeps the timer on the microwave, not the home screen. The practice stops competing with guilt.
