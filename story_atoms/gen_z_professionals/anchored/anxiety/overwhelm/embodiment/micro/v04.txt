Priya is at the grocery store on a Sunday and she cannot decide between
two things. She has been standing in this aisle for longer than the
decision warrants. She knows this. She picks one. She puts it back. She
picks the other. She puts both in the cart because the act of choosing
has become something she doesn't have. She walks to the next aisle and
cannot remember what she came for. She pulls up her phone to check the
list she made. The list is in a note from two Sundays ago that she forgot
to update. She buys what she can remember and goes home and realizes at
9pm that she forgot the one thing she actually needed.
