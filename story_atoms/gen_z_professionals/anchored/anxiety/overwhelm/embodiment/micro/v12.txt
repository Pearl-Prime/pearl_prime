Zoë sends the handoff email Monday morning. It has the client list, the status notes, and a line that says "contact Marcus for anything urgent." She does not add a second line. She closes her laptop without reopening her inbox.

She drives to the trailhead with her phone in her bag. She does not check it at the gas station or in the parking lot. At noon she eats a sandwich on a rock and the phone is still in the bag.

She does not take it out.
