The spike comes mid-morning. Nia notices it before she reaches for her laptop. She puts her hands flat on the desk. She looks at the window for three minutes. Not long. She is not doing anything with the three minutes except being in them.

Then she opens the log. She types the date, the trigger — an email from the senior partner, subject line "need to discuss," sent at 7:52am — and a seven. She types two contributing factors. She submits the entry.

She puts a small star next to the row number. She has not done that before. The star does not mean anything yet. It means the three minutes happened first.
