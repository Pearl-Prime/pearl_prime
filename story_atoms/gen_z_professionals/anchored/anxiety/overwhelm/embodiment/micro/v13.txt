Chris's mother asks how work is going. They are at the kitchen table. He has been home for three hours.

He says "it's been harder than I expected."

She puts her coffee down. Not quickly. She sets it on the table and her hands stay around it. She doesn't say anything for a moment.

Then she says "tell me."

He doesn't have the full answer. He doesn't have a sentence that covers it. He opens his mouth and says the thing that is closest to the edge, the one about the 7pm Fridays, the one about not knowing who to ask. His mother's hands are still around her mug. He is still talking.
