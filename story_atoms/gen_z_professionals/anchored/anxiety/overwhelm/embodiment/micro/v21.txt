The ninety-second close-out happens at the kitchen counter after she finally closes the laptop for real. She puts both feet flat, names three sounds she can hear — fridge hum, street, her own exhale — and lets the retrospective play once without editing the dialogue. The pause at minute twelve stays a pause. It does not become a verdict. Her jaw unclenches one click. Her shoulders come down a centimeter she did not know they were holding. She does not feel peaceful. She feels finished, which is different and more useful. The first week she manages the ritual twice and forgets it three times, and on the nights she forgets, the meeting follows her to the couch and sits with her until bedtime. But on the nights she remembers, dinner tastes like dinner instead of like an agenda. Priya notices that her body can learn an ending if she performs one on purpose — ninety seconds of deliberate closing against three hours of accidental reopening. She washes the lunch container she abandoned at noon. The meeting stays closed when she goes to bed.
