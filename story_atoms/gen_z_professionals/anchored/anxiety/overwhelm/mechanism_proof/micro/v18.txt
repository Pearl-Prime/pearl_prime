The fourth deliverable goes out at 4:58pm. Zara sits back from her monitor for the first time since 8:51am. Her neck is tight in a way she does not remember it becoming. She reaches up and presses two fingers against the muscle below her left ear. It has been like this for a while.

There are three messages in her inbox marked with a yellow star, which means she put them there herself at some point today, which means she decided they could wait until after 5pm. She opens the first one. It is not urgent. She reads it to the end anyway. She opens the second. Also not urgent. She reads it. She opens the third. She already knows what it will be. She reads it to the end. She opens a fourth message that has no star.
