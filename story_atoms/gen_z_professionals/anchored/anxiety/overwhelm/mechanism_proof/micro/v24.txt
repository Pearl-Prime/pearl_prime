The activation does not respect boundaries between prep and performance. Priya is in the bathroom three minutes before she joins the Zoom and her reflection looks ready — hair pulled back, shirt pressed, face composed. Her stomach disagrees. She runs through the opening line one more time and watches her shoulders creep forward as if she could physically lean into the screen before it wakes. Down the hallway she hears a colleague laughing on a call and the sound feels unrelated to her, like weather through a window. She joins the waiting room early. She mutes. She un-mutes to test audio and hears the tremor she hoped was private. The host admits her. The deck loads. Her hands are visible on camera and she puts them flat on the desk because flat looks steadier than they feel. The presentation is not the hard part. The hard part is that her nervous system started without her and will not let her catch up until she gives it the ninety seconds no argument can replace.
