Someone from the implementation team stops by his desk and asks how he's adjusting to the new role. He says "honestly, great — learning a lot."

This is the seventh time in three weeks he has said this sentence. The word "honestly" is in it every time.

The part he says is true. The part he does not say is that he stays until 7pm because he is the last person to understand new processes and he needs the hours after everyone leaves to catch up without anyone watching him catch up. He has not said that part to his colleague. He has not said it to his manager. He has not said it to his roommate, who asked once and got "honestly, great — still figuring out the rhythms." He closed his laptop after that and went to get water he didn't want.
