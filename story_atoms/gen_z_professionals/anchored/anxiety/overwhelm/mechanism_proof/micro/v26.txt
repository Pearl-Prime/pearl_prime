The replay has its own schedule. At 4:19pm Priya is rinsing a mug and her mind returns to minute twelve of the retrospective — the pause before her teammate answered, the half-second she interprets as disapproval. She was not presenting. She was not on the hook. The pause belonged to someone else's story. Her body files it under evidence anyway. She hears the sentence again with a harsher tone than it had in the room, and each replay sharpens the tone another notch, the way a rumor worsens with retelling, and she is the only one telling it. She rehearses a reply she will never send. She rehearses a better version of the comment she actually made. The water runs. The mug is clean. The meeting is still over. The mechanism is simple and cruel: the nervous system treats unfinished social data like open tabs and keeps refreshing until she gives it a close command it understands. She does not get one from the calendar. She gets one from the body, or she does not get one at all — and tonight, without one, the loop follows her to bed, where she lies exhausted and still editing a pause that lasted half a second.
