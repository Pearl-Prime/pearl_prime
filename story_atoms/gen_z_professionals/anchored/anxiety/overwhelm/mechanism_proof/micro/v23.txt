The pattern follows her into rest. She is watching a show on the couch — nothing work-related, nothing urgent — and catches herself leaning forward as if the screen were a standup. Her shoulders are up. Her drink is untouched and going warm. She consciously drops her shoulders and they rise again during the next scene without permission. She is not anxious about the plot. Her body is not tracking the plot. It is tracking readiness: the same braced posture she held through four hours of meetings earlier, extended into an evening that does not require it. She pauses the show and tries to locate the tension the way she would locate a sound — jaw, neck, upper back, hands still shaped around an invisible mouse. The armor outlasted the battle. That is the mechanism. Priya names it quietly and does not fix it yet.
