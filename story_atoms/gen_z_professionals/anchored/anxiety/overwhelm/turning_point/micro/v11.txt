The doctor asks how he's sleeping. Tariq says fine, pretty good, the usual. The doctor looks at the chart and then looks up and asks again — not how many hours, but how is he sleeping. Tariq opens his mouth. His shoulders come up slightly and do not come back down. He says fine again, and the word lands wrong, and he can tell the doctor knows it landed wrong.

He drives home without pulling into the parking garage. He does not know he has done this until he is already in the elevator. He sits on the edge of his bed with his shoes still on. It takes three hours to understand why he went straight up.

He does not understand it yet. He is still sitting there.
