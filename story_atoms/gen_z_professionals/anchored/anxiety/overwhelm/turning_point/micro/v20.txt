The ninety-second grounding happens in the parking garage before she walks upstairs to the conference room — feet flat, press down, count to six out loud like the chapter said. A car door slams three levels below and she loses count at four. She starts again. Heel, ball, toe. Five. Then six. The deck is on her laptop in the bag. The room is one elevator ride away. Her hands are still cold but her breath has dropped one notch from shallow to something she can use. She does not wait until the activation disappears. She waits until her body has one piece of evidence that the floor is real. In the meeting she stumbles once on a transition and recovers without apologizing twice. Nobody notices. Her chest is still loud at minute twelve. It is not running the meeting anymore. Priya marks that as enough for today.
