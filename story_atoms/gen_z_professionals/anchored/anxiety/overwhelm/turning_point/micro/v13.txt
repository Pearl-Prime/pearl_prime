Nia brings the log to her third session. She opens her laptop and turns it so the therapist can see the columns. She explains the zero-to-ten scale and why she weighted contributing factors separately from the primary trigger. The therapist reads for a moment.

The therapist asks: "What is it like to feel the entries, rather than read them?"

Nia says, "I don't understand the question."

The therapist waits.

Nia says, "I guess I'm usually already in my head by the time I write it."

The therapist writes something down in her notebook. Nia watches her write it. She does not ask what it says.

On the walk home, Nia takes out her phone and opens the log. She does not add an entry. She just looks at it. It looks the same as it always has. Something about it is different anyway.
