The red circles arrive at 2:14pm — someone in the engineering channel typing we need this today in all caps. Her name is not in the thread; the fix is not her ticket. Priya reads anyway — line by line, twice — and feels her chest contract as if the caps lock were addressed to her ribcage. Her manager is in a separate window asking if she can join a bridge call in five minutes. She has not finished the task she was already doing; she will not remember where she left it. She joins the bridge with her camera off and listens to three people describe an outage she cannot see on her dashboard. Her breath climbs into her collarbones and stays there. She wants to help and there is nothing to hold — no ticket, no access, no assignment, just heat. When the call ends she opens the channel again even though nothing new has posted, and refreshes it twice more before catching her own hand in the act. The urgency in the room was somebody's job. The urgency in her body outlasted the room by twenty minutes and did not ask whether it belonged to her. By the time it fades she is too spent to find her way back into her own afternoon.
