The presentation starts in forty-three minutes. Suki is in the hallway outside Conference Room B, walking the same eighteen feet she walked the first time she gave this talk, and the second time, and the third. She has the slides memorized. She confirmed this at 7am by running through them in the shower without looking at anything. She knows this.

She has given hundreds of talks. Not dozens. Hundreds. The pacing does not scale down with the number. She walks the eighteen feet again and turns. She is not waiting for the slides to come back to her — she has those. She is not sure what she is waiting for. The hallway is the same hallway. The forty-three minutes is the same forty-three minutes. The hour before is always this, and she is starting to notice that it is always this.
