The calendar alarm fires at 6:47am and she does not get up. She lies still and opens the app on her phone. The first meeting is in seventy-three minutes. She does the math she always does: shower fifteen minutes, commute twenty-two minutes, coffee five minutes, buffer ten minutes — except the buffer is already gone because she is still in bed. She gets up without showering. She makes coffee with her phone propped against the kettle so she can read the attendee list for the 9am standup. Her chest tightens as if the meeting is happening now, in the kitchen, with wet hair and no slides prepared. She opens the deck, closes it, opens the calendar instead and stares at the cascade: 9am, 10am, 11:30am, 1pm, 2:30pm, 4pm. Each block compresses the one before it. By 7:15 she is dressed and still has not eaten. The spiral is not the calendar. The spiral is Priya's body living all six hours at once.
