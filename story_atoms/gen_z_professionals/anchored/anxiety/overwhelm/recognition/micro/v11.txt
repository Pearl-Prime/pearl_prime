Chris is home for Thanksgiving weekend. His mother is at the kitchen table with a mug in her hand, pouring from the carafe without looking up. She asks how work is going.

He says "really well, actually."

She says "that's good, honey" and sets the carafe down. She asks if he wants eggs. He says sure. The conversation moves into eggs and whether the grocery store on Peachtree was busy yesterday.

He stands near the counter and watches her crack two eggs into the pan. The word "actually" is still in the room somewhere. He put it there and he doesn't know what it was for. He meant something different by "really well." He does not know what that different thing is. He knows he meant it.
