The retrospective ends at 4:02pm with thank you everyone and a string of hand-wave emoji. Priya closes the laptop lid and stays in the chair, ears still holding the last comment — not about her, but about a deadline — while her mind replays it on a loop with her name inserted where it does not belong. She walks to the kitchen and fills a glass of water she does not drink. She opens the laptop again to read the notes doc. She closes it. She opens Slack to see if anyone followed up. Nobody has. She reads her own last message in the thread three times, checking it for a tone she did not intend and cannot find. The meeting is over. Nobody told her body. She stands at the counter feeling like she left something unfinished, though the agenda shows every item checked — and the feeling refuses to accept the agenda as evidence. The body is still performing a meeting that ended twenty-three minutes ago. By five she is more exhausted than the meeting could account for: a second shift, unpaid, performed on a stage no one else can see.
