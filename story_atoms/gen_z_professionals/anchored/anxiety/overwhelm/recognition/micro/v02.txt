Daniel's manager walks past his desk for the second time without looking
up. Last month she would stop. She would ask what he was working on in the
way that meant she was actually asking. Now she moves through the open
office the way people move through a space they are already past. He
watches her reach the glass conference room and close the door. His name
is not on the invite on the shared calendar. He knows because he checked
it this morning before anyone else arrived.
