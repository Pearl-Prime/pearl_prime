By the second Friday Wren has a name for the window and a ritual inside it. The paycheck lands at 6:02 and Wren watches it the way you watch something that is leaving — through the standup, through lunch, thumb going back to the app between messages. Screenshot the balance at its peak, because in four hours it won't be true. The autopays fire one by one and Wren feels each one leave, refreshing to confirm the drop, rent gone, phone gone, the minimum on the card gone, until the number is the small number again, the one that has to last. Then Wren keeps the app open anyway, watching a total that has stopped moving, doing the days-until-next-Friday math against a balance that already knows the answer.
