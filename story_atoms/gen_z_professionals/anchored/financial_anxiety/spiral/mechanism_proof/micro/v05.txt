It's 1:40 a.m. and Kofi is doing the math again. The transfer is a fixed thing now, a floor he will not touch — his mother's word "small-small" sitting in a voice note he keeps replaying. So the number that moves is his own. Rent is fixed. The transfer is fixed. What flexes is him: the lunch skipped, the ride not taken, the dentist pushed another month. He recalculates, and each pass the remainder for himself shrinks, and each pass he finds it, somehow, again.
