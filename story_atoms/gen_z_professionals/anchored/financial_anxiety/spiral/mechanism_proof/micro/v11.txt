The deposit hits at 6:02 a.m. and Quinn is awake to watch it land. For ninety seconds the number has a comma in it and she looks at it like weather. Then she starts subtracting out loud in the dark — rent, the card minimum, the loan, the forty she still owes for March. By the time she reaches the storage unit she never canceled, the comma is gone. She checks again at 6:40, and on the train, and once before she badges in, each time already knowing the number.
