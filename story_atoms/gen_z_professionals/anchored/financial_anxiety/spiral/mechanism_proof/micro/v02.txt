Nia has started counting sleep as a budget line. If she takes the 6 a.m. airport run she loses two hours, but that's nineteen dollars, and nineteen dollars is most of the phone bill. She does this arithmetic on the couch at 1 a.m., the delivery app open, the freelance invoice open, a third tab loading. Her friends stopped inviting her; she is always working, or too tired from it. She calls it discipline. Three apps open and still behind, and the being-behind is what keeps her reaching for the phone.
