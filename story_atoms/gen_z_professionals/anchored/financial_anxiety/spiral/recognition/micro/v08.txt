The raise hit the account eleven days ago and Lena has the spreadsheet open again, the one she rebuilt the night it landed. The new number is in the top cell, bigger, black, correct. Her cursor sits blinking in the projection row where it always sits. She adds four hundred to the monthly transfer, deletes it, types three-fifty. Same tightness behind the sternum as the old number gave her, the exact same. She keeps scrolling down to a total that never arrives at a word like enough.
