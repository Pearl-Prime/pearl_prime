Devon is three sips into the eight-dollar oat-milk latte when the number starts. Eight dollars, and the flat white Priya just bought her, and the tip he rounded up because the barista saw him hovering — sixteen-fifty, gone, and they have not sat down yet. Priya is telling him about her sister. He is nodding at the right places. Under the nodding a second column runs, adding, and he is doing it against a rent number he already knows by heart.
