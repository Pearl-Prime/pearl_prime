The delivery app is open before Nia has decided to open it, a $5.25 offer counting down under her thumb. This time she doesn't tap accept. She says the real number out loud in the quiet car — not the nineteen dollars, the sleep — and lets the offer expire. She drives home with nothing scheduled after, which feels less like rest than a debt she forgot to pay. She puts the phone in the glovebox and sits in the driveway a minute, learning the shape of an hour she is not selling.
