Devon is halfway through pricing the dinner — whose turn, what he can skip Sunday to cover it — when he stops and puts his phone face-down on his thigh. He does not do the division. Nasir is telling him about the job that fell through and Devon just listens to it, the whole thing, without the second column adding underneath. The number is still there. He can feel it waiting at the edge. He leaves it there and asks Nasir what he is going to do now, and means only the question.
