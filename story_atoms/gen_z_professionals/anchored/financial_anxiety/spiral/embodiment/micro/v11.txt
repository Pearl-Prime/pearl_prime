The deposit lands and Quinn does the thing she read about, which feels stupid: she leaves the phone face-down on the counter and boils water for oatmeal instead. She can feel the number across the room, pulling at her thumb like a held breath. She eats standing up. She rinses the bowl. The balance has not changed and she has not checked it, both true at 7:04 when she finally turns the phone over — not because the number moved, but because it's time to leave. She reads it once. She pockets it.
