Theo opens the spreadsheet at 1 a.m. out of habit, the rent cell already selected. He looks at the number. He does not retype it. He scrolls to savings, reads it once, and lets it be the number it is. Then he closes the laptop with the total still on screen, unchecked, unproven. His teeth are unclenched. Nothing is solved — rent is still most of it — but he plugs in his phone, turns it face down, and lies in the dark without the arithmetic running.
