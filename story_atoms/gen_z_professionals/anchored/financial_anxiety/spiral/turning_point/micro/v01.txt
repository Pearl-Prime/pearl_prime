A coworker, splitting the bill, watches Theo do the four-way math three times on his phone and says, easy: 'Dude, the number isn't going to move. You already know it.' Theo laughs it off and pays. But that night, sheet open, rent cell selected, his thumb hovers over the arrow key and doesn't press it. He sits with the total just sitting there, unrecalculated, the cursor blinking. His shoulders are up by his ears. He notices they are, and can't say why that's new.
