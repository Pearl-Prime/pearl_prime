Quinn's roommate leaves a takeout container in the sink and says, easy, just Venmo me whenever. Quinn hears her own voice come out too fast — no, now, let me do it now — thumbing the app open before the sentence is done. Her roommate looks at her, not unkindly: it's eleven dollars, Quinn. It can wait. And Quinn cannot remember the last time she let eleven dollars wait, when waiting itself started to feel like the edge of the drop. She sends it anyway. But she does not close the app right away.
