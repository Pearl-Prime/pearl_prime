Ash misses a deadline — actually misses it — and the client just says, "no rush, next week's fine, your stuff's always worth the wait." Ash reads it three times. There's no bill for the panic, no penalty, no proof the danger was ever real. Ash starts to type an over-apology, deletes it, sits with the cursor blinking in the empty reply. The runway math is still open in the next tab. For a second Ash doesn't switch to it, and the not-switching feels strange, almost like standing on a floor that was there the whole time.
