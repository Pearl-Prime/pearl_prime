Her sister, who has never once beaten a budget, glances at the open laptop and says, "You got the raise you wanted and you look worse. Maybe the math was never the thing keeping you up." Lena laughs it off, says the market's insane, closes the tab. But loading the dishwasher after, she stops with a bowl in her hand, because for the first time the number going up and the feeling not moving are sitting in the same room, and she can't make them explain each other.
