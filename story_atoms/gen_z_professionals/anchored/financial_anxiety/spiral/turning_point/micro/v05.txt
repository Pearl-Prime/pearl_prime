A coworker sees Kofi decline dinner for the third Friday and doesn't let it slide. "You send money home every month?" Kofi nods, ready for the admiration. Instead the coworker says quietly, "My dad did that for fifteen years. Nobody ever asked what it cost him. I'm asking you." Kofi laughs it off, says it's nothing, changes the subject to the bill. But on the train home he catches himself not doing the conversion for once, just watching the dark windows go by.
