Twenty-two years in, Denise still runs her classroom the way she always
has, but the classroom is no longer the job. There is the new-teacher
mentoring she never applied for and can't say no to, the department chair
duties nobody trained her for, the accreditation binder that grows by a
tab every month. She has an index card taped inside her desk drawer with
this week's non-teaching tasks; she stopped counting how many cards have
filled that drawer over the years. She finds herself proud, some days, of
how much she carries. Other days she finds herself just carrying it,
proud nowhere in the feeling at all.
