Denise tells the incoming principal she'll mentor one new teacher this
year, not two, and does not offer a justification for the number. The
principal says that's fine before Denise has finished bracing for
pushback. She adds an index card to the drawer that isn't a task list —
just a line: "one is a full job." She doesn't take it out to look at it
again. She doesn't need to. She already knows the binder will still get
done, on the same timeline it always has, without the second mentee
riding on top of it this time.
