On Sunday, Rachel writes three things on a sticky note and puts the rest
of the list in a drawer, closed, not thrown away — she isn't ready for
that yet. The IEP meeting. The observation feedback email. One stack of
papers, the front-seat stack only. Everything else waits. The curriculum
map is not on the note. She feels the old alarm about that, faint but
present, and answers it out loud in the empty kitchen: "It'll still be
there Monday." It is still there Monday. She starts the three things
anyway.
