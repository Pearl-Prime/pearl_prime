The trunk stack slides forward when Rachel brakes hard for a light, and
one folder splits open across the back seat — six weeks of a single
student's late work, all of it stapled, none of it graded, all of it now
irrelevant because the grading period closed Friday. She pulls over. She
does not cry. She sits with the folder in her lap and does the math on
what those six weeks actually cost: not the student's grade, which the
school will absorb somehow, but the version of her that thought carrying
it all quietly was the same thing as doing the job well. She drives home
and does not open the trunk that night, or explain to anyone why.
