One of the new teachers Denise mentors breaks down crying in her
classroom after school, overwhelmed by exactly the load Denise has spent
two decades absorbing without a word. Denise sits with her and hears
herself say, "You don't have to carry all of it to be good at this." She
says it to comfort the younger teacher. Driving home, she realizes she
has never once said that sentence to herself, and is not sure, twenty-two
years in, whether she believes it applies to her the way it applied in
that classroom. She doesn't resolve the question that night. She notices,
for the first time in a while, that it's a question.
