The department chair asks Marcus to take on the testing coordinator role
too, framed as a compliment — "you're the one who always makes it work."
He opens his mouth to say yes and instead hears himself ask, "What would
I stop doing to take that on?" The chair doesn't have an answer ready.
Neither does Marcus, which surprises him more than the question did. He
says he'll think about it and actually does, on the drive home, for the
first time letting the question sit instead of resolving it fast. He
still doesn't know what he'll say. That not-knowing feels different from
the usual immediate yes — slower, and less like relief.
