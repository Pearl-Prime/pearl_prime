By November, Rachel has a system: the papers that matter go in the front
seat, the papers that matter less go in the back seat, and the papers she
has decided don't matter go in the trunk, where they will stay until June.
She has stopped telling herself this is temporary. The trunk is a decision
she makes fresh every week and calls an accident every time her husband
asks about it. She has missed two dentist appointments and rescheduled
her own physical twice. Both times she told the receptionist something
came up at school. Nothing came up. The week was simply the same size
it always is, and she was, again, smaller than it.
