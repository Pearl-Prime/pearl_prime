Marcus keeps a mental ledger of the yeses, and by February the ledger has
a shape he doesn't examine too closely: tutoring Mondays and Wednesdays,
yearbook Thursdays, coverage whenever coverage is needed, and grading
somewhere in whatever is left, which lately is late at night with a
basketball game playing muted on his phone for company. His girlfriend
asks when they're going to make dinner plans that don't get cancelled. He
says next week, meaning it, and next week a new thing arrives that he
says yes to before he's finished hearing it. He has started to like being
the person who's always available. He has not asked himself what he's
available instead of being.
