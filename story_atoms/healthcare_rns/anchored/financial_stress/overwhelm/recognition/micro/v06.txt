Colleen keeps the Post-it in the inside pocket of her scrubs, folded twice so the number doesn't show through the fabric. On break she smooths it flat against the med cart and does the subtraction again — years left times what she can put away, minus what breaks. The answer is the same answer it was yesterday. She writes it a fourth time in smaller digits, as if a smaller number takes up less of the future, then folds it back into her chest and clocks in.
