The phone lights up on the breakroom table, an 855 number Amara doesn't recognize, and her thumb moves to Decline before the second ring, the way it silences a monitor alarm she has already assessed. She has three of these numbers memorized now, the way an area code can mean a hospital and mean a collector. In recovery she titrates a stranger's pain to zero. Her own account went to collections in March. She turns the phone face-down and calls the next patient back.
