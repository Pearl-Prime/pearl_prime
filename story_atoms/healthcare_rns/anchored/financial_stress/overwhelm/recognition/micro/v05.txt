The Zelle screen is already open when Yesenia sits down in her car after a twelve. She sends the amount to her mother first, the way she always does, before the pump even reads full. Then her son's therapist, the copay, second. Only after both go through does she let herself look at the glovebox, where the other envelopes live — the gas company, the card, the one from her own hospital with its own logo on it. She does not open the glovebox. She starts the car.
