The glovebox is open before Yesenia has decided to open it. She does not pay anything yet. She takes the envelopes out, all of them, and lays them on the passenger seat in the parking-garage light, face up, in a row — the gas company, the card, the hospital that signs her checks. This is the part she has never done: let the faceless ones have a face for one minute. Not paid. Not fixed. Just no longer in the dark where a stranger keeps the light on for her.
