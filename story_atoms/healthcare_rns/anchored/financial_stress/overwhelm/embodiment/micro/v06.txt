The statement envelope has been on the counter nine days, unopened, the pile behind it thicker. Tonight Colleen doesn't recalculate first. She takes the top one, slides a finger under the flap before the pocket-square math can start, and reads the actual figure — lower than the folded number, higher than nothing. She sets it in a real stack, opened. The pocket still has its Post-it. But one true number is now on the table instead of a copied one, and she leaves the light on.
