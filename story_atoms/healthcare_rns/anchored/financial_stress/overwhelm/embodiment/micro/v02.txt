The job board is open before Dev has decided to open it, the accept button already under his thumb on a contract two states away. This time he does not tap it. He says the number out loud — the real one, the sleep he is selling for eleven dollars — and lets the quiet Saturday be a Saturday instead of a hole. He books the closer contract that pays less, and then, still not sure it is allowed, he puts the phone in a drawer and closes it.
