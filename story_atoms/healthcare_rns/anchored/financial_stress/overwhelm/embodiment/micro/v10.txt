The odometer is lit and his phone is already up when Beau stops in the driveway. This time he says it to the windshield — I'm counting because nobody's going to count for me. He lets the sentence sit. Then he sets the phone in the cupholder, face-down, and drives the 41 miles to the Hollis place without photographing a single number — not solved, not raised a band, just one route he takes without doing their arithmetic first.
