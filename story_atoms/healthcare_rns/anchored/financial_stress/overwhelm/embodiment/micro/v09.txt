The float list pings with a twelfth visit and Grace's thumb is already moving toward accept. This time she says it to the empty car — I'm booking because four visits feels like drowning. She reads the number on the phone: eleven. She lets the twelfth go to someone else and sits a moment before turning the key, hands loose in her lap. Not a fixed week. Not a safe one.
