The 855 comes on her lunch and Amara's thumb is already there. This time she lets it ring once, twice, watches the screen the way she watches a rhythm she's decided not to treat yet. She doesn't answer. She doesn't decline. She sets the phone face-up on the table and eats, the call going where calls go — not the debt paid, not the shift lighter, just one number she let exist in the room without racing it to zero first.
