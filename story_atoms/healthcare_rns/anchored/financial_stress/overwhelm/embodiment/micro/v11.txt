Someone mentions a cruise and the arithmetic starts up under Priyanka's ribs, familiar as a pulse. This time she catches the how-do-they-do-it in her throat before it becomes the shift. She sets her own number down on the counter, just to look at it: what she carries alone, out loud, only to herself. Then she asks Danielle which port. Not comparing — asking. Her coffee is still warm. The tally is still there. She just isn't the one it's measuring today.
