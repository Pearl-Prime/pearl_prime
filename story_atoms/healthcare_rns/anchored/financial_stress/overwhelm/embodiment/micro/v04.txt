The staffing text comes at 6:50 — OT, tonight. His thumb finds the keyboard the old way. This time Marcus reads the message twice and sets the phone face-down on the counter without answering. His wife is at the table. He sits down across from her, still in his coat, and does not pick the phone back up. It buzzes once more and goes quiet. Somebody else will take it. Not fixed, not caught up — just one night he is in the room he is paying for.
