The app is in her hand again before she has decided to reach for it. This time Nora says the sentence out loud, barely a whisper so it won't carry: I'm checking because I want to feel safe. The number is the same. But naming the want instead of the number, she sets the phone face-down on the nightstand — not fixed, not cured, just one night she meets the reach a single breath before it becomes the whole night.
