In the break room a nurse he trained years ago watches Marcus sign up for a third double and says, quiet, You keep buying everybody time except you. Marcus says the mortgage does not pause and turns back to the board. But scrubbing in, hands under the water longer than the count needs, he notices his shoulders have dropped a half inch off his ears, and he cannot say when they last were. The water runs warm over his wrists. He does not reach to shut it off yet.
