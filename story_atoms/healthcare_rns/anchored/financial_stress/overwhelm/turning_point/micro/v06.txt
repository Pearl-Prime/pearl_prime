A newer nurse, maybe thirty, sees Colleen unfold the square at the med cart and asks, plainly, not unkind, what she keeps checking. Colleen says it's nothing, just a reminder, and slides it away. But the word reminder catches on the way out. She stands at the cart with the empty pill cup in her hand a beat too long, aware for the first time that a reminder is something you look at once — not something you copy forty times so your hands have somewhere to put the fear.
