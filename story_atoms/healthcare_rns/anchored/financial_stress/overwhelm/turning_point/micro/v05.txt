A patient's daughter presses forty dollars into Yesenia's hand at discharge, for coffee, for the nurses, please. Yesenia says she can't, it's policy, and means it. Driving home she catches herself doing the sort: this forty would cover the gas bill, the real gas bill, the faceless one. Then she notices she gave it back without a second of the freeze that grips her at her own glovebox. She sits at the green light until it turns red. Somebody behind her leans on the horn and she still can't say what just moved.
