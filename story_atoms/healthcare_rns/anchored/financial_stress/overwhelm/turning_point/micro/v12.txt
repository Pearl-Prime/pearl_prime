A staffing nurse Sam has never really talked to watches him refresh the tab a fourth time in the med room and says, quietly, "You know per-diem means they carry the risk, not you — you just picked up the whole hospital's uncertainty for free." Sam laughs it off and signs up for the shift anyway. But walking to his car he notices his thumb has stopped, mid-reach, the phone dark in his hand, and he stands in the lot a second longer than he needs to before he can't say why.
