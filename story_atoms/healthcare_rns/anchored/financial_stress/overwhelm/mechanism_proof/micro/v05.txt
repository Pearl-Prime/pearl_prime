Yesenia can name every dollar that has a face attached to it. Her mother's electric. Marco's Tuesday sessions, the ones that finally got him talking. The envelopes with no face she cannot touch — they have sat in the glovebox six weeks now, and she has stopped counting them the way she stopped counting the sharps she disposes of. The late fees compound in the dark of the glovebox. She keeps paying the faces. The faceless ones keep the light on for someone she will never picture, and so they wait, and so they grow.
