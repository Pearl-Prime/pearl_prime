Sam picks up the Saturday night nobody wants because it fills the last gap in next week's grid, and the grid filling is the only thing that lets his shoulders come down. He has not eaten since noon. His phone is at nineteen percent and he is rationing it for the app, not for calls. A coworker offers him half a sandwich and he waves it off, thumb already dragging the schedule down to see if a day-shift dropped. The gap closes. Another one opens two weeks out.
