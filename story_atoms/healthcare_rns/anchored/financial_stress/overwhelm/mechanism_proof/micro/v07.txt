Amara extubates a man whose surgery cost less than her own did, and stays with him until his numbers hold. The unknown 877 comes twice during the shift; both times her thumb finds Decline through the glove. By the third call she has stopped hearing it as a ring and started hearing it as a task completed. The gap it leaves is the size of a sentence she cannot say on this floor: the nurse can't afford the care the nurse gives. So she doesn't say it. She charts.
