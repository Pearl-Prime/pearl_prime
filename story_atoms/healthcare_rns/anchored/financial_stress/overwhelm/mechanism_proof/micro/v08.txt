The swap board is Thanh's real budget. She has the color-codes memorized: yellow shifts pay the car, green covers the loan, the red overtime blocks are the ones she stacks so nothing bounces. It is 5 a.m. and she is picking up her fourth double this week because a coworker's name opened a slot. Her hands smell of sanitizer through two washes. The overtime that was supposed to buy her air has become the thing that leaves none.
