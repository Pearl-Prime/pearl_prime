After his shift Rafi sits in the parked car in the garage before going up, engine off, hands on the wheel. He used to walk straight in carrying it. Now he gives himself the four red lights on the drive to run the calls back, and when he pulls into the space he says the last patient's outcome out loud, once, and is done with them. His hand still checks his hip for the shears on the elevator. But tonight he notices it happen, lets the hand come back down, and goes in.
