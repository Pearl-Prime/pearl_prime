The search bar is open again, 3 a.m., the old reflex. This time Marco doesn't type a job. He types the true sentence — I'm exhausted, not broken — reads it once, and closes the browser instead of the feeling. He gets a glass of water he actually drinks. Nothing is decided; the shifts are still there Monday. But he said the word tired to himself in his own kitchen without dressing it as a career, and goes back down the hall a little lighter for having stopped pretending it was research.
