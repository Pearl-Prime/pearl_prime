The next patient is scared and Denise reaches for the cadence, the quick-and-beautiful line already loaded. This time she doesn't run it. She sets the clipboard down, looks at the man's actual face, and says, "This one's rough. I'll stay till it's in." It isn't smoother than the script. It is slower, and her chest tightens saying it, an ache where the nothing usually sits. She gets the line in the same as always. But she was in the room this time, and she stands a second after, not charting yet, the ache still there.
