Room 6 goes bad on a Thursday and the drive home starts the way it always starts — the tempo rising, the hands wanting the wheel. This time Junior says it into the dark car, low: "That was Mr. Adigwe. He was seventy-three and I did everything right and he still died." He names him instead of counting him. The hands find the wheel once, then rest. He is not okay. But he pulls into the garage and sits one minute, not eleven, and names one thing he did well before he goes up.
