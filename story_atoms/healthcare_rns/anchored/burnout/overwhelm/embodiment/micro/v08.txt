The cot is right there again, fourth hour of the night, and the old rule stands up in her: sitting is for people who sleep. This time Bett doesn't argue it. She sets a nine-minute timer, lies down in her shoes, and puts one hand flat on her own sternum the way she'd steady a patient. She doesn't sleep. The floor is still short. But for nine minutes she is a body being kept, not spent, and when the timer goes she stands without announcing to anyone how little she got.
