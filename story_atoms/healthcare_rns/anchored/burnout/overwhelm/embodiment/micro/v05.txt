The drawing is still there. Tam does not take it down and does not turn it toward the wall. Standing at the fridge with the milk in her hand, she lets her eyes go where they have not gone in months — the purple house, the sun, the dog bigger than the house — on purpose, for the length of one breath. Then she closes the door. Not healed, not done grieving him — just one morning she lets the picture be a picture, and pours the milk, and goes to work.
