Room 9, a frightened man before a scan, and Hale does the level voice and the moving hands as always. Then, before she can decide against it, she stops at the door and turns back. She does not manufacture a feeling she doesn't have. She just stays four seconds longer than the task requires and lets herself notice his actual face, the specific fear in it. Nothing floods in. But she names it silently — this is a person — and walks out having reached, once, toward the thing she stopped reaching for.
