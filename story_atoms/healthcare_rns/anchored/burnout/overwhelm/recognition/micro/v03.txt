Elena scans her badge at a unit she has never worked. The doors open on a floor plan she has to learn from scratch: which hall holds the crash cart, which nurse to ask, which patients are hers for the next twelve hours. She stands at the assignment board and copies six room numbers onto her palm in pen, the way she does every shift, because there is no report she gets to keep. Somewhere a monitor alarms. She turns toward it and cannot tell if it is her patient.
