It's 4:40 a.m. and the break room is empty except for Bett, who has not sat down. Thirteenth hour. The cot is right there, the twenty minutes are hers, but she eats crackers standing over the sink, because sitting is for people who might sleep. When the new grad asks how she does the nights, Bett hears herself answer like it's a rank: "You get used to running on nothing." She means it as instruction. It lands in her chest as a fact she can't take back.
