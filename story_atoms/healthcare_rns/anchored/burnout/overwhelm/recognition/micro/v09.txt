Coop has been in the apartment eleven days and the duffel is still packed, standing by the door in its go-position, scrub-mesh laundry bag zipped inside. He tells himself it's efficient. He knows the codes for four airports and cannot remember the name of the patient who coded on him Tuesday. Tonight he lies on top of the made bed with his shoes near the frame, phone lit, already three tabs deep in next-contract listings, and he cannot say what he is looking for, only that it is not here.
