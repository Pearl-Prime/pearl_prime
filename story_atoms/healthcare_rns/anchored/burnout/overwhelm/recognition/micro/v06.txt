Denise starts the bag of saline, tapes the line, and tells the frightened woman in bed nine the same thing she has told a thousand beds — this part is quick, you're doing beautifully — and the sentences come out in the exact warm cadence they always have. The woman's shoulders drop. Denise watches her own hands finish the tape, neat, sure, twenty-five years sure, and feels nothing move behind them. She has said the words. She was not in the room when she said them.
