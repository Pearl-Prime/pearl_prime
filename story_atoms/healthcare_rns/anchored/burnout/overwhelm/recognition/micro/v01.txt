Priya's relief nurse has been at the board for six minutes. Report is done, the pager belongs to someone else now. Priya is still walking the hall. She stops outside 12, a patient she signed off forty minutes ago, and reads the monitor through the glass anyway. The numbers are fine. She knew they would be. Her feet carry her to 14 next, then 15, the same loop she runs every night after she is technically no longer here.
