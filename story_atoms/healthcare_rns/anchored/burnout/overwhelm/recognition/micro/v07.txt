Rafi has been home for two hours. The apartment is quiet, his boots are off, but his right hand keeps going to his hip, patting for the shears that live on his scrubs. There is nothing on his hip. A car door slams outside and his shoulders load, his weight shifts onto the balls of his feet, ready to move toward it. Nobody is coding. He sets the glass down, untouched.
