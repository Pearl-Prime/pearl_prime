It's 3:11 a.m. and Marco is standing in the kitchen with the lights off, thumb-typing into the phone's search bar. He deletes it and types it smaller: jobs no patients. The results load — logistics, insurance, medical coding from home. He does not tap any of them. He reads the titles the way he reads a monitor he isn't allowed to leave, standing there in his scrub bottoms and one sock, until the screen dims itself. Then he taps it bright again, and reads the same six titles once more.
