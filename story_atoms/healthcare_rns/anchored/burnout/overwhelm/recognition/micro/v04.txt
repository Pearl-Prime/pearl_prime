Bed 6 loses his patient at 4:40 a.m. Wes charts the time, then his thumb goes to the inside of his own wrist and presses, counting — not the pulse, the number. Forty-one. He has never written it anywhere; it lives behind his sternum like a second badge. The family is still in the hallway. Wes walks past them toward the supply room, restocks a drawer that does not need restocking, and lines the flushes up so their labels all face the same way.
