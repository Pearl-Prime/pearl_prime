At home the drawing is still on the fridge — a purple house, a sun in the wrong corner, a dog bigger than the house. Tam eats facing the other counter now. She has started taking the long way through her own kitchen. When her sister asks about the boy from March, Tam hears herself list his labs, his gases, his last set of vitals, clean and in order, the way you give report. Her sister goes quiet. Tam does not notice she has stopped saying his name.
