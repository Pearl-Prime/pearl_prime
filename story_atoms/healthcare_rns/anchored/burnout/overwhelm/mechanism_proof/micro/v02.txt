It runs three times on the drive now, then four. Junior takes the long way home so the reps have room to finish. At a red light he mouths the compression count to no one — and-two-and-three — watching the crosswalk timer tick like a code clock. He does not cry. He notices he has stopped being able to. In the garage he sits with the engine off for eleven minutes and can't name one thing he did today, only the ones that ended. He calls it decompressing. He tells no one.
