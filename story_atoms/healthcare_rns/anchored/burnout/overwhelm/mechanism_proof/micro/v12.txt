The new grad on days keeps crying in the med room and Hale has started to find it unprofessional. She teaches her the trick without calling it that: keep the voice level, keep the hands moving, let the name become a room number before it can reach anything. When a man Hale has cared for a week dies on her shift she notes the time, calls the family, and eats her sandwich in the break room afterward, on schedule. She has begun to call the flatness competence.
