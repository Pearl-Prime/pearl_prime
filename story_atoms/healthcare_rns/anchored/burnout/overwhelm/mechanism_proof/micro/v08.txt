On her Tuesday off, the alarm she forgot to cancel fires at 5:40 and Bett is already awake, eyes open, counting who's short today. The house is quiet and her body won't believe it. She makes coffee she doesn't want because her hands need the four-a.m. shape of a morning. Her daughter says, "you're off, Mom, come sit," and Bett says "in a sec" and keeps wiping a counter that's already clean. Off doesn't reach her. She's started wearing that as proof she's the one who can be counted on.
