Report is at 0700 and a new grad is asking Marco how he stays so calm. He walks her through the potassium, steady, the voice everyone borrows. On his break he sits in the stairwell and opens the search bar. Salary for phlebotomy. Do coders need a degree. He never finishes reading a page; his eyes slide off the words the way water leaves glass. Fourteen years of knowing what to do, and here his thumb hovers, and nothing in him will let the sentence I want out finish forming, even alone, even here.
