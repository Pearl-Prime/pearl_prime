Three days off, and Wes is at his kid's soccer game when the number arrives at forty-three. He did not summon it. A boy goes down mid-field, gets up laughing, and Wes has already clocked the fall, the angle, the airway — filed it before the whistle. His wife says something about orange slices. He nods at the right place. On the drive home he cannot remember the score, only that he ran the count again at a stoplight, thumb to wrist, the way other men check a watch.
