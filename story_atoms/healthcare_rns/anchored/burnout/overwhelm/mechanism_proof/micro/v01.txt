It is 7:52 and Priya's car is the last one under the lot lights. She has driven this exit ramp a hundred times; tonight she cannot make the turn onto the road. She keeps the engine running and scrolls the unit census on her phone, counting beds that are no longer her count. Someone texts asking if she left. She reads it twice and does not answer. Her hands are on the wheel. The clock changes and she does not notice it change.
