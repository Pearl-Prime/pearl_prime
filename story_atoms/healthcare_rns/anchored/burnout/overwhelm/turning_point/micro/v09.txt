His contract ends Friday and for once Coop has not signed the next one. The stillness of an unbooked week arrives and he cannot power down inside it; he cleans the counters twice at 3 a.m. A per-diem tech he barely knows watches him restock a cart nobody asked for and says, quiet, "You know the running's not resting, right." Coop says something about being efficient. But he doesn't reach for the phone that night, and lies in the dark noticing the duffel by the door the way you notice a held breath.
