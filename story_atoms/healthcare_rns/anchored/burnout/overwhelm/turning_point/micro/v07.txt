His daughter is telling him about a lizard at recess and Rafi realizes he has heard none of it — that he has been listening past her to the hallway for a sound that isn't coming. She stops. "You do the thing," she says, "where your eyes go somewhere." He starts to explain the shift, then doesn't. He crouches to her level, which puts his back to the door, and for one breath he lets it stay there. His shoulders don't drop. But he asks her the lizard's name, and means it.
