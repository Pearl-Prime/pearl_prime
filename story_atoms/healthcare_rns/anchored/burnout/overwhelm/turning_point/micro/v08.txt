A tech twenty years her junior is charting beside Bett at 3 a.m. and says, not unkindly, "You talk about being tired like it's your name." Bett starts to explain the census, the holes in the schedule. The tech shrugs: "I'm not saying you're not tired. I'm saying you introduce yourself with it." Bett mutters something about coffee and turns back to the monitor. But on the drive home she catches herself before the usual sentence — the one about running on nothing — and lets the silence sit instead.
