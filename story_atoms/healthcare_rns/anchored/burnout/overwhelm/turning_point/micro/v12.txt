An old patient remembers her from four years back, before. "You held my hand the whole time," he says, delighted. "You cried a little. I never forgot it." Hale says something warm and level and it costs her nothing, and that is the part that catches — she can picture the nurse he means and cannot feel her way back to being her. She finishes the round on time. But at the med station she stands with her hand flat on the cool metal, a second too long, waiting for something she can't name.
