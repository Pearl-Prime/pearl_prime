The night charge, forty years in, finds Priya redoing a handoff sheet she already gave away. She doesn't take it from her. She says, "You built this floor to run without you. That's the job. Standing here proves you did it wrong." Priya says the assignment looked heavy and keeps writing. But in the elevator, going down instead of back, she notices her thumb is not moving toward the census, and the quiet where the counting was feels less like danger.
