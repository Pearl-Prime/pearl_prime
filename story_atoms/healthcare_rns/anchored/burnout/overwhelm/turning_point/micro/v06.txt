A new grad shadowing her asks, after the family leaves, "How do you stay so calm? I felt everything in there." Denise starts to say you learn to hold it, the line she has handed down for years, and it stops in her throat. She is not holding anything. There is nothing in her hands to hold. She sends the girl to chart vitals and turns to the sink. Scrubbing longer than the count needs, she notices the water warm on her wrists, that she can feel it, and can't say when she last did.
