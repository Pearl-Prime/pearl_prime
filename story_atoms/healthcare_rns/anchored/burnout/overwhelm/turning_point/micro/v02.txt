The night charge nurse walks Junior to his car because he is standing in the lot, not moving. She doesn't tell him it gets easier. She says, "Nobody debriefed you. You've carried every one of them home alone in that car — that's not weak, that's nobody catching you." Junior says he's fine, gets in. But at the first red light his hands stay flat on his thighs instead of the wheel, and the count doesn't start, and the quiet where it usually runs feels less like relief than something he's allowed to set down.
