A resident he trained, now attending, catches him tallying at the shared workstation. She doesn't ask what the number is. "You keep a ledger," she says, "but you only ever enter the debits." Then she's paged and gone. Wes sits with his thumb still on his wrist. He has, he realizes, no column for the woman in Bed 6 whose grandchild he coached through CPR at 2 a.m., who lived. He starts to add her and doesn't know where she goes.
