A retired medic bags groceries beside Marco and asks, flat, not fishing: "You still in it, or you just haven't said it out loud yet?" Marco laughs, says he's fine, loads the eggs. But in the truck he doesn't start the engine. He gets the phone out and, for the first time, types the whole thing into the bar — I want to leave nursing — and just looks at it sitting there, real, spelled out, his thumb not deleting it, the cursor blinking after the word like it's waiting for him.
