Sarah sends an email to her three direct reports on a Tuesday morning. She lists four projects and writes: I'm handing these off. Here's the owner for each. Bring me decisions, not status. She does not apologize for the handoff. She does not over-explain. Her hands are warm on the keyboard. The chest is open. One report writes back with a clarifying question. Sarah answers in two sentences. She does not offer to do the thing herself if it gets hard. At 12:30 she goes to lunch. Alone. She sits at the counter and eats a sandwich and does not look at her phone. The shoulders are down. She is not drowning today. She is delivering less and the delivery is sharper. She pays the check and walks back. She is not late. She is not early. She is on time for what she actually owns.
