Elena picks her daughter up at 3:42 on a Thursday. They drive to the grocery store. They buy lemons and a rotisserie chicken and a bag of arugula. Her phone is in her bag. She has not checked it since 3:00. Her daughter is talking about a girl in her class. Elena listens to the whole story. She asks one follow-up question. Her shoulders are not at her ears. The throat is open. They get home and her daughter does her homework at the table and Elena makes the salad. The phone vibrates in the bag. Elena finishes the salad. She checks the phone at 6:00 like she said she would. The thing was not urgent. It will keep until tomorrow. She closes the phone. The afternoon has felt long in a way she had forgotten afternoons could feel.
