Sarah opens the calendar request from a former colleague. Pick your brain over coffee, an hour, Tuesday. She reads it twice. Her chest tightens out of habit. Then she does something she has not done in three years. She declines. She types: I'm not taking on advisory coffees right now. If you want a structured consult, my rate is $400 for the hour and I'm booking out four weeks. She rereads it. She sends it before she can soften it. Her hands are cold for thirty seconds and then they are not. The colleague replies an hour later: totally understand, will hold off for now. The world does not end. Sarah looks at the email. The throat is open. She has said the word rate out loud in writing for the first time. She blocks her Tuesday morning back. She labels it: writing.
