Elena writes one line in her calendar: core hours 9 to 3. She blocks the rest. She sends a note to her team: I'll respond to non-urgent things in core hours. For urgent, text me. Her hands are cold when she sends it. The throat is tight. Two people reply right away with thumbs up. One person replies with a question that is not urgent. Elena does not answer it until 9:04 the next morning. Her shoulders drop a quarter inch. At 3:15 she closes the laptop. Her daughter is in the kitchen doing homework. Elena sits down at the table. She does not open her phone. She asks about the band trip form. They sign it together. Her shoulders come down another quarter inch. The two things she wants to do perfectly are still incompatible. She has just stopped pretending otherwise.
