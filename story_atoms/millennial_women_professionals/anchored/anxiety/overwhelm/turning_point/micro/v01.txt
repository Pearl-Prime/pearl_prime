Carmen books fifteen minutes with her director on a Tuesday. She types it into the calendar invite: visibility on the operating model framework. She does not soften it. Her stomach drops. Her hands are cold when she clicks send. The director accepts in two minutes. In the meeting Carmen says: I designed the framework. I want my name on the next deck and the next walkthrough. The director looks at the screen for a beat. Then he says, that's fair. He adds it to his notes. He says he'll update the deck himself. Carmen leaves the meeting and her hands are still cold but her shoulders have come down half an inch. Invisibility is a tax on competence, she thinks, walking to refill her water. She had to send the invoice herself. The next deck has her name on slide one.
