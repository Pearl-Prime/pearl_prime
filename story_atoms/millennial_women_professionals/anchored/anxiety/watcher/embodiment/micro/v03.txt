Sarah sends the Monday email. It hands four projects to her three reports with named owners and a one-line context for each. She does not soften the handoff with apologies. She does not promise to "stay close" or "be available for questions any time." She writes: I will check in on Friday. The watcher reads the email and does not flag the missing softeners. The watcher has, over the last three weeks, slowly gotten the message that softeners are not always required. Two reports thank her by Tuesday. One pushes back. Sarah meets with that one for fifteen minutes, listens to the concern, holds the boundary, ends the meeting on time. The chest stays at neutral throughout. By Friday the projects are running. Sarah eats lunch away from her desk for the third time this week. She watches the watcher try to file the lunches in the worry column and watches the watcher fail because there is no worry to attach. The lunches are going in a different column now. The watcher does not yet have a name for that column. Sarah is letting it stay unnamed. The body knows what the column is. That has turned out to be enough.
