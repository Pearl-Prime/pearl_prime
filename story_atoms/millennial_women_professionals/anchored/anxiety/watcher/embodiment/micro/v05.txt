Rachel is in the partnership meeting. It is the meeting she has been preparing for, in some sense, since her first year as an associate. The senior partner looks across the table. Rachel says: I have brought in 1.2 million in new client revenue this year. I have resolved two complex cases ahead of timeline. I am ready for the partnership track conversation. Her voice is steady. She is not asking permission. She is naming the data. The watcher is recording. The watcher has been recording in this format for fourteen months without ever once getting to deliver this content. The senior partner nods. He says: we'll move you to the next round of consideration. Rachel walks out of the room. Her hands are warm. The watcher logs the warmth precisely. She walks to the elevator. She watches herself walk and she also walks. Both at the same time. The watcher has not gone away. The watcher has, however, become more like a colleague she nods to in the hallway than the supervisor whose approval she needs to pass through every door. The elevator opens. Rachel gets in. She does not check her phone in the elevator. She just rides it down.
