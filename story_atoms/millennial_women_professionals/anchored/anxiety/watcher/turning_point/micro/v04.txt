Mia writes the memo Sunday night. One page. Title: Feature Development Process and Authorship. She lists the dates and the authors and she lists herself first because she was first. She does not hedge in the framing. She does not call it clarification. She sends it to the team thread Monday at 9:04am with three sentences of context. The watcher waits. The watcher had several catastrophe scenarios ready, ranked by likelihood. None of them happen. One reply by 10am: appreciate the clarity. Two replies by lunch: same — and someone says they had been wanting to write a similar memo about their own work and now feels they can. The watcher logs: peer signal, unanticipated category, possibly significant. By 1pm the PM publicly thanks Mia by name in the team channel for the next-feature spec. The watcher pulls up its old data on this PM and finds twelve previous opportunities for that thank-you that did not occur. Mia reads the message twice. The jaw does not clench. The watcher waits for it to clench. It does not. Mia closes Slack. She opens her laptop and works on the next thing. The watcher follows along, recording. The watcher has never had to record a Mia who was not also being erased.
