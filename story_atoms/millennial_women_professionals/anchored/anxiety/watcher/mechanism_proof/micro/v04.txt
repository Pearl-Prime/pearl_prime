Mia is in the launch retro on Thursday at 2pm. Six people on the call. The PM thanks engineering. The PM thanks design. The PM thanks marketing. The PM thanks the data science partner from the other team. The PM does not thank product, which is Mia, which is the function that owns the spec Mia wrote and rewrote twice and shipped on time. Mia watches herself smile at the right intervals on camera. The watcher times the smiles. They are 1.2 seconds, slightly under the natural minimum, calibrated to read as gracious without reading as performed. The jaw clenches at minute fourteen and again at minute twenty-two. The hands feel separate from the rest of her body, which the watcher has now logged at thirty-eight separate occurrences across the last calendar quarter. Mia takes a screenshot of the meeting in her own head, frames it, files it. The watcher files it for her, more precisely. After the call she opens the spec doc and looks at her name in the author field. It is in twelve-point Arial. It is small enough that the watcher considers whether the document itself is participating in the erasure. Mia does not have a working response to that question. She closes the doc. She opens it again. The watcher logs the reopen.
