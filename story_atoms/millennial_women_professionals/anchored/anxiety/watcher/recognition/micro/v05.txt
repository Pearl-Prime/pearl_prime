Rachel looks at the case file and finds the inconsistency. She could raise it at the 10am. She watches the option pass through her — the body notices the option arrive and the body notices the option leave and the watcher catalogs the transit time, four seconds, the same as Tuesday's option, the same as last Wednesday's. Her hands go still on the page. The throat closes the way it always closes when the watcher's verdict has come in before the speaking-self has reached the door. The watcher's verdict is: the partner will claim it, someone else will run with it, the credit will land elsewhere, you will be the thorough one and not the brilliant one, this has been catalogued thirty-seven times in the last fourteen months. She says nothing in the meeting. Afterward, the watcher rates her silence at a six out of ten — quieter than last week, more porous than the silences of women already partnered. Rachel walks back to her office. She watches herself walk.
