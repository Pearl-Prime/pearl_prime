The HR director is asking about the headcount slide. Elena hears the question through a small delay, like a satellite call. She watches her own face arrange itself into a listening expression. She watches her thumb under the table type "yes love" in the eleven-second window between slides. The throat does its quarter-inch close. The watcher catalogs it the way the watcher catalogs everything now — shoulders at two-and-a-half inches above resting, hands cool against the conference table, breath shallow at the top of the lungs. None of the noticing reduces any of it. The noticing has become its own layer of the day. Elena says, can you go back one. She watches herself say it.
