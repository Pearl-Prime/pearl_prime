Alina is on the client call when the finance lead pushes back on the proposal fee. She has the counter-argument ready — she wrote it herself, she knows the math cold. Her hands go cold under the desk. Her chest tightens. Someone else on her team jumps in first with a softer number. Alina lets it stand. She types the harder number into the chat instead of saying it out loud. Nobody reads the chat. The call moves on. She unmutes to add a supporting point and finds her voice has gone thin. She says it anyway, quietly, and the client doesn't react to it at all. After the call she rereads her own notes. The number she wrote down is the number that should have been said out loud. Her stomach is a fist. She closes the doc without sending it anywhere.
