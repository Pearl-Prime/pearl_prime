Renata sees the launch date slipping the moment she opens the sprint board. Two weeks, minimum, maybe three. Someone has to tell the client before Friday's check-in, and she is the one who knows the number is real. She drafts the message. Subject line: timeline update. She writes three paragraphs of context before she gets to the date. She reads it back and the date is buried in the fourth line, softened by two clauses she doesn't need. Her hands are cold on the keyboard. She saves it as a draft instead of sending it. She tells herself she'll confirm the estimate with engineering first, even though she already confirmed it twice. The check-in is in four days. The draft sits unsent in her folder, growing another paragraph each time she opens it and can't make herself hit send.
