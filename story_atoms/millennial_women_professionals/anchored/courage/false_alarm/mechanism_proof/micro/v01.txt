Three weeks later Alina is in the renewal call and the same finance lead cuts the scope in half without cutting the fee. This is the exact moment her memo warned about. Her hands are cold. Her chest is tight. She has the line ready: if the scope drops, the fee has to reflect it, that's not negotiable. She opens her mouth. Her manager speaks first, agrees to the cut, thanks the client for their flexibility. Alina's throat closes around the sentence she didn't get to say. She sits through the rest of the call and does the math in her head: this is the third renewal this quarter where the number moved and she watched it move without a word. She writes the real number into her private notes afterward. She does not send it to anyone. Her jaw stays tight for the rest of the afternoon, into the drive home, into the next morning's standup.
