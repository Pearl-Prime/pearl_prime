The panel roster goes out Friday afternoon. Priya's name isn't on it. Someone two years behind her in tenure is, someone whose project she reviewed last quarter. She reads the bios twice. Her chest tightens. She scrolls to check if she's listed as an alternate. She isn't listed anywhere, because the form was never submitted, because she closed the tab four separate times and never once hit send. Her stomach twists — not at losing the spot, she tells herself, but she knows that isn't quite it either. She pulls up the draft one more time. Her own unfinished sentence is still sitting in the box: I led the— and nothing after it. She reads it three times. She does not delete it this time. She leaves the tab open on her second monitor for the rest of the day, where she can see it.
