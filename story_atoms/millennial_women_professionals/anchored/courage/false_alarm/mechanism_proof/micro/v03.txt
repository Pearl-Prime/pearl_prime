Friday's check-in arrives and Renata still hasn't sent the email. The client asks, live, on camera, whether they're still on track for the original date. Her chest tightens. Her account director, who does not have the real number, says yes, on track, barring anything unforeseen. Renata says nothing. She could correct it in the next ninety seconds and doesn't. The meeting moves on to the next agenda item. Her stomach twists through the rest of the call. Afterward her director messages her privately: are we actually on track? Renata has to type the true date into a private Slack message instead of having said it out loud, in the room, when it would have cost the client three fewer days of planning around a number that was already wrong. She reads her own message twice before sending it. Her hands are cold the whole time she is typing it.
