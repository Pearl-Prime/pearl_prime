Alina is in the Q3 pricing review and the room goes quiet after the CFO asks whether the standard fee still holds. Her chest is open. Her hands are steady on the table. She says: no, and here's why — the fee model was built for last year's scope, this year's scope is forty percent larger, the number needs to move with it. She does not soften it with maybe or I think. The CFO asks a follow-up. She answers in two sentences, cites the delivery hours, sits back. Someone across the table nods. She does not rush to fill the silence that follows. She lets the number sit there and do its own work. Her stomach is settled. She is not bracing for the room's reaction the way she used to. She already said the true number out loud, and saying it did not cost her anything she can find.
