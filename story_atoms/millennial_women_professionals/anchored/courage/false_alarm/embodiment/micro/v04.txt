Devi is in the next architecture review when a different senior engineer proposes skipping the same rollback step to save two days on the timeline. Her chest is open. She says, out loud, in the room, before the slide advances: I want to flag that we hit exactly this gap on the last migration, it cost us four hours at 2am, I think the two days are worth spending. The room goes quiet for a second. The senior director asks her to say more. Devi lays out the incident in three sentences, no apology in front of it. The proposal gets amended on the spot to include the rollback step. Her hands are steady the whole time. After the meeting a junior engineer messages her privately: glad you said that, I was thinking it too. Devi reads the message and does not feel the need to downplay her part in it.
