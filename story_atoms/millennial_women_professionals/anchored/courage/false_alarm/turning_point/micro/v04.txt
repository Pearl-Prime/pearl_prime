Devi opens the old doc comment, three weeks stale, and finally posts it — not because it will change the past migration, but because the next review is Thursday and the pattern will repeat if she stays quiet again. Her hands are cold typing it. She tags the director directly instead of leaving it as an anonymous-feeling comment in the margin. She hits post and closes the laptop before she can go back and soften the wording. Nobody responds for six hours. When the director does respond, it's one line: good catch, adding it to the checklist. Devi's shoulders come down. She has a name for the cold hands now: the exile reflex, the same circuitry that would fire if she were actually about to be cast out of the group, firing instead over a Google Doc comment aimed at a senior director. The alarm is accurate about the sensation and wrong about the danger. She starts leaving the comment in the room, out loud, before the meeting ends — not after, in a doc nobody reopens.
