Priya opens the draft with the unfinished sentence still sitting in it: I led the—. She sets a timer for ten minutes and tells herself she only has to type until it goes off. Her hands are cold on the keys. She finishes the sentence. She writes two more. She does not reread it five times the way she normally would; she reads it once and hits submit before the timer ends. Her stomach drops the second it sends, the same drop as always, and she waits for it to mean something has gone wrong. Nothing goes wrong. An automated confirmation lands in her inbox. She sits with the cold hands until they warm up on their own, which takes about four minutes. She has a name for the drop now: the exile reflex, firing off over a web form the same way it would over a real verdict from a real tribe. The form doesn't know the difference. She does, now. She keeps typing anyway.
