Tara leaves the phone in the car. Just this game. Ninety minutes. The lock chirps and she walks toward the field with her hands in her pockets because she does not know what to do with them. Her palms are damp inside the pockets. She finds a seat on the second row of the bleachers. The ref blows the whistle. Her daughter takes the field. Tara watches the actual game — the run, the pass, the missed header, the recovery. Her daughter scores in the sixty-third minute and looks straight at the bleachers. Tara is there. She is looking back. Her chest does something she has not felt in months. After, walking to the car, her daughter says, "You saw it." Tara says, "I saw it." Her hands are warm now.
