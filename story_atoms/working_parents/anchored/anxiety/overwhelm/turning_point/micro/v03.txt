Nina puts the pump time on her work calendar like it is a meeting. "Pumping — 12:30 to 12:55." She does not put it in italics. She does not add an apologetic note. The first day a colleague asks if she can move it. She says, "I can't, but I'm free at 1." The colleague says, "Cool." That is it. The whole conversation. Her throat does something that has been waiting. By Thursday she stops apologizing in her head before each block. Her shoulders are an inch lower than they were on Monday. The deadline she said yes to last week still happened. The Saturday shift still happened. But the pump block held. The block held because she held it. She is not sure when she last held something just because it was hers to hold.
