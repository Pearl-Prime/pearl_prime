Greg posts in the team channel: "I'm unreachable from 6:30 to 8:30. Family dinner. Page me only for actual outage." He turns notifications off. He sets the phone face-down on the bookshelf in the living room. The first eleven minutes his chest does something that feels like waiting for a verdict. His daughter pulls out the essay again. She reads him the second paragraph. He listens to all of it. He has an actual opinion about the verb in the third sentence. He says it. She nods, marks it. They argue about the closing line. At 8:32 he picks up the phone. Sixty-three messages. None of them are an outage. His stomach does something complicated. The urgency he felt at 7:52 was not real. It was trained. He sees that. He still feels it.
