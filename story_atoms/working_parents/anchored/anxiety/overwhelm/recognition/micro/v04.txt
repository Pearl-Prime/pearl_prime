Kira opens the calendar at 7:02am. The kindergarten spring concert is at 9:30. Her morning meeting with the regional director is at 9:00. She knew this last week. She has known this for nine days. She has not moved either thing. Her breath is shallow in a way she only notices because she is a social worker who teaches breathing exercises to clients. Her ribs feel small. The kindergartner is in the kitchen asking about a missing sock. The preschooler is asking about juice. Kira opens her email and types a message to the director asking to push to 10. She deletes it. She types it again. She does not send it. She closes the laptop. She finds the sock. She does not know yet which of the two she will choose. The choice has already been making itself for nine days.
