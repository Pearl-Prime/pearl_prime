Nina pulls into the daycare lot at 5:47pm. Pickup closes at 6. The project deadline is tomorrow at 9am and the second draft is not started. Her shoulders are up around her ears in a way she only notices when she shifts the car into park. Her newborn is asleep in the carrier in the back. She sits with her hand on the gear shift. The thirteen minutes she has before the late fee kicks in are the thirteen minutes she needs to think through the second draft. She cannot do both. She knows she cannot do both. She gets out of the car anyway. Her chest feels compressed in the cool spring air. She walks toward the door. The deadline does not move. Pickup does not move. She is the only thing that moves.
