Greg sits at the kitchen table at 11:14pm with his laptop open. His daughter's fever broke an hour ago. The pediatrician's nurse said hydration and rest and call back if it spikes again. The Slack channel about the infrastructure migration has forty-one new messages, three of them tagged with his name. His jaw is clenched in a way he can feel in his molars. The kitchen is warm — the radiator clicks. His hands are cold. He types an answer to the first tagged message. He retypes it because the first version was sharp. He looks at the monitor on the counter where his daughter's breathing shows green and steady. He looks back at the screen. The cursor sits in the reply box. He does not know which of these things he is doing.
