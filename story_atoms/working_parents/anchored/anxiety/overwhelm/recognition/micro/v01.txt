Tara opens the calendar at 2:54pm. Pickup is at 3:15. The quarterly review email is two paragraphs in, the third paragraph the one her director is waiting for. Her palms are damp against the trackpad. She types another sentence. She deletes it. She types it again. The drive is twenty-two minutes in current traffic — she just checked. She has nine minutes of writing time and twenty-two minutes of driving time and the math has been wrong for an hour. Her throat closes around something that is not a word. She picks up the keys. She puts them down. She picks them up again. The email cursor blinks at the end of the sentence she has not finished.
