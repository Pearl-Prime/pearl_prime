Nina's manager pings her at 8:14pm: "Any chance you can take the Saturday slot too? Coverage gap." She is on the couch with the laptop balanced on the armrest, the second draft half-written. Her newborn is on her chest. Her partner is already working Saturday. Her stomach drops before she finishes reading. She types "yes." She deletes it. She types "let me check." She deletes it. She types "yes" again and sends it. The relief is immediate and lasts about three seconds. Then the dread. She does the math: four hours of her parents' help, a bottle of pumped milk, the second draft due Monday now instead of tomorrow because she said yes. Her shoulders are at her ears. Her chest is hollow and tight at the same time. The newborn shifts. She does not move.
