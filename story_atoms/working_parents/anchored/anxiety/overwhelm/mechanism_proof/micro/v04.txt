Kira goes to the meeting. She watches the recording of the spring concert that her partner sent her at 9:47am, on her phone, in the bathroom of the office, between two agenda items. Her kindergartner is in the front row in a yellow shirt. The video is forty-three seconds long. Kira plays it twice. Her ribs are tight in a way that is starting to feel structural. She walks back into the meeting. She nods at the right times. At pickup the kindergartner asks, "Did you see me?" Kira says, "I saw the video. You were perfect." The kindergartner watches her face. Says nothing. Holds her hand on the walk to the car. Kira's hand is cold in the warm afternoon. The mechanism is visible: she chose the meeting, she lost the concert, she lied a little, the kid noticed, and now she gets to carry it.
