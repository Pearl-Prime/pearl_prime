Tara stands in the kitchen at 6:48pm. The phone is in the other room, on the counter, face-down. Her daughter is at the table doing pre-algebra and complaining about a teacher. Tara is chopping peppers. She is not also editing a deck in her head. Her shoulders sit lower than they used to. Her chest is open. The quarterly review went out yesterday and the world did not end. She does not know how many emails are sitting in the inbox in the other room. She will know later. Right now her daughter is asking her opinion on the teacher. Tara has one. She gives it. Her hands are warm against the cutting board. The peppers come out uneven. Nobody at the table cares.
