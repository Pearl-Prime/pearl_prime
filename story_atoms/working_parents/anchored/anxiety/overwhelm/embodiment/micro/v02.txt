Greg closes Slack at 6:25pm on a Tuesday. The phone goes face-down on the bookshelf. It stays there until 8:30. His daughter has stopped checking his face during conversations to see if he is half-listening. Tonight she tells him about a friend who is making a bad decision and asks what he thinks. He thinks for a minute before answering. His jaw is not clenched. His hands are warm around the mug. The infrastructure migration shipped last month with him present at home four nights out of five and the world did not collapse. The whisper that says he should have been on Slack still arrives. He hears it. He does not obey it. His daughter laughs at something he said. He hears that too.
