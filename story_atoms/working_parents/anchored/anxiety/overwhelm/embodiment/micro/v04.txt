Kira blocks the school events on her calendar at the start of the term. All of them. Concerts, field trips, parent-teacher, the random Friday that ends at noon. She sends the list to the director in one email: "Heads up — these are non-negotiable, will plan around them." The director replies "thanks for the visibility." Kira reads it twice. The next concert she sits in the second row in a folding chair with both kids' jackets in her lap. Her phone is in the bag at her feet. Her ribs feel the right size for her lungs. The kindergartner finds her in the audience and waves. Kira waves back. Her hands are warm. She is a social worker who is also a parent and the two things are not in a fight today. She breathes all the way in. She breathes all the way out.
