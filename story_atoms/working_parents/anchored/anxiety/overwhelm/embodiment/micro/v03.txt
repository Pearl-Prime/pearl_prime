Nina says no to the Saturday shift. She types it without preamble: "I can't take Saturday — I'm out for family." She does not add an apology. She does not offer to make it up. Her thumb hovers over send for a second longer than usual. She sends it. Her chest does not collapse. The manager replies "okay, I'll find someone." That is the whole exchange. Saturday she takes the baby to the park. Her partner is there. She does not check the work app. Her shoulders sit at a height she had forgotten was available. She watches her newborn track a leaf in the wind. Her hands are warm on the stroller handle. She is one person doing one thing. The other things still exist. They are not on her chest right now.
