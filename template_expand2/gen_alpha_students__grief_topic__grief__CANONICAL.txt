## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — PERSON LOSS — MID — THROAT TIGHTENING
---
Maya sees grandma's number still in her phone. Three months now. The contact exists. The person doesn't. Her throat tightens scrolling past it. She could delete it. She doesn't. The number sits there like a door to a room that's empty. Grandma answered that number for sixteen years. Now it just rings. Maya keeps scrolling. The number stays. The throat stays tight.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — PET LOSS — LATE — HOLLOWNESS
---
Jaden comes home and listens for the tags. Max's collar used to jingle at the door. Six weeks and he still listens. The silence where the jingle should be opens something hollow in his chest. The leash is still on the hook. The bowl is still by the fridge. He moved the bed but he can see where it dented the carpet. Max isn't here. The shape of Max is everywhere.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — PLACE LOSS — EARLY — HEAVINESS
---
Zoe drives past the old house. Someone else's car in the driveway now. Someone else's curtains. Her room was the window on the left. She learned to ride a bike in that driveway. Measured her height on the door frame in that kitchen. The heaviness fills her chest. The house exists. Her house doesn't. Same address. Different everything. She keeps driving. Doesn't slow down. The heaviness rides with her.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — RELATIONSHIP LOSS — MID — CHEST ACHE
---
Leo sees Kenji in the hallway. They nod. Keep walking. Two years of being inseparable and now they nod. The friendship didn't end—it just stopped. No fight. No moment. Just less and less until this. Leo's chest aches watching Kenji laugh with someone else. The inside jokes are still in his head. The shorthand. The history. Kenji has it too, probably. They just don't use it anymore.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — OBJECT LOSS — LATE — EYES STINGING
---
Aisha reaches for the necklace that isn't there. Grandma's necklace. Lost somewhere between school and home. She's checked everywhere. Retraced everything. It's gone. Her eyes sting in the bathroom mirror. The necklace was the last thing grandma touched. The clasp grandma closed. Now it's in a gutter somewhere, or a trash can, or nowhere. The absence sits on her collarbone where the weight used to be.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN — MID
---
The waves come when Maya expects them and when she doesn't. Sunday dinner—grandma's chair is empty. Wave. Wednesday—song on the radio grandma liked. Wave. Friday—nothing specific, just the bus ride home and suddenly the missing is enormous. Three months of waves. They don't get smaller. They just come less often. Then they come again. The pattern is: okay, okay, okay, wave, okay, wave, okay.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS — EARLY
---
Jaden finds Max everywhere. The squeaky toy behind the couch—wave. The vet's number on the fridge—wave. The specific way 5pm used to mean walk time—wave. He gave away the food, the bowls, the bed. The triggers stayed. They're in the carpet dent. The hook by the door. The silence at 5pm. Max is gone. The ordinary things that held Max are still here, triggering.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING — LATE
---
Zoe forgets, then remembers. She thinks "I should tell mom about this" then remembers mom is in Arizona now. Thinks "let's watch that show" then remembers the TV is in a different living room. Every forgetting is a tiny gift. Every remembering is fresh. She's been doing this for a year. The forgetting doesn't stop. Neither does the remembering. The gap between them is where the wave lives.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS — MID
---
Leo finds the Kenji-loss in different places. Monday—someone mentions the camping trip they took. Door opens. Tuesday—the inside joke almost comes out of his mouth. Door opens. Thursday—Kenji's birthday, remembered too late to say anything. Same loss. Different entry points. The friendship ended quietly. The reminders are loud. Each door opens to the same empty room.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK PERMISSION — EARLY
---
Aisha is fine in class. Fine at lunch. Fine walking home. Then she touches her collarbone—the bare spot—and the wave arrives without warning. In public. On the sidewalk. Eyes stinging, throat closing. The necklace has been gone for two weeks. The waves don't care about time. Don't care about location. They arrive when they arrive. She keeps walking. The wave walks with her.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY — MID
---
Maya expects the wave to flatten her. Grandma's birthday. She wakes up braced for devastation. The wave comes at breakfast—the empty chair, the cake they're not making. But she doesn't drown. She cries in the bathroom. Washes her face. Goes to school. The grief is huge. She's still standing. The day happens around the loss, not instead of it. She thought it would stop her. It didn't.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH — LATE
---
Jaden braces when he finds the squeaky toy. The wave comes—but this time there's something else in it. Max destroying that toy on Christmas morning. The ridiculous squeaking. Mom yelling from the kitchen. Max's tail going crazy. He almost laughs. The grief is still there. But the memory brought Max, not just the absence. Max existed. Made ridiculous sounds. The wave passes. Something warm stays.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — SOMEONE MENTIONS THEM — EARLY
---
Zoe freezes when Dad says it. "Remember when your mom burned the turkey that year?" Mom's name, out loud. Zoe braces. But the wave doesn't drown her. Dad is half-smiling, remembering. Other people knew her too. Other people have memories. Mom exists in conversation, not just in absence. The missing is still there. It has company now.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — STILL SAD STILL OKAY — MID
---
Leo sees Kenji's birthday post. The wave arrives. He lets it. Doesn't fight. The friendship changed. That's true. The distance grew. Also true. Kenji is still here—just not here-here. Leo misses his friend and he's okay. Both things. He doesn't comment on the post. Doesn't reach out. Just sits with the missing. The missing doesn't require action. It just requires space.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — GRIEF HAS A NAME — LATE
---
Aisha realizes what this is. The necklace loss isn't about a necklace. It's about grandma. The object was holding the bigger loss. Now both are loose. Naming it doesn't fix it. But it makes the size make sense. She's not crying about metal and chain. She's crying about a person who touched her every morning when she clasped it. The name doesn't stop the tears. It holds them.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — KEEPS THE RITUAL — MID
---
Maya still sets grandma's place at Sunday dinner. Empty plate. Empty chair. The family doesn't say anything. It's just what they do now. The ritual doesn't bring grandma back. It keeps the shape of her. The space she took. Maya passes the bread past the empty plate. The absence is acknowledged. Not fixed. Just... included.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — SAYS IT PRIVATELY — LATE
---
Jaden stands in the backyard at dusk. Max's favorite time. The time they'd throw the ball until it was too dark to see. He doesn't have a ball. Just stands there. "I miss you, buddy." Quiet. To no one. To Max. The words land in the empty yard. Max can't hear them. That's not the point. Jaden needed to say them. The saying is the point.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — TOUCHES WHAT REMAINS — EARLY
---
Zoe keeps the photo from the old house on her desk. Not framed—just printed, tucked by the lamp. Her and mom on the front porch. The porch belongs to someone else now. The moment doesn't. She touches the edge of the photo sometimes. The paper is real. The day was real. She can't go back. She can hold the evidence that it happened.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — WALKS THE OLD PATH — MID
---
Leo takes the long way home. Past the park where he and Kenji used to sit. The bench is there. The tree. The path they wore into the grass. The friendship isn't there, but the path is. He walks it sometimes. Not to fix anything. Just to keep the route familiar. Memory walks. The person isn't there. The walking remembers anyway.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — WEARS IT DIFFERENTLY — LATE
---
Aisha buys a simple chain. Plain. No pendant. She clasps it herself in the mirror. It's not grandma's necklace. It's just weight on her collarbone. The place that felt empty. She'll never get the real one back. This isn't a replacement. It's acknowledgment that something belongs there. Something was there. The chain sits where the loss lives. Both things on her skin now.
