# PERSONA: SF Founders/Startup
# ENGINE: Too much input at once → system freeze/blur/shutdown

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — TASK LOAD — HEAVINESS
---
6am. Jake opens his laptop to the list already waiting. Investor deck needs revision. Engineer needs feedback. Customer demo at 10. Payroll by EOD. The heaviness arrives before the coffee. Slack is pinging. Email count climbing. The board meeting is Friday. The list doesn't pause for prioritization. He stares at the screen, not sure which window to click first.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — SOCIAL LOAD — BUZZING
---
Priya's phone buzzes through the product meeting. Investor checking in. Lawyer needs signature. Co-founder asking about the roadmap. Customer support escalation. Her head is buzzing, not just the phone. Everyone needs a piece of her. The meeting continues. She's nodding. Not tracking. Too many inputs to process any single one.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — DECISION LOAD — FOG
---
Hire the senior engineer or the two junior ones? Keep the current pricing or go freemium? Raise now or wait for metrics? Take the partnership or stay independent? Marcus has seventeen decisions queued. None of them are clear. A fog settles behind his eyes. He opens a doc to think through one. Types half a sentence. Can't finish it.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — TIME LOAD — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
Three hours until the investor meeting. Nadia needs to finish the deck, prep the team, review the data room, and answer fourteen urgent emails. Her body moves slower, not faster. Each task takes twice as long. She stands at her desk between the slides and the inbox. The clock moves. She doesn't.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — CONTEXT SWITCHING — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
9am: engineering standup. 9:30: sales pipeline review. 10am: customer call. 10:30: investor update. 11am: HR issue. Alex never finishes a thought before the next thing starts. The pressure builds behind his eyes. By noon, he's been in five meetings and completed zero tasks. The day is half gone. Nothing is done.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — TASK LOAD REPEATS
---
The pattern runs all day. Jake starts the investor deck—Slack notification. Answers Slack—remembers payroll. Opens payroll—email from board member. Starts the email—engineer needs help. By 9pm, the heaviness is in his shoulders. Ten hours of touching everything. Finishing nothing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERYONE NEEDS SOMETHING
---
Priya tracks the asking. 7am: investor wants metrics. 9am: engineer wants direction. 11am: customer wants call. 2pm: lawyer wants decisions. 5pm: co-founder wants strategy session. Each answer opens a new thread. Each thread needs follow-up. By 8pm, she has six half-finished conversations. Everyone waiting.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — DECISIONS COMPOUND
---
Marcus's decision queue grows daily. Monday: three decisions pending. Tuesday: five. Wednesday: eight. The old decisions don't resolve themselves. The new ones keep arriving. By Friday, there are seventeen things he can't figure out. The fog doesn't lift. The queue doesn't shrink.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — TIME COMPOUNDS
---
Nadia's time deficit grows with each day. Monday: thirty minutes behind. Tuesday: two hours. Wednesday: a full day of tasks pushed to Thursday. The pushed tasks meet the new ones. The pile doesn't shrink. She makes it to the weekend. Barely. Next week starts the same way.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — NO RECOVERY TIME
---
Every context switch costs Alex five minutes of reorientation. He switches contexts thirty times a day. That's two and a half hours just getting bearings. The meetings don't count the switching cost. They just keep arriving. The pressure behind his eyes is the reorientation that never completes.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — EXTERNAL REDUCTION
---
End of day. Jake is listing the tasks still undone. Then the email arrives: "Investor meeting pushed to next week." One thing lifts. Just one. Something shifts in his chest. That's when he realizes how much he was holding. The remaining tasks are still there. But there's a gap now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAMES THE PILE
---
Priya has been frozen at her laptop for twelve minutes. Then she opens a doc. Types a list. Just the things. No solving. No ordering. Investor deck, payroll, customer issue, team conflict, lawyer signature. She looks at the list. Five things. It felt like fifteen. The pile has edges now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — SOMEONE TAKES ONE
---
Marcus is drowning. His co-founder walks by. Sees his face. "I'll handle the HR thing. You focus on the investor prep." One thing leaves his hands. The load lightens before he can say thanks. He still has the other six decisions. But she took the HR thing. That's enough to start.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — TIME OPENS
---
The 10am meeting cancels—client conflict. Nadia's whole morning opens. The relief is physical—her chest unlocks. The tasks are still there. But they're not stacked in thirty-minute blocks anymore. There's room between them. She starts the first one. Works slower than she has all week. There's time.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — TURNS OFF NOTIFICATIONS
---
Alex silences everything. Slack: muted. Email: closed. Phone: Do Not Disturb. The pressure behind his eyes persists for ten minutes. Then something eases. The inputs stopped. The processing can catch up. He doesn't know what's piling up. For one hour, he doesn't need to.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — ONE SMALL ACTION
---
Jake has been staring at the pile for fifteen minutes. He picks one thing—the shortest email—and sends it. Just that. The pile is still there. But one thing moved. His hands did something. He picks the next smallest thing. Sends that too. Two done. The mountain is the same. He's moving through it anyway.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — CLOSES SOMETHING
---
Priya has twenty-three browser tabs open. Investor portal, metrics dashboard, docs she started, articles she'll never read. She closes one. Then another. The screen gets quieter. She closes ten more. The tasks exist. The tabs don't have to.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — STEPS OUTSIDE
---
Marcus doesn't tell anyone. Walks to the parking lot. Stands in the sun for three minutes. The company exists inside. The decisions, the asks, the pile—all inside. He stands in the parking lot not being a CEO. Three minutes of not being in it. The sun is warm. That's all he has to feel right now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS NO TO ONE
---
Nadia looks at the calendar. Eight meetings tomorrow. She declines one. A sync that could be an email. One gap opens. She didn't solve the overwhelm. She made it seven meetings instead of eight. The difference is small. The reduction is real.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — DRINKS WATER
---
Alex's head is full. The meetings, the decisions, the pile. He walks to the kitchen. Fills a glass. Drinks the whole thing slowly. Water going down his throat. That's the only thing happening. Thirty seconds of just drinking water. The calendar is still there when he finishes. But he drank water.

**Surface swaps:** Work deadlines → startup deadlines. Boss demands → investor demands. Team meetings → all the meetings.
**Engine intact:** Too much at once, system stalls, small reducing action helps.
**Resisted:** Making startup overwhelm "just how it is." The engine is neutral.
