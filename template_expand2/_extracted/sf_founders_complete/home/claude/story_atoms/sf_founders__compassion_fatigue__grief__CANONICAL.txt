## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — THE CARING THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Jake remembers how he used to feel in one-on-ones. Present. Engaged. Actually curious about his team. Now he's performing the conversation. His throat tightens looking at old notes where he wrote "great energy today." That energy is gone somewhere. The founder who felt that energy isn't in this room anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — CAPACITY THAT LEFT — HOLLOWNESS
---
Priya used to be able to hold everyone's feelings. Year one: absorbing the team's stress felt natural. Year three: there's a hollowness where the capacity used to be. She can see where the caring was. The space is still there. What filled it isn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — ENTHUSIASM GONE — HEAVINESS
---
Marcus finds an early Slack message. His own words: "I'm so excited to build this together!" Four exclamation points. The enthusiasm is gone. The heaviness fills his chest reading his former self. That founder existed. Built this company with excitement. That founder isn't typing anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — PRESENCE MISSING — CHEST ACHE
---
Nadia watches herself in the team meeting. Physically present. The presence that used to be there—the actual being-in-the-room—is missing. Her chest aches for the version of herself who could really be in meetings. Not just perform being in them.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — CURIOSITY ABSENT — EYES STINGING
---
Alex used to ask questions because he wanted to know. Now he asks because a good founder asks. The curiosity is absent. His eyes sting in his office, alone, missing the person who actually cared what the answers were.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Jake expects them and when he doesn't. Monday—sees an old team photo where he looks happy. Wave. Wednesday—someone mentions how supportive he used to be. Wave. The caring self surfaces in reminders. Then it's gone again.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Priya finds the caring self in ordinary places. The one-on-one template she used to fill with real notes. The Slack channel where she used to send personal messages. The coffee shop where she used to actually listen. Triggers everywhere for a person who isn't here.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Marcus finds the enthusiasm through different doors. Monday: early deck where he wrote "we're going to change everything." Tuesday: co-founder mentioning "remember when you were excited about this?" Same loss. Different entry points.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Nadia forgets, then remembers. She starts to really engage in a meeting, then remembers she doesn't do that anymore. The forgetting is a gift. The remembering is fresh loss. She used to be here. Now she's not.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Alex is fine in the board meeting. Fine in the standup. Then someone asks him a question with genuine curiosity, and the wave arrives. For who he used to be when questions interested him. The loss doesn't care about timing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Jake expects the old team photo to flatten him. It doesn't. The wave comes—he sees his former enthusiasm. But he doesn't drown. He can hold the grief and keep working. The loss is huge. He's still here.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---
Priya braces for the old Slack messages. The wave comes—but there's warmth. She was really kind in those messages. Really present. The caring existed. It was real. Missing it is sad. But it was real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IT WAS REAL
---
Marcus looks at the early deck. "We're going to change everything." Naive, maybe. But real. He felt that. Built from that. The enthusiasm isn't here now. But it existed. The grief acknowledges something that was true.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — FLICKER RETURNS

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — QUESTION INTERESTS HIM

Someone asks Alex something, and for a moment, he wants to know the answer. The curiosity flickers. Brief. But real. The caring self isn't dead. Maybe just very far away. Reachable, possibly.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — KEEPS THE MESSAGES

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — READS THE EARLY VISION

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — MAKES SPACE FOR FLICKERS
---
