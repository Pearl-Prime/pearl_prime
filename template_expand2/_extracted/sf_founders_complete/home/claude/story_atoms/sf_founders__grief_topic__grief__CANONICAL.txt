## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — CO-FOUNDER GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maya's desk is still there. Six months since she left to take care of her mother. Never came back. The desk has someone else's stuff now. Jake's throat tightens walking past it. She built this with him. The walls remember both of them. Only one of them is still building.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — MENTOR DIED — HOLLOWNESS
---
The email came in March. Heart attack. Priya's first investor, the one who took a chance when no one else would. The hollowness opens checking her phone, still expecting his Sunday "how's the company?" texts. The texts aren't coming. Won't come.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — FATHER PASSED — HEAVINESS
---
Marcus's father never saw the company succeed. Died the month before the Series A. The heaviness arrives when he hits milestones. No one to call who'd understand what this meant. The person who wanted this for him isn't here to see it.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — FRIEND LOST — CHEST ACHE
---
Nadia's college roommate. The one who convinced her to start the company. Car accident, two years ago. The chest ache arrives at random—signing a big customer, remembering who she'd tell first. That person doesn't have a phone anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — DOG DIED — EYES STINGING
---
The office dog. Everyone's dog, really, but he was there from the beginning. Alex's eyes sting at the empty bed in the corner. The bed is still there because no one can move it. The space where he slept is the space where he's gone.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Jake expects them and when he doesn't. Maya's birthday: expected wave. Random Tuesday seeing her handwriting on a whiteboard: unexpected. The loss doesn't keep a schedule. The throat tightening arrives on its own terms.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Priya finds the mentor everywhere. His portfolio on LinkedIn. A restaurant they went to once. The investor update template he helped design. The ordinary things hold the absence. Every trigger is just a thing. Every thing opens the hollowness.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Marcus finds his father through different doors. A song on the radio. The smell of cigar smoke. The phrase "your old man would be proud." Same loss. Different entry points. The heaviness arrives through whichever door is open.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Nadia forgets, then remembers. She reaches for her phone to text her friend about the win. Then remembers. The forgetting is a gift—a second of the friendship still existing. The remembering is fresh every time.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — HE'D BE PROUD
---
The Series B closes. Marcus thinks of his father. The heaviness, yes. But also: he'd be proud. Not here to see it. Would be proud anyway. The absence holds the pride that would be there.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — STILL INFLUENCES
