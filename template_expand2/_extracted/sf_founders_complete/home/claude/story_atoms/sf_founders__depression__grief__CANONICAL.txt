# ENGINE: Loss woven into depression — mourning energy, aliveness, former self

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — ENERGY THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Jake finds a video from the seed round celebration. He's animated. Laughing. Gesturing with both hands about the future. His throat tightens watching. That energy is gone somewhere. He doesn't know when it left. The founder in the video built something. The founder watching can barely get through email.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — ALIVENESS MISSING — HOLLOWNESS
---
Priya used to feel the wins. Really feel them. Now the customer signs and there's hollowness where excitement should be. The aliveness that used to show up for good news is missing. She can remember having it. Can see evidence it existed. The space where it was is just space now.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — DRIVE ABSENT — HEAVINESS
---
Marcus built this company from nothing. Eighty-hour weeks felt like purpose. The drive that made those weeks possible is absent now. The heaviness fills his chest where ambition used to live. He has a company. He's not sure he has the energy to keep it.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — FUTURE THAT'S FLAT — CHEST ACHE
---
The roadmap used to excite Nadia. The features they'd build, the customers they'd win. Now she looks at it and feels nothing. Her chest aches for the version of herself who could feel possibility. The future exists. Her excitement about it doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — CARING THAT LEFT — EYES STINGING
---
Alex used to care so much. About the product, the team, the mission. The caring left at some point—he can't identify when. His eyes sting looking at old emails where he was obviously invested. That founder cared. This one is going through motions.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Jake expects them and when he doesn't. Monday—sees energetic self in old photo. Wave. Wednesday—someone mentions how passionate he used to be. Wave. The former self surfaces in evidence. Then it's gone again. The loss is ongoing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Priya finds the aliveness in ordinary places. The Slack channel where she used to post excitedly. The meeting room where she used to pace with ideas. The whiteboard with her old handwriting, all caps, underlined. Triggers everywhere for a feeling that's gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Marcus finds the missing drive through different doors. Monday: early investor email where he wrote "we won't stop until we win." Tuesday: co-founder asking "do you remember when you were obsessed with this?" Same loss. Different reminders.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Nadia forgets, then remembers. She starts to get excited about a feature, then remembers she doesn't feel that anymore. The forgetting is a tiny gift. The remembering is fresh loss. She used to have this. Now she doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Alex is fine in the board meeting. Fine in standup. Then someone asks "are you still passionate about this?" and the wave arrives. For the caring he used to have. The loss doesn't care about context.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Jake watches the old video. Braces for collapse. The wave comes—he sees his former energy. But he doesn't drown. He can hold the grief and finish the day. The loss is huge. He's still functioning.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY HAS WARMTH
---
Priya braces for the old Slack messages. The wave comes—but there's warmth. She was really alive in those messages. Really excited. The aliveness was real. Missing it is sad. It was real though.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IT EXISTED
---
Marcus looks at the old email. "We won't stop until we win." He meant that. Built from that. The drive isn't here now. But it existed. The grief acknowledges something that was true.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — FLICKER OF FUTURE
---
In one meeting, something flickers. Nadia feels something about the roadmap—brief, thin, but something. Interest, maybe. The feeling isn't fully gone. Just very far away. The flicker happened.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — MOMENT OF CARING
---
Alex reads a customer email. For a moment, he cares what happens to them. The caring is small but real. Not performed. The former self flickered through. Reachable, possibly.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — ACKNOWLEDGES OUT LOUD

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — KEEPS THE EVIDENCE

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — READS ON PURPOSE

Marcus reads the old emails sometimes. Not to feel bad. To remember. He was that person. The drive existed. The reading is honoring.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — MAKES SPACE
---
