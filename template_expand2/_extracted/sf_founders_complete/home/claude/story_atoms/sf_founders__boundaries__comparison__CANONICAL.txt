# ENGINE: Sees others hold boundaries → shrinks by comparison

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v01 — THEY SAY NO EASILY — TIGHTENING
---
Jake watches his YC batchmate decline the investor meeting. "Doesn't fit our timeline." Four words. No apology. The investor moves on. Jake's chest tightens. He's never said no to an investor. His meetings have meetings. That founder just... declined. Same stage. Different ability to protect time.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v02 — THEY KEEP THEIR EQUITY — SHRINKING
---
Priya hears the other founder at the conference. "We didn't give board seats until Series B." Her board has three investor seats at seed. She gave them away. Something shrinks hearing how others negotiate. Same stage. Different leverage. Or maybe just different willingness to say no.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v03 — THEY PUSH BACK FAST — PULLING INWARD
---
Marcus watches another founder in the partner meeting. The investor suggests a pivot. The founder says, "That's not our direction." Immediate. Clear. Marcus has been nodding along to advice he disagrees with for months. This founder just... disagreed. Priya pulls inward watching someone do what she can't.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v04 — THEIR NOS ARE RESPECTED — CONTRACTING
---
Nadia's chest contracts watching her co-founder. When Maya says no, people accept it. When Nadia says no, there's negotiation. Same company. Same title. Different reception. Maya's no is a wall. Nadia's no is a starting point.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v05 — THEY PROTECT THEIR TIME — GETTING SMALLER
---
Alex watches the successful founder's calendar philosophy on the podcast. "I take no meetings before noon." His calendar is a war zone from 7am. Same job. Different boundaries. Getting smaller listening to someone describe the time he doesn't protect.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY NO HIGHLIGHTS THE GAP
---
Jake tracks other founders' nos. Monday: batchmate declines intro request. Tuesday: peer says no to advisory role. Wednesday: friend passes on speaking engagement. Each clean no highlights his cluttered calendar. The comparisons arrive automatically.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — THEIR TERMS STAY THEIRS
---
Priya watches deals close around her. That founder kept their board structure. This one kept their valuation. The other one kept control provisions. Her terms always get negotiated down. Same market. Different ability to hold.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — THEIR PUSHBACK LANDS
---
Marcus watches other founders disagree. The disagreement gets heard. Considered. Sometimes wins. His disagreements get steamrolled. Same investors. Different weight. He's not sure if it's him or them.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — THEIR WEIGHT IS DIFFERENT
---
Nadia compares the reception. Maya says "not now": accepted. Nadia says "not now": questioned. Same words. Different results. The comparison runs constantly. Her boundaries are lighter somehow.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — THEIR CALENDARS ARE CLEANER
---
Alex sees inside other founders' days. Somehow they have thinking time. Somehow they have protected hours. His day is meetings until he can't think. Same role. Different architecture. The comparison happens every time someone mentions their "focused mornings."

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v01 — SEES THE MEASURING
---
Jake catches himself mid-comparison. Measuring his calendar against the podcast founder's. Different company. Different stage. Different everything. The comparison was assuming a shared standard. The standard doesn't exist.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v02 — DIFFERENT LEVERAGE
---
Priya thinks about the founder who kept board seats. Hot company. Multiple term sheets. Of course they kept leverage. Her situation was different. One term sheet. Take it or close the company. The comparison wasn't apples to apples.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v03 — THEY MIGHT STRUGGLE TOO
---
Marcus talks to the founder who pushed back. "It took me years to learn that. I used to say yes to everything." The clean disagreement was learned, not natural. The gap between them is practice, not personality.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v04 — DIFFERENT RELATIONSHIP
---
Nadia realizes Maya has known the board member for five years. Different relationship. Different trust. The nos that land easily land on old foundation. Nadia is building that foundation now. The comparison was skipping context.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v05 — THEIR PATH ISN'T MINE
---
Alex stops the podcast. That founder's boundary system is theirs. Built over years, in different circumstances, with different costs. His system will be his. Not a copy. Not a comparison. Just his.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v01 — DOES OWN VERSION
---
Jake sees someone decline a meeting. The tightening happens. He lets it. Then tries his own version. "Can we push this to next week?" Not as clean. Not as confident. But said.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v02 — STAYS WITH OWN PACE
---
Priya watches Maya negotiate smoothly. Doesn't try to match. Her own negotiation style is slower. More careful. That's her pace. She negotiates anyway—her way, not Maya's.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v03 — PUSHES BACK AT OWN SPEED
---
Marcus sees another founder disagree instantly. He doesn't try to match. His pushback comes an hour later, over email. "I've been thinking about your suggestion, and I have concerns." Not instant. Still said.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v04 — BUILDS OWN WEIGHT
---
Nadia's no gets questioned. She answers the question. Says no again. Gets questioned again. Answers again. Her no requires more work than Maya's. She does the work anyway.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v05 — PROTECTS WHAT HE CAN
---
Alex can't do no-meetings-before-noon. His company isn't there. He protects one hour. Tuesday morning. His version. Not the podcast founder's version. His.
