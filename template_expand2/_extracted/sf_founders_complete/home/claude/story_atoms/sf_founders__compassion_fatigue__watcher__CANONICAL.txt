# ENGINE: Performing care from distance → watching self go through motions

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Jake is in the one-on-one before he registers arriving. His voice is making supportive sounds. "I hear you." "That sounds hard." His face is nodding. He's somewhere behind his eyes, watching a founder perform empathy. The employee is sharing something real. Jake is watching from far away.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED CARING
---
Priya hears the team member's news. Mother is sick. The words land. She says appropriate things. The caring arrives three seconds later, muted. By then she's already moved to the next part of the conversation. The emotion and the response are out of sync.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — SUPPORT MONTAGE
---
Marcus sees himself in fragments. Hand on shoulder in one-on-one. Face concerned at standup. Voice warm on the call. The in-between is gone. He's a montage of supportive gestures, not a continuous person caring. The caring motions happen. He's not inside them.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — GOING THROUGH MOTIONS
---
Nadia asks the right questions. "How are you really doing?" "What would help?" Her voice knows the script. She watches herself perform a caring conversation. The employee seems to feel heard. Nadia is present in body. Her caring is somewhere else.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Alex sits across from the struggling team member. The person is crying. Alex can see the tears—theoretically. His hand reaches out. The touch happens. He's watching it happen from behind glass. The moment is important. He's receiving it on delay.

---
The pattern runs all day. One-on-one: autopilot. Team meeting: autopilot. Investor call requiring warmth: autopilot. Jake is technically present for all of it. The caring is performed correctly. He's not inside any of it.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good news: team member's parent is improving. Sad news: another's relationship ended. Both arrive at the same muted frequency. Priya's face does the right things. Happy, then concerned. The emotions are performed. They don't reach her.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SUPPORT SCRIPT
---
Marcus has the conversations. "I'm here for you." "Take what you need." "We'll figure it out." The words come from somewhere trained. The team feels supported. Marcus watches himself be supportive. Technically there.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — DAYS OF DISTANCE
---
Nadia's care has been distant for weeks. Same one-on-ones. Same supportive words. The connection that used to happen doesn't. The people across from her get a performance. The real caring is behind glass somewhere.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — TEARS DON'T LAND
---
Alex has sat with three crying people this month. Each time, the right responses emerged. Each time, the tears landed far away. The sadness reaches him eventually, late, after the person has left. The tears happen. He's not present for them.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
Jake is in another one-on-one when something shifts. The engineer says something about their kid. Jake's own experience with his nephew surfaces. He's here. In this moment. For three seconds, the glass isn't there. Then it closes. But it opened.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAME LANDS
---
"Priya." The team member says it twice. Then something catches. She's Priya. That's her. She's in this conversation about their mother's health, not watching it. For five seconds, she's inside the caring.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
The team member reaches for Marcus's arm. The touch arrives immediately—warm, present. Not delayed. He's in his body. Feeling the grip. The watcher distance collapses for a moment. Then returns. But the touch was real-time.

---

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — TEARS BREAK THROUGH
---
Alex is watching another person cry. Then his own eyes water. The tears bypass the glass. He's not watching himself get teary. He's getting teary. In the room. With the person. For a moment, the caring is real.

---

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE NEWS
---
Priya hears about the parent. The drift starts. She doesn't let it. Stays in her body. Feels the response—whatever it is. Small, but hers. The caring is thin today. It's real though.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — TOUCH STAYS PRESENT
---
Marcus puts his hand on the team member's shoulder. He's about to drift. Doesn't. Feels the warmth through his palm. Stays in the touch. The shoulder is real. The person is real. He's in the moment of supporting them.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — ASKS FROM HERE
---
Nadia catches herself going automatic with the questions. Stops. Takes a breath. Asks something from here, not from script. "What's actually happening for you right now?" The question comes from presence. The team member notices.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — CRIES WITH
---
Alex lets the tears come. Doesn't observe them from outside. Sits in the room, teary, with the crying team member. "This is hard," he says. Meaning it. Present for the hard thing together.
