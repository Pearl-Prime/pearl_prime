# ENGINE: Financial exposure → self-collapse → urge to hide

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — COULDN'T MAKE THE NUMBER — FACE HEAT
---
Board meeting. Jake presents the revenue numbers. 30% below forecast. The board's faces shift—just slightly. His face heats. He's the one who made the forecast. He's the one standing here with a miss. The questions start. Reasonable questions. His face stays hot. He said a number. The number didn't happen.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — ASKED FOR BRIDGE — CHEST DROP
---
Priya sends the email to the lead investor. "We need to discuss bridge financing." Her chest drops as she hits send. She raised money nine months ago. Now she's asking for more. The plan didn't work. Everyone will know the plan didn't work. The email is sent. The drop continues.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — HAD TO CUT — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Marcus announces the layoffs in the all-hands. Four people. His decision. His shoulders curl as he reads the names. He hired them. He promised opportunity. Now he's un-hiring them because the numbers don't work. The room is quiet. His shoulders stay curled.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — COULDN'T PAY ON TIME — STOMACH SINKING
---
The vendor email: "Your invoice is 45 days past due." Nadia's stomach sinks. She knows. She's been stalling. The cash isn't there yet. Now someone external knows they're struggling. The professional face she shows to investors—this vendor sees something else.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — TOOK THE DOWN ROUND — THROAT TIGHT
---
Alex signs the term sheet. 40% lower valuation than the last round. Down round. Everyone will see. Crunchbase will show the decline. His throat tightens signing. This is what saving the company looks like. It also looks like failure.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP
---
3am. Jake is replaying the board meeting. The faces when he showed the revenue. The pause before the questions. He should have set better expectations. Should have seen the miss earlier. The replay runs again. Same slide. Same faces. Same heat.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — BRIDGE = FAILURE
---
Priya's bridge email lives in her head. Every investor knows. Her last update said "on track." Now she's asking for money. The gap between the update and the ask tells a story. The story is failure. The story replays.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — NAMES ON LOOP
---
Marcus hears the names in his sleep. The four people he let go. Each name is a promise broken. He promised growth. He delivered cuts. The names rotate through his mind. Same shame. Different name. Repeat.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — VENDOR KNOWS
---
Nadia thinks about everyone who sees the late payment. The vendor's accounts receivable person. Their manager. Their finance team. Every person in that company now knows her company can't pay on time. The exposure expands in her imagination.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — PUBLIC DOWN ROUND
---
Alex checks Crunchbase. Not yet updated. But it will be. Everyone will see the new valuation. The math is public. His failure is documented. He checks again. Still not there. He'll keep checking.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — BOARD MEMBER FOLLOWS UP
---
Email from the board member. "Tough quarter, but your handling of the miss was impressive. Most founders hide. You showed up." Jake reads it twice. The miss happened. The handling was noticed. The board saw him fail. They also saw how he failed. Both things.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — BRIDGE IS NORMAL
---
Priya talks to another founder. "We've done three bridges. Every company does." Three bridges. She's done one. The shame assumed she was alone. She's not. Bridges are part of the game. The email she sent is normal.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — THEY LANDED
---
Marcus gets a text from one of the people he laid off. "Got an offer. Better than what I had. Thanks for the support." They're okay. Not all of them, maybe. But this one. The names in his head—one of them landed. The cut was real. So is the landing.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — VENDOR UNDERSTANDS
---
The vendor responds: "We've worked with startups before. Let us know when you can pay. We're not worried." They're not worried. They've seen this before. The late payment that felt like exposure was just business. Normal business.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — DOWN ROUND, COMPANY LIVES
---
The money wires. Alex's company has eighteen months of runway now. The down round was the price. The company existing is the result. The valuation hurt. The survival happened. Both things on the same term sheet.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v02 — SENDS THE EMAIL
---
Another bridge needed. Priya drafts the email. The drop starts. She sends it anyway. The drop doesn't have to stop her from sending. The company needs money. She asks for money.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v03 — MAKES THE CALLS
---
Marcus has to make another cut. One person. He makes the call himself. Doesn't hide behind HR. His voice is steady. His shoulders are curled. He says the words. The curl doesn't stop the words.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v04 — PAYS WHEN SHE CAN
---
The cash comes in. Nadia pays the late invoice immediately. Includes a note: "Thank you for your patience." No over-explaining. No elaborate apology. Just payment and thanks.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v05 — ANNOUNCES THE ROUND
---
Alex writes the blog post. The round closed. The valuation is lower. He doesn't hide the number. "We raised at a lower valuation. Here's why we're still excited." The truth, without performance. The throat is tight. The post is published.
