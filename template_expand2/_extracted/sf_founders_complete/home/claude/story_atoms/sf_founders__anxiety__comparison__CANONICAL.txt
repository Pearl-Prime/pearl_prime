## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v01 — FUNDING ANNOUNCEMENT — TIGHTENING
---
TechCrunch: "YC alum raises $30M Series B." Jake's chest tightens. He was in the same batch. Same demo day. His company raised $3M seed and is grinding. That founder raised ten times that. Same starting line. Different outcomes. The tightening happens while he closes the tab. He's supposed to feel happy for them. He feels measured against them.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v02 — HEADCOUNT GAP — SHRINKING
---
Priya sees the competitor's careers page. Twenty-four open roles. Her company has eight people total. They're hiring more than she employs. Something shrinks in her chest scrolling through the listings. Senior engineer. VP of Sales. Head of Design. She's wearing all those hats. They have people in all those seats.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v03 — VISIBILITY GAP — PULLING INWARD
---
The podcast features a founder Marcus knows. "How she scaled to $50M ARR in three years." His ARR is $2M. Same industry. Same starting year. She's on podcasts talking about scale. He's in his office trying to close the next deal. He pulls inward listening to her talk about her team, her processes, her vision. Same race. Different laps.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v04 — EASE GAP — CONTRACTING
---
Nadia watches her YC batchmate announce their acquisition. $200M exit. Five years. The announcement sounds easy. "Grateful for the journey." "Right partner at the right time." Her company is still fighting for product-market fit. Same batch. Same pitch practice. His journey sounds smooth. Hers is not smooth. The contraction happens while she types "congratulations."

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v05 — CONFIDENCE GAP — GETTING SMALLER
---
Conference panel. Alex watches the other founder answer questions. No notes. Confident. Funny. Articulate about strategy. When it's Alex's turn, his answers feel thin. Same stage. Same questions. That founder makes it look easy. Alex is making it look like work. The smallness settles into his shoulders.

## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY SUCCESS TRIGGERS IT
---
The pattern runs constantly. Monday: former colleague raises $20M—tightening. Wednesday: industry peer gets featured in Forbes—shrinking. Friday: YC batchmate announces partnership with Google—contracting. Every success in Jake's feed triggers the same measurement. Different founders. Same internal math. The comparison engine doesn't take days off.

---
Priya tracks everyone from her accelerator batch. Founder A: acquired for $100M. Founder B: $40M raise. Founder C: profitable and growing. Her: still fundraising seed extension. Same batch. Same resources. Same mentors. Different math. The tracker in her head updates with every announcement. She's always behind.

---
Marcus notices who gets the press, the panels, the podcast invites. It's never him. Same industry. Same stage. The spotlight finds them. He's building in the dark. The comparison isn't rational—visibility isn't success. But his chest pulls inward every time someone else gets the feature.

## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — EVERYTHING LOOKS EASIER
---
From outside, every founder's journey looks smooth. Nadia has the data on her own journey—the struggles, the pivots, the near-deaths. She has headlines on everyone else's. Headlines are smooth. Reality isn't. The comparison engine uses headlines. The measurement is rigged, and she keeps doing it anyway.

---
