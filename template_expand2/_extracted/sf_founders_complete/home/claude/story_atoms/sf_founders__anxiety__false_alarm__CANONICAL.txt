## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v01 — INVESTOR EMAIL — HEART RACING
---
The email arrives from Sequoia. Subject line: "Following up." Jake's heart races before he opens it. They're passing. The meeting went wrong. Six months of runway and now this. He clicks. "Thanks for a great conversation—let's schedule the partner meeting." Partner meeting. His heart is still racing reading good news. The body didn't wait for the content.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v02 — CO-FOUNDER TEXT — STOMACH DROPPING
---
"Can we talk?" Three words from Maya, his co-founder. Priya's stomach drops. She's leaving. She's unhappy. The company is splitting. Priya calls back immediately, voice tight. Maya: "I have an idea for the homepage redesign. Wanted to run it by you." A design idea. Her stomach dropped for a design idea. She says "sure, tell me" while the dropping continues.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v03 — METRICS DIP — SHOULDERS CLIMBING
---
Daily active users down 3% from yesterday. Marcus's shoulders climb toward his ears. The product is failing. Users are leaving. The whole thesis is wrong. He refreshes the dashboard. Still down. Refreshes again. He checks last month—normal variance was 5% either way. 3% is noise. His shoulders are at his ears for noise. They don't come down.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v04 — CALENDAR INVITE — BREATH CATCHING
---
Unexpected calendar invite from the lead investor. "Quick sync - 15 min." Nadia's breath catches. They're pulling the term sheet. They found something in diligence. It's over. She joins the call at the scheduled time, breath still caught. "Hey, just wanted to say the team's excited. Anything you need from us?" Support. They were offering support. Her breath stayed caught through the whole call.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v05 — SLACK SILENCE — HANDS COLD
---
The engineering channel has been quiet for two hours. Dev usually posts every thirty minutes. Alex's hands go cold. Something's wrong. The deploy broke. Dev is job hunting. The company is falling apart without him noticing. He walks to the other room. Dev is at his desk, headphones on, deep in code. "Making good progress." The silence was focus. Alex's hands stay cold.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY PING
---
Jake's body responds identically to every notification. Investor email: heart races. Slack from co-founder: heart races. Calendar update: heart races. Customer support ticket: heart races. The content varies. The response doesn't. Some messages are good, some neutral, some need attention. All of them get the same alarm. The system doesn't differentiate.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY CONVERSATION REQUEST
---
"Can we talk?" gets Priya every time. Monday: co-founder wants to talk—stomach drops—she has a product idea. Wednesday: investor wants to talk—stomach drops—they're wiring the money. Friday: engineer wants to talk—stomach drops—he wants a raise, which is reasonable. Same phrase. Same physical response. Zero correlation with actual content.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — METRICS ALWAYS ALARMING
---
Marcus checks the dashboard four times a day. Each time, his shoulders climb before the page loads. Up 5%: shoulders climb, then release. Down 2%: shoulders climb, stay up. Flat: shoulders climb, stay up. The direction doesn't matter to his body. The act of checking is the trigger. The numbers are secondary.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — INVESTOR CONTACT = EMERGENCY
---
Any communication from investors puts Nadia's body on alert. Email: breath catches. Text: breath catches. LinkedIn notification: breath catches. They've sent twelve messages this month. Eleven were positive or neutral. Her breath caught for all twelve. The pattern doesn't update. Investor contact means emergency, regardless of evidence.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — QUIET = CRISIS
---
Alex's body reads quiet as crisis. Slack quiet: hands cold. Office quiet: something's wrong. No customer emails: they're all leaving. The quiet never means crisis. Sometimes people are working. Sometimes customers are happy. The cold hands don't learn. Every silence is a sign. Every sign is wrong.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v01 — EMAIL WAS GOOD
---
Jake opened the investor email expecting rejection. Partner meeting invitation. He sits with the mismatch. His heart raced for two minutes about a yes. The body had already decided it was bad news before he read a word. The yes exists. His body's prediction was wrong. Both things are true. The email is still good.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v02 — TALK WAS FINE
---
"Can we talk?" led to a thirty-minute product brainstorm. Priya's best conversation with Maya all week. She noticed: the stomach drop was for nothing. The drop predicted disaster. The reality was collaboration. The physical response and the actual event had no relationship. She felt one thing. Something else happened.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v03 — VARIANCE IS NORMAL
---
Marcus looks at three months of data. The line goes up and down. 3% swings happen weekly. His shoulders climbed for something that happens constantly. The pattern is: line moves, shoulders climb, line is normal variance. The climbing never needed to happen. The chart didn't know his shoulders were involved.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v04 — SEES THE PATTERN
---
Nadia's breath caught at the calendar invite. Then she paused. This is the sixth time this month. Same catch. Same neutral or positive outcome. The breath is a reflex that hasn't been right once. She can see it now—the catching that predicts nothing. It's still happening. But she's watching it happen.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v05 — SILENCE WAS PRODUCTIVE
---
Two hours of Slack silence. Alex's hands went cold. Then Dev pushed the feature—best code he'd written in weeks. The silence was flow state. The cold hands were wrong. Alex is learning something: his body's emergency signals have no predictive value. The signals keep coming. They're just signals.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v01 — WAITS BEFORE OPENING
---
Investor email arrives. Heart races. Jake doesn't open it immediately. Lets the racing happen. Finishes his coffee. The email will say whatever it says. Racing or not racing won't change the content. He opens it five minutes later. Neutral follow-up. The wait didn't hurt. The racing was just weather.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v02 — ANSWERS WITHOUT SPIRALING
---
"Can we talk?" Priya's stomach drops. She types back: "Sure, give me 10 min." Doesn't ask what it's about. Doesn't preload disaster. The ten minutes pass. Her stomach is still dropped. She joins the call anyway. Product idea. Fine. She talked through dropped stomach. The drop didn't require investigation.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v03 — CHECKS ONCE
---
Marcus looks at the dashboard. Shoulders climb. He notes the number. Closes the tab. Doesn't refresh. Doesn't check again in ten minutes. The number is what it is. His shoulders stay somewhat elevated. The dashboard stays closed. One check. The world didn't end in the gap.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v04 — JOINS THE CALL
---
Unexpected investor sync. Nadia's breath catches. She joins anyway. Doesn't rehearse disaster scenarios in the waiting room. "Hey, just wanted to connect." Her breath is still caught while they chat about nothing important. Caught breath, normal conversation. Both happening. The call ends. She was fine. Breath and all.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v05 — LETS QUIET BE QUIET
---
Slack is quiet. Alex's hands are cold. He doesn't ping anyone to check. Doesn't walk to the other room. Lets the quiet exist without investigation. His hands stay cold for twenty minutes. Then Dev posts an update. Normal. The quiet was just quiet. Alex survived not knowing.

**Resisted:** Making startup anxiety "valid" because stakes are real. Engine is the body pattern, not the stakes.
