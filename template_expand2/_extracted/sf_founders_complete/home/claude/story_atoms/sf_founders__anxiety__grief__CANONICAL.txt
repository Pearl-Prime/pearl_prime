## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — CO-FOUNDER LEFT — THROAT TIGHT
---
Jake opens the Slack channel. Maya's name is still there, grayed out. Six months since she left to join Google. The channel exists. The partnership doesn't. His throat tightens scrolling past her old messages. They built this together. Now he's building it alone. The company continues. The shape of them as a team is a ghost in the architecture.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — EARLY TEAM GONE — HOLLOWNESS
---
Priya looks at the photo from the launch party. Eight people crowded into the first office. Three of them are gone now—better opportunities, burned out, life changes. The company has forty people now. Success by most metrics. The hollowness opens looking at those eight faces. The company that exists isn't the company they built. That company is gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — IDEA THAT DIED — HEAVINESS
---
Marcus finds the old pitch deck in Google Drive. The original vision. The product they were going to build before the pivot. That product doesn't exist. Will never exist. The pivot was right—metrics proved it. The heaviness fills his chest anyway. He believed in that idea. Put two years into it. Now it's a file in a folder, never to be opened again.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — INVESTOR RELATIONSHIP ENDED — CHEST ACHE
---
The lead investor from seed didn't follow on. "Not the right fit for this stage." Nadia understood the business logic. The chest aches anyway. She pitched them first. They believed when no one else did. Two years of quarterly updates, advice calls, celebrating milestones. Now they're names she'll cc on the announcement, not partners in the building.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — EARLY DAYS GONE — EYES STINGING
---
Alex walks past the old office. Someone else's company is in there now. New logo on the door. He can see through the window—they repainted. The wall where they taped the first customer order is beige now. His eyes sting on the sidewalk. They scaled out of that office. That was the goal. The office is still gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Jake expects them and when he doesn't. Monday—someone uses Maya's old shortcut in the code. Wave. Wednesday—an investor asks how the co-founding team is doing. Wave. Friday—nothing specific, just the emptiness where partnership used to be. The waves don't shrink. They just come less often. Then they come again.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Priya finds the early team everywhere. The Slack emoji someone created that's still in the system. The process doc the ops person wrote before leaving. The standing meeting that still has the wrong name. She's had fifty employees since. The triggers stayed. They're in the small things, referencing people who aren't here.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Marcus finds the old vision in different places. A customer asking if they'll ever build that feature. A competitor launching something similar. The deck he finds in the drive. Same loss. Different entry points. The pivot was three years ago. The reminders are current.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Nadia forgets, then remembers. She starts to text the old investor about a win, then remembers they're not partners anymore. Thinks about who to call with the crisis, then remembers that relationship has a different shape now. Every forgetting is a tiny gift. Every remembering is fresh.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK PERMISSION
---
Alex is fine in the board meeting. Fine in the standup. Fine until he walks past the old office and the wave arrives without warning. On the street. Eyes stinging. For a building. The loss doesn't care about timing. Doesn't care that he's supposed to be celebrating growth. The wave arrives when it arrives.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Jake expects the anniversary to flatten him. One year since Maya left. He braces for devastation. The wave comes—her name in old code, the chair she used to sit in. But he doesn't drown. He acknowledges it. Keeps working. The grief is huge. He's still building. The day happens around the loss.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---
Priya braces when the launch party photo surfaces. The wave comes—but there's something else. That night. The champagne someone brought. The terrible cake. Everyone so sure they were about to change the world. She almost smiles. The grief is there. The warmth is there too. The team existed. Made those faces. The loss holds the having.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IDEA WAS REAL
---
Marcus opens the old pitch deck on purpose this time. The vision that died. He reads it. The idea was good. The market wasn't ready. The pivot was right. All of this is true. The idea was real—he didn't imagine it. Two years of work happened. Just because it pivoted doesn't mean it wasn't real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — STILL CONNECTED
---
Nadia gets an email from the old investor. "Saw the news. Proud of what you've built." They're not partners anymore. They're still connected. The relationship changed shape, didn't disappear entirely. The ache is there. So is the message. Both things.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — GROWTH WAS THE GOAL
---
Alex stands outside the old office. The wave is there. But something else too. They outgrew this place. That was the goal. The grief is for the going. The going was success. He can hold both—missing the small office, being glad they're bigger. The loss doesn't erase the growth. The growth doesn't erase the loss.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — KEEPS THE CODE
---
Jake finds Maya's code comment. "// Maya's shortcut - don't touch, it works." He could refactor it. Remove her name. He doesn't. The shortcut stays. Her name stays. The code works. She was here. The comment is small. He keeps it anyway.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — SAYS THEIR NAMES
---
All-hands meeting. Priya mentions the early team. "Sarah built that process. James set up this system." Their names out loud. They're not here anymore, but they were here. The current team hears who came before. The naming doesn't bring them back. It acknowledges the building.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — KEEPS THE DECK
---
Marcus doesn't delete the old pitch deck. Doesn't archive it where he'll never see it. Keeps it in the folder. The vision that died is still in the drive. Part of the company's history. Not the current roadmap. But real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — SENDS THE UPDATE
---
Nadia adds the old investor to the announcement email. They're not partners anymore. They were part of this. "Thought you'd want to know." The relationship changed. The courtesy continues. Different shape, not nothing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS PAST ON PURPOSE
---
Alex takes the route past the old office. Not avoiding it. Not dwelling. Just walking past. The wave is smaller now. The building is there. He's still building somewhere else. He can walk past the place they started and keep going to the place they are.
