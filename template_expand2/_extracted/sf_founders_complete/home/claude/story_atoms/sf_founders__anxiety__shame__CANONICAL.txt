## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — MISTAKE IN PITCH — FACE HEAT
---
Jake says the wrong number. In front of the partner. Market size: he said million when he meant billion. Three seconds of silence. His face heats immediately. The partner says "you mean billion?" Jake nods, corrects it, continues. The pitch goes on. His face stays hot. He's watching himself be someone who can't say numbers right in front of investors. The mistake was small. The heat is total.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — CAUGHT UNPREPARED — CHEST DROP
---
The board member asks about customer retention. Priya doesn't have the number. She should have the number. Her chest drops while she says "I'll follow up with that data." The meeting continues. The drop stays. She runs this company and she didn't know the retention rate. Everyone in the room saw her not know something she should know.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — PUBLIC CORRECTION — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Marcus's co-founder corrects him in the team meeting. "Actually, the launch is Thursday, not Wednesday." Small thing. His shoulders curl anyway. The whole team heard him get the date wrong. His own launch date. He's supposed to know the calendar. He's supposed to know everything. He didn't know Thursday.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — INVESTOR SAW — STOMACH SINKING
---
The demo crashes. Live. In front of the lead investor. Nadia's stomach sinks while Dev frantically restarts. The investor says "these things happen" but Nadia is already shrinking. They saw the product fail. They saw the thing she's supposed to have figured out not work. The restart succeeds. The sinking continues.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — VOICE CRACKED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Demo Day. Alex's voice cracks on the third slide. Nerves. He clears his throat, continues. The voice holds for the rest. After, his throat is tight. Five hundred people heard his voice crack. They saw him be nervous. They saw him be human in a room where everyone's supposed to be polished. The rest of the pitch was fine. The crack is all he can think about.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP
---
3am. Jake is replaying the million/billion mistake. The partner's face. The pause. The correction. He should have practiced the numbers more. He should have slept better. He should be someone who doesn't make that mistake. The replay runs again. Same three seconds. Same heat. Different angles of the same failure.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — PATTERN OF NOT-KNOWING
---
Priya tracks her gaps. Monday: didn't know the churn rate. Wednesday: couldn't remember the engineer's start date. Friday: blanked on the customer's company name. Each gap brings the same chest drop. The same sense of being caught. She runs this company. She should know everything. The gaps accumulate like evidence.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — MAGNIFICATION
---
Marcus said Wednesday. It was Thursday. The correction took three seconds. He's been thinking about it for two days. The team probably forgot by lunch. The correction lives in him full-size. The mistake was small. The shame didn't get the memo.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — STILL SINKING
---
A week later, Nadia still sees the frozen demo screen when she closes her eyes. The investor said it was fine. Sent a positive follow-up. The deal is proceeding. The sinking hasn't processed the outcome. It's still back in that room, watching the crash happen.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — VOICE CRACK ECHOES
---
Alex is preparing for the next pitch. The voice crack was two weeks ago. He's practiced this deck fifty times since. The crack still echoes. Every time he starts slide three, he hears it. The audience that day has forgotten. His body hasn't.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — NOBODY MENTIONED IT
---
Two weeks since the million/billion mistake. Jake has talked to three people who were in that room. Nobody mentioned it. Not awkwardly avoiding—just didn't bring it up. The mistake that's been running on loop in his head didn't make it into anyone else's memory. He's been replaying something that doesn't exist for them.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — DEAL CLOSED ANYWAY
---
The investor wires the money. The same investor who watched Priya not know the retention rate. The check clears. The gap she was so ashamed of didn't stop them from investing. They saw her not know something. They invested anyway. Both things happened. The gap was smaller than it felt.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — EVERYONE FORGETS
---
Marcus asks his co-founder about the Thursday/Wednesday correction. "What correction?" She doesn't remember. The thing he's been carrying for a week doesn't exist in her memory. The team meeting moved on. Only he stayed in that moment. Everyone else kept going.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — NAMED INTERNALLY
---
Nadia is preparing for the next demo. The crash memory surfaces. This is shame, she thinks. Not the crash—the feeling about the crash. The demo was fixed in ten seconds. The shame has lasted three weeks. They're not the same thing. The crash was a moment. The shame is something else.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — VOICE CRACK WAS HUMAN
---
Alex watches the recording of Demo Day. Gets to the voice crack. It's barely audible. A slight roughness for half a second. He'd built it into a disaster. On camera, it's just a person giving a talk. The crack was real. The disaster was constructed.
