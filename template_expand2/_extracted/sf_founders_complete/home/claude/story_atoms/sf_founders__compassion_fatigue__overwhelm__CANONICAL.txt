# Topic: COMPASSION_FATIGUE
# Engine: OVERWHELM
# Total: 20 stories (5 rec, 5 mp, 5 tp, 5 emb)
#
# TOPIC SURFACE: giving too much, empty tank, can't feel it anymore
# NOT: burnout discourse, self-care advice, "you can't pour from empty cup"
#
# ENGINE: Too many people needing support → system freezes

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — EVERYONE NEEDS — HEAVINESS
---
Jake opens Slack to seventeen messages. The engineer needs direction. The sales rep needs backup. The designer needs feedback. The VP needs a decision. The investor needs an update. The heaviness arrives before he can respond to anyone. Everyone needs something from him. His capacity to care is somewhere behind the weight.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — EMOTIONAL QUEUE — BUZZING
---
Priya's one-on-ones stack. Maya is frustrated about the roadmap. Dev is anxious about job security. The customer success lead is burned out. Each person needs her to feel with them. The buzzing in her head isn't the notifications—it's the emotional load. She can feel all of it. She can't feel any of it well.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — SUPPORT PILE — FOG
---
Marcus has four team members going through hard things. New baby, sick parent, relationship ending, visa issues. Each one deserves attention. Real attention. A fog settles when he thinks about the care he owes. The caring he could do if there were one of them. With four, he's spreading thin enough to be nearly absent.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — ASKING STACKS — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
"Do you have a minute?" The third time today. Nadia has been someone's support for two hours already. Her movements slow before answering. The giving has been continuous. The tank refills between conversations—except there's been no between. Just conversation after conversation of needing to be present.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — EMPATHY OVERLOAD — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Alex listens to the engineer's frustration. Then the designer's anxiety. Then the PM's overwhelm. Then the VP's concern. Each person's feelings enter his system. The pressure builds behind his eyes. He's holding everyone's emotional weight. His own feelings are somewhere underneath the pile, unreachable.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — NEEDING STACKS
---
The pattern runs all day. Jake finishes with the engineer—VP needs him. Finishes with VP—designer pings. Finishes with designer—investor calls. By end of day, he's been a resource for seven hours. The tank doesn't refill between draws. It just gets lower.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EMOTIONAL CARRYOVER
---
Priya carries the one-on-ones with her. Maya's frustration is in her chest through lunch. Dev's anxiety joins it during the afternoon. By 5pm, she's holding feelings that aren't hers but live in her body. The emotional residue accumulates.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CARE COMPOUNDS
---
Marcus's care obligations grow. Week one: one team member struggling. Week two: two. Week three: four. The care doesn't become more efficient. It just divides into smaller and smaller portions. The fog thickens with each addition.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — MINUTES ADD UP
---
"Just a minute" happens twelve times. Nadia counts at the end of the day. Twelve minutes times twelve people is two hours of support. Each minute felt small. The total is enormous. The tank drains in tiny increments.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — FEELINGS STACK
---
Alex's empathy absorption compounds. Morning: engineer's frustration. Afternoon: designer's anxiety, PM's overwhelm. Evening: VP's concern, co-founder's doubt. Each feeling layers on the last. The pressure behind his eyes is the geological strata of everyone's emotions.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — ONE RESOLVES
---
The engineer figures it out. Doesn't need Jake anymore on that issue. One person's need lifts. Something shifts in his chest. He'd been holding seventeen things. Now sixteen. The lightening is small but real.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — SOMEONE ELSE HOLDS ONE
---
The VP takes Maya's one-on-one this week. Priya doesn't carry that conversation. One person's feelings aren't in her chest today. There's space where Maya's frustration would be. The fog is slightly thinner.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — NAMES THE LOAD
---
Marcus writes down the names. The four people going through hard things. Sees them as a list. The fog lifts slightly. The care doesn't reduce. But it has edges now. Four people. Not an infinite obligation.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — CLOSES DOOR
---
Nadia closes her office door. Thirty minutes of no one needing her. The minutes don't stack. The tank sits still. By the time she opens the door, something has refilled slightly. The closed door was a pause.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — FEELS OWN FEELING
---
Alex realizes he hasn't checked his own state in hours. The pressure behind his eyes is there. But underneath: tiredness. His own tiredness. A feeling that belongs to him. It emerges for a moment from under the pile.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — RESPONDS TO ONE
---
Seventeen messages. Jake picks one. Responds fully to that one. The pile is still there. One person was helped. The heaviness is the same. He picks the next one.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — SCHEDULES GAPS
---
Priya looks at tomorrow's one-on-ones. Adds fifteen minutes between each. The conversations will still be emotional. There will be space between them. The care will have breaks.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — LIMITS TIME
---
Marcus gives each person thirty minutes this week. Not less care—bounded care. The fog doesn't lift entirely. The care has containers now. Four people, thirty minutes each. That's what he has.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS "LATER"
---
"Do you have a minute?" Nadia says, "Can we talk in an hour?" The person says sure. The tank gets sixty minutes of not draining. One conversation, delayed. The pause is enough to feel something return.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS BETWEEN
---
Alex has five minutes between meetings. He walks outside. Doesn't check messages. The pressure behind his eyes doesn't disappear. It doesn't increase either. Five minutes of nothing stacking. The walk is a gap.
