## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Jake is in the investor meeting before he registers arriving. His mouth is saying the pitch. The slides are advancing. His hands are gesturing at the right moments. He's somewhere behind his eyes, watching a founder perform. The investors are nodding. He's supposed to be here. He's technically here. He's watching from far away.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED SENSATION
---
Priya's co-founder hugs her after the term sheet arrives. She feels the pressure three seconds later, muted and far away. "We did it!" Maya's voice reaches her eventually. The term sheet exists. The company just got funded. She's receiving the news of her own success on delay. The champagne is being poured. She's watching it happen.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — MEMORY GAP
---
Marcus can't remember the all-hands meeting. He ran it—the deck is still on his screen. He answered questions. People are back at their desks, energized. But the meeting itself is a gap. He was at the front of the room. The memory isn't. The company got aligned on something. He was technically there.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — EMOTIONAL LAG
---
The product launches. Users are signing up. Slack is exploding with celebration. Nadia's face does excitement. Her voice says "we did it." Twenty minutes later, alone in the bathroom, she feels something. Pride? Relief? The feeling arrives late. She wasn't excited in the room. She's something now, in the bathroom, when no one can see it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Demo Day. Alex walks on stage. Hundreds of investors watching. The lights are bright—he knows this theoretically. The microphone is in his hand. He's presenting. The words come out. The slides advance. The five minutes pass. He walks off stage. He must have done it. He was somewhere else while it happened.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — DAYS BLUR
---
Monday looks like Saturday which looked like last Thursday. Jake writes code, takes meetings, checks metrics. The days don't separate. The company is always running. He's always at the desk or in a call or somewhere in between. The founder motions happen. He's present for none of it distinctly. Weeks pass. He floats alongside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — FOUNDER MONTAGE
---
Priya sees herself in fragments. Hand signing the term sheet. Face at the board meeting. Body at the team offsite. The in-between is gone. She's a montage, not a continuous CEO. One moment she's pitching, then she's hiring, then it's quarter end. The transitions evaporated.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — PERFORMANCE AUTOPILOT
---
Marcus runs the one-on-ones, the standups, the investor updates. The words come from somewhere trained. He watches them land appropriately. The team seems motivated. The investors seem satisfied. The autopilot leads adequately. Marcus was technically there for all of it.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — BODY ON SCHEDULE
---
Nadia's body knows the routine. 7am: check metrics. 8am: team standup. 10am: investor call. 2pm: customer demo. She watches a competent founder work. That founder is using her hands, has her face, knows the calendar. She's in there somewhere. Or behind the glass.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good news—deal closed. Bad news—engineer quit. Both arrive at the same muted frequency. Alex is present for both Slack notifications. Touched by neither. His face does the right things. Celebration, then concern. The emotions are performed correctly. They don't reach him.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
Jake is debugging at 2am when something shifts. The code is elegant. Not elegant like a fact—elegant like a feeling. He's here. In this moment. Solving this problem. For three seconds, the glass isn't there. He's not watching himself code. He's coding. Then it closes. But it opened.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAME LANDS
---
"Priya." The investor says it twice before she registers it's her name. Then something catches. She's Priya. That's her. She's in the negotiation, not watching it. They're discussing valuation. She answers from here. The moment fades. But for five seconds, she was inside the conversation.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
Marcus spills coffee on his laptop. The splash is immediate—sharp, here, now. Not delayed. He's in his body. Grabbing napkins. Feeling the hot liquid on his hand. The watcher distance collapses. Then returns. But the spill was real-time. He was in it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v04 — LAUGH HAPPENS
---
Nadia laughs before she can stop it. Something the engineer said—actually funny. The laugh comes from her chest, not from the performance layer. She feels it happen. Feels the surprise of feeling it. The sound is hers. Then it passes. But the laugh was real.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — COLD AIR BREAKS THROUGH
---
Alex walks outside after Demo Day. The cold San Francisco air hits his face. It bypasses the glass completely. He's not watching himself feel cold. He's cold. In his body. On the sidewalk. The watcher can't observe cold from distance. For a moment, he's inside.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01 — STAYS PRESENT
---
Jake is in the investor meeting. He notices himself starting to drift—the familiar float upward. He doesn't follow. Keeps his attention on the question being asked. The specific words. The investor's face. He stays in the answering. One question, fully present. Then the next.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE WIN
---
The term sheet arrives. Priya notices the watcher trying to pull back—observe the celebration from above. She doesn't let it. Stays in her chest. The tightness. The relief. Whatever this feeling is. She's feeling it. Not watching herself feel it. For this moment, she's inside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — RUNS MEETING FROM HERE
---
Marcus is leading the all-hands. He catches the drift starting. He comes back. Feet on floor. Eyes on the team. The slide is about Q2 goals. He presents it from inside his body. Not watching from above. When the meeting ends, he remembers being in it.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — HAND ON DESK
---
Nadia puts her hand flat on her desk. The wood is cool. She feels it—solid, smooth. She's about to drift. She doesn't. Keeps her hand there. The desk is real. The company is real. She's sitting at this desk running this company. The touch anchors something.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — SAYS IT FROM HERE
---
Demo Day Q&A. Someone asks about the business model. Alex catches himself going automatic—the rehearsed answer. He stops. Takes a breath. Answers from here, not from performance. The words are less polished. More real. The investor nods differently. Alex was in the answer.
