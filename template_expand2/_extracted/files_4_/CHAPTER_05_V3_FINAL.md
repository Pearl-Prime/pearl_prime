Here's what you might be thinking:

*"Okay, but... what if I just wait a little longer?"*

*"What if I just get a little better at calming down first?"*

*"What if I'm almost ready, and I just need a little more time?"*

And I get it.

The idea of acting while anxious feels... risky.

But here's what I need you to see:

**Waiting isn't neutral.**

It's not like you're just... pausing until you're ready.

**Waiting has a cost.**

And the longer you wait, the higher that cost gets.

And permanent delays have permanent costs.

#### Variant 1

Here's what you're actually losing:

Every time you say "once I'm not anxious, then I'll do it," you lose:

**The moment.**

Not "a" moment.
**The** moment.

The one where you could've said the thing.
The one where you could've been seen.
The one where you could've shown up as yourself.

And once that moment passes —

You can't get it back.

You can tell yourself: *"I'll do it next time."*

But next time isn't the same.

The conversation has moved on.
The person isn't thinking about it anymore.
The version of you that existed in that moment is gone.

And you can't recreate it.

#### Variant 1

Here's the hardest part:

**The version of you that's waiting to not be anxious —**

**— is the version that never shows up.**

Think about it.

How many times have you had something to say, but you didn't say it because you felt anxious?

How many times have you wanted to share something, but you didn't because you felt uncertain?

How many times have you wanted to be yourself, but you waited until you felt more confident?

And how many times did that version of you — the confident, ready, not-anxious version — actually show up?

**Never.**

Because that version doesn't exist.

The version that exists is **this one**.

The one that's anxious.
The one that's uncertain.
The one that doesn't feel ready.

And while you've been waiting for the other version to show up —

**This version has been sitting on the sidelines.**

#### Variant 1

Here's what needs to change:

You need to stop asking:

*"When will I be ready?"*

And start asking:

*"What am I losing by waiting?"*

Because the answer to the first question is: **Never.**

You will never feel "ready" in the way you think you need to feel ready.

The anxiety will always be there in some form.
The uncertainty will always exist.
The fear of being seen will always feel uncomfortable.

But the answer to the second question?

**Everything.**

You're losing everything.

Not in one big moment.

But in a thousand small moments where you chose to stay invisible instead of showing up.
