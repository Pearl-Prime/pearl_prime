Here's the thing about gratitude journals.

Everyone tells you they work. Every therapist. Every influencer. Every person who's definitely doing better than you and wants you to know it.

"Write down three things you're grateful for every morning. It'll change your brain."

So you try it.

Day one: I'm grateful for coffee. I'm grateful for my bed. I'm grateful for... I don't know. The sun?

Day seven: I'm grateful this journal wasn't expensive because I'm about to stop using it.

Day thirty: You stare at the blank page. You know you should feel something. Gratitude. Joy. Warmth. Anything.

But there's nothing there.

Just a flat, gray, nothing.

And the voice in your head says: "What's wrong with me? Why can't I feel grateful like everyone else?"

Here's the truth nobody tells you:

**You can't access gratitude when you can't access your feelings at all.**

Numbness isn't a lack of gratitude.

It's a nervous system in shutdown mode.

And no journal is going to write you out of that.

**Your numbness isn't a character flaw. It's your nervous system protecting you from something it thinks you can't handle.**

The fix isn't more journaling.

The fix is thawing the freeze.

#### Variant 1

Here's why the mainstream approach fails:

Gratitude practices assume you have access to your emotions.

They assume the door is open, and you just need to walk through it.

But for someone who's numb, the door isn't open.

**The door is locked, barricaded, and booby-trapped.**

All those well-meaning suggestions — "focus on what's good," "choose happiness," "practice positivity" — they're asking you to describe a room you can't even enter.

And then there's the toxic positivity layer.

"You have so much to be grateful for!"

That's not helpful. That's gaslighting.

Because you're sitting there thinking: "I know I should feel grateful. I know my life isn't bad. So why can't I feel anything?"

And the answer is: because numbness isn't about your circumstances.

**It's about your nervous system.**

And your nervous system doesn't respond to affirmations.

It responds to safety signals.

Which means the path back isn't through your thoughts.

**It's through your body.**

#### Variant 1

Let me explain what's actually happening in your body.

Your nervous system has three main modes.

**Mode one:** Safe and social. This is where connection happens. Where you feel things. Where you laugh, cry, love, and live.

**Mode two:** Fight or flight. Danger mode. Anxiety lives here. Anger lives here. It's unpleasant, but at least you're feeling something.

**Mode three:** Freeze. Shutdown. This is where numbness lives.

When life gets overwhelming — when there's too much pain, too much stress, too much everything — your nervous system makes a decision.

"We can't fight this. We can't run from this. The only option left is to shut down."

And so it does.

This isn't weakness. This is brilliant engineering.

Your body protected you when fighting or running wasn't possible.

It numbed the pain because feeling it would have been too much.

**Freeze is a survival strategy. Not a flaw.**

But here's the thing: the danger passed.

And your nervous system is still in freeze.

It's like a fire alarm that went off during an actual fire — and then never stopped beeping.

The fire is out. But the alarm is still screaming.

Except it's not screaming. It's doing the opposite.

It's silent.

**And the way to reset it isn't through thoughts. It's through gentle, body-based practices that signal to your nervous system: "Hey. It's over. You can come back online now."**

#### Variant 1

**Reconnecting with Sensation**

We're going to start simple.

Not with emotions — those will come later.

Just with sensation.

Your body has been in shutdown mode. The first step is just noticing that it exists.

*"We're not going to try to feel grateful. We're not going to try to feel anything specific. We're just going to notice what's actually here."*

---

**5 Senses Grounding**

This is a basic reconnection practice. You're going to use your senses — not your thoughts — to come back into your body.

---

**SIGHT**

Look around wherever you are.

Name five things you can see.

Don't judge them. Don't evaluate. Just notice.

A wall. A window. A shadow. A color. A shape.

Five things.

---

**TOUCH**

Now four things you can feel physically.

Your feet on the floor.

Your hands on your legs.

The fabric of your clothes.

The air on your skin.

---

**SOUND**

Three things you can hear.

Maybe a car outside.

Maybe the hum of a building.

Maybe your own breath.

---

**SMELL**

Two things you can smell.

If you can't smell anything, that's okay. Just notice what the air smells like. Neutral counts.

---

**TASTE**

One thing you can taste.

Whatever's in your mouth right now. Coffee. Water. Nothing in particular.

---

Okay.

You just did something important.

You came out of your head — and back into your senses.

That's the first step.

Not because it feels amazing. It might feel like nothing.

But you just proved to your nervous system: the body still works. The senses are still online.

**The signal is weak. But it's not gone.**

#### Variant 1

**Body Scan Thaw**

Now we go deeper.

The grounding practice brought you back to the room.

This practice brings you back into your body.

*"Your body is like a house you've been locked out of. We're going room by room, turning the lights back on."*

---

**Body Scan with Breath**

Find a comfortable position. Sitting or lying down — whatever feels okay.

Close your eyes if that feels safe. If not, soft focus is fine.

---

We're going to scan through your body, part by part.

Not to fix anything. Not to change anything.

Just to notice.

---

**Start with your feet.**

What do they feel like right now?

Heavy? Light? Numb? Tingly? Cold? Warm?

You don't have to feel anything specific. Just check in.

Take a breath. Let it fill that area.

Exhale. Move on.

---

**Your lower legs.**

What's there?

Any tension? Any sensation? Any absence of sensation?

Breathe in. Breathe out.

---

**Your upper legs and hips.**

Check in.

What do you notice?

---

**Your belly.**

This is where a lot of frozen emotion lives.

Don't force anything. Just notice.

Is it tight? Soft? Churning? Empty?

Breathe into it gently.

---

**Your chest.**

What's your chest holding right now?

Pressure? Openness? Nothing?

All answers are fine.

Breathe.

---

**Your shoulders and neck.**

Often the most tense places.

What do you feel?

---

**Your face.**

Your jaw. Your eyes. Your forehead.

What's happening there?

---

**Your whole body, all at once.**

Take one full breath.

Feel yourself — all of yourself — in one moment.

---

Good.

You just did something many people never do.

You checked in with your body without trying to fix it.

That's not just a practice.

**That's the beginning of thawing.**
