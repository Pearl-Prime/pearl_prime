#### Variant 1

**The 3-Second Check-In**

Here's a practice you can use anytime you notice anxiety showing up.

It takes **three seconds**.

---

**Step 1: Notice (1 second)**

Ask yourself: *"Where do I feel this in my body?"*

Chest? Stomach? Throat? Hands?

Just notice. Don't fix.

---

**Step 2: Acknowledge (1 second)**

Say to yourself: *"Yeah. That's anxiety. It's here because there's uncertainty."*

Not "I need to get rid of this."

Just: *"It's here."*

---

**Step 3: Decide (1 second)**

Ask yourself: *"Is this dangerous? Or just uncomfortable?"*

If dangerous → listen.

If uncomfortable → act anyway.

---

That's it.

Three seconds.

**Notice. Acknowledge. Decide.**

And then you act.

#### Variant 1

**Box Breathing (While Anxious)**

Not to *fix* the anxiety.

**To prove you can breathe while it's still there.**

Breathing gives you something to **anchor to** while the anxiety is present.

It's not a cure.

**It's a companion practice.**

---

**Box Breathing: 4-4-4-4**

Inhale for 4 counts.
Hold for 4 counts.
Exhale for 4 counts.
Hold for 4 counts.

---

**Inhale... 2, 3, 4.**
**Hold... 2, 3, 4.**
**Exhale... 2, 3, 4.**
**Hold... 2, 3, 4.**

**Again.**

**Inhale... 2, 3, 4.**
**Hold... 2, 3, 4.**
**Exhale... 2, 3, 4.**
**Hold... 2, 3, 4.**

---

Notice: Did the anxiety disappear?

Maybe. Maybe not.

But you just breathed — **while the anxiety was there.**

**That's proof you can do something while feeling this way.**

#### Variant 1

**5-4-3-2-1 Grounding**

This isn't about calming down.

It's about **bringing your attention back to the present moment** — even while you're anxious.

---

**Name 5 things you can see.**

Look around. What do you notice?

---

**Name 4 things you can touch.**

Your shirt. Your chair. The floor under your feet.

---

**Name 3 things you can hear.**

A car outside. The hum of the room. Your own breath.

---

**Name 2 things you can smell.**

Coffee. Fabric. Air.

---

**Name 1 thing you can taste.**

Whatever's in your mouth right now.

---

This isn't making the anxiety go away.

It's just bringing you **here**.

To this moment.

Where the anxiety is present — but so are you.
