#### Variant 1

Here's the important distinction:

**A slip is one moment.**

**A collapse is when you let that moment define everything.**

A slip:

You freeze once.

You notice it.

You choose differently next time.

A collapse:

You freeze once.

You tell yourself you failed.

You stop trying.

You go back to the old pattern permanently.

The slip doesn't hurt you.

**The story you tell yourself about the slip does.**

#### Variant 1

Here's how to recover from a slip:

**1. Acknowledge what happened.**

*"I froze. I didn't send the text. I waited instead of acting."*

**2. Don't spiral.**

*"This doesn't mean I'm back at square one. It just means this situation activated the old pattern."*

**3. Do the thing now (if you still can).**

If the moment hasn't passed — do it now.

Send the text.

Say the thing.

**While anxious.**

Right now.

If the moment has passed — let it go.

You'll get another chance.

**4. Notice what you learned.**

*"Okay. So that situation triggered the old pattern. Now I know."*

*"Next time something similar happens, I'll be ready."*

**5. Keep going.**

Don't let one slip turn into ten.

The next time you feel anxious about something —

**Act while anxious.**

Prove to yourself:

*"That was one moment. Not a permanent state."*
