#### Variant 1

Let me explain what's happening in your nervous system.

You have two main modes:

**Sympathetic:** Fight or flight. Action mode. Stress response.

**Parasympathetic:** Rest and digest. Recovery mode. Safety response.

In a healthy system, these toggle back and forth throughout the day.

You work (sympathetic), you rest (parasympathetic), you work, you rest.

But in chronic burnout, something breaks.

The sympathetic system gets stuck in "on."

It keeps running even when there's nothing to run from.

This is called **sympathetic dominance.**

Your body is stuck in a state of perpetual readiness.

Even when you lie down.

Even when you take a vacation.

Even when you have nothing to do.

The alarm is still ringing.

---

Here's why:

Your nervous system doesn't respond to your thoughts.

It responds to your physiology.

It's reading signals from your body: heart rate, breath rate, muscle tension.

And if those signals say "danger" — fast heart, shallow breath, tight muscles — your nervous system believes them.

It doesn't matter that you're safe.

It doesn't matter that you have time off.

**Your body is sending the wrong signals.**

So the fix isn't more time off.

The fix is learning how to send different signals.

That's what the practices in this chapter do.

They teach your body a new language: the language of safety.

#### Variant 1

**The Permission to Stop**

Here's the hardest exercise in the book for overachievers.

You're going to do... less.

Way less.

Your only job for the next six minutes is to breathe slowly.

That's it.

Watch how much your body resists.

*"This is where you find out how addicted to busyness you are."*

---

**Resonance Breathing — 6 BPM**

We're going to breathe at about 6 breaths per minute.

That's inhale for 5 seconds, exhale for 5 seconds.

This pace is specifically designed to shift your nervous system from sympathetic (stress) to parasympathetic (rest).

There's science behind this. It's called resonance frequency breathing.

Your heart rate and breath sync up. Your nervous system starts to calm down.

---

Find a comfortable position.

Close your eyes if that feels okay.

---

**Inhale slowly... 2, 3, 4, 5.**

**Exhale slowly... 2, 3, 4, 5.**

---

**Again.**

**Inhale... 2, 3, 4, 5.**

**Exhale... 2, 3, 4, 5.**

---

**Keep going at this pace.**

**Don't try to do anything else.**

**Just breathe.**

[Silence for practice — about 2 minutes]

---

**Notice what came up.**

Did your mind resist?

Did you feel the urge to stop?

To check something?

To "get back to" something?

That's the nervous system fighting for the old pattern.

But you stayed here.

You breathed.

You proved that you can stop.

**That's the first step.**

#### Variant 1

**Heart Coherence Training**

This practice trains your heart and brain to work together.

It builds flexibility in your nervous system — the ability to shift between active and restful states.

*"If you're stuck in sympathetic dominance, your heart rate variability is probably low. This practice raises it."*

---

**Heart Rate Coherence — HRV Training**

Heart rate variability (HRV) is a measure of how well your nervous system can adapt.

High HRV = flexible, resilient, able to switch modes.

Low HRV = stuck, rigid, unable to downshift.

This practice raises HRV through rhythmic breathing and heart-focused attention.

---

Find a comfortable position.

Place your hand on your heart.

---

**Breathe at a slow, steady pace.**

About 5 seconds in, 5 seconds out.

---

**As you breathe, focus on your heart.**

Not thinking about it — feeling it.

Feel the warmth of your hand there.

Feel the rhythm if you can sense it.

---

**Now add a feeling.**

Gratitude. Appreciation. Calm.

Something that feels gentle and good.

It doesn't have to be big.

Just hold that feeling while you breathe.

---

**Inhale... feel the warmth...**

**Exhale... feel the calm...**

---

**Continue for the next few minutes.**

[Silence for practice — about 2 minutes]

---

**Good.**

You just trained your nervous system to downshift.

This isn't a one-time fix.

It's a practice.

The more you do it, the easier it becomes for your body to enter rest.

#### Variant 1

**Sleep Reset — 4-7-8 Breathing**

This practice is specifically for the "wired but tired" state.

For when you're exhausted but you can't turn off.

For when you lie in bed and your mind won't stop running.

*"This isn't a sleep hack. It's a way to tell your nervous system that the day is over. That you're safe. That you can let go."*

---

**4-7-8 Breathing**

Inhale for 4 counts.

Hold for 7 counts.

Exhale for 8 counts.

The extended exhale and hold activate your parasympathetic nervous system.

This signals: "Time to stop. Time to rest. Time to be done."

---

Let's try it.

**Inhale through your nose... 2, 3, 4.**

**Hold your breath... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.**

**Exhale slowly through your mouth... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8.**

---

**Again.**

**Inhale... 2, 3, 4.**

**Hold... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.**

**Exhale... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8.**

---

**One more round.**

**Inhale... 2, 3, 4.**

**Hold... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7.**

**Exhale... 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8.**

---

How does your body feel now?

Heavier? Slower? More settled?

Use this before bed. Or any time you need to downshift.

It's not magic.

**It's physiology.**
