# Tier: ANCHORED

# PERSONA: Parents of teenagers (13-19)
# ENGINE: Thought chain builds catastrophe from nothing

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v01 — GRADE CHAIN — 4 STEPS
---
Linda sees the C on the progress report. The chain starts immediately. C means he's struggling. Struggling means something's wrong. Wrong means distraction—drugs? depression? The distraction will spread to other classes. Other classes fail. College applications ruined. Future employment compromised. She's built unemployment from a C in chemistry. Her son is eating cereal, unaware of the career she just destroyed in her head.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v02 — SILENCE CHAIN — 5 STEPS
---
Marcus's daughter didn't respond to his text. She always responds within an hour. If she didn't respond, she's upset. If she's upset, it's about what he said yesterday. What he said was wrong. She's pulling away. This is how it starts—the pulling away that becomes estrangement. Adult children who don't call. The text was "pick up milk?" The estrangement is fully built before he remembers she's in class.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v03 — FRIEND CHAIN — 4 STEPS
---
Karen hears her son mention a new friend. A name she doesn't recognize. New friend means new influence. New influence could be bad influence. Bad influence leads to bad decisions. Bad decisions at sixteen have permanent consequences. She's standing at the sink building a criminal record from "I hung out with Jake today." Jake might be fine. The chain doesn't wait to find out.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v04 — LATE NIGHT CHAIN — 5 STEPS
---
David's daughter is texting at midnight. If she's texting, she's not sleeping. If she's not sleeping, there's something keeping her up. Something keeping her up is anxiety or drama or worse. The worse spirals into self-harm, eating disorders, things he reads about. He's built a hospitalization from seeing a phone glow under a door crack. She might just be texting a friend about homework.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v05 — PARTY CHAIN — 4 STEPS
---
Sarah's teen mentions a party this weekend. Party means alcohol. Alcohol means poor decisions. Poor decisions at a party means the kind of night that follows kids forever. Photos. Arrests. Assault. She's built a life-ruining event from "can I go to Emma's party?" Emma's mom will be home. There will be pizza. Sarah's chain doesn't include pizza.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — SAME STRUCTURE DIFFERENT CONTENT
---
The chain runs the same way every time. Monday: late homework → failing → held back → ruined transcript. Wednesday: skipped breakfast → eating disorder → hospitalization. Friday: new friend → bad influence → drugs → arrest. Different triggers. Same architecture. Linda's brain builds the same tower from any foundation. The content changes. The construction doesn't.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — SILENCE TRIGGERS CHAIN
---
Marcus builds from silence. Text not returned: chain. Bedroom quiet: chain. Car ride without talking: chain. Each silence is a foundation for catastrophe. The chains are different—estrangement, depression, secret life—but the building is identical. Silence offers no information. His brain fills it with disaster. Every time.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — EVERY HESITATION BECOMES EVIDENCE
---
Karen collects hesitations. "How was school?" "Fine." Hesitation before fine: hiding something. "Who's that text from?" "Just a friend." Hesitation: lying about the friend. "Are you okay?" "Yeah." Hesitation: definitely not okay. Each hesitation feeds a chain. Each chain reaches crisis. Her son pauses before speaking because he's thinking. She hears the pause as confession.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — CHAIN RUNS WITHOUT NEW INPUT
---
David built the chain Monday from the guidance counselor email. By Wednesday, no new information. The chain runs anyway. "Let's chat about college planning" became "there's a problem" became "he's not going to get in anywhere" became "his whole future is at risk." The email sits in his inbox, unchanged. The chain has added twelve floors since Monday.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — NEUTRAL BECOMES NEGATIVE
---
Sarah's brain converts neutral to crisis automatically now. "I'm going out" → sneaking somewhere dangerous. "I'm fine" → hiding pain. "I ate at school" → skipping meals. Every neutral statement gets fed into the chain-builder. The output is always catastrophe. The inputs vary. Her daughter's life is normal. Her brain doesn't process normal.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v01 — INTERRUPTION
---
Linda is four links into the chain when her phone rings. Work call. By the time she hangs up—fifteen minutes later—she's lost track. The C-to-unemployment tower is half-built somewhere in her head. She never finished it. Her son is still eating cereal. Still fine. The tower just... stopped. She forgot to keep building.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v02 — SEES THE CHAIN
---
Marcus is mid-construction when something shifts. Text not returned → she's pulling away → estrangement → holidays alone. He sees himself building. The links are visible—not as truth, as construction. He's done this before. Same shape. Different trigger. Last month it was a short phone call. The chain is familiar. Not comforting. Just recognizable.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v03 — CHAIN CONTRADICTED
---
Karen built the whole tower. New friend → bad influence → drugs → arrest. Then she met Jake. Honor student. Chess club. Planning to study engineering. The tower doesn't collapse—it just stops making sense. She built a drug arrest from a chess player. The chain was solid. Reality wasn't. Both exist now, not fitting together.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v04 — PATTERN RECOGNIZED
---
David notices he's building again. The guidance counselor email is halfway to "homeless at thirty" already. He catches himself adding the next link. The next one would be "we failed as parents." The one after would be "he'll never forgive us." He knows these links. He's added them before. The chain wants to keep going. He can see it wanting.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v05 — CHAIN STOPPED BY FACT
---
Sarah is building: party → alcohol → assault → trauma. Then her daughter says, "It's at Emma's. Her mom's chaperoning. No boys allowed." The chain short-circuits. Three facts that don't fit the tower. The building materials don't work. She's still anxious—the feeling doesn't update as fast as the information. But the chain has nowhere to go.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v01 — DOESN'T FOLLOW
---
The thought arrives: he failed the test. Linda feels the pull—the next link forming. Failed means struggling. Struggling means— She stops. Not arguing with it. Just not following. The thought sits there, unfinished. One link, no chain. She asks her son about the test directly. B-. Not failed. The chain never got built.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v02 — NOTICES AND PAUSES
---
Marcus catches himself mid-chain. The unreturned text was two hours ago. He's already at "she's distancing herself." He notices. The chain is half-built. He doesn't add more. Two hours without a text sits there, incomplete. She might be busy. Might be in class. Might have forgotten. He can't know from silence. The uncertainty is uncomfortable. He sits with it.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v03 — FIRST LINK ONLY
---
Karen hears "new friend" and her mind starts: new friend means... She catches it there. One thought. New friend means new friend. Maybe good, maybe neutral, maybe not great. She doesn't build the tower—bad influence, drugs, arrest. Her son is still talking. She's still listening. "Jake's really into coding." That's information. The chain doesn't get it.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v04 — ASKS INSTEAD OF BUILDS
---
David sees the guidance counselor email. The chain wants to start. He doesn't let it. Opens the email instead. "Excited to discuss scholarship opportunities for your son." Scholarships. Not problems. He could have built a tower of failure from that subject line. He clicked instead. The tower never existed. The information was right there.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v05 — THOUGHT EXISTS, NOT CHASED
---
The thought is there: she's hiding something. Sarah heard something in her daughter's voice. The thought wants to chain. Hiding something → lying → serious trouble → broken trust → estrangement. She doesn't chase it. The thought sits in the room like a guess she doesn't have to process. Her daughter sounded off. That's what she knows. The rest is construction she's not obligated to build.
