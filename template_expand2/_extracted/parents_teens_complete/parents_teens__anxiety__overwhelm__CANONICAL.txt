## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — TASK LOAD — HEAVINESS
---
6:15am. Linda opens her eyes to the list already running. Permission slips due. College application deadline Friday. Parent-teacher conference rescheduled. Work presentation tomorrow. The heaviness arrives before her feet hit the floor. Her son needs lunch money. Her daughter needs a ride. The dog needs to go out. The list doesn't wait for coffee. She stands in the kitchen, not sure which task to start.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — SOCIAL LOAD — BUZZING
---
Marcus's phone buzzes through dinner. Soccer coach about practice. Ex-wife about the schedule change. His mother asking about Thanksgiving. Work email marked urgent. His son asking about the car. The buzzing isn't just the phone—it's him. Everyone needs something. He puts down his fork. Stares at the screen. Types half a reply. Forgets which conversation it's for.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — EMOTIONAL LOAD — FOG
---
Karen is carrying everyone. Her daughter's anxiety about the SATs. Her son's friend drama. Her husband's work stress. Her mother's health scare. A fog settles behind her eyes. She's listening to her daughter talk about test prep. The words reach her muffled. She nods at the right places. Somewhere underneath all their feelings, she can't find her own.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — TIME LOAD — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
Three hours until the school event. David has to pick up the cake, print the programs, get his daughter's dress hemmed, and respond to six work emails. His body moves slower, not faster. Each task takes twice as long. He stands in the kitchen between the car keys and the laptop. The list is in his head. His feet don't know which direction.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY LOAD — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Saturday morning. Sarah's house is full. Her son's friends playing video games—explosions, shouting. Her daughter practicing violin upstairs. The dishwasher rumbling. Phone ringing. Dog barking. The pressure builds behind her eyes. She stands in the middle of the living room. The sounds layer. She can't find a first thing to do. She's just standing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — TASK LOAD REPEATS
---
The pattern runs all day. Linda starts the permission slip—phone rings, school nurse. Answers the phone—remembers the presentation. Opens the presentation—email notification about college app. Starts the application—son needs help with homework. By 9pm, the heaviness is in her shoulders. Four hours of touching everything. Finishing nothing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERYONE NEEDS SOMETHING
---
Marcus tracks the asking. 7am: wife asks about pickup schedule. 8am: boss asks about the report. 12pm: daughter texts about money. 3pm: son's teacher calls about grades. 6pm: mother calls about Sunday dinner. Each answer opens a new thread. Each thread needs follow-up. By 9pm, he has six half-finished conversations. Everyone waiting.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CARRYING EVERYONE
---
Karen absorbs feelings all week. Monday: daughter's test anxiety. Tuesday: son's rejection from the team. Wednesday: husband's job stress. Thursday: mother's fear about the biopsy. By Friday, the fog is constant. Other people's feelings live in her chest now. Her own aren't—no room. The fog doesn't correspond to anything on her calendar. It's everyone else's weather.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — TIME COMPOUNDS
---
David's time deficit grows daily. Monday: thirty minutes behind. Tuesday: an hour. Wednesday: two hours of tasks pushed to Thursday. Thursday: the pushed tasks meet the new ones. The pile doesn't shrink. His awareness gets shallower. He makes it through the week. Barely. Barely is the speed now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — SENSORY STACKING
---
Every room is loud. The kitchen: dishwasher, microwave, radio. The living room: TV, video games, teenagers talking. The bedroom: husband snoring, phone charging, street noise. Sarah moves from room to room trying to find quiet. The noise follows. The pressure behind her eyes is constant now. Saturday was supposed to be rest. Saturday is just differently loud.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — EXTERNAL REDUCTION
---
End of day. Linda is listing the five things still undone. Then the email arrives: "School event postponed to next week." One thing lifts. Just one. But something shifts in her chest. That's when she realizes how much she was holding. The remaining tasks are still there. But there's a gap now. Something moved.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAMING THE PILE
---
Marcus has been frozen at the kitchen counter for ten minutes. Then he opens Notes. Types a list. Just the things. No fixing. No ordering. Soccer, presentation, mom's call, kids' schedules, work deadline. He looks at the list. Five things. It felt like fifteen. Seeing the number doesn't solve anything. But the fog lifts slightly. The pile has edges.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — SOMEONE TAKES ONE
---
Karen is holding too much. Her husband walks by. Sees her face. "I'll handle the school thing. You focus on your mom." One thing leaves her hands. The load lightens before she can say thanks. Her shoulders drop half an inch. She still has the other six things. But he took the school thing. That's enough to sit down and breathe.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — TIME OPENS
---
David checks the calendar. Soccer practice canceled—coach is sick. The whole evening opens. The relief is physical—his chest unlocks. The tasks are still there. But they're not stacked on top of practice anymore. There's room between them now. He starts the first one. Works slower than he has all week. There's time.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — ONE SMALL ACTION
---
Linda has been staring at the pile for twelve minutes. She doesn't try to solve it. She picks one thing—the permission slip—and signs it. Just that. The pile is still there. But one thing moved. Her hands did something. She picks the next smallest thing. Signs that too. Two done. The stack looks the same. She's moving through it anyway.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — CLOSING SOMETHING
---
Marcus has fourteen browser tabs open. School portal, work email, soccer schedule, three articles he started, college research. He doesn't try to finish anything. He closes one tab. Then another. The screen gets quieter. He closes four more. The articles he wasn't reading. The site he already checked. The tasks exist. The tabs don't have to.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — STEPPING OUTSIDE
---
Karen doesn't tell anyone. Just walks to the backyard. Closes the door behind her. The kids exist inside. The husband, the phone, the noise—all inside. She stands in the grass. Three minutes of not being in it. The tasks don't disappear. She's just outside of them for now. The grass is cold on her feet. That's all she has to feel right now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYING NO TO ONE
---
David looks at the list. Six things. He calls the soccer coach. "We can't bring the snacks this week—can someone else cover?" The coach says sure. One thing off. David didn't solve the overwhelm. He made it five things instead of six. The difference is small. The reduction is real. Five is lighter than six.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WASHING HANDS
---
Sarah's head is full. The schedule, the kids, the noise, the needs. She walks to the bathroom. Turns on the water. Washes her hands. The water is warm. The soap smells like soap. She watches it swirl down the drain. Twenty seconds of just water and hands. The list is still there when she dries off. But her hands are clean.

**Engine intact:** Too much at once, system stalls, small reducing action helps.
**Resisted:** Making overwhelm about "modern parenting is too hard." The engine is neutral.
