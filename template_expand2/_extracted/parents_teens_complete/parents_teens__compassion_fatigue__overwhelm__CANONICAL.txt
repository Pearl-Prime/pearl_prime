## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — EVERYONE NEEDS — HEAVINESS
---
Linda walks in the door to requests. Her daughter needs help with the college essay. Her son needs a ride to practice. Her husband needs to vent about work. The dog needs to go out. The heaviness arrives before she puts down her bag. Everyone needs something from her. Her capacity to care is somewhere behind the weight.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — EMOTIONAL QUEUE — BUZZING
---
Marcus's evening stacks. His daughter is crying about her friend group. His son is anxious about the test. His wife is frustrated about her mother. Each person needs him to feel with them. The buzzing in his head isn't the TV—it's the emotional load. He can feel all of it. He can't feel any of it well.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — SUPPORT PILE — FOG
---
Karen has three family members going through hard things. Daughter's first heartbreak. Son's college rejection. Husband's job uncertainty. Each one deserves attention. Real attention. A fog settles when she thinks about the care she owes. With three, she's spreading thin enough to be nearly absent.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — ASKING STACKS — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
"Mom, can you—" The fourth time in an hour. David has been someone's support all evening already. His movements slow before answering. The giving has been continuous. The tank refills between requests—except there's been no between. Just ask after ask.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — EMPATHY OVERLOAD — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Sarah listens to her daughter's drama. Then her son's frustration. Then her husband's worry. Each person's feelings enter her system. The pressure builds behind her eyes. She's holding everyone's emotional weight. Her own feelings are somewhere underneath the pile, unreachable.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — NEEDING STACKS
---
The pattern runs all evening. Linda finishes with daughter—son needs her. Finishes with son—husband needs her. Finishes with husband—daughter's back with more. By bedtime, she's been a resource for four hours. The tank doesn't refill between draws.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EMOTIONAL CARRYOVER
---
Marcus carries the conversations with him. Daughter's tears are in his chest through dinner. Son's anxiety joins it during dishes. By 9pm, he's holding feelings that aren't his but live in his body. The emotional residue accumulates.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CARE COMPOUNDS
---
Karen's care obligations grow. Monday: daughter needs processing. Tuesday: son needs support. Wednesday: husband needs attention. The care doesn't become more efficient. It just divides into smaller portions. The fog thickens with each addition.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — MINUTES ADD UP
---
"Just a minute" happens fifteen times. David counts at bedtime. Fifteen minutes times fifteen requests is four hours of support. Each minute felt small. The total is enormous.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — FEELINGS STACK
---
Sarah's empathy absorption compounds. Morning: daughter's social anxiety. Afternoon: son's school stress. Evening: husband's work frustration. Each feeling layers on the last. The pressure behind her eyes is everyone's emotions stacked.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — ONE RESOLVES
---
The daughter figures out the college essay. Doesn't need Linda anymore on that. One person's need lifts. Something shifts in her chest. She'd been holding four things. Now three. The lightening is small but real.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — SOMEONE ELSE HOLDS ONE
---
His wife takes their daughter's conversation tonight. Marcus doesn't carry that one. One person's feelings aren't in his chest this evening. There's space where the daughter's drama would be. The fog is slightly thinner.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — NAMES THE LOAD
---
Karen writes down who needs what. Daughter: heartbreak. Son: rejection. Husband: job worry. Three things. She'd felt like fifteen. The pile has edges now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — CLOSES DOOR
---
David goes to the bedroom. Closes the door. Twenty minutes of no one needing him. The minutes don't stack. The tank sits still. Something refills slightly.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — FEELS OWN FEELING
---
Sarah realizes she hasn't checked her own state in hours. The pressure behind her eyes is there. But underneath: tiredness. Her own tiredness. A feeling that belongs to her.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — HELPS ONE
---
Four people need things. Linda picks one. Helps her daughter with the essay. Just that. The pile is still there. One person was helped. She picks the next one.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — SCHEDULES GAPS
---
Marcus looks at the evening. Builds in fifteen minutes alone before dinner. The conversations will still be emotional. There will be space before them.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — LIMITS TIME
---
Karen gives each family member twenty minutes tonight. Not less care—bounded care. Three people, twenty minutes each. That's what she has.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS "LATER"
---
"Dad, can you—" David says, "Can we talk about this in thirty minutes?" The person says okay. The tank gets thirty minutes of not draining.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS OUTSIDE
---
Sarah steps into the backyard. Three minutes. The family exists inside. She stands outside not being anyone's support. Three minutes of nothing stacking.
