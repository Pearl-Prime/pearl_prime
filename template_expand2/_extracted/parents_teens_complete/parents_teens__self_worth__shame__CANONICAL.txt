================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — NOT ENOUGH FOR THEM — FACE HEAT
---
Linda watches her kids navigate things she can't help with. AP Physics. College essays. Social dynamics she doesn't understand. Her face heats. She's not enough for them anymore. The parenting they need is beyond her. She's sitting in a role she can't fill.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — DON'T DESERVE THE GOOD KID — CHEST DROP
---
Marcus's daughter is turning out amazing. Kind, driven, thoughtful. His chest drops when people compliment his parenting. He didn't do anything special. She's just her. He doesn't deserve credit for who she is. The good outcomes aren't his.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — SHOULDN'T WANT TIME — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Karen wants time alone. Away from her kids. Real time, not twenty minutes. Her shoulders curl at the wanting. What kind of mother wants that? The wanting feels like proof she's not built for this. Good mothers don't want escape.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — WHO AM I TO GUIDE — STOMACH SINKING
---
David gives advice to his son about life choices. His stomach sinks as the words leave his mouth. Who is he to guide anyone? His own life is full of mistakes. The guidance feels like pretending. He's not wise enough to advise.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — NOT THE PARENT THEY NEED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Sarah sees what her kids need. Emotional availability. Patience. Consistent presence. Her throat tightens. That's not her. Not anymore, maybe not ever. She's not the parent they deserve. The gap between what they need and what she is—she feels it constantly.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY FAILURE CONFIRMS
---
Linda's inadequacy finds evidence everywhere. Can't help with calculus: not enough. Doesn't know the friend drama: not enough. Missed the subtle signs: not enough. The face heats the same way every time.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY COMPLIMENT DROPS
---
Marcus's chest drops at every praise. "You raised her well": drop. "Great kid you have": drop. "Must be proud": drop. The compliments feel like misunderstanding.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — EVERY WANT CURLS
---
Karen's shoulders curl at every desire. Want a weekend away: curl. Want quiet: curl. Want to not be needed: curl. Each want triggers the same "bad mother" response.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — EVERY ADVICE SINKS
---
David's stomach sinks before every guidance moment. Talk about college: sink. Talk about relationships: sink. Talk about values: sink. Same "who am I" every time.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — EVERY GAP TIGHTENS
---
Sarah's throat tightens at every evidence of the gap. Too impatient: tighten. Too distracted: tighten. Not present enough: tighten.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — THEY DON'T NEED PERFECT
---
Linda's daughter asks for help with an essay—not the physics, just the words. Something Linda can do. The help works. They don't need a genius. They need a mother.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — HE SHOWED UP

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — WANTING IS HUMAN

Karen's friend says, "I need a week alone. Just to remember who I am." Another mother. Same want. The want isn't bad mothering. It's being a person.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — ENOUGH RIGHT NOW
