# PERSONA: Parents of teenagers (13-19)
# ENGINE: Detachment from present, watching self from outside

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Linda is making dinner before she registers deciding to. Her hands are chopping vegetables. The knife moves. She's somewhere else, watching herself be a mother in a kitchen. Her son walks through. "Hey Mom." Her mouth says "hey honey" from a distance. The dinner gets made. She's technically present for all of it. Technically.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED SENSATION
---
Marcus's daughter hugs him goodbye. He feels the pressure of her arms three seconds later, muted and far away. "Love you, Dad." He says it back. The words land eventually. She's out the door before the hug fully registers. He's receiving his own parenting on delay. The morning happened. He's getting the news of it afterward.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — MEMORY GAP
---
Karen can't remember the conversation at dinner. She was there—her plate is empty. Her son mentioned something about school. She responded. The evidence exists: he's doing homework now, so the conversation ended fine. But the dinner itself is a gap. She was at the table. The memory isn't.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — EMOTIONAL LAG
---
David's daughter gets into her first-choice school. She's jumping, crying, hugging him. His face does joy. His voice says congratulations. Twenty minutes later, alone in the garage, his eyes water. The feeling arrives late. He wasn't happy in the living room. He's happy now, in the garage, when she can't see it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Sarah drives carpool. Four teenagers in the backseat, talking, laughing. The sound is far away. She's navigating traffic, making turns, but the car feels like it belongs to someone else. She drops them off. Says the right things. The car is quiet now. She must have driven. She was somewhere else while it happened.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — DAYS BLUR
---
Monday looks like Saturday which looked like last Thursday. Linda makes breakfast, drives to work, comes home, makes dinner. The days don't separate. Her son is always at the table or in his room or somewhere else. The parenting motions happen. She's present for none of it distinctly. Time passes. She floats alongside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — PARENTING MONTAGE
---
Marcus sees himself in fragments. Hand signing permission slip. Face at the school play. Body in the bleachers at the game. The in-between is gone. He's a montage, not a continuous father. One moment he's helping with homework, then he's saying goodnight, then it's morning. The transitions evaporated.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CONVERSATION AUTOPILOT
---
Karen does the conversations all evening. "How was school?" "Fine." "Any homework?" "Some." The exchange happens eight hundred times. The words come from somewhere trained. She watches them land appropriately. Her daughter seems fine. The autopilot parented adequately. Karen was technically there.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — BODY ON SCHEDULE
---
David's body knows the routine. 6am: wake son for school. 7am: drive to bus stop. 5pm: ask about homework. 9pm: say goodnight. He's watching a competent father work. That father is using his hands. Has his face. Knows the schedule. He's in there somewhere. Or behind the glass.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good news—daughter made the team. Bad news—son got a detention. Both arrive at the same muted frequency. Sarah is present for both announcements. Touched by neither. Her face does the right things. Happy, then concerned. The emotions are performed correctly. They don't reach her.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
Linda is folding her son's laundry when something shifts. His old t-shirt—the one from sixth grade camp that still fits somehow. The fabric is soft. Not soft like a fact—soft like a feeling. She's here. In this moment. For three seconds, the glass isn't there. She's a mother holding her son's shirt. Then it closes. But it opened.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAME LANDS
---
"Dad." His daughter says it twice before Marcus registers it's him. Then something catches. He's Dad. That's him. He's in the conversation, not watching it. She's asking about the car for Saturday. He answers from here. The moment fades. But for five seconds, he was inside the parenting.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
Karen trips on a shoe in the hallway. The stumble is immediate—sharp, here, now. Not delayed. She's in her body. Aware of catching herself. Of the shoe her son left again. Of the hallway. The watcher distance collapses for a moment. Then returns. But the stumble was real-time. She was in it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v04 — LAUGH HAPPENS
---
David laughs before he can stop it. Something his daughter said—actually funny. The laugh comes from his chest, not from the performance layer. He feels it happen. Feels the surprise of feeling it. The sound is his. The moment is his. Then it passes. But the laugh was real.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — SMELL BREAKS THROUGH
---
Sarah is cleaning her son's room. The smell—teenage boy and old socks and something that's just him. It bypasses the glass completely. She's not watching herself smell it. She's in the smell. In the memory of him at five, at ten, now. The watcher can't observe a smell from distance. For a moment, she's inside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01 — STAYS PRESENT
---
Linda is helping with homework. She notices herself starting to drift—the familiar float upward. She doesn't follow. Keeps her attention on the math problem. The specific numbers. The pencil in her son's hand. She stays in the helping. One problem, fully present. Then moves to the next.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE HUG
---
Marcus's daughter hugs him goodnight. He notices the watcher trying to pull back—observe the hug from above. He doesn't let it. Stays in his arms. The pressure. The smell of her shampoo. He's hugging. Not watching himself hug. For this one goodnight, he's inside it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — DRIVES, DOESN'T FLOAT
---
Karen is driving to school pickup. She catches the drift starting—the familiar lift where her body keeps driving and she leaves. She comes back. Hands on wheel. Eyes on road. Her daughter gets in the car. "Hi Mom." Karen says hi from here. Not from above. Present for the pickup.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — HAND ON SHOULDER
---
David puts his hand on his son's shoulder. Just passing through the kitchen. He feels it—the bone underneath, the warmth. He's about to drift. He doesn't. Keeps his hand there an extra second. The shoulder is real. His son is real. The touch is his. He walks on carrying the realness.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — SAYS IT FROM HERE
---
Sarah is saying goodnight to her daughter. She catches herself going automatic—the rehearsed "love you, sleep well." She stops. Takes a breath. Looks at her daughter's face. "I really love you." The words come from here, not from performance. Her daughter looks surprised. "Love you too, Mom." Both of them present for a moment.
