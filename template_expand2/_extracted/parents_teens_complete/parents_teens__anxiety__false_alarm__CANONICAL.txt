## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v01 — PHONE CALL — HEART RACING
---
The school's number appears on Linda's phone. 2:47pm. Her heart races before she answers. Something happened. He's hurt. He's in trouble. She answers with held breath. "Mrs. Chen? Just confirming the permission slip for Friday's trip." Permission slip. Her heart is still racing as she says yes, she'll sign it. The call was routine. Her body didn't know that.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v02 — CLOSED DOOR — STOMACH DROPPING
---
Marcus stands outside his daughter's room. The door is closed. It's been closed for three hours. His stomach drops. She's upset. Something happened at school. She's pulling away. He knocks. "What?" "Just checking if you want dinner." "I ate." The exchange is normal. Teenager normal. His stomach is still dropped, waiting for the crisis that isn't there.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v03 — LATE TEXT — SHOULDERS CLIMBING
---
It's 11:15pm. Curfew was 11. No text. Karen's shoulders climb toward her ears. Car accident. Something happened at the party. She should call hospitals. Her phone buzzes. "Traffic on Main. 5 min." Her shoulders stay high through the five minutes. Through the door opening. Through "hey mom." The danger was invented. The relief doesn't fully land.

## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v04 — GRADE NOTIFICATION — BREATH CATCHING
---
The parent portal sends an alert. New grade posted. David's breath catches before he opens it. Failed. He failed something. This will affect college. This will affect everything. He opens it. B+ on a quiz. His breath is still caught looking at a perfectly acceptable grade. The plus sign doesn't calm the catching. He closes the app. Opens it again to make sure.

## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v05 — QUIET DINNER — HANDS COLD
---
Dinner is quiet. Sarah's teen is eating, not talking. Normal teenage behavior. Her hands are cold around her fork anyway. Something's wrong. He's not telling her something. The silence means distance. The silence means loss. He finishes, says "thanks," goes to his room. The dinner was fine. Her hands stay cold through washing the dishes.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY NOTIFICATION
---
Linda's body doesn't learn. Monday: school email—heart races—routine newsletter. Tuesday: text from son—stomach drops—asking about dinner. Wednesday: call from unknown number—breath catches—college mail survey. Each notification triggers the same alarm. Each alarm finds nothing. Thursday's notification will get the same response. The pattern doesn't update.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY CLOSED DOOR
---
Marcus tracks his reactions. Bedroom door closed: stomach drops. Bathroom door locked: chest tightens. Car door slams: shoulders climb. Same response to every barrier between him and his daughter. The doors are normal. Teenagers close doors. His body treats each one as evidence of emergency. The emergency never arrives. The drops continue.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — EVERY LATE MINUTE
---
Karen's shoulders climb at 10:01 when curfew is 10. Climb at 10:05. Still climbing at 10:08. The car pulls in at 10:12. Safe. Tuesday: shoulders climb at 11:01. Car arrives 11:09. Safe. The pattern is identical. Every minute past deadline gets the same alarm. Every alarm is wrong. Every wrong alarm happens again the next time.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — CHECKING PATTERN
---
David checks the parent portal three times before bed. Grades haven't changed since an hour ago. He knows this. He checks anyway. The checking is the alarm in action—verifying the catastrophe that isn't there. He closes the laptop. Opens his phone. Checks the portal app. Same grades. Same checking. Same nothing wrong.

## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — SILENCE INTERPRETATION
---
Sarah interprets every silence. Quiet in the car: something's wrong. Quiet at dinner: something happened. Quiet while doing homework: struggling, hiding it. The silences are teenage silences—normal, developmental. Her body reads them as symptoms. Each silence gets the same cold hands, the same tight chest. Each silence turns out to be just silence.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v01 — CALL WAS NOTHING
---
The school called. Linda's heart raced through the whole conversation. Scholarship paperwork needed a signature. That's all. She hangs up. Her heart is still fast in a room where good news just arrived. Scholarship. Her body didn't hear "scholarship." It heard "school calling" and built a crisis. The crisis wasn't there. The racing was.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v02 — DOOR OPENED FINE
---
Marcus waited outside the closed door for an hour. Imagined everything—drugs, depression, running away. He knocked. His daughter looked up from her laptop. "Yeah?" She was watching a show. Headphones in. Normal teenager with a closed door. The hour of dread was fabricated. She wasn't hiding anything. She was just in her room.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v03 — SEES THE PATTERN
---
Karen's shoulders are climbing again. 10:03pm. Then she catches herself. This is the fourth night this week. Same climb. Same nothing. Same safe arrival. Her body is running a program that has never once been correct. The shoulders are still high. But she's watching them be high now. Watching the alarm that lies every time.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v04 — GRADE WAS FINE
---
David spent two hours dreading the report card. Failed classes. Summer school. College dreams dying. The report card arrived. B average. One C+ in chemistry. Fine. Totally fine. He sits with the mismatch. Two hours of dread for a B average. His body wrote a tragedy from a notification. The notification was just a notification.

## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v05 — SILENCE MEANT NOTHING
---
Dinner was quiet for the third night. Sarah's chest was tight through all of it. After dinner, her son stops at the door. "Thanks for dinner, Mom. Love you." Then goes to his room. The silence wasn't distance. The silence was just eating. He still said love you. The tightness was for nothing. The love was there the whole time.

## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v01 — DOESN'T CALL BACK
---
The school called and went to voicemail. Linda's heart races. She looks at the phone. The urge to call back immediately is strong. She waits. If it were urgent, they'd call again. She finishes her meeting. Checks the voicemail after. Routine reminder about picture day. She survived not knowing. The waiting didn't kill her.

## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v02 — DOESN'T KNOCK
---
The door is closed. Marcus's stomach drops. He stands in the hallway. Doesn't knock. Lets the door be closed. His daughter is seventeen. Closed doors are allowed. His stomach stays dropped while he walks downstairs. He makes dinner. The door opens on its own an hour later. "Something smells good." The waiting was survivable.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v03 — SITS WITH THE LATE
---
10:07pm. No text. Karen's shoulders are high. She doesn't text "where are you." Doesn't call. Sits with the not-knowing. The shoulders stay high. 10:14pm. Car in driveway. "Sorry, parking was crazy." Karen nods. Her shoulders stayed high for fourteen minutes. She didn't act on them. The alarm fired. She didn't obey it.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v04 — DOESN'T CHECK
---
The portal has new grades. David sees the notification. His breath catches. He closes the app. Doesn't check. Puts the phone in another room. The grades exist whether he looks or not. He eats dinner. Watches TV. Checks later—everything fine. The gap between notification and checking didn't collapse anything. Just delayed the nothing.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v05 — LETS SILENCE BE SILENCE
---
Dinner is quiet. Sarah's hands start to go cold. She notices them. Doesn't fill the silence with questions. Doesn't probe. Lets the quiet be quiet. Her son finishes eating. Goes to his room. The silence was just silence. Her hands were cold in a room where nothing was wrong. She let them be cold. Dinner ended anyway.
