## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — HAD TO SAY NO — MID — FACE HEAT
---
Linda said no to the class trip. The money isn't there. Her daughter's face fell. "But everyone's going." Linda's face heats. Everyone's going. Except her daughter. Because of Linda. Because Linda can't provide what other parents can. The heat spreads as her daughter walks to her room. Linda is standing in the kitchen, face burning, failing visibly.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — CHECK BOUNCED — LATE — CHEST DROP
---
The school called. David's check bounced for the activity fee. The chest drop is immediate. The school knows. The office staff knows. They had to call him about a bounced check. The phone is still in his hand. The chest is still dropped. His son will need to bring a new check tomorrow. Everyone will handle it. Everyone will know.

## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — COULDN'T CONTRIBUTE — EARLY — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
The team dinner. Each family was supposed to bring something or contribute $30. Karen sent crackers. Crackers from a box. Other families brought catered trays, bakery desserts. Her shoulders curl watching the table fill with expensive items and her crackers. Her son is eating someone else's food. She brought the cheapest thing there.

## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — TEEN KNOWS — MID — STOMACH SINKING
---
Michael's daughter asked why they can't go to Disneyland like her friends. He said "we can't afford it right now." Her face shifted. She knows now. His stomach sinks into the knowing. His child knows they're different. Knows they have less. The sinking continues as she nods, says okay, doesn't ask again. She learned something. He didn't want to teach it.

## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — OTHER PARENTS SAW — LATE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Fundraiser night. Susan didn't buy anything at the auction. Other parents were bidding, laughing, writing checks. She sat with her hands in her lap. Her throat tightens at the memory of sitting still while everyone else participated. Her son noticed. Asked later why they didn't bid on anything. The throat is still tight. They saw her sitting out.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP — MID
---
Linda replays the trip conversation. "Everyone's going." Her face. Her daughter's face. The no that came out because the money wasn't there. She replays it driving. Replays it cooking. Replays it at 2am. The loop doesn't add new information. It just delivers the same heat. The same face-fall. The same proof of what she can't give.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY COST HIGHLIGHTS — EARLY
---
David collects evidence of his failure. The bounced check. The time he said "not this month." The car he can't replace. Each cost he can't meet becomes another exhibit. The chest drops accumulate. He's building a case against himself. The prosecution is thorough. Each expense is an opportunity to fail.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — OTHER FAMILIES MEASURE — LATE
---
Karen tracks what other families do. The Hendersons: Disneyland. The Morenos: summer camp. The Smiths: new car for their teen. Each thing they do measures what she doesn't. The shoulders curl at every comparison. She wasn't counting before. Now she is. Every abundance someone else has curls her shoulders tighter.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — CHILD SEES — MID
---
Michael's daughter has learned to not ask for things. That's worse than the sinking. She's adapted to their reality. She stopped wanting out loud. The sinking happens when he notices her not asking. When her friends talk about plans and she says "we'll see." She's managing him now. Managing his limitations.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — EVERY EVENT EXPOSES — EARLY
---
Every school event exposes Susan. Auction: couldn't bid. Bake sale: store-bought. Fundraiser: minimum contribution. Each event is an opportunity to be seen as less-than. The throat tightens at each calendar notification. Another event. Another exposure. She goes anyway. Sits with the tight throat. Watches others participate at a level she can't.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — TRIP ISN'T EVERYTHING — MID
---
Linda's daughter didn't go on the trip. Her friends did. They came back with stories and photos. Her daughter was fine. A little left out, but fine. She had other plans that weekend. The face-heat assumed devastation. The reality was adjustment. The trip was one thing. Not the defining thing Linda's shame made it.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — CHECK REPLACED, NOBODY CARED — LATE
---
David sent a new check. His son brought it to the office. The office staff said thanks. That was it. No looks. No conversation. The bounced check existed for one day in their awareness. It lives in David's chest forever. But the staff processed it and moved on. The shame stayed longer than the problem.

---
Karen's crackers got eaten. Every single one. Someone complimented them—"these are perfect with the cheese." The shoulders uncurl slightly. Her contribution was small. It was still contribution. It still got eaten. The expensive items were there. So was her box of crackers. Both filled plates.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — DAUGHTER STILL LOVES HIM — MID
---
Michael's daughter knows they can't afford Disneyland. She asked about camping instead. "The state park trip was really fun, Dad." She pivoted. Didn't pile on. Found something they could do. The sinking pauses. She knows and she still wants to spend time with him. The limitation is there. So is the love.

---
Susan's son didn't care about the auction. "That stuff was boring anyway." He was more interested in the dessert table. The tight throat assumed he noticed her sitting out. He was eating cookies. The shame was hers. The event was his. He had a fine time. The sitting out was her story, not his.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v01 — SAYS NO ANYWAY — MID
---
Another expense Linda can't afford. The face heats in advance. She says no anyway. "We can't do that this month." Her daughter sighs. Linda sits with the heat. Doesn't explain. Doesn't apologize for being a person with limits. The no is the truth. The heat is the cost of the truth. Both exist.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v02 — DOESN'T HIDE — LATE
---
Another bounced check risk. David doesn't send the payment until the money is there. Waits two days. The chest wants to drop at the late fee. He lets it drop. Pays when he can. Doesn't pretend to have what he doesn't. The drop is uncomfortable. The check clears. That's what matters.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v03 — BRINGS WHAT SHE HAS — EARLY
---
Another team dinner. Karen brings chips. Decent chips, but still chips. Her shoulders start to curl. She lets them curl and puts the chips on the table anyway. Someone opens them immediately. "Oh, I love these." She stands near her curled-shoulder chips and watches them disappear.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v04 — TELLS THE TRUTH — MID
---
Michael's son asks about a concert. $200 tickets. "We can't afford that right now." The stomach sinks. He doesn't add excuses. Doesn't promise later. Just the truth. His son nods. "Yeah, I figured." They watch TV together that night. The truth was said. They're both still there.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v05 — GOES TO THE EVENT — LATE
---
Another auction. Susan goes. Doesn't bid. Her throat tightens among the participating parents. She stays anyway. Eats the free appetizers. Talks to other parents about non-auction things. The tight throat is there the whole night. So is she. She doesn't leave early. Doesn't hide. Just exists at her participation level.
