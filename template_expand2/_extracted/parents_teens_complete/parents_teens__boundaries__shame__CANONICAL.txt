## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — SAID NO TO TEEN — MID — FACE HEAT
---
Linda said no to the party. The look on her daughter's face—betrayal, rage, disbelief. Her face heats in the aftermath. "You never let me do anything." The bedroom door slams. Linda stands in the hallway, face burning. She had reasons. The party was too far, too late, too unclear. The reasons don't cool her face. The no made her the enemy.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — SET A LIMIT — LATE — CHEST DROP
---
David told his son he couldn't use the car this weekend. Needed it for something. His son's jaw tightened. "Whatever." Walked away. David's chest dropped watching him go. One boundary. One word—no—and now there's a wall between them. The car wasn't that important. The limit feels cruel now. He wanted to be the yes-dad. He's the no-dad today.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — PROTECTED OWN TIME — EARLY — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Karen said she couldn't drive Jake to the mall. She had a headache. Needed quiet. His face fell. "It's fine." But it wasn't fine. Her shoulders curl around the refusal. She has needs. Parents have needs. The curling says something different. The curling says she chose herself over her child. The headache is still there. So is the guilt.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — ENFORCED CONSEQUENCE — MID — STOMACH SINKING
---
Michael took the phone. His daughter missed curfew, so the phone went away for a week. She's crying in her room now. His stomach sinks. He's doing what parents do. Consequences. Follow-through. His stomach doesn't care about parenting principles. It sinks at the sound of her crying. He made her cry. That's what the stomach knows.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — SAID "NOT NOW" — LATE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Susan's son wanted to talk. About something. She was on a work call. "Can it wait twenty minutes?" He shrugged. "Never mind." Her throat tightens. He came to her—rare, precious—and she said not now. The call ended. She found him. "What did you want to talk about?" "Nothing." The door closed. The throat stays tight. She missed the moment.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP — MID
---
The party-no replays. Linda sees her daughter's face—the betrayal—on loop. In the shower. Driving. Trying to sleep. She said no for good reasons. The reasons don't stop the replay. The face keeps appearing. She's been the villain for three days in her own head. The no was Monday. The replaying is now constant.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY LIMIT MAGNIFIES — EARLY
---
David counts his nos. Monday: no to the car. Wednesday: no to the later curfew. Friday: no to the money for something vague. Each no gets bigger in his head. He's not setting boundaries. He's building walls. By Sunday, he feels like a jailer. The nos were small. Reasonable. The magnification made them monstrous.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — TAKE-BACKS — LATE
---
Karen's headache boundary lasted two hours. Then she drove Jake to the mall anyway. The guilt won. She needed the rest but she couldn't sit with the curling in her shoulders. The limit didn't hold. She took it back. Drove with the headache. He didn't even thank her—why would he? She said no, then said yes. The no didn't exist anymore.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — PHONE-CHECKING — MID
---
Michael took the phone Monday. By Tuesday night, he's checking on his daughter every hour. "You okay?" "Fine." The stomach keeps sinking. He enforced a consequence. The consequence is correct. His stomach doesn't recognize correct. It recognizes her red eyes. It sinks every time he sees them. The sinking doesn't care about principles.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — APOLOGY PATTERN — EARLY
---
Susan apologizes before she sets limits now. "I'm sorry, but no." "Sorry, I can't drive you." "I know this is hard, but sorry, no." The sorry arrives before the no. Cushioning. Softening. Making herself smaller before she takes up space. Her son barely registers the apologies. She registers them. They're evidence of the guilt she can't shake.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — LIMIT HELD AND NOTHING COLLAPSED — MID
---
The party-no stood. Linda didn't take it back. Her daughter was angry Friday, quiet Saturday, normal by Sunday. The world didn't end. Her daughter still asked about dinner. Still watched TV in the same room. The no existed. The relationship continued. The face-heat replays slowed. Not gone. Just... smaller. The limit held. So did they.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — HE SURVIVED IT — LATE
---
David's son didn't have the car this weekend. The world continued. He found another ride. By Sunday he was asking about next weekend, normal tone. The wall David felt building wasn't a wall. It was a moment. The no wasn't catastrophic. His son adjusted. David's chest is still recovering from a disaster that didn't happen.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — NEEDED THE REST — EARLY
---
Karen took the headache rest. Didn't drive to the mall. Slept for an hour. When she woke up, the headache was gone. Jake found another ride. Nobody died. She needed the rest. She took it. Her shoulders are still curled slightly. But the headache is gone. The limit produced the outcome she needed. That's allowed to matter.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — CONSEQUENCE WORKED — MID
---
Phone gone for a week. By day four, Michael's daughter made curfew. Didn't argue. Came home on time. The consequence did what consequences do. His stomach still remembers the crying. But the curfew is being met. Something connected for her. The sinking was the cost. The connection might be the result.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — HE CAME BACK — LATE
---
Susan missed the moment. "Not now" and the door closed. But Thursday night, her son comes downstairs. "Can we talk?" He came back. The window she thought she closed forever wasn't forever. Her throat loosens. "Of course." The twenty minutes she asked for Monday didn't erase the relationship. He came back. The moment returned.

## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v01 — DOESN'T TAKE IT BACK — MID
---
Linda's face is still hot from the party-no. Her daughter asked again. "Please?" The please is designed to undo her. Linda says, "I know you're disappointed. The answer is still no." Her face stays hot. The no stays standing. She doesn't explain again. Doesn't apologize again. The no exists and so does the heat. Both true.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v02 — SITS WITH THE WALL — LATE
---
David's son is quiet after the car-no. The wall feeling is there. David doesn't try to tear it down. Doesn't over-explain. Doesn't buy something to make up for it. He makes dinner. Offers a plate. "Thanks." One word. The wall is smaller than it felt. He sat with it instead of dismantling his own limit.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v03 — KEEPS THE REST — EARLY
---
Karen has a headache again. Jake asks for a ride. "I can't today." The curl starts. She lets it curl. Lies down anyway. The guilt is there. The rest is also there. She chooses the rest this time without taking it back. The curl exists. The headache gets better. Tomorrow she'll drive him somewhere. Today she rests.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v04 — HOLDS THE CONSEQUENCE — MID
---
Day three without the phone. Michael's daughter asks for it back. Early release for good behavior. His stomach sinks at her hopeful face. "The week isn't over." She slumps. He doesn't add time. Doesn't give it back early. Holds the line he drew. The sinking is the cost of holding. He holds anyway. She'll have it Friday.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v05 — LETS THE MOMENT GO — LATE
---
Susan missed the talk Monday. The throat was tight for days. She doesn't keep bringing it up. "You wanted to talk the other day—" She stops. He'll come back when he's ready. The missed moment was one moment. It doesn't need to become a conversation about the conversation. She lets it sit. Thursday he comes back. She's available then.
