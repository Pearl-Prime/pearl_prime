## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — ENERGY THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Linda finds a video from the vacation three years ago. She's animated. Chasing the kids. Laughing. Making plans out loud. Her throat tightens watching. That energy is gone somewhere. The mother in the video built sandcastles. The mother watching can barely manage dinner.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — ALIVENESS MISSING — HOLLOWNESS
---
Marcus used to feel the small things. Saturday morning pancakes. The drive to practice. The random hug in the kitchen. Now there's hollowness where the feeling should be. The aliveness that used to show up for ordinary moments is missing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — DRIVE ABSENT — HEAVINESS
---
Karen planned elaborate birthdays. Road trips. Adventures. The drive that made those happen is absent now. The heaviness fills her chest where ambition used to live. She has the same kids. She's not sure she has the energy to parent them the same way.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — FUTURE THAT'S FLAT — CHEST ACHE
---
The college brochures used to excite David. The kids growing up, the possibilities ahead. Now he looks at them and feels nothing. His chest aches for the version of himself who could feel anticipation. The future exists. His excitement about it doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — CARING THAT LEFT — EYES STINGING
---
Sarah used to care so much about the daily details. The homework, the friendships, the small dramas. The caring left at some point—she can't identify when. Her eyes sting looking at old texts where she was obviously invested.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---

---

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
