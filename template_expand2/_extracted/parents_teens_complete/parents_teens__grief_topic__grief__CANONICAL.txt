## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — PARENT DIED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Linda's mother died in spring. The kids never got the full goodbye—COVID, then suddenly gone. Linda's throat tightens when her daughter asks about grandma's recipes. The recipes exist in a handwriting that won't write anymore. The grandmother is in the kitchen, absent.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — CHILD'S FRIEND DIED — HOLLOWNESS
---
Marcus's son lost his best friend in a car accident. Eight months ago. The hollowness opens when Marcus sees the empty seat where Tyler used to sit at dinner. His son sets it sometimes without thinking. Then notices. The absence is at the table.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — SIBLING GONE — HEAVINESS
---
Karen's brother died two years ago. Her kids' only uncle. The heaviness arrives at family gatherings—the chair that's missing, the jokes no one tells anymore. Her daughter says "Uncle James would have liked this" and the heaviness doubles.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — PET DIED — CHEST ACHE
---
The family dog. Fourteen years. There since before the kids were born. David's chest aches at the empty spot by the door. The collar is still on the hook. The bed is still in the corner. The dog is everywhere in the house, gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — MARRIAGE ENDED — EYES STINGING
---
Sarah's divorce was final last year. The marriage is over. Her eyes sting when her daughter asks why Dad doesn't come to dinner anymore. The family that was is gone. The family that is has a different shape.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Linda expects them and when she doesn't. Mother's Day: expected wave. Random Tuesday finding her mom's handwriting in a cookbook: unexpected. The loss doesn't keep a schedule.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING

David forgets, then remembers. He starts to call for the dog, then remembers. The forgetting is a gift. The remembering is fresh every time.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Mother's Day. Linda braces for collapse. The wave comes—seeing the cards other people send. But she doesn't drown. She acknowledges it. Keeps functioning. The grief is huge. She's still here.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---
Marcus's son talks about Tyler. The wave comes—but there's warmth. Tyler was funny. Really funny. The stories make them laugh through the grief. The loss holds the having.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — HE WAS REAL
---
Karen's daughter says, "Tell me about Uncle James again." The telling brings the wave. But also: he was real. He existed. The stories keep him alive somehow.
