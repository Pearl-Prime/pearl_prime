================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — EVERYONE NEEDS — HEAVINESS
---
Rachel walks into the office to requests. The associate needs guidance. The client needs reassurance. The partner needs the analysis. The team needs direction. The heaviness arrives before she sets down her bag. Everyone needs a piece of her. Her capacity to care is somewhere behind the weight.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — EMOTIONAL QUEUE — BUZZING
---
James's day is stacked with people problems. The VP is upset about comp. The associate is struggling with the workload. The client is anxious about the deal. Each person needs him to care. The buzzing in his head is the emotional load. He can hear all of it. He can't feel any of it.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — SUPPORT PILE — FOG
---
Victoria has three direct reports going through hard things. One divorce. One health scare. One performance issue with real stakes. Each one deserves attention. Real attention. A fog settles. With three, she's spreading thin enough to be nearly absent.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — ASKING STACKS — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
"Do you have a minute?" The fourth time in an hour. Daniel has been someone's support all morning already. His movements slow before answering. The giving has been continuous. The tank refills between requests—except there's been no between.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — EMPATHY OVERLOAD — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Sarah listens to the analyst's career concerns. Then the associate's performance anxiety. Then the client's deal worries. Each person's feelings enter her system. The pressure builds behind her eyes. She's holding everyone's emotional weight.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — NEEDING STACKS
---
The pattern runs all quarter. Rachel finishes with associate—partner needs her. Finishes with partner—client needs her. Finishes with client—associate's back. By 9pm, she's been a resource for twelve hours. The tank doesn't refill.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EMOTIONAL CARRYOVER
---
James carries the conversations with him. VP's frustration in his chest through lunch. Associate's anxiety joins it during the afternoon. By evening, he's holding feelings that aren't his.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CARE COMPOUNDS
---
Victoria's care obligations grow. Week one: divorce conversation. Week two: add health scare. Week three: add performance concern. The care doesn't become more efficient. It just divides smaller.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — MINUTES ADD UP
---
"Just a minute" happens twenty times. Daniel counts at 6pm. Twenty minutes times twenty requests is six hours of support. Each minute felt small.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — FEELINGS STACK
---
Sarah's empathy absorption compounds. Morning: analyst anxiety. Afternoon: associate fear. Evening: client worry. Each feeling layers on the last.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — ONE RESOLVES
---
The associate figures out the analysis. Doesn't need Rachel anymore today. One person's need lifts. Something shifts in her chest. She'd been holding four things. Now three.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — SOMEONE ELSE HOLDS ONE
---
The partner takes the VP's comp conversation. James doesn't carry that one. One person's feelings aren't in his chest this afternoon.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — NAMES THE LOAD
---
Victoria writes down who needs what. Divorce: listening. Health: flexibility. Performance: clarity. Three things. She'd felt like ten.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — CLOSES DOOR
---
Daniel closes the office door. Thirty minutes. No "do you have a minute." The minutes don't stack. Something refills slightly.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — FEELS OWN FEELING
---
Sarah realizes she hasn't checked her own state in hours. Underneath everyone's feelings: her own tiredness. A feeling that belongs to her.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — HELPS ONE
---
Four people need things. Rachel picks one. Helps the associate with the analysis. Just that. The pile is still there. One person was helped.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — SCHEDULES GAPS
---
James blocks 30 minutes between emotional conversations. The meetings will still be draining. There will be space between them.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — LIMITS TIME
---
Victoria gives each direct report twenty minutes today. Not less care—bounded care. Three people, twenty minutes each.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS LATER
---
"Do you have a minute?" Daniel says, "Can we talk at 3?" The person says okay. The tank gets an hour.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS OUTSIDE
---
Sarah walks to the lobby. Three minutes. The floor exists above. She stands below not being anyone's support.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Rachel is in the development conversation before she registers it starting. The associate is talking about his career concerns. Her mouth is saying supportive things. "I hear you." "That makes sense." She's somewhere behind her eyes, watching a leader perform mentorship.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED CARING
---
James hears the news. The VP's father is in the hospital. He says appropriate things. The caring arrives three seconds later, muted. By then he's already moved to logistics. The emotion and the response are out of sync.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — LEADERSHIP MONTAGE
---
Victoria sees herself in fragments. Hand on shoulder after the hard conversation. Face concerned during the update. Voice warm at the check-in. The in-between is gone. She's a montage of supportive gestures, not a continuous person caring.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — GOING THROUGH MOTIONS
---
Daniel asks the right questions. "How are you really doing?" "What would help?" His voice knows the script. He watches himself perform a caring leader. His team seems supported. He's not inside any of it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Sarah sits across from the crying analyst. The tears are visible—theoretically. She's watching them from behind glass. Her hand reaches out. The touch happens. The moment is important. She's receiving it on delay.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — CARE ON AUTOPILOT
---
The pattern runs all week. Monday meeting: autopilot. Tuesday crisis: autopilot. Wednesday check-in: autopilot. Rachel is technically present for all of it. The caring is performed correctly.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good news: associate got the project he wanted. Bad news: VP's father took a turn. Both arrive at the same muted frequency. James is present for both. Touched by neither.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — LEADER SCRIPT
---
Victoria has the conversations. "I believe in you." "We'll figure this out." "You've got my support." The words come from somewhere trained.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — DAYS OF DISTANCE
---
Daniel's caring has been distant for weeks. Same conversations. Same supportive words. The connection that used to happen doesn't.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — TEARS DON'T LAND
---
Sarah has sat with three crying direct reports this month. Each time, the right responses emerged. Each time, the tears landed far away.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
Rachel is in the conversation when something shifts. The associate laughs at something—real, surprised. Rachel laughs too, before she can observe it. For three seconds, the glass isn't there.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAME LANDS
---
"James." The VP says his name twice. Then something catches. He's James. That's him. He's in this conversation, not watching it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
Victoria knocks her coffee over reaching for the tissue box. The spill is immediate—here, now. Not delayed. She's in her body.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v04 — LAUGH HAPPENS
---
Daniel laughs before he can stop it. Something the associate said—actually funny. The laugh comes from his chest.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — TEAR BREAKS THROUGH
---
The analyst is crying about her grandmother. Something about the specific grief breaks through Sarah's glass. She feels it. Present tense.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01 — STAYS PRESENT
---
Rachel notices herself starting to drift. The familiar float. She doesn't follow. Keeps her attention on the associate's face. His specific words.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE NEWS
---
James hears about the VP's father. Feels the drift starting. Doesn't let it. Stays in the feeling. Whatever it is. Small, but his.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — HAND ON SHOULDER FROM HERE
---
Victoria is about to touch the shoulder automatically. Catches herself. Does it from here instead. Present. The shoulder is real.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — ASKS FROM HERE
---
Daniel catches himself going automatic. Stops. Takes a breath. Asks something from presence, not script. "What's the hardest part right now?"

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — SAYS IT FROM HERE
---
Sarah is finishing the conversation. Catches herself going automatic. Stops. Looks at the analyst's face. "I really do care about how you're doing." From here.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — THE CARING THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Rachel remembers how she used to feel in development conversations. Present. Engaged. Actually curious about people's careers. Now she's performing the routine. Her throat tightens looking at her calendar from three years ago. That leader isn't in this office anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — PATIENCE THAT LEFT — HOLLOWNESS
---
James used to have patience for the emotional conversations. The same concern raised twice: no problem. The slow processing: fine. A hollowness opens where the patience used to be. He can see where it was.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — JOY GONE — HEAVINESS
---
Victoria finds an old email where she's celebrating an associate's promotion. Real celebration. Not performed. The heaviness fills her chest reading it. That leader existed. She hasn't felt that about anyone's success in a year.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — PRESENCE MISSING — CHEST ACHE
---
Daniel watches himself in team meetings. Physically present. The actual being-in-the-room that used to be there is missing. His chest aches for the version of himself who could really be with his team.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — CURIOSITY ABSENT — EYES STINGING
---
Sarah used to ask questions because she wanted to know. What people were working on. What they hoped for. Now she asks because a good leader asks. The curiosity is absent. Her eyes sting missing the person who cared what the answers were.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Rachel expects them and when she doesn't. Old team photo: expected wave. Random Tuesday seeing her old enthusiasm in a sent email: unexpected.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
James finds the patience in ordinary places. The conference room where he used to have long conversations. The coffee shop where he mentored. Triggers everywhere for a person who isn't here.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Victoria finds the missing joy through different doors. Monday: old celebration email. Tuesday: someone asking "remember when you were so excited about the team?"

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Daniel forgets, then remembers. He starts to really engage with someone, then remembers he doesn't do that anymore. The forgetting is a gift.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Sarah is fine in the partner meeting. Fine at the desk. Then she finds an old email where she wrote three paragraphs caring about someone's development, and the wave arrives.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Rachel finds the old calendar. Braces for collapse. The wave comes—she sees her former engagement. But she doesn't drown. She can hold the grief and close the laptop.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY HAS WARMTH
---
James braces for the coffee shop memory. The wave comes—but there's warmth. Those conversations were real. He was really present.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IT EXISTED
---
Victoria reads the celebration email. She felt that. The joy was real. Just because it's gone doesn't mean it wasn't real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — FLICKER RETURNS
---
In one meeting, something flickers. Daniel is actually interested in what someone is saying. The presence isn't fully gone. Just very far away.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — CURIOSITY SURFACES
---
An associate mentions a project. For a moment, Sarah wants to know more. The curiosity flickers. The caring self isn't dead.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — ACKNOWLEDGES OUT LOUD
---
Rachel tells her spouse. "I used to care more about my team. I don't know where that went." The loss is named.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — KEEPS THE EVIDENCE
---
James doesn't delete the old mentoring emails. They're proof. That version existed.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — LOOKS ON PURPOSE
---
Victoria reads the celebration emails sometimes. Not to feel bad. To remember. She was that leader.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — MAKES SPACE
---
Daniel clears one afternoon. Space for presence to maybe return. The flicker needs room.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — FOLLOWS THE CURIOSITY
---
The project interested Sarah. She follows it. Asks more. Listens to the answer. Not performing care—actually caring. Just for this moment.
