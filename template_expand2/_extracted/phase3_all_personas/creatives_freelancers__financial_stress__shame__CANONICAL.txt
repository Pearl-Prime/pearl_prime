# ENGINE: Financial exposure → self-collapse → urge to hide

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — COULDN'T PAY ON TIME — FACE HEAT
---
Maya's credit card payment bounced. Not enough in checking. The notification came while she was at a client meeting. Her face heats looking at her phone under the table. She makes money. She still couldn't cover the minimum. Everyone in her life thinks she's doing well. She couldn't pay a bill.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — HAD TO CHASE PAYMENT — CHEST DROP
---
Jake sends the third invoice reminder. "Friendly reminder that this invoice is now 45 days overdue." His chest drops as he hits send. He's chasing money like he's desperate. The client will see him as desperate. Maybe he is desperate. The words "friendly reminder" feel pathetic.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — LOWERED THE RATE — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Chloe quoted lower than she wanted to. Needed the work. The client said yes immediately—too fast. Her shoulders curl. She undervalued herself. They would have paid more. She'll never know how much more. The yes came too easily.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — COULDN'T TAKE THE PROJECT — STOMACH SINKING
---
Dream client. Dream project. The rate was too low to justify the time. Marcus had to pass. His stomach sinks explaining why. "It's not in my budget right now." The truth is his budget requires more than they're offering. The sinking is for needing money like that.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — BORROWED FROM FAMILY — THROAT TIGHT
---
Sasha asked her parents for a loan. Slow month, rent due. They said yes, no judgment. Her throat tightens cashing the check. She's a professional. She freelances. She still needed her parents' money to cover rent.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP
---
3am. Maya is replaying the bounced payment notification. The amount. The timing. The client meeting where she saw it. She should have transferred money sooner. Should have watched the balance. Should be someone who doesn't bounce payments. The replay runs again.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — CHASING PATTERN
---
Jake tracks his payment reminders. First reminder: professional. Second: starting to feel needy. Third: desperate. The chest drop gets deeper with each send. The pattern is visible. He does good work. He still has to chase money.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — MAGNIFICATION
---
Chloe accepted a lower rate one time. She's been thinking about it for three projects since. The client moved on, happy with the work. The yes-too-fast lives in her full-size. She'll never know if they would have paid more.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — STILL SINKING
---
A month later, Marcus still feels the dream project pass. "It's not in my budget." He said that out loud. To a client he wanted to work with. They probably think he's expensive. Or desperate. Or bad at money. The sinking hasn't processed.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — BORROWED ECHOES
---
Sasha paid her parents back. In full. The check is cashed, the debt cleared. The throat tightening returns every time she sees them. The borrowing happened. The repayment doesn't erase it.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — EVERYONE BOUNCES
---
Maya mentions the bounced payment to a freelancer friend. "Oh, that's happened to me three times this year. Cash flow timing is brutal." Three times. To a successful peer. The bounce isn't her personal failure. It's a business reality.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — CHASING IS NORMAL
---
Jake learns that every freelancer chases payments. It's in every forum, every community, every group. The third reminder is standard. The chest drop was for something universal.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — RATE LESSON
---
Chloe uses the "yes too fast" as calibration. Next similar project, she quotes 20% higher. They say yes—not too fast. The earlier quote was data, not failure. Now she has better data.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — PASSING WAS RIGHT
---
Marcus hears about the dream project from the designer who took it. "They were nightmare clients. So demanding for that rate." The pass wasn't desperation—it was instinct. The stomach sank for the right call.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — PARENTS DIDN'T JUDGE
---
Sasha's parents mention the loan casually, without weight. "Remember when you helped us move? This is like that." Help flows in families. She helped them. They helped her. The borrowing is exchange, not failure.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v01 — PAYS WHEN SHE CAN
---
Next month, payment covered easily. Maya sets up auto-pay. The bounce happened. She fixed the system. Moving forward without staying in the shame.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v02 — SENDS THE REMINDER
---
Invoice is overdue. Jake sends the reminder. Chest drops. He sends it anyway. "Following up on invoice #247." Professional. Necessary. The drop doesn't have to stop him.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v03 — QUOTES HIGHER
---
New project. Chloe quotes her actual rate. Shoulders curl waiting. They negotiate slightly. She holds most of it. Not perfect. Not the too-fast yes.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v04 — PASSES WHEN NEEDED
---
Another below-budget project. Marcus passes. Stomach sinks. He passes anyway. "Thank you but the budget doesn't align right now." Professional. True. Sinking and all.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v05 — ACCEPTS THE HELP
---
Slow month again. Sasha considers asking. The throat tightens. She asks someone else this time—a friend. The help is help. The asking is asking. Both things.
