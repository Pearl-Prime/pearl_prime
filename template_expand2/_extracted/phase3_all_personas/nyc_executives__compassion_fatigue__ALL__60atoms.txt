# BATCH 17: nyc_executives / compassion_fatigue / overwhelm
################################################################################

## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — EVERYONE NEEDS — HEAVINESS
---
The associate needs feedback on the memo. The VP needs a decision on headcount. The partner needs input on strategy. The client needs reassurance on timeline. Rachel's door is open but the heaviness arrives before anyone enters. Everyone needs a piece of her judgment. Her capacity to care about each one is somewhere behind the weight.

## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — EMOTIONAL QUEUE — BUZZING
---
James has three people struggling. The analyst whose father is sick. The associate going through a divorce. The VP who didn't get the promotion. Each one needs him to feel with them. The buzzing in his head isn't the market—it's the emotional load. He can hold their situations. He can't hold his feelings about all of them well.

## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — SUPPORT PILE — FOG
---
Victoria is managing multiple crises. Her mentee's career anxiety. Her peer's political situation. Her team's morale. Each deserves real attention. A fog settles when she thinks about the care she owes each one. Three crises mean she's spreading thin enough to be nearly absent for each.

## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — ASKING STACKS — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
"Do you have a minute?" The fourth time today. Daniel has been someone's support all morning. His movements slow before answering. The giving has been continuous. The tank refills between asks—except there's been no between.

## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — EMPATHY OVERLOAD — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Sarah listens to the associate's worry about the deal. Then the VP's frustration about resources. Then the partner's concern about client. Each person's stress enters her system. The pressure builds behind her eyes. She's holding everyone's weight. Her own state is somewhere underneath.

## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — NEEDING STACKS
---
The pattern runs all day. Rachel finishes with associate—VP needs her. Finishes VP—partner needs her. Finishes partner—client needs her. By evening, she's been a resource for eight hours. The tank never refilled.

## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EMOTIONAL CARRYOVER
---
James carries their situations with him. Analyst's father is in his chest through the afternoon. Associate's divorce joins it before dinner. By 9pm, he's holding feelings that aren't his but live in his body.

## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CARE COMPOUNDS
---
Victoria's care obligations grow. Monday: mentee needs support. Tuesday: peer needs advice. Wednesday: team needs direction. The care doesn't become more efficient. It just divides into smaller portions.

## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — MINUTES ADD UP
---
"Just five minutes" happens twelve times. Daniel counts at day's end. Five times twelve is an hour of being someone's resource. Each minute felt small. The total is enormous.

## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — FEELINGS STACK
---
Sarah's empathy absorption compounds. Morning: analyst anxiety. Afternoon: VP frustration. Evening: partner concern. Each feeling layers on the last. The pressure is everyone's emotions stacked.

## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — ONE RESOLVES
---
The associate figures out the memo. Doesn't need Rachel anymore on that. One person's need lifts. Something shifts in her chest. She'd been holding four things. Now three.

## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — SOMEONE ELSE HOLDS ONE
---
HR takes point on the divorce situation. James doesn't carry that one today. There's space where the associate's crisis would be.

## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — NAMES THE LOAD
---
Victoria writes down who needs what. Mentee: career. Peer: politics. Team: morale. Three things. She'd felt like fifteen. The pile has edges.

## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — CLOSES DOOR
---
Daniel closes his office door. Thirty minutes of no one needing him. The asks wait outside. Something refills slightly.

## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — FEELS OWN FEELING
---
Sarah realizes she hasn't checked her own state in hours. The pressure is there. But underneath: tiredness. Her own tiredness.

## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — HELPS ONE
---
Four people need things. Rachel picks one. Helps the associate with the memo. Just that. The pile is still there. One person was helped.

## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — SCHEDULES GAPS
---
James blocks 2-3pm. No meetings. No open door. The conversations will still be emotional. There will be space before them.

## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — LIMITS TIME
---
Victoria gives each person twenty minutes today. Not less care—bounded care. Three people, twenty minutes each. That's what she has.

## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS "LATER"
---
"Do you have a minute?" Daniel says: "Can we talk at 4?" The person says okay. The tank gets hours of not draining.

## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS OUTSIDE
---
Sarah steps outside the building. Three minutes. The team exists inside. She stands outside not being anyone's resource.

# BATCH 18: nyc_executives / compassion_fatigue / watcher
################################################################################

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Rachel is in the feedback conversation before she registers starting it. Her mouth is saying supportive things. "I understand." "Let's work through this." Her face is appropriately concerned. She's somewhere behind her eyes, watching a manager perform care. The associate seems to feel heard. Rachel is technically there.

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED CARING
---
James hears the analyst's news about his father. The words land. He says appropriate things. The caring arrives three minutes later, muted. By then he's already moved to action items. The emotion and the response are out of sync.

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — LEADERSHIP MONTAGE
---
Victoria sees herself in fragments. Hand on shoulder after the bad news. Face concerned at the one-on-one. Voice warm at the team meeting. The in-between is gone. She's a montage of supportive gestures, not a continuous person caring.

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — GOING THROUGH MOTIONS
---
Daniel asks the right questions. "How are you really doing?" "What would help?" His voice knows the script. He watches himself perform a caring leader. His direct report seems supported. Daniel is present in body.

## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Sarah sits across from the struggling VP. The tears are visible—theoretically. Her hand reaches out. The touch happens. She's watching it happen from behind glass. The moment is important. She's receiving it on delay.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — CARE ON AUTOPILOT
---
The pattern runs all week. Monday feedback: autopilot. Tuesday crisis support: autopilot. Wednesday check-in: autopilot. Rachel is technically present for all of it. The caring is performed correctly. She's not inside any of it.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good news: team member promoted. Bad news: team member struggling. Both arrive at the same muted frequency. James's face does the right things. The emotions are performed.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — LEADER SCRIPT
---
Victoria has the conversations. "I'm proud of you." "That must be hard." "We'll figure this out." The words come from somewhere trained. The team feels supported. Victoria watches herself be supportive.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — DAYS OF DISTANCE
---
Daniel's caring has been distant for weeks. Same conversations. Same supportive words. The connection that used to happen doesn't. His team gets a performance.

## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — TEARS DON'T LAND
---
Sarah has sat with three crying people this quarter. Each time, the right responses emerged. Each time, the tears landed far away.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
The associate says something unexpected. Actually funny despite the serious topic. Rachel laughs before she can observe it. For three seconds, the glass isn't there. She's in the room with another person. Then it closes. But it opened.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAME LANDS
---
"James." The analyst says it twice. Then something catches. That's his name. He's in this conversation, not watching it. For five seconds, he's inside.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
Victoria knocks over her coffee in the one-on-one. The mess is immediate—here, now. Not delayed. She's in her body. Grabbing napkins. Laughing. The distance collapses for a moment.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v04 — LAUGH HAPPENS
---
Daniel laughs at something his direct report says. The sound comes from his chest. He feels it happen. The caring underneath the laughter is real for that moment.

## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — EMOTION BREAKS THROUGH
---
The VP thanks Sarah genuinely. Something breaks through the glass. She feels something—gratitude, connection. Brief but present. Then the distance returns. But it was there.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01 — STAYS PRESENT
---
Rachel notices herself starting to drift. The familiar float upward. She doesn't follow. Keeps her attention on the associate's face. Her specific words. Stays in the listening for this one sentence.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE NEWS
---
James hears about the father. The drift starts. He doesn't let it. Stays in his body. Feels whatever this is—thin, but his.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — TOUCHES SHOULDER FROM HERE
---
Victoria is about to pat the shoulder on autopilot. Stops. Does it from here. Feels the contact. The fabric. The person underneath.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — ASKS FROM HERE
---
Daniel catches himself going automatic with the questions. Stops. Takes a breath. "What's actually happening for you right now?" The question comes from presence.

## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — SAYS IT FROM HERE
---
Sarah is wrapping up the conversation. Catches herself going automatic. Stops. Looks at the VP's face. "I really appreciate you telling me this." The words come from here.

# BATCH 19: nyc_executives / compassion_fatigue / grief

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — THE CARING THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Rachel remembers how she used to feel in one-on-ones. Present. Invested. Actually curious about her people's lives. Now she's performing the meetings. Her throat tightens looking at old emails where she was obviously engaged. That leader isn't in this office anymore.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — PATIENCE THAT LEFT — HOLLOWNESS
---
James used to have unlimited patience for his team. The same question asked three ways: no problem. The slow development: fine. A hollowness opens where the patience used to be. He can see where it was. What filled it isn't.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — INVESTMENT GONE — HEAVINESS
---
Victoria finds an old development plan she wrote for a direct report. The detail. The thought. The obvious investment in their growth. The heaviness fills her chest reading it. That leader existed. Cared specifically. That leader isn't here now.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — ENERGY MISSING — CHEST ACHE
---
Daniel watches himself at the team meeting. Physically present. The energy that used to animate his leadership is missing. His chest aches for the version of himself who lit up for these people.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — CURIOSITY ABSENT — EYES STINGING
---
Sarah used to ask questions because she wanted to know. What happened over the weekend. Who the person was beyond their role. Now she asks because good leaders ask. The curiosity is absent. Her eyes sting missing the person who actually cared what the answers were.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Rachel expects them and when she doesn't. Team anniversary: expected wave. Random Tuesday finding enthusiastic old email: unexpected. The caring self surfaces in evidence. Then it's gone again.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
James finds the patience in ordinary places. The development conversation he used to love. The coaching call that used to energize him. Triggers everywhere for a person who isn't here.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Victoria finds the investment through different doors. Monday: old development plan. Tuesday: team member saying "remember when you used to..." Same loss. Different reminders.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Daniel forgets, then remembers. He starts to get energized about the team meeting, then remembers he doesn't feel that anymore. The forgetting is a gift. The remembering is fresh loss.

## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Sarah is fine in the board meeting. Fine with clients. Then someone mentions how good she is with people and the wave arrives. For the leader who actually was.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Rachel finds the old enthusiastic email. Braces for collapse. The wave comes—she sees her former engagement. But she doesn't drown. The loss is huge. She's still leading.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY HAS WARMTH
---
James braces for the coaching memory. The wave comes—but there's warmth. He was really patient then. Really invested. The patience was real.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IT EXISTED
---
Victoria looks at the development plan. She wrote that. Cared about that person's growth. The investment existed. Just because it's gone doesn't mean it wasn't real.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — FLICKER RETURNS
---
In one team meeting, something flickers. Daniel feels something about someone's win—brief, thin, but something. The energy isn't fully gone.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — CURIOSITY SURFACES
---
Her direct report mentions something personal. For a moment, Sarah wants to know more. The curiosity flickers. The caring self isn't dead.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — ACKNOWLEDGES OUT LOUD
---
Rachel tells a peer. "I used to care more about my team. I don't know where it went." The loss is named.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — KEEPS THE EVIDENCE
---
James doesn't delete the old coaching notes. They're proof. That version existed. The patience was real.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — LOOKS ON PURPOSE
---
Victoria reads the development plan sometimes. Not to feel bad. To remember. She was that leader. The investment existed.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — MAKES SPACE
---
Daniel clears one afternoon from meetings. Space for energy to maybe return. The flicker needs room.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — FOLLOWS THE CURIOSITY
---
The direct report mentioned something. Sarah follows it. Asks more. Listens to the answer. Just for this moment.
