## BATCH: grief_topic / grief (20 atoms)

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — PARTNER DIED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maria's business partner of fifteen years. Heart attack, sudden. She reaches for the phone to ask his opinion. Then remembers. Her throat tightens. He would know what to do. His desk is exactly as he left it. The business continues. The partnership doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — FATHER BUILT THIS — HOLLOWNESS
---
Tony's father built this store. Fifty years. Tony took over when he passed. The hollowness opens walking through. His father's hands touched every surface. The store continues. The builder doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — EMPLOYEE PASSED — HEAVINESS
---
Sarah worked here since the beginning. Twenty years. Cancer, quick. Jennifer feels the heaviness at her station. Someone else stands there now. The work continues. Sarah doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — MENTOR GONE — CHEST ACHE
---
The owner who taught Marcus everything. Showed him the trade. Believed in him before anyone. Died last winter. His chest aches seeing his handwriting on old notes. The teaching continues. The teacher doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — MOTHER STARTED THIS — EYES STINGING
---
Denise's mother started this bakery forty years ago. Her recipes, her dream. She died three years before Denise could make it profitable. Her eyes sting kneading dough. Her mother's hands taught her this exact motion.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: The waves come when Maria expects them and when she doesn't. His birthday: expected. Random moment reaching for the phone: unexpected.
v02: Tony finds his father everywhere. The shelves he built. The relationships he made. The customers who still ask about him.
v03: Jennifer finds Sarah everywhere. Her station. Her jokes. The way she handled difficult customers.
v04: Marcus finds the mentor in small things. This technique—he taught him. That phrase—he said it first.
v05: Denise finds her mother in every batch. This recipe. That technique. Every morning starts in hands that learned from hands now gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: Maria scrolls past his name. The wave comes. She doesn't drown. The grief is huge. The business she promised to keep going is still going.
v02: Tony walks through the store. The wave comes—but there's warmth. His father built something that outlived him.
v03: Jennifer tells new employees about Sarah. The stories carry warmth. Twenty years. The memory keeps her present.
v04: Marcus uses the mentor's advice on a hard decision. The person isn't here. The wisdom is.
v05: Denise bakes her mother's bread. The wave comes. She keeps kneading. The bread rises. Something continues.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Maria keeps his desk exactly as it was. He existed. The desk proves it.
v02: Tony keeps his father's picture by the register. "Your dad would be proud." Customers still say it.
v03: Jennifer tells Sarah's stories. "She would have loved you." The name out loud.
v04: Marcus credits the mentor. "My teacher taught me that." The teaching named.
v05: Denise bakes the recipes. Her mother's name on the menu still. The continuation is the honoring.
