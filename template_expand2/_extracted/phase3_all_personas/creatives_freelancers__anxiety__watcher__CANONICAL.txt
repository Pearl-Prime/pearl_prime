================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Maya is in the client call before she registers it starting. Her mouth is saying the right things. "Absolutely." "I can do that." Her hands are taking notes. She's somewhere behind her eyes, watching a freelancer perform the meeting. The client is nodding. She's supposed to be here. She's watching from far away.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — DELAYED SATISFACTION
---
Jake posts the piece he worked on for weeks. The likes start coming. People are commenting praise. He feels the response three seconds later, muted and far away. The work is being received. He's receiving the reception on delay. The celebration happens around him.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — MEMORY GAP
---
Chloe can't remember the morning's work session. She was at the desk—the file is updated, the progress is visible. But the actual sitting and typing is a gap. She made something. The making itself isn't in her memory. She was technically there.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — EMOTIONAL LAG
---
The client approves the final piece. Marcus's face does satisfaction. His voice says "great, so glad you're happy." Twenty minutes later, alone in the kitchen, he feels something. Relief? Pride? The feeling arrives late. The good news is old by the time he actually feels it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSORY MUTING
---
Coffee shop office. Sasha is surrounded by sounds—espresso machine, conversations, music. She knows this theoretically. The noise reaches her eventually, muted and soft. She's working. Technically. She's watching herself work in a coffee shop from somewhere else.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — DAYS BLUR
---
Monday looks like Thursday which looks like last week. Maya works, emails, posts, invoices. The days don't separate. The freelance life is always running. She's always at the desk or on a call or somewhere in between. The weeks pass. She floats alongside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — CREATIVE MONTAGE
---
Jake sees himself in fragments. Hand on mouse, adjusting curves. Face lit by screen at midnight. Body hunched over tablet. The in-between is gone. He's a montage of creative gestures, not a continuous person making. The transitions evaporated.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — WORK ON AUTOPILOT
---
Chloe runs the client process on autopilot. Brief received. Questions asked. Drafts sent. Revisions made. Invoice sent. She watches the freelancer do freelancer things. The process is performed correctly. She's not inside any of it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — NOTHING LANDS
---
Good review: muted. Payment received: muted. New client: muted. Project killed: same muted. Marcus processes everything at the same distance. The outcomes vary. The flatness is constant.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — BODY ON SCHEDULE
---
Sasha's body knows the routine. 8am: coffee shop. 9am: client work. 12pm: post content. 3pm: admin. She watches a competent freelancer work. That freelancer uses her hands, has her face. She's in there somewhere. Or behind the glass.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01 — CRACK IN GLASS
---
Maya is on a client call when something shifts. The client makes a joke. She laughs—actually laughs, from her chest. For three seconds, she's here. In this call. Talking to this person. Then it closes. But it opened.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v02 — MAKING LANDS
---
Jake is adjusting colors when something catches. This blue. This specific blue. He chose it. It's right. The choosing happened from inside, not from observation. For five seconds, he was making, not watching himself make.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v03 — BODY RECLAIMS
---
Chloe spills coffee on her keyboard. The splash is immediate—sharp, here, now. Not delayed. She's in her body. Grabbing napkins. Feeling the wet keys. The watcher distance collapses. Then returns. But the spill was real-time.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v04 — APPROVAL BREAKS THROUGH
---
The client says "this is exactly what I wanted." Marcus feels it—actually feels it. The words land in his chest, not in the observation deck. For a moment, the approval is his. Then it fades. But it arrived.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v05 — TASTE ANCHORS
---
Coffee. Sasha takes a sip and tastes it—actually tastes it. The warmth, the bitterness. It bypasses the glass. She's in the coffee shop. Drinking coffee. For a moment, she's inside.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01 — STAYS IN THE CALL
---
Maya notices herself starting to drift in the client meeting. She doesn't follow the float. Keeps her attention on the client's face. Their specific words. Stays in the meeting for this one question.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v02 — FEELS THE CHOICE
---
Jake is about to drift while choosing colors. He comes back. Looks at the blue. Makes the choice from here. This blue. He chose it. Present for the choosing.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v03 — HAND ON DESK
---
Chloe puts her palm flat on the desk. Cool surface. Solid. She's about to drift. Doesn't. The desk is real. The work is happening here. She stays with the cool surface.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v04 — RECEIVES THE PRAISE
---
Client approval arrives. Marcus catches the drift starting. Doesn't let it. Stays in his body. Lets the "this is exactly what I wanted" land where he can feel it.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v05 — SECOND SIP
---
The coffee landed. Sasha takes another sip on purpose. Tastes it again. Two sips of being here. The glass will return. Two sips happened without it.

# DRIFT NOTES

**Surface swaps:** Office routine → freelance routine. Team meeting → client call. Work motions → creative motions.
**Engine intact:** Detachment, watching self, crack in glass, brief presence.
**Resisted:** Framing detachment as "creative focus" or "flow state." It's a pattern, not a feature.
