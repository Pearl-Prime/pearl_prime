## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — CREATIVE PARTNER GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maya opens the old project folder. The one she and Dani built together before Dani moved away, stopped creating, stopped responding. The files are still named with their shorthand. The work exists. The partnership doesn't. Her throat tightens scrolling through layers they built together. That collaboration is a ghost in the architecture.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — MENTOR RETIRED — HOLLOWNESS
---
Jake gets the email. His mentor, the one who gave him his first real feedback, is retiring. Closing the studio. Done. The hollowness opens reading the announcement. He can't send work for critique anymore. Can't call for advice. The door that was always there just closed.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — STYLE THAT DIED — HEAVINESS
---
Chloe finds her old portfolio. The style she used to work in—bold, colorful, maximalist. She pivoted years ago. Clients wanted minimal. She followed the market. The heaviness fills her chest looking at the old work. That artist existed. Made this work with joy. That artist doesn't make this anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — CLIENT RELATIONSHIP ENDED — CHEST ACHE
---
The long-term client is moving everything in-house. "We've loved working with you." Five years of steady work, ending in a professional email. Marcus's chest aches reading the gratitude. They're not unhappy. The relationship just ended. The retainer that was always there won't be anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — COMMUNITY SCATTERED — EYES STINGING
---
Sasha walks past the old coworking space. Closed during the pandemic, never reopened. The regulars scattered—some left the city, some left the industry, some just disappeared. Her eyes sting looking at the dark windows. That community was real. Those daily coffees and casual critiques. All gone to different places.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Maya expects them and when she doesn't. Dani's birthday: expected wave. Random Tuesday finding their old shorthand in a filename: unexpected. The loss doesn't keep a schedule. The throat tightening arrives on its own terms.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Jake finds the mentor everywhere. A color palette they discussed. A technique he learned in that studio. A phrase from an old critique. The ordinary tools hold the absence. Every trigger is just a creative choice. Every choice opens the hollowness.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Chloe finds the old style through different doors. A song from that era. A client who wants something similar. The Pantone color of that period. Same loss. Different entry points. The heaviness arrives through whichever door is open.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Marcus forgets, then remembers. He starts to add the client's next project to his calendar, then remembers there isn't one. The forgetting is a tiny gift. The remembering is fresh every time.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Sasha is fine at the coffee shop. Fine on the call. Then she walks past the old coworking spot and the wave arrives. Eyes stinging. For a building. For people scattered. The loss doesn't care about timing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Maya opens the collaboration folder on purpose. Braces for devastation. The wave comes—their old files, their shorthand, their shared vision. But she doesn't drown. She acknowledges the loss. Keeps working. The partnership is gone. She's still creating.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---
Jake thinks of the mentor with something new. Not just loss—gratitude. That studio shaped him. Those critiques built his eye. The door is closed. What came through it remains. The hollowness holds the fullness too.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — STYLE WAS REAL
---
Chloe looks at the old portfolio without shame. That work was good. That artist was real. The market changed. She adapted. Both things happened. The old style deserves acknowledgment, not erasure.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — STILL CONNECTED
---
Marcus gets an email from the old client. "Saw your new work. So good." They're not partners anymore. They're still connected. The relationship changed shape, didn't disappear entirely. The ache is there. So is the message.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — COMMUNITY RECONSTELLATES
---
Sasha runs into one of the old coworking regulars at an event. They exchange numbers. "We should grab coffee." The community scattered. Parts of it can regroup. The window is dark. The people still exist somewhere.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — KEEPS THE FILES
---
Maya doesn't delete the collaboration folder. The partnership ended. The work remains. Evidence they made something together. She keeps it in the archive. Not using it. Not erasing it.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — USES THE TEACHING
---
Jake applies a technique from the mentor's studio. The critique about negative space. He uses it on purpose. The door is closed. The learning walks through anyway.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — BRINGS SOMETHING BACK
---
Chloe puts one old element in a new project. A color from the old palette. A gesture toward who she used to be. The style isn't dead. It's incorporated. Changed form.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — SENDS THE UPDATE
---
Marcus adds the old client to his newsletter. They're not working together. They're still connected. "Thought you'd like to see this." The relationship continues, different shape.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — REACHES OUT
---
Sasha messages one of the old coworking people. "Coffee this week?" The community isn't gone. It's scattered. She can gather pieces of it. One coffee at a time.
