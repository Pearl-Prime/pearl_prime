# TOPIC SURFACE: not enough, don't deserve, shouldn't want, who am I to

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — NOT A REAL ARTIST — FACE HEAT
---
Maya's at the gallery opening. Surrounded by artists with MFAs, gallery representation, museum shows. Her face heats. She's not a real artist. She makes things for clients, for Instagram, for money. Real artists suffer for their work. She just... does it. The heat is for calling herself what they are.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — DON'T DESERVE THE RATE — CHEST DROP
---
Theo sends the proposal. The rate is what he needs, what his experience warrants. His chest drops as he hits send. Who is he to charge that? Real professionals have degrees, agencies, teams. He has a laptop and some software. The rate feels like fraud. The chest drop is for asking.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — SHOULDN'T WANT SUCCESS — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Jenna wants the feature, the followers, the recognition. Her shoulders curl at the ambition. Who is she to want that? Real creatives care about the work, not the audience. The wanting feels shallow. She minimizes the browser. The curl is for having desires above her station.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — WHO AM I TO TEACH — STOMACH SINKING
---
Marcus is asked to do a workshop. Share what he knows. His stomach sinks at the invitation. Who is he to teach anyone? He's figuring it out as he goes. The teaching feels presumptuous. The sinking is for having something to offer.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — NOT ENOUGH TO CALL IT A CAREER — THROAT TIGHT
---
Priya tells people she's a freelance designer. Her throat tightens every time. It's not a real career. Real careers have offices, benefits, business cards. She has a couch and wifi. The title feels borrowed. The tightness is for claiming a profession.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY CONTEXT WRONG
---
Maya's inadequacy follows her everywhere. Gallery: not real artist. Client meeting: not real professional. Coffee shop working: not real job. The rooms change. The not-real is constant.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY RATE FRAUDULENT
---
Theo's chest drops at every proposal. $500: drop. $1000: bigger drop. $5000: same drop, magnified. The amounts vary. The fraud feeling doesn't.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — EVERY AMBITION CURLS
---
Jenna's shoulders curl at every desire. Want the feature: curl. Want the client: curl. Want respect: curl. The wanting is constant. The shame about wanting is constant too.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — EVERY EXPERTISE SINKS
---
Marcus's stomach sinks at every moment he's asked to know things. Workshop: sink. Interview: sink. Client asking advice: sink. Same "who am I" every time.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — EVERY TITLE TIGHTENS
---
Priya's throat tightens at every label. Designer: tighten. Creative: tighten. Professional: tighten. The words are accurate. The claiming feels false.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — THEY THINK SHE'S REAL
---
The gallery artists ask Maya about her work. Genuinely interested. They don't know she's not real. Or maybe she is. They treat her like a peer. The realness might be something she decided, not something verified.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — CLIENT PAYS WITHOUT QUESTION
---
The proposal is accepted. Full rate. No negotiation. Theo's chest dropped for a number that was apparently reasonable. The market agreed with his ask. His body disagreed with the market.

## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — WANTING IS ALLOWED
---
Jenna's mentor says, "Of course you want recognition. That's not shallow—that's human." The wanting isn't shameful. The curl doesn't have to be the response.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — THEY LEARNED SOMETHING
---
After the workshop, an attendee says, "That completely changed how I think about this." Marcus taught something. Someone learned it. The "who am I" was answered by the learning that happened.
