# NOT: assertiveness training, empowerment, "learning to say no"
#

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — SAID YES WHEN MEANT NO — FACE HEAT
---
Maya says yes to the rush project. The client needs it by Monday. She had plans—the first weekend off in months. The yes came out instead of no. Now the client is thanking her, already sending files. Her face heats. She agreed to something her body can't afford. The weekend is gone. The no is still in her throat.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — DIDN'T PUSH BACK — CHEST DROP
---
The scope keeps growing. "Can you also add..." "While you're in there..." Jake watches features get added to the fixed-price project. He could say it's out of scope. The moment is right there. His mouth stays closed. His chest drops. He let an impossible timeline pass without comment. He'll pay for his silence in hours.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — LET IT SLIDE — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
The client used her concept without credit. Posted it as their idea. Chloe saw it on LinkedIn. Her shoulders curl reading the comments praising "their" creative direction. Her creative direction. Her silence. The curling is for what she didn't protect.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — ABSORBED THE PROBLEM — STOMACH SINKING
---
Marcus agreed to fix the developer's mistake. Not his job. Not his scope. But the client was frustrated and he said "I'll handle it." Now he's looking at code he doesn't understand. His stomach sinks. He took on what others should carry. The taking was voluntary.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — DIDN'T PROTECT TIME — THROAT TIGHT
---
The client calls at 9pm. Sasha should say she doesn't take calls after 7. Should say she has a life outside this project. She says, "Sure, what's up?" Her throat tightens. The evening disappears. She gave it away with three words.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — PATTERN OF YESES
---
Maya tracks the yeses she didn't mean. Monday: yes to the rush job. Tuesday: yes to the extra round of revisions. Wednesday: yes to the scope addition. Each yes came with the same face heat after. The pattern is visible. She says yes. She regrets it. She says nothing about the regret.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — SCOPE CREEP REPEATS
---
Jake's scope creeps have a shape. Q1: didn't push back on additions. Q2: same. Q3: same. Same silence. Same chest drop after. The projects pass. The pattern accumulates.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — CREDIT TAKEN REPEATEDLY
---
Chloe's ideas keep walking out the door. The concept. The strategy. The visual direction. The silence after each one curls her shoulders the same way. Small surrenders. She could say something. She doesn't.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — ABSORBING CONTINUES
---
Marcus's problem absorption is constant. Every project, something lands on him that shouldn't. Every time, the stomach sinks the same way. He sees the overreach. He doesn't stop it.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — EVENINGS DISAPPEAR
---
Sasha's evenings erode steadily. Monday: 8pm call. Wednesday: 9pm "quick question." Friday: "Can you just..." The throat tightens the same way each time. The boundaries don't exist because she never drew them.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01 — SEES THE YES HAPPENING
---
Maya is about to say yes to another rush. The yes is forming. She can feel the automatic agreement. She pauses. Doesn't say no yet. But the yes doesn't come out either. The pause is new. The client waits.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v02 — SCOPE BECOMES VISIBLE
---
Jake is mid-project when he sees it. The feature list has doubled since the contract. His chest is dropping. But he's watching himself not-speak. The silence has a shape. He's choosing it.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v03 — NAMES THE TAKING
---
Chloe sees her concept on LinkedIn again. Different client this time. Same pattern. She thinks: "They're using my thinking and I'm letting them." The words are internal. But they're words. The letting has a name now.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v04 — OVERPROMISE CAUGHT
---
The client has another problem that isn't Marcus's job. The yes is forming. He feels his calendar. Already impossible. The yes would be a lie. He says, "Let me see what's realistic."

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v05 — NEED ALMOST SPOKEN
---
The 9pm call request comes. Sasha's throat tightens. The need is right there—"I don't take calls after 7." Five words. She opens her mouth. They almost come out.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v01 — SAYS MAYBE INSTEAD
---
Maya hears the rush request. The yes is forming. She doesn't say it. "Let me check my calendar and confirm" comes out instead. Not a no. But not the automatic yes.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v02 — NOTES THE SCOPE
---
New feature request. Jake doesn't agree immediately. "I want to add that—it's outside the original scope, so I'll send an updated estimate." The words exist. The chest drop is there. The boundary is named.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v03 — NAMES THE WORK
---
Chloe's about to let the credit slide. Instead: "This is the direction I developed in our strategy session. Excited to see it moving forward." Not aggressive. Just true. Her name is on the work now.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v04 — SAYS "NOT THIS ONE"
---
The problem isn't his. Marcus says, "I'm not the best person for this fix—let me connect you with someone who can help." The words come out. The client recalibrates.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v05 — PROTECTS THE TIME
---
The 9pm request. Sasha says, "I'm offline for the evening—can we connect tomorrow morning?" The words exist now. Out loud.
