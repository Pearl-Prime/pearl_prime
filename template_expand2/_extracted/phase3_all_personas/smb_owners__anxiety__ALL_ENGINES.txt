# BATCH 1: smb_owners / anxiety / false_alarm
# ============================================================================

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v01 — BANK CALL — HEART RACING
---
The bank calls about the business account. Maria's heart races before she answers. The loan is being called. The line of credit is revoked. Something's wrong with the business. She answers. "Just confirming your updated address for statements." Her heart raced for address confirmation.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v02 — SLOW TUESDAY — STOMACH DROPPING
---
Nobody came in until 11. Tony's stomach drops watching the empty store. This is it. The business is dying. Customers found somewhere else. The lunch crowd arrives at noon, busy as always. His stomach dropped for a slow morning.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v03 — EMPLOYEE REQUEST — SHOULDERS CLIMBING
---
Sarah asks to talk to Jennifer "when you have a minute." Jennifer's shoulders climb toward her ears. She's quitting. The best employee is leaving. The whole operation falls apart. Sarah wants to swap shifts for a dentist appointment. Shoulders climbed for a schedule change.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v04 — YELP NOTIFICATION — BREATH CATCHING
---
The Yelp notification pops up. Marcus's breath catches before he opens it. A bad review. The business's reputation destroyed in one post. He opens it. Five stars: "Best service in town!" Breath caught for a compliment.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v05 — LANDLORD EMAIL — HANDS COLD
---
Email from the landlord. Denise's hands go cold before opening it. Rent increase. Non-renewal. Eviction. Something that ends the business. She opens it. "Please disregard last email—wrong attachment." Her hands are cold for a clerical error.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: Maria's heart races at every bank contact. Call: race. Email: race. Statement: race. Different communications. Same alarm.
v02: Tony's stomach drops at every slow period. Slow morning: drop. Slow week: drop. Different durations. Same alarm.
v03: Jennifer's shoulders climb at every employee request. "Can we talk?": climb. "Quick question": climb. Same alarm every time.
v04: Marcus's breath catches at every notification. Yelp: catch. Google: catch. Every review platform triggers the same alarm.
v05: Denise's hands go cold at every landlord message. Email: cold. Call: cold. Letter: cold. Same alarm regardless of content.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: Maria answers five bank calls this month. Zero problems. Five routine matters. The heart racing predicted disaster from administration.
v02: Tony tracks the slow mornings. Every one is followed by a normal day. The morning doesn't predict the week.
v03: Jennifer counts "can we talk" requests. Eight this quarter. Zero were quits. Eight were schedule swaps and questions.
v04: Marcus counts the notifications. Twenty-two this month. Nineteen were positive. The catching predicted damage from praise.
v05: Denise tracks landlord emails. Twelve this year. Eleven were maintenance or routine. The cold hands predicted eviction from paperwork.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Bank calls. Heart racing. Maria answers anyway. Handles the address update. Racing heart, completed call.
v02: Slow morning. Stomach dropping. Tony opens anyway. Serves the lunch crowd. Dropping stomach, normal day.
v03: Employee wants to talk. Shoulders climbing. Jennifer has the conversation. Handles the shift swap. Climbing shoulders, easy resolution.
v04: Notification arrives. Breath catching. Marcus opens it. Reads the good review. Catching breath, positive content.
v05: Landlord emails. Hands cold. Denise opens it anyway. Disregards the wrong attachment. Cold hands, no problem.

# BATCH 2: smb_owners / anxiety / spiral
# ============================================================================

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v01 — SLOW WEEK — 4 STEPS
---
Sales are down 15% this week. Maria's chain starts. If sales are down, cash flow suffers. Cash flow suffering means payroll problems. Payroll problems mean losing employees mean closing the business. She's built closure from one slow week. Weeks vary.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v02 — EMPLOYEE SICK — 5 STEPS
---
Sarah called in sick. Tony's chain: if she's sick, she might quit. Quit means training someone new. Training means reduced service. Reduced service means bad reviews means business decline. He's built decline from a head cold.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v03 — RENT INCREASE — 4 STEPS
---
The landlord mentions a possible increase next year. Jennifer's chain builds: if rent increases, margins shrink. Shrinking margins mean cutting staff. Cutting staff means worse service. Worse service means losing customers means closing. She's built closure from a casual mention.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v04 — SUPPLY COST UP — 5 STEPS
---
The supplier raises prices 8%. Marcus's chain: if costs increase, prices increase. Price increase means customers leave. Customers leaving means revenue drops. Revenue dropping means default on the loan. He's built bankruptcy from an 8% increase.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / RECOGNITION v05 — COMPETITOR OPENS — 4 STEPS
---
A competitor opened two blocks away. Denise's chain: if they're here, they'll take customers. Customers leaving means revenue drops. Revenue dropping means she can't make rent. Can't make rent means eviction means done. She's built closure from competition existing.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: Maria's chain runs from every sales fluctuation. Down 15%: closure. Down 5%: closure. Down 2%: closure. Different drops. Same endpoint.
v02: Tony's chain converts every absence to decline. Sick day: chain to closing. Vacation: chain to closing. Different reasons. Same catastrophe.
v03: Jennifer's chain runs every cost increase to closure. Rent: close. Insurance: close. Different costs. Same doom.
v04: Marcus's chain runs every price change to bankruptcy. Supplier up: bankrupt. Utilities up: bankrupt. Different expenses. Same endpoint.
v05: Denise's chain converts every competitor action to her ending. New store: closing. Marketing campaign: closing.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: The next week is up 20%. Maria's 15% down was variance. The chain built closure from noise.
v02: Sarah returns, healthy. The chain built decline from a cold. She's back at the counter.
v03: The rent increase is 3%, not the 20% Jennifer imagined. The margins accommodate it. The chain built closure from a reasonable adjustment.
v04: Marcus raises prices 5%. Customers stay. Revenue improves. The chain predicted exodus from an adjustment that worked.
v05: The competitor draws different customers. Some overlap, but Denise's regulars stay regular. The chain built doom from market expansion.

================================================================================
## SPIRAL / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Sales down. Maria feels chain start. Stops at link one: cash flow might be tight this week. One thought.
v02: Employee sick. Tony feels chain forming. Stops at: we're short-staffed today. One fact.
v03: Rent might increase. Jennifer feels chain start. Stops at: margins might shrink slightly. Not closure.
v04: Supply costs up. Marcus feels chain building. Runs the actual numbers. The math doesn't support the chain.
v05: Competitor opens. Denise feels chain start. Watches actual customer behavior. The chain's prediction can be tested.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v01 — MOTOR AUTOPILOT
---
Maria is behind the counter before she registers opening the store. Her hands make change. Her mouth says "Have a nice day." She's somewhere behind her eyes, watching a business owner function. The day happens. She's technically there.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v02 — NOTHING LANDS
---
The best sales day of the month. Tony should be thrilled. His face does satisfaction. The achievement lands like sound through water. Record day. He's receiving it on delay.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v03 — GOING THROUGH MOTIONS
---
Jennifer handles the morning rush. Same orders. Same faces. The motions happen through her, not from her. She watches herself run a business.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v04 — DAYS BLUR
---
Marcus can't distinguish Tuesday from Saturday except by traffic. Both had customers. Both had the performance of being present. The days don't separate.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / RECOGNITION v05 — SENSATION DELAY
---
A regular thanks Denise for ten years of great service. The warmth reaches her three seconds later, muted. The gratitude exists. She receives it eventually.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: Maria sees herself in fragments. Hand on register. Face greeting customers. Body stocking shelves. The in-between is gone.
v02: Good news: record sales. Bad news: difficult customer. Both arrive at the same muted frequency.
v03: Jennifer's routine is perfect. Open. Serve. Close. The structure holds. The person inside is missing.
v04: Weeks end. Marcus knows this because the deposit goes to the bank. Months end because rent is due.
v05: Denise maintains the store because it's there. Opens because morning comes. The body maintains the business.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: Maria steps outside for a delivery. The cold air on her face. The sensation arrives immediately. She's in her body. On the sidewalk. Here.
v02: "Tony!" A regular says his name twice. Something catches. He's Tony. He's here. In this conversation.
v03: Jennifer burns her hand on the espresso machine. The pain is immediate. She's in her body.
v04: Marcus laughs at something a customer says. Before he can observe it. The laugh comes from his chest.
v05: Denise tastes the soup she's serving. For a moment, really tastes it. Present tense.

================================================================================
## WATCHER / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Maria notices the drift starting. Doesn't follow. Keeps attention on the customer's actual words.
v02: Record day. Tony feels the drift. Stays in the feeling.
v03: Jennifer catches the drift. Comes back. The customer in front of her. Really seeing them.
v04: Marcus is about to drift. Hand on the counter. The surface is real.
v05: Denise catches the delay starting. Feels the gratitude in real time.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — EVERYTHING AT ONCE — HEAVINESS
---
6am. Maria opens her laptop to payroll, inventory order, vendor dispute, and broken equipment. The heaviness arrives before coffee. Four things that need her. Four directions pulling. The list doesn't wait for prioritization.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — OWNER PILE — BUZZING
---
Tony is owner, manager, bookkeeper, maintenance, HR, and marketer today. The buzzing in his head is all the hats. He can see all the jobs. He can't do any of them clearly.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — CUSTOMER + BUSINESS — FOG
---
A line at the counter while supplier is on hold while the bank email needs answering. Jennifer stands between them. A fog settles. Three things need her right now. She can do one thing right now.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — STAFF PROBLEMS + BUSINESS PROBLEMS — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
Employee conflict needs mediation. Inventory needs ordering. The refrigerator is making sounds. Marcus's movements slow standing in the middle. Each problem requires attention. Attention doesn't multiply.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — END-OF-MONTH PILE — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Rent. Payroll. Taxes. Insurance. Vendor payments. The end-of-month pile sits on Denise's desk. The pressure builds behind her eyes. Five things due at once. One checkbook.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: Maria finishes payroll—inventory is urgent. Finishes inventory—vendor calls back. The pile grows while she's in it.
v02: Tony wears the bookkeeper hat—manager crisis arrives. Handles manager—maintenance needed. The hats never get put down.
v03: Jennifer serves the line—supplier hung up. Calls supplier back—bank email urgent. The three things become four.
v04: Marcus mediates conflict—refrigerator gets louder. Orders inventory—more staff issues. Problems compound.
v05: Denise pays rent—sees payroll due tomorrow. Does payroll—tax deadline appears. The pile renews.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: Payroll is done. One thing off the pile. Maria's list is still full. But shorter by one.
v02: Tony hires part-time help for one hat. Bookkeeping goes to someone else. One less role.
v03: Jennifer takes the supplier call. Line waits. One thing at a time. The line waits.
v04: Marcus fixes the refrigerator. It's fixed. One problem actually resolved.
v05: Rent is paid. Denise's pile has four things now. The five became four.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Four things need attention. Maria picks one—payroll first. Just that.
v02: Five hats today. Tony does one—serves customers. Other hats wait.
v03: Three things at once. Jennifer serves the line. Line is the priority.
v04: Multiple problems. Marcus picks one—refrigerator. Fixed, then next.
v05: Five payments due. Denise writes one check. Then the next.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — BUSINESS STRUGGLING — FACE HEAT
---
Maria runs into a friend who asks how the store is doing. "Great!" she says. Her face heats at the lie. The truth is barely scraping by. The heat is for pretending to thrive while struggling.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — CAN'T PAY WELL — CHEST DROP
---
Tony's employee deserves more. He knows it. Pays what he can, not what they're worth. His chest drops when he hands over the paycheck. The drop is for being an owner who can't compensate fairly.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — OWNERS ARE SUPPOSED TO KNOW — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Jennifer doesn't know the answer to the tax question. She owns a business. Her shoulders curl at not knowing. Owners are supposed to know these things. The curl is for the gap between title and expertise.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — ASKING FOR HELP — STOMACH SINKING
---
Marcus needs to ask his father for a short-term loan. His stomach sinks dialing. He owns a business. He should be self-sufficient. The sink is for needing help.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — BEHIND ON EVERYTHING — THROAT TIGHT
---
The invoices are late. The books are messy. The storage room is chaos. Denise's throat tightens looking at it all. She's behind on everything an owner should manage. The tightness is for the accumulated mess.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05
---
v01: Maria's face heats at every "how's business?" Acquaintance: heat. Family: heat. Every question triggers the lie.
v02: Tony's chest drops at every paycheck. Employee A: drop. Employee B: drop. Each underpayment drops.
v03: Jennifer's shoulders curl at every gap. Tax question: curl. Legal question: curl. Each not-knowing curls.
v04: Marcus's stomach sinks at every ask. Father: sink. Friend: sink. Each help request sinks.
v05: Denise's throat tightens at every mess. Invoices: tight. Books: tight. Storage: tight. Each behind tightens.

================================================================================
## SHAME / TURNING_POINT v01-05
---
v01: Maria tells one friend the truth. "Actually, it's tough right now." Friend nods—their business struggled too. The truth was possible.
v02: Tony's employee says, "I know you pay what you can. I stay because I believe in this place." The chest drop didn't see the loyalty.
v03: Jennifer asks an accountant. "These questions are why I exist. Most owners don't know this." The not-knowing is normal.
v04: Marcus's father says, "I borrowed from my father three times when I started. This is how it works." The asking isn't failure.
v05: Denise hires someone for four hours a week. Just the invoices. The behind becomes catching up.

================================================================================
## SHAME / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: "How's business?" Face heats. Maria says, "Tough, actually." The truth is out.
v02: Paycheck time. Chest drops. Tony says, "I wish I could do more. I'm working on it."
v03: Tax question. Shoulders curl. Jennifer asks the accountant. Learning.
v04: Short-term need. Stomach sinks. Marcus asks his father. The help arrives.
v05: Chaos everywhere. Throat tight. Denise tackles one corner of the storage room.

---

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v03 — THEY'RE ON INSTAGRAM — PULLING INWARD
---
Jennifer sees another shop's Instagram—professional photos, engaged followers, beautiful aesthetic. She pulls inward. They have a brand. She has a store.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v04 — THEY'RE SO CONFIDENT — CONTRACTING
---
Marcus watches the other owner speak at the chamber meeting. Confident. Successful-sounding. He contracts. That owner has it figured out. He's still improvising daily.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v05 — THEY PAY THEIR PEOPLE BETTER — GETTING SMALLER

v05: Denise's employee says, "Chain offered me more. I stayed. You treat me like a person." The wage gap wasn't the only factor.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v01-05

v02: Friend scaled. Tony looks at his store. The right size for the life he wants.
v03: Their Instagram shines. Jennifer serves the customer in front of her. Her version.
v04: That owner seems confident. Marcus does what he does. His way.
v05: They pay more. Denise treats people well. Different offering.

## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — PARTNER DIED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maria's business partner died two years ago. Heart attack at 54. She reaches for the phone to ask his opinion. Then remembers. Her throat tightens. He would know what to do about this supplier problem. The partnership is still in the business name. The partner isn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — FATHER BUILT THIS — HOLLOWNESS
---
Tony's father built this store. Forty years. Tony took over when he passed. The hollowness opens walking through. His father's hands built those shelves. The store continues. The builder doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — EMPLOYEE PASSED — HEAVINESS
---
Sarah worked here for fifteen years. Cancer took her in six months. Jennifer feels the heaviness at her station. Someone else stands there now. The station exists. Sarah doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — MENTOR GONE — CHEST ACHE
---
The business owner who taught Marcus everything. Retired, then died a year later. His chest aches at random—reading advice the mentor gave him, remembering conversations in this very store.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — MOTHER STARTED THIS — EYES STINGING
---
Denise's mother started this bakery. Her recipes, her customers, her dream. She died before the business hit ten years. Denise's eyes sting kneading dough. Her mother's hands taught her this.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01-05

v04: Marcus finds the mentor in advice given. This decision—what would he say? That challenge—what would he do?
v05: Denise finds her mother in the recipes. This dough. That technique. Every batch holds her hands.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01-05

v02: Tony walks through the store. The wave comes—but there's warmth. His father built something that outlived him. That's something.
v03: Jennifer talks about Sarah to new customers. "She would have loved you." The memory is warm.
v04: Marcus faces a decision. Uses the mentor's advice. The person isn't here. The wisdom is.
v05: Denise bakes the recipe. Her mother's. The wave comes. She keeps kneading. The hands are hers now.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01-05
---
v01: Maria doesn't remove his name from the business. He built half of this.
v02: Tony keeps his father's picture by the register. Customers remember. "Your dad would be proud."
v03: Jennifer tells Sarah's stories. The name out loud. The fifteen years honored.
v04: Marcus credits the mentor. "He taught me that." The teaching carried forward.
v05: Denise bakes the recipes. Her mother's first, then hers. The continuation is the honoring.
