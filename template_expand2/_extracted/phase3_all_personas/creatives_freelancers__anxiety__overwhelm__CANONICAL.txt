================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v01 — TASK LOAD — HEAVINESS
---
Maya opens her laptop to the list already waiting. Client A needs revisions. Client B needs first draft. Invoice to send. Taxes to organize. Content to post. The heaviness arrives before the coffee. The inbox is pinging. The deadline is Friday. Another deadline is Monday. She stares at the screen, not sure which file to open first.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v02 — PROJECT PILE — BUZZING
---
Jake has four projects in different stages. One in concepting, one in revisions, one in final approval, one in invoicing. His head buzzes holding all the contexts. Each project needs a different version of him. The buzzing is the switching cost. He can hold all of them. He can't hold any of them well.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v03 — ADMIN DEBT — FOG
---
Chloe's admin pile has been growing for weeks. Receipts to file. Contracts to review. Portfolio to update. Website broken. Email backlog. A fog settles when she looks at the list. None of it is creative work. All of it needs doing. She opens a doc to start one. Types half a sentence. Can't finish it.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v04 — INPUT OVERLOAD — SLOWED MOVEMENT
---
Marcus has too many tabs open—literal and mental. Client brief. Reference images. Competitor research. Tutorial for new software. Email about the other project. His movements slow. Each task takes twice as long. The tabs multiply. The work doesn't.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / RECOGNITION v05 — LIFE + WORK — PRESSURE BEHIND EYES
---
Sasha's freelance life has no boundaries. Work emails at dinner. Life errands during work hours. Client calls during grocery trips. The pressure builds behind her eyes. Everything bleeds into everything else. By evening, she's been half-present for all of it. Fully present for none.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — TASK LOAD REPEATS
---
The pattern runs all day. Maya starts the revisions—email ping. Answers email—remembers the invoice. Opens invoice—client calls. Takes call—loses the revision thread. By 9pm, the heaviness is in her shoulders. Ten hours of touching everything. Finishing nothing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — CONTEXT NEVER CLEARS
---
Jake's contexts stack without clearing. Morning: Project A headspace. Afternoon: still holding A, add B. Evening: A, B, and C. The previous projects don't release. Each new one adds. By week's end, he's holding fragments of everything.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — ADMIN COMPOUNDS
---
Chloe's admin debt grows compound interest. Monday: ten things. Tuesday: twelve. Wednesday: fifteen. The pile doesn't shrink while she works on client projects. The fog thickens with each addition.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — TABS MULTIPLY
---
Marcus's tabs reproduce. Close one, open three. Every task requires research. Every research opens rabbit holes. Every rabbit hole spawns more tabs. The browser is a monument to incomplete thoughts.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — NO CLEAR EDGES
---
The life-work bleeding is constant. Sasha never fully works. Never fully rests. The pressure behind her eyes is the cost of no edges. Everything at once. Nothing complete.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v01 — ONE THING FINISHES
---
End of day. Maya submits the revisions. One project done. Something shifts in her chest. The other projects are still there. But this one is sent. The heaviness lifts by one project's worth.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v02 — NAMES THE PROJECTS
---
Jake writes them down. Four projects. Names, stages, next actions. Sees them as a list. The buzzing doesn't stop, but the projects have edges now. Four things. Not infinite things.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v03 — SOMEONE TAKES ONE
---
Chloe's partner says "I'll do the taxes this year." One thing leaves her list. The admin pile shrinks by one massive item. The fog is slightly thinner.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v04 — CLOSES EVERYTHING
---
Marcus closes all tabs. All of them. Blank browser. The pressure behind his eyes persists for ten minutes. Then eases. He opens one tab. Just the project that matters most today. One tab. The world didn't end in the closing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / TURNING_POINT v05 — MAKES ONE EDGE
---
Sasha declares: no work after 7pm. Tonight. Just tonight. The edge is arbitrary. But it exists. She puts the laptop away at 7:01. The pressure behind her eyes doesn't disappear. But there's a wall now. One wall.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v01 — ONE SMALL ACTION
---
Maya has been staring at the pile for fifteen minutes. She picks one thing—the shortest email—and sends it. Just that. The pile is still there. One thing moved. She picks the next smallest thing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v02 — ONE PROJECT AT A TIME
---
Jake closes three project folders. Opens one. Just the one that's due first. The others exist. He's not looking at them right now. One context. That's what he's holding.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v03 — FIFTEEN MINUTES OF ADMIN
---
Chloe sets a timer. Fifteen minutes of admin. Just receipts. Just this one category. The pile doesn't vanish. Fifteen minutes of it gets done. The fog is the same size minus one small clearing.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v04 — WRITES BEFORE RESEARCHING
---
Marcus doesn't open a new tab. Writes with what he knows. The research can wait. The writing can't. He types from the closed browser. Imperfect and moving forward.

================================================================================
## OVERWHELM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WALKS AT 3PM
---
Sasha steps away at 3pm. Twenty minutes of not working. The work exists inside. She exists outside. The pressure is still there when she returns. But she walked. That's something.
