================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v01 — CLIENT EMAIL — HEART RACING
---
The email arrives from the client. Subject line: "Quick question about the project." Maya's heart races before she opens it. They hate it. They're canceling. The whole thing is wrong. She clicks. "Can we move the call to Thursday instead of Wednesday?" Schedule change. Her heart is still racing reading a calendar request.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v02 — INVOICE SILENCE — STOMACH DROPPING
---
It's been twelve days since Theo sent the invoice. No response. His stomach drops checking the sent folder. They're not going to pay. They found someone cheaper. He's been ghosted. He refreshes email again. Nothing. The invoice is just sitting somewhere, probably in someone's to-do pile. His stomach dropped for a delay.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v03 — FOLLOWER DIP — SHOULDERS CLIMBING
---
Down forty followers since yesterday. Jenna's shoulders climb toward her ears. The algorithm is punishing her. Her work isn't resonating. She's losing momentum. She checks the analytics. A bot purge happened platform-wide. Everyone lost followers. Her shoulders are at her ears over platform maintenance.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v04 — REVISION REQUEST — BREATH CATCHING
---
"Can we chat about some changes?" The client's message. Marcus's breath catches. They want to start over. Everything is wrong. He's going to lose the project. He calls back, breath still caught. "Love the direction—just want to tweak the headline." One headline. His breath stayed caught for a headline tweak.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / RECOGNITION v05 — QUIET WEEK — HANDS COLD
---
No inquiries in five days. Priya's hands go cold checking the empty inbox. The work dried up. Word got out she's not good. Her career is ending. She looks at her calendar—she's been heads-down on a big project, hasn't been posting. The quiet is the project. Her hands are cold for focus.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY CLIENT MESSAGE
---
Maya's body responds identically to every notification. Client email: heart races. DM from prospect: heart races. Comment on post: heart races. The content varies—praise, questions, scheduling. The racing doesn't. Every ping is an emergency that isn't.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — EVERY PAYMENT GAP
---
Theo's stomach drops at every invoice gap. Day 10: drop. Day 15: drop. Day 30: drop. Some clients pay at 45 days, always have. His body doesn't learn their rhythm. Every gap is the gap where he gets stiffed. Every gap resolves.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — EVERY METRIC SHIFT
---
Jenna's shoulders climb at every number. Followers down: climb. Engagement down: climb. Reach down: climb. The metrics fluctuate constantly—that's what metrics do. Her body treats each dip as career-ending. No dip has ended her career.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — EVERY FEEDBACK REQUEST
---
"Can we talk about the work?" catches Marcus's breath every time. Monday: caught breath—client loved it, wanted more. Wednesday: caught breath—client wanted one small change. Friday: caught breath—client referring him to a friend. Same phrase. Same alarm. Zero correlation with outcome.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — EVERY QUIET PERIOD
---
Priya's hands go cold every time inquiries slow. Between projects: cold. During holidays: cold. When she's visibly busy: cold. The quiet always has a reason. Her hands don't learn the reasons. Every quiet is the end.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v01 — EMAIL WAS FINE
---
Maya opens the dreaded email. Schedule change. That's all. She sits with the mismatch. Her heart raced for "can we move to Thursday." The body prepared for disaster. The disaster was a calendar adjustment. Both things true—the racing happened, the disaster didn't.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v02 — INVOICE PAID
---
Day fourteen, the payment arrives. Full amount. On time by their standards. Theo's stomach dropped for two weeks about money that was never at risk. The client always pays around day fourteen. His body didn't have that information. The check cleared.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v03 — FOLLOWERS RETURNED
---
The platform announces the bot purge ended. Jenna's followers are back, plus some. The dip was housekeeping. Her shoulders climbed for platform maintenance. The algorithm wasn't punishing her. It was cleaning up.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v04 — SEES THE PATTERN
---
Marcus counts the feedback calls. Twelve this year. Eleven were positive or neutral. One was a genuine problem. His breath catches at 100% of calls. The calls are 92% fine. The catching and the outcomes have almost no relationship.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / TURNING_POINT v05 — QUIET HAD A REASON
---
The quiet week ends. Three inquiries land Monday—people saw her project launch, waited until she seemed available. Priya's cold hands were for people being polite about her bandwidth. The quiet was respect, not abandonment.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v01 — OPENS ANYWAY
---
Client email arrives. Heart races. Maya opens it anyway, racing heart and all. Schedule change. She responds, heart still fast. The racing doesn't have to stop her from reading. The email got answered. The racing was just weather.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v02 — DOESN'T CHECK TWICE
---
Invoice is out there somewhere. Theo's stomach drops. He doesn't check the sent folder again. Doesn't send a "just following up" at day eight. The invoice exists. The dropping exists. He lets both exist without action.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v03 — CLOSES THE APP
---
Followers dipped. Jenna's shoulders climb. She closes Instagram instead of refreshing. The number is what it is. Her shoulders stay high. The app stays closed. She can be tense without feeding the tension.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v04 — TAKES THE CALL
---
"Can we chat about changes?" Breath catches. Marcus takes the call anyway. Talks through caught breath. Headline tweak. He says "sounds good" while still catching. The call happens. The alarm was just noise.

================================================================================
## FALSE_ALARM / EMBODIMENT v05 — WORKS THROUGH QUIET
---
Five quiet days. Hands cold. Priya keeps working on the project instead of panic-posting. The cold hands accompany the work. The inbox stays empty. The project gets done. The inquiries come when they come.
