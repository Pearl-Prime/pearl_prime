## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — MENTOR DIED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maya's mentor died in April. The one who saw her work before anyone else did. Her throat tightens opening the folder of his feedback. "You have something here. Keep going." His voice in the words. The words exist. The voice doesn't. The folder is full of someone who isn't anywhere anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — COLLABORATOR GONE — HOLLOWNESS
---
Theo's creative partner moved across the world. Not death—but gone from the daily making. The hollowness opens in the studio that used to hold two people's energy. His best ideas bounced off her. Now they bounce off walls. The partnership is in a different timezone. The work feels quieter.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — FATHER PASSED — HEAVINESS
---
Jenna's father never saw her work take off. Died the year before her first big feature. The heaviness arrives when she achieves things. No one to call who'd be proud in that specific way. The success lands without its witness.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — FRIEND LOST — CHEST ACHE
---
Marcus's best friend from art school. The one who understood the struggle without explanation. Overdose, two years ago. The chest ache arrives at random—finishing a piece, remembering who'd get it without being told. That person doesn't have a phone anymore.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — GRANDMOTHER DIED — EYES STINGING
---
Priya's grandmother made things too. Quilts, mostly. Different medium, same impulse. Died last fall. Her eyes sting at the fabric store, seeing patterns her grandmother would have loved. The maker who came before her is gone. The making continues without her.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Maya expects them and when she doesn't. His birthday: expected wave. Random Tuesday finding his handwriting on an old sketch: unexpected. The loss doesn't keep a schedule.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Theo finds the partner everywhere. The mug she left. The playlist they made together. The shortcut she taught him. The ordinary things hold the absence. Every trigger is just a thing. Every thing opens the hollowness.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Jenna finds her father through different doors. A song he liked. Something he would have said. The imagined phone call she can't make. Same loss. Different entry points.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Marcus forgets, then remembers. Finishes a piece and reaches for his phone to share it with him. Then remembers. The forgetting is a gift. The remembering is fresh every time.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Priya is fine at the client meeting. Fine at the desk. Then she sees a quilt pattern and the wave arrives. For a grandmother. In the fabric store. The loss doesn't care about context.

## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Maya opens the feedback folder on purpose. Braces for collapse. The wave comes—his voice in the notes. But she doesn't drown. She reads them. Cries. Keeps working. The grief is huge. She's still making things.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY WITH WARMTH
---
Theo plays the playlist. The wave comes—but there's warmth. They made this together. The songs still play. The collaboration echoes. The loss holds the having.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — HE'D BE PROUD
---
The feature publishes. Jenna thinks of her father. The heaviness, yes. But also: he'd be proud. Not here to see it. Would be proud anyway. The absence holds the pride that would be there.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — INFLUENCE CONTINUES
---
Marcus makes something and realizes—the color choice came from his friend. The friend isn't here. The influence is. The loss doesn't erase the shaping.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — LOVE REMAINS
---
The quilt pattern. Priya stands in the fabric store. The wave is there. So is the love. Generations of makers. The making continues. Her grandmother started something that's still going.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — KEEPS THE FOLDER
---
Maya doesn't delete the feedback folder. His voice in the notes. She reads them sometimes. Not to feel bad. To remember who believed first.

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — PLAYS THE PLAYLIST
---

## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — TELLS HIM ANYWAY
---
Jenna talks to her father sometimes. Out loud, alone. "Dad, I got the thing." The words go nowhere and somewhere. The telling continues without the hearing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — CREDITS THE FRIEND
---
Marcus mentions his friend in interviews sometimes. "My late friend taught me that." The name out loud. The influence acknowledged. The loss carried forward.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — MAKES IN HER HONOR
---
Priya makes something with a quilt-inspired pattern. For her grandmother. Who'll never see it. The making is the honoring.
