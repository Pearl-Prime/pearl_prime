## SHAME / RECOGNITION v01 — TYPO IN FINAL — FACE HEAT
---
Maya spots the typo after she's sent the final files. "Teh" instead of "the." In the header. Her face heats immediately. She sent that to the client. They'll see it. Everyone will see it. She sends a correction immediately, face still hot. "Sorry—updated file attached." The mistake was small. The heat is total.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v02 — WORK REJECTED — CHEST DROP
---
The client doesn't like the direction. "This isn't quite what we discussed." Jake's chest drops reading the words. He misunderstood. He spent thirty hours on the wrong thing. Everyone at that company saw his mistake. The drop continues while he types "I'll revise based on your notes." He was wrong. Publicly.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v03 — LATE DELIVERY — SHOULDERS CURLING
---
Chloe missed the deadline. By a day. She had a reason—other project exploded—but the lateness exists. Her shoulders curl sending the apology email. She's unreliable now. They'll tell other people. Her reputation as someone who delivers is damaged. The project is done. The curling is for being late at all.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v04 — PRICE TOO HIGH — STOMACH SINKING
---
Marcus quotes his rate. The client pauses. "That's a bit more than we budgeted." His stomach sinks. He asked for too much. He's out of touch. He should know his worth and clearly he doesn't. He offers to adjust. The sinking continues. He was exposed as someone who doesn't know what they're worth.

================================================================================
## SHAME / RECOGNITION v05 — POST FLOPPED — THROAT TIGHT
---
Sasha's post gets twelve likes. Twelve. Her peers are getting hundreds. Her throat tightens looking at the number. The work isn't good enough. Everyone can see it's not good enough. The algorithm told the truth about her. Twelve people care. The tightness is for the visible evidence of mediocrity.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — REPLAY LOOP
---
3am. Maya is replaying the typo. "Teh." In the header. She should have proofread more. Should have waited a day. Should be someone who doesn't make that mistake. The replay runs again. Same word. Same heat. Different angles of the same failure.

================================================================================
## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — PATTERN OF MISSES
---
Jake tracks his rejections. Monday: wrong direction. Last month: scope misunderstood. Three months ago: style didn't match. Each miss brings the same chest drop. The same sense of being caught misunderstanding. The drops accumulate like evidence.

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — MAGNIFICATION

## SHAME / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — TWELVE ECHOES
---
Sasha checks the post again. Still twelve. It's been three days. The number is carved in her brain now. Twelve. Every new post, she fears the twelve. The audience moved on. Her body hasn't.

---
Two weeks since the typo. Maya has talked to the client three times since. Nobody mentioned "teh." Not awkwardly avoiding it—just didn't notice or didn't care. The mistake that's been running on loop didn't register for them.
