## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v01 — PEER'S FEATURE — TIGHTENING
---
Maya sees the feature article. Her design school classmate, profiled in the industry magazine. Same graduation year. Different trajectory. Her chest tightens scrolling through the photos. They started from the same place. That person is being profiled. Maya is still pitching. Same education. Different visibility.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v02 — FOLLOWER GAP — SHRINKING
---
Jake checks a peer's Instagram. 80K followers. He has 3K. Same style. Same niche. Different numbers. Something shrinks in his chest scrolling their grid. Their work isn't better—maybe. But the audience is twenty-six times larger. Same output. Different reception.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v03 — CLIENT ROSTER — PULLING INWARD
---
Chloe sees the "select clients" on a competitor's website. Nike. Apple. The New York Times. Her client list has no logos anyone would recognize. She pulls inward scrolling through their portfolio. They work with giants. She works with startups no one's heard of. Same skills. Different tier.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v04 — RATE GAP — CONTRACTING
---
Marcus learns what the other freelancer charges. Three times his rate. For similar work. His chest contracts hearing the number. They can charge that. He can't. Same deliverable, different value. He's worth a third of someone doing the same thing.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v05 — EASE GAP — GETTING SMALLER
---
Sasha watches the successful creative on the podcast. "I just post when I feel like it. Clients come to me." No hustling. No chasing. The work just flows. Sasha is still cold-emailing, still pitching, still chasing. Same field. Different gravity. Getting smaller listening to someone describe her struggle as unnecessary.
