# Topic: DEPRESSION
# Engine: GRIEF

# ENGINE: Loss woven into depression — mourning energy, aliveness, former self

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v01 — CREATIVITY THAT'S GONE — THROAT TIGHT
---
Maya finds an old sketchbook. The pages are full of ideas—wild, unfiltered, excited. Her throat tightens flipping through. That creative energy is gone somewhere. She doesn't know when it left. The artist in the sketchbook made things for joy. The one holding it can barely start a client project.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v02 — PASSION MISSING — HOLLOWNESS
---
Theo used to love the work. Really love it. Staying up late because he wanted to, not because deadline. Now there's hollowness where the passion should be. The drive that used to show up for new projects is missing. He can remember having it. The space where it was is just space now.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v03 — EXCITEMENT ABSENT — HEAVINESS
---
A dream client reaches out. Jenna should be thrilled. The heaviness fills her chest instead of excitement. She has what she wanted. She's not sure she has the energy to do it. The goal arrived. The feeling that was supposed to come with it didn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v04 — VISION THAT'S FLAT — CHEST ACHE
---
The personal project used to excite Marcus. The thing he'd make when he had time. Now he has time. He looks at the notes and feels nothing. His chest aches for the version of himself who could feel possibility. The project exists. His excitement about it doesn't.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / RECOGNITION v05 — JOY THAT LEFT — EYES STINGING
---
Priya used to feel joy in the making. The actual doing of the work—hands moving, ideas flowing. The joy left at some point. She can't identify when. Her eyes sting looking at old work where she was obviously invested. That creative felt things. This one is going through motions.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — WAVE PATTERN
---
The waves come when Maya expects them and when she doesn't. Finding old work: expected wave. Random Tuesday hearing herself described as "so creative": unexpected. The former self surfaces in evidence. Then it's gone again.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — ORDINARY TRIGGERS
---
Theo finds the passion in ordinary places. The software he used to open excitedly. The coffee shop where ideas used to flow. The notebook full of concepts he can't remember caring about. Triggers everywhere for a feeling that's gone.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — SAME LOSS DIFFERENT DOORS
---
Jenna finds the missing excitement through different doors. Monday: old client email where she was obviously thrilled. Tuesday: friend asking "remember when you couldn't stop talking about this?" Same loss. Different reminders.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — FORGETTING THEN REMEMBERING
---
Marcus forgets, then remembers. He starts to feel something about the project, then remembers he doesn't feel that anymore. The forgetting is a tiny gift. The remembering is fresh loss.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — WAVES DON'T ASK
---
Priya is fine in the client meeting. Fine at the desk. Then she finds a piece she made three years ago and the wave arrives. For the joy she used to have. The loss doesn't care about timing.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v01 — WAVE DOESN'T DESTROY
---
Maya finds the sketchbook. Braces for collapse. The wave comes—she sees her former creativity. But she doesn't drown. She can hold the grief and close the book. The loss is huge. She's still functioning.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v02 — MEMORY HAS WARMTH
---
Theo braces for the old project files. The wave comes—but there's warmth. He was really into that work. Really alive making it. The passion was real. Missing it is sad. It was real though.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v03 — IT EXISTED
---
Jenna looks at the excited emails. She felt that. Built from that. The excitement existed. Just because it's gone doesn't mean it wasn't real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v04 — FLICKER OF INTEREST
---
In one moment, something flickers. Marcus feels something about the project—brief, thin, but something. Interest, maybe. The feeling isn't fully gone. Just very far away.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / TURNING_POINT v05 — MOMENT OF JOY
---
Priya is making something and for a moment, she feels it. The old joy. Small but real. The former self flickered through. Reachable, possibly.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v01 — ACKNOWLEDGES OUT LOUD
---
Maya tells a friend. "I don't feel creative like I used to. I don't know where it went." The loss is named. Spoken. Real.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v02 — KEEPS THE EVIDENCE
---
Theo doesn't delete the old project files. They're proof. That version existed. The passion was real. He keeps the evidence.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v03 — LOOKS ON PURPOSE
---
Jenna looks at the old emails sometimes. Not to feel bad. To remember. She was that creative. The excitement existed.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v04 — MAKES SPACE
---
Marcus clears his afternoon. Space for something to maybe return. The flicker needs room. He makes room.

================================================================================
## GRIEF / EMBODIMENT v05 — FOLLOWS THE JOY
---
The moment of joy happened. Priya follows it. Keeps making. Stays with the feeling while it's there. Small. But present.
