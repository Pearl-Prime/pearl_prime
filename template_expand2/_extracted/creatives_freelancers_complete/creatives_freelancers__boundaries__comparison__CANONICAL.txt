# Engine: COMPARISON

# ENGINE: Sees others hold boundaries → shrinks by comparison

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v01 — THEY SAY NO EASILY — TIGHTENING
---
Maya watches the other freelancer decline a project. "Doesn't fit my schedule." Four words. No apology. The client thanks them and moves on. Maya's chest tightens. She's never said no that cleanly. Her projects always get squeezed in. That person just... declined. Same role. Different ability to protect time.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v02 — THEY HOLD SCOPE — SHRINKING
---
Jake sees how the established designer handles scope creep. "That's outside the current contract. Happy to quote an add-on." Immediate. Clear. Jake has been absorbing additions for months. This designer just... doesn't. Same clients. Different spine.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v03 — THEIR CREDIT STICKS — PULLING INWARD
---
Chloe notices how the senior creative handles attribution. Their name is on everything. Credit follows their ideas automatically. Chloe's ideas walk out the door under other names. Same quality work. Different claim on it.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v04 — THEIR RATES ARE FIRM — CONTRACTING
---
Marcus watches another freelancer negotiate. "That's my rate. I can adjust scope if budget is tight, but the rate stays." No flinching. His rate always becomes a discussion. Their rate is a fact. Same market. Different firmness.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / RECOGNITION v05 — THEIR TIME IS THEIRS — GETTING SMALLER
---
Sasha sees the Instagram post. "I don't check messages after 5pm. Boundaries are non-negotiable." The comments are supportive. Sasha is checking client messages at 10pm. Same business. Different walls. Getting smaller reading about someone's protected evenings.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v01 — EVERY CLEAN NO HIGHLIGHTS THE GAP
---
Maya tracks other freelancers' nos. Monday: peer declines a rush project. Tuesday: acquaintance says no to a bad-fit client. Wednesday: colleague turns down revision round. Each clean no highlights her cluttered yes. The comparisons arrive automatically.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v02 — THEIR CONTRACTS STAY HONORED
---
Jake watches scope boundaries get maintained everywhere except his projects. That designer holds the line. This one adds fees. The other one just refuses. His contracts always expand. Same paperwork. Different enforcement.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v03 — THEIR NAMES STAY ATTACHED
---
Chloe sees credit stick to other people's work. The retweets with attribution. The portfolio with clear ownership. The "designed by" that follows the project. Her work travels anonymously. Same effort. Different attachment.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v04 — THEIR NUMBERS DON'T MOVE
---
Marcus compares negotiations. That person's rate: firm. This person's rate: firm. His rate: always negotiated down. The rates start similar. The outcomes differ. His number moves. Theirs don't.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / MECHANISM_PROOF v05 — THEIR BOUNDARIES ARE RESPECTED
---
Sasha compares what happens when boundaries are stated. The "no messages after 5" person: clients respect it. Sasha: clients message whenever. Same words, different results. Or maybe different words. Or different conviction.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v01 — SEES THE MEASURING
---
Maya catches herself mid-comparison. Measuring her inability to say no against someone else's apparent ease. Different clients. Different situations. Different relationship to need. The comparison was assuming a shared baseline.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v02 — THEY LEARNED IT
---
Jake talks to the scope-holding designer. "I used to absorb everything. Took me years to learn to hold the line." The holding was learned, not natural. The gap between them is practice, not personality.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v03 — DIFFERENT LEVERAGE
---
Chloe realizes the credit-keeping creative has different leverage. Bigger following. More demand. The credit sticks because they can afford to insist. Chloe's position is different. The comparison skipped context.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v04 — FIRMNESS HAS COSTS
---
Marcus learns the firm-rate freelancer loses 30% of leads. The firmness has a price. They can afford to pay it. Different business stage. Different reserves. The comparison wasn't apples to apples.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / TURNING_POINT v05 — THEIR PATH ISN'T MINE
---
Sasha stops scrolling the boundaries post. That person's system is theirs. Built over years, with different clients, different financial cushion. Her system will be hers. Not a copy. Not a comparison.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v01 — TRIES HER VERSION
---
Maya sees someone decline easily. The tightening happens. She tries her own version. "I can't take that on right now—here's someone who might help." Not as clean. Not as short. But said.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v02 — HOLDS WHAT HE CAN
---
Jake sees others hold scope better. He tries one: "This addition would need a change order." Not perfectly firm. But the words exist. His version.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v03 — NAMES HER WORK
---
Chloe can't enforce credit like the senior creative. She can name her contribution when it's relevant. "This is the direction I developed." Her version of keeping her name attached.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v04 — HOLDS THIS ONE
---
The firm-rate freelancer doesn't flinch. Marcus can't match that. He can hold on this project. "My rate for this scope is X." One project at a time. His pace.

================================================================================
## COMPARISON / EMBODIMENT v05 — MAKES ONE WALL
---
Sasha can't do "no messages after 5." She can do "no calls after 7." One wall. Her version. Not the Instagram version. Hers.
