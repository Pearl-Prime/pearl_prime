## RECOGNITION v01 - morning before presentation
---
path: gen_alpha_students/anxiety/false_alarm/recognition/micro/v01.txt
---
It's 6:47am. Maya is already awake. The presentation isn't until fourth period. Nothing is wrong yet. But her chest is tight. Her hands are cold. She checks the slides again. Nineteen times since yesterday. The screen glows in the dark room. Her body screams danger. There is no danger. Just a Tuesday. Just a presentation she's done three times already. She pulls the blanket up. Her heart pounds like something is chasing her. Nothing is chasing her. The door is closed. The house is quiet. Her body doesn't believe it.

## TURNING_POINT v01 - storm that didn't come
---
path: gen_alpha_students/anxiety/false_alarm/turning_point/micro/v01.txt
---
Aisha spent all week waiting for the storm. The friend group drama. The confrontation. She rehearsed what to say. Planned escape routes. Lost sleep over it. Then Friday came. Nothing happened. Everyone ate lunch together. Laughed at the same jokes. No storm. She sat with her tray, heart ready for impact. Impact never came. Her body had prepared for a war that wasn't real. She wonders, just for a second: what if her warning system doesn't know the difference? What if the alarm is just... an alarm? Not a prediction. Not a fact. Just noise.

## EMBODIMENT v01 - alarm trusted once
---
path: gen_alpha_students/anxiety/false_alarm/embodiment/micro/v01.txt
---
Tyler sets the alarm. 6:30am. He watches the red numbers lock in. His hand moves toward the phone to check again. Then stops. He breathes. The alarm is set. He saw it. He lets the phone stay on the nightstand. The urge to check buzzes in his chest. He lets it buzz. Doesn't answer it. Twenty minutes later, he's still awake. But he hasn't checked. The alarm will go off. It doesn't need his worry to work. He closes his eyes. The phone stays dark.
